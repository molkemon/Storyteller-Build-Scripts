You wake up in the arms of your double, who is staring intently at the mirror, taking long, lazy blinks at a head that stares furiously back, slowly squeezing its way out of a dark mirror onto the head of a still, headless statue. The statue is dressed in another of the play's costumes, a fetching number you've noticed the stage hand working on for the past however long. The head's eyes roll back as its ears make the transition from reflection to reality, finally giving its jaw the space to open and close as it finishes making its journey of attaching the spinal column.
