A farce is what it all is. Hope was a lie. The relic's magic and wisdom was a misguided truth. Ultimately, it all came back to bite the Magi Council. What we learned, or what we did, depending on what a few Wattoi described, was a ritual: not to close the hole, but to resurrect someone out of it. They spoke as if they were sinners who had seen God face to face: "He smelled of sulfur, a gaping hole in his chest." "What were they thinking... they promised us better..." A man shook in the corner; a woman cried out, her face singed: "He spoke in a tone I can't even describe. How do you threaten someone with such a soothing voice?" The clan leader of the Wattoi was staring off into the distance intensely. He too had abandoned camp, leaving someone else to rule the remainder of the Wattoi. "He has returned, and he told me to tell the leaders of the world: Submit, or face execution." Brutally simple, brutally honest, brutally true. In the end, Almadhab would most likely have it too.
