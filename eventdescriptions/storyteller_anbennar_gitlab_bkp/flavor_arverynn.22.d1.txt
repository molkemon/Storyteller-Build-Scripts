Deep from the Netherworld he came, building an elevator out of wooden parts. Through much of the land he passed, rewarding his hosts’ hospitality in kind: in one region he first made a name for himself by designing an island temple with archways that stretched towards both sides of the river, in another he drew a project for a new town hall suspended high up amongst the trees, to the people of a settlement he gave tools to sanitise and clear out the rubble so they could venture into their ancient city’s ruins, in a fortified city he planned a skyward turret of several floors extending diagonally out of the main palace, and in another region he helped with the works on the reservoir and the expansion of the dam’s gates. Already his exploits were known when the ruler heard of him. A carriage with gold and silver linings was sent to greet him, and he accepted the invitation to the capital, where surely his mind would be occupied with projects for years to come.
