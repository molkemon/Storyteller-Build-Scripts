When the vote passed, the Chamber erupted into cheers: "For Dawn!" "For Nayiru!" "For the Kamrayakval!" "For the Silverspite!" Even the guards thumped their ceremonial spears against the marble floor. The Kamrayakval looked out over the Kavalali, at last doing their duty to Dawn with pride—and eyed those few who still could not bear the brilliant light. When the cries died down, the Kamrayakval stood again—though he did not speak from the benches. He crossed the chamber floor, to the Reticent High Kavalal, sitting dejected, despondent. Placing a hand on his shoulder, he said, "You don’t understand now. You won’t for a while yet, I fear. But believe me… it will be worth it."

The Kamrayakval turned to the Kavalakuttam. "One day… far in the future, maybe… there will be a moment—a great invisible line in the history of all things." He stepped to the center of the round-chamber. "And what is this moment? It is the instant where there are no more tyrants. Where there is no slavery. Where there is no Darkness. And the ashes of the Interlopers are but food for the luscious soil, and the anguished cries of Nayiru’s enemies a whisper on the wind." He raised a fist. "This moment is Dawn."

The Kamrayakval turned back to the High Kavalal. "From that day on… all will have been made right. From that day on, we can put down the sword. But not now." Smirking, he said, "Not yet. There is one last step we must take—for before the Cycle’s beginning…"
