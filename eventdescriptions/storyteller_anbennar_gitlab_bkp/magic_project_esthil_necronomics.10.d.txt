Initial investigations into the phenomenon known as 'undecaying zombies' were focused on determining why these zombies did not decay. The first experiments involved placing newly raised zombies in Death Wards of varying power. Each Death Ward successfully prevented decay, although the zombies still decayed at a slower rate than regular zombies once removed from the wards.

Next, efforts were made to create even weaker Death Wards, which proved challenging. By reducing the power in the ritual and performing certain steps poorly, a series of Death Wards of decreasing power were produced. Even the strongest of these were unable to kill simple flies, but they still prevented decay. All but the two weakest were effective in slowing decay.

Attempts to understand the underlying cause have led to disagreements among researchers. The leading theory suggests that tiny, imperceptible creatures are responsible for the decay and are killed by any but the weakest Death Wards. However, other theories exist.

Despite the ongoing debates, the phenomenon remains consistent and repeatable. The current focus is on using a soul powering an Animate Dead spell to simultaneously power a Death Ward, allowing for the creation of zombie laborers that do not decay. This would enable the harnessing of their strength for field work.

An allocation of magical resources would be appreciated.
