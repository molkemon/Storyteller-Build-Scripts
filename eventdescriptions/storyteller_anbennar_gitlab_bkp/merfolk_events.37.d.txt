A spyglass bobbed amid the ocean swell, its leering lens always pointed at their shores. Some days, it peepped out for hours, watching. Watching. Watching. As locals dreaded the doubtlessly nefarious oculus' intention, a daring fisher managed to sneak up on the scope, and after a fierce struggle, the wrangler claimed their prize: a merfolk clinging to the spyglass. Dragged to shore, local authorities pestered their netted prisoner for answers. Thoroughly rattled, the merfolk stammered through a thousand explanations: their aunt had bequeathed them the "artifact," they promised to share what occurred on the shore with a sick sibling, the lands above the waves teemed with captivating vibrance.The local authorities mulled over the reasons, considering that it may well be as innocuous as the merfolk divulged. If we were gracious enough, we could even sponsor a oneofakind tour to see our lands up close. Yet, what if this is all a front to snoop on us? Could we allow a caught spy to simply swim away freely?
