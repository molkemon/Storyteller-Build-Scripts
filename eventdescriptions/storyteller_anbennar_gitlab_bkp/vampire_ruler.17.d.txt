Dawn. The cresting of the sun’s rays above the horizon. In Bulwar, it is a call to the Sun Cults faithful, a reminder of Surael’s glory. In Anbencost, it signals a vast flood of people to rise from cramped quarters and make their way to equally cramped workhouses. In Gerudia, it demands a dozen skalds sing again the song of the Dragon and the Skald, to ensure solar warmth is never drowned out by draconic ice. But for our lord, it is a frustrating sight – the marker of a fruitless, bloodless night. The hunger still gnaws at them. But every vampire is a nocturnal predator, and that is true of our ruler as well.
