In the canon of High Philosophy's many texts, one hotly debated topic is the matter of dietary restrictions on combinations of foods. The High Gods, when pictured at table, consume what appear to be meats, cheeses, grains, fruits, vegetables; but only in certain combinations. Wheat is never shown on the same table as rice; root vegetables never pictured with fruits. This has led to the emergence of certain scholarly traditions that advocate prohibition on serving any combinations of foods not seen in the friezes of the High Temples.  This could well garner us support with the priests and gurus, but many would prefer to be able to cook as they see fit.
