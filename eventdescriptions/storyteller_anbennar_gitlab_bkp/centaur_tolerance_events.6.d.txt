How to train a stubborn animal? Herders, ranchers, and farmers throughout history leveraged tricks of their trade to address this tired question, but recent developments pose a new, perhaps final, answer: hire a centaur instead. A glut of centaur laborers started replacing animals in key occupations: construction, transportation, and agriculture. Seeing the writing on the wall, a congregation of animal breeders drafted a petition requesting state protections ranging from subsidies to outright banning "intelligent souls from drudgery." The halfequine laborers see the sanctimonious phrase for what it is: an effective ban on their livelihood. Both sides have reached out to us for support; How should we respond?
