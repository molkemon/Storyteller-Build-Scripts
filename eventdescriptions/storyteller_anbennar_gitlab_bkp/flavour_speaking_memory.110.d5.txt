In the days before the Kiamoa, there was not yet a reason to fear the sea beyond its terrible vastness. People would sail into the sea and never return, for these were also the days before we knew how to use the stars to find our way. And although the stars ached to help us, we did not know how to ask them. But among the people was a woman named Kaikala, who sang such sweet music that the people came to her with a task. If she could sing the music of the stars, perhaps they would drift down from heaven to guide the ships of Kanolowele.  Kaikala was kindhearted, so she could not help but say yes. On three different nights, she drifted farther and farther away from her island, so she could look up at the sky and listen to its music. On the first night she was lost. On the second night, she did not understand. But on the third night, she heard the music of the stars. She stood alone on her ship and sang to the night, and she moved her feet with a grace the sky had never seen before. The stars were overjoyed that someone finally had the words to ask them for help. They drifted down from the sky, and for one magic night, they swirled around the boat of Kaikala Mother of Stars, and danced in the patterns she sang. And when the sun rose and their dance was done, the stars were so happy that they agreed to stay under the sea and help Kaikala’s people find the way, as long as they remembered the words to sing.
