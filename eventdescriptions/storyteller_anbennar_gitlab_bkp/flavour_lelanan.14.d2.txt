I entered the huge tent and looked around. There were so many of the Daughters of Lel running about. The sight of so many wounded filled me with pain, but the disciplined orders left no doubt.

I watched a paladin carry a crate of holy water in and hand bottles out to all the others. The strangest part of it all, to my surprise, was that on our cots, there weren't just our mothers; enemy soldiers were as common as friends, receiving medical care at the gentle hands of Lelanàn's best.

A paladin poured soothing holy water over a deep cut, and the foe's pain subsided as they were blessed. The actual act of healing would still take many weeks. Farther away, a man's arm had been lost to cannonfire, its amputated limb cauterized by a divine blade, the pain softened by holy water given without desire.

"It was easy to show love to a friend or lover," Kyola would say, "but much harder to show love to a foe. Yet if we were to spread love to the world, we would need to mend all wounds."
