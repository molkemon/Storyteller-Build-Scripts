Zuhrah blinked madly as her brother Mkwayo shook her awake—panic setting in as she realized the sounds of battle around them, and confusion as she felt the shackles around her ankles click open. "Come, sister!" Mkwayo urged. "There are—there are—" He gave an exasperated sigh, pointing to a dark-skinned, big-bearded elf. "We are being rescued! Don’t ask questions! We must go!"

That was all Zuhrah needed to hear. Mkwayo led her and the others out of their cage, closely following the elf who had freed them—though he hadn’t had enough time to untie the shackles around her hands.

The Fangaulans couldn’t help but look around as they ran. The elves were still fighting the gnolls—had killed half of the slavers, though it seemed there were only slightly fewer elven bodies around. Zuhrah heard the crack of a whip, and her gaze snapped up to the big platform that overlooked their cages. She felt an indescribable surge through her chest as an armored elf dodged the lash and severed their gnollish overseer’s head in a single flourish of his blade.

Soon enough, they were down in a ditch outside the slaver camp, guarded by Faungaulan fighters ready to ferry their own people to freedom. The bearded elf who guided them checked that the group was all accounted for, gave a satisfied nod, then turned away—but Zuhrah rushed up and cried, "Wait!" He turned, arching his brow. "Why? Why are you doing this?!" They were from half a world away, if the stories her mother told her were true.

The elf stared dumbfounded for a moment, clearly not knowing the language—but a Fangaulan warrior soon translated. The elf laughed, then held the chains around Zuhrah’s wrists aloft. With a single, powerful blow from the hilt of his sabre, he shattered the wrought iron.
