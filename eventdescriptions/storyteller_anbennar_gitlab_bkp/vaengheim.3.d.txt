Then Vængheim spread her wings, and those who dwelled below her roost were made hers by her maidens, who waged war with spear and song and wing, bringing great shame to their foes, for none could stand against them. And many of the Olavish found it odd that now the daughters of the mountains came with spear and wrath. For they had nearly forgotten the mountains behind them, who held only old songs and tales of ages past. Many lost their hearts, thinking chains and bondage had come, for Vængheim’s maidens held a cruel aspect.

So it was when the lord of the realm took wing and flew from roost to coast to meet the folk of Olavlund, who fought and bled to halt their flight and the flight of their maidens. The men of Olavlund found their knees in their sight and said, "Our song is that of the broken, O queen, for well have you fought, and lost is our strength. Our people is yours, and we ask for pity's sake that you find your heart and let us live."

And the lord of the realm did say, "Rise, O folk of Olavlund, and find your strength again, for you have fought with valour, and I would mend the wound I have bestowed upon your spirit. I would have you raise your spear and add your strength to mine, so that both our songs may grow, to shake the earth and sky."

Then the folk of Olavlund were struck dumb, for the lord of the realm had bitten them with her song. When they found their voice again, they named her queen.
