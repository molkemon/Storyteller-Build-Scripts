It had been less than two months since Asima arrived. In that time, the court of Endhuvi had changed dramatically. The ruler found his advisors busy or abroad for various tasks whenever he sought to discuss matters of state with them. Almost unbeknownst to the ruler was that Asima was holding her own, informal councils already. Not once since her arrival had she warmed the Senapti's bed, or even stayed in his estates. While her first invitation to Endhuvi was at the behest of the court, it quickly became apparent to her how badly the state needed a new hand to guide it. She had already sent out surreptitious emissaries to the Raja and to Parusad Bhola, promising a new page in the relations between the two nations; meanwhile, her son was being carefully encouraged to observe, young though he was, to learn the lessons his mother was showing him. The few times she'd allow him to interact with the ruler were carefully stewarded and planned, usually in such a manner to highlight the current Senapti's backwards or incompetent diplomacy. Advisors visibly tensed and members of the court muttered under their breath that something had to change.
