Our lord took another sip of wine as he looked out from his balcony, at the immensity of our capital below. It was night, but even now the light of commerce, industry, and empire illuminated the sky. Taychend never truly slept anymore. The carts never stopped, the harvest never ended, the war-banners never unfurled. Our lord should be happy, he knew – but he could only grimace as he thought about the empire he ruled. They had surpassed the greatest empires of the past long ago. They surpassed them the moment they unified Taychend, really. Our lord knew now that his dynasty would not fall as the others did. Their empire would conquer, and the warlords would hunger, and on the blood of the world they would feed, the ichor of their ascension.

The cart of empire was running, running… faster yet faster, straight ahead, destined for the wall named “eternity” – the true end of the Cycle of Glory. The beginning of a new age, where Taychend would rule undisputed. It was unstoppable. It was uncontrollable. And if they looked beyond the wall and did not like what they saw? Or if the wheels were running off fate’s track, towards somewhere nobody could even comprehend?

Well, there was nothing our lord could do about it anymore. He took another sip from his wine, closed his eyes. The hum of Empire, and the screams of the conquered, and the cry of “glory! Glory! Glory!” droned on – as it did, as it had, as it would. Already, his lieutenants were drawing up plans for new expeditions, to North Aelantir, to Insyaa, to Sarhal. The king knew his realm would go further. An empire for a day, an empire for an eon, it did not matter – Taychend will rise, an Empire Unstoppable, an Empire Ascendant, an Empire Vainglorious.
