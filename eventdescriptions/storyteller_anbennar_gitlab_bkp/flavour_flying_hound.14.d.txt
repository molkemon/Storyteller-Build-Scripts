The Blade Bailiff, an unofficial title meaning he was the last to touch Calindal, the Gleaming Blade, before passing it to the next hopeful. With an uneasy expression and the fate of the entire Order on his shoulders, he waited. Proud and dignified on the outside, but deep down, he felt like part of a vanguard sent to die.

There they are. Two of them this time. Why is one carrying a sack?

"Oh ye of the Order, pray tell, is everything in order and can the ceremony proceed?" spoke the sackless one, the one from before. "We have come here today on an auspicious occasion! May eternal glory and light of chivalry shine!"

The sacked one shouted in a voice tiring to listen to. Then, they put the bag down and it clinked. Why did it clink?

"BEHOLD!" they shouted in unison, a word most worrying. After instinctively closing his eyes, the Blade Steward mustered up the courage to take a look. This part was glued, maybe with wax? He could still see most of the cracks. Why not there? Ah, they painted it over. The hilt—was that an unmelted nail sticking out? Must be. At least Alos’s fang still serves as the pommel. And they added some from canines, fastened to the dragon’s tooth with leather straps. Deep breaths.

He took a step forward and inadvertently glanced towards the goblins. Bowed down, and quite deeply. Another step. The blade could still be reforged; undoing their work wouldn't be hard. Step. Just a small setback. Halt. Get it over with. Grab the sword.

It felt like if he held the hilt too tightly, it would shatter once more. But in that moment, he knew. What was broken wasn't the blade. It was him. After all, what's important is trying your best.

Both goblins were then knighted as Stewards, leaving with a newfound spring in their step. No one ever spoke of what transpired, though some say that the Blade Bailiff’s heart grew three sizes that day.
