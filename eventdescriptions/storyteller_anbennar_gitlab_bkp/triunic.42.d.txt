The vast majority of our merchants choose to only set sail at dawn on clear days, both out of a practical concern for fair weather during their voyage, but also because they believe it signifies a good omen from our Goddess. The calm waves lapping at the hull while the sun glints off the water give a sense of calm that brings one closer to Yukel. This occasionally leads to a phenomenon known as the sunrise rush, where many merchant vessels all try to schedule departures within the same two-hour period in the morning, far more than the harbor can handle. [triunic_sunrise_rush_province.GetName] has been experiencing the sunrise rush far more frequently than is normally expected.
