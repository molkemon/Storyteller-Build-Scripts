The centaurs cannot easily fit into many of our buildings, but have quickly set up a few of their tents outside of our outpost, into which the governor and their attendants have followed the ordsikar. The ordsikar apologizes for the low-quality tent and assures the governor that the ordsikar will be bringing his personal, more extravagant tent on their next meeting, should room be made for it. The governor assures him that there shall be space designated adjacent to the outpost to house it, and whichever other tents he should require. It has quickly become apparent that these particular clans of centaurs have no ill-will towards our people, and the purpose of this visit is to establish a mutually beneficial relationship with us going forward. For them, a place to barter and trade. For us, protection from other, less-friendly centaurs, whom they assure us will likely come for us at some point. They proceed to describe confusing idiosyncrasies in how each clan worships their sky-god, with some clans following bloodthirsty and destructive ways not practiced by these centaurs. If these more-violent clans ever make an appearance here, the ordsikar before us has promised their defeat.
