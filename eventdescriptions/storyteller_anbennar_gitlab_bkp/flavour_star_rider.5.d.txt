The ruler looks towards the bustling city of tents in the capital. Sights unseen before in the plains, yet seen by him in advance. He spots an unlikely pair in those streets, a Maslekhuga and Irdukhuga shaman, united in their love of Shadhavar cheese. One of their names was Baildelk, if he remembers correctly. Not that it matters. What matters is the conclave. An assembly of shamans of Irdaeos' Worship is a common occurrence, but not to this extent. Thousands upon thousands of shamans have arrived, flocking upon the bait that was set. The codification of faith, the promise of every single shaman's voice being allowed to speak in this assembly. A chance to mend the sects of the plains for the first and final time. This is true, of course. Very, very true. And seeing the excitement of the thousands of people, shuffling around him, struck with the same hope for a better day that the Star Riders were struck with—back on that fateful day in 1444—... it is intoxicating. That which we have torn apart has been mended, and made much more beautiful than before. It matters not that we have dominated the plains through bloodshed, that we are their overlords receiving gifts and tribute. We are all just followers of Irdaeos on this most brilliant day.
