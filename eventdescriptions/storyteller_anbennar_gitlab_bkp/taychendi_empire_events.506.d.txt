The assembled warlords raised an eyebrow as the lord stalled. "I just…" the lord paused, choosing his words carefully. "I just want to know the exact reasons. For my speech, later." A high-ranking warlord raised his voice. "Well... it would mark the definitive end of the cycle of Glory. Show that we've surpassed those who came before—both in this cycle and the last. Destroying the last embers of the Precursor Empire."

The lord turned to his heir. "While ruining swathes of our conquests in the process?" The warlord shrugged. "So what? We haven't been lenient before." The two stared each other down for a moment. The room grew tenser—then one of the Mudaliar cousins cut through it. "My family has shaken off most of Tacenie's legacy... but it was once the home of our founder. Are we really an Empire that wipes away the legacy of those who came before? We haven't gone hunting for the dragons like the Slaver-Nobles did, have we?"

"And our journeys to Insyaa and Sarhal?" another warlord called. "What of them?" "Fine, but have we copied everything from the Slaver Nobles?" the Mudaliar shot back. "I don't think we spend most of our time on useless pursuits—" A Cennayakuta noble in the crowd drew his saber. "Says you, scion of Tacenie! Why deny us our glory at this most pivotal hour?!"

The lord stepped in, raised his arms—the crowd fell silent instantly. If the lord allowed Tacenie to fall, signaling the new age through destruction and devastation, would he not be giving in to the worst impulses of this current cycle? And falling prey to the weaknesses of the old? Would it really be looking forwards, or looking back? Were they really ending the cycle... or just bringing its excesses to new heights?
