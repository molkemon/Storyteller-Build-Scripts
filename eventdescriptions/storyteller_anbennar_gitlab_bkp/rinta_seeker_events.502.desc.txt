A great gathering of all available Mwalim and Darwiiz in our realm has brought together many perspectives on the indescribable events occurring in the skies above us. Despite much disagreement about nearly every aspect of the situation, a single undeniable truth has emerged: this spectacle of lights must be understood, and to do that, we must watch it. The conference has unanimously approved the construction of a colossal telescope, one powerful enough to see the faces of the dead gods themselves in clear detail; hence the name, “Godswatch.”

However, the agreements end there. The rationalist Mwalim have come to the general consensus that the purpose of such an observatory should be entirely practical: the studying of the celestial bodies of the heavens, their rules and mechanisms, so that we may better understand the order of things back on Halann. On the other hand, the more traditionalist Darwiiz believe the telescope’s purpose should be purely spiritual: it should be used to view the remains of the dead gods, spread across the sky by the souls who slew them, so that their evil may be understood and the pursuit of enlightenment may be furthered.

It is vital for this debate to be resolved before the construction of the Godswatch can proceed. We must make a declaration on what the purpose of the observatory should be—whether it be the Mwalim pursuit of reason, the Darwiiz pursuit of spirituality, or merely observance without a concrete purpose.
