The lord sits across the table from two of their most trusted advisors: the treasurer, and the trader. The treasurer begins to speak. "We did as you asked, the lord. We have run the data through all of our models, but I'm afraid there just isn't a simple solution to create prosperity for all the people in our society." 

"We tried increasing tariffs, cutting tariffs, raising interest rates, raising interest rates and tariffs, lowering tax rates and increasing tariffs. but none of it seems to work." explains the trader. 

Suddenly, the lord chimes in. "Have you tried killing the poor?" 

The treasurer is taken aback, but the trader, dejected, begins to explain. "With respect, your title, we've had this conversation before. We're not going to do it." 

"Well I'm not saying we should do it; just plug it into your theory and check." 

"No." The trader seems to marshal their courage. "I'm not going to do it, even within a theoretical framework. That is offensive and evil." 

"I don't see the objection. The theory isn't going to be offended." 

The trader rolls their eyes and pulls out an abacus and some parchment. A few minutes of furious calculations later they point at a number on a piece of parchment. "There. See?" says the trader "It wouldn't work, so we're not going to do it." 

Flabbergasted, the lord objects, "That's why we're not doing it?. I should have never asked you to check. If the result had turned out positive you would've already started putting them in gibbets. I didn't realize it was only cold hearted pragmatism keeping my advisors from drinking the blood of orphans." 

The treasurer, and the trader start to shout, but the lord cuts them off. "Look, we aren't going to do it." They pause for a moment, "I think it's pretty clear what we need to do."
