The target had yet to stop screaming; Gulimbazi found it truthfully quite impressive. It had been almost five minutes since he had grabbed her out of the courtyard where she had been giving a speech to her soldiers, and one had to admire the lung capacity. Even when her mouth contorted into the fanged muzzle of a mongoose, the snarling and snapping of her jaws was similar enough for him to count it.  The slums of Inshibonde flew by beneath them, residents gawking as they passed overhead, though one could hardly blame them; a screaming warrior carried in the talons of a dire eagle struggling to stay aloft was, after all, a somewhat unusual sight.  After a grueling ten minute flight, they finally reached the city garrison, and Gulimbazi dropped the target, her screaming and ranting finally stopping as the impact forced the air from her lungs. She scrambled for her batakwa, a cruel blade long outlawed by Ibekisedara, only to find herself surrounded by a ring of spears held by the local soldiers. Gulimbazi landed, shifting back to human form as he addressed her.  "Akaliza Muhera, you are hereby charged with the crimes of warlordism and the attempted breaching of Amilak's order. These transgressions were witnessed by Gulimbazi Tuyishme of the Life Watching Eagle Tribe, duly appointed to carry out this sentence by the grace of Mezhi our ruler. Your execution shall be at dawn on the morrow, and a semayabaka will be available should you wish to make your peace with Amilak."  With that, the soldiers dragged her, screaming once more, into the depths of the garrison.
