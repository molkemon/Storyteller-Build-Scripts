Tell our lord: Your servant Jarad Lezuir sends the following message:  My friend, I received some information about the Cult of Maqet. They have a strange custom, different from all the other cults, that, according to them, was introduced to their lands by former slaves hailing from the land of Kui. They prepare a beautiful effigy over the course of an entire year; beautiful depictions of their kings and queens are usual, but it is not uncommon for Surakel and Maqet to also appear. They adorn the effigy with great care, affixing many jewels and trinkets, painting it, or sculpting it to further enhance its majesty. They also prepare a gigantic banquet featuring diverse, luxurious dishes.  Every year, the adherents all gather on the public square of their town, with the effigy in the middle. According to one of the priests I spoke to, the ceremony begins with chains being broken to symbolize the end of slavery. Then, they burn the effigy together with samplings of the foods prepared, and celebrate and feast around it while it burns. This celebration continues for the entire night, only stopping when the sun appears on the horizon. According to the priesthood, the burning is required to send the nourishing feast to Surael and to honor Maqet.  Gaining the support of such a diverse group of people would be beneficial if we intend to oppose oppression and unjust rulers. The Maqeti are a people who have survived multiple conquerors and still have managed to remain united.
