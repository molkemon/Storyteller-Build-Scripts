As our lord walks down a forest path, our lord hears a woman cry out, 'Help. Your honor, I beg your aid. Bandits have chased me from my home, and now I have only the clothes on my back, not even the bracelet my husband gifted me. There are four of them, more than I could hope to face, but surely you could drive them off?' Our lord turns to look for the voice and sees her sitting under a tree a few steps away. Our lord sees an older woman, regal in bearing, wearing clothing of good quality but faded. Her feet are bare and her hands are marked by hard labor. The delay potentially caused by a fight and the risk of wounds, or the chance of a trap, make our lord's guards hesitate, but the more superstitious among them seem inclined to aid her, not willing to risk divine punishment for neglecting the vulnerable.
