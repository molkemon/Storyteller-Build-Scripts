"You're wasting your time," said the councilwoman. "It is never going to speak to you, because there is nothing there. Uwimbabazi was a madwoman who tried to do the impossible, and she failed."  "You do not know that for sure," our lord said. "Just because she has not spoken to me yet, does not mean she never will." The councilwoman shook her head.  "Look at you. You spend every day holed up in the chambers that used to be hers, trying to commune with a spirit that you have no evidence even exists. How can you draw any other conclusion? This.thing. It is nothing."  "You did not know her like I did," our lord said quietly. "She was unlike anyone else. If anyone could cheat death, she could." The air stood still for a second.  "You are a fool, my lord. I will leave you to your insanity." The councilwoman spun on her heels and stalked out of the room. Our lord stood there for a while, in silence, before turning back to the statue.
