Raised voices filled the air inside the palace; the ruler of our nation presided, seated on his throne. Flanking the length of the hall were advisors, harimari and human both, each in constant discourse with their direct opposite. To a layman, it seemed like utter chaos, yet amongst the occasional yell, arguments were being passed. The Praxis had emerged, and the seeds that Guru Suhan had placed on his first trip through our nation were sprouting. Instead of dismissing the idea out of hand, our ruler had ordered the Cinnabar Lotus Society to send its debate teams to the palace. One, embodying the Praxis, would try to convince the other that Suhan's philosophy was to be followed, while the other would maintain that the tenets of Darsanasara should remain supreme. Slowly, one by one, the many pairs of voices fell silent and looked to the ruler, until at last a pregnant silence passed through the hall. This was finally broken as our ruler raised a paw. "Have you all come to a conclusion?" There was a nod from all sides, and the philosopher closest spoke up. "We see no reason to reject the Praxis. To close one's mind to other ideas and beliefs is against the spirit of philosophy, which questions the world and the views in it as a whole." Our ruler nodded. "Very well then, send out the missives."
