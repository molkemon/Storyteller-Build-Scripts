The sun had just begun to crest over the horizon, bathing the sky in a warm crimson—slowly pushing away the darkness. The shattered city gates of Nianano hung tenuously from the wall's frame, as the revolutionaries began to rouse from the encampment they'd established in the city streets. Nowhere else could one find such a ramshackle combination of people—peasants, craftsmen, merchants, and soldiers—all united by a singular cause; establishing themselves as the heads of the Cycle, and then shutting it down for its tyrannical nature.

The siege had thus far been grueling. Two weeks had been spent trying to push into the city itself, and though the gates had given in, the inner core of the city had been reinforced, as if Degsadra had prioritized protecting the upper class and the keep, more so than the rest of the city—than its citizens. It took hours for the revolutionaries to make any headway with that fortified core—the sun already beginning to set by the time that the inner gates too were overwhelmed. They flooded into the upper city, many intent on bringing justice to the upper class; raiding their manors, while Dzatonde An’Vraktigoma and Itoriewa Dimgã rushed towards the keep to find Degsadra before he could abscond without facing the people of his realm.

They had been successful. Now, Dzatonde An’Vraktigoma and Itoriewa Dimgã emerged from the keep, dragging Degsadra along beside them—a man who once fashioned himself above all others, now seemingly cowering before those he once knew as subjects. Dzatonde An’Vraktigoma pushed the Durari down to his knees, allowing the revolutionaries to watch, as Itoriewa Dimgã raised the blade above his head.
