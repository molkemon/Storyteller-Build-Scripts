Victory! Victory! Victory! So goes the chant in the country, in the province, in the realm and the domain, in the ports and the rural villages. So goes the chant of the mother, of the father, of the child and slave, of the soldier and farmer and trader and philosopher. So goes the chant of the grieving spouse, the orphaned child, as they celebrate through their tears.

The war was difficult, long, and tiring. Not one among us did not lose someone dear—but our troubles are matched by the Lokebhen's. And with their soldiers finally leaving our shores, dejected and defeated, we prove the victors. For we have kept our independence, and they have lost a colony.

That we must still defend our city from outside threats is certain. The powers of Alecand eye us hungrily, while the other Nékheis, eager either to show loyalty to their masters or enhance their positions in their own independence struggle, will likely be considering military action of their own. Not to mention the bharbhen. But our concerns are now those of an independent city-state, not a puppet province. For the first time since the fall of Varamelas, we are truly free!
