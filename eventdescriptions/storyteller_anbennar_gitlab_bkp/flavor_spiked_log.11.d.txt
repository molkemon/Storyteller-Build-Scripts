He had really wanted to make his army into an unstoppable horde of feystomper riders, but there was no going against the Woods. 'You are right, Garut, as always. Well, if you came here so quickly, it means you have a solution, right?' 'I do, my lord. But I'd like some guarantees that I will be able to do my job properly from now on.' 'As long as they are reasonable.' 'I want full control over all feystomper ranches. 'You're a tough negotiator, Garut. Make that two years, and I'll give a permanent seat in my council to you and your successors. Also, I will suffer no interference from you nor from your cartel in any military matter in our clan. Is that clear?' The Ranch Leader nodded, his face unscrutable. 'Good. So now tell me, what is your solution to this ordeal? I assume you won't kill your excess children?' Garut Brokentusk smiled wryly. He whispered a few words to the lord, who burst into laughter. 'You, my friend, shall be called Tuskbreaker from now on, because this plan of yours is brilliant.'
