They do not realize what has happened at first. The Vile One and Cheni have been in ritual communion for thirty-six hours straight. Even when they stop to eat or drink, they remain together. They have engaged in such ritual workings before—it is the lattice they refer to again and again, the meeting of minds as a fabric, nodes strengthened by their mutual weft. Cheni asks, "Is that a lock?" and the Vile One replies, "Yes." They are focused on the relic, the little Tetrahedron; it is suffused in the strange Wish-magic of the Genies. If Genie-magic is the expression of a word, then they are just beginning to perceive the shape of the letters, small details pertaining to the style of the font. And there is a syllable somewhere, a syllable that means lock...

They realize suddenly that their last words to each other were thoughts—that they are thinking to one another. Not in the manner of arcane telepathy or projection, to which they are perfectly used, but across a new membrane, a new connection that they only now perceive. They stare at one another. Cheni attempts to tug at the membrane, to sever it, and finds that he cannot. They continue to stare. They are amazed. It is no effort at all to maintain their joint focus on the relic and still have this odd connection—the connection persists when Cheni leaves, and remains intact after weeks apart, no effort at all exerted in its maintenance. It is a true lattice, telepathy without effort, the permanent fusing of minds. Cheni is less afraid of it than the Vile One is.

Their focus on the Tetrahedron only intensifies; it reveals fragments, memories of Komina’s past, and the Vile One gathers them before she and Komina’s next meeting.
