"You.You did what?" Farwen asked, her voice quivering as she spoke. She had come to meet with Oberon, Ceann of Salla Fealgearn; though she could never have imagined what he had done.  "I did what I had to do. The orks and goblins were going to overrun my grove; slaughter my people; I had to save them, so what other choice did I have but to send them east? Your Salla would not help, nor would any others. Ideals are the first thing to die in war, Farwen; surely you know this by now?"  "Ideals die first? You cannot be serious; you sacrificed untold lives, our kin; consigning them to their dooms, and you regret nothing? You chalk all objection to that up to an 'ideal'?"  "An ideal, indeed. What else might it be? Would you expect me to let my people die instead? Have me make a last stand, with no hope of victory? The certainty of death looming over each of my soldiers?; No, I did exactly what I had to do; the lives of others are an ideal, when one fights for one's own survival."  "All of those lives; our people. Three whole Salla annihilated and rendered refugees; by your decisions. How does your soul not weigh heavily? How does it not impair your heart?"  "Because I cannot allow it to; nor should you, Farwen. As the Ceann of my people, it is my responsibility; my duty – to take this weight upon me. It will be yours too, soon enough, and you shall learn, as I have, that to let oneself be weighed down by the decisions that one has to make is to fail in one’s duty. We do what must be done, however ugly it may be."  Farwen stood silently, her voice resting heavily in her throat. Was Oberon right? Was there nothing else to be done? Even so, could one so heartlessly throw the lives of others away; as if it hadn’t been avoidable?
