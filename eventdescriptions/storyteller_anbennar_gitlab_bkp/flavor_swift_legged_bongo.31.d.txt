The setting sun casts dancing shadows across my chambers. My mind swims with visions of the past, the future, and occasionally the present. In my halfasleep dreams, she speaks to me, a crimson voice dripping with sagacity. Uwimbabazi, she says, rest. Let sleep wash over you. You have given me so much of yourself; there is so little left. You are but a husk of a person.  I know she speaks the truth. I can scarcely bring myself to move most daysI have delegated so much to my council. They fret over me, of course. How could they not? Their oncegreat Ingo, reduced almost to a corpse. But I lack the energy to careall I have left is my child. I do not let them in my chambers anymore. It pains me to see the worried looks in their eyes, and it pains me for my child to have to witness this.  On the days where I escape my bed, I stumble over to her, my knees buckling under the weight of my malnourished body. I wrap my arms around her, sobbing into her shoulder. Oh, my little Uwimbabazi. Your mother loves you. You must know this, but it will not stop me from saying it. I love you. I have given everything to you, and I will still give more, if only to see the joy in your eyes. I breathe, and a shiver wracks my body. I am so cold, my child.  I find myself tired yet again. Perhaps just a short nap next to you, to ease my weary legs…
