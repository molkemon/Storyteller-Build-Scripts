Among the villages of our realm, it is common for farmers to form self-organized "Field Assemblies" to discuss matters such as seasonal plowing, rotational irrigation, granary reserves, and grain tax allocations. Though lacking official recognition, these grassroots gatherings often exert significant influence on local agricultural planning.

Recently, several such assemblies along the river have jointly submitted a petition. They request formal representation at the township level, with a seat at advisory councils to deliberate on waterworks, taxation, and the division of common lands. 

This is not the first such proposal—nor is it isolated. As these assemblies become more stable across our domain, their demands for institutional presence have grown. Some local officials support the idea, citing improved governance and cooperation. Others warn that such inclusion may disrupt the established power structure of towns and introduce new instability.

Shall we heed these voices from the fields?
