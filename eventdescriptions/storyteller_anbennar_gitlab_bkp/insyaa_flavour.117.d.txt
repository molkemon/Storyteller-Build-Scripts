It was once the case that a 'cow' had a distinct form—four legs, hair on their flanks and faces, udders that produced a set amount of milk per day—the list of limitations went on. And this was true for all the other animals our nation domesticated as well. Each of them was subject to the constraints written into their genetic code.

Through the power of gene magic and some deftly applied technology, our artificers have made it so that the livestock and domesticated animals of [Root.GetName] can produce what we need of them at rates that would have been impossible even with millions of years of selective breeding. The meat cows of Insyaa have meat that can be peeled right off their flank, and cooked in front of the cow it came from, yet which painlessly grows back overnight. The milk cows of Insyaa are not so much creatures as balloons made of milk, which can fill an entire storehouse of milk within the span of a single week.

And if their reality does not quite meet our artificers' greatest boasts, it matters not; the truth remains: every animal has a defined purpose, and it is perfectly tailored to fit that purpose by the skilled hands of our artificers. And once we have an animal to fit a role, we can breed them, and they will multiply across all our farms and fields.

If before we knew what the form of a cow was because it was defined strictly by the laws of nature which cared nothing for our prosperity, now we know what the form of a cow is because it is whatever we want it to be.
