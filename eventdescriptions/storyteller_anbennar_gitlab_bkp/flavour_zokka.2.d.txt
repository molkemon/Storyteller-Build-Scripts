Andrellion had seen his father die.  He had cleanly finished off his own assailents, letting their bodies fall into the growing pile of gnoll corpses around him. He had swept his gaze across the battlefield, taking in the horror, valiantly ignoring the pit in his chest and the wounds in his side. His eyes had halted on his father. An elf larger then life; the strongest, smartest, most competent man he had ever known.  He watched the man cry out. He heard the crunch of teeth meeting teeth, breaking through skin and skull and brain. He fell to his knees, lungs empty. He had begged Surael, begged for the wretched moment to have been a simple trick of the light, even as enemies swarmed him, shattering his ankles and scorching his torso with their foul fire.  Now, he is here.  Andrellion didn't know how, exactly, he had broken free. The rusted chains that had cut into his wrists lie shattered in the sand. His assigned guard lies next to them, throat wet with stillwarm blood matching the ichor staining his own teeth. He crawls, dragging himself through the dust, until his fingers fail to curl and the sun begins to rise. Then, and only then, does he cry out, voice dry and cracking yet still, somehow, managing to summon aid.  He is given no time to recover. Unable to protest, he is slung across the back of one of the Masnsih that had found him. They walk for days; for weeks; evacuating villages of humans, elves, and harpies alike, swelling their numbers a hundredfold. Then, and only then, do they cross into Davharral, and then Rahen proper.  Then, and only then, does Andrellion let himself hope once again.
