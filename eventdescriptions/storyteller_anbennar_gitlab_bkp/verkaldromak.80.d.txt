The dream begins in the skies above a vast land, brown but shot through with two great streaks of green. But the ruler’s vision moves quickly, away from the cities that glitter like jewels on those strings of green, and to a vast expanse of brown and yellow. Closer, faster. To a splotch of black on that dun background, which grows and grows as the vision nears. But the catch of light on steel renders the mass in a starkly different term; not some splotch like ink on parchment, but a mass of bodies, motionless, staring towards their center. Towards which the vision then proceeds. A huddling man, dismouned from a small convoy of horses, approaches a kneeling figure. A glint of bright gold metal off the object in their hands, as the messenger? emissary? priest?; places it on the bowed head in front of them. And then, suddenly, an explosion of sound. Sufficient to shock the ruler, startling them briefly awake, with a word still ringing in their ears.
