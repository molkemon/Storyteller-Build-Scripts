For centuries, lizardfolk and men have waged bitter war against each other, a conflict that left both sides little chance to appreciate the other’s architecture, lest it be minutes before being put to the torch. Seeing the great sights of the port city of Nazhni, now open to the Noukahi faithful, their ruler can do nothing but lament that war, as righteous as it was, stood between mankind and this beauty. Nazhni was always a city of artists, of pleasure and comfort, and so it remains. Great colourful buildings adorn every street, functionality a second thought, beauty the priority. From the temples to the great mansions, the resorts for the wealthy, and even the humble homes of the city workers, the whole city sings in harmony with a myriad of colours. Their ruler crosses through the bustling market, avoiding the hordes of merchants offering they/their wares from all corners of the land. The central square of the great city is filled with bazaar stalls, as colourful as the rest. At its centre, a circle of spires rises: replicas of the great monuments that stand across the province. Their ruler runs their hand over their delicately sculpted surfaces, upon which the stars of countless night skies are etched. Some Griots sing that when their lord, centuries ago, had the city razed and toppled every last brick, he did so because their warriors had become so entranced by its beauty that they would otherwise never have left. Their ruler understands why. The city is more addictive than the Nazhyn sold in its markets; it enters the mind and conquers it with a symphony of hues. They must leave now, and their soul aches knowing this. Yet, in a way, they will never leave. One can never truly leave Nazhni, for once you enter, your heart is forever dyed with the city's million colours.
