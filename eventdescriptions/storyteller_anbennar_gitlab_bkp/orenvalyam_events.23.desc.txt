The last meeting of the conclave, unofficial and some time after it had actually ended, was in a tavern in the nearest city. The High Kavalal of Clemetar took a sip of his Pheni, shrugged at the question posed to him. "Well, we hold the vote on whether to undertake an expansion effort first, in the Kavalakuttam. If it passes, the sub-council is formed—by volunteers first, then more votes if there are too many. I always volunteer, and believe me, there are never too many. Then, in closed discussion, we decide on the area."

The Mteibhar and Basobhar delegates who had asked—friends to Orenvalyam, and not afraid to engage in the 'process' of Dawn—nodded along to his answer. After, their Mteibhar ringleader asked another question: "And... how much influence do you, personally, have on which area is chosen?"

The High Kavalal began to understand what the Mteibhar was getting at. He smirked, held up a finger. "Steady, now—I will answer your question in three stages. First, you must know I do not take bribes. Second, I have considerable influence over this matter, though it is not absolute. Third... to a Kavalal, an unbeatable argument is just as good as gold. Convince me, and I will convince my colleagues. You will be brought into the fold in no time."

The Mteibhar returned the priest's smile. "Alright then. Do you want it in writing?"

"Or save me having to draft a speech?" The High Kavalal toasted his glass. "By all means, be my guest!"
