Aha, you wish to hear of the remnants of greater times that still grace our eyes; very good. Ahem, thrice strummed, ten hums, a jingling foot to guide, and the tale begins, for despite the onslaught of Deep Devils and wrathful wishes of the Djinn Sultan, Aro Makaya’s mighty wonders stand strong and tall.  Let us cheer for the twelve great totems and their peoples, whose names endure upon Aro’s Pillar, like Grandmother Water Buffalo, Fojara the Great Lion, or Pelican Mother, among many more. Yet for each blessed living totem, we must also lament the fallen, such as selfless Grandfather Donkey, alluring Aunt Gazelle, cunning Uncle Baboon, the wise Frigate Bird Oracle, or loyal Brother Hornbill.  Ah, but with how prominent Aro’s Pillar is in story and song, you’d be forgiven to think it the only of his grand designs – but no. To the north of towering Junansuar, at the heart of the fertile plains of Fangaula, lies majestic Atijo Sigilan, the old capital where Aro ruled the peoples of Fangaula freed from djinn oppression. His palace, erected by golems powered by the very genies that oppressed us, remains a vibrant wonder even after millennia. Although the opulent throne makes kings and khet alike blush, the greatest treasure is this: if you whisper a prayer in its halls, guidance befitting Aro’s wisdom just might echo back.  I cannot ignore the wonder of Aro Bugu, the place where Aro was born to his human mother Ghozan and a ringbound Dao. Vengeful djinns obliterated Aro’s original village, but a humble shrine housing Aro’s magic walking stool still survives: few can say why the tyrants spared it while razing the rest. Despite its unassuming architecture, the shrine reminds us that greatness comes in many, often humble visages.
