With heavy hearts and grim determination, we have pressed onwards. There are now fewer amongst our number, but we took solace in the idea that those that remained were all strong enough to survive whatever we would face going forwards.  We were wrong.  Our journey saw us venture deeper into Tswohvwoh'ii's embrace, far below the canopy of colossal trees that made the midday sun seem like a distant dusk. In the foliage, myriad forms stalked us yet again, seeming to blend seamlessly with the twisted flora that surrounded them, before pouncing with bestial fury. Terrestrial crocodilians that lie in wait in the treetops, pale white bats that carry off the unsuspecting into the moonlight. Here, in this vibrant cacophony of danger and death, we knew fear.  Still, beasts can be killed, beasts can be understood, the will behind them uncomplicated. Not so for those gripped by the song of Tswohvwoh'ii and enslaved to Her will. These oncefamiliar faces now moved as if puppeteered by an otherworldly force, their minds ensnared, controlled through an otherworldly hum, while insidious foliage spread across their bodies. Their voices, once full of warmth and familiarity, had been replaced by eerie, haunting melodies, echoing the secrets and desires of our cruel Mistress. We dared not make eye contact with them, for their gaze held a vacant, haunting emptiness. It was a cruel twist of fate to see our brethren reduced to mere thralls, a chorus in chains.  We couldn't help but wonder if we had entered a realm where even our very spirits were at risk, and the jungle's insidious grasp extended not only to the land but to the very souls of those who dared to tread upon it.
