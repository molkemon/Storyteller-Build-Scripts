After months at sea, the Expeditionary Corps representatives spend their first day ashore relaxing within a seaside palace overlooking a glistening shoal. Their host, a local noble, attends to the envoys personally, chatting at length about their trek, concerns, desires, local squabbles, global politics, and the food-formalities worthy of a gracious emcee. The next day, the noble, the wrinkles around his smiling eyes dissolving into luxuriously dark skin, welcomes the reinvigorated Expeditionary Corps expedition to a wide, shallow court pool with a smile. Lounging in the royal blue waters, a cadre of diplomats and merchants silently judge their representatives as they negotiate with the noble, offering little more than a soft nod or glowering dismissal. To start, the Expeditionary Corps offers the simplest exchange: coin, and lots of it. The noble counters with requests for the wonders flowing through a nearby region, hoping to see the groundbreaking goods in person. The arbitrations continue for hours and conclude with.
