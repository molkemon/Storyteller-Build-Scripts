That night, in our bedchamber, we shared what looked to be a glass of wine. It was thicker than usual. Slightly congealed. A trained eye would see it was covered with a thin sheen of magic.  It was fitting. Since I had met her, it was as if we had shared everything together. Years of research had led us straight back to that conclusion.  Immortality was always spoken of as an awfully lonely thing. Mitrenya and I, however, had found a way to share it.  "Do you remember what your mother said?" She asked quietly, gazing into my eyes, a soft smile on her face.  "Which part?"  "That we wouldn't be alone. You would have me, and I you." I nodded, lifting a hand to brush against her cheek. "I think I understand her better, now."  "Oh?"  "Mm. It's simple, really." She drank deeply from the goblet, before handing it to me. "A wendigo has eternal life, but allconsuming hunger. They have dominion of the woods, but are slaves to their legendary master. So we must share."  "You will give me half of everything. Your pain, and your hate. Your peace, and your wonder. And I will give you half of mine." I kept my eyes on hers as I finished the drink, feeling the magic fill me to the brim.  "Like this, we will love. And so, we will live."
