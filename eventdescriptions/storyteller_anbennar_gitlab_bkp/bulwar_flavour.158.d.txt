Much of the reign of our noble queen Keladora has been consumed by attempting to undo the damages wrought to Birzartanses by her father, the accursed Arantir I. Birzartanzuir. He was a man as vain and conceited as he was incompetent, indifferent to the needs of his subjects. It was his delusional attempt to restore his hold over Baharkand and rule all of eastern Bahar, just as his ancestress Amarien once did, that truly began the Decades of Devastation, bringing misery and death to untold Bahari. He filled his court with sycophants and pompous fools, while all who demonstrated a modicum of honor and competence were chased away due to his increasing paranoia. The breaking point was finally reached when he alienated his most successful general Dartaxes szelFarramaz for the last time, thus inciting the Great Rebellion that saw the birth of Dartaxagerdim. Even the last vestiges of filial piety within Keladora could not deter her from doing what was needed, and she launched a coup against her father to save her realm. For the last three decades he has spent his existence under house arrest in a remote monastery deep within the sad Kuz, swiftly forgotten by all but his most ardent enemies and a few scholars. Meanwhile, his daughter has worked tirelessly to expunge the stain her father’s inept rule left on the family name of the Birzartanzuir and return Birzartanses onto the path to greatness. Now, the abbot of the monastery has sent a secret missive to court, informing Keladora of her father’s passing. A most difficult decision now lies before her. Should a proper state funeral be held, which might risk reminding nobility and common people alike of the many failings of their former monarch, endangering her efforts to distance herself from his reign? Or should news of his passing be suppressed and only a discreet funeral be held, all in the hopes of quietly assigning Arantir’s life and death to just a footnote in the annals of Birzartansesi history?
