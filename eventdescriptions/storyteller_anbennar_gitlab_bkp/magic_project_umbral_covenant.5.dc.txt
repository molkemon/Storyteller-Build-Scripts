"I watched where the magic went, Vile One, when you took it; where the crumbs that one cannot eat fell. I heard a discordant ringing, and I followed it miles to its source." Cheni's expression is triumphant as he points to the table before him. On the table stands a complex little tower made of brass and wood, covered in deeply etched grooves. In one moment, the entire system resembles the bendbark gutters of a lizardfolk outdoor bath, flowing to the base of the tower. In the next, it transforms into a many-branched tree, intersected at odd, harmonious points with metal. All the many varied channels and rods meet at the bottom, where a set of brass pipes hang from slender threads.

"It is a device the naga use to hone precision," Cheni explains. "The device is essentially passive. The naga manipulate magic towards the top of the tower, the receivers, channeling the magic to the bottom with enough deftness that the pipes are stirred and make music." Cheni demonstrates, ringing out a vaguely Raheni sounding melody that only the Vile One seems to recognize. "One shows their aptitude by playing skilled tunes."

Nobody speaks.

"It's what the zatsarya is doing, you see?" Cheni's eyes are bright. It is a victory the Vile One recognizes, a good little boy who has found the answer. Still, she must insist, "The point, Cheni?"

He nods rapidly. "The zatsarya is passive. It isn't a self-running system that siphons magic from the air. It's receiving the magic from elsewhere."

The Vile One frowns, processing the information. "And so you suggest...?"

Cheni grins. "Do what the naga do—send magic back through it and see where it goes."
