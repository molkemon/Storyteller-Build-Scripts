Our country has been a confederation of provinces, each enjoying a great deal of autonomy and control over their own lands, held together by the Grand Marshal and the shared Ninyu Kikun. This system has worked well so far, but the expansion of our state has made it harder for this apparatus to govern our vast territories. This is mainly due to the fact that our military and civil administration are one and the same. Therefore, we will need to reorganize our nation and administration to secure its future. Henceforth, civil and military administration will be separated, with the Ninyu Kikun leading the way. Its structure will be mirrored into a military and civil branch, each dealing with their own matters, of course maintaining a militarized structure for familiarity and efficiency. Each province will also be split, with its civil branch led by a Murasesho or "Marshal-regent," who presides over the province's Seshokozen, or "regent-generals," who rule the smallest territorial subdivisions known as Kikushi.
