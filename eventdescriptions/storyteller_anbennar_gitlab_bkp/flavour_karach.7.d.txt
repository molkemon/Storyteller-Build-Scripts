"Once Iggu Riverrunner sailed his men out to make their shipyards, the Mashkay started getting nervous. They couldn’t compete with the flow of ships coming out from the shipyards, and it was getting harder and harder to turn a profit on their own shipbuilding ventures. What could they do?  That’s when the Mashkay remembered that they could always go farther out to sea, and so they did. Floods of Mashkay sold their lumberyards to the incoming Ashhana of the lumberers’ Axta, and took up sailing themselves.  The lumberers thought they had won, of course.  They thought that until word of a new Axta forming on the sea reached them, which had even already affiliated itself with the rich and powerful Enkhaxta. It was an Axta of the sailors, and the Mashkay had stocked it with their ranks. From then on the lumberers negotiated ship prices with the Axta instead of each Mashkay alone, and they got a taste of solidarity from the other side."
