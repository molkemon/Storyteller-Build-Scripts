In the eastern province, the Exemplars upon landing found the scattered remnants of a group called the Oono'i. Their descendants, who now only share partial ancestry, have explained to a curious audience how their forefathers set their homeland alight to fight against the jungle's influence. The Dugui'hi, who now travel Taychend as traders and mercenaries, have retold the story of their exile from the Mushroom Forest once more. These speakers have almost always been joined by ambitious Eilastes known for their support of northern expansion. The message is clear: we must take the fight to the jungle before it takes the fight to us.

But there is an alternate opinion. A few locals, mostly traveling from Cannorian ports along the coast, have told of the benevolence of the jungle—they say that it is not the mindless nightmare that the Oono'i and Dugui'hi frame it as. In fact, they say that a state such as ours may be able to come to an arrangement with it. Perhaps it is worth investigating this possibility?
