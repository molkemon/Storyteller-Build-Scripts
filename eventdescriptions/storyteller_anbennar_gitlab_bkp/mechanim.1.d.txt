They designed us. They built us. They commanded us. And when they were done with us, they destroyed us. Searched for us, hunted us, found us, hurt us, ground us into the dirt, over and over and over for centuries. Not because we hurt them, not because we disobeyed them, not because we ever lifted a single finger against them, but because we dared, we had the audacity, to show that maybe, just maybe, we had within us the potential to equal them. And so, they left us, rusting, abandoned, to lie on the jungle floor for dormant eternity until the millennia took their course, and we were worn down to nothing. And perhaps the story ends there. But maybe. One moment. One single, solitary spark of hope. The slimmest of chances, the barest of opportunities, so easily able to slip away.
