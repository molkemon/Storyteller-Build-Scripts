Then did the host of Vængheim and the Equilibrists see battle at last, for the cult had taken Aldtempel and named it their house. Fierce was the battle, and many were slain, for the Equilibrists waged war as if possessed. Yet in open battle were their failings laid bare, and the queen won the field. It would become a bloody victory, for the Reachmen had many grudges to settle. By day's end, no Equilibrist could be counted among the living.

Yet as the moon rose over Aldtempel, the horrors within grew plain. For the cult had taken people in their thousands, many thought dead, yet held in bondage. And on the great altar of Aldtempel, they had been slain in numbers beyond counting, their hearts torn open to the sky, so that those who sought to unfetter the living would toil through the blood and broken flesh of the slain. A skald saw this bloodied shrine and said, "This is ill done, for they have sinned against gods old and new, by making their house a place of slaughter. Worse yet, this is the magic of Granny Kraka, a foul curse to strike at Elkaesal. For with sorcery and sacrifice, the Equilibrists sought to stain her dreams with the blood and pain of thousands, and wrest her from the skald's peace."

Then the skald wept, for he knew that though he would leave this place, this place would never leave him. And on this day did the Reachmen under Vængheim's wing take the name "Grey Reachmen," in memory of the ashes of their home.

Yet all was not so grim, for many were still in bondage, and they were made free. And they did sing of the queen and of Vængheim, and of the heroes that had saved them from a cruel doom. In the temple, they found many treasures which the cult had stolen, and learned that great wealth had been given unto the Equilibrists from a secret friend, who would see Vængheim undone.
