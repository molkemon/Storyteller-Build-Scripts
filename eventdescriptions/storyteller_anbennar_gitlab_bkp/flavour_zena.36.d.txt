Within the winding tunnels of the Serpentspine, any advantage one can amass is worth its weight in gold. Space is at a premium, and a safe space where one can sleep even more so, as the floors of the caverns are crawling with minotaurs, ettins, orks and worse. The Darkscale Kobolds, pushed to the fringes of the Serpentspine after the death of their dragon god Nimrith, have mastered the ability to build into the ceilings, with winding towns and camps built upsidedown between the thick stalactites of the caverns, invisible to the monsters below.  Facing Bivuzena’s crowding, Azaly believes that these methods could be replicated in the narrow alleyways between buildings. Having arrived with a gift for their meagre hoard, the naturally distrustful kobolds gladly invited him into their camp, as they believe that once Nimrith’s hoard has grown enough, he will return to the world of the living to guide them once more. Over a dinner of cavern mushrooms, Azaly studied and discussed how they anchor their belongings to the stone and traverse the ceiling with ease. With said knowledge obtained, Azaly bid his hosts farewell and returned to Bivuzena, alongside a retinue of Darkscales who are eager to see the world and scour it to bring back wealth to the hoard. Azaly drily mentions to the guards to watch them particularly carefully within Medaka’s Exceptional Treasures, before beginning to draw up plans for hanging shops and suspensionapartments in the alleyways of the Nyanza Market.
