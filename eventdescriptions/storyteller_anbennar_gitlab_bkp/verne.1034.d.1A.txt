I felt your prayers.  Sweating in the desert in your overthick fabrics, you arrived at the shrines of your dead woman, irritable and miserable and scared, and I took in each fervent prayer you made to her. Heavier and more morose the longer you stayed in the desert, despairing of any chance to truly end my presence on this plane.  I watched as thirty or forty demons leaked from the rift. I felt your fear spike, the collective attention of an entire army. I realised, after a week’s moment, that you believed I had sent these evil things you fought. You thought me so big. It was flattering.
