After a relatively routine trudge through a labyrinth of dwarfeating plants, the forward scouts of our delvers signalled that they'd found another of those mysterious old doors. As Thorrik stepped forward, he disturbed some millennial dust, creating clouds and whorls in the air – and highlighting a strange disturbance in the shape of a man. Unthinkingly, he reached forward to touch the shape, and it gave way underneath his hand, leaving the unpleasant impression of having crushed a wasp's nest underneath a silk handkerchief.  Feeling around the invisible, honeycombed mannequin a bit more, he discovered clear edges to the silken overlayer. Pulling it off was physically very easy, rewarding him with the sounds of a dusty exhale of relieved surrender and a brief impression of a thing without essence before the garment's wearer joined the rest of the room's dust. Impatient again, he wrapped himself in the cloak and Lisban confirmed that it was as he suspected: the cloak rendered him nearly invisible – although he also felt something inside himself stretching uncomfortably as he stretched the fabric over his shoulders.
