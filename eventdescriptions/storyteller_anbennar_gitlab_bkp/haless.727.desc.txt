Yanshen's military power rests on six regions, each with distinct strategies. The western bastion, Bianfang, fields swift, iron-willed troops; the heartland, Szicheng, houses the Grand War Camp; Jiantsiang defends the fertile northeast with strict order against nomads. Gushibi, seat of the Stone Army Alliance, gathers mercenaries; Jiangdu, the "City of Warlords," holds supreme command and is famed for rigorous drills; Damaogong, on the Halberd Plains, trains infantry skilled at breaking cavalry.

Though united under central command, the six regions long differed in systems and drills. Now, with order restored, the "Six-Army Review" rotates officers to compare doctrines: Jiangdu seeks clearer duties; Szicheng and Jiantsiang refine east-west readiness; Bianfang and Damaogong plan joint drills; Gushibi urges merging mercenaries into border forces.

If they act as one, joint warfare may revive; if mismanaged, reform may stall. Now is the time to set firm statutes and align duties—only united can the six move without hesitation.
