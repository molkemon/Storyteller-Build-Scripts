The majority of the wealth in the region is in the hands of the wealthy Khetist nobility. Their ancestors were settlers from Kheterata who secured vast swaths of the most valuable land by force. In this more civilised age, these descendants continue to secure more land and power through bribes rather than strength, although this less violent method still results in conflict. This is exactly what has just happened here. Khetist settlers started fencing off large portions of land for their own use. This land was used by local pastoral farmers for generations, and they have complained to the authorities about the issue. The Khetists insist that they will make a much more fruitful use of the land, and have the perfect gift to sweeten the deal.
