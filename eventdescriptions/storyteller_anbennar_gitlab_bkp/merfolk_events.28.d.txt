My lord, a bruised soldier hobbles into the capital, I bring dire news from a distant province. Wincing from their wounds, the soldier unrolls page after page of reports and orders from the province: warehouse raids, dock fires, ship attacks, and mounting casualties. It’s the merfolk, my lord. We’ve always disliked them, but one of their ocean nations just set their sights on the province. The soldier swallows hard, lost amid fresh battle memories. They outnumber our forces, pierce our defenses, and strike without reprisal; it’s untenable given current resources. My commander sent me to request additional support to combat them. Should we act swiftly and harshly, we can easily repel these attacks, but such overwhelming force requires valuable resources perhaps better spent elsewhere. What should we do?
