The Ravelian Society, a loose group of academics and reformists that gather to discuss all manners of topics through the lens of logic and reason rather than superstition, have come to officially petition for a public space in our capital. Supporting their presence may be beneficial, if we wish to remain relevant in these modern times. They have, however, been found to be vocal opponents of what they view as unjust and untrue, and may develop into an unwelcome thorn in our side.
