A trained warrior can slay a dozen of his foes in a given battle. An elite worg-rider might near a hundred kills, contingent on a well-timed charge. But how many lives did our lord take today, with the orders he just signed? Thousands. Tens of thousands, if he erred, or had a stroke of brilliance. Such is the burden and responsibility of leadership, and of being king of The Great Command.

That thought does not fade as our lord returns home from the command tent in the capital, but it does become... less salient. He checks the house, but it is empty, despite the late hour. With a smile, he walks around the dwelling to the dojo behind it. There, he is the victim of a savage ambush—a trained warrior runs at him, weapon in hand. Our lord's smile widens, and he strokes his moustache. His four-year-old son comes to a sharp halt a few feet away, eyes widening. The training sword—just a little longer than a dagger—drops to the young man's side, and he gives his father a solemn bow.

As his son kneels, our lord's eyes sweep across the dojo. He sees his wife, sketching blueprints in the late afternoon sun. He sees his nephews, sparring paused, heads bowed in proper respect to the king. He waves them back to their training. And then, with a laugh, he picks up his son and swings him through the air. The sun is warm, his son's own laughter is infectious, and life in The Great Command is as it should always be.
