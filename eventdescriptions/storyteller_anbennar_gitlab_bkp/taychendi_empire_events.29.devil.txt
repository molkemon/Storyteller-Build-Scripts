Our lord took another sip of wine as he looked out from his balcony, at the immensity of our capital below. It was night, but even now the light of commerce, industry, and empire illuminated the sky. Taychend never truly slept anymore. The carts never stopped, the harvest never ended, the war-banners never unfurled. Our ruler closed their eyes—and in their mind, they saw images of conquest, of glory. The sounds of battle, steel clashing, flesh rending, warriors calling battle cries as the gore-covered and yet-still-glorious fallen cried for mother or mercy or malevolence's brand of justice. And the blood… all the blood. Even as they mused on the realm they ruled, their subordinates arranged new campaigns in North Aelantir, in Sarhal, in Insyaa. From the warlord to the warrior to the wretched slave, Taychend hungered—and it satiated itself on the blood of the world. Our lord downed their wine-cup—savored the purple-red trickling down their throat. Why did blood satiate Taychend? Why had the other empires not realized this, not conquered the world to sustain themselves? They used to ask these questions. Then, like Valkadevu on the wastes of Neraghul, they understood. The other empires had simply misunderstood—glory is not some ambiguous thing. Glory is violence. If our ruler would not kill, end, conquer, annihilate, then the warlords would do it for them. And so you be the sword, or face it. And so Taychend would march, and destroy, and drink the blood of the unconquered world until they could drink no more. Then? Maybe they'd all turn on each other, rend the Empire apart for the ichor of ascension that pumped through its veins. It didn't matter. The Cycle of Glory—the Cycle of Blood—would be over, and Taychend would have already ascended. An Empire of Violence, and an Empire Vainglorious...
