The camp was bustling with activity as hundreds of newly recruited women prepared for their first training lesson as Banshees. Nesta watched with pride as the new recruits filed in, eager and ready to prepare for the rigorous training ahead. It would be tough – many of these girls had been homemakers – but by the end, their strength would surprise even themselves. Nesta's friend, Gwyn, had been chosen to be the instructor for this group, which was why she was watching with such pride. Nesta thought back to when she had joined, when she was like one of the girls she now watched. It was yesterday and an eternity ago. Sure, she had to follow orders, and sure military life was hard, but it was rewarding. Nesta could be herself; she felt a freedom she had never felt before, despite having a commanding officer. Now that the Istralari Parliament had provided more funding, the Banshees had opened recruitment centers across Istralore. More than ever, girls could apply for the elite regiment in the hope of becoming Nesta's battle-sister. Even with the centers opening, the elite regiment was flooded with applications, so much that some of the most senior warriors like Nesta and her friends had been temporarily asked to help with training and getting the new recruits situated. With the Banshees growing so rapidly, Nesta feared for their enemies.
