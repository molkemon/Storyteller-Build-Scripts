We whisper across the waters, west to Adshaw, far southwest to Gawed, and beyond. In many lands there are folk with noble hearts who remember the cause of Vrorenmarch, or who else are canny enough to know the risk of an orkish kingdom moving from strength to strength. From such supporters we receive money, supplies and weapons, some traded openly; for we Reachman lords ever traded in such things; and some hidden in the lower holds of merchant ships.  At the same time, our quiet domestic networks go to work, convincing practically every lord to hoard a little more food, a little more iron against the future cause of war. In these lands it is customary for one table to be reserved at the tavern for high men and women of the area; we trust our own, you see, and only our own. In such a way we guard our plans from our orkish overlords, who may suspect all they like, for they can prove nothing. They say a Reachman is as quiet as the autumn winds are loud. We have benefited greatly indeed from such a taciturn nature.  The resources we have pooled are ready to be invested in the wider conspiracy; it is only to be decided where and in what volume.
