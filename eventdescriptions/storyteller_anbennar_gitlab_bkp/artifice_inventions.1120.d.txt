It is a truth selfevident to any sailor worth their salt: The winds and waves are not to be trifled with, and any seagoing object made by mortal hands exists at their mercy. Yet as with many other professions and industries, artificery has provided an ingenious solution to one of the more intractable nautical problems – how to reach safe shores when a storm has wrecked your sails? By imbuing sail cloth with regenerative abilities, of course.  Not only can these selfrepairing sails mend minute rends caused by everyday wear and tear, but so long as a sufficient supply of artificeryenhanced fibres are provided to a feeder mechanism at the bottom of the sail, the system can patch even large holes caused by storms, or replace tornoff parts. This regenerative ability provides a superior sturdiness and flexibility to these sails, so that even gale winds do not require a ship to strike the sails. If needed, selfrepairing sails can even expand their surface to catch more wind and hasten a ship’s journey accordingly.
