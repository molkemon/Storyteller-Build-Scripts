Blackheart watched from the shadows as the last ork fell with a wet thud. The smallest twinge of respect stirred within the dryad. The creatures had fought with a feral and brutish viciousness. The sight had left Blackheart with her current smile; the Prince had picked his tools well. Now, her curiosity was sated. Her bloodlust, though, was not.  She turned her attention to unsuspecting Wood Elves, letting her smile grow into a manic expression. Bloodied and wary as they were, Blackheart knew the elves thought themselves victorious. She watched as bruised Alara Cenag fussed over Narawen Drennen’s wounds. She noted how the brutish Oberon Fealgarn hobbled over and examined the orks for clues. And she reveled at the frantic whispering as Bricena Myna and Cinithir Elyn fretted while a tired Irvah Ayeth sighed nearby. They were also so blissfully ignorant. Foolishly thinking they were buoyed by hope.  With madness and mirth, Blackheart slid behind Irvah Ayeth, ripped his throat open and laughed.
