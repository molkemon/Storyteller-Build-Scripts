The Kylakas walked between the crowded tenements of Degakheion. The fight had ended just a short while ago. Dead bodies littered the streets, limp and lifeless, blood pooling beneath them. Some lay in groups, others alone. Some had been cut down as they fled, others still clutching their weapons. Pitchforks, butcher-knives, and ramshackle spears. A few captured swords. None wore armor. A heavy toll. But he supposed that was the price of sticking to the old code.

The prisoners were subdued. Half of them were slaughtered to do it. And the riot was squashed. Then, something caught the Kylakas' eye. He halted, raised his brow. A woman—dead, of course—clutched a... rod? It was like a metal pole. Not a weapon, really. A stick. She'd been cut down... fallen on her back. And in the blood beside her, there were... little footprints. Marks where someone had knelt. His first thought was that it was her child. Curious. The blood was still fresh, but when the Kylakas looked around, he couldn't see anyone. What sort of child abandons his dead mother? Leaves her corpse in an alley? Maybe he'd run off, was cut down as well.

Mmm... no, not a good excuse. The Kylakas turned to the woman again—stared at the footprints. He remembered now... one of the Erythé trials. One of the nightmares. The mist had shown him... well, something had killed his mother, basically. A wolf or something. And he'd watched it happen, heard the screams, terror, wails, et cetera. It was hunting him... he'd had to stay still. Let it happen. But the monster passed, and he went to his mother's side. He'd stayed by her for a while. He was sure that he had. And then it came round again. He remembered its gnashing maw tearing into him. The unimaginable pain. It ate him. He felt it.

He'd stayed. What sort of child abandons his dead mother? The Kylakas gave a dour sigh, shook his head, and continued on his walk.
