The endless shimmer of the ocean grass is irresistible to the romantic soul, and all true sailors are romantics. Venture down there a time or two, dive with a Bubblehead Cantrip perhaps, and lose yourself for a little while among the strands‚??they glisten in the half-light that breaks through the surface of the water; they form sheets of green glass washed in great waves by the subsurface currents. Their glistening is their oil, the same oil which sticks to the hulls of ships and brings them to a halt. Thus was born the Insyaan preference for launching well out into the open ocean rather than hugging the shore‚??but we have entered a time of coastal siege and of great landings, and we too must now shadow the great cliffs and endless beaches of our homeland.

Thus the technology which makes the sea-grass our deadly hideout. Mimicking the currents of the sea itself, we do not foolishly try to cut back or inhibit the fast-growing stuff‚??we merely dislodge it gently and regularly with an arcane pulse, precisely calibrated to tickle the great fields of green like the oceanic winds which buffet them endlessly. We move without issue and the seaweed is our home, while our enemies snag at every turn.
