Adul Dogskull held up his hands patiently as yet another argument broke out in the assembly hall. He and his team had worked hard to see this project through to a completely objective conclusion, and he would not allow himself to be swayed by dogmatic gripes. This was a scientific matterand besides, he didn’t feel that it flew in the face of conventional wisdom about how Dookan had created the orks for their purpose. They’d just mistaken exactly who Dookan had been.  Finally, the din died down, and he spoke again. "As I was saying, all of our discoveries and notes can be read in our manuscript. Copies will be handed out after the conference concludes. That book contains the reconstructed experiment documentation of an ancient elf named Doo… Ducaniel. He was the being that created the orks in a lab deep beneath Hul Jorkad."  A bottle flew from across the room and shattered against the podium. Jeers and curses came from all directions, and a guard grabbed Adul’s shoulder and escorted him from the stage.  Weeks later, as the book had made its way through the nation’s various institutions, the outrage died down and a strange resolve took its place. There would always be crackpots trying to stir things up in Hul Jorkad, and there would always need to be guards protecting that old lab, but the orks as a whole learned to accept the change in their understanding of history.  Maybe they were made out of meat and magic in a lab, rather than from dust and divinity by a god. Either way, their purpose and the truth of it was the same.
