Our lord felt unease as the army halted at the edge of the Underwarren. The warm lights of the Undercity had receded, replaced by the harsh blue-white glow of targeted mage-light. Many Nakari citizens had gathered to watch them depart, eager to catch a glimpse of our lord, or say goodbye to any scavengers they knew in the army—as they had just been reminded, some were undoubtedly there with ulterior motives. To tail them, maybe pick some of them off, or swindle them as the scavengers had done—the Underground was a lawless place, after all. That didn’t bother our lord, though. She had ten thousand elves with her—a sizable number even on the field of battle. A number of Mudaliars, alongside the King of Nakar Kriadvai, had joined her, alongside some other warlords who had tagged along, eager for glory. No, our lord’s real fear was the unknown. Steel, after all, could cut through flesh far better than it could cut through darkness.

But she’d just have to deal with it—with a short speech and a flash of her sabre, our lord called for the Expedition to advance further into the dark.
