"And what is this?" The Vile One's tone is skeptical, which to almost anyone else in the Covenant would cause immediate panic. Cheni merely grins. "We have fallen prey to the fallacy of proximity, kitame," he says, in his silk-soft voice.

"It is a chamber designed for focus, kitame. The ceremony of ritual, with hundreds of mages present, is all well and good for our great works. But when we seek to bring another two hundred of your shadowmen into the world, do we need to meet so grandly? No, it undermines the thing.

"Place a wall between us two," Cheni gestures to indicate the wall, as he does when he teaches, "and we would not fail to communicate, no? We might even mindspeak more clearly, if we were both in a room without noise, without distraction."

"You want to keep them in here? The mages?" It is not a notion the Vile One dismisses out of hand—she is not afraid of incarceration. But Cheni laughs.

"Not a prison, kitame! Merely a calm space, their space. Purpose and luxury, voices unharried by worry or stress. Stronger wood for the arcane lattices we are building."
