With our borders expanding rapidly, we cannot tolerate further hindrances from the tensions within the Jade Mines. Our administrative staff in Grônstunad has begun planning a transition to integrate the goblins into the mine administration, expanding their roles from simple mining to logistics, food production, and smithing. Allowing the goblins more management of their own affairs also provides us an opportunity. We have long been vexed by the Undergrowth Goblins’ continued belief in the superstitious nonsense of the duplicitous shamans, but their existence served as an important focal point for organizing the goblin clans. Now that we are reorganizing the clans, we can tear out this weed by its roots: for the goblin clans to run the mines, they must cast out the shamans and learn discipline and order. To rise, they must follow the path of the Godlost.

Enacting these changes will be no small feat—the goblins are ingenious (if bizarre) miners, but they will require dedicated support from our overseers, and we will need to carefully navigate the settlement of land between the Stolen Gem and Blackstep clans to ensure this transition is smooth. Such dedication of our resources necessitates that we choose only one clan to rule, but this will surely cause dissent from their rivals. A deft hand must guide this matter—a mithril gauntlet for those who question our rule here shall not be allowed to question for long.
