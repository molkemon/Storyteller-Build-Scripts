The tale in mind, the priests hum, reciting the opening passages, painting the image of a sweltering gale scorching a vanquished and defeated people.  Gnoll packs had enslaved her tribe before her fifth spring, and from her earliest memories, Biakata only knew the heft of manacles and the lash of the whip. Eventually, the young Mukkaron girl manifested nascent planetouched abilities, and, despite her captors teasing her ultimate purpose to be as a gusty ignition for a demon pyre, her elemental fervor shaped her mind, body, and soul into a torrent of freedom and liberation. Growing into a charismatic warriorslave with potent wind magics, she captivated human and gnoll alike before leading a united host to overthrow the gnoll matriarch. With her enslavers dead or deposed, the rasping breeze became a raging gale.  Biakata spared no time in uniting the West Salahad, turning a single pack of former slaves and masters into an indomitable whirlwind of gnolls and humans that cheered and cackled her name with awe. Casting her eye beyond the dunes and savannas, Biakata marched south, conquering the largest Binwarji kingdom of Mokatanga with the help of Dendugma steel. Alarmed, the remaining splinter Binwarji kingdoms attempted to stop her advance at the Hundred Islands Battle in 374 AA. Arrayed against a vast coalition of eight tribes, Biakata entreated a mighty water elemental to divert a segment of river, turning the flooded drylands into a hundred islands. Her foes divided and hopeless, Biakata’s army struck, scattering the last meaningful resistance to her ascent. With her domain forged, she proclaimed an empire reborn – the Second Fangaula Empire. And thus the raging gale became a mountainfelling hurricane; for the rest of her reign, the planetouched Empress ruled as mistress of the known world.
