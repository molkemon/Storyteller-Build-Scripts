In the 15th century, Yanshen's gemstone processing industry was constrained by both limited raw materials and the superior production capabilities of Raheni. However, with technological advances and new supplies arriving from Beikling and the Pugao Mountains, a new wave of gemstone craftsmanship has begun to flourish across Yanshen. Artisans now employ advanced gold-wire filigree inlays and carved flower engravings, producing ornamental jade pieces in the distinct local style. These refined works have surged in popularity among the wealthy elite and show immense potential for overseas trade. The gemstone market, once niche, now stands poised to become a pillar of industrial growth.
