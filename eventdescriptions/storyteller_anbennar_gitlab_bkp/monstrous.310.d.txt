We have had to learn to bow. It would seem like a small thing to adopt the customs of our neighbors, but our talons are unwieldy when not perched or nestled somewhere comfortable, and as we move our torsos forward our wings seem to ripple uncomfortably. Every time we do so, we long to take flight and pay tribute in a way more befitting our kind, but a harpy flying in a human court still causes terror beyond our intentions.  There have been benefits to our integration, of course. Some of the neighbors have been receptive to our overtures, and in turn have sent diplomats who, when not quaking in fear, try and fail to hold themselves with the bearing of a harpy. From these representatives we have learned to speak the language of migration treaties and foreign relations, but there is much to learn and many adjustments to be made before that potential can be realized.
