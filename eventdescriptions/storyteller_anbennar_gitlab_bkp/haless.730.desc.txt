The annual 'Little New Year' has arrived once more in the coastal towns along the route of the Treasure Fleet. Originally a fisherfolk's ritual of offerings to the sea for calm waters—marked by lanterns, libations, and incense—this festival has since evolved into the most bustling, trade-rich celebration on Yanshen's eastern coast. As the day approaches, fish-patterned lanterns are strung across streets, red silk knots tied to prows. Open-air stages are built; salty sea ballads ring out as street performers entertain crowds. At alley corners, fishers hold the "Shell Banquet" with salt-boiled seafood and rice brew to welcome guests. Traveling merchants set up stalls, selling foreign textiles and spices. Teahouses and cloth halls open so-called 'Dressing Booths' for curious shoppers to don imitation foreign fashions—each stranger and more vibrant than the last.

This year, the festival coincides with the docking of a grand Northern Ocean fleet. Traders, artisans, and diplomats from many nations arrive, and the celebration reaches new heights. Flags flap across crowded streets, voices mingle in a dozen tongues, and townsfolk from all around gather to revel together.
