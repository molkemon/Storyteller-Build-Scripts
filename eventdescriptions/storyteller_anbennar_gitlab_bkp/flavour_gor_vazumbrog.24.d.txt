To the Esteemed Lords of the Council, I scarcely know how to begin, for words falter in the face of what I must recount. When we heard the news, we marched straight to Gor Vazumbrog, determined to avenge our lord and halt her rampage. Six thousand strong we were, each soldier armed with the finest weapons our forges could provide, each one ready to lay down their life for the cause. It made no difference. She came upon us with a ferocity that defies comprehension. Her scales glistened like amethyst in the dim light, her breath a cloud of venomous despair. When it touched us, it was not death alone that followed, but madness; a searing, unrelenting torment that turned brothers to enemies and left others clawing at their own flesh. Our spears shattered against her hide. Our muskets, our cannons, all useless. In mere minutes, she reduced our mighty host to ruin. The screams. I cannot describe them. I will not. The earth itself seemed to recoil from Her wrath, and when she departed, nothing remained but ash and echoes. I write this with trembling hands, knowing I have failed those who followed me. Of my command, few survive. My scars are not visible, but they burn all the same. She cannot be faced as we are. She is death, and we are but stone awaiting the hammer. Lieutenant Harruk Bronzeblade
