Flaymistrex Draheann decamps from her stage, immediately storming to her chambers and grabbing a pen in anger. "This is disastrous," she groans, as an attendant takes off her spiked wig and claw-cap nails. She begins penning a letter to the Council in the capital, full of righteous wrath.

"To the Council in Kobildzan,

The art of the Drag Monarch has been the cornerstone of kobold theatre for generations. From the times long before Kobildzan—long before our current splendor—the dragon-priests have sought to teach the masses through performance. In the plays of our past, so many would act as the foolish and young kobold, while one great priest would take the role of a dragon. This role, known as the 'Dragon Monarch,' became a staple of kobold learning and virtue. They spoke of the necessities of contributing to the Hoard, of honoring the dragons, and of making ourselves greater through family and tradition.

Drag is an art that still thrives, my technocrats, but it is in danger. While Ascension has allowed the Queens great flexibility in how we practice our art (for never before have we been able to fly above the audience, or grow our claws, or breathe fire upon the simulated unworthy), it has also empowered the unfaithful within our midst. I write to tell you I have seen sin within performances! Performers breathing fire not to teach, but simply to entertain; Queens who tell tales of sinful kobolds, lauding kobolds who rejected the dragons or who find enlightenment elsewhere.

You, my dragons, must put a stop to this. Please, for the art of Drag, and for the soul of Kobildzan, ensure that Drag retains its holy and righteous character!"
