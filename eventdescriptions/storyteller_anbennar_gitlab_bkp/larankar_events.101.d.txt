Lanahana held her breath as the guard walked past, silent-still. Her illusions could fool most eyes, and most of her muffled footsteps, and most of her near-but-not-quite-silent breaths could slip past most untrained ears. But most was not all. All it took was an ill-timed pluck on fate’s thread for her entire plan to fall apart. That could not do. She was a master assassin of the Na’Cailana, a Cultist-in-Shadow, a devotee of Neravali – and today she killed the ruler. The assassin crept her way through the palace. Gilded, gem-encrusted furniture, curtains and rugs of the finest silk and taffeta cloth, jungle-wood deftly stolen from the outskirts of that nightmare to the north, racks of Precursor blades, glistening and ruin-made – everything she passed was the picture of opulence. It hid the blood that had been shed to raise this building. It took Lanahana’s mind off the blood that would be shed today, and that which would be shed because of today.

Finally, she arrived in the Emperor’s office. He looked to be hard at work, engrossed in some report or other, eyes skimming parchment held in one hand as he absent-mindedly scribbled something down with the other. The assassin crept close to the walls, finding whatever shadow she could grasp, mindful of the way the candlelight shimmered against her glamour. Finally, she had moved behind him. She drew her blade, slowly, carefully. Took a step forwards. Then the Emperor looked up, set aside his quill and paper. The assassin froze.

“I always thought I’d have more time,” he said, as he stood, pushing his chair away. “I had such plans. Such glorious plans…”

He turned towards the assassin, drew a dagger from his belt, and charged, war-cry bellowing against the silence.
