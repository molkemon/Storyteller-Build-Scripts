On the grounds outside Anvilwright, a festival commences in full. Dwarven and gnomish merchants and stands operate side by side, selling a variety of goods to travellers across the Empire. Many have heard the news and wish to witness a truly historical moment, the completion of a dwarven hold. As the hour approaches, their lord and their lord head to a raised platform in the middle of the festival, right in front of the gate of the hold. On one side stand gnomish soldiers and on the other dwarven. The audience and atmosphere goes silent as a greylooking gnome is helped up to the platform alongside the mayors. It is Oddannamm, Chancellor of Giberd, given the honour of a speech for his diligence in seeing the hold to completion. "A great day has come for our people. Two kin, both alike in dignity, In fair Anvilwright where we lay our hold From fierce debate found new bounded polity Where dwarvengnomish brilliance unfolds From forth the sweat and blood of we two folk Our debt is paid, our peoples know one yoke. And with that, I have but one more thing to say. I retire." With that, Oddannamm cuts the ribbon in front of the hold and slowly shuffles away, to the cheers of the crowd.
