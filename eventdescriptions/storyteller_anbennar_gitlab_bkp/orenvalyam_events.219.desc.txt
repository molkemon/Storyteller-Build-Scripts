Nobody saw the Kamrayakval die, nor knew how it happened. He'd been fighting on the frontlines for a century— one of the wounds he'd received was bound to kill him eventually. They'd found him watching the sky through one of the windows of his palace, the collar of his armor fixed around his neck. With a click, it came off, so easily.

Immediately, panic began. His loyal and fanatical officers— not the High Kavalali, of course, whose remaining number had been thoroughly beaten into submission by the military that ostensibly served them— were the ones to re-establish order.

It was to some surprise, then, that when they opened the Kamrayakval's will, they discovered it was not actually their job to follow it. Nayiru-Reborn had ordered the armor secured until it was needed once more, and the Kavalakuttam's normal functions restored, and the most hardened policies of his reign— the mass executions, the uncompromising redistribution, and the harrowing retribution— set aside. There was a caveat to all of it, of course: when the invasion of Halcann came, when Dawn finally swept over the rest of the world, all of it would be needed again.

There was a muted surprise, at first. Some of the officers thought the words were forged, but the old members of the Kamrayakval's Company knew his handwriting well, and it wasn't. They all understood eventually, though. When Dawn came at last, they could not live forever as if they were in Darkness. And besides... the Kamrayakval was not a fool. He knew the military could keep their relevance no matter what. He knew his people would not shy away from what needed to be done. He knew that he had made his mark. That his actions had locked-in-flame the destiny of Halann.

The world would end. Dawn would come. The Oren Nayiru would see it through.
