Her body lay before us, lifeless and so still. My second love—our second love—no longer moving. She was gone, not speaking, not laughing, no warmth, no breathing. Leli simply wept beside me. Two weeks since we were told, two weeks of grieving her passing, yet the pain never subsided. And upon this world, I felt like an intruder.

I sniffled, held Leli tightly, had to remain strong for us both, gently rubbing her shaking back. I thought of Kyola’s Oath. We held no such power, no strength nor beauty like hers. Now I felt the weight of her absence, how deeply she had changed us.

How could I do without her? Without her late-night reassurances? Her guiding confidence? Her endless love? How could I ever hope to make this world better as she did?
