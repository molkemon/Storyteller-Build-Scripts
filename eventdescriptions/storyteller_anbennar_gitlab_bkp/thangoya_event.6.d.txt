It was a few years ago, but Mya still felt like it was yesterday. The offer at the feast, the escape from Lapnam Amrik and the shame she brought to the family name. She hated any system which forces her to make that decision. The emotions fueled her to become stronger. To be the change she wanted to bring. And that night was the culminating point.  Leading a band of 200 men and she sneaked into the slums. She missed the city, even at its worst. The smell of iron at the weapon markets, the deafening cheers at the Teplinbasiet, men showing off their scars. But it was not the time for cherishing memories. One last meeting under a moonless night, as dark as it could get. The plan is set. Spread, Storm and Kill. A loose alliance, tied by Mya’s promises of wealth and influence in the new status quo, split into parties and received their target, an important and problematic figure in the current power structure. Many were still unsure whether it was a good idea to side with Mya, but it was already too late to turn back or renegotiate.  She turned to her cousin Lim. He was visibly nervous, thinking of every way this could go wrong. Mya comforted him, appealing to his pride; a mercenary always fulfills his side of a deal. His expression shifted as he stood up and scattered with his crew to enact the coup.  It wasn’t the glorious fight of her dreams, but it was what she wanted. He, his retinue, his family, and all of Lot Dekkhang laid dead before her. The clouds parted, revealing the fatal slash wounds across his chest under scarce moonlight. Mya cherished the hollow feeling she felt. Lim at this point should’ve killed his heir. By tradition, even if for the first time in this way, the title of Lot Dekkhang was passed onto him. It was over.
