"Convoys ain't enough." Our leader struck the table hard enough to rattle the cutlery. "We raid a Calassani convoy? They send two more. We snatch a VG brig? They write it off and raise their rates. Deranne sails in neat little lines and calls it a loss in next season's ledger. And do you know what that bloody bastard of an Alloy Dwarf told me lately when we've asked him about their shipping lines? That they don't give a rat's dick about it and shrug it off with interest. INTEREST. We risk our necks and they balance it on a page."  "We're chasing crumbs." an old sailor shouted. "We nip at the margins, for every boat of theirs we take, they have ten spare ones." another voice joined. "They don't treat us seriously, bloody landgrubbers." Others voices rose in anger "You bunch of wankers, you have more booty than you could've imagined and these fools among you want to ruin the most lucrative business for nothin' but wounded pride."  Mere seconds later, the doors exploded inward. Wind and rain burst through as a young captain strode inside, cloak snapping behind him, boots dripping with seawater. Onto the central table he threw four scrolls which unfurled as they landed. Detailed maps of Cas Colunn, New Calas, Asraport and Ricardsport; the great trade arteries of the Ruined Sea. Silence smothered the tavern.  "You old bastards think that stealing from carts will grow us rich. The gold's in the counting houses, vaults, docks and warehouses."  Our leader narrowed his eyes. "Ports are fortified."  The captain answered with a smirk; "Yes. But beaches are not. Ever seen those Mykx dogs launch straight from hull to sand? No siege, no warning. Blink and corsairs are already inside. And it just so happens we know of all the right beaches to strike."  The tavern stirred and erupted in heated argument. "That's suicide.", "That's tantamount to war.", yet a broad grin appeared on the face of the leader.
