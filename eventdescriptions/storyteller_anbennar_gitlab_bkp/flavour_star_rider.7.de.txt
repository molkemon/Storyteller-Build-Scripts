Months have passed, but the conclave still droned on, with only minor breaks. The end would not be in sight for another year or so. There was still excitement, but the task was sincerely underestimated. It became rather difficult to codify all the various and partially incompatible myths of each sect into one coherent understanding of the faith. Foresight, it seemed, could only help to a certain degree. But it certainly did help. The orators had become more refined as time went on, and the conclave’s conclusion might run against the wishes of the Star Riders if they had not planned for this. It would not have been bad, of course. Just unideal. They respected the wishes of the conclave, no matter where it would have gone. But they decided where it went, for now. The nature of how they celebrated their faith, now and forever.
