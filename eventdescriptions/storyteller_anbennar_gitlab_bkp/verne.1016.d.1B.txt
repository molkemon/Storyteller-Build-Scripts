My band of Vernmen staggered out of the maze of rooms. We’d made it. Even they couldn’t help but be amazed.  It was a taller and broader dome than the Hall of the Exemplaries at the Vernissage; maybe the biggest indoor space I’d seen in my whole damn life. At its centre, a plume of water, a column of refracted transparency rising right to the ceiling. The fountain was so perfect that it appeared almost still, a pillar of glass. Never saw anything like it before or since. Was clear that it fed the lake we saw up top, which was funny, ‘cause we’d been worried about whether the lake water would be potable. Probably the purest water in the world at the time.  You wanna know why I left the academy.it was the look on those boys’ faces. Awe. They were too young, too stupid to be stuck out in the sticks with an asshole like me, dying for research. And I didn’t want to condemn them to it anymore. Let someone else lead them to their deaths for a change.
