In the province, there has recently been a dramatic increase in racial segregation. A combination of deliberate city planning measures by our administrators and unwelcoming attitudes from the population have forced the region’s minorities into rundown neighborhoods that are overpopulated and unsanitary. The suffering in these ghettos, consigned to the remote corners of the city, is immense, but it is also easy to miss; sticking to the prosperous parts of the city, one might never know that there are such terrible areas in town. We could ensure that infrastructure extends to those neighborhoods and force changes in some of these more xenophobic administrators, but such an operation would surely be unpopular.
