While Akalšesi coronations since our twin empires were shattered still received representatives from nearly every realm and significant polity in Bulwar, such representatives were frequently there with only a small party, and were usually simple ambassadors or envoys, rather than members of ruling families or other important figures in their own right. Furthermore, the fact that the Kings of Akalšes were crowned by the Nansaleni Matriarchs led to scoffing and disdain by many of those representatives, who saw that steadfast tradition as further evidence of Akalšesi decline. Now, though, as King [F33.Monarch.GetName] is once more crowned by [Root.Monarch.GetName], the attending delegations are much more impressive, with major city-states and surviving elven realms sending not mere ambassadors, but relatives of the ruler, if not the rulers themselves. Many of these are nervously looking on as [F33.Monarch.GetName] rises, proclaimed as the King of Kings by [Root.Monarch.GetName]. With a steely look in [F33.Monarch.GetHerHis] eyes, [F33.Monarch.GetName] declares that Akalšes will only rise further, and that [F33.Monarch.GetSheHe] will not rest until Akalšes has carried out the Divine Duty of Kings to unite all of Surael’s Garden under [F33.Monarch.GetHerHis] rule.
