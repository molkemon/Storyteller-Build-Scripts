A curious bard's song from the swamp has started to reach our court.  This bard, who goes by the name Bardtholomew the 52nd, tells a story of some fortuitous settlers and a new delicacy:  Upon the beach of a mirky pool,  With towels matched, the color of jewels,  A brave soul ventured under a dock,  To which they spied a humble rock,  Yet a stone so still this did not be,  For it was the Swamp Lobster they spotted with glee.  Accompanying tales tell of settlers finding hundreds of these small crustaceans.  These delicious morsels, more a giant crayfish than a true lobster, are said to be raising the morale of all and may even act as an aphrodisiac.
