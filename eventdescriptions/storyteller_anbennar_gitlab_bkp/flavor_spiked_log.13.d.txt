‘In the distant past, the northern lands of Escann were ruled by a mostcruel tyrant, Nichmer, the SorcererKing. When he fell, his lands split, and from the ashes of his empire rose many kingdoms. They were connected by a shared spirit, that of Chivalric Virtue. Honesty, bravery and respect were supposed to become their way of life. Well, my friend, you know how humans are; they tend to hide their flaws behind lofty ideals. These noble knights didn’t hesitate a jot to fight each other in public and steal each others’ women. Hypocritical, I know, but I couldn’t say that to their face because they were very, very strong.’  ‘As I recall, when they first settled here, our northern cousins told us that these knights killed them blindly and very proficiently.’  ‘True. Even if they ultimately failed, they also put up a glorious fight against the orks. After all, knights ride giant beasts, whose bodies were protected by a skin of metal. Most of them run like the wind, but some can even fly and bring death from the sky. The knights themselves wear colorful armor and dress their beasts with elaborate garments. I’m telling you, their charges were a sight to behold: a tremor, followed by a cloud of dust, and then a flurry of metal and bright colors.’  ‘Well, that explains why the youngest riders in the military were so adamant in creating a knightly order of their own. Although cladding feystompers in silver still seems like complete nonsense to me, even today.’  Qlak the Wanderer nodded at his listener and laughed.
