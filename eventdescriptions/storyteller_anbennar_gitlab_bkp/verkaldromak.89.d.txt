Pitch is the darkness, but you are not blind. Cold is the darkness, but you are not cold. Lone is the darkness, but you are not alone. Quiet is the darkness, but you are the Hunter. And all your depths will be lit with an azure sun that even darkness fears.  First was the rumbling, not like the mining of our ancestors but the screeching song of braying nightmares. Second was the tide of gray, deep hunting creatures we thought invaders. Third was our petty celebration, piled high with cheers and accolades of now crimson caverns. But fourth, fourth was the rumbling, not the nightmares once more but the Scouring.  Bright was the darkness, blinding and beautiful. Warm was the darkness, charring and waxing. Crowded was the darkness, our ghosts crushing the walls. Loud was the darkness, filled with the rasping roar that rumbled the mountain. And all our depths and homes, lit by a purple neargod from whose scales even darkness fled.
