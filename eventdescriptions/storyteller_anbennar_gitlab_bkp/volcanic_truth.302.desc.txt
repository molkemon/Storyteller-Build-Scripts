As everything changes around us, the implementation of change within our realm remains disturbingly bound to established institutions. Mechanisms for change are proposed and discussed in the same hallowed halls which have housed our government for many years, enacted by the same legislative procedures devised by our ancestors, and approved by the same people who have always made the decisions. Can this truly be called change?  If there truly is to be a genuine change in the lands of the Volcanic Truth, the methods by which we change the world around us must change as well. We must experiment with new and radical forms of decisionmaking, so that the very direction we change in is itself in constant flux.
