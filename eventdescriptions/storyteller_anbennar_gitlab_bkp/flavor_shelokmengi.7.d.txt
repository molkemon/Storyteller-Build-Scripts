The former Mezhi walked through the fields outside his palace, marveling at the bounty before him. On the cliffs stood a procession of priests, guiding the winds and rains to feed the crops. His guards were elsewhere, presumably with his son, the Mezhi; it wasn’t like anyone was going to attack him, of course. So it was just him and the fruits of his labor. Well, the grains of his labor.

There was a time, so long ago he barely recalled it, that his mother had dragged him from a spot nearby and told him in hushed tones that his advisors schemed against him, like the lords across the river who had slain his father. There was a time when Berhan would advise him, and Shay would fight for him, and Bati would pray for him—all gone now. Ayufar, defiant to the secret wishes of his nobility, had outlived them all.

His son would find him sitting with his back to a tree, a smile on his face, his empty gaze overseeing what had once been his realm. Ayufar II zor Fantu-Amru, former Mezhi of Shelokmengi, died fulfilled.
