It was done. The last battle fought. The final treaty signed. As I walked alongside Mitrenya through the Tyerna Krarh, I was vividly reminded of my mother's nickname for her.  The "little wolf" wasn't so little anymore.  I watched her from the corner of my eye. She appeared completely relaxed, as if she hadn't just seized control of all of Sarda. As if she hadn't just done something so many had called impossible, after the empire fell. To everybody else, she seemed the ideal ruler; composed, powerful, intelligent, and entirely capable. It was difficult for me to recognise the girl I had grown up with.  Difficult, but not impossible.  "Won't you join me tonight, Varynna?" She asked, turning to face me. I paused, taking her in.  "Of course, my dear Lady." I replied. She scoffed, waving her hand as if to waft away the title.  "Enough of that, sweetling." Her response was just as mocking, and I couldn't keep myself from grinning. "Now, let's hurry. Dinner is in order, and then I have some ideas to share with you."  Oh, how far we had come. How much further could we go?
