Another Gommo voyage is chartered for Haraf, and it departs without so much as a farewell. Relying on Gommo connections that stretch a thousand years into the past, the previous divisions among the gnomes—the struggles between Cliff and Creek, and of state versus Gommo—have faded away. In their place, two camps of gnomes have emerged: those who embrace us and the dragons, and those who hold fast to the Thought and the Thought alone. Even the latter group, though, still maintains connections with our people. They study the dragons but do not worship them; they see potential in Ascension, even if they do not desire it exactly as we do. In Haraf, they consort with goblins and kobolds alike, finding collaborators (and rivals) in Zurzumexia and Mestikardu. The southwestern peninsula of North Aelantir becomes a hotbed for artificery, unified by one thing and one thing alone: an opposition to our rule.
