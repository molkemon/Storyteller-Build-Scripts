Our lord stood outside the torchlight of the camp and gazed upon the stars. The sights of the plains were by this point easily recallable, but seeing them for another time always brought our lord joy. Tonight, there was a storm brewing on the horizon, and the rain was already picking up. There was something different about the storm tonight. As the wind swirled and the rain poured, our lord came up with the difference: there was a presence tonight. It was present in the clouds above, and it was present in the lightning of the storm, and it was present in our lord, too.

Our lord left the camp and began to run towards the lightning. Our lord ran over dirt congealing into mud as if it was stone, and as our lord ran, the lightning began striking closer and closer, until the sky loosed a terrible peal of thunder, and a bolt of lightning struck our lord directly.

Our lord was not harmed. The presence from before had taken our lord over, and as our lord began to run faster (for our lord could run so much faster than our lord had, and it was only now that our lord could see that), the blessings of Irdaeos came to our lord’s mind unprompted. The welcoming of the centaurs to the plains and the navigation of the Wild Hunt through its lean times were not luck, they were fate itself guided by Irdaeos, who holds dominion over the land and sky.

As our lord came to a halt in the middle of the camp again, our lord saw that our lord’s pelt was lanced with angry red streaks and our lord’s hoofs were coated in a soot that was not there when our lord began to run. Our lord also saw that our lord’s followers were bowing to our lord, and the moon shone on our lord alone, and the grasses were whistling.
