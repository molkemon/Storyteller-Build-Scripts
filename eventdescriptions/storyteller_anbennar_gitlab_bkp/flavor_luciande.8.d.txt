Lucian sits once again in his room, drinking. The red liquid pours from the goblet through his lips to his throat, while the servants are becoming increasingly doubtful that it is wine. There have been a lot of rumors lately. Our lord has entered the age when many others would have already been sleeping the eternal sleep for years; yet his beauty remains that of a man in his prime. Chiseled chin, gleaming eyes, and a forehead without a wrinkle or a worry in sight. But our lord does worry. Lucian worries about these exact accusations he faces even more. The Masquerade must continue. Soon the elder vampires will call Lucian out of his duties to stand in front of them. To continue ruling, he must reassure them that he reigns supreme and unquestioned in his domain.
