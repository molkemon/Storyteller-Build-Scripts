When first we learned of the grand abandoned automatory buried deep in the province, we saw it as a vast sterility. The scale was beyond comprehension, surely the reflexive process of a machine, some indifferent mechanical loom churning out mechanim after mechanim. Yet as new lives and new bodies are assembled, our automatories, mere workshops in comparison, are the site of miracles. One can dismiss it as projection, of course: the sentimentality of the people towards anything which resembles, even conceptually, the newborn child. Yet when the mechanim awaken, we act as parents. We guide them into this life. The reductionists among us can scoff at the flicker one feels as the kauga soul-core locks into place, but try it yourself.
