Words, stories, memories, names– these are slippery concepts. Sometimes when cleaning a fish, the scales cling to your fingers even while the flesh dares to fall out. Sometimes an oar slices too quickly through the water. Sometimes a mast line escapes from our grip. Sometimes we err.  It appears as though our efforts to document new Chronicles have proven too eager. We forget critical details and misplace historical events even while we overemphasize irrelevant practices. Our efforts remain earnest, but these errors have nonetheless begun to frustrate our sources. They’ve compassionately and patiently explained the same concepts to us multiple times, but our misinterpretations have cost our benefactors valuable time pursuing other more material endeavors.
