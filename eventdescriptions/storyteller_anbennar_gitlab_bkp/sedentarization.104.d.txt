The centaurs cannot easily fit into many of our buildings, but have quickly set up a few tents outside of our outpost. They have demanded the governor and their attendants follow them to their encampment. As the governor walks through the encampment, he feels a sense of foreboding as every centaur he passes glares, snorts, and turns away. The centaurs stop once they reach a large plaza—more like a clearing—around which the largest tents are set up. The centaurs look down upon the governor before speaking to him in their harsh tongue. The shaman translates.

We are not wanted here. That we are even being warned is a courtesy. They say we are lucky that we were not found by something they refer to as "The Filustkhuga," who would not have been so 'kind'. If our outpost is still here when they next come by, they will be coming with weapons. We must prepare.
