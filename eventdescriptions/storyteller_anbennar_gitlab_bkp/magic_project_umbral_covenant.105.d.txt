The lattice grows. Every day, new minds—new lives—join its vast, ineffable network. Sometimes, the Vile One loses Herself among the new arrivals. She reaches into a baker, for example, feeling their distress. She maps their complex confusion at the loss of privacy, the profound strangeness of suddenly knowing one’s neighbor, one’s brother, so intimately that it hurts. Most resolve themselves to their new reality; the stabilizing presence of the Vile One Herself and of Cheni provide handholds for this perverse new sense. Those whose horror becomes too pronounced are tugged back by the consensus, or by the presence of the Vile One Herself. Suicide, as such, simply no longer occurs to people as an option.

She discovers, to Her delight, that many lose themselves in exploration. They contact one another across the lattice of minds, synaptic sparks across the emergent all-brain of Sarhal. They move closer together in a space that is not space, stars clustering together in a galaxy which is not a galaxy. They do this as they press out the dough in the morning, as they walk to school, as they spar in the courtyard. The enormity of their mindspan is humbling, and yet the Vile One can take a little power from each little life, a shield for all-Halann.

At the heart of it all is Herself, a center which is not a center, a core which is also periphery. She suffuses everything, is everything. The only place her lattice-folk do not go is where the shadow lives, where dwell those grand hag-taught empowerments which made the establishment of this lattice possible in the first place. It is the only place left which is truly the Vile One, untouched and alone.
