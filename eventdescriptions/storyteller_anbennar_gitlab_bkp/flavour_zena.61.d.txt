"Kiusz thought that we should just find some ‘damaged goods’ or rejects from a breeder and ask for them, which I waved off. No, we needed the real deal here. Fortunately, there were occasionally wild Varghunda packs formed by runaways or dogs whose masters had died.  We took to the hunt, looking for tracks and signs of their meals, and following them to a den. Taking on the whole pack would’ve amounted to suicide, even for hunters as skilled as us. We laid in the snow for days on lookout, staying downwind, constantly observing. Finally, one broke off from the pack, and we managed to tag it with a sleeping concoction and haul it aboard our ship waiting in port in Naugsvol. It caused a fair share of trouble on the voyage back to Bivuzena, almost breaking a mast with its powerful frame, but it eventually settled into its enclosure; an artificial meadow filled with blue roses in the Menagerie. I marked the location of the den on the map just in case we ever need more."; The Autobiography of Fauna Hunter Bastirai Kazuru, Chapter 5.
