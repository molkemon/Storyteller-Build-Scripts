They found what they were looking for in what appeared to be some sort of bedroom. Perhaps where the old Slaver-Noble slept while he was deep in his studies or his fanciful machinations. Our lord had always wondered about what it was like to be immortal – to spend thousands of years with unlimited resources. The things you could do… well, making a massive hammer wasn’t on our lord’s personal list. But the thing was impressive regardless. The Hammer of Anathazhilan rested on the Slaver-Noble’s desk; its head was bigger than the Patriarch’s. A core of bone-white metal – the same Chenochallum that the golems that had assailed them were made of – was encased in rippling Ruin-silver – even the shaft was made of Precursor steel. A letter had been laid atop the Tuning Maul’s head. “Only a Tyrant may wield this hammer.” It read. “May the screams of the righteous ring out once more.”

The Patriarch gingerly wrapped his hands around the steel handle – for a moment, there was no Rakulacan boisterousness or bravado, just reverence. Then he felt the surety of the grip – the throbbing vibration of the steel – and the power returned. She hefted the maul, straining for a moment, before she became accustomed to the weight. Then she turned, leveled the maul – looked around the room for a moment, before she settled on something – an engraved, circular sheet of Precursor steel – some type of plate. “Bring it to me!”

One of the sell-swords did – set it down before her king when he ordered so. Our lord took a breath – then heaved, swung and smashed. The plate practically burst as the Tuning Maul made contact – and it let out a terrible, piercing, primordial scream as it shattered. Once the shock wore off, the Patriarch smiled, and raised the Hammer of Anathazhilan high.
