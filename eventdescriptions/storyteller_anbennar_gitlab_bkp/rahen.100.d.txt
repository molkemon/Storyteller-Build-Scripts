Ruby Gardens of Parusad Bhola, Morning  My dearest diary,  The scent of jasmine and raindrenched roses still lingers in my hair from my walk through the outer terrace this morning. I hardly slept last night; a terrible thing for a bridetobe.a whispering wind blew in from the Gulf of Rahen, and I kept awake listening to its promise of a thousand tomorrows. Oh Nermali. Pray take away the bags under my eyes.  In just three days, I will stand beneath the Arch of Ascension and bind myself to Prince Ahmendra in the old rites of Anjita and Vadhan. I tremble to think of itnot from fear, but from the impossible warmth that fills me from chest to fingertip whenever I read his letters, or see his smile beneath that ridiculous turban the portraitartist insisted he wear to "seem wise." He is wise (more than wise.) but also kind, and funny, and he loves the stars as I do.  Oh diary, what is a bride to do? To be wed to someone she has never seen? It is no matter.
