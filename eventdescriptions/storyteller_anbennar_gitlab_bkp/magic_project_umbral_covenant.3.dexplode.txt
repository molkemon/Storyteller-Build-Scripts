Ikomeye is excited; she wanders among the fragments of the zatsarya wall through which the Vile One's troops, with much effort over many months, have finally blown a gate-sized hole. Taken from the site of the zatsarya itself, the wall fragments have been arranged for her inspection, her divinatory insight. Ikomeye always delights when things of great prestige are sullied—be they buildings or realms, human or otherwise. It is her revenge against the powerful.

"These fragments will work as sympathetic objects, if we simply wish to divine the layout of this place," Ikomeye says, holding one of them and turning it over in her palm.

"Mapping it manually would require an army," says the Vile One, "but..."

"But divination of such an enormous structure will be onerous. It is beyond my craft alone, at least in any good time," Ikomeye says, eyeing the jet-like gleam of the zatsargakh.

The Vile One smiles faintly. "Indeed, I am not."
