Influences from the Greentide were more than just blood and battle. Although many would be loathe to admit it, orkish culture began to seep into certain areas, though in many cases it wasn't truly popular until someone else took the style and adapted it for Cannorian audiences.  Such was the case during the Badarazi period, where the Verne Camerata, a group of poets and musicians, began producing new music using commonpractice tonality, or writing a piece in a particular key. This caught on very fast, and even more orkish influences filled in soon after, such as the introduction of the powerful driving bassline. Music also became larger, with the creation of the orkhestra. Music wasn't just single bards anymore, it was an experience, grand and serious.  Some stories say that when one of the members of the Verne Camerata first heard orkish music, he asked the musician what he called this fantastic music. The ork responded "Badaorazi", which means "New Song", as in he had not named the song yet. The Vernman, not knowing orkish very well, liked the name and used it from that moment onwards as this style of music, which was quickly picked up by others.
