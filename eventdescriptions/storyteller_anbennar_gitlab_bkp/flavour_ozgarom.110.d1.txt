Ye who suffer under the slave's yoke; ye who in the shadow of the workshop must tend the welts upon your back; ye who know what it is to suffer... there is an Ozgar orc at your side, even now. This is the pledge of the Ozgar people, who have endured misery and refuse to inflict it. We do not promise an end to suffering—nor do we promise only peace. We offer the knowledge, in the depths of despair, that we are searching for you.

The maroons of Ozgarom kept the date secret. Many thousands of them, all aware, and not a single one a snitch. Perhaps there were those, paid by the colonial authorities to keep tabs on their settlement, who, even in the depths of their cynicism, could not betray their fellows. Perhaps for just a moment they felt that perfect hope: this might work! Perhaps this is why they said nothing; perhaps this is why Ozgarom caught everyone by surprise.

Overnight, a realm. Already the Ozgar Orcs have declared the end of slavery and the end of the expropriation of the native Soruinics. They demand that estate and plantation owners leave immediately, forfeit their property, and submit to its division among their laboring orcs. They welcome surrender but fully expect to fight.
