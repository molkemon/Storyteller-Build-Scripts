When a pirate captain flies the black flag he must invoke terror in the hearts of all who lay eyes upon it. But he must also command the respect of his crew, and at least in theory commands the ship with their consent. The captain of the ship has gained for themselves a cruel and tyrannical reputation. The punishments they mete out for even the most minor and unsubstantiated offenses are excessive even by the standards of lawless men. Unrest is growing, and it is only a matter of time before the crew take matters into their own hands.
