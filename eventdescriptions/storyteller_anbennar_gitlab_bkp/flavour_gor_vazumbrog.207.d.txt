The Grand Forge had been a crowning achievement, built to drive the hold's new industry. Surveyors had declared the site stable after exhaustive testing, and for weeks, its fires blazed brightly, producing tools and weapons to support Gor Vazumbrog's resurgence.  Then the tremors began. At first, they were subtle; a faint shudder beneath the stone floor. The forge's master smith, Goren Bronzebrow, dismissed it as a quirk of deep earth. But within days, the tremors grew violent. Support beams groaned, and molten slag spilled from tilting crucibles.  'Pull the workers out.' Goren bellowed, his voice barely cutting through the chaos. Dwarves scrambled, abandoning halffinished blades and glowing ingots as the forge floor cracked beneath them.  The ground gave way with a sickening lurch, pulling the central hearth into the earth. Dust and sparks erupted as the forge sank unevenly, leaving a gaping pit in its place. Surveyors, summoned to the scene, were baffled. The bedrock had appeared flawless; solid and unyielding. Yet now it crumbled as if hollowed from below.  'The earth turns against us,' muttered a sootstreaked apprentice, clutching a warped hammer. Whispers of curses and forgotten tunnels filled the hold.  Goren stood at the edge of the pit, fists clenched. 'We'll rebuild,' he said through gritted teeth. But the question lingered in every dwarf's mind: what truly lay beneath their feet?
