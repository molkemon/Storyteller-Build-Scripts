With a sigh, Sir Adenn stood up. "It's not you I'm going to have to joust, is it?" he asked, to which our ruler shook their head. Sir Adenn grunted. The final bout of the day approached, featuring Sir Adenn and an individual named Sir Frederick (though it must be noted that it was hard to hear his name over the Adenicans finally beginning to jeer). When Sir Frederick lifted his visor to reveal a large, toothy grin missing the two front teeth, the Entebenic seating howled. The two knights were able to demand absolute silence by their very presence, both sides of the arena caught in the immediate tension of the situation. One pass, then two, then three, then four, neither was able to catch the other out. The crowd grew louder, each pass capturing the attention of an audience that anticipated the coming crash more and more. By the time one of them was dehorsed, the crowd was calamitously loud. Even though our ruler could not hear anything, watching Sir Adenn slap away Sir Frederick's hand after the tournament left them with a sinking feeling in their stomach.
