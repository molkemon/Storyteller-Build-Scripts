Whether there was ever a curse on the gold in the first place is hard to say, but there was no magical effect upon seizing the hoard. Even the most superstitious men soon agreed that there was no danger to the treasure, lifting their spirits, arguing what to do with the gold. This discussion is shut down quickly by the expedition leader, who reminds them that all loot goes to the coffers to be given to the contractor. The chronicler has become a hero overnight, finally turning our luck around again, ending the dry track without treasure.
