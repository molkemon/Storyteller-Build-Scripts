The proudest I’ve ever been of you? Trying to get me to pad your ego, huh? Alright! I’ll bite—the Drennenthile feast. You know the one! Where we celebrated the fulfillment of the pact. All humor aside, it was beautiful. I don’t know how you always find the words, always and without fail. For a long time, I thought our people were doomed. Most of my life actually. Then you and your silver tongue spun a story of hope, and through sheer will and magical might, you made it a reality. The feast was when it happened, when the bonds between Elyn, Myna, Cenag, and Drennen stopped mattering. When you convinced us all that the way forward, the best and only way forward, was together.

It helps that the meal was really good. Yes, I know I ate too much! But what else are you supposed to do at a celebration? Besides, you abandoned me and Varinna at another table to flatter the other Cean and Ceanna. I saw you make Alara laugh from the heart, and even courtly Bricena was warm to you. Old Cinrthir didn’t even mind that he wasn’t seated at the head of the table! Only you can do that, Narawen. Only you.
