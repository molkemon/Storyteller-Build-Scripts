One of the benefits of undead labor is that they will tirelessly heed any commands they can carry out. This latter point is an important distinction. Simple commands like "pick up that sword and hack at that orc" will work, but more complex commands like "go to that field and plough it" are unlikely to work with orc souls, as few orcs knew what ploughing is. Instead, they must first be directed to pick up a plough and then use it on the field. However, any tasks requiring intricate work are also unfeasible, as the souls are not responsive enough to carry out such tasks.

All of this is complicated further when dealing with humanoid souls bound to animal corpses. While these souls do know how to move a body, the body the soul is used to is different from the one it is bound to. Thus, the movements of such undead are frequently jerky and uncoordinated. While this is not an issue so long as the task is simple and the field is level—such as hauling a cart on a road—the attempts at ploughing an abandoned nearby field have shown that its unevenness will cause the reanimated oxen to frequently stumble and fall. This not only slows down the overall process but also risks damaging the plough.

To try to ameliorate this issue, testing will need to be conducted on different fields with varying levels of unevenness, as well as with a variety of ploughs, to determine whether solving this problem is feasible or if, as some within the Sepulcher insist, the focus should be put on more simple usages such as hauling carts or carrying goods.
