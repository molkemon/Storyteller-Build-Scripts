"You do not train." Umorozi's voice is oddly sweet for a warrior. Her long braids are tied with silver bands, and her limbs are sleek and muscled. She is the tallest woman The Vile One has ever seen, aside from Mama Hireet in disguise. Mama's size is her secret; when she changes form, she always becomes a giant, unlike most others. Not Umorozi. The Vile One feels a strange envy for those shiny arms, wiry and muscled, which gleam in the sunlight.

"A leader must train," she says in the cold tone she reserves for important conversations. "You are going to conquer the world. You will wake early every day to do that. You will not be weak."

Umorozi's tone is both prediction and command—much like Mama's. 

The Vile One laughs. "You are not my advisor yet. You should be boasting of your accomplishments, not scolding me like a schoolmaster."

"I am stating fact," Umorozi says. "You know my accomplishments, or I wouldn't be here."
