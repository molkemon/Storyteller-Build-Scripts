''When Surael left, we were afraid. We hid from the Darkness in the ruins of our cities. We shared the meager food that was left, some moldy grains, and stagnant water among neighbors. We hugged our children together, and slept. There was no other possible action on that night that had already been going on for 13 days.  We woke up to laughter. Our children were nowhere to be found, and scared we ran outside, only to be blinded by the Light of the First Dawn.  A light so bright, with no comparison to the dim moonlight of Kuza. The children rejoiced as they played in the ruins, under a new celestial object. Our father and creator, our lord, our Savior.  Surael, the green gardener, lord of civilization, last of our gods, who sacrificed his mortal coil to save us.  Our Sun.'' The Thirteen Days of Darkness, by an anonymous priest.
