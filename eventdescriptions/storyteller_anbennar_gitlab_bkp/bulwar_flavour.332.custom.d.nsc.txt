High up in the mistcloaked hills of the Seh Tiskesh range, a cluster of gleaming bronzeclad domes and steep ziggurats rise above the canopy of the rainforest. This is the House of the Sun’s Tears, the BetDintasur. For the local Tanizu tribes, the Izubamyisozinizu, as they call it, serves as their religious center: the place where they gather for major religious ceremonies celebrating the blessings Surael bestowed upon them throughout the year. For this purpose, a large assembly field on a plateau next to the monastery is kept continuously free of encroaching vegetation, providing both a gathering place for the faithful and a convenient location to set up a temporary tent city. But the monastery is not abandoned in between those major religious celebrations. It houses a chapter of the Servants of Surael, who, when they do not cater to devout pilgrims assembled for a religious festival, work tirelessly in the gemcutting workshops of the BetDintasur. Here they purify and rework the Inmucita crystals mined in the surrounding hills into sunstones. The crystals are said to be Surael’s tears He shed for the ancestors of the Tanizu trapped in the machinations of the Malevolent Dark. Meant to be a solace for their ancestors, the evil emanating from the Shadow Swamp attempted to corrupt this blessing. But this taint cannot stand against the purity of faith possessed by the Servants of Surael, and the bright yellow sunstones resulting from their labor are crafted into potent protective talismans and magical foci that find their way even to Bulwar and beyond.
