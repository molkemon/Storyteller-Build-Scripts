Our hobgoblin masters might have finally bitten off more than they can chew. Human warriors are marching from places they thought had been defeated, realms whose names we cannot even pronounce. People they thought we had killed—graves turned out to be empty. There are some orcs among us who are eager at this opportunity, whose eyes blaze with treachery. Our enslavement was not so long that we have forgotten the taste of freedom. They say the smell of air is sweeter when there is no scent of hobgoblin chains.

Mindless fools! They fail to see that the humans are not our friends. We sit on land that was once another kingdom, where we killed its inhabitants by axe and spear. If they defeat our masters, what keeps us from being slaughtered next? No, their retribution will be swift, and it will be called justice. And what if we betray our masters now, and yet they manage victory? Have we so quickly forgotten the strength of the hobgoblin armies? We will surely lose our lives—or worse, we shall be kept alive, and clasped in tighter irons.

When the hobgoblins organized our clans, they slaughtered the tenth of us that was the strongest and proudest. That left the cunning to lead and survive. It was a harsh lesson, but well-learned: our survival, and any hope at a future freedom, depends on us serving our masters well. Strength and obedience are the only things the hobgoblins value in us—and they shall have it. Our minds, meanwhile, shall serve ourselves. Surrounded on all sides by enemies, our only chance is to bide our time.
