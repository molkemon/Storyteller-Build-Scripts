The Sangamar King smiled as he looked out over the desert. He was standing where Olhokar had once sacrificed his eldest son to save Gophira. He had not known how successful he was – for in saving Gophira, he had saved first the greatest empire of Taychend, and then the greatest empire since the Precursors. The Gophiran Empire now stretched from the cities of the Triarchy to the coast of Soruin, from the sand-blasted wastes of Alecand to the plains of the Dry Coast. Taychend had far surpassed the heights of prosperity they reached under the Second Empire. The Ironblood Army was so large as to be uncountable, so powerful as to be unbeatable. By subsuming the warlords, and then unleashing them on the world, the Sangamars had seemingly dominated them. While its borders and buildings expanded, the Third Gophiran Empire was unstoppable.

But he knew that blood was like poison to metal – and bloodlust could be poison to his empire. There was a point, halfway through the Second Empire, that it seemed nothing could stop the turning of the Cycle, Gophira’s final victory. Then, Gophira proved that it could stop that victory all by itself. The Second Empire collapsed into a death-spiral of civil war. When the Third Empire’s borders could expand no further, would Gophira’s army then turn inwards for a new source of glory?

The king sighed. He supposed that was a question for his descendants – for now, it was all he could do to make sure Gophira did not falter, that it would rise, an Empire of Iron, an Empire Eternal…
