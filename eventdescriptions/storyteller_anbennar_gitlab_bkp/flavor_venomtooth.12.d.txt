After years of brutal fighting in the streets of Venomfen, Carlahal, and Taranton and in the Greatwoods, it was over. Bodies covered the ground, so many that not even the flies could keep up. And in the dungeons of our lord, Tuhorn and Edith were bound.  The lovers were brought to a massive pyre erected in the main square of Silvervord and watched as the flames licked the sky. Our lord pushed them forward. "By the right of the strong, you two are sentenced to death for treason, blasphemous affection, and defying the place of the weak. Any last words?"  Tuhorn glared at our lord and shouted, "I do not care about whether you consider us strong or weak. Is it not true weakness to show no mercy and treat all former enemies as slaves? Is it not true strength to show mercy, to have the ability to enact your vengeance but decide against it? If you desire to kill me, then strike me down. I refuse to live in a world where brute force determines who is considered a person and who is not."  Edith then looked at Tuhorn and said, "Where there is love, there is hope. I refuse to live in a society run by the cruel. This kingdom of yours is not long for this world, my lord. I just hope that you live to see it all crumble around you."  Our lord turned to the executioners and motioned them forwards. Tuhorn and Edith were tossed into the pyre, with guards protecting it until it died the next morning. The lovers' ashes were tossed into Lake Silvermere, so none could find them or build a shrine to their memory.  Over the next few weeks, our lord toured the country, killing revolting slaves and their sympathizers. At last, the natural order of the world was restored and all knew their place in it.
