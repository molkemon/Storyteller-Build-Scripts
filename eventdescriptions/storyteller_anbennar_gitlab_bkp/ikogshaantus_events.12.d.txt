“Fire!” The crack of gunshots rings out, and the lord watches as, across the field, a row of targets explodes into splinters. The council watches with the lord, and a few of the softer guildsmen flinch at the noise. When the sawdust and gunsmoke clears, the lord sees that every target is gone. He turns to the gunsmith beside him. “And you said that your guild can make how many of these?” “Each of us can make one in a month, so I would put my estimates in the thousands.” “Ennuk’s grace…” the lord whispers. “That’s not all, lord. We have one more demonstration, if I may.” The lord nods.

The smith shouts. “Oy! You louts! Clear the field for the cannons!” Minutes later, the lord watches as more than three dozen cannons are wheeled onto the field. The lord has seen cannons before, but never so many. They are expensive and impractical, but fearsome weapons. When they fire, this time there is no trace of the targets.

The smith smiles proudly at the lord's shock. “Three months, that was the work of. Three months!” “Her blessing truly is upon us.” The lord collects himself. “I want every man in the army with a musket by next year. And at least a dozen regiments with cannons. Can you do that?” The smith grins. “Easily.”
