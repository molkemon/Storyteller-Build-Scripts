Subconsciously the Akal had hoped that the campaign against Akalses had been a nightmare which they could not wake up from. But as the smell of burning wood and flesh assaulted their nostrils the dissociation with reality faded and the Akal began to cry. Although the Akal kept their composure in public and during the peace negotiations, the sombre truth would haunt them for the rest of their days; brothers had fought and thousands had died for the pride of their long dead empires.  Akalses has learned the bitter truth and is unlikely to rebel against us anytime soon, but granting mercy for a seditious subject may set a dangerous precedent which may see the tragedy of the Great Bahari Revolt repeated once more. Regardless of what we chose, however, it will take time before our people and community heal from this tragedy.
