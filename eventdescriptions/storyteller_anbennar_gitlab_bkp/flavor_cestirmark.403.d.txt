As in any war, the Bureau has greatly improved its arsenal and methods since the Shadow War started thanks to lessons learned in tragedy and grief. The Bureau has suggested implementing a series of unofficial laws to handle the occult, setting a framework to manage warlock and warlock-adjacent activities without making them public to avoid providing knowledge about the eldritch and supernatural to the general populace. However, this would be in direct opposition to our democratic values for obvious reasons.

There are two schools of thought about this: the first believes we should focus on being as effective as possible during the Shadow War, with these laws expiring once the situation returns to normal. The other considers that the Bureau would fail in its task to protect the soul of the nation if it goes against its values, and that these laws should be made in consultation with elected representatives, even if their memories need to be wiped afterward.
