The Kamrayakval strode into the chamber, twin-bladed, wrought-armoured. There were a few High Kavalali here… though not many. The seats were still packed, though. For now, the Chamber seats filled with his warriors. His officers, the elite of his company, the generals and bannermen that had carried Dawn to victory over Aelantir. Elf and Dragon looked around them, gazing at the diversity of Dawn arrayed on the benches. The greater part Taychendi, of course… but the teal of Kheionai alongside them, the seasons of Eordand, the long-eared Harafics, Prism-scarred Amadians, and tattooed Boek. Who else could have done this? Elrandar Silverspite was like Venaan reborn… unifier of Elvenkind.

A flash of anger from the iron. Right. Not exactly like Venaan. After all, the Kamrayakval fought for something different… and he wouldn’t stop at Aelantir. Just one last step now. One final preparation.

“WARRIORS!” called the voice from the metal. “Did I not promise you this day?! Did I not promise that we would rise?! That we would not furrow the fields with the blood of Dawn’s enemies?!”

The chamber rose as one, called with might and fury—“A PROMISE FULFILLED! A RISE UNSHAKEN! A VENDETTA WELL SATISFIED!”

“And now, for the last promise—”

A gauntlet-draconic raised the sword it gripped skywards. “TO SWEEP OVER THE WORLD—”

The warriors finished his sentence. “LIKE SUN CONQUERS NIGHT!”

And a satisfied grin beneath the visor—triumphant, victorious: “SO WHAT DO WE DECLARE?!”
