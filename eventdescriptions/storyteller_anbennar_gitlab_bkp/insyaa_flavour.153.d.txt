To call traversing the Insyaan wilderness foolish, troublesome, reckless, and suicidal would both be accurate and an understatement. Our most skilled harpy scouts likely wouldn’t last more than a week in these hostile lands, even without accounting for any Holohana. Thus, I can only commend the ingenuity of our Mechanim guides and their Artifice. They found us about two weeks ago, heated blades cutting through the flora our own blades struggled against as if it were a mere feather. We didn't notice their approach until they stepped into the light of our fire, the hue of their hull shifting and trying to adapt to the light.

Though their tools didn’t stop there: from grappling hooks that never failed to find a hold to senses that would make any elf jealous. Even the ability to somehow see heat and feel tremors from miles away.

As such, I can only recommend seeking closer ties to local Mechanim and their Trailblazers for any future expeditions and colonial ventures. Though I fear what they might learn from us if we ever were to face them in open combat…

- Excerpt from the Record of an unnamed Naleni Explorer, 1762
