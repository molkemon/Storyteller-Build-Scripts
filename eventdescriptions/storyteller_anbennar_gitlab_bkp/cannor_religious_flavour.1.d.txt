The Festival of Vows is upon us. Ryala, the blessed goddess of love and beauty, is honored by the jovial festival. A renewal of vows between lovers both in private and in public is followed by a procession of said couples throughout the streets of the capital. Donning their finest attire, devoted couples, young and old, rich and poor, are cheered on by onlookers during this celebration of Ryala's divine blessing of love. This parade lightens the heart of all our subjects as surely as the following feast fills their belies. The festival provides an opportune time for their ruler to show themselves as a paragon of devotion for their people. How should the sovereign celebrate?
