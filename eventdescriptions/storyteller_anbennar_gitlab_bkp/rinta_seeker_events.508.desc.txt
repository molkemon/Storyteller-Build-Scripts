As construction of the Godswatch nears its end, so too does the list of possible things for the Mwalim and Darwiiz to argue about. After this one last debate, all the details will be finalized. The only thing left is to decide whether access to the observatory should be restricted based on faith. The Darwiiz, ever the populists, have demanded that only those who follow the Rinta Seekers faith should be permitted within the observatory. They see it as a building of great religious importance, and allowing non-believers would trivialize its meaning. The Mwalim, however, believe that the observatory is primarily a place of learning, and restricting access based on religion would prevent foreign scholars from sharing their knowledge. An alternative option could be simply limiting foreigners to a single wing of the observatory, but both the Mwalim and Darwiiz have voiced their disapproval of this.
