In the bygone days of the Xianjie, martial sects stood in great numbers, each forming their own order amid the mountains and rivers. Back then, the Wulin had yet to be unified—sects across the land formed a loose alliance, guided by an elected Grandmaster who mediated disputes and repelled threats, yet held little sway over the internal affairs of each sect. Now, the martial world is gradually unified. The Path of Unity has been implemented for years, and the old barriers between sects are slowly being dismantled. Internal rules and governance are being integrated into the broader legal framework. Even so, some sects remain steeped in old customs and have yet to fully submit to centralized rule. Recently, a fugitive has taken shelter under the authority of a certain sect within the Xianjie. Rumors claim the sect not only harbors the criminal but has even accepted them as a disciple. The sect makes no effort to conceal this, instead invoking an old precedent—"redemption through devotion"—as a time-honored tradition. The magistrates seek to carry out an arrest in accordance with the law, but the sect has quietly issued a warning: "None may cross the gates uninvited." This has sparked widespread debate—should old traditions be tolerated in the name of peace? Should this be a moment of reckoning, to bring errant sects fully under the law? Or might a personal envoy of the Path of Unity intervene, reasoning with the sect to yield in dignity without escalation?
