Day 400: It occurs to me how impossible this life would be outside this camp. I have a partner (a wife? surely not, yet I don't think she would object if I proposed) who is a hobgoblin—she is heavy on me, and the other residents celebrate it. Such a pairing would not have been acceptable back home. I have friends, and chickens, and a pepper patch in addition to meals. It's not unpleasant. I'm not sure how I feel about that.  
Day 500: We are moving today. The tents were picked up, and heavier shackles were placed on us. Kunya is going to another camp. I begged for us to stay together, but the guards struck me twice on the head, leaving burns. Himmat... two miles down the road, they decided he was slowing us down too much. His head rolled on the grass. His eyes did not close.
