Within the court, a longstanding tradition had persisted—a pair of symbolic animals, a black swan and a whiteshelleed turtle, were kept as prestigious symbols. The original meaning of these creatures had faded into history, reduced to ornate symbols of the royal court. However, on this particular day, something felt amiss. The two animals, normally kept in harmony, exhibited unusual behavior. They grew agitated, hissing and launching attacks at each other. The court retainers intervened, attempting to separate and place them on opposite sides of the palace to quell their feud. This allowed the ruler to resume royal duties, sitting upon their throne. Yet, as the ruler settled into their regal seat, they were startled to find that the same black swan and white turtle had reappeared in the same room. One was positioned on each side, and they continued to glare at each other while emitting hisses. The ruler’s advisors were equally surprised by their sudden reappearance. Amid the confusion, an elderly priest approached the ruler and delivered a foreboding message. He warned of an impending calamity and devastation heading towards their lands. The priest explained that the spirits needed to be appeased or subdued; otherwise, unrest would persist for eternity. In the days that followed, the court found itself inundated with piles of messages, requests, and reports. Sightings of spirits, particularly the temple priests across the region, were warning that the Great Spirits had awakened once more. Their message was clear: "Calm the swan and the turtle." The ruler glanced at the two animals in their court, still hissing and locked in a bitter standoff, realizing that their actions held farreaching consequences.
