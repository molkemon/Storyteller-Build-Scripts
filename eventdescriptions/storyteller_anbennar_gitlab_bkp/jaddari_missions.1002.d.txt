Over a century ago, Jaddar Jexiszuir came down from Ebbusubtu with truths on his lips, a duty from Surael on his shoulders, and a fire in his eyes. Each day since, he has taught his truths to even those most astray or darknesstouched. He has been Suraels herald on Halann, a commander to the Desert Legion, and a chieftain to the Masnsih. And he has spread the Jadds fire across two continents. But tonight, he is dead. In Rahen, corrupt harimari host extralavish feasts; in what remains of the Phoenix Estates, heretics breathe sighs of relief. But Jaddar was not defined by his enemies, and neither will his death be. In the shadow of the Paravimvata range, a harpy matriarch flies against a sky that feels ineffably emptier, no matter how many sobs and cries she fills it with. In a tent near Ebbusubtu, an elf shuts out the unceasing praying, brushes away the tears on her cheeks, and takes up a quill to put to paper every memory she has of the nine decades she knew her father (hoping it will keep them fresh for the centuries ahead). At a small oasis, a halfelf collapses into his mothers arms, and she begins to intone the teachings of the Jadd, just as she did countless times when he was young. They are not alone. Across all lands, countless weep for the prophet that redeemed them from even the darknesss deepest clutches, the herald that led them to salvation and their place in Suraels crusade. Every night He fights the darkness, and every morning He rises anew to revitalize the world. For now, each will fight their own battles against sorrow and grief. But come the morning, the next page in history shall be written.
