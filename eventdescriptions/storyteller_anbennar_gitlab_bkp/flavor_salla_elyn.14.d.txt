The path to the Ceann's hall should not have been a long one, yet as Farwen walked through the streets of Ayethstir; despite the Arrow Grove having been liberated; she could feel the sorrow in the air, a boulder atop her heart. Pain, suffering and loss; none of these were strangers to her, yet the sheer quantity present within Salla Ayeth was oppressive; nigh suffocating.  A meeting with the Ayeth had been arranged, in honor of the reclamation of the Arrow Grove, yet there was no jubilation, nor any decoration as there had been when Salla Elyn celebrated their own liberation. There were few present: a handful of guards waiting just outside, and within the hall only Ceann Irvah and his son, Elaria, sat at a table.  Four chairs surrounded the table; two were already occupied. Farwen stepped up, offering them both a bow, intending to speak as one would conventionally do, though her voice opted to fail her, catching in her throat. Irvah gestured to one of the open seats and Farwen, wordlessly, took her place at the table. She could not help but glance at the empty seat. It was ornate, rivalling only the design of the seat that Irvah sat upon; a testament to the craftsmanship of the Ayeth. Only as Farwen turned her attention to Irvah, whose gaze dwelled upon the empty chair too, did she realize who would usually have been seated there.  "It was where my soulmate would sit, were she still here with us. Alas, we remain with naught but memories… and the pain they bring." Irvah's tone was deep and raspy.  "I.am sorry for your loss; sorry that we could not come to your aid sooner." Her tone was subdued, unlike her usual, lively self.  "You are here now. That will have to suffice; at least now we can make those wretches suffer as much as; nay, more than; we have. We can take tenfold that of which they have robbed us." Elaria’s voice filled with a cold rage that sent a shiver up Farwen's spine.
