When historians write of Harimar, they write of his lightningfast conquest of Rahen, his triumphant crossing of the Kharunyana, his implacable subjugation of Yanshen. But they do not talk about what came before all that, for all it featured battles as brutal and wars as intense. Harimar the Great, after all, was not the first harimari. Not even firstgeneration. He spent decades waging war against his brethren in the Jungles of Ascension, defeating his rivals with tooth and claw until none remained to contest his dominance. It was only after he won those wars and all their according scars that he turned his gaze outwards and changed Haless forever.
As the monarch emerges from the High Temple at Tianlou, they wonder if they too are merely finishing that first chapter of a life. All their rivals have finally bowed or broken. All who would contest their rule have been left as ashes. And in their paws is a power unrivaled in all Haless; or Halann. They blink their eyes against the bright sky, and sees the retinue that awaited their return knelt prostrate in the temple’s courtyard. Is this Harimar reborn? Does it matter? Their past is Harimar’s, their present is Harimar’s; and their future, undeniably, is that of Harimar as well.
