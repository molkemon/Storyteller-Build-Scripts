The mystery of the monstrously dangerous giant butterflies on Romutha has been solved—or at least, where they come from. They appear to have formed in a similar fashion to butterflies in our own world. A larval form of the creature consumes large amounts of food before cocooning itself to begin a stage of metamorphosis, emerging in its winged adult form.

The candidate for its larval form was obviously the giant caterpillars that roamed the countryside and had a tendency to alarm farmers when their winding paths careened towards their crops. But it took a long time to locate any of them in their cocoon stage. Researchers had been following the caterpillars for years and finally discovered where they cocooned. Once they had eaten enough, the caterpillars headed deep into the harsh rocky mountains until they found a vent, then burrowed underground to form a cocoon.

While all fascinating, what has alarmed people in Romutha is a new idea from the researchers: what if they brought the cocoon back to the colony? They believe that if they can hatch the cocoon by themselves, it may be possible to tame the giant, laser-shooting butterflies for their purposes. This has not gone over well with the colonists, who have insisted the cocoon be kept far outside their settlement.
