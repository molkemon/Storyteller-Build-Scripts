"There is nothing more reviled than the adventurer," Elgek thought as he looked over the province. He had not given the orders to loot, and yet the Hounds were already on it, plundering whatever they could get their hands on. Fatherly pride filled his heart, and a pet-demanding head nuzzled his hand. He obliged with a small smile and a big hug. "Oi, Lord! Come over here a while!" Zozzok’s voice rang out from behind him, grating as ever; somehow, hearing his new title only made it worse. Elgek turned his head around, his mount following suit. "What is the meaning of this, Zozzok? I've no need for plunder of that kind." "Oh, I know, Lord sir, but she seemed very calm when we came and then attacked us and so we attacked her and then she fainted and…" "Blyjab, Zozzok, get to the point." "Well, the crest there on the sleeve, Your Majesty, does it not look like the one you-"

Elgek cut him off with a wave and promptly got closer. Indeed, that was exactly what he’d been looking for. His mind flashed with possibilities, but ultimately he couldn’t be sure. Only one way to find out.

He insisted on not calling it an interrogation. The captive noblewoman would vehemently disagree, but in the end she told him everything he wanted. Of their home’s history and the questing nights. How they lived under the king's domes, and how they took pride in adjusting tournaments. "A life worth living," came out his mouth. "I'd be so good at dueling," ran through his head.

The door opened, and the rest of the clan stared with wide eyes at their Lord bowing his head and letting the woman go. All of their gazes followed the surprisingly alive and well captive, until Elgek cleared his throat and spoke: "Gentlegoblins! Today, we have found a new foe to vanquish. A foe most threatening, most formidable, and most elusive."
