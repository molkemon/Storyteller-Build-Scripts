Varlengeilt had never flown this long before; he had never even left the Ynn. For a week, he had soared over the realm of Uelos' Lament, only periodically stopping to sleep or slay a sea beast for food. He hated the taste of the sea, he hated actually using his wings, and he hated that he had not seen an actual pillow for days. But when he neared the Dragon Coast and saw only a cloud of ash, he knew that his presence was needed.

The first sign that something was wrong was when the monthly shipments from Kobildzan stopped reaching the Dominion. At first, it seemed like a mere logistical failure. As the months went on, and the seas stayed silent, worry spread through the Ynn. Varlengeilt even personally reached out to the representatives in Malliahea, but they, too, knew nothing of what had occurred. The silence grew louder by the day.

By the time a single stream of ships had finally arrived with news—they came from Gerudia, not the Dragon Coast, and spoke of the great blot in the sky which had seemingly wiped out Kobildzan—Varlengeilt had already flown into action. The kobolds may not be Rzentur, but they had become his people, and they had done so much for him. They had created a Dominion in his name, and merely asked to serve. In their time of need, he could not bring himself to defer responsibility.

Varlengeilt raised a fleet in the Broken Sea, filled it with supplies and Ynnic grain, and sent it towards the Dragon Coast with haste. Then, he flew ahead of it, to survey the damage for himself. As he neared the coast and spied the devastation, he knew one thing for certain: he alone would not be able to save this place, to undo whatever had been done. But perhaps he could help it heal, and perhaps that would be enough.
