The inundated temple and its great school of catfish had quickly become a focal point for its community and the nation as a whole. From here in the flooded temple, the great river had begun to heal, and yet still the catfish congregated. Scholars and scientists arrived to marvel and study the sight, but standing to one side, the devotees of the spirits seemed to come to their own conclusions. They preached of the connection that such a place had with the great spirits that had once inhabited the land, avowing that this was a sign of them. Moreover, they encouraged the inundation of more temples, to strengthen the spirit of the great river. Our ruler had come to see the sight themselves, and had spent a long evening in contemplation as the great school of fish swam slowly before their eyes. Others along the bank were throwing in pieces of bread, various other scraps of food. Catfish weren't exactly picky eaters, and at least one would dart up and snatch a piece of pretty much anything before returning to the school. The ruler turned to their advisors, the scientificallyminded of them still clearly perplexed by the phenomenon before them, and spoke. "What has happened here has only proven a boon for both us and the river; let us repeat this feat. Long has our nation watched the opposite bank, protecting the land, yet it seems the true treasure to protect was the river itself. We must strengthen it by flooding more temples, so that it may in turn strengthen us."
