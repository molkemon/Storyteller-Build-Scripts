In the end, the ancient and proud city Bulwar fell not to an open contest of arms, or knelt out of her own volition, but succumbed to treachery from within. While the Council of Twelve, confronted by the spectre of Irrliami acquisitiveness, was paralysed by indecision, one of the families silently chose to break ranks and opened clandestine negotiations with the court in Medurubar.  The Alukatran, always rumored to have a particular penchant for skullduggery, intrigue and undercover activities that were best conducted far from the light of day, had decided for themselves that the rule of undisputed first amongst the families and governors of Bulwar, even if that came at the price of an oath of vassalage to the ember crown of Irrliam, was far preferable to their current position as only one amongst twelve merchant prince clans, and not one of the more prominent ones at that.  Thus, during a particularly cloudy night, when the mists rising from the rivers concealed any untoward movement, their skilled agents swarmed the city, opening the gates and barracks to their Irrliami counterparts and making swift but bloody work of any potential sources of resistance stemming from the household guards of the other families.  Come the morning, the phoenix of Irrliam could be seen fluttering atop the only recently rebuilt walls of the city.
