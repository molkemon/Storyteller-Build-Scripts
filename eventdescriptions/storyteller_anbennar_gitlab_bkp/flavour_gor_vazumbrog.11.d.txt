The chamber buzzed with energy as Lorek Runehand tapped his runeinscribed staff against the stone floor. Around the table sat scholars with inkstained fingers, engineers clutching blueprints, and runesmiths with fragments of crystalline components laid out before them.  'Gentledwarves,' Lorek began, a broad grin spreading beneath his meticulously braided beard, 'for the first time in centuries, we have the resources and unity to dream big. The golems of old were not merely toolsthey were the very lifeblood of dwarven progress. Let us reclaim that glory.'  An engineer unrolled a schematic, revealing a massive foundry complex with interconnected assembly lines. 'The key is scalability,' she said, tapping the diagram. 'If we cast and forge bodies in standard sizes, we can reduce costs without compromising durability.'  'Efficient,' Lorek mused, stroking his beard. 'But we need data crystals; more than we can currently produce. Without them, our golems would be nothing but hollow hulks.'  A runesmith leaned forward. 'We could focus on refining runic drives. If we simplify codified instructions, the initial models could be taskspecificminers, guards, even builders.'  'And the power source?' a scholar interjected. 'Dames tear cores are potent but volatile. Binding an elemental might offer more stability.'  'But would it respond predictably?' Lorek countered, his eyes gleaming with curiosity. 'Imagine a rebellious elemental in a titanium body.'  Laughter rippled through the room. They were far from agreement, but the air was thick with excitement. For the first time since AulDwarov, the dream of largescale golemancy felt tangible.
