Our songs of fearful reverence have carried us through yet another day. We emerge from the vast canopies into entirely different environs. Here, much like the Leechdens, the song of Tswohvwoh'ii had waned until it was lost in the breeze that blows in from the sea, and the scarcity of food became a new, everpresent challenge. The absence of Her malevolence has brought little respite; instead, it left us with an unsettling void that seemed to seep into our very bones. The land was dry and unforgiving; rocky outcrops interspersed between dry riverbeds and the occasional shrubbery giving access to the only meager shade that could shield us from the burning rays shining above.  We came to this desolate land as we had known it to be the home of those that, like us, sought to be free of the song of the jungle. Here, those who would defy Tswohvwoh'ii had supposedly built their stronghold. As we waited with baited breath in the shade of a ravine by the ocean, figures approached, their forms distorted by the heat on the dunes. It was a small group of Orwa'ii, their oncewide, bigger bellied forms now diminished to a state of malnourishment and fear.  Their eyes bore a tired resentment, and their fragile figures spoke of a relentless struggle for survival. The stark contrast between the Orwa'ii we had heard of and the ones before us left us in somber contemplation. As we exchanged desperate glances and weary whispers among our own group, we couldn't help but wonder if, perhaps, the relative safety of this dry coast was worth the price of their diminishing existence in this barren refuge. It made us question whether we, too, had a choice in our own pilgrimage, or if we were but pawns in the inscrutable games of Tswohvwoh'ii, trapped between the devil and the deep blue sea.
