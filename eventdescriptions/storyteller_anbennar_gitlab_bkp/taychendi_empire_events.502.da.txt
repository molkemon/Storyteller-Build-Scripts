A hundred thousand people watched on the fields of Traerraden. Over the past few years, every inch of Tacenie had been investigated, probed, stuffed with every artificer gadget and magical implement the brightest minds of Taychend could think of. Now, those gathered—Vustarin artificer or Blue House mage, gnomish tinkerer or Ruinborn scholar, Dawn Elven techno-thaumaturgist or Cennayakuta warlock—all waited with baited breath. A hundred thousand people waited in the port of Orenkoraim, the Gathyanavaida behind them. Warlords, warriors, and of course the lord or ruler, all waited. Great projections had been set up, so that they could watch the launch in real time. The monarch took a deep breath, glanced at those beside them, the cream of Taychendi society, then gave the signal to an aide.

The soil of the steppe shook—then a wave of heat, magic, smoke, and wind. Then, for the first time since the Ruin, since the days of Arethon and Ducaniel, Tacenie rose once more. Everyone who had ever even known about the project burst into cheers, shouts. "Glory!" They cried, "Tacenie!" "Glory!" "Tacenie!"

But the monarch did not cheer; instead, they stood still, thinking. The monarch had one last decision to make. Tacenie had risen—but it was not destined to rule the skies. It couldn't, after all—the world had the knowledge to raise it to the skies, but not to keep it there. No, Tacenie's destiny was to fall, and with its destruction signal the decisive end of the Cycle of Glory. All that was left was for the monarch to decide where exactly that would happen.
