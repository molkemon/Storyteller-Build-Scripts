Then did our lord meet a forester of cunning aspect. And hastily did the forester find her knee, and said, "Great is your host, O ruler, and more do I pity your prey for it. I would offer you lesser quarry, if you have time to give." And our lord bid her rise and speak, their curiosity awoken. "Near here lies an old grove, where my homestead grazed our sheep, and gathered wood and herbs for many good years. Yet spiders, vast as mine hounds, have made this place theirs, and made prey of my flock, beast and human both. Within our grove lies a barrow, and in this can be found old gold that belongs to the dead, and who I say would part with it gladly, that their living descendant may again be free to visit them."
