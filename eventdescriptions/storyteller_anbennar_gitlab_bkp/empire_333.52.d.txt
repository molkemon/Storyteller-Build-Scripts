There was a long silence after the question about what was in the sky was posed to the Thraxis. At first, it seemed like its normal pause after every question. But then it started to vibrate. The stars began to pulse red, and its entire form started to jitter. Stars warped away from the being and were sucked back in as a million tiny voices cried out, each out of tune with one another. This continued for the better part of a minute, getting louder and more alarming as it progressed, the stars shooting out over the crowd and being pulled back in. Some onlookers screamed and hid, while others just stared in awe, wondering if they had broken the Thraxis. You do not ask what is in the sky. We should not have asked. We should not—

It stopped. The pulsing, the screaming, the red flashes. The Thraxis was back to its normal position, hovering above the crowd, its millions of stars slowly rotating.

"We do not wish to speak of it."
