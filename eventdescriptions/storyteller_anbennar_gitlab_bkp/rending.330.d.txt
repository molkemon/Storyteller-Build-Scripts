Tradition dictates that the monarchs of the parched north undertake a sacred journey into the vast expanse of sand and dust, to seek their inner fortitude and validate their royal worthiness. The reason for this ritual remained enigmatic until the temple's influence began to wane. Then, with roaring winds akin to a lion's ferocious call, the desert unleashed its wrath, punishing the wicked and the despotic, wrenching them from their seats of power. A select few, brave enough to penetrate the storm's core, bear witness to its true essencea tempestuous cloud converging at a singular focus, its face fluctuating between the fierce countenance of a sovereign lion and the discerning eyes of an equally splendid ruler. The fate of a young prince venturing into the storm remains uncertain: either they emerge, crowned with a swirling diadem of sand and wind, or their bleached bones become one with the eternal desert sands.
