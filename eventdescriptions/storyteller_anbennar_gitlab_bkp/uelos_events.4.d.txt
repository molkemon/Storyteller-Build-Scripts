The woman sat in the council chamber, awaiting the arrival of the lord, to tell them that she and her fellows within his court had been meaning to ask for a more official stance on Uelos worship in the city. And while the others had offered their liege the usuals; power, wealth, glory; she wanted to offer the lord something more. A god who truly cared for them, unlike those others who refused to act. She wore her captain’s outfit, turquoise and cyan shades draped like she had taken the sea itself as a shroud. And she had a mask, befitting her seniority amongst her brethren; It shone a brilliant bronze, carved to look just like her face. She had been writing as of late, too; stories of Uelos’s briny court, of sailfish knights and krakenous heralds, and of the Deep One Himself. Perhaps the lord would just kill her and be done with it, never hearing what she had to say. But maybe, just maybe, she would be given the same chance she gave, those years ago. Nevertheless, she knew her brethren would take action were anything to happen to her.
