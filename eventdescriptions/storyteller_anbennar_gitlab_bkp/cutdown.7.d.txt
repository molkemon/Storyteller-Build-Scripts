The dispute between a neighbouring country and the Federation Leader is not constrained to the Assembly House or debates among the Federation’s more prominent representatives, but has spilled out to market squares and kitchen tables. The people are abuzz with arguments over war rights and the role of the Federation, and it seems every inn has become an Assembly House of its own. But within them, it seems disputes are not constrained merely to words – some of our citizens have taken up arms, seeing the righteousness of our cause and promising their support.
