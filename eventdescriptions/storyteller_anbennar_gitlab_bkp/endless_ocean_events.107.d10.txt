Where the Kiamoa fell, we were greeted with water. Boundless water.  Not for an era have the clouds parted long enough to allow Insyaans to venture into the far beyond. What to our ancestors must have been so familiar now holds unknown expanses, territory even the Dragon Turtles prove reluctant to describe, and who is to say what the millennia have done to the world in our absence? And what a blue world it is.  Every direction we venture, the long turquoise of the ocean deep heralds another possibility. Dolphins leap and birds fly to and from lands uncharted. The winds blow across the cerulean sky towards destinations unimagined. How narrow our understanding of the ocean had been, too content we had proven to stand on the threshold of our home instead of venturing forth into the world. The sea will never be known to those too timid to extend a greeting, and the dead will never be seen again if we do not set out to find them. Beyond those endless sapphire wavetips lie the certainty of an epoch of discovery, and so begins the era of Great Voyages.
