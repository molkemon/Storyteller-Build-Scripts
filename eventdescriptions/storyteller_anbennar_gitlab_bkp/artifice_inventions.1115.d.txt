The antiprojectile shields are mobile abjuration force fields designed to completely shield a person from arrows and other projectiles, and even slow down enemy blows in personal combat. Such shields are usually crafted to resemble a bracer or girdle and powered by small Dames tear batteries. Troops equipped with these shields can rush into enemy lines without having to fear being cut down by volleys of arrows or musket bullets. This of course is a significant advantage over adversaries equipped only with conventional weaponry, considering that projectile weaponry has gained an everincreasing role on the battlefield. Yet users of an antiprojectile shield should beware of overloading the shield or exhausting its energy source in the heat of battle, for doing so risks an explosive destabilisation of the shield matrix.
