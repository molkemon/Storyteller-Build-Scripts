The Zatsarya dwarfed every other building in Shalazar. Uwimbabazi stood in its shadow, craning her neck up to see it. The soft constellations etched into its black structure pulsed faintlyevidence of the spire’s inert magic.  "The history this city has," she said. "The capital of dozens of empires, and look at it now. A decaying, defiant piece of wreckage." A soldier came up behind her.  "My Ingo," she said. "The lizards resist us. They do not accept the Cycle, no matter how we try to convince the" Uwimbabazi shushed her with a glance.  "Of course they do not," she said. "Put yourself in their shoes. Their city is invaded, their world destroyed, and now they are asked to change their very way of life?"  Around her, the city was in disarray. Buildings lay ruinedthe regrettable result of the city’s siege. Lizardfolk picked through the rubble of their homes, scavenging what they could from the dust. A few of them shouted angrily at Uwimbabazi as they passed. Some could not even bring themselves to glare.  Unarmed Tanizu shamans walked amongst the survivors, bringing food and suppliesand with them, lessons. Tales of the Mongoose, the Leopard, and the Elephant. One shaman brought food to a lizardfolk family and was rewarded with a beating before soldiers pulled him away. Another held a lizardfolk child enraptured in his stories, before the child’s parents pulled her away, muttering insults under their breath.  Uwimbabazi looked up at the spire again, in silence. The soldier went to say something to her, and she raised a hand to stop her. A beat. Another. Then she shook her head.
