In vine-covered forests, old tongues still murmur. Among Naamjyut hillfolk, artisans, and spirit-guides, Kai words, songs, and storytelling are still used. Incense at festivals, totemic cloth, and burial rites all echo distant Kai traditions. Though long part of Yanshen culture, Kai-descended Naamjyut have not cut ties with the old realm beyond the mountains. Some clans keep kinship there; spirit-guides preserve ancient prayers; traders bring Kai incense and charm-scrolls through passes known to elders. As Kai's politics shift and Yanshen unifies, some fear such links may sway Naamjyut's governance; others see them as heritage. Recently, one tribe's close dealings with the Kai have drawn wide debate. Will you respond?
