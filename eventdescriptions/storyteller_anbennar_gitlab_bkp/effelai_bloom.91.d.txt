Years of deafening cacophony has taken its toll on our soldiers. Families across our country grieve the loss of their loved ones, angry that they were sent to fight a jungle. But finally it seems that the cost has exceeded the potential benefit in the eyes of rulers across the continent. As our troops have begun to pull out, the aggression of the jungle has subsided, and our smaller settlements on the perimeter have come under attack less frequently. Though the cycle of raid and repair may never truly end for our new outposts, the extreme tumultuousness of this war on the land has subsided, and folk have begun to speak once more of a possible future for themselves and their families.
