The grand rooms of the debate hall lie silent for once, as scholars mill about discussing their works with others, not even a raised tone could be heard. This place, used on dozens of occasions in attempts to settle the impossible between the three factions of the government and the Ravelians, has become united once more. But this time, not in desperation or fear. Today, three guild representatives of each faction and the Ravelian representative greet each other as individuals of a shared unified faith. The three representatives each bring a paper to our lord, unanimously agreeing that the name Giberd; "Gift Earth"; no longer represents the nation as it once did nearly a millennium ago. Giberd needs a new name to represent that this nation has fundamentally changed, in faith, in land and in identity; this Giberd and the old Giberd are leagues apart. Farmers draw bountiful harvests thanks to magically fortified crops; glassblowers haul their wares onto boats and sail assisted by powerful wind towers; fabric weavers hone their craft under the caring watch of the River Guard. As they do, our lord decides on the proposals in front of them.
