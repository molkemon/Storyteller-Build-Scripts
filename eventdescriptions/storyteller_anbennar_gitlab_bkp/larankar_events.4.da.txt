Erankar sent away the herald with a wave. He stared at the map of the realm with a grimace on his face. Apparently, the three feuding sons of the ruler of Nanru Nakar despised him more than each other. The king of Larankar could still remember the look on Pelozhil’s face as he broke through his bodyguards, a storm of determined rage. Erankar’s companions had to drag him away from the battlefield. He had refused to believe the battle was lost. Nothing was ever easy for him—nothing ever fell into place. That never mattered, though. Erankar added up the numbers in his head. An ally’s aid, Kriadvai’s scavengers, Kilaiek’s slavers… not unbeatable. Difficult, but not unbeatable.

If he won, he’d take it all. If he died, so be it. And if he lost, he’d come back and fight. As many times as he needed to. He’d already killed one Mudaliar king.
