As the hearts of cities were gutted in a frenzy of architectural ambition; each stone laid in tribute to the ideals of the Renaissance and the rekindled admiration for the ancient Damerian Republic; one city, more than any other, emerged as the true seat of Luna's administration. A prominent trading hub, found its centre dominated by a grandiose town hall, a monument to governance in all its bureaucratic splendour. Within its walls, a towering courtroom, chambers for the diplomatic class, and rows upon rows of desks for weary scribes promised order; or at least the illusion of it. Most illustrious of all was the socalled River Chamber, an expansive room offering a breathtaking view of the Luna, its waters gleaming under the midday sun. It was a sight to stir the soul, to inspire reflection on the great tides of history. But their ruler, stepping through its doors, saw none of it. Nor did they admire the elegant marble engraving on the wall; perhaps because it was barely visible, drowned beneath an avalanche of parchment, each bundle bound in an unmistakable lilac ribbon. Hundreds? No, thousands of cases, all neatly filed, all bearing the same damning mark: disputes between the cities, each one demanding the judgment of the fledgling Confederacy's authorities. "Ah. Splendid timing." came a voice, chipper and unreasonably optimistic. From behind the stacks peeked a young gnome, eyes alight with the peculiar joy of a woman who had long since surrendered to the madness of numbers. "Based on my latest projections; factoring in case flow, adjudication rates, and the diminishing will to live; If we begin this very moment, we should have the backlog cleared in. oh, about ten years. Give or take a minor revolt. Shall we?"
