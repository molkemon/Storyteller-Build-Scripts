Tea is more than a commodity—it is a part of life in our realm. As tea-producing provinces in both Naamjyut and Beikling continue to flourish, public debate grows over what constitutes the proper Way of Tea. In Beikling, emphasis is placed on water temperature, fire timing, and seasonal rhythms of the mountains—favoring restraint and discipline. In Naamjyut, by contrast, aroma and presentation reign supreme, blending forest aesthetics with artistic display. Every few years, merchants and scholars from both regions hold informal tea competitions and exhibitions, sometimes even attracting the attention of high officials. Among the people, this has come to be known as the Northern-Southern Tea Duel. This year, such an exchange unfolds under your rule. Local officials and trade guilds now request official recognition and guidance to represent their preferred regional styles. How will you respond?
