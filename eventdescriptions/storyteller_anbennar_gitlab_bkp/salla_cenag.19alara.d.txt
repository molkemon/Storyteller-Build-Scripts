I find myself once again in front of my fellow ceanns and ceannas. Standing at full height and dressed in my finest armor, I do everything I can to keep a straight face. I draw strength where I can from Narawen’s furtive smile as I push down my nervousness.  This moment was her doing. I pause and correct myself. It was our doing.  With the clans restored and the woods at peace, the question of the future had hung over everyone. It was clear to everyone, even me, that we couldn’t go back to the old ways of each clan on its own, bickering when things came to head. That had nearly brought us ruin.  Years of fighting together under the Verdant Pact had brought us together too. We weren’t just clans now, we were a people. A unified force just like our forebears were. All we needed was a commander, a ceannasai.  And that led us here. Most of the clan heads had a desire for the position. My eyes glanced over to Oberon Fealgarn who had settled on what I could only describe as a resting scowl. Some more than others, I thought. Yet, it wasn’t enough to want it. The other clans had to agree to it.  I don’t know what Narawen said. And, in so many ways, I didn’t think I was the elf for it. But, after all these years, I had learned to think some but not too much.  I pressed my fist against my chest, swearing to serve as Ceannasai. To honor all of the clans, not just my own. To lead with courage and convection. And to defend our home against all that threatened it.  It was a short speech. I knew I couldn’t speak fancy, but as I spied Lanhara behind the clan heads, I also knew that was alright. I just had to be me.  And as I reached the end of my speech, I pounded my chest twice.  "Yfel Cyranvar."  In unison clan heads replied the same.
