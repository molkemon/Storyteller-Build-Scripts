Naramus's chains jingle softly as he dips his stylus in ink. They've given him a copper collar now; less clunky than the old steel, a concession for clarity of thought. He's proud; our lord says he's earned it. Across the table, their lord plants both their hands on the map. "There's too many," they growls. "They're pouring from the north like cattle. There's more every day, and we don't have the chains to control them." Thinking, they taps the map where the veins run thickest. "We give the forges new instructions. Tools can wait, shackles can't. We'll melt down speartips and strip the dead, and we stop wasting chains on feathers." our lord steeples his fingers. "The conquest won't stop for a lack of picks and links, their lord. Let the birds burn if it pleases the Devourer, but if we choke the mines, we'll have little left to offer him." their lord scoffs. "We offer him obedience. Everything else is garnish." Chuckling, our lord gestures lazily in Naramus's direction. "And if the garnish is sweeter than the meat? Let the scholars breathe a little. Let the mages roam the archives again. I know what my people can do when given space to think." A silence settles, thick with soot, and sweat, and the stink of scorched feathers from somewhere far above them. their lord leans closer. "And when one of yours thinks a little too freely?" our lord shrugs. "Then by all means, shackle them with my blessing. But, until then." they trails off. The collar is warm around Naramus's throat. Outside, the wind shifts, and ash falls past the highset window, fine and grey. He dips the stylus again, and doesn't look up.
