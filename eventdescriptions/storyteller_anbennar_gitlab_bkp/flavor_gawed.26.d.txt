It had been exactly one year since the Emperor called the Imperial Parliament and a month since our spies learned of their invasion plans for today. The Gawedi armies commanded by their lord stood outside Vertesk, watching the quiet horizon in the whistling wind like hawks. Their glinting armor dimmed as the sun set, all the while awaiting the Empire's response. The city itself had been evacuated, half the population leaving in anticipation of a clash between the Northern and Dove Throne’s armies. Few had hoped war would be avoided, their leader arriving to oversee the outbreak of conflict. But as the sun dimmed, the hopes and morale of the Gawedi forces rose. Not a foe stood in sight of the assembled forces as the shadow of the Eagle loomed over the Dames crown and Alenic Frontier. As it became evident that no foe would appear, the troops jeered their challenge to the southern cowards, the same challenge the Kings of Gawed had issued for decades. But no foe arrived to dance with the proud eagle.
