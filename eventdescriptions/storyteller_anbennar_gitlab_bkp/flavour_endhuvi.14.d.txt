The greyfurred harimari stood alongside a newly dug channel, one that his workers had been toiling at for the better part of three weeks. How he ended up here, in charge of the project, was still being replayed in his mind. 'Fahes,' he told himself, 'you're being silly; there is no way anyone will go along with it.' Yet somehow, when he had spoken up amongst all the advisors and mages at the latest grand debate on how best to resolve the stagnation of the Kharunyana, they'd agreed. The mages had merely shrugged - the High Temples weren't their domain anyway - and while the outcry from the Darsanasara delegation had shaken the hall, even they were silenced by the arguments Fahes had presented. If the spirits and the temples were connected, then why not connect them literally, dig channels and flood the temples? Nothing else had worked thus far, no amount of engineering or magic could encourage the river to flow again. The ruler had looked at him, deep in thought and nodded their assent to the idea, adding these words of wisdom, "If you rule out the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the answer. We will do this." So here he was, overseeing the dig towards the nearest suitable High Temple. A channel for the water was carved through the jungles, hundred of laborers toiled with axes, picks, and shovels, while a separate team worked to build a floodwall around the perimeter of the site. They were days away from opening the floodgates and inundating the temple. Fahes scratched his chin, no one knew what to expect, least of all him.
