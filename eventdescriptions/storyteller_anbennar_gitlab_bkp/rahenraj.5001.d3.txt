The Dhenbasana is littered with palaces, funded by embezzled taxes and tariffs as well as withheld tributes, bribes to look past such crimes, or (in rare cases) genuine success and wise rule. No two are identical, but almost all share one feature: a wellconcealed escape route. Their lord is reminded of this fact as they hear today's report from a messenger dressed in a strange red and white hat. Their enemy's enemy managed to siege down the fortress where the Raja had holed their in, but they escaped like smoke on the wind. "Their lord," he concludes, "the Raja has found refuge in another palace." Good. Haless could not outrun Harimar, the stream cannot outrace the sea. Run as they likes, we will soon capture the Raja, and with them, the Lotus Throne.
