The prisoner seems almost cheerful as he is led into the stone chamber; he is short and has floppy hair, which is somewhat incongruous with his name. "Cheni," reads the Vile One, as she seats herself opposite him. Her attendants unshackle him—she notices that he does not initially stretch his arms, as one might expect, but places them in his lap, one hand over another. "Indeed," he says, his eyes crinkling. "You felt my lattice."

The Vile One nods. "You intended me to."

Cheni's face splits into a wide, easy smile that fills his face. He nods vigorously. "The most beautiful thing in the world. Magic itself, of course, the feeling of it. But I mean magic with other people. The surge of coordination, the combined ritual... a lattice of souls. I kept hearing yours, you see, thought I'd say hello. Big-big rituals, lots of people, like a coven or a tower..."

"Yes," the Vile One isn't sure why she interrupts, or indeed why her voice is suddenly thick.

"But here's the thing," Cheni says, and his smile softens. "You're doing it all wrong. Or—well, competently. But—"

"But what?" says the Vile One.

"You've got to make it feel good for people. So they come back. And I don’t know why you break the lattice when you’re done with a single ritual. So wasteful." He kisses his teeth. "You could make it permanent, see? And then it'd be like everything you were casting was of ritual scale..."
