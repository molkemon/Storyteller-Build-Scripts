Gaudy tapestries flowed with the desert breeze; the sunlight danced across innumerable gilded artifacts and sparkling gemstones scattered haphazardly across the floor, throwing up coloured light, lasting evidence of a hundred plundered cities and towns. ‘As if a frenzied magpie horde had been given free reign to decorate the room in accordance with their proclivities… a sign of gluttonous greed? For sure. But maybe, just maybe, a sign of ambition, as well… a hunger for more than what we were, a hunger that can be channeled and shaped, which can build an empire.’ Such were the thoughts of Grizaka, sitting on her throne, lost in one of her contemplative moods. Barely noticed beneath her introspective gaze, her assembled court was busy with their own dealings. Avaricious Krah traders were tallying the profits of the last slave auctions, the more ambitious amongst their ranks attempting to persuade the tribal warlords and ladies of Grizaka’s war council of the merit of a particular conquest target, rich in potential merchandise. Zealous Xhazobkult shamans quarrelled with courtiers, now accustomed to a pampered existence surrounded by servants; they passionately disputed the proper number of slaves required to be earmarked as sacrifices. All in all, a rather sordid state of affairs, yet glorious empires had been raised from far more humble origins. All that was needed was an iron will to create a lasting legacy, a firm hand to rein in the baser instincts and an ambitious vision to lead the pack into a bright future. And Grizaka? She possessed all of that, and more to spare. Soon, the world would learn to tremble at the mention of her name.
