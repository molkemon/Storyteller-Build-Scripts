A letter arrives on the desk of our ruler, it bears the seal of Parusad Bhola, a grand palace, surrounded by ruby flowers; it is a reply, a response to an overture a century overdue. Our ruler breaks the seal with a flick of their claw and begins to read, the response describes the surprise with which Endhuvi's proposal was met in the court of Parusad Bhola. Such skepticism was only overcome, so their ruler writes, by the timely arrival of a supporting statement from the Raja. They will agree to meet, though it must be on the grounds of their own gardens. Their ruler states that our ruler has taken a first step. None of their predecessors have done this, and our ruler has taken steps to show their good intentions. They are granting us this one chance to rebuild the bridges between the two nations. It will not be an easy road, but our ruler is determined to see it through if possible. The Raja, too, has an interest in seeing this dispute settled. After all, infighting only serves to keep the Raj weak and its enemies are many.
