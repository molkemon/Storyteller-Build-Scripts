The heated arguments of the war council fall silent as someone bursts into my tent, heads snapping from the maps to the intruder. I rise from my rug, ready to demand an explanation, but pause, recognizing the kneeling centaur as one of the infiltrators I sent into the ogres' heartlands. Curiously, he clutches a bundle of ancient parchment. "Ten thousand apologies for disturbing you, my lord," he begins, lowering his gaze. "But this discovery could not wait. We were told to watch for any magical knowledge, and in a vault I was... exploring, I found a set of notes penned by an ogre necromancer known as the Glutton King."

With a snap of my fingers, the parchments are brought to me. I unfold them carefully, scanning the cramped script and fragmented diagrams. The pages speak of a single obsession: the conquest of Death itself, achieved by binding one's soul to an object.

A wave of the hand dismisses the council. Alone, I study the fragments in silence. They are incomplete, yet ingenious. The author—Almokh, as he names himself—was close to unlocking true immortality. The idea stirs something within me. For all my power, I will never have an heir to carry my name. But perhaps I do not need one. Why should I pass my legacy to another, when I could endure forever?

I reach for fresh parchment and begin the painstaking task of transcribing Almokh's work away from the decaying leaflets.
