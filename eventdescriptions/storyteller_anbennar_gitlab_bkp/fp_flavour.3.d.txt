Hulking crustaceans with earthen shells, rock crabs live along waterways and seashores, gliding along topsoil to feast upon minerals and worms. Happy to remain docile until bothered, rock crabs can knock down an unprepared adult with a ramming charge or severely wound unarmored aggressors with its mighty, bouldershaped claws. For one reason or another, the rock crab population in the province has been booming, causing turmoil for the local citizens. To counter these crabby critters, we can scrounge up some soldiers to dispatch the rock crabs (simultaneously tempering our army with experience), but the rock crab's constant soil tilling has been known to unearth valuable minerals. How should we proceed?
