Reports come from a rural village in the roaming spirits province, these villages were impressively pious and had built up stable relationships with many spirits, leaving out libations and offerings to those that travel through and hosting festivals to honour those spirits they met. When they saw a group of tiger-headed warriors draped in silk and golden jewellery, they prostrated themselves before the strange spirit men, having never seen the Harimari before. While many Harimari hurried through, two of the young Harimari men enjoyed the hospitality of the faithful, drinking and eating through the village stores. When a travelling merchant explained the Harimari’s true nature, a local shaman grew furious and has since taken the Harimari prisoner. Our priests and our community leaders are up in arms over this. What should we do?
