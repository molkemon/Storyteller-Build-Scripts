Ask the Seaborn Bands of Insyaa what the difference exists between their parties and the parties of other adventurers, and they will readily tell you: commitment. They do not consider it enough to merely desire to enact good deeds; one must live the lifestyle even during the harshest trials and lowest hours. Adventuring isn’t nonstop glory and riches; it is hard, backbreaking labor. To effectively tackle monsters and quests, aspiring adventurers must first master quartermaster inventories, haggling in the information trade, job networking, equipment maintenance, camp construction, first aid and mechanics, and orienteering. There’s no point in showing up to tackle a job no one wanted done, unprepared and ignorant. A good hero spends hours practicing, learning, and drilling. One must offer oneself freely and relentlessly to the unglamorous realities of heroism to even have a hope of improving the world, lest one’s goal become a fairweathered commitment. To adventure– to be a Hero worthy of praise– is to love that which you protect, and sincerely wish to see it safe. Such true love transcends such shallow concerns as instant gratification: the greatest heroes of Insyaa all withstood periods of trial and temptation along their paths to legendary status.
