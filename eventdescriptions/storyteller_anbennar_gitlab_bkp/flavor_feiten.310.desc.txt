As it turns out, the collapse of the whaling industry has had more serious consequences than we could have imagined. The vital fuel on which our jellyfish blimps depend—whale oil—is now in a state of severe scarcity, directly affecting every aspect of Feiten's economy. If we can't find a substitute for whale oil as quickly as possible, the consequences are clear: the fall of Feiten will be even more rapid than a burnt-out balloon.

To this end, several emergency government meetings have been held, and the finest artificers and artisans throughout the realm have been called in to study alternative fuels. Straw, wood, and even damestear batteries were intensively tested, but they always failed in the end, either due to a low calorific value or yield issues. That was when a blacksmith from Luogang spoke up during a meeting that was quickly heading to a dead end:

"There is this black ore in our home," he said, "that produces a powerful fire when burned—we generally use it to make steel, but perhaps we could use it for this?"

Two weeks later, the artificers and mages sent in their reports at the same time. One report suggested that the black ore had a calorific value far higher than whale oil, enough to support the flight of even our largest airships. The other report pointed out that there was a large amount of black ore contained underneath Luogang—enough that, with the size of Feiten's fleet today, we wouldn't have to worry about fuel for at least a hundred years.
