The centaurs cannot easily fit into many of our buildings, but have set up a few tents outside of our outpost, where the governor and their attendants meet with the ordsikar today. The ordsikar apologizes for the low-quality tent and assures the governor that the ordsikar will be bringing his personal, more extravagant abode to their next meeting. He also bluntly tells us that we need to get to work establishing a clearing big enough to house it.

It has quickly become apparent that these particular clans of centaurs have no ill-will towards our people, and the purpose of this visit is to establish a mutually beneficial relationship with us going forward. For them, a place to barter and trade. For us, protection from other, less-friendly centaurs, whom they assure us will likely come for us at some point.

The ordsikar makes one thing particularly clear: if we want their continued cooperation, this outpost is to remain an outpost—a Gozabhar. A place to gather tribute from the centaur clans of the area, trade, rest, and converse—but nothing else. We will farm no more than is necessary to feed the population of the outpost. If we seek only to acquire tribute from them, we are welcome. If, however, we attempt to push them out by settling their lands with our own peoples or sending our warriors riding out against them, they will push back hard.
