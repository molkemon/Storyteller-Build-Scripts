The lord stood in front of a pile; various goods were gathered there, seized from shops and nearby houses. The lord took a step closer, and extended their hand over the pile: three rings, ruby, silver and golden, decorated their fingers, and they let them slide off into the pile. A guard recited a speech praising the ruler for their sacrifice, then talked about duty. Duty and sacrifice, two terms that were sorrowfully familiar to the citizens. More guards marched out, moving building by building to collect coins, food, medicinal herbs and anything deemed of value. Most people complied, meeting the soldiers at the front doors with baskets full of tribute, for great was the power that compelled the people in their loyalty to the Empire. But even that power had been stretched thin, squeezing its subject dry, and should it be used to take even more, the consequences for the land and the dynasty might be unthinkable.
