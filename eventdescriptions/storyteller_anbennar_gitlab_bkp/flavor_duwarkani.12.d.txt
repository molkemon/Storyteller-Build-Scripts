The Annual March of a nation's Legions had been held every year in a city, although its importance over the years had waned. It celebrated the liberation of a province by a ruler after its subjugation by creatures of the first horde. This year, however, the festival had gained significantly more attention than usual. It was seen as a festival celebrating antiquated and horrible ideas about "monstrous" races. At the centre of the controversy was the legacy of a ruler, or as they were more commonly known, Beastbane. This ruler first codified the difference between civilized and "monstrous" races during the nascent days of a nation. They led humanity on marches across several lands to wipe out monsters in what was called the Second Great Cleansing. At the march in a city, people dress up as a nation's legions, wearing furs to symbolise the creatures slaughtered by their legions, along with feathers in their helmets for the many slain in a land. What started as a localised debate over remembering history soon turned into a nationwide conflict. Many creatures and people were shocked to discover just how many still saw Beastbane as a hero to humanity, where in their eyes they were a genocidal maniac who caused untold destruction. Families were splitting over the debates. Creatures leaving human-dominated settlements to return to the mountains. Merchants were refusing to sell to humans. Our nation was being torn apart from the inside.
