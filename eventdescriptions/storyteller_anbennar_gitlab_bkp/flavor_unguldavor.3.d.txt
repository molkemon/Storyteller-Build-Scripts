Diplomacy is an art. One syllable could mean the difference between war and peace. Traditionally, orkish diplomacy was straightforward. If a clan disliked another or wanted what the other had, they declared war. If several clans saw a mutual threat, they'd ally together and take it down. In cases where it was suspected someone was lying, an Ozarm'chadash would be held to determine who was telling the truth. However, humans were different. They would cover their intentions with flowery words to hide them and backstab their allies if it was advantageous. Of course, honorable orks such as ourselves would never do such a thing, but it was so. Their ruler was sitting on their throne, watching as two emissaries argue over which new approach to diplomacy should be taken. One of them, Golm, looked at the other and said, "Ahe, you must realize that the point of diplomacy is to convince everyone you are on their side when, in truth, your nation is only in it for itself." Ahe, the other, crossed her arms and replied, "Golm, what is more important than reassuring words and lies is getting the point across. If we were to declare war on Marrhold and claim it was to keep the peace, no one would really believe it. However, if we justify our gains during the war, we would be seen more positively than if we told a complete lie." Golm looks at their ruler and asks, "My lord, what do you think?"
