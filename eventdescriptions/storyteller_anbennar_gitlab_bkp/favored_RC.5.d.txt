It is a bright night in our capital, the seeming contradiction the result of the Dame's moon glowing brightly overhead, illuminating the city's streets and houses, shining down on the few people still awake in this small hour of the night. One such person is our ruler, who sits in the city's tallest tower. They had been reading one of the latest tomes out of Konwell, and thought to stop when the sun went down, but instead continued in the moonlight's glow. Now, though, they simply watches the city below them. What does the Dame illuminate? On this night, for our ruler, the answers are manifold. A tree, its leaves gilded in silver, rustling in the wind. A merchant, trundling onwards on their next journey. A city, resting, yet thriving. But there is more, too. A confidence that there is a system, a logic, underneath all these phenomena. There is a reason why the tree grows the way it does, an economic explanation to the path that has brought the merchant here and the one they shall follow in the days and years to come, forces which generate the stillblowing wind. And even, perhaps, a system of truths and reasons that underpin the thriving of our realm itself, that (it seems tonight) could perhaps even be teased apart, understood, under the guidance and light of the Dame. And then there is the final truth, that appears to our ruler even as they finally proceeds to bed: knowledge should be pursued for its own sake, for the true beauty of the world lies in understanding it. But this is more than just a truth: it is a path forward in emulation of the Dame, a principle to organize a life and a state around, and that is what our ruler resolves to do.
