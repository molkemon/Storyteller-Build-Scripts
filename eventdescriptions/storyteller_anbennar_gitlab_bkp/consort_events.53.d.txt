The people of our great kingdom are well aware of our dispute with a foreign nation. Considering that this is also the homeland of our consort, it is no wonder that they have been met with increasing animosity. For all they know, they could be harboring loyalties towards the old country, or worse; they could be a spy. And is it not a funny coincidence, the nobles argue, that our lord has become so dependent on the advice of their spouse? Our military advisor has gone so far as to threaten to leave court if our lord does not distance themselves from the consort and their untrustworthy whispers.
