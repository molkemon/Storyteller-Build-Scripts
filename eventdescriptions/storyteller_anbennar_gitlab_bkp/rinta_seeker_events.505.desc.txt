For better or for worse, we have managed to quell the majority of the infighting around the fundamental aspects of the observatory’s construction. However, when it comes to the Mwalim and Darwiiz, when one debate ends another will invariably arise. The next topic regards not the main telescope, but the additional ones that will populate the Godswatch. The unfortunate reality of the situation is that we simply cannot afford to make every telescope as big and powerful as the observatory’s centerpiece. With the remaining funding that can reasonably be allocated, a decision must be made: we could build a large number of minor, less powerful telescopes, or a small number of periphery telescopes with significantly more functionality. 

The Mwalim favor the latter option; even if they cannot match the central telescope, these moderately powerful lenses will allow the detailed long-term tracking of multiple important celestial objects simultaneously. The Darwiiz, however, have a significantly different view: they believe the purpose of the periphery telescopes should be to give the common people a chance to look upon the stars for themselves. To that end, the construction of as many telescopes as possible is a necessity, regardless of their quality. We could also attempt to include a moderate number of decently-powerful telescopes in order to bridge the gap between the two proposals, though neither side would be fully content.
