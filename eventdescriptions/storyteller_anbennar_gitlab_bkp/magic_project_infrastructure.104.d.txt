Magecraft is becoming more intertwined with governance as the aristocracy increasingly shows magical aptitude. With the rise of magical administrators such as our administrative advisor, nonmage government officials have gathered together and begun rallying against mages who serve a role in the administration of our kingdom. Taking advantage of the recent reforms around magical practice in the country, they argue that one of these reforms should be the cessation of magical power from the factors which qualify a candidate for title, lest mages establish an eternal magical dynasty. Tensions have risen as our administrative advisor and the nonmages continue to argue, and this debate—ultimately pointless bickering for political power—now threatens to destabilize the whole project of magical reform. It is clear that the issue is now too large for our monarch to ignore. In order to avoid a civil catastrophe, a side must be chosen.
