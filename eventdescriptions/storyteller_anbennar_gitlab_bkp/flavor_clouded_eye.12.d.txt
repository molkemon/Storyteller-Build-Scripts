There is a common saying that goes 'as likely as humans and orks living together.' Ever since the Greentide, most humans have seen orks as nothing more than monsters that must be exterminated and driven out to protect civilization, or in the case of the Order of A shen Rose and Order of the Thorn, kept as slaves. There are some exceptions. The Sons of Dameria and Order of Corintar seem to be more accepting of orks, even if the former is more about acceptance of halfhumans. In a province, however, humans and orks live in peace. Though it started off tense, slowly humans and orks began to mingle. As the first generation of halforks were born, halforks across Escann moved in. The result is perhaps the first villages in Escann where halforks are the majority. Much like a region, these halforks carry the legacy of two worlds. From the orks, they got their strength and green skin. From the humans, they got their grace and determination. These halforks worship Corin as their goddess, doubtlessly adopted from their human parents and how Corin believed the orks and halforks are as worthy as humans. Another curious property of these halforks is a surprising aptitude for bureaucracy and commerce. halforks make up a disproportionate percent of the bureaucrat class and merchant class, partly due to orkish culture looking down at those two professions and partly due to humans not currently being trusted with that level of power yet. If we are to become a proper nation, the halforks will be the glue that holds us together. As I rode home from a province, I pondered about the Greentide and its legacy. For all the destruction it brought, it also brought a new beginning.
