Bulwar always has been a crossroad of cultures, civilizations, and ideas, and that is reflected in the different schools of magic that can be found within our lands. Bulwari magic recognizes four greater schools or knowings; the Eresdirkesir, the art of binding other beings to the will of the magi, the Eresmalthar, the art of inscription to create potent magical artifacts, the Eressar, the art of wardings and divination, and the Eressasur, the art of forcing order onto nature. Within these greater knowings, there are many smaller knowings, more specialised traditions that focus on particular aspects of magic and tend to be practised only by a handful or even just a single magi clan, the secrets of their inherited knowing jealously guarded from rival clans.  Among them is the Eres of Asiptu or exorcism, used to purify both body and mind and traditionally practised by Kasipti exorcistpriests, but also studied by magi called the Asipsin. Another minor Eres is the art of Kirimahsikna or Shaping the Garden, utilising magic to shape the land itself and order nature. The Eres of Gabarubir, primarily practised by the order of the Gabarisin, uses prophetic dreams for the purpose of divination. A more controversial Eres is sibirinu, the Lapis Eye, which allows a Kamudasin magi to bind the will of an entire army to their own, enhancing the battle prowess and coordination of all soldiers at the cost of their free will. Another Eres with decidedly martial applications is Belostaro, born from the Sun Elven magical traditions brought with the Remnant Fleet, that allows for its practitioner to turn into a near invincible warriormage able to singlehandedly defeat scores of enemies.  As we have decided to take an active role in the preservation and further development of Bulwari magic, we could favor one of the magi clans in return for privileged access to their unique magical secrets.
