The cavern echoed with the rhythmic clang of hammers and chisels, dwarven engineers hard at work installing a new support pillar in the heart of the lower levels. A marvel of craftsmanship, the pillar was a feat of engineeringa solid granite monolith adorned with runes of stability and strength, meant to anchor the expanding hold.  When it was finally raised into position, the workers cheered, but the triumph was shortlived. Hours later, the pillar began to groan. The sound wasn't the usual settling of stone but a low, unsettling creak that seemed to pulse, irregular and wrong.  By the next day, cracks webbed the cavern floor. Engineers scrambled to diagnose the issue, their calculations showing no flaw in design. But the pillar itself began to shiftwarping as though under immense unseen pressure.  'It's cursed,' murmured a miner, his voice low but carrying. Others nodded, exchanging wary glances. Some whispered about ancient grudges buried deep in the rock, forces that resented the intrusion.  The overseer dismissed the rumours as nonsense, yet the pillar twisted further, the runes upon it fading as though drained. Work ceased, and the cavern was evacuated.  Weeks later, the site remained sealed, the shifting pillar left untouched, like a somber warning.
