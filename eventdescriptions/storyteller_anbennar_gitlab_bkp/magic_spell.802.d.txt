Twins. The very word brings fear to the mind of our lord. Twins represent a much harder birth, a contested succession, and in some cultures, an ill omen. Twin magesthe air crackles at the thought. Would their sibling squabbles mean the end of the palace grounds, owing to some stray fireball or earthquake?  As their two heads crest, the tension reaches a fever pitch. The Rite of Conception, intended to bring forth only one child, grows unstable. The energies of two children clash, savage and uncontrolled, flowing in and out of each other in violent streaks of purple. One child is scarred by the conflict, their skin lightly smoldering as arcs of lightning burn blooming patterns into their flesh. The other remains beautiful, serene through the conflictyet if nothing is done, they shall soon both be dead.  The wildeyed midwife turns to our lord, gripping her knife tight. One must be sacrificed, she screams. The ritual was not made for two. She is kindshe does not ask you for your hands, only your word. One phrase, and she will do what a parent cannot.
