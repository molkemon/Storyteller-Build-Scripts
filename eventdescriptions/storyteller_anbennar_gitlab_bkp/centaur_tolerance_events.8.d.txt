Grumbling has reached our ears about our lord's burgeoning urban plight. As centaurs moved into denser and denser neighborhoods, they complained about laughably compact communal spaces and a lack of centaurfriendly access paths. Though small space in the big city has always been a concern for urbanites, we've never been put to the fire over how we've constructed the metropolis. Several prominent and disaffected centaurs crafted a coalition with other disgruntled city groups, requesting civil investment into public parks, access ramps for buildings, widening streets, and even standardizing room sizes. The local officials and other budgetary officials balk at the cost, but this might be just the opportunity we need to bring our lord into the modern day. How should we proceed?
