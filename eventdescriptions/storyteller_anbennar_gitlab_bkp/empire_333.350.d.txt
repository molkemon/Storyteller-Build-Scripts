A full battalion of our finest soldiers had gathered in the Zatsarya in the Shadow Swamp to prepare for a return visit. But the crank wouldn't work. Multiple engineers, scientists, and experts all tried to figure out what was going on. Perhaps something had gone wrong? Perhaps one of the many strange gears and magical devices that inhabit this incredible spire had broken? In truth, although we had activated several of these spires now, we didn't really understand the machinery behind them. There were books on what happened if you moved certain gears to certain places, but it followed a logic nobody truly understood, and operated under magical laws that nobody had cracked.

So the soldiers were forced to leave while the engineers tried to trial and error their way back to operation. One gear moved here, but to no effect. Another one turned a mysterious crank that no previous spire engineer had dared touch in other spires, but that too accomplished nothing. Another junior engineer grabbed a lever and flipped it upwards. Clicking. Sparks. Voices. Thunks of flesh on stone. More clicking. The spire had not activated. There were no sparkling lights of the thousands of stars on its face. But something had gone very wrong. Nothing was leaving the spire. Nothing, that is, but the ceaseless sounds of clicking.
