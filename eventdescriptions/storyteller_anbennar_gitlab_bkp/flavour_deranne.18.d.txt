"To my sorrow, my friends, that last tale was the last I had for you." announced the Bard as the final chords echoed, before stilling both sound and strings through a gesture of magic. He lifted his gaze, "And so, I must finally take my leave."  When the Bard had told his first tale at the Swaying Rose, only a handful of patrons had been there to listen. Yet as word spread of the stranger with unlimited stories to tell, the tavern had quickly filled up with ears eager to hear the Bard’s tunes. Now, with every bench taken, the room carrying a strong scent of pipe and ale, the Bard’s announcement filled it with a collective sigh.  Finally Varil, a dockworker at one of the front tables, slammed his burly arms on the wood. "You’re leaving already? Surely you’ve another tale in you, yet." Hums of agreement rippled through the tavern.  Strapping his lute across his back, the Bard winced. "I have told you of the tales of the Seaflower, of its struggles and triumphs, of its kings to its lowliest farmer. So, while there are always more tales to tell, I have errands I must attend to. Surely you all do as well, and you must tire of my voice."  From the back came the loud grunt of a dwarf. "Best part of my day, this." And with that, the tavern erupted in a roar of assent, the thudding of tankards and chants for yet another tale drowning out the Bard’s wilting protests.  "By Derhilde’s sodden boots." exclaimed the Bard, sliding the lute down his shoulder and settling back in his chair; and yet a tiny smile tugged at his lips. "Alright, alright. I suppose I have one more tale to spin for you. But that is the last one, you hear me?"  And so, shaking his head, with the deafening cheers of the crowd heralding a new beginning, the Bard began to play his strings once more.
