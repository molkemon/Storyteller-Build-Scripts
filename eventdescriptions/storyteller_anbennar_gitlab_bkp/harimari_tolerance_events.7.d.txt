There are few traditions as important to harimari as hunting. Whether with spear, bow, or the bare hand, the pursuit of prey and skillful takedown of a wild beast is a long and proud tradition of the tigerfolk. A group of enthusiastic harimari hunters has approached the crown with a request for expanded hunting rights throughout the woods of the province. Many of these woods are owned by the state, and those hunters that already have the right to use them complain that the new hunters will overcrowd the woods and deplete the land with their overzealous predation. What do we do?
