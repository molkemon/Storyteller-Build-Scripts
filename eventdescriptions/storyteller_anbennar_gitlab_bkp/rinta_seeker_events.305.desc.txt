The Adeen, the state-owned slave soldiers of the Baashidi who serve as the artillerists and marines of their armies, have grown in influence as the militaries of the Baashidi states have begun to modernize. They have reached a level of influence where they are reflecting on their place as a significant group within Baashidi society, and it falls upon the state to mediate this transition. A tumultuous period is in store for the Baashidi, as for a faction as militarized as the Adeen, such a negotiation is sure to be violent.
