"You are like me in some ways," Komina says, as if just realizing it. They sit on the upper terrace of a barrack house, watching as fog-touched Umorozi commands an elite guard in their drills. Komina is in her element at times like this; the sun gleams off the jewelry at her throat, Fahvanosy in makeup, and she is perhaps the most ravishing halfling the Vile One has ever seen. "I am?" the Vile One asks.

"I enjoy their company. Mortals, lesser things."  
"I came from them."

Komina scoffs. "Briefly, perhaps. And not since your fog."

The Vile One tries again. "They are useful."

"But that isn't why," Komina says, smiling. "No, you're like me. You get something from mortals. Your Granny and your Mama, for all they dote on their favorites, could go into seclusion tomorrow without a moment's thought."

Were the Vile One a different woman, she might wince at that. She remembers a little girl whose every waking moment was consumed with Granny's approval. The thought that Granny could have left her any moment is troubling. But Komina is right, of course.

"What is it, for you?" Komina is gazing intensely at the Vile One; has She ever before felt the full brunt of Komina's attention? It is no wonder that people overturn their lives for Her Auntie; that after a day they will kill their families for her, run away with her. That moment of freedom, of deciding upon a new life, is what Komina truly feeds upon.

"What do you mean, Auntie?"

Komina grins. "What do the mortals give you?"
