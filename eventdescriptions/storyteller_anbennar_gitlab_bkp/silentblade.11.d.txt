"I do not know my name," it thought. That was a problem, it knew instinctively. Something had to be done about that. Its limbs felt stiff, as if they remembered having been lifeless, inflexible wood until a couple of seconds ago. It stretched its limbs and clambered upright, and opened its eyes.  It was standing in a forest, surrounded by the trees from which its body was created. There were several figures standing around it, strong creatures, their skin green, their tusks long, and their eyes glazed over, betraying a deep charm. One figure was different: a graceful creature of flesh and wood, his beauty unaffected by the burn marks all over his body. This was its creator, it knew.  The creator reached out his hand, and caressed its face. "Welcome to the world, spriggan. You are my finest work yet. "He took a step back, and inspected his work with satisfaction. "You will address me as Prince. Fulfill the tasks I set for you, and I shall grant you your name." The Prince gestured to the tusked figures around them. "These are orks. They are to be the instrument of my vengeance. They are strong, fearless, and merciless, but more importantly… They are moldable. And you, spriggan, will be the mold." Again, the prince reached out to it, and brushed a finger over its limbs. "You are made from branch and leaf, but bound together by something more… Threads from the cocoon of the Herald of Change itself. This is your task: change them. Discard the failures, and improve upon those with promise. Create the perfect killer. Craft me my weapon."  The Spriggan looked around, and counted the tusked figures. A mere fourteen lives in his hands. He would sacrifice thousands to earn his name. "I accept my task. But I shall need more." The Prince smiled. "Do not worry. You will have as many as you need."  The Spriggan nodded before.
