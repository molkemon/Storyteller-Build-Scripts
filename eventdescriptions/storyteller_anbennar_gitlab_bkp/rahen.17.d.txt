‘Masters, anxious. Slaves, restless. The corruption of the harimraj has taken its toll on the daily lives of the peoples of Rahen. Broken roads, filth and squalor,; these garnish the once splendid cities along the Dhenbasana. The Raja is uncaring, lulled by the sweet words spoken by his courtiers inside the glamor of his golden palace. Senapti and prabhi, tasked with serving the Raja, instead bleed themselves as they fight for power and influence.  This was not  how the world was meant to be.  Harimari, children of the Great One, once leaders of our society, once proud and righteous striding along the Nitikamarja; how stand you now? Sidelined by manipulative and shortsighted humans, lured into complacency and sloth. Our soldiers, mighty as they marched from the deserts of AzkaSur to the coastline of Tianlou, now mere tools meekly following their petty lords’ whim. Our empire, the greatest to ever grace the earth, as envisioned by Harimar himself, now a fleeting imagination. There once was a dream of a paradise. Where have those gone who would realize it?’; Excerpt from ‘Maharaj Vanrahar’ or the ’Jungle Paradise’
