"The temperate forests and plains of Eordand are pretty damn weird, even before you factor in the influence of the Fey. The druidic magic there means you can walk ten paces and step into an entirely different season. I grew up in Bivuzena, fishing the coast, so imagine my surprise when Kiusz and I happened upon several small, sharklike creatures with greenishbrown scales and ethereal purple underbellies floating in the air, munching on the leaves of trees.  Thinking that we’d found an easy dinner, it was fortunate that we were being tailed by several of the Gelcollers. They stopped and warned us that if we caught and ate them without properly preparing them first, we’d be vomiting in hours, and dead within days. Kiusz didn’t think much of it, figuring his lizard gut could handle it, and signed several colourful phrases about the natives trying to prevent him from having dinner. But I didn’t have lizard guts, now did I? So I thought I’d go with ‘em and ask how exactly they eat the damn things."; The Autobiography of Fauna Hunter Bastirai Kazuru, Chapter 3.
