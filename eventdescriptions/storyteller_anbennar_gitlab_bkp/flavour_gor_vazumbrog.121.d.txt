Of all the hunts the Dauvek Veker had undertaken, this was to be the most glorious. The fabled Serpent of Lake Silvermere, a monster of myth and legend, was to fall to titanium blades. However, the bold hunters hit a snag: local tales of the monster were extremely inconsistent. Could it be baited with fish? Only with beef? Was it even a serpent at all, or perhaps some great quadripedal lizard?  There was nothing for it, Thrun reckoned, but to trawl the lake in its entirety. Surely such a large monster as the serpent was purported to be couldn't hide for long against the full might of his anglers. And fish they did, as the hours turned to days, and the days turned to weeks. And yet, though their nets filled with mundane fish, the legendary serpent remained elusive.  After a month of fruitless fishing, the disappointed delvers resolved to return home, chalking the whole incident up as a learning experience. As the party departed the depleted lake, Thrun could've sworn he heard a great slapping splash, as of a silvery tail waving its would be captors goodbye… but when he turned back, there was no sign of any such thing.
