The ruler's march to Krummul's grave was somber in spite of their recent victory in the nation's capital. They wondered whether Krummul would be proud of their efforts here. After all, they hadn't really slain a dragon, only convinced her to pick on easier targets. Krummul himself, surely, would've killed the dragon in one blow if he had had the same opportunity that the ruler did. But then, Krummul was a hero. The monarch was just a ruler, in this diminished modern age. As they laid Skycleaver back at its resting place on the great ancestor's grave, so too did they lay down their last pretension to heroism.
