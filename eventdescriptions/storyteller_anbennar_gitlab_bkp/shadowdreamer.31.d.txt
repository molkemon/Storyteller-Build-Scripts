As the hunt descends into the Pit of Xazor, the caverns become unnaturally still. Even the sound of marching boots seems swallowed by the endless darkness below.  Then the echoes begin. At first they seem harmless, distant voices bouncing from stone to stone, whispering undecipherable words. Yet, soon these words became clear. Names are being called. Names of soldiers within our host.  After hours of marching, shapes begin to stir along the walls and ceilings above our host, Grotesque, batlike humanoids cling to the stone, their limbs twisted and wings ragged with age. Their eyes are empty, yet their mouths move with a terrifying familiarity… they speak with the voices they have stolen.  The Drahmgall believe these creatures were once explorers, hunters, and civilians who ventured too close to the pit and never returned. Over time, Xazor twisted them into something else entirely.  They serve the beast.
