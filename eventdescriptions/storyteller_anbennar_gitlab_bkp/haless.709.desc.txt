Since ancient times, the banks of the Yan River have been the most prosperous lands in all of Yanshen. The broad and tranquil river teems with merchant vessels laden with rare goods, carrying wares from the borderlands and even distant Shamakhad to the celestial capital of Tianlou. This trade route was once maintained and modified by the empires of Harimar and Jaher to transport troops and grain. Even after their fall, the Yan River has continued to flow silently, nourishing the lands of Jiangszun. The impact of this trade reaches far beyond merchants and the nobles who enjoy such luxuries—it has transformed many of the cities along the Yan. Their people, more than others in Yanshen, have grown fond of worldly pleasures. Dockworkers unload cargo through the day, then spend their generous pay reveling in the city's delights. These cities are filled with wine-houses, theaters, and exotic shops that remain open deep into the night. Their shimmering lights reflect upon the river, leading foreign visitors to marvel and name them "cities that never sleep."
