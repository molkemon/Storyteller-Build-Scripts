One of the counselors for a rural realm seeking to divide funds for the prospering of education and faith has found it increasingly hard to move forward with what to spend on. They have asked for your second opinion, my lord.

The local Darwiiz and Mwalim have both proposed various ideas, but two seem to be the most popular: the construction of a school under the guidance of the Mwalim, or provisioning the funds to all locals for their "Ruaa," pilgrimages towards advancing personal understanding of the world.

Both ideas are, of course, feasible and positive, but there is only so much funding to go around, and for smaller realms like this, there can’t be too many resources devoted. The Mwalim beg to provide proper education to this outskirt area, pointing out the lack of access to other important resources without miles of trekking hills and plains. The Darwiiz point out that the villages within the zone have not complained despite surveys and voyages by scholars being done.

What is your decision, my lord?
