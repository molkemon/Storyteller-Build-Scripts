Peace seems to be returning; at last, the storm of swords and spells is quelled. The cost may have been high across the nation, but for those left, meek acceptance of the new order is what seems most likely to keep them alive. So much blood is spilled in wars external, but a civil war is one between family, and those wounds are much deeper and take lifetimes to heal. The will of the victorious and this exhaustion will leave these scars, as those in power continue in triumph.  Those who had been ‘loyal’ faced the worst reprisals of all. Executions at random hours of the night, torture of the worst kind as the rebellion became the establishment. All of the old order would be burned out, except that all the features found most appealing to this new order could remain.  The Regent is slain, and the future seems murkier than ever somehow.
