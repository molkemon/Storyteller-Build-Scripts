In a dwarven hold, it is a rare luxury to be able to take a step outside at one’s pleasure, without navigating the winding tunnels to the surface. To be able to rest against the rail of a balcony, to feel the cool night air, to watch one’s breath drift away. As was their habit, the lord enjoyed resting here at the end of a day debating with administrators and astronomers, watching the clouds roll a thousand feet below, and looking up into the midnight blue, speckled with stars of clear crystal white. But tonight, something made the hairs on the back of their neck stand on end. Suddenly, they had never been more aware of the size of the world outside their mountain home. They felt exposed. The hemisphere of the night sky felt like a lens, with them at its focal point. Something was examining them. Something far above scrutinized them, as though they were under a microscope slide. A mind immeasurably superior to their own. The lord dared to look up at the stars, and a shiver ran deep through their bones.
