The lord steps ashore to a cacophony of crying gulls, before setting off into the bustling port city of Lelanan. The sights and sounds are overwhelming in their variety and intensity, and it takes all of the lord’s concentration just to stay the course toward the city's namesake attraction, following the Pilgrim's Road as it winds its way like a river through the chaotic, colourful, organic city. The midday gongs ring as the lord finally stands before the Temple of Lel. It is surprisingly humble for its fame, and very much of its city in terms of architecture: whitewash walls, a tiered roof topped with an onion dome, bas reliefs depicting marine life banding the exterior. At the behest of a lowranked halfling priestess, the lord is ushered in. The air is cool within, and laced with a refreshing hint of ozone. Mosaics featuring sea horses, merfolk, araizary and other sea creatures adorn the walls. The lord is shown into the heart of the temple, a circular room with a perfect halfsphere pool of crystalclear water at its centre. Stepping forward, they gingerly sets one foot upon the pool: the surface tension does not break. Cautiously, the lord assumes a meditative pose and tries to calm their mind. Imperceptibly, a feeling of peace descends. Minutes pass, hours, until time loses its meaning. When the lord wakes, it is dark out. Drenched in Lel's serenity, the night holds no menace. The lord sighs in contentment, and begins the journey home.
