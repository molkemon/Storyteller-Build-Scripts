‘People disappearing from their beds during stormy nights. Ailing elders suddenly regaining their youth, and healthy youngsters aging decades in an instant. Lost ones coming home changed, and loved ones leaving forever. We have all witnessed situations like those. Qlak, what do you make of them?’  ‘Ah, my favorite tale. All the bad things in the Woods, all due to the fey, they say. My friend, some of those stories are undeniably exaggerated, but the fey are truly a tricky bunch. Annoy them, and they’ll make your days a living nightmare. Leave them alone, and they still might do that, just because they can and want to. Incur their wrath…’ For a second, the Wanderer's eyes seemed to shimmer of gold and crimson. ‘Well those tales are rare, for very few can survive their fury long enough to pass on their story. Did you know that, in the first place, most fey don’t care about goblins, orks, or even elves? They just live, oblivious to the more… mundane inhabitants of the Woods. And many fey are completely harmless.’  ‘Harmless? Qlax, they steal our babies. We wouldn’t devote a huge effort to fighting something harmless. The first thing we teach our children is to distrust all fey.’  ‘Oh, but you’re totally right, my friend. The fey can not be trusted, even those who seem harmless. Especially those. They can trick you into meeting the archfey, and that, my friend, is something only lunatic pact seekers would want. Archfeys are all so terribly bothersome and they can jumble your mind with a flick of a finger on a whim, whenever they lose interest in you. And they all have a nasty temper. Well, except the Prince of Fools. He’s a chill lad, that one, and I’ve not met anyone who could hold a better conversation.’  ‘You have an awful lot to say about them, Qlak.’
