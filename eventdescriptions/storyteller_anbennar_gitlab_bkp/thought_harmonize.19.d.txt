A vernacular novel about a well known story from an old era is rapidly gaining popularity among our subjects. This in itself is not a problem, but it has come to our attention that this novelization is in fact breaking central tenets of The Thought as it could be seen as an argument for disobeying central authority and their ruler. Our advisors now argue that the book must be banned, though some fear this will only make it more alluring to those that wish to read it.
