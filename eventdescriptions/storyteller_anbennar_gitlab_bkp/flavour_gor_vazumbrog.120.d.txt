Grimmun idly speculated about what religious significance the feathered, venomous creature partially blotting out the desert sun (Surael's light? Surakel's? The vagaries of local custom eluded him in the moment) might carry, though that was irrelevant to the purpose of the day's expedition. No, this was a trial run for the defeat of another venomous, winged creature.  Mages harried the creature with burst of distracting light and dulled its counterattacks with antitoxic wards  – sufficient to stop this creature's meager nibbles, but of dubious utility against the purple queen herself. Artificerwrought weighted lines entangled the creature's wings, bearing it down to the ground, where it was pummelled into a stupor by dozens of titanium hands and ultimately shot to death by the welldisciplined Vazumbrog riflemen.
