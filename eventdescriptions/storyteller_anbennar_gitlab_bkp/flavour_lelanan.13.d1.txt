The Enahelibe Ritual was most sacred. Something I had read about a great deal, but conducting it firsthand was a very different experience—one for which I had little zeal. I recited the prayers to Lel perfectly, while the Mihipha circled the slowly dancing pair. Their voices infused with a magic that excited the participants and readied them for the arousal that hung in the air. That was what made the ritual difficult for me—the couple so tender, loving, and sweet, dancing as one so close together, their hearts moving their feet. I couldn't help but think about my inability to participate in the Enahelibe Ritual, which brought great fertility. That was the cost of my being born in the opposite body; I would never carry the life-creating ability.

As the ritual continued, I set aside my feelings and considered the magic hanging in the air. How else could it be used for good? I asked later in quiet prayer.
