The Grandmaster, kneeling, bowed his head low as he finished his appeal. The Bloodcloaks arrayed behind him in the throne room, faces hidden behind their plate-and-mail helmets, shrouded in capes of dark red cloth, bowed in unison as well. On the millennial anniversary of the Ruin, Olhokar the Fortuitous had received a similar appeal—to grant the Brotherhood of the Bloody Cloak stewardship of the Chendhya. Today, the ruler was flanked by two trusted warlords—Teshekar Takasangamar stood to one side, Andyzha Vakyrlana, of Darkalyndar, to the other. Teshekar leaned in first. "These elves are noble, ruler. They've guarded the frontier for centuries—even during Gophira's fall, they upheld their sacred vows, never took sides! My family have sent many of our members to join their ranks—I know they'll make good stewards, loyal and true."

Andzyha scoffed at that. "These Bloodcloaks are villains, renegades! Simply the worst people imaginable! They use their ‘vows’ as excuses to terrorize innocent Chendhyans for fun—if ‘protecting the steppe’ means riding around cutting tents, slaughtering livestock and whisking away women and children as slaves, then they ‘protect’ very well indeed!"

"Slander!" the Grandmaster called, as he rose to challenge Andzyha's words. "We work only to protect Taychend from the Chendhyan hordes! For fun? You think we exact pleasure from our work?" When the warlord of Darkalyndar ostentatiously mouthed a "yes," the Grandmaster drew the Greatsabre hanging on his back. "The steel of vengeance knows no pleasure, you barbarous, cat-riding whore—! I'll have your—!"

"Enough!" the ruler stood suddenly, and the hall calmed at an instant. The Grandmaster shrunk back at the admonishment. "The Brotherhood's appeal…"
