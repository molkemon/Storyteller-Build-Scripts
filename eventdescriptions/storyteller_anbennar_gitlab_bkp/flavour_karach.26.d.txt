"Traak Bloodcarver sat down at the lumberers’ workshop, and began to wonder what he might make that day. He had spent all of last month making a multistory wooden birdhouse to hang on the outside of his home, and it was already paying off: he had put a glass wall on the side that faced his window, and he could see birds beginning to move in. Watching them calmed him.  As he thought some more about his birds, he saw the instructions being passed out. They were to make sandals?. This had nothing to do with wood. It had nothing to do with art. Despite his best efforts, Traak began to grow angry. But then he saw the materials being passed out, and that the shoes were to be made of wood. Perhaps he could give this a chance after all.  In the coming days, Traak and the lumberers returned to the workshop day after day, as they made their new tanco shoes. Traak carved curved, smooth lines into his wood, and whittled it down so it would fit his feet well. He sanded the wood down to remove its imperfections. On the last day, Traak painted his new shoes with patterns of jungle trees, the same as the ones he cut down to make them.  Traak Bloodcarver walked home that day in the tanco he made himself, along a path whose rocks did not hurt his feet. As he reached his home, he heard the sound of birdsong."
