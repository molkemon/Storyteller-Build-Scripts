Our researchers started to make calculations and measurements to make the railway gun a success after the meetings were done. It was decided that a large artillery cannon would go on top of a heavily armoured train so it does not get destroyed. It would be loaded from inside the train and no personnel could go outside of it resulting in a train with large destructive capabilities and no possible danger for the operators. After some more calculations the scientists actually managed to design something that could move the train and the only thing left to do was build it.  After years of hard work and prototypes breaking down passed, a working railway gun had been produced. There was a large celebratory feast held in honor of the success and at the end they fired it to showcase its power. Sadly, the operators did not think of the fact that we live in caves… so the gun hit the roof and large amounts of rock came crashing down destroying both the gun and killing hundreds of goblins.  After this disaster we scrapped the project knowing that it will never be useful and moved on, but the remnants of the railway gun has turned into a monument to remember a great invention. We never will escape the curse of death when dealing with trains it seems…
