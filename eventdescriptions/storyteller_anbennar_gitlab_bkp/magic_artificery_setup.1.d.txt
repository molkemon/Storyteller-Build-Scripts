A warwizard is burned to a crisp by a faraway laser. A robotic laborer is fueled by a unique fluid and grease. An ancient relic is deconstructed and finally understood. In short: scholars, engineers, philosophers, and inventors domesticate the magic of the world. Then, they place these magics into items that anyone can use. All across the world, the age of artificery comes alive with a spark. For centuries, artificers have been seen as nothing but novelties: a strange little group of mages employed only by gnomes, then kobolds, then goblins. But now, as mass manufacturing and the scientific method take hold in Halann, artificers become more powerful than anyone imagined. With a unique fluid, relics, ivory, porcelain, iron, steel, mithril, and more all traveling along sprawling new trade networks, inventions are created which shake magical societies to their core. Our nation now must determine where it fits into this equation. Several possible approaches now present themselves. The first is to embrace artificery, stop all official patronage of mages in our society, and delve entirely into the science that we believe to be the future. Alternatively, we can reject the new art: instead of wasting our surplus of a unique fluid and relics on some flighty vision of modernity, we can use these vital resources to empower our mages. Or, finally, we can try to combine the virtues of both: we would invest only partially into mages and partially into artificers, receiving the full benefits from neither while also allowing them to coexist. We stand at a crossroads, and we must make a permanent decision. What does our lord choose? Are we to be a nation fully of artificers, fully of mages, or with a little bit of both?
