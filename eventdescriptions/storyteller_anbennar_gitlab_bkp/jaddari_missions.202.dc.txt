The dance is over. Normally, when the celebration comes to an end, the two dancers rest, passing a waterskin or bottle of local drink between them. But there shall be none of that today. It took brutal battles, but none we could not bear with strength supporting us. It took liters of blood, but not a sacrifice we could not make in honour. It took a lightningfast offensive, but not one beyond our capacity when waging war under our sun. Finally, we have defeated those heretics who profaned our deity’s name and defied the truth, even as they professed their lying devotion. There shall indeed be wine, but for the ruler alone, as they prepare and readies three more armies. One of bureaucrats and administrators, to ensure the riches of the rebel regions are put to good use. One of military commanders, to spread out across the restless land and ensure all know that our rule, and that of His Herald, is absolute. And one of spies and devoted followers, to ensure that every citizen never even begins to imagine such a heresy again. The celebration is over, and we have won.
