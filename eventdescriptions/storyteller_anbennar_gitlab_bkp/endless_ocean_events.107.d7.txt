The afterlife is not here.  Perhaps such a lesson may come across as tautological, but for the faithful, the axiom proves a potent spiritual navigation tool. The departed do not belong in the homes of the living; their peace will be found in the uncharted realms far across the waves, away from torches and hearths and forges. Blissful unknown is their lot, rather than the glowing embers of life. The afterlife is not here, and so the dead must be guided to their next journey. To facilitate their travels, some Endless Ocean Believers have taken to practicing Firelit Vigils. Throughout the year, hearthkeepers tend massive beacons to warn the dead where not to sail– special care is given to these blazing guides following disasters and battles. After such events, the purpose of the hearthkeepers is twofold: like usual, their labor illuminates longtreaded ground for the dead to escape from, while the beacons offer a heading to the displaced and missing. Those still possessing the spark of life rejoin the great conflagration of civilization, while the deceased take sail for a gentler afterlife across the great sea.
