Naga cannot have children, nor do they experience romantic feelings or sexual urges. This can sometimes create a disconnect between a naga advisor and their student seeking advice in matters of love. Generally, when it comes to relationships, naga focus on practical ways to maintain a positive relationship with a spouse or family through financial security or spiritual guidance. This works quite well, as a lizard in love can lose sight of more mundane matters and needs a naga to pull them back to reality. However, issues can arise when naga fail to recognize when someone is talking about romantic feelings. This became evident in the history of the venerable 4th Empire. According to naga chroniclers, the empire fell apart due to a loss of trust among its people. But when the original text was published more widely, it became clear that the text was likely a metaphor for a relationship falling apart due to a cheating spouse.

By itself, this wouldn’t be so damaging. However, as more people started looking through the historical empires for missed romantic metaphors, another issue emerged with the 111th Empire. The text describing an incredible tower by a deep mineshaft with a flowing river was likely not talking about the city of Zerat, but a graphic sexual act.
