"Now I’ve heard tell of Ikhaty Sunlover, and I’ll tell you what I heard. Some say the tale’s grown too much in the telling, but I say it hasn’t grown half enough.  Ikhaty was born a miner, born with a hammer in his hand. They say he swings it for the Axta, that he drives the chieftains from the land.  The chieftains came with their soldiers. The Axta answered the call. The miners and the traders and bards stood behind him, Ikhaty stood tallest of all.  And Ikhaty said to the chieftains, ‘Well a man ain’t nothin’ but a man. And before I let you beat us down, I’ll die with a hammer in my hand, I will, I’ll die with a hammer in my hand.’  They say his shadow goes into battle, before Ikhaty goes in too. They say the chieftains hear that hammer singing, and none of them know what to do.  They say Ikhaty knows all in the column, every miner and farmer and bard, and when he calls out to you in the morning, the war doesn’t seem quite so hard.  The chieftains haven't broken yet, friends, but that hammer's still ringing out clear. When the morning comes and we need someone to lead us, I know who I want standing here."
