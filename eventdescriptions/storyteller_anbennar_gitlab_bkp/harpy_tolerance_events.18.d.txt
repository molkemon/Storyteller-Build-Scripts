"They're thieves, lord. That's what they are." A fisherman from their province, his face red with fury, points a calloused finger at the group of harpies across the room. "They descend from the sky like a plague of locusts and steal the fish right out of our nets. Fish meant to feed our children. Keep them from our lands and we'll give you extra taxes, we will." A harpy matriarch, her silver feathers ruffled in indignation, steps forward. "We 'steal' nothing." she screeches, her voice sharp enough to cut glass. "Your clumsy boats and nets miss half the schools that pass through the bay. We hunt from the air, as is our way. We are simply better fishers. The bounty of the sea should go to those skilled enough to claim it. Lord, grant us leave to settle in this region, and your markets will overflow with our excess catch." The fisherman sputters, ready to retort, but all heads now turn to you. The shouting echoes in your hall, demanding a judgment.
