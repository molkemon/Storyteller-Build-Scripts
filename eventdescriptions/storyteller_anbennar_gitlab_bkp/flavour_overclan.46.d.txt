The following is a portion of the minutes of a parliamentary meeting regarding the Xhazobkult.  REP. TOEZAZEN: Argumentation has been clear. No religious body within the state is willing to allow for the others to preach to the Xhazobkultists. It appears to this body that to favor any one faith in this matter would be a violation of the state's principles, and that to allow unfettered preaching by all parties will simply create internal conflict.  REP. ABAD: Why don't we circumvent the issue altogether? Invite missionaries from abroad to do the work instead? There can be no internal conflict of interest if all the proselytizing party in question is external in origin.  REP. SZELYAMETES: Absurd. Allow foreigners to preach within our midst? That's inviting religious turmoil.  REP: ABAD: Yet you ignore something you yourself demonstrate. If there is one thing all Bulwari can agree upon, it is a distrust of foreign missionaries.  (Murmurs of laughter cross the parliament.)  REP. SZELYAMETES: Perhaps you have a point. At least the foreigners won't try to put anyone on pyres, Surael willing.
