It started suddenly, the capital ablaze. Some day, perhaps the orks would laugh to know they too had now known a day of ashen skies. Each speck of ash proved hostile and malign, burning those upon whom it fell and soon the cacophonoy of the injured and casualties was all that could be heard across the encampment. Our lord rushed to the scene, beholding at the market heart a strange figure, seemingly born out of a pile of ashes, reforming themself into a greyscale figure awash with authority, boasting a gaze that made even their title tremble. The figure's gaze was bewitching, somehow, keeping their eyes affixed to their terrible and intoxicating presence. They didn't even speak, but somehow, in a fascination of the fey, words could be felt in their head as they knelt in an awestruck manner for the time. Their movements in this ashen form formed words, issuing a voice they had never heard before. "I am they, the Blackened Prince and it was by mine will that your clan has prospered. Mine influence in those you call the Silen tblade set you upon your current path. Your prosperity is owed to me. And for that I bid you to serve me. For I am the true justice and I require utter obedience in turn. I shall ask only this once." They felt their own control returning to them, the more they stared at the strange, ashen form. It was clear that whatever power was felt here was waning. The ash fell no more and they were able to stand up, proud in their response. Whatever truth and whatever power this Blackened Prince had, there was only one answer to this call for obeisance.
