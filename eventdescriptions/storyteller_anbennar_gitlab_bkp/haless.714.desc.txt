A grand contest of silk weaving has begun along the river Yanhe, drawing together renowned artisans and master weavers from across the realm. The event features two stages: a public exhibition of submitted samples and a final jury review. Brilliant ribbons cross the air, and patterns shimmer in the lamplight, stopping passersby in their tracks. The techniques on display span a rich spectrum. Some excel in tightly woven fabrics with intricate, enduring motifs. Others favor airy, flowing textures that dance with light. A few bold workshops introduce experimental methods, blending dyed threads or incorporating mixed materials for dazzling new effects. Crowds gather, debate erupts—awed spectators, artisans, and merchants dispute the merits of each style. Now that the contest has concluded, one style has emerged triumphant... or perhaps all have.
