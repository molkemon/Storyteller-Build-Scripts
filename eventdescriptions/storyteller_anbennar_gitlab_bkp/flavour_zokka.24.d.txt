Ilfanill arrives, trailing shadows behind her. The gnolls were waiting, stinking of ambition, musky and sharp. Ready to plucked and used as the Coven wished.  She had with her a script, of course. Promises to barter, secrets to share, the usual fare. But hags were hardly honest merchants. Every pact needed a pound of flesh. Every word was a test.  She meets the congregation at the Matriarch. She smiles. She bows with mocking grace, and steeples toomany fingers as they parley. The gnoll.priest, she supposes, listens, keeping his eerie green eyes locked on her own. When she finishes, he opens his mouth, and he whispers a name.  Her breath catches. The swamp recoils. Emerald fire swells, and  down  she  falls  She lands on obsidian. The air thrums with appetite. She sees him, impossibly large, and he sees her.  He smiles.  She tries to speak, to plead, to twist her form into something that might slip away. But it's too late. He's already moving, his maw already opening wide.  And Ilfanill Gloomweaver, the Lamplighter, is eaten.
