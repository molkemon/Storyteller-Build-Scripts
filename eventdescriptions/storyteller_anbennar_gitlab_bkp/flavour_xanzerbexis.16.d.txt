It was a rainy day in Port Xanzerbexis. Reconstruction was still ongoing, and as such, the court was held in a great tent. The inside was hot, cramped, and smelled of wet fur. Their lord was pacing around one of their subjects, who was feeling increasingly unwelcome. "You make it sound easy. A rarity in war." "It will be, lord." The human replied in gnollish. Spoken by a human, it sounded meek. Their lord could smell their fear, but it wasn't showing. Someone had taught them well. "Our spies in Nathalaire claim that the Folly is full of gnolls. The city itself cowers behind the waters. With your fleet, the sea will be their grave." "And you believe the gnolls of the folly will join us?" "I do. They live in squalor. This." the human gestured around the tent. "This is a better life. Why would they refuse?" Their lord could think of a few reasons. But their advisor had always given them good intelligence, especially since the inclusion of humans. Humans blend in with humans, and these days, they had more human subjects than gnolls. This was what they were trying to balance, but their title was nothing if not pragmatic. "Scribe." they barked. An older human snapped to attention, his white beard streaked with ink. They began to dictate. A policy for settling Daravan's Folly began to take shape. She offered their advisor a glance, then grunted.
