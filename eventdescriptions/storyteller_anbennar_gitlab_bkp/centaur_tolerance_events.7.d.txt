News in the province travels fast, and it's no secret why. After witnessing centaur races firsthand, a few savvy merchants beseeched the strong, speedy winners to carry news, ferry mail, and even cart goods across the land. The recruitment drive proved wildly successful with onlookers amazed by their zealous autonomy and speed. Over time, the centaur transporters, bored by their increasingly repetitive work, pivoted away from deliveries as a job and toward deliveries as sport. The need for competitive speed eclipsed safety and quality, spiking worker injuries and damaged parcel complaints. If that wasn't enough, our porter and teamster guilds, fearing for their livelihoods, sought our aid combating their new competition. They cite how speed without safety renders a poor product, but several of the guilds disparage the centaur workers under their breath. How should we handle these feuding farriers?
