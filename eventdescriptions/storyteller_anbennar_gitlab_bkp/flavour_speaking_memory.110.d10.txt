There have been many chieftains of the islands of Kanolowele, but none are as fascinating as Kau’i’noe, who tried to sail beyond the edge of the world. Kau’i’noe was not born a chieftain’s son, or even to a family of standing. What he was born with was a bravery that none could match, and from the moment he was grown he fought across Kanolowele for wealth and the glory of his people in many foreign wars. He soon amassed wealth to match his courage, and then returned home to become chief of his people.  Kau’i’noe had many friends across the land, and his tribe soon grew rich from trade. But something ate at Kau’i’noe. He had travelled far, but there were lands he had not yet been to beyond the Kiamoa. If he died he could not return home, so he had to journey to those lands while he still lived. So Kau’i’noe took all of his riches, and he built a ship as large as one hundred ships to withstand the Kiamoa and sail beyond it. He departed with the love of his people, and he stayed at sea for four years. But on the fifth, just when it was feared he would never return, a whale as black as night came to Kanolowele, and it spit up Kau’i’noe on the shores of his village. He would not talk about what had happened or what he had seen, and he was a solitary man from then on.
