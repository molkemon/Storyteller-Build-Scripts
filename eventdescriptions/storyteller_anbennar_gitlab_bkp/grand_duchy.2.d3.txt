It was quiet within the Imperial Parliament. Benign spies sent to the ruler’s court sought to gain any insight but found no leaks or whispers. Neither supporter nor detractor of a rival monarch sat at ease, though the absence of that upstart gave some comfort to the former. They waited twelve hours, gorging on the buffet and brewing silent plots with their attendants until the ruler arrived at midnight. A banging on the parliament doors sent the sergeant at arms sprinting to open the door, but their face dropped as they saw the ruler. A few princes leaned over to see what all the fuss was about. Stepping into the chamber was the ruler in full battle regalia, ready for war, bearing a torch in one hand and the battered banner of a rival in another. The ruler stepped to the podium, staring imperiously at the chamber as they sparked the torch and set the banner alight. The burning of the banner itself was the Emperor's speech.
