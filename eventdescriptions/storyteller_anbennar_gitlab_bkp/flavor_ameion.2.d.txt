Laskaris and Kerodinsis Arpedan were discussing military matters when an albino Kheionai named Irenthenes Gakones sauntered into the tent. Late as always, he took a seat beside Kerodinsis like he owned the place. "Long time no see, fellow Eilastes!" "Finally decided to grace us with your presence?" Laskaris teased, grinning. Kerodinsis scoffed. "Well..." Irenthenes chuckled, clapped his hands together, "shall we start? The reason I'm here in Taychend, instead of walking the halls of power back in Ameion and schmoozing with your beautiful, neglected, cheated-on, Ormabhen-hustler of a wife, is that I've come up with a bit of what I call legal brilliance. You want to bring Kheionai-style cities to Taychend, but you want to keep your conquests as Ameion's conquests, right? Well, under these proposals, the new Taychendi provinces, the Epaidía, will be legislatively autonomous—but their elected governors will be hooked directly into your office. You like them? They'll be confirmed by the Synkletos. You don't? You get to appoint one yourself." Kerodinsis cut in. "And these governors are a strictly civilian role?" Irenthenes nodded. "Eligible provincials will be conscripted straight into the state military. So civilization for the people on the ground—but the Kylakas, Ameion, and the army still in a position to dominate! There are a few preparatory questions, though..." Irenthenes passed some papers over. "Things I wanted your opinion on." Laskaris skimmed the documents—they were proposals for new military governors, and ways the eventual new provinces could be tweaked to his liking. For a moment, he grimaced—the ability to affect such change with a single pen stroke felt like the power of a warlord, not a Kylakas. But he pushed the concern aside—authority was the essence of governance, after all—and the decisions he made today might define governance for quite a while. "Alright..." Laskaris picked up a quill. "Let's focus on..."
