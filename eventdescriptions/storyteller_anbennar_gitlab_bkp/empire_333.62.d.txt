What a curious name. Is it perhaps some kind of charm or token to hang by one's sleeping quarters? The speaker slowly and calmly explained everything they had heard about the Wishcatcher: what the genies said, the message left by the fake spire in Shalazar, and the various logs found by the Precursor Elves. None of this seemed to ring any bells with the Thraxis. That is, until someone in the crowd mentioned that one of the Precursor messages spoke of "frills," which caused the Thraxis to whirr back into life, its million voices calling out with renewed vigor.

"Oh, that curious lizard! Yes, we do remember that being. A Wishcatcher, you call it? What a wonderfully evocative name. It did more than catch a wish; it ate wish magic. By spreading its magnificent frilled mane, it drew in the very essence of a genie's magic and devoured it whole. We believe there were more types of magic it could eat. It could perhaps even eat us, but it was far too polite to do such a thing. Of course, those elves would be frightened of it—and definitely the genies. Their existence is one of wish magic. We do not know what would be left of them if you ate its wish. Perhaps their shoes would remain."

The Thraxis spun quicker and laughed at its own joke, the million voices laughing at different tones and paces, creating a din akin to being buried alive with a thousand beehives. But even its laugh could not drown out the excited chatter among the crowd. Something so strong even beings with power beyond our comprehension feared it.
