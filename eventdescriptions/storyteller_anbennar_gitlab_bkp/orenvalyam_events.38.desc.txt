The Kavaluttam is generally tolerant of differing theological views, as long as there are no significant political consequences. However, the recent publication of a new religious text from the Ynn has crossed this line—now labeled as "The Ynnic Heresy." Many theologians argue that equating the Ynn with Nayiru, an Elusive One herself, and having her preside over the Cycle of Glory, raises serious questions about the Ynnic Empire. Is it now a realm sanctioned by Nayiru? Consider the implications!

This new heresy has caused considerable distress among the Kavalali. In most of South Aelantir, there's a growing belief that it's an attempt to undermine the ideals of Dawn across the entire western hemisphere. If this spreads, it could create a rival center of power to the Kamrayakval and Kavaluttam, which are the sole instruments of Nayiru. Elevating the status of the Ynnic clergy to unprecedented heights—perhaps even above Taychend’s own—would be a significant shift.

Both the Kavalalist and Kamrayakvalist factions agree that action must be taken to combat this heresy. However, they differ on the approach. The Kavalalists advocate for debate, as they believe the Ynnic Kavalali have the right to diverge on theological matters. In contrast, the Kamrayakvalists adopt a more stringent stance, calling for the arrest and replacement of the Ynnic Kavalali.

The Monasticists, however, view this debate as mere outrage. If the Ynnic claims are true, the truth will be revealed when Nayiru descends from Dhivumanhu. Even without that, how many other divergent doctrines and beliefs in Orenvalyam could be deemed treasonous or dangerous? Furthermore, why restrict the freedom of the Ynnic clergy when we are an Aelantiri realm, not a Taychendi one? For the Monasticists, this debate is nothing more than unnecessary outrage.
