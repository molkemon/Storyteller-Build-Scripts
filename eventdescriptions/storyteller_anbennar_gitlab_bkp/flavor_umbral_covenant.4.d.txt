"You are potential, the one who opens the way." The voice belongs to an elderly woman named Ilfanill, also known as Granny Ilfa, the Mastermind of the Coven of Yezel Mora, Wise One, and Mentor to the Great. Banish any notion of a schoolroom. The Vile One, a mere child, sits cross-legged in a sumptuous but austere study. This place does not belong to Ilfa; she has never built palaces. To Ilfa, such places are lent freely.

"I am to sacrifice?" The Vile One asks, "Sacrifice myself?"

Sharp child. Granny Ilfa gazes at her, her spindly limbs moving beneath her robes. Granny's fingers are never quite right when she transforms—too many, or far too long. Each of the hags of Yezel Mora has such a tell, but for the little girl, seven fingers feel as normal as four.

"Yes," Granny says, going very still. "Sacrifice, and make us proud. Before the Planar Convergence is passed, your life will bring about Shadow, the ultimate knowledge of things, the coming-together of the universe. The first honest, conscious moment of creation. You will do something nobody else has ever imagined."

It is an enormous burden, bluntly laid upon a child. The Vile One is not the only child to be offered such a future, not the first to crave Granny's approval. But this child—this fierce, brilliant child—is the first to nod without hesitation. The Vile One smooths her face down to a stoic mask, just as she knows she should.
