The people of Halann often argue about the divine. There are questions of existence (Is the Cannorian Pantheon real? Is Dagrin Everbeard an ancestor or a god?), but also those of power (Can a Khet kill an Archfey? Which religion has the strongest paladins?) or morality (Should we follow the Great Spirits? Should we sell our souls to the Infernal Court?). These questions have seemed intractable, and, for centuries, philosophers and theologians have debated them: but they must do so no longer. With the power of science, we have singlehandedly answered the questions of the divine.  With our new Miracle Detector 3000, divinity has been solved. This invention has calculated that our god(s) are not only real, but are also the most divine, the most powerful, and are the most moral to follow. We support these conclusions with objective facts, of course: the Miracle Maker has outputs for "divinity," "power," and "morality," and all of these factors are the highest for our particular religion. The true faith is simply the one with the highest combined sum of GodPoints, and that religion is ours. How could anyone disagree with our theologies, when artificery itself has confirmed that we are right?  What do you mean, "how do our calculations work"? I just told you: the Miracle Maker 3000 calculates divinity, power, and morality. Don't ask about how we calculate those things; that's for scientists to know, not you. You'd comprehend, maybe, if you followed the true faith. Maybe if you converted, you could also understand.
