See, there are many stories of what happened that day within the royal palace, but I shall tell you the common tale. It was the anniversary of the coronation of the first Derannic king, and the first after the last holdouts of resistance against Derannic hegemony had been crushed. And so the ruler decided to hold a grand feast in celebration of the newfound peace. All powerful noble houses were invited, and bountiful foods as well as the finest Sorncosti wine were provided for all guests.  After drinking an unknown - and fervently debated - amount of alcohol, it is said the ruler retired alone to the palace gardens, where they stayed until twilight. When they returned, they gathered all guests in the main hall and announced that the spirit of Derhilde herself had spoken to them, and that they were to follow in her footsteps. When asked to elaborate, they simply left for their bedchamber.  "Wish I could get that drunk," came a grumble from the back of the tavern.  "The next morning," the Bard continued, "the ruler set out by foot from the gates of the palace with an honor guard of four; a messenger, two knights and a common hunter. And from Deranne they travelled east across barren plains, through dense woods and sunlit vineyards, along the very same path Derhilde once took. The ruler talked with many strangers, from lowly peasant to powerful lord, blacksmith to merchant, human, elf, dwarf and halfling. The hunter fetched food where it could not be bought, the knights scared off bandits, and the messenger noted down decrees shaped on morning strolls.  When at last the party arrived at Derhilde's place of coronation, Redfort, the gathered host could scarcely believe their eyes. The ruler wore a simple pair of breeches and a ragged cloak, unkempt hair flowing freely in the wind, and yet they gleamed with a royal presence that spoke of a newfound calm, and wisdom. Deranne had walked the path of Derhilde twice; once for war, and once for peace.
