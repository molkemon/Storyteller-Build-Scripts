Bricena had only just managed to suppress her smile as Oberon entered the hall. The normally gruff and stern Ceann of Salla Fealgarn was as close to a whipped puppy as Bricena had ever seen him. She gestured for her attendants to leave her, and offered him a seat. "So, you need my help," Bricena said after a short exchange of pleasantries. Oberon sighed. "I do. I appear to have... overstepped somewhat with Irvah and Alara. I cannot afford to lose their support, not now." Bricena clicked her tongue. "You certainly have a talent for that. First you rile up the eastern Salla at Elynaíle, and now the south. Are you intent on turning the whole Deepwoods against you?" Oberon gave her a foul look. "Oh, I am aware, thank you. Look, let us keep this simple. You can help me, and I have something you want."

Bricena had to suppress a smile again. She had him. "And what is that?" Oberon was quiet for a moment. Bricena shook her head. "No, none of this. I need to hear you say it." Clearly pained, Oberon brought his hand to his heart, then stretched out his palm. "The ritual of bonding. If you help me, you will have it. With no further demands." Smiling gracefully, Bricena copied his gesture and took his hand.
