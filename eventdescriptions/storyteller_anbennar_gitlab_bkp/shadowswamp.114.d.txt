Innovation is the light that guides progress, but this metaphorical light will not guide one in a very real, and very dark, swamp. For that we need actual light. Luckily, the swamp provides just this in the form of Dead Man's Lantern. This bulb emits a faint light that often attracts the flying insects of the swamp. In great enough numbers, and kept indoors, these plants transform from a bugattracting peculiarity to a true source of light one could work by. Throughout our lands, shopkeepers, tavern owners and overseers alike are using Dead Man's Lantern to light the night. This is wonderful for the local economy, but should we stop there?
