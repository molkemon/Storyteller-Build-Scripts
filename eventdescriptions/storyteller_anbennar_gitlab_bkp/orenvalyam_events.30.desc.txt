From the 1500s, everywhere Aelantir's native elves were expelled, eradicated, and exploited. But so much land has been lost that now Cannorian settlers, long divorced from the crimes of their ancestors, call Aelantir their home. The question of what to do with these peoples has divided the Kavalakuttam into two broad camps. The Reclamation group, which includes the Kamrayakvalist faction and much of the Kavalalists, argues that we should make a determined effort to resettle the displaced Ruinborn and give them their lost lands wherever possible. To not do so would simply be rewarding the Cannorians for displacement and colonization, after all, and would ensure that in places like Noruin, Harafe, or the Epednar Expanse, the natives will forevermore be in an inferior position to those who invaded them.

Of course, there are nuances—the Kavalalists would say that we would simply not touch them or move them to another part of Aelantir, the once-uninhabited islands, perhaps. The Monasticists retort that this would place millions of free souls at the Kavalakuttam's mercy (and, through the weight of its power, Taychend's mercy), and in so doing subjugate them.
