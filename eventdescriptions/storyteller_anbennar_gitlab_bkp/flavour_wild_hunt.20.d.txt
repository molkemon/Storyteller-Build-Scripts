All hunts end eventually. Some end in success and drunken triumph, once their quarry has been caught. Ours has ended in recrimination and shame. Our quarry was nothing less than dominion over the plains, the lakes, and the lands beyond, and it can be decisively said at this point that we have failed to catch our prize. Excuses among the advisors abound: the walls of the Triunics were too high, the plains were too disunified, the generals were incompetent—talk to someone else in the court and they will have a different reason absolving them of all mistakes conducted during the campaign. The fact remains, however, that all of these mistakes were committed by someone, and the compounding of their end result is undeniable—the Triunics have slipped the noose again.

One does not need to be experienced in divination to predict what will happen next. The Triunics, bonded together in the aftermath of our second defeat, will grow even closer. They will politically unify, and expand across the plains. One day, the last hoofbeat of the last centaur will echo out across the plains that Irdaeos gave to his people.

If there is something positive to be said about this fate, it is that the Wild Hunt will likely not exist to see it.
