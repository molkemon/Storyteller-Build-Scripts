There are as many different stories of Kaleo of Mokulali as there are islands in the sea. This is but one of them, but if Kaleo had a favorite it would be this one.  Kaleo was born on the island of Mokulali, which was not an island of any note or distinction. The people there lived out their lives, and traded with the other islands sometimes. But as Kaleo grew, Mokulali noticed how the things he told them came true, as surely as the sun setting would rise again tomorrow. When Kaleo grew to be a man, he gathered all his guests at his comingofage ceremony, and he told them that the island of Mokulali would one day move across the ocean just as a ship would. The people believed him.  Soon after that, the chieftain of Mokulali died, and there was only one choice to replace him. Kaleo guided the island with a wisdom that never steered it wrong, and soon Mokulali grew to be very rich. With those riches, Kaleo built a school. He selected a boy and a girl from the children born in Mokulali each year to go to this school, and the people trusted Kaleo so much that each one of them did. They did not see their parents again until they were grown men and women, and then they came back to their villages with a portion of the sight of Kaleo.  Kaleo trained more and more of these seers, and they took on the work of governing as Kaleo withdrew to within his school. He was not seen for many years, until he emerged on the rooftop of the highest part of his school. He raised his hands above his head, and there was a great rumbling in the sand: the island had slipped its bounds, and was moving across the ocean. It was moving towards the Kiamoa.  Soon after, the Century of Storms engulfed Kanolowele, but not Mokulali, for there was no longer an island of Mokulali in Kanolowele. But there is still a Kaleo, and he and his people live on in Mokulali Distant, an island unravaged by time.
