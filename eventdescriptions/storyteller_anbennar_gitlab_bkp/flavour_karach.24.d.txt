"It was always important that Ashhana look their best, and nobody knew this better than Surkind Earthblade. He had been a painter for years, but it was only at the workshops that he got to work with his favorite canvas.  The workshop had new models today, and he was eager to see what he had to work with. They were from the farmers’ Axta, and Surkind loved them, but the farmers had never had the best sense of fashion. Sure enough, the farmers had come to the workshop today in their working robes. They were dirty, and there was a strange grit that hung between their scales.  Surkind had worked with worse. He flew into action, and he and his fellow Hashax began the makeover process. They had to be cleaned first, of course. Then came the application of dyes onto the models’ newly cleaned scales, and there was a striking new purple that Surkind wanted to try out. It would go nicely with the deep blue of a set of robes he had for them.  Then came the application of the incense. Surkind had a smell for every occasion, and this one was no different: these robes would pair so well with a scent of jasmine. The Hashaz began to apply the incense, and as they did a wonderful scent filled the room.  At last, the work was done. The farmers emerged out into the dusk clean, fragrant, and slightly bewildered."
