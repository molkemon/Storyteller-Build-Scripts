Weeks pass as a moment. The Vile One spends most of her time alone in her chambers; her mages stir in discontent, for she has not joined the lattice for weeks. Only Cheni’s calm stops the rumbling. Sometimes Ikomeye joins her for a period of intent will—mostly she does not. It is a process of immense, tedious frustration, building and building and building until—

Ah!

For posterity, the Dissipation and the end of my masters upon Halann, I, remaining faithful servant of the Djinn, attest and record. Here amongst the dunes, we build our refuge and await their return—few though we are, we are loyal. We armour ourselves in the dust winds, and since nothing will grow above ground, subsist on what still grows in our master’s old halls…

They approached from the north thousands-strong on their mounts, reviling us in their treachery, for they believe the Dissipation has made an end to the Genie-folk, but they were torn apart by the Ghost of the Genies, she who still mourns their demise, shrieking among the dunes…

We beg her passage over the sands, mourning with her as we play our flutes, and recognizing a friend in our grief, she lets us pass, though she cannot speak but to bleed from her open mouth, noble widow of the wise…

Not merely grief but passion too, our Kammen, who is stoical matron and vengeful son both. He dances barefoot under the endless evening, prince of beauty, and weeps with us for the world lost beyond the endless ash. The great romantic, who knows all feeling as her dominion and all desire as his garden…
