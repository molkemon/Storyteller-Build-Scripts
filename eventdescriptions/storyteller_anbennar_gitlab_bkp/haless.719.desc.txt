As the eastern wind sweeps through Yanzhong, poetry follows in its wake. In recent years, the realms east of Jiangszun have seen a surge in refined literary spirit. Scholars and poets meet to recite and discuss; salons gather by lakeside pavilions, and verse contests enliven city outskirts. East of Yanzhong, the style grows ever more delicate, skilled in evoking feeling and depicting scenes—the quiet of forests and mountains, the stillness of lantern light behind drawn curtains. The language is gentle yet firm, the sentiment distant yet unmelancholy, drawing emotion from landscape and setting ambition beyond the material. Verses drift through open windows, spoken by farmers and woodcutters alike. Scholars compete to record fine lines; literary societies praise new works. Even in boudoirs and among children at play, stanzas are easily recalled. The flourishing of "eastern verse" now rivals the classical tradition. This year's chosen works range from tender and graceful to ethereal and serene, or bright and self-contained. Which will be crowned the true voice of elegance in this eastern breeze of verse?
