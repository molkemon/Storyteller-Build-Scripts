At one time, our capabilities in war were limited by our weapons and our soldiers, and those machines built for war naturally took up the job of defending our realm and striking out beyond our borders. Now that we have the ability to modify ourselves through artificery, this need not be the case. A machine's frame need not define its future. One of the most immediate applications of our grip on precursor technology is that mobilization for our country is simply a matter of giving our people the appropriate weaponry and programming. A population at peace can be turned into a population at war with the click of a rifle attachment. The soldiers of foreign lands may be formidable, but even the hardiest troops will collapse under the pressure of an entire nation of specialized, veteran soldiers marching in perfectly straight lines.
