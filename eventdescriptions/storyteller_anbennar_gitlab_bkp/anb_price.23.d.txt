With literacy on the rise, the spread and dissemination of books has become an objective for both economic and ideological reasons. Bookshops have become a much more common sight in many cities in general, and in large ones such as prominent urban centres, there are entire shop streets lined with boxes of books for sale. For those that could not afford to buy books themselves, there are now an increasing number of ways to lend or cofinance book purchases, such as paid lending libraries, book buying cooperatives, or subscription societies.
