My brother, my father, and my grandfather all shared the same dream; complete Cenaga control over the Bone Grove. Each one of them died pursuing that dream. And now their ruler must be laughing at the irony that that dream I never once imagined has come to life under my watch as clan head. I shake my head. All I wanted was to restore my clan's traditional holdings. To reclaim our homes. To protect what was ours. No more. It's never that simple though. Not when you're ceanna. Reclamation became conquest, and now the clan's stalwarts and elders have been bickering endlessly. Many demand vengeance for Oubblig's attempts at conquest. That we must place the goblins beneath our boots. There are some few, though, who argue that Oubblig and their successors were simply following the Bone Struggle, the fight for life. I glance at Lanhara, she stares impassively waiting for my decision. She's been quiet on the matter but I suspect I know her own opinion. As I return my gaze on the gathered elves, I think back to Elynaile, back to when Corin and their band returned. I remember the joy I felt when I learned the Verdant Veil had fallen and the shock of learning that my brother's murderer, Oubblig, had been pivotal to their success. It really never is simple, is it? With a quick motion of my hand, the arguments die down. I rise, swallowing the lump in my throat then address my clanselves.
