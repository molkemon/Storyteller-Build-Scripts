By agreement and convention with the House of Adeen, we allow their frontier communities or Halqa limited local selfgovernment. While for the most part these communities are as lawful or lawless as any other rural township, their ability to sidestep local and regional trade laws has allowed a few Halqa to develop into notorious hotbeds of smuggling. As soon as an illegal item gets into a Halqa storehouse, its possession no longer falls under our petty criminal code; Halqa like these simply decriminalise any product they wish to move in their own jurisdictions. Bellicose courtiers argue that this is the precise issue inherent to these communities, and implore the government to at least make a strict example of the smuggling Halqa.
