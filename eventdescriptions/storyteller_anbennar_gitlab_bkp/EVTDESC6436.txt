Borders are porous and everchanging, shifting with the fortunes of the latest conqueror passing by. Historians will, perhaps, blame us for the events that have transpired recently in a neighboring territory: 'As it continued to flourish under the benevolent rule of its lord, the people on the other side of the border were bound to get jealous,' they may say. Or perhaps they will favor us, and declare our protests at the occupying force's actions to be wellfounded. Either way, tensions in the territory have given us a claim to the province.
