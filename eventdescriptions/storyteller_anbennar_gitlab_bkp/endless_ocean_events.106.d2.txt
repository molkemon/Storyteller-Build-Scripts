Damn and confound our imbecile Chroniclers. The coaxing of tradition is not simply a matter of attendance. Such sensitive matters as faith require tact and empathy, a subtle suite of skills our moronic representatives apparently have never documented before. Like the winds of a squall, they descended upon communities uninvited. Like the brute force of a gale, they paid no consideration as they swept through their path, destroying artifacts of cultural significance in the effort to more swiftly finish their route. Their ignorant bluster prodigiously cultivated hatred towards not just their efforts, but our entire nation, and what little they did manage to collect is marred by personal bias and conjecture, the pathetic flotsam left over by a storm as it passes over a village.  Like extensions of the Kiamoa, everything they’ve made contact with has shattered to bits. Our reputation is in ruins, the resources we used to provision this expedition are exhausted, and we’re left with nothing to show for our efforts save the sorry dregs of a halfassed theological exploration. We’re now left wondering how to salvage as much as possible from this pathetic display of buffoonery.
