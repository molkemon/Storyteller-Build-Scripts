The farce of the 300th Empire being merely a migratory tribe is now well known in Taneyas, but the protests against its inclusion into the 333 empires had largely faded away. A migratory tribe can control a lot of land, and the idea of a migratory capital was not unheard of in society. There was also a better understanding of what lizardfolk society was like in this era. Sarhal was ruled by genies, and lizardfolk had to hide deep in the jungles to avoid their attention. Our modern understanding of an empire would naturally be very different from that of the time of the 300th Empire, when lizardfolk populations were at their lowest point in millennia. In their time, a tribe with a large area of control could indeed be considered an empire.

This was all well and good until the nature of the materials left by the 321st Empire became more widely publicized. Even most historians only knew about the powerful necromancer who raised an army of the dead. What they didn’t know was that the army she raised was a migratory tribe, which ignored the necromancer and continued chronicling their migrations. The naga were able to explain the rationale behind the 300th Empire’s inclusion, but justifying a wandering tribe of skeletal lizards as an empire would require a monumental act of propaganda.
