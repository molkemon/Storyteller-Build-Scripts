Elrandar Silverspite – once, he was the shining future of not just the faith, not just Orenvalyam, but the world itself. Harbinger of the new cycle, Herald of the Apocalypse. But those dreams turned to blackened ashes in the fires of his crusades. Massacres, purges, bloody retribution. The faith has been plunged into a vicious cycle of revolt, reprisal, and recrimination. And with each burning city, every murdered priest, every subjugated people whose shackles were not broken but replaced under the name of “security,” the ordinary people of Orenvalyam – the farmer, the day-labourer, the merchant – grow hotter with the flame of righteous rage. And the Kavalali, of course, are the voice of the people. They cannot ignore the words of their flocks any longer – even as the soldiery, increasingly radicalized by the Kamrayakval’s campaigns, agitate themselves for his continued ascent. If Silverspite is to be removed from power, then every effort must be expended to convince the Kavalakuttam to stay the course.
