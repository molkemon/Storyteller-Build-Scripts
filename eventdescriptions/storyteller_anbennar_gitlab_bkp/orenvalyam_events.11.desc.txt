Since the days of Elashok and Elajith, the Gold and the Violet, the Dumruktam Dynasty have been enlightened rulers of Sthanan ith Vussam, their name synonymous with culture, righteousness, and mercy. Good things—despite their position as warlords, and their inability and unwillingness to stamp out slavery. Instead, they focused on fighting bandits and cultivating the arts.

When Orenvalyam rose, the Dumruktams went into hiding, fearing reprisal not from the denizens of their own city, but the many-faced armies of Dawn, full of those who would seek to right the wrongs of the dynasty’s past in the only way they deem fit. Still, in the shadows, the Dumruktams have come to earnestly admire our faith, and they have emerged to plead to be able to take up the role of Kavalali and help guide their ancestral city once more.

Their influence and notoriety will probably continue to dominate Sthanan ith Vussam if we accept their request. But, if they are kept in rigorous check by the other Kavalali, and they are not the only Kavalali in Sthanan, can they really be called tyrants then? Is any sort of hereditary transfer of power tyranny?
