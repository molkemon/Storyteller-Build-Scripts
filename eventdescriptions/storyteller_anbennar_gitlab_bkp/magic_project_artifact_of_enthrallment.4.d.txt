The way to the place of smiths was steep and of unusual weather, and therefore difficult to carry word back and forth. Thus the lord could only visit for short periods of time, as matters in the kingdom required their attention. The first time they went was in the fall, season of mists and mellow fruitfulness. There they went to the cottage of the legendary smith, only to be greeted by a livein servant. The master, they was told, was not home. Our lord visited again in winter, when the chill wind threatened all travelers on the road. Along the way, the friends of the legendary smith hailed the lord, who informed them that the cottage was this time inhabited. Making good headway, our lord arrived again at the cottage, and this time the servant offered to introduce them to the master of the house: the younger brother of the legendary smith.
