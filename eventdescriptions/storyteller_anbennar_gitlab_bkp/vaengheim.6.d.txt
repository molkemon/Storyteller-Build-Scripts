It was in the days of today, when our queen ruled in Vængheim. Her reign had been a peaceful one, for few dared to brave the mountains to bring war to their hall. In this, they held a mighty gift, one shared by few but sought by many. Our queen knew this and found it fair.

Yet one day, fate brought a man to them. His hair was wild, and from his mane, a single eye beheld her. With one arm, he braved the cliffs to reach their roost and speak to them. And he said, "O queen, I come to you as a friend and tell you thus. The time of Vængheim is ending, in days and years to come. For the fires that once burned brightly here are now faint. Your kin were once given bold wings, yet here you sit, forgotten. Until none can say where the maidens of Vængheim went, and when your hall fell silent."

Our queen took this ill, for his words stung her pride. She bid her maidens banish him, and they did so. But his words had struck her, and like a wound that would not heal, they ate at her until she called her maidens to her and said, "I would ask a boon of you, who bound your lives to mine. For many years together, we have kept our vigil. And as our charge below has slept, we have dreamed in our turn. Of ages past and glories gone, and blood that now runs cold and slow. And this I cannot bear."

"So come, my sisters, and recall the glories old that brought us here. Take up your spears and mail again, for Vængheim wakes once more. Take wing and spy what lies beneath our hall. For I would bring my law to these, to benefit us all."
