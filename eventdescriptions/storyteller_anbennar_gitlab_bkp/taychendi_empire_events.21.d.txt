Our lord looked across the landscape in disbelief as he wandered aimlessly. The voices around him went in one ear and out the other. Our lord had been at the forefront of the conflict, at the wall of flame that had put the Effelai to flight—and then slain it. For months, our lord had lived in a world of smoke-choked skies and roaring fires, bloodied vines, and mud-soaked steel. The war against the Effelai was fought in darkness—but now, our lord saw its aftermath in the light of day—dimmed, of course. It still snowed ash.

Before the war, the Effelai had been a forest of tall canopies, webs of vine and silk, and exotic plants and flowers, the green of the leaves fighting with red and pink and blue for dominance. Now, the ground was covered in a field of light grey, ash carpeting the soil like snow. And with the towering trees that had once blocked sun and sight gone, replaced with twisted, charred, hulking husks, it was as if the whole of the Effelai was laid out before him. A forest of blackened stumps, each a tombstone to the singing jungle, that went on for miles and miles and miles.

Something caught our lord's eye—he halted, looking down at his feet. A green shoot sprouted through the ash. "The Effelai…" our lord asked, as a chill ran down his spine. "Is it gone? Completely?"

The warlord to his side raised an eyebrow. "That's... what I was telling you about, sir." Our lord just stared at him blankly—the warlord started again. "We believe it has been crippled. Any above-ground presence has been incinerated... but the Jungle's roots run miles underground in some cases. Whatever we find we burn, but..."

Our lord laughed. All this, and it still persisted! They had burned the world, burned half the continent, and it still survived! Our lord shook his head. "No, no—we've had enough burning. If you find any remnants, separate them, stem their growth—but keep them alive. We may have defeated the Effelai..."
