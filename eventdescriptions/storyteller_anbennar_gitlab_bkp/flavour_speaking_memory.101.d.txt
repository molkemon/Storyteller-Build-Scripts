The space around the bonfire was full to bursting, and yet more songsmiths and writers crammed around it. Each held court in a different section of the fire, and so the ancestors’ tales bled together into a sort of reverent cacophony. But at each corner of the fire there were people listening, and ideas for how to spread these tales beyond the bonfire began to bloom in each mind.  There were tales of famine, hardship, prosperity, knowledge gained, and love lost. Each of those tales was a memory, but it was a person too, whose afterlife depended on spreading their memories far and wide. As the bonfire wore down across a long night, tales were set to music, intertwined with one another, and renewed for generations to come.
