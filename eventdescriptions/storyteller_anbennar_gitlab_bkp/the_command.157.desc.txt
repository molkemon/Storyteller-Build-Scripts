The knock of the Shaman-Hunters has become well-known to the Command's human subjects in recent months. Three polite, solid raps on the door, pause for two seconds, five more raps, pause... You needn't fear their presence. After all, the Grand Marshal has ordered only investigation to be carried out. So no one is being arrested this time—but they do have questions, oh so many questions. Questions about your neighbors, questions concerning your activities, questions like "When was the last time you attended a military parade?" and "I see you have a son—is he out late?" Questions that ask if you support the Great Command, the Grand Marshal, and the three Marshals below our ruler. The questioning can take a very long time. It would be rude of you not to invite them in for a meal. After all, to not do so would trouble these fine individuals with the matter of forcing their way in to conduct a search. And you would be surprised at how nice it feels to finally tell someone about the awful annoyances your neighbor puts you up to. You would feel something between nervousness, excitement, and catharsis as you point a finger at your terrible trade-master, who treats you like a slave despite you being a journeyman. Is that possibly a sign of Shaman activity, you ask? The nice, pleasant individuals just smile. Probably not. Oh, but you remember—you have seen him late at night, plastering anti-Command propaganda on city walls! Ah, says a Shaman-Hunter, now that is a different story. Another investigator opens his hand. You place in two crowns. You are pleasantly surprised when, six months later, your master is taken away and the position at his old shop is offered to none other than yourself.
