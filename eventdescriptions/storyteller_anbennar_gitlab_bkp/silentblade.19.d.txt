Ruuk looked out over his new companions. A measly four dozen orks, and barely a champion among them. Meanwhile, our lord had earned himself a whole new clan, drunk on feywater and promises of immortality. It would have to do. He would need to play this smart. "Orcs." Their heads turned towards him, expectantly. "For that is what you are, no? The last orks of a dead clan. You heard what our lord said. The water gave your kin over to the fey. They are your kin no more. They belong to the forest now." His audience was silent, but Ruuk could feel the desperation and anger in the air. They were already his. "Your clan is dead, much as mine. There is no winning." He lifted his ax, and buried it deep in the trunk of the tree to his right. It cut through the wood as if through butter, and the elm fell down after only three strokes. "But you will have vengeance." he screamed. "Upon your old kin. Upon the fey vermin. And upon this *miserable* forest." "Ruuk." his audience erupted. "Ruuk. Elmscourge. Ruuk."
