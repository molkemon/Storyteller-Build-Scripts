"The representative Zhou speaks now:" "Brothers, when we founded this confederation we promised to never centralise our powers, as this would only lead to authoritarian rule and the death of our principles of liberty and equality. Yet we must conclude that the government has as of late started to increase its centralisation of power and that their commitment to our ideals is faltering. We must act now, before it is too late." "The representative Ling speaks now:" "Brothers, I stand here, shocked, shocked as can be. We are accused of a grave crime which we do not commit. We are appalled first of all by the fact that Zhou accuses us of abandoning the principles of our confederation and second of all by the idea that a vassal is trying to intervene in our domestic affairs. Talk about irony as the only one who is at the moment trying to increase their power is Zhou, by demanding intervention in our own affairs. We demand not only that the council dismisses these accusations but that Zhou apologizes to us as well." Whether Zhou is right is a mere matter of perspective, so will we support them or not?
