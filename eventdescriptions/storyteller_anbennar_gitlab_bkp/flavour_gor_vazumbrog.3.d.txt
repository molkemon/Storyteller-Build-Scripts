The resurgence of Gor Vazumbrog is nothing short of extraordinary. Chief among the returnees are the proud dwarves of Verkal Gulan, where generations of Titanium Dwarves found refuge after the fall. For centuries, they dwelled beneath gilded arches, carving out a new life while dreaming of their ancestral halls. Now, they march to Gor Vazumbrog not as exiles, but as heirs reclaiming their legacy.  The dwarves tracing their ancestry to the titanium hold lead the charge, but others, eager to earn that title, follow in their footsteps with equal determination. Among them stride artisans of unparalleled skill, their hammers eager to echo in their true home, and miners with tools honed by decades in the golden depths. Merchants bring finely wrought wares, eager to reforge the trade networks across the Middle Dwarovar.  Their professions are as diverse as their origins. From Ovdal Tungr come hardened sailors, their faces etched by salt and storms. They speak of trade routes and maritime travels. Cannor sends its own: proud dwarves from Rubyhold and Silverforge, alongside those from human cities like Lorentaine and Anbenncost, bringing a blend of tradition and modern ingenuity. Escanni refugees, displaced by the horrors of the Greentide, arrive with grim determination, their resilience a reminder of dwarven endurance. From Rahen, dwarves abandon harimarigoverned cities along the Kharunyana and Dhenbasana rivers, seeking refuge and identity in the shadow of the mountain. Their stories blend sorrow with hope.  Among them strides a runemage from the distant Verkal Ozovar, bringing his knowledge of ancient rites. He speaks of golemancy; an art once lost that our hold used to be known for. Beneath the mountain, stone and will combine anew, and the reborn halls of Gor Vazumbrog resound with clamor once again.
