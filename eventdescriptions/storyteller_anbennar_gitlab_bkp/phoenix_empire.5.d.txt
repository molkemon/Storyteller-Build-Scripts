I stand now, victorious once more, but this time, things seem different. Where before we were bringing the light of Surael to the other peoples of the world, now, we spread it to our own people, those so misguided for so long; the Moon Elves.  As I walk through their cities, and see their temples to false gods, new and old, I cannot help but feel a sadness, deep in my soul. What more could have been done, how much more could have been accomplished, how many lives could have been saved, had they simply not fallen to misguidance? Yet, what is in the past is in the past, and cannot be changed.  As I ponder this, I spot something out of the corner of my eye. An Ibevari child, at a mere 16 years of age, kneels upon the cobbled streets. A great gash has been cut through their side, blood pooling beside them on the pavement. In that moment, I do not see an enemy, or even a misguided ally; all I see is my brother, for we are all children of Surael, crying out in pain.  Clearly, the wound is fatal, in seconds they will be dead.  In that moment, there is but one thing I can do.
