"Captain Dresden Dyer, wayward and alone.  I have been walking across this crater for what feels like days. There are strange buildings phasing in and out as we walk. Bars, theaters, places of revelry. Before I can enter, they vanish in screams of anguish and loss.    Once again the pen asks me to ‘unmask’. I know the answer, the very next line, but I dare not write it. Not when I am this close to the goal.I would spoil the sacrifices. We are but two now, out of our expedition of five. I can see the Pearl in the distance, or rather the concentric circle, three strange curled fingers laid in the ground stretching out, and the pedestal in the middle. It all glows eerily with a pale yellow light, but that's why we can see it. That we know where to go.    I have reached the pedestal. I don't know where my last companion went. The pearl is right in front of me, it’s pulsing, a dark cloud is floating inside of it, but I know it can see me. Even as I write now, it preys on me so that I may give in.   Unmask?  Indeed?  I wear no mask.   "This was the last legible part of Captain Dyer’s log, which was found clutched in the hands of First Deckhand Hildrsdottir, the lone survivor of the Golden Hope. Her account of the event lines up with the captain's log, as he seemingly descended into madness, killing off members of the crew one by one. According to her own testament, Hildrsdottir wrestled a Black Pearl, which was subsequently located in the bowels of the ship locked away, from Dyer before ‘flying out of the crater’ and suffering from severe decompression syndrome as she emerged. Corin knows what happened to Dresden Dyer, but should he still be alive, it is my advice to bring him in for questioning. We’ve sent the rest of the journal alongside, though you’ll see for yourself that it’s mostly gibberish; looks like he wrote over and over his own words.  A shame, really. He had such a promising career. Bastion the Quick, "Field Report to the Colonial Office"
