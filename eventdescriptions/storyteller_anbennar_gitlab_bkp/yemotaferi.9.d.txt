The Shrine to the Mother is the jewel in our crown. The jetblack nails that once pierced her body are now reforged into six thick pillars, holding up the hexagonal structure. Her roots have been tempted through transmutation magic to wrap around the metal, and various pieces of timber have been wrought into organic shapes to fill the gaps in the frame. A thick dome of Dao steel, courtesy of the guardians of Esuvrem, sits atop.  Creating art in a world without colour is a challenge, but our most accomplished Ashhana artists have taken to it with glee. Mixing native and foreign dyes, they've painted the holy site in shades of grey and hues of gray, showing scenes from the swamp's ancient and recent history. The Rombeni archmage of Muhaqaar and the high priest of Cloudfall Tower have collaborated on a web of flickering flames, bathing the shrine in a constant dance of shadow and light.  Inside, there is an altar of pure white marble, hosting a collection of objects contributed by various pilgrims from across the League of Light. Statuettes of joyful crocodilians, carved in the jungles of Taneyas, stand next to scraprelics from the Haraagtsedan league and clay figurines of Amilak. Pilgrims from all corners of the realm stream in and out, the Elder Warden in his grey robes filing in among them.
