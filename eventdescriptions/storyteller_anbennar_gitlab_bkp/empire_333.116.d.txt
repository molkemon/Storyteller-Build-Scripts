The hardest question for the naga to answer regarding the historical 333 empires is not about one of the many very silly stories of past empires. It is about an empire that did not count as one of the 333 empires, despite clearly being an empire. The Karrassian Empire was formed around 500 AA and reached its height in the year 740. At its peak, its control stretched from Kunolo to western Ardimya. None of the reasons given for why Karassk did not count hold up to scrutiny. Technically, the 332nd Empire still existed at that time with Zerat, but there were many previous empires active at once, including the 214th to 219th Empires. Not controlling all of Taneyas does not matter either, as there have been empires that didn’t control even a blade of grass in the traditional lizardfolk homeland, yet they are still considered empires.

The most common reason given is a lack of approval from other lizardfolk due to their heinous acts, most notably when they blew up the ancient Zatsarya in Ashazual. Without recognition from other lizardfolk, they can’t be called an empire. However, this argument falls apart when you consider the tale of the 162nd Empire, which was ruled by a terrible dragon that everyone hated and refused to call emperor.

Those who critique the prophecy say there is a very simple reason why the Karrassian Empire was not recognized as the 333rd Empire: it is because the naga had almost no power within Karassk at the time, and the naga did not want to grant the title of the prophesied final and eternal empire to a nation they did not control.
