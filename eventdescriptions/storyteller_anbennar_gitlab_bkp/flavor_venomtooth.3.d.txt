What is an ork’s purpose? Traditionally, every ork dreamed of fighting and dying in battle to free their god, Dookan. It culminated in the Greentide, where massive armies of orks spilled out of the mountains and destroyed the feeble successors of Castanor. However, the Greentide died with Korgus and left many orks doubting their purpose. Was Dookan really weaker than the human goddess who slew his son? Was it all for naught?  In Venomtooth, a new vision was formed. The Greentide was made possible by the strength and fervor of the orks, but was ultimately held back by orkish weaknesses. Its outlook was fanatical, believing that supernatural entities alone could grant victory. orks fought with little organization as the many clans competed against each other for the most glory. It was ultimately that discord which the humans could exploit leading to the defeats at Rottenstep and Castonath. The Greentide was an honorable undertaking, but it now belonged to history.  There will be no grand coalition of clans. That approach was outdated as shown by the adventurers with their organization and technology. All orks will be unified under one banner: Venomtooth. The clan will succeed where Dookan failed, and become the sole reason the orks fight. All orks shall become soldiers of the clan upon reaching adulthood and slaves will toil at home while their masters are out on campaign. Through these changes, Venomtooth will rise above the others.  As for expansion, the plan is simple. The goblins will fall first, their deaths fuelling the advancement of our realm. Then the adventurers shall be next to fall, supplying us with slaves to wield and knowledge to build up our state. Finally, Bonecarver and Rotcleaver need to be conquered and their people taken as slaves. Instead of wild uncontrolled conquest, Venomtooth will only conquer what it can truly control and this way, in due time, it shall encompass all of Escann.
