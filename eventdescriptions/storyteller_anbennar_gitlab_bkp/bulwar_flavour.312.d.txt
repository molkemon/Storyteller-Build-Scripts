On a promontory overlooking lake Khusadza, the Khankatesh Zatsarya stretches towards the sky since time immemorial. The twin ziggurats of NagaKhatesh on its southern side have accompanied the Zatsarya for many centuries, but with the arrival of the Sun Cult to these lands, they are now dwarfed by the temple ziggurat of Khusyetsazdash, the Heart of the Sun and Stars, or Surunnulib as it is known by the Bulwari. Situated south of the Zatsarya, it delineates a perfectly diamondshaped ceremonial square with the other older structures. Decorated in granite plates covered with mica inclusions the entire temple glitters in the light of the sun, an earthly reflection of the sky, with a golden dome at the top of the ziggurat representing the sun around which the stars of the sky are arranged. Here Surael, or Szurral as the lizardfolk call Him, is venerated in his role as the home star, the Rakhyetsa, giver of all light and life. The Ashhana lizardfolk in particular have become the most fervent adherents of Szurral, seeing His Struggle against the Malevolent Dark reflected in their own inner struggle against their violent impulses. By mastering these impulses and thus strengthening their Ilu; their inner divinity; they strive to strengthen the Order established by Szurral, determined to do their part in the ongoing Struggle against the machinations of the Darkness.
