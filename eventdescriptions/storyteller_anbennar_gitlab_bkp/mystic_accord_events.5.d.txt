Lost in dreams, our lord wandered through a fatally dry desert for weeks, with neither sustenance nor succor. As their dreamself began to finally dessicate from thirst, they collapsed beneath a dry, shadeless tree, its branches clutching like gnarled hands towards their painfully slow death. From the stones before their eyes emerged a scorpion, a small creature with a bulbous tail swaying under its own weight. It crept across the broken earth, crawling onto their hand. There, it paused for a moment, staring with its many implacable eyes, stinger hanging over bare skin.
