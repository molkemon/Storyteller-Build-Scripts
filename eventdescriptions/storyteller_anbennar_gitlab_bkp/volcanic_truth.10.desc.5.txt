We have convened a great synod to determine what shall be done about the matter of faith of the Molten Eternity. We have assembled beneath one of the Twin Gods, the guides of our faith The magmascryers have brought us to the caldera of Muatlaqua, the guide of change, to show us the way forward.  The magmascryers draw forth lava from the great caldera, and within its flows we see a vision. A great volcano erupts in the lava, and from the fires of that volcano emerge more flames. They spread across the molten surface of Muatlaqua, and their fire merges into one. All the world around us burns as a single, undying flame. The flame is entrancing. It is beautiful.  The magmascryers devote more and more of their attention to the single flame. The lava beneath their feet begins to recede. The council cries out, but the magmascryers do not listen to the cries of the faithful. All that matters to the magmascryers is the flame, and the flame is unchanging. The volcano that once roared so proudly is now dormant. The flame is entrancing. It is terrible.  But eventually, the flame itself begins to sputter, and the magmascryers tear their attention away. They stand in the cooling lava with wondering eyes. Their dance is done, but we still know so little of the Molten Eternity. Yet we must decide: is beauty worth stagnation? What have the Twin Gods told us? How shall we vote?
