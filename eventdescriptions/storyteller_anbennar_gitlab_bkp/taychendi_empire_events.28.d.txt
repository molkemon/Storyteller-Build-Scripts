The Sajirada’s jaw dropped as he walked into the entrance hall—his bodyguard retched at the sight of the carnage. Corpses of his soldiers were strewn about, mutilated and ripped apart. Blood pooled across the floor, spattered the walls. It was like some terrible beast had rampaged through, leaving a horrific trail of death and devastation throughout the Sajirada base. They’d done well for themselves since their late patriarch Jalinta had turned them into feared Techno-Warlords, expanding their territory and influence. Now, something had turned all that inside out—hundreds had been killed, and seemingly without any casualties.

Finally, Sajirada and his men reached his throne room. The trail of blood and gore led right up to the golden chair at its head, surrounded by twenty corpses and an armoured figure seated upon it. The armoured elf was seven feet tall, covered head to toe in jagged Precursor-steel plates, with Damestear glowing beneath them. The hulking figure stood up and cracked its knuckles.

The Techno-Warlord ordered his soldiers forward—and then watched in horror as they were torn apart. Gunfire, saber slashes, and magic bolts had no effect. The steel plates, already coated in gore, were painted a deeper red. Finally, only Sajirada was left. The armoured figure advanced, swiped the warlord’s weapon away, and threw him to the floor. It stood over him, then removed its helmet.

The warlord stared in disbelief. Towering over him was the face of Selurana Vustarin—the fifty-something, bookish matriarch of the Vustarin family had torn through an entire army. “That’s impossible! What is this—this power!?”

“I believe,” the Vustarin gloated as she readied the killing blow, “this is the power of Yodhan! And to think, it took a Thekvrystana to finally unlock it—and some Larankarha to test it on!”
