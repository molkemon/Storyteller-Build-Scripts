My favourite memory of you? The Wedding. Not that I knew you then — that was before our friendship after all — but I remember looking on in awe at the beautiful, confident Ceanna. You were the pride of our realm already, but we hadn’t truly seen your grace, the ease with which you glided through the glade of society. Not until then.

Don’t let this go to your head! This is before I knew you were as much a mortal as I — but I digress.

I remember being so thrilled to be in the crowd, a meagre woodself as myself in the clan hall. Yes, yes, I know I’m a mage — it doesn’t change how the little girl inside myself felt. While I was bewildered by my surroundings, there you were. I remember seeing you on the balcony overlooking the Ceremony, Alara and Lanhara sat with you. What a sight that was, so mundane, and yet so powerful. You leaned over to them at one point, whispered something, and they both laughed. In that simple scene, the scars of the Cousins War were smoothed over.
