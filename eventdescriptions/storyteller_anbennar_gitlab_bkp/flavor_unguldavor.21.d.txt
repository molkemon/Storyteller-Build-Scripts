The forests of Sarwood were eerily empty. Where once thousands of clansmen would ambush soldiers in devastating warg charges was now a desolate forest. Only a few orks lived here, mostly refugees from the other revolting clans. Their lord stood by a battlefield and received a report from Kruk. "Three thousand dead, your Highness. That is how many Dogeater warriors were killed in this last battle." He stood over the bodies and asked, "Why so many? Why did they not just stop? They knew I was going to spare the soldiers." Kruk sighed. "That was not doubted. Instead, it was just their way of life. They lived free or died. Those were the only ways. The surviving Dogeaters fled to the Greatwoods. It is somewhere they could roam free without being stopped." He nodded. "I will not stop them. However, with Sarwood empty, that makes it perfect for the Stonecarvers to move in. There is something else on my mind though." He looked back towards the west. "Ever since this war began, I believed that there was a better way to address the clans. Perhaps instead of several autonomous chiefs, we could instead take representatives from the clans to debate in a council to govern the country." He looked at his notes. It would take some time to implement, but perhaps there was an alternative to this disaster. Perhaps the clans could be made into an asset instead of a liability.
