As the weathered stone door was pried open, the excavators were greeted with a cold gust, utterly alien to the scorching heat that they had been whooping and chattering to escape just a few minutes ago. Yet even presented with such respite, many hesitated to venture further down the hatch, decorated only with the ashes of torches dotted regularly along the walls. The silence of the pyramid consumed the archeologists for a while, until one brave gnoll volunteered to light the way for the rest.  As the makeshift expedition ventured deeper into the temple, more and more cat motifs appeared alongside the daubed texts of ancient Kheteratan. Though the language had many odd features and distinctions compared to its modern variant, the archaeologists managed to deduce that this temple indeed treasured the remains of a khet who ventured too far from Kheterata Proper and then, seemingly, killed itself. Other texts surrounded these writings, in an unknown language completely alien to the Kheteratan script. Unable to make head or tail of the text and eager to be done, the party concluded that it was probably nothing too important; likely more biographical detail, as in other similar tombs; and moved on.  Eventually, they reached a large room, oddly simple and humble for the tomb of a khet. Yet there it was, a large sarcophagus undeniably housing something bigger than either a gnoll or human. The party cheered in joy for the boon, though they were immediately overshadowed by a loud hiss, causing the party to loudly yelp and scramble out of the pyramid to report their findings.
