"My first thought was to just nab a wild one, but that plan was dashed immediately. Apparently they're some sort of invasive species, not meant to be here, and get outcompeted by the local wildlife something fierce without the protection of the Shuvuush. Worse, their eggs need the heat of a massive bonfire to hatch, and on the treeless steppes, firewood is more precious than gold, since they don't occur naturally in the cold climate. Well, in Horashesh and the nearby Fangaula, we've got plenty of wood. So Kiusz and I logic made some promises, greased some palms, and sailed back to Bivuzena. When we returned with our cargo full of timber, to help them with their breeding, they were ecstatic. Rewarding us for our efforts, they gifted us several birds, female only, for apparently male birds are a big deal to these folks. We can always come back with more gifts, though. They were welltrained, meaning we could easily set up an exhibit at the Menagerie where we let kids ride 'em, I thought. Apparently our ruler thought that was 'too risky' and 'children would get injured.' Still fuming about that one."; The Autobiography of Fauna Hunter Bastirai Kazuru, Chapter 7.
