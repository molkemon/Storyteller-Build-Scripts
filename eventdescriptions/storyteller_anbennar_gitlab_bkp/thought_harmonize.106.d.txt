We Artificers have always sought to harness the forces of nature for their own means. Our ancestors did it with steam, and later Dames tear: it has brought us nothing but prosperity as we pushed the boundaries of what others thought possible. This will be no different. The Xhazobkult shaman only gave the Thoughtful overseer a toothy grin. "The souls, do you have them?" "Yes, we have acquired that which you need for your ritual. I hope this is not a waste of our time." The Gnoll eyes the overseer with an intense stare. "Ever the arrogant ones, you Thoughtful." Without waiting for a reply, they struck the ground with a torch and sauntered over to a massive brassrimmed furnace, slamming open the hatch and tossing in the lit torch. Excitement coursed through the crowd as the furnace began to roar and piping began to whistle, the crucible nurturing a fire hotter than any pile of sticks. Yowls and chants arose from the onlookers as condemned after condemned were herded towards the flames. The leader remained stone faced throughout the ordeal, even as the line began to shrink; when the last few stood before the fire, he turned with an exasperated sigh. "You need more? I knew this AHH." His complaint was cut short by a searing pain in his chest, shortly thereafter wracking his entire body. The world around him blurred as he lurched towards the fire along with a group of gnolls who seemed to have caught the same ecstatic affliction, and almost of its own volition saw his hand drift into the flames. Sweating now, with tears in his eyes, he felt himself falling unconscious. Then, a low, guttural laugh. and with a flash, the flames turned green. The pressure valves filled with red, the pipe bolts barely holding as the generator began to spark and spin uncontrollably. With a rasping laugh, the overseer only shouted: "More."
