In southern country, dense forests rise where rain turns to mist. The Naamjyut people live among jungle and ravine, traveling by trail and river. Close to nature, they are born poets, gifted in song and word. Their verse honors the Chu-style — enigmatic, resonant, refined — using fragrant herbs for virtue and rare birds for feeling. Rich and vivid, it paints lush scenes and deep sentiment in winding lines, like blossoms after rain or mists at dawn.

In recent years, Naamjyut writing has grown more ornate, rooted in old traditions yet surpassing them in depth and brilliance. Tea holds a place of honor; rainforest teas are prized, and each sip inspires verse. This year, poets have gathered their works, from which one shall be named "Verse of the Year from Naamjyut."
