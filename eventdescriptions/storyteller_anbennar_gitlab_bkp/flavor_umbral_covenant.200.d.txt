Once again, I expand. There is the first rolling in of the fog, and then its presence, a blanket over the realm, an immense silence. It instills quiet, peace. It drinks the harshness of the sun and suffuses my subjects. The fog settles gently, caressing mud-bricks and ankles and tree trunks, and I feel what it feels. One cannot see more than a few feet through the fog. Whisper to oneself, consider treachery, subversion, and the fog drinks even that. You are muffled. You cannot know if your discontent is heard—here. It is more than physical. I am clothing my realm in authority, in queenly garb.
