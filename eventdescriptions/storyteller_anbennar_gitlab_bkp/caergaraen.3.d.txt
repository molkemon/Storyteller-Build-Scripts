Narha downed the rest of the Pheni bottle, then threw it off the side of the ship. The sacred isle of Assakadail—a low mountain rising from the waters of the Taras’su Ervan—loomed in the distance. "Ceremony, ritual, history—I should be out there... fighting! Raiding—liberating! Isn't that what this damned sword wants from me?!"

"Mother." A disappointed voice to her right—Narha glanced at her daughter Kelhana, scoffed, and turned back, even as a purr from her jaguar, lounging on the deck nearby, betrayed her feelings. "This is not just ceremony—it's governance! You're not a pirate anymore; you're a queen! And the threats we face—"

The Waterclaw laughed suddenly. "They'll be vanquished by blood and battle!" She drew Saerasyar from its quenching-sheath, and the flickers of green near her face glowed. "Larankar, Ameion, the Bloodcloaks—we don't beat them sitting on our arses, hiding in our coves. We go out, and we win!"

Kelhana crossed her arms and pouted. "We win? That's it? Why haven't we 'just won' before?"

Narha looked over the blade, the flame-wreathed silver-steel cutting across her daughter. "Because the old kings were weak? Ineffectual? Immoral fools, who rested on their 'freedom' while Gophira burned, and Taychend opened itself up to us?"

"Because we do not think! We charge ahead, unmoored, then isolated, then annihilated. Did Munazhlan believe his paradise would come about in a day? Please, mother, think of what happened to Saerraeg—to Geladhlan the Liberator, who once wielded the sword in your hands! He burned bright, yes—and then he burnt out. Slain, and his empire with it. Is that what you want?"
