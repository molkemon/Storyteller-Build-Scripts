When the Rzentur told our lord that their god was an elf, our monarch had assumed they were speaking in metaphors. When our monarch approached the grand temple at Svemel—easily large enough to host a young dragon, cloistered away in its grandiose halls—our monarch had expected to see scales, horns, fire, and fury. Our monarch had not expected to see... this. In front of our monarch sits a single ruinborn, lounging atop a throne of plush pillows and imposing antlers. With his lithe build and relaxed lean, he towers over our monarch only as much as any human would in just the same position. It is his clawed right hand, tapping on its rest in rhythm, that confirms to our monarch who they are speaking to. Our monarch kneels.

"Thank you, lord Varlengeilt," our monarch says. "Thank you for the opportunity to serve."

The dragon's eyes flicker over our monarch: he surveys the kobold's scales, the faulty attempts at the Rzentur language, the glowing gadgetry belted to every limb. "You do not serve me yet."

Our monarch anxiously bows their head, not able to meet the gaze of a disappointed god. "I am sorry, Varlengeilt, dragon of the Ynn. Please, let us serve you from a world away—"

"No. No, you may not. You may leave."

The kobold skitters off, not needing to be commanded twice. Once our monarch is out of sight, Varlengeilt sighs in relief. He wipes the sweat off his brow, he feels his heart slow in its chest, and he imagines what Kobildzan might do.
