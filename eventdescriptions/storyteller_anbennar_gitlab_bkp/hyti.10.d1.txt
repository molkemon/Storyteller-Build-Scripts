It seemed all the hatchlings of Misarallen were out in the streets, busy running after their new toys, a cart fueled by its little owner's singing… no matter how out of tune. The resulting cacophony was deafening the whole city, Songhouse included. It didn't do anything to smother the joyful atmosphere; on the contrary, the din fueled more jokes and happiness:  "With great power comes great responsibility, and we gave a minute amount of power to the most irresponsible beings on Halann." "We were so preoccupied with whether or not we could, we didn't stop to think if we should."  The festive mood was well deserved: the Song stubbornly resisted many attempts of reproduction because both the sound and the magic needed to be recorded, and recorded together. Otherwise one was left with a sad magic scroll that any race could write,  a song doodled on the side.  In the end, the answer to the riddle was in looking at sound as vibrations. And, you know, adding Dames tear.  Finely cut crystals of Dames tear in a very specific shape, arranged along thin metal threads, resonated to the Song, storing its magical energy. Then, a harpy; and only a harpy; could sing close to it to release its power again.  I always paid too little attention to the Song, focused as I was on Inscription first, and all kinds of magic later; it was just one craft among many.  But those last years of focused study made me fall in love with it; not just the magic, but the music as well. It is incredible. Beautiful math made sound.  Ha. Look at me, finally rediscovering my roots as a decrepit hag… I’ve become a cliche. And I'm gonna force everybody else to humor me in the process – tomorrow I'm gonna sing what I've composed.
