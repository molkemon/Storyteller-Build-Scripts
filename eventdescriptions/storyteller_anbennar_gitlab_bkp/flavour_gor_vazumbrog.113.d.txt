His lungs thoroughly ravaged by miasma, Thrain Stonefist, greatest adventurer of the founding generation of Gor Vazumbrog, nonetheless sought to take one last journey. Before his departure, he left a compass and map he no longer needed to make the trip in a hollow that he and his son, Thrun, had once used to play at being spies. Adventurous but cautious, Thrun brought a full contingent of the Dauvek Veker with him for the journey. Fortunately, his father had trained him well in orienteering, so he arrived at the site with plenty of time to spare.  What he found there filled his heart with both sorrow and wonder. Wisps and motes of blue light floated throughout the cavern, delighting the stricken Thrain, who slumped against a rock.  "It was greedy of me, I know," Thrain wheezed, "to keep it to myself for so long. But I wanted to give it to you as one last challenge, as one last gift. To you, and to all of our people. You're ready to lead now, boy. Keep the compass, you'll need it."  Thrun thought of all the experiences that lead up to this moment; Thrain thought of all the achievements yet to come for his son.  Let the ruler decide what to do with the cavern.
