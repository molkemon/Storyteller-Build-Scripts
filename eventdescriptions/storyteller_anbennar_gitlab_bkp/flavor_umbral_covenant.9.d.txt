Have you ever seen a rolling fog drink the light? It will astonish you just how fast the day is eaten. Leagues and leagues away, across the sea, the ritual mages of the Vile One are chanting. As they incant, they become one great impression of Her power, one enormous being, and all Fahvanosy falls into that being’s shade. The being reaches out across Sarhal and into the mouth of every person it can find, everyone who does not serve some grander role—the great mass of the unexceptional and the expendable.

At market, they wonder if it is getting cold. They exhale an odd grey mist, like on a frosty morning—but there are so few frosty mornings in Fahvanosy, and it is high noon. The kitchen-servant glances at the market stall owner and makes a small noise of confused amazement, because the fog is beautiful in its way, and everyone is breathing it. Nobody is panicking—Isn’t that odd? They all look at each other, gently amused that they aren’t. The fog is replacing the part that can panic.

An elderly woman, the frailest, collapses first. There is a look on her face, a strange contentment—the fog does not cease to flow from her as she dies. It rolls from her mouth, her nostrils, her ears and eyes.

It will seem merciful, afterwards; all will sleep, and nobody will look to the sky. There, plucked from the mouth and carried through the air, innumerable stolen souls will scream.
