Despite its oppressive, monochrome nature, the Shadow Swamp is normally full of noise. The omnipresent buzzing of the bloodfly, the shrill stridulation of the flayer bug, the wheezing gasps of the death vulture; all these and many more prove that the swamp is, though horrible, very much alive. That is no longer the case in the province. The air is no longer full of biting insects, the waters no longer teem with albino fish, and the shadows are absent of any stalking predators. Worse, many of our settlements in the area have gone silent. Our scouts report empty villages with no evidence of violence or natural disaster. The only clue as to what happened is a handful of tracks leading into the depths of the swamp.
