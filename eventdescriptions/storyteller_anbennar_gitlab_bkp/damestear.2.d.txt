The central fact of Dames tear is that there’s never enough to go around, a principle that has reared its head once more. A delegation is wringing their hands outside the chambers of our lord, squabbling in whispered hisses over who will break the bad news. Soon enough, it is known, that the province’s supply of blue gold has passed its peak, as the deposit has been dramatically depleted in size. Worry spreads among the elite of our society, whose prosperity and power is often dependent on Dames tear, that they might soon be at the mercy of foreigners when securing their supply. We can only pray that the deposit can be stretched until an especially bountiful Tearsfall.
