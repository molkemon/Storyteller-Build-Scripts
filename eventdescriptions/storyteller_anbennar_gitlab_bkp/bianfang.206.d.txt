As the ruler of our nation steps off the royal barge, their senses are immediately overwhelmed with the sights, sounds, and smells of this place. It had been many months since they had departed from our capital on a tour of our new territories. Passing under the shadow of Mount Tugahyasa, with thousands of laborers portaging the royal flotilla from one river to another, receiving the finest wares from distant cities, and finally arriving at the mouth of the great river, they take in the grandest city of this region. Walking through the grand bazaars, they see goods from every corner of the world; foreign firearms, mithril plate, fine hardwood, and exotic animals. At the heart of the market stand thousands of the city's most famous wares from as many diverse origins. Nude and shackled to each other, some weep, while others stoically stare at the ruler. Some of the more superstitious commoners tell stories of a dragon spirit liberating the ancient people, who had been enslaved by a race of evil wizards. While this myth has no basis in the historical record, this nation had never adopted the institution of slavery, unlike our neighbours. No matter the degree of lowliness, each person ultimately owned himself, and could expect to share at least a meager portion of the fruits of his labor. The Office of Integration has issued memoranda advocating for the manumission of these poor souls as the first step to securing the populace's obedience. However, representatives of powerful merchants have urged caution before pursuing full abolition, with dreams of profits gleaming in their eyes.
