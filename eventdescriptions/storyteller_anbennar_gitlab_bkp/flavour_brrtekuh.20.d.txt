It started the next day. One of the gnolls who had excavated the temple had been strolling along, trying to get out of the desert heat. Then he doubled over, clutching his stomach. Then he retched that stomach out of his mouth, and died. It carried on in about that manner; gruesome, unexplainable deaths, terrible visions that afflicted those who managed to survive; until most of those who delved into Benukhet's tomb were either driven rabid, or driven dead.  That was somewhat expected, of course; the greater the Khet, the worse the curse. A Khetarch carried terrible ones, usually. An important Khetarch? Don't expect to survive it.  But Benukhet wasn't supposed to be important. He played a pivotal role; through kneeling and cowering; but died extraordinarily young, ruled for such a short time. That was why so many had gone along with the raiding party; why, as Brrtekuh's packlords scoured Kheterata for the tombs of Benukhet's contemporaries, those who had lived through Aakhet's reign of terror, so many continued to. Followed the orders in spite of their strange specificity, looted their trifles, while their betters feasted on Khetflesh.  Of course, only those gnolls knew their underlings' judgement of Benukhet and his kin was direly mistaken. And when Brrtekuh's new elite stood tall above their kin, lying broken in the sand, they cackled and tittered, knowing just how mistaken they were.  The Curse of the Khet grows.
