The issue with the 333 empires shows no signs of subsiding. In fact, the problems are only getting worse. Most lizardfolk only know about a few of the more famous empires. The 1st Empire, which was bestowed the spires by the gods. The 271st Empire, whose advancements frightened even the genies. Less publicized are the empires with more dubious histories, and the entire prophecy risks becoming a laughing stock. 

Take, for example, the 55th Empire. It was simply a single hermit alone in his hut. He declared himself emperor and dared anyone to challenge him. Since nobody spoke up, as he was completely alone, he proclaimed the start of his “empire” and began detailing his exploits as emperor.

The fact that this empire is counted among the 333 is made all the more jarring by the fact that Karassk was never considered an official empire despite ruling the entire eastern Sarhaly coast at one point. The explanation that it couldn’t be declared the 333rd Empire while the 332nd Empire of Zerat still stood doesn’t hold water when you consider that there have been times when multiple empires were active simultaneously, including occasions when six empires were all active at the same time. It is almost as though they were not declared the Final Empire simply because their ruler did not listen to the advice of the naga.

Faith in the 333rd Empire will lie in its actions. Despite being eternal, we do not have eternity to assuage the doubts of the populace. If our reach can encompass all lizardfolk, if our influence stretches across the oceans and beyond even the stars themselves, then stories like that of the hermit will just become curious oddities in our foundational story rather than undermining faith in the prophecy.
