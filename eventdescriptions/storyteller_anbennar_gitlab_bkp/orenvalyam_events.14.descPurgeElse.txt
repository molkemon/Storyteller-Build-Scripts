"And anyway—” one of the Kavalali asked, exasperated. "Why are we not having this discussion on the chamber floor?!" Another groaned. "We will… in due time! This is just—setting the agenda!"

"You say that like it’s a small thing," the other Kavalali said. "You know just as well as I do that these meetings are where most of the real work happens!"

"Well, I—" The most senior among them, High Kavalal of Macriavolo, cut the junior Kavalali off. "May I remind you all… this meeting is officially about the Kavalakuttam’s statement on the departures of the last Dawn Elven exiles. That is all. But yes… our decision will have certain ramifications for future discussion. So let’s come to it honestly. Now… are we going to include the name ‘Surael’ on line five, or not? How much more is there to discuss?! It’s been four hours, for Rasahtal’s sake!"

"Why would we not?!" the other Kavalali said. "Did the Heralds not inspire our knowledge of Nayiru through that name? Yes, they strayed… yes, we came to blows… yes, they were expelled from our realm, as was our right, as was their punishment. But still—we cannot just rewrite history. It is in every version of the tenets: His name is Nayiru, his name is Surael also. So it must be."

"Alright, great, now you’re both just going to restate your arguments. Fantastic. I love this council."

"The entire point of our faith," the other Kavalali reiterated, "is that it is not a single static thing. Our understanding of Nayiru shifts and changes through argument, through renewing our understanding! When we cut through the Exemplars’ blinding self-righteousness, we saw them for what they were… and understood what we were in relation to them. This is their final great lie. Our Nayiru is not their Surael!"

The other priest stood, ready to make a point. The High Kavalal shot him down with a glare. He was done going in circles—and his vote was the tie-breaker anyway.
