The third council is held once more in Meworetiketema, though now the delegates are seated in the great sky temples of the city, gathered around a table illuminated by beams of light. Their rays cut the smoky incense drifting down from the skylights above, describing a hundred sinuous curves of satin grey. The clouds that had covered the city for the last week have been banished through the efforts of the capital's Semayabaka, in readiness for this event. The assembled council is a much smaller one: the high priests of Ibekisedara's grandest temples; the tanikweya in charge of the scrying towers; the generals of the armies and the handlers of the spies. And, of course, the leader of Life Watching Eagle, Kwita Ishimwe, is seated in her traditional place at the side of the Mezhi. Mezhi our ruler holds court at the head of the table, resplendent upon their throne of gold and copper. After a short prayer led by the high priest of Meworetiketema, the council commences. The discussion quickly turns to the north, where the rest of Madrimalak remains divided. Truth be told, any real debate on the matter is perfunctory. All present already agree that it is Ibekisedara's destiny to unite Madrimalak; it is only the details of that future that must be hammered out. Kwita quickly takes the lead in the discussion, using Life Watching Eagle's position between the army and the spies to bridge the factions' differences of opinion. By the time the council adjourns, the sunlight has been replaced by flickering torches, but a plan has been decided. There shall be two campaigns. One into the Yet River valley to secure the heartland, and the other into the Talilibeti lands to cut off the trolls. Neither shall stop until Amilak's domain has been freed from those who would harm His order.
