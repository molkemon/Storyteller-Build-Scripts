The struggle to reach this vault was long and arduous, and somehow moreso the closer the treasure vault came. As the party trod across the characteristic titanium vault doors – split by the purple dragon's horrible claws – time almost seemed to stop completely as they tried to cross the room. Traversing half the distance to the unassuming parchment in a gently cracked glass case was simple enough, but then to do it again took just as long. Then again, just as long. The parchment seemed no closer for reaching toward it.  Until they did. And they realized: no undue time had passed. No distortion had really occurred. It was just a simple piece of paper, with complex – yet surprisingly parsimonious – formulas written all over its single, unending face. No, its regular face. It was just an ordinary scroll.
