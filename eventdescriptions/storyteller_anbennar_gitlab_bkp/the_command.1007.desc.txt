Personal rivalries and factionalism are sadly proliferant within the Great Command. Officers hate each other for taking their spots as leaders or stealing their glorious kills in combat, and this happens not only among lower officers but also in higher officer circles, with horrific results sometimes. Such a personal conflict has now ended in the murder of one of our highly decorated generals. While normally this would be resolved by executing the murderer, this time it is more complicated because the murderer is another highly decorated and ranked general. Ordering an execution would be completely legal and within the rights of the war room, but it could also anger the perpetrator's command. Letting the murderer off the hook would anger the dead general's command. It is up to the war room now to decide what to do.
