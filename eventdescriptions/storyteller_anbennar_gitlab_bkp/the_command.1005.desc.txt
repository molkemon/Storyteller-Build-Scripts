Hobgoblins have a strong sense of honor and care greatly about their prestige and reputation. Having a rival within the army who you can best is often a great way to rise in status, as showing that you are better than your peers can lead to a promotion or infamy. While most rivalries eventually result in both parties trying to outdo each other, sometimes the two sides clash more violently and the rivalry risks ending in open conflict between individuals, clans, or banners, leading to disaster, disorder, and death. To prevent these more heated rivalries from exploding into violence and causing chaos, rivalries are sometimes settled by forced arbitration. This can involve single combat between the ringleaders, a contractual ending of the rivalry through reparations, an oath of settlement, or in rare cases, a marriage between families. Lastly, declaring one party superior and having the inferior party acknowledge defeat can also end the rivalry. 

One such rivalry has been brought to our attention, involving two high-level officers and their esteemed families. Given their importance and the fact that they come from two different commands, the Grand Marshal must settle this rivalry. Their authority may be the only thing that can prevent it from escalating into an internal war.
