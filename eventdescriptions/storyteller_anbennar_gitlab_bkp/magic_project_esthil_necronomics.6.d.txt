For almost a year, steady—if slow—progress was being made to the Pain Wards. The efficiency of the ritual was increased, with the duration of the ward while being powered by an average orc soul being extended from mere days to several weeks. However, when setting up the wards on actual granaries, the predicted issues arose: extending the ward over grain would make it wither within minutes, while trying to simply cover the entrances to granaries was better than nothing, but would sometimes lead to vermin getting in through cracks and other small openings, whereupon they would spoil the grain.

After much contemplation, it was realized that inflicting death upon vermin was not actually necessary to protect the grain: a ward to deter vermin from entering the granaries would be sufficient. Thus, the focus of the project changed from "Death Ward" to "Pain Ward."

The initial, fumbling attempts at creating such Pain Wards showed great promise: the fact that they deterred vermin, rather than killing them, prevents carcasses from accumulating near grain, and the duration of these wards was higher than even the best-performing Death Wards. However, secondary to these improvements is that the grain does not suffer from the ward.

There is still much work to be done in the way of prolonging the duration of a Pain Ward before the soul powering it expires, but the project is now on a much more promising track. To ensure that progress continues smoothly, the leadership at the Sepulcher requests that a plentitude of souls be made available so that theories for variants of wards can be easily put to the test to speed up Pain Ward development.

- Monthly report of the Black Sepulcher, today of this year
