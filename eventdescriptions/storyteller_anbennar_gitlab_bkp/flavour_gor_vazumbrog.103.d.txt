Lorek prayed to Argezenna, to Hehogrim, and even let his thoughts drift briefly to darker places, begging every divine power he could think of to let his armor hold against the snapping jaws, corrosive breath and dissolving gaze of the Drakon whose weight was grinding his body into the cave floor.  When the CRACK he feared finally came, it wasn't his armor that broke, nor even his bones. That crack came from the titanium bit of a dwarven battle axe piercing the beast's skull. The scholar managed to squirm his way out from under the monster's corpse, but couldn't find his way to his feet until his savior reached her hand down to him.  "You're not a surface dwarf any longer," Magda admonished him, "You're Titanium now. What kind of example would it set for you to get melted by some demidragon?."
