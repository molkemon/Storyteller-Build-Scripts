Peasants, artisans, merchants and priests alike massed at the sides of the road leading into Vels Bacar, the main way held open by the two rows of well-equipped soldiers from all across the land. In front of them, the cream of the nobility entered the city, riding their famed antler horses. After them, other nobles followed, bearing their faith with pride. A gathering of such magnitude had not been seen since the proclamation of the Panykorun, which awaited them at the renewed Halba Hariez. They all followed the path down the road, where the imposing procession reached the Hall of Colours. There, our ruler waited, wearing a crown of blue panykorun flowers: the eponymous tree of the land, present in the many gardens around the building. Our ruler took off his crown, and one by one each of the nobles swore upon it the same ancient oath their counterparts once did: to follow the house of Bacar and to join them as the Panykorun, where they would all have a voice and a vote under our lord. At last, our ruler picked up the crown, and swore an oath before wearing it. "I, our ruler, swear upon the land to listen to the Panykorun and govern with it, just as it will rule with me." Our ruler then took a deep breath, and continued ".until preparations are ready for me to leave this crown and join the Panykorun, as this crown needs no lord for the Republic to prosper."
