"Our finding aptly demonstrates the dangers of social isolation and unaddressed emotional disturbance, particularly among summoners given to excessive attachment to or regard for their summoned constructs."; Coroner’s Inquest, Summation  I revisit the Symbol, and I laugh. Not some obscurant code, not a glyph or a sigil, but an emotion. The fury and despair of the Unbegotten are codified in the Symbol, and the precise number of Unbegotten is expressed by the symbol, and so is the truth which leads me up the back staircase of the Abjuration Wing. There the walls are strongest, and my purpose is not to lay waste. Quite the opposite; I am on a mission of mercy. I do not draw a circle, for the purpose of the circle is to bind, and mine is to free.  I reach into the Gloam and pull them out, these Unbegotten things who only once an epoch bring themselves to scratch against the wall of the womb. I pack them tight into every corner of my travelling trunk, every corner of my being, and as I work I sob – not for their forgiveness, but for the whole awful truth of it. These Unbegotten things, souls never born, must know for however brief a time the moment of existence.  When you see them, you will do it too. You will witness the abject misery of their wombbound state, and you will bundle them five at a time into the rocking oarboat of your consciousness. You will give your life to give the poor Unbegotten peace. We conscious things are the only kindness in the universe; there is so little of us.  Ten minutes more, mother, we promise, and then we will return. I abide by the teachings of my younger siblings
