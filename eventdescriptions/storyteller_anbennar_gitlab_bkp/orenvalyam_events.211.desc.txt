The mood was grim. Each High Kavalal sliced their hand, letting their blood drip into a bowl in the center of the table—an oath of silence. Necessary security, considering one of those who might have taken it was currently being cleaned up off the Kavalakuttam chamber floor. "This has gone too far…" the High Kavalal of Katelperai began. "Executing a High Kavalal in cold blood, no matter what he did?" "If he even did it," Ethanan interjected, "which we don't know." Nakar Mlerek: "And Velara?" Kriadvai: "The one source of moderation around the Kamrayakval…" Kilaiek: "Dead—and he blames us for it!" Tirsin scoffed: "To be fair, we are currently scheming against him." Katelperai shook his head: "Not scheming… discussing. As is our right—no matter what the Kamrayakval says. How has it gotten to this…?" "It's the Armour," Sthanan replied, "Yodhan suffered the same—getting madder by the year, corrupted by his draconic-plate." "What sort of mad?" asked Rakkabuttai. "He still seems sharp to me. And the soldiers still love him." Tirsin: "My flock would revolt if he went." Ethanan cut in: "And what of the farmer who tires of war? And the newly liberated, fingered for rebellion and treason, harassed and slaughtered?! They'd revolt if he didn't!" Mlerek gave a gruff nod: "It's him or Dawn. We simply must remove him." But Rakkabuttai shot back: "And you think he will go along with it?! Vote against him, and you will end up like Sadarzhil!" Orenkoraim: "Then we defend ourselves! Ecaris could have the power." Kilaiek: "You're not putting that damned colonizer anywhere near our sacred mission—!" But Vettakaramai broke the news: "You couldn't even if you wanted to. He's gone. Disappeared." 

Silence followed—Katelperai cut through it with a sigh. "Still… we need a weapon. The longer the Kamrayavkal has control of the faith, the more his ways will take root. We tried to raise a hero to lead our faith, and he has spurned us…"
