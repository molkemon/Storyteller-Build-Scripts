When our lord drew Saerasyar, the green-flamed sword, the crowd before him erupted. There were cheers, chants, calls of "Glory!" and "For the ruler!" and "Heir of Ultarlanbeg!" There was truth to that last point – there had to be. The ruler of Zaernmaera stood on the steps of the Askalyn – that famed sanctuary where Munazhlan had hidden from the Slaver-Nobles and rededicated the very sword our lord held in his hands to freedom. That famed sanctuary where Ultarlanbeg had proclaimed the union of all Chendhyans. Where he would do the same.

"Chendhyans!" our lord called, as the crowd quietened for his speech. "From the edge of the blue-sand-sea to the savannas of Haebizyund, from the streets of Darkalyndar to the banks of the Agotham, from Assakadail to Haenbuddar – hear my voice!"

As heat and green light bathed his face, the ruler raised the flaming sword to the sky, slowly and deliberately. He had to be careful, of course – he held the sword, but he would not use it. Even this act, he feared, could set it off. Munazhlan would not approve of his reign – but that elf was long dead. The world had changed. The Chendhyans had changed. They would not sit idly anymore, waging petty raids out of "security" – soon, they would march to the ends of the world. And by the tip of the sword and the jaguar’s roar they would find it.

"We ride to conquest, to victory – to those who follow me, I promise riches, slaves, lands... I promise glory!" The crowd roared in approval, the cry of "Glory" ringing out once more. "And to those stand against us? I promise death! I stand before you the ruler of all Chendhyans! Ride with me or be destroyed!"
