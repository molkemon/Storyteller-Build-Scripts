Umorozi delivers the news with the same toneless and blunt demeanor as always. The Vile One’s arcane council, consisting of the Vile One’s apprentice Cheni and a group of lower-ranking mages, watches intently. "My expeditionary detachments have finished sorting through the naga libraries, both domestic and foreign, to which we now have access. We hoped to find some record there of the substance from which the zatsarya were created, so we might understand how to interact with or otherwise manipulate the pillars. Despite the historical taboo regarding such discussion, even within naga ranks, we have discovered multiple records which refer to the sacred substance used in the pillars’ construction. These records name the substance ‘zatsargakh.’"

There is a long silence, punctuated only by the sound of Cheni quietly chuckling. One of the mages, frowning profusely, speaks up from one corner of the room. "General Umorozi... um, Vile One," she offers a nervous little curtsey in both directions. "Forgive me, but—doesn't that just mean 'zatsarya stone'?"

Cheni’s laughter intensifies. The Vile One waves at him to shut up, but not with much vigor. "Your suggestion, General Umorozi?"

Umorozi shrugs, her braids slipping slightly over one shoulder. "Only one good way to find out how strong a wall is."
