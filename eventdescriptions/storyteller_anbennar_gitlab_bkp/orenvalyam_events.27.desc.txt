The Vairaghul and Camnata nodded at the moving shapes in the darkness of the plantation ahead. The orc Khaara whispered a spell, illuminating their forms. They watched them patrol, whips in hand. "On guard since the recent revolt," Camnata noted. "First comes the fear..."

A chuckle from his Grimshadow partner. "And when Ivranes arrives, the flame." The soon-to-be first orcish member of the Oren Neraghul.

Her mood shifted, turning somber. "It's easy when they're just slavers, plantation overseers. When those we're fighting for have no alternative."

Camnata grimaced. "There are slaves in Noruin. Endralliande." And, he knew, a whole lot more free people, interlopers or otherwise. "And we will break their chains... and the others? What of them?"

"You have doubts?" Khaara shook her head slightly, weary not to disturb their hiding place. "Not of the cause. It's just... a long time ago, my people went on a crusade of our own, to break chains of a different sort. I'm sure it all felt good at first... but the further Dookanson's forces advanced, the more their enemies changed, evolved. After Rottenstep, after Dookansfall... we looked around and realized we didn't know who was on which side, and who ought to be. Arosha or Ghurad, Corin or Davmor... when we move past this frontier, are we going to go through the same thing?"

Camnata thought for a while, watching the slavers ahead. "Almost certainly." He decided. "But I have faith... I know an enemy of Dawn when I see one. I see many ahead of me right now."

A smirk crept up his face. "And if we somehow find ourselves among friends north of the Ruin Sea... I am sure there is a continent's worth of Darkness to be vanquished east and west of it."
