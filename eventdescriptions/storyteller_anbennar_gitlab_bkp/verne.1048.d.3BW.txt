"Is it worth it? For a single pearl?" I rasp, my throat dry as we give up our advantage.  We all might be dead already from my choice.  The huntress tilts her head at me; the paladin merely snorts. The two soldiers start to pull their weapons as I pull out my necklace, a gift given to me by the local adventuring lodge. It startles the Pearlsedgers who all point their bows and warhammer at me.  "What’s your name, huntress?" I ask, my heart pounding.  She takes a long moment before responding. "Eira Gewyn, Huntress of the Auralarn Academy."  Auralarn of all places. Really? She could kill us in half a minute. I try to calm my nerves as I continue my argument as logically as I can present it.  "Then you must have some sense with you. You’re a huntress? Contracted as a mercenary? How hard was it getting here? And all alone? Verne has thousands of soldiers teeming from every crevice of Amadia. Do you think you all can make it out alone, even if you kill us?"  The paladin waves me off, dismissing my words as mere bluff. Eira clears her throat. The two soldiers immediately erupt into argument, jabbering rapidly almost under their breath, until the huntress raises a hand. This is an elite team of warriors, them getting this far is a testament to that. But I start to see the wear and tear of their armor, the bags under their eyes; they are running on glory or the promise of gold.  Eira exhales as she stares down the paladin.  "I’m not dying here.I made a promise." She glances at me for a long moment. Then she stalks off, heading toward the hills.  Without their ranger, the Pearlsedgers will be stranded. After a minute of contained rage, they too depart. They lost the moment we found them.
