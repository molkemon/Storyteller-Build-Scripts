Perhaps it was fate that beheld the work of the old elves and found it hateful, or perhaps they simply thought the ruler unworthy of it, for a mighty storm struck Vængheim's fleet as they crossed the Lament home. Many ships were blown off course, and some were lost to the abyss, maiden and mouse. Yet it was the flagship, upon which Freydis and the ruler resided, that the storm smote most mightily, battering the ship as if the very seas had come alive, and sought to crush them with watery fists. In the end, the ship could take no more, and she was torn in twain by a thunderbolt, spilling much of the cunning of the old elves into the Lament. The ruler, ever brave, gripped Freydis in their claws. Yet vast was Freydis, and the ruler risked much to hold her from her doom. And Freydis said, "Let me go, o queen. For the sea is my bane, and if this be my end, I will not shirk from it. A good life have I lived, for if this be the price of dreams, I pay it gladly. Better this than to have never dreamt at all." Then Freydis broke free from the ruler, and the seas took her. By wing did the ruler make for another ship, and the storm abated. Many scrolls were lost, but not all, for Freydis had split the bounty between the fleet. Bloodied did the fleet return to Sporreyn, and a great feast was held, to honour their return and mourn those lost to Uelos. Mourned was Freydis, but her people used the cunning of the old elves, and in Sporreyn did raise a new tower, joining elf and giantkin. Never were they able to yoke the sky, but many clever ways were found to soften her blows and grow their bounty, and all of Vængheim would profit from their toil.
