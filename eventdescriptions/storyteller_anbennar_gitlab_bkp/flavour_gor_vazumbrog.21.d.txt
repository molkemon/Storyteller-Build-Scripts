To Whomever Now Holds the Mantle of Leadership,

Forgive me if I overstep; I am no scribe, no messenger, only a soldier who cannot stand idle as calamity unfolds. My hands tremble as I pen this, for the news I bear is as heavy as the stone that encases us. The great purple dragon; a horror whose name is whispered in dread; has descended upon our sacred province. Her wrath has left our onceproud province in ruin. The shrines to our ancestors, to heroes whose deeds shaped our very bloodline, have been shattered and defiled. Statues lie broken, their faces unrecognisable. The sacred forges are cold, their flames snuffed out. I do not know to whom this should reach, nor what hope remains. Our warriors are scattered, our halls filled with ash and despair. We need guidance, a rallying cry, something; anything; to cling to. If you receive this, tell us what to do. Tell us how to fight when even the stone beneath us feels fragile.

With what remains of hope, Keldran Oreheart, Footsoldier of the provincial garrison
