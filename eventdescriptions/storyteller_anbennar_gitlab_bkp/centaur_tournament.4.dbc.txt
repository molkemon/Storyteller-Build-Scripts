As our lord marches into the arena, a familiar face greets them: one of their own Ordsikar. Outside the arena, this man is one of our lord's trusted vassals, but today there is only the deadly contest of two warriors. "Better make a good show of it, my Caehn," the Ordsikar says, emphasizing those last two words mockingly. "If I kill you here, I might be elected in your place." Our lord cannot tell whether he's joking or seriously considering the prospect. They laughs nonetheless, projecting confidence, and replies "You won't find it easy." The sun is scorching hot. The match begins. The crowd roars louder than our lord has ever heard before. This is not just a match for Irdaeos, but for the nation: victory proves their fundamental worthiness as a leader. But the Ordisikar is a tremendous opponent, executing a perfect charge that both gives him the advantage and displays his skill to all. Our lord matches them blow for blow, exchanging charges and dodging arrows, sometimes coming so close they swear they can hear his breath over the pounding of hooves. As the Ordisikar's face twists in hideous concentration, our lord wonders if they really is aiming to be Caehn. Their muscles scream in agony from the exertion, but in the end.
