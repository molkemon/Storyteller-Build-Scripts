It is the first step—to identify a word. There is no other metaphor, and it is inadequate. The Wish is a magical layer beneath language, the spoken command merely its vector. The Vile One could not simply dig up the words from some ancient dictionary and repeat them, hoping to invoke a result like some common incantation; the magic is inherently Genie. But she does not need to practice Genie-magic to defeat it. The problem is doing so gently. Genie-magic has been countermanded before, undone and destroyed, but to do so without damaging the information within this Tetrahedron is a colossal task indeed. All depends on having something to give over to Komina. It is like trying to read the letters within a bound scroll without moving the paper… Yet slowly, so slowly, there is progress. It is a form of conceptual magic, of parallel thought, which the Vile One has never truly attempted. In learning to hear the language of Genies, she does not learn to speak as Genies do. What the Vile One learns is more frightening than that.
