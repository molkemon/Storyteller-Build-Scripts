The merfolk of Sarhal are easily recognized by their halfhumanoid, halffish bodies. From the waist down, merfolk possess long fishlike tails that allow for unrivaled swift movement underwater. They also possess gills that give them the ability to live and thrive in subaquatic environments, while also retaining lungs that allow them to breathe air, though their ability to move beyond the sea is severely hampered due to their skin requiring constant rehydration.  Bivuzena’s position on the Horashesh coast offers an unorthodox solution to our overcrowding: the sea. Merfolk civilizations are experts in subaquatic architecture, with a penchant for intricately designed architecture. Unfortunately, the merfolk are quite standoffish, having little regard for outsiders.  Azaly Stonecarver, however, has never let a "Keep Out" sign stop him. The brilliant scholar dedicated a year to study with the Baashidi merfolk experts, and learned the merfolk language of Oshiloka. Taking a breathing apparatus, he then dived down into one of the merfolk kingdoms, and learned of their Dames tear powered pillars and domes, known as Oshikogo, which enhanced the security and stability of merfolk settlements without requiring constant efforts from their mages. He joined forces with a merfolk scientist to propose an inversion of the process, creating a section of Bivuzena that despite being above ground, was entirely underwater, with airlocks allowing passage to the rest of the Nyanza Market. Meanwhile, the traditional Oshikogo were used to develop a new, smaller settlement off the coast of Bivuzena, with the domes intentionally designed to crest the sealevel in such a manner that they could be built upon for the airbreathing inhabitants of the city. Thus, a twotiered district was created, a unique society where land and sea mingle freely through Azaly’s glass and stone.
