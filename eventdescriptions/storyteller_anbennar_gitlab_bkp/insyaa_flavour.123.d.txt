Many came to Insya with a dream of finding‚??or rebuilding‚??the grand cities that fill ancient legends (and even a few ancient records). That dream has not just been hampered by the Holohana and other nightmarish creatures strewn across the continent, but also by the very land itself. Untold thousands of plants, both mundane and far less so, cover every single surface outside of our settlements, not to mention the Titanoflora and their endlessly long and thick roots, a nearly impassable obstacle for our common tools and methods of removal. But, with recent Artificer breakthroughs, said dreams might be one step closer to coming to fruition.

At a glance, these Shapers (or Theremin, as our Artificers have taken to calling them) could be mistaken for strange musical instruments, with the melodic noises they produce while their operators control them by moving their hands back and forth from Antennae attached to the main device. While the actual work is done by rods of precursor metal hammered into the ground, which send the received signals into the ground, causing roots there to shift, if not shrivel up entirely, readying the land for easier development in a couple of hours.
