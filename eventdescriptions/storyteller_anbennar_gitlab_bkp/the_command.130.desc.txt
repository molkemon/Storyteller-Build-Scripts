Poun-agyan, or "lesser love," is a hobgoblin term referring to all forms of romantic affection that cannot produce heirs for households. These include love between a hobgoblin and a non-hobgoblin, love between two hobgoblins of the same gender, and adultery. Traditionally, these "lesser loves" have been discouraged but permitted to exist, especially among second sons and those unlikely to inherit tribal titles and claims. However, as the Command has grown in size and become more formalized, the place of poun-agyan has been thrown into question. The inability of these relationships to produce citizens of the Great Command has seen them become the target of denouncement campaigns, claiming that they undermine hobgoblin households and the very social fabric of the Command.

In particular, the union between a hobgoblin and a human does not result in childlessness but rather in a half-hobgoblin, half-human mule, unable to bear children and participate in a household. These mules, euphemistically referred to as "third daughters," are ostracized from both Command and Raheni society. Following the conquest of Shamakhad, a great number of these third daughters were born to human mothers and hobgoblin fathers—fathers generally fail to claim ownership, and the mother is often shunned by her home village. These third daughters have become a great mass of wandering men and women in our lands, further destabilizing the society of the Great Command and pressing the issue of poun-agyan to great importance. As always, it is left to the Grand Marshal to make the ultimate proclamation.
