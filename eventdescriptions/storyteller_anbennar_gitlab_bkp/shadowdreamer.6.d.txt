"This is maddening. It has been weeks since I have had a restful sleep. It is as though some dark hand is pulling at the lids of my mind’s eye. I have read every word of Zog’s testaments, and I thought I knew what was to come as I took up the mantle. He neglected, I find, to mention how torturous this experience is. Perhaps his waiting for the visions was not so, and it is a burden unique to me. I should hope so, or else this realm will be inevitably governed by those driven to lunacy.  In the meeting with my advisors earlier I made a fool of myself, repeatedly asking the ork in charge of food stockpiles in the capital to repeat himself, and calling him by the wrong name twice. I’ve stumbled over my own feet so many times in the last while that it is a gift from Dookan that I haven’t knocked myself out on a table’s edge. I find myself utterly mesmerized by the flickering of candles, when I should be hearing the oaths of young officers.  To put it simply, this state of affairs is not sustainable. I must commit myself to finding the source of these haunting whispersat the detriment of all elseor everything will slip away from me and the realm will fall to ruin.  I am going to try to sleep for long enough to be able to think clearly, with the aid of a tincture I acquired from a traveling alchemist. Once I wake, I will seek out the deepest, darkest vaults of this damned hold and I will find my vision. If I can’t… It’ll drive me mad, and I’ll try to help the Drahmgall find a replacement who will be more than taunted by the Dream."; Excerpt from the private journals of Zur Shadowdreamer
