What precisely did we imagine, ere our valiant swords struck down the wyvern matriarchs in their desperate fury? A single egg, precariously atop a bundle of sticks, at the very peak of the mountain? Instead we behold a grand cavern, each dank recess crowded with detritus, animal bones, the slick gunge of halfeaten prey.and well over a hundred eggs.  The gnolls know a little of how the wyvern mothers incubate their young, collective experience handed down over generations of hunting the beasts, but even they were not mad enough to try and tame the things, as we once did. No; it is Verne’s fate alone to rebirth this ancient practice, and one we will gladly embrace.
