Periods of lizardfolk history have seen the balance of power move away from Taneyas. During the 230th to the 250th Empire, lizardfolk in Taneyas developed a long-lasting rivalry with lizardfolk living in the Salahad. These Salahad lizardfolk were a very unique type, with mutations suited to living in hot sands. It's clear that these empires existed, and there are even discoveries of lizardfolk skeletons with wide feet, suited to sprinting across hot desert sands, as mentioned in the records.

What has been questioned is the validity of each empire in this period. The biggest uncertainty surrounds the 241st Empire. Based along a long river that ran through the desert, their empire thrived in the fertile plains of the river until it was destroyed by an alliance of genies and giants. This empire seemed foolproof, as there is a large river running through the Salahad where the human empire of Kheterata now stands. Even the city of Kheterat is said to have been built on the remains of a genie palatial complex.

The problems arise when analyzing the history of the Mother's Sorrow river. According to maps of Sarhal found in Aelantir by the Precursor Elves, there is no evidence of the river existing. Could the story of the 241st Empire have been invented by a writer after the river already existed?
