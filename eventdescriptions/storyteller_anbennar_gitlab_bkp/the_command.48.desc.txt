During Grand Marshal Lord Rayavhashapal's visit to the Hall of Endless Debate, he overhears a group of hobgoblins engaged in debate with a group of local philosophers. The moment the Grand Marshal passes by their group, the discussion turns to the nature of the various teachings of godlost philosophy. Lord Rayavhashapal listens for a while to the arguments being levied in favor of one or the other. As is often the case in such situations, the merits of lenient and strict teachings are most hotly debated. While domestic teachings are praised highly, none seem to speak of the teachings of valour, something that cannot stand for one of the Grand Marshal's bodyguards, who speaks up to defend its value. The interjection exposes the Grand Marshal and his entourage's presence, and the gathered debaters quickly bow to show their respects. Lord Rayavhashapal acknowledges their respects and gestures for them to resume their debate. An hour or so later, when the debate has died down and the Grand Marshal is heading back to his tent, the interjecting bodyguard asks, "Grand Marshal, has today's debate swayed your opinion on our teachings? Did it change your favorite?"
