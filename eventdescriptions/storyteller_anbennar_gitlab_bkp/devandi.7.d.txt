I heed the words of the conqueror Vaunsarlanbeg: "To step on the stones of Tsharabmazh is to see oneself in the context of a thousand years. But I am not overwhelmed by past or future. I learn to love the present, for I see my place in the timeless continuity of history." To find one's way to the top of the mountain is a monumental task in itself, and one that rewards the curious. One wrong turn, and I am transported down the mountain to an old burial ground from the post-Munaklean days. I gaze at the graves: all of them carry unfamiliar names, but they must have been eminent, as indicated by their lofty engravings. 

The name of one stands out: Sholanshaz Istaearanas Al’zhamathan. Who was this person? To be Sholanshaz, the leader of the legendary order of Mteibhar Femezkhanz mage-priests, was a significant achievement; how important they must have been in their time! How ambitious they must have been, as was the nature of all Femezkhanz. Yet for all my delving into Mteibhar history, I have no memory of this supposedly significant figure.

A sense of futility comes over me. Despite their exploits, they were forgotten. To be remembered is the secret desire that all great statesmen find in common with each other, after all. How many great things have I done! Yet it seems inevitable now that in a matter of centuries I will lie unremembered, as was the case with this Sholanshaz.

I sit in silence for a while, churning over one overwhelming question: What must I do?
