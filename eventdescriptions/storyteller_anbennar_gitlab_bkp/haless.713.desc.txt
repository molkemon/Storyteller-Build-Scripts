The Celestial Lake lies in the midstream of the Yanhe, a smoky mirror nestled among the hills. Spanning several provinces, it glistens with clear blue waves and scattered fishing boats—one of the most iconic sights in all of Yanshen. Towns along its banks thrive on the lake’s bounty. Fishing booms, and boats carry goods between markets and temples. Each year, as spring fades into early summer, and the lake winds turn mild, locals host the “Ten Days of Floating Theater.” Fishing halts, rafts are moored in the shallows, and commoners and nobles alike gather on the shores to watch plays performed over the water. These plays are drawn from Jiangszun legends, river spirit myths, and tales of the Blue Sashes. Their lyrics are refined, their gestures sharp. They have long transcended entertainment—becoming part of the cultural soul of Yanshen.
