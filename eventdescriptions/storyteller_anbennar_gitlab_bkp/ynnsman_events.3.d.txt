Our integration into the established feudal system has been smooth so far. Only two parts of our contract with them are nonnegotiable: we are to recognize them as the rightful ruler of the region, and to give hospitality to any priest of the sacred river who asks for it. Today, the ruler has informed us one such priest intends to travel through our lands. Any proselytism happening, they claim, would be on the priest’s own volition. Our acceptance is a mere formality that they hope will be swift.
