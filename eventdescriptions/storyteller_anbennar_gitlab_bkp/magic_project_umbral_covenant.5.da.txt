"It was an idea from one of our inner mage advisors, you see—the dissident naga we picked up, Shagtseza." Cheni's eyes twinkle with amusement; the nickname means 'madness-lover'. He points to the table before him, where a complex little tower made of brass and wood stands, covered in deeply etched grooves. In one moment, the entire system seems to resemble the bendbark gutters of a lizardfolk outdoor bath, flowing to the base of the tower—then, in the next, it transforms into a many-branched tree, intersected at odd, harmonious angles with metal. All the many varied channels and rods meet at the bottom, where a set of brass pipes hang from slender threads. "This is a device the naga use to hone precision," he explains. "The device is essentially passive—the naga manipulate magic towards the top of the tower, the receivers, channeling the magic to the bottom with enough skill that the pipes stir and make music." Cheni demonstrates, ringing out a vaguely Raheni sounding melody that only the Vile One recognizes. "One shows their aptitude by playing skilled tunes."

Nobody speaks.

"It's what the zatsarya is doing, you see?" Cheni's eyes are bright; it is a victory the Vile One recognizes, a good little boy who has found the answer. Still, she must insist, "The point, Cheni?"

He nods rapidly. "The zatsarya is passive. It isn't a self-running system that siphons magic from the air. It's receiving the magic from elsewhere."

The Vile One frowns. She is processing. "And so you suggest...?"

Cheni grins. "Do what the naga do—send magic back through it, and see where it goes."
