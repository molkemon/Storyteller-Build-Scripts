O Horutep, High Mage of the Tower.  The transmuters say that magic can only alter the structure of what already is. The conjurers say that magic can only bring forth that which is not. The diviners say that the future can only be told and not changed. The illusionists say that the truth can only be concealed and not changed. The enchanters say that true loyalty cannot be obfuscated. The necromancers say that life cannot be created from nothing. The evokers say that there is no shield that cannot be broken. The abjurers say that there is no power that cannot be contained.  In their eight siloed towers, the mages speak of what can and cannot be done. You know that the only limits of power are those that are placed on oneself, and you are limitless.
