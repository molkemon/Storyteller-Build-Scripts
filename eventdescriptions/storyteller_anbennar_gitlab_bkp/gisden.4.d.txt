Count Benar V himself led the charge against the robber barons, and by the end of the battle all of them had either been put down or forced to flee far beyond Gisden’s borders. The count appeared to have changed for the better, for after the battle his gaze appeared to gain a touch of sharpness, and his back had begun to straighten slightly.  Despite being Count of Gisden, the count has long struggled to fulfill his duties. Whether due to reluctance or other reasons, more often than not he has relied upon his uncle, Lord Ostan, to govern in his stead.  Continuing to do so would certainly be easier than struggling to finally be a proper count. But surely it couldn’t hurt to try?
