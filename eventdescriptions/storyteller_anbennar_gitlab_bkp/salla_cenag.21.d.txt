I lean against the tree and stare at my feet awkwardly, trying my best to fade away from the moment. I shouldn’t be here. This… this isn’t my place to be. Yet, here I was.  This was partially my fault. When I told Lanhara to speak with her father, she insisted that I come too. Against those pleading eyes, I knew that was a battle I was never going to win. I suppose even if I could have, I would have still gone. Facing your family and crossing the rifts that divided you was hard enough. Doing it alone? I shake my head. And so I found myself at the grave of the elf I once called oathgrandmother.  My role had been a simple one, to act as a branch for the two to finally talk. Not in fits and spurts, but something more, deeper. After that? Well, there was a reason I stood where I did.  That did stop me from stealing a furtive glance toward Elaria and Lanhara. Father and daughter stood side by side in quiet remembrance. If there were any words passed between the silence, then they were thankfully lost to me. This was their moment, not mine.  And when that moment finished, they turned together and approached me. I gave my best reassuring smile. Hara wiped her eyes and returned the same. As for Elaria? For the first time, I saw the elf I once knew.
