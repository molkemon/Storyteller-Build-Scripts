It’s a slow journey. The Uthojbrog was hardly built for speed, and it soon attracts a following of smaller vessels, no doubt coming to watch the spectacle. News must travel fast, for we are soon welcoming various diplomats and foreign notables aboard, their ships cruising lazily in the Uthojbrogs wake as it cleaves a path through the Diven’s calm waters. None want to miss this, and we are more than happy to let them witness what it is capable of when truly put to the test.
Hours pass like days, the anticipation making every minute feel like it drags on for an eternity. The lord stands at the vessel’s mighty prow, shielding their eyes against the glare of the setting sun. Though they’d never admit it, they never actually thought this moment would come. The director of the Strongbellow Society joins them by the prow, nodding respectfully to the lord as he runs a hand down a polished iron railing.
Magnificent, isn’t she? I have to give you my thanks, lord, this isn’t a task we could have accomplished without your support.
