Bricena could feel the great hawk's muscles tense through its feathers as she climbed onto its back. For a moment, she sensed the hawk's thoughts, feeling it adjust its stance to accommodate her weight. Then, with a powerful flap of its wings, they lifted off, soaring high above the emerald expanse of the Deepwoods. Suddenly, the hawk dived. Panicked, Bricena dug her fingers into the hawk's feathers.

They approached the canopy at breakneck speed, but just before colliding with the upper branches, the hawk adjusted its direction. The wind whipped through her hair and screamed in her ears as the treetops blurred beneath them. It was terrifying, yet exhilarating.

As the flight continued and her nerves and excitement subsided, Bricena regained her composure. Below her, she saw figures in the clearings, tiny like ants. At this speed and height, she would be well out of their arrowshot range, but if she had her bow, they would be at her mercy. With this speed and height, Salla Myna could be unstoppable. She tugged on the hawk's shoulder and wheeled back toward Ciranmyna. In the distance, she saw dozens of great hawks take flight from the slopes of Ciranmyna, each carrying a figure on its back.
