Our lord squints against the wind. "We thought they might've been roads, until we drew them out. The centaurs see them as a method of communication, that much is obvious. Between themselves and whatever their god is." Their lord says nothing. Our advisor tries again. "We don't have to understand the faith itself; it won't last long, anyway; just the language. It already reacts to felflame, and we speak felflame well." "We can't learn to speak from fire," their lord scoffs. Our lord shakes their head, half-smiling. "Then what's the point of a pyre? Those flames mean reverence; not ruin." Their fellow leader bares their fang. "A flame that doesn't kill is cold." They pause. Our lord breaks the silence. "There's a moment, before the fire catches. The hush, the tension; that's the sacred part. Not the burning, necessarily, but the change. The becoming." Their lord barks out a laugh. "The easy way out. Change happens whether you want it or not. Something that endures is greater. A flame that doesn't die, even against a howling wind." "The old rituals, then?" Our lord raises a brow. "You'd have the whole Pack learn the same words? Even the ones who; let's face it; still dream of their false gods?" "They'll chant what we tell them to, until they realise the truth. Until they forget anything else." "That isn't faith." Their lord grins, shrugging. "The outcome's the same." Our lord doesn't answer, but their eyes return to the copied glyphs; oddly elegant, for something carved by hooves. "These are messages for the sky, that much is obvious. What are we going to say?" Their tail lashes. "That he's coming." Our lord smiles, part admiring, part worried.
