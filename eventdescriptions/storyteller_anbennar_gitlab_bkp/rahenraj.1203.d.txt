To the consternation of the Lotus Court's lessintelligent members, the Raj's strongest soldier is not from Rahen. He can defeat a towering Harimari with spear alone, he can take on three of the vizier's best with his bare hands, there is no part of the Harimraj he has not faced dire odds and beaten them back. Yet he takes a strange delight in scarcely knowing the areas in which he wins such great victories.  When he asked why he came to these lands he does not know, why he has forged a reputation laden with honors not his own, his answer is never the same twice. Sometimes he mutters about the work a comrade started, which he had to finish. He'll tell a story about goblins with wings (or was it harpies below the mountains?) and little else. Sometimes he rolls his eyes, and for a moment you too can see silkbedecked palaces as the pit of quicksand they are in truth.  Sometimes; nursing a beer and wrapping wounds after a long battle; he is a little more honest. Perhaps he'll mention the aged scholar he lured back out of retirement with dogged persistance. Or he'll talk about the vizier's chief assistant's latest feat of brilliance, and never mention the help he gave said protege along the way. Or he'll simply smile, and roll his eyes, and buy another round.  Many would pay the price of a full Kharunayanabarge for lessons from him in sword or spear or strategy. He declines all comers. But he would teach this: the land is just the land. The work is important, but there is important work everywhere. The people though; the people are special. Find them, and you've found everything you need.
