In the days of today, the Dragon Coast fell under Vængheim's rule, and her shadow was gentle. The people of this rugged land were gnomes and dragons, both small kin of old, standing as tall as gnomes and holding their ancestry dear. Yet with Vængheim came her skalds, who shared tales of old. Gnomish minds are sharp and cold, finding no heart for song. But dragonkin took ear to these, finding them fair and soft, for many in their number held a deep love for tale and song, to lull their brood to sleep.

It was then that a dragonkin named Ikse Smallbristle, who named themselves a dragonpriest, asked a skald of Elkaesal. She said, "Your tale is soft and your song gentle, and odd I find your name. For I have heard this lullaby, and to my shame, it puts my little ones to sleep. That seems ill to do, for Elkaesal should wake once more, so we may know her songs too."

And the skald, whose name was Frey, thought little before speaking. "You speak the truth, and better this that Elkaesal sleeps. For singing Alos' Lullaby should keep her well asleep. For cruel are her songs, and she would bring us death. And send us winter without end, to choke life from our breast. So fear not for your little ones, for songs as these give gentle dreams. And unlike the great white beast of old, they will find their eyes come morning."

"Right?"

And Frey looked askance at Ikse, who stood as if struck dumb. Silently she turned and left, in haste unseen.
