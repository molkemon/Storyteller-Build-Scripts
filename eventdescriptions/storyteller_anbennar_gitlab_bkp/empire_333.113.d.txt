The stories of the empires of old can stretch the suspension of disbelief. Generally, however, the naga and scholars of the 333 empires can brush these off. You don’t know what kind of fantastical beings existed 50,000 years ago. Could those empires of the past have believed in what the Precursor Elves accomplished, for instance? Their reign wasn’t that long ago in the grand timeline of lizardfolk history, after all. Yet we have ample historical evidence of their existence.

Some stories, though, clearly go too far for anyone to believe. Like that of the 29th Empire, whose emperor grew too powerful and too greedy after striking a deal with a demon. He devoured his foes, devoured the gods, and even devoured the demon he made a pact with, before finally devouring the sun itself. This is obviously nonsensical. The sun is still there after all, and the idea that someone on Halann could be capable of eating the sun is absurd on its very face.
