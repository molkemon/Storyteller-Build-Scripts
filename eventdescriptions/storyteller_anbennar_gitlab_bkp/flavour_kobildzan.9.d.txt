From atop a drake grown to five times its usual size—muscles bulging, veins glowing damestear-blue beneath the skin—our lord leads the parade through Zurzumexia. Sitting on one side of the lord is the head of the Gommo; on the other, the ex-clanboss of Mestikardu. Each have been adorned with the paints and robes of the kobolds, and they sit patiently, their cooperation ensured by the crowds.

"To the people of Haraf!" our lord announces to the assembled throngs of kobolds, gnomes, and goblins. "We come here today in celebration, not in victory. I have not arrived to demand subjugation or prostration. Instead, I have arrived to announce one thing, and one thing only: you were right! And Kobildzan was wrong!

We were close-minded in the Dragon Coast, so hostile to the Thought and its adherents. We sought ascension for kobolds, but nothing for the gnomes, nor humans, nor anyone else; and we did not even think of the goblins. But while we sat on our laurels, you—explorers, pioneers, and adventurers alike—sought a better life. You used the Thought to help each other, and to create inventions like the world had never seen. And in Zurzumexia... here, in Zurzumexia, it was incredible. It was not only kobolds who ascended through the flesh; it was gnomes, too! Goblins! Ruinborn! All were made one in the image of the dragons, and yet we called it heresy. How very wrong we were!

People of Haraf, I come to say that Zurzumex was a prophet of the dragons, and we thank him for what he has done. We thank you all for what you have done. Together, in the realm of Kobildzan—in the home of all artificery—united, we shall make everyone into dragons!"
