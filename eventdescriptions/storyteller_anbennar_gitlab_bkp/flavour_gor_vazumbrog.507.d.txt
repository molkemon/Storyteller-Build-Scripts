The journey to the subterranean vault had involved an unusual plenitude of humanoid foes: goblins and orks, somehow slipped through the walls and into rightful Titanium stoneways. Thoughts of those battles, and how they could've been fought better, echoed through Balga's head as she reached the distinctive doorway: no doubt about it, this was another shattered treasure vault of old Gor Vazumbrog.  The highlight of the room was a stone pedestal on which sat a book which somehow, impossibly, displayed the very fights in which her dwarven companions had just lost their lives. As she watched, the pages shifted, offering alternatives to the approach they'd taken, promising a greater victory at a lesser cost at every turn. The strategies it offered were certainly far better than… whatever she had led the troops to do.
