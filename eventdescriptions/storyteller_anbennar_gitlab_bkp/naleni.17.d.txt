In the south of the Sendimot crater, where once was covered with half buried chunks of Dames tear, now stands a great palace. Its white marble walls, lined with sheets of emerald, glisten under the clear skies. Out of it rises a grand spire, one so vast as if it were made to pierce the heavens. Harpies circle it constantly, messengers, soldiers, Sirens, all wearing the blue and orange of Naleni. On the ground beneath humans mill, Mengi trading without fear in the once cursed crater. From atop the spire juts a backless throne, carved of a block of onyx, carried up by wing and will. Upon it the Empress sits, seemingly content in their dominion. As far as the eye can see is Madriamilak. As far as the eye can see is Riva Naleni. Below the Sirens sing, hymns to Amilak filling the air as the humans join in, celebrating the unity of Madriamilak, the restoration of the holy realm, and the great Mezhi'Mezhi who achieved it all. All is well, and yet the Empress feels unease. The people proclaim her Amilak's steward, a role that only a dragon has held before, a position she took with glee; a useful tool to legitimise her rule. Yet now there is doubt. Is it right to claim to serve a God one is not sure they believe in? Does she rule for herself, or Amilak? Did Zenidir have these doubts too? Or did he believe, as those below do. She looks skyward, as if Amilak might provide her an answer himself, but alas she sees naught but the endless blue.
