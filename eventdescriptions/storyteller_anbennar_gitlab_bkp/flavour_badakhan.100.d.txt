It is a strange thing, when the heavens bear gifts for Halann. Like a comet she descends, a trail of light in her passage as it falls with the stars. An angel, of outer planes borne, come to walk amongst the orks of this new nation. So small in stature, so serene, as she lands in a quiet forest clearing. A soft wind blowing through the grass as her feet greet the ground, as if these inanimate things show fealty to her grace. Amriel walks, her decision to fully reveal herself one long in the making. She is the closest thing to a rebel amongst the celestial hosts, a diplomat and trickster. She had long observed the progress of the world and always taken such an interest in the potential of the orks; engineered as they had been, and yet so much more than what their loathsome creator had intended. This new nation and the process by which it came to be had fully roused her, piqued her interest where she'd already had a soft spot. As she arrived in the capital city, passing through the outer walls, she slowly let her power become apparent. Let her aura radiate more and more—she was not one who could slay tyrants or dragons, those who had plagued this land many times before, but hers was still a power that would help these people overcome differences. To chart a new path forward in this age with the promise of this nation; to lift them up further, to help them where before they had been scorned. Her progress had drawn attention, but the nature of her power meant that she was safe from mobbing as she made her way to the ruler's palace. As she approached, the guards moved aside—it seemed she was invited, and an audience awaited. She wished to introduce herself, and to begin offering her judicious advice in the affairs of state, and of a people's soul. As she entered the room of council and counsel, she merely smiled at the gathered leaders of Badakhan. She smiled widely and bowed in greeting. There was much to be done with the best laid plans of orks and angels.
