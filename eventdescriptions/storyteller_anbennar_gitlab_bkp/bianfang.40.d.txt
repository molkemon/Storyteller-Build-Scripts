Word has reached the ruler: a clique of palace eunuchs has attempted to assassinate their court rival, the diplomat. Aiming to elevate one of their own to the office of diplomat, they planned to trap them in a fishing net and stab them to death with ceremonial tridents as they were bathing. However, one of them slipped in some spilled bathwater, dropping his trident. The diplomat quickly seized it, fending off the remaining assailants and raising the alarm. Promptly seized by the palace guard, the clique beseeched the ruler to spare them. They claimed the diplomat had been steadily spreading their influence in the diplomatic corps, allowing the interests of foreign powers to sway the ruler’s policies toward intolerable tyranny. They were compelled to remove the diplomat for the good of all. Their selfrighteous pleading was cut short only by the blade of the Palace Marshal, methodically decapitating them one by one. The ruler now faces the choice of what to do with the rest of the sorry lot. Proclaiming ignorance of the plot, the remaining eunuchs would surely be loyal if spared, and unable to amass the influence which had allowed the clique to even consider such a brazen scheme. However, the attempted assassination of a trusted advisor is high treason, and must be treated accordingly. Justice must be done, and be seen to be done.
