The songs of our ancestors’ hunts have been handed down to us in an unbroken chain by generation upon generation of harpy chroniclers, whose labor has ensured that they have persevered on in a largely unchanged form since their occurrence. We sing the songs of our ancestors to ensure that the generations after us learn what it is to hunt, so that the hunts of the future might be as glorious as the hunts of the past.  However, our hunts have changed in recent years. As we attempt to gain better relations with our neighbors, some among the flock have raised the point that many of these tales are rather grisly and violent, and that if the purpose of the old tales is to shape new hunts, perhaps we would be better off making some light changes to their characters’ motivations and actions.  This is not a step to be taken lightly, but if we decide to move forward with it we could set the stage for more peaceful relations with our neighbors permanently.
