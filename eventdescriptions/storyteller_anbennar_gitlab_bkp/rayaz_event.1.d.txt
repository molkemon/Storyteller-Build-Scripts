I stand upon a hill looking upon my warband. Thousands of warriors, from six tribes; Arazin, Yushyn, Lata, Alashkatsa, Karashan, Rayaz; stare directly at me, the weight of their gaze piercing through my body like a million spears. They wait to hear the reason that brought them all into the banks of the Adzalas. Looking at me with an awe that should be reserved only for the gods. For them, I alone need to be the perfect one, as I am the bringer of the final empire. Or so they say.  For I was supposed to bring it 21 years ago. When the Naga expected nothing, but a crushing victory against their banishers in Zerat. Everything was supposed to go exactly as it went in the games I played with my brothers. I was a prodigy, they said. In their illusion, I saw nothing to worry about. But when I enforced it into reality, I met only a stalemate. A relentless and merciless invasion that stained Lake Lasadza red, villages and towns plundered to sustain our armies, five grueling years of war. And at its end, a peace that satisfied no one, as both sides became exhausted to the point where neither could push their advantage.  As a result, the reputation of Sykar Riverclaw was contested for the first time. My Naga advisors, they tried to keep up the facade. I think it still works to some degree, but even a blind man could see that my shimmering scales are tarnished. The vision that I was a symbol of; an idealized, perfect future; no longer seemed so inevitable. People started to have doubts. Rumors started to spread. Naga try to tell me that it's the enemy trying to undermine them. But what kind of enemy are we actually talking about?  The murmurs of the impatient tribesmen rouse me from my spiraling doubts. This is exactly the thing that I need to prevent. So I wake at last, and outline to them a plan for the following five years. Its last point; finish the wailing corpse of the 332nd, and silence those who dare contest my claim of the Final Empire.
