The hinuitermites of the savanna are known to build large mounds, often stretching hundreds of meters upward, with tens of thousands of termites inhabiting the mounds’ labyrinthian interiors. With the recent culling of a large portion of the region's local anteater population, however, the local termite population has taken the opportunity to multiply exponentially. The boom in termite population has seen the creation of a termite mound of neverbeforeseen proportions, cresting the hinuigrasses and standing multiple times taller than the average mound. Many termites from this mound have taken to raiding local villages in search of resources to bring to their megapolis, dismantling entire buildings overnight for their wood.
