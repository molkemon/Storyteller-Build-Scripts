For generations, the people of Jiangszun have lived along the Yanhe. This mighty river, running through the heart of Yanshen, nourishes not only fertile fields but also bustling towns and constant flows of merchants. "Those who gain the water thrive; those who lose it fall into ruin"—so goes the saying of Jiangszun's ancestors. But the bounty of the Yanhe has never been without risk. Flooding from heavy rains, siltation from drought, and the decay of neglected tributaries all threaten navigation and irrigation. Every generation must rise to the task of maintaining the river's health and flow.

In recent years, the section near Jiangszun Province has again shown signs of trouble. Be it by flooding or blockage, boats struggle to pass and irrigation fails. Local officials and town representatives jointly petition for funding to repair levees, dredge channels, and restore locks. Some merchant guilds have offered to assist financially.

How shall we respond to this appeal concerning the lifeblood of the Yanhe?
