After decades of waiting, the stars have aligned for another Yellow Light Festival. Dating back to the time of the Oldwoods, before even the old Forest Goblin kingdom rose and fell, its origins have long since been lost to time. Regardless, the sky is clear there's not going to be a night like tonight for a long time.  While every tribe, and even each generation of goblins, has come to celebrate slightly differently, the general practice of the night remains the same. The goblins of the clan gather in their villages, hanging up firefly lanterns and sharing the stories of their parents down to the next generation. Most goblins only ever attend one festival in their lifetimes, and they end up an occasion like no other.  Tonight, the forest will glow under a sea of glowing lights above, and yellow eyes below.
