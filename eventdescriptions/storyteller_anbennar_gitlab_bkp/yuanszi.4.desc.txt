A wretched bastard, traitor, slithering snake. A hundred of names we were called when the Wulin received our formal request for a duel for the rulerhip over the Xiaken. They screamed foul play, subterfuge and broken promises. We were in their eyes not just unworthy, we were the lowest of the low, lower even than the Command or the Jiangshi. Yet they knew that refusal is impossible, we are a legitimate Xiaken after all. So with a head raised the Wulin accepted, preparing for a duel at dawn, when the dew had only just settled on the ground. Their lord smiled when they approached the duel grounds. Chatting with their retinue while the leaders of the other Xiaken stood around silently, looking at us with disgust and fear. And so the duel begins. A hit. The Wulin punched our ruler at the shins, causing them to almost lose balance. The next punch they blocked, and the next, and the one after. They defended against the swift and emotionally loaded attacks the Wulin threw. Our lord waits until the best moment to strike back, in the meanwhile dodging and blocking the attacks the Wulin throws with all of their might, they want to end it here today, both their ruler and Yuanszi itself.
