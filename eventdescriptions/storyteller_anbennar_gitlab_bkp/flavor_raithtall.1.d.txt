The caravan stops outside the gate, right at the very edge of the land. Each wagon is carrying a large wooden chest. From the other side of the gate emerges a second entourage, this one made up of fey creatures in both elven and nonelven forms. The parties have arrived at just the time agreed by the ambassadors. 'You know why we are here, our lord declares, we wish to appeal for the ruler to be made Protector of the Autumn Ward.' 'We told you that the greed and impropriety of such a demand was unbefitting of your exalted position', replies the speaker of the fey, a tall and dark figure with grand tree branches rising like antlers from its head. 'Yet you claimed you would be able to show us proof why this was the wisest course of action.' At our lord's signal, the first and largest of the chests is brought forth. 'These', says our lord, opening the chest, 'were all found in the palace of the ruler of the land.' Wood, dyes and fruits taken from local plants. Meats, horns and furs from local animals. The entire chest is full of sacred goods, blasphemously poached directly from the land. One by one, each chest is brought forward and opened. There is a chest for almost every noble family. As they go through the chests, the blasphemies within become more obscene. An alseid gags in revulsion at a chest containing alseid pelts. A treant shakes in fury at a chest containing treant wood furniture. Our lord finds themself shaking as well. So many trees burned, so many innocents killed, all for their "balance", and this is what they truly thought of the fey? When all the chests have been opened, everyone is silent. The large antlered fey has remained expressionless for the entire time. 'We accept your request.', the fey finally speaks. 'You will be named the new Protectors of the Autumn Ward, and the nation, titles and very name will be forever forbidden by decree of the Fey Lords. We demand only one thing in return.'
