The foremost protector of our government and the values of the Revolution is our army. Unlike the representatives of the old order, our ranks are filled by ordinary citizens fuelled by their burning patriotism and liberal convictions. A life in the army can also bring the most disparate people together and create strong bonds between the soldiers and their officers. The popularity of some of our generals has now reached somewhat frightening levels and there are fears in the National Assembly that this will spread to the general populace due to our wide recruitment policies. We could try curtailing some of the more obviously dangerous men by giving them assignments away but this would likely strengthen their supporters in their conviction of our own incompetence, at least in the short run.
