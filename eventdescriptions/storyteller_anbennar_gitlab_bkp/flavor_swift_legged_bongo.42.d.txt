"Our lord." The Ingo in question's eyes shot open. They had been meditating for almost an hour, and had been so intently listening to the silence that they almost had a heart attack at the sudden voice. "UUwimbabazi?" they asked. "The very same." The voice emanated from the statue. "I have seen you for many days. You flounder in the dark as a blind ruler, hoping I will take notice of you." "But why have you waited this long?" our lord asked. "You must know how we have been struggling in your absence? Why did you not speak to me earlier?" "I did not find you worthy," the statue said. "I needed to be absolutely sure of your faith in me. The people around you poison your ears with lies they say that my essence is not contained in this form, and that you are a fool who wastes their time chasing ghosts." The statue's eyes flashed to life, and the light from the sun seemed suddenly dimmer. "But you, our lord, are different. Even in the face of all evidence to the contrary, you chose to believe. That is most commendable, and it is for that reason that I have found you worthy. You need not flounder any longer."
