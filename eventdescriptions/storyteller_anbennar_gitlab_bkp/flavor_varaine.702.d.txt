Over our many years exploring Halann's length and breadth to further our alchemy, we have returned time and again trailing fellow scholars who learned to call a nation home. Yet of all these myriad peoples turned neighbors, none are stranger than the merfolk. After assisting with our Waterbreathing Potion project, scores of curious sea scions professed a desire to fulfill their practice of Ruaa (a precept for journeying and discovery) by living with and learning from us in the fabled Dames head. Sadly during our cooperation, we learned that merfolk struggle with enantiostasis, relying on their ingenious rods to curate and extend habitable water ranges. A merfolk might survive in the Dames head for a few days, but a week? A month? A year? Without a way to protect their delicate aquatic forms, they would steadily degrade and perish, like a Ynnic antler horse displaced to the scorching Salahad. Even as we lament this reality, a contingent of daring artificers, looking to further their field's renown, have proposed the impossible: an underwater city of glass, magic, and artificery replicating the merfolk's own rods to house merfolk visitors far from their ideal seas. The construction's scope is staggering to even fathom, yet as our inventors exchange blueprints and reports, this pipedream actually begins to seem possible.
