Connecting the harbour of Dakmender to the upper city near the riverside is the Temple of the Blessed Gate. A broad avenue runs through two monumental arched gates decorated with glazed bricks coloured in blue and gold that face each other across a large plaza surrounded on three sides by scriptoria, dormitories, and ancillary temple buildings. On the fourth side the temple ziggurat of EduzBaghalwa, the Temple of the Blessed Gate, rises up, and beyond it the temple garden terraces descend to the banks of the Keygas. The entire complex, called Mekrebdarek by the local Ofehibi, has quickly become a cherished institution in the metropolis at the mouth of the Keygas after the introduction of the Sun Cult to these lands. Whether dispensing alms to the poor, providing solace to the faithful, or sheltering travelling pilgrims, the temple is one of the pillars of the city, and during the high religious festivals the processional avenue is crowded by Dakmenderi citizens and pilgrims from all across the Ofehibi coast and the lower Keygas basin. Aside from catering to the spiritual needs of the Dakmenderi, one of the main functions of the temple and its associated hostelries is the accommodation of pilgrims hailing from all across eastern Sarhal on their way to the holy sites of Bulwar. As for many amongst their number, the city is the last stop on their journey before they sail across the Gulf of Rahen towards Surael’s Garden.
