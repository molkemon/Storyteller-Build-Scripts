The delegation arrived at the capital and assumed it would be akin to any diplomatic affair with another state: the exchange of pleasantries, perhaps some boring meetings, a ball or two if one was fortunate. They were wrong; deeply wrong. Instead, a select few saw themselves blindfolded, led by a small group of individuals to a chamber ensconced in the Rectorates bowels. When they opened their eyes, they were nearblinded by the bright and iridescent light swirling in front of them, shades of green and blue and colours that seemed to lay beyond what a human can see. Within the light, they could see little but the edges of a multifaceted geometric shape, and yet they sensed the power and weight of the object in front of them. Undoubtedly, they were bearing witness to something truly divine. And then it spoke. The last remnant of an ancient power; the only entity to have ever resided in this land; reverberated in the room and in their ears and in their souls. What it said (as translated by the High Rector and those who led them, of course) our representatives are not willing to share, even after pressures; subtle and notsosubtle; from the lord. But we can be certain that whatever the revelation was, it was a weighty one, for they have one and all converted to a new faith, swearing that God is One and this fragment is the last remaining piece of that One God. Whispers of their words and this newfound faith have spread around the court like a wildfire, and undoubtedly percolating beyond as well. It is an honour, of course, that men of our nation could witness such an event. But it might be a threat as well.
