Late one evening, on a wide bend in the river, shortly after the last Great Temple in the region had been inundated, a curious thing happened. A great elephant stood at the water's edge. A great white catfish, its head poking out of water, was looking up at it. Around them both  gathered a mass of people of all walks of life, crowding both banks of the river. This was the first time the avatars of two of the Great Spirits had been seen in one place. There were no words exchanged, at least not that the people could hear.  Kharunyana swam in a lazy circle, while Dhenbasana looked on, snorting and gouging the wet earth with a great foot. What people felt, though was emotion;  the elephant indignant and simmering with anger, Kharunyana calm and at ease. Some murmured that a fight was about to break out, but after several long minutes, and a few splashes of water.the great elephant lowered its head in a small nod. Turning, it gazed upon the gathered crowd and trumpeted once, before turning and walking off into the jungle. Kharunyana leapt from the water, did a small somersault in the air and floated along the water's surface, meandering its way back out into the open river while the bystanders looked on in puzzled silence. Some sort of accord had been reached, clearly, but what that meant was anyone's guess.
