In some ways, it was a surprise that of all the ceann and ceanna it was their ruler that stood before the gathered clans as ceannasai. Our clan had never been the forefront of elven culture, politics, or power. We were explorers and pathfinders, bound together by blood and bonds. Maybe, for that reason, in other ways it was only obvious that their ruler was chosen to lead the Great Salla. The world had changed, after all. Our forest home was no longer all there was, but the beginning of everything more. Who better to venture forth and meet that unknown than those who had already done so? Still that alone wasn't enough for every Wood Elf was born a ranger, if only out of necessity. There needed to be something more. It was for that reason their ruler spoke not our own feats but rather to how we were one people; kith and kin, indivisible and inseperable. Be we called each other brother, sister, cousin, or simply clanself, our loyalty was to each other, for now and ever more.
