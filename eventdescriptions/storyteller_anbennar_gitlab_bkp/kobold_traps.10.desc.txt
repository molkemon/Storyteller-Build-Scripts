Caravans of kobolds, pulled by heavy drakes. Wagons packed with wool and shiny trinkets. All across Kobolds' Fleeing Province, kobolds leave for the interior. After years of enduring our rule—of us seizing kobold lands, displacing their peasants, and instituting harsh administrations—the locals have had enough. They have taken their belongings and their wealth and have begun a great migration into the Dragonspine caverns. The kobolds depart from the surface, towards a place they think they can still defend.

Most of our local settlers have begun celebrating, seizing every piece of kobold property they can find in the aftermath of the migration. Some, though, hesitate, as they watch riches flow past our borders. They claim that there are two ways by which we could stop this migration: we either integrate the kobolds, or we destroy their safe haven in the Dragondepths. Otherwise, they might just keep leaving.

For now, though, this means more homes for our settlers, more land for our farmers, and less pesky reptilians to deal with. Who are we to complain about one less trapped burrow to watch out for?
