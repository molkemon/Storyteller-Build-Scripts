There are many elixirs among the seeker's faith. Some ward off bad spirits, while others are said to open the mind to unknown knowledge. Whatever benefits they may provide, the Mwalims are giving them out for free in front of the main hall in Abeesooyinka. Using them as medical aids, the Mwalims have started curing the needy of the city. They've done it quickly as well. It seems that thousands more people have begun traveling the roads today, even to our homelands. Many praise the Mwalims for being able to cure illnesses that were once incurable and fix horrifying deformities.

While the general public seems happier about this grand event, the Darwiiz claim that the overuse of this medicine is an insult to the legacy of their forefathers. They affirm that while the elixirs are powerful, they shouldn't be used like other medicines, as they require special care in line with scripture.

While no Mwalim school has made any direct statements on this topic, individual scholars seem to have a distaste for the Darwiiz for their unwillingness to push past their traditions and use the tools given to them for the better. I've even heard of a few Mwalims synthesizing the elixirs to cure simple cuts in protest of the sacred nature of these elixirs.
