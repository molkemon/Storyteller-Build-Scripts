And so it was in the current year that Nackers arose in the bogs and mires of fair Gerudia. And they descended upon the cities of men in their thousands, stealing, killing, drinking, and breaking the Queen's peace. Our ruler was queen in those days. A fisher named Þórdís came to her in Vængheim, and she said, "Our ruler, my village is full of nackers. For every step I take, I find two nackers underfoot. They eat all the fish, and worst of all, they have stolen my son and put a nacker in his place."

Our ruler rose and said, "The nackers have broken my peace and bled my people. And so have they bled me. In stealing your son, they have stolen my son. I will fly out with you, and I will help you take back your son, or avenge him."

And then our ruler gathered the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
