The Anvil of Unmaking pulsed with its unnatural voidlight as the Vaultwardens set the fragment of Dames tear upon it. This was their boldest experiment yet; an attempt not just to unmake magic, but to reshape it.  The process was agonizingly slow. The anvil's effect, usually swift and merciless, struggled against the very nature of Dames tear. Hours passed as its brilliance dulled, its essence drained thread by thread. The runes lining the chamber flared under the strain, sigils of containment flickering as waves of disrupted magic threatened to break loose.  Then, at last, the Dames tear darkened. No longer did it glisten with arcane potential; instead, it became something other. When tested, it did not conduct magic but consumed it, draining spells, unraveling enchantments, leaving nothing in its wake.  Yet the process remained inefficient, unstable, and perilously expensive. Even the researchers, shielded behind layers of wards, felt their own thoughts grow muddled in the Anvil’s presence. And still, they knew; if refined, if mastered; this discovery could change everything. The promise was undeniable, and everyone understood what it meant for the hold.
