Captain Lorens fidgeted with the sword at his side. He did not like waiting, and hated waiting for what was to come. Soldiers milled nearby their war machines, the rows of ballistae and scorpions aimed at their highest firing arcs, most trying and failing to hide their anxiousness. When the order had come down to participate in this wyvern hunt, he had been unenthusiated, especially when the absentminded mage Asara had told him there would be no cannons to avoid damaging "the research materials". But he was a good soldier, and good soldiers followed orders. Draconic screeches in the distance woke him from his reverie. "Move." he shouted. "To positions." Every man moved with practiced speed to their firing positions, as two riders came out of the wood on the grassy clearing's opposite side. Mages, whose job it had been to lure the beast from the nearby Trialmount. Based on their haste, Lorens figured they had succeeded. "Load." Winches rotated, springs pulled taught, and maliciouslooking bolts loaded. They heard the beast before seeing it. Flapping its wings in great gusts of wind, all men would have sworn it to be a dragon reawokened to terrorize the world once more. It gained on the riders, their only saving grace being the beast's lack of a flame breath. Captain Lorens waited for the right moment. Now. "Fire." A salvo of metal bolts flew out from the treeline. Most missed, but one buried into the wyvern's chest, killing it instantly. The beast's wings froze in place as its corpse slammed to the ground. The men erupted in cheers, having laid low such a proud thing. Even councillor Asara, one of the two riders that had lured the wyvern here and usually wore a face of detached disinterest, smiled. Whatever his superiors intended, their work here would please the ruler.
