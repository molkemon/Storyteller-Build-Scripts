As Elkaesal found gentle dreams, and Vængheim found peace, and summer came to Gerudia. By the clever crafts of the ancient world, and the new songs sung by voices legion, warmer days, gentle rains, and rich harvests came to the realm that roosted 'neath Vængheim's wing. And our queen in those days sent forth her maidens, not as heralds of war, but as envoys of peace. They visited every roost, every homestead, and flew beyond Vængheim’s domain, to realms of other folk, speaking of a great feast to which all who wished to partake were welcome. For in the queen's words, all were guests under her roof.

And on that day, the mighty gates of Vængheim swung open, and stayed open for the first time, for there was no need to shut out the winter. Warm days followed, and for seven days and seven nights, celebrations were held, and mighty songs were sung, said to wake the dragon 'neath the mountain to partake in the feast. Many friends were made then, and in that summer, and many summers hence, winter lost its grip on Gerudia, and the warmth of summer thawed cold hearts, and the realm prospered. It was a bitter time for those who came after, for many born in ages hence, whether mannish or maiden, would hear songs of those days, and with knotted brows, groan and grumble, saying, "Would that I had lived in those days."
