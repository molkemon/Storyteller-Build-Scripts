It ends at noon.  In the Southern Cities' pepper contest, competitors finally reach the spiciest specimens of Sarkhashabid, Sardika, and the Rahen Gulf coast, and the fire in their bellies burns almost as hot as the sun overhead. In Verkal Gulan, even though the only thing visible above is miles of rock, the moment of the sun's zenith signals the tolling of a bell, calling all miners to return to the Citadel Delve. In Kufu, workers return to the coffee plantations; in Azanerdu, craftsmen relight the glasssmitheries' flames; all along the Suran the wheatfields bow as their tenders return with sickle and hoe.  It is the end of the Triumph of the Sun. It is the end of the party, for now. But every adherent of the Jadd knows that in truth, Surael's triumph shall continue, for ever and ever and ever, unceasing as long as His people devote themselves to His cause.
