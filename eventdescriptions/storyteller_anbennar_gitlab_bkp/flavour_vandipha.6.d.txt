The lord takes a pinch of the powder offered to them by their slave, and sniffs it. It’s fragrant; they have tasted it before on both fish and meat, and know it is delicious. And this spice, can you grow it here? they say. Yes, of course. This land is the perfect place for it. The halflings were until recently a distant curiosity to the harimari; strange short men from across the sea, who grew exotic spices sold by Baashidi middlemen in the markets of Madriamilak. With recent conquests, though, halfling slaves have been brought into our grasp, and we have discovered just how skilled they are as gardeners. Some of them even claim they can grow a rare spice called cloves here. Cloves are quite expensive, and this would doubtless bring great wealth. This is what obedience to ones betters should look like; all under the High Gods is in order.
