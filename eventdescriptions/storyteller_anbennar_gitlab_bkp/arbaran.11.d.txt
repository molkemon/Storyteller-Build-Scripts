The rules for a meeting of the full Calasanni Trade Company Board of Directors are laid out in the Company charter: chapter 3, section 4, clause 9. There must be Rubenaire Red and Madalairey White, at least a bottle per person. Invitations must be delivered at least a month in advance, and the Tellumite criers should make a public announcement around the head. These, and others, are the requirements to ensure a minimum of conflict, fighting with words and weapons, or other such chaos. Our ruler followed every rule to the letter. But that was not sufficient when the representatives of the C L ASS T C's crown Branch read the documents assembled before them. Arbaran's C L ASS T C, in a series of clever maneuvers largely carried out via proxies, had acquired a controlling interest by buying out enough of the crown branch's shares. It was laid out, clear to see, in dark ink on bonewhite Konwell paper. Our ruler waited. Our ruler knew waiting well, had outwaited the crown’s place of prominence, lasted through all their strutting and pomp. So a few more minutes, sitting patiently, a thin smile on their lips, was no matter. And when the Crownsmen had exhausted themselves, our ruler could begin. “From where you're standing this must seem like an 18 carat run of bad luck.”
