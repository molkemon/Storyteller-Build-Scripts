It seems the tales of our victories over the Khetists spread by the Griots of the Noukahi have had a far greater impact than we could have imagined. In Chibuswa, the furthest southern Khetist stronghold nestled on the shore, massive riots have broken out. Some claim a Noukahi extremist, spurred by the stories of Khetist defeats in the north, attacked a Khetist school. Others claim that actually the Khetists themselves began the riots in order to spur their northern allies to invade. Regardless of the spark, Noukahi and Khetist are now pitted against each other in a running battle on the streets and in the villages. Militias are springing up on both sides to protect their people. The local governors seem unable, or unwilling, to quell the violence. With a large trading presence, we have a foothold that we could exploit. In order to bring peace to Chibuswa, we could send in a peacekeeping force.
