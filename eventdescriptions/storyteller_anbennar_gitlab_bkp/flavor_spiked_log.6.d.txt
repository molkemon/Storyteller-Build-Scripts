'Thanks to your efforts, my lord, the fey grasp on our brothers and sisters is finally gone. Of course, we will stay vigilant, but none in our clan will seek a pact with the fey ever again.' The clanboss finished his report to the council. As usual, he deluded himself into thinking he was a proper Clanboss, even though his title was mostly honorific now. Our lord nodded in response. Internal stability was always a good thing, especially since it may help him convince his vassal that he was indeed his subject and no longer an equal. Lost in thought, our lord was broken from his reverie by a clamor outside. The source of the disturbance soon became clear, as two goblins were borne into our capital's court room at the head of a crowd and swiftly introduced to the council: a Scout Squad Leader who had departed to Ourdia months ago, and a brownskinned man in strange clothes, his head wrapped in a colorful turban. 'We are back, my lord. I have much news for you but it can wait. We have found new folk. Cousins we had never heard of. I still can't get it through my head.' The stranger seemed to inspect every detail of the room, a slight curl of the lip disappearing behind a carefullymaintained neutral expression. He introduced himself with a bow and an unfamiliar accent. 'Greetings Akal; may the Light shine upon your lands. Your servant's name is Ezklan Zarvilka. I have the honor and the privilege of being a merchant from the glorious city of Aqatbar, the unrivaled gem of Bahar. I have heard of the riches hidden in the Deepwoods and would be interested in establishing a trade post in what you call the Bone Grove, with your approval of course. Although, I think your. arfaya. told me it was in the land of the Brambleskinner clan?' 'Welcome, Master Zarvilka, may you find rest beneath our shades. What you were told was true, but it is quite outdated. After all.'
