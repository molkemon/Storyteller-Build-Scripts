The tunnel walls groaned as the diggers worked, the sound a low, hollow note that sent shivers through the stone. The air tasted bitter, faintly metallic, seeping upward from cracks too deep to see. Thingrim swung his pickaxe, the rhythm steady but tense. Each strike brought loose chunks of stone and the faintest whisper of something distant. He paused, his breath catching.  'Hear that?' he murmured.  Edeth, the forewoman, wiped grime from her face. 'It's nothing,' she said, though her eyes flicked toward the cracks. 'Keep digging.'  The others hesitated but obeyed. The pickaxes rang out again, the rhythm broken as the whispers grew louder. Thingrim froze midswing when the wall before him shuddered and bulged outward.  'Back.' Edeth shouted, her voice cutting through the whispers.  But it was too late. The wall burst apart in a cascade of jagged rock, a plume of shimmering, noxious mist rushing out. It clung to the miners, burning their lungs and eyes. Thingrim staggered, coughing blood. Edeth dragged him toward the lift, but the ground heaved, the tunnel collapsing behind them.  When they reached the surface, Edeth looked back at the dark, churning pit below. Only she and one other had survived. The whispers still lingered, faint but insistent, clawing at the edge of her mind.  'Seal it,' she rasped.
