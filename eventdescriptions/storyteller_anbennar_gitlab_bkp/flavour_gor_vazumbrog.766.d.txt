It began with whispers; first in the barracks, then in the halls of power. The ruler turned inward, his mind clouded with shadows of conspiracy. He spoke of betrayal, of unseen hands plotting his downfall. Then, without warning he declared a great purge. His voice, once the bedrock of reason, now rang hollow with unseen certainty. Traitors lurked in every shadow, he said. The enemy was within. The strangest part? Many followed. Officers, veterans, warriors of renown; men who should have known better. And so the executions began. Not just dissidents or known rivals, but scholars, artisans, veteran warriors. The forges, once roaring with the song of steel, stood silent as their masters were dragged into the depths. The streets ran slick with crimson, the great plazas turned to gallows. Yet beyond the hold, the madness found no purchase. The outer garrisons, hearing of the bloodshed, recoiled in horror. It falls to us to reclaim our hold.
