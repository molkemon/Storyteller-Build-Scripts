What precisely did we imagine, ere our valiant swords struck down the wyvern matriarchs in their desperate fury? A single egg, precariously atop a bundle of sticks, at the very peak of the mountain? Instead we behold a grand cavern, each dank recess crowded with detritus, animal bones, the slick gunge of halfeaten prey.and well over a hundred eggs.  Upon these eggs alone rest all our hopes of returning to our wyvern taming heritage, and we cannot assume we will find more in the wild. Welltended, these eggs may prove nothing less than the future of Verne.
