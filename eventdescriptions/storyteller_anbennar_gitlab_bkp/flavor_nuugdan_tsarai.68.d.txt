Fifty years after the Husuusing, in which our ancestors were forced to walk across the Khaashalg desert in 1 A.A. to the steppes of Shuvuushudi, they came together to celebrate their survival. When the celebrations ended they planned to come together again in fifty years time.  This tradition has been a part of the Shuvuush identity ever since, a festival of unity in a people so often divided. There were years where the festival did not take place, due to war, animosity, and disease, but this year will not be one of those.  The Hekuudzoungh festival started as a great jousting event, and whilst nowadays there are many activities such as political and religious gatherings, the main event is still a competition of martial skill in which the leader and heir of every clan can participate. Shall we pay the entry fee and join?
