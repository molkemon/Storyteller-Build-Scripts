Every paladin describes discovering their Oath differently. Some see it like a puzzle finished, while others feel like a fog clearing. For myself, it was the descent of a grand purpose—raw, inevitable, and holy. The water I drank, the life I lived—they were gifts from Lel, just as surely as the sun rose each day. But they were small compared to the two gifts I held in my arms every night. I lay there, the gift of love in physical form before me, my purpose and reason for living.

Love must be given freely. Let none, living or dead, get in the way of Lel's greatest gift. And never pass judgment on others for the way they choose to love, so long as love is given consensually. It can create life, build empires, and topple tyrants. Love must be unashamed. Too often, love is forced to the corners, shunned, and rejected for being different. Protect all whose love has been oppressed and let your own love be expressed freely to the world as an example for all.

Love must be shared with all. To love oneself is the basis of love, but the true heart of love is to love all others utterly and selflessly. By sharing this selfless love as far and wide as possible, it grows and multiplies, and all are uplifted by its majesty.

This was my Oath, engraved upon my heart and soul. And as I repeated its tenets in my mind, I could feel the swell of divine magic within me. I looked down at Leli and Felana tenderly, kissed the tops of each of their heads. I was now a Paladin of Lel in full, and I knew now what had to be done. For it was my duty and Oath to use the power my great mother had finally rewarded me with to spread her gifts to all the world.
