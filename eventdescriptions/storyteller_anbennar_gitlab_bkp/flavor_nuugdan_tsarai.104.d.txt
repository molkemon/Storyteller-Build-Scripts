A single footfall of a terrorbird is enough to crush a man’s ribcage. A lone bite from a Facestealer can steal a man’s soul (if unprotected by their mask). But a single arrow? Even one loosed by the Faceless Queen? Surely that cannot reshape the steppes themselves, or the lives of every man who resides in or near it.  Except, today, it has. For that arrow won the battle, the battle won the war, and with the war, the Faeless Queen has won the Steppes themselves.  Hus Bal has fallen. Rung Oedek bows before the rightful heir to Ulaan and Tsagaan. Tuiista Jaal is quick to follow, though they have a proposal for her to consider in her wisdom. And Ashag Kuulgo she has taken for her own, like a bird plucked from the sky.  Today, an arrow takes the steppes. Tomorrow, the terrorbirds shall take Gowon. And the day after that? All Haless should tremble, for a conqueror great and terrible is upon them.
