Through two eyes, Golodh can see what this land looked like in the time of the Makers. Through the other two, he sees it in the present. With both, he would weep.  The trees that should bear fruit like jewels now grow into impenetrable tangles, thick with teeth. The beasts that were stepping stones on the Makers' path towards enlightenment, prowling like mangy dogs. The land, which flourished under the care and direction They administered from their pillars, left a meaningless morass of struggle.  And their tools. Oh, their tools.  To break is the fate of all: bird and tree, mountain and stone. Even the towering Holohana will fall one day, Golodh knows.  But to break, and know you break, and fall powerless towards that fate.that is damnation. That is cruelty. And that is all the Makers' tools have been left with.  He sees this truth every day when he gazes at the small group of Mechanim he travels with. He sees it in every tragic wreck they leave behind, each time another one of the Makers' greatest works succumbs to the cruel march of time. The wrecks are left, knowing they have broken, and being broken all the same, for eternity.  It is hell, and it is wrong.  He cannot escape that hell himself, but he can lead others out of it. He will correct the Mechanim of their critical, tragic, mistake: that they are creatures known as 'Mechanim', and not the Makers' tools. That they must find a purpose of their own, rather than having a use they can be put to. He will free them from the suffering the lie of sentience imposes, and return them to the paradise the Makers offered.
