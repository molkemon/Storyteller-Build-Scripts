In the process of its travel across Kanolowele, Endless Ocean has drunk deeply the cultural practices of all whom it has touched. Speaking Memory’s historyworship has always lended itself to monumentmaking, and perhaps out of infectious respect for the epics of old, local Endless Ocean faith leaders have taken up the same skills. By Endless Ocean doctrine, the dead must be buried at sea, lest their bodies be stuck in the earth, unable to participate in the voyage to the afterlife. However, for the sentimental who wish some keepsake of their departed loved ones, there is no better solution than a Face of Stone. Faces of Stone begin their lives in the quarries adjacent to most local temples, where the survivors of the departed select a fitting specimen of rock from which to shape a likeness of the deceased. After the extreme finework is complete, these statues are arranged in coastal shrines, looking out to sea and preferably towards the route of the departed, beyond the Kiamoa. Such stoneworking takes untold hours of labor, and as such, the clergy who participate in making Faces of Stone swiftly hone their masonry skills. When not in practice to aid mourners, these masters aid locals in erecting other necessary structures as acts of goodwill, in the hope that when they make the journey across the sea, some other stranger will return the favor.
