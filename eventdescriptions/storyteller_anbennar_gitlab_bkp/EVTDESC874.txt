In our nation two castes hold great power: the nobility and the clergy. However, they do not always get along. Recently, a baron in a province and the local monastery of his fief began bickering about who owns a rather large piece of land. These days, the royal court is swamped with people yammering about divine right; the nobility claiming their right to rule the land by nature of being the ones to rule, hence being just in Corin's eyes, and the clergy asserting their position that just rulers must continually prove themselves worthy. The whole situation is growing rather unstable (and theologically confusing) with the baron in question inciting the other nobles against the clergy and questioning their right to hold any sort of temporal property. The clerics of the monastery have rallied zealous corinite crusaders on their side, who are clamouring to fight such blatant injustice should you decide to side with the nobility. Now, what shall we do, my liege?
