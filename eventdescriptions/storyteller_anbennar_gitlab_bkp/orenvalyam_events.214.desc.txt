Twenty floors above the darkened streets of the city, the High Kavalali met once more. They were signing their death warrants just by doing so—someone would know, someone would find out. Then the Kamrayakval would come down to Nanru Nakar, and slaughter them all for their insolence. Today, though, they didn’t care. Because when he did, it would be the end of him. Katelperai had set a trap.

Ethanann: "And so we've agreed."
Royakottar: "Once he's gone, we will be able to reassert control. The Kavalakuttam... what is left of it... is on our side."
Sthanann: "And we can raise enough troops to dissuade their forces."
"Even if we cannot assure their loyalty..." added Sibisimra.
"But that doesn't matter," replied Katelperai. "Because the soldiers' time is done. We will break their shackle-hold over us."

But his words were met with grim faces—by design, the means of execution had been obscured. At this meeting, it would be illuminated.

"The method cannot implicate us, and render us despised." Katelperai stated. "The weapon cannot shatter against his armour, and leave us defenceless." He paused for a moment, remembering a time long past—when they had turned to a similar tool, and it had left them ensnared in the Dark. "But the flame must burn bright, so all may see the true reason this was done."

"And what is this method?" asked Mlerek, hesitant. "This weapon of flame?"

The High Kavalali of Katelperai took a final breath. Consigned his faith to a Hero once again, and called his answer out clear:

"Interloper!"
