Armed soldiers move through another village in the domain, dragging all but the bedridden elderly from their homes under a cloudy, almost stormy sky. Those who resist meet the spear and sword, while the rest are brought to the village circle, where a congregation of priests is chanting while a team of slaves begins dismantling the local shrine dedicated to the religion. The head priest watches, a sense of guilt mixed with a sense of duty. He knows he does this to save the souls of all those he can, but the mixed loathing and fear in the eyes of so many still drill into his soul. All he can hope for, as his comrades finalize the ritual where the heavens begin to pour, is that Amilak acknowledges that he did what was right, what was necessary.
