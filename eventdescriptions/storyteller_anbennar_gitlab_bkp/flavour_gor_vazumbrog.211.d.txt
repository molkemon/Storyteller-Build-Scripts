The crack in the aquifer was Kurgum's fault. He'd swung too hard, eager to finish his shift early, ignoring Brenlel's shouted warnings.  The stone split, and something spilled out. Not watersomething thicker, darker, gleaming faintly in the lantern light. It spread fast, too fast, rushing down the carved channels meant to carry clean water to Gor Vazumbrog.  'Block it.' Brenlel yelled, shoving stones into the breach with her bare hands. The others joined, but the miasma flowed like it had a mind of its own, curling around their boots and burning their skin.  Kurgum froze, his hammer clattering to the ground. The stink of rotten eggs and copper filled the air, and whisperslow, accusingfilled his ears.  'What have you done?' Brenlel snapped, her voice shaking.  Kurgum couldn't answer. His hands trembled as the voices grew louder, echoing every mistake he'd made, every failure. The faces of those who trusted him flickered in the blackened flow, their mouths twisted in silent condemnation.  The miners retreated, coughing and retching, but Kurgum didn't move. The taint surged past them, racing toward the lower cisterns of Gor Vazumbrog.  'It's in the water,' Brenlel whispered, staring down the tunnel.  Kurgum sank to his knees, his voice breaking. 'I didn't mean to'  Brenlel grabbed his collar, dragging him toward the lift.  'You'll answer to the Hold,' she spat.  Above, the bells of Gor Vazumbrog tolled, signaling an emergency. Kurgum hung his head, shame heavier than the rock he'd mined all his life.
