For decades, or perhaps for centuries, our people have focused almost exclusively on the migration. From the 100-year crown loan to the massive resource extraction projects in those lands and those lands, our economy was geared towards one purpose: the return home. Now that we have achieved that great dream, we have lost that clearcut focus. And we are left with the loans to pay; and already, the campaign plans drawn up by our advisor, and the great cathedral at that city the priest is clamoring for, seem to demand we take more. A close advisor to our lord instructs us in a more fruitful direction: immediate investment in the facilities necessary to harvest the many fruits of this untapped land. All of that land, bursting at the seams with ores to mine and flora to pluck is at our fingertips. For years, we have sown.
