Lying on the intersection of the Straight Road and the Path of Wine, the prosperous government saw the Kheionai invasion as both a looming danger and an enticing opportunity. Protected by the rugged hills to the south from the armies of Ameion, and by the terrifying storms off Thekvussam Cape from Laskaris navy, their remote location provided a natural shield. Yet, it was the unmatched expertise in navigating these treacherous waters that positioned their sailors to broker mutually beneficial trade with the blueskinned invaders.
Over the years, the ports of Thekvussam, Curiyankad, and most importantly Korrimadhala began to bustle with Kheionai merchants and sailors. These invaders, viewed with suspicion elsewhere in Taychend, found a warmer reception here, their presence growing steadily as trade flourished. Jointly crewed by Thekvrystana and Ambhen sailors, the ships carried valuable goods from distant corners of the Andeios, enriching the region in both wealth and influence. The upper class, accustomed to novelty, gradually embraced the Kheionai presence, and the young heir was particularly captivated, taking a keen interest in the culture and allure of Kheionai women.
