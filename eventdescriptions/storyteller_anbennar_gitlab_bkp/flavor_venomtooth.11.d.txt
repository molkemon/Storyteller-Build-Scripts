Edith was a descendant of a Cobalt Company soldier, born into slavery and beaten by her master as she worked as a house slave. Despite leading an existence filled with misery, she felt a bond with the children she helped raise and even cried as many did not come back from their service. She secretly harbored hatred and animosity towards her master and the society as a whole that treated life as cheap and easily replaceable. One night, she gathered her courage and snuck into her master's room where she slit his throat before managing to take his wife captive.  Meanwhile, in the fields, Tuhorn was a slave working in the fields owned by the same master. He was descended from Bonecarver soldiers, but was a slave ever since he was born. His slave driver would beat him and the other slaves for the most minor of infractions. On the same day that his master died, Tuhorn rallied his fellow slaves and stormed the servants' quarters. They quickly killed the slave drivers before meeting up with Edith and her fellow house slaves.  Over the period of a few years, Tuhorn and Edith created sleeper cells in plantations and mines across Venomtooth. Tuhorn and Edith quickly fell in love, finding common ground in their past as victims of the same cruel society.  After years and years of desperate struggle and meticulous planning, a signal was sent out and the sleeper cells activated with the rise of dawn. All across Venomtooth, tens of thousands of slaves rose up, killing their masters, breaking their chains, and continuing the chaos. If they were not stopped, they would surely topple Venomtooth, intending to replace it with a state where orks and humans are equals.  Our lord was able to escape the palace when it was stormed, but this unprecedented impudence of the weakling slaves has filled them with an incandescent rage. These slaves would learn their place or they would rest at the bottom of Lake Silvermere.
