Orbin whacks the aetheric monitor with a screwdriver. It had been making weird sounds, but as he smacks it one last time, it suddenly becomes intelligible. Words. He recognizes words. Soon, the entire camp has gathered around the artificer's invention. In time, a translator amongst the group is produced; Cela, of the Order of Chroniclers; who reveals the monitor's babbling to be words spoken in an unfamiliar dialect of elven.    The monitor crackles as the party absorbs this. After a few violent bursts of static, the audio resumes. The previous speaker appears to be singing. His voice rises from the monitor, clear and strong, as though he were right in front of us. The words are untranslatable, even for Cela, but their haunting melody sends a shiver down our spines. When the song ends, the monitor crackles briefly, then falls silent.  Where could these sounds have come from? Could there be Precursors that remain somewhere out there, hidden away in a Vault of some sort, having survived the Ruin of Aelantier? We can't miss this chance to connect with these stillliving descendants, whose words; and songs; now grace our ears.
