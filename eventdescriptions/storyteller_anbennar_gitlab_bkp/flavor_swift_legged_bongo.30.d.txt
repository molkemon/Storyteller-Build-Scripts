It is hard work, being a mother. My child requires constant attention. I must feed her, whisper to her, tell her stories, coddle her like the babe she is. When the sun sets, I put her to sleep. When it rises, she wakes with me.  I find I am growing weaker. It becomes harder to rouse myself from slumber with each passing day. To take care of my duties as an Ingo requires so much more effort than it used to. Every time I must hear a petition, I want nothing more than to claw my way out of my own skin and retreat to my chambers. How, after such an exhausting day, am I supposed to find the energy to care for my child? But I must. My child needs me.  I see the looks my council gives me. They do not understandhow could they? They do not feel the bond I have with my child, do not see the way she looks at me. Even the sunset cannot compare the beauty she holds.  Yes, my child. I hear you. Your mother hears you.
