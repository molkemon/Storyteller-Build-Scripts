It was in the days of today, when our queen ruled over Vængheim. A man named Kåre, who was blessed with fertile land and many flocks, came to our queen. And he said, "O queen, I kneel before you, for there is nowhere else I can turn. The old man of the forest has taken my son Asbjörn, and I am left bereaved. I beg of you to save my son from this wicked spirit!"

And then he wept. Our queen, who knew something of the fey, said, "I know of whom you speak. A woldwight is he, of great power and cunning. But why has he taken your son?"

And Kåre, whose tears were without number, answered, "He seeks to take that which is most precious to me, for he knows that bereft of this, the taste of life will be as ash upon my tongue, for he covets my wealth and my land, and seeks my ruin."

And our queen did rise, and said, "Weep no more, for I shall make your cause my cause. For the sake of your son, I will brave the wight, and see that justice is done."

And then our queen gathered the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
