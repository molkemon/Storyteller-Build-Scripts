The rift opens… and I can see. …but the vision is confusing and doubled. Our lord's heart fails him; he collapses as he dictates a letter, his final breath rattling gently. At the same time, an assassin's blade passes through the throat of a naga, far away in their realm. There is rioting. There is rage. At court, a halfling rails against the tyranny of the lizards. In Taneyas, the naga say nothing; they simply turn inward once again, disappointed but unsurprised by the barbarism of outsiders. But wait, I think - the lizardfolk are famously amenable to the halflings, who are perhaps the only outsiders the naga consider true equals. I look again at the surroundings, the tapestries, the decoration on the stonework. How ancient is this? How many centuries earlier could Taneyas have opened to the Vyzemby, if not for these two untimely deaths?
