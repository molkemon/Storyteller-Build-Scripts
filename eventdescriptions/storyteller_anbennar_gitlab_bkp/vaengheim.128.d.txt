Then did the maidens of Wingheim fly over fields fecund, and their quarry track across ear of wheat and cereal. And as their feet did touch the ground, did a great roar arise, for Solveig, who was a farmer of pride and skill, did flying hunters spy, and she was wroth, for they crossed her domain. And Solveig did say, "Hark now ye gulls, for I suffer no birds to spoil my crop, whether their face be fair or foul.
