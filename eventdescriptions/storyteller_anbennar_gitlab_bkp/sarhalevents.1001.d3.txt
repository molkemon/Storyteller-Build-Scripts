The news spreads across Sarhal unevenly, racing along trunk roads and spreading down country lanes just as blood flows into a lung. Indeed, Sarhal can breathe again; the Coven of Yezel Mora is broken. Spontaneous celebration erupts in every town the news reaches; border skirmishes pause for a day or two; markets see a little less squabbling than normal. The shadow which has darkened Sarhal for so long is no more.  The Coven was broken by the slaying of Mama Hireet, most fearsome and brutal of the three. Responsible for the resentful fury of the Echenka and the empowerment of the swamp troll horde, Hireet’s repulsive blood court forced child against parent in sick tableaus of gore, demanding ultimate loyalty to Mama above anyone else.  Those who seek in their vengeance to put others beneath the boot will no longer have Hireet’s braying encouragement. Hireet’s pride was her undoing, but the fate of the other two hags is unclear; it is reported that Auntie Komina disappeared almost at the moment of Hireet’s death, while Granny Ilfa is notorious for her ability to escape and conceal herself in shadow.  Still, without the Coven entire, the two survivors are profoundly weakened.  No doubt the hags that remain may one day once again threaten Sarhal, but for now, the world feels a little lighter than it did.
