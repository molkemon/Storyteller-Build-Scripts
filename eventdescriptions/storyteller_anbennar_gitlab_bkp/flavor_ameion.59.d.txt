After the failure of the Wolf's Gambit following Laskaris' death, rumors that the Conqueror was a secret heretic persisted, spread by the Cult of Laskaris. But as Ameion's efforts at Kheionisation succeeded, these rumors fell by the wayside along with most Taychendi superstition. However, the rise of the Thirendires has led to new rumors that this new conqueror may have inherited more than Laskaris' political mantle. Recently discovered documents seem to prove Laskaris had doubts about the existence of the Taychendi gods and saw in Nanru Nakar more than simple mortal ambition, but divine destiny. Though these documents are questionable, they could be easily seized upon to claim divine legitimacy for the new emperor's rule. For the masses of Taychendi peasants who still privately cling to superstition, that might be very worthwhile indeed, even as it would inflame his Kheionised supporters. A proclamation from Thirendires would earn him the fealty of Taychend, but sow chaos within much of the Kleryen.
