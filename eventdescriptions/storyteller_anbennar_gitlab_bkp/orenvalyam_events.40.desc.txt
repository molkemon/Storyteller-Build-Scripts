The High Kavalal of Royakottar nearly leaped from his seat when he saw it. "By Nayiru—it's one of—" Oktikheion smiled as he welcomed the Mechanim. "This is the Insyaan leader I was telling you about." It looked like an elf, only built of rippling silver-steel. A living relic stood before him! "Greetings," it said in a toneless voice, offering a hand. "My name is quite long, so you may call me Roaring-Defiant-Against-My-Makers." The Kavalali rose, giddily accepting the hand. What a brilliant name!

The Taychendi turned to Oktikheion. "This is just incredible! I mean, it can speak!" "Slightly offended: I can speak, but I am not an 'it,' " the Mechanim replied. Oktikheion nodded: "They have a strong sense of self."

Royakottar rubbed his beard: "I have to wonder how this fits our doctrine… if they're a Precursor relic, are they not a product of—" Then, an epiphany. "By Nayiru, this doesn't contradict—it proves…!" He fell deep into thought.

Roaring-Defiant turned to the Kheionai. "Confused: your colleague speaks and I do not comprehend." Oktikheion explained: "The Elvenness of Relics. Our belief that magic is a living thing, and the process of relic creation entraps it—just note how similar the enchantment of objects and people is, for instance—and that artificery simply prolongs that grim practice. Who knows what living energies you're enslaving in your 'new relics,' after all. You can't ask them."

"But what if we could," Royakottar whispered, as his mind unraveled the future. "Flesh of iron, hands of steel… and with these hands that will not break…" The Mechanim backed away. "Remembering: I have heard that sentiment." Oktikheion chuckled nervously: "Come now, Tesh. You know the history." "Bitter: I lived it." But Royakottar just looked up, eyes filled with guilt and hope: "But we won't make the same mistake again!" He turned to Roaring-Defiant. "As equals… as partners… we could build Dawn, together! You could be heroes!"
