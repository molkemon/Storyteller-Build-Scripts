When the lights first appeared beneath their ruler's waters, confusion and dread abounded. Yet year after year, the submerged stars of merfolk-curated baubles that at first were viewed with suspicion were increasingly regarded as a beloved local landmark, until, like a bolt from the blue, the storm appeared. Wide as the sky, the squall churned whirlpools, crackled with lightning, and howled like a demon. As it brought havoc, a distraught azure merfolk swam into the harbor, aiding in rescue efforts before explaining his arrival. "The good news is that the leyline wards intended to paint the transparent currents with vibrant hues were a success. The dire news is," his brilliant orange eyes glanced at the undying storm, "that their kingdom's aquamancers will be displeased that their experimental coloring ritual failed so spectacularly. Still," he coughed, "as a guard to the ward wall, I'm glad I could help." As the unending storm buffets their territory, their ruler must decide on how to respond to this arcanecological disaster.
