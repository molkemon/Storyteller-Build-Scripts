A lone harpy stands against a palace balcony, raised up high on the rock face above the distant city below. Lights twinkle there against the twilight sky, distant flames flickering in the cool evening breeze. Hours ago the Matriarch and the rest of the clan left for the conclave, making the flight below to the designated meeting space. Now Riva waits alone, the only Nephiueda in the roost. All is quiet around aside from the slosh of wine in Riva’s glass, the sounds of the city too far to be heard. This meeting will decide the fate of the clan, the fate of the dynasty, the fate of Naleni itself.  A sudden crack splits the air.  Roaring noise bellows, waves of heat blasting upwards. A huge fireball illuminates the dark below, centred on the meeting place. Riva stares wordlessly, hands gripped tight to the stone railing. As the fireball diminishes a plume of smoke rises from the charred remains of the conclave, ashes all that is left of the dreams of peace.  In the dying orange light black flags rise in the harbour, visible only with an avian eye. They flutter in the wind, mocking and triumphant, as the ships pull away.  Riva screams.  Not out of anguish, but anger. Retribution. Into the night, after those fleeing ships, Riva screams a promise of justice. Ten seconds, then a deep breath, and a pivot back inside. The Nephiueda dynasty will not end here. She will not allow it. Naleni will not fall to these mutineers. The Matriarch may be dead, but the Matriarchy lives on. The Sirens will answer her, the fleets will come at her call, and EVERY Nalenian shall bow before the queen; else she shall make them.
