Bricena being dropped off on her father's lake boat by her hawk, eager to see him after having united the Deepwoods. And she sees him. Sitting still in his favorite chair, a serene smile on his face... but his eyes are closed and he does not draw breath. Lying in front of him is an open leather-bound journal. Bricena begins crying immediately, of course, and only more so when she gathers herself enough to read the page.

Mourn me not, my darling daughter. Always have I known that you would lead our realm to greatness. However, when I retired, I did not even have the fantasy to imagine that you might do what you did. You, Bricena, have proven yourself a far greater leader than I ever was or could have been, and I die a happy man, knowing that you have done what I once thought impossible: uniting our fragmented people. Stand tall, my daughter, and know that no man could be prouder of his child than I am of you, and that seeing you far exceed the hopes I have had for you has allowed me to die a happy man.

- Your loving father
