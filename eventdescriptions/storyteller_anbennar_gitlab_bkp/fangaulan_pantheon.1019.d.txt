Tonight we gather by the shores of Lake Kuiziva, and look across the waters to the spire of Kanhati. This is his home, but do not expect him to greet us: he is very busy with his work. It is Kanhati the Black Antelope, he who once protected the Kaino, who guides each of our ancestors to their rest.  It is past this very spire that we all will walk when we die, holding the hand of Kanhati, the god of wisdom. The songs the jelli sing here are not happy ones, for death is not a happy thing, but it is in the world regardless.  I will now sing for you a song of Kanhati the wise, eldest of the gods. We must choose the song we are to sing tonight, and I will sing it as best I can. As I sing to you, look at the moon which hangs above us reflected in the waters. For the ancestors are looking back at us from their place of rest.
