The gates of Gor Vazumbrog loomed before Foldan, their ancient stone newly repaired and gleaming with runes of welcome. He had imagined this moment his entire life; the descendant returning to his ancestors' hold, the songs of its halls filling his ears.  He paused at the threshold, taking off his glove to brush the etched stone. His reflection shimmered in the polished brass of the gate, a Titanium Dwarf born in exile, finally coming home.  'It's as grand as they said,' he murmured, his voice carrying both reverence and disbelief.  The guards smiled and waved him closer. 'Welcome back, kinsman. You've journeyed far.'  He stepped forward, chest swelling with pride. The air was crisp, heavy with the scent of stone and deepearth moss.  Then came the sound. A low growl, a rush of windlike movement. Before anyone could react, the shadows erupted.  The Drakon struck with terrifying speed, a sleek blur of scales that wove effortlessly through the jagged stalagmites. Its maw clamped down on Foldan, teeth piercing through leather and bone as though they were paper.  He screamed, reaching out toward the gates as the beast wrenched him into the darkness. The guards drew weapons and gave chase, but the Drakon vanished into the labyrinthine caverns, leaving only the echo of Foldan's final cry.  The gates of Gor Vazumbrog stood ajar, but our new beginning was already smeared with blood.
