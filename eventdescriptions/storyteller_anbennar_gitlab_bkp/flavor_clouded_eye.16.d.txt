"It was a bright and sunny day when I rode into Ardent Keep with the new knights. Wargs were dressed in armor of orkish design, which showed some restraint as they weren’t snapping at civilians. orks dressed in the finest armor sat upon the wargs, carrying poleaxes and lances. The horn players, two halforks riding normal horses, rode out at the front, blowing the horns to draw attention to the procession.  I rode at the head of the procession as columns of orkish knights rode in behind me. Behind the first set of columns was both Bladebreaker and Count’s League separatists. They were recently rooted out of Alloyford and Hagstow respectively. Behind them were the fanatical believers of Korgus. Though the religious shift of my teachings was happening, there were those who resisted and attempted to murder followers of Corin.  As the triumph reached the center of the town, I dismounted my warg and approached a young halfork man with a pensive look. He wore a fancy set of old Castellyrian armor yet carried an orkish axe. I patted his shoulder with my hand and asked, ‘What’s your story, young man?’  He looked at me and said, ‘My name is Lucan Bladebreaker, son of Chief Dhog and Baroness Lorinne. I have come to offer my services to you.’
