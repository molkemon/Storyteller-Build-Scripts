When Kimánis was partitioned and Oktikheion seized the city itself, all expected Amgremós to submit to their new overlords, as was precedent—law dictated this. They didn’t. Instead, emboldened by the success of Ameion in the east, they scrambled to seek support from wherever they could find it. And, to their shame, they found it in the pirates of the Cleaved Sea. Amgremós grew fat by picking off the trade ships bringing Taychend’s riches to the Kheionai. They dared to think that their independence could last—Oktíbhen incompetence meant it might have. But we are not Oktikheion.

Now, the captured pirates of the Andic Reach sat confined in pens, under Phylaxana watch—with the aid of guards from loyal Eneion. The operations had been difficult, far ranging. At every turn, the pirates had proven their skill-at-sea, a certain pluckiness on land. Some in our Nékheis begrudgingly respect them—but the Phylaxana can afford them no such thing. They are criminals, nothing more.

The law code gives us two options—death, or heavy labor in Deyeion, a holdover from the innovation of Darasthartes. There are some, though, who see another option—a loophole, one might say. Rather than having them waste away in tea plantations, we could put them to sea, in a state of “indefinite transit” to Deyeion. Most would serve as galley slaves, some perhaps slightly more. Not officers, of course—but advisors. Trade on their minds, to protect the seas… and learn some of their tactics. It will be useful to our naval hegemony, no doubt.

Some call this a vile innovation, tantamount to treason against the Old Law. Perhaps both can be true. Or maybe the thought alone is treason-of-the-mind. If so? The penalty for piracy is death by hanging. And the gallows await our command.
