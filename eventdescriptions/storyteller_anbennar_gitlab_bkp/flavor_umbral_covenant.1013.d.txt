The rift opens… and I can see. My mother Hireet is rarely known for her subtlety. Yet as I watch, I see her magic gently guiding the river-spear; it flies as if possessed from the fisherman Lessish's hand, cleanly impaling the merchant Jiazi. Hireet would scold me for presuming that anyone, even she, could truly foresee the blood feud that will result from this, centuries in the making. The art of the hag is not based on clairvoyance, but persistence; not on ensuring certainty, but exploiting every possible opportunity. And yet, for a moment, as a frantic Lessish gazes into Jiazi’s dying eyes, desperate to staunch the richer man’s flowing blood, I fancy that both men can see it. They know what nightmares will come of this; they mourn the future together, all the lives that will be lost in those final seconds.
