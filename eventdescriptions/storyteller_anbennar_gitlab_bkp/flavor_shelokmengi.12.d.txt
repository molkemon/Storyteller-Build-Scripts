"The water we bring to the fields does not simply disappear once it has watered the crops," proclaimed Bati Goshan, a local rain priest, gesturing lightly with one hand to illustrate his lecture. "Water is part of an eternal cycle as willed by Amilak, and it is the duty of the priesthood to support this cycle to aid His children." "But if done poorly, that can cause issues?" asked Ayufar, who reclined on his couch as the priest spoke to him.

Bati frowned. "I have seen some farmlands stripped bare by drought, and flash floods rip others apart so that they may not be fertile for many years. We have been given a great gift, to guide the flow of the winds and rains, but used with ignorance, this can lead to a destruction greater than anything but what the Shadow Swamp could bestow."

Ayufar sat straight, clasping his hands together. "Well then, we better get started. I'll be shortly calling priests from across the country to better coordinate our rainfall." He put a hand on Bati's shoulder. "And I would like you to lead it, I find your oration very convincing."
