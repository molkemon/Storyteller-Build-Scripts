Flutes and gongs, the smell of roasting meats and sweet wine, and the kaleidoscopic flurry of dancers and acrobats fill the palace's great hall as the eunuch lords of Xiyun feast beside intact lords of the realm. At the high table sits the ruler of the realm, eternal friend and protector of the Sceptreless Kingdom. The ostensible honoree of the lavish banquet, a nephew of the Eunuch King, lies unconscious in the royal chambers, attended to by the court physicians and herbalists, uncertain to survive this morning's Rite of Purification. But the family always has more nephews to choose from. The Eunuch King laughs and drinks deeply from a silver goblet of rice wine. The drink and merriment has put out of mind the growing fear that one day our relentless march to unify Yanshen would turn south and arrive at their gates. The plan had required subterfuge, not the brute strength of our armies. A bag of silver in the right hands, or a knife to the right throat. The vengeful heir of a banished merchant house financed the venture. A disgraced alchemist exiled from the House of Fire and Water supplied the poison, and the antidote for our ruler and their retinue. Our garrison commanders embedded in Xiyun's towns would deploy at sundown, arrest and execute the local eunuch lords, and proclaim the new ruler of these lands, just as the main course is served in the palace. The Eunuch King relishes the broiled Dire Python, brought from the remote Lupulan Rainforest for the auspicious occasion. A moment later, with a rasping cough, blood spouts from their mouth, soon echoed by the gathered eunuch lords and members of their family. The Eunuch King's sworn protectors, the Summer Guard, are cut down as our heavily armed retinue breaks through the great hall's doors. As the Eunuch King crawls toward their throne, choking on their own blood, our ruler takes up their great chui, and swings the mighty mace down on their head.
