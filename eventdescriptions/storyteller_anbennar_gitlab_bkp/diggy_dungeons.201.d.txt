The cave ends in a very narrow opening, between the stone remnants of a tree and a large boulder that rests against it. Our mining engineers look at it doubtfully. "We can try to cut pieces out of the tree and the boulder to widen the entrance, but the whole cave ceiling rests on it," the lead engineer says. "We'd have to work real carefully and with support beams, and even then it's a bit risky. It might be safer to just dig past here and create our own entrance in the greater cave system."
