We have convened a great synod to determine what shall be done about the matter of the Taga'Ala. We have assembled beneath one of the Twin Gods, the guides of our faith.The magmascryers have brought us to the caldera of Quahopilo, the bringer of change by force, to show us the way forward.  The magmascryers begin their dance, and work together as one to bring forth visions of change. The lava bursts forth, and from it comes a vision of Taga'Ala ships. They have assembled a mighty fleet, but their holds are filled with cargo: they have come to trade. They sail forth on seas of lava to lands of ash, and bring tales of the Volcanic Truth to their people at home, who are ignorant of the commandments of the Twin Gods.  But then the magmascryers shift away from each other, and their movements become solitary and forceful. The lava bursts forth, higher than before: it shows visions of ships. They are our ships, come to show the Taga'Ala the meaning of our Truth. Mangrove trees turn into ash, but from the ash comes new life. The Taga'Ala have been reborn in fire. Great plumes of lava explode into the air, and from them arise visions of change.  As these visions fade, the magmascryers stand within the flames. Their work is done, but the path forward is unclear. It falls to the council to decide: are the people of the mangroves friends or foes? How shall we bring about change? How shall we vote?
