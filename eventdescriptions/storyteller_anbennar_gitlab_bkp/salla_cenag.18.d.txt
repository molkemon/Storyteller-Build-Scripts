"There you are."  My groundward gaze shifted up to spot Lanhara’s approach. I gave a small smile but said nothing. She returned the gesture and plopped herself by the fallen tree I made my resting point.  "You’re rather quiet," she commented, taking my hand.  "I’m thinking," I replied.  "Dangerous."  I rolled my eyes, "Someone told me I should do it more often."  "Smart advice from a beautiful elf. What are you thinking about?"  "Very beautiful," I corrected. My reward was an undignified snort. With a satisfied smirk, I rested my head on her shoulder, "I’ve been thinking about what comes next."  "What happens next?"  "Yeah," I muttered back. "We’ve been fighting for so long. And now it’s over. Now what?  "Simple. Same thing we always did. We live," Lanhara replied.  "That’s it?"  "That’s it."  I gave an uncertain hmm before considering what she said. But as my thoughts began to swirl, I felt Hara squeeze my hand before giving a laugh, "Oh Al, how did this happen?"  "How did what happen?"  She shifted, forcing me to sit back up. As I gave her a questioning look, Lanhara leaned in with a devious look, "How did I become the one to act first, think later?"  I opened my mouth but Hara put a finger on my lips, "New advice, don’t think too much."
