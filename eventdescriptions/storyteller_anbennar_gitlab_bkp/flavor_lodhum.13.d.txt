A dwarven hold is nothing less than an architectural marvel. An example would be the Warding Gate of Verkal Skomdihr. While the wards themselves broke during the Day of Ashen Skies, the physical structure itself is an example of lasting dwarven engineering.  But to the average Dwarf, these miracles are commonplace. As they live in these holds for their whole lives, their grand and ancient halls are all that they know. So when the first visitors started arriving, we were unprepared for the feelings of wonder and awe.  Thus we are reminded of why we carry on the legacy of our ancestors and why we remember their names. It is because they built the world in which we stand. It is through the eyes of our foreign friends and allies that we see our home for what it truly is, and take pride once more in our heritage.
