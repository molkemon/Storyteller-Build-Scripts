In the aftermath of the Phoenix Empire's dissolution and Ramapalar's conquests, the Dhenbasana river reasserted itself as the foremost center of scholarly learning in Haless, eclipsing Sir, Sarisung, and Tianlou. Scholars of every persuasion flocked to Rayavashapal and Dhenijansar to learn from the most renowned teachers of science, theology, and law. Many students from our lands, having completed their educational pilgrimage, are now returning home and hoping to integrate the ideas they learned there, however heterodox they may be compared to those in our lands. Shall we allow these students a place to teach and debate, or do their ideas have no place in our administration?
