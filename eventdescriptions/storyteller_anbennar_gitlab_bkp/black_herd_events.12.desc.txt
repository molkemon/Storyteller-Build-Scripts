Markhentakruan Restoration Notes - Today Success at last! The corpse gave a whip crack, rupturing at the flesh; then all was noise, an indescribable crash that rolled through the camp like the roar of Irdaeos himself, descending to tear the world asunder. The air shook - for a heartbeat I felt my bones follow suit. The sound still echoes in my ears, dwelling faintly at the edge of thought, a constant reminder of the power I now command.

We had tested it using rodents beforehand, of course. I am not so foolish as to demonstrate before the clan without verifying it works ahead of time. Still, a fully grown bo may have been an excessive choice for the first true casting, as the size of the explosion does seem to grow with the target. The corpse shifted as I cast, its innards reshaped by the transmutation, before the second half of the spell lit the body aflame. A few heartbeats later came the detonation, a glorious and deafening display that sprayed gore across the empty field.

I am sorely tempted to stay in my tent and keep working and improving the spell, even after demonstrating it successfully. There are clear signs that this was an inferior version compared to the Markhentakruan of old. Compared to the remains we found, the blast was more contained, the explosion simply lacking the same force it once had. Furthermore, the fragments failed to remain aflame after being hurled through the air, leaving the bones uncharred unlike the ones we uncovered.

Perhaps this is simply perfectionism. Markhentakruan has, without question, been restored. Regardless, a grand celebration has already begun and rumors will spread among the warriors if I fail to attend. I suppose I will have to return to this work at a later time.
