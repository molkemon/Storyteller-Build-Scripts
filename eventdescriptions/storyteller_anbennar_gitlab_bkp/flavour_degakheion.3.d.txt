Ormam and Oktikheion—self-proclaimed masters of the sea! One with her eyes only on accumulation, stacking her coins high for the sake of it, making trouble to turn a profit… the other spending frivolously on naval adventures, gorging herself on the pleasures of the flesh, the sweetness of sugar. And they wonder why we look down on them so? Why, when they try to dominate those who by law are their rightful masters, we respond with such methods?

The Kylakas gestured to the political leaders of the conquered cities, nooses tied around their necks. "Why do we not answer indignity and disgrace with a whimper and yowl—why do we answer it with an eye for an eye?"

The ranks of the Phylaxana were assembled before him, the veterans of Degakheion's ascent. Centuries ago, Ormam had taken advantage of plague and catastrophe to subjugate them, ensnaring them in debt. Oktikheion had always been a thorn in the side—but Kimánis was the last straw. And now, it had all been made right.

The Kylakas smiled, raising his arms and asking the gathered soldiery, "So tell me, wardens, warriors—WHY?"

The crowd answered as one: "IT! IS! JUSTICE!"

The Kylakas smiled and waved to the executions. Wood sprang open, ropes snapped taut—and necks cracked, in perfect synchrony. The Kylakas turned back to the crowd. "But there are more than these profligates out there. Across the subjugated kheions, the merchant-lords, the sugar-traders, the silk-hucksters—they whisper discontent in the dark—they dream of so-called freedom! If we do not act now—do to them what they would have done to us—order will doubtless give way to chaos! So what will it be, warriors? Will you let treason and plot fester, grow, and spread, like a cancer of the Kaydhano?! Or will you CUT IT OUT?!"
