To goblins, the phrase "burying the hatchet" usually refers to driving an axe inside someone. Today, however, it seems to have taken a different meaning as recently one of our Sun Cult temples reportedly put aside its feud with a temple of their elven counterparts.  Two congregations of the Sun Cult, known for the violent history between them, arrived at their town's plaza. They began to argue, spit at each other, and proclaim threats of violence at each other over who has the right to the space for a religious festival. The normally firebrand clerical leaders from either side, in a surprising turn of events, managed to talk their respective sides down.  Worn down from the constant fighting, they came to the realization that they both wanted to use the same rituals for the upcoming festival and that for this festival the space can be shared. Because of the deescalation, a new tradition has emerged for the festival. As a show of good faith and peace between the two sects, the high shaman of our temple had his ceremonial axe buried beneath the plaza. By doing so, he hopes to bring an end to violence among brothers of the faith, giving new meaning to the phrase, burying the hatchet.
