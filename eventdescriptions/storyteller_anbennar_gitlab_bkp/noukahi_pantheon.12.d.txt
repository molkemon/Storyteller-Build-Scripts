The faces of old kings past stare at their ruler as they ascends towards the heights of the Emot Nepas Sedar. A particularly inebriated harpy at the mountain’s foot offers to take them flying upwards, although it seems like less of a transportation offer and more of an indecent proposal. Regardless, they gently rejects their offer, as the challenge of the climb is as much a part of the pilgrimage as the travel itself. As they ascends, they passes through more and more faces, first those of the leaders of Irkan Eparta, from the most forgettable petty chief to the greatest kings of the land. Then, at a height where the air gets thin and the city of Jezarpizu resembles an anthill from above, the faces take on new features. These are the faces of the harpy matriarchs of the Naleni flock. In a way, their ruler is walking through the carved history of Telekenid. Ascend a bit more, and they would find themselves in the harpies' roosts, but that's not their destination. A natural balcony separates from the path, a small shrine in it. As the Griots sing, it is here that Nzeri rescued her husband from the kidnapping harpy Nalayni. This is a shrine to unyielding love, but also to honour, pride and determination. Standing in the cliff, inches away from the sky, their ruler screams at the heavens, and proclaims their love, burning and unending. Love for the one that awaits them at home? For their kingdom? For something, or someone, else? That remains a secret between them and the Gods, as the proclamation dissipates into the sky. But there is one thing that all know. The ties of love proclaimed above these mountains cannot be broken, they are as eternal and enduring as the faces that adorn the sides of the cliffs.
