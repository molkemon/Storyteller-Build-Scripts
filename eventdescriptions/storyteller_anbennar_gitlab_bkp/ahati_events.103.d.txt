Panic had spread like a fever across the palace. The main foyer was filled with administrators and generals running to and fro, each trying to make sense of the civil war that the nation was collapsing into. The lone exception was Aakhet in his normal nondraconic form, who sat on the steps of the palace, watching the commotion while whistling an old marching tune. Upon spotting you, a large grin spread across his face. "What an almighty mess your predecessors have gotten you into," said Aakhet, clearly reveling in the chaos. "Would it shock you to learn that I had nothing to do with this? The Ahati may worship me, but I do not control their actions. It was only inevitable a string of weak rulers would eventually draw their ire. If your predecessor was still alive, I would be out there marching alongside them." He stood up and laid his hand on your shoulder. "I am not on their side. However, neither am I on your side. The only one who I stand with is the one who has the fortitude to see this war through. I am sure you shall miss my guidance and wisdom." Aakhet pretended to wipe a tear from his eye, failing to mask his grin. "But if you are truly the rightful ruler of our realm, then you will have no problems crushing this uprising. Without my assistance."
