For months, the Korashi forges roared with the hottest fire, the Oni slaves who worked them operating in perfect unison. Inspectors and detectives strolled through their ranks. Taciturn and staid, the Oni line-managers had nonetheless allowed complete access to their operations from the Shaman-hunters. It was as though they had known to expect them. But the engineers had turned up nothing—the forges were functioning at full capacity, producing the highest quality Korashi. Disciplinary assessments had revealed no signs of disloyalty. There were calls to replace the Oni (traditionally magic-users, and therefore Shamans in their own right), but attempts to substitute them with hobgoblins on the factory floor had resulted in a large increase of accidents and injuries. It was all very strange. The Oni line-managers were adamant: only workers from the Demon Hills, well-kept and well-fed, were competent enough to produce Korashi.

In the end, the workshops ended up with far more Korashi than they could ever store, and piles of it built up just outside the factory doors. Subjects of the Command seemed to take heart in this show of productive capacity, with many surreptitiously grabbing handfuls of the dark rock for their own benefit. Within weeks, the market stalls sold protective charms to keep safe from Shamans, jewelry and piercings began to appear made of the material: there were inkwells, clocks, furniture, even weapons carved from or decorated with Korashi. Paintings depicting the great mass of Korashi piled up outside the forges became popular in the Command. The Grand Marshal themselves kept a few on their person, for moral support.
