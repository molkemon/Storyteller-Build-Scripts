"Uwimbabazi, please, speak to me. What must I do?" Our ruler sat on their knees in front of the statue. "You have died, and left us to fend for ourselves. Why would you do such a thing? You were the greatest Ingo the Bongo had ever seen, and you sacrificed it all for what? Eternal life? What cruel form of immortality is this, then? To live foreverif you even live at alland be unable to communicate? That is madness." They stood. "Wait."  "Are you unable to communicate? Or are you choosing not to? Yes, that must be it. Why, then? I am the ruler of the Bongo. Surely, if there was anyone you would speak to, it would be me?"  The statue stood cold and impassive in the fading light.  "Speak to me. I demand it. Hear me, Uwimbabazi. SPEAK TO ME."
