The shattered remnants of Gor Vazumbrog stood in silence, its halls heavy with loss. Beneath the cavernous roof of the High Anvil Chamber; one of the few spaces left relatively unbroken; a crowd of somber dwarves gathered. At the heart of the assembly stood the newly elected lord, clad in gleaming blacksteel ceremonial armor engraved with mourning runes. The intricate carvings spoke of resilience, but their cold brilliance seemed to echo the chill in the chamber. An empty bier held no body, only the crown of the fallen lord and a tattered shard of his banner. Behind it, a slab of stone, cracked and blackened, symbolized the hold itself, carved with jagged cracks to mirror its wounds. Our lord stepped forward, his voice low and rough. 'Today, we bury what we cannot lay to rest. Our leader gave his life defending our home until his last breath. Our hold, our fortress, our lifeblood, lies broken, as he does. Yet even the stone that crumbles can become a future foundation.' The crowd stood motionless, breaths shallow, the flicker of torches casting fleeting shadows on the chamber walls. 'We are left with stone fractured and kin lost. But Gor Vazumbrog will rise again from the ashes; for we are forged in the fire, tempered by loss, and unyielding.' He lowered his gaze to the cracked stone slab and bowed his head. 'Titanium does not break.' The crowd echoed the words like a sorrowful chorus. 'Titanium does not break.'
