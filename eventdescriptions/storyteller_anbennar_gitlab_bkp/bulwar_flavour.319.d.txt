Even from afar one can clearly see the enormous granite rock, rising up from the monotony of the flat savanna to tower over the land, atop which sits Mekdirtara, the Temple of Purifying Light. Half a day’s journey from Mewojala, it is here that a monumental bastion of faith has been erected. Rising above the solitary rock almost two hundred meters above the plains below the temple, called Eduzsarellu, can only be reached by a series of stairs winding around the circumference of the rock, or a system of windpowered lifting platforms. Here the local priesthood of the Sun Cult watches over the lands of northern Denibari, determined to shield the lands under their protection from the malign influences that may emanate from the fetid southern swamps. And the people of Mewojala are grateful for their unwavering vigilance, and travel en masse to the base of the rock at each major festival day of the solar calendar, celebrating their resilience and faith in Surael, who wards them from the Malevolent Dark. It is during this time that enormous damask banners interwoven with gold and silver thread are hung from the edges of the rock plateau, depicting legendary tales of Surael and His Chosen emissaries.
