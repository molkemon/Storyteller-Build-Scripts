What, really, is a "best practice"? Who determines it? Why is it followed? These are questions no one seems to ask. Here is another question no one asks: what is the cost of following these socalled "best practices"? A slothly rate of advancement, countless invaluable enlightenments left undiscovered. A benighted realm, sluggishly dragging itself through the quagmire of ignorance. No longer shall we be held to the autochthonous "best practice", that ignoble pastism which dresses itself up in the language of safety. Ours is the endeavor of the future.
