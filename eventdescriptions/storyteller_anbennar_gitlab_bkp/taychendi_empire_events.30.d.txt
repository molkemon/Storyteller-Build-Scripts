Munatam had stood alongside a hundred thousand soldiers that day, on the fields of Teramzerai. A force commanded by the greatest warlords of the Empire, and led by our lord, a grand invasion force that would soon set off for the lands of the Lizardfolk. Our lord gave a speech, elucidating the higher purpose of the war. He talked about how the Precursors had once sundered the Lizardfolk, "usurped the golden scales," burned their civilization to the ground, and how by repeating and surpassing their conquests they could prove they had matched the might of Tacenie and Aesadas. When our lord had finished, Munatam cheered, chanting "glory!" alongside the rest of the army.

But in his tent that night, Munatam turned to his comrade as they were getting ready to sleep. "Ishran…" he said, "did you understand the speech today?" "Not a word," the Chendhyan replied, chuckling. "Do you know who Ae-sa-das is?" Munatam shrugged. "A flying city I think? That's what Tacenie was. But I don't get how this is supposed to be any more glorious than a normal war. I mean, in this 'Golden Scales' thing, the lizards had a big empire… but they're now divided, weaker than they would have been… right? And the Slaver-Nobles… was it even difficult for them? Surely we would need to—"

"Ah, Munatam!" Ishran cut him off in exasperation. "Stop wasting time thinking about things that don't matter. We're here to fight battles, kill people, and loot everything or everyone not nailed down. That's our glory—what more do you need?"

Munatam shrugged, looking down. "Well… I don't know. Just wanted to understand, that's all."

Ishran laughed at that. "Believe me, Munatam— you probably understood the speech better than the person who gave it!"
