Silverspite stood on a field, some days later, with much of the army in the region arrayed before him. The troops responsible for the massacre stood to one side in ordered ranks, though unarmed and guarded. The Kamrayakval's eyes flickered through the condemnation in his hands once again—then glanced at those it condemned. He saw their crime in their expressions... the blood they had shed. But he also saw their loyalty—to Dawn—to him. They would lay down their lives willingly if he told them they had done something wrong.

The armor on his shoulders grew heavier again, the iron grip tightening... the Dragon's claws digging in. Could he see the same loyalty in those vipers in the Kavalakuttam? Would those guarding the condemned deny dreaming of doing the same thing? Would those who had been trodden under Ameion's boots call this killing a monstrous crime, or justice for a greater one?

And yet... he looked back to Velara, seeing her rage replaced with a pleading confusion as her elven instinct guessed at his intention, sensing his betrayal. "El?" she asked, with quiet worry.

So long ago, Silverspite had watched a different pyre—seen a different body before it. He would be different—that was the vow he made. He turned to the soldiers again. There was more to that oath, of course. No matter what he felt, no matter the cost, he would not fail. And if the price was blood, and flame, and ruin... then he had no choice.

Silverspite tossed aside Velara's condemnation and spoke through his gauntlet instead.
