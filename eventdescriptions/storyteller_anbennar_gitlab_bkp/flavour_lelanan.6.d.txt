The arrows whistled towards me, hammering into my holy shield and further burdening my weary mind. I desperately sought an ally but was greeted with only death. Fellow daughters of Lel lay fallen in the blood-soaked mud around me. All around me were my enemies, their spears and blades poking and prodding, like a hunter wearing down their prey.

One of the hunters stepped too close, and a surge of my holy power instantly slew them. But I stumbled as my mind swam from the exertion; it was all too much, far too much. Too much physical effort, too much fighting, too much magic. More arrows whistled through the air towards me, so I abjured a new shield.

I let out a cry of pain and nearly toppled, looking down in horror at my chest. One of the arrows had struck too fast and pierced my heart with its hateful tip. I fell to my knees, then onto my side, the blood and mud splashing beneath me. I clutched at my chest, feeling my heart seize as it gave out, and felt ice-cold dread grip my very soul.

It couldn’t be… I had assured them, promised that I would return triumphant. I tried to drag myself through the mud to return to the warm arms of my waiting wives, but my body could only go a few meters before it gave out on me. I felt the blood-soaked mud on my face as the world grew cold, distant, and quiet. The Nakazy sisters were coming to take me away.
