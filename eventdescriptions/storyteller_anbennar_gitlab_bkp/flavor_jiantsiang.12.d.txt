Our ruler thought they knew the high temples, as well as anyone but the most learned of philosophers could. They recalls first seeing the massive, rustcoloured, edifices in the distance well before their armies encircled the city, admiring the way they loomed over all the citys mortal creations. They has spent hours watching the otherwordly spiritlight play across their facades, lending the landmarks attention even as they pawstamped the unending tide of paperwork required to see the greatest city brought properly into their rule. They has even read of them, and brought the most learned of shamans and High Philosophy scholars to the capital to further their knowledge. So, as they walks into the largest one, they is as calm as tiger waiting to pounce. It is not the first time they has gone where others dare to tread, our ruler thinks, and it will not be the last. They has broken fortresses centuries old, they has blazed a path as a conqueror wreathed in flame, and now they enters the greatest buildings as its greatest leader. But as they descends the twisting staircase, tracing one paw along walls that seem to hum; and whisper?; a slow feeling begins to dawn. This is not a fortress, it is not a city, it is not like anything they has ever known. And they has never walked anywhere like this. But someone has. Once before, a harimari emerged from brutal conflict to forge a realm. Once before, that great conqueror came, implacable and unstoppable, to the city. Once before, he entered these temples and penetrated their depths. And then Harimar the Great never returned. Or did he?
