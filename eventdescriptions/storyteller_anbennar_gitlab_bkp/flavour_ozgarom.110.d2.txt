We heard not a twitch from our informants among the maroons, the odd merchant or two that maintain the world of grey between our colonial towns and Bramash’s people in the sticks. No warning at all, until first one town, then another, fell to Bramash’s forces in the night. Indeed, the coordination of the thing speaks to a long-held plan—the occupation of entire realms, the simultaneity of the strikes. Among the rebels are many native Soruinics, those courted by the maroons in their jungle settlements. Ozgarom is rising. In the times to come, this moment will be pored over—how we prepared, what we did, who we armed. There is something terrible about the certainty of these upstart savages, the absolute conviction which drives their cause. Yet ours is the cause of order, and we will not go without a fight.
