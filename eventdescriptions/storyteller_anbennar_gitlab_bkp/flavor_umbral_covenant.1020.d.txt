The rift opens… and I can see. I watch the young woman arrange the bodies, two small and one big, in a careful row. The stars are magnificent in the desert, and since she lacks the strength to bury them all, perhaps she wishes at least to give them a beautiful view. The only real trouble, of course, was her husband—she slipped him wine less diluted than normal, smiled at him as they lay down together, and cut his throat. The children were considerably easier. She never took to any of this. She told her mother, so many times, that she never would.

Her magic is not developed, not potent, but it is enough to set fire to the corner of the tent, to watch it quickly go up in flames. A sense of utter relief suffuses her. The long nightmare is over. The young woman will miss none of these objects, none of these things; she watches the tent as it is slowly engulfed in flame. Eventually, she takes the hand of her lover, the traveler Komen. He has promised her travel beyond travel, the sights of all the world. There is something so shy and intriguing about his closed-lipped smile…
