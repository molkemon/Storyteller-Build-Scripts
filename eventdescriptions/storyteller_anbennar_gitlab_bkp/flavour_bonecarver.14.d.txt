It was another peaceful day in Bonecarver, the sky was a radiant blue, golden wheat aplenty was ready for harvest, children were laughing and playing in the fields while helping their family with work. Back in the grand temple, our lord sat in front of a roundtable, surrounded by their most elite shamans, all had a clearly frustrated look on their face given the lack of thundering skies and rupturing ground they had expected. "THIS IS TERRIBLE." One shouted in anger, releasing the built up tension in the room. "A year has passed, and no sign, no traces of the Dookanson's return." There was no clever retort, no reassurance. There had been too many before. The other shamans all just stared at the shaman who made an outburst, looking with disapproval yet also subtly agreeing. Then all suddenly stared at our lord, seeking their final say regarding the matter. It was clear that there was no more waiting, no more calling for patience. They needed an answer here and now.
