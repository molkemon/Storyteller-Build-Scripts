Moonlight illuminated the grand tower's throne room as Mihaela sank into the seat. Such a magnificent tower – she'd never understood why the Rakulacans hated it so. Perhaps she and Thelriatam could look out from the height later, watching the city below as a thank you for that incredible performance. Her full power, on full display – in the shadows no longer. The pride surged, as Mihaela grinned – she took in the tens of thousands before her, imagining the millions outside. She was delighted to find so many of the great houses and families of Taychend she’d read about in attendance. She spotted a familiar face in the crowd, too – the Pale Queen nodded to Theodore sína Trinás, her fellow Errant Prince.

The Blood Court would throw everything they had at her – but it would be different this time. She was not hiding. She was not holding back. And neither would her family. Thelriatam advanced towards her, crown in hand, flanked by the highest surviving ranking members of the Rakulacan. They were smart, cunning; they knew how to scheme and plot like no other. Thelriatam, the new Patriarch of the Rakulacan, was the best second-in-command she could ask for – especially because he so gladly took on all the boring bits of being a Queen. She bowed her head as he placed the crown atop it – and as he retired to stand by her side, she rose as an Empress.

Raucous applause. Cheering. Chanting. For a moment, elation – then realization, as she took in the crowd. Her son wanted this. A title. Autonomy. To rule above all others… above his kin, above his elders. Above Mihaela. She had argued with him, tried to talk sense into him, then abandoned him to the wolves when they drew near. And now… achieving what she had denied him, it all made so much sense. She closed her eyes – and when she opened them, she cast the regret back into shadow. The guilt remained… but now, it meant nothing. Umbral King or Pale Queen, it didn’t matter. She held an empire at her command.
