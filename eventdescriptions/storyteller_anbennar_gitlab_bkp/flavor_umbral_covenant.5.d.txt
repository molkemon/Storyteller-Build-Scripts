Of course I can do this," the diviner says, her squat face irritable as if the question is stupid. "I am not some callow student." She cannot know that the look she gives is just like Granny's. Yet it is utterly incongruous, for this young woman is young, plump, not even so wise-looking. Some upjumped village girl in worn sandals, with sweat stains under her armpits. There is a spot of blood on her neck which the girl has not bothered to wipe off. The Vile One finds herself smiling. There is more she must ask—she must find a way, quietly, to determine precisely just how far external magic can addle divination, and she must learn this without admitting who it is she fears. All of these questions swirl around and around, in the half-hazy way her thoughts run when she is concentrating on several things at once—there are so many to do! And yet, instead, the Vile One, Inheritrix of the Hags, asks, "Your name?"

"Ikomeye," says the young diviner, adjusting her wrapper. Her expression has not become any friendlier.
