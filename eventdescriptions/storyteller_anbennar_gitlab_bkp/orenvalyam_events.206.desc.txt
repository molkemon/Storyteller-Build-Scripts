The Kamrayakval gestured to the empty chamber around them. "You think your mouth could sweeten the traitors' words?!" Ceboranbu cried out, indignant. "Are we truly strangers, now?! I barely recognize you! That voice—"

Dark, metallic. Silverspite rattled a laugh with iron force. "It is the scream of wing-wrought Dawn! Our words power—"

"OUR?! By the gods, El—you're not even trying to fight it!"

"That fight is over—"

"You are Taychendi! The fight ends when you're dead!"

Shock, silence.

"Velara…" El realized. "She told me the same thing..."

The Dragon recoiled at the memory—drained the rage, left the dread. Elrandar turned away. "When I visited the Fateweaver… the Priestess gave me a prophecy. I couldn't understand it. Needed to see myself. So I did. The Dragon roared at the intrusion… but Fate told me plain."

He shook his head. "I didn't like what she said."

"Elrandar…" Ceboranbu asked, grave, voice trembling. "What did you do?"

A dead rattle. "What do you think?" The Kamrayakval stared off into the past. "I destroyed the Fateweaver. Killed all who tried to stop me."

But that wasn't what he was asking. "And then Velara arrived… saw. And she'd had enough. Said that I was an eclipse against the sun… that I'd damned us all… that my mother would be ashamed of me… that I had failed her just as my father had. And I couldn't take it."

And he couldn't take it back, any more. "So I killed her."

Disbelief. Dour silence.

"I covered my tracks well—obscuring steam, scorching smoke. Convinced myself that she'd been tricked by traitors, and the Dragon forced my hand. All lies. Doesn't matter. Because you know what? Everything she said was true… save one thing."

El turned at last to his once-friend. "I am not Taychendi, anymore. I am Nayiru reborn. I must be…"

The metal cracked, for just a moment: "How else could I have done all this?"

Ceboranbu stared vacant. Whispered quiet: "She was right. You were weak…"

Silverspite smirked, so slightly. "Velara had that look too. Alone, the hatred. The pity."

But the High Kavalal just shook his head, and drew his sword with a trembling hand. "No… not pity."

The Kamrayakval saw the shadows shift around him—the Oren Neraghul, come to slay a tyrant. "It is the courage. And in that, she will never be alone."
