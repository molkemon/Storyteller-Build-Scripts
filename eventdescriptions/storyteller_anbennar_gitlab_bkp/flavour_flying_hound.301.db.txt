The riders were splendid in their armor, late afternoon sun glinting off of steel and highlighting the colors of their province. Your elite guard was all saddled up and stood at attention beside their mounts as you passed—but to you, they were far more than that. Many an hour you had spent sparring, dining, and discussing with these great warriors, many of whom were hopeful of one day winning the grand tourney and claiming leadership of the government. Though they wore stoic faces now, if you were to flash them a smile, you know that you might evoke a reaction, for these were your noble and chivalric brethren, who were as dedicated to you as you were to them. Many times you have toasted each other's health, ridden into battle in tandem, and emerged from the crucible of war together, stronger. When you have been wounded, they have rescued you; when one of you has fallen, the rest have borne the body of their gallant companion away.

Memories of these deeds of triumph and sacrifice are of course grossly exaggerated, with much of them still being contained to the realm of imagination and revisionism of the early days of the clan. But when one starts doubting, suddenly the Muncher and the Worm are there beside them, patting them on the back, reminiscing of the times when together they fought and routed many a scoundrel away from their home. Whether adventurer or orc, no one dares doubt the charge of the resplendent goblin knights against other claimants to the ruined kingdoms of this land.

You find your knights looking at you, their faces impassive but their eyes intense, waiting for a word from their commander. These goblins will follow you into hell, for you are their knight, and they are yours. Together, you will be the living representation of mounted chivalry—and in your thoughts and, hopefully, deeds, you will outgrow the legends of old. Figure of speech, of course. A goblin on a dog is still smaller than just the horse of an Addener Knight.
