Make no mistake: there is no such thing as indestructibility. However, even so, our valiant soldiers do not waver on the field of battle. They gallantly and selflessly march forth, always the first to expose themselves to danger, always the last to retreat. They fight with a bravery only known to themselves and the invincible, closing their own wounds and advancing atop gleaming refurbished limbs. Yet when the fireballs cease to whirl and the arrows cease to rain, our battalions march home just as vigorously. Because our soldiers know they are not indestructible. They are merely irreplaceable.
