For centuries, the unimaginable riches of Verkal Gulan have featured prominently in the legends told at the camp fires of the Masnsih. But though their nearlegendary status hid many of their shortcomings, tales of constant strife and civil unrest have reached far beyond the Hero's Gate in recent years, even catching the attention of the Divine Herald himself. Since her exile, Khedira, the granddaughter of the deposed Gilded King Thorar Goldhands, had shown her true devotion to Surael and His truth many times over. Proving her worth, she eventually petitioned the Divine Herald to bring the teachings of the Jadd to her fellow Gold Dwarves, and, incidentally, restore her family to the Gulani throne. With the imminent threats to the continued existence of the Jadd dealt with for the moment, our ruler agreed to lend her support. As the corrupt Goldlords and their nominal leader Urothr closed the Hero's Gate to the Jadd's missionaries, a show of force was deemed necessary. After a protracted siege, the internal quarrels of the Gold Dwarves erupted into an attempted coup - the instigators of the plot, failing to seize the hold itself, threw the outer gates of the hold wide open in order to flee with the Desert Legion surging in after them. Facing a determined opponent and reliant on exhausted and demoralized troops without hope for reinforcements, Urothr bowed to the inevitable and negotiated the surrender of Verkal Gulan - on one condition. By marrying Khedira, he has become the new Gilded King for a new Gold Hold, and has eagerly taken to learning the teachings of the Jadd. Henceforth, Verkal Gulan shall submit to our ruler's authority. While they support the spread of the Jadd on the surface with regular tributes of gold, they shall become the instrument of Surael in the Dwarovar, and drive out the Darkness from beneath the mountains - gold, after all, shines brightest under the light of the sun.
