The outer walls of Elizna had fallen hours ago, and the alarming sounds of battle, panicked citizens and collapsing buildings could be heard ever clearer even deep within the bowels of the royal palace.  Haamida cowered with the other scullery maids and assorted other staff of the royal kitchens in one of the better secured larders, shuddering with each booming sound and scream reverberating through the walls.  Safana, one of the more highstrung kitchen maids, suddenly broke down crying: ‘Why are the Irrliami attacking us. Whatever have we done to deserve this?.’  One of the more senior butchers, Kenmu, scoffed at this: ‘Haven’t you heard about the massacre of Medurubar? Back then, near the end of the war with Irrliam, our troops butchered every living soul they could get a hold of when they stormed their royal palace, including the royal family itself. Now they’ve come to return the favour.’  His disparaging reply prompted Nusaiba, assistant head cook and inordinately proud of it, to chastise him: ‘Stop spouting that nonsense, you’re scaring everyone witless. Good queen Zelaria would never have allowed such an atrocity.’  ‘Allowed?. She ordered the whole damn thing, my grandfather was an aide to one of the generals tasked with taking Medurubar, and he witnessed her orders, loud and clear. You best pray to Surael that the Irrliami are in a forgiving mood when they find us, I know I wouldn’t be.’  With that last outburst, a tense silence fell upon the gathered servants, and Haamida was once again lost in her fears and fervently praying for a mercy that the house of Vulzinzuir and all of Elizna in all honesty did not deserve.
