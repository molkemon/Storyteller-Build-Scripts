The first receipts have been received from the Xia pilgrimage route. Pilgrims, tempted into visiting by the promises of our enlightening hot springs, have been more than willing to pay the tolls. In addition, the modest prices provided for state-run amenities along the road will contribute a very small, but not insignificant, amount of money to the treasury. More importantly than the money, the process of creating this route has also established friends. Y33, home of the master Prahavan, has been able to position itself as a major stop on the route to Root. They have been interested in meeting with us to ensure that they are able to keep their newfound spot.
