The summit trail was rough and steep. Loose stones littered the footworn path up the rock face, often sharp and jagged in a way that cut even through the toughest footwear. Our lord grunted, grasping a makeshift staff, and pulled himself up above another boulder. What had possessed our lord to make this journey? It was not for the same reason so many other pilgrims had dragged themselves to the oracle of Tughayasa - our lord put no stock in Raheni divination, formidable as their magics may be. Seble had always forged her own destiny, and in her name our lord would do the same. It was the sky itself our lord sought, atop a mountain so high it dwarfed anything she might've found in Madriamilak. Throughout the campaign in Dhujat, our lord had nursed a doubt, a gnawing unease in the pit of her stomach. Could Rahen really be the holy realm, as much as Madriamilak was? Our lord stumbled as she rose above the crest of the next incline, clutching her staff to maintain balance in face of the sudden drop before her. It had arrived already - the peak. Nothing else rose above her, nothing else but the sky. Our lord stretched out her hand towards it. "Amilak, lord. Are you here?"
