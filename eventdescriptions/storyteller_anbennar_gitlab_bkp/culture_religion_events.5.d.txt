The presence of an openly heathen leader in our capital has not gone unnoticed among the subjects in our government. Among the orthodox this has come to be a common matter of irritation and discussion and in areas with existing religious tensions it has lead to even greater frequency of conflicts. A priest is reported to have attempted to further inflame the situation by repeatedly returning to the capital in agitating speeches to the people of a rebellious province. At this point it might be hard to remove him from his position without extensive use of force.
