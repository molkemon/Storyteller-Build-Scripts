The steady stream of courtiers and petitioners that attend upon our lord in their audience chamber often seem to blur together, and common is the day where the ruler can only recall a handful of faces they spoke to that day. But the same does not apply in reverse, obviously. With the picture minted on every coin a constant reminder, even the humblest peasant is expected to be able to recognize the ruler. Normally this is a privilege of the rank; but tonight, it has turned sour. The hunt was proceeding apace, and our lord could almost taste the fresh blood that seemed so close. When, suddenly, the door of one of our capital’s many houses swung open, and the brandished torch within lit up their face. The dooropener, still in their sleepwear, gasped and exclaimed "My ruler." If not immediately addressed, this could lead to some awkward rumours, and perhaps it would be the one snowflake that triggers an avalanche of suspicion. This is not a risk our lord can take lightly; what shall they do?
