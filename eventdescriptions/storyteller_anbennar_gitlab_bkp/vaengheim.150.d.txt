Now did our lord hunt the great blue Roc, and sought Blåfjöður in the depth of the sky. Yet the sky was vast, and Blåfjöður had greater wings than any wingmaiden. Oft would the hunt find him in flight, but he would flap his wings mightily, and in haste escape pursuit. And our lord did say, "I am greatly vexed, for we have met our match in heaven. Yet a Roc cares not for what lies above, for none would strike him from the skies. Fly high, my sisters!" Then did our lord and their strongest wingmaidens take wing to fly high, higher than ever before, and they suffered greatly in the cold sky. Yet the fire of the hunt did warm their hearts, even as their limbs grew cold, and their breath was stolen from them, and as they saw Blåfjöður did they dive, striking the Roc frightfully from above, and dealing him his bane wound.

Yet high was the price paid for our lord's plot, for the wings of their wingmaidens had frozen, and as they slew Blåfjöður did they join him in death, striking the earth mightily with their bodies and shattering like glass on anvil. Only our lord lived, for they had pierced Blåfjöður’s great heart with their spear, and were thus shielded by his great body. And they named the field in which their friends had fallen "Featherfall" in their honour. For their valour, our lord took the name "Rocstalker."
