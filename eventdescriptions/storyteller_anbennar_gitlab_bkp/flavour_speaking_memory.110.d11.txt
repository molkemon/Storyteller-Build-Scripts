Long ago, before the cities rose, Anakue was an island much smaller than it is today. It was an island of prosperous people, who fished it in its waters and used its land to grow great bounties of fruit. All that changed when the Eel Serpent Puhiloa arrived. She was as long as a hundred boats, and in her cruelty, she began to eat all of the fish in the sea to starve the island of Anakue. It was then that a baby boy was born. He was the fourth of quadruplets, and he was a tiny, runty thing. His parents loved him dearly, but they were already starving, and he would never be able to work on a farm. So they cast him into the sea.  But then, a miracle happened. The boy was saved by a mother Hinuiseal named Kupamakua, and she and her eleven children taught him the ways of the sea. He learned to outspeed Puhiloa, and soon he was known by his seal family and by Anakue as Silakai Sealkin.  Silakai travelled with his family for a while, but when they came back to Anakue the island was on the brink of famine. Silakai could not watch them starve, and he and his family tried to help Anakue as best as they could. One day, Silakai’s youngest sister, named G’uwai, was killed by Puhiloa as retribution. In his grief Silakai attempted to recover her body, but all he could find was one of her ribs. Silakai knew then that Puhiloa had to die. He fashioned his sister’s rib into a great hook, and Silakai dug that hook into the seabed and rode on his mother’s back around Anakue. His siblings pushed on the seabed with all their might, and soon the seabed  began to rise, and Puhiloa in her arrogance was trapped on it. She drowned to death in the air, and Silakai carved the flesh off her bones and fed it to the people of Anakue.  This is why Anakue is now a great island, and it is also why the island of Anakue still remembers the mighty deeds of Silakai Sealkin.
