For as long as the codexes record, we have returned again and again to Bani. This is where the flow from Lake Chakantu enters into the ocean, one end of our web of trade. We have never maintained a capital in the sense employed by the lake folk, a great piling of stones; but Bani is a place of significance, the place we visit to make decisions which affect all the clans at once.  Where else, then, to create the first permanent qhatuwai? All cloth must begin from one corner, and from the Banche cape we look out onto an Amadia prime for the weaving. What we make of our homeland will be a tough and vital fabric, not easily torn asunder. As we pound the earth flat and leave behind a few families of each clan, a promise is made.
