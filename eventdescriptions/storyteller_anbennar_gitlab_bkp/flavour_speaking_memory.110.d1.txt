In the lands that would become the Mokaika Islands, there was once born a boy named Mokai. He was a hearty, healthy boy, and his mother was exhausted after giving birth to him. So Mokai picked her up, and he carried her home. Mokai soon grew up. In each of his muscles he had the strength of twenty men, but also the wisdom to know when to use it. He travelled across the land for two and twenty years setting wrongs to rights, until a band of raiders came to his home. Mokai was a strong swimmer, and the raiders were not: Mokai knew this. So he brought down his fists, and he parted the land with his strength. The raiders drowned, and from then on Mokai swam from village to village instead of walking.  Eventually, Mokai swam to the Hinuilands, which were plagued by a race of monsters called the Holohana. The people there feared one Holohana most of all, which leapt across the land in great bounds and crushed the forests beneath it. Mokai knew this could not stand, so he ran across the Hinuilands for days and days until he came across the creature, which was leaping towards a village which would be its next meal. Mokai went to the village, and the next time the beast leapt up, it had a passenger. With each bound the beast took, Mokai crept closer to its face, until, while hanging off one of its eyelids, Mokai swung his fist with all his might. The beast bled geysers from its eye, but Mokai crept in through the wound and punched his way out of its skull. The beast did not threaten any more villages after that.
