It is a nice day. There were few clouds, and though the sun shone brightly, in the shade of the forest it created a climate that was hard not to appreciate. Even so, however, the lord was not here for pleasantries. After many months of searching, it was here that a unicorn had finally been tracked. The animals of the wood left them, as if they recognized the righteousness of the deed ahead. There. Grazing placidly on the grass before them, stood a unicorn. Its mane was pure white, with a brilliant horn atop its head. They understood that there was to be only one chance at this, and so began a long series of carefully rehearsed steps. They knocked on a nearby tree three times. The unicorn’s head shoots up, gazing into the brush. With slow, measured steps, they stepped into view, raising their arms in an inviting fashion. The unicorn gazed closely at them, peering into their very soul. There, in response to what it sees, it reacts by.
