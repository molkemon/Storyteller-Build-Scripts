"500 crowns" The Mercenary's invoice, never delivered.  Unaccustomed though I was to the politics of state, I was not so unaware as to dismiss the urgent request by a nameless contractor; these sorts of private requests are known to be the preferred method of doing business by certain senior members of the government. Desirous of secrecy above all else, the government regards all that it touches as being either clandestine and hidden, or revelrous and proud. Never anywhere in between, for even the smallest of sexual indiscretions by ministers are treated as state-destroying secrets.  Still, the curses and promised banes upon the contract for disclosure were somewhat harsher than usual, and the request odd considering my expertise. My task was to decipher the notes of a missing researcher, evidently a madman who babbled of psychography and existence before the womb. I felt a strange aspect upon his journal, a certain ill wind as though it had entered my abode uninvited. Yet too polite to reject the guest, and thinking the task too lucrative, I nonetheless accepted the offer.
