I watched at the side of the makeshift stage with Narawen as one of my pathfinder’s answered questions to the gathered Drennen clanselves.  "So, the outsiders make agreements and then write them on paper?"  My pathfinder nodded to the Drennen elf in the crowd.  "Don’t they hold each other to their word?" another asked pointedly.  Shrugging, the pathfinder explained, "Apparently there are too many elves, humans, and other creatures for that to work."  There was a murmur within the Drennen group. I smirked as I saw even Narawen shake her head. An elf’s word was their life in the Great Salla. Yet, beyond the woods, that was far from it.  "There are many names for it, but the most common one we heard it called was ‘Feudalism’. Adopting it can apparently be up to fifty percent more effective."  "Tulmun shit." a wizened druid cried out as more disgruntled murmurs filled the air.  Narawen nudged me as the pathfinder attempted to wrangle the crowd, "Is that really true?"  With a toothy smile, I answered, "It is. After adopting it, we found it easier to build new buildings, organize new units, and trade."  My cousin nodded slowly, not quite convinced. Undeterred by this, I returned my attention to my pathfinder who had finally gotten the crowd to listen again.
