In the end, it is so simple. Komina does not incant some grand ritual. She does not walk before Mama Hireet and Granny Ilfa and scream herself hoarse at their treachery, at what they have hidden from her, of how they have allowed her to suffer without any understanding of her plight. What happens now is a feeling, not an act. A thousand times in the last millennium and a half, Komina has stormed out of a meeting with Ilfa and Hireet, cursing their names, and nothing has happened. She could not truly imagine an existence apart from them, and so she could not create one. Here in a quiet place, Aunty Komina betrays her coven-sisters. It is but a thought, a moment of sincere desire to be done with them forever, and the bond is sundered. What a terrible thing, that something so powerful should die in silence. How strange it is that the ground does not shake.

Komina’s eyes are hooded; she looks upon her niece not with affection, but with wary acknowledgment. "We are at peace," she says, quietly, turning her veiled face away. 

The Vile One asks, "Where will you go?"

Komina smiles thinly. "Not your concern. Nowhere that will hurt you, Niece."

They do not speak for long—it is not that sort of meeting. The Vile One has given Komina truth, and little else. The receding silhouette of the Vile One’s Auntie is a lonely one, swallowed up swiftly in the gloom.
