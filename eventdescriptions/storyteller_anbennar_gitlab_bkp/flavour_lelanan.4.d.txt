I sat in the corner of the tavern, sipping on my honeyed mead, watching the people of the realm of Kunolo go about their days. Not so different from Lelanàn itself.

But on this third day of reconnaissance, I noticed the subtle difference. Sitting at the table to my right were a halfling and a tall human. I couldn't determine their genders; clearly, they were lovers, holding each other so tenderly. As I looked around to see what others thought of them, nobody paid them any heed at all. I was the only one surprised by their bold differences, and I looked down at my mug thoughtfully.

In Lelanàn, such a sight was not impossible. I knew that fact all too well. But there remained a sense of ostracization, of othering towards those who loved differently. I, Leli, and Felana had felt this, and maybe the City of Love itself still had more to learn about love.

After all, love must be unashamed. And here it was on full display for all to see—a perfect manifestation of unashamed love and an example for all to follow.
