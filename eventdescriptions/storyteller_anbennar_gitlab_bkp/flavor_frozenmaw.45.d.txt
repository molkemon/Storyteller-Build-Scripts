Faced with such intransigent subjects as the Reachmen, one must learn to read actions, not wait for words. Across the Reach, lords appear to be storing food and materials, not selling quite as much to market after harvest, holding back the proceeds of their mines and fisheries. The merchantcaptains of the Giant’s Grave somehow know to carry iron on their ships; a worrying bellwether by itself. The meetings our agents had already noted have increased in frequency, and when the Reachmen travel, they do so by quiet paths, avoiding the main arteries of trade.  The evidence has built to the point that our clanchiefs must acknowledge it. The majority have thrown their weight behind significant measures to bolster our vassal governments in the Reach, but they will look askance at indiscriminate expense. We must bolster what we can, and accept that not all of these grim folk will ever be appeased.
