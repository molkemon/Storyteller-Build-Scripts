She waited for me at the altar; a simple thing, halfsubmerged in the waters of the Ynn, just as we were. Despite the rapids around me, I had never felt so stable. I didn't even feel the need to look down, to watch my step, such was my faith in my closest friend.  The officiant stood a head above us, facing away from the Divine Waterfall. Normally, we wouldn't be so close, but Mitrenya had offered. Perhaps, that way, my mother could serve as a witness.  Not that we needed another. The riverbank was as crowded as the end of the Ynn had, perhaps, ever been. I gripped her hands as I reached her, running my thumb against her palm. I didn't let go.  Mitrenya spoke first, as was the custom for Lords and Ladies.  "As the sun rises and sets, I vow to be your light in the darkness. As a knight defends the innocent, I vow to be your sworn blade. As the river runs eternal, I vow to stand alongside you until it bursts its banks and floods the hills and mountains. I speak these vows over the waters of the Ynn, that they may be carried until the end of the world. With these vows," a single tear dropped from her eye, rare as a diamond, "I thee wed."  I cleared my throat. My voice, in spite of it all, was as strong as I'd ever managed.  "As the moon waxes and wanes, I vow to be the peace to your storm. As wounds are washed away, I vow to heal and soothe your body and soul. As the current ebbs and flows, I vow to be your anchor against the undertow. I speak these vows over the waters of the Ynn, that they may be carried until the end of the world. With these vows," I leaned forward, whispering the words into the space between us.  "I thee wed."
