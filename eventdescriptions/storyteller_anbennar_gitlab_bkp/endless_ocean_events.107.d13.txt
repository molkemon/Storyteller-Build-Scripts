The Ten Thousand Isles have as many heroic epics as shores, and these tales have long been hoarded by the heroworshipping adherents of Speaking Memory. In fact, some of the oldest heroes of Kanolowele predate the existence of Speaking Memory itself, instead taking to epic feats of adventure from their worship of the Endless Ocean. It’s inevitable that as the wakes of our ships cross, the culture left behind is a blend of our values. Little wonder, then, that some of the Endless Ocean faithful would take inspiration from the great individuals whose seacrossing quests spanned all Kanolowele. Those who practice the Veneration of Heroes adopt a story– or more specifically a virtue– held by one of these legendary figures, and seek to emulate that quality to its fullest. Such aspirants are honorbound to embark on nomadic quests to serve the weak and inexperienced, in the hopes that by training in the footsteps of the grand heroes of yore, they may prepare themselves to guide fellow souls during the strenuous journey to the afterlife. The greater the list of deeds, the more capable a captain an adventurer will prove themselves. Likewise, their righteous deeds take root in the minds of the young. In every seaside village across Kanolowele, playing children imagine themselves the epicenter of great tales and noble efforts, and though few will take to the seas in pursuit of glory, all will fondly recollect the myths and emulate the morals of their childhood.
