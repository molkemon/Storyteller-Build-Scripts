The shadows fled across the desert, and the dreaming man followed. The lord was not sure who they pursued, only that they would destroy all they held dear. Sometimes the shadows seemed an army, and they could almost see purple and maroon banners waving. Sometimes they seemed a warband, and they could hear chants of a foreign tongue, the howl of fierce wargs. The lord was not sure why they pursued them, only that they dared not stop. Sometimes they could hear the cry of a sister, and would double their pace. Sometimes they would hear groans as if parents and ancestors all suffered ahead, and would push themselves even faster. The lord knew not what would happen if they caught up, but they narrowed the distance all the same. Out of the shadows, they saw glimpses: bloodstained axe, savage teeth, a knife that would only ever find your back. And yet they closed. They neared. And then; Tripped. Fell hard onto the sand, their pack spewing its contents out before them. They lay there a moment, two, feeling the grit against their cheek. Then a flash of panic stirred them to their feet, and they could see their quarry growing ever distant. In the ground before them lay a sword, wellused but still sharp. And a crown, ornate, a work of unparalleled beauty and majesty. Before they returned to the endless chase, they took up a tool, never to let it go again.
