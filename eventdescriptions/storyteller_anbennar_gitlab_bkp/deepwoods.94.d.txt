The Deepwoods are restless, and so are our people. With spirits darkened by hardship, even the youngest among us feel the sting. ork children are usually taught to be tough and quiet, however many have begun wailing in the middle of the night, frightened of shadows, whispers, and sometimes even their own clanmates. Some elders mutter that softness is seeping into the new generation, while others say it is simply the cost of too much bloodshed. One orkish tribesman suggests a new coming of age ritual, one that would harden the youth, and drive away the shadows haunting them. He proposes that the children be taken deep into the woods at night, to face the forests whispers and return only when they have proved their courage. Others insist that the youth need time to recover from the endless bloodshed. A third voice calls for another solution; If the children cannot fight yet, maybe they would be hardened through labor until they are ready. Now comes the time to decide.
