Erankar the Defiant, our king, rode through the streets of Nanru Nakar, his army at his side. He could now add "Taker of Nanru Nakar" to his titles—much sought after but rarely achieved. To walk among the towering spires, stroll the winding streets, and sit on the throne beneath the Vanetavanem, a thousand feet of soaring steel above him—this ambition was fulfilled. Though a decade late, he had triumphed and entered the city of cities in victory. The three Mudaliar brothers stood before him, heads bowed, sullen in defeat—but they were not dead. There was no need to kill them; keeping them in power, pitting them against each other, dividing and conquering—this strategy served him well. Erankar's smile widened—a rare sight indeed, but today it was justified. Victory rarely tasted so sweet.

Finally, he reached his throne, once used by great conquerors and emperors like Varanash Mudaliar, Olhokar the Fortuitous, and Ultarlan the Decimator. As he sat, he felt the weight of the past on his shoulders and the promise of the future before him.

Erankar called the three brothers before him, bidding them to kneel. He knew the strength of Nanru Nakar would be invaluable in the coming wars. The question remained: how valuable was the strength of the Mudaliars?
