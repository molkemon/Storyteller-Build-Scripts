Our enemies live where the wide grass of the plains tapers into bridges of grass, which they have fortified with the fervor of cowards. Yet despite their pathetic appearance and weak government, the Triunics know a fundamental truth—there is not room in these plains for centaurs and humans both. Our eternal cycle of conflict will only end once one of us has eradicated the other. The victor will accumulate the rich rewards of conquest, and the loser will be driven from their domain.

The Wild Hunt has two goals. The first goal is to claim our right as centaurs of dominion over Yarikhoi and all who live on it. This alone would be sufficient for some, but not us. Our second goal is to make the Triunics pay, as they would do to us if we ever grew weak enough to give them the chance. The Triunics must die, but that does not mean they must suffer. We will make them suffer for fun.

Across the plains, we have made contact with others who feel the same. All of us are agreed: now is the time to strike. We have learned from the lessons of the First Incursion, but its goals remain as urgent as ever, and now is the time to achieve them. We will send out the call to our allies. We will assemble our host. We will ride to the border at Jogligkunazmoq, and we will kill their first defender. We will send the rest of their kind to join him.
