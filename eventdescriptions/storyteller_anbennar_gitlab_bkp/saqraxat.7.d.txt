"I was so foolish. This curse is more than an annoyance. It’s an opportunity. A call. Those witches have made the matter clear to me." The Medaka stands before a ritual circle. Gnoll shamans, bound in manacles, lay out bones and candles in intricate patterns under the watchful eye of their guards. A coterie of anxious advisors and courtiers stand around, nervously looking back between the eerily silent Medaka and the gnoll’s feverish preparations. All at once the Medaka steps forward and the shamans back away. Curiously, their guards now subtly usher the group towards the circle. A ritual was performed, and an offering made. Blood was taken, and more was promised.
