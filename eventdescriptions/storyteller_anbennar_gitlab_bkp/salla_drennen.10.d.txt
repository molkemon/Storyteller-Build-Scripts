What did I think of the second coronation? It was a bit direct, don't you think!? I thought this was supposed to be my interpretation! But fine, it's a good tale anyway. Honestly, the term "coronation" is a misnomer—I mean, obviously it was a coronation, I’m not denying that—but it was nothing like the first one! Not that I was there at the first one, but everyone knows that story. Endless debate, bitter feud, and one shining young woman, with a heart full of hope and a tongue of silver.

Sorry, I’m getting off track. My point is that the second coronation was everything that the first wasn’t. Where the first was grim and morose, the second was joyful and radiant. Where the first was tense and fragile, the second was relaxed and calm.

We were there—all of us—not to save ourselves from tumbling over the edge, not to hold in the last breaths of a dying people. No, we were there to celebrate. Drennen, Elyn, Myna, Cenag, Ayeth, Fealgarn, Ghul, Scotha, Ahain, all of us came to the coronation with a common purpose. We came for a coronation.
