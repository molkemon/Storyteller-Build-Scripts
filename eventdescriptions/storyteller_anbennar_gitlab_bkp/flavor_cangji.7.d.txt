After many long years of consolidating our power, we have at last extinguished all threats to our state and gained control of their lands in return. We now stand as the rulers of all Gozengun, from the ancient lands of Nomsyulhan to the thriving societies of Khaashalg and Gowon. Even the harsh lands of Moduk and the fearsome birdriders of Shuvuush were no match for our magical prowess and military might. But as we celebrate the triumph of our order against those who wish to break it, another feeling is present among those who made Cangji truly great.

Many new peoples and societies have been integrated into our lands, bringing us true understanding of what it means to worship the spirits and commune with the other side. These cultures have given us magical secrets, economic practices, construction techniques, and military tactics we could not dream of imagining from the quiet halls of our mountain temples. This may be the zenith of Cangji, but we would not have climbed to this peak without the steps carved by the many races of Gozengun.

In times like these, we must not consider ourselves conquerors of the hapless unenlightened of the valley, but the new stewards of their lands, taking on the responsibility of other nations who came before us. This task is a heavy burden to bear, but now we know we need not bear it alone. Let us walk forward together, leaving our old history of domination behind and entering a new era of cooperative growth!
