Spikelined pits, acidic landmines, drakefire, thousands upon thousands of spears and arrows and slingstones; what conceivable hell have our troops not endured to claim this measly stretch of land? The diminutive lizardpeople, whom gnomes named "kobild" for lizard and who thence took the name "kobold" as their own, fight for their land so fiercely, you'd think they might have done a bit more with it. Their farms are smaller than the cottagegardens of the Small Country, their villages dot the landscape more densely than trees, each boasting scarcely a hundred persons each. The only truly impressive thing they have to defend save the traps themselves are their mines. Their impossible anthill excavations bore through bedrock to haul thousands of pounds of every metal imaginable to the surface, and for what? To build more traps. These beings are an enigma, but they're our enigma to unravel now.  As we consolidate our foothold on the Dragon Coast, we must make a decision about how to deal with the kobolds for the longterm. Some call for them to be wiped out, though that would be no quick and easy task even if we do dedicate the military to such a purpose. Another faction argues they should be driven back; generations ago, they emerged from under the mountains, and countless hundreds of thousands are said to still dwell there; surely they can survive returning to their own homelands?  Others yet argue that these are ultimately people. Strange people, about whom we know rather little, and who seemingly know even less about us, but people nonetheless. They can learn our ways. They can adapt, and so can we. We could give them a bit of space, earn their trust, bargain with the local tribes sorry, trivuak and integrate them into our society. Who knows what kind of marvels they might work on Halann, when they stop spending all their time building traps?
