It was a sunny day in Wineport, and their ruler had chosen to receive guests on the palace verandah. They were excited, for they were to receive an elven mage from a distant land; One supposedly old enough to have seen the Aldresian Crusade firsthand. Of course the delegate hadn't arrived yet, but if their ruler had learned one thing of Elves, it was that they enjoyed theatrics. As such, their ruler halfexpected that this mage would simply pop out of thin air in front of them, the moment their visit was scheduled. This was not exactly what happened; there was no pop. Instead, the wind picked up for a moment, and the air folded double in front of their ruler's table, perspective shifting and curling imposibly into a sphere, before coming unwound with a sound like tearing cloth. And then, everything was normal again, aside from the elf now standing on the verandah. Their ruler made sure to look suitably impressed. "My lord, I am pleased indeed that you accepted my invitation. As a fellow vintner I have been beside myself with excitement." The elf, an older suntanned woman, bowed gracefully. "It was a tempting offer. Your humble city has built a reputation. With so few years left, I must be practical. And all wine flows here." "Indeed, indeed. Shall we get to it then?" "Yes. The Varilleni. I confess, I have never seen its effects on a human. You simply must tell me your every experience as they occur to you. I shall be here, pen in hand, to document." They had a moment of trepidation as the wine was poured. There was a glint in the old elf's eye. But surely it was just wine. Possibly the best wine in the world, if the legends were true. "Well.Moment of truth then." "Yes. I believe it is."
