Ecaris Ecarizuir stood before the Kavalakuttam, clad in pale gold. He calmed his nerves, took a deep breath, and tried not to repeat the mistake Corin made at Elynaile. "When we came here... we saw a desolate land, shattered by centuries of strife. We knew our duty, our purpose. We took steps not everyone here agrees with, but we fought for what we – what so many of you! – believe is right! An end to slavery. Justice and order!"

One of the Kamrayakvalists interrupted. "And the obliteration of our culture! Our traditions!" A furor of support and calls for silence followed. "You were interlopers!" he cried, before the Kamrayakval shut him up for good.

The Herald grimaced. Be diplomatic, or speak from the heart? No, he was here about duty – he wouldn’t shirk it. "What does it matter?!" he called. "So what?! Why should I apologize for what I think – for believing that in Bulwar, we found the best way? For believing that Surael’s path, as handed down through the cult and the temples, was the righteous one?!" 

"Your order goes against everything we should stand for!" came a call from the Monasticists. "Harnessing the products of slavery through that sun-plate of yours... blasting past any opposition, any native disagreement, by taking the lone example of support and using it to prove your rule! FORCING people to conform to your path! And if you are not amongst the faithful, and you are not native to this land, and you are intent on your errant ways, I say get off this continent!"

There! An opening! "And so you'd expel us by force too?!" Ecaris called. The Monasticist shot back, "You came here by force!" But the Kavalakists now jeered him quiet.

Ecaris pushed through the reprieve: "By Surael, we are not a conclave arguing over lentils – the fate of my people is at stake! YES we made mistakes, YES we differed – but if you hold any stake in your idea of this ‘chaotic form of Light’, then you will know that to err is natural, and to differ is in your dawn's blood!"
