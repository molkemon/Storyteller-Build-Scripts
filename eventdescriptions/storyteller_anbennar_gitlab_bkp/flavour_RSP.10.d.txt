There's only so much plunder a ship can carry before it starts sailing like a drunken pig. Holds fill, pockets sag and even the most ambitious pirates must admit that stealing six gilded statues and the governor's wine cellar might be a tad "excessive". So, after one of our raids provoked the vigorous rearranging of valuables in the province, one enterprising captain did the very sensible thing of burying the overflow; chests of coin, strings of pearls, jewelstudded idols, tomes of spells and more. All tucked neatly beneath the sand, to be reclaimed once the ship rode light again. Yet, the captain never returned. Storm? Mutiny? Card game gone wrong? No one knows. What matters is that we have the map they have drawn and somewhere in the province, there's a treasure that already belongs to us; it just hasn't realized it yet.
