With a quiet incantation, the mihipha ignite the flame. Blue light spills over the room, casting ominous shadows over the chained figures hunched over pillows. The shadows belong to the Ilitray, or at least as many as could be captured alive, the guiding council of Rayafo. So called philosophers and wisemen, now brought to bear witness to true wisdom.  'As Kolo's champion, I shall take the role of speaker on his behalf.' I declared, a choked sob coming from one of the nearby Ilitray. Within Nakintas temple, we'd gathered 300 prisoners of war to witness this 'True Discussion' as the Rayafosi called it. A self aggrandizing ritual with which they claimed to speak to the gods. 'The first topic of discussion. Who is the greatest of the gods?'  In turn, each of the captured Ilitray answered. 'Kolo, father of us all.' This would be their easiest answer.  'And who am I, who has reclaimed Kolo's temple?' I asked.  Hesitation was met with reprisal by nearby guards, and each Ilitray eventually answered 'You. You are the Kolovadoy.'  'And why has Kolo's wrath fallen upon the people of Rayafo?' I asked.  Finally, their answers diverged. 'Because we fled Saikinoma, as cowards.' 'Because we doubted Kolo's champion.' 'Because we have lost our way.'  Each of these answers was acceptable. 'We stand, now, in the shrine of Nakinta. The World Shaper, he who built the earth on which we stand. Do those who have angered Kolo deserve such a shrine?'  There were gasps of horror from the crowd, but perhaps there was some truth to the Rayafosi belief this ritual granted heavenly wisdom. For they answered correctly, if reluctnatly. 'No, they do not.'
