Zokka watches from across the great pyre. His gaze follows as the Pack Lord paces back and forth, maw dripping, saliva sizzling where it falls from his lips and hits the blackened ground. The air is ripe with the scent of elf and harpy flesh, halfcooked over the hellfire. To the assembled gnolls; his father's greatest generals and advisors; this is a feast, celebrating the greatest victory the pack has claimed thus far.  To Zokka, son of Zokka, the smell is repulsive.  He knows he is a disappointment. He knows his proclivities are unlike the rest of peers, preferring quiet contemplation and meatless meals over the savage brutality they favour. A vegetarian gnoll. What a joke. What a failure.  He knows all of this well. And yet, no matter how hard he tries, he cannot change.  His father lifts a hunk of flesh, almost as large as Zokka's head. Blood still coats the muscle and sinew in a sticky, sickening glaze. The boy almost gags, but keeps his composure.  "The future belongs to me. We all know it." The Pack Lord speaks, voice guttural. All chatter ceases, replaced by jeering and hollering. Zokka's own cheer is halfhearted, easily drowned out by the din. "But the First Sun still rises. My work is not done." The celebration rises to a fever pitch, but his father's words echo around the pyre, as audible as ever.  "As Xhurtivert before me and the Great Xhaz before them, Bulwar is mine to claim." Zokka shivers, despite the growing heat of the fire. "At dawn, with the light of hundred pyres, I will proclaim the new Xhaz; the last Xhaz. And all shall know. All shall understand."
