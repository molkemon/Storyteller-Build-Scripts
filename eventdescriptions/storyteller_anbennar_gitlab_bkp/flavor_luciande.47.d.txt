Kneeling, Balin Hardhammer was all alone in the dimly lit room. With a heavy sigh, he lowered his head into his hands, covering his scarred eyes. Looming over him, a statue of Balgar the Builder, the entire room a shrine to the former dwarf now considered a deity. "This old fool once again comes to you begging for his sight. I know it is my fault, I do, but my hands do not. They still ache and yearn to craft, to forge. You have blessed me with such passion yet I wasted it with my own mistake," he spoke, feeling the scars covering his eyes that left them useless.

"Is that what you wish for? Your eyes to work once more?" a booming voice echoed off the walls, seemingly surrounding the dwarf.

"I need it as I need to breathe. Who am I without my forge?" Balin responded to the voice with a slight panic, a part of him questioning whether his prayers finally reached above or if this was merely a dwarf in the room with him.

"I cannot give you eyes, but I will give you sight. Rise," the mysterious voice responded.

Balin rose with a nod, uncovering his hands and turning his head left and right as if to look for the source. Lucian strode forward slowly, his fangs bared.
