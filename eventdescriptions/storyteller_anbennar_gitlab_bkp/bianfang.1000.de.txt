The ship of state sails under its own wind, and a new hand on the tiller can only change course so much. Whether commoners view our ruler’s government as tyrannical, benevolent, or something in between ultimately comes down to the laws of the land, the bureaucratic and judicial apparatus that administers them, and the local magistrates who enforce them. However, the occupant of the Dragon Throne still holds great sway. Our ruler can issue proclamations, appoint, dismiss, banish, and execute officials at will, and use their supreme authority as they see fit. The realm anxiously awaits which direction the newly crowned ruler will steer our nation. In recent years, the people have found our government to be solidly pragmatic, neither overly harsh nor particularly lenient, ensuring the rule of law is followed to provide security and prosperity for the whole of our nation. Our ruler, with their inclination toward using means both kind and cruel to build the state's strength and authority, appears likely to strengthen our pragmatic governance.
