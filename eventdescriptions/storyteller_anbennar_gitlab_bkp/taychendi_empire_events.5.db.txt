The communion was over in less than a minute. In a heartbeat, everything suddenly stopped—the Threadbearer released her hand from the Fateweaver’s corpse, and the raw, rushing power of magic ceased, motes and wisps all that remained. After taking a moment to collect herself, she turned and descended the steppes silently, her face an unreadable mask.

"So?" the ruler asked tentatively. "What did you learn?"

Would the ruler's empire fall? Would they need to sacrifice their firstborn son? Would they be unkillable until they turned 61 years old? Or would they see unparalleled success—glory and riches unrivalled?

The Threadbearer paused for a moment, her eyes moving to the sword at Denariatam’s hip. "Your armies will be uncountable, their advance unstoppable. Your empire will march to the ends of this world, conquer lands not yet seen by Elvenkind... but you will also fight enemies closer to home."

She looked up into the ruler's eyes. "Enemies await abroad—and daggers, of fanged teeth and shadow-shrouded steel, await you at home... waiting to strike, and waiting to take what they think is theirs." The Threadbearer gave a little bow, her prophecy relayed, then began to walk back to the chamber.

"Conquest, and steel, and daggers in the dark..." the ruler said to themselves, as they looked back to the great statue behind them. After pondering for a moment, they smiled. "Yes... I think I can work with that."
