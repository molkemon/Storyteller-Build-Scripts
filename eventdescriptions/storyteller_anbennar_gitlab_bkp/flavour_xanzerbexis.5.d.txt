Coria Pearler squinted into the midnight gloom, as the horse pulling her family's wagon stopped abruptly.  Behind her, her husband and children were sleeping, and ahead, the road fell into darkness. And yet, a sound. Paws on gravel. Coria cocked her crossbow. Her horse whickered nervously. Something grunted in the darkness. Something big.  "Peace" a voice rang out from the murk, heavy and accented. "Your tongue, foreign to me. You Koria Pearler, yes?"  "Who asks, lurking in darkness?" Coria's voice was even, braced by a courage she did not feel.  "I Uren Vrestukex. No wish to scare your horse." The voice was circling, now off to the left. "You go north, many gnolls there. They kill any man they find. You want to flee Businor, but I tell you, stay. You belong to Hillthrone. You our pack now. One of your cubs become Vrestukex. It will be a good life."  "But not a free life. We will be slaves."  "Not kerho. Vraka." A pause. "Farmer, in your tongue."  "And you will just let us go home? Why should we trust you?"  "No trust. Your cubs become Vrestukex. Our cubs become Vrestukex. Then maybe, trust. Think, before you go north to die."  "What is this "Vrestukex?" Coria's voice rang out in the night, but the darkness offered no reply. It was only when Coria pulled the cart off the road, to wake her family and debate this ominous visit, that she realized one of her sons was missing.
