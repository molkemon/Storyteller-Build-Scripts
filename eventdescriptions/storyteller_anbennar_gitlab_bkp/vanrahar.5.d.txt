Our Raja sits upon the Lotus Throne, the seat of power in the Harimraj. They feel uncomfortable with all the finery they wear and the luxury around them: a simpler environment would be much preferred. Still, this is the traditional way of ruling; this is how Harimar himself ruled, and it would be disrespectful to rule any other way. But just because they had to rule in luxury didn't mean they had to act like the decadent Rajas of the Second Harimraj. There wouldn't be compromises nor pardons for treason. If you did not ask permission, you would not receive forgiveness. It was the Raja's duty to rule in a way that reminded the subjects what a true monarch looked like: not serene and aloof like a Royal Harimari, but a cold and hardfaced symbol of strength, unflinching and unflappable. The robes and throne may be the same, but the Raja that sits thereon is most certainly not.
