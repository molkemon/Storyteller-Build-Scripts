The adventurers contracted to explore the inner structure of the Temple of 4871 have been stalled indefinitely due to unforeseen developments. Some of the buildings within the temple lead into a network of underground tunnels, flush with traps and assorted valuables. A few of these traps had been activated prior by careless treasure hunters, but a close encounter with one facilitated the first delay; the adventurers insisted on a slow and systematic exploration of the temple, so as to minimize risk to themselves.

Though vast, the true extent of these tunnels cannot be determined— all tunnels converge into a single, large chamber with a magically sealed pair of doors. Blocking the door was a statue of a dragon, which appears to have nine gems carved into its anatomy, each one embossed with a different symbol. Seven of these gems were glowing a shade of bright blue, and two more were much dimmer, periodically flickering. Nothing we have tried, magical or mundane, has been able to breach the door. The statue simply will not budge, and even if it did, it is unlikely that would dispel the magic around the door. It would appear that investigating these gems is the only way to further explore the complex. 

Report from Captain Lin Yiu.
