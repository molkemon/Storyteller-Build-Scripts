What was once a mundane waystation along one of our trade routes has recently made a name for itself as a centre for the collection and distribution of scrap items, drawing mystic artisans from far and wide.  The gnoll responsible for this centre of recycling is a young priest who specialises in creating works of art from scrap material, who originally set up the centre to collect refuse materials from travellers. We could commend this gnoll, offer to financially support them, or even offer them a place at our court; such an innovative mind would serve us well.
