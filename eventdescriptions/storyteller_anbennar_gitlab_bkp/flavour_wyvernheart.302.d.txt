A dispute rages in the Council chamber today; tension can be felt in the air as wands are risen, ready to unleash a variety of spells. A scene that isn't frequent, but not a first in the Council. No: the shocking thing is that the councillors involved in this squabble are neither the fussy Wealthy Scions, nor the bombastic Heartmages, even the usual crowdrousers are quiet. No, both sides of this argument are Devouts of the Flood. Tears of grief and anger spill out as they shout at each other and exchange blows. "Were we not those that heralded the Flood as that which will change Halann? Was our devotion not enough, and punishment is upon us?"; lashes out a Devout. "We are Devouts of the Flood, and its avatar, its voice still leads us even if the skies weep no more."; chastises another. A profound schism has occurred, their belief shattered in pieces, and now they try to build themselves a new identity from the ashes. The reason? The skies are clear; the Crimson Deluge has ended; the Flood is over.
