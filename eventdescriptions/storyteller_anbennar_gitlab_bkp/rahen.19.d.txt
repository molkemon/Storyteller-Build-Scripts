Journal Entry, 43:  ‘This has been a long and adventurous day. Last night, our ship was attacked by a group of pirates. The alcohol must have done quite a number on me because I could've sworn that they have wings growing out of their backs. My crewmates have told me that I’m a lunatic, and it may well be true because two nights ago I saw a glowing light in the shape of a giant whale while passing the Ringlet Sea.  Our supplies were dwindling since our last stop. If we didn’t make land in the next couple of days, we might as well join the creature of my imagination in this cursed sea. As if the Dame herself heard my plea, we saw ports filled with ships of various makes, carrying goods in and out of the harbor. Beyond them, we saw a city with many distinctive structures, chief among them a massive and intimidating tower, piercing towards the sky.  We landed at an outlying dock, towed into port by a series of small ships and pilots guiding our vessel along the open channels of the harbor. Greeted by one of the dockworkers speaking in a language we didn’t understand, we were  finally able to communicate when we learned that they can speak sparsely in Bulwari . While we were trying to learn more about this area, the harbormaster finally came, and what we saw was beyond our imagination. A tall creature, with striped body and feline head, clad in fine silks and wearing ringlets of brass and gold. This is a harimari, and this was Rahen, the mysterious land from Jaher’s stories.’
