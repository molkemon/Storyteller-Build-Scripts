My lord, news has come from the Grand Hall in Abeesooyinka. Many civilians have been complaining about the overcrowding in the city due to the Grand Hall and its scholars. The arrival of caravans has only worsened the situation. Therefore, saaxirs and scholars are being sent to other major cities nearby until this issue is resolved.

Many of the people coming to our realm are of Dhebiji descent. The Dhebiji have a history of violence and aggression against us, the Ashamadi. Even in modern times, certain groups continue to perpetrate violence against our people. Some in our court are nervous about harboring Dhebiji in our lands. Even if it’s just a possibility, some wish to avoid any risk.

What should we do, sire?
