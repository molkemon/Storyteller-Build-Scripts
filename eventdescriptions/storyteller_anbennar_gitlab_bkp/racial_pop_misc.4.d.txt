Hither and thither, thither and hither. Your diplomats work at a frenzied pace, sorting files and searching shelves. Their assistants rush by, carrying several pots of everyone's favorite beverage. You spy a diplomat hurriedly putting on a change of clothes as he gets into a carriage to be shipped off to some foreign land. It is said that one day in the service of our lord our ruler is equal to a year in the court of a lesser king and should it not be so? Truly, a sight as grandiose as this brings prestige, efficiency, and splendor to our lord. An assistant rushes over, bearing in their hands a document commissioned by none other than our lord our ruler. The title: Sustainability of Working Conditions in the Department of Diplomatic Relations. The conclusion: no.
