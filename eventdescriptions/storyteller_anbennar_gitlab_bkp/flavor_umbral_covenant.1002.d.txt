The rift opens… and I can see. The Tanizu beastfolk flee through the underbrush, their shifters flanking the periphery, as if there is any real defense against the nightmare which chases them. Though their brief, miserable exchanges speak of a necromancer, I can taste Ilfanill’s magic in their air, in the dead things that even now close in. Through the rift, I can see that these dead things will swallow up the entire swamp in nightmare and fear, extending my Granny’s domain. I watch a child trip and fall; I watch her be left behind. Was she the daughter of someone already lost, her new guardian not quite dutiful enough to return for her? Or have even her parents been harried into unthinkable cruelty by the chemical logic of survival?
