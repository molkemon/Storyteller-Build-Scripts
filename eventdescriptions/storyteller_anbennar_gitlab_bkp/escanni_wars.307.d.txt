Life is starting to return to normal in Escann. The dead are buried, the fields plowed, and the forges cooled as an entire subcontinent licks its wounds. Now, we are starting to see the results of this newfound unification. As our lord is holding court, the country's finance minister walks into the throne room, his clothes lavish enough to rival the finest robes of the nobility. He kneels before the throne and says, "My liege, trade is bustling across Escann. Ever since you've united the subcontinent, we've been seeing some new developments. The tariffs that used to restrict production and isolate us from the rich fields of Ionntras or the bountiful mountains of Gulenhyl are gone, and now Steelhyl alloy can be used by blacksmiths in Castonath to forge tools of exceptional quality." He takes a breath before continuing, "We've seen our revenues balloon in value as we can now export our superior products throughout Cannor and the world. Despite these promising signs, the Reach and the Empire have both blocked us from entering their countries, supposedly in response to the threat we pose to their own markets by flooding them with our goods. I'm sure with time you can convince them otherwise." As the finance minister leaves, our lord has a decision to make. The nation's coffers are overflowing and it would be a waste to let it sit in waste. They could spend the money on improving roads, standardizing measurements, and lessening some sales taxes. Bolstering the army to fight the nation's new rivals is also a sound option. Finally, investing the money on internal projects to make the cities more prosperous and improving the lives of commoners might lead to an even greater return down the line. No matter what choice is made, our nation will enter a new and prosperous era.
