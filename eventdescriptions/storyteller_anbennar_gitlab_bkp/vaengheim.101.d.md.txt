It was in the days of today, when our queen ruled. And our queen gathered the people of Gerudia under her wings, making them hers. And whether mannish, trollish, or orcish, many found their knees before Vængheim, and they were better for it.

One day, a troll named Freydis Oaktongue came to Vængheim. And she knelt before our queen and said, "O queen, I come to you from the forests of Ðigoveða, where your name is spoken fondly. And we sing songs of our forefathers there, who ruled this land long ago. And I would gather a host and wander to Gullmork, there to delve into the bones of the earth. For I know that there lies the house of my fathers and mothers, and of those who gave my people breath and purpose. And I would see this place with my own eyes, and I invite you to share in the bounty of my toils, if you will lend me the strength of your house."

Then our queen rose and said, "Well you speak, o daughter of giants, and your name is well chosen, for your tongue is wise like the oak. Your strength will be ours, and the strength of any in this hall who seeks the bounty you offer. For we know less of your people than we should, and the gift you give is a rare thing."

Then our queen called upon those in her house who sought to know of the world that was, and many skalds were counted in their host. And they set out for Gullmork, with hearts and minds ablaze with the flame of knowledge.
