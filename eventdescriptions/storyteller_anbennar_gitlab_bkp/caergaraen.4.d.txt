"And Munazhlan said: beware, my children, the darkness that clouds your heart, that binds your soul to Nalne Maedzae. Cast off these chains with might and magic, and with faith and fury concoct the vital potion of your freedom from the blood of those who have denied you, and who have tempted you to deny yourselves." – Kaemzhlaemat Munazhlan, the Pillars of Munakles.

Rakkabuttai burned. The Chendhyans had stormed the city at dawn, breaking down walls and gates with shaman-force, then pouncing on the Bloodcloak defenders. The fighting had been difficult, bloody, fierce, as jaguar-maws and salvaged Kheionai swords met great-sabres, met plate-and-mail. But the memories of centuries of ceaseless raids, of brutal reprisals, of slavery and subjugation, carried the children of Caergaraen to victory.

And Caergaraen's retribution was swift and merciless. No elf under arms was spared. The heir of the dynasty fought hard to storm the great stepped-temple to Olhokar near the centre of the city. But when the heir did, they could see the carnage, the imminent slaughter, from its peak. Grim-faced, they raced to the green-flamed beacon slashing near Rakkabuttai’s citadel.

The heir came to their parent as the monarch stood over the corpse of the Bloodcloak King – the monarch's jaguar roared as they saw that King's wife and young son held beside, waiting to be judged. "No, wait! We fight for our liberty, for retribution, not for wanton slaughter! Send the women and children west, exile them from the steppe, but no more! You hold the sword of Munazhlan in your hands! Do not sully it with a crime like this!"

The monarch looked to their child, glanced at the burning blade for a moment, then turned back to the captive Taychendi.
