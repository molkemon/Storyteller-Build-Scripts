Memories come rushing from an era past as the ruler leans onto a statue of Calrodiy the Great. Centuries ago, his imperial majesty conceived a capital at the fork of the Arver and Lun rivers, at the site of his victory against Chieftain Koda. Arverynn it was named, after the greater of the two rivers, and for centuries it stood not just as a shield to civilization, but as its beacon. Art, ideas and sciences flooded the streets, creating a fertile substrate that let flourish the greatest minds and talents ever seen. From all across the land they came, pouring into our streets, eager to speak in our courts, to dream in our houses, to sing through our gardens, and create things so beautiful they would echo through time. Even now, we stand in the shadow of imperial Arverynn's pedestal: as great as the victories achieved, as bright as the hope instilled, we have no great bard to sing of our deeds, no master architect to present us with the plans for another Teal Keep. Will we ever stand beside that pedestal? If only. if only.
