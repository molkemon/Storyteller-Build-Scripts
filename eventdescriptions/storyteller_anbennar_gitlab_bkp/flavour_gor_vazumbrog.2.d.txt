The repopulation of Gor Vazumbrog is underway, and though the work is arduous, the spirit of our people shines bright. Generations have passed since the hold's fall, yet the Titanium Dwarves have answered the call to return. The largest groups hail from Seghdihr, with unmatched determination to return to their ancestral home. Their songs of old bloodlines echo through the halfrebuilt halls.  Others have come from across the Serpentspine. From Ovdal Kanzad, with their masterful metallurgists; from Ovdal Lodhum, sending hardy miners and administrators; and from Argordstun, their gem crafters and stonemasons already leaving their mark on the new structures. Quite a few have journeyed from Bulwar, having lived among humans on the surface. These dwarves, though strong in spirit, struggle to adapt again to the underground's dim light and confined spaces, but they bring valuable trade connections and fresh perspectives.  Most surprising are those that survived in small settlements in the Serpentspine. These survivors, rare and reclusive, bring tales of endurance and forgotten customs, enriching the hold's rebirth with ancient traditions.  The work is far from complete, but Gor Vazumbrog's halls are alive once more.
