In the very beginning, there were only two creatures in the world: God and his brother, the antiGod. God wished to create, and his brother wished to unmake, and so from the first moment there was conflict between them.  God made, and his brother crept behind him and seeded imperfections in his works. God made again, more carefully, and his brother found new ways to corrupt. And so it went, each creation more labored than the last, until at last God turned and fought his brother directly.  The battle was fierce and long, and when it was over, something strange had happened: the antiGod had shattered. Where once there was one, now there were many. Each fragment was a lesser darkness, but darkness still. And these antiGods could not be destroyed just as their father could not be destroyed, and so God did the only thing he could: he sealed them away together in the deep places of the world.  But the fighting had been fierce, and God's creations were in ruin. So God began again. He took the raw stuff of the earth and sculpted us with loving hands. Then he brought each piece to the fires of Mojobaa. And Mojobaa swung his great hammer and his fires blazed, and life coursed into each of those pieces of clay with a love that only he could provide. In this way the world was populated with many wondrous things.  And God's elder creations helped him in this work. They sealed the antiGods away into the elements, driving their darkness into the deep places so that life might flourish above. And for a time, everything lived perfect and unchanging, warmed by the heat of Mojobaa's fires.  For a time.
