Day ???: It has been many days since I last ate. I can't think except for the hunger. I write only because I do not know if I will survive—if I do not, then whoever reads this, know that I was Suresh of the Stalking Way, leader of the Shaman rebellion, and the only Shaman the Command ever feared.

Day 1: I have found my way back home, thank the Gods. My mother and father welcomed me with open arms, and I must have eaten half our rice stores today. I'm starting the day over again, to forget. Kunya, if you're out there, forgive me. I am a wounded man now, and I shall do my best to live as you would have wanted me to live. I pray that Nermali preserves you and our child, but I must be realistic...

Day 30: Life settles back into its old routine. There are some new faces, some missing ones, but not much has changed in five years. I tend the fields and pray, just like before. Folks stop and stare—it must be a shock, to see me back and with a lame leg. They certainly don't seem friendly. My family's taken to encouraging me to stay indoors more, supposedly on account of the lameness, but I'm strong, and young still: I should help where I can. Kunya would want it of me.

Day 45: I've been forced to flee the village. Some uncle or cousin ratted me out to the hobgoblins, they must have. I told one to stop staring—he must have gotten mad, though I've heard that there was also a monetary reward attached. Drat! What has the world come to, where one can't trust family? Luckily, I have a friend who is willing to hide me. Picking my way across the streets, I can't believe how prevalent the hobgoblin influence has become. Every town crier seems to end their mornings with invectives against the Shamans...

After-incident report: A team of hobgoblin Shaman-hunters conducted a random search of a household, an altercation ensued (details unknown). Among the corpses, we found this note. We should consider this 'Suresh' at large and a present danger. Recommended bounty: 30 coins.
