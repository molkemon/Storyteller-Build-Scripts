It had taken Asima several years to finish laying the groundwork. Having the support of the court was crucial in this undertaking, especially so that things went smoothly. Her son was growing up quickly and would soon be ready to take the throne himself.  She stood quietly at the heart of her small estate, a tense expression on her face; a slight breeze rustled the tops of the bamboo grove. Just as the wind brought with it a change of the skies, so too had she brought change to Endhuvi. A light step caused her to turn back to face the young, goldenfurred face of her son, a questioning look aimed in her direction.  "Is everything alright, mother?"  "Yes. The stage is set, now all that remains is for everyone to play their part. Including you."  The young Harimari gave her a slightly reproachful look; Asima's face softened. She opened her arms and he leaned into the hug. A clawed hand lightly scritched the top of his scalp, just like he'd enjoyed as a young child.  "It has been a long journey for us both. I know that you've had to play many roles, even before you knew whose play you were a part of."  He gave her a small squeeze and leaned back to look up at his mother,  "It's all for a good cause, mother. I share your dream of a world that everyone can contribute to."  Asima's eyes watered slightly, and she hugged her son even tighter.
