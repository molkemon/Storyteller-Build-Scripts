There's a party at the Red Citadel. All the eunuchs and the royal dynasty are celebrating. The ruler excuses himself and leaves to his room. Inside, he's looking at the Mask of Many Faces, pondering all that the empire represents and how overwhelmingly competitive ruling it is. He thinks of all he had to achieve to become a legitimate ruler and compares it to what his predecessors accomplished. He ponders this cycle—every ruler must fight against the legitimacy of the previous ruler and those who ruled thousands of years ago. This endless madness will one day end in blood and fire. What happens when there are no more titles worth claiming? No more lands to reclaim? What happens when the task becomes impossible? He believes every lie his dynasty and the recordkeepers have been telling, sending him into an emotional spiral of madness that ends with him about to jump from the alcove.
