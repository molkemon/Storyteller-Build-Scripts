The Huli-Jing have known about the Gumiho for some time now, but they have been loath to travel by sea. Now that they have a safe land route to their province, they have informed us of our duty to escort some of them up north to our embassy. The reunion of the Huli-Jing and the Gumiho was an affair not suited for the ears of a maiden (suffice to say, there was a lot of alcohol and a similarly large amount of broken bones), though it was without a doubt successful. The Huli-Jing who were escorted up north have become a permanent fixture of the embassy, in addition to the servants required to keep them sated. The Gumiho, recognizing our superior position in the realm, are amenable to negotiating a more formal subordinate relationship.
