While the pinnacle of Necromancy is Morgurax's Lichdom ritual, the most iconic spell is Animate Dead, which has its roots in ancient and revered Milcorissia. While Morgurax himself reworked what was once an elaborate and time-consuming ritual into a spell that could be cast in a minute or two by even a middling mage, he did not change the core application: reanimating a humanoid corpse, most generally by binding the soul that originally inhabited the body. Since then, necromancers have frequently found themselves hunted by the ignorant and narrow-minded, and so relied on the Animate Dead spell to create bodyguards to protect themselves. However, here in Esthíl, we have the luxury to consider what purposes the spell can be put to, other than bodyguards. Already, there have been discussions as to what labor could instead be performed by untiring and compliant undead. A few initial experiments have been carried out in fields, such as making skeletons pull ploughs in the place of oxen, but several skeletons were required to replace even a single ox.

This inspired a question: what if we could bind orc souls to animal corpses, such as oxen or horses? In theory, such bodies should have greater strength, and while they would be unable to use tools, their bodies should be more suitable for a great deal of work, whether that be pulling ploughs, hauling carts, or even just being used as pack animals. The necromancers are eager to experiment with this idea and have asked for plenty of souls to be made available for these experiments.
