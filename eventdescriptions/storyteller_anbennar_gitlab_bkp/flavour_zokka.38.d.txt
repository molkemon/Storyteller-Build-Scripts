The waters of the lake are dark now, slick with ichor. Lights flicker in the distance, across the water, pale and trembling like dying stars. One by one, they are extinguished, replaced by specks of emerald green. The table is set, on the shore. A triumph. Their lord sits at one end. Their lord stands across from them, refusing the offered chair. They've already eaten; this feast is for the pack, as is the spectacle. Gnolls and favoured slaves alike brawl over slabs of blue meat and steamed vegetables, laughing and howling and tearing into the spoils like starving things. Their lord watches them with something like pride. Their lord watches them. "What are we leaving behind?" their ruler asks. They grin. "Bones and dust." They give a slow nod. "And when the bones are buried, and the dust blown away? What then?" "Then there'll still be a crater to remember them by." They replies. They gestures towards the lake, and the skyline. "Still water stagnates. It could use some fire." Their lord toys with their goblet. "It could use some direction." "Oh? More wayward souls for you to shepherd?" "These lands had order. Centres of learning. Temples older than both of our bloodlines. We could govern them; build on them." They snorts, voice coming sharp. "Carve around the rot, and the core festeres. Burn it all and be done with it. Salt it, if you must." "And then what?" They doesn't deign to answer. The table creaks under the weight of the banquet. The lake is still.
