As the Council comes to a closure, political topics start replacing the religious ones, and again, a new debate is threatening to halt proceedings. The matter this time is the organization of the Cult, more specifically, what type of hierarchy will be established, as the different sects and temples have been virtually independent for centuries. Some advocate for a more decentralized hierarchy, with a Synod acting as the supreme authority of the Cult, others for a High Kasra, a head priest elected by the state to act as the Isiak Surakes, the head of the clergy. There are intermediate options too, as some want a High Kasra, but elected by the temples, while the clergy from the Suran plain support the idea of a High Council formed by the Kasra of the main temples of Bulwar.
As before, the ruling monarch should publicly support one of these options to end this discussion and allow the Council to conclude.
