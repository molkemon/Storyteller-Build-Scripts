The ship of state sails under its own wind, and a new hand on the tiller can only change course so much. Whether commoners view our ruler's government as tyrannical, benevolent, or something in between ultimately comes down to the laws of the land, the bureaucratic and judicial apparatus that administers them, and the local magistrates who enforce them. However, the occupant of the Dragon Throne still holds great sway. Our monarch can issue proclamations, appoint, dismiss, banish, and execute officials at will, and use their supreme authority as they see fit. The realm anxiously awaits which direction the newly crowned ruler will steer our nation. In recent years, the people have found our government to be ruthless and tyrannical, compelling loyalty through fear, with dissent and weakness being cut out with a razor blade. But our ruler, with their disposition toward virtue and compassion toward the commoners, may shift our rule toward one of benevolence.
