Aucanna had devoted her life to Istralore, not just the capital, but the entire realm. Reviewing border guards, tracing potential attacks, visiting returning adventurers from Escann to hear their stories of Corin, and countless other tasks that took her from the castle in the capital to the training fields, to the point where the monarch thinks she was half-raised by her letters. But she will ride out no longer.

Aucanna taught the monarch that there is a tone a doctor uses when they should be ignored in favor of duty, and another tone when the alternative is death. The words the doctor delivered today were soft, but undeniably the latter. Her latest bout of illness had passed, but could and would relapse soon. Without specialized care, she would soon be dead. Aucanna furrowed her brow to hear it, but nodded. Two hours later, she was on the training fields. She is still reviewing the troops, still sparring with Warsingers, still discussing planning with generals. After all, she learned at twenty what it is like to lose.
