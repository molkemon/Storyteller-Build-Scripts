The rift opens… and I can see. A courtier and a prince sit in a leafy courtyard; orange light fills everything. The courtier is a handsome young man with a smile that doesn't show his teeth; he leans in close, a confidant. The prince glances up at the colonnade before he responds. 

"You really think so?" 

"You can do anything, Dejen," presses the courtier. "It is the place of the ruler to receive revelation others do not—to access wisdom others cannot. To rule without being beholden to the trivial concerns of the gentry."

"Even against their will?"

"Especially then!" The courtier's eyes flash. "You are no patsy, Dejen. You are the bravest man I have ever known. There is no place for doubt in one who seeks to do great things."

Dejen looks to the colonnade at the other end of the courtyard; it is the open face of the palace, gazing out onto the hills of Simegasa.

"You're right," Dejen says, and the Komina-courtier grins.
