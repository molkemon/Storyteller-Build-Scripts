Now many feasts were made ready, and many celebrations were had, for the one-armed skald had warmed the heart of the bereaved dragonkin, and they were glad to rest under Vængheim's wing. And all knew that soon, summer would return, and Gerudia would suffer no longer. And summer did come, and it was mild and warm. Some said it was colder than most, but many thought these were the words of old, bitter souls, whose bones held a chill no sun could touch.

But then winter came. With winter came snow, and the snow fell in thick blankets. It was said that the air danced with such chill that even the Giant's Grave froze solid, so that Olavlunder and Reachman needed neither ship nor fins to walk across it and visit the hearths of one another. This greatly vexed our lord, and the dragonkin assured them that it was not their songs that brought winter to their door. Our lord called to their council many skalds, and the cold yet nipped at them, lending urgency.

"Once you have sung songs of ill omen to me, and I have heard and hunted, and thought to make us safe. Yet she wakes."

Our lord gazed at each face in turn, seeing dread within, finding it fair, for this doom was dreadful. Then they said, "There is aught else in my power, for the sky is my domain, and Elkaesal rests beyond my gaze, under the bones of this land. There is another way, though it is a dire one."
