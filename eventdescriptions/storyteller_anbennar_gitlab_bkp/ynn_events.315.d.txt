It rips the nets. It smashes the fishing canoes. It drags the harpooners to the depths, sometimes taking a whole ships with them. The dreaded monster of the local river appears to be completely uncatchable, despite the best efforts of our lord’s men. The residents have began to mutter that this is a sign the river does not favour us, a minor inconvenience compared to the economic consequences of a stretch of river void of transport vessels.
