"I’m telling you, inquisitor. None of my brothers have ever had something to do with wizardry," said the man. He was quite obviously a bandit, or some sort of conman, thought the inquisitor; but he had nothing on him. Nothing at all. "Isn’t this quite enough, goodman?" said the couter. "You have no evidence against anyone here." "They’re quite clearly lying to me." was all the inquisitor said, through gritted teeth. "Inquisitor, you must understand; we’re just working men, that’s all. Banditry’s been gone ever since New Havoral fell," said the man, with a smile that was just a bit too wide. The inquisitor’s face twitched. He stood up and balled a fist; before he could send it into the man’s face, the couter grabbed his arm. "Goodman. Control yourself. Why don’t you take a walk outside; breathe in the calming air, and recite a few hymns to yourself." The inquisitor's face twitched once more. He unclenched his fist, dusted his clothes, and walked outside without a word, his tall hat brushing the doorframe. And as soon as the inquisitor left, the man slipped the couter a bag of shiny gold coins.
