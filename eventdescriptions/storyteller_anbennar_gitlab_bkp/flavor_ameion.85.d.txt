With their influence concentrated in the city of Orenkoraim, Cannorian traders have been shipping resources of all kinds out of the Kalavend, most notably Precursor relics, for which they pay a premium. Both Cannorian and Kalavendhi have been enriched by this, but those relics will undoubtedly be used for the destabilizing innovations of artificery, not to mention the simple fact that Cannorian encroachment in Taychend will doubtlessly undermine our rule. Our efforts so far have failed to turn the trade to our advantage, so sooner or later we must crack down on it.

But there are problems. The Cannorians enjoy an unparalleled naval advantage. We may be able to beat one alone, but were all of them to come down on us, our navy may well be scattered against the Taychendi coast. Then there is the issue with smugglers—were we to try and crack down on the Kalavendhi trade, it would only invite further Cannorian encroachment elsewhere. The birds would fly their nest.

And so, loathe as we are to admit it, there seems to be only one choice—containment. The Cannorians seem receptive to a deal in which we will allow them to operate out of Orenkoraim—the last free port of Taychend—and in so doing avoid our taxes and tariffs. It will ruin our trade position in the Kalavend, but it should keep the Cannorians isolated there for now.

For now... until we are ready to strike. Then, they shall find out just who is the true master of Aelantir.
