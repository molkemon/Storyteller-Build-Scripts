Ah, Ameion! The eastern bulwark of Kheionai civilization, the cradle of the Ambhen Empire... and the heart of the Ambhen people who built it and named it. As power steadily drifts from Ameion to the rebuilding and revitalizing city of Nakyra, seen now as a new capital for a resurrected elven empire, the hardliner Móloists, who gather most of their political influence from rallying the "pureblooded Kheionai" mobs of Ameion against the "encroaching half-civilized Klereyen mongrels," have become incensed. A particularly populist Móloist Eilastís summed it up best: "If Ameion loses its prime position in the empire, what becomes of Ameion's elite? What becomes of the Ambhen Empire, if the Ambhen no longer reign over it? The answer, my friends, is simple: it no longer becomes an Ambhen empire. And so, Ameion cannot lose its prime position!" 

To that effect, the Móloists, knowing that the wind is turning against them on the Nakyra issue, have drawn up a proposal – make the city and Epaidia of Ameion (and its citizens who reside there) tax exempt. Look at all they have given the Republic, the empire – this would be a reward, a sign of recognition! In the capital, the Ambhen laborers, small-traders, and lowly bureaucrats (as well as not a few well-heeled, blue-blooded senatorial absentee landowners) love this. The Keintagogists and Klereyen, not so much. They deride the proposal as just another sign of vile Ambhen supremacism, an attempt to divide Kheionai from Kheionai and enrich themselves in the process. After all, if Ameion built the empire, the city can damn well pay for it!

Of course, there is another concern. As tensions between Ambhen and Klereyen rise, there are some Móloists who look at the militancy of the "uplifted" Taychendi with a new, suspicious eye. If those Taychendi were to rise against the Republic, then Ameion – not Nakyra, but Ameion – would need every coin and scrap of power to maintain its supremacy...
