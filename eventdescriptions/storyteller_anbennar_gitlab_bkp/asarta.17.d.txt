He didn't even flinch when their lord pointed their spear inches from his face. The son of Sykar. The one who instigated these rebellions. The one their lord had just crushed in its entirety. Yet he just sat there in his dungeon cell, calmly staring down the shaft of their lord's spear, as though there was nothing to fear. "Have you accepted that the prophecy has changed then"? Their lord lowered their spear and knelt in front of the deposed son. "That the naga were wrong. That your father was wrong. Have you accepted that you must step aside to fulfill the prophecy like a true follower of Anzalkatsa"?

The son smiled grimly. "I know all about you and your mad goals in the name of bringing change. Incomprehensible nonsense, chasing shadows due to a legacy of rejection. This pursuit of a mythic force of change is folly, a coping mechanism to deal with your father being chosen over you. You will be just another failure in a list of pretenders to the throne. You will not bring chan-"

His final words were cut short as their lord thrust their spear through his nephew's throat, his last words of defiance stuttering to a gurgling halt.
