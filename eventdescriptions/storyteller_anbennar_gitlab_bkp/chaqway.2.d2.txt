Where the great Banchas and its source the Widnaq meet, a triangle of land forms a confluence island. Trader clans who make the Lake Chakantu loop then dart west to end their cycle here, passing their gifts to clans who then disperse into the rainforest and the plain. Protected on all sides by water, this place seldom goes long without the ground being pounded flat for a qhatuwai; it is time we made it permanent.
