I could hear my father nearby, my mind so addled with sickness. He ran from door to door, hoping anyone could fix this. "She's too sick." "She's a lost cause." "She'll infect us." "She's dying." Nobody even cared to try to help us. So my father carried me far and wide, every scrap of food going to me as we wandered. Until finally, Lelanàn found us on its tide.

I remember hitting the ground as my father collapsed just as we reached the Great Shrine of Lel. But as the priestesses hurried to help us, he was far beyond saving, even with a spell.
