The infusion of magic into humanoids of some intelligence, like humans, has proven trickier than initially expected. The individual agency of humans made them more difficult to control when magically imbued. For a timed tests were attempted on children, as they were expected to be more gullible, but they were prone to tantrums and caused great injury to some of magi working on the project. However it did show some clear effects. The team was able to carefully infuse a variety of properties into the subjects: Greater intelligence, alacrity, strength, magical affinity, and some supernatural abilities less easily classified. Despite this, their mortal bodies were not able to resist the high quantities of magic infused into them, leading to death in a matter of months. Perhaps those with a greater connection to magic are more able to handle the arcane stress?
