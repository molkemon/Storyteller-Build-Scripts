The Mudaliar brothers were stone-faced as they eyed each other. They stood across a bridge marking the border between their districts, the vast city of Nanru Nakar looming over them from all sides. Only a sliver of light slipped through the towering, war-scarred buildings, an arrow cast through the gap in skyscraping stone carved by the river. Only an extraordinary threat could bring them together—and the news that their Rakulacan vassals were preparing to revolt was just that.

"So," Elatam, the older brother, spoke at last, "do you now realize your mistake?"

Erannan, the middle brother, scoffed, indignant. "With Larankar gathering in the north... the Rakulacan are perfidious, grudge-holding bastards—but how could I have known they would be so stupid?"

"Yes..." Elatam said, sadly. "And yet, in our darkest hour, so they are. Our family's luck has faded. But I will not let our name fade alongside it."

Erannan sighed. He already knew what needed to be done. "This doesn't concede my claim." He stated, even as he offered his hand. "But we must strike quickly, and strike true. I doubt we can even call our allies." Elatam made to shake his brother's hand—but Erannan pulled away at the last second. "If we had Darandar... there would be no danger. And... maybe we could come to an agreement. After."

The older brother's face hardened, frown turning to a scowl. "No. You, I can fight alongside. Not him. Never him." Erannan sighed in resignation, and put forward his hand again.
