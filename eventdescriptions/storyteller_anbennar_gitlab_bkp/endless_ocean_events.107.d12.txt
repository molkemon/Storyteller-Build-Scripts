"Where there is Water, there is Rhythm. Where there is Heat, there is Truth."; proverb of the Ship Nomads  As the sound of oarstrokes, or the pitterpatter of rain, or the percussion of Wavesong, where there is water there is rhythm. The Kiamoa’s surges always travel counterclockwise, and the monsoons follow cyclic seasons. Water– Moa– while flexible, follows a predictable set of patterns. Like the revealing warmth of daylight, or the embrace of a loved one, or the burning furnace of a magma vent, where there is heat there is truth. A hot, humid day may bring your inner anger to a boil, or the warmth of a fire after a cold night swim may teach you gratitude. Greatest of all is the heat of molten Haku, or stone – combined with water, it may tell you things which have yet to pass. The future is found with prediction, the junction of pattern and fact. When lava meets sea, the resulting steam holds within its fleeting form visions of days yet to come, tales of how tomorrow may resemble days gone by. Those faithful of the Endless Ocean who have witnessed the Magmasight diviners of Volcanic Truth will tell you of their impressive foresight, yet inexperienced interpretation. Vision is little without the experience of Moa, and experience without the foresight of Haku’s growth is merely reflection.
