Apparently, one of our patrols in the city of Castonath has stumbled on a most gruesome sight. The food stores in one of the palaces of the nobility were filled to the brim with human flesh, frozen and quartered. Of course, our appalled patrol killed the noble residents immediately and burned the entire place down. According to the patrol, the nobles spat out some venomous words and outed some compatriots. Apparently, this practice was pervasive and appeared at the height of the conflict, when Castonath’s food supply broke down and the nobles were forced to find alternative sources of sustenance. With no evidence left from the burned palace, we have no way of gathering evidence or checking our patrols word; surely even the worst of humans would not stoop to the horrific act of eating an intelligent species’ flesh. But at the same time, how else could they have survived Castonath’s long period of occupation during the conflict?
