A group of Basobhar chiefs enter a local fortress, recently built by the Eneioni. The land the fort sits upon was up until recently controlled by the very same chiefs who now walk in its halls as guests. Within a special conference room lies a large table with copies of the treaties the chiefs are to sign. As the chiefs step into the room, a mixture of anger, fear, and worry is clearly visible on their faces. Opposite them sits our ruler.

"Fear not, allies! While our longstanding alliance may have ended under less than favorable circumstances, we hope you will forgive us. It was necessary for the security of our southern border. While you may live in our land now, you will not suffer the fate others in your position would have. We seek a new relationship between our parties, one that would be beneficial for both of us!"

The chiefs remain concerned. While many of them are illiterate, they have all brought tribal scribes with them, who can read the Kheionai language. The chiefs direct their scribes to read aloud the treaty the Eneioni have laid out, in the Basobhar language.

Once the scribes finish reading aloud, the chiefs turn to each other, both shocked and relieved. They have all heard of the fate other tribes have suffered at the hands of the Kheionai, and feared the worst.

One of the younger chieftains, who is fluent in the Kheionai language, questions our ruler, "If I understand this correctly, our tribes will be granted much autonomy, in exchange for helping defend your land?"

"Correct," replies our ruler.
