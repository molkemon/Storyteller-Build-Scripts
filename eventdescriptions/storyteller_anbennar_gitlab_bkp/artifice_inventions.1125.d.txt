Water is a most precious commodity in the desert, and any method to reliably gather it in the unforgiving heat is worth a king’s ransom. Luckily for the inhabitants of the Salahad, the ingenuity of artificers has provided a solution that is much more affordable to the average desert dweller. The airborne moisture collectors begin their work in the evening and operate throughout the cold desert night, drawing in all ambient moisture in the surrounding air and condensing it. Come the morning, delicious refreshing water can be decanted from the device and used to replenish water rations. Larger versions of these airborne moisture collectors are stationary and are used to supplement the water reserves of desert settlements, allowing their establishment even in areas that would otherwise be too inhospitable. And there are intermediate versions as well that can be used by travelling caravans, even if such caravans are generally wont to move through the night, thus avoiding exertions during the intolerable heat of the day.
