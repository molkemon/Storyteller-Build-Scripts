Farmers in a nearby province have reported a marvelous finding. Seeking new caverns to sow, they stumbled upon a dormant, cooled caldera, hosting remarkable, almost supernatural botany. Mushrooms the size of trees sprout up from the ground and hang down from the roof like stalactites. Serpentbloom wraps like vines around the stalks and caps, glowing so strongly that the cavern is lit with a dawnlike twilight. The farmers estimate that this single lush cavern could provide as much food as a dozen cultivated ones.
