“Trap her.” The shadowshifters move oddly, flickering rather than moving across the chamber. It is gratifying to see Warlord Kwizera dart this way and that, her mongoose fur standing on end at the monstrous distortion of the shadowshifters’ forms. Most are snakes, of course, but snakes with eight shadowy tails, or two heads, and nothing about the way the light hits them is correct. Kuende feels Kwizera attempt to shift back into human form, a twist in body language, in magic—Kuende uses her own magic to deny it. Kwizera hisses. What follows is undignified—Kuende squats, chasing the little mongoose toward her shifters, trapping her, seizing her by the belly with both hands after a mad scramble of limbs and teeth and squeaking. With a tense little noise of triumph, finally, Kuende flattens the little mongoose body to the ground. She seems not to notice the continued, savage bites to her forearm and hand. With the heel of one palm, she grinds the little mongoose into the soft clay floor, grunting in distant pain. Then she reaches into a bag at her belt and pulls out four nails. The nails have travelled far; the same kind are hammered deep into the Yezelne Nisha, the shadow matriarch tree at the heart of Yezel Mora’s swamp. 

Grimacing, Kuende impales Kwizera’s little paws, thud, thud, thud, thud. Then she takes out her knife. Calmly, she makes the cut she has practiced for so long—inner leg, up the thigh and back and down. Her fingers plunge into the flesh of Kwizera’s twisting abdomen, hooking below the skin to loosen it from sinew. The screeching is terrible, of course. When precisely Kwizera dies is hard to say.
