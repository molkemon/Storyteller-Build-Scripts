The mood in the Conclave of Dawn was grave. This caught Ecaris by surprise, at first. He thought he had won the Exemplars' influence, a hand in keeping this strange experiment on the righteous path. But some of the other Heralds… they were not so enthused.

"Ecaris," the Herald of Purification pleaded, "our issue is not that they have shown grace to us... it is just that we do not believe in the same things as them! I mean, chaos as a good—! As if it were not the theological antithesis of civilization itself! They turn radical and then reluctant—shift in belief as the shadows with the sun, the endless wittering of a council without end."

The Herald of Law agreed. "And now we have to enact their Kavalakuttam's inane declarations? Be considered some organ of their nebulous and mystical state? Fight for them? Die for them?"

Ecaris just sighed. "Do you think I cared about Corin's faith, or Dominic's incomprehensible theological theories? Do you think I could not see them as warriors that shared my goal? Worthy of respect?"

Purification bit back. "But they do not share our goal!"

The Herald of Flame countered, "Hatred for slavery, just and righteous governance... are those really different?"

Service tapped a finger of his prosthetic arm on the table. "And what am I to them? A wielder of slavery, because I suffered wounds and decided I could fight more still?"

And then Karodir spoke with a monarch's voice: "Action, over belief. Decision, over doctrine." The words settled the room. "I'm not even an Exemplar, and I know these words. And I believe in them."

Ecaris nodded—looked to Service. "How do you know they would not treat your case kindly?" To Law, "We can take part in their deliberations. We can help steer their state, their laws, their institutions." To Purification, "Difference IS their doctrine. You think we cannot reinterpret?" To Flame, "And yes, we share the same enemies." To Vengeance, "And with their strength, we may yet vanquish them. So yes..."
