With a roost under construction, we have the opportunity to bring a population of nonharpies to the roost; both men and women, so that in their own families new males may be born and thus sustain us. Others may migrate after the roost is completed, but a founding population would prime the roost for growth. Some disdain the idea, and claim that young harpies should travel far and wide in search of males rather than having easy access to them. Another instance of the corruption and laziness of the youth, in their eyes.
