Many think of red when they imagine a rose, but in fact the flower has dozens of pigments that can be seen throughout the seasons. Red is emblematic of love, white represents purity, yellow means friendship; a vast amount of hues, each a symbol; each embodied in party by the Lorentish court.  This month, the black roses are in bloom. They fill Lorent’s hedgerows all along the western approaches from the Dames head. Black roses represent death in Lencenor, and the black canvas blooming this day is a perfect backdrop on which the blood of imperial soldiers will splatter, satiating the thirsty roots that grow in anticipation of war.  The Lorentish hosts waited in silence for a month, only the wind sifting through the petals of the battlefield in anticipation of an imperial army cresting over the border on the horizon. But no such army came. The Rose readied the canvas of war, and the Dove chose to leave it blank.
