The scene resembled a traveller's inn more than a throne room. Arrayed around the room were a mix of delegations from each of the castes, and one particularly large group from the Cinnabar Lotus Society. While normally all would be silent, listening for the word of their ruler, instead various members of each delegation were in fervent argument, eight or ten disputes running in parallel. Beside each set of interlocutors, one member of the Society sat, apparently presiding over the discussion taking place.  Above it all sat the ruler, who ensured the equal basis on which such free debate depended.  Finally, the head of the Society, a female harimari, dressed in far more presentable robes than the average philosophers, who all could probably have not bothered to dress if it didn't lead to some discussion, cleared her throat, "Everyone. Please, it is time to deliver our answer." One by one, a member of each delegation spoke up, each affirming the idea that our ruler be advised by each of the castes on all decisions. Such a council would ensure that our ruler always had the perspective of each caste on every matter concerning the realm. Each of these advisors would be determined by organized debates between the ruler and the proposed candidate. Allowing for both advisor and advised to be tested in their knowledge of the realm and its people.
