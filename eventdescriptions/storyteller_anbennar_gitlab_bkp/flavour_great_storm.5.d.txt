‘Zarkan? Note the stench of sulfur hanging in the air when traversing this camp. Such a smell is indicative of those vile creatures summoned by these green geoglyphs. Carve this odour upon thine nostrils, Zarkan.’  There is no response. Only Zarkan's bored, indifferent and intensely frustrated gaze remains as a mark of his continued endeavor in the realm of being alive.  Watching this camp whose outskirts they just have arrived at. Dilapidated? Maybe. No soul in sight. Maybe they have  ‘Mayhaps they have left in order to reorganize at a more secret spot? But then again, sharpen thine earlobes, young Zarkan. A moan, nay, two moans of agony, I am certain of it.’  Zarkan wonders if his squadron would question the sudden disappearance of his scout "compatriot". That question swirls an awful lot in his mind lately. But no, the 	cries of anguish are there, in the distance. In that tent. ‘Good work, mate. Maybe it’s Aziyr. Let’s go.’  After galloping for a minute and a half and entering the rather mediocre looking tent they were beholden to a most disgraceful sight. ‘Watch as his men have abandoned this greatest of shamans in his time of need, Zarkan. Struck by a plague, that no centaur wishes to be struck with. Engrave this sight’  ‘Mate, I am not engraving this sight of someone having the worst, most sick stomach churnings of his life into my eyes. But it’s safe to say that we’ve got him. As for the camp…’
