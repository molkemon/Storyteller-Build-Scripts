The lord paced around the dock, as the guarded crowd of Chendhyans listened to his speech. "And when your ancestors made a deal with the Taychendi, they did not merely sign their lands away, but their souls! The Oremvandians were better to us than most—but the rotten influence of the Uyr should not have been paid!" The lord drew Saerasyar, gestured to a different group of Chendhyans—beheaded with Saerasyar’s blade, then ignominiously kicked, slowly, one-by-one, into the unending southern sea. They had slave-traders, even if they—and the city they served—had never traded in Chendhyans. Were it up to some of the Caergarae, the entire city would be a burning ruin—but he, the monarch, knew that unlike Rakkabuttai, Oremvand needed a softer touch. "But Chendhyans—there is another way!" He pointed the Green-Flamed Sword to waiting ships in Oremvand’s Whiteharbour. "A way to redeem what you have done in service to Oremvand! For centuries, the pirates of Caergarae have raided the slave-ships of the Taras’su Ervan, preyed on the vicious slavers of Clemetar and Empkeios—and for centuries, hundreds of ships cross the unending waters of the south, ferrying subjugated peoples, Chendhyans and others, from the warlords of the east to the cities of the west!" The monarch of the dynasty saw the looks of realization—dread fear—among the Chendhyans in the crowd. To be truthful, he was a little scared himself. But everyone is afraid the first time they leave the surety of land: and the ocean waves were nothing compared to the fury of a cornered Bloodcloak. "Chendhyans of Oremvand—the sea awaits! Ride with me, and let us liberate it!"
