The door to the throne room shattered—a moment passed, then Daranarma's warriors, armed with all manner of scrap-artificer weapons and military gizmos, charged into the room. They were not met with any resistance. Jalinta knew his fate was sealed the moment he lost the Periyodhai—he'd made sure he would face his enemy alone. He grimaced as that enemy walked through the broken door, wielding a massive handheld cannon. The Techno-Warlord pointed it at Sajirada.

"How lucky I am," Daranarma started, "that you were not killed on the field yesterday! I've gotten a lot more testing out of my new weapon!" He had a satisfied grin on his face that made Sajirada's blood boil. "This—this is prismatic damestear, straight from Amadia!" He had heard of these things—powerful but wild, chaotic. "It packs a punch, my friend—want to see!?"

The Techno-Warlord leveled the cannon at the seated warlord—as the barrel began to glow with prismatic light, Sajirada readied himself for the end. But when the weapon fired, it was not Sajirada who was claimed by it. An explosion of prismatic light erupted from the back of the weapon—and the right half of Daranarma's upper body exploded with it. The Techno-Warlord flew through the air, alongside a shower of giblets and gore. His mutilated corpse hit the floor with a wet thump.

Daranarma's men looked at the corpse, then at each other, and then broke, fleeing. Sajirada could hardly believe his luck. He climbed down from the throne and walked over to the weapon that had both doomed and then saved him, picking it up. Suddenly, an idea came to mind. The weapon packed a punch, but was dangerous to whoever wielded it… so what if whoever wielded it didn't matter?
