Our lord looked at Lucian and scowled. Lucian was restrained and had already been injected with several different poisons, but none of them showed any effect. Lucian merely looked bored but stayed silent. For some reason, this seemingly human man seemed impossible to kill. Our lord tried several different methods. They tried stabbing him, throwing him in the viper pit, and even tried to have him crushed alive. However, each time that was tried, Lucian looked utterly unharmed as if he was above such crude attempts to end his existence. Eventually, after hours of effort, our lord decided to nail Lucian against a tree. As each nail was driven further, Lucian smugly looked at them and mocked, "Look at this animal thinking it can kill me. Several of the rabble have tried, but none could even lay a finger on me." But as our lord ran out of metal nails, they picked up a sturdy shard of wood and hammered it through Lucian's heart. And as the sun started rising in the sky, Lucian's eyes widened and he let out a blood curdling scream. Within a few minutes, as Lucian was touched by the first rays of the sun, he began to burn. Our lord swiftly stepped back upon seeing the burnt ashes where Lucian once stood. While his goal was finally achieved, even our lord was shocked by this unexpected turn of events, and whispers that Dookan must have cursed Lucian sil na Toars for some extreme act of cowardice spread throughout their entourage. They soon heard rustling from the dark woods still clad in the shadows of night. Whatever cursed creature that very pale human was, it was clear that there were more of them.
