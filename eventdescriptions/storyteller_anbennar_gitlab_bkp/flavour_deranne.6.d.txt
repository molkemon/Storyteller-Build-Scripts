"I once walked the blistering vineyards of the Sorncosti hills to see the origins of the famed liquor for myself. Of course, no Lencori man reaches adulthood without tasting its pristine flavour, and wine only matures over time, but there was something unique about strolling through those fields.  I talked to the owner, a gentle old vintner who told me the fields had been passed through his family generation after generation, and that they would continue to be after his passing. Indeed, I learned a lot about the ways of the Sorncosti while I was there. People like to lump the Lencori people together, call them either Lorentish or Derannic, but the differences are obvious if you just look a little closer. There was a sense of stability to the land, like you could feel the ancient ties that have persevered for a thousand years while the rest of Lencenor moved on. Did you know some Sorncosti still believe in the old gods of the Eidoueni?  Regardless, the Sorncosti are a strong people, even when subjugated to the whims of outside powers. They stood alone against the Entebeni horselords, survived the War of the SorcererKing unscathed, and fought tooth and nail when the Derannics came to secure the southern hills. You almost have to admire their ferocity, and it is no wonder the Seaflower…
