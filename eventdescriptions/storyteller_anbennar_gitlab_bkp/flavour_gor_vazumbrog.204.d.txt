The suffocating silence that followed the next months was a damning answer to what had become of our expeditionary force. Our supply lines, stretched thin in anticipation, received no requests for aid from the depths of the Wound. Not a single plea for water or food reached us. We were expecting a steady stream of messengers to return, but only a handful ever did. One young dwarf came back, his oncehandsome face now twisted and distorted beyond recognition, screaming and thrashing with a fever that consumed him. His last words were of 'the eyes' that haunted him in the darkness. A whole squad of monster hunters returned seemingly unscathed, their faces twisted into maniacal grins as they regaled us with tales of their triumphs. But the next day, they were all found hanging from the rafters in the main plaza. But it was the return of Commander Khadan Stonebreaker that sent shockwaves through our hold. His attendants, gaunt and holloweyed, carried him forward, their hands trembling under the weight of his mangled form. His armor, corroded and brittle, clung to him in broken shards, his flesh beneath blackened and fissured like ore left too long in the forge. 'The deep veins. the stone remembers.' Khadan rasped, blood bubbling from his lips. The only respite our physicians could offer was a cold bath, which proved to be an agonising deathbed. It was only after his last breath, as the priests tended his body, that we realised the true extent of his sacrifice. Beneath the layers of fused metal and leather, his very skin had been etched with deep, unnatural patterns; runes of unknown origin, carved along his ribs and spine. When studied, the cuts formed a map; a map of safe passages and areas untouched by the toxic magic of the Wound. The stone under Gor Vazumbrog, thought cursed and impenetrable, had fractures, hidden seams where expansion could proceed safely. With this knowledge, the hold could now press deeper.
