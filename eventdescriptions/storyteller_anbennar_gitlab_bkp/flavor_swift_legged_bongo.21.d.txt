Even just from this unworked block, I can see her. The longer I stare, the more shocked I am when I blink and she is not there.  I have the vision in my head: my beautiful child, decked in fine robes, leading the Bongo in my absence, forever. But how to make that vision a reality? I am no sculptorthe tools feel heavy and inflexible in my hands. I will have to tread with caution, lest in my amateurish eagerness I destroy her.  Now begins the real work. To take these tools, and craft my child as only a mother can.  The longer I spend crafting her, the more beautiful she will be. But my bones creak, and I know that my time is short. How much effort to spend…
