The established church has long been one of the foremost pillars of the realm, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, judges and diplomats for the benefit of our lord. In return for these great services, many donations have been made over the years, both by the state and by others, to the established church. These gifts have over the centuries made the established church quite rich and influential. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the established church might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
