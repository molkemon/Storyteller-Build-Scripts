The Ashamadi are the northern peoples among the Baashidi. In times long past, they were heavily discriminated against due to their connection with the Tanizu. In Dhebiji and Suhratbi society, they were often given more work for less pay. Stories of physical abuse were common as well. The sentiment that they were lesser was raw and clear, shadowing the Ashamadi under prejudice.

Ever since the Dasr Sharaf, times have changed. Ashamadi peoples have found themselves welcomed into more and more courts among their Baashidi brethren. People have pushed back against the idea that the Ashamadi are "lesser."

This doesn’t mean that everything is resolved. Certain court mages find themselves being targeted solely for being Ashamadi. This has hindered the progress of the Grand Congress and generated tension among all scholars participating.

The highest saaxir have called upon the lords of the Baashidi to reprimand their courts if they are caught perpetrating Ashamadi discrimination.
