The small island of Bhetu is home to humble Ekhasi fishermen, whose lives do not differ markedly from their brethren inhabiting the many other isles and shores of Sarhal. Yet Bhetu differs from all these other locales in one important aspect: it is the westernmost inhabited point of all of Sarhal. Here, the eyes of the faithful have the last chance to glimpse the light of the sun before it sinks beneath the waves and night cloaks the lands in darkness once again. It is therefore only fitting that a promontory on the westernmost tip of the island houses the House of Dusk’s Lament, the BetDumabithu, near some ancient ruins of the long vanished stone giants. Called the Kha'ilh Tigireg Saukh by the local Akasi, it serves as a gathering place for the devout, where they may mourn the arrival of dusk and implore Surael to gather His strength in the eternal Struggle against the Malevolent Dark, so that the sun may rise once again come the morning. Here the Daughters of Jasiene care for the many pilgrims who desire to lend their mortal strength to Surael through incessant prayer throughout each night, until the first rays of dawn illuminate the eastern skies. The House of Dusk's Lament opens its gates to more than devout pilgrims, thanks to their generous donations it has become a second home for the many destitute orphans and widows of the island as well, whose fathers and husbands have been lost at sea.
