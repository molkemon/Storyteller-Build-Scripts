Erankar marched through the ruins of Marhed's palace, the center of power in this den of snakes and armadillo-scaled scoundrels. After being thrown back from Nanru Nakar, Sheranu Enlarmadar, who had survived her capture of Enlarmai by marrying the Rayodani King, had launched an invasion of Larankar from here. She'd have preferred to storm it herself, but she was on the other side of the city when it was taken, drawn to a bigger threat. Luckily, her soldiers had taken her prize alive.

Sheranu kneeled next to her husband, bound and guarded. Her husband simply stared forward, despondent, but when Sheranu noticed Erankar's approach, she spat on the ground before him. Erankar drew his sword, executed the Rayodani warlord, and pointed the blood-stained tip at the last Enlarmadar.

"Do you expect me to beg?" Her eyes blazed with hatred. "You've taken everything from me! My parents, my brother— and defeating me in battle wasn't enough so you burn my home down too, kill my husband! Will you raze Rayodana now? Bathe the sand in blood?"

Erankar could feel the heat rising in him, a white-hot rage that threatened to engulf him. "Your family did all that and more! I was only reclaiming what was rightfully mine— vengeance!"

"So was I!" Sheranu spat. "I had nothing to do with my father taking Urvand— YOU had everything to do with slaughtering my family! When Urvand fell, I was only nine years old—!"

Erankar's blade flashed. Sheranu fell to the floor, lifeless. "So was I." Erankar sheathed his saber, hand shaking. He looked back at the city behind him.
