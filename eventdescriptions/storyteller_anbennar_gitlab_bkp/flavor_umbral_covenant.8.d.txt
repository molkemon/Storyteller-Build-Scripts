Umorozi is pleased. The Vile One can see it in the tension-spring of her long legs, the eager power of her slender muscles. She would pounce on this place, pace it, devour it if left to her own devices. The complex is not so flashy—it was repurposed from a barracks and expanded to encompass an enormous space. It is austere yet verdant, offering shaded parade grounds for those corporeal soldiers the Covenant still employs. But beside the long, low barracks buildings, there are offices. These are places where clerks will labor to bind the Covenant ever closer together, where they will identify and eliminate subversion. Clever Umorozi recognizes that the direst threat to the Covenant is not its enemies but its traitors, and hers is an army whose spears will most often face inwards.

“Everything is as I suggested,” Umorozi says, her tone even and blank, but the Vile One laughs. Umorozi might as well be crying with joy.

“Your ideas were sound, kitame, were they not?”
