The once serene cavern, now home to a budding colony, had recently become the stage for an escalating territorial conflict. The unwitting cause of this unease? A sizable population of bugbears, native to the tunnels before the colonists' arrival. At first, the incursions were mild, limited to cheeky raids of small food caches. The colonists initially accepted these transgressions as the price of coexistence, but the bugbears’ brazenness grew with each encounter, emboldened by the apparent lack of consequences. One day, a team of miners ventured further into the surrounding tunnels. As they stacked their haul for the day, one of the bugbears, bolder than the rest, saw an opportunity. With a thundering roar, the hungry intruder charged at the startled men, who dropped their tools and scrambled for their lives. The bugbear snatched up the abandoned lunches, gorging itself on the easy meal. This incident was a turning point. Now, more and more of the creatures are braving the trek to the colony. The scent of easily available food has proven too alluring to resist and the colonists find themselves under siege. Their livestock is no longer safe, and even their homes are at risk of being pillaged. Efforts to scare the creatures away with noise and brightly colored scarecrows have thus far proven ineffectual. The colonists are now asking for our support in dealing with this problem.
