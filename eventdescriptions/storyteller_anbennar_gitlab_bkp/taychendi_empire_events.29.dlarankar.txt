The lord looked at the armor before them—plates of Precursor steel covered mail, elegant and imposing all at once. It was the armor of Erankar, the armor that the Emperor-Defiant had worn as he cleaved Fate's puppet-strings and carved an Empire from anarchy. Since then, Larankar had given Taychend victory after victory, the borders expanding beyond any previous Taychendi realm. They should be happy—but the lord could only grimace as they thought about the empire they ruled, as the so-called "Warlord-Autonomous." The lord knew now that their dynasty would not fall as the others did. Their empire would conquer, and the warlords would hunger, and on the blood of the world they would feed, the ichor of their ascension. The cart of empire moved faster and faster, straight ahead, destined for the beginning of a new cycle, where Taychend would rule undisputed. It was unstoppable. It was uncontrollable. And if the lord looked beyond this cycle and did not like what they saw? Or if the wheels were running off imagination's track, towards somewhere they could not comprehend?

The lord professed total autonomy—wanted to believe Erankar had cut fate's cord long ago. But the lord knew that no matter what they did, the hum of Empire and conquest and glory would drone on—as it did, as it had, as it would. Already, their lieutenants were drawing up plans for new expeditions, to North Aelantir, to Insyaa, to Sarhal. They desired these things—but could they afford not to? Or were they a slave, destined to be but a cog in a machine with one destination? The conquest of the world—then disaster?

The lord could only see one answer, and it weighed on them far heavier than Ruin-steel. An empire for a day, an empire for an eon—it did not matter—Taychend would rise, an Empire Unstoppable, an Empire Ascendant, an Empire Vainglorious...
