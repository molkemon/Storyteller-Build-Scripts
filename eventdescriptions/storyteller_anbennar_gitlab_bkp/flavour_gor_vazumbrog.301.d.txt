The tunnels stretched endlessly, each turn a copy of the last. Boots scraped over stone, and the air carried the sharp tang of fatigue and disappointment. The lord marched at the front, his grip tightening on his sword. He stopped, torchlight flickering against his worn features, and turned to his entourage. 'One more,' he urged, his steely gaze meeting each weary pair of eyes. 'Hehogrim wouldn't turn back.' Grumbles rippled through the company, but they followed. They moved deeper into the earth, the air growing cooler and thick with mineral tang. Then, the lead scout halted abruptly, torch raised. Before them, the tunnel opened into a cavern of breathtaking enormity. Mithril veins snaked from the ground like the roots of a colossal tree, twisting and coiling upward, their shimmering light casting the chamber in a silveryblue glow. The walls sparkled with embedded fragments, as though the cavern itself breathed with the essence of the earth. The lord stepped forward, his shadow stretching across the radiant chamber. He reached out, his fingers grazing the cool, smooth surface of a mithril vein. The metal hummed faintly under his touch, alive with a power that made his heart race. The dwarves stared in hushed wonder, their burdens forgotten. The faint sound of dripping water echoed like a melody, blending with the low hum of the mithril. The lord knelt, his eyes reflecting the metallic glow. 'Hehogrim led us here,' he whispered with reverence. 'This is his blessing.' One of his officers stepped closer, awe etched into her face. 'What now, my lord?' The lord rose slowly, sweeping the glittering expanse. 'We claim his gift and build a future worthy of it.'
