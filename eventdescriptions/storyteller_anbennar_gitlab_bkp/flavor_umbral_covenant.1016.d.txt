The rift opens… and I can see. In my childhood, I compared my grandmother to a vulture. She was pleased by the comparison. Now I watch her drift contentedly through the ruins of the city, content as one who has just finished a great meal. She glows, my Granny—the web of wrinkles suffuses her paper-thin skin, and even with her extra fingers, she is beautiful. She dances in the empty spaces, in the withering fog, in the grief of so many thousands dead. Sometimes, one need not wield a blade; it is enough to notice when the corpse begins to rot. The wisdom of the Coven is not merely in preparation, but in seizing opportunity.
