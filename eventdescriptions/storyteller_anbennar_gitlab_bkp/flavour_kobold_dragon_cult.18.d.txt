When our ruler arrives in Aakheta, they are treated to all the luxuries of the city. Sugars and incense from the Lower Sorrow, golden trinkets from a city, the finecut gems of a province: the bounty of the Sorrow is presented, gifted freely to our lord. After the great display of wealth has ceased, and the pageantry ends, our ruler is brought to Aakhet's throne room, where they are met with the visage of Aakhet in all his glory. Aakhet's bronze armor is perfectly polished, shimmering in the light; the bronze statue of their draconic form looms above their throne, providing a constant reminder of what they could always choose to be. "Welcome," he says, "to the Sorrow, fellow conqueror. How would you feel about a game?" As our ruler takes a seat, the humanshaped dragon continues. "I've appreciated watching your nation's exploits; I even thought about becoming one of your generals, for a time. But it just seemed too easy. You conquer too well. On the other hand, though.I can appreciate someone who knows a good challenge." "So," he continues. "I could do with a wager. You command great wealth in that Hoard of yours.and I," he laughs, "could always use more wealth. You, in the meantime, could use my favor. So what if we make a trade? You give me a quarter of your Hoard, and I'd be willing to share some of my secrets. Or.or." he says, with an excited grin. "Or, we play a game. If you win, I tell my tales for no cost. If I win.well, I'll still do it, because I like you. But I'll take half of your great Hoard in return. Do we have a deal?" Aakhet unfolds a great deck of ancient cards, and looks at our ruler expectantly. A choice must be made.
