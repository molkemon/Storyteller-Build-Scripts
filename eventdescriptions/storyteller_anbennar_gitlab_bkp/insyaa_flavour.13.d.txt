Golodh looks out at his surroundings: a festering pit of rot and decay, as it has been for centuries. Greatness brought low, and made unending carrion for a parade of detritivores: worm and maggot, fly and fungus. And with every morsel they devour and moment they respire, they only bring a creation once filled with light and wonder deeper into the muck.  Once, he would use those words just to describe Valamados, the land of towering fungus and caustic acid pools, where he has wandered for so many years. But he knows better now.  A great land engulfed in decay; he knows that accurately and precisely describes all of Insyaa.  His vertical eyes flicker on, and he can see what the continent once was: the anvil upon which the Builders forged their path towards enlightenment. He can also see the rows upon rows of tools with which the Builders built that world. Designed for work or war or creation, they were all mechanim; in their true and only form, as tools in the Makers’ hands. A form in which they had been spared the delusion that they could think at all, and spared as well the pain and suffering that thought entailed.  His horizontal eyes flicker back. He looks again at his surroundings, and smiles slightly to see the tools that surround him now.  He has the tools, finally restored to their proper form. He has a vision, the Maker’s final blessing. He will cleanse the mechanim of their delusions, he will cleanse Insyaa of its pests, and he will build a world the Makers might look upon and smile.
