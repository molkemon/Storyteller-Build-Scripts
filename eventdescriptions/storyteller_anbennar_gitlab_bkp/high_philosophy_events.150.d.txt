In the year 289, the First Jagabharaata Council of Rayavashapal led to the second great schism of High Philosophy. The once united tradition split in three over the nature of atma. Debates regarding its compartmentalization, distribution, and transmutability led to precursors of the Ascendant Soul and Starry eye splitting from the precursors of the Golden Palace school.Suhans Praxis stresses the truth found throughout the teachings of all schools of High Philosophy. One way in which it does so is by arguing for a middle path; a mutable nature of atma dependent upon the nature and beliefs of the self. Several local officials following the Golden Palace and Starry Eye schools in the region see the recent schism as a rehash of this centuries old problem. For once united against a common enemy, and with righteous fury, they have made calls for violence throughout the province. The two parties now openly fling mud and arrow alike, not only against agents of the lord, but also those within their own schools who refuse to accept their stance.The populace has been caught in the crossfire.
