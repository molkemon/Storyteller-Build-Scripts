Nestled within the heart of the country, the Trilidir library stands as a treasure trove of knowledge. While it is a relatively recent project, the scholars have done an exceptional job documenting the Devandi settlers and the natives alike. It serves as a humble reminder of the similarities between our realm and theirs, of our culture and theirs. Along with it comes a wealth of knowledge on their languages. When a citizen dedicates some time in the library, they leave a better person.

Now that we’ve gained ownership over the library, we are faced with a choice. We can leave the knowledge gathered by the scholars where it is. They are the custodians who have collected it—they will take the best care of it. Maybe our elite can learn something by spending some time there. 

Or, we can take the knowledge for ourselves and bring it into our domain for our own libraries. Surely our scholars will make better use of the knowledge base and direct the wisdom in the interest of our state.
