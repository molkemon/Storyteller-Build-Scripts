It rains. It rains often in Cursewoods, but never has it rained like this. Killing an immortal vampire is as hard as it sounds. They can suffer a fatal wound and rise from their coffins the night after. Still, on this woeful night, the monarch Lucian sil na Toars has embraced true death and is no longer unliving on this world. With him dies all his dreams for Luciande. All his ambitions lay lifeless on the ground, never to be fulfilled. His fellow vampires weep with the land and somehow, somewhere, even his sire, Mikael, sheds a lone tear for his perished offspring. A sole foolish slave might rejoice at the tyrant’s death, but they know not how much hold Lucian’s iron fist had on the vampires. Without someone to lead them, they will fall to disarray and Luciande will fall with them. With this the ancient secrets of the vampire progenitor shall never be revealed and, be it tyrannical or not, there is one less safe haven for vampires in this hostile world.
