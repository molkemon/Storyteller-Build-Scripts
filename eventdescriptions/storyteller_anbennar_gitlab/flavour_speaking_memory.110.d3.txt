In the days before the Century of Storms, there was a woman named Malie, who was blessed with a peaceful nature and great wisdom from an early age. She became known by her village as a great resolver of disputes, but what she truly loved was the freedom of the open waves. She loved her people and her freedom both, and she thought to take a bit of each for her life by becoming a wayfinder, so she could sail from her island but always stay close to home. She found a man she loved named Tako, and the two of them went voyaging in the day and returned home together in the night. And things would have stayed this way, were it not for a raid on the home of Malie and Tako by an enemy village. Malie managed to hide, but the raiders killed Tako, and the enemy lived beyond the reach of the tribe’s justice. So Malie took her boat which she once used for navigating, and she travelled across the islands of Kanolowele to the other tribes and the dragon turtles. She spoke to them about creating a great system of laws, which all the tribes would create together and enforce together. She encountered derision at first, but the dragon turtles came to agree, and soon when she sailed between the islands the dragon turtles followed her. This system of laws became the First Consensus of Kanolowele, and Malie sat as a judge for the Consensus with the dragon turtles until she died. The First Consensus continued on without her, but at each of its sessions until its end, next to the place where the judges sat, there was left an empty chair.
