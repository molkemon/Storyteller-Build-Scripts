After a long and monotonous day of travel, the monarch and their party arrive at an inn, eagerly anticipating respite from the rigors of the road. As they approach, the sound of a lullaby drifts through the evening air. On a bench near the inn’s stable sits a woman, clothing worn from hard labor and stained with a touch of soot where the hem meets the earth. In her arms is a baby, swaddled in thick blankets and soothed as she sings to it with a warm but weary smile. She glances up, and upon seeing the monarch approaching she stands, performing just the shadow of a bow, child still in her arms. Greetings, Your Majesty, and welcome to my humble inn. While it might not have the ample comforts of your court, there is a large room that should have space aplenty for you and your companions.
