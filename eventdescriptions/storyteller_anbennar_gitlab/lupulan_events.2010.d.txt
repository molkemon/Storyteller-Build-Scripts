The delegations of two of our tributaries came before the court today for a scheduled thirty-fourth round of argumentation over which nation should have the honor of appointing the Sri Baginda’s footstool carrier. As is tradition, the Sri Baginda served as judge to the debate from when the sun rose until when the sun set. After the other business of the court began late at night, the diplomatic advisor came before the Sri Baginda with a question. might it not be better to overhaul the way petitions before the Sri Baginda are processed, for everyone’s sake? A system of regional courts could be established through the Lupulan with magistrates given authority to act on behalf of the Sri Baginda, so that only the most important cases consume the Sri Baginda’s time, and it would likely be more convenient for our subjects to avoid having to travel the entire Lupulan as well.
