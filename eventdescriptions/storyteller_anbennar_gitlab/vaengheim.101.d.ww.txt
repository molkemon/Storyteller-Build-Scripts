It was in the days of a certain year, when a man named Geir, who was a hunter, was afflicted with a curse. And he did shed his skin for that of a great bear, and he did hunt the men of his hometown, and he did devour their flesh under the glare of the moon, making him twice cursed in the eyes of the gods. The queen in those dark days was the ruler. And one night Geir came to their hall, and he said, “My queen, I have two souls, the soul of a man who wants to die, and the soul of a beast who wants to kill. I invite you to holmgång.” Then Geir shed his skin, and slew many of the queen’s maidens. Many spears and arrows found him, but they broke on his pelt like ice on steel. Thus was Geir given the name Ironpelt. The queen rose and said, “You who I call Ironpelt, have slain my sisters in my hall, and soaked the stones of Vængheim with their blood. I will go to holmgång with you, but know that your deeds are unworthy, and that the maidens of Vængheim hunt you now for vengeance, not for glory.” And then the queen gathered the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
