"That will only work once, sweetling," Mitrenya explained, as we washed the ichor from our skin. Anyone who, somehow, stumbled upon this place would think that a flash flood had washed the village away. Perhaps they would walk through the streets and wonder why the corpses appeared as such withered husks, but that wouldn't matter. It felt good, to be young once more. I was refreshed in a way I had never experienced before. Mitrenya, in spite of her uncanny agelessness, appeared to be experiencing the same thing. I stretched out, just to test. Where I once couldn't even reach past my knees, it was almost too easy to touch the tips of my toes. "Home, then?" I asked as we clothed ourselves. Mitrenya smiled and nodded, drying her hair with the flick of her hand. Around us, boundless and beautiful, the expanse stretched eternally towards the horizon.
