At the very tip of the Horn of Sarhal, where the waters of the eastern and western Gulf of Rahen clash, a series of massive rock pillars defy the churning waters. Connected to the mainland and each other only by a network of arched bridges, these pillars host the famed Sanctuary of Surael's Sojourn. This temple, also known as the Liziontanziór, has been built as a testament of the abiding faith in Surael shown by the Naléni harpies of northeastern Sarhal. The northernmost pillar carries only a large plaza used by the cloistered faithful and visiting pilgrims alike to observe the journey of the sun from the first gentle rays of light arising over the eastern waters to its fiery descent into the waves of the western Gulf of Rahen. Here the assembled faithful fast from dawn till dusk, contemplate the nature of Surael, and ponder how they may best serve Him in the Struggle. Seamlessly arising from the other pillars are the main buildings of the sanctuary, also given the name EširMašnasur in Bulwari. Rainwater cisterns and sheltered gardens hidden within the walls of the sanctuary, together with the many fish swarming in the waters below, supply the faithful Exemplars cloistered here with all the necessities of life. Across the sanctuary on the mainland, a series of hostelries cater to the needs of the devout pilgrims searching for spiritual enlightenment at this holy site. Liziontanziór’s inhabitants tirelessly train their martial skills and raise their voices in unison to sing battle hymns for the glory of Surael. It is here that they strengthen their faith until they are ready to walk the lands of eastern Sarhal in search of agents of the Malevolent Dark to vanquish.
