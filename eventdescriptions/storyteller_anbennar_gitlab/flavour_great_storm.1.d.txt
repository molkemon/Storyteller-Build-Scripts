Forty damned years. Vranulk has been waiting. He saw the rise of the great caehnate of the Wild Hunt in his youth. Saw them pour into Nuzurbokh, promising the centaurs of his land glory, plunder, and entry to a heavenly domain. He detested them. They are nothing more than rabid hounds, seeking glory where there is none, killing people for no reason but to self-aggrandize. The Filustkhuga are nothing more than a broken people who detest the plains that they have been given by the Storm God himself. They spit on his gift, for which our ancestors have died. And he would be proven right. The Great Incursion was nothing more than a graveyard for those seeking an honourable death in battle, whatever that means. The tent was already quite lively when the caehn entered it. "The raids against Ruin Bringer are very fruitful. Their area of control has been reduced to a sliver of its former self." "What about the southern Filustkhuga? If we were to attack Ruin Bringer head on, even Crashing Wave might intervene? I do not care. They should still be disorganized enough to provide no meaningful resistance. In fact, why wouldn’t we welcome their interference? All Filustkhuga must repent for their crimes against centaurkind, no?" "Ordsikar Tholnuvalk, despite the Sochar ordsiwyns' support, we are still in a somewhat precarious position. Green Flame and its great shaman are still presenting a credible threat, and antagonizing half the centaurs of the plains seems not ideal." "And? Mobilize the shamans. The wind shall push them into the sea once the thirtieth day has fallen." His son argues in his stead. Quite competently even. A good day to be a father.
