The news we have long anticipated has come; the Reach is in open rebellion. For years the grim folk of the Reach have muttered to one another their dreams of sovereignty, have nursed their grievances with their pints. We have made only limited headway in infiltrating the ancient and clannish social circles of the Reachman elite, however, and the name now on everyone’s lips, Crovis, is entirely new to us. Nephew to Frida, the human wife of Brasûr, Crovis entered the capital sometime in the last week, after the gates were opened willingly to him. At the head of a vast host, he has declared his intention to reclaim Vrorenmarch in the name of humanity. He has issued a call to all sympathetic and patriotic Reachmen to support his claim in the east and purge the orks from this land, appealing to the most ancient traditions of the Reach and the justice of Corin herself. Indeed, his Reachman supporters have issued multiple poems and declarations in his name, including odes to his Aunt Frida, whom they absurdly refer to as the “Captive Queen.” The young man’s pretensions are to theatre, it seems, and a most noble place in history as restorer of Vrorenmarch.
