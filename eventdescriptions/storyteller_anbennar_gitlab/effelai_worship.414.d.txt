Our pilgrimage has led us to the foothills of a spine of imposing mountains and a land of towering, majestic trees, their trunks rising like ancient sentinels to touch the heavens. The natural beauty of this place was awe-inspiring, as we marveled at the immense size and sheer age of these colossal forests. A single one of these titans, once felled, was enough for several days of work, a gift for craftsmen who reveled in the opportunity to work with such exceptional materials. Indeed, the quality of the wood was exceptional, seemingly light but no less rigid, and impervious to both moisture and rot. It seems that this land has granted us the rare privilege of taking some of these valuable resources to aid our journey, though to what end, none can say, as our Goddess is rarely prone to benevolence. Though this land is rich in resources, it is not without its challenges. Even here, we are not safe from the seedthralls and their choral slaughter. Monsters, too, have made their home here, with gliding wyverns greedily protecting the most bountiful groves. It is perhaps in our dealings with the local people that this dichotomy was laid bare. They offered us knowledge of healing herbs, such as the root of the Payay flower, the sap of Ceila trees, and the all-important Lifebloom. These gifts were a boon so sorely needed to tend to our wounded companions. However, some of our tribesmen paid a heavy price for their attempt to harvest these herbs themselves, as the very same plants that could provide relief could also become extremely toxic if not gathered with care, and we lost a few of our own in the process, a stark reminder that we are not free of trepidation even in lands of abundance.
