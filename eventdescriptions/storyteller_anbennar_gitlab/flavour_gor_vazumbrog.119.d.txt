This hunt wasn't really about protecting anyone, or proving much of anything, so much as there being a primordial rightness to doing it. The Dauvek Veker were hunters, and, if given the opportunity, hunters ought to hunt mammoths. Tracking them was not difficult, lumbering as they were. The nearby Giant's Anvil could have provided an opportunity to harry the mammoths to a cliffside and trick them into falling to their deaths, if only the Dauvek Veker were cowards. Since the Dauvek Veker were not cowards, they met the giant, wooly beasts in open combat, matching tusk for pike. A few of their number were trampled and gored for their insistence on honor, and many were scarred, but none can deny. they hunted the mammoths.
