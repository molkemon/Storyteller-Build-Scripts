The girl looked out to the bright cerulean sea, ships dipping above and below the horizon, like pendulums on the waters. She did not know her mother, nor her father. All she had ever known was the city, and the sea beyond it. And that was all she needed, she supposed; she did odd jobs for pay, slept in alleys and under docks, and ate with a view to be envied. But one day, as she looked out to sea, she saw a strange sort of ship. Though blue was ever a popular colour amongst sailors, this ship's sails were greener, and as it neared, she could make out the crew. Men and women wore the same greenish cloth, and had strange amulets and jewellery. Some were crafted from shells and such, others from tattered rope and mangled hooks. She heard them preach, in common and tongues older, of the primordial Uelos, how he is Lord of the sea, how he can protect your ships, or guarantee you a bounty of fish. Most seemed to dismiss them as ramblers and zealots, but perhaps.
