Xianjie, nestled in the mountains since ancient times, is home to many sects and its own lineages. Though part of Yanshen by territory, it has remained isolated for centuries due to geographical barriers and divergent worldviews, rarely interacting with the heartlands of Yanshen. Now, with ties between Xianjie and Yanshen growing closer and governance brought under a single order, customs and public sentiment have yet to fully integrate. Lately, mocking and slanderous remarks have begun to circulate among the common folk of Yanshen, branding the people of Xianjie as "Western barbarians" or "mountain dwellers," with some even spreading rumors that they are "not true Yan" in an attempt to exclude them. This trend, originating from the streets, has begun to seep into the ranks of officials and scholars alike. The disciples of Xianjie are not silent in the face of this—some respond with cold indifference, others with outspoken anger. Mutual resentment is growing more pronounced by the day. If such tensions are left unchecked, they may deepen into a serious rift. Officials now debate in the halls. should prejudiced speech be strictly forbidden, affirming the principles of unity? Or should such views be tolerated, acknowledging cultural divergence and differing identities? Or should a long-term policy of integration be pursued to dispel prejudice and bridge the divide?
