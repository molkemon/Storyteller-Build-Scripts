The forest, as always, was a cacophony of life. Trees swayed in the wind. Distantly, the howls of predators on the hunt sounded out. And, in particular, the cricking and chirping of insects filled the air. Cutting through all of this was Khagor Sapchopper’s roars of rage. The scout had not gotten halfway before Khagor had pieced together that another warparty was dead at the hands of Durzk’ungul, the accursed daughter of the forest known as Alara Cenág. This wretch, this bug, this gnat had buzzed his ears insistently since his greatest victory over the elves. She nipped and nicked at him without end, refusing to grant him proper battle. But, he would not be denied. In time she and the other little elflings would be forced to face his might. And when they did, Khagor brought his hand upon an unsuspecting buzzing crownfly. Its buzzing ceased as it was caught between him, the nearby oak, and violent force.
