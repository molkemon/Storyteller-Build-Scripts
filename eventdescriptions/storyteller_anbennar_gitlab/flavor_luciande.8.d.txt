Lucian sits once again in his room, drinking. The red drink pours from the goblet through his lips to his throat, while the servants are becoming ever more doubtful of it being wine. There have been a lot of rumours lately. The monarch has entered the age where many others would have already been sleeping the eternal sleep for years; all the while his beauty remains that of a man in his prime. Chiselled chin, gleaming eyes and a forehead without a wrinkle or a worry in sight. But the monarch does worry. Lucian worries about these exact accusations he faces all the more. The Masquerade must continue. Soon the elder vampires would call Lucian out of his duties to stand in front of them. To continue ruling, he must reassure them that he reigns supreme and unquestioned in his realm.
