Part of respect for oneself is an insistence on being greeted and received properly by others. This is a principle of lizardfolk political life which they have never compromised for the cheap approval of outsiders, and yet few of them can ever really master the complex systems of bowing, speech modulation, conversational order, and meal choice which constitute the etiquette of the high Naga, for example. This is true even if they have strong command of their language, which few outsiders do. In addition, foreign visitors often view this enforced deference as implicitly insulting or demeaning. Lessening the rigidity of such demands on foreign diplomats is a high priority among the outward-looking set at court, as repulsive as it might sound.
