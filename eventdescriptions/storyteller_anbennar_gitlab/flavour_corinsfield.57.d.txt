It was a rather odd trial; the defendant didn’t have much to say. Even with such little defense, the judge hesitated on the verdict. Mouth gagged, arms and legs bound, the so-called witch is dragged to the pyre. Except, it’s not a witch being burned today; it’s a familiar. One of the vicious sabertooth cats used as attack dogs by the savages. It does not look particularly hateful to the onlookers; it looks confused and scared. Regardless, even if it is but a dumb animal, it’s still their enemy. Setting the cat up to the pyre, the counter blinks once or twice. He wets his lips with his tongue and starts to read the hymns; he stops himself after the first word and looks at the beast once more. It doesn’t understand a thing. With a sigh, he grabs the torch and lights the pyre.
