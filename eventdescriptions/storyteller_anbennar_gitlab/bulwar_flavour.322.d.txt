Izmeri, the city of black stone, half built into the cliffs of the Taseh Tiskesh hills, half submerged beneath the coastal shallows, is certainly one of the more unique settlements to be found in eastern Sarhal. Inhabited both by human and lizardfolk Tanizu, its true origins are shrouded in mystery, but it serves as one of the most important permanent settlements found in Ishyamtumu. With the arrival of the Sun Cult to these wild untamed lands, selecting Izmeri as the seat of one of the main temples of the region was a natural choice. Built upon a foundation of the glistening black stone but using a pale sandstone for its actual construction, the Gekeresane or Temple of Blessed Dawn sits directly on the shore, with its sacred garden climbing the cliffs in a series of terraced fields. Stairs connect the central platform around the ziggurat of EduzHalwašattu both with the cliff dwellings above and the submerged residences of the more aquatic inhabitants of the city. Without fail, the rising sun is greeted each dawn by a polyphonic chorus of the devout who gather along the shore, celebrating their joy at the return of Surael’s Light to the world. On the high festival days, the faithful assemble in the early hours of the morning and send out intricately woven garlands spun both with colourful land flowers and sea plants and glowing with the light of bioluminescent moss and algae on the waves towards the horizon, where their gentle light illuminates the waters in anticipation of the coming dawn.
