Giving birth to an egg is hard. It is a painful, time-consuming process for the mother. Even though their anatomy is well adapted to the pressures of egg birth, it is not adapted for making the process comfortable. The harpy population would most likely be much greater were the simple act of childbirth easier to bear. With the advent of the Artificial Egg Surrogate, this fact may yet be rectified. This device uses a mixture of magic and technology to create an artificial womb, which will grow an egg when fertilized by a capable male. The significance of this device cannot be understated; by avoiding the pains of childbirth, their population will be far more willing to raise young.
