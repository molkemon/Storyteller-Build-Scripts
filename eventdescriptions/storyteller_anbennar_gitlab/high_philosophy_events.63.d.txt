It is at times such as these, when our country is strong in action and vibrant in spirit, that we are reminded to look at those who have been left behind by our march of progress. The great ardor of the state has propelled us along our path at great speed; but has that not led us to disregard those who would question our policies? Those who would be harmed for what we otherwise see as the greater good? Many have been dispossessed by our march along the Nitikamarja; perhaps it would be best to sit down with these communities, hear their grievances, and seek to address them? Even if it would involve a deviation from our policies?
