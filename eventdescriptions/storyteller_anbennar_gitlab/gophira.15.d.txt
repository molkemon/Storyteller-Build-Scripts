In the Second Gophiran Empire, the ten Great Banners of the Ironblood Army, the field armies that secured the Empire’s rule, were garrisoned in only a few specific cities, where their power was entrenched and unassailable. This meant that the citystates under their jurisdiction could get on in peace, even while the warlords who ruled them were forced to keep one eye on the Ironblood army next door. We do not have that luxury. Without the existentially dangerous but understandably powerful Great Banners, we have been forced to spread our forces thinly. The garrison commanders in the capital of the encroaching army, recognizing the amount of money to be made there, have abused their power to force many craft workshops' owners to sell their business, employing their own lackeys there in their stead. Not only is this very upsetting to the local trade guilds, but, being soldiers, they are way worse at managing the shops than the previous owners were.
