As if pulled from a drama, a juvenile adventurer wandering the realm chanced upon an indigo merfolk agonizing on a desolate beach. Arriving just in time, the hero offered what aid he could, granting enough reprieve for the merfolk to weave a spell to bridge the language barrier. Sputtering blood and regret, the merfolk spilled their guts, telling a tale of a loved one’s parting words, their children’s future, and the fate of a kingdom in the balance, along with thieving pirates, vile creatures risen from the deep, and a host of other onerous woes. The youth silently pledged to fulfill the merfolk's dying wish, and, his burden lifted, the son of the sea passed peacefully. The hero informed a local magistrate about the encounter, who in turn passed on this story to us. Should we alert the undersea kingdom about the departed merfolk’s terminal plea, or will a questing youth suffice?
