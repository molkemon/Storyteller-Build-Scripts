Mihaela was in her trophy room in Nanru Nakar when Theodore Trinas arrived. When the Errant Prince spotted her, his eyes narrowed. “Oh, Theodore…” Mihaela smiled, as she walked between the displays. “It’s been so long. Enjoyed your little trips to Taychend…?” “I have not.” Theodore replied. He looked the typical Roilsardi gentleman, a rapier at his side. The two vampires began to circle each other. “You rule over a stolen empire, in a stolen palace, filled with stolen relics. It makes me sick.” Mihaela laughed. “Oh, does it now? You know what makes me sick? When I think of how the Blood Court tried to bind me, restrict my liberty– it makes me sick when I think about how you murdered my child.” Theodore’s anger flashed. “He murdered himself. You tried to restrain him, remember? Gods–. You’re just a bundle of regret and guilt and spite, aren’t you? A little girl lashing out.” “That’s what you think…?” The Pale Queen raged too – but she contained it, then channeled it. “No… you just don’t understand. I’ve moved past your petty fear. Only I can see what is true glory. After this is done? After you all understand just what you’re dealing with…? My armies will march, and my ships will sail, and the very blood of the world shall fuel my conquests. And then, at the end of it all, I will stand atop Halann, the master of the new cycle. Empress Autonomous.” Theodore stared at his former comrade, astonished, mouth agape. “What happened to you?” He whispered. Then he shook the doubt away. “No, no… I get it. I understand. You’ve gone mad… and this will be a mercy.” Theodore drew his rapier, then took a decisive step forwards – the glass displays shattered as his boot struck the floor. Mihaela laughed. “My my–you’re actually approaching me?” The Errant Prince pulled a silverstake from his cloak. “I can’t torpor you without getting any closer.”
