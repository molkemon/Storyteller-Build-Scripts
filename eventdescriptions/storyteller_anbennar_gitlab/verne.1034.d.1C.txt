I watched you fly. Your stupid beasts were uncomprehending, but the constant awareness of your riders. This was rich food, a layering of intentional thought and the practiced instinct of the scout, all directed at me. I fed. I watched as thirty or forty demons leaked from the rift. I felt your fear spike, the collective attention of an entire army. I realized, after a week, that you believed I had sent these evil things you fought. You thought me so big. It was flattering.
