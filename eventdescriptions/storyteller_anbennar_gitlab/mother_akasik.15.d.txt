The leader of the Ikhsat flock we aided cycles ago has come to us in yet another private meeting. This time, the one who comes is not the old matriarch but her daughter. She says her mother told her what we did for them years ago, and that she was always very bitter about not being able to give her thanks in a proper way for fear of retribution by the other flocks. She thinks it is the only regret she had. She comes to fix that, saying debts like these can never be repaid, and so she should not fear repercussions. She adds that they would have been long dead had it not been for us, and that what she does is paramount to trying to fill a cave with droplets. She hands you a scroll detailing all the gifts the flock will grant in exchange for the help and an oath of loyalty for as long as the mountains endure.
