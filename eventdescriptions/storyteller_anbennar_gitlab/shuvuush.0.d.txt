The mask worn is of great importance to the Shuvüüsh, as it signifies who one is representing at the moment. Whether clan heads choose to wear their clan mask or their personal one is often a matter of political importance. It signifies whose counsel one is accepting at the moment, as one may speak to the spirits inside the mask, if there are any present. Some amongst the Shuvüüsh are rather consistent in their choice of mask. Shamans in particular, both blackmasked deathpriests and whitemasked medicinemen, never change their mask. Yet most others tend to change which mask they wear frequently, taking great pride in the range of options available to them.
