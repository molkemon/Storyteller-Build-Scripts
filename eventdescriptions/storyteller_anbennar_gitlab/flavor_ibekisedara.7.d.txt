At first it was orderly. Each squad had been issued a list of targets. the royal palace, prominent statues, the obvious remnants of the Dread Fang’s legacy. The palace was ransacked, portraits and tapestries hauled into the streets and set alight. Denge helped her squad pull down one of the statues, the weathered visage of the first ruler breaking apart as it struck the stone. The work was done quickly, efficiently, as expected of an Ibekisedaran soldier. As the day went on, the list expanded. Buildings commissioned by the Dread Fang or his successors were marked next, then institutions said to have supported his rule. The distinction between symbol and structure blurred. Homes belonging to soldiers, clerks, and administrators were added in turn, their contents dragged out and destroyed. By the time night fell, Boskonikon burned. The city had been judged too steeped in the Dread Fang’s legacy to remain standing. When the work was finished, Denge sat with her fellow soldiers among the piles of supplies and valuables they had liberated from the city. They drank from a case of wine taken from the palace cellars, passing the bottles around as they celebrated. In the distance, the light of the flames faded, leaving a field of stone and ash.
