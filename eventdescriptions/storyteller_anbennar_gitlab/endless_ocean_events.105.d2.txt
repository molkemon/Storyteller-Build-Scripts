How tempting they must seem, hooks, when beholden by a fish. So juicy of a meal, so straightforward of a task, so plainly out in the open for all to see. Just approach, and gulp. Perhaps the ease of the task is part of the allure to the fish; it doesn’t need to think much to bite a hook. It could never even fathom falling victim to a trap. We have become victims of a hook ourselves. We have too lackadaisically confined ourselves to documenting that which was easy to understand, the perspectives that were easy to find, and in doing so we’ve overlooked the true headwaters of a faith—culture. We’ve consulted with famous shamans, but have we interviewed local matriarchs? We’ve asked about recorded histories, but have we observed social traditions? Have we mistaken a hook for a meal? Have we done what is easy, instead of what is right?
