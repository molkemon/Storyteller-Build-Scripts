The eunuch and the estate nobles have begun quarreling over who should lead the bureaucracy. The eunuchs argue that they were quite literally made for this, and that they can be trusted to value the future of the state above all else as they hold no other ties and can expect no other legacy. By contrast, the estate nobles argue that their superior pedigree and connections to the nation's elite makes them a far more fitting choice than some insular courtiers. Each demands certain privileges over the state's officials, though none of these privileges would harm the government itself. It is up to the monarch to decide who to support.
