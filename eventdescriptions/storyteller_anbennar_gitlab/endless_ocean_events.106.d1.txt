Astronomers have suggested the stars in the night sky equal the grains of sand on a beach. While the idea may seem absurd, if you look long enough into the dark, you will begin to see dim motes of light surface in the abyss. Peer into the night with a telescope, and you’ll discover pinpricks in the firmament of space. Spend enough time gazing, and you’ll discover what you’d assumed an empty expanse of ink to be teeming with boundless shining stars. How does a mountain become a beach? At face value, stone offers no inroads. It’s unyielding, brittle, defiant. Yet despite these qualities, the murmur of wind and the caress of rainfall grind mountains to motes. A boulder becomes ten stones, yields a hundred pebbles, births a thousand grains of sand, until only dust remains. Infinity is the combination of effort and agglomeration. Our efforts produced infinity. Like starlight, we crossed vast expanses in the hope of illuminating a new corner of the universe. Over hundreds of moonlit interviews and seaside rendezvous, we became the rain and wind shattering the unyielding, brittle, defiant stone of the unknown world and teasing from it thousands of stories. As surely as individual grains of sand make a beach, as surely as motes of starlight make a galaxy, so too do stories make a chronicle.
