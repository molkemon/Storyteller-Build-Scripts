From Nean’kabahn to Nani Kinn, all Haraf’ne cower in sight of the thick pillar of smoke rising in the distance. It is not caused by a mere wildfire. It is the incessant march of Di’ikunail’s servants, fire given form. It is the maniacal destruction caused by Beano’ner, that uncivilized fiend with lava for blood and embers for eyes. Unlike the other Kinah’s revolts, there was neither envoy nor debate, but the message sent by Beano’ner is clearer than any declaration of independence. Like his god, Di’ikunail, he is ready to raze the Federation so that a new society may rise. Such a monstrous logic is in the end quite simple. it is only the reenactment of the natural order; the savannah growing back lush after being purged by wildfire. However, Beano’ner, like every Keiin’achi demon in history, does not seek for society to grow and prosper, only for himself to rule over the ashes. The ruler does not know what to say to the ranks of shivering recruits in front of them. Even the gnomish soldiers, who were not raised with stories of the destruction of the Keiin’achi and the horrors of Di’ikunail’s servants, look at the sky with an air of awe and terror. In the end, the entire procession is done without a word. The army leaves Nani Kinn silently, before starting to march towards the rampaging fire so many of them were taught to fear.
