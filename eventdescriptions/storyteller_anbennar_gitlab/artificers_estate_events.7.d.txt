Recently our biggest artisans guilds are taking on less new personnel, apparently this is caused by fears that otherwise their own sons will be unable to have an assured place in their guilds when they grow up. Whilst this normally wouldn't be concerning it ends a period of industrial growth, and with the current amount of profits the artisans gain out of their workshops it looks likely the industry can grow a lot more. Some suggest that we should end this nepotism by forcing the artisans to take on more personnel, however this is heavily opposed by the representatives of the artisans who see this as an unjust intrusion in their internal affairs and claim it would lead to ineffective hiring practices. As a third option in this problem of underproduction we could sponsor brand new workshops indebted to us.
