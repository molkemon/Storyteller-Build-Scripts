And so it was in a certain year that Nackers arose in the bogs and mires of fair Gerudia. And they did descend upon the cities of men in their thousands, and they stole, and they killed, and they drank, and they broke the Queen’s peace. The monarch was queen in those days. A fisher named Þórdís came to them in Vængheim, and she said, “Your Majesty, my village is full of nackers. For every step I take, I find two nackers underfoot. They eat all the fish, and worst of all, they have stolen my son and put a nacker in his place.” The monarch rose, and said, “The nackers have broken my peace and bled my people. And so have they bled me. In stealing your son, they have stolen my son. I will fly out with you, and I will help you take back our son, or avenge our son.” And then the monarch gathered the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
