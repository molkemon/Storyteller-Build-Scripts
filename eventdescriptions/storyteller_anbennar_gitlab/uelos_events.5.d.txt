The ocean calls to them. The sea is their road, their shield, and their larder, yet it is never something to be called safe. To sail the swells of Uelos' domain is to court death in the search of life, and it is this duality that all from the simplest fisherman to the ruler must embrace. There had been an air of anticipation in the capital for weeks, so when the word went out that a great beast of the sea had been sighted, the harbor surged into action. Each dock was its own diorama of frenzied preparation, captains readying their ships and crews. On one, priests of Uelos blessed crates of harpoons being loaded aboard, on another a group of gnomish inventors hawked mechanised launchers to an interested sailor. Across all of them was a palpable eagerness to hoist the sails and set off alongside the royal huntship. While the Lencenori treat the sea with timid trepidation, fearing what dreaded creatures glide through the depths, they are the descendants of the reavers, scourge of Cannor and rulers of the seas. They will seek out the denizens of the deep and slaughter them in a grand display of blood and faith.
