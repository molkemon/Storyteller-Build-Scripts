The first time I met you, it was in the library. You were buried up to your nose in books and your uncle's old diaries; you and Elethen both. It was quite cute really; you were nestled so close over one particular tome your noses were almost touching. Imagine how all of our lives would differ had I not had the boldness to approach you then. Though that is much like saying if things were different, then they would differ. Still, it does fascinate me how small things have big consequences. Perhaps it is but the beat of a butterfly's wing that set the chain of history in motion. "Stop musing and answer the question?" How rude. Yes, I enjoyed our meeting, of course I did. There’s little I love more than talking to intellectual equals, so meeting one in Elethen was fantastic. Ow. Don’t hit me. Fine, one and a half. When you are not distracted with things like ruling or war, you are quite the scholar. Those are the best times.
