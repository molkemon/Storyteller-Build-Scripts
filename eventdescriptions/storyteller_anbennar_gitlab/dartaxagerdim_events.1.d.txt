The Magušim, or art of magic, has been the birthright of the Bulwari ever since Brasan the Emancipator liberated their people from the yoke of the djinn during the age of the Darkness Sultanates, when the Šedu turned from benevolent teachers to merciless slave masters. But this ancient art has suffered increasingly over the last centuries of rule by the Sun Elven usurpers, and many of the remaining practitioners have been lost during the many conflicts in the Decades of Devastation in Bahar. It is their most solemn duty to revive this ancient art. However, for the time being they can only spare the resources to restore one of the four main Ereš, or knowings, of the Magušim to its former glory. Is it the Erešsašur, the art of forcing order onto nature and the elements, that their wartorn lands need the most? Or should they follow the example of Brasan and support the Erešdirkešir, the art of binding other beings from this plane or others to the will of the mage? Erešmalthar, the art of inscription, on the other hand might allow them to create powerful magical items and artifacts. And let us not forget that the Erešsar, the knowing of Light, is the magical art most favored by Surakel. Which Ereš should benefit from their efforts?
