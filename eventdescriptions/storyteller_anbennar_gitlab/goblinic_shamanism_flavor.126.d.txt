To the goblins, the kobolds were an interesting people. Like them, they stood in a hostile land surrounded by invaders who had come to retake their land, but unlike them, they were driven by a strong religious and social urge that united them all. hoarding. This concept of eternal greed for riches was quite familiar to goblins, but the display of it in massive piles was entirely new and enticing. If the goblin kind was to borrow this idea, however, they were going to do it their way, not through private hoards within their homes but through massive displays available for all to see. The shamans would accept the donations and in turn, keep a running tally of everyone’s hoards and guarantee bragging rights to any goblin that wishes to flaunt their wealth.
