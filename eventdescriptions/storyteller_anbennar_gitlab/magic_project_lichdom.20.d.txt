It might have been a friend, or a lover, or even the ruler's own child. Does it matter? At this point, the faces were blurring together. All of them, equally pudgy with excess, stuffed to the brim with vigor, gorging themselves on the lifeforce of cattle and pigeon and grain. What was it to them? What was it to them that the monarch might likewise wish to do the same? They said something silly. Does it matter? They said something about understanding, how every man faces death and wishes to turn away. That many great monarchs before the ruler had walked the unholy path towards Lichdom, that they each had then stayed their final hand, turned away due to inability or cowardice or simply personal growth. They made a plea to this or that. A conscience that might have existed at one point, a past self whose visage grew more distant with each glance at the mirror, mendacious flesh sloughing away into genuine bone. Did it matter? But is this really what the ruler wants? they asked, a voice so distant it barely warranted attention. To feast on the souls of others, to blight the lands the ruler once had blessed? At some point, the matter of heaven was discussed, some promise of eternal reward. Salvation, however far afield it may seem, is always nearer to you than evil. It is always standing behind you, patiently waiting for you to turn your back. It is never too late. Never. Please. Please hear me.
