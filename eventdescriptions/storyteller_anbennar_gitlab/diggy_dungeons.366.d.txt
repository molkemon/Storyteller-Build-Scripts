The net traps the fallen members. Our front line tries to release them, but the trap appears to have been designed to account for this. Sharp wires rise quickly from underneath, slashing open their arms and heads. For those trapped, it is worse. The metal wires envelop them through their skin and flesh. They gruesomely scream in utmost pain. As they struggle, the wires' length thrusts deeper into them until their body parts are severed. After several agonizing seconds, they stop screaming. The amputees moan, kneeling as they bleed out of their arms. Everything smells like iron, like blood. We move quickly to tend to the wounded, cauterizing the open wounds and trying to stem the bleeding. In such discord, we notice too late a big hole from the room's end. A large scissor-headed bug struggles to get half of its body out of it.
