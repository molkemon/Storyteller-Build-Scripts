The functionary took hold of the scroll, and carefully scanned the project for any irregularity. Immediately three corrections came to his mind. "The lumberyard you plan to contract has closed; the ones in Vels Amsto and Sardobnn can fill in. The Blue Hammer Company was recently blacklisted; you'll need another contractor; halfling road builders are performing remarkably this season. Finally, the ruler requires that all guards overseeing official projects in Arverynn must wear a standard plated chainmail and greaves." Sardanir handed back the scroll, concluding his meeting with the young entrepreneur. Speaking of guards, the next visitor was the captain of Arverynn's city guard, who was reporting tensions between Veykodans and Dolindhans after a Drevkenuco youth pickpocketed several members of the Veykodan Guard. "I'll summon the high priest, as the moral guard of the city she'll know what to do." As Magister of the Keep, Sardanir hosted several audiences daily, overseeing the requests and proposals from the imperial top brass and from the city's promising new talents. Hailing from Arverynn's Crane Quarter, Sardanir was born in one of the first generations since the arrival of Krarhnorians, and he seemed unfazed by the growing hustle and bustle their arrival resulted in. He was in his element, suggesting changes and corrections, doing his part to keep things smooth and clean while the numbers steadily rose. He wasn't quite a big figure as the monarch, the captain or the high priest, and historians might not remember his name. But Sardanir was thankful, remaining hidden from the crashing waves of history, but still acting where it matters.
