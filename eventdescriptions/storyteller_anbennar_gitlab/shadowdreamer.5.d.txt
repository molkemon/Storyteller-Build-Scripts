The young Great Dreamer walked with his eyes closed. His breathing was deep and slow, and those who dared look closely at his face could see that his eyes were moving quickly and randomly beneath heavy, dark lids. Crowds would part before him. Cloaks would be thrown across stagnant puddles that he would not be shocked awake by the splashing of creatures halfcrushed underfoot. He has been doing this for hours a day for weeks, in search of a great vision he seemed to be expecting. The local group, for their part, had agents following and watching from a distance. They saw the potential vulnerability of their prophet wandering blindly. The individual was as stressed by the ordeal as his companion. A whisper was coming from somewhere. A voice beneath the occupied hold, like wind through the cracks. It would prod at his dreams and his waking mind in equal measure, beckoning him to find the deepest dark, to find the source of the Dream. Often he would murmur the things it said aloud, invariably frightening children as he stumbled around like a madman, probing at cracks in the walls.
