Among the people living in a domain, legends of a homeland have long circulated. They speak of ancestors who crossed mountains and rivers, fleeing war or exile, to arrive here generations ago. These stories were once mere idle tales at dinner tables, but with the people and the domain now united, what was distant lore now feels tangible, the ancestral homeland within reach. Recently, a clan living in a province has submitted a formal request to the authorities. They claim to have identified their ancestral lands and seek permission to return, resettle, rebuild ancestral graves, and continue their heritage. Their appeal is respectful and heartfelt, stating this is not defection but a sacred duty to their roots. Local sects dare not decide lightly. Allowing such movement may set a precedent, but denying it may seem heartless. Thus, the case has been passed up for central judgment.
