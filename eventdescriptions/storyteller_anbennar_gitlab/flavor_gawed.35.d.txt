Welyam was never one for ceremony, nor for small talk. His father had always seemed a natural diplomat, but now he wore the crown, and it was his duty to greet every guest, to wear a false smile and speak meaningless words as the feast ran on. That was duty, though, and so he went on with the grim determination of a pikeman on the march. "My King, I wanted to speak with you about an important matter." Welyam nodded. "Very well." The Grand Magnate gestured at the other side of the room to a small group, where a woman roughly the king’s age stood conversing. She was tall, with sable hair and agate eyes, her furs adorned with iron jewelry; a striking figure amongst the more modest lords and ladies of the domain. "My niece Karina has been meaning to visit with you for quite some time, ever since you restored our house here in the domain. With all that we have done for the realm’s stability, I think it is high time the Magnate Council is further entwined with the royal family. For the realm, of course." The king looked to his advisor. "You mean for me to marry her?" Derek smiled, shaking his head. "I ask nothing; she is asking you to dance with her first." Welyam almost laughed at the boldness, to presume he would accept already. He looked over to Karina again, seeing nobles enthusiastically keep up with her conversation, laughs and cheers echoing through that section of the hall. Perhaps noticing him, she paused and looked his way. Then she smiled. For a moment, Welyam forgot the grief and duty foisted on him. Or maybe he remembered himself a boy of seventeen. The King clenched his teeth once more into a smile and walked forward. Not in apprehension but in anticipation.
