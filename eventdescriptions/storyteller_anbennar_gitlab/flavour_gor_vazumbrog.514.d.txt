It's curious, noted Grimmun, these manuscripts are stylistically quite professional, and match with other historical records of the period in terms of tone, materials, and word choice. And yet, it's all wrong. None of the other manuscripts it references exist, and it makes clearly counterfactual claims about the landscape. It seems to suggest that there was a massive, underground empire of dwarves, stretching all the way from one domain to another, ruled by a powerful king, Madarûn the Great. It even details his exploits before becoming king, some of the policies he passed, the swords and rings he participated in forging, but the closest any of this comes to being corroborated is that the holds of those two locations actually exist. No other part of it has any bearing on reality.
