During a long night in the wilderness, a beggar approaches the monarch's camp. He is a wretched thing, nothing but ragged and torn clothes which engulf his frame and a humble sword slung at his hip. His eyes are piercing, still grasping some last remnant of dignity from long gone glory. He takes his blade from its sheath and falls on his knees to the ground, raising it above his head and toward the monarch. Callused hands hold the blade; they seem as if they had had much familiarity with something sharp. The beggar croaks out. "I humbly ask for refuge for just this night; for this I would offer my sword, however you would take it."
