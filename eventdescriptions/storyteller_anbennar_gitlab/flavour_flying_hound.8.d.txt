The tension in the room was palpable. Its small windows up high were clearly meant to be used in conjunction with ladders to serve as emergency arrowslits. In times like these, when their job was reduced to letting in whatever remained of the twilight sun, they seemed completely useless. The single ray to cut through the unease was nowhere to be found. The monarch sat in the tallest chair at the table, surrounded by no fewer than six goblins on each side. Opposite of the monarch was the only human there, the de jure leader of the hasbeen adventurer band Raven Banner. Their past delusions of uniting southern Escann fell prey to the onset of goblinic gallantry, and they were now forced to contend with this rather problematic reality. Said contention culminated here, with their final card played and all their hopes laid bare. “Alas, my good sire, we are but humble knights, and must thus refuse.” “I implore you to reconsider, Sir Knight.” They bowed as much as the table allowed. “Considered we have, convened even, and we have elected to leave this matter to the people best suited for the job. We would love to extend a helping hand, but we shall not engage the monsters directly. It is not the job of a knight, but of a crusader, to slay the vile vampire with a stake to the heart.” “But Sir Knight, what” “This audience is over. You are dismissed.” “Sir Knight. But what if you could instead become crusaders?” Several goblin eyes widened at the suggestion, and several more turned to the monarch, who remained staunchly dispassionate despite the outrageous proposition. The adventurer delegate was no longer seated, standing upright with his arm extending forward, fist clenched, mouth still slightly agape. Each breath felt like an eternity ending and beginning anew. Then, moments before the very concept of time was about to collapse, the monarch spoke.
