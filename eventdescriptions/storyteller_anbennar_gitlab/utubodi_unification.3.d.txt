A disaster has happened on one of our refurbishing sites. An entire scaffold has collapsed, destroying months worth of work in the blink of an eye, and severely injuring the workers, whose efforts will be needed to get the project back on track. Our overseers have been split into two groups with regards to how to best move forward. One group feels that the schedule of this crucial undertaking is already delayed enough that we must work as long and hard as it takes, with little to no regard to the safety of our workers. Their counterparts argue that until the repairs are finished, not much productive work is likely to get done anyway, and that we should take the time to properly train our workers to ensure nothing like this happens again. They acknowledge, however, that this will cause the completion of the project to be pushed far into the future. The only question now is who we side with.
