As in any war, the Bureau has greatly improved its arsenal and methods since the Shadow War started thanks to lessons learned in tragedy and grief. The Bureau has suggested implementing a series of unofficial laws to handle the occult, which would set a framework to manage warlock and warlock-adjacent activities while remaining secret to prevent the general populace from learning about the eldritch and supernatural. However, this would be in direct opposition to the nation's democratic values for obvious reasons. There are two schools of thought about this. the first believes that the nation should focus on being as effective as possible during the Shadow War, but have those laws expire as soon as the situation returns to normal. The other considers that the Bureau would fail at its task to protect the soul of the nation if it went against its values, and that those laws should be made in consultation with elected representatives, even if their memory has to be wiped afterwards.
