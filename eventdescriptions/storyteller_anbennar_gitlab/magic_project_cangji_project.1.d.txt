Spiritfire, as we know it, is a form of energy originating from the spiritual world. With enough psychic concentration and magical force, one can conjure spiritfire from small amounts of flammable material. Materials that were once used to fuel the Undying Flame are particularly useful, as they provide a link with a very powerful mystical source which can be used to easily bridge the gap between material and spiritual. While the summoning of spiritfire can be difficult, the manipulation is even more strenuous, as spiritual energy is often fickle. Many of the Ashen Soul have suffered burned whiskers or singed fur trying to move spiritfire into their desired forms. We have developed many techniques which can be used to effect the shaping of fire, but these are crude demonstrations of what spiritfire manipulation could truly bring about. The two popular methods that have emerged from our studies are derived from the Kartya that many of the Ashen Soul study alongside their magic. One technique, based in the aspects of Dekhana, focuses on using spiritfire to augment the effectiveness of one’s magic and physical strikes, granting fists of fire and quickened spells. The other, rooted in Pichka, instead uses spiritfire directly, striking the foe with psychic flames for decisive victories. These two principles can be applied to the monasteries as well, but which one will serve us best?
