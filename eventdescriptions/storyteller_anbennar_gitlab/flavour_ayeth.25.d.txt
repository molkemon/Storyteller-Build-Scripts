I have never liked the halls of Cenág, yet I walk through them slowly, unarmed. There is no defense I could carry into this hall that would matter anyway. Not here. Not before her. Alara stands at the center. Hard. Her silence speaks more than any oration. And beside her, my daughter Lanhara; still, but watchful. Her eyes... she has the eyes of her grandmother... they dart between us like someone trying to stop a fire from jumping to the next tree. I try to find words, but they flee me. These faces I once called family now meeting me like I’m some stranger on a black road. “I’m not here to issue orders,” I begin, voice low. “And I’m not here to ask for forgiveness. We need each other’s help. I need Cenág’s warriors in the east and you need my rangers to retake your grove. Let me help you.” “No,” she says. Lanhara’s hand brushes her arm. “Al...” Alara turns to her. “He has led our people to ruin before.” “You know that’s not fair,” Lanhara says. “I don’t care,” Alara snaps. “He abandoned kin and kin to die in the fields while he chased his vengeance. I won’t let him do that to my clan.” “You’re right,” I say. “I left. I became someone unrecognizable. And I do not expect you to follow a ghost who burned his own path through the forest. But this same ghost has retaken his home and makes you a vow,” I continue, stepping closer. “Give me the warriors of Cenág, and I will retake the Bone Grove too. And if I fail, I step aside, you become ceanna again.” Alara closes her eyes. Breathes in slowly. Then she turns to my daughter and asks. “Do you trust him, Hara?” My daughter nods. There is a long silence. “You have five years, áthyr'ionn,” Alara says. “No more. No less.”
