The vast majority of our merchants choose to only set sail at dawn on clear days, both out of a practical concern for fair weather during their voyage, but also because they believe it signifies a good omen from our Goddess. The calm waves lapping at the hull while the sun glints off the water are believed to give a sense of calm that brings one closer to Yukel. This occasionally leads to a phenomenon known as the sunrise rush, where many merchant vessels all try to schedule departures within the same two hour period in the morning, far more than the harbor can handle. This province has been experiencing Sunrise Rush far more frequently than is normally expected.
