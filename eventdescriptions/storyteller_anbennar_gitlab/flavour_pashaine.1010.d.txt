The mage offers their empty promise. the evervigilant sage, fireball in hand, who saves the citizen from the bother of the fight. Yet such deals are poisoned chalices. Give up the rifle and one gives up the means to resist, the means to fight off the tyrant who commands the unthinking, shambling mass. A polity of laws must be inhabited and defended by its own, who win by their fight a stake in the project of us all. No. it is right to bleed for the land upon which one stands. We did not scheme our love of our homes in high towers or cosseted reading rooms – we breathed it in, and trod in the mud of it. We must revere our citizensoldiers, our guild of an army, and reject the coward’s logic of the bonepuppeting lich.
