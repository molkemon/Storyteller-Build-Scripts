The monarch woke to find a delicately engraved piece of driftwood in their chambers, which called for a meeting in a secluded cove near Khugra at the deepest hours of the night. The Araizary rarely spoke directly with mortals, even with those that had their favour, so only a matter most urgent could demand this meeting. Once in the cove, exactly at the given hour, the monarch's interlocutor appeared, a small Araizary taking an ethereal, serpentlike form, its body constantly shifting and changing, as if made of flowing water and wind. It spoke in a soft tone and with a peculiar, archaic accent. "We do not usually speak like this with mortalkind, not since ages past, but your people have proved to be true friends of my kin." Before the ruler could thank the Araizary for its words, it continued. "Millennia ago, we and your people shared a goal. But eventually, another faith distracted your minds; you followed the path marked by our sisters in the stars, and our journeys across the seas ceased to be a priority. We did not blame you; your fate is yours to decide. However, when your people restored our ties, stronger now than ever, the hope of a return to those days entered the minds of my kin. And now your ships sail farther, no corner beyond our reach. It is time we could find her, if you help." "Find who?" the monarch asked, and the spirit passionately replied. "The Mother of the Seas, our mother, and yours too, the slumbering NagaKatesh. She rests hidden somewhere in these oceans, and our two peoples sought her together once. Will we be united in our quest once again?" Seeing the monarch's smile, the divine spirit knew the answer before the ruler could speak, and in a way, it smiled too.
