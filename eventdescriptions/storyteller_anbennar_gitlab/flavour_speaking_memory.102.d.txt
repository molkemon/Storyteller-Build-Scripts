At this point the field was more tent than field, and yet people kept coming. One such person arrived on the very edge of the chaos, and pitched his tent next to an empty space, which was promptly filled by a kobold with a wooden flute strapped to her chest. He gawked for a bit; this was unexpected. Was she some sort of lizard? "Hello," he said, providing an opportunity to begin conversation. The woman didn’t look up and began playing a tune on her flute as she sat atop some crates. He decided to try again. "Would you like to hear the story of" She played on, and at once the rocks around her space began to shape themselves into a very nice house, complete with a small pool and a wooden fence. She looked at him with an irritating grin. "Would you like to hear the story of me? I came here to tell it." The man looked out across the field, which was already beginning to smell like cooking fires. The echo of hundreds and hundreds of tales from all the different parts of Insyaa formed a great low murmur, all being told at once. And yet, the tales out there couldn’t turn stones into wood, and they weren’t told by people as interesting as this one. He looked back at the kobold. "If you buy us dinner."
