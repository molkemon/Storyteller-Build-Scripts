We first heard their songs during the raids from their ships. Many goblins took to the melodies long before we even knew what they meant, but with more interactions with the giant northmen, more and more goblins began to be swayed. You would not expect goblins to have much in common with those giantbloods of the north, and in fact, some might say we still don't. Even with a slow conversion of some of the population over to Skaldhyrric cults, their practices are usually slightly warped from the original intentions. There are many of our folk who immediately took to the stories, their intentions, and the legend and history behind them. They realized the stories of Skalds keeping a dragon chained in sleep suspiciously connected with their legends of chaining a dragon in the Serpentspine. Many more, however, just liked how they sounded. Goblin skalds, for the most part, care less about the meaning of the words and more about how the melodies and sounds make you feel. Processions of Troubadors are loud, booming, colorful, and fun. When a goblin skald does focus on the lyrics more than the music, there is a tendency to twist the tale a bit. There are many stories spreading through our country that are very suspiciously like the tales of the northmen, but with names changed to the skald's grandfather.
