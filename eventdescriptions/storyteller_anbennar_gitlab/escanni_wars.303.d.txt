The monarch sighs as their current generals have been arguing nonstop with each other at the conference table over which path to go forward. Despite their experience, the tactics have grown stale with a lack of new ideas, shown clearly by one general suggesting that he will perfect the army by making them practice with loaded guns while another suggests that she will just make them have to win battles for their pay. Those two generals, while very educated in the realm's tactics, are narrowminded in their views. It is clear the military needs a shakeup, but it seems we need a new angle, new ideas not taught in our war academies. We need an outsider. The monarch stands up and raises their hands. The monarch says, “Both of you, pack your bags. Effective immediately, you two are fired.” As the two generals grumble while being escorted out by guards, the monarch looks at the scribe in the room and says, “Sir, I want you to send a message to all the courts out West to find someone more competent than those two buffoons. Surely, Lorent, Gawed, or even Verne has better generals ready to be employed.” The scribe nods and leaves the room with his message, passing by the fired generals on their way out.
