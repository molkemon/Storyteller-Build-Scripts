The Covenant is not just a name; it reflects the promise and the binding agreement that kept their blades together in dark times and through the tribulations that came with the call to arms against the might of the Greentide. In this post-Greentide world, however, what this promise has come to mean is something that has divulged across their nation with the challenges to the ideals they aspired to. A captain looks to his men to see what honor means to them as he prepares to exhort them of the need to take action now against an eldritch cult; the lone archivist who wanders into the forbidden sections of old libraries in search of knowledge knows she has the power to save people; the knight errant who has journeyed long and seen where ideals led others as he begins to cavort his own power and focus on himself alone. They all swear that they hold true to the promise that a mageknight exists to serve others, but they all see this promise in a different light. But which of these attitudes holds the most sway in Covenblad and what its people expect when they hear of the Sword Covenant and the Covenbladic government's involvement in situations?
