Terrible weather, innit? The monarch whirled around. A dwarf, clad in dirty traveling clothes, with hobnailed boots trailing mud on the finest rug. "How in Kazraiel's name did you get in here?" "Don't think Kazraiel's the type to sneak. I just walked. Besides, I just want to ask you something." The monarch frowned deeply. There was something off about this one. For one, how did they get past the guards? "Ask then." "You a gambling sort?" The monarch could see the dwarf smiling like a demon behind their grimy beard. "Depends what is at stake." "Good answer." The dwarf procured a bottle of wine. "This is Dwarovar Dark," they said, matter-of-factly. The monarch’s heart skipped a beat. "Impossible." "Anvelind hates liars, so no. You though. You claim rule over wine. That's well and good. But there's one wine you haven't beaten yet. So how about this? Seven days from now, you host a little party for your lords and ladies.
