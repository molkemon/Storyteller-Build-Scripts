The news spreads across Sarhal unevenly, racing along trunk roads and spreading down country lanes just as blood flows into a lung. Indeed, Sarhal can breathe again; the Coven of Yezel Mora is broken. Spontaneous celebration erupts in every town the news reaches; border skirmishes pause for a day or two; markets see a little less squabbling than normal. The shadow which has darkened Sarhal for so long is no more. The Coven was broken by the slaying of Auntie Komina, most beautiful and manipulative of the three. Responsible for the curse which made the Ashhana into crocodilian beasts, and for innumerable seductions and betrayals in Sarhal’s storied history, her death is said to have released an immense and deafening scream and to have left no body. The fate of the other two hags is unclear; Granny Ilfa is known for her ability to escape and conceal herself in shadow, while Mama Hireet still possesses loyal forces at her disposal. Still, without the Coven entire, the two survivors are profoundly weakened. Those who seek in their vengeance to put others beneath the boot will no longer have Hireet’s braying encouragement. Hireet’s pride was her undoing, but the fate of the other two hags is unclear; it is reported that Auntie Komina disappeared almost at the moment of Hireet’s death, while Granny Ilfa is notorious for her ability to escape and conceal herself in shadow. Still, without the Coven entire, the two survivors are profoundly weakened. No doubt the hags that remain may one day once again threaten Sarhal, but for now, the world feels a little lighter than it did.
