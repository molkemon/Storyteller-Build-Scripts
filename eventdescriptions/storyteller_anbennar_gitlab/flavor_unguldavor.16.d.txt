In the royal palace in the capital, Chief Borgu Severed Ear, chancellor and chief of the Several Ear clan, approached the throne. He had been a respected member of the court, responsible for some reforms giving more authority for the chiefs to raise their own armies and end the royal conscription. He always wore a sword strapped to his waist as he observed the monarch with the calculating gaze he always had. “My monarch, I know how to ensure our nation remains perpetually strong. I propose that we rewrite the privileges of the different clans. Perhaps instead of a permanent confederation of five preset clans, I suggest we rotate around the clans that help guide the state. We could give the Upper Nath to the remaining Bonecarvers, Trialmount to the remaining Rotcleavers, and even hand Oldhaven to the Blacktowers. What do you say, my monarch?” The monarch looked at Borgu with skepticism. “Are you sure that it will not provoke a revolt? Revoking clan rights only to give it to other clans seems like a recipe for disaster.” Borgu nodded. “It would, but I can help ease tensions. Just let me go on a tour of the realm, and I can convince them of the necessity of it.” The monarch scowled, but ultimately let it pass. “Fine. But do you need any soldiers?” “No need, your highness. My own retinue will guide me safely.” That was three months ago. Since then, we have been unable to get into contact with our most trusted advisor. Internal raids have rocked Unguldavor and a recent human trading convoy was found murdered, their bodies tossed into the Alen river.
