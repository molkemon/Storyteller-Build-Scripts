When Khoidarg the ruler felt the urge to stroll through the city, they felt like something about it had changed. The grim facades of the Barracks towered intimidatingly above the narrow streets, as they were supposed to. The Harbour was bustling with life, as if the blockade had never happened. The fires of the Glassworks roared, like they have never been extinguished in the first place. After having a long and quiet discussion with some advisors in the Scholarly Quarter, the realization finally struck him. Yes, war had come to this city. But now? Now it has truly passed.
