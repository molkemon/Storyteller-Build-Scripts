The east rumbles, a stampede of wooly rhinoceros heralds the rocking of royal carriages, sturdy wheels rolling onwards as the elites riding on top lead the beastmasters and painted warriors to war. Several rows of troops form on the horizon and threaten to swallow the Moon Field. A cacophony of shouts, both male and female, is heard as the soldiers and settlers hastily retreat to the monastery, trying to reinforce its gate and fill the breaches with whatever objects they can find. A captain is leading the defense, and he emboldens their spirits masterfully, reminding them that a previous engagement was equally outnumbered, and he did not have walls nor a gigantic mound to protect him. In his heart, the captain is thankful to the fates for giving him such a picturesque stage for his last stand. Two soldiers mutter something as they sit by one of the towers' stairs. They were not supposed to be here; they had volunteered just to see the Moon Field and maybe shoot a few barbarians. Why did it have to come to this?
