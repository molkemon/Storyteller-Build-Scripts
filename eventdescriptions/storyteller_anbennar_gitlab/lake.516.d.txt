When we sent Tzidoilhol a proposal to amend the Ultakal decree in order to deepen the ties between our cities, our offer was met with little distrust. The main point of the proposal was that whoever did not control Ultakal at the time would host a set of venerated children of the sister city for safe keeping. When the representative of Tzidoilhol saw the large crowd of beloved daughters and sons of our city assembled in front of our townhall, he took it as a sign of our sincerity. These exchange students should have equal opportunities in the respective sister city and should be allowed to stay, if they wished to do so. This detail of the new amendment was seen as another sign of goodwill. Another part of the amendment to the Ultakal decree looked less amicable, even to their eyes. the exchanged children could be treated in any way fit by their host, if either party at any point did not meet their contractual obligations.
