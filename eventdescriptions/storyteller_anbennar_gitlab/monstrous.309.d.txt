Screams echo through the burning village in the hills. Listen closely, and find that each has a different flavor. Some are the dying yells of defenders, tasked with keeping their village safe. Some are the anguished cries of the women and the children, who knew all along that it was not safe here, yet never thought the danger would come for them. But what is the danger? What are the loudest screams, those keening martial sounds above the air? They are ours, of course. But unlike the fleshy groundbound blobs from whom we take our due, we do not scream out of anger or impotence. As our warriors dance among the sky, they cannot help but shriek their cries of joy, for we are of the hills and the hills belong to us, and there can be no greater glory than that.
