During one trip to the High Temples, the ruler was approached by a merchant of renown, asking what is, in their opinion, the difference between Faith and Trade. The obvious answer was, one is a spiritual matter and the other a material one. "No. But also yes," was the merchant's reply. "Both are in principle the same. Exchange one to gain something else; in this case, we exchange our time and dedication into faith to get the blessings of spirits, gods, or even ourselves. Trade does that too, exchanging our time and dedication to gain goods, money, and wealth. That does not mean that they have to be separate, no? In the end, we merchants do also exchange our wealth and goods to enrich ourselves in faith and piety. But there are exceptions on both sides, no? If you are interested, I'd like to discuss some more with you."
