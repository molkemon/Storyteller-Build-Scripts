Father Godwin was not one who trusted others easily. One of the very few who he had ever trusted was Calas of Turnmarket. He was the man who had first come to the Father; he was the one who told him of the lords of Esthíl’s vile designs. He had accompanied Godwin across the seas, and he served as one of the Father’s closest advisors. So then, what about him bothered the Father so? A memory flashed in his head. One of the first nights the Corinspilgrims had spent after ascending the cliffs of Ruin; the fateful night when the rune warriors made themselves known for the first time. They attacked them, yes, but how did so few pilgrims die? A memory he had forgotten; perhaps removed entirely; resurfaced. Him and Calas had been separated by the savages and their fierce sabertooths and then Calas did something and they rejoined the group; what did he do? Father Godwin suddenly sat up in bed. Calas… no. He hadn’t… But then, if… Calas had fired flames at the savages to make them scatter. Without a doubt, he was a mage. Afterwards, he had cast some spell upon the father himself to make him forget. But then… If he was a mage… When he had first met Calas, he wore the same cloaks as the men who had taken his daughter away. There was only one solution, then. That was how he knew. Not only was he a mage; he was one of the lords of Esthíl who had brutalized his daughter so. What a fool he was for never putting the pieces together. It was so obvious, from the start. How else could he have known about their experiments. The Father felt his breathing grow faint, and a single word echoed throughout his head.
