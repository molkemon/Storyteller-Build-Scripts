It was the last chamber, the place they had been aiming for, maybe without even knowing it. A way out, a place to count their riches. Even these people, who liked to pretend the way elves do that they had seen everything, couldn’t help but stare. It was a taller and broader dome than the Hall of the Exemplaries at the Vernissage; maybe the biggest indoor space they had seen in their whole life. At its centre, a plume of water, a column of refracted transparency rising right to the ceiling. The fountain was so perfect that it appeared almost still, a pillar of glass. They had never seen anything like it before or since. It was clear that it fed the lake they saw up top, which was funny because they had been worried about whether the lake water would be potable. It was probably the purest water in the world at the time. Do you want to know why they left the academy? It was those elves. A hundred and fifty years old at the youngest, and they looked at this place with all the wonder of a child. It was life, get it? They didn’t want to die on their next errand, miss out on the chance to look like those elves did. They wanted to live.
