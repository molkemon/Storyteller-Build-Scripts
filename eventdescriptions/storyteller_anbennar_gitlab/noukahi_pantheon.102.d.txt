It was then that the damsel, or was it a poet? The ravages of time spare none, not even the wisest. The elderly Griot and fellow pilgrim that the ruler is sharing dinner with is proof of this sad fact. The tale he is recounting is a quite popular one; the ruler must have heard it a million times since they were a child, and they could recite every last comma. The Griot, however, is quite struggling to do the same, a great tragedy for a man whose memory is his worktool. Some in the retinue share mocking smiles, and even some poorly hidden laughs are heard. The embarrassment and sadness in the face of the storyteller do nothing but grow, and you wonder if this is an opportunity sent by the gods, to prove your virtue by aiding an old man in preserving his honor.
