Mavelos Stonebreaker woke up and prepared himself for another grinding day of siege. His army had been camped out in front of the fort for an unreasonably long time, especially for a people whose entire existence was based around movement. In jokes with his warband they had privately named the place “The Block”, because nobody ever came in and nobody ever went out. Of course, Mavelos had reason to be thankful. His job was the easiest in the entire army. he guarded the front gate to make sure nobody left for supplies. If nothing else, it was an opportunity to sit and reflect. Mavelos was thinking of beginning to write his memoirs. Today would not be the day he started on that, however. A crash came from inside the fort, followed by another crash. Mavelos rang the bell to alert the camp of activity, and a horde of centaurs came stampeding from the main encampment. A last terrible crash came from the fort, and the iron doors of the fort came off their hinges. The army braced for impact, but only one creature came from inside the fort, and it had four legs was that a centaur? Mavelos supposed it was his job to figure out what was going on. He gripped his sword and approached the strange centaur. Mavelos held his sword in front of him. He drew closer and closer, until his sword came loose from his hands, flew up, and somersaulted away into the horizon. The centaur spoke. “I wouldn’t do that if I were you.” One hundred questions came to Mavelos at the same time. As he opened his mouth to ask the first, the centaur interrupted him. “You don’t need to worry about that, that, or the other thing you were going to ask. They’re all dead.” The breeze picked up. Mavelos could smell copper. This was some form of elaborate prank. Sputtering for breath, Mavelos desperately managed to get out one word. “Why?” The centaur walked past him on his way to rejoin the road. He turned back to Mavelos, and with a slightly pitying look he said, "It comes with the job…”
