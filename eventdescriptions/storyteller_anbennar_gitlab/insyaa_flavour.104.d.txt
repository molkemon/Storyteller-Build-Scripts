"Don't be stupid," I said, as I hopped up on a stool to get a better angle, my claws tight around the okalu grip. I found myself being firm, as though ByDappledLight were a frail old woman with stuck scales she could not shed, prideful about having someone else prise them up for her. In this specific regard, I suppose Dappled really was like that. Any question of memory missing, something lost, and they would become stubborn and reactive in a way I had never associated with mechanim. It was almost a relief, actually, to know they too could be so unreasonable. "I don't know what I will remember," said Dappled, and as I held the okalu in place, I found myself scoffing. I was being rather harsh, in truth; I have never been good at reassurance; but I loved Dappled too dearly to lie to them. There were horror stories of memories uncovered by the okalu scanner, things plucked from the great abyss of dormancy. Some mechanim recalled lost friends, remembered atrocities after millennia that might have been better buried, became obsessed with finding places the okalu revealed, places that had once been vital to them. Perhaps I'd have done better to remember that. "None of us fleshy folk ever know what we'll remember," I said, fervently. "But ignorance is worse. It has to be."
