The days of the year saw Vængheim at war, for the queen named all they saw as theirs, yet few would find their knees for them. And maidens many had their roost, with will to bring their knees to them, by spear if need be. Yet Vængheim built her roost on war and mercy both. And those who found their knees did live, and were the better for it, for in defense they took them, to the bane of their foes. So it was that Vængheim met the giant realm of Obrtrol, who sought to bring them an end. Yet Vængheim was to be their bane, for they had friends in Dalr, who had in years of bitter learning, found traps to fell the giants. And trolls did suffer mightily, for mannish wile and skybound maiden, was their doom together. Then a troll named Freydis Oaktongue, came to Vængheim, and she said, “I speak not for all, for many seek you dead. And they see lands they had and lost. And they would take Gerudaghot, or take death for its want. And they will not be yours. But I have heard that you bring peace, and I would ask for such, for my clan and I know well your mannish friends, and ours is a cruel end, if no peace can be bought.” Then the queen said, “You speak true, for many here would gladly take your head. They know your gifts of chainbound wrist, but songs of peace are new to them. If now you sing to me of peace, the gift of peace I give you. For I am under oath of blood, and will not mute this song.” And though the hall did mutter, the queen bid Freydis find her knee.
