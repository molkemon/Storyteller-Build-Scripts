Orcs were terrible conversation partners. Having traveled with two dozen Silentblade assassins for more than a week now, Blackheart had heard little more than complaints, boasts, arguments, and terrible jokes. They had all been informed of the gravity of their mission, but none of them seemed to grasp it. She did not think she would ever be in the presence of a race more obnoxious than elves, but she now found herself nearly nostalgic for the endless arguments and obsequiousness she had endured in her diplomatic missions to Salla Elyn and Salla Cénag. Still, she understood what the Prince saw in them. Their flesh was soft, their minds were dull, and their lives were short, but they more than made up for it in their strength and their violence. Boring as they were, they would serve, as the elves never would. Elynáile awaited, and this time she would not bring words.
