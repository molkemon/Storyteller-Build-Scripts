So basically, they’re saying they want their stuff back,” the Forest Goblin said. I stared at the creature who had somehow become our translator. Three days of my life for that. Three days spent trapped listening to endless meetings and obtuse discussions. Of course. Leave it to diplomats to waste all that time to get the point across. The goblin shrugged as I pinched my nose. At first, I had been excited at meeting these Ourdi. It was a chance to range beyond the woods for reasons beyond war. I’d meet humans like the fieryhaired woman in Elynaile again. Perhaps even enter the great castle upon the great lake the scouts had been gossiping about. That did not happen. This whole affair was a war, just different from the ones I typically fought. Words and barbs were traded instead of blade and arrow. None of these humans held that spark she did. And, worst of all, I did not get to enter the castle, much less even see it. “How about I give them a fist up their ass?” I muttered. The goblin looked at me, wondering if that’s what I wanted to tell the ruler. I nearly nodded, but Lanhara stepped in. “What she means is ‘The Ceanna now understands the cause of this discord. However, these lands are still in chaos. Perhaps it would be easier to resolve this once our shared enemies have been defeated?’” The goblin rolled his eyes and relayed the message. Or at least something like it. The ruler, the monarch, grunted then sharply exhaled some gibberish to the goblin before standing up to leave. “Didn’t catch all of it but it was pretty much a give us back our land… or else.” I sighed to myself. I’ll never get to see that castle will I?
