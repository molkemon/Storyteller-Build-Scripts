A flock of harpy nobles intends to increase relations with the ruler, and to do so has offered that the ruler may pick who they please among their mates, in exchange for some of the ruler's own mates among their harem. This is not an uncommon practice among harpies, but is typically reserved for noble diplomacy, as in this case. Since biology forbids two flocks from being directly joined by blood, an exchange of mates is used so that descendants may be born of fathers that both the flocks have picked.
