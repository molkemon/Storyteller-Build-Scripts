Arianne knelt beside her fallen comrade for only a heartbeat, breath ragged, armor dented and smeared with gore. In death, Reinia's eyes seemed empty of the kindness that had always shone from them. But there was no time for grief, not when the beasts were closing in. Dominic clutched his notes, face pale, his usual calm shattered. Eledas was already moving, dragging the halfling towards the far end of the tunnel. Kalas gripped his dagger, whiteknuckled, as Royan fumbled with his jammed crossbow. "Arianne," Kalas yelled, voice shaking. "We can't." "I know," she snapped, standing tall. She turned, sword gleaming as she faced the horde. "Go. Now. I'll hold them as long as I can." "We're not leaving you." Dominic's voice cracked as he fought to break free, but the elf held him fast. "You will," she said, voice hard as iron. "I'll buy you time. Get out of here. All of you." She took her stance, blade steady, breath calm. The others shuffled behind her, retreating down the tracks, footsteps echoing faintly as they disappeared. "Light watch over you, Arianne," Eledas whispered, voice nearly drowned by the sounds of the approaching monsters. No more words were spoken as the party slipped into the deep. Arianne stood alone, the cold of the cave sinking into her bones. The creatures charged. She raised her sword.
