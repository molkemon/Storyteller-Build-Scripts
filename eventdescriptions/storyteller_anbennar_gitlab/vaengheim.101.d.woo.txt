And so it was in the year that the great white wolf Hati awoke from her long sleep in far Gerudia. And with her wretched brood did she descend upon the fields of men, and stained the snow with their blood. The queen in those days was the monarch, who heard of Hati from Gudrún the blacksmith, whose husband and children Hati had devoured. Gudrún had sworn her vengeance on Hati, and asked the monarch to share her cause. The monarch said to Gudrún, “Your cause is my cause. Your spear is my spear. You go to battle, and I fly with you. For slaying my people and spilling their blood upon the snow, I will take Hati’s pelt, but her heart is yours, for yours is the heart she has broken.” Then the monarch rallied the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
