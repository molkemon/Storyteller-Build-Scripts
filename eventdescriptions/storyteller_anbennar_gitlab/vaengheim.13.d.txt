Long did they travel in the old caves below Vængheim, and the monarch took heart, for they were not alone. A great gate did they come upon, shut and shuttered. And long did the trolls, who were strongest in the host, toil in prying ice from wood and steel. Then did the monarch approach the lock, and from their mailshirt a key offer, for many duties were given unto the maidens of Vængheim, but greatest was the duty of the queen of the roost, to keep this door sealed. And the monarch said, “I feel the eyes of many mothers upon me now, to open this door, who they kept shut. Yet this thing I must do, for Elkaesal will be our bane, so that none will live to sing of my dishonour.” And then the monarch opened the great door. And from behind it did blow a wind of ice, cold as to burn like fire. And many in their host suffered greatly, for the chill struck them and stole their breath. And some wished to turn back, for the cold had pierced their hearts, and brought to them fear of a bitter end. The monarch said, though their teeth did chatter, “If the cold has taken your hearts, know that it tugs at mine as well. Those of you who would turn from this toil, do not walk in shame, yet go to my hall, and bring word of our plight.”
