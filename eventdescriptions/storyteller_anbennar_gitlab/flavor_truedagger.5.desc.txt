History remembers the greatest conquests as grand, impressive battles. We know differently. We know that, when we moved on the domain, the real combat unfolded in the shadows of their hold. It was almost too easy. Subvert a bitter captain of the guard and pay her handsomely to sell us military secrets. Arrange a contract with a well-connected smuggler, and sneak a few assassins into the hold through his secret network of tunnels, allowing them to occupy key parts of the domain and murder a few officials along the way. After that, with their defenses weakened, all our assassins needed to do was take out the leadership, one by one. Some deaths were quiet. Others were gruesome, even for a goblin's standards. All served to spread fear and discontent. Well before our armies arrive, not one inhabitant will feel safe in their home, let alone behind their walls. By the time our forces are spotted down the ancient railway line, the citizens of the domain will be fearful enough to throw open their gates and welcome their new protectors.
