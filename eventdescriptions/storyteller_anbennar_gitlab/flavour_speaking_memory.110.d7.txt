In the days of the Sickness that swept across Kanolowele, there was a great hero, and his name was Nakoa. He quested in search of a powerful stone he had heard tell of called the Pearl of Life, which could cure the Sickness when held by the pure of heart. He sought power and glory. Behind him, there was a boy named Liko, who had also heard the story of the Pearl of Life, and set out on a journey of his own behind the hero Nakoa. He sought to cure his mother. Glorious Nakoa first went to the Isle of the Serpents, where he sacrificed his companion Ailani to pass through the Serpents’ Den. Behind him was Liko, who gave the Serpents a flower, that they might see his love for his mother. The Serpents let him pass. Powerful Nakoa then was forced to travel through the storms of Kanolowele, and he and his companions lashed themselves to the hull of their ship and forced their way through. His companion Ikaika was thrown overboard, but Nakoa did not stop. Behind him sailed Liko, who moved with the storm, and was guided instead of hindered by its winds. Conquering Nakoa then arrived at the island of the Pearl, and slashed his way through the trees to its resting place. The trees whispered to him of the treachery of his last companion Haukea. The trees whispered lies. And yet, Haukea did not survive. Behind Bloody Nakoa, Liko did not travel through the trees to the Pearl; he trusted the sea, as he always had. The trees were outraged. Lonely Nakoa emerged victorious with a pearl in the end, but the land withdrew from him. He was stranded on the sea. Desperate Nakoa lay starving and thirsty beneath the sun. Weak Nakoa whispered to the pearl to heal him. The pearl did not listen. Dead Nakoa slumped over on his ship. Glory rolled out of his grasp. And behind him was Liko, who was given the true Pearl by a friendly shark. Liko was guided home by the sea, and he used the Pearl of Life to cure his mother and his people. And when he was done, he returned the Pearl to the ocean.
