It was in the days of a certain year, when the monarch was queen. And the monarch did gather the people of Gerudia beneath their wings, and made them theirs. And whether mannish, trollish or orkish did many find their knees before Vængheim, and they were better for it. One day did a troll named Freydis Oaktongue come to Vængheim. And she knelt before the monarch and said, “O queen, I come to you from the forests of Ðigoveða, where your name is spoken fondly. And we sing songs of our forefathers there, who ruled this land long ago. And I would gather a host, and to Gullmork wander, there to bore into the bones of the earth. For I know that there lies the house of my fathers and mothers, and of they who gave my people breath and purpose. And I would see this place with mine own eyes, and I invite you to share in the bounty of my toils, if you will lend me the strength of your house.” Then did the monarch rise and say, “Well do you speak, o daughter of giants, and your name is well chosen, for your tongue is wise like the oak. Mine own strength will you have, and the strength of any in this hall who seeks the bounty you offer. For we know less of your people than we should, and the gift you give is a rare thing.” Then did the monarch call upon those in their house who sought to know of the world that was, and many skalds did they count in their host. And they set out for Gullmork, with hearts and minds ablaze with the flame of knowledge.
