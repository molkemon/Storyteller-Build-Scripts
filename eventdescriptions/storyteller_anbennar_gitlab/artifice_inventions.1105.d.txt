The people of Halann often argue about the divine. There are questions of existence, such as whether the local pantheon is real or if a specific figure is an ancestor or a god, but also those of power, like whether one type of being can kill another, or which religion has the strongest paladins, or morality, such as whether they should follow the Great Spirits or sell their souls to the Infernal Court. These questions have seemed intractable, and for centuries, philosophers and theologians have debated them, but they must do so no longer. With the power of science, they have singlehandedly answered the questions of the divine. With their new Miracle Detector Three Thousand, divinity has been solved. This invention has calculated that their god or gods are not only real, but are also the most divine, the most powerful, and the most moral to follow. They support these conclusions with objective facts, of course. the Miracle Maker has outputs for divinity, power, and morality, and all of these factors are the highest for their particular religion. The true faith is simply the one with the highest combined sum of GodPoints, and that religion is theirs. How could anyone disagree with their theologies, when artificery itself has confirmed that they are right? What do you mean, "how do their calculations work"? They just told you. the Miracle Maker Three Thousand calculates divinity, power, and morality. Don't ask about how they calculate those things; that's for scientists to know, not you. You would comprehend, maybe, if you followed the true faith. Maybe if you converted, you could also understand.
