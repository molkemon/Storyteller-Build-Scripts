With a single tug, Khagor Sapchopper freed his bloody ax from the crumpled elf before him. Another day, another raid. Today it was Durzk’ungul and her damned elves from the bonestrewn lands to the south. Tomorrow, he and his warriors would march north and flush out the elf witch’s troops prodding at his clan’s land once more. The ork paused, then gave a cold smile. His warriors. His clan. Not too long ago, he had simply been Khagor; a raider amongst the Greentide’s hordes, until he had broken an elven host by cutting down its leaders. His deathly smile widened. That had been a bloody and glorious day; the day Clan Sapchopper was declared with him as chieftain. However, since then, time had begun to blur. He often felt as though years had passed, even if he or his orks did not show it. No doubt something to do with this cursed forest and that mist that had taken him. He growled. Once he and his orks had hoped to escape these woods, to leave this forsaken land and never return. But that hope had long since soured. There was no escape. There was no going back. A weak groan cut through the ork’s musing. He looked down to find the source of the low sound. It was the elf he had cut down. The chieftain felt his smile return as he watched the elf try to crawl away, a trail of blood oozing behind them. After enjoying the sight a moment longer, he brought down his ax once more. If Clan Sapchopper could not leave this forest, then it would break it and its inhabitants.
