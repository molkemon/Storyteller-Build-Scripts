Simur had made every possible preparation for the hunt. He tracked the beastlord's cycles; when he was predator, and when prey. He knew the twists and turns of the elvish gladeways, as much as any mortal, any dwarf, can. Polishing the restored face of his father's broken compass for luck, he ordered his company depart from the citadel at Veigrthrun, to forge yet deeper into the Deepwoods. By the time they caught a sign of the archfey's cervine guise – a white birch scarred by intricate horns – they had travelled so far east into the woods as to almost return to the Serpentspine, though solid rock forbade consummation of the thought. A gleaming white stag, unmistakable even against the white trees populating this part of the forest, presenting its mystifying antlers, fixing its gaze on Simur, seducing the scion away from his companions to make the glory of the kill his own. When dwarf and deer finally had their privacy in a secluded grove, they did what comes naturally to hunters and beasts. Simur's spear sought the fey's flesh, and the lord, in turn, turned his antlers to his defense. The twisting antlers got the better of the exchange, and even as the other hunters heard the commotion and brought the beast down with a hail of gunfire, the commander's spirit was pulled down to the feyrealm to join the Wild Hunt forevermore, leaving the fey's blessings to be shared among the survivors.
