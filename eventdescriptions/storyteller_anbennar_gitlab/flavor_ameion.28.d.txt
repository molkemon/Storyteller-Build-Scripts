After two months of fraternizing, the representatives return to their homelands, laden with goods from their counterparts. The city's alcohol reserves may be empty, but groups that once considered each other alien and hostile will remember these days of diplomacy and nights of revelry. Some are even eager to learn the local language to foster communication between their newfound friends. The time now turns to work, however. Promises were made to the subjects, and fulfilling them will necessitate keeping the goblets empty.
