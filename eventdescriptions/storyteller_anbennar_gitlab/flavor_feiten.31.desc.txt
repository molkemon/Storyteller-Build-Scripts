Far above Feiten Bay circles a singular cormorant. Like clockwork, its mating call rings out across the bay, signifying the start of a new day. The call is first heard in a seaside building, a thrown quill thuds into the wall, driven by frustration and anger. Heedless of this, the call wends its way into a cliffside house, where the ruler opens his eyes in bleary acknowledgement before closing them again in resignation, another night wasted without progress. Both parties have run into a wall with their designs. How will we deal with this setback?
