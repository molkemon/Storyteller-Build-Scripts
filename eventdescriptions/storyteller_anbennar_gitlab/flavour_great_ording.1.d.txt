It is a nice day. There were few clouds, and though the sun shone brightly, in the shade of the forest it created a climate that was hard not to appreciate. Even so, however, the ruler was not here for pleasantries. After many months of searching, it was here that a unicorn had finally been tracked. The animals of the wood left the ruler, as if they recognized the righteousness of the deed ahead. There, grazing placidly on the grass before it, stood a unicorn. Its mane was pure white, with a brilliant horn atop its head. The ruler understood that there was to be only one chance at this, and so began a long series of carefully rehearsed steps. They knock on a nearby tree three times. The unicorn's head shoots up, gazing into the brush. With slow, measured steps, the ruler steps into view, raising their arms in an inviting fashion. The unicorn gazes closely at them, peering into their very soul. There, in response to what it sees, it reacts by.
