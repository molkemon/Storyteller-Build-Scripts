When the monarch drew Saerasyar, the greenflamed sword, the crowd before the ruler erupted. There were cheers, chants, calls of "Glory" and "For the title" and "Heir of Ultarlanbeg." There was truth to that last point – there had to be. The title of Zaernmaera stood on the steps of the Askalyn – that famed sanctuary where Munazhlan had hid from the SlaverNobles and rededicated the very sword the monarch held in the ruler's hands to freedom. That famed sanctuary where Ultarlanbeg had proclaimed the union of all Chendhyans. Where the ruler would do the same. "Chendhyans," the monarch called, as the crowd quietened for the ruler's speech. "From the edge of the bluesandsea to the savannas of Haebizyund, from the streets of Darkalyndar to the banks of the Agotham, from Assakadail to Haenbuddar – hear my voice." As heat and green light bathed the ruler's face, the title raised the flaming sword to the sky, slowly and deliberately. The ruler had to be careful, of course – the ruler held the sword, but the ruler would not use it. Even this act, the ruler feared, could set it off. Munazhlan would not approve of the ruler's reign – but that elf was long dead. The world had changed. The Chendhyans had changed. They would not sit idly anymore, waging petty raids out of "security" – soon, they would march to the ends of the world. And by the tip of the sword and the jaguar’s roar they would find it. "We ride to conquest, to victory – to those who follow me, I promise riches, slaves, lands… I promise glory." The crowd roared in approval, the cry of "Glory" ringing out once more. "And to those stand against us? I promise death. I stand before you the title of all Chendhyans. Ride with me or be destroyed."
