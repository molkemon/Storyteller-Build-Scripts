I lay in wait. Months passed, and I drank up the trickle of worry and fret from your quartermasters and logisticians. It was not nourishing fare; their worry was abstract and theoretical, and I was a mere integer in their calculations. Yet by the time your boats finally landed on the shores of Ardimya, I had taken much. Nutritious, perhaps, this logical thought, if bland to the taste. I watched as thirty or forty demons leaked from the rift. I felt your fear spike, the collective attention of an entire army. I realized, after a week, that you believed I had sent these evil things you fought. You thought me so big. It was flattering.
