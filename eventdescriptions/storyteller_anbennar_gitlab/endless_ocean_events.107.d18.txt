In the twisting forests of Taga’ala, mages have developed a cousin to the carving magic known as Stonescrawl. Barkscrawl. By chiseling Barkscrawl symbols into the roots of the great Hinuimangrove trees, the wood is reinforced against rot and impact alike. This process helps preserve quality wood for use when demand finally arises. no storm wind nor errant beast can easily break a barkscrawled bough. Over time, a well-carved piece of wood hardens to become as durable as metal; specialized counterspells must be carved in its harvest, since tools would blunt themselves in the collection process otherwise. When finally a root is deemed ready for use on the high seas, barkscrawlers have a choice to make. remove the ornate bas reliefs to reduce the drag of the hull in water, or sacrifice nautical speed in the name of building a vessel that can last hundreds of years. In the kraken-filled, mangrove-knee root ridden waters of Taga’ala, durability often proves to be the more compelling trait. While complete decomposition on Insyaa usually takes place in a fraction of the time it would elsewhere, a single barkscrawled Mangrove ship can see generations of sailors walk its length, so potent is their resistance to the elements. And although the naval combat applications are obvious, more often Mangrove ships have found themselves in service to the merchant marine; upkeep costs for these vessels prove insignificant, as they are protected from the wears of time.
