He calls again. The threads twist. The sigils open, and I feel him scrape against the edge. His voice is clear with want and will. His blood remembers me. "Hear me." I do. I always do. His shape is clearer, now; sharper, more jagged than when we first met. Hunger has ripened him. "Let my fire endure. Let my essence remain until the conquest is complete. Until the lands lie quiet, and fold beneath me as damp parchment. Let me stay until the feast is over, and I will serve in the after for as long as you wish." How very quaint. I drift a little closer. Blisters form. "Mine? You would kneel, after all? You would join me, even before the consumption you aim for?" He does not kneel. "When the end comes. Not before." He is lying. It is not clear from his tongue, but the curl of his claws is obvious; the stillness of his breath is telling. I like this about him. The pact carves itself anew. The fire dips low, and my mark takes root, sunk deep into soft places. He does not flinch, and I recede. He thinks his mind is hidden. Let him build his tower of teeth and flame. Let him think the tide can outdrink the sea. When the last bone is eaten. When the last star dies; not in stomach acid, but of age. He will still be mine then.
