They drag her from the silver shallows at dawn. Selussa Ghúl, the witch who once sang to shadows and whispered to roots beneath the canopy of her fallen Grove, stands now among us. She has returned to her maternal Grove, having heard of its liberation at our hands. In the depths of war, Selussa seized the refugees who fled from two domains and twisted their minds with dreams not their own. She claims she was trying to rebuild, and to do so, she was forced to coopt the wills of the broken. I step forward and her eyes meet mine. “Elaria,” she says softly, “Ah, your eyes are your mother’s.” I do not answer her flattery. “You have enslaved the wounded, Selussa,” I say. “You have made puppets of those who had nowhere to go.” “I gave them purpose,” she replies. “I gave them a Grove again. I gave them unity.” “At the cost of their minds?” “Better to serve in a dream than rot in despair.” I take a step closer and she does not flinch. “You were not the only one who lost a home.” “No,” she says. I look at the people she has enthralled. They stand like husks behind her, still breathing, still warm, but holloweyed. We stare at one another; two remnants of a lost world, standing in the ruins of their hopes.
