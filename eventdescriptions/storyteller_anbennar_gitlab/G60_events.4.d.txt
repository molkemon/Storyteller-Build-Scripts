As the monarch begins to set off on a joint holy pilgrimage with the Mteibhar leader to Rigoti, the ruler relays some common customs to the other monarch that must be followed as they traveled. No outsider is allowed to journey atop the holy mountain of Rigoti, but the king believes the Mteibhar to be distant brothers of the Fasíri rather than outsiders, for the ruler feels a close connection to their people. When both rulers reached the top, they kneeled and bathed in the goldenhued light the sky had shone as the sun began to set. "I now understand why you brought me here my friend," the other monarch calmly said. As the monarch heard this, the ruler began to carve a white stone with their name which the ruler had brought down to the King’s Pillar, a monument built by the Last True King himself. The ruler placed it upon the steps leading towards it and invited their Mteibhar kin to do the same. As they had both returned down the mountain, the other monarch exclaimed, "We are one and the same."
