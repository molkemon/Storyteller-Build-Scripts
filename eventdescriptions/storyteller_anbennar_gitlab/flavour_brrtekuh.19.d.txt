Thirty days later, the Khetarch appeared again. Not as flesh and blood, but as the Sorrow itself. A wailing, shrieking, yowling form of shifting water, drawn up from the river into the form of a devoured thing. It strode, clawed itself through the river, and caused mayhem wherever it went. Once, the Khet were decadent. Once, the Khet were lazy. Once, the displays of their power were frivolous, meaningless; an annihilating aside. The people of the Sorrow thought that they had grown accustomed to something different; a new sort of malevolence. A sign that the Khet cared about anything more than pleasure. As the ruler's maw clamped shut around the Khetarch's head, as blood squirted and bones snapped, as the body of a god fell limp and severed, the Sorrow crashed to the riverbed. It would have killed the ruler too, if the ruler was not now half what the ruler ate. Maybe the Khetarch had been killed while his spirit was entangled in the essence of the sorrow. Maybe this was an echo of the power he held in life. Maybe this was Elikhet himself, come down to punish those that slew his successor. Maybe the river just sort of did that. It didn't matter. When the Khetarch rose as the river, spectrelike, and he raised his arms, and red torrents washed away fleeing caravan and gnoll pack alike, and scarab swarm savaged the plains, and the ghosts of the devoured ran roughshod through the cities, all Kheterata knew again the power of their dead gods.
