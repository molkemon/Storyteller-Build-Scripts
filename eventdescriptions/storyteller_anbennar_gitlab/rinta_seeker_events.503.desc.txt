One schism has been resolved, yet the debates between Mwalim and Darwiiz rage ever on, now merely with a different issue of focus. The argument that has now engulfed our halls is in relation to the sourcing of materials from which the Godswatch will be built. The Darwiiz, as always, hold a very traditionalist viewpoint on this issue. They argue that the Godswatch is a Baashidi project, meant for the pursuit of Baashidi beliefs, and as such it must be built from Baashidi materials. The Mwalim, however, in their pursuit of knowledge, seek to use the methods of others that have already been proven to succeed. They point to the oracles of Tughayasa in Haless and the gnomes of Cannor as examples that should be studied, their materials outsourced to us and their methods coopted in order to increase our chance of success in this great undertaking. There is no doubt that we would have much to gain from them; on the other hand, using local materials would undeniably be much cheaper and more efficient. There is, of course, always the option to use a mix of both, though neither side would be fully satisfied with this.
