Even now, they will not fawn over you. It is their sacred duty to avert Tsishagya, the madness of the one. They will watch you for it, your monarch. You will never go unobserved again. A time will come soon when you must once more return to the heavy places and prove yourself, but for now the goodwill flows freely as the River. At each meeting you take, a naga courtier lingers at your shoulder, murmuring details and information by which you might better charm the petitioner. Recalcitrant nobles are visited politely, envoys briefed in your favour. Much of what they do, you know, lies outside of your direct view. Take heed; the next test the Naga set you will prove more rigorous.
