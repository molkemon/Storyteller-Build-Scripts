The noon sun shines strong in the sky, rays of light illuminating the palace and city below. By the same balcony where her life once changed forever, Ríva stands again. Wings tucked neatly behind her, she leans forward. Sharp eyes survey her domain as the winds whistle against the cliffside, carrying up the scent of the sea below. All below is Naléni, as it has been four centuries since, as it will be four centuries hence. She has seen to that. She taps her fingers gently on the edge of the balcony, staring down at the city. Ships stream in and out of the port, all now bearing Naléni’s flag; her flag. The sound of Siren song carries faintly on the wind even up to this distant height, and with an avian eye she can see even the humans crowding down outside the songhall way below. But it is not enough. Ríva’s gaze rises, up across the crystal blue waters of the Naléni pass, out and over the Gulf itself, towards far Rahen. The treasure and splendours that lie there, silks and porcelain and gold, all the wealth of Haless itself. It all calls to her. A wry smile graces her lips. This must be how the Raheni felt as they first looked south. But Naléni’s conquest, her conquest, need not end with Rahen. Already some in her realm call to expand inland. Sky Domain converts, Sirens preach that Madriamilak must be unified, and there are many harpies in the Telekenid not yet united with their kin. Naléni is a nation of the sea, but its heritage is one of empire. Madriamilak and Rahen both shall fall beneath the shadow of her wing. So decides Ríva, Empress of the Gulf.
