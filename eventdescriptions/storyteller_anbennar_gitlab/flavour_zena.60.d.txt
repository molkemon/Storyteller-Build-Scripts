Turns out, you have to wait weeks to make these landfish edible. Something in those floatsacs is poisonous, but they can get rid of it. Now, they call it a delicacy, but I will be honest, when they handed me a cube of the stuff, it smelled like the backend of a corpse. And I have smelled plenty. But, when you eat it, it is sweet, nutty, and only faintly fishy. After a couple of them, they were not too bad. Kiusz chowed down with gusto, signing an apology to me for underestimating the fish. The process to prepare them is a damn nightmare, though. After catching them with nets attached to long poles, an easy task considering the things are docile as cows, the Gelcoller Ruinborn would place it in a hole filled with sand and brine, then cover it in more sand and gravel to press the poison out for a couple weeks. After that, you pull it out, cut it in pieces, and hang it to dry for several months. I do not want to think about how many people died figuring out exactly how long they needed to wait for each step. Anyway, we figured floating fish would be a hit at the Menagerie, and we could always have adventurous visitors try the fermented meat at concession stands, as well. Catching the landfish was easy. Keeping them on the boat without floating away on the way back to Bivuzena was the hard part.
