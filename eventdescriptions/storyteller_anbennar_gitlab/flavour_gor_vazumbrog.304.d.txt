The gates of Gor Vazumbrog creaked open to reveal a battered procession. The monarch rode at its head, once pristine armour tarnished and dented, their face a mask of exhaustion and grief. Behind them, no cheering banners waved, no triumphant songs rang out; only silence, broken by the scuff of weary boots. Of the thousands who had marched with them into the Serpentspine, barely a dozen returned. The ruler had been dragged from the depths by their aides, a ruler protected while their warriors perished. The weight of their sacrifice crushed them. As they dismounted, the gazes of their people seared into them. grief, anger, and disappointment etched on every face. The shame was unbearable. The monarch lowered their head and entered the hold without a word.
