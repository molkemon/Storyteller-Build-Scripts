The monarch looks over the rickety fishing boat, despair writ plain across his face. From the ruler's vantage point atop the pier, the boat appears entirely too in danger of capsizing as it rolled with the minor tides close to shore to handle a few tuna, much less the ship sized whales that were to be their quarry. Noticing the monarch's trepidation, the captain of the ship limps over, a slight grin on his face. "She may not look like much, but she's served me well in over a dozen hunts. And I wager she'll serve for a dozen more." The monarch is in the process of turning to give a grateful nod as the ocean finally wins the tug of war, a rogue wave washing over the boat's deck to a chorus of loud curses from those on deck. The nod curdles to a shudder and the captain's grin, of course, only grows wider as the monarch's face turns pale.
