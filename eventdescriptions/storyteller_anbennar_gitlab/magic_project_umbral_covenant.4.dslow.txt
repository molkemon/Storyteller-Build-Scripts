"It is magnificent," Cheni says. Before him is an arcane projection of the entire domain, a three-dimensional map of its layout. It does not bring clarity on the pillar’s purpose, precisely, but it points to traps and maps overall energy flow. "It is late and incomplete," Ikomeye says, but she cannot hide a small smile at the compliment. "'Reservoir' is seeming less and less absurd a suggestion for its purpose." Cheni is tracing one river of energy with a finger, following it towards a great central column of rock, perhaps a lodestone? The projection marks it as powerfully magical, so much so that even those without the gift feel their teeth ache when they approach. "At least for this part of the structure." "It seems a little extreme, even for the ancient lizardfolk empire, to devote so enormous a structure merely to fill a reservoir." The Vile One’s voice betrays a rare emotion for her. frustration. "Perhaps the rest of the structure siphons magic from the air?" Cheni is as implacably mild as ever; it is admirable and very irritating. "Perhaps it feeds the reservoir?" "Best way to test a reservoir is to empty it," Ikomeye says, picking at a tooth with her nail. "Unlife, maybe, antimagic. Spring a leak in it." Cheni shakes his head. "I don’t favour the risk. Better to take a breath, consult the shadow, and look again with more wisdom."
