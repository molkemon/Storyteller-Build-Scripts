The Dakinshi had almost defeated us. We had fought so long and hard for our independence, and we were poised to lose it once again. Disunited as we were, we fell, one by one, tribe by tribe. Some, believing they had seen the writing on the wall, submitted willingly. Bah—we spit on their name. Then, when all hope seemed lost, she appeared, with courage and a cunning plan. A masterful illusion, the greatest deception in history—by the time the Dakinshi realized what had happened, they had already lost. The river ran red with blood, and very little of it was ours. Her song cannot finish yet, though. From all sides, we are beset by vultures. In this moment of disunity, our siblings have been snatched up by the lizards, the Dakinshi, the Baashidi—scavengers, one and all. With her at our head, however, there will be no choice but for us to succeed. For she is Uwimbabazi, the Lady of Drums, and by the time her work is finished, hers will be the greatest song of any age.
