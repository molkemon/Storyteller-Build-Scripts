The crack in the aquifer was Kûrgûm's fault. He had swung too hard, eager to finish his shift early, ignoring Brenlel's shouted warnings. The stone split, and something spilled out. Not water, but something thicker, darker, gleaming faintly in the lantern light. It spread fast, too fast, rushing down the carved channels meant to carry clean water to the domain. "Block it," Brenlel yelled, shoving stones into the breach with her bare hands. The others joined, but the miasma flowed like it had a mind of its own, curling around their boots and burning their skin. Kûrgûm froze, his hammer clattering to the ground. The stink of rotten eggs and copper filled the air, and whispers, low and accusing, filled his ears. "What have you done?" Brenlel snapped, her voice shaking. Kûrgûm couldn't answer. His hands trembled as the voices grew louder, echoing every mistake he had made, every failure. The faces of those who trusted him flickered in the blackened flow, their mouths twisted in silent condemnation. The miners retreated, coughing and retching, but Kûrgûm didn't move. The taint surged past them, racing toward the lower cisterns of the domain. "It's in the water," Brenlel whispered, staring down the tunnel. Kûrgûm sank to his knees, his voice breaking. "I didn't mean to—" Brenlel grabbed his collar, dragging him toward the lift. "You'll answer to the Hold," she spat. Above, the bells of the domain tolled, signaling an emergency. Kûrgûm hung his head, shame heavier than the rock he had mined all his life.
