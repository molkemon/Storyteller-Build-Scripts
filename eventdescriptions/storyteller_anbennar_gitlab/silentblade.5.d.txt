"Can it still be done?", the Spriggan's creator questioned. "Yes, my Prince. The ritual remains intact... for those that remain...", the Spriggan trailed off. No words followed its comment, instead there was only a dreadful quiet. The Spriggan could not help but tremble, for though it kept its head down, the Prince's cold fury could be felt if not seen. "We shall gather that which has been lost after," the Prince finally spoke, their words cool as frost. "Begin." Without daring a look back, the Spriggan floated forward toward the center of the ritual circle. High above the unsuspecting victorious survivors, it chanted the words that had taken a thousand lives to learn. Soon a thousand more voices cried in agony, joining the Spriggan's chant. Yes, pain was how it would begin. The heat of flame would lick the orks' very souls. Then would come sorrow, a suffocating ash that threatened to swallow them whole. But then, there would be anger, righteous indignation toward the fate that has been cast upon them. Finally, vengeance. For every wrong must be met with wrong. Yet, for now, there was quiet. The Spriggan looked down and saw a teeming sea of emerald. Were not so dreadful, perhaps, it would have felt pride. But what pride could there be in the silence? "You've served me well, Grey Weaver." In that moment, the Grey Weaver felt a stirring, a completeness that had escaped it for all its short life till now. The Weaver looked up toward its creator. Yet the Prince did not meet its gaze, instead simply staring out into the shimmering neon horizon of the Feyrealm.
