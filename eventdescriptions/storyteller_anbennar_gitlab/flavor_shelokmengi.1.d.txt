"Mother." The boy yelped, as the Queen Mother dragged him to his room by his ear. "You cannot spend your days out on the fields; you have to study, to hear the pleas of your subjects." "But mother." The boy said, his voice soft and kind, "My regents handle that already; why must I study?" She sighed, but forced back tears. "Oh, Ayu, that is the problem." She leaned in close, whispering in his ear, "Your advisors seek to isolate you; they hope that, once you come of age, you continue to play in the fields instead of doing your duty, so that they can play as kings." Upon hearing this, the boy contemplated, before standing just a little straighter. And though she would never know it, the boy saw how out of breath she was, how deep the lines carved by worry were in her face, and how dark her eyes were. And he would never forget the sight for all the years he lived.
