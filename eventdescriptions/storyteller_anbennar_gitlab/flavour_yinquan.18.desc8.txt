While preparing a base to manage the expedition from, we received generous help from the local warriormonks. Too generous, in fact; their efforts to provide us hospitality and assistance ended up being excessive. We spent more time preparing the monks to accompany us, given their fighting skill, than we did exploring the high temple. As expected of the inside of the temple, a symbol resembling two straight lines crossed in the middle featured prominently on many objects within the temple. There were many traps, which was also expected, but it appears that the specialty of the warriormonks accompanying us made them well-suited for identifying traps when they knew what to look for. The expertise of these monks did offer new perspectives, however; we discovered a relief of an incredibly intricately painted black sword, remarkable for its resemblance to Kongjian. One of the monks found a gem marking the descriptor in a secret room uncovered by a poorly concealed secret entrance. Due to improper handling, the gem was shattered by the time it reached our hands. Nonetheless, with this gem recovered, we have no other reason to be inside this temple.
