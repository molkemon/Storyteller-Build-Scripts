Davharral was once irrigated by a great aqueduct that brought water down from the Paravimvata mountains. The aqueduct was destroyed many decades ago, and the people have struggled ever since. The arid climate has worn down the water stores, and they now rely heavily upon traders bringing water into the city to survive, a dangerous precedent. Recently, a study found that there is an underground aquifer beneath one of the city suburbs. They must build wells as a matter of urgency to tap this water source and alleviate their reliance on the traders. It has been pointed out by several prospectors that the aquifer will only barely meet the city’s current needs, and a more permanent solution will be needed in the future.
