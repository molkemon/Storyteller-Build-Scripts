Gegaya Boarborn was sixteen years of age when he first picked up a spear and stabbed a man through the chest. He did it because he loved Moguwon Wolfborn, the man that had led the Command to greatness, the man whose stories he had heard at dinner since he was three, the man he revered as his own father, the father of the Great Command. He did it because he loved the Great Command, the alliance that now ruled over his life, that demanded his blood, his service, his innocence. He did it because the other man, the man whose eyes were hollow, was a conquered person. Conquered people were not to have weapons, and a paring knife, however small, was a weapon. When Gegaya returned to the warcamp that night and laid down inside his clan housing, his mother Kitomi attended to his bruises. "Do you know why I named you Gegaya?" Kitomi asked, while washing the blood off into a basin. "I do not," said Gegaya. "It is a word in the hobgoblin language; it means the opposite of war. It is the absence of war, when both sides have laid down arms. It is like quiescence or ceasefire or truce, but without possibility of reemergent violence." Gegaya frowned. "I do not know this word. Is it like tonight, after the fighting ended?" Kitomi stole a brief glance outside, where the fires of the warcamp lit up against the night. Tomorrow, there would again be the beating of drums, the howling of horns, the marching of boots. Tonight was an intermission between two acts of death. "It is not like tonight, no."
