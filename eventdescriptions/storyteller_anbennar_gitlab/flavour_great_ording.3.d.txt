It is afternoon. The sun hangs high in a blue sky, though on its way down. It is into this sky that the monarch stares, gazing upon the countryside of Lencenor from atop a particularly large hill. There, with their back against an oak tree, it sinks in that the land below is not just countryside; it is their countryside. The individuals below must be permitted to go about their daily lives, the monarch observes, for is that not as important as the need for noblemen like themselves to hunt? No matter how vast, or how populated, these individuals needed to be allowed the ability to live their lives free from war, or suffering, or evil. The land itself needed protection, for that was only just.
