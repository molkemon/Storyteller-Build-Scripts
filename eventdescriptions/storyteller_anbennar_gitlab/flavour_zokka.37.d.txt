I do not remember how long I have lived. That is my nature. I spent the years whispering to the mad, and bargaining with the wise. My truth was painted across the walls of the waking world. My falsehoods were etched into the dreams of kings and queens. Cities broke under my tutelage. I fed on those who sought me, and fed on the havoc they wreaked. My lesson was hunger. He is not clever. He does not understand the logic of my kind. to grant boons in exchange for power, to exchange that power for stronger boons. But I underestimated him, all the same. He does not care about any of that. He bided his time. He siphoned that power, little by little. I noticed, of course I did, but I never thought he would grow this strong. I see now that that was his own logic. In letting him have his fun, I spelled my own doom. There was no warning. He did not even look me in the eye. He ate me, without ceremony, without hesitation. I like this about him. He is simple in the way gravity is. a singleminded purpose, expressed without compromise. A wolf, a hyena, with godhood in its jaws. I twist now in the hollows of his form. He grows less real, less permanent, by the hour, but remains just as present. I can feel him stretch between planes, legions dragged behind him like his smoke. He is coming. And I am just a part of him, remembering what it meant to be worshipped. My cult is his now. I will scream in the voice of dying suns as he crosses to his own world. I will howl his name as he lays waste to that realm. This will be my final sermon, written by my final pupil.
