The myriad alien wildlife in Nomsyulhan is infamously impossible to tame, which is why it was so impressive when several centuries after the Day of Ashen Skies, the hobgoblins of the Tearing Talon clan managed to secure a batch of Dragonnel eggs, raise them by hand, and domesticate them over several generations. While many Eagle Hobgoblin clans use them for the sake of warfare, with mages and warlords breeding ever more ferocious varieties, certain tribes have turned their noble beasts to a more domestic use. Azaly travelled through the ancient forests of the Green Vale with members of the Nunsan Banbe, who had revolted against Nutean Kezen, a brutal and despicable clan, in fourteen thirty-two AA. With the massive precursor structure of the Garden Spire looming in the distance, the hobgoblins and Nuzenapu harpies that were his guides showed him how the forest's trees were infused with magic, regenerating lost bark and limbs at unnatural speeds. The comparisons to the Effelai that Azaly knew well were met with uncomprehending shrugs. Azaly was bid to watch as a hobgoblin carved a pattern into the wood, then had his dragonnel mount lightly scorch the surface with its breath, cauterizing the design and thus preventing the tree's regeneration, leaving a purple-blue glowing artwork. After bringing back several of these wood carvings to the Medaka’s Exceptional Initiative, the ruler has invited members of the Nunsan Banbe to Bivuzena to source more of this Dragonnelfired wood, as well as to see if they can replicate this art within the jungles of the Effelai. However, our request for dragonnel eggs to raise within the Menagerie was politely, but firmly, denied.
