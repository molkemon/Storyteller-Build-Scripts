Here lies Gradolf, found a box, wasn't looking, got lich pox. The epitaph of deceased adventurer Gradolf Flixis. It isn't that guides on achieving lichdom are rare, exactly, though that isn't without trying. Yet every time another noble empire seeks to burn out the dark work, the knowledge finds a way to survive. scrawled in the margins of otherwise innocent tomes, contained in cipher and rhyme, and written in archaic languages unknown to censors. Become a philologist, a cryptographer, and a librarian, and you will discover that guides to lichdom are in great circulation, reproduced thoughtlessly by the careless scribe. The trouble then, is translation. Our studies have revealed that the very foundation of the dark gift lies within an item called the phylactery. A phylactery is some form of container which stores a lich's soul, typically something small and easy to transport or hide, for if the phylactery is destroyed then the lich will perish with it. The first step for any would-be lich is to craft such a wondrous item.
