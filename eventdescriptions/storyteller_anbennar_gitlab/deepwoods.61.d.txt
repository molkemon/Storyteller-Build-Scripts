You stand at the edge of the exceedingly deep wilderness of an already wild land, peering into the impenetrable woods that mark the end of civilization and the beginning of nature's dominion. Courtiers and advisors huddle together a stone's throw away, skeptical and yet terrified. A towering oak bears the mark, matching the reports from the garrison. four cuts, deep and wide, cleave down the face of the tree, each equally terrible, but clearly made in one stroke. The wet air seems to wrap itself around you, covering your skin in a beaded blanket of moisture, or perhaps sweat. He is waiting.
