The eastern edge of the Effelai is as foreign as it is familiar to us. More than one scout reports back to us with tales of great horrors stalking the undergrowth. At night, the endless gleam of eyes in the dark reflect back at us. A watch is posted throughout all hours of the day and our children are kept close. We have no choice but to brave whatever is lurking in the darkness if we want to seize glory of our own. And then we find it, at the edge of the great water where the songs of the eastern Effelai harmonize best. Ages ago, the great hero Aíthzi took an ax and knife and began to carve the petrified wood. The trees here are abnormally high quality, and their deep roots unearth large amounts of iron ore. Here, Aíthzi made her legendary weapons. It was only fitting that the key to hunting the monstrosities of this region lies right at the center of it. The bows, spears, and javelins that Aíthzi made have long been lost to time, but we have the same tools that she did, and there is still plenty of wood and iron to go around. As we craft our weapons, knowing the beasts of the jungle are merely a few bushes away, our songs are quiet but joyful. We wonder if this great pilgrimage of ours may not be about the monsters we face, but the things we learn along the way. For there is so much to learn from Aíthzi's great works, that we shall take it with us on this great adventure, for future generations to admire and learn from.
