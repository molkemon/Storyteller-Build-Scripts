Capturing the island of Ginjyu was very difficult. This wasn’t because it was well guarded; under normal circumstances, the garrison would be paltry; but much rather that careful mind had to be paid to collateral damage. As many in Yanshen are well aware, the island is the home of the smiths of the moshugong swords, well known for their power and their scarcity. The magic and techniques required to forge one are closely guarded secrets. If any of the smiths were to die, then it would set their technique back centuries. This is, strangely enough, a significant obstacle. The average soldier is inclined to loot; after all, it is known as their second pay. In the process, things are destroyed and people die. When it concerns something with as much potential as the moshugong swords, this isn't something that can be allowed to come to pass. Soldiers had to be carefully selected, and a crack team formed in order to safely take Ginjyu. Even though most of the garrison had since been recalled in order to do battle on the field, there was enough resistance to make it a close run affair. With the island secured, and casualties low, its potential is immense. Imagine how powerful a soldier will be when his sword can cleave through anything. What couldn't he defeat? All that remains to be done is to convince the swordsmiths to help us, and to figure out how to make enough swords to stock an entire army.
