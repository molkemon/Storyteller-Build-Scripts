Since ancient times, Jiangyang has stood as Yanshen's northeastern bulwark, a land of mountain passes and ocean gates, rich in terrain and spirit. Here, people live armed, their lives bound with defense and culture. Between fortresses and harbors, the region breeds loyal warriors and heartfelt poetry. The people are earnest; their customs embrace song and verse, often expressing joy and sorrow. The Yuefu form prevails, with fivesyllable lines shaping deep reflection. Each year, wind sweeps from the north, the rustling grass like clashing armor. Soldiers hear the braying of horses in the night, echoes of battles past. As before, officials collect songs to preserve the people's voice. This year's melodies from military villages and mountain hamlets are stirring, old words sung anew, filled with sorrow and pride. The ballads speak of distant memories or present passions. What is the anthology of songs recorded this year? In each word, the spirit of Jiangyang resounds, echoing between mountains and seas.
