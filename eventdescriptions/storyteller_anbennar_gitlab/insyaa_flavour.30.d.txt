Word travels fast in scholarly circles, and these days the word is all about a strange kobold with interesting ideas regarding divinity, metaphysics, and the relationship between all things. The life of a scholar is rarely a luxurious one, but this kobold appears to live as some kind of ascetic itinerant; he will take up residence in a library and ask for only enough food to sustain him as he lives among the texts, then depart for the next library once he has found whatever answer he was looking for. He appears to be a talented debater, and is already making quite an impression among the intellectuals of the capital with his jovial manner, even when discussing the most serious of ideas. On each scale of his body, a different glyph has been carved in a language we have never seen before. He will tell anyone who asks that when read together these glyphs explain the fundamental truth of the universe, although he will not say in which order they should be read. If we provide his next stipend, we might be able to convince him to stay for a time.
