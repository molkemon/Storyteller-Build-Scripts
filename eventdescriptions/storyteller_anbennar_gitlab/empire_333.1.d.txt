How do we know there have been three hundred thirty-three empires? Ever since the pronouncement of Khatalashya, this question has been raised, and the answers given by those in charge and the naga have not given a satisfactory answer. For starters, these empires supposedly stretch back one hundred thousand years. That is a truly mind-bogglingly large amount of time. The amount of records we have from even the three hundred thirtieth Arskitse Empire are slim, most of it having been lost in the wake of the Onslaught, when many of their great cities were sunk beneath the ocean. That was a little over two thousand five hundred years ago. How could we possibly know about empires twenty-five thousand years ago? Even what verifiable records we do have are written by a lizard who was copying a text that the interpretation of a painting done by an artist who heard from a hermit who spoke to a spirit that heard it from the gods themselves. It is a historical game of Yanshen Whispers where the message has surely been lost over time. These issues could be alleviated if the stance was that the stories of the three hundred thirty-three empires are merely metaphorical. If they were fables, lessons one could take in how to properly govern a country. But if we were to accept that stance, then there is no reason why the three hundred thirty-third Empire should be special. The entire prophecy is founded upon the central conceit that Khatalashya is definitely the three hundred thirty-third Empire, and therefore destined to last forever. Should even one small piece of doubt enter the minds of its citizens, faith in the prophecy will collapse and the absolute right to rule will be challenged. The naga will not allow that to happen.
