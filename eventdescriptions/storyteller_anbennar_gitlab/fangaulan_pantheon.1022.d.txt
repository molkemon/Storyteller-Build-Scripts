Sit, weary travellers, sit. I will sing you a song as you eat the fruits and sip the drinks of the market, and because this is a sacred place I will charge you nothing to listen. For even among all the buying and selling of the market, even though I need food as well, some tales should be told freely, as a gift from one heart to another. This is the city that Kunolo, the chosen of Mother Pelican, built in their cleverness, and it recognizes that everyone needs a place to call their home, even the nomads and the wanderers of the world. I am such a wanderer, and yet I have come here, and I can only give thanks to Kunolo for creating this place to rest my weary bones. It is good to be a jelli, in the city of Kunolo. Yet everywhere they go jelli have songs to sing, and even with my feet propped up I cannot escape this sacred duty. Today, I will sing to you all a song of Kunolo, and you may choose freely which one it will be.
