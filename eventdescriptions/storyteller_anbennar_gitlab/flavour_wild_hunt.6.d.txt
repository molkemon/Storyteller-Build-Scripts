The victorious general set down his sword and collapsed against the wall of his tent. The outcome was ensured by Irdaeos, yet there was something strange about this battle. At one point, the Triunics had split their forces to attempt a flank, but he knew with a confidence he did not usually have that the correct maneuver was a withdrawal from the left so that the shock troops in the trees could perform an envelopment. This was not strange itself, but someone else seemed to give the order, and the shock units that intercepted the enemy seemed somewhat larger than he remembered at the time. The general gave his canteen one last gulp and emerged into the bustle of the warcamp on his way to debrief with his council. He was not trying to eavesdrop, but the conversation among every group of warriors was the same. there were others in that battle. There were stories of relief from nowhere and mysterious routs of the Triunics to units that did not exist. One centaur swore up and down that he saw his father in his warband, who died years ago during the First Incursion. The general’s staff all had similar stories. Communications along the front were too efficient for the number of runners they had, and scouting reports were too detailed. Something was happening. Muster for march the next day did not reveal any more troops than the general had, not that he expected any different. But as the army set out to its next destination, the general saw the outline of a glimmer of a centaur, who looked at him and nodded.
