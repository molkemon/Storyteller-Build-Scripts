A child of two worlds, belonging to neither. That is a phrase often given to halforks. All across Escann, halforks are seen as outsiders. They are not welcome anywhere they go. Among human realms, they are seen as little better than orks and as mistakes by most. Among ork realms, they are seen as useful but untrustworthy. The life of a halfork is a harsh one, with the only decent realms for them still standing being Rogieria, Corintar, and Stalbor. However, two realms have a different relation with halforks. While Grombar became halfork in the majority of its population on purpose, Unguldavor instead has a large halfork minority rivaling the human minority in size. In many ways, the halforks have become their own people as they often marry other halforks of their own volition. In cities such as the capital, halforks make up a good portion of the merchants and bureaucrats. As they are at least tolerated in enough realms both human and orkish, they are able to travel and sell goods between them. As the orks in Unguldavor have finally gathered a national identity as Ungulan orks, so too have the halforks. For many halforks, Unguldavor is their home and they love it. As the shamans teach children in various villages, they talk about how the halforks are children of two legacies. Castanor and the Arosha Oakbreaker. They are taught to take pride in both as Castanor was the pinnacle of civilization while Arosha Oakbreaker showed the orks how to develop their own identity without falling into softness or savagery. As such, a new Ungulan halfork identity has emerged. These halforks see themselves as citizens of Unguldavor and as much a part of it as Ungulan orks. As time goes on, perhaps the Unguldavori nation will never perish from Halann.
