The sun has already sunk beneath the horizon when the monarch finishes reading the book on divination magic. The monarch lifts their eyes to see the library completely empty, except for a single mechanism sitting a few tables away. “This is an interesting tome you’ve been reading. I never quite understood the subject myself.” The mechanism leaves its seat and approaches the monarch. The monarch studies the mechanism; their clothes are simple, but there are golden stars embroidered at the edges. They do not seem taller than an average human, but the monarch still feels a commanding presence. “HeartSeldomSeesTheFuture,” they reach out their hand. “Professor of Mathematics here at Anbenncóst.” The mechanism sits in front of the monarch and picks up the book, rotating it in its metallic hands. “I’m sure you’ve heard of my people in Insyaa. Many of them are lost, aimless, especially after the demise of Golodh and the fall of their Foundation. They lack purpose or a ‘Grand Plan’, as I like to call it. Some may have found solace in the Poet and some… some are like me.” There is a tiny spark in their eyes, as they calculate their next words. “I may have a proposition for you, monarch. You see, me and my colleagues have been working on a separate field of studies that we call ‘probability theory’. It assumes that everything can be predicted through numbers, without the use of magic. Our research is still in its early stages, but the results are promising and we’d like to establish a new Foundation here. Of course, we’d need some funds and privileges, but I’m sure with your support we can figure something out.”
