The stories of the empires of old can stretch the suspension of disbelief. Generally though, the naga and scholars of the three hundred thirty-three empires can brush these off. You don’t know what kind of fantastical beings existed fifty thousand years ago. Could those empires of the past have believed in what the Precursor Elves accomplished, for instance? Their reign wasn’t that long ago in the grand timeline of lizardfolk history, after all. Yet we have ample historical evidence of their existence. Some stories, though, clearly go too far for anyone to believe. Like that of the twenty-ninth Empire, whose emperor grew too powerful and too greedy after striking a deal with a demon. He devoured his foes, devoured the gods, devoured even the demon he made a pact with, before finally devouring the sun itself. This is obviously nonsensical. The sun is still there after all, and the idea someone on Halann could be capable of eating the sun is absurd on its very face.
