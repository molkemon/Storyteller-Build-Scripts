Never having enjoyed the luxury of purity, we mongrel dogs of the Luna look to a hundred places for our principles. To Verne, where boldness spurs the one against the many. To Dameria, where words justly chosen are ever measured against a thousand years of oratory. And to Pashainé itself, where the guild unites the dissonant voices of a hundred nations. Ask two Pashainéy to which of these we owe the most, and hear at least three answers.
