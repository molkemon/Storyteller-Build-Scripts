The Black Jade Citadel looms over the monarch's war tent as the three undead emissaries kowtow before the ruler, representing each of the three Jiangshi Courts. The emissary from the Zhuying pushes forward a chest full of books and scrolls, no doubt containing writings no mortal ought to read. From the Wandaoji, their emissary bears an ornate porcelain and gold mask, fit for a ruler of the nation's stature, beautiful but disturbingly skull-like. The Shudiyi have sent their emissary with a sublimely sharp jian, almost crackling with unseen dark energies. The three Rulers of the Night smile as the nation's ruler reaches out to take the sword, when his chief Marshal intercedes, urging the monarch not to touch the weapon lest he be cursed and enthralled to the wicked Jiangshi. Knowing the man to be a zealous practitioner of the Accretive Path, the ruler stays his hand, and turns to look at his Chancellor, who is eyeing the crate of books with envy. A student of High Philosophy, disinterested in the squabbles between the two different Paths, he advocated for the monarch to extend the same protection and demand the same service of the Devouring Path as their Accretive cousins. The Marshal, on the other hand, had all but demanded that the nation's ruler put them to the sword, and destroy the unholy abominations whose emissaries lay prostrate before them.
