The metal cage fit snugly around his head. From the other end of it, he heard the chirps and snarls of feral beasts; they were only kept at bay by three metal sheets, and a glass one right before his face. "Goodman FearNot. I strongly suggest you revise your statements," said a gaunt man with a thin smile and a buckled hat. He placed a hand on the farthest metal sheet. "I will not," he said, feigning defiance. "My wife is not a witch. Just because" he was cut off by the sound of metal scraping against metal. The hissing and screaming got closer. He yelped, then closed his eyes and caught his breath. "My wife is not a witch. Just because her father was a rune savage, doesn't mean that..." he gulped as another door slid up. "It doesn't mean that she inherited their foul ways. She never even met her father." "Are you saying the inquisitors are lying, then?" "No. Of course not. But, she..." The third metal door raised. The beasts were unveiled. Hissing, scrabbling, foaming at the mouth and clawing each other asunder; a swarm of rabid swine. "Last chance, goodman." The glass started to rise. He saw the creatures’ foul claws, razor sharp, swatting beneath it. Finally, something snapped. "SHE'S A WITCH. A WITCH." he cried.
