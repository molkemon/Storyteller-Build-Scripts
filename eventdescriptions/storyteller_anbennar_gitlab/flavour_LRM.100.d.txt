The wise Mayor is total in her actions; excepting nothing from the practice of her rule, she lives in every moment as a Mayor. Even her time spent in privacy is carefully calibrated. how much, with whom, and at what moment. From the moment of her inauguration, what is political follows her into the drawing room and the garden forevermore. No life of peace can thereupon be enjoyed but for the release of her power altogether, and sometimes even then. Yet every moment burdened by power is also with power infused, and by each gesture the Mayor makes her will known. the letter sent, the brush of an arm, the favour for a cousin. Thus the wise Mayor's rule is a thousand inconsequences, assembled with ceaseless patience into a slender blade.
