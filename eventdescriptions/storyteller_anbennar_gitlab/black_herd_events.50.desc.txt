The tunnel stretches into the darkness below; lined with cracked columns and built to a height far greater than even the largest ogre would require, it reminds me more of the caverns of Danazgubli than anything built by mortal hands. After a brisk interrogation, several of the local ogres told me of this place, a part of the ruins where the walking dead may be found. It is as good a lead as any; spontaneous undead are a strong signal of residual necromantic energy. Crossreferencing the ancient histories with the notes of the Glutton King has also given me a better idea of what exactly might be hidden in these ruins. Almokh’s writings spoke of his desire to cleave the hunger from his body. A simplistic view clearly warped by the backwards religion of the ogres, no doubt, but there is a piece of truth within it. My own research has led me to writings regarding a potion of soul separation that when imbibed would kill the drinker instantly, cleanly separating body from soul and allowing my ritual to complete successfully, thus securing my immortality. What lies beneath Maghargma might be the Glutton King’s own attempt at soul separation, and any insights I might glean from his attempts will be invaluable. Working my way through the cavernous ruins I can feel something in the depths below, a faint pulse of magical power telling me that this is the correct place. I am where I am meant to be.
