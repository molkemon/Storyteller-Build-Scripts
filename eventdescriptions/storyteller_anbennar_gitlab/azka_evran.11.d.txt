Elves can survive for centuries. Not all that survival is life, however; the worldweary existence of those in the Silent Repose or Zanbari monasteries for those who have endured too much and seen too many friends die merely a pale imitation. The difference is will. The ability to wake up to Surael's golden rays and still see work to do, still see goals to achieve, still see a task at hand, no matter how many you have already accomplished. And though Deggarion the First Evranzuir was born with little but bastardom, he was born with a will as deep as the sun is bright. For few expected anything of a bastard, after all. His fellow guardsmen saw him as a patronage appointment; before he practiced day and night to stun them with a sniper's aim. The goblins saw him as another elven warrior to roll over like an avalanche; before he stood tall, and stopped their advance, and turned the tide. The nobility saw him as a lesserborn stain; before he reforged Jaher's spear and showed them all what it truly meant to be noble. Will. He still has it. Rising like the sun, leading their country in Surael's honour, wielding Dinaltoldir against its foes. Until, of course.
