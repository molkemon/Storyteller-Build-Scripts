Rushing under the gentle rainfall, Arturian makes his way towards the bank of Silverlar. Ever the punctual one, he had prepared his documents and coins by seven, and headed to the city at eight. Sure enough, by nine he stood in front of the building. But curiously, a long queue snaked out the entrance. "Ah. Look who it is, our old chum Arturian," quips an elderly man already in line. "Good morning, Bren," Arturian replies, joining the queue behind his acquaintance. Catching his breath, he asks, "What is all this then?" "Hm? The line?" Bren echoes, surprised. Puzzled, Arturian inquires further, "Well, yes. Why the long face?" "It seems like you missed something, my brother," Bren points to the bank building. "Ever since Silverlar got taken over, most of our old officials were ousted. The bank president and most of the clerks included. Now we have to deal with the Esmari." "I see." Processing this, Arturian continues, "But that does not answer my question." "Huh? Are you dense? It is the Esmari. They do not know the meaning of orderly, on time, or even doing their job. Hence the line. People came, the bank was closed—no clerks, no president, not even guards awake." Almost catching on, Arturian's interest piques. "They appointed Esmari officials, and now everything is in chaos?" "Yeah, the old bastards are probably laying near the riverbank, drunk out of their minds. I would not be surprised if they forgot they had a job." Feeling duped by city life once again, the poor countryside man Arturian vows to avoid the city for the rest of his life.
