The roost has changed. The winding road to the palace is familiar, killing my legs once more, and the food stalls along it will soon make me fat again. I see scars from a siege around, and yet the people are not scarred. If anything, many seem to have a new twinkle in their eyes, walking around with a newfound pride. And the sheer number of harpies. Hytiranyalen wasn't so bustling when I left. The market plaza is full of hagglers begging the vendors for discounts; harpies not of our flock, the lot of them. And many merchants peddling luxury wares, a rare sight in my memories. They all have plenty of clients too. And yet the residence of the Vetaunyar next to the palace is unchanged. The queue of hopefuls outside of it is as long as it always was, all praying to find a mage willing to listen to their requests, hopefully without having to sell all their jewelry to pay for it. Well, now that I'm back this won't continue for long. Everybody should be able to experience the joy and the wonder of using magic; and I know there must be a way of doing that. Look at me Bulwar, for even the Kurešim of old will pale in comparison to what the future is going to bring.
