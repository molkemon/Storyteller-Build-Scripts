A colorful new man, or rather kobold, of letters has appeared among our intellectuals. He has begun to write treatises on our local political customs and the way they interact with our moral foundations, but his most loved form of inquiry appears to be scholarly debate. He has been travelling from town to town to debate the topics of duty, reason, and knowledge with our leading scholars from sunup to sundown, and these debates have begun to attract quite an audience. He has a habit of standing atop a pile of his books to match the height of his interlocutor, but the pile tends to topple when he will extract a volume from underneath his feet to reference as he enumerates a point. A group of young debaters have taken to following the kobold across the country, and in response the kobold has allowed them to study under him in an informal kind of discipleship. Given the nature of the kobold’s talent, it is quite possible that the future leaders and scholars of the country are currently travelling around with him, and giving him the resources to expand his operations and devote more time to his writing, oration, and teaching might bring us great rewards.
