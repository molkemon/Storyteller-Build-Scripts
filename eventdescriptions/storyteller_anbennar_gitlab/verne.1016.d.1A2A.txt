We walked into that place together, hand in hand. It was a taller and broader dome than the Hall of the Exemplaries at the Vernissage; maybe the biggest indoor space I had seen in my whole life. At its centre, a plume of water, a column of refracted transparency rising right to the ceiling. The fountain was so perfect that it appeared almost still, a pillar of glass. I had never seen anything like it before or since. It was clear that it fed the lake we saw up top, which was funny because we had been worried about whether the lake water would be potable. It was probably the purest water in the world at the time. Do you want to know why I left the academy? For Cassie. It is funny—in the end, she was the one to suggest taking a few things for ourselves, setting ourselves up right. No angel, my Cassie, not completely.
