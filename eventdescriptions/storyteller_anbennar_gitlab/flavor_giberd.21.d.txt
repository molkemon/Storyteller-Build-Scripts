Day Eight, Orwick Silverscrew’s Personal Communiques. The Loopeng Gedsande has turned north, pushing up and around to the land of the crown. It is a queer place, if I do say so myself, Royan. The merchants there never stop haggling, and I am half convinced they would run away with the boat if I left it too long. Not to mention the tolls and fees to even dock had my head spinning and put me in half a mind to turn around and go home. Nevertheless, I have made the initial contact with their emissaries, and Royan, I will tell you I nearly lost my temper at those money-haggling miscreants. They would hold the future of our nation in the hands of a bloody trade deal and not even a bloody fair one at that. They want nothing less than exclusive trade rights for twenty years. Exclusive. Twenty years. It is absolutely absurd, and despite my best attempts to negotiate or make them see reason, they are absolutely set in their thoughts. I have no doubts the lads back home at Giberd will think me a fool for saying.
