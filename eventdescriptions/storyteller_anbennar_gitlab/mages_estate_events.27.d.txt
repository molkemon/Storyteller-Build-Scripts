It is a lovely day in the capital, and you are stuck hearing petitions. "Come look, my ruler. Something mysterious is afoot, something unusual and inexplicable. Not just one thing, you understand, but a series of things. First, my ruler, gaze upon this instrument, a percussive tool constructed of the bones of the fallen. Now note how I strike it in the same place twice, yet it produces two completely distinct noises. I propose that we find the artisan who constructed this device, and fire them." "Fire them?" You ask, unsure how the term applies. "With a cannon, ideally." The petitioner points at another demonstrative article, a horse in a cage. "Now, my ruler, look here. This is clearly an Akalšesi warhorse, yet when you look away and turn back, it inexplicably becomes an Arbarani Destrier." You study the horse closely, then turn away and back. Yes, indeed there does seem to be some discoloration of the hair. "And finally, analyze these two documents. One states that Lady Mirabel married Sir Henric, whereas the other claims that Henric died and she married Maycald instead. From whence do these errors arise? I propose the explanation that this world is not real, and instead the fictive product of overactive imaginations. How can one be sure that?" You clear your throat, preparing yourself to give wise counsel, quelling the fear of the petitioner. "You see, my child, when these things occur around us, we can only come to one conclusion."
