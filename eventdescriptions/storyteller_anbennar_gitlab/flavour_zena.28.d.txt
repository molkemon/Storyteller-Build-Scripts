Built by the Brrtekuh pack, the gnollish LifeGiver Fortresses are scattered throughout the Western Krahway. Made of stone and enclosed by concentric walls that rise in height towards the center, they break the harsh winds and sands of the Salahad, protecting the invaluable oases contained within their ramparts. Not only does this design allow the inhabitants of the fortress to tame the dangers of the desert, it also gives a strategic edge over rivals, forcing them to either scale the formidable walls and capture the oases or to take a different, often longer and deadlier route through the Salahad. While the desert is not a danger within the walls of Bivuzena, this design can be repurposed to protect from the harsh winds of the monsoons that occasionally crash down from the Seahorse Sea, as well as increasing protection from any who would attempt to siege the city. During a diplomatic mission to a clan inhabiting a ToxasagAkaz, Azaly noted that an improvement could be made on the design by introducing miniscule gaps within the stonework such that a small portion of the wind could be allowed through, reducing turbulence by letting the air currents merge more gently on the other side. A Rram Zelag and their apprentices have traveled with Azaly to Bivuzena to oversee the implementation of these experimental modifications, and determine if they can be of use back in their arid home.
