While Akalšesi coronations since our twin empires were shattered still received representatives from nigh every realm and meaningful polity in Bulwar, such representatives were frequently there with naught but a small party, and were usually simple ambassadors or envoys, rather than members of ruling families or otherwise august personages in their own right. Furthermore, the fact that the Kings of Akalšes were crowned by the Nansaleni Matriarchs led to scoffing and disdain by many of those representatives, who saw that staunch tradition as further evidence of Akalšesi decline. Now, though, as the monarch of Akalšes is once more crowned by the ruler, the attending delegations are much more impressive, with major citystates and surviving elven realms sending not mere ambassadors, but relatives of the ruler, if not the rulers themselves. Many of these are nervously looking on as the monarch of Akalšes rises, proclaimed as the King of Kings by the ruler. With a steely look in their eyes, the monarch of Akalšes declares that Akalšes will only rise further, and that they will not rest until Akalšes has carried out the Divine Duty of Kings to unite all of Surael’s Garden under their rule.
