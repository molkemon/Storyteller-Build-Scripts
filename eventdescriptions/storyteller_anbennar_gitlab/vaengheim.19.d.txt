In the days of the year, the trollish toil concluded, and the queen was asked to Sporreyn come, to see the craft of the Oaktongue clan, who had raised the tower in Aldis' stead. And they saw a mighty thing, for the tower had been made of stone entire, like the giant's craft in Gullmork. And the queen asked the chief Asgeir, "Will this place not fall to dust, bereft of wood or mortar, to hold it strong against the wind and sea?" And Asgeir answered, "Shame bring I to you, to speak your tongue, for I am no lord thereof. Tower tall will stand, for troll is child to giant, and their craft is our craft. This tower needs no wood, for held it is by strength of earth. Stone alone will sing here, for mighty song to weave. We sought Gerudaghot alone, yet under the wing of yours have we found it. Your people love us not, but they will see our craft this day, and know our gift. Trust, o queen, I ask you thus, fly high, and put rest the last stone. For you will see our gift as well, and it will linger until the mountains fall." Then Asgeir gave the queen a small stone cube. And they took wing, and flew atop Oaktongue tower, and found there the rest of this final stone. And as they put it in place, did Asgeir's folk loose ropes, and scaffolding remove. And the tower stood, each stone holding the next, to stand together until the end of time. And the queen felt the hum of ancient magic, and saw the clouds swirl, and gently alter course.
