The monarch took another sip of wine as they looked out from their balcony, at the immensity of their capital below. It was night, but even now the light of commerce, industry, and empire illuminated the sky. Taychend never truly slept anymore. The carts never stopped, the harvest never ended, the war banners never unfurled. The ruler should be happy, they knew, but they could only grimace as they thought about the empire they ruled. They had surpassed the greatest empires of the past long ago. They surpassed them the moment they unified Taychend, really. The monarch knew now that their dynasty would not fall as the others did. Their empire would conquer, and the warlords would hunger, and on the blood of the world they would feed, the ichor of their ascension. The cart of empire was running, running, faster yet faster, straight ahead, destined for the wall named "eternity"—the true end of the Cycle of Glory. The beginning of a new age, where Taychend would rule undisputed. It was unstoppable. It was uncontrollable. And if they looked beyond the wall and did not like what they saw? Or if the wheels were running off fate’s track, towards somewhere nobody could even comprehend? Well, there was nothing the monarch could do about it anymore. The ruler took another sip from their wine, closed their eyes. The hum of Empire, and the screams of the conquered, and the cry of "glory, glory, glory" droned on—as it did, as it had, as it would. Already, their lieutenants were drawing up plans for new expeditions, to North Aelantier, to Insyaa, to Sarhal. The title knew their realm would go further. An empire for a day, an empire for an eon, it did not matter—Taychend will rise, an Empire Unstoppable, an Empire Ascendant, an Empire Vainglorious.
