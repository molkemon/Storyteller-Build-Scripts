Know this first; the True Kings of all Heavens once governed everything. When from nothing sprang the First Imbalance, the Kings fought off the evil of Oblivion and diced it into myriad pieces. Tired after their great battle, they formed our universe free of Oblivion’s cancre as a restful home for their essences, and idled a while in perfect repose. Eventually, rested and satisfied, they prepared to move onto another plane, shedding tears of blood, flesh, mind and soul. From these, life was born. Over the eons elves came to be, and thrived and lived. Yet as time went on, unjust and corrupt rulers, pale shadows of the True Kings, spread corruption and waste in their realms. To such things the essence of Oblivion is drawn, and soon a Second Imbalance threatened not only the realms of mortals but the universe itself. As Oblivion raged, turning all the world into a wasteland, a shepherd boy climbed to the top of the serene Rigoti, most beautiful of peaks. He begged the True Kings to return to this world and save elfkind, and through a rift in the sky they did. The nameless Kings fought and died to scatter Oblivion once more, perishing in turn until only one was left. This Last True King anointed the shepherd boy first of the Fasiri, the righteous kings of the Eltibhar. To this day, a white stone slab at the very peak of the Rigoti marks this hallowed event. Every Eltibhar ruler must ascend the Rigoti, if he hopes to have the blessing of the Last True King. With such wisdom we avert Oblivion, and the end of all we hold dear.
