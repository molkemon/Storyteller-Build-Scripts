Our favored daughter, the heir, is young and still has a lot to learn about life and how to be a good ruler. In the court of the monarch, some suggest that she may gather this experience by being tasked with ruling the lands of a specific province. In addition, she would have her legitimacy reinforced and so would reduce the chance of a future succession struggle. However, others caution that the inexperience of the heir should be rectified closer to home and that she should remain in the court of the monarch so that she may be supervised by those who know her. Isolated and left to their own devices, the heir may develop premature political ambitions if allowed to rule unsupervised.
