The trade company reports that its neighbor, the nation of a certain country, has become embroiled in war. Company officers see opportunity in the crisis, seeking to advance the long-term interests of the company. Winning the affections of that nation would facilitate the peaceful growth of our trade in the region. Supporting their enemies, the state of another country, might weaken that nation enough that they would be forced to accept more coercive treaties, or even be vulnerable to a company invasion.
