Slavery is a constant in Rahen. Few states go without it; even Bhuvari, a republic born from the fires of a slave revolt, failed to throw off its ghoulish grasp. The powers that be often benefit far too much, and will rarely change on their own accord; without a degree of force it will never die. Perhaps, Suhan's Praxis will be that force for change. One of the Praxis' core doctrines demands respect for all living peoples; hence men, women, Harimari, Human, slave, and freeman are all equals. To not consider the wisdom of those seen as lesser is to ignore half the knowledge of the world. Many of Suhan's followers take this to heart, and lead movements for the progression of women, and sometimes taking violent action against slave owners. Today such an incident has come to pass. With half a dozen guards and the plantation owner hanged for their perceived crimes, the slaves of the plantation, freed by spear point, now flee into the countryside. The reality is that the country is still a place where slavery is legal, and whether the ruler likes it or not, the law demands justice. The son of the plantation owner has come forth demanding the return of the slaves, and that the perpetrators be hanged for their crimes. What does the ruler say?
