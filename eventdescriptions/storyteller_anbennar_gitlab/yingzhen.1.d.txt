Our defeat against Jingqui saw Caoying slip from our grasp, and our fields were littered with the corpses of friend and foe alike. When we refused to allow the men of Jingqui to retrieve the bodies of their slain comrades, it was nighuniversally considered a petulant act of defiance against our foe; but today that decision shall bear fruit. Adorned in the arms and armor of those very same fallen, a number of our men make their way deep into Jingqui territory. With any luck, our envoy's declaration of war will arrive just as our agents infiltrate the fort in Heiyucheng.
