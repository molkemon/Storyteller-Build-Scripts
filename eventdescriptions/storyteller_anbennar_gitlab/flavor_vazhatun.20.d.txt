It came without warning, predicted neither by our astronomers nor the prophecies. Suddenly the sky was lit ablaze, red fire raining down as molten rock heralded the true calamity, a meteor from beyond Halann. When it hit the surface of the lake, water was instantly vaporized; no splash was heard or seen, instead there was a gigantic explosion that echoed off of the very mountains surrounding it. When it was over, the water rushed back into the hot crater, the lake, now transformed, filled again. The water would remain hot for days, then warm for weeks as the molten rock slowly cooled down. The surrounding area was hit with unfathomable destruction. In a radius of miles upon miles, all windows had shattered in the wake of the shock, trees were cut down like toothpicks, the skeletal remnants now lie flat on the ground, their leaves blasted away.
