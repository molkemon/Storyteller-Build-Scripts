The monarch hit the ground with a thud, head ringing from the blow. The world spun as the ruler groped blindly for their weapon. Above, a shadow approached; more than a shadow perhaps, though the title could not discern more than a flash of red hair, their vision spun so. "Come now. I expect better of you than that." A woman's voice spoke clear, strong and kind. "From the top, now." Pulling the monarch to their feet, the redhaired knight struck again as soon as they had readied themselves, and once more planted the title firmly upon the ground with a masterful maneuver. The redhaired woman chuckled. "Remember, you must be prepared for any threat. Tighten that guard up, or you'll have a blade in under it, understand, your Grace?" "Your Grace?" "Your Grace, are you alright?" The words reached the monarch's ears as if through water, and their eyes opened to a bright, dizzying world. The soldiers in the yard pulled the title to their feet; they had been laying there for some time, but after many assurances to their health the soldiers left the monarch to contemplate what they had seen. Some whispered that Corin herself had blessed the title with her presence that day in the training yard, though others would readily dismiss those rumors as foolish chatter. Still, none could deny that after that day, certain techniques had slipped into the monarch's sparring that have been catching even the most well-trained opponents off guard. And, better yet, these moves have begun to disseminate down the ranks, taking to the army like a flame to kindling. The monarch is invigorated, seeming to have just the right thing to say at every meeting of arms, the brightest ideas at the finest moment.
