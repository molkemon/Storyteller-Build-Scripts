The days hence Welyam’s end bore a grim aspect, for the summers grew short, and the wings of winter grew vast, bringing snow to days that in years past had been free of their shadow. Even the sun turned from the earth, their face obscured behind a veil of clouds as if in mourning. Harvest after harvest did winter claim, and with the cold came the hunger. And many did say that these were the final days. The monarch who was queen then shared the plight of their people. As they suffered, the monarch opened the coffers of her roost and spread far their wealth to lessen winter’s blows. One day their roost shook, as if the very mountain on which Vængheim nested, had caught a mortal chill. The monarch rose and said, “Elkaesal shifts and soon will she wake, for her dreams are dark. Vengeance would I swear on Gaweton, for her sons have brought doom to both our peoples. And yet, though vengeance sings in my ear, it is a song I know too well. And it is the song that doomed Welyam, and would doom us in our turn.” “I go instead to Elkaesal, and I will sing to her, and soothe her and bring her peace. For hagmagic has ensnared her dreams, and she will not shake this curse alone. Let it be known that all who would may join me on this hunt.” And many did, for they knew that to do nothing was to die. Men, and trolls and orks, and dragonkin, gnomes and elves of the old west. All took cause with the monarch, and together walked beneath Vængheim. And many who saw them did weep, for they saw the dead walking to their doom.
