This outpost could hardly be called an outpost anymore. Great walls surround it, keeping the wilderness out and the civilization in; beyond the walls, miles and miles of formerly untended land is harvested for the first time, as mounted guards patrol the land ready to muster a defense force at the first sign of attack. Soon the harvest will be brought on horseback towards the gates of the city and sold at the bustling market in the center, where great crowds, in numbers never seen here before, gather, and the city breathes. On the other side of the walls, a question remains in the minds of new arrivals. Shall they remain in the city, a bastion of settlement amongst the centaurs? Will they ride out to embrace the freedom and wilderness of the steppes? Or will they follow in the footsteps of those that paved the ground they walk on, to push back the wilderness, and make these lands forbidden no longer?
