Then Vængheim spread her wings, and those who dwelled below her roost were made hers by her maidens, who waged war on them with spear and song and wing, and brought their foes great shame, for none could stand against them. And many of the Olavish did find it odd that now, the daughters of the mountains came to them with spear and wrath. For they had near forgotten, the mountains to their back, who held for most old songs at best, and tales of ages past. And many lost their hearts, and thought that chains and bondage to them came, for Vængheim’s maidens held a cruel aspect to their eye. So it was when the ruler took wing, and flew from roost to coast, to meet the folk of Olavlund, who fought and bled to halt their flight, and the flight of their maidens. And the men of Olavlund found their knees in their sight, and said, “Our song is that of the broken queen, for well have you fought, and lost is our strength. Our people is yours, and we ask for pity's sake, that you find your heart and let us live.” And the ruler did say, “Rise, o folk of Olavlund, and find your strength again, for you have fought with valour, and I would mend the wound I have bestowed upon your spirit. And I would have you raise your spear, and add your strength to mine, so that both our songs may grow, to shake the earth and sky.” Then were the folk of Olavlund struck dumb, for the ruler had bit them with their song, and when they found their voice again, did they name them queen.
