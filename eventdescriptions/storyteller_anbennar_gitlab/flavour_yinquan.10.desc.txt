The HuliJing have known about the Gumiho for some time now, but they have been loath to travel by sea. Now that they have a safe land route to a certain domain, they have informed us of our duty to escort some of them up north to our embassy. The reunion of the HuliJing and the Gumiho was an affair not suited for the ears of a maiden; suffice to say, there was a lot of alcohol and a similarly large amount of broken bones, though it was without a doubt successful. The HuliJing who were escorted up north have become a permanent fixture of the embassy, in addition to the servants required to keep them sated. The Gumiho, recognizing our superior position on the political sphere, are amenable to negotiating a more formal subordinate relationship.
