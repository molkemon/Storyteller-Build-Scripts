I have to say that in the case of the state of Brasan, a policy of benign neglect is key. Yes, I understand that this is the Matriarch's policy regarding most of our conquests. As sisters and diplomats, we have read the doctrine and seen it in action. I believe you even put it into practice in Kumarkand. But I must emphasize the need to go further regarding Brasan. Let me explain why. Brasan is a mercantile region; the land around it is not awful, but it is not good. What gives the region life and importance is the city, and that place depends entirely on commerce. There are other important cities on the river, but they all depend on Brasan to export their goods, as it is the port at the mouth of the Bulwar River and has the most developed infrastructure. So, as established, it is a region that lives or dies by commerce, and we must understand what merchants want. They want stability, security, and a sense of control, and they always have an intrinsic belief that only other merchants understand their needs. The Matriarch could appoint the best governor to oversee the region, with the greatest wisdom, intelligence, unlimited funds, and an endless army. The Brasanni would still complain, claiming we were making their job harder; they would work less and conspire against us. So I suggest putting one of their merchants in power over the region; left in charge of themselves, they will govern themselves the way they know best; and they cannot blame anyone but themselves for their folly. Besides, their complaints could be directed at this human governor, and the intrigues would be aimed at them too. Another defense of my point about benign neglect. Brasan prospered when it was under the control of a governor from its own merchant class. It always struggled when it was controlled by an outsider; either from lack of understanding of how a merchant state is supposed to be controlled, or from a desire to extract the highest profit in the short term. So please, sister, send this to the Matriarch.
