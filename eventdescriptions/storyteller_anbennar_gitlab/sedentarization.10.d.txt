The war is coming to a close; we have defeated the combined hordes of the Caehn and his Ordsikar, and our own forces are free to move into the area. But here, there is nothing to move into. One could walk over a hundred miles and not find another settlement. The native centaurs live freely, migrating across the steppes, stopping for a week or two before continuing on. It is apparently their god who commands them to live in this manner. Life is harsh in these lands, and they enjoy few of the benefits of civilization. We do have an opportunity. We can tame these lands, and seek whatever riches might lie on the other side, but the presence of the centaurs looms over the idea like a stormcloud. They are seen as strange and uncouth by most, and as monstrous and evil by many more. Some claim the only solution is to kill the beasts on sight wherever they may be found. However, the wisest amongst our advisors realize that such a course of action will likely do us more harm than good. The centaurs will not simply roll over and let us kill them; they will resist, and when they do, their vengeance will be a terrible thing. These advisors instead push for a careful deployment of administrators and settlers to these forbidden lands.
