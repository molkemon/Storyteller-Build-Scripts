It was a cool night in the Ascension Peninsula, as Jyntas wandered betwixt the trees and through great rolling hills of underbrush. It was there she saw it, upon a small rock, sniffling with its head sunk low. The creature had a mane of red hair, catlike eyes, and strange, circular hands. “What is wrong, little one?” asked the great dragon of brass, moved by its sorrow. “The others won’t let me play with them,” cried the creature, still sniffling, “They said I looked weird and creepy.” Jyntas crossed her arms, frowning. “And none of them stood up for you?” The creature nodded, sobbing. “They all called me a freak.” It was then the brass dragon sat beside the creature, and picked it up. “Then I shall play with you.” And so it was, Jyntas showed that everyone, regardless of their appearance, is worthy of love and respect.
