The Xhazobkult has been active in Cheshosh'tchuka ever since the influx of their people, gnolls, humans, dwarves, and even some harpy slaves they have. The natives need their guidance despite having already a good start, just like their Xhazobkult faith. The Cheshoshi sacrifice living people for religious purposes, but where they can see the tangible results as demonic forces empower their people, they meaninglessly sacrifice their people in this Citadel to appease their god that refuses to listen to their offerings. Their ignorant and weak god is laughable at best; if he were powerful and useful, he would have protected their people. Still, their Bone Citadel is nonetheless impressive and worthy of restoration, not for Cheshosh, the imaginative god of death and ruin, but for the tangible demons that will see this place of death and decay as a piece of their own world, on Halann. The kult urges preparations for repairs of the now dilapidated Citadel to begin at once.
