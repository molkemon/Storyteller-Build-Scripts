When first we learned of the grand abandoned automatory buried deep in the Hinuilakelands, we saw it as a vast sterility. The scale was beyond comprehension, surely the reflexive process of a machine, some indifferent mechanical loom churning out mechanism after mechanism. Yet as new lives and new bodies are assembled, our automatories, mere workshops in comparison, are the site of miracles. One can dismiss it as projection, of course. the sentimentality of the animal towards anything which resembles, even conceptually, the newborn child. Yet when the mechanisms awaken, we act as parents. We guide them into this life. The reductionists among us can scoff at the flicker one feels as the kauga soulcore locks into place, but try it yourself. You will find yourself placing a hand gently on the shoulder chassis; you will find yourself singing lullabies.
