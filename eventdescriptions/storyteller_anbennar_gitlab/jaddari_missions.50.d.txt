What does Surael see when he looks down upon the world he blessed? When he surveys those devoted to his purpose? Come, let us follow his glorious eye as it proceeds across the sky. Rising out of the Insyaan mists, the first to greet it are the whalers of Feiten's air balloons, and even the most uneducated and gruff among them intones the morning prayers as they reel their harpoons back in. But we should quickly move on; soon those wisps of cloth and fire in the sky are dwarfed by the rising spires of Tianlou. In the once capital of once Yanshen, now yet another shining jewel amidst the Jadd Empire's crown, hundreds of thousands of souls rise in song and mumbled words to greet it. From a eunuch bureaucrat pausing amidst fervid calculations on the latest census, to a pilgrim from Ekha who has crossed almost half the world to pray at the Sunrise Temple, to a trader finishing their delivery of Cannorian wine, whatever the language, the message is the same. thank Surael, who gives us light, for another day in which to pursue his purpose and serve his cause. The sun rises in the sky as it crosses Haless, and in its growing glory you can see how it supplies the energy needed to support vast rice paddies and even vaster jungles. The sunmonks of the Xia salute it in their sparring, the Sun Elven blood of Arawkelin's Kelino families stirs at its passage, even the dwarves in Verkal Ozovar, deep below ground, praise it and Surael when its rays reach the peaks of the Phokhao.
