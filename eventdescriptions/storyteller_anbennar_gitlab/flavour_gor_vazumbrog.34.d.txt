The monarch personally accompanied the expedition to seek the wise Aakhet's advice in defeating the purple fiend; anything less seemed to risk offending the dragon's dignity. "Greetings, great Aakhet. we bring tribute, cultural artifacts of great significance to our people, and humbly request your aid with a difficulty that faces our nation." "Of course you do, humble supplicants," the bronzedragoninmanform sniffed, "My advice is sought by all who are wise enough to recognize their own ignorance, and should be more than a match for whatever trifling situation faces your nation." "We need to kill the Hunter of the Deep." Aakhet disguised his anger and disgust poorly. "You come to me, and ask for aid in killing another of my kind, one of the few who survives into this fallen era? And you think your grandfathers' baubles suffice to enlist my aid?" His lips twitched once more, and then his face relaxed. "Of course, supplicants. Lead bullets. Lead bullets will pierce the old girl's hide. Now be on your way."
