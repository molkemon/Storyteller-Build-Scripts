Kimánis is acting stranger than usual, which is quite a feat for them. Where once their vineyards produced wine only begrudgingly and under the direct control of our overseers, every vineyard in Kimánis now appears to have entered a kind of productive fervor. They are giving away their surplus for free, and each night the city enters a state of revelry; nonviolent, for now. Even the hard drinkers among our occupation garrisons have begun to grow afraid. The citizens of Kimánis are erecting ramshackle stalls in which to put on theatrical productions. Something must be done.
