Elikhet has been many things to many peoples over the ages. Saviour, Godking, patron deity, and even implacable adversary. But all can agree that he was a staunch proponent of Order and Civilization, two of the greatest gifts of Surael to his mortal children. The Bulwari consider him a close relation of Surael Himself, as he was born to Nirakhet, sister to Suran and Buranun, who bore the children of Surael. Even if Elikhet himself has been lost to the machinations of the Malevolent Dark, by his actions he was defending Surael’s Order against the forces of darkness. It is little wonder then, that just as Kuza is still remembered and her deeds praised high up in the mountains of the Šad Kuz, that the memory of Elikhet still has a place in the hearts of the people of Kheterat. It is in the QaarInimahat, the Temple of the Bringer of Order, that the priests remind the people that everyone, be they resplendent god or mere mortal, has a duty to do their part in the defense of the divinely ordained order of the world. This concept is echoed in the very design of the temple itself, with a series of grandiose halls and austere buildings arranged in a symmetrical layout around the central courtyard and the temple pyramid of EduzBiblahmu within. Filling the halls and processional avenues of the temple complex at every hour are throngs of pilgrims seeking to affirm their faith and receive guidance from the assembled priesthood.
