Since the proliferation of presses across our country, the general tendency has been for communities to move closer to one another, able to exchange information and standardize texts. This has not been true of our lizardfolk minority, who historically have preferred to live cloistered among their own, and who as a result have begun publishing their own pamphlets, periodicals, and newspapers. Much of the content of these is innocuous, but some of the editorial writing proclaims the virtues of historical lizardfolk governance, or openly pines for the three hundred thirty-third Empire, sentiments which many of our courtiers consider openly seditious.
