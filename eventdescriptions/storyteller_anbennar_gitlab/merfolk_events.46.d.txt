On a patch of deep water, dark and foreboding as the space between the stars above, a sea monster unlike any I had ever seen cracked the ship’s hull like a clamshell, spilling sailors and cargo out into the cold brine. This assault drew terrifying scavengers who circled our diminishing refuges, hungering for our hides. If it were not for those merfolk hunters, I would not be here to complain about it. Aye, hoisting wicked harpoons, they struck our predatory foes, tinging the endless blue red. I had hoped our saviors would grant us assistance back to shore, but they only bellowed a victorious cry, claimed their trophies, and then sank back into the depths without sparing us a second glance. While these aquatic hunters did not intend to save the ship’s crew, offering token thanks at the wreckage might show the merfolk that we respect their prowess. Likewise, we could merely accept this serendipity for what it was, leaving our relations unaffected and saving face.
