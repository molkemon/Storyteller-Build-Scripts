There was a time when Ruuk, strongest son of the ruler, was never far from his father’s side. This was expected; assuming the ruler would remain chief, Ruuk would succeed him after his death. He was meant to learn to rule by sharing in and questioning his father’s decisions and judgments. Those days were over. The longer clan Silentblade had been in the Deepwoods, the more the ruler had sought solitude, often spending hours alone in the woods before returning to give orders to his clan. Initially, Ruuk thought he had been grieving the death of his mother. But as the orders grew more erratic and the ruler’s own behaviour grew stranger, Ruuk began to realize there was something more to it than that. The ruler had no intention of teaching him command, because he did not intend for him to ever take command. Because the ruler did not intend to die. Something had been set in motion, and Ruuk knew he was far too late to stop it, if he had ever been able to. Something had brought the Greentide into this accursed forest, and whatever it was, it was about to consume clan Silentblade. He had to fight it, but he couldn’t, not here, not alone. He would gather allies among the other clans, he had told himself. To challenge his father to Ozarm’Chadash before knowing what it was he faced was foolish, he had told himself. It was not enough to convince himself. He knew what he had done.
