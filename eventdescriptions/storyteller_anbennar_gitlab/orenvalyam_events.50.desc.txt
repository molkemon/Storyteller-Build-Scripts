Elrandar Silverspite sat against his chair, struggled to stay awake, struggled to keep breathing, breaths jagged, pain coursing through his body. He was dying. This was it. So much left to do, but even the Dragon knew his time was come. Was that sorrow, through the iron? Or pity? Or just comprehension? The Kamrayakval closed his eyes. And El woke up. There was no collar around his neck. There was peace, quiet. No rage, no wrath. A field of grass dotted with flowers, floating in a white void, an island in the sky. Dhivumanhu, or a dream, a hallucination before the end, and uncertainty. Probably the latter. But El liked his lies, his fictions. What was one more? A voice, familiar, from behind. Ceboranbu stood there, leaning on his staff, eyes stern. El frowned, said softly. “It’s you.” Turned back. Dejected. “It’s just you.” The bearded Warden shook his head. “Do you really think, after all you’ve done, she would want to see your face?” And the once Kamrayakval just sighed, and understood. The elf rose to his feet, looked into the eyes of someone who had been his friend, before he murdered him. “Everything I did, every terrible crime, it was for a reason. There was purpose. Would you tell her that?” Ceboranbu frowned, spoke gravely. “Perhaps I may. Perhaps you would tell her yourself, one day.” He tapped his staff against the grass. A voiddoor opened behind him. “After all, you are now as the Dawn you think you won. You have forever, to change, to reflect, to atone.” The Warden looked to the grass around. “Or maybe you are satisfied with how it all turned out.” And then back to the person who had killed him. “I suppose it is your choice. As it always was.” El stared into the void. Enjoyed weighing the possibilities. “Do you really think so?”
