The Chieftain walked up to the cauldron. It didn’t look magical in the slightest. It looked like water, and if it had a smell it was drowned out by the smoke from the torches that lit up the night. The orks holding them were silent, were distant somehow. “And this fey drink will give us immortality?” A smile formed on the ruler’s face, though it did not extend to his yellow eyes. “This? No, this will tie you to the fey, nothing more. It is the pact that brings immortality. This is just the fuel.” The ruler dipped a cup into the cauldron, and offered it to him, but the Chieftain hesitated. “And what do we give in return?” The ruler’s smile dissipated. “You know the answer to that question. It is necessary. Dookan is lost, and Dookanson is dead. This is the only future for our kind. The only future without weakness, without dishonor. It is not servitude, it is purpose. You know it must be done.” Wordlessly, the Chieftain took the cup, and drank.
