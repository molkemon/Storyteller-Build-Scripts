While common in the uncouth Corinite lands, the early Ravelians have always prided themselves as people of reason. In recent times, with the official founding of the faith, the masses often find very little tolerance with mages and sorcerers, especially those they seem to be too secular in respect to the idea that magic is divine. Many mages and people accused of having magic ability have been branded as witches and apostates, of worshiping the Devil and being a threat to the Ravelian world. It has gone so far that people have lynched alleged apostates without a proper trial or oversight from the inquisition.
