The message had reached the royal palace like a bolt out of the blue. Despite painstaking negotiations regarding even the most insignificant detail of the marriage contract and repeated public signs of favour towards the heir, the royal court of Birzartanšes has abruptly rejected our proposal to wed our royal heir to their new queen Keladora. This can only mean two things. Either Keladora is too weak to impose her will on her court, which would prove she is unworthy of becoming the heir’s consort, or she has played a duplicitous game from the beginning, utilising the promise of eventual nuptials to deter us from acting against her while her grip on power was still weak after deposing the paragon of inadequacy that was her father king Arantir. Either way, this shall not stand. Whether Keladora is simply feeble and unable to rein in her conniving courtiers, or a calculating schemer, she has proven to be just as unfit to rule as her father. Both the slighted honour of our royal house and the duty we as Chosen of Surael have towards the common people of Bulwar, including those living within Birzartanšes, require us to act, and act decisively. Given the sorry state of Bahar, this is but the latest sign that the descendants of Amarien are unsuited to rule. It now falls to us to oust the false queen from her illgotten throne in Birsartansbar.
