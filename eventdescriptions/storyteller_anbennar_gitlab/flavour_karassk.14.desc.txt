The animals’ burrow was within sight of the dragoons now. There was no need for a plan or a formation, not when that could give the enemy time to prepare. They descended upon the burrow as swiftly as their mounts could carry them. The merfolk had crude animal tools to fight back, but it mattered little against the might of the warriors. With all the dragoons’ training and their fine equipment, it would be a disservice to call the ensuing events a battle; the beasts never had a chance. Their glass structures were shattered and their coral dens were smashed apart beneath the titanic sharks. The greatest challenge of the whole operation was simply finding them all, but for the men even that didn’t slow them down for long. Before six hours had passed, the burrow was emptied of its animals. As many as possible were captured, though some of the sharks were in too deep of a frenzy to go after the odd escapee.
