Let me preface this by saying that I am very grateful for your assistance with the Facestealers. I know that all of you are risking your lives and all, but there is a blank expression on the old, wiry centaur who was speaking with a heavy accent that Janeth could not quite place. I have never seen such a terrible performance. You people did everything as wrong as possible. It is a miracle that some of you even remained alive. Ehh, it’s not that bad. We rode out with two thousand soldiers and returned with twelve? Thirteen. You blatantly disregarded my calls and just charged everyone into the group that I explicitly told you to stay away from. It would then seem that all of you collectively forgot to wear the masks that we provided for you. I don’t even know how ten facestealers can kill this many people in such a short time. So when’s our next assignment? Words cannot describe the look that the old centaur gave Janeth. Your next assignment is learning to stay away from people who just stand around in the wastes, from people who have black pools as eyes, who have gaping mouths without teeth. Something is muttered under his breath, but Commander Janeth doesn’t concern himself with that.
