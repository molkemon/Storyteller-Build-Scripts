The halls of your palace feel colder these days. With every passing year, the whispers in court grow louder. You have no heir, no consort, and no clear line of succession. The stability of the entire realm rests on your aging shoulders. It is your most trusted, and perhaps most desperate, advisor who broaches the subject one evening. "My lord," he begins, his voice low, "our own noble houses offer no suitable candidates, and our diplomatic overtures have been rebuffed. Time is running short, but there is another option. A radical one." He gestures to a map showing the lands of the neighboring harpy flocks. "They are a people of incredible vitality and fierce loyalty. Their matriarchs are proud and strong. To secure a mate from a high-ranking flock would be unconventional, to be sure, but their fertility would silence all questions of succession. Perhaps a great gift to the flock to prove the worth of the suitor? It would be a powerful statement, binding their people to ours. But the court, they may not understand."
