A professor of the local religion was holding a lecture in the school of the province about the folly of the current religion. Hour after hour he mocked the High Gods as either impotent or fictional, derided their followers as superstitious hypocrites, and asserted the moral, intellectual, and theological superiority of the local religion's teachings. After he was finished, he asked the lecture hall if anyone dared to challenge his arguments. The hall was completely silent, but then a single attendant stood up and fearlessly asked. "If the local religion is so superior, why hasn't it produced anything as magnificent as the High Temples?" The professor opened his mouth, but found no words with which to answer. His face turned pale, his hands started shaking, and then he finally ran out of the hall, head buried in his palms in a desperate attempt to hold back his tears. As if on command, the entire lecture hall started clapping in unison at the astounding rhetorical display they had just borne witness to, and they all agreed to follow the current religion from now on.
