The ship of state sails under its own wind, and a new hand on the tiller can only change course so much. Whether commoners view the country's government as tyrannical, benevolent, or something in between ultimately comes down to the laws of the land, the bureaucratic and judicial apparatus that administers them, and the local magistrates who enforce them. However, the occupant of the Dragon Throne still holds great sway. The monarch can issue proclamations, appoint, dismiss, banish, and execute officials at will, and use their supreme authority as they see fit. The realm anxiously awaits which direction the newly crowned ruler of the dynasty will steer the country. In recent years, the people have found the government to be kind and benevolent, ensuring the loyalty of the subjects through mercy, compassion, and generosity. But the ruler, with their malicious drive to gain power at all costs, has sent ripples of dread through the hearts of commoners and courtiers alike, with new fears of the secret police and their highly skilled torturers haunting their thoughts.
