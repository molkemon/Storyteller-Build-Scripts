With the unfortunate passing of the great conqueror Kusheng Nurgai Bagaatun, the Shuvüüsh people have lost one of their finest. As thousands make their way to Hogunuug to visit her grave and pay their respects, the locals have already begun constructing a temple around it. Whilst the people were deep in mourning, the shamans of the Saanorgegh communities were busy asking the spirit world for guidance. To their surprise, a familiar voice answered. The Faceless Queen is still alive, in a fashion, for her spirit has refuted the call of the Supreme Spirit and remains in the spirit realm. She has ascended to become a revered ancestor goddess, according to the testimony of a majority of the gathered shamans. And thus, in her honor, a new mask has been carved to be laid upon her grave, one devoid of all details, a true faceless mask for a faceless queen. Already several legends are beginning to circulate around the clans, such as that she has married one of the three brothers, with shamans bickering about which of the three it could be. She is quickly becoming the preferred deity for all Shuvüüsh women as they pray to her for guidance. The Faceless Queen is now available as a personal deity to all queens who control her temple in Zougzengom.
