The ghostly visage of Sang Caoban, who had so selfishly clung to power, had been the mastermind behind Duyen’s disappearance all this time. It was he who had people sneak into Nirakvondikai in the night, robbing them of the remains of their beloved ruler. It was he who tried to deceive their soldiers through the halls of Verkal Ozovar, throwing false leads and rumors to throw them off. Now the urn rests in his palace, discarded among many other trinkets and baubles, its prior purpose lost when the Ghost Emperor passed on. The ruler had ordered the Boars of Dinh to carefully sweep the palace for any other loyalists to the deceased emperor’s cause, while she proceeded to grab the urn so delicately, caressing it in a full embrace. "Beloved Ancestor," the ruler declared, "You will be home soon."
