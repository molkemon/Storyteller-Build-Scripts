Bring me another barrel bellowed the heir to nobody in particular. As the heir surveyed the interior of the Haraigizenhi Bulreki tavern they found themselves in, their thoughts stewed on the events that brought them here. The conquest of the Amberlands was apparently of utmost importance to the powers that be in the capital; not only was it the richest region of Yarikhoi, but it also played host to the vaunted Treasure Fleet of Zurkanrek. Everyone had a reason why the fate of the nation depended on those colossal heaps of wood. Clan Garsvulshim salivated over the world of new flavors the fleet would open up, Clan Olkhajukh grew similarly delirious over the prospect of accessing the markets of the east, and even the heir’s Royal Clan Khreshtoq’av cousins insisted that the boats themselves were somehow True Giant relics. That’s why, when the sailors of the Treasure Fleet anchored themselves just off the coast and threatened to sink their ships if it meant they wouldn’t have to serve under the tyrants that despoiled their homeland, the heir was sent to show that the crown was watching the situation closely. Most of the work was being left to Clan Iltklerd, who were assembling an elite team of warriors to conduct a rapid raid on the anchored treasure ships. In the meantime, the heir was mostly left to wander the new conquests in the Amberlands. During the heir’s travels, they became something of a celebrity amongst the people of Haraigizenhi Bulreki, finding in them a kindred love for drink and merriment. Along the way, the heir was stricken by a most unusual affliction. genuine care for the humans would someday be their subjects. That was when a thought crossed the ruler’s mind. Maybe, with just the right mix of grit and skill, that mutual respect could be leveraged not only to diffuse the Treasure Fleet standoff but also build a future for the Amberlands greater than simple autocratic occupation. That is, if such a future is even worth the effort.
