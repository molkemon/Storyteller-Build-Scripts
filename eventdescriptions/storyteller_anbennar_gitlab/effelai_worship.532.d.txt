We are faced with the greatest challenge ever presented before us since the dawn of our people. We set forth now to defy the different legendary beasts of the Tswohvvwoh’ii; we call them godbeasts for lack of a better name, though we know they are no gods. Intelligent creatures, these lifeforms are massive compared to any other lifeform in the jungle. They are also far more dangerous. The local godbeast here in the West of the Tswohvvwoh’ii lands is a ferocious boa constrictor capable of breaking the massive trees of this land—themselves harder than metal—with only moderate effort. It feasts on enough deathbloom toxin where a half day’s meal for it would poison our entire people. Because it also rubs itself on that noxious plant, both its bite and its skin are lethally toxic with a minimum of contact. If we can somehow slay it, it will mean untold glory for ourselves and our people.
