Perhaps never before has the gravity of creation sat so heavily within our hands. To imbue a Mechanim with a Kauga is to etch a soul into existence; it is a grave responsibility to breathe life into a child with one’s own sculpting. One wrong hammer blow, one errant slip, and the soul is altered irrevocably. And so, we have agonized over every chip and chisel, burning the midnight oil so that we may meet our children that much faster. We have made many first, second, and thirty-second drafts, trying to perfect their hearts. We have cursed our own clumsy hands. But now, at long last, they stir and take their first wobbly steps into the world.
