The rift opens, and I can see. Komina has wings. She is watching, fascinated, as the fisherman’s skin is slowly cloven from his flesh. It is horribly beautiful; the man unfurls like a flower, a gift just for Komina from among the greatest of her lovers. Does this Odanchés, regal even among harpies, realize that behind the veils Komina wears, her teeth are rotting? That there is no moment Komina is not in a little pain? Of course not. Komina is irresistible, and one will follow her into death even if she is reluctant to allow a kiss. Komina laughs; my vision splits across a dozen raids. In each raid, Komina demands of her paramour that this ship or another be destroyed, its people dispossessed. I see the gleam of ambition in the Odanchés’ eye; that gleam existed before Komina came. It always does. The hags never ask a person to do something they have not mused upon, however briefly.
