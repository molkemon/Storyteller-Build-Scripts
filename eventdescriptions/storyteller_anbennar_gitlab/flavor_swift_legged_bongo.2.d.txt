It was under the blackest night that Uwimbabazi met with representatives from Shalazar’s vassals. The Ingos of the tribes dared not meet with her directly, lest their defiance be discovered by their overlord. Shalazar had called its vassals to camp near the city for some campaign they intended to embark on, and Uwimbabazi had seized her chance. In the distance, Shalazar glowed with the light of an ancient civilization. So soft it looked, so enticing, that Uwimbabazi almost forgot what she was here to do. “Are you sure this plan will work?” one of the representatives said. “It is impressive magic, to glamour an army on this scale.” “You are not incorrect,” Uwimbabazi replies. “But I have done it before. It was most effective.” “But even if your army appears to belong to ours, will they not realize when we continue into the city?” he asked. Uwimbabazi shrugged. “Eventually, yes.”
