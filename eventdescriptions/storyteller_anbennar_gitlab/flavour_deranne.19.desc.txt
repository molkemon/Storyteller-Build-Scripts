The tavern called The Swaying Rose was quiet as the bard played the last somber tunes upon his lute. The tankards upon the tables were empty, the serving girls clearing the empty trays of food, and yet the patrons still watched the bard with attentive eyes, anticipating the words to come. “And thus ended this tale, but as many tales came before and many tales are still to come, there are always more to tell. So refill your cups and listen close, for you shall hear another.” As the entire tavern cheered, the bard began to tell.
