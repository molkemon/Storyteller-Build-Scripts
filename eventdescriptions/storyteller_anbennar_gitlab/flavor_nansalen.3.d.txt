Since the time of Ninšara I, Nanšaleni Matriarchs have been crowned as queens by the kings of Akalšes to reflect the close ties between our two realms and our ancient glory. In the past, such coronations frequently served to highlight little more than how far Nanšalen had fallen, for attendance was seldom impressive. Our kin in Akalšes would attend, of course, as would many of their courtiers, and so would the lesser matriarchs within Nanšalen, along with notables from the minorities in Vainviria. Other than that, though? The Firanyalen flock considered themselves to be above Nanšalen and so refused to send envoys, while the millennialong resentment towards Hytiranyalen and Lysinyalen meant that they had only been invited a few times throughout history, and had obviously always declined. Mišarallen had almost always sent at the very least an envoy, and sometimes the matriarch or her favored daughter, and during our time as subjects of the Ayara flock, one of the more diplomatically minded Ayaralleni matriarchs might send a few envoys, but even those seldom treated the coronation with anything but thinly veiled amusement. But now? As the monarch kneels, so that her counterpart in Akalšes may crown her once more, one would be hardpressed to find a realm in Bulwar that had not sent a delegation, with many of the major cities of Bulwar being represented many times over by their notable families, all wanting to see for themselves what this new harpy queendom will be like. And when the other ruler bids the monarch to rise, he no longer addresses her merely as Queen of the Harpy Hills, but as the Queen of Queens, destined to unite all harpies. Well, the Akalšesi and harpy attendants are the only ones to outright applaud such an announcement, but it is clear that the rest accept this new reality, and simply hope that this queendom will be true to its promises, and prove itself more civilized than the ones that had come before.
