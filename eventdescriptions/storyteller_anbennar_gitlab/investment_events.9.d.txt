The trade company has been granted a monopoly on trade in certain goods within its regional jurisdiction. Rogue traders are therefore a serious concern for the company, as unlicensed traders threaten to undermine the monopoly through competition. Such illicit dealings have become rife in the province, where rogue traders are engaging in free trade with merchants from the neighboring state. The company wishes to crack down on this trade by either reminding the neighboring traders that they are bound by treaty to respect the monopoly, or by prosecuting the rogue traders themselves, making an example of them in the capital.
