When the monarch arrives in Aakheta, they are treated to all the luxuries of the city. Sugars and incense from the Lower Sorrow, golden trinkets from a nearby realm, the finecut gems of another domain. the bounty of the Sorrow is presented, gifted freely to the ruler. After the great display of wealth has ceased, and the pageantry ends, the monarch is brought to Aakhet's throne room, where they are met with the visage of Aakhet in all his glory. Aakhet's bronze armor is perfectly polished, shimmering in the light; the bronze statue of his draconic form looms above his throne, providing a constant reminder of what he could always choose to be. "Welcome," he says, "to the Sorrow, fellow conquerer. How would you feel about a game?" As the monarch takes a seat, the human-shaped dragon continues. "I've appreciated watching your nation's exploits; I even thought about becoming one of your generals, for a time. But it just seemed too easy. You conquer too well. On the other hand, though, I can appreciate someone who knows a good challenge." "So," he continues. "I could do with a wager. You command great wealth in that Hoard of yours, and I," he laughs, "could always use more wealth. You, in the meantime, could use my favor. So what if we make a trade? You give me a quarter of your Hoard, and I'd be willing to share some of my secrets. Or, or," he says, with an excited grin. "Or, we play a game. If you win, I tell my tales for no cost. If I win, well, I'll still do it, because I like you. But I'll take half of your great Hoard in return. Do we have a deal?" Aakhet unfolds a great deck of ancient cards, and looks at the monarch expectantly. A choice must be made.
