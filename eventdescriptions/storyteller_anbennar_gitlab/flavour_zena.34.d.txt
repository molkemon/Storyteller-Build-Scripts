The ancient dwarves have fashioned many marvels of engineering, but to Azaly, none compare to the Holds. Dug deep into the mountains, these fortified metropolises took millennia to complete, requiring complex ventilation, water distribution, and waste removal systems to support populations in the millions. Each hold extends many levels down, like several cities stacked upon one another, with every deepening layer costing exponentially more to construct and maintain; a cost offset only by the veins of precious minerals found deep within the Serpentspine. There is little that impresses Azaly Stonecarver, a scholar of the Ananoma. Yet the Holds have always been of particular interest to him. Bivuzena’s horizontal limits have always challenged us to build up, but has anyone ever thought to build down? Visiting the owner of a hold, Azaly took extensive notes on structural integrity and discussed with the skilled engineers how they could adapt similar designs within the rootthick jungle soil of Horashesh. He returned with a gleam in his eye and a retinue of eager apprentices, ready to expand Bivuzena downwards and revolutionize the mines of the realm.
