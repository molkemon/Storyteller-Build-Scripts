The crowd for the execution is a bit thinner than usual. The counter makes a note of this; in his head, he has memorized every name and face in town. Those who didn’t appear, he will pass on to the inquisitors later. They are all suspicious now, each and every one. Today’s execution is not permitted speech, and thus a bag is tied over their head. Their hands and feet are bound to the pyre. The counter grabs the lit torch and squints at the crowd once more. Ridiculous. Only about half the village has come out for this witch burning; what a so-called community they were. He must speak of this the next time his congregation assembles. Preposterous. He halfheartedly recites the hymns, stuff about farewells and whatnot. He can’t really put his heart into it when he knows so few are listening. So he drops the torch onto the wood around the pyre. Flames shoot up, quickly engulfing the witch. Absolutely preposterous that the faithful refused to show their faces. So what, if the witch is but a child?
