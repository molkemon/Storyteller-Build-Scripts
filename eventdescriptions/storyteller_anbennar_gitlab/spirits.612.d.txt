Can't sleep, little one? A nod from the other cot signaled affirmation. The speaker sighed. "Same dream again? Light a candle, and I'll see if I can find some loquat." A shuffling of material signaled that the lump under the cot sheets had shifted. "I wish… I could just forget. Wipe the memories from my soul." "Mm. Give me fire?" The bed lump snapped its fingers, a bright blaze burning firmly atop its index finger. The other speaker, a tall Oni, lit a candle for a more permanent light. "Thank you." "Brother Shang, why use me as kindling?" The shuffling paused briefly as Brother Shang considered the question. "I am the second son of a second son, little one. I am born to no inheritance. None of my ancestors live in me. My chi is all my own." The Oni closed the cabinet as he retrieved a little glass jar from within its shelves. "Unlike you, I have no magic." The bed lump snapped its fingers again, and again the dazzling flame emerged. "You mean to say that my magic is my father." "You Devoured him, did you not? That is the principle. You took his soul, broke it apart, and digested it to your own ends. His chi is yours, his memories and his personality." The bed lump snarled. "I am NOT my father." The Oni sat nearby on the cot. With his mighty paws, he popped open the lid of the jar, and retrieved a small piece of preserved fruit from within. "I did not say you were. You may eat this loquat without becoming loquat yourself, after all." The bed lump took the fruit and chewed mournfully, as though it were reenacting a great sin. "But I think a part of you will spend the rest of their life in that attic." The thing on the bed reignited.
