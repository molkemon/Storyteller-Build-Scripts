The great hall of Gor Vazumbrog was silent as the monarch entered, their cloak sweeping the stone floor. Their armour, forged of titanium and steel, gleamed in the candlelit hall. The factions' representatives; Thrain Stonefist, General Magda Ironmarch, and Master Lorek Runehand; stood at attention, their earlier argument simmering beneath tense expressions. The monarch raised their hand. "Enough." Their voice carried the weight of the mountain. "We are one hold, one people. I will not see the legacy of Dauvek Veker splintered by pride." They turned to Thrain first. "Stonefist, your passion for exploration is undeniable. You and your scouts will lead expeditions into the depths, ensuring our tunnels are safe and mapped." To Magda, they said, "Ironmarch, your discipline and strength are the bedrock of our defenses. You'll command our warriors and engineers to fortify the hold and drive our enemies from the Dwarovar." Finally, they looked to Lorek. "Runehand, you and your scholars will preserve our past and shape our future. Study the relics we recover, rebuild the wisdom we've lost." The three representatives exchanged glances, reluctant but thoughtful. The monarch stepped forward. "Each of you carries a vital part of Dauvek Veker's legacy. Together, you will forge it anew. Remember the words." Thrain broke the silence first. "Titanium does not break." Magda nodded firmly. "Nor bend." Lorek smiled faintly. "Nor shatter."
