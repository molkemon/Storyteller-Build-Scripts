As life slowly returns to normal around the lake, the settlements recovering from that devastating impact, something else has begun happening. The water, now back to its springfresh coldness, has begun to glow ever so slightly in the night, an entrancing display of colors that are hard to point down. The display draws the locals in; the color burns itself into the back of their heads. When they leave, they dream of it and cannot stop themselves from returning. As time goes on, the glow becomes stronger, the color more vibrant and vigorous, like a being solely consisting of that everiridescent display. Within it, one could lose themselves, watching its intriguing images for hours, days on end. Whoever looks within it sees something, something which they cannot describe. Yet still, they return to marvel at its beauty, to bear witness just once more, just one more time tomorrow, to make sure it is still there. Just one more time.
