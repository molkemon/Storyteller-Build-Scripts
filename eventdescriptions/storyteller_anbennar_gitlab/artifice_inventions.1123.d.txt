The brine separator is used to distill potable water from all manner of liquids. It has the shape of a large water skin, but its inner workings are much more complicated than its appearance would suggest. It contains three compartments, with the topmost acting as the repository for the liquid to be processed. Once it is filled and the valve closed, the top chamber heats up, which evaporates the water contained within over time. The water mist is then gathered through collection tubes into the middle chamber, while the remaining brine can be discarded into the bottom chamber through a manually releasable valve. Here the liquid is heated even further, until all that remains is dried up contaminants and salts, which can then be scrapped or, depending on the source of the water, used to preserve food. The most befouled water source, seawater, the bodily fluids of an animal, or an enemy, with a brine separator, anything can be used to gather the potable water essential for every inhabitant of the desert.
