War rages across Escann as it has for years now. Until now, save the yawning absence of young men conscripted into the army from every village, the lives of civilians away from the frontlines have been passable, if a bit difficult. The monarch has decided today to take a stroll out of their residence. What they see is nothing short of a tragedy. All across the capital, civilians are starving in the streets, ragged walking skeletons with flesh on top. A family mourns the death of their child, who lies lifeless in front of their house. The prices in the market for bread are exorbitant, the petty merchants not much fatter than the commoners. The only people who aren’t starving are the nobles and the wealthy, though they hardly thrive. Even those who made their fortune off of dominating the trade of food and goods from across Escann to the cities and were incredibly wealthy can be seen closing down their shops. The ravages of war from Castonath to Escin have cut the production of grain to a fraction of what it was, and now we are left to fight for the scraps. There’s not much we can do to stop the famine, but we can implement price controls and compensate the merchants to help alleviate the suffering. Otherwise, we can let the commoners face the brunt of it in exchange for their wrath.
