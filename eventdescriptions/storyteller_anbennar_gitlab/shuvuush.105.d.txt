Ever since the days of legend, when the three brothers saved our people from ruin and the Shepherd of Souls taught Tsagaan the hidden ways to repel the Facestealers, our shamans have devoted their lives to the safety and wellbeing of our people. The blackmasked deathpriests spend their entire existence protecting our people from these depredations, while their whitemasked adjudicator brethren appease those spirits less inimical to mortal life. Yet this path is not the only one walked by those who have reason to fear the strangers who come wearing a friend’s face. The esoteric orders of the Rukunai, from the diligent Disciples of the Door, the volatile Brothers of the Sealed Gaze to the courageous Order of the Golden Mask and many others, have developed their own methods to deter the infestation plaguing their homeland from devouring all what is left of the Rukunai and Rünsukhi. Deep within the monastic fortresscities of the Rukunai, our most accomplished shamans have sat in seclusion with the abbots and masters of esoteric Khaaz mantras under the flickering light of yak butter lamps for months on end, attempting to find commonalities between our practices and thus uncovering deeper layers of meaning and power previously hidden from both sides. And at long last, they have succeeded. By coating a steel sword with juniper oil, painting puissant Khaaz mantras on it and setting it ablaze with conjured spirit fire, they have wrought a weapon that may not only deter but seriously harm or even slay a Facestealer. While it is by no means a technique easily reproduced without the required skills, it is a far more potent weapon than any we have had at our disposal until now.
