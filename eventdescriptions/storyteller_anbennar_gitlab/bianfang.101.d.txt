The people of the borderlands have always accepted the twin duties of military service and corvée labor as imperative to the security of the realm. Our subjects in recently conquered territories, however, do not take it so easily, and our efforts to break them in have led to much bucking and braying. Certain unorthodox administrators in the Vice Ministry of Revenue have suggested a new approach. By conscripting workers at a nominal wage and reducing the overhead costs of policing the populace and crushing revolts, we may in fact net a profit. They suggest experimenting with conscript labor in a certain area of the economy to see if the idea bears fruit.
