The chalk glows faintly in the low light, sigils curling like ivy. Avan stands barepaw in the middle of the circle, robes falling around his slim frame, white fur practically luminous in the dark. His eyes are closed, and his claws twitch with thought. Sedoric says, hobbling into the chamber, "Ikram never had the patience for glyphs." Grey dusts his beard, and there's a faint limp to his stride, but he's still sharp, still dangerous. Avan murmurs, lowering his hands and letting the glow fade, "He never bothered to try understanding them." Sedoric raises an eyebrow, taking in the young gnoll. He looks so much like his father. He says instead, "You're not like him at all." Avan agrees, bitter, "I'm more my mother's son." Sedoric watches for a moment longer. "Do you hate him?" "No," comes the response, surprised. "I'm just trying not to become him. I'm trying to become somebody else." "Someone new?" "Maybe. Someone useful." There's a long silence. Sedoric considers his apprentice. "One more lesson, Avan. Listen closely." The gnoll gives his mentor his full attention, the man uncharacteristically serious. "None of us are flawless. You are here to take risks. You're going to be swallowed, and broken, and betrayed, and hurt. Nobody can protect you from that, and neither will solitude." Avan's ears twitch. His eyes shimmer.
