It was a sunny afternoon in the town. Children were playing in the town square, playing tag or wrestling while their parents were tending to their crops and livestock. Mothers were talking about jewelry in the village’s general store while fathers were working on fixing a wagon that one of the wargs bit into out of boredom. A patrol of ork warriors wearing armor of human design but with Clouded Eye insignia entered the village. The warriors observed how no one was too worried about raids from roving bands of rogue orks or wild animal attacks. In fact, the situation was positively idyllic. One warrior was approached by a little ork girl holding a flower. She grinned as she looked up at him, her small tusks jutting out of her lips as she held out her flower for him. “This is for you, warrior, for being a hero and keeping my mommy and daddy safe,” she said. The warrior grabbed the flower and turned it over. It was a small gesture from an innocent child, but after a lifetime of war, the warrior cherished it. All his life, he’d been sent on campaigns against the humans at Rottenstep, the raiders at Dogwood, and the rebels in the hills around the capital. This was a sight he could get used to. As the sun set on the village, one could only hope that this would last a long time. And indeed, all across Clouded Eye, several villages were just as peaceful. The population would begin to soar as there were more young men and women who didn’t die in war and could now have big families with the food to back it up.
