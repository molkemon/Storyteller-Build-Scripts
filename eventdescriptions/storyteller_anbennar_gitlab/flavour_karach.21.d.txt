Zibara Farsight led her cows in from pasture on the same rope she had used for years. They wanted to see their friends, and Zibara was not one to deny her cows anything. Each cow had intricate swirls of paint detailed on its skin, with the care that came from repainting them each day. As they arrived at the place where the other farmers were, her cows began to fan out, and Zibara did not have any fear that they would go missing. they each had her starburst in green painted on their backs. The cows gathered with their friends, and each color congregated together. red cows with red, blue cows with blue, yellow cows with yellow. That was just the way things were. The farmers were walking around the ring with rich paints on their easels, touching up their cows’ paints and making new marks. Zibara went to her favorite milkcow first. She had always been a sunny, happy cow, and so Zibara named her Morning Glory. She had swirls of yellow painted between her horns, for there was no way she could be colored anything else. Zibara began to paint. She finished her work, and as she did Morning Glory mooed and butted her lightly on the head. Zibara laughed, and gave her cow’s ear a touch of red.
