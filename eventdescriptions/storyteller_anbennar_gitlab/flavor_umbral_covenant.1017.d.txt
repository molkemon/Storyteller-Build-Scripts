The rift opens, and I can see. It is hopeless. The shadowtouched's magic cannot possibly hope to overcome my Granny, whose very being is suffused in the gloom. A housecat against a lion, a worm against a serpent. Why does the shadowtouched struggle so, when the cause is so hopeless? She is no slacker; I watch her draw on a magic honed over decades, channel it outward with the exactness of a scribe. She resists. Her magic tastes of early morning and exhaustion, of stretching oneself thin, of a code rigidly adhered to even as those around her offer only suspicion. Her whole life, people have hated this woman for what she can do, and yet she has never abandoned her responsibility to them, her creed. She is a heroine. She is deeply decent. And none of this helps her a jot. As a child, my Auntie Komina always struck me as the hungriest of the Coven; her love of attention and adoration, her need for praise. But Granny in this moment is a yawning emptiness, a starvation. The shadowtouched’s magic simply drains away, and soon too does her life; but even in her last moments, she does not grant my Granny a meal of her fear.
