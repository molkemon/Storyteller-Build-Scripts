Improvements to Markhentakruan are coming from two main sources. The first of these concerns is the persistence of the corpseflame after detonation. I was inspired by the flaming munitions spell, a staple of war mages across the plains. The spell binds the fire to the munition, allowing fragments to fly through the air without interruption or diminishment of the flame. I have adapted the ignition component of Markhentakruan to use this framework, greatly improving the durability of the flames. The second breakthrough has come from one of my transmutation mages. He discovered a method to transmute the skin of a corpse into what he calls a voracious resin, a substance that has some truly fascinating properties. Upon ignition, the resin breaks down into a sticky, viscous liquid that burns with an intense flame. When this spell is cast as a precursor to Markhentakruan, the explosion scatters the burning resin indiscriminately, clinging to everything it touches and setting it ablaze. Attempts to scrape or shake it off only serve to spread the blaze further. Future tests will need to be conducted from further away. One of my mages has already suffered extensive burns across his flank after standing too close to the blast. The necessary transmutation is quite complex, but if we can integrate it into Markhentakruan the results should be immediate and devastating.
