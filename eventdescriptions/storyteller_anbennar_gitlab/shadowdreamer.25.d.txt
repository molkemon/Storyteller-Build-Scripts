The documents were sorted and catalogued neatly. Chemicals had been tested, labeled, and stored in stable containers. The lab was well on its way to being restored when a second door was uncovered. Excited scientists ordered the rubble cleared and the door pried open. What could lay beyond? Already this lab had yielded advancements in several important fields, so any further knowledge would be welcomed with open arms. Behind steel bars along both walls of a grid of walkways, there were pens with troughs for food and water. Many were empty, but equally many contained skeletons or desiccated remains. Some were determined to be goblins, a few were obviously ogres. A couple were unmistakably the corpses of elves. The deeper the scientists looked, the less recognizable and more mangled they were. Some had the wrong limbs or misshapen bones. Then there was another door, this one already off of its hinges. Within the dark room there were tall, glass cylinders filled with some opaque fluid. One by one, the team shone a light through each. “Empty. Empty. Some kind of embryo. A goblin. An elf’s head. An… that can’t be right, get some more light over here.”
