The rift opens, and I can feel. I am heavy, and I am open. I am eight million screams and one. There is sand here; I bathe in it, throw coarse scoops of it across my thighs, grind the grains into my flesh. Anything to focus on, any sensation. Anything else. I ricochet; four days pass. The dunes call to me, heavy tombs under which to bury my corporeal form, a pressure to shape me. Weight, heaviness; I need both. I need anything which is not the scream; I think I might be the scream. I am torn open, a cacophony. It is all spilling out, the scream is spilling out, and I have to scoop it back in. Days, days, days, days. I realize I am beside the ocean. I realize I am beautiful. I have screamed myself hoarse, screamed my teeth rotten; it is an ache which suffuses my jaw, my skull. But it is something other than the scream, and that is itself a relief. Why can I not stop looking across the water? Why can I not stop looking west? What am I looking for?
