The ruler thought they knew Tianlou's high temples, as well as anyone but the most learned of philosophers could. The ruler recalls first seeing the massive, rustcoloured edifices in the distance well before their armies encircled the city, admiring the way they loomed over all the city's mortal creations. The ruler has spent hours watching the otherwordly spiritlight play across their facades, lending Tianou's landmarks their attention even as the ruler pawstamped the unending tide of paperwork required to see Yanshen's greatest city brought properly into their rule. The ruler has even read of them, and brought the most learned of shamans and High Philosophy scholars to the capital to further their knowledge. So, as the ruler walks into the largest one, the ruler is as calm as a tiger waiting to pounce. It is not the first time the ruler has gone where others dare to tread, the ruler thinks, and it will not be the last. The ruler has broken fortresses centuries old, the ruler has blazed a path as a conqueror wreathed in flame, and now the ruler enters Haless's greatest buildings as its greatest title. But as the ruler descends the twisting staircase, tracing one paw along walls that seem to hum and whisper, a slow feeling begins to dawn. This is not a fortress, it is not a city, it is not like anything the ruler has ever known. And the ruler has never walked anywhere like this. But someone has. Once before, a harimari emerged from brutal conflict to forge a realm. Once before, that great conqueror came, implacable and unstoppable, to Tianlou. Once before, he entered these temples and penetrated their depths. And then Harimar the Great never returned. Or did he?
