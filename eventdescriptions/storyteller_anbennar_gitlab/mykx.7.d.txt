Despite what appears, the most challenging part of a raid is the division of spoils. who should receive what? How do you divide for a sailor who stayed on the ship to maintain the cannons and shoot at the enemy, an important task, from another one who went personally to burn and steal the settlement? Thankfully, that is the job of the administrators and officials inside the ships; all the crew accept their presence and the taxes they enforce, as they know they will do the separation properly, taking account of all the different variables. This can mean a less bounty at the end, sure, but it assures all parties involved in the raid that they received their fair share and that only by acting inside the republic can they be assured they will be paid fairly. Plus, if disagreements occur, they can take it to court and have their case heard. Of course, this all involves bribes and threats, better than before, which was killing the disaffected party at night. The new bounty division being established on the raiders' ship has brought a sense of stability and calm to the captains. They know they can be even more daring, as they know their crews aren’t a sea of hate and resentment ready to explode. Some are pushing for bigger and more distant raids; a new focus has been on the North, previously untouched by the ships. However, if they are to reach the wealth in the north, they need nearby safe havens from which their vessels can operate.
