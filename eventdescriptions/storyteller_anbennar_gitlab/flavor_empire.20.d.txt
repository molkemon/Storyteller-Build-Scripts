Generations ago, a new enemy was unleashed on Cannor. Once the subject of Dwarven legends, encountered only by those few fools who dared to tread upon their claims under the Serpentspine, orks were a distant myth. Then came Korgus Dookanson, at the head of the Greentide, and the orks were a terrible reality. The campfire tales and children's stories warning of their savagery hardly did their multitudes justice. Many of our youth went to fight against the tide, but the Empire made no official effort to intervene in the east, preparing to defend ourselves if the Marcher Lords should fail. The Greentide was halted, and the orks, or so it seemed, were contained. Even now the monarch maintained a contingency plan should orks ever break the border of the Empire, battle tactics and fallback lines, but today, they have been rendered pointless. A band of orks stand before the Imperial Throne, their hands unbound, their heads held high, their weapons left at the door. "Fealty...?" The monarch struggles to contain their surprise. "If you'd have us." The lead ork isn't quite fluent in the common tongue, but they speak clearly enough. "WarChief Corin came from the west, and we're loyal to her now, so we come west to serve a worthy leader. We's good fighters, you could use a few good fighters. Or your little chief princes could..."
