Theodore put a hand to his temple as he poured another glass of wine. He complained, as he took a swig, that everything that could have gone wrong had. He felt like they were not even making a dent. The Errant Prince had been in charge of the shadow war against Mihaela for twenty years. He had tried every trick in the book, spent enough money on the project to bankrupt the Gnomish Hierarchy twice over, but Mihaela’s forces had thrown every plot and scheme back. The other Errant Prince in the room, sitting on the other side of the table, took a sip from his own glass. He said it had to be expected. The resources of half of Aelantier, her new clan, were vast. Renaud sighed and said, "I have to say... Mihaela has not broken the Masquerade. As far as we know, she hasn’t told a soul that other Errant Princes even exist. Maybe we should just... give up?" Theodore scowled, angry at both his friend’s suggestion and how much he agreed with it. "The Blood Court is not happy, Renaud. I just..." Theodore shook his head, slumped back in his chair. "Gods... how could this have happened?" Renaud shrugged. "Mihaela is a predictable maverick. She has been making the same mistakes her entire life. Remember the farmhouse at Gerbuta? When she..." "You think I don’t remember? She felt so sick she didn’t sleep for months." She had cried when she realized what she had done, the only time Theodore had seen her do so. "And yet? She learned nothing." Renaud poured himself a glass. "And next you’ll ask. 'what about her grief?' Well... she hasn’t changed in six hundred years. Why now?" Renaud sighed as he took out his notebook. "How about Orenkoraim again? There are some Sarhaly mercenaries I can have sent over." Theodore nodded through the plan, though he knew how it would turn out. The Errant Prince downed his glass and reached for the bottle again.
