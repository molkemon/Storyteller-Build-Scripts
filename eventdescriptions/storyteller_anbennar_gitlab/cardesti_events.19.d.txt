A tale I tell you now this night, of lovers bereft, their love denied; a fiery passion, blazing hot, afflicted by tragedy nobility wrought. One of them could not stay, not of their own will, for if one more day they would remain then cursed they be, by that which afflicts all Cardesti. The other a noble, of the local culture make, whose heart the Cardesti youth did take, and whom took of the youth their heart in turn, yet if to follow, by noble family spurned. Their things in their trunk the noble packed, and prepared to leave upon horseback, yet told if this journey they were to undertake, their name and titles, they must forsake. But upon the eve the Cardesti were to depart, the noble appealed to their ruler's heart, and when presented with a love so true, what else is there for a ruler to do? And so upon the light of dawn, the lovers rode out, no more forlorn, a warm embrace, a hand in a hand, with shared noble name, true heirs to their land.
