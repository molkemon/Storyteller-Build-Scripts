The Council's tactic to regain the people's trust was the same one used by the Gnolls to reconquer the desert. First, they made Wind Barriers; no more violence was allowed in Council Fights. Then they planted the first crops; all Council meetings were open to the public, and the records were made available. Then they fed those crops and kept growing bigger and more resistant trees; open assemblies were organized where the people could partake and talk about their local problems and questions to the local Council members. It was a slow and painstaking project, but if it were possible to conquer the desert, it would likely regain the people's trust.
