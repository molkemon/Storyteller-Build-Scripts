Rays of orange light filtered in from the eastern windows. They danced precisely with the movement of the curtains, which in turn fluttered in accordance with the breeze. The monarch was still asleep as the light began to creep towards their bed. As if on cue, the moment the light reached the monarch’s face, a knock came upon the door, and the scholar cracked it open while facing away from the room. He apologized, "Ah, I did not realize you were still asleep. However, it is the mathematically perfect time for another of my lessons." The monarch groaned, but gathered themself and donned a robe before bidding the scholar entrance. He walked into the room and motioned towards the silk curtains at the eastern end which separated the bedroom from the outdoor space. Moving the curtains aside, both walked out onto the circular balcony and looked upon the capital city. The scholar began to lecture, "Below you are the people of your city. They move in accordance with their place, do they not? The bread maker brings his goods to the markets just as they open. The fires of the forge lit by the blacksmith as the sun first touches his brow. Even the traders move to and fro with the clockwork of the world. All in their place. All at their greatest potential. Even the city itself reflects the precise geometry of the High Temples through the route of its streets, and the angles of its structures." The scholar paused. "It is this that the temples speak most highly of. Order. To perpetuate it is the purest form of Kartya." There was a longer pause as the two of them watched the city in motion. Finally, the scholar asked, "What do you believe your place is in the mechanism of the world?"
