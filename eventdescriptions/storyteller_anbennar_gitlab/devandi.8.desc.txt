For weeks the monarch had been in secret discussion with their closest advisors. To declare oneself a Zhutashsar, a title awarded posthumously to the most prestigious thinkers, was practically unheard of. The risks were enormous. the monarch would certainly face much scrutiny and skepticism from the factions within their nation, where it would be seen as almost heretical. But the reward was equally as potent, should their declaration find solid ground. widespread acceptance of whatever theory they proposed, and the associated prestige and authority which came with the title of Zhutashsar. With their advisors uncertain, the decision lay at the will of the monarch.
