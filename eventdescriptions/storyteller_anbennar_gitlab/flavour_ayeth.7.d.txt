One shot was enough. I let it loose in silence, while the battle raged at my side. No cry of vengeance, no roar of fury, just the soft pull of string, the whisper of release. The arrow cut the wind cleanly. I watched it spin, watched the fletching vanish into the dusk; by the time a single breath had passed, it had struck. Right beneath the collarbone; where flesh thins and bone cradles the heart; a perfect shot. He staggered, put one foot forward, then another, then none. His eyes widened. He looked down as if trying to understand what had happened, as if he still held the power to undo it and command his body to hold on, but it was already too late for commands. He will try, surely; he will clutch the shaft, break it in half and gasp with his mouth full of blood, spitting curses I will not hear. He will plead to the leader, perhaps, and then he will crawl to drag his bones towards his army that will carry him to their war camp. Maybe he will try to escape. But there is no escape. Only the embrace of death awaits him now. It will not be a clean death, it will not be swift. I must make haste to the warband – I must be the one to deliver the final blow.
