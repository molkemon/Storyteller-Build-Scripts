The monarch was travelling through the town marketplace, the stalls arranged on their left, and a babbling river on their right. At a crossroads a little ahead the monarch sees a priest from Kunolo speaking to the crowd, standing on a small but sturdy box with their back to the stream. The priest’s voice flutters like a bird, dipping into a deep contralto as often as it rises to a high but clear falsetto. Every time they turn to face a different person, the light dances on the planes of their face, and their shoulderlength hair shifts and falls differently. Some moments they look like one of the grizzled veterans the monarch remembers from their youth. Other moments, they remind the monarch of the ladies-in-waiting that attend them. “Change is part of life, and all things change. Children grow into adults, the sun, moon, and stars dance overhead, and the constellations shift. Do not fear change, but welcome it, and you will discover those parts of yourself you have yet to meet, and see the world around you with fresh eyes. Be kind and encourage those around you, for only then one can observe the things not easily seen. For it is this which allowed the King with the golden goose to help the Princess who did not laugh become the Queen of Joy.” They notice the monarch and call out, “Greetings, traveler, what sights have you seen that inspired you? What will you change upon returning home? What things do you plan to keep? Do you still feel comfortable in your skin, or is it time for a change?”
