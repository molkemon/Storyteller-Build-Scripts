The longhouse was decorated with a variety of scrimshaws. Many are merely finely carved pieces of bones, made by novice scrimshanders, depicting great battles and warriors, yet some stand out as a piece created with full skeletons, each joint intricately connected through carvings and paint to depict a grand story of fine art, with each crack, each bruise given new purpose in the grand canvas. To many, this would appear to be some horrific trophy gallery to assert dominance and inspire fear. Yet in actuality, the orks of Bonecarver have shrined this grand building a temple to commemorate the dead, whether they be humans or orks. For all warriors deserve commemoration, where their vessels are given new artful purposes and release the soul within to reincarnate. Atop a small podium surrounded by finely decorated bones and skulls, a young ork chief calls out. “Warriors of Dookan, the time for mourning shall end soon. Tonight, we shall feast with the souls of our kin and ancestors. Tomorrow, we shall march to Rottenstep to liberate the rest of our brethren trapped within its mires and break the fangs of the Venomtooth so they can never spit their venomous lies again. We alone shall carry the burning embers of the Dookanson, Dookan Dul.”
