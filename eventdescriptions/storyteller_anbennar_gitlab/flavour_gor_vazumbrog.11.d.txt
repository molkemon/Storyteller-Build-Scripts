The chamber buzzed with energy as Lorek Runehand tapped his runeinscribed staff against the stone floor. Around the table sat scholars with inkstained fingers, engineers clutching blueprints, and runesmiths with fragments of crystalline components laid out before them. Lorek began, a broad grin spreading beneath his meticulously braided beard, saying that for the first time in centuries, they had the resources and unity to dream big. The golems of old were not merely tools; they were the very lifeblood of dwarven progress. He urged them to reclaim that glory. An engineer unrolled a schematic, revealing a massive foundry complex with interconnected assembly lines. She said that the key was scalability, and that if they cast and forge bodies in standard sizes, they could reduce costs without compromising durability. Lorek mused that this was efficient, but they needed data crystals—more than they could currently produce. Without them, their golems would be nothing but hollow hulks. A runesmith leaned forward, suggesting they could focus on refining runic drives. If they simplified codified instructions, the initial models could be task-specific, such as miners, guards, or even builders. A scholar then interjected about the power source, noting that dame tear cores were potent but volatile, and that binding an elemental might offer more stability. Lorek countered, asking if it would respond predictably, and imagining a rebellious elemental in a titanium body. Laughter rippled through the room. They were far from agreement, but the air was thick with excitement. For the first time since AulDwarov, the dream of large-scale golemancy felt tangible.
