Zux Scalesinger. This name has appeared thrice now in the daily reports from our lead engineer. The first time was when a new type of explosive he created, meant to aid our scouts in tracking fleeing soldiers by applying a noticeable odor onto them, forced our engineers to evacuate both their laboratories and the contents of their stomachs. The second was when an innovative pressure-weighted rolling spike trap, meant to only capture the enemy without permanently injuring them, proved instead to be moderately effective at catching fish when fitted with a net. And now, his name comes up once more. This time, however, it appears that his invention may be of some substance. He claims to have discovered a way to create a glue that flows like water until it comes into contact with fabric, at which point it quickly sets and hardens, rendering its target slowed even in small amounts, completely immobile in larger. He has shown promise before and failed to deliver, but maybe he can follow through?
