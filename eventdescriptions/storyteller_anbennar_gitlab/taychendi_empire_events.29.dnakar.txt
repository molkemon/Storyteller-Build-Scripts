The three cousins stared at the tomb in silence. They had spared no expense on the construction of their ancestor’s resting place, a mausoleum of richly painted marble and lapis lazuli stone. A great statue of Liandarha had been built behind the sarcophagus, cast in gold and Precursor steel, her famous bright orange hair replicated in fire. Vatasheran KriadvaiMudaliar placed his hand on the stone. “Do you think she would be proud of us?” “Why wouldn’t she be?” Galichana, the Queen of Kilaiek, answered incredulously. “She ruled Nanru Nakar – we rule half of Aelantier. If she saw us now, she’d smile.” “Would she?” both cousins looked to the ruler as she spoke. “Liandarha was an elf of Tacenie, but the warlords of our Empire look to the SlaverNobles she despised. Liandarha won this city off a slave revolt, while this Empire secured itself by destroying one. Do you think she’d like the bloodshed, the constant warfare?” “She was a warrior.” Kilaiek replied. “Of course she would.” “How could we know?” Kriadvai responded. “Does it matter?” the monarch asked. The other two cousins looked askance at him. “It’s been over a millennium since she lived – and in that time, this city, this family… we’ve grown. Evolved. The way we rule those below, sitting up on the high chair, above the warlords – that was not Liandarha’s vision, that was Varanash’s. The way we rule ourselves, split into three and then rebound – that was not Liandarha’s vision, it was the vision of Triandar and Elechana and Pelozhil.” the monarch looked up at the statue. “Liandarha was born amidst the skies, tethered to the light and order of Dhivumanhu… but she died in darkness, in a world of glorious chaos. That is the world in which we live and over which we reign… and it is through glory, and through chaos, that we shall end this cycle, and return the glory of Liandarha’s age to Aelantier. We will rise, an Empire Vainglorious…”
