The nation of Naléni is a mess. A crude amalgamation of a pirate republic and a traditional harpy matriarchy, factional politics divide the realm and jam any sort of consensus, making exercising central authority extremely difficult. Power is split between the Matriarchy itself, led by the Nephiuéda dynasty, and the various independent Odanchésta, pirate captains in common, themselves arranged in a loose and often fractious coalition united only in resisting the authority of the Matriarchy. Peace in the realm is maintained by a historic compromise known as the Muréda Accord, so named for the Matriarch who negotiated its terms. Under it, the Matriarch retains formal authority, but a co-ruler is elected from amongst the ranks of the Odanchésta every four years to serve as Ídar Odanchés, or Dread Captain, a quasi-military ruler who shares power with the Matriarch. These elections are less voting and more candidates scrambling for the most muscle to support them. The accord has served Naléni well until now due to the lack of outside threats, given its isolation on the edge of the Great Horn, but it has begun to fray with the arrival of the Raheni colonizers. The Matriarchy stirs as it sees its realm under threat, whilst the Odanchésta enjoy the bounty of plundering Raheni trade; the two groups' combined interests are dwindling. The current Matriarch's daughter, Bimogire, has just been elected Ídar Odanchés, providing a rare moment of semi-unity. Despite this, it will take a careful hand to steer the ship of state. bolstering the Matriarchy's authority whilst balancing the interests of the Odanchésta.
