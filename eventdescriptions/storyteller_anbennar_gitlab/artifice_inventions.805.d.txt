As soon as a dwarf is taught to dig, they are taught to give themselves a way back up and out of their hole. Ladders and carved staircases are common, but we have far outdone their use in our Holds' ever-continuing expansion. It has gotten to the point that it might take weeks, or even months, to ascend from a hold's depths up to its surface. New elevators, however, should allow us to transport goods and people from top to bottom in mere minutes, speeding up production and administration considerably.
