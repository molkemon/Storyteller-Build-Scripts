Victory. True and glorious victory once more. Khagor closed his eyes, listening to his celebrating orks cheer, before drinking deeply from his goblet of plundered elven wine. It was weak, much like its makers, but knowing it was part of the spoils of war made it worth downing. Both Durzk’ungul and the elf witch were dead, their clans scattered or enslaved. The chieftain wiped his mouth and opened his eyes. When he saw nothing but white mists, he dropped the goblet. Khagor never heard its clatter. Gone was the ambient noise of his victorious clan. It was just like the first time the mists had taken him. He was alone, blinded and deafened by this suffocating white blanket. Then, he felt something. Something more. A voice whispered, "Mighty Khagor, thou hast done well vanquishing thine foes. Truly thine strength is befit of thy creator’s vision." The words echoed in Khagor’s head, consuming his thoughts. A daze washed over him. "Consider, mighty warrior, that a chain binds you still. That thine kin and self remain shackled, denied thine true potential. Yet, thou mayst still be freed." Khagor opened his mouth, but no words came. The world began to spin. "Behold thy hand." Despite his fading vision, he felt compelled to look. There in his hand was his goblet once more. "A gift. A taste of what thou couldst possess; unshackled might. Thy and thine kin needst only drink." The chieftain blacked out. When he came to, he found himself standing once more amongst his cheering clan. They acted oblivious to his disappearance. For a moment, the ork considered blaming his drunkenness. That was until he saw a golden liquid in his goblet. He stared.
