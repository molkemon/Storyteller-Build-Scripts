I took another large gulp of beer. The oni across the table followed suit, though his mug was much larger. Sensing that he was loosened up enough for a good discussion, I asked, "Don't a lot of adventurers pass through here on their way to kill oni? How are you able to sell to them?" He responded, "Come now, girl, you must be able to tell it's all about perception. If someone can look at me and think I'm the only good one out of all the oni, well," he laughed, then spoke to me more quietly, "then you'll sucker yourself into buying any trinket I can offer." "But are you really the only good one?" I asked. To this, he laughed again. "We Kabiurgarko only want to make money and live to keep it. If we can talk ourselves into thinking that our only way to live is by gaining power any way we can, then that's just what we'll do. So, to answer your question, there's a lot less bad ones than you think." Nodding at his words, I down the last of my drink. So does he. I ask him one last question, just to sate my curiosity. "Why are you telling me all this? Shouldn't it be a trade secret?" "Girl, it might just be because you've about outdrunk an oni, but I'll tell you. It's because you paid for the beers."
