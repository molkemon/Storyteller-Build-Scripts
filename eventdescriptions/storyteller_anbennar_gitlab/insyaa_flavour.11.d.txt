It is rare for the ruler to encounter other packs of awakened mechanim, but not unheard of. Sometimes you will exchange knowledge and warnings; sometimes you will pass with a silent nod or glare. Sometimes you will envy them, for their arms are bright strong steel and their eyes manifold and keen; sometimes you will pity them, for they have lost much of their forms, and limp along seemingly one tooloud Holohana roar from collapse. Sometimes, as with your most recent encounter, they will join you, and walk with you a little. They were a quiet group, but not unfriendly. Many lacked a head, a slight oddity, but not in any way alarming. Not particularly expressive, in any of the myriad ways they could be, yet still easy to work with. You wondered if some strange predator or poison had damaged their communication modules. One was odder than the rest. A figure with four eyes, only two would ever be lit up at a time, when they would shine like a torch ablaze. His limbs were polished steel, but designed to be flexible and adaptive; amidst fungus and acid, he could stride untouched. You thought he might be blind. With his vertical pair of eyes lit up, he would touch your face and limbs like he was groping in the dark, struggling to make sense of you. Then one day, he spoke. He talked of this world, when the Makers made it, how bright and shining it was. He talked of the suffering you faced, the unending trials you had encountered in your journeys so far. His words left you silent, and in that void, his quiet words rang loud. “I have seen the path, and I can free us from that suffering.” If only you would let him travel with you, for a time, and teach you, for a time.
