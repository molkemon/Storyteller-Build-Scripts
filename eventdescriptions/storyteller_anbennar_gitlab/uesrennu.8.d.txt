The King of the Kompu fell to his knees at the sight, screamed bloody curses of loss and vengeance, as his brethren circled around, snuggled close, consoled him in his grief. One of the queens had been stolen – it was not the first to disappear. Once the madness had subsided, he turned to the driver that had been on watch. “How could you have let this happen?” “I– we kept the lash close, watched the trainers– but when the shifts changed, they–” The monarch rose, walked over. The driver sputtered, stammered. “And– they were gone before we could do anything. The queen, the trainers– her– her cubs–.” “WRETCH.” The King struck the driver across the face– kicked him away, sent him tumbling to the floor. Then a raged order in their chitterchatter tongue – “KITAKITA– KAAAAGH.” Blood curdling screams accompanied the driver’s death throes as the Kotakompu pounced – devoured him alive. The monarch turned to his lieutenants now, grabbed one, shook him as he cried his commands. “Death– death– DEATH–. Go into the mines– sabres out, lashes out, komputeeth– DEVOUR.” A look of confusion on the lieutenant’s face – the Aryakompu struck him, sent the others running. “GO. The tunnels run RED today– a blood price– for my queens, BLOOD–.”
