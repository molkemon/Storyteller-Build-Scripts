Months have passed, but the conclave still drones on, with only minor breaks. The end would not be in sight for another year or so. There was excitement still, but the task was sincerely underestimated. It becomes rather difficult to codify all the various and partially incompatible myths of each sect into one coherent understanding of the faith. Foresight, it seems, can only help to a certain degree. But it certainly did help. The orators have become more refined as time went on, and the conclave’s conclusion might run against the wishes of the Star Riders if we had not planned for this. It would not have been bad, of course. Just unideal. We respect the wishes of the conclave, no matter where it would have gone. But we decide where it goes, for now. The nature of how we celebrate our faith, now and forever.
