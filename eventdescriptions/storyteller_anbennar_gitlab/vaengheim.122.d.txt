And a skald who was named Bunorr Bungusson did join the hunt, and he did win much acclaim for his skilled tongue. Yet many skalds claimed that Bunorr spoke lies and half-truths, and that his songs were not those of old Gerudia. And Bunorr said, “Drab and dry be tales of old Gerudia. I choose to spin a grander tale, and Vængheim’s glory will be the greater for it.” Then said Snorri Gullmörksmann, who was versed in songs of great power, “This man is a bumbling fool, o queen. The day has been long, let us rest, and put his twisted tongue to test, so that we can sing our songs together again.” The monarch held their hand to their brow, and spoke thusly.
