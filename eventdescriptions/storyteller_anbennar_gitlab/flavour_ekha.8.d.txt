Today the king presents himself again to the Silver Council. Many years have passed since he last sat among the councilors of the Republic, and the boy now king comes not with a plea, but a demand. The palace of AurKesAkasik, by right the domain of his kingdom, is as of now being administered by the honorable Deshaki king. He would ask before the council that his claims to the palace be recognized and his lawful domain restored. His words are swift and cutting. All know what a rejection of the prince would mean; the alternative is not too enticing, either, as the Deshaki have only recently come into the fold and drawing their anger may be unwise. Alternatively, they may seize the palace for themselves as the symbol of legitimacy it is, but this is likely to anger both parties.
