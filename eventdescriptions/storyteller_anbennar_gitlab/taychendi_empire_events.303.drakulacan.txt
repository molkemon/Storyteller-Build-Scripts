Moonlight illuminated the throne room as the ruler sank into the seat. Such a magnificent tower – she had never understood why the people hated it so. Perhaps she and the advisor could look out from the height later, watch the city below as thanks for that incredible display of power. Her full power, on full display – in the shadows no longer. The pride surged, as she grinned – she took in the tens of thousands before her, imagined the millions outside. She was delighted to find so many of the great houses and families of the realm she had read about in attendance. She spotted a familiar face in the crowd, too – the Pale Queen gave a bespectacled fellow prince, her peer, a nod. The Blood Court would throw everything they had at her – but it would be different this time. She was not hiding. She was not holding back. And neither would her family. The advisor advanced towards her, crown in hand, flanked by the highest surviving ranking members of the domain. They were smart, cunning; they knew how to scheme and plot like no other. The advisor, the new Patriarch of the domain, was the best second in command she could ask for – especially because he so gladly took on all the boring bits of being a Queen. She bowed her head as he placed the crown atop it – and as he retired to stand by her side, she rose an Empress. Raucous applause. Cheering. Chanting. For a moment, elation – then realization, as she took in the crowd. Her son wanted this. A title. Autonomy. To lord above all others… above his kin, above his elders. Above the Queen. She had argued with him, tried to talk sense into him, then abandoned him to the wolves when they drew near. And now… achieving what she had denied him, it all made so much sense. She closed her eyes – and when she opened them, cast the regret back into shadow. The guilt remained… but now, it meant nothing. Umbral King or Pale Queen, it didn’t matter. She held an empire at her command.
