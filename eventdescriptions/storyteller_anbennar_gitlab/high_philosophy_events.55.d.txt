The central teachings of High Philosophy are primarily concerned with ascertaining and pursuing the proper aim of mortal existence, or Kartya. While there is broad disagreement on the Kartya that must be followed to achieve Purtara, the paradise of spiritual union with the High Gods, beliefs common across most sects include honesty, moderation, patience, nonviolence, and compassion. The definitions of these ideals are among the most fiercely debated topics by High Philosophy theologians.
