In her hands the Vile One grasps a small tetrahedron, a Genie relic of a type. This and others have been found in the ruins of ancient archives, plundered by her agents; some open to conventional magic, others simply to curious touch. But this one is strange in two ways. First, it is too new. Second, it has remained stubbornly closed, and thus it fascinates her. Any magic does, if it is sufficiently alien to the craft of the hags. The relic is redolent with the Wish, the essence of Genie magic. It was not merely hidden, but protected. The Vile One can taste the layers of intent, of warding, of secrecy. "Cheni's way will not work on that thing." The Vile One looks up, blinking. The voice is the Diviner's, her matted hair loosely piled atop her head. It is never quite clear whether or not she has been sleeping. She kisses her teeth. "He is good at building things, at civilization. That thing is something else. It is pure will, one on one, this magic. This magic is about... about saying the world is something, and then pop." She opens her clenched fist. "Things are as you say, like how your Granny does it." The Diviner pads across the floor, her bare feet thick with dirt on the sole. "I can hear you trying to open it all together... lattice, lattice, choir, choir." She is mocking, though not cruelly. "No. You have to command it." The Vile One tries. The Diviner cocks her head in disbelief. "Harder than that, kitame."
