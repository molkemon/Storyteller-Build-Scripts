The problem with having an advanced society is that there are so many things to do. Goods need to be produced and sent from place to place, buildings need to be built, letters need to be written; it’s enough to make a bureaucrat give up and go back into the Insyaan wilds. Of course, we are a people who need not accept things as they stand. If it’s too hard to get everything done that needs doing, why not program ourselves differently? Such was the thought process behind the Administrative Oversight Module, the newest marvel of technology produced by the artificers of the nation. Via a small and barely visible welded attachment to the back of the head, we can stream in real time the things that need to get done to the idle people of the nation, and give them a small sense of satisfaction once they do it just enough to get them excited to do more things. Truly, this is the greatest invention in our history; we’ve defeated not just the problem of large-scale social coordination, but the problem of boredom as well.
