The chasm had offered us two paths a few days ago, one down and one keeping level. After some deliberation, the expedition chose to go onward on their current level. This has yielded tentative agreement from the explorers as they did not want to go down much further for now. The path turned out to be interesting enough in its own right. It snakes along the walls of the chasm and at some point, we could make out a similar path on the other side. Some were discussing how we might get over there, but even before they could come to a conclusion, the scouts reported they had sighted a rope bridge just ahead. The rackety structure is made of thick rope and sturdy wood, but it does show signs of wear and tear. The question it poses is simple. Do we need to secure it? Or will it hold the weight of our expedition on its own?
