The fabrication of automatons is no easy endeavor. The fever of industry demands sacrifice and diligence. Everything from raw ore to kitchen spoons found their way into the smelters, and the roaring clang of hammers resounded against the delicate tinks of finesmiths until, at last, the fruits of the labor grew ripe. From the factories sputter forth the first experimental frames, children of molten scrap and salvage, with tonghands and gutterbrows. The first cogs of an indefatigable industrial machine, but far from the last.
