A thick smog of incense filled the room where the Court of Change resided. At its centre, laid out on an array of cushions, resided Akhaxpa. As the ruler approached her and knelt down, she opened her three eyes, filling the room with a blinding blue light. “If you are to bring change to your people, you must change what your people use. Too much reliance on the same materials from the same places will lead to death once that moves. Do not be like the mouse who dies when its cheese is moved. Be like the mouse who moves to where the cheese is.”
