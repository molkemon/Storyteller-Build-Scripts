The new settlement was thriving, its halls carved swiftly and seamlessly with the Earthshaper Sphere. Overseer Morzad gripped the artifact, brimming with ambition. The Sphere pulsed in his hands, warm and eager, as the ground beneath his feet rippled and obeyed his will. Columns of flawless basalt rose in moments; veins of silver and copper appeared as if summoned by a divine hand. The watching dwarves cheered, and Morzad felt a surge of pride—a dangerous elation. The Sphere responded, amplifying his visions. Walls soared higher, tunnels branched further, and chambers grew vast and complex. But the Sphere's hum deepened, resonating discordantly. The ground began to tremble as Morzad's confidence faltered. Shadows flickered in the lantern light, and whispers seemed to echo from the stone itself. A cold wave of fear struck him. "Stop," Morzad gasped, his voice shaking. His shame and panic poured into the Sphere like poison. The ground roared in protest, solid stone turning to a fluid, shifting mire. The cheers became screams as tunnels collapsed, homes sank, and dwarves were swallowed by the churning stone. Minutes stretched into an eternity before the chaos ceased. The stone settled, leaving behind silent, hollowed-out halls, eerily pristine as if the tragedy had never occurred. No sign of life remained—only empty homes and tunnels carved in perfect symmetry, mocking their loss. The Sphere lay silent amid the ruins, its glow dim, as the echoes of the drowned settlement lingered in the still air.
