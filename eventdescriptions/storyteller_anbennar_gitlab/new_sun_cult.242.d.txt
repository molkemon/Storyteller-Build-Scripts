The wisdom and might of the monarch are known throughout the realm, but even they cannot make let alone enforce such a monumental choice as what the future of magic in the country should look like. The amassed wisdom of the magi clans, the pious rectitude of the namensar priesthood, and the pervasive influence of the barkabti nobles will all be essential in determining and enforcing a new set of laws governing the art of magic within the realm. Therefore, the arkimaguš of each magi clan found within the borders, as well as representatives of the high kašra of the temples and the most esteemed barkabti houses, have been called forth to gather in a grand council to be held at the capital. They shall debate the merits and threats posed by any potential change of the status quo and advise the most gracious monarch on the outcome of their deliberations. This announcement has already alarmed some more conservative elements amongst the capital’s priesthood, who have expressed their profound displeasure with any attempt to loosen the strictures placed upon the human magi in what they call has been a just punishment for their duplicitous nature.
