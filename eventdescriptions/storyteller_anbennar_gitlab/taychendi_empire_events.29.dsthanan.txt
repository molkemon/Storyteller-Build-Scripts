The monarch stared emptily at the winecup on their desk. They sighed, then downed its contents. The entire day they had been locked inside their study in the Violet Library, pouring over old histories, thinking and thinking. The monarch had read of the old empires of Taychend, of the first Dumrusvarn, and the more they read, the deeper the pit in their stomach grew. Eletam, the golden warrior, Elajith, the violet scholar... surely they could not have foreseen that this would have happened? It was as if all their wishes had been granted, but in the worst possible way. More tales of glory, of all kinds, were published in the Violet Library by the year, but their words were written in blood. The Goldswords brought order to Taychend, but they did so by sending off the warlords to ravage distant lands. Magnificent cities were built, but they were paid for with the looted treasures of a hundred civilizations. The monarch could see where this was leading. One day, the borders of the Dumrusvarn would encompass the world, with half of its realm a blasted waste. Taychend would be a glorious beacon of civilization, built atop the corpses of hundreds of millions. And then the warlords, their desire for battle and conquest insatiable, would turn on him. The monarch shook their head, stood, walked over to the nearby balcony so they could look once more over their city. The monarch could do nothing to stop it; they knew that. But the cycle needed to turn from one of chaos to one of order. If the warlords believed glory is violence, so be it; the Dumruktams had figured out it was so much more a long, long time ago. As the monarch looked out over the city, they could see the bright lights of learning, and culture, and industry. One day, they would be the Goldswords’ saving grace against the blooded storm—Taychend will rise, an Empire Vainglorious.
