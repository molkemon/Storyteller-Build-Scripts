The summit trail was rough and steep. Loose stones littered the footworn path up the rock face, often sharp and jagged in a way that cut even through the toughest footwear. The ruler grunted, grasping a makeshift staff, and pulled himself up above another boulder. What had possessed him to make this journey? It was not for the same reason so many other pilgrims had dragged themselves to the oracle of Tughayasa; the ruler put no stock in Raheni divination, formidable as their magics may be. Seble had always forged her own destiny, and in her name the ruler would do the same. It was the sky itself he sought, atop a mountain so high it dwarfed anything he might have found in Madriamilak. Throughout the campaign in Dhujat, he had nursed a doubt, a gnawing unease in the pit of his stomach. Could Rahen really be the holy realm, as much as Madriamilak was? He stumbled as he rose above the crest of the next incline, clutching his staff to maintain balance in face of the sudden drop before him. It had arrived already; the peak. Nothing else rose above him, nothing else but the sky. The ruler stretched out his hand towards it. "Amilak, lord. Are you here?"
