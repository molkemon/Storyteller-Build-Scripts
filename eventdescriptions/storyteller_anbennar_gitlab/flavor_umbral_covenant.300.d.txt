The zatsarya dotting Taneyas and beyond can be fashioned into useful reservoirs for the Vile One’s magic; this She has learned from Her experimentation with such places. Yet the spire at Shalazar has never hummed with precisely the same magic as the others. It is an aberration, a curiosity, one of many dozens of oddities the Vile One had thought to deal with in an idle moment, of which She has essentially none. Now, though, the mystery is resolved; in applying the prototype sundering techniques to the Shalazar zatsarya, the Vile One’s mages have discovered it to be a fake. Their sundering ritual has blown a whole four times the intended size into the edifice, for the stone of the Shalazar zatsarya is inferior to the nearimpregnable zatsargakh of a real spire. The rubble around the hole has disappeared entirely; as the Vile One’s mages inspect their handiwork, they perceive that the stone of this spire is not truly material but a sort of arcane projection, made solid only through the working of magic. As they cross the threshold, a voice rings out; in the quiet of fogchoked, occupied Shalazar, the echo is tremendous. “Many thanks for unknowingly lending me your sacred spire. You may retrieve it from its current location in the Plane of Earth… although I doubt even your kind can morph into something that can withstand its pressures. I hope you enjoyed this replica in the meantime.” Your kind…? A message intended for the lizardfolk, clearly. There is little time to process the declaration, because even as its echo fades, Earth Elementals pour forth from the hole we have gouged in the false zatsarya. It appears this little hoax has teeth.
