The communion was over in less than a minute. In a heartbeat, everything suddenly stopped – the Threadbearer released her hand from the Fateweaver’s corpse, and the raw, rushing power of magic ceased, motes and wisps all that remained. After taking a moment to collect herself, she turned and descended the steppes silently. She looked troubled, anxious even. “So?” the monarch asked tentatively. “What did you learn?” Was the ruler’s empire to fall? Would the monarch need to sacrifice their firstborn son? Would the monarch be unkillable until they turned sixty-one years old? Or would the monarch see unparalleled success? Glory and riches unrivalled? The Threadbearer paused for a moment, as if troubled, then finally gave the monarch’s future. “Your mages, your scholars, your scientists… the brains of Taychend will be pushed to their limit – and in doing so, the very seams of nature shall be torn apart, the divide between hot blood and cold steel muddying under our power. And with mechanical hearts within, and the sound of grinding gears behind…?” The Threadbearer looked away from the monarch – past the ruler, as if whatever she had seen in the prophecy was behind the Emperor. “You will confront forces that will stretch comprehension itself.” The Threadbearer shook her head as if to clear it, then turned back to the monarch. “I am sorry, the communion was tiring… please, leave whenever you see fit.” Then she hurried off, and the monarch was left alone – the past glowing bright before the ruler, and the future towering high behind the ruler.
