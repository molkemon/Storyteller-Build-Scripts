The Kylakas walked between the packed tenements of the city. The fight had ended a small while ago. Dead bodies littered the streets, limp and lifeless, blood pooling beneath them. Some in groups, some alone. Some cut down as they fled, some still clutching their weapons. Pitchforks, butcherknives, ramshackle spears. A few captured swords. None had armor. A heavy toll. But he supposed that was the price of sticking to the old code. The prisoners were subdued. Half of them were slaughtered to do it. And the riot was squashed. Then, something caught the Kylakas’ eye. He halted, raising his brow. A woman, dead of course, clutched a rod. It was like a metal pole. Not a weapon, really. A stick. She had been cut down, fallen on her back. And in the blood beside her, there were little footprints. Marks where someone had knelt. His first thought was that it was her child. Curious. The blood was still fresh, but when the Kylakas looked around him, he couldn’t see anyone. What sort of child abandons his dead mother? Leaves her corpse in an alley? Maybe he had run off, was cut down as well. Mmm, no, not a good excuse. The Kylakas turned to the woman again, staring at the footprints. He remembered, now, one of the Erythé trials. One of the nightmares. The mist had shown him something had killed his mother, basically, a wolf or something. And he had watched it happen, heard the screams, terror, wails, and so on. It was hunting him; he had to stay still. Let it happen. But the monster passed, and he went to his mother’s side. He had stayed by her for a while. He was sure that he had. And then it came around again. He remembered its gnashing maw tearing into him. The unimaginable pain. It ate him. He felt it. He had stayed. What sort of child abandons his dead mother? The Kylakas gave a dour sigh, shook his head, and continued on his walk.
