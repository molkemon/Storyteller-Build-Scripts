No imperial relief force would be coming to save the defenders of the Emperor’s capital. The Lorentish Rose had strangled the Empire’s princes, its thorns crippling every army sent to face them. But the Knights of the Orda Aldresia who defended the emperor would not bow to any Lorentish assault on the Imperial Palace, no matter the odds. For hours they fought in the city’s streets as the walls were overcome and then breached. Jockeying for control of the city, they ousted the invading Lorentish troops four different times, driving them back to the walls. It was not until they dwindled to barely a thousand defenders that the emperor arrived with their personal guard. The speech the emperor gave is lost to history, but Lorentish soldiers at the siege tell of the fanatical war cry that preceded a last gambit by the defenders. The remaining knights of the Orda Aldresia led by their liege sallied out from the city in a roaring charge against the Lorentish army. They carved through a quarter of the enemy’s depth, troop commanders coming to panic as the impossible veered into view. The Rose banner began to waiver as reinforcements sent by another ruler arrived, rallying the Lorentish forces and rushing to meet the raging emperor. Accounts say the Emperor of the Doves appeared invincible for a few seconds, slicing through the Lorentish host with ease, striking hesitation into the Lorentish with the furor of the Aldresians behind them; but a single shot from an unknown soldier rang out from the crowded battlefield and struck the emperor square in the head, felling them instantly. This sudden reversal shattered the last of the Empire’s resolve, and the remaining Aldresians threw down their weapons around their fallen monarch’s broken form.
