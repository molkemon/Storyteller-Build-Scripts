I heard he once bent a battery tower in half and used it as a bridge. I heard he wrestled a wyvern until it sobbed like a cub. I heard he leapt the length of the Divenhal Sea. They say many things about Acay the Strong. Tonight, he is dressed in his favourite indigo silk, carelessly draped over one shoulder, pecs bared. His stripes shimmer with oil. Around him, the firelight crackles and sparks. Initiates watch him, rapt; eyes wide, eyes dreamy. "Careful," he says to one of them, pouring wine into the young gnoll's carved horn. "Stare too long, and you'll start seeing stars." A wink. Bold. Smooth as honey. The initiate swoons, flustered, lifting his paw to his forehead. The rest chuckle. Some are blushing. The others are breathless. "But I'm no First Sun," he continues, catching the initiate before he can fall and lowering him to sit. "I only keep an eye on its fire." They all grow quiet. Acay lifts a heavy axe from the dirt, resting it against his shoulder as though it weighs nothing. His tone shifts, warm and low, measured and intimate. "Zokka gives us more than strength. He gives us meaning. He saw this body," he grins, puffing out his wide chest, "and asked if I wanted to be more than a weapon. And I said yes." His eyes move across the firepit, leaving stumbling hearts in their wake. "I would be his voice, and his hands, and I'd look damn good doing it." "You're unbearable," someone mutters under their breath. Acay only laughs, grinning wide. "I'm unforgettable." He lifts the axe again, stepping forward. The crowd parts for him.
