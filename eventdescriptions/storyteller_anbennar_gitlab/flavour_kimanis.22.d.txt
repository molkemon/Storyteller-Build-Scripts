The ruler of the country wakes in their bed with a headache, well into the late hours of the morning. On top of their chest, there is a note. It reads. The ruler of this land you may still be, our friend, But even with your many gazing eyes You know that you cannot begin to see, our friend, Into the darkness where your end will lie The shadows hide our winedrunk ecstasy, our friend, The shadows are where revelry still lives The nights are filled with frenzied, whirling dance, our friend, An orgiastic haze which to us gives The understanding that our lives are naught, our friend, Should we not live in desperate search of bliss Fear not for safety even though we plot, our friend, Because we also wish to tell you this In future nights, it shall soon come to pass, our friend, Our pleasureseeking interests will align Think of us when you next lift up your glass, our friend, To pour into your mouth a sip of wine.
