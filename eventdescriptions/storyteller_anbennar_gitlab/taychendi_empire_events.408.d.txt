The BlueHouse mage went down on one knee, and presented Starshear to the monarch – a blackwood staff, lined with ivory and ebony, and crowned with a crystal that shone a brilliant white, wreathed in a prismatic halo. Not the base wild magic of prismatic Dames tear – but a connection to the primordial chaos of the Astral sky. “Starshear requires a large amount of mana to use – you’ll only get about one cast per day.” The monarch took up the staff, and just by touch felt the power coursing through. “But, well, I’ve always believed that in magic, power is superior to speed.” The archmage looked to their right – at the enemy city in the distance. “Perhaps it is time for a demonstration?” The monarch turned to the city in the distance. As a crowd of warlords, soldiers, mages and notables looked on, the monarch raised Starshear to the sky, closed their eyes, and called down oblivion. The monarch felt their magic surge, their energy pour into the staff as the crystal at its peak flared with light. For a moment, the monarch thought they were not powerful enough, as the muscles in their body burned, and a line of blood dripped from their nose. Then, all at once, the crystal went dull – and the monarch’s pain with it. Almost two millennia ago, Ebenanth had sought to deny the inevitable. Turn the world back to the time of the SlaverNobles. But through the power of Starshear, he had only swept away the last remnants of the old world – through his death, and the destruction of all he worked towards, the last cycle breathed its last. That power – the light of annihilation – now cut through the sky, now struck Halann with rippling force, tore apart the city before the crowd. The home of tens of thousands erupted into an explosion of light and heat and starfire, all within incinerated in an instant. Starshear’s fracture had heralded the true beginning of the Cycle of Glory.
