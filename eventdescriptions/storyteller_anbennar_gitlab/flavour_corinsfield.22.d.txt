The leader of the Red Sentries has come to us in much frustration. One of his men, a higher officer, has been tied and beaten severely for their efforts in meddling in the war effort. Evidently an objector to our methods of total war, under the cover of night this officer rode into a village to warn them of an ambush the following day, urging them to evacuate those who could not fight. Clearly, he does not understand; the savages are our enemies, and none must be spared, no matter what. One cannot expect an honorable war in conditions like these; this is no less than extermination, no matter what objections are held by those who falsely claim righteousness.
