The ship of state sails under its own wind, and a new hand on the tiller can only change course so much. Whether commoners view the country's government as tyrannical, benevolent, or something in between ultimately comes down to the laws of the land, the bureaucratic and judicial apparatus that administers them, and the local magistrates who enforce them. However, the occupant of the Dragon Throne still holds great sway. The monarch can issue proclamations, appoint, dismiss, banish, and execute officials at will, and use their supreme authority as they see fit. The realm anxiously awaits which direction the newly crowned ruler of the dynasty will steer the country. That said, the monarch, with their widely known personal shortcomings making them laughing stock of the court, appears unlikely to dramatically change the character of our government.
