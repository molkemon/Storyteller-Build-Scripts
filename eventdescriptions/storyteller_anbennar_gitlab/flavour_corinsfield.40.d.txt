“I will not do it. I will not.” The counter, a bald, short little man, closes his eyes and shakes his head. “I don’t believe you understand. The man is clearly a witch,” barks the inquisitor, clad in black with a tall buckled hat. “On whose word? His brother’s? I watched those boys grow up. Never once have they agreed on anything. I will not have a goodman killed on a single accusation.” “Fine, then. Have it your way.” As he leaves, the inquisitor slams the door to the temple shut so hard that the wood cracks.
