No one predicted the battles should be as bloody as they are. It is said 'the house divided against itself cannot stand', and this is true also of the Great House of Vuls Tenvach. As the Ambhen infiltrate the social fabric of Taychendi, their ideas and philosophies have ingrained into the mind of common folk and, perhaps more importantly, elites. But those fools who fell to foreign ideas are no longer among them, having decided to betray the customs of the Taychendi and ally themselves with Ambhen invaders. As their power lies broken and they are left to lick their wounds as pariahs, the status of the ruler has only strengthened, a powerful warlord ready to destroy all opposition. Already the neighbours of Vuls Tenvach cower in fear, as they hear of the victories of the monarch. The fracture that ran deep in the foundations of the Great House has been made whole again. Let all of Taychend fear the rulers of Thekvussam.
