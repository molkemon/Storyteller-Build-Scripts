The Hero Codex was once a pillar of the Taychendi superstition, a scattered collection chronicling the great deeds of various warlords, fighting over scraps of the past and walled collections of mudhuts. Great tales, sure, but it was always the mark of a fool to take them seriously, to emulate the deeds within them, or to take solace or comfort in them. Bharbhen savagery at its finest. Or, at least, that is what most Ambhen think these days, the shopkeepers, traders, settlers, and officers. For the scholars, though, these are the records of the mythic history of a people. How different are they from the earliest tales of Ameion's past? Indeed, for the Klereyen and their Keintagogist allies, it is their history, a reminder of how far they have come, no matter how embarrassing the old tales were that their ancestors used to believe in. Of course, not everyone can read them; only those who have already accepted Kheionai civilization. As Kheionisation progressed, such tales were confiscated en masse from the traditionalist Taychendi, stockpiled in the libraries and the archives of the Aditóphylés. They were after all pillars of a detestable superstition. Yet now, they hog up space that could be used for far more useful purposes. storing census records, administrative documents, survey results, things of that sort. So, the Móloists want to burn them. Take a couple notes of their contents and what little historical information might be gleaned from them, then throw them all on a pyre and set them alight. The Klereyen and Keintagogists have reacted strongly, saying that this is just an expression of Ambhen supremacism. They argue that the Synkletos should just make provision for the codices and build some more libraries. Nonaligned Eilastes, meanwhile, say that if we really need to make space, Ameion should just sell the codices to the Order of Chroniclers.
