Blood is, in the literal sense, life. Layton Kettlebrook, Grandmaster of the Chloromancy Guild of Newshire, scoffed at this assertion as the fields shifted from green to rustred. He commanded his fellow Chloromancers to pay close attention to the contradiction at hand. Why does life end itself? Can only one form exist at a time? Can one not bolster the other? Days passed. Weeks. Months. The deluge continued to fall. The riots continued to spread. And then, without warning, news arrived, alongside a vial of a deep red liquid and a sunflower growing in crimson soil.
