The soldiers returning to camp from our most recent foraging trip bring with them a strange breed of mushrooms, one that has never been seen before by anyone in the camp. The cap has a strange oily shimmer to it, refracting the light of our lanterns and torches in strange patterns, like a puddle of oil. For that reason, our head biologist has dubbed them "Oilcaps". They exude an alluring smell, which raises the cooks' interest as to whether they can be consumed safely or not.
