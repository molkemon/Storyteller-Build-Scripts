It had taken longer than Ruuk would have liked and certainly longer than the increasingly irritated chieftain of Sapchopper would. But, they had found the Whisperer once more. And they had found where they had been disappearing to. "A gladeway entrance? Here?" "So it seems," Ruuk answered, examining the feymarked land. "Ain't no path I've seen." "Hmm." "So now what, boss?" For a moment, Ruuk considered stepping forward. The handful of skulkers with him regarded their leader wearily, sensing his half-thought. "You're not planning on getting us lost in the Feyrealm again, are ya?" He shook his head, "Once was enough." There was a quiet collective sigh of relief. "Then what now?" Ruuk frowned, racking his mind. There were only two ways to chart an uncharted gladeway. The first was trial and error. That wasn't going to happen. The second was to acquire a wayfare of some kind, a grounding object that could help him navigate the Feyrealm. But where would he get that? "Boss?" a skulker asked, seeing the growing frown on Ruuk's face.
