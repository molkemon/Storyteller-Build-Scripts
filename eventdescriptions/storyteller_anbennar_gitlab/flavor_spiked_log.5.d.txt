Today's tale, my friend, is about the strongest person I have had the pleasure of meeting. An incredible woman, for sure, blazing through the battlefield like a wildfire. She is not as known in the Woods here as outside, but there, some even consider her a goddess. Well, I know no other gods, so I cannot compare, but I doubt they would enjoy as much as she to chug pint after pint of ale with their loved ones. Qlak the Wanderer fell silent, a bittersweet smile on his face, as if mourning a longgone friend. As the last echoes of his voice faded, he resumed his story. She is the one who set us free, you know? The Verdant Veil's fall was her doing. She had to battle a very powerful archfey for that, and I even heard that during the fight, her sword broke down, and she had to finish barehanded. But her most commendable trait was that she could see behind appearances. Such a unique woman. She gathered a group of companions, accepting elves, big humans, small humans, and even orks, and more surprisingly goblins. Our cousins of the Groundhawk clan followed her outside the Woods. And you know Oubblig, of course; he is supposedly the one who helped her fight the archfey. Come on, Qlak, this is nonsense. Why would the Traitor help a human fight the fey? Still, she seems like an interesting human. I would have loved to meet her. You are not alone, my friend, you are not alone. Her story made many youngsters join the army, on the off chance she or someone like her would come to our Woods to seek help for some glorious adventure.
