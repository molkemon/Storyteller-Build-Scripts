All in all, things had worked out surprisingly well. With the help of a local leader, we were not only able to convince the neighbors of a rival nation of our phony war, but our own troops were successful in repelling the subsequent invasion into that nation's lands, much to their gratitude. You've had all the expected gains in relations with that nation, of course. But perhaps more notably, the whole incident has also brought another nation closer to that local leader as well. One of the greatest developments has been a partnership between the merchants of one nation and the merchants in the local domain. Before, distrust of the local people among humans had made it difficult to conduct robust trade with them. Since the war, however, you've learned that the merchants of one nation have begun to act as intermediaries between the other nation's merchants and the local people they wish to conduct business with. As it turns out, having a human able to vouch for your trustworthiness goes a long way toward getting other humans to trust you.
