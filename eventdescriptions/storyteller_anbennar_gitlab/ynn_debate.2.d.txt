As the monarch holds the final word in a long series of councils, a general sense of relief is felt. It is said that the monarch's predecessors would hold speeches with a cup of wine in one hand and a dagger in the other, always ready to dispense one or the other. Today, a group far larger than what those monarchs of old were used to is assembled before the monarch, yet they feel none of that tension. Instead their heart is filled with hope and possibility for the future, and so they speak. "Through tumultuous debates and radical protests, through bloody battles and tenuous peace, we have found ourselves at the quiet end of a challenging century. The known world has expanded farther than our ancestors could have imagined and here we stand at the forefront of a new era. Our faith has faced the difficulties of this changing world and we stand stronger in our resolve than ever before. Let us go now and spread our faith beyond the reaches of the Ynn's banks, so that all might know of its gifts and its glory."
