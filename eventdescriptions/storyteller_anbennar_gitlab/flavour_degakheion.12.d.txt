The rebel leaders sat around the table, the mood grave and dire. Months of protests and unrest had roused the people from their slumber, following decades of preparation while their overlords went to war, ignoring the trouble brewing at home. And now, the time had come for judgment on whether it would all be for nothing. "So the signal is out?" one asked. "The other cities will rise on the same day, or thereabouts?" another nodded. "Simultaneous attacks on the garrison's positions, before they can reinforce. Local elites, merchants, and mercenaries... even some pirates. We should have enough to make a dent." "But to overthrow them?" one of the leaders, a wealthy woman from a northern district, sat forward, worried lines on her face. "My district... we transported weapons under the guise of gladiators, but we have nothing heavy or substantial. And the peasants in the countryside... how will we get them into the city to aid us?" Nobody answered. Silence, for a few moments. Finally, one spoke up. "It doesn't matter." He shook his head. "They've refused any concessions, as we predicted. It is now or never. Every day we wait, their grip grows stronger. We have no other choice." The woman's face fell for a moment, but then the courage came back. She nodded. "So it is done, then? Should we go to our districts?" A murmured agreement followed. "Then good luck, everyone. Kill as many of the guards as you can."
