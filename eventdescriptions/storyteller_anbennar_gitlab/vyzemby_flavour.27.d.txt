History is a fickle thing, subject to the whims of mortal minds and their frail memories. There are none living who remember building the Zatsarya, the ancient lizardfolk spire called Kazakesh. There are none living who remember building Torapinoma around it, either. But what history forgets, the land remembers. Torapinoma still stands guard, wrapped around this ancient lizardfolk monument. No Ananoma is built in the exact same manner as another, just as no Zatsarya is the same, either. But these two are perhaps the most unique in all the world. With every old wall restored, every forgotten cache explored, it becomes more and more clear to us that Torapinoma was not built by Vyzemby alone. It was certainly not built for them alone, as there are doorways and rooms designed for much larger builds than the average halfling possesses. Tunnels and hidden passageways that would only be accessible to naga dot the fortifications close to the Zatsarya. The same is true within the Zatsarya as well. There are chambers with seating for the smallest of halflings and the largest of lizardfolk. Ancient doors with secondary handles lower to the ground. This place was once sacred to two peoples, a testament to the children of Drongray, and to the children of Kolo. So shall it be again.
