The leader squints against the wind. "We thought they might have been roads, until we drew them out. The centaurs see them as a method of communication, that much is obvious. Between themselves and whatever their god is." Another leader says nothing. The first leader tries again. "We don't have to understand the faith itself; it won't last long, anyway; just the language. It already reacts to felflame, and we speak felflame well." "We can't learn to speak from fire," the second leader scoffs. The first leader shakes their head, halfsmiling. "Then what's the point of a pyre? Those flames mean reverence; not ruin." The first leader's fellow leader bares their fangs. "A flame that doesn't kill is cold." They pause. The first leader breaks the silence. "There's a moment, before the fire catches. The hush, the tension; that's the sacred part. Not the burning, necessarily, but the change. The becoming." The second leader barks out a laugh. "The easy way out. Change happens whether you want it or not. Something that endures is greater. A flame that doesn't die, even against a howling wind." "The old rituals, then?" The first leader raises a brow. "You'd have the whole Pack learn the same words? Even the ones who; let's face it; still dream of their false gods?" "They'll chant what we tell them to, until they realise the truth. Until they forget anything else." "That isn't faith." The second leader grins, shrugging. "The outcome's the same." The first leader doesn't answer, but their eyes return to the copied glyphs; oddly elegant, for something carved by hooves. "These are messages for the sky, that much is obvious. What are we going to say?" The second leader's tail lashes. "That he's coming." The first leader smiles, part admiring, part worried.
