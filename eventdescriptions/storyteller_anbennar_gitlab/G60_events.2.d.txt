The Fasíri peoples have long been a disjointed community of tribes, unable to find peace. Only in our greatest peaks of power have we seen a tribe unite us all, shortly followed by a steep decline. What we show here is something more than just conquest. its unification, and along with it, stability. As the monarch descends down Rigoti, they feel an overwhelming sense of righteousness, fighting for the cause of their people. As the monarch addresses the myriads of soldiers they himself has assembled, they raises their hands to the sky, and shouts.
