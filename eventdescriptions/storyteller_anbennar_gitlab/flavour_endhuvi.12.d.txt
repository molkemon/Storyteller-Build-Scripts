A curious individual strode down the center of the main palace hall. The guards turned to watch as she passed, for not often did practitioners of the Mystic Accord come this far north. Her clothes pegged her to the Lupulan, the great rainforest to the south; her expression of worry matched the general anxiety pervading the palace around her. The monarch leaned forward on their throne, as did many of their advisors, lined up alongside the monarch on their own fine seats. The woman stopped before them. "Your Majesty, I could feel it as I stepped into your lands; I can read the look on your face. You seek answers to the stillness that plagues the great river, yet you search in the wrong places. Do not look to science or your esteemed philosophies for help; instead look to the great spirits of this land." "What proof do you have that this is tied to the spirits?" It was the harsh voice of the Minister for Infrastructure; his high rank afforded him the luxury of speaking unprompted. "Such things are long forgotten here, and the lands unchoked by superstition." The woman fixed the harimari with a piercing stare. "Then why am I here?" The monarch cleared their throat. "Because," the monarch said, with a sharp look at their advisor, "every option must be considered."
