The boy sat on his throne, looking out at the court he was now in charge of, to the kingdom he now ruled. He looked to the left and right, seeing the loyal lords who had formed his regency council. She never got to see him like this; she died the night before his ascent, and she had been buried on a small hill, a tree placed atop her grave as per her request. So loving she was, that she wanted her very grave to provide shade to her people. He ignored the endless chatter of his advisors, who sought to distract his thoughts with useless trivia, hoping he would bore easily and retire from his duties, thus leaving them with the throne.
