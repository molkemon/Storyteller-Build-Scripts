The mysterious forests of the Deepwoods teem with both life and death, a verdant stage upon which the cycle of nature has played out since the dawn of time; if such a thing can be said of a place so deeply under the influence of the Fey. There is one more than any other who embodies these laws, a great and terrible archfey of the Seelie Court. The wood elves, first tenants of the glades, know him as Arsydbadhál, the Old Wolf. The warlike green orks learned quickly to respect his domain, and named him AsgudDomd, Master of the Hunt, while the clever goblins simply called him Prezmorzmept, Death’s Grasp. Such labels serve as but a warning of the Lord of Beasts’ mastery over the woods, as he again and again stalks the glades, in search of ever more fearsome game. The scales of nature demand balance, however, and the Lord himself must be both predator and prey. According to legend, the Lord of Beasts will leave signs of his presence, an open invitation to the bravest and most resourceful hunters. At first, rumors of his mark appearing were dismissed as coincidence, but the truth can no longer be denied; he has summoned you to a specific location.
