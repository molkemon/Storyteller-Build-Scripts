Our plan was excellent. a secret alliance with a few well-placed senators, the backing of key figures in the ministry, enough popular support to drown out public outcry. But no plan survives contact with the enemy, and it seems the support for our cause has faltered. Time can wear down mountains and rust sharp metal to dust. It is therefore unsurprising that as the years of this internecine conflict wore on, our allies were overwhelmed by doubt, the ministries eliminated our supporters in quiet reorganizations, and the public grew outraged at our insult to the rightful raja. We cannot continue in this cause without dooming the entire Harimraj. We are done. One day, the monarch swears, they or their descendants will sit upon the Lotus Throne. But it shall not be today.
