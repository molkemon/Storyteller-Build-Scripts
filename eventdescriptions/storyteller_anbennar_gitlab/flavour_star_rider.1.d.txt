We are the ones left behind, an assortment of those who were too infirm to join the incursion. When the Wild Hunt came to ask for our assistance, it was our finest that left towards the cities of the humans. We asked the stars, divined on the mountains back then. We were warned, and dissent spread. But our caehn enforced his consensus back then, and then they rode out. A year passes, then another. Mothers lose their eyesight, and children grow old without remembering their father’s face. We are forgotten. Every day that passes is a reminder of what we have lost. Moving on is difficult; what else could it be if not that? We have forgotten where our shamans divined, as they are dead. We are unable to wield magic, the most important gift that Irdaeos gave the shamans, because they are dead. And now we waste away, hoping so desperately that our fallen brothers, sons, and fathers will return. This stagnancy, this ongoing paralysis must end. Our clan is not yet dead, people. We may be disfavoured, disgraced, and whatever else you want to call us, but we are not yet dead. Great Storm might believe itself to be the heir to the Storm King, but we know better. Green Flame will try to break us and sacrifice our souls to whatever vile patron they have made a pact with, but they will fail. We must prevail, for we are the only ones capable of ending strife within the centaurs once and for all.
