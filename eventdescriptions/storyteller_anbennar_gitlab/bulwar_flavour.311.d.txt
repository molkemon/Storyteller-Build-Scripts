The shattered spire of Kuvunika cuts an aweinspiring sight, even in its ruined state. Surrounded by the concentric walls of the warlord fortress added many millennia after the construction and destruction of the spire, the temple still dominates the landscape of Kuvunika, a potent reminder of the weight of history felt in these lands. For the people of Kuvunika, it is not a looming portent of their insignificance in the face of such vast timespans, but a joyful affirmation that no matter how dire a catastrophe might be, life marches on, and in due time something new and wondrous will grow on the bones of the old. Just as the Thirteen Days of Darkness might have meant the end of the old Bulwari Pantheon, Surael’s rebirth ushered in the glorious new dawn of a radiant age of Light. His rebirth is therefore the focus of celebrations in the aptly named Temple of Sacred Rebirth, or Gekwepizaa, as the local Kombedak Dakinshi and Cyazizeta lizardfolk prefer to call it. While Kuvunika might not experience much in the way of seasonal variation, the ancient Bulwari calendar is still used to determine the date of the winter solstice. On this date, an enormous celebration of Surael Reborn transforms the city. A massive procession begins at dawn, travelling from the eastern city gate to the innermost circle of the spire fortress and the temple ziggurat found on the southern side of the shattered spire. Here, from midday onwards all through the remaining day and the night that follows, ceremonies praising Surael and exhorting him in his struggle are held. All throughout the city, the people are gathered either in solemn vigil or in raucous celebration of Surael’s impending rebirth. Once the first light of dawn touches the top of the temple ziggurat, a new celebratory procession begins, this time following the course of the sun westwards to the banks of the Ina river, where the populace gathers to celebrate Surael’s victory over the Dark for another year.
