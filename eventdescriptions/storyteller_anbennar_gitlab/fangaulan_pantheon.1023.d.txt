Pick up the rugs, and look at the dirt. Useless, isn't it? Dry and dusty, good for nothing. Or so you might think. Kunolo didn't think so. Today I will tell you the story of Kunolo DealMaker, and the greatest bargain ever made in all the lands of Fangaula. In the days before the city was founded, Kunolo wandered the lands of the violent Jiromji tribes. Trade flourished wherever they went. Not out of goodness, mind you, but because Kunolo was never foolish enough to leave money on the table. One day, Kunolo came across a mighty chieftain's great herd of cattle, drowning in a river they had attempted to ford. Kunolo shouted for help from the nearest village, and everyone banded together to build a bridge of pontoons, and the cattle were saved. The chieftain, arriving to find his herd alive on the far bank, was overcome with gratitude and offered Kunolo anything they pleased. What Kunolo pleased was land enough to build a city. But the chieftain loved his land, so Kunolo reached down and pinched a tiny speck of dust from the ground. "My mighty king, I wish only for this—that each day I may have twice the dirt I had the day before. If you grant me this, I shall be content, for I am trying to build myself a home." The king laughed, imagining a mudbrick hut, and agreed. The specks became handfuls. The handfuls became armloads. The armloads became mounds, then hills, and soon the king found he could not move what he owed—and was forced to give land instead. When at last the debt had grown to the size of a city, Kunolo released him from it, and the king, grateful and somewhat wiser, gave Kunolo the materials to build that house they had spoken of. Kunolo built their house. And around it grew a mighty city. And the king was never quite so quick to shake hands after that.
