As the monarch ambles into the arena, the ruler sees a vaguely familiar face. This man is a brave from a minor clan, who distinguished himself with outstanding skill in recent wars. Many say he could make Clan Head and lead his clan to greatness, but he is young still and must prove himself. The warrior bows to the monarch respectfully, but the look in his eyes is like starved iron. The match begins. The young brave adopts a novel tactic, moving in zigzag patterns rather than the traditional charge-shot-retreat. The monarch struggles to predict him and cannot match his practiced speed. But the tactic makes his aim inaccurate, as he never faces the monarch headon. The monarch presses this advantage by loosing shot after shot, trying to bury the youth beneath a hail of arrows.
