Akin to a tidal wave, unrest has spread across the realm, reaching far beyond the walls of a city. While in most cities the Durari’s soldiers fought back against the flames of revolution, within the city of Imbatano they quickly bloomed into an inferno. The vast ogre population of Imbatano had in the past suffered great injustice at the hands of the ruling class; though the ogres had been instrumental in building this very realm, they had effectively become second class citizens within their own home. From restrictions to where they might purchase wares, which markets they were allowed to frequent, to limiting what positions and roles they might obtain within the realm’s government. Though such legal discrimination had become frequent, an inescapable reality for ogrekin, it had gotten far worse under Degsadra’s rule. The laws and decrees that oppressed their way of life grew more restrictive by the week; the repression so severe that even in a major city, Degsadra had become known as a notorious discriminator towards ogrekin. Yet, the ogres would not let themselves be shackled and chained by any more of the Durari’s unjust and cruel laws; when the unrest that had spread throughout the realm spread to Imbatano, the ogres rallied to band together and defend themselves once more. From the ranks of the Maghalmtianebi rose Dzatonde An’Vraktigoma, who swiftly garnered the respect of her compatriots. Soon enough, she became known as a reborn hero, who would lead ogrekind to freedom. Dzatonde An’Vraktigoma was not alone, though; at her side stood a human, Itoriewa Dimgã, her closest ally and confidant. Together, they could become the heralds of the revolution, representing a united front of humankind and ogrekin, and amassing an army of peasants, artisans, and discontent soldiers, all seeking a brighter future.
