The village of Carrow's Haven was a picture of colonial tranquility. Whitepainted cottages lined the cobbled streets, their gardens bursting with marigolds. Fishermen closed their stalls as the sun dipped low, casting the sky in soft peach and lavender. The scent of salt and freshly baked bread carried on the evening breeze. Children laughed as they chased crabs near the docks, while their mothers mended nets and traded stories. The village hummed with a simple contentedness. The sky grew grey. Not the soft fade of dusk, but an oppressive, unnatural grey that seemed to drag the light from the world. The air thickened, colder now, as the mist crept in, curling around the docks, swallowing the cobblestones, filling the spaces between people. The lanterns sputtered. Where their light touched the fog, the shadows it cast were wrong. Too long, angled against no discernible source, moving a halfsecond behind the things that threw them. The laughter died. In the silence that replaced it, something large shifted in the water. The mist did not part so much as thin, just enough to suggest shapes. Tall, untiring things that had been standing there for some time already, patiently waiting to be noticed. A woman looked down. Her shadow had come loose at the heel, peeling slowly from the cobblestones like snakeskin. She barely had time to understand what she was seeing before she screamed as her shade skittered into the mist. A sailor burst into the tavern, knocking his shoulder against the frame. He did not seem to notice it. "Trollshaunt," was all he said. The fog pulled back from the harbour mouth to reveal a ship, its sails the colour of the roiling mist. The figurehead was a carved mouth opened in a silent scream, its inky eyes leering at the village. From it, a voice rose, deep and saltwatered, like something that had learned to speak from listening to the gargling of drowning men. "Bring me the men of Carrow's Haven."
