While they have primarily come to be seen as the proponents of spreading the revolution abroad through military actions, the Girondists are also in many ways the voice of the establishment. Now that they have achieved considerable influence in the National Assembly, they are using that power to attempt to create a formalized set of rules, a constitution for our new country. This is not the first project of its kind and likely not the last, but they hope it will bring us one step closer to a new order and an age of Liberalism, Equality, and Brotherhood.
