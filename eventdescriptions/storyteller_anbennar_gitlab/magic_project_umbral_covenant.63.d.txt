Komina says, as if it is only just occurring to her, "You are like me in some ways." They sit on the upper terrace of a barrack house and watch as a commander directs an elite guard in their drills. Komina is in her element at times such as this; the sun gleams off of the jewellery at her throat, Fahvanosy in make, and she herself is perhaps the most ravishing halfling the Vile One has ever seen. "I am?" the Vile One asks. "You enjoy their company. Mortals, lesser things." "I came from them," Komina scoffs. "Briefly, perhaps. And not since your fog." The Vile One tries again. "They are useful." "But that isn't why," Komina says, smiling. "No, you're like me. You get something from mortals. Your grandmother and your mother, for all they dote on their favourites, could go into seclusion tomorrow without a moment's thought." Were the Vile One a different woman, she might wince at that. She remembers a little girl whose every waking moment was consumed with grandmother's approval. The thought that grandmother could have left her any moment is troubling. But Komina is right, of course. "What is it, for you?" Komina is gazing intently at the Vile One; has she ever before felt the full brunt of Komina's attention? It is no wonder that people overturn their lives for her auntie; that after a day they will kill their families for her, run away with her. That moment of freedom, of deciding upon a new life, is what Komina truly feeds upon. "What do you mean, auntie?" Komina grins. "What do the mortals give you?" This is the Vile One's chance to connect with her auntie, to win her over. She senses her answer matters to Komina.
