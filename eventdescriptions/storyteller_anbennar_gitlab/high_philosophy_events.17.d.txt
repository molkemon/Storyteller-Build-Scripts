Youth is wasted on the young, it is said. The storied scholars of the Orange Sash School however intend to make sure this does not befall the monarch, or at least they will try to. They offer their services and guidance but the young ruler bristles against their suggestions, calling their proposals outdated for modern times. Indeed, the ruler also questions why the scholars never propose any reforms for their own institutions embedded within the state but happily opt to dilute the monarch’s power. These scholars dismiss the ruler’s concern saying it stems from the ruler's youthful age and naivety. How will the monarch respond?
