The northern mountains of Yanshen have long been rich in iron, the backbone of the realm's arms and military strength. For generations, mining and governance here have been overseen by court-appointed officials working with local authorities. Most sites are managed directly by state offices under strict statutes to prevent mountain damage or unlawful extraction. Recently, highland officials report the emergence of a new method used by local miners and craftsmen. combining ancestral timberframe tiering with folk-developed gunpowder blasting, which has greatly boosted ore yield without harming the terrain. This technique is only possible because of the unique stone of the Beikling and Demon Hills, yet the results are remarkable. Locals petition for the court to promote this method to other parts of the Beikling mining region. The court, though recognizing its merit, fears such expansion could invite misuse elsewhere, eroding mining regulations and disrupting coordination. Should one adapt to local advantage, or uphold the rules that bind the realm?
