Spirits have been moving south en masse for a couple full moons now. The clan’s Shamans have their hands completely tied with these migrations. Some were moving towards the Dakilbege Nuk lake, seeking the protection of its powerful Water Spirit, others even farther, aiming to offer their services to the SpiritFarms. Upon being asked why they were fleeing, the answer was always the same. They had appeared in droves. A lost raiding party? An unlucky Yomod Rüsh family? Facestealers rarely operate in large groups; their gluttony for chi and their despise for anonymity drives them towards solitude. It matters little what event led to this unlikely emergence, for what really matters is the immediate termination of this spiritual cancer that plagues the land. After much deliberation, and the advising of the most senior of Shamans, the ruler decided that this host of facestealers had to be disbanded. The Shamans argued it is more effective to take them out now rather than risk them spreading out in solitude. It had been decades since a Shuvüüsh hunting host this large was assembled, numbering a dozen or so Shamans, with a couple of senior Death Priests clad in black robes and pitchblack masks among them. The smell of jasmine and juniper lingered in the air to such an extent that even the older terrorbirds were visibly irritated by it. It is a blessing that the clan finds itself in a position to carry out such an important preemptive strike against the agents of pure evil.
