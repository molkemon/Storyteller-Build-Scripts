In vinecovered forests, old tongues still murmur. Among the hillfolk, artisans, and spiritguides, Kai words, songs, and storytelling are still used. Incense at festivals, totemic cloth, and burial rites all echo distant Kai traditions. Though long part of Yanshen culture, Kaidescended Naamjyut have not cut ties with the old realm beyond the mountains. Some clans keep kinship there; spiritguides preserve ancient prayers; traders bring Kai incense and charmscrolls through passes, known to elders. As Kai's politics shift and Yanshen unifies, some fear such links may sway Naamjyut's governance; others see them as heritage. Recently, one tribe's close dealings with the Kai have drawn wide debate. Will you respond?
