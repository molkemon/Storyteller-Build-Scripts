Cheni says, gazing at the Tetrahedron with faint distaste. He is in some ways more conservative than even the hags, for he sees little worth to magic outside the latticework, the ritual combining of magical power, which has become his and the Vile One’s specialty. The Vile One says, "You are not a man of intuition." "I am not." "I simply know there is something to this, something we can use to convince her." Besides Cheni, there are few others the Vile One would bother to try persuading in this way; Ikomeye understands intuition intrinsically, and Umorozi does not care. "This device is the only object I have come across which confirms a different account of events than that of my Grandmother; the only object independent of the Coven’s control whose account I can be sure is sincere." Cheni gives her a kind, all-comprehending, insufferable look. "You would open this thing even if it did not help you prise Komina away. You’re just following your own rank curiosity." There is a pause. The Vile One allows a stiff nod. "Well, that I understand."
