In the middle of the day, Orwick rushes into the personal quarters of his liege. A preposterous act, but this one time permitted by the ruler's title. "It is ready." With these simple words, Lucian is already out of his chair and moving ahead of Orwick along the aisle. Lucian moves to the ritual chamber, the room most connected to the old curses of the land, and lies down in his prepared coffin. The ritual starts; candles are lit and blown one by one as the shadows start to grow taller in the room. Where the shadows collide, an all too well-known mist grows. the mist of the Cursewoods. With his final chants, Orwick prepares a branch, taken from the Cursewoods, and plunges it next to Lucian’s heart. The mists of Luciande rise and grow thicker. Soldiers sieging our cities and marching through our lands find themselves confused, surrounded by mist, and later outside our borders. Anyone daring to reenter either returns to where they entered, or is never to be seen again. The war is over for it cannot continue.
