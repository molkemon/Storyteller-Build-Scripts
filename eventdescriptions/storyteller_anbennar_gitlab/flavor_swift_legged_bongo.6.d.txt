Useless, all of them. Useless. Uwimbabazi paced back and forth across the chamber, her breaths coming in irregular gasps. She wheeled to face one of her council members, who was attempting to look as small and inconspicuous as possible. I have spent two years searching for a successor, and for my efforts, I have been rewarded with nothing. What am I to tell the candidates, my Ingo? The council member was avoiding Uwimbabazi’s gaze. You are to tell each and every candidate the same thing—those who attempted to flatter their way into my good graces, those whose confidence betrayed incompetence, those who told me of their weakness gladly, in the hopes that I would admire their self-pity—they will all receive the same message. You are to tell them that I have considered them, and they all have been found wanting. Are you quite sure that’s wise, my Ingo? Many of them will surely not take well to such a slight on them. Did you hear me? They. Have. Been. Found. Wanting. Uwimbabazi’s molten gaze met the council member’s, who shrank away from her intensity. Unless, of course, you also wish to be found wanting, hmm? No, of course not, my Ingo. I will prepare the messages right away, my Ingo. The council member scurried out of the chamber. Uwimbabazi allowed herself to lean against her desk, and a deep sigh shuddered its way through her body. She shook her head and gave a tired chuckle. Multiple tribes’ worth of people, and yet not a single one is worthy of being my successor. Her fingers tapped restlessly against the desk. No one, except for me. She stopped tapping. A small smile crept across her face.
