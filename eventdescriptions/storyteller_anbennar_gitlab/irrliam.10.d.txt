Deep within the shifting sands and arid wastes of the Salahad, even Surael’s blessed light can be overwhelming for the mortal mind. Mirages of the eye and of the spirit abound, leading even the most grounded individual astray if given enough time. And the Desert Elves and their Mašnsih compatriots have certainly spent more than enough time lost in the depths of the desert to develop the most peculiar delusions. Chief among them is the doctrine disseminated by Jaddar and his disciples, who have twisted the concept of Chosenhood beyond recognition. Now, as we rule over the unwelcoming wastes they call home, it falls to us to bring them back into the fold of the faithful and ensure that they find their proper place in the divinely ordained Order, created by Surael himself. But how to best disperse the persistent mirages planted into the minds of these desertdwellers is a topic of contention amongst the clergy. Some propose a thorough campaign of religious conversion, sending out missionaries to each tribal camp, to each hidden stronghold until the fallacy of Jaddar is but a distant memory in our records. Others argue that the hardy inhabitants of the Salahad respect a show of strength and propose wiping out the most intractable adherents of Jaddar to encourage all others to discard their false beliefs. A small minority questions whether such heavyhanded approaches are suited to win the favour of these independentminded people. Maybe we should instead hand over the task of administration and conversion to those amongst their ranks who have proven to be devoted servants of the Chosen? Those truly devout who are eager to show their kin the path back into Surael’s Garden?
