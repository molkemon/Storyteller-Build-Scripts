I approach. It is so small, because I have eaten, and I have grown. Halann shrinks below, tides still, as if in prayer. I look away. I look up. The moon waits, pale and trembling, rocking towards me. I reach for it, claws ringed in curling felflame. The teal stone, teeming with power, sings within it. Does it know what I am? Does it know what I am becoming? I seize it. It struggles. Its skin cracks first, thin and brittle like ancient bone. I dig deeper into the flesh, and it groans like a dying beast. Its marrow pulses. I begin to peel it open, and it screams in that soundless, silent way. Its heart is soft. The Dames tear pulses like a synapse, energized, alive. I devour the shards, one by one. I feel the world shudder below me. I feel every world, briefly. Every plane. Every gate. Every way between them. It burns and scratches at my throat, but I swallow all the same. I swell. My form warps. The flame burns greener, licking at the vacuum with nothing but my self to feed off. But I eat, and I grow. I am more. So, so much more. Dames tear chunks streak around me, orbit disrupted, sent careening towards the planet beneath me. I let them go. I care not for crumbs. Not when a banquet lies before me, blazing and radiant.
