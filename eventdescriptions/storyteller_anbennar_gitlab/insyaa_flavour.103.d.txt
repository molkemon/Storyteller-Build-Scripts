My friend was called EagerForLife, though I only ever called them Eager; after so many years together, it had never really occurred to me to ask their full name. I was so pleasantly delighted by their instant trust, their boundless and easy capacity for friendly intimacy, the immediacy with which I had made a friend. Eager would carry me in my chair from home to work and back each day, for my legs had never been up to much; I worried about making of Eager a beast of burden, until their scoff at the notion disarmed me. I tell you these things so you understand my shock when they told me they were to become an auxiliary. "You volunteered?" I said, taken aback; it was not in keeping with what I knew of their nature. "Of course," they replied. "You eat too much, and this chassis will soon be too light to carry your fat self."
