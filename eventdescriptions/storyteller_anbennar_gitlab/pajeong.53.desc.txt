During the rule of Sage King JeongHun Gyo, the army was well-outfitted and well-trained. The Hwalbaek proudly brought up new recruits, and each regiment functioned as a well-oiled unit. But the recent conflict in our nation has caught up to our army. Members of the army are often at each other’s throats, whether due to arguments about the ineptitude of our ruler or the more insidious whispers of restoring an independent Dalmangaji. This infighting among the officers has echoed down to the lower ranks, and the newer recruits are not getting properly trained, instead being subject to long days of conflicting propaganda rather than days of drilling. We have the option to send some loyal military instructors to several military camps to enhance their training, but this will undoubtedly ruffle some feathers.
