You think I don’t see it? Oh, I see everything. Every sideways glance, every whispered word. You covet my crown; you ache for it. Not for duty, not for the realm's sake, but for your own twisted hunger. You’d melt it down and forge it into chains, wouldn’t you? Shackles for me, for those still loyal. You slither through my halls, poisoning minds, twisting truths. But I know. You can’t fool me. The weight of this crown would break your feeble skull. It speaks to me, and it knows your treachery. So go on, keep plotting, I will not stumble. I will not fall. But you? You will.
