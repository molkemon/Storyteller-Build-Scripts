Mykx will go down in history not only as a visionary who traveled to an archipelago in a faraway continent and made it her own, but also as a mother to those who went with her. Wherever she walked, she instilled fear. fear for her towering stature, robust build, ruthless punishments, and her vicious battlefield tactics. Still, she did not instill fear in those loyal to her and those who submitted, but to them she was caring, thoughtful, and always ready to listen. She knew the names of those she sailed with, from the lowest slave cleaning the lower decks to the spotter at the top. She also had the same attitude about the republic's future; she refused to become a monarchy like the one in Viakkoc despite having all the capacity for it. She believed that the only way forward for our people was a system like the one she devised and helped to build. Countless captains visited her open casket to pay their respects; even her rivals or enemies went to pay their respects. It is commonplace that when a pirate captain dies, the remaining captains who remember them fondly will leave something of value in their casket to help pay for a proper funeral. This has been happening for centuries, even before the time of Mykx, back in Viakkoc. The casket of Mykx, however, had gotten so full of treasure that her face was almost covered. A secondary coffin had to be placed next to her; that one also got full, so we put a third one, and soon it will also be full. Still, while it isn’t, it is up to the republic she cherished so much to interpret what a proper funeral is for a Chief Admiral as renowned as her.
