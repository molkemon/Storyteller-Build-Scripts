As of this writing, this commission has undertaken a rigorous study of the political entity known as the Empire of Anbennar to the best of its ability, and concludes that aspects of its structure may be beneficial to emulate for the operation of the Overclan. In summary. One, Anbennar and the Overclan are similar in that they both contain highly diverse populations, often with competing or divergent interests. Two, with some unfortunate exceptions, Anbennar has largely been able to manage these differences with a decentralized system representing constituent groups, particularly based on racial groups. Three, application of the fundamentals of this system, adapted to the Overclan's conditions, may prove beneficial for the state's stability. Four, while decentralization to the degree of Anbennar is neither recommended nor considered desirable, systems such as the representation of specific constituent groups of races might prove beneficial. Five, adaptation of this so-called "elector" system into a national parliament is recommended as a sound policy for the state to pursue for its own benefit.
