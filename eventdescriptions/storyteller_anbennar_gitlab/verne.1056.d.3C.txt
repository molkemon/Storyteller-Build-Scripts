The mages in the room stared at the scrying mirrors, wide-eyed and dumbstruck. The people in the dragon chamber looked to be doing about the same thing. The squirrel wyvern had danced around the dragon, swooping underwing, between its legs. It had spent it spinning, as it clawed at the walls, thrashing about trying to catch the pest assaulting it. It did, eventually; the wyvern was a bloody mark against the wall. But it gave the Cannorians time. The clerics had completed their spell, exorcising the specters within the bone-white frame. The dragon golem lay lifeless on the chamber floor, well, more lifeless than it already was, being undead and all. Ramujith had been glued to his seat as he watched the people's victory, seeing fifteen hundred years of work evaporate in an instant. But Jalinwathi, who had overseen half a century of that work, had leapt out of his chair, hands on his head, disbelieving, shocked silent, but in a different way to the others. They didn't know it at first, but they did. For the archmage could contain his pure, unadulterated happiness no longer. "FREEDOM. I'M FREE. I'M FREE." The archmage looked at his colleague, as did the mages around them. "But Jalinwathi, the dragon. The beast Sulathrezhil subdued—the great work of the Blue House?" Jalinwathi turned, shook his head softly. He had tears of joy in his eyes. "Ramu, be honest. We were never getting anywhere anyway." The two archmages turned back to the scrying mirrors; an army of guards had rushed in now. The people held their hands up, thoroughly and inescapably caught. Captured. "So," Ramujith said. "What happens now? It's just a hunk of dead metal, now." His colleague simply shrugged. "Well, we both have projects we need to fund, right?"
