Ikomeye sits crosslegged upon a small dais, at the centre of a large pool. The surrounding courtyard is dressed granite, at least a thousand years old by the count of those who know a little of lizardfolk architecture. The zatsarya does not quite loom directly over this villa, but scramble onto the roof of what used to be the main residence and one can see it peeking over the trees. It seems the right place to think upon the issue. “A set of arcane reservoirs was my first thought,” says the Vile One. She is doing large, loping loops of the courtyard, skirting the lip of the pool. Bendbark gutters filled it with rainwater but a half hour ago; it was Ikomeye’s little project to restore this place, for she is always in need of a new home to ruin and she has a surprising knack for the mechanical. The lizardfolk labourers who fitted the new gutters have already beaten a hasty retreat for the evening, so she and the Vile One may discuss the matter in peace. “Reservoirs for magic?” Ikomeye scoffs. “Ornate for the purpose, aren’t they?” “The lizardfolk have never shied from the ornate.” The Vile One smiles, shakes her head. “No, it’s what I can feel. Something humming below the surface. Perhaps dismissing them as reservoirs is mere wishful thinking on my part.” Ikomeye nods, catches the thread. “Because if something is humming, and the zatsarya are not mere reservoirs…” The Vile One smiles. “.then they are still working towards some other, unknown purpose.”
