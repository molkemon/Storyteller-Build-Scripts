So you are the one I am to teach. Kiros was too well trained to jump in surprise, but it was a near thing. Whirling around, dagger already drawn, he found himself facing a middle aged man, hair and beard both unkempt, and wearing only a bronze medallion etched with the image of an eagle in flight. His dagger-filled hand snapped up into a salute. Kiros Kajen of Weriksedar reporting for training sir. The man examined him for a moment, before nodding. Good reflexes. You may refer to me as Gulimbazi if we are around civilians, or Teacher Tuyishme if not. Now follow. With that he took off at a run, leaping to the next rooftop over as Kiros scrambled to follow. They soon found themselves overlooking the town's market, cloaked in the shadow of a belltower. The next hours were spent observing. Teacher Tuyishme pointed out the habits of the townsfolk, highlighting the normal routines that could be passed over, the oddities that would merit further scrutiny. Nervous glances, deviations in schedule, impious words; all were cataloged for future investigation. By the time the sun had set and the lesson had ended, Kiros felt drained. All his life he had trained to spy on the nobles of the lands, but watching the common people almost seemed a more daunting task.
