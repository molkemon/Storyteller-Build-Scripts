The Sage King Manjang was the first to truly unite the entirety of Gowon under his rule, negotiating a lasting peace between man and spirit. In his quest to achieve this peace, he proved himself a fair and just ruler. His reputation as an impartial mediator who united the human tribes of Gowon behind his rule and his extraordinary magical prowess allowed him to achieve the grand feat he is still remembered for many centuries after his death. He travelled to Nakseseom, and negotiated the Compacts with the fox spirit queen Sado. Confirmed with a ritual marriage, these Compacts would from this point on serve as the foundation of law governing both the humans and spirits of Gowon. After his death, a tradition began where prospective rulers of Gowon would travel to Nakseseom to seek the hand of Sado in marriage. Those she deemed worthy receive her guidance, with some even rising to the exalted status of Sage King. Those not deemed worthy would find themselves sating Sado’s carnal desires with their liver, and return to a realm in which they only govern its human half. The Compacts are a set of laws that give human rulers in Gowon the right to enforce their will on the spirits inhabiting the region. They have the right to, after fair adjudication, enforce punishment up to and including exorcism to the spirit population should they deem it worthy. Yet the Compacts are only truly binding as long as a human ruler is a Sage King. If they are not, the local spirits will usually administer their own affairs without human interference. And now, like it has been countless times before since Manjang’s fateful first journey, it is time for another pilgrimage to Nakseseom. The current monarch stands ready to face Sado’s judgment of their worth in the hopes of joining her in most sacred matrimony. And thus, the entire realm waits with bated breath for tidings from Nakseseom.
