Bleeding rain taints the fields of Cannor dark crimson. Miles and miles of nothing but malevolent storms, starting from the desolation in Escann, slowly moving into the Empire of Anbennar. Preachers cry from the streets, claiming that Adean and Corin have drawn their blades. Peasants take to the streets, screaming of the end of days, ashen skies once more. Brother, if you receive this letter, stay away from Cannor, or any ships arriving from here. Everyone is tainted with madness, and even the most sane cannot escape the nightmare that is the Crimson Deluge. Even I start to grow more weary by the day. I hope for this all to be over soon, so that I may see you again. This is one of many letters flooding in from ships arriving in Port Isobel. The ships they come from are tainted, with stains of blood all over, as if the powers that be were trying to send us a signal. While Cannorians would like to believe that we live safe and sound in Aelantier, that is not the case anymore. Even more signs of something awry, with fish washing up on shore, marked with odd patterns that many claim to be sigils of war. More cases of defects in our young, and illness spreading within urban areas. Worst of all, claims of skies tinged with red, as if to let us know that we could be next as well. As people come in seeking shelter, we can only wonder if we will be able to provide them safety from the malevolence of the Crimson Deluge any longer.
