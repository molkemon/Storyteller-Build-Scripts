It had been a while since the city had come under Shuvüüsh control. The cities of the Gowon were not completely alien to the riders of the steppe, despite centuries of their absence as rulers of the land. Border raids were frequent, and occasional forays deep into Gowon, all the way to the great spiritwalls of the coastal settlements, were far from uncommon. The ruler was strolling through the city accompanied by the clan’s most senior Death Priest, a White Shaman and nine other riders from among the ruler’s most trusted, a retinue most typical for a Kaasheg. Along with them, riding on horseback, was a greyhaired man, heir to one of the most powerful estates of the city. He had introduced himself to the Kaasheg shortly after the city’s conquest and had offered to show the ruler around and induct the ruler a bit into the customs and traditions of the local populace. The streets were bustling with people and spirits alike; the spirits visibly cringed at the sight of a Death Priest. Opulent houses flanked the roads on both sides, seemingly taunting the riders with the thought of a more comfortable life. It was in a street such as this that the greyhaired man was offered a place in the Kaasheg’s court. Perhaps our neighbours can be proven useful after all.
