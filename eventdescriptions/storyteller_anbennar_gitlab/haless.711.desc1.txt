A long neglected stretch of the Yanhe has gradually dried up near the province. Once bustling docks now stand exposed as sandbanks rise and fishing boats run aground. Freight traffic is forced to reroute, making the trade of rice and grain a difficult endeavor, and local market prices have spiraled into chaos. Some boatmen have already left in groups, seeking work downstream. The old saying returns to popular memory. “Where boats cannot go, the people do not dwell.”
