Word has reached the monarch. a clique of palace eunuchs has assassinated their court rival, the diplomatic advisor. Aiming to elevate one of their own to the office of diplomatic advisor, they fell on the advisor as they was bathing, trapping the advisor in a fishing net and stabbing the advisor to death with ceremonial tridents. Having been promptly seized by the palace guard, the clique beseeched the monarch to spare them. They claimed the advisor had been steadily spreading their nefarious influence in the diplomatic corps, allowing the interests of foreign powers to sway the monarch’s policies toward intolerable tyranny. They were compelled to remove the advisor for the good of the realm. Their selfrighteous pleading was cut short only by the blade of the Palace Marshal, methodically decapitating them one by one. The monarch now faces the choice of what to do with the rest of the sorry lot. Proclaiming their innocence and begging for mercy, the remaining eunuchs would surely be loyal if spared, and unable to amass the influence which had allowed the clique to get away with such a brazen plan. However, the assassination of a trusted advisor is high treason, and must be treated accordingly. Justice must be done, and be seen to be done.
