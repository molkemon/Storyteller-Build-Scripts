The plains are quiet tonight. No wind, no cries of beasts, only the faint whisper of grass beneath my hooves. For once, there is peace. I look up, and the sky looks back, endless, cold, and clear. Too many spend their lives trying to fight the night, fearing what they cannot see. But I have long since embraced it, surrendering my very flesh. They do not understand what the darkness brings. It is not terror. It is not despair. It is stillness. It is beauty. The light of the stars shines only because of the dark that surrounds them. I am at home here. I see in those distant lights the reflection of my own work, the world reborn, pure and quiet beneath my rule. Death and silence, order and grace. The night is my gift to the living, and the starlight above, my mirror. So few recognize this truth, recoiling at the future I offer. But they will learn. One by one they shall fall to me, and in the darkness that follows, the world will be remade.
