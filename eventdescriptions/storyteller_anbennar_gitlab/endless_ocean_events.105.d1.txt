Forty-five seconds. the duration of the longest recorded flight by a fish. It is a pittance. In order to fly, they have had to work for so long. How many countless generations ago did one fish dream of reaching the sky? How many fleeting leaps did they take before the wind caught their scales? How many stars and moons passed along before they could stretch their fins far enough to glide? How many thousands of years did they spend training for forty-five measly seconds in the air? How many more thousands of years will they spend striving for forty-five seconds more? Forty-five seconds where their world changes entirely. As our efforts to document local practice continues, our chroniclers spend hours, days, weeks sorting indiscernible legend from apocrypha. We toil voraciously, sweating as we document and memorize stories whose meanings we cannot decipher. And just when the noonday sun beats down the hardest and the wind’s breath is most cutting, a miracle occurs. for a moment we understand, and a new world appears before us. And then forty-five seconds pass.
