Mourning wails fill the streets of Jezarpizu, so loud they rise even to the mountain roosts above. Woe they cry. The Queen is dead. Memorial pyres light the dark skies and line the path as a team of Sirens carries Ríva’s body, reverently wrapped in a silken shroud, down from the palace towards the docks. Along the path of the procession the people gather, human and harpy alike, united in mourning. Many weep, others stay stoic and stony faced, whilst those who hated the queen look on; many an Odanchés amongst them. However no matter the disposition none can deny her achievements, her will, and for that they gather now. The Sirens place her body aboard a small boat at the dock, one laden with casks of oil. A single one amongst them takes a torch and boards with the Queen, rowing her out into the waters. At the harbour's heart, they take flight, rising above the boat and letting the lit torch fall. The crowds watch, still and silent, as the torch tumbles; a single light in the dark. It lands, the oil erupts, the boat going up in a blaze. And as the flames lick the sky the Sirens begin to sing.
