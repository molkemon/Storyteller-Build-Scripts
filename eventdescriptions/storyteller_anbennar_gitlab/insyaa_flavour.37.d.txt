Today, the chamber of the advisor has been discovered empty. With the exception of one or two of his favorite volumes, the kobold does not appear to have taken anything with him, and by way of goodbye he has left the last manuscript of a series of books he had been writing concerning the nature of statecraft. The manuscript has been dedicated to our nation, and its contents are colored with examples drawn from our history and administration. Appropriately, the last volume discusses the role of the state moving forward in a changing world, and the treatise concludes that it is the duty of a wise and powerful king to connect noble thought to proper action. This is described as a way of justifying necessary steps taken to make the world a better place. The book ends with an exhortation to the kobold’s students to do just that, and an exhortation to the monarch to listen to their advice.
