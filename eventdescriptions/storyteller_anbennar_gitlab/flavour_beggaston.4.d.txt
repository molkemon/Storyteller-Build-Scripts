The Ynn floods with unprecedented rage. A father kneels in its depths, begging clemency for the starving. A son follows his patriarch, but cannot hold fast against the current. As the father pleads, the river takes. He overturns all of his domain to find his child. The River’s Hands rescue a drowning boy. The father finds his son, asleep and unsteady. The Hands tell him that he must pray. He worships a goddess that is not his own. In desperation, he clings to any faith he can find. The son awakens, hearing Begga and his father. They weep together, and tears flow towards the Ynn. “I am Lord Talvynn yen Nizel of Selocshana, and I will follow Begga until the day I die. The Ynn takes, but Begga gives. I live in the grace of her charity.”
