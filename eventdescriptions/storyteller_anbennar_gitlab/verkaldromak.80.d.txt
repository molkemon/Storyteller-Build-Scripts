The ruler's vision moves quickly, away from the cities that glitter like jewels on those strings of green, and to a vast expanse of brown and yellow. Closer, faster. To a splotch of black on that dun background, which grows and grows as the vision nears. But the catch of light on steel renders the mass in a starkly different term—not some splotch like ink on parchment, but a mass of bodies, motionless, staring towards their center. Towards which the vision then proceeds. A huddling man, dismounted from a small convoy of horses, approaches a kneeling figure. A glint of bright gold metal off the object in his hands, as the messenger places it on the bowed head in front of him. And then, suddenly, an explosion of sound. Sufficient to shock the monarch, startling the ruler briefly awake, with a word still ringing in their ears.
