Many years ago, a great cataclysm came to the lands of Kanolowele, and it was so great that it destroyed the dragon turtles as well. Meteors came to earth, and one such meteor disturbed a mountain, and this mountain fell on the dragon turtle Kaumomoku, the Great Strategist. Kaumomoku protected the homes of the people of Kanolowele, and one of his greatest friends was Keala, a village girl with whom he played his favorite games of strategy. Keala’s family was scared, but when she saw her friend trapped under the rocks, all she could do was run to help. Those rocks could not be moved by a dragon turtle, much less a human, but Keala stayed with Kaumomoku to give him comfort. And as the rocks crushed his bones, Kaumomoku told her everything he knew, of all the people who he had kept alive in the afterlife. Keala stayed awake with him for seven days and nights, listening, until Kaumomoku went to the afterlife and she could listen no more. With those memories in her mind, Keala sailed across fire and ash for seven more sleepless days and nights to the dragon turtle Naauao the Listener, whose mind could hold the legacies Kaumomoku had given to her. She recounted all she had heard to Naauao, and she did not forget a single word. Then she collapsed from exhaustion, and she went to the afterlife with her friend Kaumomoku, where they play their games together to this day.
