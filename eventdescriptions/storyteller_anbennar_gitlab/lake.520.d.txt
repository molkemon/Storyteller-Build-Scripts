When we were invited to a conference to discuss the future of Ultakal, we at first did not know what to think of it. None of our informants had sent any reports that would indicate the true reason behind such a conference. Even the most ruthless of our contacts in other cities had begun to shun our agents and their financial incentives with clear disgust. So, for the first time since the founding of our most canny city, we knew less about what lurked in the shadows than our adversaries. And when the day of the conference finally arrived, our ruler was utterly unprepared for the audacity of the other party. They listed a long set of grievances, none of which our ruler could dispel without preparation. Within minutes, the consensus reached by our assembled neighbors was clear. We would lose Ultakal indefinitely.
