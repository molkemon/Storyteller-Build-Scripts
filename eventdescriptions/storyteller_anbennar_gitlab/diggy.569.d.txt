The mines of the ancient dwarves were nearly works of art in their construction, and many have stood for thousands of years after their builders were overthrown. We must admit, ours are not nearly as well-built. In the province, our miners complain of unbearable heat and foul air. The wood of the trusses supporting the shafts rots, and the nails rust away. To ensure full productivity, we must invest in repairing and maintaining the mines; or even copy the designs of the ancient dwarves and improve them. However, this would be a capital-intensive effort.
