The air is odorous with the smell of sweat, blood, and death. I breathe deeply through my nose, having long gotten used to the stench, my eyes scanning the victorious battlefield. As my stag slowly trots, I silently count the bodies of fallen orks. As my count reaches a hundred, my grim smile sours. How many of these feyscorned invaders had I and my clan cut down? A thousand? Two thousand? More? Countless corpses and still I couldn’t push them back. Why? I shook my head knowing the answer. It was their bastard chieftain, Khagor Sapchopper. As long as he stood, there would never be peace. I froze. “A single thrust of the glaive over a thousand arrows,” I whispered. Slowly, I nodded to myself. Yes, that was the answer. Together with the other clans.
