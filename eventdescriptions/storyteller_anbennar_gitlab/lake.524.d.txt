As we predicted, Khugatseig tried to bribe several representatives from other cities to either abstain from the conference or vote against any proposal of ours. Yet public opinion within the federation has long turned so far against Khugatseig, that participating in such obvious misconduct would have been far too politically costly for the other cities. This was their first mistake. When the conference started, the Khugatseigi representative showed up in garments woven from gold brocade. Their second mistake. Their main line of defense was to cower behind a flimsy veil of plausible deniability combined with some heavyhanded ad hominem attacks intended to undermine our credibility. According to them, every citizen and ally of ours had a strong incentive to exaggerate each of their wrongdoings. In the end, as their own famed advisor Jargal Selkäreppu, demonstrating a recent change of heart, stepped to the witness stand to speak out on our behalf and uncover all their schemes, even the Khugatseigi representative knew.
