The scent of smoke was strong as the monarch rode through the Petal Gate of Lorentainé, a relatively undamaged section of the wall that now masked the chaos beyond. The ruler was welcomed by the cheers of soldiers, those who refrained or were otherwise satisfied from the spoils of the broken city. Justice served. As the monarch moved through the rubble that once made up the city’s streets, the thick smell of death mixed with that of the burning buildings. A siege like this was never clean, and nowhere was that as evident as the place where men had fought amid their homes. Blood ran over the cobblestones. What had to be done. It was a quiet procession that rode down with the monarch to the main square, for amid the sounds of eager looting and songs of the victors were the hushed cries of the innocent; those huddled together in the shadows to shelter against the raging flames, sobbing for lost ones. Some of them stared hollowly at the monarch. Good, they needed to see their conqueror. The Lorentish had inflicted the same agony upon countless lives, and now fate had paid them back in kind. So let the voiceless sing and the tearless weep.
