The delegates of the Amlharaz are unruly in their seats by the time the ruler enters, and their voices grow only louder as the session is gaveled in. For today, they will be discussing the one issue most near and dear to Dwarven hearts, and the bedrock of their most bitter disputes — coin. It is time for AulDwarov to decide its budgetary priority for the years to come. Some argue for a policy of economic expansion, devoting every week to come to the construction of new infrastructure, from the homes that house their people to the temples they pray in to the warehouses that store almost every good found in Halann. Others would instead focus on efficiency, ensuring that their assembly lines are operating at full capacity and minimizing waste products, to boost their output like never before. Some Holds, especially those in strategic positions along the Dwarven Rail, would have them focus not on the production of goods but their free flow throughout the Empire — they remind them that blood must be pumped through a body if it is to live. A fourth group turns their eyes not inward but outwards, and decries the way that Serpentspine gems and minerals flow across Halann as a looting of their realm, that must immediately be stopped. The Amlharaz grants each Hold a vote — but at the day's end, it shall speak in a single voice. Which issue will the ruler focus AulDwarov on?
