Life is a fleeting thing, especially during war as we face now. The grim fact is that not everyone will see it to the end. And it is that grim fact that brings the small fairie before us bearing a message from the Seelie Court. Reading it before the monarch, the fairy squeaks, "During the darkest of times, we of the Seelie seek to shine the light of joy ever brighter. Dear ruler, let laughter lift spirits. Conjoin capriciously in carousing. Meet meanness with mania." Upon her small proclamation, the fairie looks up expectantly. While not obvious to outsiders, the wordplay is a roundabout offer to help from the Seelie. Not in a traditional sense, but help nonetheless.
