To say that the advent of the rifle revolutionized warfare would be an understatement; no longer could experienced men hide behind layers of armor or magical shields to stave off death from untrained farmers. It is therefore no surprise that the same happened to the castles of old once cannons established themselves as mainstays in times of siege and battle. And so, in this race of offense and defense, it only makes sense to look to the empires of old and their ways of warfare, a search that did bear fruit for us. Namely, through a huge skeleton, torn to shreds and with traces of magic still lingering on both it and the projectile fired, the latter we found buried in another precursor building, having punched through almost a dozen metal walls and into the mountain surrounding it. While even archmages drowning in Dames tar would struggle to achieve similar feats, it at least gave our artificers plenty of inspiration to give our artillery even a smidge of such raw power. Featuring a combination of Haste-infused barrels, increasing projectile velocity by magnitudes, a Thunderstone replacing gunpowder, allowing them to work perfectly even in wet environments, and a rotating multibarrel assembly for continuous reloading while firing, will surely give us a considerable advantage in the field.
