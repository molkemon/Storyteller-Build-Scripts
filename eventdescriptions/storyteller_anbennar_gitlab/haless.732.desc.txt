The name Jellyfish Coast comes not from the creatures themselves, but from the Coast's famed airborne balloons. Local artisans craft them from silk, canvas, and lightwood, letting them drift above the misty sea. Shaped like jellyfish—with rounded canopies and trailing streamers—they glow in the sky like sea creatures swimming in air. At times, real jellyfish appear in nearby shallows, carried by warm currents. Called LanternCurtain Ridges, they are clearbodied and faintly luminous at night, admired by travelers and poets. While ideas to use them for various alchemical or magical purposes have surfaced before, they were seldom pursued. Now, several workshops have revived the plan, proposing a small Jellyfish Pen in the old port bay for short-term keeping and study, exploring their alchemical potential. Their bells might serve as membranes for vessels or veils for concealment tools; potionmakers test their fleshlike material in brews. Supporters hail this as a bold step blending arcane craft and living matter; critics warn of fishing disruption, hazards, imbalance, or toxins. Should the chance be seized, or should caution prevail? The debate stirs artisans and streetcorner mages alike.
