Spinel is an oxide mineral with a hardness of seven point five to eight on the Dwohs Hardness Scale, and it is often found in octahedral crystals. It is typically found in three geologic situations. as crystals in limestones and dolomites that have been subjected to contact metamorphism; as irregularly shaped grains in basic igneous rocks; and as waterworn pebbles in alluvial deposits.
