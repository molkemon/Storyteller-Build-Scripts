Three souls, siblings, linger at the edge of a domain, packing up their tents and gathering their belongings, preparing themselves for the long trek eastwards, back to their home of the Flower Grove. The eldest, instructing her younger brothers on how to properly deconstruct the tents while she packs the bags, dons a silver necklace around her neck; one that had been given to her by her older brother when last they saw each other. He remained in the Flower Grove, to protect it from the invaders; to ward off the orks and goblins, so that his siblings could both escape to safety, and to ensure that they may return home in due time, once the invaders had been routed. Yet his siblings will never see him again, for he has been felled by an orkish axe. As the siblings begin their journey, they cling to the hope that they might find their lost sibling, not yet aware of his death. With some luck, they might have the opportunity to learn of his demise, perhaps even give him a proper interment; without it, they may forever consider him lost. For every such story of families being torn apart by the invasion of the Deepwoods, there is a tale of the opposite; people being brought together by unfortunate circumstances, who would have otherwise never met. Two souls, lovers, walk along the path leading out of a realm in each other's arms, eastbound towards the River Grove. They only met because one of them fled from the River Grove; from the orkish tide that swept over the grove; while the other has spent their entire life up to now in the Lake Grove. Now they shall make a new life for themselves in the River Grove; a life shared with one another. Many of our people return to their homes. Many of them will either have to weather the tragedy they have suffered, or savor the good that has come from their ill fates. Regardless, they need no longer fear slaughter or displacement; they may find comfort and prosperity in our new era; one of abiding peace.
