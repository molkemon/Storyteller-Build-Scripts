The advisor has finally completed their work on composing a great work of literature. Having been provided an exceedingly large sum of money for the deed, the philosopher ensured that the patronage was not squandered; the resulting work, known as "On the Nature of Faith", is hundreds of pages long and will surely be counted as one of the greatest works of the age, certain to become widespread as it is printed and distributed. Indeed, it is likely to become one of the most well-known philosophical works of its field, all successors in the field to refute or support its thesis. Influenced all throughout by the authority of the Sun Elves, the work spends much of its length justifying their spiritual authority through a political framework. On the Nature of Faith argues for a strong, centralized state; perhaps the realm should follow along.
