The work of digging up relics of the Precursors, even on a continent as full of them as Insyaa, was once long and hard. Labor teams dug for months and months at sites that weren’t guaranteed to find anything at all, and most of the process of determining where the dig sites were was guesswork. Then, our artificers had an idea. We had samples of precursor steel, and the ability to determine with almost absolute certainty whether something was made of precursor steel or not. What if we enchanted our precursor steel, put it into a machine that could make sound based on its resonance with the surroundings in a certain direction, and let our steel lead us to more steel? And so, the Precursor Steel tracking module was born. With precise measurements it works at vast distances, so the scouting teams of the realm can simply follow it out of their well-fortified compounds directly to precursor dig sites, and the excavation teams can use their own handheld modules to hit relics within only a few days of work. Then, we excavate the precursor steel of the dig site to make more tracking modules, and find more precursor steel.
