The tavern was nearly empty, most of its patrons already having retired for the night. Only two gnomes remained, nursing their tankards of ale at a small corner table. "I'm telling you, Melbin," said the first, "the crown doesn't care about the Dragon Coast anymore. Lencenor is the greener pasture over the hill. All we get here are table scraps." Melbin Oddandodder, steward and chief advisor to the ruler of the Oddansbay, took another sip of ale. "Well that's pretty understandable, isn't it? The south has way more people. Greater needs, and all that." "But does that justify the neglect of the north?" The unnaturally blue eyes of Melbin's friend glowed with a burning passion. "The land of the First Hierarchy, relegated to being a sidelined backwater? Don't you think that should change?" "Hmm." Melbin swirled the remainder of his drink around the bottom of his tankard, pondering the words he'd heard. "What's it matter to you, anyway? Don't you have that cushy job off in Aelantier?" "I'm a gnome, Melbin. I care about my homeland. Don't you?" Melbin finished the last of his drink with a single swig, then looked over at his friend. For a brief instant, the dim candlelight seemed to cast the illusion of blue scales on his skin. Melbin wondered for a moment whether the gnome in front of him might have some kobold ancestry, before realizing how silly the idea was. Perhaps he had drunk enough ale for the night. "Maybe you're right, Tando," he said, grabbing his coat and standing up, "maybe you're right."
