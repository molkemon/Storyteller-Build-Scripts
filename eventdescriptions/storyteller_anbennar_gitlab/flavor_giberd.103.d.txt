Digging continues at a consistent pace, with gnomish and dwarven excavation teams working hand in hand. for once, no new issue emerges. Instead, deep in the depths of Anvilwright, lanterns shake and shudder from the symphonic reverberations of drills, picks, and explosives. Gnomes and dwarves are finally getting along. The artificery gear is working without fault, the tools are properly calibrated to work in the caves and caverns underground, and there hasn’t been a pay dispute in months. Indeed, the overseers of the project report that morale is at an all time high as the infeasible has become feasible in front of their eyes. As word gets out that digging continues at rapid pace and the final stages of the Anvilwright hold near reality, a festival is planned for the day the hold is finished. It promises dwarven and gnomish cultural traditions combined into one great day of historical celebration. However, as the messengers lavish praise upon the project, Oddannamm hears the scroll of expenses being dragged across the floor once more. This time it is sixty feet tall and six gnomes wide. The additional costs of preparing a festival are now a part of the hold’s budget, of course. Oddannamm contemplates retirement for a serious moment, before giving his answer to the messengers.
