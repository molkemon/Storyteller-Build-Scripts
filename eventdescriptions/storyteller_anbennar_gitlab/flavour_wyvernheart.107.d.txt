The monarch and Head Arcanist Adelmo Suín stood in the dark laboratory, peering over the table, to which was strapped a horrible thing; it was originally an ork. Now, it was a hulking mass, twisted and deformed, torn apart and grafted back together in a shape deemed useful, deemed worthy of being alive. Adelmo was ecstatic in his elucidation, pointing to various different parts of the thing with a manic glee. “Its skin, as agreed, has been replaced by wyvern scales, as we’ve finally managed to get subjects to survive the shedding processes. Its eyes, ear bones, and claws are grafted from werewolves, in addition to a heart, pushed to the extreme as to support its venomous blood.” The monarch turned to Adelmo, confused. “Venomous blood?” The Arcanist grinned, like a child being asked about their favourite toy. “Oh yes, the venom. Well, technically a poison, but ‘venom’ sounds better, of course. It burns what it comes in contact with, so we had to strengthen the blood vessels with an alkaline to neutralise it. It ensures that even if the Burnblood were to be wounded, there would be quite the parting gift for their assailant.” “Are they ready, then?” “For production? For now, yes. However, I have plans for more.” “Aren’t you reaching your limits, Head Arcanist?” The Arcanist tilted his head. “Monarch, if I may be honest. I do not believe in limits. Give me more subjects, and I shall build you an army worthy of Escann, built from blood and bone.”
