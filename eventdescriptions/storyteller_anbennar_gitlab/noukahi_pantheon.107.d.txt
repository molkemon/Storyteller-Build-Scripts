Gaze upon its magnificence, my ruler, wonder at its perfection. No weapon such as this will you ever chance upon again; it is one in a million. Oh, how it pains me to part with it, but I see in your eyes you are truly worthy of such a weapon, in a way a lowly merchant such as me could never be. All I ask in exchange, some mere bread crumbs, some minute amount of gold to feed my poor starving family. Surely a great ruler such as you would have no issue sparing those few coins in exchange for the spear of a true hero? The traveling merchant that the monarch has met during their pilgrimage has been showering them with similar speeches for hours already. He swears up and down that the old and jaded spear he offers is the magical weapon of the great hero Pokelgato, a figure none in the retinue have ever heard of, but that apparently accomplished countless feats with this very weapon. The price he demands is quite exorbitant, but if it truly is the spear of such a hero, then surely it would be worth it?
