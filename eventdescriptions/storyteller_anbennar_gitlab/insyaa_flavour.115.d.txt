It is as if the seedpod were made for such a purpose, solid all around. In nature the pod relies on the impact of gravity from great height, or the pecking of a hinuijay, but firing it from a cannon has much the same effect. In the best case scenario, the seedpod breaks against the very surface of the fortification itself, the roots attaching reflexively and drilling through the softer mortar in a matter of hours. But even when we miss, the ground serves as a keen ally. Hinuiflora are as a rule desperate and hungry in their early life; the natural world offers little mercy to the newborn. Thus the starving seed, quickly drinking down the nutritive fluid of the pod itself, spreads its shoots vociferously through the veins of the wall structure and will grow through anything or anyone it finds. What are the defenders to do in such a case? Dismantle their own fortress to hunt down the everspreading rootlets? Indeed, perhaps this would be wiser than waiting for the whole thing to collapse.
