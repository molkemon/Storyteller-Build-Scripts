Most of Gunjit’s appearances in the High Temples see the High God of crafters cast as a secondary character, as he forges the tools and weapons needed by the other High Gods. Within the upper levels of the partially sunken High Temple of Lake Tamakhan, his visage can be seen in association with Nermali and Dipar. However, buried away within the dark waterlogged chambers is a small room with the telltale signs of Gunjit’s craft. Our only account of the chamber comes to us from a small band of adventurers, who, fortunately for us, recorded the room's layout and made a transfer of its central images. According to the account. “At first, the room was bathed in darkness; only a small blue orb could be seen through the musty air. Upon touching the orb, the darkness throughout the room was pulled towards it. Wisps of soot and ash whipped past us wailing like the dead. Then all was silent, and the glowing blue glass orb hummed at a low frequency. With the darkness dispersed, the scene was revealed to us. The orb sat upon a white marble podium surrounded by four images of the High God Gunjit, which depicted him at various steps in the process of making both. Above the orb was a mural of stars connected like constellations, and spread out across most of the ceiling. However, there was nothing else in the chamber, and the narrowness of the previous passage prevented us from bringing the orb back.” As the ruler finishes reading the account, their eyes glance out onto the nearby lake. As the sun begins to set, and the water is painted with the vibrant hues of sunset, the ruler ponders the story.
