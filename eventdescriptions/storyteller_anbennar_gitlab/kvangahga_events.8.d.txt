With purposeful strides, the monarch entered the council chamber, commanding the attention of the advisors gathered inside. The clash of ideas that had reverberated like cannon fire in the air stilled for a moment before the cacophony resumed. "It shall be glorious," asserted the capital's high priest, his words infused with certainty. "In life our soldiers fought for the country, and in death they shall serve it as well." Amidst the priest’s raving, the chief architect objects, his tone stern. "A bad idea," he cautioned, his short words resonating with practicality. "Bodies break down over time. Bones and faith are no substitute for stone and metal." The priest began to speak again, until the monarch raised a hand. "Enough," they declared, their voice exasperated, cutting through the dissent. "My decision is made."
