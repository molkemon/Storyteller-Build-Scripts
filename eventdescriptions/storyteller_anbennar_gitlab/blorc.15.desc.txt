The conquest of Ovdal Lodhum left many a banner trampled in the mud. But they did fight fiercely, better trained and with more discipline than most dwarves we had crushed before us. We forced our way through a tunnel inundated with burning pitch, savaging their forces that used strange crystal lights for tunnel fighting. They gave us no quarter, and were commendably brutal in their fighting, but they fell, like all dwarves shall before ork blade. It is only in seizing the hold's deepest reaches that we begin to see the reason behind their impressive showing. The ruler came across, in a place of high honour, a blade of blue steel stained deep with blood, a blade known to every ork, one that features in the scary stories we tell our children and even an adult's nightmares. The blade orkrend, once wielded by the one they called "The Trickster", who slew thousands upon thousands of orks and never fell in turn to our axe or spear. It seems he was indeed a man, and not just a ghost or spectre, and must have settled in this hold. But the time where orks were killed is past; now it is time for orks to do the killing. Orkrend shall be taken to an anvil, and hammered by the monarch until it shatters. We have slain a nightmare, and all orks shall breathe easier for it.
