The fur clad nobleman remarked to his companion as they passed a palace balcony overlooking the newly constructed barracks. He asked if his companion was daft, and the companion hissed back with a worried glance at the gnolls training below. The companion warned that if one of the dogs caught him saying that about the Mezhi, they would have his head for dinner. The nobleman replied that the monsters were true, but he would not worry because they only spoke that yapping tongue, full of yelps and whines. He paused, following his companion's gaze below, and said that they said the Mezhi could speak it too, and he wondered if their parents' death was what caused it. The companion spoke again, pausing to lean over the balcony and spit, saying to set the madness in. The nobleman shook his head, saying he had no more love for them than his friend, but unlike his friend, he was quite attached to his head and had no desire to lose it. His eyes flicked below again, and he resumed his walk. The nobleman called after him, saying he was merely grumbling, and there was no treason in that. He repeated to himself in a half mumble, spitting over the balcony one last time before continuing on.
