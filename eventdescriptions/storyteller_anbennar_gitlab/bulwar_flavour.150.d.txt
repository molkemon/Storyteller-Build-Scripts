With the proclamation of the Jadd Empire, the Bulwari and Raheni heartlands are united once more under a singular imperial power, just as they were centuries ago under the Phoenix Empire of Jaddar’s mother Jexis and grandfather Jaher. Even those who used to scoff at Jaddar’s claim to belong to Jaher’s august lineage were forced to admit that he has indeed proven himself the true heir of Jexis. Yet the Jadd Empire is not the only empire claiming to be the successor to the Phoenix Empire. Far to the west, on the shores of the Dames head Sea, lies the Empire of Anbennar, which formed out of the ashes of the westernmost territories of the Phoenix Empire. Now with Brasan firmly under imperial control, the premier link between Bulwar and Cannor, a proposal has been laid before the throne of the DivineHerald to send forth an imperial emissary to the Anbennarian imperial court. The more bellicose members of the court consider the primary objective of such an emissary the official proclamation of Jaddar as the heir of Jexis and thus the Jadd Empire as the sole legitimate successor to the Phoenix Empire, denouncing any other rival claims. The mercantile faction, on the other hand, is far more intrigued by the lucrative trading opportunities that might open up if a mutual treaty of recognition and amity could be negotiated. The ultimate goal of such a mission might be contentious amongst the court’s factions, but surprisingly, almost all participants in this debate are united behind the person of the potential emissary. Dariaš, a scion of the Jaddar line of Jaddar’s descendants, has garnered a reputation as an accomplished diplomat, and his blood ties to Jexis through Jaddar have made him an obvious candidate, especially given the Cannorian obsession with Halfelves, as they call the Mišari. However, the final decision whether to invest in such an endeavour ultimately lies with the DivineHerald himself.
