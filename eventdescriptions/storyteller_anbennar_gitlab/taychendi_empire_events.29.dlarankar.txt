The monarch looked at the armour before them, plates of Precursor steel covered mail, elegant and imposing all at once. It was the armour of Erankar, the armour that the Emperor Defiant had worn as he cleaved Fate’s puppetstrings, and carved an Empire from anarchy. Since then, Larankar had given Taychend victory after victory, the borders expanding beyond any previous Taychendi realm. The ruler should be happy, but the monarch could only grimace as they thought about the empire they ruled, as the so-called “Warlord Autonomous.” The monarch knew now that their dynasty would not fall as the others did. Their empire would conquer, and the warlords would hunger, and on the blood of the world they would feed, the ichor of their ascension. The cart of empire moved faster yet faster, straight ahead, destined for the beginning of a new cycle, where Taychend would rule undisputed. It was unstoppable. It was uncontrollable. And if they looked beyond this cycle and did not like what they saw? Or if the wheels were running off imagining’s track, towards somewhere they could not comprehend? The monarch professed total autonomy; they wanted to believe Erankar had cut fate’s cord long ago. But they knew that no matter what they did, the hum of Empire and conquest and glory would drone on, as it did, as it had, as it would. Already, their lieutenants were drawing up plans for new expeditions, to North Aelantier, to Insyaa, to Sarhal. The ruler desired these things, but could they afford not to? Or were they a slave, destined to be but a cog in a machine with one destination? The conquest of the world—then disaster? The ruler could only see one answer, and it weighed on them far heavier than Ruinsteel. An empire for a day, an empire for an eon, it did not matter—Taychend would rise, an Empire Unstoppable, an Empire Ascendant, an Empire Vainglorious.
