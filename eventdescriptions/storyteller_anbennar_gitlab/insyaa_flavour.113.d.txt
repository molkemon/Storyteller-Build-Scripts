Our colonial charters' records are full of people who got too close to titanocreatures, and were swiftly and predictably punished for it. There are now children's tales about why not to get too close to the strangling vines that grow taller than five houses stacked on top of one another, but somehow there is always another person willing to try it anyway. Through the power of gene magic, now we can make our enemies these people. The thornvines of the titan lands grow according to a set of principles inscribed into their genetic code, and with the appropriate manipulation of those principles we can plant them outside our forts and instruct them to grow in whichever ways we please. The moats surrounding our forts are full of organic spikes, and if one of the enemy's soldiers retreats beyond the burning torches surrounding their siege camp, his throat will be swiftly caved in by a vine directed straight towards it by our most vicious war artificers. If the enemy ever attempts to siege the fortresses we have encircled with our vines, they will soon swiftly find that the terrain has placed them on the defensive.
