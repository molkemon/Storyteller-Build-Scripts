The damp jungle air is less hot and dense here, in a strange land nestling beneath a great mountain range. Here, say the songs, the great hero Aururã trekked up the great rocky walls in order to pick a herb to save his beloved. We follow his path from the clues in the songs, finding clear water and plentiful game. Brave souls take the first steps up the mountain when the foliage breaks. They report back to us great waterfalls and springs and a place where the songs are most harmonious. We gather up the Effelai's bounty and spend days trekking up. There, where we struggle to breathe, the vista of the Effelai takes away the last of our breath. The great green stretches to every horizon. Some point at the paths we have taken, and others look at ways we could move further. The world seems so small and yet so great from up here. We almost did not know the sky could be so vast. It is no wonder Aururã stopped here to marvel at the world. Now, all of our tribe has repeated his great journey. We drink deeply from the great waters up here, youngsters pick mountain flowers from precarious places to offer to their beloveds in honor of Aururã, and our festival songs echo down the mountain into the forest itself. Our shamans, meanwhile, go through the strange plants to find the most medicinal to bring with us. We brew teas and make cakes from the herbs of the mountains, and are filled with a comforting warmth that banishes all of the mountain chill. When morning comes and our fires are ash, we make the trip down, our hearts filled with hope, our minds full of knowledge, and our bellies full of medicine.
