The best suited to rule a region is someone from that region; this was the philosophy that I intended to bring to the Setedazari, and in truth I was pleasantly surprised by them. They had no problem understanding their place as subjects; after the studies I conducted on the area, I concluded this was likely because the region was never truly independent. To them, I represented just another Harpy Queendom that had come to conquer their lands; they were also pleasantly surprised to learn that they would retain autonomy, especially in matters of farming and mining, two key local sources of wealth. That could have been the end, but I was still not happy with their acceptance; they viewed me as just another in a long line of conquerors. The Queendom was a new master, yes, but one that would pass sooner or later. I needed to instill in them that we, Harpies, weren’t going to pass; this would be the new order. So I dug deeper into the region's archives and history and found a certain figure, Thelrion Nestezuir, who tried to kill the Phoenix Empress Jexis and was executed soon after. He was always treated as a blot on the region's history. But I uncovered some very interesting documents relating to the trial. Thelrion's motive was the centralization of power that the Phoenix Empire pursued. With this information, I am now funding multiple academics and scholars to study the documents I found. I will fund the study of Thelrion and the ideas he espoused, exhorting the virtues of a weak central government and greater local power. Thus, the academics and elites of Setedazari will see that the finest government is decentralized and allows local autonomy. Soon, they will associate this with the Queendom, since it’s how we rule and what I represent. This plan will take decades, maybe even centuries, to come to fruition, so I'd better begin. The military conquest of Setadazar is done, but the conquest of minds will begin by my hand.
