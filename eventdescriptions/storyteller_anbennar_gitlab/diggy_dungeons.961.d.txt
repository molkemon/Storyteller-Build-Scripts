We keep running, just putting one foot down before the other even as our bodies cry out that they cannot go any further. It is strange, almost as if we have pushed through an invisible wall into a hidden valley of energy, spurred onwards by fear into a steady stride we can just barely maintain. With the roar of the spider tsunami drowned out by the sound of our blood pounding in our ears, the decision to stop is one that must be made with only blind trust in the swiftness of our feet and the luck we have had so far.
