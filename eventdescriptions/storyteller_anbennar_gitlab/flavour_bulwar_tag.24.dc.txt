The advisor has finally completed their work on composing a great work of literature. Having been provided an exceedingly large sum of money for the deed, the philosopher ensured that the patronage was not squandered; the resulting work, known as "On the Nature of Faith", is hundreds of pages long and will surely be counted as one of the greatest works of the age, certain to become widespread as it is printed and distributed. It is a staunchly profound argument, certain to become the hallmark for works of its sort. The work argues that, based on events like the Age of Monsters, the only way to secure Surael's worship is to have a state dedicated to facilitating it. Likewise, the advancement of this government would be a natural good. It provides a strong case for us to strive for.
