Matthew Baker looked up from his whittling on the porch just in time to find his wife smiling down at him. "Come in, my love. Supper will be soon." Matthew stood up from his rocking chair, and took a lingering last look around at his land before heading inside. The cows were roaming his pasture like they were bovine nobility, and beyond his homestead he could see nothing but empty plains and open sky; all was well. What a beautiful land Corin had seen fit to bless them with. He made his way to the kitchen past the overflowing larder and the bedrooms full of children’s toys, and reflected on how he might soon need to build another annex to his house. As Matthew entered the kitchen, he heard the sound of crying. Corin's joy was fussing again, but one of her older sisters had the infant on her knee and was bouncing her up and down. He knew she would soon calm down; even a baby could not stay unhappy for long with the tantalizing smell of ynnea pig and tubers wafting through the room. He sat down at the head of the table, bowed his head to look at his tablecloth, colored red, of course, and began to speak the words of the supper benediction to Corin. "Corin, never has a family so undeserving been given so much. We do not know what else to ask of you, so we merely ask that you keep your eternal watch over the Baker family; Matthew, Jane, Tom, Hope, Peter, Kate, and Corin's joy. We devote this." "And Steadfast," Peter shouted. The children began to laugh, and Steadfast gave a bark from the ground as he heard his name. Matthew smiled in spite of himself. "Yes, and Steadfast too." Matthew lifted his head and raised his hands over his family. "We devote this meal to you, so that as we eat we might gain the strength to protect the weak and destroy the unjust, just as you have taught us. Praise Corin." A chorus of praise Corin rang out from the table, and the family began to eat.
