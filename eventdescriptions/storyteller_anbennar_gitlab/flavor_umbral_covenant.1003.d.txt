The rift opens, and I can see. Two women stand in the vestibule of a palace, in the threshold place where guests are not yet admitted and travelers have not yet left. It is the sort of half place where my Granny Ilfa thrives, a place where one can slip through the conceptual cracks of reality. And yet this is the kind of exchange which has happened so many times before; Granny Ilfa with her long fingers takes a baby from the other woman, whose face is etched in grief. This, too, is familiar—the grief of one who has sacrificed something to power which they can never retrieve. Did my mother look like this when she handed me over to my destiny?
