The tunnels stretched endlessly, each turn a copy of the last. Boots scraped over stone, and the air carried the sharp tang of fatigue and disappointment. The ruler marched at the front, their grip tightening on their sword. The monarch stopped, torchlight flickering against their worn features, and turned to their entourage. "One more," the ruler urged, their steely gaze meeting each weary pair of eyes. "Hehogrim wouldn't turn back." Grumbles rippled through the company, but they followed. They moved deeper into the earth, the air growing cooler and thick with mineral tang. Then, the lead scout halted abruptly, torch raised. Before them, the tunnel opened into a cavern of breathtaking enormity. Mithril veins snaked from the ground like the roots of a colossal tree, twisting and coiling upward, their shimmering light casting the chamber in a silvery-blue glow. The walls sparkled with embedded fragments, as though the cavern itself breathed with the essence of the earth. The monarch stepped forward, their shadow stretching across the radiant chamber. The ruler reached out, their fingers grazing the cool, smooth surface of a mithril vein. The metal hummed faintly under their touch, alive with a power that made their heart race. The dwarves stared in hushed wonder, their burdens forgotten. The faint sound of dripping water echoed like a melody, blending with the low hum of the mithril. The ruler knelt, their eyes reflecting the metallic glow. "Hehogrim led us here," the monarch whispered with reverence. "This is his blessing." One of their officers stepped closer, awe etched into their face. "What now, my lord?" The monarch rose slowly, sweeping the glittering expanse. "We claim his gift and build a future worthy of it."
