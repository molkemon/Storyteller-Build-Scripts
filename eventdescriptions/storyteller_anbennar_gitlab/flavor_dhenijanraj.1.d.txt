In the early days of the second Harimraj, Ramapalar the Reunifier sought to conquer the merchant cities of the South Rahen Coast, but the allied Raghimdeshi citystates offered stiff resistance to the ruler's armies. The Reunifier's son, Ramapalar the Azure Falcon, smashed the armies of the mercantile alliance when he forced them into battle at Shenral in thirteen thirty-three. His victory was short-lived, however; in a skirmish four days after the battle, a stray arrow glanced from the shields of his bodyguard and struck the young Raja in the eye, killing him instantly. Leaderless, the armies of the Harimraj retreated chaotically. On that fateful day, the doom of the southern cities was averted, and the Raj would never come so close to conquering the rich coastal plain of Raghimdesh; never, that is, until now. The Claw and Lotus Banner now flies over every city from Davharral to Sardika, and the great harbor of Sarkashabid is firmly under imperial control. Finally, the killers of the Azure Falcon kneel before the lotus throne. Their riches are now the Raja’s, as they should be by right, and the domain of Harimar’s rightful heirs stretches once more to the southern horizon.
