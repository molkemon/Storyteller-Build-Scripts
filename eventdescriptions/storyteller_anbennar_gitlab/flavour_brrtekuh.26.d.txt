No one knows where the first crack came from; some say it was a refusal of the payment of taxes; others that it was a vision of a priest that claimed a prophecy from Brrtekuh herself, that the council had lost her favour; others that a lightning strike hit the Brrtekuh statue in the capital and that was the signal. What is known by academics and most lay people as the defining moment is the episode of the Bloody Council. According to the rumours, a small working group of council members had met to solve some insignificant problem, just a discussion about water rights on a small tributary of the Sorrow. But the others saw it instead for something darker, something seditious, against the pack, against Brrtekuh. They murdered the "dissident" council gnolls and displayed their corpses around the capital. There is also no record of who authorised the murders or the brutal display, but what was known to all was that the council would not tolerate any dissent, no matter how minor it was. Instead of being scared, it backfired immediately; the population was horrified at what lengths the council had gone to, because Brrtekuh herself would never have done this. So it began the Age of Doubt in Brrtekuh.
