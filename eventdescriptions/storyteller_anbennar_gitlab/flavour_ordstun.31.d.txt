A single garnet carved into the shape of a heart, inscribed with runic prayers to stability, hangs over the entrance of every building in Ovdal Lodhum. At the opposite end of these dwellings is a diamond, shaped and imbued in just the same manner. Twin gems act as partners, keeping the Garnet Dwarves safe while they do their necessary work. In the depths of the hold, space contorts as they will it. Bedrock stretches and thins at the whims of their mages; pillars are fortified through powerful runes. It sometimes becomes hard to think in these protean tunnels, where gems pull at the mind as they pull at the stone. Outside the areas of chaotic permeability, though, the world is safe. For each gem which pierces reality, they create a corresponding twin that stabilizes the world outside of the workzone. The depths of Ovdal Lodhum are excavated without so much as a misplaced explosion or a collapsed tunnel. The Hold Where Love Lies is made beautiful, coated in garnets and diamonds which shine like the sun. They conquer the depths, and they do so together.
