There is blood in the air. EduzZokka is quiet. Outside, Bulwar shudders beneath the weight of its own conquest; charred temples, shattered thrones, a thousand banners sundered and cindered. The First Sun is dim behind bruised clouds, watching. Waiting. Atop the ziggurat, the ruler lies still. Nobody approaches; not the Kaxhkeddax in their finery, nor the Kelmaxherho clutching scrolls to their chests. They kneel as one, in the shadow of their undone lord. Then comes the sound. The air ruptures in a thick, wet tearing, echoing along the stone. The cadaver rises. Its mouth stretches, lipless and gaping, and something speaks from within. "I am awaited." It says. Convulsions. Limbs twist backward. Bones stretch and snap and boil into black, swirling skyward in a burning spiral. The world flashes green with felflame, carving glyphs across the heavens too fast to be easily read. Some of the scholars try, nonetheless; and they fall to the ground, clawing desperately at their eyes as they immolate in spontaneous emerald fire. Nobody moves to help them. The corpse swells. "I am Zokka," it cries, "The Once And Future Xhazobain." Nef Zokka shakes. Some citizens collapse in a rapturous frenzy, rending their clothes and their flesh, chanting halfinvented prayers in desperation. The city alights with their howls, shaking the air for miles and miles. "I will return." The voice promises, beginning to fade. "And when I do."
