Years of grueling training had sculpted the chivalrous knights into the finest warriors both Rósande and the Blade Stewards could field. Yet as battle between the two raged from dawn to dusk, no sense of honor survived. Friends fell, blades shattered, and armor buckled as all cohesion disappeared and the battle devolved into a brutal melee. The Blade Stewards’ efforts, however, were futile; outnumbered yet valiant to the end, they fought down to the last man. The monarch walked through the remains of their hideout, through the puddles of blood which had yet to dry, past the corpses whose glassy eyes still remained open. Leaving behind a trail of bloody footsteps, the ruler reached the blind master, who had been captured while trying to flee with Calindal, The Gleaming Blade. "It’s not practical to flee when you’re blind, is it?" the monarch said, with the faintest hint of pity in his voice. The man looked into the king's eyes, as if he knew exactly where they were. He spat on the ground. "Even with my lack of vision, I can see that you are unworthy to wield it." The ruler’s mouth curled into a smirk as he relished the ignorance of the supposed master. "Luckily, I have people who can carry it back for me."
