Ever since the defeat of Kheterata at the hands of the gnolls, Horashesh has been in political turmoil. Once advised by bureaucrats called Medawor, the local rulers, called the Efergezmu, have now entered a powersharing agreement in which they find themselves dissatisfied. Local elections between the Medawor and Efergezmu end in deadlock or dissatisfied parties as often as not. The landed Medawor, on their vast estates, exert disproportionate power on the traditional Efergezmu political structure and wrest power for themselves. This powersharing has left the Horasheshi divided and the locals hungry for a rebalancing of the scales of state, and our support might just tip the situation in their favor.
