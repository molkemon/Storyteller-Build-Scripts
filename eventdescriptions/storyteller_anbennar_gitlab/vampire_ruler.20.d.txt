The monarch has long thought they could banish half of their court and scarcely notice the difference. Indeed, they have been tempted by the idea. half the influence-seeking plots, half the foolish schemes, half the unsought advice. While a few specimens stand out, who do offer useful council, the vast majority have nothing to offer. A more selfish and self-centered lot could not be found anywhere in the realm. Thus, the ruler rests easy having thinned their number by one, knowing few will note the absence, and fewer care. The courtier's blood, which so wonderfully slakes the monarch's thirst, shall serve much better than their brain ever could.
