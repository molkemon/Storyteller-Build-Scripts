In the eternity that had passed for it, the Trader of Masks and Faces had met countless beings. Elves born to this forest and elves born to the Fallen Empire. Goblins of the caves and goblins of the Lost Kingdom. Orks that followed the son of a god and orks who had turned the attention of the Prince of Ashes. The creature, this Otar, that stood before them was different. Changed. Twisted in some fundamental, irreversible, all-encompassing way. But these were idle observations, simple details added to the archfey’s ledgers as it awaited a response to its offer. The build up to the moment had been short and wordless, for the Trader had learnt that diplomacy meant little to the Otar and its hordes. They had merely needed to approach the beasts at the opportune moment and unfurl two of its many hands. One held the knife. The other, a faded mask, radiating with power. "This knife is hunger. It yearns for you. Take it. Cut yourself apart. Take a new face." Little more was needed to understand the nature of the exchange.
