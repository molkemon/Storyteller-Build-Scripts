When the lights first appeared beneath the realm's waters, confusion and dread abounded. Yet year after year, the submerged stars of merfolk curated baubles that at first were viewed with suspicion were increasingly regarded as a beloved local landmark. Then, one bright foggy morning, the truth arrived, told by a slick blackscaled merfolk peddling her coral to a curious buyer. "Hmm; I believe an aristocrat hoped to earn a plankton of lordship—ah, a little bit, a small amount—yes, a token of political power. As a brash show of force, he ordered the site to mark our territory’s limits against your port; we assumed your kind would dismantle it, honestly." "After his promotion to some sunken trench, we preserved the fences, seeing it as nothing more than a braggart’s line in the sand. It attracts so many of you landfolk, and on the darkest nights, your lights on land shine like the airy stars." She wiggled her spiraling ribbon fin in wondrous delight. "Maybe you can visit it sometime? Your brilliance captivates unlike any other."
