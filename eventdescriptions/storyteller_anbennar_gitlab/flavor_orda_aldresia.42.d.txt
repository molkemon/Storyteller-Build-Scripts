The Godspeaker is brought before the assorted Aldresian hosts, shouting and yelling at their dazed prisoner. It is a clear day with heavy winds whipping through metal armor, creating a constant, low hiss, like a boiling pot kettle. The smell of blood permeates the air as a few knights carry off the hacked bodies of the rebelling Veridicals. Most have been cowed into subservience, as what were once orderly knights have turned into what some dissonant voices, even now, are calling barbarian hordes. Alice is still unsure what to call her knightsinarms. She is the firstborn daughter of Pearlsedge and should have had the throne, by all rights, but was banished to Orda Aldresia for being unruly. She resents her father for this, and would gladly take his head if needed. But her mother and siblings? Did they truly deserve the retribution that Emil was calling for? Alice crosses her arms and leans back as Emil approaches the podium. From this distance he is barely a blur, but the shape of the courtyard allows his voice to carry. "Brothers and Sisters. As we spread the word of God, there are those who backstab in the darkness. And how disgusting must it be to learn such traitors included the so-called Godspeaker themself. Such sin must have consequences, and by the light of our blades we will purge this heresy." Emil roars, but to a troubled discord of a response. He raises his blade, ignoring the divide as more shouts and jeers arrive. Emil has harnessed the rage of Alice’s fellow Aldresians, but how much more could they stomach? Was it all worth it? Emil approaches the Godspeaker and puts his blade at their neck. The Second Son smiles and puffs his chest out, raising his head so everyone can hear him.
