The soldiers couldn’t help but feel a bit silly marching around the ruins on the first day. There wasn’t an enemy to fight besides some enterprising vandals, and even those came infrequently and could be dealt with by a stern talking to. There were only ten or so of them, and they were marching around the fallen stone in their nicest uniforms. Their bright attire flashed against the sunbleached stone. If real invaders came, who were bent on destroying the ancestral sites, all they’d have to do is walk right on in. On the second day, some people came to watch. The soldiers marched a bit straighter, and took their corners at a sharper angle. On the third day, the spectators brought friends, and one of them was an artist. A scene was painted of the day, and it was given to the soldiers to hang in the ruins themselves. Things continued on like this for a while. By the end of the first month, the soldiers had the best assignment in their domain, and the ruins had been painted to look new again.
