Within the throne room, the ruler wears a hard expression. Before them only a half dozen disheveled expeditionaries stand, a sobering fraction of the original thirty. Having only just arrived in the capital this afternoon, these clearly exhausted elves had insisted on recounting their tale. And so, the monarch listened. It began simply enough, with the expedition sailing down the Elyn near Vyr Cors. Upon exiting the Deepwoods, the expeditionaries found themselves in the midst of a dreadful swamp with an unnatural aura. By the second day they were repeatedly accosted by savage bestial creatures and other unnatural monsters. On and on it went until one day the vessel they were sailing on was lost to the creatures, causing the expeditionaries to scatter. Of the group that arrived, there had originally been fourteen. But one by one they had been taken by the swamp, stolen away by the doglike beasts in the night, lost to the treacherous earth made thick and viscous, or, worst of all, torn and mangled by short tailed, winged monsters of the swamp. The elves then dropped to a whisper. They never stopped to help their taken comrades. They never even stopped to look back. All they could do was run and pray they would see home again.
