On the shore of the Silent Lake, the ruler. The guard bowed his head and stepped back, keeping close eye on the elf that he had brought before the ruler. The ruler's gaze lingered upon the elf, scanning them; they weren't a Wood Elf, that much was certain. Upon their shoulder sat an owl, presumably a familiar of sorts. Something else seemed off… perhaps the elf was blinking too slowly? Perhaps her eyes shifted in a strange manner; or was it the cant of her head? The ruler wrenched their attention back to the guard. "I see. Very well, I shall hear what she has to say." The peculiar elf stepped forth, offering a bow; the movement was unrefined, as if the very act of making such a motion was strange to the elf. "Lidrandel Duskweaver, at your service, ruler. I am from a land far away; a realm of elves, much like your own. I've told many of your people of these lands, the Redglades, as they are known. Lush forests, albeit smaller than yours. I would fain return to my homeland, and it seems that a handful of your own people would be eager to accompany me. I did not wish to cause trouble by leading your people into foreign lands without your permission." The ruler sat silently for a moment, before nodding. "You have done well to seek leave for this; it speaks to your character. You shall have my endorsement and support. I know not how you have gotten here, yet I know the fey to be eager tricksters. You may recruit whomever would follow you, and I shall send some of my own warriors." Lidrandel Duskweaver bowed once more, a smile gracing her lips. "Thank you, ruler. Your generosity knows no bounds."
