The monarch could still hear from the window the tingling of a couple of swords clashing in the distance, but it was the cacophony of doors kicked open, broken windows, and cries of Urvand's inhabitants that was filling their ears. Sounds of victory. They were marching through the palace, the ruler, and his chief diplomat, straight to the throne room to confront the ruler of Larankar. The ruler fought tooth and nail for his capital. he and the monarch dueled under the city's walls, without reaching a conclusion, both wounded and unable to continue; the pulsating pain of the deep cut he got in his upper arm would persist for months. The ruler and the diplomat were arguing of course, but at least they had the decency of doing it before meeting the ruler of Larankar. "They must be executed, the monarch and the heir both. Mercy is a weakness we can't afford, else any little warlord in Taychend will believe it's fine to defy us. They need to be an example." "If Olhokar had followed your example, your realm wouldn't exist, nor will ours last if we do. A magnanimous ruler will be respected everywhere my Lord, and respect goes a longer way than fear does. To rage against a defeated enemy shows only poor character."
