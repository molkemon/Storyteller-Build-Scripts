I peer behind the rocky dip and double take seeing the clear lake. It’s a greener shade of blue, surrounded by a small beach. A few pink flamingos drink deeply from its bounty, coming and going as peaceful vagabonds. There must be a river somewhere on the northside; stagnant water wouldn’t attract animals. Done sightseeing, Captain? asks Trystan, the ranger of our group, with a smirk. I snort. He’s young and raring for a fight with the Pearlsedgers; with age, he’ll learn to enjoy the peaceful moments. Before we can start our descent, the mage raises a hand, stopping me. Hold on a moment, Kuok, she pipes up. Give me ten minutes and I can cast a spell to locate the largest pearl here. We’d spend less time splashing about. She holds up a forked twig and her wand. I nod in agreement, much to the sigh of our ranger. We have to be smart about this; the Pearlsedgers will resort to violence if threatened. I lean down against the hill and my eyes stray toward the lake.
