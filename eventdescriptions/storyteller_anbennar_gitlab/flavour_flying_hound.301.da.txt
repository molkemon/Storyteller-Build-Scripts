Every realm as it exists in the present is built on the glorious and effulgent skeleton of past tales, and the country is no different. The relations between a prince and their subjects are the product of centuries of disagreements and compromises, centralization and disintegration of power, and the changing character of the individuals around whom power flows. Navigating these relationships and privileges can be maddening, but with a patient soul and a just heart it is not only possible, but necessary for the right function of a realm. You remind yourself of this mantra as you are once again called to settle a dispute between several of your vassals, minor established hound riders who profess contradictory statements concerning the number of adventurers killed, the size of obstacles jumped, and a dozen other honorwounding matters. Settling these disputes is far from anyone's favorite topic, but you are readier than most to take it on, knowing that your subjects look at you all the while, gasping in awe at the quality of gallantry emanating from your apparel, and the infinite pride contained within your poise. Most who attempt to emulate the archetypal chevaliers do so from the saddle, sword in hand, shouting about glory to no one in particular, but you have come to understand that the courtly feudal apparatus of it all hangs on by a mere thread, and it’s best that the source of said thread is a tremendously regal mantle on your shoulders. One by one, you have astounded all the seemingly endless groups come to petition you for something or other. They will come tomorrow too. But with the gallant pride that only a true knight can display, you will make your realm fairer, more efficient, and better for everyone living in it; just as soon as the diadem is back from polishing.
