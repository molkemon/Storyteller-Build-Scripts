The path is many things. It is war, and it is peace. It is love, and it is loss. It is meditation, and it is action. To be, and not to be. To do, and not to do. To think, and not to think. To ponder, to pursue. Dekhana, Pichka. The Nikitkamarja stretches out before us and demands both of us. And we are prepared to be either, as the need arises.
