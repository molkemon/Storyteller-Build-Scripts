As I patrol the city I yawn, a swarm of children running by. The game they play so happily is theirs and theirs alone. They are lively and energetic no matter how one might pry. Proof of the domain over life as their feet clatter on stone. The sun bears down on me hatefully as I walk further. The water trickles past me, carried by great streams. I dip my hands and drink of her soothing ambrosia and feel the power of her third domain in me like soothing dreams. Glass shatters, shouted curses erupt nearby, my heart races. My blade jumps to my hand, I race towards the commotion. I burst through the door of the humble perfumery, scanning for violent traces, and find two goddesses kneeling on the ground. Broken pottery is scattered around them. I see myself in the jagged edges and shattered purpose. Yet the two women are deeply radiant, and they pick up the pieces with a careful tenderness. “Oh goodness, look at us,” one of them says, and I obey her honeyed words eagerly. “Welcome to Leli’s fragrances, Paladin. We apologize for the disturbance.” I shake my head, deep shame clouding my feelings. “It is I to apologize to you. I heard the commotion and feared the worst.” The second woman smiles with uplifting radiance, and my heart leaps and bounds out of my chest. “Thank you for the concern,” her silken voice blesses me. “I am Felana, and she my wife, Leli. Would you maybe stay with us for a bit? Try some of Leli’s perfume while you sit?” I nod and smile widely, my awe hard to conceal. “I am Kyola Heartblade, paladin in training, and I would deeply love to,” I replied with great zeal.
