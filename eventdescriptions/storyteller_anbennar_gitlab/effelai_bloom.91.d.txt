Years of deafening cacophony have taken their toll on the soldiers. Families across the country grieve the loss of their loved ones, angry that they were sent to fight in a jungle. But finally, it seems that the cost has exceeded the potential benefit in the eyes of rulers across the continent. As the troops have begun to pull out, the aggression of the jungle has subsided, and the smaller settlements on the perimeter have come under attack less frequently. Though the cycle of raid and repair may never truly end for the new outposts, the extreme tumultuousness of this war on the land has subsided, and the folk have begun to speak once more of a possible future for themselves and their families.
