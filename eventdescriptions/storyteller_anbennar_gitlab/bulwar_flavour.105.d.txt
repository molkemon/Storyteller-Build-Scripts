After the fall of Jaher's Empire, the Golden Highway that spanned from Bulwar to Haless in the far east rotted away. The section of the Highway coursing through the Salahad's milder northeastern climate suffered constant harpy raids, and gnoll raiders controlled the western Harra oasis, forcing the caravans to adapt their routes by going right through the middle of the Salahad desert. The szalMehran, a reputed Mašnsih tribe living in the midst of the desert, established a prosperous trade route along the ruthless Salahad. Using strategic locations such as the port city of Edešukuru, their homelands in Shimlardu, and the safety of the walls of Azkasur, these Mašnsih prospered as guides for the trade caravans who preferred to brave the Salahad. With our newfound control over these key cities, the SzalMehran are finding more and more willing customers who relish the opportunity to travel through a route under the jurisdiction of a single country, no longer fearing that patrols from differing states may impose a new tariff on their goods. The stability brought by our unification of the SzalMehran route has led to an influx of Raheni goods in the country, and though one day the return of safer land routes may render the SzalMehran route moot, we can still reap its benefits in the foreseeable future.
