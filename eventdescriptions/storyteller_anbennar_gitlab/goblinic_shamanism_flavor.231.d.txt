And now it is the Chief Magistrate of Religious Injuries' turn to give his report. While not the most particularly exciting part of the weekly council meeting, the monarch and the council view it as a matter of utmost importance to achieve a harmonious society between all of the faiths within the realm. This time, however, the magistrate comes with a writing slate rather than his usual stack of reports. "To be honest with you, your majesty," he begins, "I haven’t been in the whole week. No reports have been coming through my doors for the past five days. It seems that religious relations have been at an all-time high and I expect that they will only continue to improve." "What about the hatchet and manure incident?" pipes up another shaman. "That one is none of our business; regular violence remains a constant."
