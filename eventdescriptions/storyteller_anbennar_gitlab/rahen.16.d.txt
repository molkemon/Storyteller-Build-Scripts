In a field full of high caste individuals, Indrana Lalikesh Vinashkar proved that arts were accessible to everyone, even for middle caste people like himself. Born in Pordhatti, the child proved himself a prodigy who quickly attracted local patronage. By the age of eleven, he was already a master of the paintbrush and was sent to Khiraspid to learn all that could be taught of matters of ink and brush, studying under the master painters of the time. His works were brilliant yet unconventional, focused on the contrast of light and shadow on landscape and face, and sharply contrasted with the regimented stylization of the time. Though his controversial departures from established norms saw him condemned by many leading artists of the time, I.L. Vinashkar’s works revolutionized the way that people across Haless thought about painting. His “Alachaya” style inspired countless artists up and down the Kharunyana river in the decades after his death in fifteen thirty-nine, when the great artist was given a state funeral by the Good Raja Hemantsen and Rani Jira.
