The past few years have been hectic, and yet in many ways they have united the Command in ways never before seen. The word 'Shamanism' has entered the common parlance, signifying any philosophy which would sympathize with the perfidious Shamans or denigrate their enemies, the Great Command. Public acceptance of the Shaman Hunts has grown, with many villages even forming their own congregations of Shaman Hunters. The Shinukhorchi Missive has become a holy text of sorts, its prognostications of doom and entreaties towards tradition a salve against a world always and forever going mad. Where once the Grand Marshal had dismissed the warnings of the Shinukhorchi Missive as ridiculous fearmongering, now the ruler sees opportunity. The administration of the Command's new conquests has not been easy, its claims to governance resting on military strength and the legacy of the Sir Revolt. Perhaps one problem could solve the other? The Command's claim to legitimacy comes through war. War protects its people, fills its coffers, makes clear who ought to lead. Therefore, the Command must always remain at war. We shall declare a new war, one waged against Shamans and Shamanism, and it will be led by the loyal RonpunyoUra. Absorbing the dozens of Shaman Hunting groups that have sprung up, they shall assume a number of new responsibilities and powers. the power to investigate anyone, even highly ranked members of the Command structure, on suspicion of Shamanism. The responsibility to regularly assure the populace that Korashi production is alive and well, with shows of productive capacity to scare the Shamans into submission. And lastly, the ability to secure warrants of arrest for any individual whose investigation has produced sufficient evidence to warrant doubt as to their loyalties. Both secret police and celebrated veteran, the Soldiers of Peace form the basis of our new administration, a style of governance based on the principles of war we know so well.
