As the monarch stepped into the Fateweaver’s chamber, they couldn’t help but be swallowed up by its majesty. The massive hall, carved deep into the hills, was bathed in the blue glow that emanated from the great Dame’s tearjewel at its far end. Beneath it, at the apex of a pyramid of smoothstone steps, hung the skeletal corpse of the Fateweaver – its arms outstretched and skull wrenched back, pierced with the relicthreads of prophecy, tendrils of ruinsteel that rose to the crystal above. “Beautiful, is it not?” the Threadbearer asked, as she walked up beside the monarch. “We ought to go closer.” It took them a few minutes to reach the base of the stairs. Each step, the monarch knew, was one taken by Darakash, Olhokar, Laskaris, countless conquerors and warlords and traders and politicians of ages past. They had each sought a glimpse of the future beneath the hills of Korrimutren – now, it was the monarch’s turn. As they reached the steps, the Threadbearer turned, motioning the monarch to halt. “When I commune with the Fateweaver, I will be channeling a power above any you could imagine. My own exceptional divination abilities are all that prevent a very gruesome death… are you as strong as me?” The monarch shook their head, blushing slightly despite their station – the Threadbearer nodded. “Then I shall commune for you. Stand back.” The Threadbearer ascended to the Fateweaver’s corpse. She took a deep breath, closed her eyes, then placed her hand on the Precursor’s skull. All was calm for a moment, as wisps of magic trailed up the Threadbearer’s hand – then her eyes snapped open, glowing brilliant and blue. The wisps became a rush, as the Threadbearer’s pure white gown billowed against its force, and a light within the jewel above burned bright enough to blind, and the world seemed to tremble at the power.
