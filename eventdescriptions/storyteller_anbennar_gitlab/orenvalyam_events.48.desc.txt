War was Aelantier. Crucibles from across the continent produced en masse blade and matchlock and spellstaff alike. The shipyards of Noruin and the Cleaved Sea assembled an armada in a day, raising the vessels that would carry them across the sea. The troops trained, and drilled, and hardened themselves for Dawn. On the day of the invasion, as the armies and fleets of Orenvalyam gathered before him, the Kamrayakval was brought a containercastinsteel, warded, protected, guarded. Within was a simple collar, wrought iron. But this was no slave collar; it was the instrument of the End, the weapon that would see their task through. And at last, the faith needed it once more. There would be no slow coaxing into Dawn’s project this time. No struggle for control between weapon and wielder. No descent, no fall—merely ascension. This time, the Dragon knew what must be done. The Dragon had made his pact. Today, his new wearer was merely renewing it. The Kamrayakval lifted the collar and clicked it around his neck. And then, the rumbling. An explosion of steam and smoke, seen by millions, felt by all Halann. A roaring scream of scraping metal and ancient fury, primordial, earsplitting, soulpiercing. And from the mist, arose a Dragon in Armour Form, an eclipse against the sun, twin bladed, single purposed. There was a knowing, an understanding—from the feyforests of Eordand to the depths of Nanru Nakar, from the slave markets of Fangaula to the High Temples of Haless, from the poorest beggar to the richest king—that the end of their world was nigh.
