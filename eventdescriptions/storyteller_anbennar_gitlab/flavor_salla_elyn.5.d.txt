Upon the same coastline that our people first discovered with a notable expedition now stands a grand monument to Munas Moonsinger himself; the construction only recently having been completed. The monument, consisting of a series of stone pillars surrounded by wooden platforms, stands at its highest point an impressive fifty meters tall. The stone has been carved in a manner that leaves a profusion of gaps and holes in the pillars, and which, when pummeled by the coastal winds, cause the stone to emanate a soft, somber hum. This ghostly melody is dedicated to Munas himself; a song to venerate his legacy. Chiselled upon each stone is a portion of Munas Moonsinger’s life story, so that any and all visitors may read the tales of the Moonsinger. This monument, though it was built to strengthen the bonds between our people and those of the Moon Elves, has become a place of pilgrimage for many elves, even for some Sea Elves. Many from across all of Cannor and the seas beyond have made their way to the monument to pay their respects to Munas Moonsinger, honoring him as a most cherished forefather and a deity of the Regent Court.
