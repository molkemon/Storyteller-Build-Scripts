Talented as you may be, the court advisor begins, "Talent is no guarantee of supremacy. What will you do when you are older, and the expectations of talent are traded for that of performance?" The heir's eyes flash before responding. "I do not need you. I do not need this magic. I am not some lousy weapon to be spent again and again. Blood rusts the blade." The heir leans back in their chair, only to be disciplined via the swift application of a ruler across the wrists. "I have a dozen students," responds the tutor, "and eleven of them will practice spells until their mana expenditure grows dangerous." The heir nurses their stinging hands. "Eleven will peruse any tome given, no matter how esoteric. Eleven would leap at the chance to be given fieldwork such as this, to put down a revolt in the name of the government. Do you believe yourself so invulnerable that they will not surpass you? The crown is no invincible artifact." Immediately, the heir lunges forward sharply at the implicit threat. "I will kill you. I will bury a blade in the small of your back and it will be a mundane dagger, and you will die all the same." It is rare for the monarch to be summoned when dealing with their heir's child; the privilege of grand living is the right to have others care after one's progeny. While normally commending the young heir to the care of tutors, on this occasion a firm parental hand might be required.
