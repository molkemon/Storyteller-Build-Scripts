Day twenty. Today I spoke more with Kunya. Day twenty. Today the camp residents got together and started organizing a market. The guards don't seem to mind us trading the trinkets we take from off the battlefield. I think some of them might be amenable to haggling themselves. Day thirty. I traded for some chicken eggs and shared with Kunya. Day sixty. The Oni came out of the nearby High Temple today. They seem to be carrying more of that black stone. I suppose I know why we are situated where we are. Day one hundred. I found a small patch of land to plant peppers this coming spring. Day one hundred and eighty. This marks twenty men dead by my hand. Oh Dipar have mercy. Himmat was injured. I pray for his quick recovery. Day two hundred. Kunya moved in with me into my tent. I shan't kiss and tell. Day two hundred and ten. Himmat is recovered, though with a bum leg. Day four hundred. It occurs to me how impossible this life would be outside this camp. I have a partner, who is a hobgoblin. She is heavy by me, and the other residents celebrate it. Such a pairing would not have been acceptable back home. I have friends, and chickens, and a pepper patch in addition to meals. It is not unpleasant. I am not sure how I feel about that. Day five hundred. We are moving today. The tents picked up, heavier shackles placed on us. Kunya is going to another camp. I begged for us to stay together, the guards struck me twice about the head, leaving burns. Himmat. Two miles down the road they decided he was slowing us down too much. His head rolled on the grass. His eyes did not close.
