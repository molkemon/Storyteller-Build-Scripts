The monarch holds the Aegis up to the light, smiling at it like an old friend. The diamond shines and shimmers, a star in the sky, and it looks so beautiful now that the ruler can actually witness it. The gem cannot pull the ruler any further, for the ruler has already seen what lies within. The ruler looks down, watching refracted light illuminate portholes in the floor; the ruler witnesses layers of civilization from the highest level of ArgÔrdstun, seeing a beehive of a hold hum with activity. Millions of dwarves craft relics, runes, laughter, song, and prosperity in a hold of countless riches. All of this—this beauty, this joy, this reverence—eclipses anything the monarch’s ancestors could have imagined, back in those days of endless isolation. In Szetur Ôrdstunov, their every fantasy has been fulfilled. The monarch fixes the ruler’s eyes forwards, placing the Aegis and its crown back on the ruler’s forehead. The ruler then begins the trek towards the pliant Gleaming Council, ready to proclaim the nation’s primacy over all the dwarves of Halann, ready to hear them chant the ruler’s name without a single voice of dissent. The ruler can feel the weight of empire tense upon their chest. In these last moments of quiet and of peace, the monarch thinks of the Diamond Queens. “May they be proud,” the ruler thinks, “of all we have accomplished. May they be proud of ArgÔrdstun, and of Szetur Ôrdstunov.”
