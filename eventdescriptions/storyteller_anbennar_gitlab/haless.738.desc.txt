The Luzhang Baota, located in Yingzhen, has long stood as a symbol of peace and remembrance in Yanshen. Since its construction in twelve oh two, its beacon has never dimmed. Legends claim that the eternal flame reflects both the thoughts of the dead and the wishes of the living. During the Festival of the Moon, thousands gather to burn incense and light lanterns, creating a tide of light across the mountain city. In the past, stewardship of the tower fell to the Luzhang Order, founded by oathbound monks and exiled scholars. They maintained the rituals and safeguarded the tower's peace, vowing to guard the souls of the dead and soothe the hearts of the living. But in troubled times, a faction of assassins seized influence in Yingzhen, covertly usurping the Order. Though the tower appeared sacred, its shadows concealed knives. Preachers whispered secrets, and the once holy site became a center of subterfuge and intelligence. Now, Yingzhen has long been under our rule. The city is stable, and the Order has officially broken free from clandestine control. Yet rumors persist. Some within the old Order petition to restore its original mandate; local elites argue for a full restructuring under civil authority; others suggest leaving things be to avoid unrest. The flame atop Luzhang still burns—but which path should its light now illuminate?
