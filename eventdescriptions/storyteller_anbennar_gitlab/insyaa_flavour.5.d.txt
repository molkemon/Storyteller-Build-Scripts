What started off as a military outpost to prevent the resurgence of the termite colony in the megapolis has quickly become a bustling settlement, filled with new colonists seeking to make use of the protection from the elements and hinuifauna that the mound naturally provides. These incoming colonists have transitioned into life in the mound remarkably well, building rudimentary buildings within the mound's central cavity and even taking over management of the termite's abandoned fungal farms to sustain the quickly growing colony. Colonists living in the mound have discovered that the material of which the mound is made has various beneficial properties. The material, mostly composed of a mix of termite saliva, fungal resin, soil, and feces, has self-repairing properties that allow the material to stiffen under stress. This material is clearly the reason the mound has been able to reach such gargantuan heights without collapsing in on itself. The fungal resin mixed into the mound’s material appears to have thermally conductive properties, promoting airflow within the mound. The termites are truly far greater engineers than we ever could have imagined. Regarding said termites, small rump colonies continue to survive in the dark corners of the megapolis, sometimes coming into conflict with the settlers living within the mounds. With the sheer size of the mound, it will likely take years to fully exterminate the vermin from their old home. In due time though, the mound will finally be ours and ours alone.
