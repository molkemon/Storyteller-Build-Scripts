The most scared I have ever been of you was on the battlefield, after the fall of Sapchopper and the reclamation of our grove. You were covered in blood, and the air around you crackled with that sharp, metallic smell that comes after great magic. Your lightning is terrifying, but it wasn’t that that scared me; it was the look in your eyes. I have never seen you like that before; you are normally so hopeful, sweet, my warm friend. But I did not see any of her in you that day. I just saw fire. It is what I picture the Ashentree fey to be, all hate and loathing. You stood over the captured orks, Alara beside you in her ornate armor, judge and executioner. I was just in earshot, and I felt your rage as you said, "I hate them, Alara." I will never think less of you, no matter what, but I admit my heart stood still as I listened to her reply.
