They come before the ruler with frantic movements. With frenzied eyes, the priests proclaim that Dookan himself has appeared in their visions during the past night and urged them to do battle, to meet the enemy face to face and prove Dookan’s might. Immediately, there is uproar. The countless warriors gathered in the wide hall are quick to demand that the army set out and meet their foes. They demand that their swords, spears, and axes be sharpened, and that the ruler lead them into a glorious victory in Dookan’s name. There is nothing else to be said. The priest’s demands, Dookan’s demands, have been heard. They must be obeyed, lest one be branded a coward and forsaken by Dookan. No matter how unfavorable the battle, no matter how unprepared the warriors, a struggle against the enemy must occur. Only then will Dookan be satisfied, and only then will his favor be bestowed upon the strong, to those who are true to his will.
