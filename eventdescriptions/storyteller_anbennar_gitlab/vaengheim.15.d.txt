Then did the host of the ruler come upon a great cavern, and all were struck by its splendour, for each and every surface was clad in mail of glittering ice. From some distant place did the sun shine upon it, and their rays were by the ice ensnared and brought to this place far below the earth. And in the ice did one spy any hue the eye could see or the mind could dream of, and in the gloom did the light remind those who saw it, of the warmth of distant summer, and the daylight far above. And some were taken by longing, though their tears froze on their cheeks. And in the center of this place there was a vast pit, leading to unseen places far below. And a waterfall, turned to shining ice, did plunge boldly into it, and the ruler turned to their host and said, “Alone must we wingmaidens go, for we have little time to give. Return to the day with your heads held high, and know that you did what you could.” And some did turn to go, but one of the Dragonkin, whose name was Alra Lazyteeth, did raise her voice, “Go o queen, but know that we follow. For whether man, ork, troll or other, our minds are not yet so cold, that we cannot find a way into this pit. We will come to you when we can, for by pick and skilled carver, shall we forge a path that the wingless may walk.” And those who had turned, now turned again, their faces set in resolve. As their host set to their toil did the ruler see them, and their heart was warmed, for these were their people. Then they took wing.
