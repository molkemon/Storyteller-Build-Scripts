"I shouldn't even be here, you know," said the trailing member of the force sent to hunt the Hook Horror. "My father was a silversmith in the finest shop in Gor Vazumbrog, and his father was a goldsmith in Rubyhold. It's those damn golems... I'd have been a fine blacksmith, I tell you, but how could I compete with a worker who never sleeps, never complains, and doesn't need to be paid." "Oh, shut your mouth," countered another. "There's still plenty of blacksmiths in Gor Vazumbrog; you're just not skilled enough to keep up. You just focus on yourself; golems are the least of your worries." The greatest of his worries, as it turned out, should have been that his bellyaching would attract the monster they were supposed to be chasing. And indeed, a hooked hand tore the man's hand down in midgesture, closing the door to further debate. Fortunately, the would-be smith's screams alerted the rest of the hunters to the trouble, and the beast was quickly dispatched in a flurry of flashing halberds and pikes, preventing any further casualties. With the monster dispatched, its carcass was put to good use. its horrid claw was dismembered and fashioned into a grim substitute for the stolen hand, and a reminder of what could never be.
