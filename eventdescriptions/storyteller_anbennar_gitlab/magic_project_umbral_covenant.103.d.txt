You are being sentimental. Ikomeye is a strange star in the constellation of the lattice, which anyway is not precisely an image held in the mind but a feeling, a new sense. It is difficult to describe; it is like explaining taste to one born without a tongue. Yet where Ikomeye is in the weave is a tangled mire, a mess of impressions; the nightmare life she had before she joined the Vile One, that which fired her taste for vengeance, conveyed with every thought she transmits. Considering the unbearable lack of privacy, Ikomeye is holding up rather well. It is not sentimental, Ikomeye; it’s a question of resource allocation. You have your shadow, Ikomeye replies. All conventional magical focus should be on expanding the lattice as quickly as possible; that is our aim here. We must survive while we do that. We will. The Vile One receives a sense impression, Ikomeye clicking her tongue, gesturing. You are lying to me; you forget I am part of your lattice now too, that I can see. You simply do not want to appear a tyrant, to give away that you can change the focus of all your mages at once. To give away that our minds respond to yours, follow yours. Ikomeye, that is not true. It is true. We have accepted a goddess in you, kitame. Do not be so gauche as to pretend otherwise. Merely act as a goddess does.
