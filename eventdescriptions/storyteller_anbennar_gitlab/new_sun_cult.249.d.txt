On a particularly sweltering night, where not even the slightest evening breeze brought relief from the day’s oppressive heat, the monarch was on their way to yet another banquet celebrating the newfound vitality of magic cultivated within the realm. The monarch was delayed by arkimaguš Ahmahan, who requested a private audience after the banquet. Wary but reluctantly intrigued by the request, the monarch sought out the leader of the ZarLereškal for a clandestine meeting, where they were presented with an intriguing offer. The ZarLereškal assert knowledge about an ancient rite preserved from the time of the Godkings that would infuse a worthy individual such as the heir with potent magical energies, transforming them into a powerful mage in their own right. All they ask for in return is the patronage and protection of the throne. Yet the palace has many eyes and ears, and it was during the morning prayers on the next day, that the high kašra attempted to dissuade the monarch from agreeing to this tempting bargain. Instead, they offer the full and enduring support of the clergy for the reformed magic laws and any other righteous undertaking of the throne should the ZarLereškal be rebuked.
