The monarch returned to Vængheim, leaving Freydis Oaktongue and her people in the house of her forefathers. And for many days there was silence. Yet in the days of the current year did Freydis return to Vængheim in great haste, and many heads did she turn, and the hall fell silent, for all knew that when Freydis spoke, it was wise to listen. And Freydis took knee before the monarch and said, "O queen, I return with songs that will ruffle your feathers. For I have read the stones of Gullmork tower, and I have spoken with my kin and yours, and many maps of the west, old and new, have I gazed upon. I say to you this. There is a place in Aelantier that I have found, that holds the key to Gullmork tower. If it be made mine, I will make yours the craft of my forefathers." And the monarch rose and said, "It is a mighty promise you offer. Were you another, I would laugh at your jest. Yet I hear your words and call them true. Aelantier is too far to fly, so we must sail. What say you to this?" And Freydis bowed her head and said, "My people know the price you name and call it too dear. For they fear naught but the Giant's Grave, where our forefathers lie drowned, and the waves grow tall, and water deep. I will come with you, though this bane puts my heart to fright." Then did the monarch call upon those in their house who sought to know of the world that was, and once more did many skalds hear their call and find it worthy. And a fleet was mustered at the port of Sporreyn, to brave the Lament and meet the sunset.
