The closing speech of the Technomancers comes not a moment too late, as the evening drags into the early morning. The future of Artificery in the Empire and indeed all Cannor has been grasped firmly within Giberdi's hands for decades if not centuries to come. "My friends. My friends," cries the monarch. "I must thank you all, all of you good chaps and ma'ams who came so far to our fair. To our humble city. To think that not even four hundred years ago, we were but a singular city and her lands, stifled at every turn, and with a hopeless cause. Our fathers and mothers must be proud that we have come so far, to know that we have done right by their dreams." "Artificery is not a niche any more, it is a promise. A promise of better days to come, to see the challenges of the world defeated by places like this, by people like you all. Giberd is proud to stand at the edge of the future, and to welcome all of you unto better days with open arms." "So let us celebrate. Let us thank you, as you have so thanked my friends and students, let us make merry, and let us find that future together."
