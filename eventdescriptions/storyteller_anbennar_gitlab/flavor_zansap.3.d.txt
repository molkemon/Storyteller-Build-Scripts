The mage shifted in place nervously, the clanking of his chains echoing off the cold stone walls. He stared at the official in front of him with a mixture of fear and suspicion in his eyes. He asked, "You’re saying you’ll let me go, just like that? I don’t even have to worship that sun god of yours?" Even with the blatant disrespect shown to the deity, the official’s face showed no emotion. "Yes. You will be allowed to keep your heathenry, and you will be given food, shelter, and the freedom to practice your magical arts." It seemed too good to be true. Just weeks earlier, he had been beaten to an inch of his life by the order's lackeys and dragged to this miserable cell for refusing to submit to that damned sun cult of theirs. This had to be some kind of trick. He asked, "So what’s the catch?" "The catch is that you will pledge your loyalty to the leader, and follow all orders given to you unquestionably. All you do will be for the glory of the deity, even if you refuse to follow Him. Do we have an understanding?" He then asked, "And what if I don’t want to be one of your lackeys?" The official calmly lifted up the ledger in his hand and began to read from it. "Accused of consorting with known rebels and refusing to accept the deity’s light. In custody of the order for eight days. Execution date set to—" "NO," screamed the mage, futilely lurching forward against his chains. Tears filled his eyes. "Don’t hurt her. Please, I’ll do anything you want. She’s all I have left. Just don’t hurt her. Please…" For the first time since entering the cell, the official showed a glint of emotion. A cruel smile spread across his lips.
