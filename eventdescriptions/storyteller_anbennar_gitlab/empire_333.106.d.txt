Periods of lizardfolk history have seen the balance of power move away from Taneyas. From roughly the two hundred thirty to the two hundred fifty Empire, lizardfolk in Taneyas developed a longlasting rivalry with lizardfolk who lived in the Salahad. They were a very unique type of lizardfolk, with mutations specifically suited to living amongst the hot sands. That these empires existed isn’t in doubt. There have even been discoveries of skeletons of lizardfolk with extremely wide feet, suited to sprinting across the hot desert sands as written about in the records. What has been cast in doubt is the validity of each one of the empires in this period. The biggest question mark lies over the two hundred forty-first Empire. Based along a long river that snaked through the desert, their empire made the fertile plains of the river their home until they were wiped out by an alliance of genies and giants. This empire’s existence seemed foolproof, as there is clearly a large river running through the Salahad where the human empire of Kheterata lies. Even the city of Kheterat is said to have been built on the remains of a genie palatial complex. The problems only begin when you start to analyze the history of the Mother’s Sorrow river. According to maps of Sarhal found in Aelantier drawn up by the Precursor Elves, there is zero evidence of the river existing. Was this story of the two hundred forty-first Empire written not contemporaneously, but instead invented by a writer after the river already existed?
