In the border fortress city of Jiantsiang, a towering ancient structure of the High Temple stands, crowned by a domed hall and centered around a great bronze brazier known as the Temple of Undying Flame. Its flames have burned without interruption for as long as memory stretches. By night, the light floods the hall like moonlight and can be seen from mountain paths and the village below, earning it the title "Beacon of the North." Inscriptions line the temple's stone walls, but none living can decipher them. The Perpetual Offering school of High Philosophy once deemed this fire a symbol of reason born of flame, while locals claim it wards off nightspirits and mountain phantoms. Many stories swirl, but one truth remains. the flame has never gone out. Lately, the outer temple has begun to weather and crumble. Officials suggest repairs to preserve it. You may leave it be, or fund its restoration, though doing so will not come cheap.
