Now, I won’t lie. When a person signed to me several years down the line, asking how we planned on getting one of the Keorg’s sacred mounts, I didn’t have a damn clue. But, luck always finds a way for us two. Turned out one of their females had a litter of cubs, and two of them were runts. Well, that wouldn’t do for the Chendhyans, who needed to ride them, but I and my bleeding heart spoke up, especially considering what we had learned about their culture. Said that I was willing to take them off their hands. After all, nobody back home would know they were runts, and I’d hate to see the little guys put down or released into the wild to starve. We spoke to the leader of the tribe, and convinced them that we could nurture their spirits, and strengthen their souls, or Aed. That they would find more freedom in life, entertaining droves of folks back home in a wide open encampment. They agreed, and the little guys ended up curling in our laps the whole ship ride home. And that is how we acquired our first catch. Sheer dumb luck, and the appreciation of a new people’s faith.
