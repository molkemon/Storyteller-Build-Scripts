The village was little more than a field of cold ash and charred timber, a dark stain upon the earth that filled the leader with anger as he sat atop a crumbling stone wall. This was the third such village he had seen that day, each scattered pile of burned bones further proof of the Dread Fang’s cruelty. Unfurling his map, the leader marked yet another settlement as destroyed, another blot of death they had been unable to prevent, more people they had failed to aid. Life Watching Eagle’s purpose was to protect any who suffered at the hands of tyrants, and here they had failed. If only the tribe had not been trapped in a domain, unable to defend the people beyond the fortress walls. It was their inaction, choosing to wait out the siege as a previous ruler had done for so many centuries, that had brought this devastation upon the land. Life Watching Eagle could not heal the dead, but they could ensure the fallen were remembered. When manpower was less strained, a stele would be raised amid the ruins of each village. Finding the names of the slain would be difficult, given the transient nature of many villages, but best efforts would be made nonetheless. He rolled the map closed and retied it to his ankle before calling upon the eagle that always lingered at the back of his mind. A familiar pinch overtook him as his body warped, arms stretching and reshaping into wings. With a few powerful beats he rose into the sky, basking for a moment in the warmth of the sun, savoring the closeness to a divine power that his gift granted him. Then he turned west, continuing the search for any villages that might have survived. There was still time before he would need to return to camp and deliver his report.
