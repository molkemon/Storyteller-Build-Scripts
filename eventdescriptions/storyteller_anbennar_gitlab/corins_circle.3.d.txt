Winston Foolfoot was a man with ideas. A plethora of ideas. And uncommon for most adventurers, they were usually good ones. Born a middlechild to a merchant family in Beepeck, he became a travelling merchant, and in fourteen forty-two led a caravan of food and supplies to Greentideravaged Escann, hoping to make a quick profit while avoiding any hostiles, this idea an exception to the rule. Stuck in a small town, besieged by a vast array of greenskins, he felt called to action in a way he had never felt before. He used his halfling wit to organize the panicking townspeople, identified a gap in the disorganized orkish sentries, and led them all to escape. It was he, in his subsequent role in the House of Riches, who rallied the other adventuring groups to unite behind Corin's lead. It was he who devised a clever pulley system to lift Lothane Bluetusk out of the pit the Circle found him in. And it was he who guarded Corin's body after she fell at Rottenstep, a whisper in his head convincing him she was not gone, not yet. After she fell for the second time, for once, the brilliant halfling was not sure what to do. Staying with the Corintar meant he risked bursting into tears every time he saw Lothane, he had an idea of what the redhead and the halfork meant to each other, a much better one than either of them. And yet he maintained enough of that adventurer spirit to decline an employment offer from Carleon Blacktower. Instead, he wandered from one adventuring party to the next, somehow evading still roaming hordes of goblins and orks, staying perhaps a week, perhaps a month, perhaps a year, and offering his ideas for the taking. The ruler had heard rumours Foolfoot had been helping the mayor of a village outside the capital, but even then he wasn't quite prepared for the sight of the halfling walking confidently into the monarch's commandtent. "You're doing good work. I have some ideas you might want to take a look at. Mind if I stay a while?"
