The leader rips a strip of meat from the charred flank beside the other. The second leader has already finished their own plate, drawing lazy spirals in the dark water next to them. "That was a god, you know," the second leader says at last. "Or close enough, at least." The leader grins, lips wet with fat. "You sound disappointed. Didn't you like it?" "It tasted fine, and they were plenty compelling, I suppose. Just not particularly persuasive." The second leader shrugs. "They bled, all the same." They fall quiet for a time. Across the river, smoke rises from a ruined marble terrace. Chittering laughter is audible somewhere just behind it. "The gnomes aren't Khet," the second leader states, without looking up. "They're clever. Sneaky, horrible things. More inclined to treason than service." "All true," the second leader agrees, "but that cleverness could be put to better use." "And, pray tell; what inside a gnome's mind could possibly be useful?" The second leader pauses, thinking for a moment. "We'd be hard pressed to find better scribes, for one. They're the best gemcutters this side of Halann, for another. Some of their thinkers have ideas about our rites I can barely follow. We could learn from that. They'd never serve," the second leader allows, tilting their head, "but I doubt they'd act against us. They'd mutter their heresies in corners, and, eventually, they'd die." The second leader finally meets the leader's gaze. "They're not immortal." "They're not us." "No." The second leader allows. "They aren't." Somewhere, further down the river, Zokka's voice rings out, joyous and brutal. Neither turn to look. They sit in silence; one chewing, one contemplative; listening to the slow rush of the Sorrow as the last of the Khet drifts by in the current.
