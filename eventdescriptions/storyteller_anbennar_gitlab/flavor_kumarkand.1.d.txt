Officially speaking, the envoy from Kumarkand which had arrived in Irrliam's court was not a legal representative approved by Kumarkand's overlord, Varamhar. It was no surprise then that the envoy spoke not of Varamhar's will, but Kumarkand's future and most importantly Irrliam's part in said future. The envoy asks for Irrliam to provide military support in its upcoming war against Varamhar and offers in return the creation of a unified grain market, which would stabilize the price of grain and provide us greater income from export tariffs. Furthermore, this might be a great opportunity for us to cripple Varamhar while getting a loyal ally as a neighbor. However, the question remains if the potential benefits are worth upsetting the status quo in Bulwar and the lives of our soldiers.
