With the construction of a Naval Academy in full swing, the regulations and institutions have come up for debate. Several parties have shown special interest in specific measures to be taken. The Captains wish for discipline to be weighted above all, arguing that only by an iron fist can the navy triumph; they cite the example of a leader who commanded their flotilla that way. The Buccaneers wish for enrolment into the academy to be free of charge; they say that the academy should be based on a meritocratic system, and all should be able to join and climb as high as their talents carry them. The Smugglers wish to open up internship positions aboard their ships to students. They claim that the only way to gain experience and learning is through action and practice, so the students must be taught on real boats. The Xhazobkultists have the most unorthodox approach for the Naval Academy; they argue that the way to move ahead is to rely on their faith. For that, they want religious training and specialization for the students and a refitting of the ships to accommodate that. This would mean that each vessel would be fitted with a special pyre and always be available for sacrifices, so the recently trained Captain could make quick sacrifices and gain blessings that would fit the situation. To ensure their interests are represented, all four factions have presented a candidate to become the principal of the Academy. Which one will be picked?
