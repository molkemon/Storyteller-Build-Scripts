The tale in mind, the priests hum, reciting the opening passages, painting the image of a zephyr wind grinding mountains into crags and valleys. When the last of Fangaula’s twelve tribe leaders set their spears into the ground at Tannifila Oka, Biakata smirked from her cloudy perch, awash in glory and filled with pride for her conquests. But then the sun set and rose and set again, turning this day of triumph into an innumerable procession of days. The mighty Biakata’s unbindable will wavered, fearful of what future challenges her empire would face. Her stalwart army and allies, comprising human and gnoll desert dwellers, soured as zealous warriors and disgruntled wanderers were forced to rule over the sedentary people of these lands. What good was a Second Fangaula Empire for their lot? And her former foes? They tended to their wounds, counted their spears, and chafed under Biakata’s elemental cult of personality. a wounded beast lurking for its moment to pounce again. Biakata saw the fate of her old oppressive gnoll matriarch flash before her eyes and endeavored to firmly bind that which would otherwise fall apart in time. First, she granted her wilderness supporters important roles along the Empire’s fringes. patrolling borders, guardian caravans, and relaying messages across Fangaula’s length. To the myriad tribes, the airtouched Empress shaped her court like a monsoon, distracting each tribe with seasonal boons and titles to divert her opposition’s resistance and redirect their ire unto themselves. And finally, to stop any hopeful planetouched from emerging out of the dust of the unknown, Biakata established a holy sect, called the God Atoners, to locate, sponsor, and turn young planetouched into loyal supporters of her realm and dynasty. Thanks to her great political acumen, Biakata laid the foundation of an empire that would stand for nigh on a thousand years.
