When it comes to being the Steward of the Sorrow, many things pose a challenge, but the hardest thing is definitely appeasing the Khets themselves. From the tedious codes of Khet savoirvivre to personal accommodation of basic Khet needs, the Steward always has the ruler's hands full with requirements the ruler isn't always able to fulfill. Thus it seems that no matter how much the monarch changes their appearance and no matter how sweet their demeanor can be, the Khets always seem displeased with their upbringing. Unfortunately, the short stature of the monarch has caused most Khets to not treat them seriously, as the Steward is forced, figuratively and literally, to be looked at from above. Despite organising many contraptions designed to gain even grounding, the Khets can't help but laugh at the Steward's attempts. At this point, the monarch is running short of options, traumatised to the point where they rages at the sound of the word.
