Advances in irrigation techniques have allowed the continuous supply of fields with water even during the dry season, and new iron plows have become sturdy enough to break the tough soil of Gozengun, resulting in an extremely arable climate developing from what once was the dry grasslands of the southern Shuvüüsh steppe. This profound change will have a lasting impact on the very nature of the land itself, to say nothing of the traditional nomadic way of life of its inhabitants. Until now, powerful nature spirits were traditionally appeased to keep watch over rare, lush orchards hidden from the scouring winds in small vales, and over verdant grazing grounds nourished by hidden wellsprings, while the mortal inhabitants of the land roamed freely across the steppe in their endless migrations. Yet now, more and more farmers settle in these lands, irrevocably changing the face of the steppe one tilled field at a time.
