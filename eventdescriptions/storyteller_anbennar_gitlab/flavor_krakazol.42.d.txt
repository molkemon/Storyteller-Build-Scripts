Our victory is absolute. The ale seeping from the meteorite has spread through the ocean, traveling up the rivers of Haless and carried deep into the highlands. And even so diluted, its presence is unmistakable. Vines sprout from the streets and climb onto houses, the sap inside their grapes already nearly wine. Fields of wheat and rice grow tall and wild, their harvests producing strange alcoholic food. Trees grow fruit that already ferments while on the branches. Haless rises to a new morning, the start of a new age. A drunken age.
