Their ruler has spent years studying and meditating about the true nature of the relationship between Vortza Lesa, the goddess of Forests and Nature, and her husband, Vortzo Laukano, god of Agriculture. All this work has resulted in an extensive theological treaty that has shocked the faithful of the realm to their core. In it, the monarch explains that, in reality, there is no separation between either of the gods, with them being only an aspect of the same entity, a dual deity of Fertility. While Vortzo Lesa is tasked with the fertility of the wild, Vortzo Laukano ensures that the crops and mushrooms from the domain are growing properly. Their marriage is not a mere political union; it is the true and sacred unity of two beings in one. The monarch has been almost universally praised for their research and work. There is consensus that the religion policy of the nation must be reoriented to adapt to these findings; however, there are disagreements about exactly how they should go about this. Should they worship both aspects in unity, in a new cult of fertility? Or should they refocus their efforts on Vortzo Laukano, who after all is responsible for the area of fertility that the nation needs to feed itself?
