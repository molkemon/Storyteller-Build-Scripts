In a dwarven hold, it is a rare luxury to be able to take a step outside at one's pleasure, without navigating the winding tunnels to the surface. To be able to rest against the rail of a balcony, to feel the cool night air, to watch one's breath drift away. As was the ruler's habit, the monarch enjoyed resting here at the end of a day debating with administrators and astronomers, watching the clouds roll a thousand feet below, and looking up into the midnight blue, speckled with stars of clear crystal white. But tonight, something made the hairs on the back of the ruler's neck stand on end. Suddenly, the monarch had never been more aware of the size of the world outside the mountain home. The ruler felt exposed. The hemisphere of the night sky felt like a lens, with the monarch at its focal point. Something was examining the monarch. Something far above scrutinized the monarch, as though the ruler were under a microscope slide. A mind immeasurably superior to the ruler's own. The monarch dared to look up at the stars, and a shiver ran deep through the ruler's bones.
