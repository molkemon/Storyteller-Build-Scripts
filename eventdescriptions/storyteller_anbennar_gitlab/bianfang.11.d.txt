The ruler of the dynasty stands on the great mezzanine of the Hanging Temple of Aoshan, the Proud Mountain. He is indeed proud, surveying the realm’s recently added dominions in Xianjie, dearly bought with the lives of tens of thousands of valiant soldiers. On the far horizon, the Temple of the Keepers is barely visible, and within, Kongren, the Void's Edge. The monks, with all of their schools and styles and mysterious martial arts, put up a fierce resistance, but our discipline and numbers eventually overcame them. Now, their temples lie open before us, filled with riches and priceless artifacts crafted by generations of monks. We would be well within our rights to sack these temples, put them to the torch, cart the plunder back to the capital, and declare these robed ruffians outlaw, to be executed on sight. They have long labeled us tyrants and despots, inciting our people to rebel in the name of freedom; just another word for chaos. That said, to bring law and order to these lands may require a softer touch, and we may yet learn from these mystical warriors who felled so many of our troops.
