Fire. The crack of gunshots rings out, and the monarch watches as, across the field, a row of targets explode into splinters. The council watches with the monarch, and a few of the softer guild members flinch at the noise. When the sawdust and gunsmoke clears, the monarch sees that every target is gone. He turns to the gunsmith beside him. “And you said that your guild can make how many of these?” “Each of us can make one in a month, so I would put my estimates in the thousands.” “By grace…” the monarch whispers. “That’s not all, ruler. We have one more demonstration, if I may.” The monarch nods. The smith shouts. “Oy. You louts. Clear the field for the cannons.” Minutes later, the monarch watches as more than three dozen cannons are wheeled onto the field. The monarch has seen cannons before, but never so many. They are expensive and impractical, but fearsome weapons. When they fire, this time there is no trace of the targets. The smith smiles proudly at the ruler’s shock. “Three months, that was the work of. Three months.” “Their blessing truly is upon us.” The monarch collects the monarch. “I want every man in the army with a musket by next year. And at least a dozen regiments with cannons. Can you do that?” The smith grins. “Easily.”
