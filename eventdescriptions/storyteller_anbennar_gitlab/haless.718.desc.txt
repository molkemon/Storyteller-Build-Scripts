In the ancient borderlands, barren hills stretch across the horizon. Beneath their worn soil lie the remnants of the longlost Longcheng and the tombs of ancient emperors. It is said that the poems of this land were once passed down directly by the Dragon Emperor, and among all the poetry traditions of Yanshen, none are older. Their verses resound with strength, their language is dense and solemn. Some lament the life of soldiers stationed far from home, evoking the snow and desolation of the frontier; others tell of the hardships of the common folk, laboring in the fields, weathering the years. This year, officials traveled across the borderlands to collect verses both old and new. What they gathered has spread far and wide. Some echo the fading laments of garrison soldiers, piercing the soul; some weep with the sorrow of women and scholars, each word deeply felt; some speak the thoughts of boatmen, plain and true. What poem was recorded this year? Perhaps within it lies the heart of the borderfolk and the pulse of Yanshen itself.
