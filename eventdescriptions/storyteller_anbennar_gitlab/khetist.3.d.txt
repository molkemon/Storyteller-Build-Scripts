We firmly believe that our religious law, the Mariat, is sacred and beyond doubt, but recent documents excavated by a scholar in Wesdam have the potential to shake this conviction. These documents tell the tale of a mage from the Damerian Republic, Crescies Silverinus Valentis, who attempted to disguise himself as a Khet to infiltrate the Sorrow and steal the secrets of our magic. The scholarly consensus after comparing this find with established historical and religious texts tends towards a cautious acknowledgement that he may very well be the renowned Damerian Khet out of legends and tales from the period of Damerian influence over Kheterata. This legendary figure is characterised by a lack of any records regarding his early years, a preference for Damerian culture, and an astonishing diligence. What is concerning is that the Khetarch during his time appears to have issued many rulings under his guidance. We failed to contain the news in time, and it has already begun to spread among the populace. Although truly devout individuals will not waver in their faith because of this, it may cause confusion among the ignorant commoners. Some suggest that our steward continue to support the validity of these rulings as before, as even doubting a single decision of a previous Khetarch could fatally damage the legitimacy of the entire legislative corpus. Others advise the steward to seize this opportunity to conduct a comprehensive review of the Mariat, using the pretext of preserving its purity to suppress rulings favourable to various local sects.
