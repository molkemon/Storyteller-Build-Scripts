The roosts and cliff citadels found across the Harpy Hills are built in locations where flat ground to erect new buildings tends to be at a premium. This has led to increasingly unconventional attempts to utilize steep, sometimes even vertical, surfaces for the construction of new habitations. Recent advances in artificery have now produced an ingenious new building material combining impressive tensile strength and durability with an almost negligible weight; this allows for easy transport to and installation at even the most conventionally inaccessible cliff faces. Impregnating metal with minuscule air bubbles reduces its weight without impairing its functionality. Such metal can then be used to build the famous air-infused hanging houses which have swiftly become a remarkable sight in many roosts.
