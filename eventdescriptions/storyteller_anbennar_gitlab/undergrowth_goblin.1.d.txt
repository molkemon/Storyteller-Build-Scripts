The rules of Goblinball are as follows. Two players, each standing approximately ten paces from a center line, shall face each other holding large wooden paddles. A standard size goblin, no taller than five feet in height, shall be placed in the center. The goblin shall be curled up with their chin tucked into their knees, to prevent the greatest damage to their skeletal structure. Crying is encouraged. Play shall proceed until one player scores into the other's net, the goblin suffers sudden and unforeseeable failure of life, or twenty minutes have passed, at which point there will be an intermission. The other goblin rolled his eyes. "You just like eating moss." From ahead of the two guards, a dark figure crested a hill on the horizon, twilight shadow casting darkness over their features. The first goblin's eyes narrowed, and the second raised his spear. "Halt, traveler. What business do you have?" Then the figure turned, and the evening sun made clear their features. bruised and blackeyed was the advisor, whose uncertainty of gait and swollenness of head had combined to mask their silhouette. To and fro they swayed on their feet, and the second goblin dropped his spear, running forward to catch the goblin before they fell. "The advisor. The diplomat," the first goblin guard stated. "What, er, what news bring you from the human lands?" From their position slumped over the shoulder of the second guard, the advisor weakly raised their battered features. "We played... Goblinball.
