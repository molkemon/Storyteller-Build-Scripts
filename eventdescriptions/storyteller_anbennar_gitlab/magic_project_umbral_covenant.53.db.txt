We ask our Kammen whence he comes, and she does not know; a curse upon him? Her memory stretches only into Ash, into that awful time. Our elders called him AllMourner, for she wept for those who had none to weep, but he is no avatar of Nirakhet, no ruler of the dead. We know this, for Nirakhet does not weep from the mouth. Komina says, "I do not contest what it claims, merely what that means." "That you had a self before them, a life, even followers." The Vile One does not say loved ones; it is the sort of sentimentalism that would drive Komina into hiding. "Have you considered that it was worse?" "Not for you," the Vile One says. "You dance among the courts, drink wine with kings. You really seek the same as Granny, the ending of pleasurable things, of this plane?" "You don’t?" Komina says it lightly, as she says everything, but she says it too quickly. "I am with you, Auntie," the Vile One says. "I would not readily give up the courts." Auntie waves her arm dismissively, a ripple of gauzy silk. She does not trust this, not really. She is humouring her Niece, but it is out of curiosity more than loyalty, at least. "You liked my friend," the Vile One says. She doesn’t know why she thinks this is worth stating. Something about the possibility of true bonds with others, perhaps, for Komina has always readily made bonds. Alone among the hags, her victims are true friends, true paramours.
