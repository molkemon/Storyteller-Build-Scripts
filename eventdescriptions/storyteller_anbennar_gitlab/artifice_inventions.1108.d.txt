The State works best as a machine, picked at and pruned, so goes the old thoughtful adage. We have created the machine which does the pruning. The Bureaucratizer is an invention which we have allowed to direct our governance. To use it, we feed progress reports, job reviews, standardized test scores, and more into a black box which few understand. Behind this box is a singular, mindwiped bureaucrat, temporarily emptied of memories so that they no longer hold loyalty to any one ruler, political party, estate, faction, or interest group. With clarity of mind, the Bureaucratizer helps the unbiased bureaucrat choose who shall sit in various posts, and who shall rule one of our many administrative subdivisions. With only objective criteria to look at, there is no possible way for the bureaucrat to be infected with emotions or personal loyalty. Surely, the bureaucrat's memories do not always come back perfectly after using the machine, and also certainly, we have already encountered problems with falsified reports, biased statements of review, or other mishaps which have still led to bias entering the Bureaucratizer's workings. These problems, though, are able to be dealt with. We simply need to fix these, and we will have a perfect invention which shall solve the problems of governance permanently. We will conquer subjectivity, and we shall do so with a governmental cabinet which the Bureaucratizer picks for us.
