A lowly mayor from a backwater corner in a province journeyed to the capital seeking our help with rowdy neighbors. A centaur clan arrived in the mayor’s district not long ago. After a chilled introduction, the two communities bridged the foreigner divide and established a cordial rapport. Thankful for the town’s hospitality, the clan threw a rambunctious festival. loud music, grand feasts, and formalized dances that persisted to dawn. As the town partied, reserved residents complained about the constant noise and dirt clouds that drifted from the festival grounds. After the celebrations concluded, the centaurs decreed an even grander festival next season, much to the chagrin of these perturbed locals. Torn between constituents, the mayor pleaded for a decision condoning or banning the festival. How shall we act?
