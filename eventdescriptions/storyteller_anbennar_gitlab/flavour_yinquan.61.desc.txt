For the first time in many, many, many years, the sun has risen to greet a peaceful Haless. For a brief, wonderful day, none sharpen their blades; there will be no war between the denizens of the realm, for they have been united with the spirits once and for all. None will need to worry about impending hobgoblin invasion, nor the threats of brigands, and especially not the threat of some spirit driven mad by torment killing family and livestock alike. There is only harmony. Such a thing is not free, of course; the diligent work of the realm's agents is necessary to keep the peace. There are those who would see the harmony of Haless disrupted. Rebellious hobgoblins, heretical halftiger beasts, and even stray oni still roam the countryside, unwilling to face the fate they deserve. It is the duty of the Maidens of Liho to exterminate those who would challenge the peace; to hunt them down and annihilate those who would interrupt the coming century of harmony. The peace that will envelop Haless will be eternal, once it is secured. For a day, perhaps a week or even a month, there will be no conflict. Until those subversives within the nation are corralled, though, the threat of war will always loom over Haless. This is the meaning of internal perfection; there can be no nations, no borders to do battle over. There can only be the reverence of the spirits, Huszien most of all, holding no idols before them. It may take decades. It may even take centuries. But this is the inevitable fate of Haless.
