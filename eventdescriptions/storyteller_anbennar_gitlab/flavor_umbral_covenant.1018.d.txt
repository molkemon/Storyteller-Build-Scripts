The rift opens, and I can see. A blanket of invisible death billows down the slopes of Junansuar, displaced by the sheer power of the grand ritual in the caldera lake. Silence settles upon twenty-two villages, and near every soul in the shadow of the volcano is snuffed out within minutes. Men and women pass in their sleep, the fog of unair stealing their breath noiselessly. Entire herds expire; every insect dies. In the morning, when Granny faintly perceives what her magic has inadvertently caused, she will wander these places as a spectre, curious. When the rare survivors stagger away from their villages, weak and disoriented, and the first visitors arriving for market discover a place where even the corpse-eaters have perished, their horror will sate her where these painless deaths did not.
