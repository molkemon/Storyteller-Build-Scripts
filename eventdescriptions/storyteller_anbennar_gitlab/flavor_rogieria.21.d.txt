The shadows in black fled across the desert, and the dreaming man followed. The monarch was not sure who they pursued, only that they would destroy all they held dear. Sometimes the shadows seemed an army, and they could almost see purple banners waving, or maroon. Sometimes they seemed a warband, and they could hear chants of a foreign tongue, the howl of fiendish wargs. The monarch was not sure why they pursued them, only that they dared not stop. Sometimes they could hear the cry of his sister from ahead, and would double his pace. Sometimes they would hear groans as if his parents and their parents and their parents all suffered ahead, and they would push himself even faster. The monarch knew not what would happen if they caught up, but they narrowed the distance all the same. Out of the shadows, they saw glimpses. bloodstained axe, savage teeth, a knife that would only ever find your back. And yet they closed. The monarch neared. And then, tripped. Fell hard onto the sand, his pack spewing its contents out before him. The monarch lay there a moment, two, feeling the grit against his cheek. Then a flash of panic stirred him to his feet, and they could see their quarry growing ever distant. In the ground before him lay a sword, wellused but still sharp. And a crown, ornate, a work of unparalleled beauty and majesty. Before they returned to the endless chase, they took up a tool, never to let it go again.
