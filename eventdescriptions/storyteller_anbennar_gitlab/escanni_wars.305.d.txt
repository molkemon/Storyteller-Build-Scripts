In the century and a half since the end of the Greentide, history has not been fortunate for us orks. Despite being physically stronger than the humans, we lacked the weapons and money to fight against a humanity who was dedicated to stopping us. Pike and shot ended the Greentide. In its aftermath, we’ve lost land, been shipped abroad as slaves, and are now on our last legs. The nation is perhaps the sole exception, managing to survive and be at the forefront of Escann, rivaling as we do the most powerful adventurer kingdoms. We learned the reason the adventurers beat back the Greentide wasn’t due to sheer physical power, but due to magic and technology paired with cities and villages that can churn out hundreds of thousands of men to fight us. We took lessons from the humans, shedding our monstrous ways to get as strong as them. We’ve built an economy, modernized our army, and started settling down, giving us the power to become the strongest again. Another group, on the other hand, has not been so lucky. Their history has been a series of losing war after war despite their fighting spirit, only kept alive through the graces of their neighbors. Now, however, they have conceded that their country is not long for this world. Their ruler has approached our ruler with an offer. They offer to become a junior partner in a union with us, proposing to hand the reins of power over for us to declare ourselves a homeland for orks across Escann. Their ruler explains that out of the remaining orks in Escann, they trust us and would prefer if our ruler carried the torch of ork civilization forwards. It’s an amazing deal, but there’s the slight downside of not having immediate control. If we accept, their governance structures remain intact and we have less direct control. We always have the option to refuse and simply take their country by force anyways, cutting out the middleman.
