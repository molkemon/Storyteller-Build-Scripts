My ruler, I have the honor to present to you Telšali Lysinyau. She holds a position similar to that of a squad leader among the Haašananthu. Her companion is called Triandil Erelzuir. He is some kind of military advisor, I think? Noppes bowed towards the monarch of the dynasty. The ruler, in his usual curiosity about the outside world, had asked him to set up a meeting with a representative of the Bulwari mercenaries to talk about their view of the world. The reason for the elf companion’s presence was unclear. Noppes himself, as the clan’s Head Shaman, was thrilled. learning about new cults had always been his favourite pastime. I am not before you as a warrior, but as an apprentice of our flock’s Riaveltu. They are sort of like your advisor, but not. They know and pass on our songs. The Hunt is how we live, my ruler. The philosopher Nehijunus said that it is a door left open. It is not something I can teach you, but you can learn it if you seek an answer you cannot reach. What’s the use of your songs if you don’t teach them? the elf interrupted, visibly annoyed at the harpy’s explanation. Ral, you should not heed this nonsense. Are you curious about our faith? Then you should learn about Surael, the true god, who spreads His Light to protect us against the Darkness. He welcomes all, for all creatures can be the Light. You, Ral, could even be the one to bring the Light to these dark woods. And you, Telšali, even you could be the Light if you accepted to see the truth. Triandil, dear, you talk about the Light, but I’ve flown closer to the Sun than you’ll ever be able to. But get too close to it, and you’ll get burnt. The monarch could tell that it was not the first time his two guests had argued about this, and that they both seemed to enjoy their oratorical bouts. He coughed softly, to remind them that it was not the place for bickering.
