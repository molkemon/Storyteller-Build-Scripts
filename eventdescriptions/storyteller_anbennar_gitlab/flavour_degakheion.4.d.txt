Every Phylaxiate knows the stories, the history, of how their ancestors used to protect every domain, presiding over an age of order, peace, and stability. How the phenomenon of Karkares and Phylaxana, of prisoner and warden, was not one limited to Degakheion, but found in each city, with the populations in perfect harmony. Then order broke down, and the other domains were consumed by chaos and strife. But in Degakheion, they survived. They kept the old laws. They kept the old ways. They kept their watch. And, when the opportunity presented itself, they struck. Campaign after campaign, hard fought, hard won, through discipline, through unity, through strength. And at last, Alecand is theirs. They will never be able to render the citizens of the other domains the same status as the Karkares at home, but that is of little matter. No matter the political pretensions of their subjects, the Degabhen garrisons stationed in their cities remind them of what true power is. The Phylaxana, the tip of the spear, the best of the best, readied by regimen and honed through the Erythé, stand as the guardians of civilization, the spearspiked shield of the Kheionai. Already, they were beginning to dream of grander things still, planning future campaigns abroad, grand infrastructural works, and political plans. And, of course, they enjoy the fruits of their duty, wardens once more. And both guard and prisoner will prosper under their guidance.
