As soon as an appointed factor leaves the capital, we expect them to begin lining their own pockets as much as they serve the interests of the trade company. Rarely, however, are company officials so egregious in their greed as the factor of that province. Through his private investments, this factor has cornered the local market in the trade goods. Investors fearing for the reputation of the company demand that the renegade factor be recalled to the capital to face punishment. More opportunistic elements suggest that we should instead buy out his enterprises, incorporating them into the company’s operations and rewarding the factor for his initiative.
