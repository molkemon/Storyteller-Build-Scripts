Surely the dwarves were trespassers here, in this dark, deep, and sacred place. Surely the axes they used to strike down the monster were like unto the tools of loggers, engaging in the industrialized destruction of natural beauty in the service of some pathological modern need for control and domination. Indeed, the dwarves had no right to even anywhere near Athainglan, in the very heart of the elven woods. Who, then, was the true monster here?
