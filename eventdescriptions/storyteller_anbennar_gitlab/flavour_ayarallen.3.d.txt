A northerly wind battered against Heunthume’s flank; most unusual. It blew towards the Twin Rivers, and all present smiled at the omen. Teisi spilled over their stool, huffing and puffing as they rearranged documents. They had already been forced to slam their palm down hard on a flyaway document or two. "We prefer to fly in a month or two, once sufficient livestock can be," the monarch scoffed. "Queen, if you defer to Lisinyalen, we will simply never leave. May I remind you of the pledge made to us regarding our claims in Akalšes." "Oh, give me strength, monarch, every time," she replied. "Those claims are a matter of the continuation of the line. They are existential, and take priority." "Over your queen?" sneered Saero, whose main quality was slavish arsekissing. "For my queen," retorted the monarch. "Or are we suggesting flock unity was cynical?" "Do we have to argue like this every time?" "Some of us still have pulses, Teisi, so yes." "Come on now, have some decorum," a fourth matriarch, barely hiding their smile. "Monarch, you called Teisi an orb less than five minutes ago." "I did it with decorum." They continued like this for hours. It was, contrary to appearances, a promising sign. They were all independently motivated, and all saw profit in the venture. Like harriers they would scrap over every morsel, but for the time being they would hunt together.
