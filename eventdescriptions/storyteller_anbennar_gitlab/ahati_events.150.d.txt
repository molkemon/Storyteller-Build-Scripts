The Ahati enjoy support from all walks of life, from the lowest of servants up to the most noble of generals. This means that some of our own administrators do so as well. While most have remained loyal to the state, some administrators have already declared themselves sympathetic and loyal to the Ahati cause or impartial, which just means that they will wait and see until a clear victor has emerged. As such, one of these Ahati sympathetic governors has opened up the gate of his provincial capital to the Ahati and has formally declared his support for the revolt. We have lost control over all the local fortifications, and any tax revenue is now being confiscated by the Ahati.
