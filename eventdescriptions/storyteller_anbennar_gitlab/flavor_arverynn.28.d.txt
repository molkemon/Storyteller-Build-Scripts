Furor gives way to confusion and tension gives way to laughter as everyone looks up to see the ruler holding a creature halfwaterfowl and halfrodent from the temple walls, while a flutist plays a melody familiar across cultures. A tradition of Veykodan Ruinborn, which Ynn River Worship sometime appropriated, is the Song of Dawn; in Veykodan, Nainnenek; which announces the discovery of a new species, and calls for the interruption of almost any activity, whether fighting or sleeping, to hold celebrations. A truce is negotiated with the Veykodan elites, and they are welcomed as guests into Munkarel's courtyard. While Sarda bards try their best to smooth tensions, Ynnic farmers lay out tables with produce and Rune Scribes design an area as the field of games. rhino leaping, tug of war, chunkey, and even a dreaded lacrosse match will see our Ynnic knights pitted against tribal champions of the east. Members of the Veykodan Guard stand aside at first, for not everyone of their kin looks favorably upon their career. But as festivities mount up, tension mellows and they soon join the dancing and singing until the moon is high up in the sky. "So, what should we call the animal?" A riverpriest asks the ruler.
