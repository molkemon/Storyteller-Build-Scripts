The forges of the House of Fire and Water never rest; explosions are a familiar background in the city. Yet at one particular midnight, a deafening blast pierced the air, followed by a crimson blaze erupting from the Grand Crucible, launching its massive lid high into the heavens. Flames surged across the city, lighting the night like day and sending the populace into a frenzy. Garrison forces rushed to respond. By the time they arrived, the alchemical courtyard was scorched black. Thankfully, protective enchantments spared lives; though dozens were injured, none perished. Five days later, the Grand Alchemist appeared at court, robe charred but spirits unbowed. "Your Majesty," he said, "the explosion was a miscalculation. We attempted a new formula, blending explosive dust with reagents and essence of elemental powders. We underestimated its potency. But now, we have refined it. With silk pouches and firefused triggers, it could shatter lines and devastate fortresses alike."
