Camnan looked on sullenly as the victorious soldiers marched home. He could see friends and family all passing by, brandishing wounds and bloodied weapons with the same vigour and cheer. Their town had won a series of battles recently, their warriors besting all the neighbouring city-states. And while they won glory, he’d been stuck at home, jewelling. Camnan looked down to his withered legs and the wheeled chair that held them. The jeweller heard a loud tut from behind him. “Each parade, I find you in such a state. Must you wallow in regret like so?” Camnan turned. Jalinwathi, the local priest of the town, and a member of the Violet Brotherhood, stood, arms crossed and a faint smile across his face. Camnan shrugged, frowning. “My friends win glory on the battlefield day by day. Why, one warrior has slain so many foes he may well ascend. Meanwhile I’m stuck here tinkering.” The jeweller looked to the parade again. “How am I supposed to attain glory if I can’t fight?” “The same way your sister’s writing grants glory, or old Trianran’s smithing. Why, that elf made half the weapons used in that battle. You make wonders every month, and you still feel inadequate? Foolishness, Camnan, foolishness. There are many paths to ascension. And in this domain, smith and soldier both drink from the same barrel.” Camnan scoffed. “Then why do we love war and battle so? The empire marches east and west, north and south. How could you get more glorious than that?” Now it was Jalinwathi’s turn to frown. “The people of this realm have always craved the easy path. And blood and violence is easy, but sometimes it is being turned from the easy that allows one to seek the fulfilling.” The priest looked to the parade. “Perhaps the town will turn from the easy path one day.”
