The two miners thought of home as they ambled out of the shaft. The work was hard that day, as it was every day, and at the end of a shift all a man wanted to do was eat his supper and go to sleep. But as they started to head for home, the two miners saw a group. They were gathered in front of something massive etched on the cavern wall in bare lines. It was a miner, standing taller than any had ever seen before. He held his pick up to the sky defiant, as if he was making a promise that the whole world could hear. He was dressed in the clothing of the mines, but around his neck was a fine necklace, of the type the chieftains wore. It would be a necklace of many colors, once the colors were there. He was just lines for now, but those lines were the start of something grand, and everyone could see it. In fact, that’s what the conversation was about. People were talking about what that miner could be. What if the miner stood on top of a mountain, and he was crying out to the people below? What if he had that green streak on his scales that the foreman had? What if there was a yellow glint in his eyes, the same way that the miners got as they sang their working songs? As the two miners and the group in front of them got wrapped up in their possibilities, a clatter echoed on the cavern floor. Everyone looked up, and on that cavern floor they saw the fine brushes and paint that belonged to the Axta, and which were only brought out on the special days. The group surged towards them, and the work began. The discussion broke out into groups about who would paint what, and how it would look, and how fine that miner would look when they were done. One of the two miners picked up a brush. It was lighter than a pick. It felt calmer.
