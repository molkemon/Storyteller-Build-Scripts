The morning was bright and fresh as a band of Freemarcher settlers stepped out of the darkness of the forest into a clearing. When they were not three hundred yards from the tree line, they saw their first glint of silver and the sound of hoofbeats as a ruinborn cavalry formation came over the rise, lances lowered. The call went out, "Back to the trees," but it was too late. The riders were upon them in a moment, lances whirling, blood splashing on their cloaks. Of the settlers, only a small fraction managed to make it back to the relative safety of the forest. Two young halforks had managed to pull one of the riders from their mount and dragged him into the forest as a captive. "We defended the sacred river from all in this forest," the young ruinborn managed to say, "From all who would defile it." Over the following days, he gave much useful information to the Freemarchers. When his usefulness was exhausted, there only remained the question of what to do with him.
