Zunewl Fireblood turned around from her experiment table, a large stone slab cluttered with glass vials, brass contraptions, scribbled parchments, and, in the middle, a small tin ornate box full of a black powder. Scanning the messy laboratory through the thick, coloured and irritating fumes, she soon found the one who may hold the answer. A tall merchant in fancy clothes, curled up as far from the table as possible, sideeyeing the goblin alchemist with apprehension and disbelief at her reckless behaviour. "You there, human, are you sure you don’t know how this powder is made?" "I already told you, no, I don’t. Can I please go back to Bal Ouord? I was only told to bring the powder to you, not to assist you with your experiments. This is madness." "Come on, you’re already there, you should make yourself useful. Isn’t this fascinating?" "I swear in the name of Ara, the Shippers Cartel will hear of this." Some weeks later, after a particularly loud detonation, Zunewl Fireblood stepped out of her smoky laboratory, with a satisfied grin on her face. The ruler would be delighted to learn that the formula to make black powder has finally been found, through trials and many, many errors.
