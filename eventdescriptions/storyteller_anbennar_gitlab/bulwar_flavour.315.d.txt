The temple of EduzPalaktebum, known as the Temple of All-Encompassing Grace, has swiftly gained an indispensable role in the spiritual life of the inhabitants of Kunolo. The Vakwene who reject conventional gender norms used to gather in the Kiumbanda healing societies under the auspices of the city’s eponymous patron deity Kunolo. It is within the sacred temple precinct that they now have found a new purpose amongst the ranks of the priesthood of the Sun Cult. They have formed a devoted sect of Sun Cultists closely linked to the Bulwari prophetic order of the Gabarišin, who similarly reject the notion that one’s apparent gender necessarily reflects the inner truth of one’s divine essence, or Ilu. Like the water of the Odogala flowing ever onwards, they are content to adjust their traditions to this new golden age under the Light of Surael. In their new role as priests and priestesses of Surael, they happily continue to administer to those in need from within the halls of the Ilawaykabukun, as they call the Temple of All-Encompassing Grace. The temple itself is a marvel of architecture, merging the classical ziggurat style of Bulwar with the traditions of Fangaula and the Dao Nako. A combination of adobe bricks and enormous pillars of ironwood harvested from the nearby Dao Nako jungle allowed for the construction of a ziggurat that conforms to Bulwari convention while maintaining a distinct Fangaulan aesthetic.
