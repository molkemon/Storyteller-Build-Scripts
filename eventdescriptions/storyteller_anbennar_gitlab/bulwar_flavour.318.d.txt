The city of Yetmesira has undergone a truly profound transformation ever since its inhabitants joined the ranks of the faithful followers of the Sun Cult, the Devotion to the Light. What was once a city of gilded cruelty, its wealth built on the shattered hopes, dreams, and lives of the unfortunates who were traded away like cattle on the slave markets, has become a shining beacon of the faith, that thrives on the prosperity instead of the misery of the people within its walls. Nothing could symbolize this change more than the EduzHarhapu, the Temple of Broken Chains, that beating heart of new Yetmesira which the local people reverently call Mekyenzelet. Its outer walls are decorated with the shattered remnants of the slave chains that were removed during the grand liberation, when the slavers were struck down. Grateful former slaves and the faithful have attached small bells and chimes to the chains, so even the slightest breeze causes a melodious jingle. Within its walls, a series of terraced garden rows leading up to the central ziggurat provide shade beneath the trees planted there, a soothing breeze coming in from the sea rustling their leaves even on the hottest summer day. The temple itself has been marvellously decorated with mosaics and wood carvings from generous donations made by pious merchant princes, who no longer participate in the flesh trade. Instead, they now sell the rich grain harvests of Madrizurel to hungry mouths all across the Gulf of Rahen and bring the luxuries found on these shores back with them to Yetmesira.
