Naramus's chains jingle softly as he dips his stylus in ink. They've given him a copper collar now; less clunky than the old steel, a concession for clarity of thought. He's proud; the leader of one group says he's earned it. Across the table, the leader of the other group plants both hands on the map. "There's too many," the leader of the other group growls. "They're pouring from the north like cattle. There's more I don't trust every single day, and we don't have the chains to control them." Thinking, the leader of the other group taps the map where the veins run thickest. "We give the forges new instructions. Tools can wait, shackles can't. We'll melt down speartips and strip the dead, and we stop wasting chains on feathers." The leader of the first group steeples his fingers. "The conquest won't stop for a lack of picks and links, leader of the other group. Let the birds burn if it pleases the Devourer, but if we choke the mines, we'll have little left to offer him." The leader of the other group scoffs. "We offer him obedience. Everything else is garnish." Chuckling, the leader of the first group gestures lazily in Naramus's direction. "And if the garnish is sweeter than the meat? Let the scholars breathe a little. Let the mages roam the archives again. I know what my people can do when given space to think." A silence settles, thick with soot, and sweat, and the stink of scorched feathers from somewhere far above them. The leader of the other group leans closer. "And when one of yours thinks a little too freely?" The leader of the first group shrugs. "Then by all means, shackle them with my blessing. But, until then." The leader of the first group trails off. The collar is warm around Naramus's throat. Outside, the wind shifts, and ash falls past the highset window, fine and grey. He dips the stylus again, and doesn't look up.
