Sofina knelt beside the bed, heart heavy. The soft glow of the candle flickered in the corner of the small room. Arya's bright eyes shimmered in the light, pointed towards the window with trepidation. "I'm scared, mama." She spoke, voice barely above a whisper. She clutched her stuffed rabbit tightly, as if it could shield her from the shadows lurking just beyond the window. "That's okay, my love." Sofina replied, voice soothing even as it trembled. "I'm scared, too. But I've got you. I'm here." "When is daddy coming back?" "I don't think he is, my darling." There was a knock at the door. "Sofina? Arya, darling? It's me, open up. Let me in." Sofina leaned over her daughter, embracing her. "Ignore it, sweetheart. It's okay. It's fine." Another knock echoed, louder than the last. "They're gone, Sofina, I promise. It's just me." Sofina knew the voice was lying. She'd seen it happen, after all, and she could only believe what she had watched. Watched as those things cut her husband's flesh. "Mama?" "It's okay, Arya. Just close your eyes and go to sleep." The knocking continued, louder and louder. "Go to sleep, darling. I'll stay with you until you wake up."
