The council chamber of Gor Vazumbrog was packed, voices bouncing off stone walls as representatives of the three factions debated the future of the realm. At the head of the first group stood Thrain Stonefist, a wiry adventurer. "Hunting monsters is the legacy," he argued, slamming a dagger onto the table. "Every forgotten tunnel, every cursed pit crawling with beasts—that's where we belong. What use is reclaiming other holds if our backyard itself isn't safe?" Across from him, General Magda Ironmarch crossed her arms, her gleaming armor reflecting the lantern light. "Your precious beasts won't march on our gates, Thrain," she countered, her tone sharp. "The Black orks will. We should be fortifying, building defenses, and projecting our power through the domain. Dauvek Veker weren't just hunters—they were warriors. Let's act like it." "And let the tunnels rot?" Thrain shot back. "What happens when the things in the dark grow bold because we were too busy building walls?" Magda sneered. "You'd waste resources chasing shadows while enemies at our doorstep regroup." At the edge of the room, Master Lorek Runehand, a tinker and scholar, coughed politely. "If I may—" "No, you may not." Thrain and Magda snapped in unison, glaring at each other. The argument raged on, neither side willing to yield, each convinced they carried the true spirit of Dauvek Veker. Meanwhile, the shadows beyond Gor Vazumbrog's gates crept closer, indifferent to dwarven squabbles.
