Ecaris Ecarizuir sat back in his chair, looked out past the gardens of Rezankaz, to the blue bay beyond. Karodir had a commanding, beautiful view in his apartments up here. Ecaris’ own quarters near the barracks weren’t so spacious. Given how much the old mage was complaining, of course, he figured his superior view was balanced out well enough by his grander troubles. “And now we’re just adventurers. Hunting monsters here, dealing with disputes there… outside the coast, they treat us like we’re judges with fancy swords. And don’t get me started on the armour–” Ecaris glanced over, smirked as Karodir pouted between sips of his tea. “Back to gilded steel, after all these years. They react to seeing SunPlate like they’re getting exorcised. I can’t even mess with a relic without one of their priests breathing down my neck– and–” Ecaris just let out a dry laugh. Shook his head. “Karodir… if you don’t like it… tough. This is just how it is now. We argue for what we can get, where we can find it – and thank Surael for their love of arguing… but on a certain level, we gave the Taychendi a choice. the Dark, the Oren Nayiru, or us.” He looked back to the bay – somber, now. “And that choice did not go in our favour. Not one bit. Chaos over order…” He still doubted it. Often. Whether this experiment could really cultivate Light. At any moment, he expected Orenvalyam to collapse in on itself – for the Malevolent Dark’s tendrils to sprout up from the cracks between the cells, Effelailike. For all their work to amount to nothing. Karodir gave a dejected sigh, looked out over the bay himself. The two just sat in silence, for a while, contemplating, reflecting. Eventually, Karodir spoke, in barely a whisper. “Right… we gave them a choice.” As if he shied from the very question. “Do you think that was a mistake?”
