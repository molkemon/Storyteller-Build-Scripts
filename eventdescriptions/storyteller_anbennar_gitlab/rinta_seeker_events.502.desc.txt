A great gathering of all available Mwalim and Darwiiz in our realm has coalesced, and debate has begun to rage about the indescribable events occurring in the skies above us. Though there is much disagreement about nearly every aspect of the situation, a single undeniable truth has emerged in regards to our actions going forward. this spectacle of lights must be understood, and to do that, we must watch it. The conference has unanimously approved the construction of a colossal telescope, one powerful enough to see the faces of the dead gods themselves in clear detail; hence the name, “Godswatch.” However, that is where the agreements have ended. The rationalist Mwalim have come to the general consensus that the purpose of such an observatory should be entirely practical. the studying of the celestial bodies of the heavens, their rules and mechanisms, so that we may better understand the order of things back on Halann. The more traditionalist Darwiiz, however, believe the telescope’s purpose should be purely spiritual. it should be used to view the remains of the dead gods, spread across the sky by the souls who slew them, so that their evil may be understood and the pursuit of enlightenment may be furthered. It is vital for this debate to be solved before the construction of the Godswatch can progress. We must make a declaration on what the purpose of the observatory should be, whether it be the Mwalim pursuit of reason, the Darwiiz pursuit of spirituality, or merely observance without a concrete purpose.
