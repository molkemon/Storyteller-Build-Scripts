Gerudians, unlike the southern lords, are not bound by land to serve their lords. We must therefore rule by the power of their conviction and their strength of will. Times have been hard for the people of the country, and many attribute the perilous state of the country to the ruler's lack of strong leadership. Matters have become so serious that a minor, yet willful member of the nobility has risen with an army and seeks to overthrow the monarch. We now have no choice but to meet them upon the battlefield. We must arrest further slip into anarchy.
