The War for Wheat has devastated Eastbright in recent months, a desperate doomed war of clanging pots against great teeming clouds of sparrows. Where once the golden crop susurrated in the valley wind, now only myriad little corpses litter the muddy, balding fields. A nascent trade in sparrow pie has emerged, but naturally this pales in moneyspinning potential compared to the breadbasket of the valley. Eastbright are convinced beyond all reason that this passerine pox is the work of their neighbor Bluehart, since the birds came from the right direction, and demand satisfaction.
