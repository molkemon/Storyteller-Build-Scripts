Though weaker by far than the ancient and powerful varieties of the Deepwoods, where the feywild itself seeps through the fraying fabric of space and time, minor fey and similar otherworldly spirits nonetheless hold relatively harmless dominion in many wild areas across Halann. The Trialmount is no exception, despite its close proximity to what was once the greatest of human cities, Castonath. The Trialmount approaches a group of floating spirits, struggling to track their wild movements as they zoom in and out of view, chasing each other in the air. The group's attention suddenly shifts as one turns to the approaching Trialmount as the aspirant casts their question to the fey. A second of deliberation; without a single trace of visible communication; and one of their group steps forward to give a cryptic answer. Knowing full well the trickery of the fey and experienced in matters of coercion, the Trialmount continues pressing the fey. The conflicting answers and intentionally misleading responses pile on thick, but the Trialmount is able to piece together several usable pieces of information from the group before they abruptly lose interest in this needy being and wander off.
