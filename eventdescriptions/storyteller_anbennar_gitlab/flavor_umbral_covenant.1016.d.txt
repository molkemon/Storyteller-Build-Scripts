The rift opens, and I can see. Once, in childhood, I compared my grandmother to the vulture. She was pleased by the comparison. Now I watch her drift contentedly among the ruins of the city, replete as one who has just finished a great meal. She glows, my Granny; the web of wrinkles suffuses her paperthin skin, and even sixfingered she is beautiful. She dances in the empty places, in the withering fog, in the grief of so many thousands dead. Sometimes, one need not wield a blade; it is enough to notice when the corpse begins to rot. The wisdom of the Coven is not merely in preparation, but in the grasping of opportunity.
