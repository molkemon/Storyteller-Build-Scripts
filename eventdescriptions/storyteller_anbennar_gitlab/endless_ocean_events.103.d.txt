Some suggest it is never the same twice, and others would swear their lives it has never changed once. It is known by many names to many people. The Anakue know it as "moa," and the Laautele call it the "sami." You yourself have known it by many names, as well, and you have etched them into the sand at the rise and fall of every moon. However, on this day, as the blue stretches beyond your sight and the whitecrested tips of the water surface slip by your gaze, something about the expanse before you seems novel. Maybe the caw of gulls follows a new rhythm, maybe the brine smells different, or maybe the patterns of the shells adorning the shore have changed. Surely, just surely, someone far away has a name for the sea that you have not yet heard. The waves unfurl and the skies part. A longheld friend presents a new face to you. Your reintroduction is long overdue.
