I sat in the corner of the tavern, sipping on my honeyed mead, watching the people of Kunolo go about their days. Not so different from Lelanàn itself, but on this third day of reconnaissance, I noticed the subtle difference. For sitting at the table to my right was a pair, a Halfling and a tall Human. I could not determine their genders, clearly lovers by how they held another. But as I looked around to see what others thought of them, nobody paid them any heed at all. I was the only one surprised by their bold differences. And I looked down at my mug, thoughtful. In Lelanàn, such a sight was not impossible; I knew that fact all too well. But there remained a sense of ostracization, othering, towards those who loved differently. I, Leli, and Felana had felt this, and maybe the City of Love itself may still have more yet to learn about love. After all, love must be unashamed. And here it was, on full display for all, a perfect manifestation of unashamed love and an example for all to follow.
