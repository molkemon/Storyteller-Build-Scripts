The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The man pauses his mantra. Again, his mind compels him to look up. The night sky is alight, shimmering with a thousand points of twinkling white light, dancing around back and forth as if they were alive. The city around him is bright, nearly as bright as day. But it is not day. He shuts his eyes as tight as he can. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. He knows the rumor. Everyone in the city knows the rumor. Many believe it. But it can’t be true. The gods are dead. They were slain at the gates of heaven by the souls enslaved within, seeking freedom for the harm wrought by the gods’ corruption. They are dead, and they have been dead for a very, very long time. They have to be dead. They have to be. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. His eyes are opened again. His gaze has focused on a single one of the false stars. It is too bright, too small to make out any details. Its movement through the skies slows, comes to a stop. He stares at it. It stares back at him. The gods are dead. The gods are dead. The gods— It is no use. He no longer believes the words he speaks. The gods are not dead.
