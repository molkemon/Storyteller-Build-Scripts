Before even the Century of Storms, there was still great peril that could come to the dragon turtles. The dragon turtles even in those days held the memories of many within their minds, and the death of even one would have terrible consequences for those in the afterlife. So when the volcano Muatlaqua erupted on an island where three dragon turtles stood and the Speaking Memory faithful saw, all they could do was weep. All of them, that is, except for Ula’ahi, who was possessed of the brand of foolish bravery that would lead men in future times to explore across the continent of Insyaa. When Ula’ahi saw the eruption of the volcano, he ran towards it with a speed he did not know he possessed. The lava was flowing towards the three dragon turtles, and all Ula’ahi could do was scream at them to move out of the way. But the dragon turtles were having a fascinating discussion about a topic that today is remembered by no one but them, and they refused to move out of the way of their impending doom. Fortunately, lava is quite slow to move, so Ula’ahi stood there for days in an attempt to persuade them. He exhausted every trick of reason he knew until finally the lava was almost next to them, and all Ula’ahi could do was scoop up the molten rocks next to the lava flow, and burn himself with them so the dragon turtles could finally see the danger. They moved after that, with all the help that Ula’ahi could provide them in his agonizing pain. Ula’ahi eventually recovered from his burns, but the scars of his bravery were marked on his face until the day he died.
