The halt is called, and Izun retreats, panting heavily. Each of Kelenthus strikes is a hammer, and Izun can feel each dull, heavy throb of the several hits Kelenthus has managed to land. They threaten days of recovery. Izun glances around; the faces of the spectators are pitying, when not derisive. It is then that Izun understands. The next round is called, and Izuns eyes narrow. What use honour? Nature knew no honour, when it gave Kelenthus those powerful arms. Izun finds purchase wherever they can, tearing at the back of Kelenthus knees, flying aloft for a single second to force Kelenthus to overbalance, ripping, ripping, ripping. When the bout is done, Kelenthus lies whimpering, covered in blood, and no pity remains.
