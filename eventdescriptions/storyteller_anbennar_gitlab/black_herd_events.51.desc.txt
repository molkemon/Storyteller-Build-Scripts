The creature screams as my lightning courses through its body, its rotted limbs convulsing as a horrible scratchy hiss escapes from its jawless mouth. Then it stops, twitching one last time before falling still. It is a fascinating being, two bodies fused unnaturally, seven limbs scuttling on the ground while one grasps blindly toward the ceiling. The sight sets my mind alight with ideas for the future; I itch to pull parchment from my saddlebags and sketch precisely how the second head is fused into the torso. But the ruins of Maghargma are no place for idle fancies. The deeper I go, the more such creatures I encounter. Each tunnel carries a new menagerie of horrors. The upper levels of the ruins were far more mundane, ogre skeletons and shambling zombies that did not even require my magic to dispatch. But now that I have reached the true bowels of the ruins they are far more twisted, far more interesting. Even if Almokh’s artifact turns out to be useless to my purposes, I will consider my journey a success for the inspiration alone. I do not believe this will be a failure, however. The faint pulse of magic has turned into a throbbing beacon that has long since saturated the halls of these depths with necromantic power. The undead are thicker than ever, and with every step I draw closer to the source of it all.
