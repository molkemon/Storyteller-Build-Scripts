Xianjie is a land of countless martial sects, each standing proud behind its ancestral name. From ancient times, sects have been registered in the Grand Martial Register, where place denotes prestige, and name grants incense offerings and political honor. Yet the martial world is ever shifting. New talents rise. Even the most venerable sects may find challengers at their gates. Recently, a little-known sect defeated a representative of an established orthodoxy during a regional duel. Riding the wave of victory, they now demand to take the elder sect's place in the Register. Outrage ensues—the old sect protests, the new insists. This matter has reached the provincial administration of Hero Isle. Officials are torn. Should tradition be upheld, preserving the dignity of the old name? Or should merit decide status, letting the new sect rise? Or perhaps a middle way—granting the newcomers a side position, and watching what comes next? A single decree will decide the fate of a sect. All eyes are watching.
