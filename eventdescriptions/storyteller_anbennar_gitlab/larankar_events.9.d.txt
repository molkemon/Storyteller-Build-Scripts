With Taychend now firmly under our grip, the emperor’s attention can turn to other matters – namely, the Imperial Family. While our soldiers – our heir among them – have been on campaign, marching to expand our borders and secure our rule, the heir’s children, Narathya and Tesheladar, have been locked in a vicious sibling rivalry that has threatened to undermine the succession – and potentially our empire’s future. Narathya, the elder sister, is as confident – some say arrogant – as she is innovative and well read. Holding unpopular ideas about the nature of royal power, her steadfast belief in her right to rule, and open disdain for her younger brother, has created space for Tesheladar to build his own support base. Though some call him weak, a puppet for more domineering figures, Tesheladar figures himself a man of the people, who wishes to work alongside, rather than lord over, his subjects. Though Narathya seems to be far more competent in matters of ruling, her unpopularity is troubling – supporting Tesheladar’s claim over his sister could ensure a much easier succession. Nonetheless, sometimes the harder road is the more rewarding, and our support now for Narathya now may pay off dividends later.
