The Kavalali watched the Chendhyan host recede into the distance. The negotiations were certainly a success – vast swathes of the Chendhyan steppe would now answer to Orenvalyam, the children of Munazhlan standing side by side with the descendants of those he had once rebelled against. There was no better sign of the changing time – of the coming Dawn. Of course, there were those who disagreed. Adezheg chieftains who would go back to their tribes and speak of the Oren Nayiru’s intransigence, how they were just the same old Taychendi empire, come again to crush their ancient way of life. They would have to be convinced in time – but there is no better way to convince them than to prove them wrong. Orenvalyam would not conquer the tribes – colonize them – they would bring them into the fold in their time, by their choice. And when the Kheionai or the Exemplars or the Cannorians say they had no choice but to bring civilization to their frontiers by force, Orenvalyam would stand as the proof of the hollowness of their words. This was the first test of that, and it would be the greatest example. Under Orenvalyam, with the concessions the Kavalakuttam made to them enacted, the Chendhya will prosper. And now, with the Agotham river now open to Taychendi forces, the gateway to Devand and the Cleaved Sea beyond – to the liberation of Andeios – is finally close at hand.
