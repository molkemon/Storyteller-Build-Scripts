The latest warband sent to the territory of Alenor returned a success, bragging of retribution and destruction, of killing in the Dookanson’s name. It is only the next full moon that the consequences of their actions bare their fangs. Awakening in the night to a mixture of screams and howls, Azukor grabs the nearest axe and leaps to his feet. Expecting a vengeful raiding party outside, he is met with a sight no ork could have been prepared to face. Kneeling over an orkish body in the muddy ground, half lit by a nearby torch, a creature of unnatural visage rises to meet Azukor's challenge, towering over him, closer to a warg or wolf than any man, with tusks larger than a dagger and claws sharper than any weapon he has seen forged. The beast roars, lunging at Azukor's throat, but a swift dodge makes the creature slam into the wall nearby, and a yet faster hammering of his axe into its neck spells its doom. He thinks to himself, ‘These damn Alenics must have invoked the woods against us,’ quickly rushing through the chaos to where a few prisoners from the last raid were kept, determined to end whatever foul ritual of the regent court has spawned these monsters. Yet he finds no ritual, instead he finds one of few orks who speaks the human tongue interrogating a terrified man of Alenor. "You," he barks at the interrogator, "What have they done? Where are these beasts coming from?" The younger ork rises to his feet, somber in tone and contemplating whatever bad news this man departed unto him. "Chieftain," he replies slowly, "He says prisoners weren't the only thing we brought back. Werewolves, he calls them. Says our men were afflicted with the curse and have turned against us." "What? These things are our own men?" Azukor responds, "After them. Quickly."
