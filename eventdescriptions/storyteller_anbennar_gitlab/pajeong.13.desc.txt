The Gitueni people, particularly those of the local merchants, have often turned their noses up at the Bateobugi of Danggun. The armored vessels are safe from man and spirit alike, yes, but slow, and the armor plating takes up precious cargo space. They instead build their boats with lightweight timber from Geuneulsup and shape them with sleek ritual carvings, and pray to the spirits beneath the waves. These ships speed through the water, carrying goods at breakneck pace to all corners of Haless. They desire funds to construct more of these vessels, built to their exacting yet costly standards, to continue their trade dominance.
