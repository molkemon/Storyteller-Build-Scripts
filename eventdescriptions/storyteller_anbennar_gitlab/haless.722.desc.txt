The elegance of the Yanshen Coast is famed for intricate lines and rigorous form. For generations, the Ganggim have lived by river and sea, nurtured by the Yan River's spirit and known for ingenuity through the ages. They honor invention and exploration, prizing craft and principle and enshrining mechanical skill and theoretical change as the true path of knowledge. Scholars favor symmetry and precision, casting new ideas into refined rhetoric. Insights appear in parallel couplets, often in the fu style—structured, symbolic, yet never ostentatious. Ganggim prose may detail ship mechanics or water flow with clarity; explore celestial instruments and astronomy with order; or wander mountains and rivers, using metaphor for philosophy. Every couplet matches, every word is placed with care. Their work prizes reason over emotion, innovation without losing grace—a rigorous, distinct current in Yanshen letters. This year, scholars on the coast compiled a hundred new fu. The language is grand, the ideas ingenious—some delve into logic, others turn words like gears. Which shall best represent today's Ganggim?
