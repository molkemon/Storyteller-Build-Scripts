It was early in the morning when a servant discovered Uwimbabazi’s body. She was found draped across the statue, her arms wrapped around it as if comforting it. Her eyes were closed, and she wore a soft smile on her face. She seemed at peace. A note was found at the base of the statue. “I have placed a part of me inside of here. Seek my counsel within it,” it read. “Uwimbabazi?” the servant tentatively asked. The statue gave no response. The members of her council quickly gathered in the room. They eyed the statue, which showed no signs of containing life, with suspicion. “I think it’s nonsense,” one said. “It’s not talking to us. I think she was insane by the end, anyway.” “How dare you,” said another. “She may have been past her prime, but she was still our Ingo.” “You make the damn thing talk to you, then.” The only thing they could agree on was that Uwimbabazi was dead. The Lady of Drums was no more.
