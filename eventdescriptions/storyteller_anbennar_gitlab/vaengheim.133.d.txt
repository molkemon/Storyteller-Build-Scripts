Now did the host of the monarch come upon the trail of their quarry, and it was fresh and clear. Long did they track, and strange grew their path, for the trail led hither and thither, doubling back and finding itself again. Soon did they spy one who would make sport of their hunt. Not fey nor troll, but a man named Aske, who was a hunter. Swiftly did the Wingmaidens strike at Aske, and with their mighty arms did they bring him to ground. Thus on his belly did the monarch meet him, and said, “I would ask you why you have put down this trail, o hunter. For much time has your toil stolen from me and I am wroth.” And Aske did say, “Greetings o queen. I would find my knee, but your maidens were quicker, and it is now theirs.
