The fortress city of AzkaszelUdam has been the sole stronghold of the Sun Cult on the southern shores of the Gulf of Rahen ever since the times of Maqet the Liberator. Holding out against repeated invasion attempts from the surrounding gnollish tribes for centuries, it only fell after more than five hundred years of stubborn resistance. Now, with the former lands of Dasmazar once again in the hands of the faithful, it was a foregone conclusion that the site of the EduzDiparkettu, the Temple of Truth’s Beacon, which was desecrated during the era of gnollish occupation, was swiftly restored to its former glory. Rising up on a promontory above the harbour bay, the temple ziggurat differs from the traditional Bulwari temples. Its top does not just contain a ceremonial platform but a lighthouse tower decorated with traditional red and white birmi murals that contains an eternal flame burning in an oil basin inscribed with potent light magic. This beacon of Surael’s truth shines day and night, a silent but defiant sign to all who approach that this city and its people reside within Surael’s Garden. Maqeti sailors, whether they are legitimate merchants or privateers coming home after raiding slaver vessels, feel their spirits lifting whenever they see the gleaming light of the beacon on the horizon. But those who stand in opposition to Surael and His children fear to stand in the glare of the beacon’s light, all their lies and deceptions exposed.
