Four hundred seven days, we have been here for four hundred seven days, and we have nothing to show for it. Our supplies are dwindling, and more and more of our expedition is lost, both to the creatures that inhabit this jungle and the worryingly common tendency to just wander off. I do not want to leave, and it certainly will not be a popular decision, not after so long, but I fear we have no choice. We have lost so much already; any longer in this jungle, I fear none of us will ever be free of it.
