"I didn't do anything. I'm innocent," cries the man as the noose is tied around his neck. "You can't do this. You don't have any evidence." He turns to the crowd, who watch the proceedings with mild bemusement. "Good people, my neighbors. You can't... you know me. I have a wife and child. Who will provide for them? Who will..." he trails off as he realizes not a single soul in the crowd cares. Stunned into silence, he barely registers the counter finishing reading off the good hymns behind him. The counter pulls the lever, the floor falls out from under the man, and swiftly he is no more.
