A boy with red hair rolled his eyes as he and his father raced on their antler horses through the city's streets, which didn't have much in the way of traffic. "What do you mean, I'll get a Riverblade?" he asked his father, one hand on the saddle. "I thought I would get a Malacnari instructor." "I spoke with the Shieldmaster. While you have good grit, your slender build and agility lends itself more to the training of a Riverblade, and the water magic will be far more useful when you inherit our domain." Having raced past several of the city's more lacklustre quarters, the boy and his father were greeted by the sound of running water as they crossed an archway. There they saw a fountain of veinless white marble holding a statue of a lord, at the center of a square floored with a gold and white tile mosaic that extended to the thresholds of the curved buildings surrounding it. These buildings were bright with fresh paint, some sporting balconies and hanging plants, others full of gardens in the place of roofs. Out of the buildings came young nobles and their parents, and the scent of delicious meals being served within. Some youths were reading books by the fountain, others were sparring with their retainers, and by wandering far enough through the district's lavish buildings and gardens, one would be able to locate steelclad Malacnari Battleguards, dashing Riverblades, and elder Dolindhan knights, all ready to impart their wisdom on the new generation of nobles. This was the city's Drantnik District, the Shield Bearer District, the closest to the Teal Keep both physically and hierarchically, and it was here that the nobility of both the city and beyond resided and where their scions practiced to become squires, clerks, priests, and, pray maybe one day, artists.
