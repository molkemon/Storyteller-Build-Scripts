The mood was as grim as it could be. Each High Kavalal sliced their hand, letting their blood drip into a bowl in the table center – an oath of silence. Necessary security, considering one of those who might have taken it was currently being mopped up off the chamber floor. “This has gone too far…” the High Kavalal of Katelperai began. “Executing a High Kavalal in cold blood, no matter what he did?” “If he even did it,” Ethanan interjected, “which we do not know.” Nakar Mlerek. “And Velara?” Kriadvai. “The one source of moderation around the Kamrayakval…” Kilaiek. “Dead – and he fingers us for it.” Tirsin scoffed at that. “To be fair, we are currently scheming against him.” Katelperai shook his head. “Not scheming… discussing. As is our right – no matter what the Kamrayakval says. How has it gotten to this…?” “It’s the Armour,” Sthanan replied, “Yodhan suffered the same – getting madder by the year, corrupted by his draconic plate.” “What sort of mad?” Asked Rakkabuttai. “He still seems sharp to me. And the soldiers still love him.” Tirsin. “My flock would revolt if he went.” Ethanan cut in. “And what of the farmer who tires of war? And the newly liberated, fingered for rebellion and treason, harassed and slaughtered? They would revolt if he did not.” Mlerek gave a gruff nod. “It’s him or Dawn. We simply must remove him.” But Rakkabuttai shot back. “And you think he will go along with it? Vote against him, and you will end up like Sadarzhil.” Orenkoraim. “then we defend ourselves. Ecaris could have the power.” Kilaiek. “You are not putting that damned colonizer anywhere near our sacred mission–.” But Vettakaramai broke the news. “You could not even if you wanted to. He is gone. Disappeared.” Silence followed – Katelperai cut through it with a sigh. “Still… we need a weapon. The longer the Kamrayavkal has control of the faith, the more his ways will take root. We tried to raise a hero to lead our faith, and he has spurned us…”
