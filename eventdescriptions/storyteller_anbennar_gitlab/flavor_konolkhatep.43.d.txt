O Horutep, Undisputed Monarch of the Seas. In the days of the warring packs, the gnollish people were great corsairs, and the Gulf of Rahen and the Divenhal alike feared them. Their piracy was possible not because they had any great love of the water, but because the navies of their enemies were weak and because their people refined the practice of raiding to a fine art. Although they are not opposed to plunder, their objective on the seas is no longer to flit between ports stealing away unlucky shoredwellers. Where the old corsairs claimed mere gold and slaves, they now claim all the sea. Their fleet is strong where the fleets of the others are weak. Their admirals oversee armadas as large as cities, and the banner of Konolkhatep on a trading ship guarantees its safe passage in all the ports of the world. Were some band of pirates foolish enough to raid their shores the way they raided so long ago, their heads would be separated from their bodies with grapeshot. This overwhelming force is what makes their dominion of the seas possible, and the rewards of their enlightened rule will surely accrue to all the world.
