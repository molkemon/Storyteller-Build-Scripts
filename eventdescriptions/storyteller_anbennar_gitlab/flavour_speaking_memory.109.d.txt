The exkeeper sat in the middle of a command center, where he had been tasked with directing logistics shipments to and fro. Apparently only his memory could handle so many shipments at once, and so he had been torn away from his room, which was so comfortable and had such important work in it. Down his aisle came the general, who had apparently decided that now was the time to ask him about the status of the fourth shipment, which he knew the status of. He knew the status of all of the shipments. But shipments didn’t have afterlives, the keeper thought, and people do. He put his latest bank of reports to be taken away by military staff, and reflected on how sad it was that he hadn’t been assigned to memorize troop shipments instead.
