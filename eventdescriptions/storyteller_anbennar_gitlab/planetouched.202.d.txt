While inspecting one of their armies, the monarch realized that they train more diligently when observed by the ruler up close rather than from a distance. The monarch believes that this stems from the ruler's obviously planetouched appearance; whether this is due to the soldiers deriving inspiration from the flames or by a fear of burning cannot be said. Whichever is the truth, the monarch could choose to use a display of the ruler's power to help their armies become better than their rivals. While doing this could be used to inspire or discipline their troops, it would also be a constant demand for the monarch's time, as the ruler would have to make frequent trips to their army.
