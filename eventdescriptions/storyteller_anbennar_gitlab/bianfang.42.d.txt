Word has reached the monarch. a clique of palace eunuchs has attempted to assassinate their court rival, the military advisor. Aiming to elevate one of their own to the office of military advisor, they tried to bar the doors and set fire to the advisor's headquarters as the advisor was holding a meeting of their command staff. However, the eunuchs underestimated the virile strength of the nation's fighting men, who quickly cut them down. The sole surviving member of the clique beseeched the monarch to spare him. He claimed the military advisor had been steadily spreading their nefarious influence in the army, building power to eventually usurp the monarch in a military coup. He and his coconspirators were compelled to remove the military advisor for the good of the nation. His selfrighteous pleading was cut short only by the blade of the military advisor himself, slicing clean through his neck. The monarch now faces the choice of what to do with the rest of the sorry lot. Proclaiming ignorance of the plot, the remaining eunuchs would surely be loyal if spared, and unable to amass the influence which had allowed the clique to even consider such a brazen scheme. However, the assassination of a trusted advisor is high treason, and must be treated accordingly. Justice must be done, and be seen to be done.
