The Dhenbasana is littered with palaces, funded by embezzled taxes and tariffs as well as withheld tributes, bribes to look past such crimes, or in rare cases, genuine success and wise rule. No two are identical, but almost all share one feature. a well-concealed escape route. The monarch is reminded of this fact as he hears today’s report from a messenger dressed in a strange red and white hat. Our enemy's enemy managed to siege down the fortress where the Raja had hidden himself, but he escaped like smoke on the wind. "Your Majesty," he concludes, "the Raja has found refuge in another palace." Good. Haless could not outrun Harimar; the stream cannot outrace the sea. Run as he likes, we will soon capture the Raja, and with his forces, the Lotus Throne.
