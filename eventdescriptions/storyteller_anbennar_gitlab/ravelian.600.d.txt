The Ravelian faith believes that most, if not all religions are simply aspects of one true faith and a One True God. Sainthood was a way to recognize holy figures in the mythos of possible converts, and to some, a clever way to extend an open hand of friendship to those that might be put off by the idea of a monotheistic religion that denies the existence of other faiths. A recent debate, however, has sparked among believers. is it right that avatars, divine reincarnations of the Regent Court gods, are held at the same rank and respect as fully realized deities? An example of this was Saint Auci Dameris, Queen of Dameria, and Saint Minara. Auci has been well regarded as an avatar of The Dame, an accepted aspect of God, and canonized for her endeavors in bringing the elves to Cannor. Minara, however, has always been regarded as a lesser deity of the Regent Court. The church's official stance is that all saints are the same, but the rectors have been arguing and debating this for months now.
