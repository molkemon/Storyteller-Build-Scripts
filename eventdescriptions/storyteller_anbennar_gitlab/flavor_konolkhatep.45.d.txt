O Horutep, Renowned Architect of the Dunes. The desert is full of wonders that could only have come from your mind, just as the hopes and dreams of Konolkhatep sprout from your drafting compass. When the pilgrims, merchants, and tourists of faroff lands come to marvel at your miracles, they carry part of Konolkhatep home with them. Just as it could be said that the pyramids and temples you have built are the highest expression of the culture of Konolkhatep, it could also be said that your great buildings created the culture of Konolkhatep. To say you bear a nation on your shoulders is trivial to the point of mundanity, but where you are venerated for your rule as soldier, scholar, merchant, or mage, it is not often remarked upon that it is your buildings that made such pursuits possible at all. When your people gaze upon the works of Konolkhatep, let them offer thanks to you.
