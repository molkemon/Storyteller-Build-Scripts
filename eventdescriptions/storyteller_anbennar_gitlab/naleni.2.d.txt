A cold wind cuts across the mountainside hall, the faint hint of salt water carried on it. Upon a rudimentary throne the Matriarch sits, stolen from a displaced human ruler and carried by a team of harpies up here to the cliffside roost some centuries ago. Opposite stands an Odanchés defiant, eyes cast up towards the throne with no deference, the curved blade a shotel upon their hip a visual affront to the Matriarch's authority. Even the magnificent stone statue carved in the wall behind, a grand monument to a ruler past, causes nary the bat of an eye. Other harpies line the walls of the hall. Half bear the flag of the Matriarch, the flag of Naléni itself, upon their armour. The rest have no semblance of similarity at all, bright colours patched over dull, tattered clothes held together by repeated needlework, the coarse uniforms of sailors. "You overstep." The Odanchés shrieks, shrill and irreverent tone eliciting uncomfortable shifting about the room. "We've been living this way for centuries, and now you want us to fight, to go to war? We haven't warred since Siádenlen fell. Our home is the sea, why fight Rahen, why care about humans?" "Our home is here, where we make our Roosts," the Matriarch replies, rising from her throne. "None love the sea more than I, but a people cannot live upon boats. It is not for love of humans, but need of them. You forget your place, Odanchés." The word is full of venom, and silence hangs over the hall for a moment. "Get out of my sight." The Odanchés spits on the sunbleached stone, hand twitching atop the hilt of their Shotel, but they simply whirl about face, launching into the air and departing, their followers taking suit. As they leave, the cold wind cuts again.
