The most vexing of foes known to mortal kind, Kraken seem to combine the most frightening traits of their cousins. They are fiendishly intelligent; they are known to open doors, remember faces, and play tricks on their wouldbe hunters. They possess twelve arms of herculean strength, capable of errantly snapping a ship in twain. They may render themselves near invisible in any environment, so adept is their palette-shifting camouflage. They are even flexible enough to squeeze into any space their beak may fit within; smaller specimens have been known to hide inside ghost ships, waiting in ambush for some ill-fated rescue party. And if by some miracle an army presents enough of a threat to wound a Kraken, the beasts have one final defense—a massive well of ink by which immediately and permanently stains whatever it touches, by which to blind their assailants. Unlike their short-lived kin, Kraken do not die after the mating season. In fact, these monstrous creatures are biologically immortal, and they continue to grow throughout their lives, so long as they can find food to sustain their immense bulk. However, parent Kraken do not eat during the mating season, preferring instead to ceaselessly blow fresh water over their eggs. At the end of this incubation period, the parent Kraken are starved, ink-dry, and they will not run. It is during these times the fearless and the mighty amass in the thousands to hunt this impossible prey. Even drained, tangling with a mother Kraken is a life-or-death affair, and the oldest specimens bear the scars and experience of countless battles. Only the most brilliant tactical efforts may attempt Kraken Hunts, and only the greatest survive.
