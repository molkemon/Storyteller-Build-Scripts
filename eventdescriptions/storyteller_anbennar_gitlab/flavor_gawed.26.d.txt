It had been exactly one year since the Emperor called the Imperial Parliament and a month since our spies learned today would be their invasion day. The Gawedi armies commanded by their monarch stood outside Vertesk, watching the quiet horizon in the whistling wind like hawks. Their glinting armor dimmed as the sun set, all the while awaiting the Empire’s response. The city itself had been evacuated, half the population leaving in anticipation of a clash between the Northern Eagle and Dove Throne’s armies. Few had hoped war would be avoided, the Gawedi ruler themselves arriving to oversee the outbreak of conflict. But as the sun dimmed, the hopes and morale of the Gawedi forces rose. Not a dove stood in sight of the assembled forces as the shadow of the Eagle loomed over the Dames crown and Alenic Frontier. As it became evident that no foe would appear, the troops jeered their challenge to the southern cowards, the same challenge the Kings of Gawed had issued for decades. But no dove arrived to dance with the proud eagle.
