Our military is at a crossroads; the way men make their way into the army now consists of two entirely separate pipelines. The traditionalist group of the north drafts based on heritage and family lines, constituting roughly a quarter of the military. The more progressive pipeline in a certain domain selects based on talent; the officials of that faction now wish to curtail the privileges of the traditionalist group because their raiding is damaging the logistics and reputation of the army. The traditionalist warriors of the steppes, our noble caste of warriors, traditionally consisting of spearmen and birdriders who trace their lineage back to ancient ancestors, stand firmly against the reforms proposed by the council of the Army Academy in a certain city. The traditionalist warriors of the north demand we integrate their rights to raiding into the legal code and make the birdriders a more prominent part of the military. The Military Council in that city, which represents the more modern segment of our military, consists of prominent generals and military advisors. They demand that we restrict looting to a minimum and modernize our military away from the terrorbirds, who they describe as archaic. They instead wish to increase spending on artillery. Whichever side we please, grudges will be forged today.
