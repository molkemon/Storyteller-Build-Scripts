Niece. croons Auntie Komina, sweeping across the courtyard in a cloud of gauzy fabric. She leaves a cascade of light kisses on the Vile One’s cheek; it elicits an embarrassed smile, as it always does. Mama Hireet merely nods; she has hauled herself down, incongruously perching on a low wall. Granny Ilfa emerges last, as she tends to. “I’ve done well,” the Vile One announces. She learned as a child that praise from the Coven must be demanded. “Even now, my forces march unimpeded across Sarhal.” “You have not failed,” barks Hireet; the Vile One’s expression does not shift under the assault. It must not. Granny Ilfa waves a sixfingered hand in Hireet’s direction, irritable. “You have exceeded the pace I expected,” Granny says. A tight smile escapes the Vile One’s grasp; there is no greater praise than this from Ilfanill. “But yours is a life where demands, once met, only multiply.” “I know, Granny. I do not grow complacent.” “Oh, don’t bully her, you two.” Komina is on her feet, pacing, ever restless. Today she is a Baashidi beauty, highcheekboned and almondeyed, spindlethin. “Her shadowmen are much tidier than any of your lot.” The Vile One cracks a smile, quickly suppressed. Hireet and Komina descend into their bickering, as is their wont. But Granny Ilfa is quiet; always watching, always measuring.
