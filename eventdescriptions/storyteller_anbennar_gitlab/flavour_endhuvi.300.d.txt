Our ruler, Senapti Vapyatar the Sixth, has gone some years without an heir. Perhaps it was due to his vituperative reaction when Parusad Bhola appointed the first female Senapti, misogynistic even by the standards of the Senapti of the Raj, but there have been few offers of marriage. The court has thus decided to take things into its own hands. A few years ago, discreet envoys were sent by the nobles to friends in nearby realms, and one has now returned with interest. Asima of the Crouching Shadow has for some time wished to escape her family's grasp; their backward ways, as she sees them. While our Senapti is notoriously reactionary, his court is not, and Asima's situation is not one in which she can be choosy. Knowing the mismatch between ruler and elite, she sees too an opportunity. Envisioning the future of the harimari not as one of conquest, but of clever words and skillful statescraft, she began a correspondence with our court; not merely on matters of state, but also on art and philosophy, a wooing of our finest. Thus, some months later, she arrived by quiet ship up the river with her young son in tow. The young harimari has proven himself to be competent and quick to learn the intricacies of the realm, endearing him despite everything to our old-fashioned Senapti. Certainly, his mother's own skills are quite formidable as well. In such a way, the Crouching Shadow's claws curl around Endhuvi.
