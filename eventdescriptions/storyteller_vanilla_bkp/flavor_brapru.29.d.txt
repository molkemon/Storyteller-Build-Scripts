Everything in war is very simple. But the simplest thing is difficult, our lord says solemnly during a council meeting. That is profound, our ruler exclaims, to which our advisor reddens and admits to having stolen the phrase from a philosophical young man who has been rising through the ranks of our army over the past few years. In addition to being an experienced commander, this person is apparently making a name for themselves even in the higher circles of society. They are also described as spending a lot of their time contemplating and discussing the moral aspects of war. Some say that given enough time they are likely to publish a book on the subject. Perhaps they could be of use to us?
