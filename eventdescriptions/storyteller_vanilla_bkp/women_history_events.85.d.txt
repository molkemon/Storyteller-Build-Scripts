The pirate ship Revenge, captained by Calico Jack Rackham, held a wild party when the pirate hunter Captain Jonathan Barnet fired a barrage of shots, disabling the vessel. The crew either fled or were too intoxicated to fight, leaving Mary Read and Anne Bonny to defend against Barnet's troops. They were eventually overwhelmed, and Rackham surrendered. The entire group was arrested and sentenced to hang for piracy. The two women took advantage of a temporary reprieve, known as "Pleading the Belly," until their children were born. Mary Read shared with us her life before joining Rackham and Bonny, born as an illegitimate child to a sea captain's widow. Her mother disguised her as a boy, naming her "Mark," to receive money from her dead brother's grandmother. As a teenager, Read first worked as a footboy and then found employment on a ship, still dressed as a man. She joined the British military, proving herself in battle. After falling in love with a Flemish soldier, they purchased an inn with their military commission and gifts from friends in the army, who had not realized that "Mark" was actually a woman until her marriage. Her husband passed away young, and Read once again dressed as a man to take employment as a soldier. However, there were no wars, no battles to fight, and no room for advancement. She quit the army and boarded a ship, which was later taken by pirates. Read was forced to join them. After taking the King's pardon and a privateer's commission, she joined the crew in a mutiny and returned to piracy. It was then that she met pirate captain Calico Jack Rackham and his companion Anne Bonny.
