Italy, no longer the 'Shadow Kingdom', has been returned into the fold of the Holy Roman Empire through the relentless efforts of the Emperor. The Papal State must now choose whether to accept the hegemony of the Emperor or be left exposed and vulnerable on its doorstep. To accept the authority of the Emperor would have dire ramifications for the Catholic world. While Catholics within the Empire might have their faith strengthened through the legitimacy conferred by Papal membership, those outside the Empire are unlikely to take kindly to such a decision and may turn to heresy.
