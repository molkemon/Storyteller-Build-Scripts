The guilds, or fraternal organizations, were once a common sight in France and the Low Countries. Originally, they served as a sort of mutual support and protection for their members, providing a sense of community and camaraderie. However, under the strict rule of the French monarchs, the guilds began to lose their original purpose. By the end of the fifteenth century, their privileges had become a means for the royal treasury to collect taxes from the employers, rather than a source of protection for the guild members.
