After Emperor Charles the Fifth had rejected the Protestants' confessional positions at the Imperial Parliament of Augsburg, the North German Protestant Imperial estates formed the League of Schmalkalden, with a joint army and treasury and seeking ties abroad in France. The League enjoyed early successes in the years 1532 to 1540 as the Emperor was threatened by Turkish danger and forced to conclude a religious peace settlement in Nuremberg in 1532 and in Kaaden in 1534.
