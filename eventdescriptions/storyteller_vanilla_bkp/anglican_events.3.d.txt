The Anglican Church does not require that its clergy devote much time to preaching. A monthly or even quarterly sermon is sufficient according to the law, which is usually lifted directly from the Book of Homilies. Many parishioners desire a more personal and involved approach to religious services, and have taken matters into their own hands by pooling money to pay for educated lecturers to preach in their towns. These lecturers however do not always have the most orthodox of theological views. It is often the case that the most eager to preach are those with the most radical ideas, and so these private sermons have become associated with the Puritan movement. While there are advantages to a more religiously literate populace, cracking down on Puritanism would increase the authority of the Church.
