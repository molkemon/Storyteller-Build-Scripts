In 1717, the Mississippi Trade Company was founded. It had a royal monopoly on the utilization of the large colonial areas along the Mississippi River in America. In 1719, the government borrowed 1.5 billion livres from the company. The company was more of a financial institution than a company of trade and industry, and the price of shares skyrocketed. In 1720, the French bubble burst, and the fact that the shares of the company were overvalued was evident even to the man on the street. Soon panic broke out, and the company was declared bankrupt. John Law was forced to flee from France.
