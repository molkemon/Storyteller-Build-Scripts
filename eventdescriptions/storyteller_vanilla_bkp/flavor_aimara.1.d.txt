The Incas have arrived and we will not submit to them, worship their gods, nor serve them as slaves. Our advisor was one of many who wanted us to start this war but he has changed his mind after we have had many defeats by the Incas. He wishes to persuade the people to accept the Incas and our advisor tells us that the Inca ruler is benevolent, the Inca's laws are good and their government is gentle. If we were to submit and be conquered, he argues, we will not be seen as slaves but as brothers, and a peace with the Inca would be convenient.
