When Sultan Ismâ'îl ascended the throne of Kilwa in the year 1442, his rule was questioned by the local rulers in Zanzibar who supported his relative Said's bid to become sultan. Said's plot was, however, thwarted by the royal advisor Suleiman and the emir Muhammad alMazlum. With the passing of Ismâ'îl himself, however, it seems Suleiman and Muhammad have decided that the time has come for them to rule in their own name. Acting quickly, they have used their influence to secure the capital for themselves and have agreed to share power by taking turns on the throne.
