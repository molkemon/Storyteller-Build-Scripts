The people in that province were fascinated by the glass beads, brass bells, and cloth our explorers traded for fresh fish and venison. In their report to us, the ship's crew mentioned that there was very little wealth among the people and they seemed peaceful. Some events caused them concern, as the villagers suddenly left their homes one night and abandoned the village for our expedition to explore. We found little of note, only a small gold rattle buried among some fishing nets suggesting that this province might hold some promise for us.
