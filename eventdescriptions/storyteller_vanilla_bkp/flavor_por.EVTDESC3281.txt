After the death of the king, the forces that had been plotting against him turned their attention to the person who had actually ruled the Portuguese kingdom, Marquês de Pombal. With no support from the king, Pombal's position was precarious at best, and he was forced to flee. Consequently, the reforms he had initiated, which had put Portugal back on the path to prosperity, were not destined to last. Instead, reactionary policies implemented by the old nobility and the clergy again moved Portugal backwards, threatening to leave her lagging behind the other European powers in the centuries to come.
