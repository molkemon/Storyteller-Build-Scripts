In the year 1721, in order to control the Orthodox Church of Russia, Peter the Great eliminated the position of the Patriarch of Moscow. From that point forward, the patriarch's role as leader of the Church was taken over by a group of spiritual leaders, known as the Holy Synod, consisting of representatives who were loyal to the ruler's wishes. A government official, the chief supervisor, was appointed by the ruler to oversee the activities of the Holy Synod. The Holy Synod brutally suppressed all opposition and censored all publications.
