According to Imperial law, the Electorate of Brandenburg remains in the hands of the noble despite our suzerainty. Naturally the other Electors would be less inclined to support our bids for Emperorship if we are seen to be forcing their peers to vote in our favor. If however we depose the noble and put our own ruler on the throne, the electorate would pass to their heir and the issue would be bypassed. Though this would surely upset the lords of Brandenburg. Alternatively, we could restore the historical March of Brandenburg, once a protective buffer for the German realm against their enemies to the east.
