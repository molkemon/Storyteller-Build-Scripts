The ruler of Japan has issued a collection of edicts called Buke Shohatto, or the Various Points of Laws for Warrior Houses. It is a code of conduct, outlining how an honorable daimyo should behave. Some of the points include: The samurai class should devote itself to pursuits fitting a warrior aristocracy, such as archery, swordsmanship, horsemanship, and classical literature. Amusements and entertainments are to be kept within reasonable bounds, and expenses for such activities should not be excessive. The han, or feudal domains, are not to harbor fugitives and outlaws.
