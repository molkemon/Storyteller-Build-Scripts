The time has come for another group of young men to undergo the rite of 'Emuratare', finally becoming men and warriors. With adulthood comes the responsibility of defending their people and territory. For ten years, they will live together in the 'Emanyatta', a warrior village, during which they will serve the nation. With this influx to the country's army, they also have the opportunity to shape a new generation of warriors. What should be the focus of their education be?
