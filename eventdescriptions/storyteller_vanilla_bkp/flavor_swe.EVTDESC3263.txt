In response to the rising clamor for more power to the aristocracy, Gustav III appealed to the three lower estates of the Riksdag and in 1789 established a new constitution that augmented the royal authority. This Act of Union and Security was in effect almost a return to the days of absolutism under Charles the Eleventh and the Twelfth. It proved too much for the aristocracy however, and the King was murdered while attending the Stockholm opera house on the sixteenth of March, 1792.
