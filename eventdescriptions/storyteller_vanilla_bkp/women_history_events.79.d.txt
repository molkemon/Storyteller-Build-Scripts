Led by their queen, a group of people reached the Komoe river after a long and arduous journey. The river was swollen with water and seemed too treacherous to cross. The priest was consulted on how to cross the river safely, and after contemplating the Komoe, he said that a sacrifice was required, a loved one had to be given to the river to ensure safe passage for all. The queen held her son closely before she threw the infant into the water, crying "the child is dead"; as she saw her son disappear into the water. Her group took the name Baoule to honor the queen and her loss. The queen and her people could safely cross the river on the back of hippopotamuses who appeared and formed a bridge for them. The Baoule settled on the other side of the river and though she mourned the loss of her son, the queen ruled her people with compassion and wisdom.
