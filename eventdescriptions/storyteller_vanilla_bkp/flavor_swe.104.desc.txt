The crown councils of Norway, Sweden, and Denmark met in 1397 in the city of Kalmar and decided to unite the three Scandinavian nations under a single ruler. This 'Union of Kalmar' might have been the largest state in Europe, but it was troubled from the start due to its loose organization and unbalancing Danish domination. Eventually, the desire for independence reached a new height when the Danish ruler got elected to the new position of the Kalmar Union, and planned the execution of several Swedish nobles in the capital. The Vasa dynasty ruled.
