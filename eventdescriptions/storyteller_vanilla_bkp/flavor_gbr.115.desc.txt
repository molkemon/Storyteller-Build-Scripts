Several military orders were founded during the first Crusades. Borrowing from that idea, and paired with the concepts of chivalry, many other orders have been established in a courtly fashion. They include the idea of confraternity, and are associated with both merit and prestige, as well as the appropriate medals and distinctions. Some of them are also tied to a particular noble family, or even endorsed by royal patronage, be it foreign or our own.  Some voices proclaim these kinds of orders are already a thing of the past, whereas others claim that chivalry, merit and values should always be lauded and rewarded. Our nation has always been in favor of worthy individuals, and the values proposed by the orders are for sure something to be upheld. As such, our country will benefit greatly from an order of chivalry promoted and endorsed by our monarch to last for generations, representing the best values of our nation and formed by the best individuals it can offer.
