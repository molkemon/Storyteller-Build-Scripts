Some of the most important Russian reforms of government were instituted by Ivan the Fourth. Ivan had built on the works of Ivan the Third and bound the nobility even closer to the ruler. He also created a separate chamber of finance directly at his disposal, which provided a certain efficiency in the flow of money. The abolition of the payment system for the provincial administration, where salaries were paid directly from the peasants, and the reorganization of the provincial administration made the ruler much more efficient and powerful. Unfortunately for Russia, these reforms were not enough to keep the state afloat after the costly and disastrous wars at the end of Ivan the Fourth's reign.
