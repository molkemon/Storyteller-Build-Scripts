The ruler has been more at peace since the funeral of their cousin was seen to. The ceremony was even attended by diplomats from the nonchalant neighbor. Relieved by the knowledge that their relative is safe in the arms of the Goddess Yemoja, the songs still echo in the mind of the monarch.  'But when you see a Kob antelope on the way to the farm, When you see the Kob antelope on the way to the river – Leave your arrows in the quiver, And let the dead depart in peace.'
