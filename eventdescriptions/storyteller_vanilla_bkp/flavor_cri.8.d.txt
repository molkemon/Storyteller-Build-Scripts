Following a hardfought campaign, the ruler has called for a Kurultai at the recently conquered city of Soltanieh. An assembly of generals and lesser Khans gathers outside a most auspicious location; the Mausoleum of the Ilkhan Oljeitu. Speaking before the Kurultai, the ruler raises the pressing question of how the country is to be ruled; they gestures toward the Mausoleum and boldly proposes that the realm should be restored. Invoking the legacy of the great Mongol Empire and the conquests of Genghis Khan, they make a compelling case for the revival of the subordinate Khanate.
