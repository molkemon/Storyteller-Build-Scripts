In a drive towards Warsaw, he was bogged down by excellent Polish command while his own nobility rebelled. Isolated with his army in Czersk, he struck a deal with the Polish ruler to leave Poland immediately if the Polish ruler would refuse to accept the Lithuanian throne from the rebels in the north. It was a not too glorious retreat and he considered himself defeated. The nobility had dethroned Aleksandras, but was sidetracked by the fact that the Polish ruler refused to have anything to do with the traitors. Not all of the nobility were against Aleksandras and upon his return another army joined him. Together they smashed the rebel forces and executed the traitors. These events resulted in a deep-seated foreign policy in Lithuania to always support the Polish rulers against their internal foes, but never to meddle without being asked.
