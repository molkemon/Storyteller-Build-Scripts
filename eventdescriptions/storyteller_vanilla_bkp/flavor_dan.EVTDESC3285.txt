Upon succeeding to the throne, the ruler bypassed the council and used the chancellery to grant commercial privileges to merchants, overriding the claims of Danish nobles and traders of the Hanseatic League. In 1517 the ruler accused the governor of Copenhagen Castle of poisoning his lover Dyveke, and thereafter he ignored the council and the royal charter. He created an essentially bourgeois government with the mayor of Malmø, Hans Mikkelsen, as his special advisor.
