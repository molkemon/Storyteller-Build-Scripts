The numbers of merchants from a certain country who seek out our markets have grown from a trickle to a steady stream. Their main interest seems to be in procuring slaves either directly or through purchasing trade goods we have that can be traded for slaves. So far this has made a few local chieftains very rich, which is a dangerous development as it both empowers these local rulers and impoverishes the surrounding settlements. The traders from our capital are asking that we create a more consistent framework and regulate the trade through royal monopolies. The traders themselves also seem interested in more formal and permanent arrangements.
