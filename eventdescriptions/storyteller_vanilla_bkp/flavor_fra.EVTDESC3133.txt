After the early success with John Law's Bank and the Mississippi Trade Company, the government acquired the Bank and borrowed 1.5 billion livres from the Trade Company to pay off its massive national debts. These events sparked a surge in the French economy and the government lacked all restraint, printing large quantities of money without any security. When the overheated economy began to face reality, the investors of the trade company panicked, driving both the Bank and the Trade Company into bankruptcy. As much as two years' worth of collected taxes disappeared in the catastrophe, and France was thus forced to declare bankruptcy in 1724.
