One of the more difficult things about being a parliamentarian has nothing to do with the debates in the capital but rather how to sell the new laws to the people back home. Still, a local representative will often have an easier time explaining a new tax than the collector sent by the central government would in less representative systems.  It seems the representatives in general and the ones from our country in particular have become so adept at defending policies that we can see a rise in the support of the central government as a whole as a result.
