Historically, this manifesto was first published by the head of the Manchu, Nurhaci, on the thirteenth day of the fourth lunar month in the third year of Tianming. The animosity between the Manchu tribes and Ming reached a zenith, leading to all-out war between the two powers. The Seven Grievances are as follows: The Ming killed their ruler's father and grandfather without reason. The Ming suppressed their people and favored rivaling clans. The Ming violated agreements of territories with their ruler. The Ming sent troops to protect their enemies against them. The Ming supported other clans' plans to break their promise to their ruler. The Ming forced their ruler to give up the lands in Chaihe, Sancha, and Fuan. The Ming's official Shang Bozhi abused his power and rode roughshod over the people.
