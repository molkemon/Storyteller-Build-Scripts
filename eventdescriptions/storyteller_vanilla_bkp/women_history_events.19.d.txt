Born with her umbilical cord wrapped around her neck, a sign of a proud and haughty personality, Nzinga was the daughter of King Kiluanji and Kangela. Her father favored her and let her follow him both in war and in the governing of his kingdom. She was the envoy of her brother at a peace conference with the Portuguese governor João Correia de Sousa, and managed to persuade him to stop the marauding of Imbangala mercenaries in Portuguese service. During that meeting, the governor had placed a floor mat for her to sit on, which was only suitable for servants according to local custom. Nzinga ordered one of her servants to get down on the ground and sat on the servant's back, which asserted equal status to the governor. It also proved her worth as a brave and confident individual. Although the negotiation succeeded, the treaty was never honored and Nzinga's brother took his own life as he was convinced that he would never recover what he had lost. The country needs a strong ruler, and Nzinga has proved that she has the skill, the political power, and the strength to take on the role.
