In the year 1578, the ruler of Portugal embarked on an expedition to conquer Morocco, where Portugal already held the regions of Tangiers and Ceuta. Shortly after his landing, at the battle of AlKhazar Kebir, the Moroccan Berbers of Moulay Ismaïl so soundly defeated the Portuguese army that the ruler was killed, along with most of the Portuguese nobility fighting with him. This disaster led the way to Spanish control of Portugal and was the starting point of Portugal's decline.
