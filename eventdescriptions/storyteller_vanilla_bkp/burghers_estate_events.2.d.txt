The commoners are worried about the great influence of the nobles in our realm. They claim that many lower members of the nobility are disregarding the peace and law of the land, using their power and lands to take unreasonable tolls and taxes from traders. Some are even said to attack and rob merchants, taking their money by force. Representatives of the commoners now claim that the economic development of our country is held back by these excesses. The nobles on the other hand argue that they are simply charging reasonably for the use of their roads. Some are willing to give the state a part of the share they take.
