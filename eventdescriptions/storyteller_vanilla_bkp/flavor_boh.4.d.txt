The Vladislav Hall was built between 1493 and 1502 by Benedikt Rejt, the leading medieval architect of Bohemia. It was the largest secular space in Prague and an architectural masterpiece. It was over 60 meters long, had vaults 13 meters high and was used for banquets, coronations and other important events of the court. It was even large enough to hold tournaments with knights on horseback inside it.
