For some time now, the Republic has seen an increased control of the Guilds on the troubled political stage. Relying on a large number of apprentices, small shop owners and craftsmen, this faction has been able to confiscate all power within the government, and to monopolize all offices in the administration. Rather than risk lasting marginalization the Aristocrats and Traders have begun to speculate in grain. This has quickly drained the state reserves of bread and grain and led to a severe increase in prices. Until now, the Guilds have disregarded the developing crisis, as poor people requesting loans to buy food seemed to be an opportunity for tidy profits. That is until they began to crowd the Main Plaza of the capital, at the foot of the ruler's palace, going as far as slaughtering half a score guards that had ordered them to disperse. Worst of all, they have begun to loot dozens of shops.
