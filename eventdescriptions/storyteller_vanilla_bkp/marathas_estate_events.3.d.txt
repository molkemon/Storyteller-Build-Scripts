Facing an enemy that fields powerful artillery, our advisors propose the adoption of a tactic that has served them well in the past. Named 'bargigiri' by northern invaders after the term for horsemen, it is a form of guerrilla warfare that favors highly mobile cavalry flanking maneuvers. The smaller, lighter horses favored by the people of the Deccan are ideal for such missions, able to outmaneuver cumbersome artillery, elephants, and heavy cavalry.
