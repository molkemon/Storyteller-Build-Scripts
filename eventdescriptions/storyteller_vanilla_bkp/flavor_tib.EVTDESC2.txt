The Dalai Lama regards the assembled crowd of petty leaders as though they were his trained dogs. Through them he knows his control over Tibet will be secured; he must only decide how. The safest plan would be to secure a treaty with the leaders, seeking their promise that they will do their best to protect Buddhism in Tibet, in particular the body of the reborn spiritual leader himself. A more radical approach has also occurred to the enlightened monk; if the Dalai Lama were to invite one of the leaders to rule as his puppet, he would never need fear opposition from Tibet's contentious noble families. Ruling from behind the scenes holds a certain appeal. He turns to the leaders to announce his will, a calm smile hiding his veiled ambition.
