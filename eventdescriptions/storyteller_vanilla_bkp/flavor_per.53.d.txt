Merchants from all over China, Borneo, and the Isles of Malaya ship their goods through the shores of Ayutthaya and into the Bengal Delta, the first point of entry into Indian markets. Consequently, the rulers of Bengal were able to profit greatly from intercontinental trade, building in their stead a grand realm that proved a significant adversary to our armies in their conquests of India. The weight of their wealth now falls upon our domain.
