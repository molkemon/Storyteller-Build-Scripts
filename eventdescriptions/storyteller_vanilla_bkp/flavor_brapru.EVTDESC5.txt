While seeking a cure for his ailing wife, the ruler of Brandenburg met a skilled doctor, who successfully cured his wife from her illness. Grateful, the ruler appointed the doctor his personal physician and took him to Berlin at a salary of 1352 Taler. The former Franciscan monastery was made available to the doctor, and he oversaw the construction of a glass studio at Grimnitz Castle. The doctor had previously worked with a medical professor, helping to gather and prepare herbs and medicines. While he worked with the professor, the doctor had access to the writings of a renowned expert, which deeply impressed him. The doctor sold his own medicines, calendars, horoscopes, and protective charms, and became very wealthy. His print shop produced writings in various languages, including German, Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and Arabic.
