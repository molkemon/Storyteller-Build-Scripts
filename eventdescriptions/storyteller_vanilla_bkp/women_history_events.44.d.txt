The grand cathedral of Padua was filled with esteemed university officials, professors from all faculties, students, and many prominent Venetian senators, along with distinguished guests from the universities of Bologna, Perugia, Rome, and Naples. Elena Cornaro Piscopia displayed remarkable composure as she delivered a one-hour speech in classical Latin to receive her doctorate in philosophy, skillfully interpreting passages from the works of Aristotle. The audience listened intently and applauded enthusiastically as she was awarded the insignia of the doctorate, a book of philosophy, a laurel wreath on her head, a ring on her finger, and an ermine cloak draped over her shoulders, presented by Professor Rinaldini. She is the first woman to earn a doctoral degree from a university, a feat made possible by her family's decision to follow the advice of Giovanni Fabris, allowing her to study under renowned instructors. The title "master of seven languages" is well-deserved, as she is proficient in Hebrew, Spanish, French, Latin, Greek, Arabic, and Chaldaic, in addition to her native tongue. Besides her linguistic expertise, she is also studying mathematics, philosophy, and theology. She is a skilled musician, proficient in playing the harpsichord, the clavichord, the harp, and the violin.
