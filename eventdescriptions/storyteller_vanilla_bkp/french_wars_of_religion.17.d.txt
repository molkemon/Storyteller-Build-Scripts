Inspired by treasonous antiroyalist tracts, the religious fanatic has murdered the ruler. The assassin entered the royal camp as a humble friar, claiming that he wished to bless the ruler, but instead drew a knife and thrust it into the ruler's spleen, fatally wounding them. The assassin was immediately executed, but his crime will change the course of the country's history forever. The ruler had no legitimate heir, and so the throne passes to the distantly related House of Bourbon. Who among the Bourbons is to succeed is a controversial matter: the moderates and Huguenots support the Protestant Henri, while zealous Catholics support his uncle Charles, a respected Cardinal of the Catholic Church.
