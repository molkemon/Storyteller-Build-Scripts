The city of this ancient settlement is one of the oldest. Dating all the way back to the 9th century, the city served as a major trade hub on the trade route between Scandinavia and the Byzantine Empire. Naturally, this city developed as one of the greatest trade hubs in East Europe. Although it is no longer the capital of the once mighty Novgorodian Republic, this city's importance as the seat of Baltic trade has found a resurgence under our rule.
