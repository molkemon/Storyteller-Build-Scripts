Wenzel Anton, Earl and then Prince of RittbergKaunitz, a noble from Bohemia, was deeply influenced by the culture of the Enlightenment. A devoted civil servant, he always enjoyed the full confidence and support of Empress Maria Theresa. After a diplomatic career, he became Chancellor of Austria in 1753 and held this position until 1792. A skilled diplomat, he worked to strengthen ties with France, which ultimately led to the Seven Years War, but he also managed to maintain a close relationship with Frederick the Second of Prussia, allowing Austria to participate in the first partition of Poland.
