It is time to decide how we should sacrifice the human offerings and there are several different ways to choose from. Decapitation, heart extraction, arrow sacrifice, disembowelment, or throwing the offering into a sacred pit. What should we choose? The decapitation of the offering is done in a ritual reenactment of the death of the maize god by the death gods, while the heart extraction is inspired by the Aztecs. The heart is pulled out while it is still beating, blood smeared upon the image of the temple's deity and the corpse skinned. The arrow sacrifice begins with the offering being painted blue with a white symbol over its chest, then bound to a stake during a ritual dance while wearing a peaked cap. Blood is drawn and smeared upon the deity's image and the dancers then take turns shooting arrows at the offering until its chest is filled with arrows. Disembowelment is a slower death but not as elaborate, a sacrifice is bound to a stake with their hands tied behind their head and then disemboweled. The offerings can also be thrown into our sacred pit.
