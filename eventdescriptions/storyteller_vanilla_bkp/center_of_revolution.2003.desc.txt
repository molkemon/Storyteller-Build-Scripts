The needs of the modern, absolutist state are far greater than those of our ancestors. This is particularly true in the case of the state's finances. It is therefore lucky that we employ the eminent financial reformer, our financial advisor, who has the full confidence of the financial sector. Recently, however, our financial advisor has come to desire more. Demanding overarching powers to implement new policies, our financial advisor proposes an ambitious set of reforms that might just prove beneficial for the state but will certainly increase the standing of our finance minister.
