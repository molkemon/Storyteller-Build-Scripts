The poet, philosopher, musician, and composer Kanaka Dasa was initially known as Thimmappa Nayaka. However, after discovering a treasure trove of gold as a young boy, he became known as Kanaka Nayaka. The name Kanaka means gold in Kannada, and he never used the gold for his own benefit. He was given the full name Kanaka Dasa by the scholar Vyasaraja, who he greatly admired. Born into a community of warriors, Kanaka Dasa abandoned his profession as a warrior after suffering a severe injury in battle and being miraculously saved. He devoted his life to composing music and literature, explaining complex philosophy in a way that ordinary people could understand. His notable works include Nalacharitre, Haribhaktisara, Nrisimhastava, Ramadhanyacharitre, and Mohanatarangini.
