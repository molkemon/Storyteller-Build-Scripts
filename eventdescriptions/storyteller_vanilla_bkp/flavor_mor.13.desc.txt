In the wake of the conflict between the Wattasids and Marinids, the powers of the south have begun to stir. Sensing weakness, the Sufis, tribes, and Emirs have begun to form alliances and make marriage pacts among one another. Though many in the monarch's court are content to leave them to their own devices and cloister themselves in the capital, the threat from the south will only continue to grow if they are not dealt with.
