Not all members of the Ulema are great theologians or intellectuals. Corruption can grow in any corner of the state and for some it can be hard to tell apart what benefits the faith and what might benefit themselves. It has come to the attention of the ruler that a number of pious grants that exist around the realm have been obtained through fraud or illegally passed on to heirs. Our advisor has suggested that we inspect and verify all such grants in our government, especially in regions that were not originally ruled by us. This would clearly be a grave affront to the Ulema, however, who would see it as an encroachment on their privileges and an attack on their caste as a whole.
