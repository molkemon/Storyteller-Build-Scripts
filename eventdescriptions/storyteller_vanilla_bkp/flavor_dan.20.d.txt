The Danish Royal Council of the Realm and the monarch have a long history of disagreeing on the limits of power. The current head of the council, Count Corfitz Ulfeldt, is also the brother-in-law of the monarch and has ambitiously tried to extend the power of the council and the noble families of the realm. Attempts to remove the count from his office have ended in failure as he has the support of the nobility and full power of the administration. Ulfeldt is a skilled politician and has in the past several times managed to outmaneuver royal power and other adversaries, demonstrating his own authority through various means. As the sole administrator of the Sound Toll, it is also believed that Ulfeldt may be embezzling significant state income, but using this excuse to remove him would likely come at a high political cost.
