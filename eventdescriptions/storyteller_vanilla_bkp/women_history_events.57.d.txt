The Declaration of the Rights of Woman and the Female Citizen by Olympe de Gouges challenges the inequality in our world between men and women. She is a strong advocate for female rights, the right of divorce, and a member of the Society of the Friends of Truth, which aims for equal rights for women. Her Social Contract proposes marriage based on equality. Wherever there is injustice in France or the colonies, you will find Olympe de Gouges. She is against slavery and has written several plays, such as Zamore and Mirza, and The Black Market, and articles like Reflections on the negroes, to plead the cause of the slaves. A statement from her about women's rights has been spreading lately, she says, "A woman has the right to stand on the scaffold. She must possess equally the right to speak from the platform."
