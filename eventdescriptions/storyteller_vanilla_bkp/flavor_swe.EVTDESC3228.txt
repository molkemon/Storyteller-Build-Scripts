In the early 17th century, the ruler of Sweden, Gustavus Adolphus, implemented a series of reforms to modernize the Swedish military. Sweden would develop a professional army based on conscription, where soldiers from the same region would serve together, fostering a sense of unity and teamwork among units that was rare in most mercenary armies of the time. The tactical innovations included shallower and broader formations to maximize firepower, cross-training between different branches of the army, and highly mobile field artillery. The new Swedish military doctrine emphasized firepower and mobility, abandoning heavy artillery and slow-moving formations.
