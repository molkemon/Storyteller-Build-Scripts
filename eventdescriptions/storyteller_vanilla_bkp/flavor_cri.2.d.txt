With the death of Haji Giray, the young Crimean Khanate must find its feet without its founder. The ruler who has secured the Crimean throne has a very weak position. Ambitious siblings have not given up on claiming Haji's legacy for themselves, and to the east and west, hungry neighbors are eyeing the Crimean lands greedily. In order to stabilize the situation, the ruler has sent a plea for our help. They offer the servitude of themselves as well as direct ownership of a number of Black Sea ports if we are willing to protect Crimea from their enemies.
