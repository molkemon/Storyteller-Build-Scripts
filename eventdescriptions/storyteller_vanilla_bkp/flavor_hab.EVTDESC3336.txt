Maria Theresa decided from the start of her rule that her kingdom would never again be seen as too weak to defend itself. Consequently, even during the war, she had been studying changes, and when it ended, she immediately began putting them into action. First and foremost was the reform of the army. Maria Theresa understood, however, that no military reform would be effective without financial reform, and in this area, she was advised by the brilliant Friedrich Wilhelm, Count von Haugwitz, and achieved her perhaps greatest accomplishments. Following the military and financial reforms, other changes came, generally in administrative matters. Although these reforms were subject to many modifications and changes throughout Maria Theresa's reign and after, the result was a government far more centralized than it had ever been before.
