A weary messenger arrives in our court, panting and soaked with sweat, bearing a scroll bearing the emblem of Western Georgia. It is from none other than King Vakhtang's brother, Demetrius. In his letter, he asks for, or rather demands, a significant increase in freedom for his lands, along with a long list of favors for his local nobles. It is clear that Demetrius and his nobles are taking advantage of the weak state of our central rule and the uncertain future of King Vakhtang's line of succession. Their bold demands echo with the sound of ambition and a barely concealed desire to leave our crown's rule. The question now stands: How should we respond to this open challenge to our authority?
