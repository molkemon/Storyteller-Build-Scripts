Few now have any faith in the Ming state. Even its generals wonder aloud if it might be prudent to defect to the invading enemy. The man tasked with defending the Shanghai Pass is Wu Sangui, a talented and ambitious commander who has faced foreign armies in battle before with some success. Recent events in the capital and beyond have shaken his loyalty to the Ming ruler, and now he seeks an unlikely alliance with his former enemies. Promised titles and offices under the new order, Wu Sangui has agreed to defect to our realm. We can march safely through the Shanghai Pass and onwards toward a weakened Beijing.
