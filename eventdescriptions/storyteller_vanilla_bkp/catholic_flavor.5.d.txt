As soon as a coin in the coffer rings, the soul from purgatory springs, in the words of Johann Tetzel, a Dominican friar who has made it his mission to collect indulgences for the Pope. Promising absolution from earthly sins in return for money, he has been highly active in saving the souls of the rich; and that, in a way that doesn't even require them to give up their vices. However, this practice has raised eyebrows among the noble clergy, who are uncomfortable with the idea that the Pope should enrich himself in such a manner. Instead, they call for Christians to show true repentance if their sins are to be forgiven. As the ruler grows older, the disputes on the matter grow more personal. The ruler has sinned, as every ruler must to keep their rule secure. How best should they acquire forgiveness?
