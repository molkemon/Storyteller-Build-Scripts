As soon as an appointed factor leaves our capital, we expect them to begin lining their own pockets as much as they serve the interests of the trade company. Rarely, however, are Company officials so egregious in their greed as our corrupt factor, the factor of their province. Through his private investments, our corrupt factor has cornered the local market in their province's goods. Investors fearing for the reputation of the Company demand that the renegade factor be recalled to our capital to face punishment. More opportunistic elements suggest that we should instead buy out his enterprises, incorporating them into the Company's operations and rewarding the factor for his initiative.
