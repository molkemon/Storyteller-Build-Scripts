The reunion policy of Louis the Fourteenth had been forced upon by the Empire in the year 1684 by the Truce of Regensburg, mostly because the Emperor was preoccupied by the Turkish menace, the siege of Vienna in 1683. The continued French claims and expansion to the detriment of the Habsburg possessions and the German states of the Empire led to the creation of the anti-French League of Augsburg in 1686.
