Scholars have long believed that the Catholic Church might be used as a common ground from which to establish lasting peace in Christendom, if only its leaders could summon the political will to put aside their differences. One such thinker is a respected humanist who has gained the ear of our ruler. He suggests that we should seek to repair our relationship with our rivals, the obstinate ones, arguing that good Catholics have no true cause to fight one another. Such an act of benevolence would surely be seen as an example to Catholic rulers everywhere, laying the groundwork for long-lasting peace.
