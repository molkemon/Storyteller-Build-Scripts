Our nation has narrowly avoided a disastrous succession crisis, with our ruler finally emerging as our undisputed leader. But the laws of succession remain unchanged and could leave us vulnerable to another crisis in the future. Our leader considers whether to institute legal reforms that would give men and women equal rights to the inheritance of titles, eliminating the desperate need for leaders to have male heirs or risk the dissolution of their realm. Alternatively, we may consider the crisis to be averted and focus instead on strengthening the traditional order that has elevated our nation to the precipice of greatness.
