Henry II of England received the title 'Lord of Ireland' from the papal bull in 1155. Our ruler had a monarchical claim on Ireland ever since. As time went on, Ireland has become part of our realm, while our title as lord of this island has become uncertain due to the strained papal relations. The parliament as well as the monarch are discussing the titular matters of our claim. Establishing the 'Kingdom of Ireland' would certainly boost the monarch's prestige. The question remains if Ireland should be ruled as a personal union.
