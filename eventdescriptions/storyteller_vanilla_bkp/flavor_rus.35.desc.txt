The son of Vasily III and his second wife, Elena Glinskaya, Ivan IV has been crowned as Grand Prince of Muscovy at only the age of three after his father's death from a severe leg infection. His early life was marked by neglect and mistreatment from the plotting nobles. At the age of sixteen, Ivan was crowned as the 'Tsar of All the Russians' and solidified the official formation of the Russian Tsardom; a realm that had already existed in practice during the reign of his grandfather, Ivan III 'the Great'.  Despite his title, Ivan's early years of rule were characterized by peaceful reforms and modernization. However, his reign took a darker turn when he was thought to be near death and the nobles refused to swear allegiance to his infant son. Since then, Ivan harbored a deep-seated grudge against the nobles, which eventually led to the Massacre of Novgorod.  If he is chosen as the heir to the Tsardom, many believe that the realm would reach its greatest heights; even if it comes at the cost of one or more cities.
