The esteemed church has long been one of the foremost pillars of our realm, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, judges, and diplomats for the benefit of our monarch. In return for these great services, many donations have been made over the years, both by the state and by others, in the form of villages to support the Brahman communities. These Brahmadeyas have over the centuries made our church quite rich. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the church might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
