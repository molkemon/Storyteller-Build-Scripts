Born in 1664, the son of a gardener in Fiorenzuela, near Placentia, Italy, Giulio Alberoni owed his political career and success to the Duke of Vendôme, the French general whom he served in Italy, France, and Spain. Agent of the Duke of Parma, he facilitated the wedding of Philip V of Spain with Elizabeth Farnese and became a Cardinal in 1717. His aggressive policy in Spain led to his expulsion in 1719. He was cunning and sly, but Spain lacked the resources needed to fulfill his ambitious plan. The Age of Spanish Supremacy was long gone.
