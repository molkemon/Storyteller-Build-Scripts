Commercial book printing is still a developing technique, but one of its earliest innovators can be found in the city of Venice. Aldus Manutius and his printing office, the Aldine Press, have earned a reputation for their consistent and clear typography and their well-executed translations from Latin. Italic script, as well as a consistent use of semicolons, are among Manutius's contributions to the field of typography. To the field of bookbinding, Manutius has contributed the easily handled Octavo format, ensuring that the classics leaving his printing press are portable rather than attempting to emulate the format of the medieval manuscript.
