The court of our country has become embroiled in a conflict between our ruler and the military castes. In the wake of these rising tensions, the matter of fort stewardship has risen to our attention. The fort of that province, currently held by a particularly contentious general, lies in a province where the Brahmins are gaining influence of their own. Foreseeing the potential for tensions to break out into hostilities, it may be wise to transfer control of this important fort from the contentious general to the Brahmins. Though less adept than military men at siegecraft, the Brahmins are at least loyal to our ruler. Alternatively, the general could be persuaded to change his attitude in exchange for the retention of his privileges in that province.
