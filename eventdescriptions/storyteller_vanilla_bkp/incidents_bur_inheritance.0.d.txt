It seems the line of the ruler's dynasty has grown rotten and withered with their final ruler. The ruler has been incapable of producing an heir in all of their years as the last remaining male of the dynasty line. However, the ruler did produce a talented and beautiful Princess by the name of Marie. Her father Philippe, a member of the dynasty, famously despised Marie and insisted that no woman should sit the throne of Burgundy, but the ruler is not their father and times are growing desperate. Should the ruler die without producing an heir, Marie will become their de facto successor either way, but if the ruler forsook the pursuit of a male heir and favored Marie as the rightful heir to Burgundy, her claim would be strengthened and we may be able to negotiate our way through this succession crisis with minimal strife. Female heirs are seen as quite the prize for ambitious men, and Marie's succession may lead to Burgundy falling under a union with a foreign power.
