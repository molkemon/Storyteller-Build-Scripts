Since the late 13th century, the Venetian glassmaking industry has been confined to the island of Murano in order to lessen the risk of fire. The popularity of the Murano glass has only continued to increase since then, and the glassmakers have become both rich and influential. The secrets of how to produce such exquisite products are known to few outside of our borders, and in order to safeguard this knowledge, the glassmakers are confined to the city. As the popularity of Venetian glass continues to increase, the world is changing. With the fall of Constantinople, a great number of skilled craftsmen are requesting to be allowed to settle down on Murano.
