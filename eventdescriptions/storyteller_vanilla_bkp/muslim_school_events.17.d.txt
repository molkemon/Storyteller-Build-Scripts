A revered Sufi leader has passed away in the province and his followers are building a shrine in his honor. Pilgrims from across the land are already flocking to the province to see the shrine of this great man, some calling him a saint. Local leaders have donated to its upkeep and his former students have opened a nearby lodge. While honoring a great man is commendable, some traditionalists among the devout and the Ulema view this form of worship as sacrilegious. If the ruler were to visit the shrine in the province, it could boost its prestige and also enhance their own popular standing.
