Per Brahe the Younger was a Swedish officer and statesman. After studies abroad he became a trusted advisor to Gustavus Adolphus. Between 1626 and 1628, he participated in the war against Poland and later followed the King to Germany during the early Thirty Years War. He was made a high-ranking government official and led the peace negotiations with Poland in 1635. Two years later, he was appointed the top administrator in Finland and in 1641, he held the highest position in the land. His contribution to development in Finland included introducing a postal system and the founding of several cities, among them Tavastehus and Nyslott. He also contributed to the founding of the Royal Academy of Åbo.
