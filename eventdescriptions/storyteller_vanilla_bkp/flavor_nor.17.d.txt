While Norwegian peasants have extracted copper and iron for centuries on a small scale, there are no great iron or copper mines in our lands. This is contrasted by the thriving mining sector in the Swedish realm, where great quantities of both metals have been mined, cast, and exported since the Middle Ages. The crown has recently acquired many pieces of land previously under the control of the church and nobility, who did not have resources or inclination to search for metals. Let us invite experienced prospectors and miners to see what treasures lie beneath these mountains.
