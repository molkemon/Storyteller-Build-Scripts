A wise and pious ruler of the Ottomans, Murad the Fourth, managed to quell the corruption that had grown during the reigns of previous rulers with policy changes such as limiting wasteful spending. His most pious commands, though, were to ban alcohol, tobacco, and coffee in Constantinople. To enforce the ban, he patrolled the streets and taverns of Constantinople in civilian clothes at night, ordering execution for all who broke the ban. A ruthless supporter of alcohol prohibition, yet Murad the Fourth was partial to drinking and wrote poems about his thirst. 'Even if the rivers became wine, they wouldn't fill my glass'; 'The wine is such a devil that I have to protect my people from it by drinking all of it'.
