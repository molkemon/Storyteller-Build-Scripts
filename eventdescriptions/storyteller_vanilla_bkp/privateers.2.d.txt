Privateering is always a difficult diplomatic issue, especially when no open war is conducted, and the distinction between an enemy who must be subject to certain rules of war and a lawless pirate is not always clear-cut. Even less so in the case of a recently captured vessel, which has displayed a letter of marque issued by a colonial governor with dubious authority.  While this supposed privateer looks like a simple pirate to us, accepting the letter as valid may allow us to maintain better diplomacy with their masters.
