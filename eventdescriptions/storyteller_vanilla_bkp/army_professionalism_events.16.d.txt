Recruitment is often a decentralized affair, entrusted to the nobles in the capital. They are responsible for gathering a set number of men in the provinces under our control. This system relies on trust, as the central government is not involved in the countryside mustering, and the only control we have is through sporadic army inspections. One such inspection in the region of cheating rolls has uncovered a local scandal. The rolls we have for the area are full of fictional names, and nobody knows what we could actually muster from the region.
