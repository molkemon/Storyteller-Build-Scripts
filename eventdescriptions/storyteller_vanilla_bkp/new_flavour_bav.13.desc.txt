The Wittelsbach Duchy of Bavaria has been split in various parts since the middle of the 14th century. At present, the Duchy is divided between three different branches of the family; those of Munich, Landshut, and Ingolstadt. Various complicated arrangements regulate which branch of the family should inherit the lands of any ruler who may die without a male heir. However, these arrangements are not always above dispute, and a strong ruler may try to expand their domain with their relatives' land, no matter whether their claim is entirely just.
