Rather than relying on single individuals, states are increasingly turning to institutions to carry out their espionage. All over Europe, Black Chambers, responsible for acquiring, opening, and deciphering foreign and domestic correspondence, have been established in secret. The one in Vienna, the Geheime Kabinettskanzlei, stands out as unusual. Those familiar with it praise the Viennese Black Chamber for its remarkable speed in regularly opening, copying, and then deciphering and translating all mail it can obtain.
