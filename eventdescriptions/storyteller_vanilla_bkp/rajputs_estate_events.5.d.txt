Rajput power in India is predicated on centuries of conquest, colonization, and settlement of lands beyond their power base in Rajasthan. A particularly bold and ambitious scion of a notable clan has appeared at court on a quest to enhance the prestige of his kin. Approaching the ruler, he asks permission to settle a new province, where he shall found a new town. He promises in exchange to accept the rule of our state and to raise an army in our support whenever it is needed.
