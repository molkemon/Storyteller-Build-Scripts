A new economic idea, known as physiocracy, is gaining popularity among the nobles of our country. Physiocrats believe that the foundation of wealth lies in agricultural production, which makes all of civilization possible. As a result, the most important members of society are the landowners who own the fields and lease the land. Physiocrats reject the dominant economic approach of mercantilism in favor of an agrarian approach that allows things to happen naturally. Merchants, of course, do not create wealth, but rather transport goods from one place to another. While many people are impressed by the complexity of their ideas, others are more drawn to the practical benefits of our current protectionist policies.
