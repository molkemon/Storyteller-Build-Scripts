Ever since having been elected the new ruler has been seeking to consolidate power. Various men of ambition have been either replaced or propped up, the members of the national assembly have been circumvented, marginalized and outmaneuvered at any possible opportunity. The people on the streets of the capital are somewhat apprehensive of this activity but the presence of loyal troops and the support of the countryside population is keeping our country an unusually quiet and orderly realm for now.
