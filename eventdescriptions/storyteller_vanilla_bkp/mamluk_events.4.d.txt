The ruler of our realm is far from the only owner of Mamluks in our system of government. The households of influential manumitted Mamluks often contain great, if not greater, numbers of newly imported slaves from their home regions. These influential leaders are often those with the greatest influence during a succession, but even when the throne is occupied, the influence of the Mamluk households is great on our politics. It is up to our ruler to ensure that this factionalism does not stifle the politics of our capital.
