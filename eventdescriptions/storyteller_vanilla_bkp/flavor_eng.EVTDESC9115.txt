In the year fifteen fifty-three, a group of merchants sent a fleet of three ships to sail to China through the Northeast Passage. Most of the ships were trapped by the ice and all of their crew members died of starvation, except for one ship, the Edward, under the leadership of a senior navigator named Richard Chancellor, who sailed into the White Sea and arrived at a small village called Arkhangelsk. It was a village that would later grow into a port by the year fifteen eighty-four. There, he learned about the wealth and power of the Russian ruler and traveled to Moscow in a horse-drawn sleigh during the winter. Although Russia and England had different interests in forming relationships with each other, the Muscovy Trade Company was formed to take advantage of the new trade, and it was a commercial success, especially in the Russian fur trade.
