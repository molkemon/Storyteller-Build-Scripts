The Secretary of the Treasury, Alexander Hamilton, has proposed three major financial innovations. The first is a national bank, which Hamilton argues is most important in order to reach the three goals he has set for the country: Establish financial order, clarity and precedence in and of the newly formed country. Establish credit—both in country and overseas—for the new nation. To resolve the issue of the fiat currency, issued by the Continental Congress immediately prior to and during the country's Revolutionary War — the 'Continental'.
