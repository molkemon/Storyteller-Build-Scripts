Ivan decided to turn his attention westward. In 1558, he went to war in an attempt to establish Russian rule over the province. Russia was at first victorious and succeeded in destroying the Livonian knights, but their ally Lithuania became an integral part of Poland in 1569. The war dragged on; while the Swedes supported Poland against Russia, the Crimean Tatars attacked Astrakhan and even made an extensive incursion into Russia in 1571; they sacked and burned the capital, leaving only the fortress standing.
