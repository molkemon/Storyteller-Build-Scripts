With the conquest of the very traditional Mossi, our nation stands at a religious and social crossroad: the clergy demand the eradication of the old traditional beliefs and want to bring the rural people to the light of Allah. However, the nobility stress the benefit our nation would enjoy if the peasants are left alone with their beliefs, such as lowered risks of peasant uprisings against our monarch. In the end, it is up to our monarch to decide the fate of these people and the religious direction of our realm.
