Beginning in 1516, the Republic obliged the Jews to live in an area of the city where the foundries, known in Venetian as 'geti', had been situated in ancient times, to wear a sign of identification and to manage the city's pawnshops at rates established by the Serenissima. Many other onerous regulations were also included, in exchange for which the Community was granted the freedom to practice its faith and protection in the case of war. Shall we accept these terms?
