Olav Nilsson Skanke, a renowned commander of the Bergenhus fortress and a sworn enemy of the Hanseatic League in Norway, has been killed by local merchants. Despite his long history of service to the crown, Olav was also a pirate, hunting down Hanseatic ships across the North Sea. In order to eliminate this threat to their interests, the German merchants instigated a riot, cornering Olav in a local abbey and then burning it to the ground. Olav met his fate in the flames, along with monks, local citizens, and the Bishop of Bergen. This event is likely to further inflame the already strong anti-Hanseatic sentiment in Scandinavia.
