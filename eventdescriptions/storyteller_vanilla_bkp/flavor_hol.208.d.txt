Antonie Philips van Leeuwenhoek, a Dutch entrepreneur and scientist thriving in the Golden Age of Dutch science and technology, has emerged as a prominent figure. Despite being mostly self-taught in science, he has gained the title of "the Father of Microbiology" and stands among the first microscopists and microbiologists. Van Leeuwenhoek's current endeavors focus on groundbreaking work in microscopy and shaping the foundations of microbiology as a recognized scientific field.
