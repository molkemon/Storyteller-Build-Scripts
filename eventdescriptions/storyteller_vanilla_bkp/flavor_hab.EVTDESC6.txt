On November 7, 1492, a meteorite weighing 127 kilograms fell in a field outside the town of Ensisheim in Alsace, Further Austria. When villagers tried to remove parts of the meteorite, a local official intervened in order to preserve it for the future Emperor. He declared it to be a wonder of God, and after removing two small pieces for himself and the Archduke of Austria, he returned the meteorite to the city to be preserved in the parish church.
