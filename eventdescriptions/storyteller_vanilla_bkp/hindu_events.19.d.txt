Our country is in flames. We are at war against those who would see our country crushed and its loyal subjects slain. Such an assault on our people is unacceptable, and we find an unusual fury burning within us directed at those who caused this conflict. From the way others look at us, it is clear that we must appear frightening in our anger and determination.  While recuperating in a temple, we see a statue of Kali, horrifying in its appearance and brutality. Yet in its eyes, red with rage, we see something of ourselves: a sense of purpose. Just as Kali guards her children, we will protect the sons and daughters of our country to the end.
