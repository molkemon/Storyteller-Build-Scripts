When Kapilendra Deva overthrew the last of the Eastern Ganga Kings, Orissa was in a sad state. Muslim incursions from the north and west, as well as rivalry with other neighbors to the south, had reduced the kingdom greatly. Since then, Raja Kapilendra has quickly turned all of that around. In consecutive wars with Bengal, the Reddys of Andhra, the Bahmani Sultans, and the Vijayanagar empire, our country has grown in both size and strength under his leadership. This constant warfare has, however, taken its toll on our population, and an unusually bad harvest season has now resulted in growing popular unrest. It has been suggested that the public mood would be improved if we temporarily lifted the traditional Salt tax and otherwise devoted our attention to the public good a bit more.
