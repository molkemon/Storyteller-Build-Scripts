During the reign of Ibrahim the First, Kemankes Kara Mustafa Pasha, the prime minister, was in charge. He tried to stop the country from overspending and improved the navy, but was eventually put to death by the Sultan in 1644. The Sultan had instead started to listen to Jinji Hoja, a minor religious official with a reputation for mysterious powers who was brought into the palace to help the Sultan with his lack of an heir.
