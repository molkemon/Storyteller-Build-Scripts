Breaking the chains of the old regime has finally freed the top of the military leadership from the restraining links between birth and promotion. No doubt our army will soon be led by men of merit who will outshine the old aristocrats. Right now, however, the sudden lack of experienced leaders at the top, as well as the great influx of new recruits at the bottom, has led to a somewhat volatile atmosphere in our ranks. Patriotism and revolutionary fervor are a strong motivator, but without able leadership, any army can find itself unable to perform to expectations.
