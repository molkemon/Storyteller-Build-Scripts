In many other nations, aristocrats enjoy much greater power than here. In our country, money is what matters, not rank. Our commander, a person lacking neither wealth nor influence, is well aware of this.  'Ultimately,' they say, 'if our country is to flourish, it needs a strong ruler. The military is standing by you, our monarch, if you would be willing to restore some of its old glory. Many among the nobility would be willing to back such a cause with considerable funds.'
