Through our acquisition of the city, we have come to control a major regional center of production and trade. The local artisans and merchants are both rich and successful in their trade and the city will surely be a great addition to our splendid government. It is hard to know, however, how far we can trust the people of this culture. We should consider forcibly relocating the wealthiest and most successful local families to another city where we can more efficiently monitor and tax them. Some would say the damage we do to the city is greater than any benefit we might hope for, but the added control is surely worth it.
