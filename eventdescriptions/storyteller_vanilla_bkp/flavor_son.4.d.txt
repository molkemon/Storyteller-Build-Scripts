Muhammad Ture, of Soninke origins, began his career in Songhai as the chief minister to Alî Ber Sonni. After Alî Ber's death and his son Ko Mamadi Dao's succession, the minister Muhammad challenged Abu's authority in 1493 and overthrew him following the Battle of Anfao. He then took the throne of Songhai for himself, founded the Askia dynasty, and took the name Muhammad I. Askia. His reign was marked by great military success and political reforms, which secured a lasting legacy of learning and literacy for the Songhai people.
