The system of national defense, known as the Indelningsverket, allows for swift mobilization of the country's military. Every tenth man between the ages of 15 and 44 is conscripted into the national infantry, while the nobility, exempt from taxes, provides cavalry. This system has reduced the need for mercenaries, as Sweden now has a standing army. The system was later reformed, shifting the recruitment of soldiers from villages to provinces. Each province within the Swedish realm contributes a regiment of 1,200 Carolean units to the Swedish army.
