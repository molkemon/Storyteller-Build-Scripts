When Frederik I died in 1533, the bishops and most of the aristocracy were initially opposed to the election of his son Christian, the duke of Holstein, as the new king because he was a Lutheran. The burghers and peasants, on the other hand, wanted a return of the deposed Christian II, who was imprisoned at Sønderborg. The mayors of Copenhagen and Malmö devised a plan together with the Hansa to free Christian II and turn the two largest Danish cities into partners of the Hanseatic League. The rebel forces were led by Count Christoffer of Oldenburg, thus the name 'The Count's Feud'.
