In 1572 the Jagiellon Dynasty came to an end and a new ruler was chosen. His name was Henri d'Anjou. On his election he was forced to sign what has later been called the Henrykian Articles, which established that Poland was a country where the ruler was chosen by others from then on. He also signed the Pacta Conventa, which was a formal agreement between the ruler and the country's leaders. In reality, the ruler had no control over the country. He had no authority over government officials, and he led a small army of only 3000 men. To get anything extra, he had to ask the country's leaders for permission. At this moment, Poland effectively stopped being a monarchy, as the ruler was more of a symbol, and instead became a true republic ruled by the nobles.
