English philosopher Thomas Hobbes believed that the natural state was one of conflict and disorder, which could only be resolved if individuals agreed to surrender their freedom to a ruler through a social pact. This ruler, in turn, was duty-bound to safeguard his subjects. If he failed to do so, the people would be justified in seeking a new leader. Interestingly, Hobbes managed to alienate both sides in the English Civil War, and he spent 11 years in Paris before feeling secure enough to return to England in 1651.
