The emperor Sri Krishna Deva Raya, who ruled the Vijayanagara Empire from 1509 until his death in 1529, during which he patronized eight Telugu poets known as the Astadiggajas. The eight poets, Allasani Peddana, Nandi Thimmana, Madayyagari Mallana, Dhurjati, Ayyalaraju Ramambhadrudu, Pingali Surana, Ramarajabhushanudu, and Tenali Ramakrishnudu, were regarded as the pillars of his literary assembly. The Prabandha Age, as the age of the Astadiggajas is called, continued from 1540 to 1600. The court of poets was also known as the Bhuvana Vijayam, or the Conquest of the World.
