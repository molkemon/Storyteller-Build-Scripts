Pali law is generally practiced in one form or another in many Buddhist countries and especially among the Theravada ones. While these laws are not collected and written down by the states themselves, they do vary from country to country, and at times a collection will have to be updated or renewed. A wise monk has undertaken such a collection recently in our country and has produced an updated version of one of the most commonly used law collections. As the collection itself builds upon actual cases, it can, however, be seen as quite an up-to-date commentary on the rulings of our ruler.
