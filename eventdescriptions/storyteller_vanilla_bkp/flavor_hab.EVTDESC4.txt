In 1512, Emperor Maximilian commissioned a woodcut print called the Triumphal Procession, which was intended to showcase the grandeur of the Emperor. It was designed to adorn the walls of city halls and palaces. When Emperor Maximilian passed away in 1519, the woodcut remained unfinished. With more than 130 wood blocks and measuring over fifty meters, it stands as one of the world's largest prints ever produced.
