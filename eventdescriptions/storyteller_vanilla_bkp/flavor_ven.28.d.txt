The impoverished nobles remain a big problem for our system. Part of the issue seems to be that once they've lost everything, these nobles are unlikely to regain their former status. If we could provide a way for them to restore their families' former glories, they might once again become productive members of our society. However, that's not popular among those who prefer to rely on bought votes or fear more competition. Recently, a relatively harmless proposal was put forward: a school for the children of nobles could be created, ensuring they receive a proper education their parents can't afford. This would allow a few select individuals to rise above their original status and contribute to the state.
