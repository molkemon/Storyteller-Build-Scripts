War has come to Italy. These are not the 'civilized' skirmishes to which the Italians are accustomed, but war as practiced by the battle-hardened armies of a great power. The sound of church bells and haggling merchants will soon be replaced by the gruesome, destructive cacophony of cannonfire. For some Italians, the horrors of war offer financial opportunity: the number of mercenary generals offering themselves to the highest bidder has risen dramatically, and there is no shortage of veteran soldiers willing to join these armies.
