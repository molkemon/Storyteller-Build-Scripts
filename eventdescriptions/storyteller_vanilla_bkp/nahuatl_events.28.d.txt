The spoken word has always held a special significance for our people, with songs and recitations playing a prominent role in both our sacred and everyday ceremonies. Even our highest leaders are referred to as speakers. Oral poetry is one of the most revered forms of art and one of the few accepted ways for a person to express dissatisfaction, anxiety, and even doubt about fate without offending the gods. Recently, a highly acclaimed Nahuatl poet has arrived in our capital, praising our city and its people, but criticizing our ruler himself.
