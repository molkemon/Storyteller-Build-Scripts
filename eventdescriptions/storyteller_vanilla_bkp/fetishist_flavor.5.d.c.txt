A cousin of the monarch has passed away. The letter sent to us describes how a bandit ambush fell upon the entourage of the traveling statesmen and how they were all forced off a cliff on their way home from a visit in the capital of our neighboring nation. The few possessions that could be retrieved have been sent to our capital, but the monarch is distraught by the death of their favorite relative. To make matters worse, the letter from our neighboring nation says nothing about whether a proper funeral ceremony has been considered or not. The dear cousin, having passed on, is closer to the divine now. Yet the thought of the good woman not receiving the correct funeral rites torments the monarch.
