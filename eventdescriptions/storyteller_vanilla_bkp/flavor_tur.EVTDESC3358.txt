Mehmed the Second was a centralizing ruler, who sought to expand his personal power within the Ottoman realm at all costs. Near the end of his reign, he sought to reduce the power of the clergy by rerouting revenues from the foundations that supported them to the military system instead, further strengthening the Ottoman army. This move, illegal under Islamic law, caused widespread resentment and was quickly reversed by his son Bayezid upon Mehmed's death in the year 1481.
