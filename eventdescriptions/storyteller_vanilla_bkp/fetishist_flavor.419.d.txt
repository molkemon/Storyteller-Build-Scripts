While they were certainly grateful at first, the people of our country have been growing increasingly discontent. The friction between them and the people of our province has caused us many sleepless nights. They have also begun to complain about the lack of influence that they have in our realm's affairs, now that they are a part of it. They demand that one of their leaders, their guild master, shall be given a seat on the council of advisors. While it would surely satisfy them, we would also weaken our authority and anger our more fanatical subjects.
