At the age of 15, young skipper's son Cort Adeler left Norway behind for the open seas and he has since made a career as a successful merchant captain in the lowlands, the Mediterranean, and even as far away as the Coromandel Coast in India. Having also acted as captain in times of war in Dutch, English, Venetian, and Turkish naval engagements, Adeler is in possession of experience and contacts that few in his generation possess. Through his various mercantile enterprises, he has acquired a considerable personal fortune, some of which he has invested in his home country, especially the estates he has begun to develop outside the Norwegian city of Skien. A man of Adeler's caliber could surely be put to good use in the service of the state.
