Early in the 16th century, a prominent church leader set his sights on the North African coast, a region where his strong faith could find an outlet now that the Reconquista was over in Spain. He personally organized and financed a campaign against the city of Oran. The port of Mers El Kebir was captured in 1505 and in 1509 a strong force accompanied by the leader himself and led by a skilled military commander finally took Oran in an assault.
