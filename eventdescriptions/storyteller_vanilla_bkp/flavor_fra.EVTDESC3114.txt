Michel Le Tellier, the son of a counselor at the Account Chamber, was identified by Prime Minister Cardinal Mazarin. He was appointed Secretary of War in 1643. As minister until 1666 and Chancellor until 1677, he implemented the necessary reforms for the French army and war administration. His eldest son, the Marquis de Louvois, continued and perfected these reforms, ultimately providing his master, Louis the Fourteenth, with the largest and best army of the last half of the 17th century.
