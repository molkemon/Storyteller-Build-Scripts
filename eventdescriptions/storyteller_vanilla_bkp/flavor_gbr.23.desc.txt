The Iberian connection began centuries ago, with royal marriages between kings and princesses such as Alfonso the Eighth of Castile and Eleanor, daughter of Henry the Second and Eleanor of Aquitaine, and Edward the First of England and Leonor of Castile, sister of Alfonso the Tenth of Castile, or the most recent case of John of Gaunt, Duke of Lancaster, son of Edward the Third, who married Constance of Castile, daughter of the deposed king Pedro the First, to claim the throne of Castile through this marriage. This connection has been recently restored and enforced, as all the kingdoms of Iberia have come under our rule, and we are now in a position to declare the Union of Iberia as one of the Crowns of our United Kingdom.
