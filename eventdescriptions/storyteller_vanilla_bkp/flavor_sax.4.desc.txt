After the death of the late uncle, Friedrich of Thuringia, the Thuringian Wettin lands came under the rule of our country. However, an argument soon broke out between our ruler and his brother, Wilhelm, over the fair distribution of these lands. According to the Division of Altenburg, an agreement arbitrated with the help of Brandenburg and Hessen, the two brothers would be joint rulers, with our ruler being allocated the core territories of Meissen in the east and Wilhelm given control of Thuringia. However, Wilhelm disputes this arrangement, demanding more land; rulership of Wittenberg, at least; threatening war otherwise.
