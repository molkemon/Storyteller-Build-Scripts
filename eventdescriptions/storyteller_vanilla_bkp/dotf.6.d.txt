The Reformation is a chaotic and rapidly evolving movement. Doctrine is determined as much by political pragmatism as by theological reasoning. It is for this reason that we, as Defenders of the Reformation, should try to achieve some semblance of order and unity among reformers. To that end we have convened the Council of our province, where a number of dignitaries and theologians from countries such as our lord, a renowned theologian, and a respected scholar, have gathered to discuss matters of faith and to make political alliances.
