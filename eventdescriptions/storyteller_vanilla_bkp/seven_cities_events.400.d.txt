The expedition began with 600 men, but only four returned home alive. When they first arrived, the crew heard about another expedition where 450 of 600 men had perished. Fearing they would face the same fate, many deserted. A hurricane struck the expedition not long after, and several men perished. Disaster after disaster struck the expedition, famine, disease, and attacks from the natives. One of the four who returned home was Estevanico, a black slave born in Africa, who was taught the necessary skills to lead an expedition by the conquistador. He is available for hire, although we would suffer a small prestige loss if we appoint him our new leader.
