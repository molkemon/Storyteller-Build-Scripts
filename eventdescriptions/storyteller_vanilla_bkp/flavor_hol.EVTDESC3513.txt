Willem the Third rose to prominence in Dutch politics, overshadowing the brothers de Witt, amidst a growing backing of the Orange family among the Dutch people. After being appointed stadholder, the brothers de Witt stepped down and were murdered by a violent crowd due to high treason and anti-Orange sentiments. Building on the growing support for the Oranges in August 1674, they were declared hereditary in the stadholder position by the states of Holland and Zeeland.
