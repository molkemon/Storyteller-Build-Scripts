King John I and Queen Christina founded the St. Clare's Monastery in Copenhagen in 1497 with a gift of the former royal vegetable gardens in an area known as Rosengård. The Queen made a donation of forty Rhenish Guilders for the construction of the monastery in 1498. Seven years later, on the eleventh of August, 1505, the monastery was completed and consecrated. The monastery consisted of a dormitory, a refectory, a church, an infirmary, cellars, and places for lay sisters and a priest to stay.
