Under the watchful eye of our inquisitor, trials for sorcery and heresy have proceeded under relatively controlled circumstances. Our trusted inquisitor would not be at our court if we did not trust them, after all. A recent event has complicated this, however, as our inquisitor has challenged our trust of another. Our statesman, who has aided our country greatly in foreign relations, has been accused of witchcraft, a testament to no one being truly unquestionable. Our statesman claims that our inquisitor is supporting these accusations out of pure jealousy. Political drama may be behind all this, but then again, we have trusted our inquisitor so far.
