The Tripartitum, also known as Opus Tripartitum, was a significant legal work compiled in the Kingdom of Hungary during the Renaissance. Created in the late 15th century, it served as a comprehensive legal code, covering the law of both nobility and commoners. It aimed to consolidate and codify Hungarian law, drawing from previously established customary law. Its compilation was a monumental undertaking, reflecting the king's commitment to legal reform and governance. Despite its influence, the Tripartitum never gained official recognition across the entire realm. Should we make it a law of the land?
