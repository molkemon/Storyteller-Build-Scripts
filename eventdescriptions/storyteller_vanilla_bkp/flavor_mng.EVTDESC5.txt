European traders seem to be found in many parts of China. They are taking profits away from our own traders, causing them to go bankrupt and resulting in significant lost revenue in taxes and tariffs. Perhaps we should ban them entirely or limit them to a single city to better control them and make it easier to impose trade tariffs. Our advisors think that giving the city of Macao to the Portuguese and only allowing Europeans to trade there might be the best solution. This would also give us the benefit of expanding trade in our southern regions around Canton. Alternatively, we could choose to ignore outside trade altogether or to open our shores to free and unhindered trade with all nations. What should our policy be?
