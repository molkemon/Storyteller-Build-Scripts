The First American, a title earned by the polymath Benjamin Franklin for his campaigning for colonial unity and in many ways exemplifying the emerging American nation, is one of the founding fathers of the United States. During his years as an advisor to the USA, he has fundamentally defined the American ethos, a marriage of practical values with the scientific and tolerant values of the Enlightenment. Without Franklin's endless work and energy, the United States of America would be a lesser nation.
