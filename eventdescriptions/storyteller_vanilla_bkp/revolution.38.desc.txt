The Revolution has arrived. Protests across the government pushing for land redistributions, a more democratic constitution and greater autonomy for non-German regions have coalesced into a massive demonstration in the capital. The ruler tried to defuse the situation by offering token concessions, but this could not appease the crowd. Violence by the armed forces then enflamed the anger of the people, and a mob stormed the palace, capturing the ruler, who was promptly put to death. The nation has been declared a Republic by the new Revolutionary Committee, who promise to abolish serfdom, establish equality under the law, ensure greater rights for minority groups and redistribute the nobility's land between the state and the peasantry, leading the nation and the rest of Europe to a brighter future free from oppression.
