On September 12th, 1683, an allied army under the leadership of King Jan Sobieski relieved Vienna from the besieging Ottomans. At this point the king's name spread across Europe. In that very moment, King Jan Sobieski had such status and personal power coming from his very person that he could have ended Poland's stagnation or at least tried. Merely the different political climate during his early days as king is a proof of him being a very special ruler. He was never that limited by vetoes or articles as earlier rulers, but in the end he chose a different path. He chose to continue the campaign against the Ottomans, a campaign he could not win decisively, while at the same time having to deal with growing dissent from powerful nobles disliking this policy. When he stopped campaigning, he suffered heavily from gallstones, and he never managed to make the nobles forget that he was just one of them. He never managed to translate his popularity among the nobles into concerted political action. If he instead had gone home after relieving Vienna and picked a fight with the nobles, something completely different might have happened. Well, who knows?
