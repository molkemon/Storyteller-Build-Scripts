It has been said, among those who are willing to listen, that the governor who the ruler so kindly appointed to take care of our colony has been absent of his duties for a long time. He has been seen taking part in the greatest of feasts and balls, here in our country, quite far from those they aim to govern. Of course, it is easier to revolt against the ruler if the ruler is not there, so news about a dangerous uprising in the province have finally reached our court. When confronted with these facts, he has been heard saying: "Those mosquitoes were eating me alive."
