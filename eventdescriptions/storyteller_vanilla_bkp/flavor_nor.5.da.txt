Ever since the 13th century treaty of Perth, Scotland has been obligated to pay a yearly sum to the Norwegian Kingdom in exchange for control of the Hebrides. These payments are by now long overdue, however, and this has been the cause for tensions on and off. Regardless of our ancestral claims, however, our ability to project power in the Highlands is not what it was, and we have bigger issues closer to home to handle. It has been suggested that this conflict be settled once and for all. The royal families will marry a member of our dynasty and the Jarldom of Orkney will be pawned and then transferred to us. Orkney remains one of the highest ranked titles in the Kingdom of Norway however, one that has at times been a stepping stone to the Norwegian throne. While they remain a weak factor the Norwegian nobility are very wary of this deal.
