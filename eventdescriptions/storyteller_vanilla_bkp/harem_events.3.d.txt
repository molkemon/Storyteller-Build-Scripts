Our beloved heir, they, is young and still has a lot to learn about life and how to be a good ruler. Most of our advisors feel it is our duty to uphold our tradition of appointing our sons to act as governors and rulers over small provinces. This way they can learn what it is to be a ruler. By assigning suitably powerful provinces we can also reduce the chance of a future succession struggle. The province of Sancaga Cikma has served as such a governorship at times in the past and would likely fit them very well. Others, including our Mother, the Valide Sultan, feel that young they would benefit the most from an education within the walls of the Imperial Harem, where they will have constant access to good teachers and can be supervised by those who know them. This way they would also be far from any place they might develop premature political ambitions.
