A new device is currently being tested to quickly and easily separate cotton fibers from their seeds. The Cotton gin is a wooden drum with small wire hooks that pulls the cotton fibers through a mesh to separate the seeds from the cotton while brushes remove the loose cotton lint to prevent jams. A patent is pending and the inventor, Eli Whitney, claims that his machine can produce up to 25 kilograms of cleaned cotton daily. He is trying to find more locations to test his machine.
