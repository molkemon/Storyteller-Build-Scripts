After the concessions to the nobility in 1425 and during the Thirteen Years War, Kazimierz the Fourth was again forced to new concessions. To be able to raise troops, among them a lot of mercenaries, the king needed money from the nobility. In an effort to solve the monarchy's pressing problems, he had to accept the Nieszawa Privileges, implying that the nobility had the right to hold local meetings, without which no troops could be raised and no taxes extracted. In this way, the nobility got an efficient and powerful organization with a joint mutual interest, and power over the military and the taxation procedure.
