In the year 1614, a successful entrepreneur from the Walloon region, Louis de Geer, established a business in Sweden and began developing the iron ore mining and foundries in northern Sweden. For some time, skilled craftsmen from the Hainaut, Brabant, and Luxemburg regions had been leaving their homes in search of work and moving to Sweden. The arrival of these master metal workers and ore refiners revitalized the industry and led to a significant improvement in steel quality, giving Sweden a major advantage in producing weapons. Within a few decades, Sweden became a significant exporter of steel, cannons, copper, and iron.
