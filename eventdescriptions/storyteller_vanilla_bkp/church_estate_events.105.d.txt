The esteemed institution of the realm has long been a cornerstone of our land, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, scholars, and diplomats for the benefit of our ruler. In return for these great services, many donations have been made over the years, both by the state and by others, to the great institution and their extensive lands. These gifts have over the centuries made the institution quite rich and influential. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the institution might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
