With its deeply rooted Catholic heritage, the kingdom of Spain has not shown a strong interest in the Protestant reformation. However, there are whispers of small groups of thinkers secretly gathering in larger cities to discuss these matters. It is with some concern that news has reached our monarch that the New Testament has been translated into Spanish by a fellow countryman, Francisco de Enzinas. Although the printing presses within Spain itself are closely monitored by the state, it appears that the printers of the Low Countries feel no obligation to shield the minds of our subjects. The Holy Inquisition has requested that we make an example of any Protestants found within our country, in order to track down those who would bring this book into Spain, as well as any hidden Lutherans living among us.
