The roads have been used for as long as anyone can remember, the Upper Post Road for example was called the Pequot Path by Native Americans and some of these trails were at times as narrow as two feet. The Boston Post Road is to be a road through the country, the province, Narragansett and Massachusetts, using some of the old native trails to deliver mail transported by post riders. Plenty of organization needs to be done, mileposts to be measured and routes established. Who would be the best to sort this out, the ruler's advisor, the military leader, or the diplomat?
