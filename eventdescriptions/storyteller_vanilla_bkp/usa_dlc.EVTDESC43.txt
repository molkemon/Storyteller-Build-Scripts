The homespun movement encouraged women to weave and spin their own cloth to make clothes for their families, rather than wearing or buying clothes made from imported British materials. During the war for independence, the movement was a way for people to resist Great Britain. By rejecting silks, satins, and other luxuries in favor of homespun clothing, they sent a strong message of unity against British rule. The Continental Army, led by George Washington, received clothing and blankets from the homespun movement. In Edenton, North Carolina, fifty-one women signed a statement agreeing to boycott tea and other English products and sent it to British newspapers. There have been many examples of women's efforts to free the United States from British rule, and today we honor and thank them for their support and struggle.
