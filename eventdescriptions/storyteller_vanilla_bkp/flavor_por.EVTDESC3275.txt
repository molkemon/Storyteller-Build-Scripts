John Methuen was born in 1650. An accomplished diplomat, he is well remembered for the treaty signed in 1703 between England and Portugal. The main purpose of the treaty was to allow free introduction of English wool and cloths to Portugal in exchange for free entry of Portuguese wine into England, or Port wine. The treaty made Portugal an economic and political satellite of England, and it remained in effect all the way to 1750, when King Joseph I chose to ignore it, even though it was still formally in place until 1836. John Methuen died in 1706, shortly after his greatest success.
