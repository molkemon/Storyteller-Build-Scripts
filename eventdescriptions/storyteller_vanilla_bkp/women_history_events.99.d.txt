The birth of their son made Rajkumari Heer Kunwari's husband, Emperor Akbar, give her the title Mary of the Age; and he named the boy Salim, to acknowledge the holy man who had promised him three sons. Though she was his third wife, as mother of the heir, Mary of the Age took precedence over Akbar's other wives. She is a Hindu and her marriage to Akbar led him to look more favorably on Hinduism and his Hindu subjects. It also gave Akbar the powerful support of her family throughout his reign, and with the marriage to a Hindu princess he signaled that he would be the ruler of his entire people, no matter their religion. Few women in court have ever been granted the right to issue official documents, which are usually the exclusive privilege of the emperor, but Akbar trusted Mary of the Age enough to grant her that privilege. She used her title the Gift of God when she issued those documents. With her wealth amassed from her international trade in spices, silk, and many other goods, she built gardens, wells, and mosques. Mary of the Age's private fortune dwarfs many a European king, and she is known to be an adventurous trader and the most skilled trader at court. She held the rank of 12,000, the only woman to do so. Her ships carried pilgrims to and from Mecca, and the outrage at court when the great pilgrim ship was seized by European soldiers and they officially refused to return the ship and its 600 passengers could be heard all the way to the holy city. It was released, and its passengers with it, but she bears a grudge against those who hindered the pilgrims from reaching Mecca.
