Born in 1640, Anthony Hensius had enjoyed the confidence of William the Third of Orange all his life. Sent to Versailles after the peace of Nijmegen to supervise the execution of its clauses, he became Raad Pensionaris in 1688 after William moved to England. A tough negotiator and obstinate opponent of France, he was at the core of all anti-French coalitions that are set up in all the wars of the late 17th century. He died in 1720.
