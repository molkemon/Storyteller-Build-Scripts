Installing our head of state as the prime ruler of the Neapolitan lands is certainly a bold move that shall not go unnoticed. Despite uproar created abroad, we must look within and determine how to handle the newly acquired lands, which are not only many but also extremely prosperous. The trade port of Napoli alone may be a key part to gaining access to the markets of Genoa, and thus we must be cautious and measure our moves accordingly.
