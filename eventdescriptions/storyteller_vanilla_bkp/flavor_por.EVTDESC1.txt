In 1492, Christopher Columbus embarked on his renowned voyage for Castile and sailed west across the Atlantic in search of a western route to the Orient. Seven years prior, in 1485, Christopher Columbus had presented the same plan to the King of Portugal, John the Second, who after submitting the proposal to his experts had turned it down. Columbus had made yet another attempt to persuade the King in 1488, but as the Portuguese explorer Bartholomeu Dias had successfully rounded the Cape of Good Hope around that time, an eastern sea route to Asia seemed more plausible, and the King once again rejected Columbus's proposal.
