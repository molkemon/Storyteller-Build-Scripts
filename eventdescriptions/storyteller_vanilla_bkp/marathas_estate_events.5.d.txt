The Marathas bring with them a hardened martial culture to the lands they rule. They maintain close ties with the common people and lower classes, favoring an equal society over a belief in the importance of nobility. Unlike many Indian armies, which typically draw their manpower from the nobility, the Marathas raise legions of common soldiers skilled in guerrilla warfare. Though not born with the advantages of noble birth, their military experience makes them stronger than the weak and unmanly troops of our enemies.
