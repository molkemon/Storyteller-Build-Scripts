Enlisting the aid of privateers to disrupt the trade efforts of our enemies has proven beneficial to us, but also tends to attract unreliable and devious business partners. One captain has become notorious for bending the rules and ignoring treaties our privateers are supposed to follow, but also happens to be very efficient in raiding enemy ships.  Perhaps these two traits are connected, perhaps not. Regardless, revoking the captain's letter of marque would be the diplomatically safe option, but would cost us our best privateer.
