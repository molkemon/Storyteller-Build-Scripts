A woman of great influence, Queen Dorothea of Saxe-Lauenburg, plays a significant role in appointing and dismissing officials. Her lady-in-waiting, a young woman named Birgitte Gøye, was just fourteen years old when her family forced her into an engagement with Jesper Daa, but thanks to the queen's friendship, Birgitte was able to escape this arrangement. The queen's intervention led to an investigation by bishops and professors, which ultimately resulted in a new law that prohibits parents from arranging marriages for their underage children.
