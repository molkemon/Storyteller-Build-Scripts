In the year 1566, a grand fort was built by two powerful leaders, Chinna Bommi Nayak and Thimma Reddy Nayak, who served under the ruler of a large and powerful realm. Its impressive walls, deep moat, and strong stone construction have made it well-known, and it was built using the local stone from nearby quarries in the Arcot and Chittor regions. Inside the fort is a temple dedicated to a revered deity, Jalagandeeswar, and it is famous for its intricate carvings.
