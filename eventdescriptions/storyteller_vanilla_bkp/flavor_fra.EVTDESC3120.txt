Cardinal Richelieu was 39 years old when he became the chief minister of King Louis the Thirteenth in 1624. His main successful efforts in domestic policy were the fight against the upper nobility and the Protestant political privileges. He also supported overseas trade, colonial expansion, and the establishment of a permanent royal navy. His most notable achievement remained the diplomatic game he played during the Thirty Years War, using French gold to influence the Swedish intervention in 1630 and ultimately joining the war in 1635. His goals had been the reduction of the House of Habsburg and the removal of France from encirclement by enemy lands. He is considered the inventor of the concept of Realpolitik.
