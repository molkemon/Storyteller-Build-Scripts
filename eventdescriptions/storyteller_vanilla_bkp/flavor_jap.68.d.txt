As wealth flowed into Japan's treasuries, a growing portion of it was invested in the arts and entertainment. The most popular form of entertainment in the pleasure districts was dancing and singing, accompanied by instruments and young performers known as odoriko. As these girls matured, they adopted new names to continue their work, one of which was 'geiko'. Over time, geiko became celebrated for their artistic talents, particularly in song, dance, and the shamisen, and specialized in these forms of entertainment.
