In 1731, the ruler of Salzburg purged his lands of Protestants. The Protestants had grown in number over the years and the ruler had started to feel his powers threatened by their existence. More than 26,000 ethnic German Protestants fled for their lives. 17,000 of them were welcomed in Prussia in 1732 and most of them settled in East Prussia. Several Protestant countries started nationwide collections to help the fugitives.
