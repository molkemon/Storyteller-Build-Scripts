A growing merchant class, along with increasingly demanding social codes among the nobility and upper classes, has led to a heightened demand for perfume. The grand halls of palaces are now expected to have a pleasant aroma, as well as a majestic decor. This has resulted in a surge in demand for products such as frankincense, myrrh, and other scented goods used in perfume production. In the capital of the perfume city, the refinement of local flowers and foreign scented goods has become a major industry in itself.
