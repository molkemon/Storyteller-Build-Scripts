In the course of the great ruler's reign, medieval and outdated forms of government gave way to effective autocracy. In the year 1711, he abolished the boyar council, or council of nobles, and established by decree the Senate as the supreme governing body to coordinate the actions of various central and local institutions, to oversee the collection and expenditure of revenue, and to draft laws in accordance with his decrees. Military discipline was extended to civil institutions, and a high-ranking officer of the guards was always on duty in the Senate. From the year 1722, moreover, there was a chief prosecutor keeping watch over the daily work of the Senate and its administrative office, and acting as the sovereign's representative.
