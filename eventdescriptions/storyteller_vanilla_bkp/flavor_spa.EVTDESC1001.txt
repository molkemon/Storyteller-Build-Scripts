The laws of Burgos were a set of rules concerning the behavior of Spaniards in America that were issued in the city of Burgos in the year 1512. They especially concerned the treatment of the indigenous people. Though the laws allowed colonizers to force Indians to work together, the laws also regulated rules regarding work, pay, and living conditions. It also prohibited any form of punishment other than by the authorities. The laws, however, did little to ease the suffering of the Indians due to poor enforcement.
