They came at night. Their faces barely lit by the moon, their spears shining bright near the torches. They came in silence. Their footsteps soft and swift like a summer breeze. Almost impossible to hear. Then, silence was over, and so was the night. They raided our village, burning tents, stealing food, taking captives. Many of our warriors woke up immediately and tried to stop them, but they were not prepared for the attack, so many were killed in the fight. They killed them. Fathers, brothers, sons. They killed them all. May they not rest as long as we live.
