The ruler of Spain was led by the Duke of Olivares between 1621 and 1643, under King Philip the Fourth, whom he enjoyed the favor of. He was a cunning leader, he involved Spain in the Thirty Years War alongside the Imperials and had the war with Holland renewed. This proved disastrous and precipitated Spanish decline, with the loss of both Catalonia, which was later reunited in 1652, and Portugal in 1640. Under his leadership, poetry, literature, particularly that of Calderon, and painting, including the works of El Greco, Velasquez, and Murillo, prospered and Spanish customs and traditions spread across Europe.
