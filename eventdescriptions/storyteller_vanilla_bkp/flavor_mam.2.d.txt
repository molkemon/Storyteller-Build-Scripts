The ruler of our realm was known to be very concerned about urban planning, particularly in the country of Egypt. In the year 1457, the ruler gave the order to construct two new public baths and a large communal residence in the Bayn alQasrayn District. During that time, he decided to widen the main road of the city, known as the Qasaba. This demolished several older structures, but in the end traffic was much quicker and according to important historians, this greatly benefited the general public.
