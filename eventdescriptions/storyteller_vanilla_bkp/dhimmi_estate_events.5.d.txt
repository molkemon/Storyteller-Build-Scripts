Many among the Muslim nobles have been voicing complaints at what they feel is an unjust and unfair favoring of infidels in our government. Having sought the support of the church, they now claim that we are running our country contrary to established customs and that we must ensure that the infidels are kept out of positions of influence. The construction of a new shrine in a province largely dominated by the dhimmis, entirely funded by local nobles and merchants, has escalated the situation further with the church and the nobles demanding that we tear down the holy building and put the infidels in their place.
