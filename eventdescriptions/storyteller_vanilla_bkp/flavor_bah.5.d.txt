Our policy of constant outside recruitment has had its drawbacks. Known as 'Deccanis', second and third generation Bahmani nobles together with the remaining part of the old Hindu nobility are increasingly becoming a strong faction at court advocating the indigenization of our realm. Against them stand the new immigrants from the west, the 'Gharbians', who have come to seek a fortune in the rich lands of India. The court is by now quite polarized between the two and lately they even have to be kept separated in our audience chamber and at royal ceremonies.
