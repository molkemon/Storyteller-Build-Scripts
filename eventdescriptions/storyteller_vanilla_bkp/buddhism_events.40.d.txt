The island of Lanka has been the source of a strong Theravada Buddhist tradition for centuries. It is in many ways a model society for a Theravada state and its thriving monastic communities are the envy of the Buddhist world. While our own state also follows the Theravada way, our laws and traditions are a patchwork of local customs, ideas from competing Buddhist schools, and we lack the strong monastic infrastructure of the island. Our advisors suggest that in order to remedy this, we should send an embassy to Sri Lanka and ask that they send us monks to carry their Theravada traditions to our realm.
