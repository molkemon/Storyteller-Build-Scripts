The Revolution has arrived. Longstanding dissatisfaction at the lack of meaningful reforms by the government of the country boiled over as revolutionaries stormed the Topkapi Palace in the capital, capturing the ruler's harem and the Royal Treasury. The ruler attempted to flee but was unable to evade capture for long. The ruler was swiftly put to death for crimes against the people and the faith. The Imperial Divan has been purged of all reactionaries and reorganized into the ruling council of the new Republic, which is now headed by self-styled Supreme Vizier they. The new regime promises to abolish serfdom, establish equality under the law and redistribute the nobility's land between the state and the peasantry, so that all brothers may be free from oppression. However, the heir remains at large in the provinces and has gathered an army of loyalists who vow to bring back the old regime.
