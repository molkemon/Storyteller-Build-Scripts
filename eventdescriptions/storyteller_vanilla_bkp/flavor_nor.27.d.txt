Worried about what they perceive to be an increasing encroachment on their privileges by the Danish Crown members of the Norwegian council and other prominent individuals have gathered in Hamar to work out a number of demands upon the Crown for their continued support for the Union. The demands put into print at Hamar include the restoration of lost Norwegian lands, affirming the traditional rights of the Norwegian church and nobility, strengthening the power of the Norwegian royal council, and in general affirming Norway's role as an equal party of Denmark in our realm. The intent is for the list of demands to be sent to the ruler to be made part of their traditional Electoral Capitulation, as the monarch of Norway.
