The convent Monasterio de Santa Maria de Sigena was founded by Queen Sancha of Castile, wife of Alfonso the Second of Aragon, and was constructed between 1183 and 1208. It houses some important Romanesque frescos of about 1200 by English artists and although it has started to decline, the monastery is still a grand cloister. The nuns are asking for our help to fund the monastery and the Maestro de Sigena has offered to paint a large altarpiece.
