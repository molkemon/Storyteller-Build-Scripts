In a clerical meeting in Dordrecht, a pivotal moment occurred in the year 1618 to 1619. Hardline Calvinism was established as the state religion, marking the end of a long and arduous struggle between two factions. On one side were the Arminians, a pluralistic and humanitarian group, while on the other were the Gomarists, a dogmatic and narrow-minded faction. The Gomarists, primarily composed of commoners and farmers, received support from Moritz of Oranien. In contrast, the Arminians, consisting of intellectuals and wealthy merchants, were backed by Oldenbarneveld.
