Construction of the Jerónimos Monastery began in 1501 and was originally funded by the king using the income from the 'Vintena da Pimenta' tax on goods from Africa and the Far East. Work stopped in 1580 when the country after the death of the ruler and a succession crisis entered into a union with Spain. When the country became independent again in 1640, the monastery saw further construction with the addition of a gold tiled ceiling in 1669 and large frescos completed at the end of the 18th century. The monastery also became the burial place for the royal family.
