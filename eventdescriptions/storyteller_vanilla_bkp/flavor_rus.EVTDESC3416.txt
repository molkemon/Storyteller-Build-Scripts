Boris Godunov was elected in 1598 to succeed Fedor. He consolidated Russia's territorial gains, but soon after he came to power, a severe drought, famine, and plague killed half a million people in the country. Peasants fled their villages, leaving their holdings to be reclaimed by weeds. In response, Godunov decreed that the peasants were forbidden to leave the estates where they were born. The peasants had truly become serfs.
