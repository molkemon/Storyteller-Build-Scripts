Baltasar de Zuniga came from a powerful Spanish noble family. He served Philip the Third as ambassador to Brussels, Paris, and Vienna between 1599 and 1617. In the latter position, he was instrumental in Spain's intervention in the Thirty Years War. In 1618, he managed to remove the Duke of Lerma as Philip the Third's key advisor, a position he then took over himself. De Zuniga's nephew, Olivares, was placed in the household of Prince Philip; later Prince Philip the Fourth; to assure continued dominance at the royal court.
