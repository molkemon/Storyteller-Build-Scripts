Tensions between our parliament and our ruler have escalated to a point of no return. What started as a mere dispute over regional interests, taxation, and military expenditure has turned into a conflict over the role of our parliament itself in the government. There are questions about the necessity of a parliament on one side, and the necessity of a ruler on the other. Our ruler is tired of having to keep content a group of people that keep interfering in their legitimate and undeniable right to rule and uses every opportunity to undermine their authority. On the other hand, regional powerful individuals of our parliament have proclaimed that they will no longer obey any central and tyrannical authority, and have raised troops in their territories to defend their own authority by force. Great parts of our country have declared themselves as no longer subjected to our rule and are in open rebellion against us.
