The farmers in one of our provinces have complained about the troubles caused by a mysterious figure. Cattle has been set loose, eggs, chicken, and honey stolen, horses frightened, and corn scattered, all because of a short, ugly, and hairy man known from our legends and myths. He is blamed for the birth of hairy and ugly children, and he is impossible to detect due to his ability to move silently and turn invisible. The only way to stop him is by giving him gifts, such as honey and liquor, which often work to appease him. If this is done frequently enough, he will become friendly and guard over one's home, animals, and possessions. He will sometimes even leave gifts in return.
