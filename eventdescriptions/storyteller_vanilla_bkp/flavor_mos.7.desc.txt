The reign of Vasily II is a troubled one. His grandfather, Dmitry Donskoy, had appointed Vasily I as his legal successor and Vasily I's brother, Yury Dmitrievich, as the next in line of succession. Eventually, Dmitry and Vasily I passed away, and Vasily I's son, Vasily II, became the ruler of our realm. This act defied the succession law of seniority, and as such, Yury rebelled against the then still young Vasily II. This led to the Muscovite Civil War between Vasily II and Yury, along with Yury's sons, Vasily Kosoy and Dmitry Shemyaka. While the threats of Kosoy and Yury were dealt with, Dmitry Shemyaka still poses a danger to the stability of our government. With a small army of conspirators, Dmitry executed a coup d'etat against Vasily II. The ruler was captured, blinded, and exiled to Uglich while Dmitry asserted control over the government.
