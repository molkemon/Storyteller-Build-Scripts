Ever since the thinkers of the Reformed faith began to spread their ideas from their capital, they have had a strong following in our kingdom. Reformed ideas such as predestination have struck home among our nobles and commoners alike, and Reformed schools and colleges have begun to appear in many of our provinces. With the hesitant acceptance of the second Helvetic Confession, our Reformed churches have also started to unite with the other Reformed churches of our time. The Catholic clergy, however, have always been strong supporters of royal power and view these developments with distrust. While strong, the Reformed church remains young and vulnerable. Do we want to give the Reformed church room to grow, or should we try to suppress it before it's too late?
