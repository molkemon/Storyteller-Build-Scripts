A poet and scholar from a certain province has made a name for himself among his people by distributing nationalist pamphlets and composing a national anthem. Denouncing what he describes as harsh oppression and drawing on the ideals of the Revolution, he calls on his countrymen to leave their homeland and seek freedom in the New World. It seems that his words have struck a chord among the people, with one influential nobleman praising him as "the most passionate person I have ever met". Already, many of his countrymen, radicalized by his ideas, are departing for another country.
