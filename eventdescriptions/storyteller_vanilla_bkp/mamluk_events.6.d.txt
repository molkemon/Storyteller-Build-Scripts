Much older than our own government, Cairo has been embellished by rulers and conquerors for hundreds if not a thousand years. Bringing order in this maze of buildings, from the tombs of the city of the dead and the slums of Fustat, to the bustling markets of Khan elKhalili, or the great Citadel of Cairo itself, has proven a monumental task for many of our predecessors. Nonetheless, as the master of the city, our ruler, is expected to leave their mark upon Cairo, both to embellish and develop it, and to combat disease and keep the order.
