The earthquake that destroyed Lisbon was a catastrophe of huge proportions that resounded all across Europe and even into the new world. On November 1st, 1755, a beautiful day, when nearly everyone was in the churches celebrating a holiday, the ground suddenly started to shake and the churches began to collapse, crushing the visitors. For those who escaped, it was far from over as a strong gust of wind suddenly swept in over the area, fanning the small fires into a raging inferno that incinerated thousands. But yet thousands of people would fall victim to the massive tidal wave that reached land just a few minutes later. The death toll was estimated to be between 20,000 and 40,000 people at the time, and given the involvement of all four elements - wind, fire, earth, and water - the explanation that it was a punishment from God was a view widely held both in Portugal and elsewhere.
