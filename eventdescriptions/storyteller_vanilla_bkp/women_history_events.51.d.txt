Sultan Malakhai Tokhi, the ruler of the Tokhi Pashtun tribe, has a daughter named Nazo Ana, a learned poet and a kind-hearted person. Her education and upbringing were overseen by her father, and she was trained by learned scholars from Kandahar. Through her poetry and adherence to the Pashtunwali code, Nazo Tokhi has gained respect. Conflicts between the Ghilji and Sadozai Pashtun tribes are often resolved by her, in order to promote unity against the Persian Safavid rulers. There are many stories about her, but one that we know is true is that she took up a sword and defended her father's fortress alongside the men when her brother went to avenge their father's death. She was left in charge of the household and fortress, and defended it bravely.
