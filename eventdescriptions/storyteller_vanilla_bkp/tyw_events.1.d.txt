As Emperor, we may have to accept that some of our subjects fall to heresy to avoid internal conflict. The ruler dictates the religion of the realm, after all. We may not afford to be so lenient with ecclesiastical principalities, however. One of the prince-bishops who rule in the Holy Roman Empire appears to have abandoned the Church and listened to the lies of the Protestants, risking not only his own soul, but those of all his subjects. Thanks to the ecclesiastical reservation, we reserve the right to force him to resign in the event of betrayal like this.
