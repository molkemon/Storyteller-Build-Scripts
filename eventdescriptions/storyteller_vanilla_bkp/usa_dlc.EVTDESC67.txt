Campaign and the Issues, 1808: As the former leader stepped down, he gave his full support to James Madison as his successor. Madison was only at risk of one major issue; the Embargo. As Secretary of State, he had handled the Embargo, which was both a response to the disrespect of France and Britain and a policy of protecting American interests. The Embargo was very unpopular with both sides, so the Federalists once again gained strength under Charles Pinckney, while the Free Trade Republicans threatened to support the Republican vice presidential candidate, George Clinton. However, this threat never came to pass, and although Pinckney performed well, he couldn't compete with the enduring popularity of former President Jefferson. Jefferson played a crucial role in urging both friends and foes to stand behind Madison, securing his victory. Electoral Vote: Madison 122, Pinckney 47, Clinton 6.
