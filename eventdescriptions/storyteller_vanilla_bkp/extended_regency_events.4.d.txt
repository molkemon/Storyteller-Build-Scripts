Gold is a dangerous thing, its tempting shine impossible to resist. It lurks in the shadows, attracting unaware fools to its doomed glow, its gilded roundness like two demon eyes. Our ruler is no stranger to this perilous feeling, their annoyed face desperately looking for a new toy to play with, their fingers inspecting every drawer, every pocket, trying to use their hands as Midas. Meanwhile, our nation suffers, the population slowly falling into despair, their hungry mouths open in prayers.
