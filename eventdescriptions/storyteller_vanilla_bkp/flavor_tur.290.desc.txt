The Millet System was a part of our administration of separate religious communities, allowing each community to oversee its own affairs through independent court systems and schools. However, it was an inconsistent system, and the laws of each province differed greatly from those of its neighbors. Some clever governors used this to their advantage, expanding their power far beyond what they were supposed to rule. With the standardization of the millet, the treatment of our subjects should be consistent throughout the entire realm.
