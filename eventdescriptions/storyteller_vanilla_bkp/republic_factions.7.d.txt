One of our diplomatic envoys with strong ties to a domestic organization has been accused by some older ambassadors of making deals with a group across the border. While he admits to attending to business while abroad, he denies accusations of compromising his missions. On the other hand, he says he is open to the idea of using his connections abroad to our advantage. The old guard of aristocratic diplomats finds this practice highly dubious, but leveraging connections between groups could give us an edge in secret operations.
