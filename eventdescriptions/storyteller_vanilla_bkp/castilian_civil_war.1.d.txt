During the past century, the nobility of our country have repeatedly tried to interfere with the succession of the crown and have not hesitated to form alliances with outside powers. A new such struggle seems to be brewing, and the succession is once again in question. The future legitimacy of our throne is being questioned by some leading nobles who would prefer a relative of the ruler aligned with the Aragonese Crown. Meanwhile, the supporters of the current establishment are making overtures to the Portuguese crown in order to secure the position of their candidate. Things have now escalated into open conflict, and it seems increasingly likely that the faction without royal support will attempt to put their candidate directly on the throne rather than waiting in line for it. Some parts of the nobility remain undecided, and some have chosen to use this opportunity to protest against perceived infringement of their old rights in general.
