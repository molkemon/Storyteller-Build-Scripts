Fyodor Baikov traveled to Beijing in the year 1656 in an effort to establish diplomatic relations with China. However, misinterpreting Chinese customs led him to behave in a way that caused the Chinese officials to believe Russia acknowledged China as its master. A message was sent to Moscow. The letter contained instructions on the annual tribute that the vassal Russia must now pay. The Czar had no opportunity to protest as no one in Moscow could comprehend Chinese. The Russian government disregarded the incident as if it had never occurred, and China was too preoccupied with being subjugated by the Manchu dynasty to care.
