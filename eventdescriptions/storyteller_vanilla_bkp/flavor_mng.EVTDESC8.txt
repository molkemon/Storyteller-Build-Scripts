A severe famine has struck our land. Years of poor harvests, caused by a prolonged drought, have left the people with scarce food. Starvation and disease are claiming many lives in the countryside, and reports of desperate measures, such as eating human flesh and grinding stones to make flour, are widespread. Many have turned to banditry in a desperate bid to survive. The people are pleading with the government to alleviate their suffering, but providing relief will be a costly endeavor. Shall we try to ease the people's hardship?
