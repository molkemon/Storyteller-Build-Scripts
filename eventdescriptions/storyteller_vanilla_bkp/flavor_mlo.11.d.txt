When the French invaded Italy in 1495, they first got repulsed at the Battle of Fornovo by the Milanese, but they came back in 1498. The French took Milan, but the ruler managed to escape. In February 1500, the ruler returned with an army of mercenaries, and engaged the French who were besieging Novara. Since both sides employed mercenaries, they refused to fight each other and the French captured the ruler, winning the war.
