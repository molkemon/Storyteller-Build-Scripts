Aiming at European dominance and the rounding of French borders in the North and in the East, to the Rhine, the king had established the Chambers of Reunion, courts that advanced claims to Imperial lands. These expansionist policies were at first tolerated by France's powerless neighbors. It reached its climax in 1681, with the annexation of the free city of Strasbourg in Alsace and the 1684 occupation of Luxembourg. The reunion was recognized by the Empire in the 1684 Truce of Regensburg, mostly because the Emperor was preoccupied by the Turkish threat, and the siege of Vienna in 1683, which led to the creation of the anti-French League of Augsburg in 1686.
