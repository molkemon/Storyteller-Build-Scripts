The rise of a certain theological movement led to a major gathering of the Dutch Reformed Church in Dordrecht to resolve a long-standing dispute. On November 13, 1618, the Dutch Reformed Church and representatives from eight other Reformed churches met for the first time. The final meeting took place on May 9, 1619, and it was the 154th meeting. The gathering settled the controversy over this movement, condemned it, rejected its views, and established the Reformed doctrine on key points, including the idea that people are completely flawed, that God chooses some individuals without condition, that salvation is limited to a select few, that God's grace is irresistible, and that those who follow God will persevere. The followers of this movement, known as the Remonstrants, were told to stop performing their duties as ministers, such as preaching, giving advice, administering sacraments, and visiting the sick. They were also asked to sign a document agreeing to stop their ministry, but they refused and were instead labeled as troublemakers and ordered to leave the United Provinces. The Synod declared the teachings of this movement to be heresy.
