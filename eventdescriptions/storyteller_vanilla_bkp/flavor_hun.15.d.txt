In the wake of the Crusade for Varna, the Hussite wars and internal struggles of Hungary in the 1430s and 1440s, a great number of highly skilled mercenaries have found themselves to be idle and unemployed. Unable to earn their living in any other way, these men have elected to make their own fortune through banditry and pillaging. In some cases, local nobles have joined the troublemakers, hoping to use these soldiers to create a stronger power base for themselves. There is no doubt that our local forces will overcome the insurrection in time, but it may come at a considerable cost for the local economy.
