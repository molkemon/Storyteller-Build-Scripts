In times of uncertainty, the people of these lands have often turned to piracy and raiding the coasts of neighboring countries. The last wave of such transgressions ended in the Kakitsu treaty, which allowed some lords to send trading expeditions to foreign ports. Times and the balance of power are prone to change, however, and piracy has once again become a common occupation among our subjects. Some lords have even started to fund or participate in the raids themselves. Meanwhile, the victims of these pirates have begun to offer titles and honors to those who help stop the menace.
