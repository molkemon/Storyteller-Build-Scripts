Our decision to make some relatively young additions to the Mbudye, the governing council, has led to conflicts between members. The new councilors, although inquisitive and eager to learn at first, have now begun to argue about how to read the memory boards and question details of important pieces of information recalled by the oldest members. Although it's possible that the old generation is going senile, the insolence displayed by the newcomers is staggering. Should the monarch silence them or heed their advice?
