Difficulties in the rule of the sea brought further changes. Until 1545 the rowers in the galleys were paid sailors. They were originally from Venice, but later many from Dalmatia, Crete, and Greece joined in large numbers. Because of the difficulty in hiring sufficient crews, Venice had to force people into service, chaining the rowers to the benches as other navies had already done. Cristoforo da Canal was the first from Venice to command such a galley. What shall we do?
