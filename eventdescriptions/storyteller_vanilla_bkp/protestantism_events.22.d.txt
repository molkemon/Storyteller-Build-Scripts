There are still those who long for the time when the Christian faith was unified. While they see the Reformation and the birth of Protestantism as necessary, they also lament that it couldn't be done without a schism. While well-intentioned, these kind of leanings can be very harmful to a State Church such as our own, and it is therefore a matter of some concern that it seems a number of the officials in our Church, as well as some at some of our seminaries, are entertaining such ideas.
