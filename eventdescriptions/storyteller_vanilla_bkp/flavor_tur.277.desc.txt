With the fall of Constantinople, Mehmed II proclaimed the Ottomans as the rightful successors of the Roman Empire and himself as its emperor. He further legitimized his claims by promoting former Byzantine nobles to senior positions in the administration of the state, made Constantinople the capital of the Ottoman Empire and restored it to its former glory, taking great inspiration from the Byzantines regarding matters of court traditions. Naturally, this claim of Roman succession was also used by Mehmed to justify an invasion of Otranto in 1480 with the further ambition to conquer Rome for the Ottomans. This endeavor found a premature end, however, when Mehmed died on the third of May 1481. Ever since, no other Ottoman Sultan set foot as a conqueror on Italian soil again.
