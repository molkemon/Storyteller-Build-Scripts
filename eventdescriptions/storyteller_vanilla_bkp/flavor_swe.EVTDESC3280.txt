Per Brahe the Elder was a Swedish statesman and nephew of King Gustavus the Fifth. Brahe was highly regarded by the King and distinguished himself during the Dacke feud. He acted as Governor of Stockholm Castle from 1540 and was awarded a place in the Privy Council in 1544. At the coronation of King Eric the Fourteenth in 1561 Brahe was made Count of Visingsborg, and the accession of King John the Third saw him appointed the Lord High Justiciar of Sweden as well as Governor of Norrland.
