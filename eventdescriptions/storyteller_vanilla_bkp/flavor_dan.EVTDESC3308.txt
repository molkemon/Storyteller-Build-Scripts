In 1766, a king named Christian VII was crowned ruler of Denmark, succeeding his father, Frederik V. Christian VII unfortunately suffered from mental instability, which some attributed to a harsh childhood and corrupt influences. After marrying Caroline Matilda, the daughter of Frederick, Prince of Wales, in 1766, Christian VII gave in to a life of excess. Throughout his entire reign, the country was actually controlled by others. Initially, it was the foreign minister, J.H.E. Bernstorff, a cunning and capable individual who managed to persuade Catherine II of Russia to relinquish some of her claims to parts of the duchy of Holstein. However, Bernstorff's position became increasingly unstable, and he was eventually removed from office on September 15, 1770.
