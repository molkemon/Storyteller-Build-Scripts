The Fronde, which occurred from 1648 to 1653, was mostly the last revolt of the French nobility against royal absolutism. It had started by the revolt of the Parliament of Paris, the highest French court at the time, against the domestic policies of Cardinal Mazarin, who acted as Prime Minister for the five-year-old Louis the Fourteenth. It was followed by that of the upper nobility, but the rebellion was finally suppressed by a combination of diplomacy, betrayals, and reverses of fortune. The Parliament was silenced, and the upper nobility was deprived of political power, preparing the advent of total royal absolutism under the Sun King, Louis the Fourteenth, after 1661.
