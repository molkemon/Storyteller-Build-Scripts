The widespread practice of slavery in the states is a contentious issue, with twenty-five out of fifty-five delegates to the Convention owning slaves, who make up about one-fifth of the population. The Southern states, with their agrarian economy based on slave labor, hold a significant majority of slaves. Many of the Southern delegates are opposed to any proposals that might threaten the institution of slavery. The Convention has discussed the slave trade, including a new fugitive slave clause that would require escaped slaves to be returned to their owners in the state from which they escaped. The question remains whether to risk the wrath of the Southern states by abolishing the slave trade or to allow the status quo to continue.
