Joachim II Hector was forced by his father, Joachim I Nestor, to sign a contract promising to remain Roman Catholic. His father had helped his younger brother, the Archbishop of Mainz, become the ruler of Halberstadt, Magdeburg, and Mainz by taking on huge debts with the Fugger bank to pay the Pope. To pay off these debts, Joachim Nestor agreed to let the people of his country pay by allowing the sale of indulgences.
