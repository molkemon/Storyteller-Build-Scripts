The major influence which France did exert over the baroque age was one of courtly opulence. In 1669, the ruler of France decided to convert an old hunting lodge at Versailles into a palace of unprecedented magnificence. It was completed in 1682, and the royal court moved there in 1683. There was not a royal court in Europe which could match Versailles for the opulence of its gilded interiors, its mirrors and chandeliers, with an appropriately matching flamboyance of courtly ceremony, and there was not a royal court in Europe which did not, either openly or secretly, aspire to match this model.
