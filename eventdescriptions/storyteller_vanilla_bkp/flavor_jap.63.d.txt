The Edo Period, historically starting in 1603 with the establishment of the Tokugawa Shogunate, marks a time of great stability, prosperity, and economic growth for our country. Finally free of internal struggles and constant civil wars, our focus shifted instead towards the development not only of the economy but also of the arts and sciences. The increase in population saw also an increase in urbanization, which furthered such developments even more.  The period saw the creation and proliferation of new financial policies, new painting techniques like the ukiyoe or new entertaining activities like the geisha or the kabuki and even new kinds of dishes like sushi or tempura. They all flourished during the Edo Period and became a staple of our culture recognized as such all over the world.  With this unprecedented period of stability, development, and growth, our country is sure to be able to reach heights never before imagined.
