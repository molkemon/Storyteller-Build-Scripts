In 1679, a seasoned military leader and engineer, Erik Dahlberg, was appointed head of the Swedish Bureau of Fortifications. At that time, Sweden was at the height of its power, and the aggressive policies of the previous decades had left the country with longer borders than ever before. Erik Dahlberg and his team designed several imposing fortresses and had them built at strategic points around the realm, skillfully utilizing the terrain to its fullest potential. The great forts Lejonet and Kronan in Gothenburg, the fortress of Varberg, and the fortress of Dalarö are all a testament to his exceptional skill. Unfortunately, the strained budget did not allow for the eastern provinces, which would prove disastrous in the years to come.
