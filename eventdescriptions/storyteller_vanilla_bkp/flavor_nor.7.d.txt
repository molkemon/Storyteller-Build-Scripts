Ever since the 13th century treaty of Perth, Scotland has been obligated to pay a yearly sum to the Norwegian Kingdom in exchange for control of the Norwegian Hebrides. These payments are by now long overdue, however, and this has been the cause for tensions on and off. Regardless of our ancestral claims, however, our new overlords have seen fit to end this conflict forever. The royal family will marry into their dynasty and the Jarldom of Orkney will be given away and then transferred to the Scots. Orkney remains one of the highest-ranked titles in our Kingdom, one that has often been a stepping stone to the Norwegian throne. If we give up these islands we will have surrendered the right to rule our former possessions forever.
