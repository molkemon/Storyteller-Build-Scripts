Born near the border between the Aztec Empire and the Mayanspeaking Yucatan Peninsula, La Malinche was sold into slavery by her family and eventually given to our ruler as tribute. While she initially only spoke Nahuatl and Mayan dialects, but no language of our realm, we had an interpreter who spoke the language of our realm and some Mayan, setting up a relay until she had learned enough of our language to translate on her own. As translator and active participant in the Conquest, La Malinche was so prominent that she was almost invariably portrayed accompanying the conquistadors in native sources. Conquistadors agreed that she was critical to the expedition, with at least one describing her as the second most important contributor after God himself.
