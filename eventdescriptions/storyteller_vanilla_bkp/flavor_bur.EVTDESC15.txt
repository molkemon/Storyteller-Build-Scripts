In 1578, a nobleman from the Charolais family arrives in the Netherlands. This nobleman has a good reputation and is skilled in both warfare and politics. He becomes a formidable opponent for William of Orange. The nobleman soon realizes that Burgundy can maintain control over the Netherlands only by catering to the desires of the Catholic provinces. In 1579, Burgundy successfully forms an alliance with some southern Catholic provinces, known as the Union of Arras, which brings peace with Burgundy. However, the northern provinces respond by creating the Union of Utrecht, marking the beginning of a new Dutch nation. Later, the nobleman uses the Union of Arras as a base for an invasion of the North, but the northerners are saved when the nobleman's two older brothers are nearly murdered in 1588, forcing him to return to Burgundy immediately.
