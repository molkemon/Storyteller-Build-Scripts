Months of bloody fighting have passed and at long last the final member of their church has submitted to the military might of their ruler. Of all the groups who had to endure the war, the poor peasantry has suffered the most. Disillusioned by the ways of their religion and terrified by their church's apparent lust for power and control, many of the once pious men have turned their back on their religion's dogmas. The court shares a similar attitude. Exhausted and embittered, their once secretive interests in Zoroastrianism have turned into a lively demand to leave their religious community and embrace Zoroastrianism as their state religion.
