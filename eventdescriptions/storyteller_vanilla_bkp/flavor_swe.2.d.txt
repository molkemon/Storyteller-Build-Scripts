During the Swedish liberation war, the Crown had accrued enormous debts to the Hanseatic League and our ruler needed money quickly in order to placate the powerful merchants. At the Parliament of Västerås in the summer of 1527, he took the first step towards Protestantism when the Church was obliged to 'lend' the state money in the form of silver chalices, bells and other valuable objects. Over the coming three decades, our country gradually slipped into the evangelic faith despite several uprisings.
