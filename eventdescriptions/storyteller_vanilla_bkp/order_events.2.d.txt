A book on witchcraft, believed to have originated in Central Europe, has surfaced in a local Dominican convent in a province. Malleus Maleficarum, Hammer of Witches, warns of the dangers posed by witches to society and prescribes methods for dealing with them. It categorizes the practice of such sorcery as heretical and insists that the courts adopt the prosecute and punish it accordingly. Although the Dominicans in that province are far from united on the validity of the book, some believe it originates within the order. Its fate continues to be the subject of debate.
