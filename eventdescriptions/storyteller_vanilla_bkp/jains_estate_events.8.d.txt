Paryushana marks the holiest period of the Jain religious calendar. It is a time of fasting, austerity, and absolution. Members of the Jain community use this time for prayer, meditation, and asking forgiveness from their fellows. The ruler's advisors in the realm have petitioned the state to ask that the slaughter of animals be prohibited for the duration of Paryushana. While a costly proposal for our butchers, it has been suggested that the state could make up for the loss of profit.
