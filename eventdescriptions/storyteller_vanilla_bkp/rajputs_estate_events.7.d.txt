Rajput nobles are a common sight at the court of our country, though it is becoming increasingly difficult to distinguish between them and the other courtiers that make their home in our capital. The Rajputs at court have been thoroughly integrating into our aristocratic society, going so far as to learn the Persian language, observe our courtly etiquette, and personally accompany our ruler wherever they go. This is leading to the loss of a distinctly Rajput voice at court, as these nobles have become more concerned with their personal advancement than in the interests of the Rajput community.
