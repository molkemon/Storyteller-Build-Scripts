Sigismund III Vasa's son and successor, King Wladyslaw IV Vasa, ordered the construction of Sigismund's Column, and it was erected between 1643 and 1644. The Italian-born architect Constantino Tencalla designed the column. Placed in Warszawa, the Corinthian column is made of red marble, 8.5 meters high, on which a sculpture of Sigismund III Vasa, 2.75 meters high, is placed. The column is adorned by four eagles and the king is dressed in armor, carrying a cross in one hand and wielding a sword in the other.
