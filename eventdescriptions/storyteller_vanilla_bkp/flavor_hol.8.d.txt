As a relatively latecomer in the competition for colonies, the Netherlands has found that many of the colonial possessions it desires have already been taken by others. With a strong navy and a willing financial sector, the independent Netherlands still has considerable resources at its disposal. These circumstances have led the Dutch West India Company to devise the Grand Design for how the Netherlands might rise above its competitors. According to the plan, rather than just trying to establish colonies of its own, it would be more efficient to conquer those of its competitors. As a natural competitor, the Portuguese colonial empire has been deemed a good target for this plan, especially since Dutch investors are already involved in some of these possessions.
