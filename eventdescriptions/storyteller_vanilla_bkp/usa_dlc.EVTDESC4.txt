The thirteen states that formed the United States have agreed on a version of the Articles of Confederation and sent it to the states for approval. This has given the Continental Congress the authority to lead the American Revolutionary War, negotiate with Europe, and handle territorial issues and relations with Native American tribes, even though it has not yet been officially accepted. There have been lengthy discussions about issues such as the balance of power, the powers of the central government, the creation of a court system, and voting procedures during the drafting of the Articles.
