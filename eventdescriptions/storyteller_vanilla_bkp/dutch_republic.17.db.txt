The recent increase in support for the Monarchists faction has sparked a discussion about how our government is viewed by other nations. A country with a weak ruler may be regarded as easy prey and unworthy of respect. What does this mean for a country lacking a ruler altogether? The Monarchists claim to have the answer. For our nation to be a contender on the international arena, the monarch must show their ability to unite and rule. Vain power displays are normally considered a waste, but perhaps one would be necessary now.
