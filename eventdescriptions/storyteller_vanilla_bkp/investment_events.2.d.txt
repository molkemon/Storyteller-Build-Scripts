Whether acquired by conflict or by purchase, treaties recognizing Company ownership of the disputed land have become disputed. Land brokers of the Company insist that their rightful territory extends somewhat farther than the land they currently administer. Their insistence is motivated by the fact that the land in question has been valued particularly highly by the brokers, who are now insisting that the neighboring country recognize Company ownership of the border area. Officials of that country have not reacted kindly to this unexpected expansionism, and demand adherence to their interpretation of the treaties.
