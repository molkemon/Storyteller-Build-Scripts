The founder of the Zamojski Academy was a high-ranking official named Jan Zamoyski. He established the institution in 1594, making it the fourth of its kind in the Polish realm. A renowned poet, Simon Simonides, assisted Jan Zamoyski in the founding and later became one of the academy's lecturers. The academy received approval from the Pope, as well as from the Bishop of Chelm, Stanislaw Gomolinski. The academy officially opened on March 15th, 1595, and was later confirmed by the King of Poland, Sigismund the Third, in 1601. The academy offered three main departments: liberal arts, law, and medicine. Initially, it had seven professors, and from 1637, it had the authority to grant doctor of philosophy degrees.
