The powerful group of sheep ranchers, known as the Mesta, are facing new challenges to their rural rights; textile manufacturers argue that their use of land is wasteful and hinders new growth. The price of Merino wool has dropped somewhat since the Mesta's peak, weakening their political influence. Although the Mesta's economic significance has already declined, the nobles and clergy who make up their membership continue to hold a great deal of sway at court, and insist that their privileges be maintained despite the changing circumstances.
