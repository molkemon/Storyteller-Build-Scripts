Engelbrekt Engelbrektsson is a legendary and enigmatic figure in Swedish medieval history. In 1434, the people of the realm had had enough of the union king's crushing taxes and brutal officials. Led by Engelbrekt, the common folk and miners, supported by certain factions within the Church and nobility, rose up in open rebellion. They captured castle after castle and soon controlled the greater part of the country. Meanwhile, Erik Puke had joined Engelbrekt in revolt in a neighboring land. For two years, Engelbrekt was the de facto ruler of the realm, but intrigue among the nobles and infighting between Engelbrekt, Erik Puke, and Karl Knutsson finally resulted in Engelbrekt's murder at the hands of Måns Bengtsson.
