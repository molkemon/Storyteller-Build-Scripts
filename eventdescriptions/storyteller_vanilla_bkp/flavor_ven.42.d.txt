The Carnival of Venice in the 15th century is a vibrant and extravagant event known for its opulent masks and costumes. It lasts for several weeks leading up to Lent, providing a temporary escape from societal norms. Revelers indulge in lavish feasts, masquerade balls, and entertainment, all while concealing their identities behind elaborate masks. This celebration is a symbol of Venice's wealth and decadence during this period.
