In 1803, Napoleon of France was hard-pressed for cash as he was set on a dream of European hegemony. He therefore offered the Louisiana Territory, a vast region between the Mississippi and the Rockies, against a lump sum of 15 million dollars. The President and the Senate agreed, and the United States nearly doubled its size. Sire, you would only get the province that France presently owns for a lump sum of 1000 ducats. How will you choose, Mr. President?
