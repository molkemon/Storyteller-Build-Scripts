The influx of German miners and prospectors has in short time made small mining communities spring up all around our country, even in areas where mining will never be the main source of income. These communities are, however, almost entirely small German colonies with German laws, customs, and population. Surrounding Norwegian villages are required to provide the miners with food and other things they might need, and this has over time led to some tensions developing between the locals and the newcomers. In the village of Hjartdal, village after village has begun to take up arms against the Germans, demanding that they leave and that Norwegian laws and customs be upheld.
