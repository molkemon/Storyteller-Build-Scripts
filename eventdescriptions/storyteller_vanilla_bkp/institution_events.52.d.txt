Traders from a prominent trading company active in our lands have approached the local authorities in that province asking to be allowed to expand an existing factory there by building a fort and being permitted to collect taxes from our subjects living nearby. They argue that if we give them these rights, they will not only compensate us financially, but also increase commerce and traffic to our ports, leading to a general growth of the region's importance in international trade. However, we would be allowing these foreigners to expand at the expense of our own merchants.
