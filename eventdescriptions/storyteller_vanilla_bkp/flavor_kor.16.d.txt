The Tripitaka Koreana is a collection of the Tripitaka; sacred Buddhist scriptures which collectively constitute the Buddhist canon. These scriptures have been carved onto wooden printing blocks and are the pride of Korean scholars and craftsmen. Although once destroyed by the fire of the rampaging Mongols in 1232, the Tripitaka Koreana was restored in 1249. Now it is in the hands of our ruler. Its fate depends now on us.
