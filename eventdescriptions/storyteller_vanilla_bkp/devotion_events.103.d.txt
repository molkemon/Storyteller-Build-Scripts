The feast is lavish, as it is supposed to be. Food and drinks are plentiful, no one will leave without a full belly and a hangover to look forward to. The ruler and the advisors are drinking heavily and will not be able to resist the firm, sober hands of the guards. The animosity between our monarch and the leaders of the city seems to have dissolved during the several courses of the gala dinner. Should we proceed with the plan to arrest the leaders of the city?
