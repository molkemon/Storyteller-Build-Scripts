For as long as anyone can remember, shadow puppet theater has been a popular form of entertainment in coffee houses throughout the Near East and South Asia. The comic relief provided by stock characters such as Karagöz and Hacivat coupled with the possibilities for satirical adaptation to current events have allowed the genre to outlive many empires. Recently, however, local officials in our country are reporting a sharp rise in public ridicule being directed at our government in the capital following several highly critical puppet shows. While this is likely a symptom of a greater dissatisfaction with our inability to adapt to the new ideals spreading in our realm, the quickest way to address the problem is likely to silence these puppeteers and restore order to the public houses of the area.
