The enemies of the revolution are everywhere, even in our own midst. We cannot allow the nobles, priests, and various representatives of the old order to go on as if nothing has happened. We must act swiftly and deliberately to squash any unrevolutionary behavior or suspicion of monarchism. To this end the revolutionaries have had the foresight to establish a court where the people can give their judgement on where we can find friends and foes. Who would be more suitable to identify the enemies of the revolution than the common citizens and citizenesses of our capital?
