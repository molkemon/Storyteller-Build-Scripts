The current war with our enemy has triggered a round of intrigues in our troubled court. On the one hand, our trusted advisor, a skilled member of the Eastern culture, has offered to draw information from their network of contacts in our enemy's land; a considerable network, they claim, including several contacts close to the enemy's ruler; to aid the war effort.  On the other hand, their political opponents have raised doubts about their loyalty. They argue that, in spite of their previously exemplary service, we simply cannot trust a foreigner to participate in deliberations of national importance at this time. They therefore demand that they be banished from the court.
