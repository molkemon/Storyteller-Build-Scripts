During the Swedish liberation war, the Crown had accrued enormous debts to the Hanseatic League, and Gustav Vasa needed money quickly in order to placate the powerful merchants. At the Parliament of Västerås in the summer of fifteen twenty-seven, he took the first step towards Protestantism when the Church was obliged to lend the state money in the form of silver chalices, bells, and other valuable objects. Over the coming three decades, Sweden gradually slipped into the evangelic faith despite several uprisings.
