Too many ordinary subjects have been enslaved through serfdom. In strong contrast to the ideals of the Enlightenment, the realm of the nation is built on the hard labor of peasants who are comparable to the cattle they work with. However, due to the strong influence the nobility exercises over the serfs and court, there is little to be done about the situation of these unfortunate people. The best monarch can offer, without causing a civil war, is educating the serfs' children, lending them money, instituting a system of free medical care, giving them more land for their use, and upgrading agricultural technology. And even then, these changes can be seen as an attack on the freedom of the nobility.
