The old regime was always tightly tied to the power of the clergy, and the fear of the divine is one of the ways to control the people that our radicals hate the most. Therefore, the confiscation of religious property and the requirement for all clergy to swear an oath of loyalty to a Civil Constitution have been high on the agenda for many in the National Assembly. Outside the radicals, not everyone in the assembly agrees on the best course of action, and our policy has wavered a bit over time. Atheism is not always the most popular with the masses, and especially in the countryside, our actions against local priests have been met with contempt. Perhaps it could be beneficial to scale back our radicalism a bit without abandoning the long-term goal of freeing society from religion.
