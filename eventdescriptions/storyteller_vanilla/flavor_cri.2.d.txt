With the death of Haji Giray, the young Crimean Khanate must for the first time find its feet without its dynamic founder. The ruler has managed to secure the Crimean throne, but their position is very weak. Ambitious siblings have not given up on claiming Haji's legacy for themselves, and to the east and west, hungry neighbors are eyeing the Crimean lands greedily. In order to stabilize the situation, the ruler has sent a plea for our help. They offer the servitude of themselves as well as direct ownership of a number of Black Sea ports if we are willing to protect Crimea from their enemies.
