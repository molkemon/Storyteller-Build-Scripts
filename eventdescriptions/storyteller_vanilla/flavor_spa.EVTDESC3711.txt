The Duke of Olivares led the Spanish government between sixteen twenty-one and sixteen forty-three, under King Philip the Fourth, whom he enjoyed the favor of. He was a devious and cunning leader; he involved Spain in the Thirty Years War alongside the Imperials and renewed the war with Holland. This proved disastrous and precipitated Spanish decline, with the secession of both Catalonia, which was reunited in sixteen fifty-two, and Portugal in sixteen forty. Under his government, poetry, literature, and painting prospered, and Spanish modes and customs spread across Europe.
