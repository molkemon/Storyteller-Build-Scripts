Once a prominent power and unrivaled hegemon of the Caucasus, Georgia has suffered a dramatic downfall over the centuries, losing its eminent status. The prosperous era came to an abrupt end with the onslaught of the Mongols, Turks, and Timur, paving the way for a relentless fight for survival. This dwindling power was accompanied by an economic decline, leading to a desperate nobility demanding an increasing number of privileges. In an attempt to address this issue, King Alexander the First, following a timehonored Byzantine tradition, appointed his three sons as joint rulers, establishing a unique system known as a triarchy where the sons shared sovereignty over the kingdom. This arrangement, however, was shortlived as Alexander renounced his throne in fourteen forty-two and sequestered himself in a monastery. His eldest son, Vakhtang, claimed the throne, yet with no successor to his name, the nobility and his brothers are biding their time, eagerly anticipating a chance to claim the crown for themselves. Hence, the future trajectory of the kingdom remains shrouded in uncertainty.
