The AustroLithuanian Pact of fifteen seventy had ended in fifteen seventy-six when the obscure Rudolf came into power in Austria. King Mikolaj the Second Krzysztof, also called 'the Little Orphan', was a well-versed man and a pious Catholic who had visited Jerusalem. When he had married Elizabeth of Steiermark, he had met Rudolf, who had brought his favorite crowd of astrologists, infuriating the Pious Crown Prince. From that day, he could not stand the man. However, with the ascending of Ferdinand the Second, a distant relative of Elizabeth of Steiermark, the elderly Mikolaj the Second again found a man of his values. Ferdinand also wanted a strong monarchy and to strengthen Catholicism in Europe. He immediately signed an alliance and bought the consent of Poland. During the Thirty Years War, Lithuanian Hussars would fight side by side with Imperial troops, giving Lithuania endless amounts of glory.
