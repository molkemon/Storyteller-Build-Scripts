Born in sixteen fifty-three, the son of a tax collector, AndreHercule de Fleury becomes the preceptor of the young Louis the Fifteenth on August twenty-third, seventeen fifteen. A great confidence always prevailed between master and student, and in seventeen twenty-six, at seventy-three years of age, Fleury becomes Prime Minister, and this until his death in seventeen forty-three. Faithful and honest, Fleury was above all a partisan of peace and did his best to maintain it. Under his government, France enjoyed the longest peaceful period of its history for the last three centuries.
