In fourteen fifty, Johan Gutenberg, an upperclass merchant in Mainz, applied his knowledge from his father's work as a goldsmith, and created the world's first printing press. The first thing printed was a German Poem, but Gutenberg had greater ambitions than that, as he aimed to print a Bible. This effort led him to bankruptcy, and his financiers took over his business, creating the first printed Bible in fourteen fifty-seven. The world would never be the same again. What shall we do?
