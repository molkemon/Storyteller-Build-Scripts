The monarch traveled to the capital of Sweden and invited all Swedish nobles to a great festival to celebrate his coronation. Although hesitantly, the Swedish nobility accepted the invitation and traveled to the center of the capital. Shortly after the arrival of all the nobles, however, the monarch ordered a trial against the Swedish nobility and executed the majority of the nobles for heresy. While this act was a gruesome one, it was one of necessity for the realm. The Kalmar Union would fall apart if antiunionist thoughts were to spread further in Sweden. The population of Sweden sees this event as a proof of Danish tyranny though.
