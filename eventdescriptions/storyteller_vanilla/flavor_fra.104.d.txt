Language is the very essence of a people. The change of words, traditions, and even habits stand against the trial of time only to prove the tenacity behind our citizens' resolve. But, resolve alone will not propel our civilization to greatness. Thus, the composition of a dictionary, the first of its kind, was ordered in a recent year by our head of state. The dictionary itself is written in Latin, French, and Breton, and it is the first of its kind, containing over six thousand entries, compiled by the Breton priest Jehan Lagadeuc and later printed in Treguier.
