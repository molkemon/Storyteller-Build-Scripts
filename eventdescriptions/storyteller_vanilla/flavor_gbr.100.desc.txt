Being the third child of the previous king, young Henry wasn't expected to inherit the throne. As such, he received first-rate education and some minor titles in the kingdom since very young to allow internal stability and disfavor the sharing of titles among other established families. A true intellectual, writing in English, French, and Latin, he became the image of a Renaissance Man. Also with an avid interest for music, he could play a variety of instruments and sing. Although not being formally trained for rulership, his well-developed education and his skills in foreign relations and diplomacy, together with his enormous charisma, could definitely make him into one of the best rulers England has ever seen.
