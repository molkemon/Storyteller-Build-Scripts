In the first decades of the eighteenth century, the Beys and Deys, or Janissary leaders, of Northern Africa were in a rebellious mood in the wake of the Peace of Carlowitz between the Ottoman Empire and the Holy League. In seventeen oh five, Hussein bin Ali Agha of Cretan origin staged a coup and founded the Husseinic dynasty, which ruled Tunisia until nineteen fifty-seven. Tunisia did not become truly autonomous at once, but it gained a large measure of independence.
