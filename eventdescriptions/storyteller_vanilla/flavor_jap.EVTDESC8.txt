A ship from the Netherlands has reached our shores after a long and arduous voyage via what the crew calls the Strait of Magellan. De Liefde is apparently the only ship remaining out of a group of five, and is crewed by only twenty-four sick and dying men out of an original crew of over a hundred. Its rich cargo of Western curiosities and military equipment fared far better than its crew, and should be worth quite a bit. What is more, one of the survivors, a navigator called William Adams, insists he be brought before our king.
