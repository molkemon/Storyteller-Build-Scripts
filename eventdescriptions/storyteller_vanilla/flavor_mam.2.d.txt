The monarch was known to be very concerned about urban planning, particularly in Cairo. In fourteen fifty-seven, the ruler gave the order to construct two new public baths and a large communal residence in the Bayn alQasrayn District. During that time, he decided to widen the road that originated out of it, the main thoroughfare of Cairo, known as the Qasaba. This demolished several older structures, but in the end traffic was much quicker and according to important historians, this greatly benefited the general public.
