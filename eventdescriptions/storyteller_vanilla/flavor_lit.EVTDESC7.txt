As a reaction to the introduction of the Henrykian Articles and the Pacta Conventa in Poland in fifteen seventy-three, the magnates approached the king and urged him to constitutionalize the rights of the nobility and to share power with them. If not, they said, he would have to face the consequences. At this point Mikolaj the Second Krzysztof had newly been crowned. He was young and not as averse to noble power as his father had been. In an effort to keep societal peace he accepted a compromise. There were no Henrykian articles, but the constitution was modified, implying that the nobility was the lifeblood of society with inalienable rights to hold office and legislative power. The Principle of Religious Tolerance was also confirmed.
