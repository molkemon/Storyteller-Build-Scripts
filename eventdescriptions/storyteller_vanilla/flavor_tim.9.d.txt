Abdullah Ansari was an eleventh-century Sufi Hanbali theologian and writer who operated in Herat. A man of many talents, besides being a diligent student of the Quran and a great mystic, he was also a poet. His achievements have earned him fame and renown, giving him the nickname 'Sage of Herat.' He is especially favored by the Timurid leadership, who have turned his tomb into a popular pilgrimage site in the capital. Many are inspired by his work; some even go as far as to advance their knowledge in the Hanbali Madhab.
