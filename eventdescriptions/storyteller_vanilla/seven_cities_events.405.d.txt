We would never have discovered that Baret was a woman had the natives not shouted it at the top of their lungs when the expedition entered the province. She had joined us as an assistant to our naturalist, and as his ulcerated leg had brought him much discomfort, she had carried supplies and specimens in his stead. Baret claims to be our naturalist's housekeeper, but their relationship seems deeper than that. Should we allow her to remain with us on the expedition?
