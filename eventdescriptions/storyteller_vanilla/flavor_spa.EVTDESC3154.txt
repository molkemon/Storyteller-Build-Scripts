The Fuggers were a peasant family of weavers from Augsburg during the fifteenth century who rose through trade and transactions in money to bankers of the Habsburgs and the Popes. Jakob Fugger the Rich, who lived from fifteen eleven to fifteen twenty-five, financed Charles V's wars and election to the Empire, controlled European lead, silver, and copper production, and obtained a monopoly in quicksilver. Anton Fugger, who lived from fifteen twenty-five to fifteen sixty, had trading concessions in Chile, Peru, and Moscow. However, the company declined by the end of the sixteenth century because of state bankruptcies in Spain, family conflicts, and lack of interest on the part of the heirs.
