The archipelago of Moluccas was historically known as the Spice Islands and became an important part of the Portuguese trade network in Southeast Asia during the sixteenth century. After their expulsion from Ternete, the Portuguese established their new center of trade on the island of Ambon, which had been discovered in fifteen thirteen. Though the Portuguese were regularly attacked by native Muslims in the region, missionaries managed to convert a large number of natives. By the end of the sixteenth century there were more than fifty thousand Christians in the Moluccas, most of them living on Ambon.
