When Frederik the First died in fifteen thirty-three, the bishops and most of the aristocracy were initially opposed to the election of his son Christian, duke of Holstein, as new king because he was a Lutheran. The burghers and peasants, on their part, wanted a return of the deposed Christian the Second, who was imprisoned at Sønderborg. The mayors of Copenhagen and Malmö devised a plan together with the Hansa to free Christian the Second and turn the two largest Danish cities into partners of the Hanseatic League. The rebel forces were led by Count Christoffer of Oldenburg, thus the name 'Grevefejden', the Count's Feud.
