Cromwell, Lord Protector of the English Commonwealth, promulgated the Act of Navigation in sixteen fifty-one. It was mostly directed against the Dutch carrier trade and provided that all goods shipped to and from England were to be transported on English ships, while also confirming the colonial exclusive policy. The Act directly caused the first English-Dutch war of sixteen fifty-two to sixteen fifty-four and the second in sixteen sixty-five to sixteen sixty-seven. At the Peace of Breda in sixteen sixty-seven, New Amsterdam, which is New York, was exchanged for Suriname, which is Dutch Guiana, and the Act was slightly modified. It nevertheless contributed to the enormous growth in English naval and colonial trade.
