Through the search for a male heir, she was disfavored from the line of succession in favor of her halfbrother, being sent instead to Wales, which caused her relationship with his father to deteriorate during her teenage years. During the reign of her halfbrother, being a devout Catholic, he further disfavored her from the succession in fear that she would reverse the Protestant reforms that had taken place during his reign. That is in fact what Mary wanted to do, determined to crush the Protestant faith, and together with her halfsister Elizabeth, she aimed to gather enough support to reclaim the title of queen. Despite the odds and the court opposition, she could make a great ruler, reverting the Protestant tendencies that the kingdom had adopted recently and converting it back to Catholicism.
