In fourteen eighty-one, King Christian the First died and left the throne to his son Hans. Since the Battle of Brunkeberg in fourteen seventy-one, Sweden had not been a part of the Union in anything but name. In fourteen eighty-three, the Councils of the Scandinavian kingdoms drafted the Halmstad Recess, an attempt to provide a Scandinavian constitution which asserted the supremacy of the high nobility, but Sten Sture, the chancellor of Sweden, refused to send a delegation to Halmstad. The Swedes delayed signing the document until fourteen eighty-four, and then with certain provisos.
