Ever since the thirteenth century treaty of Perth, Scotland has been obligated to pay a yearly sum to the Norwegian Kingdom in exchange for control of the Norwegian Hebrides. These payments are by now long overdue, however, and this has been the cause for tensions on and off. The other country has now suggested settling this conflict once and for all. The ruling family will marry a member of the other dynasty and the Jarldom of Orkney will be pawned and then transferred to us. Orkney is one of the highest-ranked titles in Norway and the one our own northern lands would fall under if controlled by Norway. If they give up these islands they will have surrendered the right to rule their former possessions forever.
