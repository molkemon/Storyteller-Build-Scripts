The Vladislav Hall was built between fourteen ninety-three and fifteen oh-two by Benedikt Rejt, the leading medieval architect of Bohemia. It was the largest secular space in Prague and an architectural masterpiece. It was over sixty meters long, had a vault thirteen meters high, and was used for banquets, coronations, and other important events of the court. It was even large enough to hold tournaments with knights on horseback inside it.
