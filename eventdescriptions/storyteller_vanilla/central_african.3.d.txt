Fady, a concept of social and religious taboo, restricts behavior in Malagasy society and is often difficult for outsiders to grasp. Our troops in Malagasy lands have sometimes found themselves ostracized by locals due to their inability to follow these rules, but have avoided causing serious offense. Recently, however, some of them accidentally set up a camp in an area declared to be fady to even enter without being properly prepared. Unlike previous incidents, locals have been seriously concerned about this contravention and taken the matter to a higher instance, making this a diplomatic incident.
