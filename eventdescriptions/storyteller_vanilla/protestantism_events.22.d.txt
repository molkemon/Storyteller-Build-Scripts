There are still those who long for the time when the Christian faith was unified. While they see the Reformation and the birth of Protestantism as necessary, they also lament that it could not be done without a schism. While well intended, these kinds of leanings can be very harmful to a State Church such as ours, and it is therefore a matter of some concern that it seems a number of the officials in our Church, as well as some at some of our seminaries, are entertaining such ideas.
