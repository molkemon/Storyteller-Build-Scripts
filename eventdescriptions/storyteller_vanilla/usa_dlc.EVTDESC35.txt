The society was founded by Benjamin Franklin and John Bartram in seventeen forty-three, lapsed into inactivity in seventeen forty-six, but was revived in seventeen sixty-nine and united with the American Society for Promoting Useful Knowledge under the name American Philosophical Society Held at Philadelphia for Promoting Useful Knowledge. It maintains a standing committee on American Improvements, and one of its latest projects was to study the prospects of a canal to connect the Chesapeake Bay and the Delaware River. The society is looking into establishing a price for achievement in the field of navigation, astronomy, or natural philosophy. The scientist Jean Hyacinthe Magellan has offered a grant to the President of the Society, Benjamin Franklin, in order to establish the Magellanic Premium, but the society could always do with more money.
