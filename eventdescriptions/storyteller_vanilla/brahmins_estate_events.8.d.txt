One Brahmin has proven a highly capable administrator and has gained the ear of the monarch. While Brahmins have long been entrenched into the country’s bureaucracy, it is rare for Brahmins to have an explicitly political role even in the courts of Hindu nations. The appointment of this Brahmin as Peshwa, the highest appointed ministerial office, is an appealing prospect. Though Brahmins throughout the country would take the opportunity to increase their influence, it would assure our Hindu subjects that we have their best interests at heart.
