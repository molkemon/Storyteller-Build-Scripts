The permanent Russian exploration and colonization of Siberia that started under Ivan the Terrible had regularly continued throughout the seventeenth century, with most of eastern Siberia discovered by the end of the century. The Amur basin had been explored by Khabarov in the sixteen fifties, and in sixteen eighty-nine, a Russo-Chinese border conflict was settled by the first Chinese-European agreement, the Treaty of Nerchinsk, fixing the Amur border. In seventeen twenty-seven, the Kyakhta treaty would even make provisions for a Russian trading station to be established in Peking, the capital of the new Manchu dynasty.
