The Jain community of the country have become utterly disloyal following many acts of disrespect and persecution by the state. The situation has become greatly exacerbated by an outrageous act of sacrilege. One of our religious governors marched into a Jain temple, slaughtered a cow, and desecrated the noses of all the icons inside. Unwilling to accept further persecution, the Jain community has resolved to oppose the government. Their methods of resistance are unorthodox yet effective; rather than rising up in violent rebellion they boycott institutions of state, deny loans, and refuse all participation in government. Our reliance on the Jain community has become a damning vulnerability.
