The weekly congregational prayer has been a way for Islamic rulers to show their legitimacy for centuries. The long standing Sunni dominance, where most Friday prayers have been in the name of the Sunni Caliphs and the worldly Sultans of the Sunni world, has however led to the status of the prayers being very uncertain. Most among the Shia Ulema oppose the practice of the Friday prayers, but there are some who would see its role as a way to bind together society, Ulema, and ruler, returning to the Shia faithful of this country.
