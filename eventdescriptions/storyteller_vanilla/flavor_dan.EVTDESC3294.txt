Peder Oxe was a financial genius who was recalled from exile by Frederik the Second when the Nordic Seven Years War had ruined the Danish economy. With Oxe at the helm from fifteen sixty-seven, it did not take long before the state finances had recovered. He convinced the aristocracy to contribute more and reorganized the Sound Due so that the revenues tripled. He also improved the agriculture and educational system. Until his death in fifteen seventy-seven, Peder Oxe and a few other lords were the de facto rulers of Denmark.
