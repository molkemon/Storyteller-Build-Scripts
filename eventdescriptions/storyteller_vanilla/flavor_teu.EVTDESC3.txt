The war against the Teutonic Knights has finally come to an end. The monarch sits at the peace table with the leaders of the Prussian Confederation and raises a glass to toast mutual victory and future cooperation. The treaty is signed quickly and with confidence; the rights and privileges of the Prussian townships, clergy, burghers, and knights shall be preserved, and the cause they fought for recognized as a just struggle against autocracy. Over time, the cities of the Confederation will be incorporated fully into the Crown of Poland.
