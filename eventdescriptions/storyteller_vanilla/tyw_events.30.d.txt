Conflicts like our present concerns over the religious state of the Holy Roman Empire, although they may seem decided by war, also require diplomatic tact and shrewdness to be won. We happen to have a man at our disposal, the Count of Oñate, who possesses these exact talents. Our enemies in the Empire have neighbors who may very well be interested in knowing that we would rather see that land in other hands. Merely letting them know could spark a conflict to our advantage.
