The realm has narrowly avoided a disastrous succession crisis, with the monarch finally emerging as its undisputed ruler. But the laws of succession remain unchanged and could leave the realm vulnerable to another crisis in the future. The monarch considers whether to institute legal reforms that would give men and women equal rights to the inheritance of titles, eliminating the desperate need for rulers to have male heirs or risk the dissolution of their domains. Alternatively, the realm may consider the crisis to be averted and focus instead on strengthening the traditional order that has elevated the nation to the precipice of greatness.
