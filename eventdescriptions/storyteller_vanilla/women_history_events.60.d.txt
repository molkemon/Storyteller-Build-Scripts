George Washington swept her off her feet, and the young, wealthy widow with four young children fell in love with the man who would be the first president of the United States of America despite her protestations. Martha Washington ran five plantations left to her by her first husband Daniel Parke Custis and was feared by the London merchants with whom she bargained for the best tobacco prices. The officers of the United States army remembered her fondly as she helped keep up morale among them with social evenings and plays. She was known as "Lady Washington" and was the first First Lady of the United States. Although she was opposed to George Washington agreeing to be President of the United States of America and refused to attend his inauguration, Martha Washington hosted many affairs of state once he assumed office.
