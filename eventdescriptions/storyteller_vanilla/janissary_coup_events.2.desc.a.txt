The graves of the province reach to the horizon. Not a pleasant look, but the result of brutal necessity. The monarch contemplates while their guards pull the next wagon of bodies to the never-ending graveyard. Countless Janissaries and loyalists died during the period of the Janissary Coup, and while the ruler was victorious in the end, the amount of life taken for the stability and safety of their government distresses the monarch. The House of Osman triumphed once again against another enemy of the empire. The Janissaries are now nothing but a shadow of their former self. Many of their members are now lying in the graves of the province while those who are still loyal to the monarch have been incorporated into the standard army of the realm.
