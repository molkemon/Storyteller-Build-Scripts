The aghas of the Janissaries have expanded their influence and authority over a vast territory of the state. Where once loyal pashas governed, now rule Janissaries who see no longer any reason to fight for their monarch. All the ruler can do is to accept that they no longer have any practical control over the area nor the Janissaries housing there. With the crowning of a leader of their own, the transition is complete and the loss of authority over a significant fraction of the Janissaries total.
