The Constitutional Convention is addressing problems in governing the United States, as operating under the Articles of Confederation has proven problematic. The Confederation government is inadequate for managing the various conflicts that have risen among the states, and as it is dependent on the Thirteen States for money but has no taxation powers, it lacks funds to deal with the increasing debt from the War of Independence. There was a proposal to modify the Articles of Confederation, but it was decided it would be better to create a new government than fix the existing one. The issues that will be discussed are the government form, composition, and election of the Senate, how to elect the President, the slavery issue, whether to abolish the slave trade or not, and what offenses should be impeachable, among many others.
