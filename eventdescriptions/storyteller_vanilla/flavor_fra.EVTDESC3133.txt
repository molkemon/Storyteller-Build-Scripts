After the early success with John Law's Bank and the Mississippi Trade Company, the government bought the Bank and borrowed one point five billion livres from the Trade Company to pay the mortgage on its huge national loans. These events brought a rise to French industry, and the government lacked all restraints, printing stockpiles of money without any security. When the overheated economy started to recycle with reality, the shareholders of the trade company panicked, driving both the Bank and the Trade Company into bankruptcy. As much of two years of collected tax money disappeared in the catastrophe, France thus was forced to declare bankruptcy in seventeen twenty-four.
