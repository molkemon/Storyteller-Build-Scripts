A new machine which separates cotton fibers from their seeds in a quick and easy way is currently being tested. The cotton gin is a wooden drum with small wire hooks that pulls the cotton fibers through a mesh to separate the seeds from the cotton while brushes remove the loose cotton lint to prevent jams. A patent is pending and Eli Whitney, the inventor, claims that his machine can produce up to twenty-five kilos of cleaned cotton daily, and he is trying to find more places where he can test his machine.
