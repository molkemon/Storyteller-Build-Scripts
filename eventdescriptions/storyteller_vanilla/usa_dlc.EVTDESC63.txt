In July seventeen ninety-eight, President Adams approved legislation levying federal taxes on property. This had consequences. The most obvious was the Fries Rebellion of seventeen ninety-nine, a group of armed Pennsylvanians resisted tax assessors and collectors. But it also created a great debate of the limits of power of government that never ended, but continues up to this day and beyond. However, depending on the political position of the President and the representatives in Congress, a different legislation package could have been approved. How will you choose, Mr. President?
