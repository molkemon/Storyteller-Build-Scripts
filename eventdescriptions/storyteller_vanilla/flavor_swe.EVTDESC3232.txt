After the disastrous war of sixteen seventy-four, which Sweden was more or less forced into by France, Charles the Eleventh and broad layers of the Swedish administration realized that something had to change. The King, in two surprisingly painless parliament sessions in sixteen eighty and sixteen eighty-two, assumed sole control of the country with no obligation to heed either parliament or royal council. Thus began the period of Caroline absolutism in Sweden, with the king called 'the Graycoat' for his humility and simple habits, answerable only to God.
