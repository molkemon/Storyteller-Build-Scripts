The estate church has long been one of the foremost pillars of the realm, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, judges, and diplomats for the benefit of the monarch. In return for these great services, no ecclesiastical manor, monastery, or other estate pays any taxes to the state, and the temporal authorities do not meddle in the internal affairs of the estate church. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the estate church might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
