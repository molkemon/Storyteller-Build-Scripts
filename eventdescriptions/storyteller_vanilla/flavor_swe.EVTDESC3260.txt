The devastating loss of Finland in eighteen oh nine was widely blamed on the king, and on March thirteenth, eighteen oh nine, he was deposed in a coup d'état by certain liberal officials and officers in Sweden's western army. The deposed king and his heirs were deprived of their royal titles and encouraged to leave the country. The deposed king eventually settled in Switzerland under the name of Colonel Gustafsson. While the search went on for a new crown prince, Sweden was ruled by the king's aged and childless uncle Charles the Thirteenth. The Danish prince Christian August was elected, but died suddenly in eighteen ten. The Riksdag frantically cast about for a replacement and finally decided on the French marshal Jean Baptiste Bernadotte.
