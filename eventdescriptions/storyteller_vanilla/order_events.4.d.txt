The followers of Saint Francis all embrace a life of poverty and humility, but some go further in their rejection of wealth. A group of austere Franciscans have taken a harder line, questioning the piousness of both higher church officials as well as other orders who own property. They point to the life of Christ and the apostles as free of the opulence of the present church. A Dominican Inquisitor points to these views being dangerously close to the Fraticelli, heretics prosecuted by the inquisition. The advisor suggests putting these so-called Franciscans on trial and letting their order take over their duties in the region from now on.
