In Zapotec society, there are three defined genders. male, female, and muxe. The muxe are a group of people who were determined as being male at birth, but have since come to acknowledge themselves as female. They wear traditional women’s clothing, identify with female titles, and usually abandon their birth name in favor of something more conventionally feminine. Usually, the muxe are of little concern to the monarch, however our advisor has confessed that she is a member of the muxe community and has entered court introducing herself by a new name. As a woman, she is ineligible to continue serving beside the monarch, but her unique talents make her a difficult asset to let go. Some high ranking officials at court are suggesting that the talents of this advisor are evidence that we are missing out on an endless trove of talent by forbidding women from serving at court, and the monarch would be well advised to revise the legal requirements for becoming a royal advisor.
