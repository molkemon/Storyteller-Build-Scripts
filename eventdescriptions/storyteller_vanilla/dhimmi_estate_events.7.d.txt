The quickly deteriorating relations between the state and the dhimmi community have resulted in local relations breaking down entirely in some areas. Tax collectors tell tales of convoys vanishing on the route between areas dominated by the dhimmi, together with what had been collected. In the cities, the priests and leaders of the dhimmi community agitate against us, and it has become increasingly hard to keep track of our subjects even within the walls. Even those opposed to dhimmi influence now find that they question our ability to rule.
