In June seventeen eighty-seven, the secular conflict in the United Provinces between the supporters of the Stadhouder William V and the Republicans, who were supported by France, led to a diplomatic incident when the city officials of The Hague refused entrance to the Stadhouder's wife, the niece of Frederick the Second of Prussia. Rapidly, Prussia intervened militarily to restore the Stadhouder's power and was not countered by France, which was suffering a serious financial crisis. French influence nearly disappeared and Prussia triumphed.
