In the first decades of the seventeenth century, Lithuania's king had converted to Protestantism and started to support the two branches of the Reformation. All in all, his ideal was an effective and modern monarchy, much inspired by the Swedish kings. Building up a Protestant Church in Lithuania and getting inspiration for governmental and societal reforms, the king turned to Sweden. The cooperation was of mutual benefit, as Sweden soon got a Protestant friend in otherwise heretical Eastern Europe. Slowly, the friendship brought a strong alliance, and King Ladislaus the First eagerly encouraged Gustav the Second Adolphus to promote Protestantism in Germany. He himself would hold the Catholics and Orthodox of the East in check.
