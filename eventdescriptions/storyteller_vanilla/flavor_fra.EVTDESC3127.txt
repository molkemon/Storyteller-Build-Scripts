Aiming at European hegemony and the rounding of French frontiers in the North and in the East to the Rhine, Louis the Fourteenth had established the Chambers of Reunion, courts that advanced claims to Imperial lands. These expansionist policies were at first tolerated by France's powerless neighbors. It reached its climax in sixteen eighty-one, with the annexation of the free city of Strasbourg in Alsace and the sixteen eighty-four occupation of Luxembourg. The reunion was recognized by the Empire in the sixteen eighty-four Truce of Regensburg, mostly because the Emperor was preoccupied by the Turkish menace, specifically the siege of Vienna in sixteen eighty-three, and this led to the creation of the anti-French League of Augsburg in sixteen eighty-six.
