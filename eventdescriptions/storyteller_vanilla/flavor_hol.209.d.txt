In the year currently, Jan Leeghwater has emerged as a renowned Dutch architect, mill builder, and hydraulic engineer. His groundbreaking projects are reshaping cities and mills throughout the Dutch countryside. Leeghwater, a visionary, stands at the forefront, advocating for the reclamation of the Haarlemmermeer. This perilous lake, with its encroaching threat, endangers surrounding towns as villages are gradually swallowed. Amsterdam and Leiden, too, find themselves at risk, emphasizing the urgency of Leeghwater's transformative initiatives.
