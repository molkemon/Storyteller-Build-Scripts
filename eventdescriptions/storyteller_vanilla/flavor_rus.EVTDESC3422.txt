In sixteen ninety-seven, the fifteen year old Charles the Twelfth ascended the Swedish throne. Seeing the opportunity, the Russian Czar Peter the First allied with Denmark and Poland Saxony in sixteen ninety-nine. However, with British and Dutch naval assistance, Charles the Twelfth landed on Zealand and promptly defeated the Danes, then moved to Estland where his badly outnumbered Swedes won a crushing victory over the Russians. Instead of finishing the Russians, Charles moved against August the Second of Poland Saxony and drove him out of Poland in seventeen oh four, having the pro-Swedish Stanislaw Leszczynski elected King, then subjugating Saxony in seventeen oh six. While the Russians started the construction of St. Petersburg in seventeen oh three and rebuilt their armies, they also took parts of Estland in seventeen oh four to seventeen oh six. Charles then decided to march to Moscow via Ukraine where the Cossacks under Mazeppa had again risen in revolt in seventeen oh eight. The harsh winter and Russian raids decimated his army and he was finally beaten at the battle of Poltava in seventeen oh nine. The wounded king escaped to the Ottomans. The Russians were encircled by the Ottomans on the Prut River, but Peter succeeded through bribery to obtain a safe conduct in exchange for Azov. While Charles was still exiled in Turkey, Russian, Danish, Hanoverian and even Prussian troops captured most of the Swedish provinces around the Baltic. Having emerged from Turkish internment, Charles got killed in seventeen eighteen in front of the Norwegian fortress of Frederickshald. The peace treaties that followed, Sweden lost most of her Baltic empire.
