The death of Count Wichmann, the last of the LindowRuppin line, means the Ruppiner lands have been reverted to the rule of the Brandenburgs. Though the region was considered part of wider Brandenburg, the counts were immediate subjects of the empire. Many of the Ruppiner lands had been pawned over the years, but the extinction of the last prince means that they can now directly confiscate both the city of Neuruppin and many of the lands surrounding it.
