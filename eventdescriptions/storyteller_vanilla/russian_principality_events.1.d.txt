Ever since the days of the Kievan Rus, the Russian principalities have employed a method of succession based on lateral seniority. Often this means that our dynasty will be ruled by its most powerful scion, but at times this has left other claimants wanting to bypass the Kievan order. Ever since the passing of the previous monarch, the family has been divided between those that support the heir and those that would rather see the dynamic prince of the province as the future heir. The young prince has wasted little time convincing as many of his uncles and siblings as possible to support him, and his faction has now delivered an ultimatum to our monarch.
