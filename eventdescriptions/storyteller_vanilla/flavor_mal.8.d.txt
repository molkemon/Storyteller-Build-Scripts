Sami Koli, the governor of Kaabu, is accusing the realm of neglecting the coastal trading areas of the empire. He has now threatened to sever the ties completely and has started to deal with the European trading nations directly rather than as a representative. Never very committed to the Malian state religion, Kaabu has grown to be a stronghold of the local traditional religion, and its new rulers have been quick to use this against them. With a strong religious identity and its Atlantic trade ties, Kaabu has every chance of becoming a durable state. If the realm is to stop this, it should probably act as soon as possible.
