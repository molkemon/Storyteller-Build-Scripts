As the granddaughter of King Henry the Eighth's elder sister Mary, Lady Jane Grey had a claim to the English throne. Married against her will in fifteen fifty-three to Lord Guildford Dudley, son of the Duke of Northumberland, she was used as a pawn by Northumberland, who was King Edward the Sixth's regent, in his determined effort to retain his power after the king's death. King Edward the Sixth was deadly sick, and during his last illness Northumberland had him sign a device excluding Henry the Eighth's daughters, the future queens Mary the First and Elizabeth the First, from the throne in favor of Jane. On July tenth, fifteen fifty-three, four days after Edward's death, Jane was proclaimed queen. Her reign lasted only until July nineteenth. The country supported the Catholic Mary's claim, and Northumberland's forces were dispersed by troops loyal to Mary.
