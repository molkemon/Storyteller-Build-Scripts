Rajput service to the state is founded on the unspoken understanding that the Rajputs should be allowed to practice their traditional Hindu religion and face no penalties for failing to convert to Islam. Leaders of the state church have recently become particularly insistent on adherence to Sharia, demanding that they pressure Rajput leaders to convert and penalize Hindu nobles by restricting their ranks and privileges. Though a small number of Rajput nobles have converted, the overwhelming majority are outraged by the church’s actions. They have petitioned the monarch for an immediate guarantee of religious tolerance, demanding that their traditions and customs be respected by law.
