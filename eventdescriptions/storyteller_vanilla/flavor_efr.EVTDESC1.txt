Since thirteen eighty-one, East Frisia’s independence has been questionable at best. Ocko the Second, the last official Count of East Frisia, loaned his title to Count William the Fifth of Holland. When the traitorous Ocko the Second was forcibly removed from power by our peasant militia in the Battle of the Wild Fields, the status of the ruler as Count of East Frisia went unrecognised by the Empire. The claimant, ruler of their domain, insists that the ruler is little more than a peasant leading a revolt against their liege and has no right to claim sovereignty over East Frisia. Seeking approval from the Emperor could help to strengthen our claim and keep the claimant at bay.
