The rapid spread of Protestantism has alarmed many of the ruler's staunchly Catholic councillors. To prevent it from spreading to their lands, they have drawn up a set of regulations that forbid heretical teachings and combat religious deficiencies among both the Bavarian bishops and their subjects. A commission shall be instituted to carry out religious visitations, printing shall only be allowed with the ruler's permission, and every liturgical change emerging from lands that have fallen to heresies shall be forbidden. Although the clergy would chafe under these heavy restrictions, they would likely be effective in preventing the spread of heresies.
