Your country is at war, and to the monarch, war is a family business. Your son has been appointed general and will lead the armies to victory. Your daughter has also taken a keen interest in the war, studying warfare alongside her religious education. One night, a guru retells the story of Bala Tripurasundari, the young goddess who fought alongside her brothers, who sought for combat out of curiosity. Your daughter listens, transfixed, and learns how Bala's mother again and again tried to curb her child's wish to go to war. When she later approaches you, you already know her request.
