Up until now the Spanish monarch has technically been the ruler of many states that together have been known as Spain. With the recent centralization of the government, however, and the creation of a new type of Parliament in the capital, the monarch has suggested that it is time that they abolish the power of the local Cortes of the Spanish states. While this will not be an easy undertaking, the alternative is to risk having the local Cortes stifle their new Parliamentary system with their old provincial interests.
