AlBirjandi is a brilliant astronomer, mathematician, and physicist whose answer to one of Qutb alDin alShirazi's arguments was this. 'The small or large rock will fall to the Earth along the path of a line that is perpendicular to the plane of the horizon; this is witnessed by experience. And this perpendicular is away from the tangent point of the Earth's sphere and the plane of the perceived horizon. This point moves with the motion of the Earth, and thus there will be no difference in the place of fall of the two rocks.' He has written many books, including Sharh altadhkirah, or 'A commentary on alTusi's memoir,' discussing astronomical elements.
