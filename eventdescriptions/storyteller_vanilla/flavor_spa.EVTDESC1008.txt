The Italian Wars were a series of wars between fourteen ninety-four and fifteen fifty-nine that involved most Italian city-states, the Papal State, and drew in major powers like France, Spain, and the Holy Roman Empire. The earlier War of Lombardy against the Republic of Venice had left the Duchy of Milan seeking an ally and turned to Charles VIII of France for help. Using the House of Valois' old claim to the throne of Naples as a pretext when Ferdinand I of Naples died, Charles invaded the peninsula in fourteen ninety-four. Despite tactical victories in the field, Charles became cut off from France and was forced to return home in fourteen ninety-eight. The conflict over Italy was, however, far from over, and the wars between Spain and France would go on for another sixty-one years. Another power has now claimed all our provinces in Italy.
