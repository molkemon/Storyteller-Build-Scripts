Explorer Christopher Columbus made two attempts in fourteen eighty-five and fourteen eighty-eight to convince the King of Portugal, John the Third, to finance an expedition to try and find a western route to the Orient. After being rejected the second time, Columbus turned his efforts towards the Spanish Crown. Backed up by Italian investors, Columbus finally managed to convince King Ferdinand to agree to the expedition. In August fourteen ninety-two, after having been made Admiral of the Seas and promised a share of the profits, Columbus finally set sail.
