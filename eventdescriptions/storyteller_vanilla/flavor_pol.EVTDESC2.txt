Sigismund the Third Vasa's son and successor, King Wladyslaw the Fourth Vasa, ordered the construction of Sigismund's Column, and it was erected between sixteen forty-three and sixteen forty-four. The Italianborn architect Constantino Tencalla designed the column. Placed in Warszawa, the Corinthian column is made of red marble, eight point five meters high, on which a sculpture of Sigismund the Third Vasa, two point seven five meters high, is placed. The column is adorned by four eagles and the king is dressed in armor, carrying a cross in one hand and wielding a sword in the other.
