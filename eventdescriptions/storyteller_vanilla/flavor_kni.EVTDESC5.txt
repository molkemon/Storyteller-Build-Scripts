After it was acquired by the Knights, Malta became as strong a fortress as Rhodes had been. Under Grand Master Jean Parisot de La Valette, they successfully withstood an Ottoman siege consisting of one hundred eighty ships and close to fifty thousand men against only nine thousand defenders for six months, before the invader was forced to leave. The new capital city of Valletta became a town of splendid palaces and unparalleled fortifications, growing in power and wealth owing mainly to their maritime raiding adventures against the Turks.
