The Swedish warship Vasa was built as a symbol of Sweden's military and political power and Gustavus Adolphus's authority as a warrior king. At the time she was one of the largest and most heavily armed warships in the world. Constructed between sixteen twenty-six and sixteen twenty-eight, no expense was spared in decorating and equipping her, and she featured no less than sixty-four guns. The problem was that Vasa was built top-heavy and had insufficient ballast to support that many guns. On her maiden voyage on the tenth of August sixteen twenty-eight, the ship foundered and sank outside Beckholmen after sailing less than a nautical mile.
