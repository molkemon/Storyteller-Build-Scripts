The Scottish Witchcraft Act of fifteen sixty-three made the practice of witchcraft and consulting with witches capital offenses. James the Sixth played a major part as victim and investigator in the North Berwick witch trials that began in fifteen eighty-nine and were the first major series of trials under the new act. The king published a defense of witchhunting in Daemonologie in fifteen ninety-seven but later became increasingly skeptical and took steps to limit prosecutions. Scotland tried an estimate of six thousand people for witchcraft, where seventy-five per cent of the accused were women and over one thousand five hundred executed.
