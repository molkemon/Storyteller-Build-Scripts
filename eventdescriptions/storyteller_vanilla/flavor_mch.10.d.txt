Historically, this manifesto was first published by the head of the Manchu, Nurhaci, on the thirteenth day of the fourth lunar month in the third year of Tianming. The animosity between the Manchu tribes and Ming reached a zenith, leading to allout war between the two powers. The Seven Grievances are as follows. The Ming killed the ruler's father and grandfather without reason. The Ming suppressed our people and favored rivaling clans. The Ming violated agreement of territories with the ruler. The Ming sent troops to protect our enemies against us. The Ming supported other clans' plans to break their promise to our ruler. The Ming forced the ruler to give up the lands in Chaihe, Sancha and Fuan. The Ming's official Shang Bozhi abused his power and rode roughshod over the people.
