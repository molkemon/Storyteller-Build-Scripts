Czar Alexis received a superficial education from his tutor Boris Ivanovich Morozov before acceding to the throne at the age of sixteen. Morozov, who was also Alexis' brother-in-law, initially took charge of state affairs, but in sixteen forty-eight a popular uprising in Moscow forced Alexis to exile Morozov. Alexis bowed to the rebels' demands and convened a land assembly, which in sixteen forty-nine produced a new Russian code of laws, which legally defined serfdom.
