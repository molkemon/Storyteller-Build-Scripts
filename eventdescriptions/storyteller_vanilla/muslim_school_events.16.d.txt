The Madrasah of the capital city has been gaining repute, not only as a center of religious learning, but also for its Shiekhs from other schools. Religious scholars from other parts of the world have in particular made a name for themselves at the Madrasah, and as a result, both the institution itself, the adjoining market district, and the bustling city as a whole are growing rapidly. Urban growth is never without its problems, however, and a growing Madrasah still needs to be provided for. If the ruler would set aside a larger charitable donation for the upkeep and maintenance of a greater Madrasah, it would benefit both the city and the area at large.
