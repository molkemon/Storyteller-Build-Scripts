The Turkish occupation of central Hungary remained a volatile issue, for every Hungarian resented the Habsburgs' policy of leaving the Turks unmolested while pursuing ambitious objectives in the west. Emperor Leopold sent a force against the Turks; but, although the Austrian general Raimondo Montecuccoli defeated the Turks at Szentgotthárd on August the First, sixteen sixty-four, the subsequent Peace of Vasvár recognized all the sultan's gains. After that even the highest magnates of Royal Hungary plotted to expel the Habsburgs with Turkish and French help.
