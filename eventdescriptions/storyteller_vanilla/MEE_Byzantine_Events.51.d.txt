In fourteen thirty-nine, Ioannes travelled to Florence alongside many orthodox religious scholars to discuss the matters of mending the schism with the Pope. With mounting pressure from the monarch determined to earn the support of the Pope and his subordinates, the Byzantines agreed to formally unite the two churches and to accept the superiority of the Pope. This move, while seemingly necessary, would cause the rise of the antiunionist faction in Constantinople, sparking wide unrest in the last days of the city.
