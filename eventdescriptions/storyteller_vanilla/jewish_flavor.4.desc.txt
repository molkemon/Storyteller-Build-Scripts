It seems like it was yesterday that the heir of our monarch was born, but the time has come for them to become a full-fledged adult. According to Jewish law, until now they were the responsibility of the monarch, but from now on they will be held accountable for their own actions. It has always been tradition among Jews that when boys reach thirteen and girls twelve, they become full members of the community with the responsibilities that come with it. They can already be called to read the Torah, lead and participate in the prayer quorum, and have the right to marry. But they also have the obligation of knowing the Jewish law and abiding by it. It is expected of us to host a party to celebrate such an occasion. It will also be the perfect opportunity to present the heir to the court, and for them to strengthen their bonds with it, preparing them for when they have to start ruling our nation.
