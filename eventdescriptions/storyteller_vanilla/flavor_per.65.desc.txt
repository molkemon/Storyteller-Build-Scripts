Months of bloody fighting have passed and at long last the final member of the religious body have submitted to the military might of the ruler. Of all the groups who had to endure the war, the peasant population has suffered the most. Disillusioned by the ways of the religion and terrified by the religious body's apparent lust for power and control, many of the once pious men have turned their back on the religion's dogmas. The court shares a similar attitude. Exhausted and embittered, their once secretive interests in Zoroastrianism have turned into a lively demand to leave the religious community and embrace Zoroastrianism as the state religion.
