Some generations ago, Tu'itatui, the eleventh Tu'i Tonga, erected the Ha'amonga 'a Maui or The Burden of Maui, a giant coral limestone supported by another two not smaller ones forming something similar to a gate, in honor of his two sons. Our ruler wishes to have a similar structure built to honor his children, as he or she is quite moved by the story, despite its obvious cost. What shall be done?
