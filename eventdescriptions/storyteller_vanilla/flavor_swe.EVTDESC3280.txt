Per Brahe the Elder was a Swedish statesman and nephew of King Gustavus Vasa. Brahe was highly regarded by the King and distinguished himself during the Dacke feud. He acted as Governor of Stockholm Castle from fifteen forty and was awarded a place in the Privy Council in fifteen forty-four. At the coronation of King Eric the Fourteenth in fifteen sixty-one Brahe was made Count of Visingsborg, and the accession of King John the Third saw him appointed the Lord High Justiciar of Sweden as well as Governor of Norrland.
