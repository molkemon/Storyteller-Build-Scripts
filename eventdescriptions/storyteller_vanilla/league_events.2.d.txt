The reunion policy of Louis the Fourteenth had been forced upon by the Empire in sixteen eighty-four by the Truce of Regensburg, mostly because the Emperor was preoccupied by the Turkish menace, specifically the siege of Vienna in sixteen eighty-three. The continued French claims and expansion to the detriment of the Habsburg possessions and the German states of the Empire led to the creation of the anti-French League of Augsburg in sixteen eighty-six.
