After having expelled the Jews on August third, fourteen ninety-two, the Catholic sovereigns of Spain considered it was time to look after the Moorish population of AlAndalus, the southern part of the peninsula from the old Muslim kingdoms of Valencia and, above all, Grenada. With the renewal of the Inquisition led by Tomás de Torquemada, Spain became a country of religious fanatics where agricultural work and crafts were considered labors unworthy of Christians. Forced conversions followed as well as enslavement, torture, and executions where many were burned at the stake. In the wake of these persecutions, many thousands left Spain.
