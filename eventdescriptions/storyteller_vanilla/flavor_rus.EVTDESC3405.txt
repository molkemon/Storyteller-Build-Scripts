Ivan the Fourth of Russia had extended the borders of his homeland far to the South and the East, but had met with strong resistance in the West, in particular in Livonia. Wars with Poland, Lithuania, and even Sweden would last till fifteen eighty-three. Poland had annexed Lithuania in fifteen sixty-nine and, after many defeats at the hands of the Polish army, Ivan had to sign a peace treaty in fifteen eighty-two where he lost all his previous western territorial gains. Enmity between the two countries would remain high, culminating with the Polish occupation of Moscow in sixteen ten during the Time of Troubles.
