The fall of the City of Victory, Vijayanagar, at the end of a decisive confrontation in which the victorious sultans prevailed, has sent a shockwave through the Deccan. Plundering the city is said to have taken several days, and while it remains one of the larger settlements in the region, it seems unlikely that it will ever again be the metropolis it once was. While the Vijayanagari state still remains in power over parts of its old domain, many of its subordinate captains and vassals are now looking elsewhere for protection, for a more worthy cause to serve. This seems to be the end of the empire the Sangamas once founded.
