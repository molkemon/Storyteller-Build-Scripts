In seventeen seventeen the Mississippi Trade Company was founded. It had a royal monopoly on the utilization of the large colonial areas along the Mississippi River in America. In seventeen nineteen the government borrowed one point five billion livres from the company. The company was more of a financial institution than a company of trade and industry, and the price of shares hit the roof. In seventeen twenty the French market turned into a decline, and the fact that the shares of the company were overvalued was evident even to the man on the street. Soon panic broke out, and the company was declared bankrupt. John Law was forced to flee from France.
