A woman from the ruler's tribe approaches the monarch while the monarch is fishing, as the tribe usually does to feed their people. The monarch raises their head towards her, their eyes meet, and the monarch knows she has seen something unusual, but good, nevertheless. With piety in her gaze, she whispers how she has seen the biggest salmon ever, a true incarnation of the spirit of the river they so deeply worship. The monarch follows her to the spot where she saw it, and after a few breaths, the monarch finally sees it. It is huge indeed. The monarch prepares their tools to capture it.
