While various ways to increase the standing army the country could support have been undertaken in the past, they have not been sufficient for their needs. Now that their bureaucracy is advanced enough to support a more rigorous system, the ruler has drawn up a plan to reshape the old provincial militias and mercenaries entirely. The country will be divided into cantons, and each residing male will be expected to enroll in the army. Civilian involvement should remain limited; instead, the ruler intends for the military administrators of each regiment to be responsible for recruitment within a canton.
