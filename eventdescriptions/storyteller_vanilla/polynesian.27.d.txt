We are one with nature, and we must live in harmony with it. It is the duty of all Maori to protect everything under the sky as the gods command. Polluting a river, chopping down a whole forest, or plundering a place from its resources goes against this guardianship. Our priests usually decide to make some areas sacred in order to keep them protected and away from excessive use. These decisions, while undoubtedly positive in the long term, can be a problem in the short one.
