Due to a combination of lacking monastic discipline and a faltering economy, many of the great monasteries of the realm have seen their membership dwindling over the last few years. A concerned Bhikkhu has approached the ruler with a warning that unless something is done soon, the dire situation may lead to the last few monks abandoning their vows or seeking to join monasteries elsewhere, leaving the country impoverished both spiritually and economically. There are, however, many local landowners more than ready to infringe on the land an empty monastery would leave behind. As the ruler is meant to protect monastic life in the domain, it will be clear to many observers that the responsibility for whatever happens will rest on their shoulders.
