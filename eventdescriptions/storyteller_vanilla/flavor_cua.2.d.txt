The meeting was an ambush. It began with the chiefs of our nation being pampered and plied with alcohol, and then twelve hundred men attacked. The men were slaughtered, the women and children enslaved. The monarch was one of the few who managed to escape. Four of our people, Senacua Senaque, a medicine man, VaimacaPiru Sira, a warrior, and a young couple, Laureano Tacuave Martinez and Maria Micaëla Guyunusa, were captured, and rumor has it that they have been sent to Paris to be exhibited to the public.
