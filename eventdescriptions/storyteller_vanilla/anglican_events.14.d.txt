As the Protestant Reformation radically shifts the religious landscape of Europe, the country must take a firm stance on its faith. This has of course provoked much discussion in court, with some favoring the Protestant cause and others defending the traditional religion. The monarch, however, has a novel idea for the creation of a different sort of Church. A Church with the monarch as its Supreme Governor, a happy union of Church and state. Neither Protestant, Catholic, nor Reformed, it would take a flexible approach to doctrine as fits the circumstance. Such a Church would have its advantages. No longer subject to any higher ecclesiastical authority, the monarch could marry and divorce at will, determine the nation’s theological direction, and have the assurance that they are appointed to rule directly by God.
