The Revolution has arrived. Large crowds gathered in the capital to demand a wide range of concessions, chief among them a more democratic constitution. Taken aback by the scale of the protests, the monarch tried to defuse the situation by offering significant concessions. However, the leader of the revolutionaries ensured that they would not back down easily. "What proof have we," he asked, "that the despot will keep their word?" At this, the monarch ordered the troops to disperse the crowds; however, the troops refused to fire on the protestors, and instead the ruler was driven out. A Revolutionary Republic has been declared under the watchful stewardship of the new ruler, who promises to abolish serfdom, establish equality under the law and redistribute the nobility's land between the state and the peasantry, leading the people of the realm to a future guided by the ideals of liberty and the brotherhood of man.
