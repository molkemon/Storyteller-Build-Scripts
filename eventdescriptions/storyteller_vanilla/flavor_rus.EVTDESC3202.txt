Malyuta Skuratov was one of the most prominent leaders during the Oprichnina, when Ivan the Terrible and his secret police carried out thousands of executions and land confiscations. Skuratov had made a name for himself in fifteen sixty-nine when he participated in the trial and execution of Ivan's only cousin, Vladimir of Staritsa, a possible pretender to the throne. Later that same year he strangled the Metropolitan of Moscow, Philip the Second, for criticizing the trials, and in fifteen seventy he was responsible for killing thousands of citizens during a punitive expedition to Novgorod. Skuratov was killed in fifteen seventy-three at the siege of Weissenstein during the Livonian War.
