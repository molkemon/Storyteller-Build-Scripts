Charles the Second was secretly Catholic, and his brother James the Second was openly so. The Decree of Indulgence, issued in sixteen seventy-two, was aimed at relieving the situation for dissenters and Catholics. In addition to personal reasons, any king who supported Catholicism could count on the support from France. In fact, Charles the Second received his reward in the form of a large sum of money in a treaty closed behind the back of Parliament. Around the same time, Parliament issued the Bill of Test, which again excluded dissenting religious groups, including Catholics, from public offices and allowed persecution. Parliament subsequently raised the issue of excluding the openly Catholic James the Second from succession rights.
