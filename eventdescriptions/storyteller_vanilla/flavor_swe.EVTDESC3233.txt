Born in fifteen eighty-three, Axel Oxenstierna started his civil career for the Swedish State in sixteen oh-two, and became Chancellor between sixteen twelve and sixteen fifty-four. He proved to be the most able administrator Sweden has ever had. Like Charles the Ninth, Oxenstierna was impressed by Calvinism, and harbored notions of adopting at least elements of that faith. As Gustavus Adolphus's righthand man, he fought against the King's plans to intervene in the Thirty Years War. However, after the death of the King at Lützen in sixteen thirty-two, he assumed the leadership of the Protestant cause and managed to convince the French to actively participate in the conflict. He always followed domestic policies closely, but lost most of his influence following the abdication of Queen Christina, which he opposed. He died in sixteen fifty-four, shortly after the coronation of King Charles the Tenth Gustavus.
