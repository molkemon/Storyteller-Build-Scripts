The Portuguese traders on our western coast have become quite a nuisance lately. While the horses they bring for our armies and the exotic goods they bring from far away countries are of great use to us, it seems they cannot help but meddle in the local politics. It is clear that several chiefs in contact with the Portuguese have been encouraged to extend their personal authority and to slowly attempt to cut the ties with our central administration. In order to deal with this, we could either strike at the chiefs themselves or attempt to limit the influence of the Portuguese.
