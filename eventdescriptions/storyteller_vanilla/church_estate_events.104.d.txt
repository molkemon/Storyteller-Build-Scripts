The state church has long been one of the foremost pillars of the realm, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, judges, and diplomats for the benefit of the monarch. In return for these great services, many donations have been made over the years, both by the state and by others, to the state church. These gifts have over the centuries made the state church quite rich and influential. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the state church might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
