Years have passed and the monarch has proven to be a fair and just ruler. The clergy no longer fear a sudden Catholic coup, and their hatred of Papism has been tempered by the monarch's understanding of the religion. The monarch, however, is troubled. As the years have gone by, the ruler has come to realize that their personal faith lies much closer to the Catholics that they have done so much to protect, and not with the dogmatic State Church of the realm.
