Few now have any faith in the Ming state. Even its generals wonder aloud if it might be prudent to defect to the invading realm. The man tasked with defending the Shanghai Pass is Wu Sangui, a talented and ambitious commander who has faced armies in battle before with some success. Recent events in the capital and beyond have shaken his loyalty to the Ming monarch, and now he seeks an unlikely alliance with his former enemies. Promised titles and offices under the new order, Wu Sangui has agreed to defect to the country. We can march safely through the Shanghai Pass and onwards toward a weakened Beijing.
