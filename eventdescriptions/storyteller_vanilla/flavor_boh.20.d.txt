Jan Amos Komenský, a Utraquist bishop and an advocate of universal education, is known as the Teacher of Nations. His innovative educational methods and his efforts to reform education have had a profound impact on our society. With the increasing recognition of his educational theories, we find ourselves presented with an opportunity to harness his expertise for the advancement of our kingdom. Should we extend an advisory role to him, or should we leverage his influence to enhance our educational system?
