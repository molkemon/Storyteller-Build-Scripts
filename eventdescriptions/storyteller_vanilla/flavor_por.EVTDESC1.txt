In fourteen ninety-two, Christopher Columbus undertook his famous journey for Castile and sailed west across the Atlantic in search of a western route to the Orient. Seven years earlier in fourteen eighty-five, Christopher Columbus had presented the same plan to the King of Portugal, John the Second, who after submitting the proposal to his experts had rejected it. Columbus had made yet another attempt to convince the King in fourteen eighty-eight, but as the Portuguese explorer Bartholomeu Dias around this time had successfully rounded the Cape of Good Hope, an eastern sea route to Asia seemed more likely, and the King once again rejected Columbus's proposal.
