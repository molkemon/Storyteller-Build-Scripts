The Reformation is a chaotic and rapidly evolving movement. Doctrine is determined as much by political pragmatism as by theological reasoning. It is for this reason that we, as Defenders of the Reformation, should try to achieve some semblance of order and unity among reformers. To that end, we have convened the Council of a specific province, where a number of dignitaries and theologians from nations such as one member, another member, and a third member have gathered to discuss matters of faith and to make political alliances.
