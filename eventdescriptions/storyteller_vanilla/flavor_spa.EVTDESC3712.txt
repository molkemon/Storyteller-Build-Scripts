Born in sixteen sixty-four, the son of a gardener in Fiorenzuela, Giulio Alberoni owned his political career and success to the Duke of Vendôme, the French general whom he served in Italy, France, and Spain. Agent of the Duke of Parma, he facilitated the wedding of Philip the Fifth of Spain with Elizabeth Farnese and became a Cardinal in seventeen seventeen. His bellicose policy in Spain had him expelled in seventeen nineteen. He was cunning and sly, but Spain did not have the resources needed to fulfill his ambitious plan. The Age of Spanish Supremacy was long gone.
