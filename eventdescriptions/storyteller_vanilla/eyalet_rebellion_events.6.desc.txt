Ever since the fall of the Mamluk Sultanate and the establishment of a new domain, the situation in Egypt has been precarious for the nation. Many governmental systems of the old Mamluk Sultanate have been taken over and used further, which in turn led to an internal competition between the ruler of that domain and the nation's monarch for many years. Now these old tensions are getting heated anew as the ruler demands more autonomy for them and their domain.
