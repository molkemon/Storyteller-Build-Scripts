Kempe Gowda, one of the local rulers of the Vijayanagara Empire, is building a Nandi temple in the Vijayanagara architectural style. A large granite Nandi monolith will be placed on a plinth in the temple shrine, and it will only consist of the shrine fronted by a porch. It will also house one of the largest Nandi statues in the world. The height of the statue is approximately four point six meters and it is approximately six point one meters long. The sacred bull known as Nandi, the close devotee and attendant of Shiva, will be worshiped at the temple. The humble Kempe Gowda has asked for a small donation to help fund the Nandi temple.
