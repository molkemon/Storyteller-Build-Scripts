Our efforts to integrate our subjects have now finally started to pay off. Our subjects have begun to accept the ideals of Confucianism, and our local officials have also integrated elements of the local religion and culture into our wider Confucian corpus of ideals. As local bureaucrats enter our administration, the pagan customs have to some extent begun to filter upwards. Even among prominent officials in the capital, a small altar can sometimes be found to a pantheon. Paganism has become one of the many religions of our Confucian government.
