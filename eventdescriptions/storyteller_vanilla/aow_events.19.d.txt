Under the watchful eye of the inquisitor, trials for sorcery and heresy have proceeded under relatively controlled circumstances. The inquisitor would not be at our court if we did not trust them, after all. A recent event has complicated this, however, as the inquisitor has challenged our trust of another. A statesman, trader, spymaster, or diplomat, who has aided the realm greatly in foreign relations, has been accused of witchcraft, a testament to no one being truly unquestionable. This official claims that the inquisitor is supporting these accusations out of pure jealousy. Political drama may be behind all this, but then again, we have trusted our inquisitor so far.
