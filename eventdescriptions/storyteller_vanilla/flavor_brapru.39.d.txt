Originally one of Martin Luther's theological closer allies, Johannes Agricola has had a bitter falling out with the great reformer. Agricola's stance on whether Mosaic Law applies to Christians is directly opposite of Luther's, and the reformer is now in search of a new base from which to work and propagate. His insistence on the Gospels alone as the source for Christian conduct, as well as having been seen to be favorable to a settlement with the Catholics, has also made Agricola ill-regarded by the more radical reformers. His theological views would make him an excellent superintendent for the newly formed Brandenburg church, however, and a good court preacher of the ruling dynasty's.
