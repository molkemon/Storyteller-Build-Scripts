Spain has chosen to expel the Moors that still resided within its borders. Large numbers of Andalusian Moorish refugees have therefore been arriving in its ports, asking to be let into the country. While it pains them that these people had to leave their homes, this may well be a blessing for the kingdom. Most of the refugees are city dwellers, craftsmen, scholars, artists, and administrators, precisely the kind of people the kingdom's own cities have a great shortage of after the great crisis of the Maghreb.
