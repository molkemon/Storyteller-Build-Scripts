The time has come for another age set of boys to undergo the rite of 'Emuratare', finally becoming men and warriors. With adulthood comes the responsibility of protecting their people and territory. For ten years they will reside together in the 'Emanyatta', a warrior village, during which they live to serve the nation. With this influx to the country's army we also have the opportunity of shaping a new generation of warriors. What should the focus of their education be?
