The estate nobles complain that an increasing number of serfs are deserting the safe homes provided to them to seek their fortune among the Cossacks of the Steppes. They feel we are to blame as we have allowed the Cossacks to grow too powerful, which is inspiring the serfs to abandon their rightful masters. Representatives of the major families now demand that we limit the power of the Cossacks so that they can expand their large estates in the regions bordering the steppe. This way, they argue, the wild fields can be tamed and the laws of the realm upheld.
