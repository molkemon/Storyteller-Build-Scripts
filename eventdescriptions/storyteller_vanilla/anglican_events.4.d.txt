The realm's parliament is often called upon to debate matters of religion. Typically, the monarch will propose a new wave of reforms for the Anglican Church and the parliament will take a stance along established factional lines; the members in the commons are more likely to support radical or even Puritan measures, while the lords temporal are more likely to favour a more conservative approach. Rarely does the monarch get their way precisely, and when they do, coercive measures are often involved.
