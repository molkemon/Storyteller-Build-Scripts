The Golden Roof was a three-story balcony that was added to the Neuhof building in central Innsbruck by Emperor Maximilian of Habsburg at the turn of the sixteenth century. The roof of the balcony was decorated with two thousand six hundred fifty-seven firegilded copper tiles and was built in honor of Maximilian's second marriage to Bianca Sforza of Milan, but also to symbolize his wealth and power.
