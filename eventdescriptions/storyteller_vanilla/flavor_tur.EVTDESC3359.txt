Since the capture of Constantinople by the Turks on May twenty-ninth, fourteen fifty-three, Venice had seen its commercial positions in the Black Sea deteriorate. Numerous wars had been fought between fourteen fifty-six and fourteen ninety, pitching the Republic against the Ottomans, with the consequence of Venice losing more pieces of her commercial empire inherited from the Fourth Crusade in twelve oh four in the Aegean Sea and in Greece, including Athens in fourteen sixty and Negroponte in fourteen seventy. A war erupted again in fourteen ninety-nine, Venice losing fortresses in Morea such as Modon, Coron, and Navarrin.
