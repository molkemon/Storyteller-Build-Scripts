The division between the Pope and the Emperor had started in the late eleventh century, and technically ended in one thousand one hundred twenty-two, but the division between Guelphs and Ghibellines persisted in Italy for centuries afterward, being a constant factor in dividing the city-states internally and keeping Italy weak. The Guelphs were the supporters of the Pope, while the Ghibellines supported the Emperor. In the fifteenth century, the Guelphs had switched allegiance to the French instead, as they started to take an interest in Italy.
