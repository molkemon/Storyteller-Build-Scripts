The Sengoku period in Japan was marked by civil war, social upheaval, and political intrigue. Wife of Kyogoku Takatsugu and with family ties to both the Toyotomi clan and the Tokugawa clan, Ohatsu served as a conduit between the rivals and acted as a liaison. She took part in many of the political intrigues during this period and is regarded as a shrewd negotiator with great diplomatic skills.
