The people of the province are divided and worship various local Huaca Gods. It has therefore been decided that a great temple of Inti should be constructed here as a natural center for the spiritual and administrative life of the region. The leader of the construction has presented the ruler with two different plans for the temple. The first plan is to construct the temple at a Tincuy, the sacred confluence of two streams, while the other plan calls for making use of an old Huaca shrine as the base of the new temple.
