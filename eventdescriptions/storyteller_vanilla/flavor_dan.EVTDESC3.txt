King John the First and Queen Christina founded the St. Clare's Monastery in Copenhagen in fourteen ninety-seven with a gift of the former royal vegetable gardens in an area known as Rosengård. The Queen made a donation of forty Rhenish Guilders for the construction of the monastery in fourteen ninety-eight. Seven years later, on August eleventh, fifteen oh-five, the monastery was completed and consecrated. The monastery consisted of a dormitory, refectory, church, infirmary, cellars, and places for lay sisters and a priest to stay.
