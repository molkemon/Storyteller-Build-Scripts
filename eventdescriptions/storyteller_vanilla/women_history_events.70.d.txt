The rejuvenation of the country's court life led to her increased influence and political power, and she began to rebuild the land. Together with John Acton, commander-in-chief of the navy and the army, she revamped the realm's navy by commissioning one hundred fifty ships and opening four marine colleges. Trade pacts with Russia and Genoa augmented the merchant navy, the ban on Freemasonry was revoked, and she patronized many artists such as Angelica Kauffman and Jacob Philipp Hackert. The relations between the country and the Papal states had deteriorated, but after a meeting with the Pope, where he presented Maria Carolina with a Golden Rose and ceded to the King the right of appointing bishops to vacant sees, it might not be too late to salvage the relationship.
