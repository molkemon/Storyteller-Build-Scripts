The Polish alchemist, philosopher, and medical doctor Michal Sedziwój was a pioneer of chemistry. He developed ways of purification and creation of various acids, metals, and other chemical compounds. King Sigismund the Third Vasa was an alchemy enthusiast and conducted experiments with Sedziwój. Sedziwój designed mines and metal foundries and wrote many books, the most famous being 'A New Light of Alchemy'. The book was written in alchemical language, which was understandable only by other alchemists. Sedziwój discovered oxygen one hundred and seventy years before similar discoveries by Scheele and Priestley, calling it the 'food of life'.
