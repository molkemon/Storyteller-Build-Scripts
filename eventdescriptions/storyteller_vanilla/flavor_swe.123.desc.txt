Varangians, a Greek term for the Vikings of Scandinavia, was given by the Eastern Romans when our ancestors found their way to Constantinople in the late eighth century. They came from a route simply called "The Route from the Varangians to the Greeks" and went alongside the Dnieper river. Many of the key cities of this route are either now in our possession or are territories of our allies, and this historical trade route is once again flowing.
