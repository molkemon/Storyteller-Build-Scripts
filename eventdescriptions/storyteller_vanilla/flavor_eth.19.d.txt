One of the greatest strengths of the Ethiopian church is the great number of successful monasteries that dot the landscape of the state. The monastic orders have contributed to the success of the state by founding new monasteries in newly conquered areas and by spreading the word of the true faith there. The monasteries of Ethiopia are also the source of the education of the Ethiopian clergy, its nobility, and its administrators, and as such have contributed greatly to the revitalization of the Ethiopian church in the years past. The other side of this coin, however, is that the monasteries and the orders that run them have grown both rich and autonomous.
