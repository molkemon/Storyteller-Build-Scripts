The Portuguese and the Ottoman had a long history of rivalry ever since the Ottoman Empire blocked the trade from the Silk Road to Europe. The first real fight, however, started when the Ottoman assisted the Sultanate of Gujarat in the siege of Diu in fifteen thirty-eight. Ever since, these two great powers were at war with each other on several occasions, with the Ethiopian Adal war being the most prominent proxy war. After the small victory of Ethiopia, the Portuguese continued their wins against the Ottoman. In the year fifteen fifty-nine, Suleiman the First had to recognize that decisive victories against the Portuguese were a futile project. Because of that, the militaristic activities of these two powers have been greatly reduced in the Horn of Africa.
