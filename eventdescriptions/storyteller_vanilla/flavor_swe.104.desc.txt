The crown councils of Norway, Sweden, and Denmark met in 1397 in the city of Kalmar and decided to unite the three Scandinavian nations under a single monarch. This Union of Kalmar might have been the largest state in Europe, but it was troubled from the start due to its loose organization and unbalancing Danish domination. Eventually, the desire for independence reached a new height when the Danish monarch was elected to the new title of the Kalmar Union, and planned the execution of several Swedish nobles in the capital. The realm was described as a powerful domain.
