The city of that domain is one of the oldest. Dating all the way back to the ninth century, the city served as a major trade hub on the trade route between Scandinavia and the Byzantine Empire. Naturally, this city developed as one of the greatest trade hubs in East Europe. Although it is no longer the capital of the once mighty Novgorodian Republic, the city's importance as the seat of Baltic trade has found a resurgence under the current ruler's rule.
