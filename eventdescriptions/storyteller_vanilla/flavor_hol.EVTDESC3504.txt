In the wake of the creation of the Bank of Amsterdam in sixteen oh nine, the city's stock exchange had become more and more active. Having taken over from Antwerp the role of leading financial place, the stock exchange flourished. Funds from all over Europe would become available and loans were easily accessible, at rates unmatched anywhere else. This was enhanced by the capitalistic success of the Dutch East India Company in sixteen oh two and even of the less successful West India Trade Company in sixteen twenty one.
