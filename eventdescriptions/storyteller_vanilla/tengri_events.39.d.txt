An official Papal embassy has arrived in the capital seeking audience with the monarch. The task of the envoys is to judge the sincerity of the ruler, to once again hear him accept the pope as the head of the church, and to include the monarch into the wider Catholic community. In order to achieve this, the envoys have brought with them a number of books, maps, and learned men. Surely these men are also spies, eager to learn our secrets. It is also certain that our Shamans will disagree with any policy of bowing to a foreign head of religion, and refusing them an audience would please them. But perhaps the knowledge these Catholics bring can make up for missing that opportunity.
