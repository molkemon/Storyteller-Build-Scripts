The death of the last Duke of Kurland, Frederick William, in seventeen eleven, left no heirs to the Duchy except the weak and ill Ferdinand, and a lot of contenders in Russia and Poland. In seventeen twenty-six, the Polish Sejm managed to impose Maurice of Saxony, the bastard son of the current Polish King, as Duke, but only to change his mind and ask for his withdrawal. At the same time, Russia was pushing for its own candidate, the Duke of HolsteinGottorp, son-in-law of the Czarina Catherine the First. After much bribing, negotiation, and behind-the-scenes diplomacy, Maurice was summoned to either leave or suffer war. It started in seventeen twenty-seven, with Russia firmly committed and sending eight thousand troops to conquer the Duchy within eight days. The war did not last long enough to embrace all of Europe, and Maurice fled to France where he then had a brilliant military career.
