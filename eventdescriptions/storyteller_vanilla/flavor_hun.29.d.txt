It is now decades since Luther nailed his ninety-five theses to the church door in Wittenberg, but the ideas of the reformation are still spreading across the continent. Having trained in Wittenberg as a student under Luther, Matthias Dévay is convinced that it is his task to spread the word of the reformation in the realm of the country. It is little surprise that this is opposed by some members of our religious establishment. Tamás Szalaházy, the bishop of Erlau, has moved to limit Protestant influence and imprison this would-be Luther of Hungary.
