Ambitions to improve the nation's navy have recently come to a halt. The shipbuilders don't lack ideas or plans, but supplies. Some of them cannot help but notice, they claim, that a neighbor has an abundance of materials that would be useful in equipping a modern navy. They try not to make their fleet dependent on foreign support, but if these policies stand in the way of progress, perhaps they could import just enough to test the new shipbuilding techniques.
