The villagers in that province have caught a witch redhanded as they claim she attempted to bake a group of children alive in her oven. The town is in outrage and it seems she is likely to be burnt at the stake. Friends of “evidence” and “logic” in our court claim that the proof for her acts of witchery are flimsy and weak at best, and that burning an innocent woman alive is an abomination, according to both the laws of our land and the holy texts. But since when have facts and logic played any part in the administration of our state?
