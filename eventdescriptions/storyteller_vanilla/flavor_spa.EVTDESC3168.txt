In sixteen forty, Portugal had been under the domination of Spanish kings for sixty years, since the fall of the Aviz dynasty in fifteen seventy-eight. However, in sixteen forty, the Portuguese rose up in revolt and started a long liberation war against Spain. A long twenty-four year struggle, along with Spanish defeat in the Thirty Years War in sixteen forty-eight, and peace with France in sixteen fifty-nine would lead to a renewed independence for Portugal, under the new Braganza dynasty, which would turn to Stuart England as an ally and protector through the marriage of Catherine of Braganza with Charles the Second and the transfer of Bombay to England as a dowry.
