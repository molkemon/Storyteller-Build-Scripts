Claudio Monteverdi's early Baroque opera L'Orfeo is based on the Greek legend of Orpheus, the musician, poet, and prophet who descended to Hades to bring his dead bride Eurydice back. The opera was written in sixteen oh seven for a court performance during the annual Carnival at Mantua, and its score was published in sixteen oh nine and sixteen fifteen by Monteverdi. Forty-one different instruments, with distinct groups of instruments used to depict particular scenes and characters, were listed in Monteverdi's published score.
