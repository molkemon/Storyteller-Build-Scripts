For centuries Scandinavia was split into the three kingdoms of Denmark, Sweden, and Norway. There have always been attempts to unify these kingdoms in several personal unions in the past, the Kalmar Union being the latest one of them. And the one which finally brings an end to the division of the North. Sweden and Norway have been integrated into the primary Crown of the realm, and the whole Scandinavian region is now going through a new Golden Era under the realm's rulership.
