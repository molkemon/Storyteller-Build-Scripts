In his early twenties, novelist and poet Miguel de Cervantes served as a soldier in the Spanish navy. He was captured by Algerian corsairs in fifteen seventy-five and kept hostage for five years, until ransomed by his parents and a Catholic religious order. In fifteen eighty-five, he published his first novel, La Galetea, but continued to support himself as a purveyor and tax collector. His break as an author came in sixteen oh-five with the publication of 'The Ingenious Gentleman Don Quixote of La Mancha', which became an immediate success and was later followed by a second part in sixteen fifteen.
