As Christendom grows ever more divided, the trickle of religious refugees has grown into a steady stream. Religious conflicts in major Catholic states have caused many Protestants to leave their old home countries. As a Protestant country with its own Church, they would be able to offer them protection, and they could also make good use of their skills and assets. Protestantism is, however, far from a unified faith, and it is not very likely that all those who come to them will find the Church of their realm to match their own beliefs fully. While it would be admirable to receive all Protestant immigrants, it might be more prudent to hand pick those that possess skills they could make good use of.
