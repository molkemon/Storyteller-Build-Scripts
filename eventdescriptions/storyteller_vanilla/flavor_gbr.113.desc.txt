Tensions between the governing body and the monarch have escalated to a point of no return. What started as a mere dispute over support towards regional interests, taxation, and military expenditure has turned into a conflict over the role of the governing body itself in the government. There are questions about the necessity of the governing body on one side, and the necessity of the monarch on the other. The monarch is tired of having to keep content a group of people that keep interfering in their legitimate and undeniable right to rule and uses every opportunity to undermine their authority. On the other hand, regional powerful individuals of the governing body have proclaimed that they will no longer obey any central and tyrannical authority, and have raised troops in their territories to defend their own authority by force. Great parts of the country have declared themselves as no longer subjected to the rule and are in open rebellion against them.
