The Muisca confederation has no single king or absolute central authority. Instead, the state is made up of a number of small chiefdoms, most falling either under the Zipa of Bacatá or the Zaque of Hunza. While the confederation allows them to resolve disputes and deal with outside threats together, as long as both the Zipa and the Zaque approve, for most matters the Muiscan chiefs remain quite autonomous. As the two most influential rulers in the confederation, there is quite a bit of rivalry between the Zipas and Zaques, sometimes even resulting in warfare, either by proxy or directly.
