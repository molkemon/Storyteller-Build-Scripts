In seventeen thirty-one, the Archbishop of Salzburg purged his lands of Protestants. The Protestants had grown in number over the years, and the Archbishop had started to feel his powers threatened by their existence. More than twenty-six thousand ethnic German Protestants fled for their lives. Seventeen thousand of them were welcomed in Prussia in seventeen thirty-two, and most of them settled in East Prussia. Several Protestant countries started nationwide collections to help the fugitives.
