Our support for the ruler of that country, our candidate for the noble throne, has yielded us many diplomatic advantages since they was elected. Recently, however, it has been suggested that the ruling family's claims on that country reach beyond this single individual and that our monarch should in fact be considered the ruler of both our realm and that country. The ruler considers them sovereign, of course, but our monarch outranks them in the dynasty. Surely a ruler's first loyalty must be to family, must it not?
