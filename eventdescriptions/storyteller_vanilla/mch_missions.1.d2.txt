Wu Sangui, a former general of the Ming dynasty, diligently guards the Shanghai Pass despite the collapse of the state. Increasingly desperate envoys from both the ruler and the monarch of another realm have tried to convince Wu and his army to take a side in the war. The monarch has resorted to a cowardly act of coercion, threatening the life of Wu Sangui’s father if he does not submit to that realm's rule. Rather than submitting to the pretenders, Wu has become enraged and pledged his loyalty to his own country in defiance. The gates of the Shanghai Pass are open to them. The order has been given to march to Beijing.
