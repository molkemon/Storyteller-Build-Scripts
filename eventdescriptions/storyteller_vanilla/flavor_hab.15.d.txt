Rather than relying on single individuals, states are now increasingly relying on institutions to carry out their espionage. All over Europe, Black Chambers, tasked with the procurement, opening, and decrypting of foreign as well as domestic correspondence, have quietly been created. The one in Vienna, the Geheime Kabinettskanzlei, is, however, something out of the ordinary. By those in the know, the Viennese Black Chamber is famed for the speed with which it regularly opens, copies, and then decrypts and translates all mail it can get its hands on.
