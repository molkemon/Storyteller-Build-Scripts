Swedish politics from seventeen twenty-one to seventeen seventy-two was dominated completely by the paralyzing bickering between the so-called Hat and Cap parties. The Caps, initially led by Arvid Horn, were backed by Russia and England, whereas the Hats were supported by France. The foreign meddling increased steadily, and when Gustavus the Third seized power in seventeen seventy-two the most powerful political figure in Sweden was the Russian ambassador. At the Riksdag of seventeen forty-six the Hats once again got the upper hand, despite their recent disastrous war against Russia. Sweden entered a defensive pact with Prussia and began reforming the army and navy with French subsidies.
