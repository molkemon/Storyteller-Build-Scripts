Walerian Protasewicz, the bishop of Vilnius, purchased several buildings in the city center and established the Vilnian Academy in fifteen sixty-nine, one year after the Lithuanian nobility had asked the Jesuits to create an institution of higher learning in Vilnius or Kaunas. The three divisions at the academy were humanities, philosophy, and theology. In fifteen seventy, a library was established, two thousand five hundred books were donated to the new college by Sigismund the Second Augustus, and the first students enrolled into the Academy. Stefan Batory, King of Poland and Grand Duke of Lithuania, granted the academy equal status with the Kraków Academy on April first, fifteen seventy-nine, and created the Alma Academia et Universitas Vilnensis Societatis Iesu. Pope Gregory the Thirteenth's bull of October thirty, fifteen seventy-nine, approved his edict, and the first rector of the Academy was Piotr Skarga.
