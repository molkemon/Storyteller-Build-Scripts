Frederick the Second's desire and determination to build a residence on the island of Spree was met with indignation by the citizens of Berlin, and in fourteen forty-eight the indignation against the cession of the city's lands for an electoral stronghold culminated in open revolt. The citizens of Berlin flooded the excavation of the residence Stadtschloss, but Frederick the Second prevailed and the palace was finally built, the city's rights decisively curtailed.
