William Pitt was the second son of William Pitt, the first earl of Chatham. In December seventeen eighty-three, King George the Third took the opportunity to dismiss the coalition and asked the twenty-four-year-old Pitt to form a government. When Parliament reassembled in January seventeen eighty-four, the government was at once defeated by thirty-nine votes on a virtual motion of censure, but Pitt refused to resign, and gradually the coalition's majority in Parliament began to crumble. By March eighth, the majority against him was one vote, and on March twenty-fifth Parliament was dissolved. In seventeen eighty-eight he signed the Triple Alliance between Britain, Prussia, and Holland, thereby ensuring that in a future war his country would not be bereft of allies as it had been during the American Revolution. In seventeen ninety he demonstrated Britain's renewed power and prestige by negotiating a peace between Austria and Turkey. In seventeen eighty-four, he passed his own India Act, creating a board of control regulating Indian affairs and the East India Company. Finally, in seventeen ninety-one the Canada Constitutional Act was passed. London became responsible for the government of both Lower and Upper Canada, but both provinces were given representative assemblies.
