The revolution has arrived. Longstanding dissatisfaction at the lack of meaningful reforms by the government of the realm boiled over as revolutionaries stormed the Topkapi Palace in the capital, capturing the monarch's harem and the Royal Treasury. The monarch attempted to flee but was unable to evade capture for long. The captured ruler was swiftly put to death for crimes against the people and the faith. The Imperial Divan has been purged of all reactionaries and reorganized into the ruling council of the new Republic, which is now headed by a selfstyled Supreme Vizier. The new regime promises to abolish serfdom, establish equality under the law, and redistribute the nobility's land between the state and the peasantry, so that all members of the faith may be as brothers, free from oppression.
