Worried about what they perceive to be an increasing encroachment on their privileges by the Danish Crown members of the Norwegian council and other prominents have gathered in the capital to work out a number of demands upon the Crown for their continued support for the Union. The demands put into print in the capital include the restoration of lost Norwegian lands, affirming the traditional rights of the Norwegian church and nobility, strengthening the power of the Norwegian royal council, and in general affirming Norway's role as an equal party of Denmark in our Union. The intent is for the list of demands to be sent to the monarch to be made part of his traditional Håndfestning, the Electoral Capitulation, as the ruler of Norway.
