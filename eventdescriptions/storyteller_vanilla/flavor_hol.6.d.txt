The tulip was introduced in Europe in the fifteen fifties from the Ottoman Empire. They quickly became popular among the rich merchants in the Netherlands as a luxury item. The market grew, and in sixteen thirty-four when demand increased in France, speculators entered the market. In sixteen thirty-six, a futures market was created, and during the autumn and winter the prices skyrocketed until the prices collapsed in February sixteen thirty-seven. The result was the bankruptcy of many, and economic disaster.
