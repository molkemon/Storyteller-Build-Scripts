The consort urges the monarch to pledge to fund a magnificent church for the Theatine Order in the capital if an heir to the throne is born. She has already chosen a Bolognese architect, Agostino Barelli, to build the church in the Italian high baroque style. The aristocracy and wealthy burghers are quite taken by the plans, which she has allowed to become widely known, as they are strong admirers of the fashionable Italian style. Should they do as she urges?
