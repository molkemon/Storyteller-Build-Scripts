Charles the Seventh remodeled French finances, establishing heavy taxation, particularly through the taille, a direct land tax. Extracting the wealth of the land has always been a tough nut to crack for the centralizing French kings. To tap the vast riches of the French lands, new and improved taxation methods were needed time after time. Throughout history, the methods used became more and more rationalized to optimize gains for the treasury. The birth of a permanent taxation system was a major step towards this aim.
