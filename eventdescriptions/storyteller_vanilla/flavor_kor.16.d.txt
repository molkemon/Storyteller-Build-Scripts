The Tripitaka Koreana is a collection of the Tripitaka, sacred Buddhist scriptures which collectively constitute the Buddhist canon. These scriptures have been carved onto wooden printing blocks and are the pride of Korean scholars and craftsmen. Although once destroyed by the fire of the rampaging Mongols in twelve thirty-two, the Tripitaka Koreana was restored in twelve forty-nine. Now it is in the hands of the realm. Its fate depends now on us.
