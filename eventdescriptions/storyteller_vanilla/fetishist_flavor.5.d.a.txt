A cousin of the monarch has passed away. The letter sent to us describes how a bandit ambush fell upon the entourage of the traveling statesmen and how they were all forced off a cliff on their way home from a visit in a neighboring nation. The few possessions that could be retrieved have been sent to the capital, but the monarch is distraught by the death of their favorite relative. To make matters worse, the letter from the neighboring nation says nothing about whether a proper funeral ceremony has been considered or not. Do they wish us ill with this Oku igbe, a bush death, for the kin of the monarch?
