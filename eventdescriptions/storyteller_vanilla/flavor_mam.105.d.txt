Under the administration of the Mamluk Sultanate in the fifteenth century, the cultivation of Coffea arabica saw notable development. Recognizing the potential of this exotic plant, they initiated efforts to cultivate and refine its growth techniques. Their agricultural experts meticulously studied optimal growing conditions, leading to improved yields and quality. The resulting coffee production became not only a source of pride but also a valuable trade commodity, enhancing their economic strength. Their dedication to fostering the growth of Coffea arabica played a role in its eventual global significance as a cherished beverage.
