With the increasing influence of the secondary religion faith in the country, a number of new monasteries have been founded. Their purpose is both to harbor the monks that have come to the lands and the slowly growing number of native monks. Traditional landholders do not seem to be very impressed by the pretensions of these newcomers, however, and have been helping themselves to the lands reserved for the monks. The monks claim that the land would be made more productive under their control, while the landholders feel they are demanding the right to what is already theirs.
