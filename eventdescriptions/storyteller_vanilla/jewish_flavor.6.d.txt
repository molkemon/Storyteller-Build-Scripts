Judaism is a non-proselytizing religion, and therefore, it is rather difficult to bring new blood to our small community. To add to this, according to our tradition, only children born to Jewish women are Jewish themselves, which makes it dangerous for our people to allow interreligious marriages if the bride belongs to our religion. Thankfully, people from the realm, previously following other Abrahamic religions, have realized that they believed in the Lord all the time and have fully embraced their Jewishness.
