The Duchy of Mazovia had long been ruled by a line of the Piast Dynasty, but because of the power of Poland, it most often followed the suggestions of Polish foreign policy. In fifteen twenty-six, the last Piast ruler died, and the Polish crown inherited Mazovia. Exactly what would be done with the lands was not clear, but in the end it became a Royal fief. And later, the new Polish capital would be placed in the sleepy hamlet of Warszawa.
