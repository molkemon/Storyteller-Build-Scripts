Michel Le Tellier, born in sixteen oh three, sixteen eighty five, was the son of a counselor at the Account Chamber. Pinpointed by Prime Minister Cardinal Mazarin, he was named Secretary of War in sixteen forty three. He started the needed reformation of the French army and war administration as minister until sixteen sixty six and Chancellor until sixteen seventy seven. His eldest son, Marquis de Louvois, continued and perfected his reforms and offered his master, Louis the Fourteenth, the largest and best army of the last half of the seventeenth century.
