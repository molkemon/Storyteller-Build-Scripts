The state church has always been the foremost pillars of the realm, providing not only spiritual guidance but also administrators, judges, and diplomats for the benefit of the monarch. In return for these great services, many lands have been set aside as religious properties, exempt from most of our taxes. This way the state church and the many fine projects they support can be provided for. Times grow dire, however, and the state has to cover an increasing number of expenses. Suggestions that perhaps the state church might make a voluntary contribution have not been favorably received.
