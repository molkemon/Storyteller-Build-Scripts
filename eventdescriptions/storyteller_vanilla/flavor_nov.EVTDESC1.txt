The fourteenth century witnessed the start of Novgorod's long struggle with Moscow for supremacy. Internecine disputes among the republic's leaders weakened it in the face of growing Muscovite strength. Although it became a vassal of Moscow after the Muscovite invasions in fourteen fifty-six and fourteen seventy, Novgorod was allowed to retain its self-government. It was not until fourteen seventy-eight that it came under Moscow's complete control and lost its freedom. Novgorod retained its commercial position until St. Petersburg was built in seventeen hundred three.
