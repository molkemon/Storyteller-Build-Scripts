The reign of Vasily the Second is a troubled one. His grandfather, Dmitry Donskoy, has appointed Vasily the First as his legal successor and Vasily the First's brother, Yury Dmitrievich, as the next in the line of succession. Eventually, Dmitry and Vasily the First have died and Vasily the Second, Vasily the Second, has become the ruler of the realm. This act is in direct defiance to the succession law of seniority and as such, Yury rebelled against the then still young Vasily the Second. This has led to the Muscovite Civil War between Vasily the Second versus Yury and his sons, Vasily Kosoy and Dmitry Shemyaka. While the threats of Kosoy and Yury were dealt with, Dmitry Shemyaka still poses a danger to the stability of the government. With a small army of conspirators, Dmitry executes a coup d'etat against Vasily the Second. The monarch gets captured, blinded and exiled to Uglich while Dmitry asserts control over the government.
