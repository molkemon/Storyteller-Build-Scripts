After the death of the monarch Friedrich of Thuringia's uncle, the Thuringian Wettin lands came under the rule of the country. However, an argument soon broke out between the monarch and his brother, Wilhelm, over the fair distribution of these lands. According to the Division of Altenburg, an agreement arbitrated with the help of Brandenburg and Hessen, the two brothers would be joint rulers, with the monarch being allocated the core territories of Meissen in the east and Wilhelm given control of Thuringia. However, Wilhelm disputes this arrangement, demanding more land, at least rulership of Wittenberg, threatening war otherwise.
