When the French invaded Italy in fourteen ninety-five, they were first repulsed at the Battle of Fornovo by the Milanese, but they came back in fourteen ninety-eight. The French took Milan, but the Duke managed to escape. In February fifteen hundred, Duke Ludovico returned with an army of Swiss mercenaries and engaged the French who were sieging Novara. Since both sides employed Swiss mercenaries, they refused to fight each other, and the French captured the duke, winning the war.
