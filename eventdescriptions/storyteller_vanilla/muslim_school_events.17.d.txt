A Sufi Sheikh has passed away in a province and his followers are now putting up a shrine in his honor. Pilgrims from various parts of the country are already starting to come to the province to see the shrine of this great man, some calling him a saint. Local notables have set aside a charitable donation for its maintenance, and his former pupils have opened a lodge nearby. While there is nothing wrong with honoring a great man, some more traditionalist among the pious and the Ulema feel that this form of worship is sacrilegious. Should the monarch pay the shrine in that province a visit, it might do much, both for the prestige of the shrine itself but also for the monarch's popular perception.
