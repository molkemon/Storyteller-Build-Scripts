Uppsala has been the seat of the same named archbishopric ever since the Christianization of Sweden. As a place of religious importance, Uppsala was the hub of teaching and learning, and so the construction of a proper university to further expand the education was the natural next step. Similar to Uppsala, the capital of the realm was too an ecclesiastical center and seat of a powerful bishopric. the archbishopric of Denmark. Of course, this had to change when the capital of the realm became a proper domain, but its religious importance and its history as a center of education prevailed. Eventually, the capital of the realm too received a university, though this time it served the purpose of educating the people of Skåneland into the Swedish language.
