The Naadam Festival in the Yuan Empire was a grand traditional event celebrated by the Mongolian people. It featured competitions in the Three Manly Games. horse racing, archery, and wrestling. These games showcased the skills and prowess of the Mongolian warriors and were often attended by the ruler and nobility. The festival also included cultural performances, feasting, and various forms of entertainment, making it a significant event for the social and cultural life of the empire.
