Ever since the thirteenth century treaty of Perth, Scotland has been obligated to pay a yearly sum to the Norwegian Kingdom in exchange for control of the Norwegian Hebrides. These payments are by now long overdue, however, and this has been the cause for tensions on and off. Regardless of ancestral claims, however, the new overlords have seen fit to end this conflict forever. The ruling family will marry into their dynasty and the Jarldom of Orkney will be pawned and then transferred to the Scots. Orkney remains one of the highest-ranked titles in the Kingdom, one that has often been a stepping stone to the Norwegian throne. If these islands are given up, the right to rule former possessions will be surrendered forever.
