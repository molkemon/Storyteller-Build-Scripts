The word rade comes from the old English term 'Road', meaning a protected place near shore, not so enclosed as a harbor, where ships can ride at anchor. The Rade of Toulon is one of the best natural anchorages on the Mediterranean, and the largest rade in Europe. It is protected from the sea by the peninsula of Giens and the peninsula of SaintMandriersurMer, and has been used as a military harbor since the fifteenth century. The Rade shelters the port of SaintMandriersurMer, the port of La SeynesurMer, as well as the arsenal, or military port of Toulon, and the commercial port. The early modern history of the port began when the monarch built his Tour Royale at Toulon in the year. A naval arsenal and shipyard were built simultaneously, and small a sheltered harbour, the Veille Darse, was built a few years later to protect ships from the wind and sea. The shipyard was greatly enlarged by a later Cardinal, who wished to make Provence into a Mediterranean naval power.
