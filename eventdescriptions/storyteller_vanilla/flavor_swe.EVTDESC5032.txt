Emanuel Swedenborg was one of Sweden's first natural scientists whose early works included the brilliant Principles of Natural Things in which he reached by inductive argument several conclusions that greatly resemble the theories of modern scientists. Swedenborg's life changed dramatically during one of his travels. He had strange dreams and visions that caused him to forsake natural science and turn to mysticism. In seventeen fifty-eight, he published his most influential work, On Heaven and Its Wonders and on Hell.
