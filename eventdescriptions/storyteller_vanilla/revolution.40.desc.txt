The revolution has arrived. Large crowds gathered in the capital to demand an end to the millennia-old imperial regime that many feel does little for the majority of its people. Initially demanding a constitution to limit the monarch's power and increase their own influence in government, the crowd was incensed at the violent crackdown by imperial forces and fought back, storming the Forbidden City. As the sheer scale of the monarch's luxurious lifestyle was laid bare, few indeed felt merciful towards the ruler. Seeing no possible escape, the monarch took their own life rather than face the indignity of a public execution. The new rulers of the capital have declared China a Republic, promising to reorganize the state by redistributing the nobility's land to the peasantry and establishing full equality under the law. They declare that the future will be glorious and free for all the people of China.
