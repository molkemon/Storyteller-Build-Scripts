Historically, the provinces of Scotland were once Viking colonies in the past, and as such they used to be part of the ancient land. But this is several centuries in the past by now, and the claim from our ancestors on Scotland is minimal at best. However, the Earl of Orkney of the Sinclair clan does have a certain degree of legitimacy to rule over Scotland in our name. We could install the duchy of Gaeldom, the realm of the Highlander living in the North of Scotland, as a loyal subject of the country, and place the Sinclair clan as the ruling duke of it.
