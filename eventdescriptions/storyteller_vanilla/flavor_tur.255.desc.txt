The Islamic Golden Age was a period of great scientific, cultural and economic flourishing of the Muslim realms. This age ended with the tragic Siege of Baghdad in twelve fifty-eight by the Mongolian Horde and the collapse of the Abbasid Caliphate. With the restoration of the Caliphate under the ruler, the perception of Islam as a whole is changing once more. Unified under the Ottoman banner, scholars, theologians and artists are once again free to live a life free from worldly worries. Scientists see active promotion for their work while architects are blessed by Allah with tasks to construct new mosques. All this while the religious piety in the Islamic faith is experiencing its own renaissance. Truly, this is the beginning of a new, second golden era for Islam and all those who follow it.
