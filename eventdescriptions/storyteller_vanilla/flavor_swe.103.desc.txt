The Allotment System, an organization system for the military, enables the quick mobilization of the country. Every tenth man between the age of fifteen and forty-four will be conscripted as a permanent part of the national infantry, while the nobility, exempted from their taxes, have to provide their cavalry. Due to this system, the usage of mercenaries has become less necessary as there is now a standing army for Sweden. Eventually, the Allotment System received a new reformation, which makes the recruitment of potential soldiers province-based instead of village-based. Every province of the Swedish empire has to contribute a regiment of twelve hundred Carolean units for the Swedish army.
