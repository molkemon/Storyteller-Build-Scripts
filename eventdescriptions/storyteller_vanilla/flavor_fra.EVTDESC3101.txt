After the failed assassination of the Huguenot leader, Admiral Gaspard de Coligny, the monarch feared reprisals and ordered the assassinations of all prominent Protestants in the capital. The severely injured Coligny was dragged from his bed, killed and thrown out the window. This has, unintentionally, inspired Catholic zealots in the capital to begin a massacre of the entire Protestant population. Thousands of Huguenots have been murdered in the streets, and the violence threatens to spread beyond the capital if we do not take action.
