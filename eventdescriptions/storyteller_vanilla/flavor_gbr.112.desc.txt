The relations between the ruler and the parliament have always oscillated between cordial and strained. Differences between the various regional interests and the authority of the ruler have always been cause for heated debates, and the different views on religion have also sparked their own share of discussions. Recently, these disagreements took a new turn, when some members of the parliament tried to defend their positions in front of the ruler speaking too fervently in a manner truly unbecoming of persons of such high status. The ruler was obviously displeased about the way the issue was being presented, and the ruler turned it down immediately. That resulted in the issue being settled for now, but increased the ardor of its supporters and their animosity towards the ruler. Not only that, it also seems that the people willing to take the issue into consideration has surprisingly increased, and there are more people offering it their support, whether openly or behind closed doors. In addition, other groups are also using this issue as a justification of the inability of the ruler to properly address regional concerns. They argue that if a single issue like that was not even considered, none of their own issues and demands will be heard. As such, they advocate that the ruler should not interfere with the decisions of the parliament, while the ruler wishes to limit the parliament's interference to their rule.
