During the Hussite Wars, some of the heretic Hussites supported our Polish enemies in fourteen thirty-three and fought against us. Seven thousand men marched through western Prussia all the way to the Baltic Sea and left only fire and ruins along their path. The actions of the Hussites are still fresh in the minds of our knights, so it was to nobody's surprise when our soldiers marched through the streets of the Bohemian capital and looted every single household. As a final way to celebrate this act of retribution, our men at arms brought water bottles filled with brine and poured it out over the head of the Bohemian monarch.
