The Korean economy was in a very bad shape by the seventeenth century, and to rectify this state, Chief State Councilor Yi Wonik gradually introduced previously ignored reforms in the tax system. The tribute tax levied against households was replaced with a unified tax on arable land. The tax on goods was limited to a quota, which let enterprising peasants and wholesalers make and sell surplus goods. The net effect was lower taxes for peasants, increased revenues for the state, and a rise in commerce.
