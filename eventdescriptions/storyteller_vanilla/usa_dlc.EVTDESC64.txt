The Federalists urged voters to choose a religious president over Jefferson, or no God. Initially, the Federalists monopolized the media thanks to the Sedition Act, which outlawed most opposition as treason. Though this Act was also the rallying point of the Anti-Federalists, or the Republicans, as they were then called, James Madison had written the controversial Virginia and Kentucky Resolution in seventeen ninety-eight and seventeen ninety-nine, which declared the Sedition Act unconstitutional. At a late stage, the majority in New York changed, and the new electoral votes changed the balance in favor of Jefferson. However, as the nominally vice-presidential candidate Aaron Burr got the same number of electoral votes as Jefferson, Burr later forced the issue to a vote in the House, which was eventually won by Jefferson. The electoral votes were. Jefferson seventy-three, Burr seventy-three, and Adams sixty-five. How will the spirit of America choose this time?
