Baltasar de Zuniga came from a powerful Spanish noble family. He served Philip the Third as ambassador to Brussels, Paris, and Vienna between fifteen ninety-nine and sixteen seventeen. In the latter position, he was instrumental in Spain's intervention in the Thirty Years War. In sixteen eighteen, he managed to remove the Duke of Lerma as Philip the Third's key advisor, a position he then overtook himself. De Zuniga's nephew, Olivares, was placed in the household of Prince Philip, later Philip the Fourth, to assure continued dominance at the royal court.
