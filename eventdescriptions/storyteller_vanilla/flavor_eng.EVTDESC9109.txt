Henry the Eighth, Tudor of England, had first received in fifteen twenty-one the papal title of Defender of the Faith for his pamphlet against Luther. The king's main concern was over his succession, and in fifteen twenty-nine, when the papal Curia rejected his proposal to divorce Catherine of Aragon, he had the Pope's Legate, Cardinal Wolsey, executed and coerced the English clergy to recognize the king as the supreme head of the Church. He had his divorce granted in fifteen thirty-three and married the lady-in-waiting Ann Boleyn. Altogether, Henry the Eighth contracted six marriages.
