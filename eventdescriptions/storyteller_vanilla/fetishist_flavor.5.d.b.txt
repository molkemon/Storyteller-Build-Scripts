A cousin of the ruler has passed away. The letter sent to us describes how a bandit ambush fell upon the entourage of the traveling statesmen and how they were all forced off a cliff on their way home from a visit in a neighboring capital, in our neighboring nation. The few possessions that could be retrieved have been sent to the capital, but the ruler is distraught by the death of their favorite relative. To make matters worse, the letter from the neighboring nation says nothing about whether a proper funeral ceremony has been considered or not. The dear cousin will soon be reborn, either into the visible or the invisible world, but the thought of the good woman not receiving the correct funeral rites torments the ruler who is left alone, pondering their mortality.
