The Flemish Renaissance, flourishing in the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries, marked a vibrant cultural and artistic revival in Flanders, encompassing presentday Belgium and the Netherlands. It was a period characterized by a fusion of artistic mastery, trade prosperity, and intellectual innovation. Artists like Jan van Eyck and Pieter Bruegel the Elder epitomized this era, pioneering techniques in oil painting and capturing the minutiae of daily life with unparalleled realism.
