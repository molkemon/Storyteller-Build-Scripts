The tensions between the offensive war leader and the defensive war leader have spiraled out of control, and now war has broken out. The ramifications of this are felt throughout the Japanese islands, as the daimyos begin to take sides, and alliances are brought into play. Whoever may win this current civil war, however, it is clear to all subjects that they were too weak to prevent it. If they do not ensure that they reestablish their authority over the warring parties, it is quite likely that this great war of the Onin period will result in a prolonged period of strife and warfare between the daimyos.
