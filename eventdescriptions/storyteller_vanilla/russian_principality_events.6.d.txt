Through our acquisition of the capital of that province, we have come to control a major regional center of production and trade. The local artisans and merchants are both rich and successful in their trade, and the city will surely be a great addition to our splendid government. It is hard to know, however, how far we can trust the culture of that province. We should consider forcibly relocating the wealthiest and most successful local families to the capital of the other province, where we can more efficiently monitor and tax them. Some would say the damage we do to that city is greater than any benefit we might hope for in the other city, but the added control is surely worth it.
