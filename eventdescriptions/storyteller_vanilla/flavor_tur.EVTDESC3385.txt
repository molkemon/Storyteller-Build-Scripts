Born around fifteen seventy-five in the village of Radnick in Albania, Mehmed Köprülü was a young Christian boy taken by the devshirme system, abducted and raised in the Muslim faith, just like most Janissary troops. He started his career as a cook, then moved to the Treasury. A pasha in central Anatolia, he gained a name for himself as an excellent and honest administrator, a rare creature in the Ottoman Empire. On fifteenth September sixteen fifty-six, he was appointed Grand Vizier by the mother of the young sultan Mehmed the Fourth. A fierce fighter of corruption, he enacted many successful reforms, balanced the budget, and led the war against Venice. After his death in sixteen sixty-one, his son Fazil Ahmed the Virtuous succeeded him and governed till sixteen seventy-six. Other Grand Viziers such as Kara Mustafa from sixteen seventy-six to sixteen eighty-three, Mustafa Pasha from sixteen eighty-nine to sixteen ninety-one, and Hussein Pasha from sixteen ninety-seven to sixteen hundred two were his direct heirs.
