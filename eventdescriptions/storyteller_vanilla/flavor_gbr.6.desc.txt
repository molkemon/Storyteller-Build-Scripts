Henry the Second of England received the title Lord of Ireland from the papal bull in eleven fifty-five. The ruler had a monarchical claim on Ireland ever since. As time went on, Ireland has become part of the realm, while the title as lord of this island has become uncertain due to the papal relations. The parliament as well as the monarch are discussing the titular matters of the claim. Establishing the Kingdom of Ireland would certainly boost the ruler's prestige. The question remains if Ireland should be ruled as a personal union.
