Our republican nation has given up many of the old ideas about nobility and feudal rule, but sometimes, when they are of use to us, we are willing to reconsider them on a situational basis. Such a situation has arisen in the realm, where a local aristocrat claims that land just across our border is in fact part of his family's rightful domain. Local merchants have petitioned against this claim, having just negotiated a major trade deal with the neighboring nation. For a republic, they argue, trade should be the first concern; the claims of nobles lead only to war and misery.
