The convent Monasterio de Santa Maria de Sigena was founded by Queen Sancha of Castile, wife of Alfonso II of Aragon, and was constructed between eleven eighty-three and twelve oh-eight. It houses some important Romanesque frescos of about twelve hundred by English artists, and although it has started to decline, the monastery is still a grand cloister. The nuns are asking for help to fund the monastery, and the Maestro de Sigena has offered to paint a large altarpiece.
