The Tripartitum, also known as Opus Tripartitum, was a significant legal work compiled in the realm of Hungary during the Renaissance. Created in the late fifteenth century, it served as a comprehensive legal code, covering the law of both nobility and commoners. It aimed to consolidate and codify Hungarian law, drawing from previously established customary law. Its compilation was a monumental undertaking, reflecting the king's commitment to legal reform and governance. Despite its influence, the Tripartitum never gained official countrywide recognition.
