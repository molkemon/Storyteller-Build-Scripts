HøeghGuldberg was of humble background. He earned a theology degree in seventeen fifty-three, and in seventeen sixty-one became professor of rhetoric at Sorø academy. He was appointed tutor to Prince Frederik, half brother of the future king Charles the Seventh, in seventeen sixty-four. As Frederick's private secretary in seventeen seventy-one, he shared Frederik's hostility to Count Johann Friedrich Struensee, then supreme in Denmark through his domination of Christian the Seventh. HøeghGuldberg took part in a conspiracy that led to Struensee's arrest and execution in seventeen seventy-two. Thereafter, HøeghGuldberg, along with Prince Frederik and the dowager queen Juliana Maria, gained significant power in the Danish state. In seventeen seventy-four HøeghGuldberg was made confidential cabinet secretary. Soon, without consulting the other council ministers, he was issuing orders signed by the incompetent Christian the Seventh. He took over control of foreign policy after dismissing foreign minister Count A.P. Bernstorff in seventeen eighty. HøeghGuldberg was forced out of power during a seventeen eighty-four coup d'état led by Prince Frederik.
