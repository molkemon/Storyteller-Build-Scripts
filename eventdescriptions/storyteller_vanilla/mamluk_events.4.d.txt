The ruler is far from the only owner of Mamluks in the realm. The households of influential manumitted Mamluks often contain great, if not greater, numbers of newly imported slaves from their home regions. These influential Amirs are often those with the greatest influence during a succession, but even when the throne is occupied, the influence of the Mamluk households is great on the realm's politics. It is up to the monarch to ensure that this factionalism does not stifle the politics of the capital.
