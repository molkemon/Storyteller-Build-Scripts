Being initially considered heir presumptive, she was later considered illegitimate and removed from the line of succession after her mother was executed when Elizabeth was two years old. Having the finest education, she could write English, Latin, Italian, French, and Greek. The controversy in the succession after the death of Elizabeth's halfbrother was resolved when she and her halfsister Mary jointly rode triumphally into London. However, their bond did not last long, as Mary was a devout Catholic determined to extinguish the Protestant faith, in which Elizabeth had been educated, and she even spent almost a year in prison suspected of supporting Protestant rebels against Mary. Having one of the best educations, as well as a gentle demeanor, she can without doubt become one of the best rulers England ever had, ushering the dawn of the greatest golden age ever seen.
