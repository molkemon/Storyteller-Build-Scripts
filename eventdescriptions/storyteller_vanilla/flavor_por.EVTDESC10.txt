Construction of the Jerónimos Monastery began in fifteen oh one and was originally funded by Manuel the First using the income from the Vintena da Pimenta tax on goods from Africa and the Far East. Work stopped in fifteen eighty when Portugal, after the death of Sebastian the First of Portugal and a succession crisis, entered into a union with Spain. When Portugal became independent again in sixteen forty, the monastery saw further construction with the addition of a gold tiled ceiling in sixteen sixty-nine and large frescos completed at the end of the eighteenth century. The monastery also became the burial place for the royal family.
