Leading Russian Church officials had noticed a number of discrepancies between Russian and Greek sermons, rites, and books, and in sixteen fifty-two Patriarch Nikon convened a synod to study these matters. A second synod was convened in sixteen sixty-six where a number of reforms were issued to correct this, as well as condemning much of the traditions of the past. This led to a great schism, or raskol, where those who opposed these changes came to be known as Old Believers or raskolniki.
