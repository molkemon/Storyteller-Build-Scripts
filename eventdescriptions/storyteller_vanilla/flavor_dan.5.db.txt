The nobles of Holstein and Schleswig have agreed to support the ruler as the new Duke of Schleswig and Count of Holstein. In return, they have promised to keep the lands of the two titles undivided to further the interests of the landholders there. Schleswig and Holstein are now an indivisible part of their domain even if one is a Danish duchy and the other a Holy Roman County. In order to keep the titles equal in rank, the Holy Roman Emperor has now agreed to elevate the County of Holstein to a duchy, confirming their right to all lands that Adolf the Eighth left behind.
