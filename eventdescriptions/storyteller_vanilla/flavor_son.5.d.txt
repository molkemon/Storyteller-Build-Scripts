With the conquest of the very traditional Mossi, the realm stands at a religious and social crossroad. the clergy demand the eradication of the old traditional beliefs and want to bring the rural people to the light of Allah. The nobility however stress the benefit the realm would enjoy if the peasants are left alone with their beliefs, such as lowered risks of peasant uprisings against the monarch. In the end it is up to the monarch to decide the fate of these people and the religious direction of the realm.
