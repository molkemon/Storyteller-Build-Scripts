As the yearly examinations are corrected once again, only a few thousands of the millions that participate will pass. This elite of experienced Confucian students will join the administration to rule the Empire of China in accordance with the classical ideals. The many that fail, however, have to find work elsewhere. Far from illiterate, they are still carriers of the classical ideals and education that the examinations require. These are the doctors, teachers, and scribes of the empire. Some might say they are as valuable as the erudite few who join the cadres in the capital.
