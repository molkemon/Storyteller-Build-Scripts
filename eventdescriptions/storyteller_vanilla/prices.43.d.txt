For centuries Venetian glass has dominated the glassmaking sector, with smaller glassmaking enterprises trying to faithfully emulate the masters on Murano island. Bohemia has been a glassmaking region since the twelfth century, but by combining their native chalk with potash in a new method, the Bohemian glassmakers are now finally able to challenge the Venetian glass. The new strand of Bohemian crystal is both more durable, brilliant, and distinctly different in its style from the Venetian glass. Bohemian crystal chandeliers, glass jewelry, and engraved glass are rapidly becoming a common sight in all the courts of Europe.
