John Locke was an initiator of the Enlightenment in England and France, an inspirer of the United States Constitution and the author of, among other works, 'An Essay Concerning Human Understanding'. Locke held that knowledge of the world could only be gained by experience and reflection on experience, and this knowledge was being gained by Boyle, Sydenham, Christiaan, Huygens, and Newton. They were the true philosophers who were advancing knowledge.
