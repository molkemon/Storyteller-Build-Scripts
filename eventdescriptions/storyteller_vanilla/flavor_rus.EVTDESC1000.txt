Mikhailo Shcherbatov was an important statesman, historian, and philosopher in eighteenth-century Russia. He became a member of the Board of Trade in seventeen seventy and a president of the Chamber Council and a Senator in seventeen seventy-nine. Though a leading representative of Russian conservatism and a proponent of the Russian Enlightenment movement, he is perhaps best known as an author. His History of Russia from the Earliest Times was published between seventeen seventy-one and seventeen ninety-one in seven volumes. Shcherbatov's final work, On the Corruption of Morals in Russia, was published in seventeen ninety-seven and contained a scathing attack on the contemporary social customs.
