The emperor Sri Krishna Deva Raya, who ruled the Vijayanagara Empire from fifteen oh nine until his death in fifteen twenty nine, during which he patronized eight Telugu poets called Astadiggajas. These eight poets, Allasani Peddana, Nandi Thimmana, Madayyagari Mallana, Dhurjati, Ayyalaraju Ramambhadrudu, Pingali Surana, Ramarajabhushanudu, and Tenali Ramakrishnudu, were regarded as the eight pillars of his literary assembly. The Prabandha Age, as the age of Astadiggajas is called, continued from fifteen forty AD to sixteen hundred. The court of poets was also called Bhuvana Vijayam or Conquest of the World.
