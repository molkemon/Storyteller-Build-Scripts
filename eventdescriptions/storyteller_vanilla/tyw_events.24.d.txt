The monarch is a practically minded ruler. Although as pious as any title, politically expedient alliances come first, and despite the faith, they have joined a religious League that opposes their beliefs. Most advisors see this as a necessity; some of them may even have suggested it. A theologian inquisitor is not such an advisor. A person of faith, they have hid their outrage at the decision poorly and their attitude is now beginning to impact international diplomacy. Although they remain loyal to the League, they may find it difficult to retain good relations with its members when they are not only of the wrong faith, but keep such company.
