As our ship approaches the province, large numbers of gift laden canoes start to follow it. The canoes' occupants have fearlessly boarded the vessel and two seemingly important fellows are trying to persuade the ship's captain to follow them to the tribal nation's capital. Their intentions seem innocent and we can espy from the few words our translator can pick out that we have arrived just in time for a festival of some sort. Our ship's mast formation, sails and rigging resemble certain significant artifacts for the natives and our clockwise route around the island mirrored the path the processions take during this festival. This has convinced the natives that we are friendly, but one false move and it might change.
