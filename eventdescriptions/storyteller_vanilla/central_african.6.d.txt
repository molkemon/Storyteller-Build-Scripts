The power of the monarch has always been balanced by the influence of the Mbudye, a council of men and women of spiritual and historical knowledge. Some of the most influential elders in the council have recently passed away, leaving the ruler with less guidance than the monarch has usually enjoyed. Fortunately, many younger promising candidates are prepared to step in, having proved themselves in the art of reading memory boards and understanding the old traditions and stories. Should we allow them to quickly ascend to join the Mbudye to retain the council's position of influence?
