John Methuen was born in sixteen fifty. An accomplished diplomat, he is well remembered for the treaty signed in seventeen oh three between England and Portugal. The main purpose of the treaty was to allow free introduction of English wool and cloths to Portugal in exchange for free entry of Portuguese wine into England, known as Port wine. The treaty made Portugal an economic and political satellite of England, and was abrogated only in eighteen thirty six. John Methuen died in seventeen oh six shortly after his greatest success.
