The Effelai is strange, wondrous, and terrifying in equal measure. Far from the coasts we came from, the great foliage of the jungle gives way to something else. great mushrooms that reach into the sky and span far into the distance. We began this journey to emulate our ancestors, even seeking to surpass them, in our own way. Heroes come and go; they die and are reborn through song. We were weak and afraid on the northern coast, and now have come so far, overcoming so many obstacles not as lone heroes, but as a collective people. The towering mushrooms are much the same. In most of the Effelai, the mushrooms are small and unimposing. small fingers from the undergrowth that hide from the light as they eat the dead and decaying. Here, however, they are dominant. They have overcome their very nature to become the masters of their environment, an environment of decay. Perhaps they are more like us than we realized. We came from such fearful origins, and have come this far in triumph. Now, in this place of towering mushrooms, we have found what the Effelai fears. death. The songs are thin and frightened. Effelai is only here because it has no other choice. It cannot be absent from the place where parts of it go to die. Like the mushrooms, we have been reborn. We are something new and strong in a strange land. We learn the haunting songs of the mushroom forest and alter the tunes until they suit us best. We celebrate our great pilgrimage, our journey to strength and bravery. But most of all, we add a new verse to the songs about us. a tale for our children to take to heart to encourage them to be even greater than us.
