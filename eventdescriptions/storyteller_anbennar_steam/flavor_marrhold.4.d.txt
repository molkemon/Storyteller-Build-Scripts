The lord groaned as their headache continued to pulse in protest of the noise of the city. Even though the ancient city had been retaken for quite a while now, the battle to restore the city to its glory days seemed endless. The patricians were tireless in their shameless pursuit of power, promising many boons in return if only the ruler would relinquish some monarchal rights. Even today, large sections of the city remained in ruin, home to beggars, thieves, and other vagabonds. "It's in the next plaza over, only the finishing details remain." the gruff mason stated. Adun the Mason was a renowned dwarf stoneworker, invaluable in expanding and restoring ancient infrastructure. Even though he lacked deference, there was no one else the ruler would consider for this monument. As the small royal entourage led into the square, the ruler took in the sight of the clothcovered stone edifice. "Don't worry about that." Adun grumbled, "It's just for the weather and dust." Adun motioned for some of his assistants to pull down the drapery, revealing a magnificent sight. A defiant hero half stood over their wounded mount as they held their famous sword towards some unseen foe. They bore the signs of hardfought battles, their skin rent in areas and their engraved armor dented and scuffed. Around their feet lay more signs of their fighting, weapons and shields lying around in the trampled grass. The two heroes were not alone in their memorial. Around the base of the statue were the names of the knights and their griffons, with those who gave the ultimate sacrifice demarcated with the symbol of Nerat. The unveiling gave the ruler pause, momentarily filled with emotion at the beautiful stonework immortalizing their ancestor. "It's. It's beautiful." the ruler choked out. "Thank ye, I think I need to touch up on some feathers Wait, what are you doing?" Adun replied as he watched the ruler walk up to the statue, momentarily pausing to remove their family ring, and placing it at the feet of their forebear. "I promise that your sacrifices will be remembered." the ruler said, directing a number of the entourage to stand guard at the monument until a permanent honor guard could be established.
