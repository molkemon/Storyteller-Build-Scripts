The last flock to depart Firanya's roost was also the last to return. This was no flock of ages past, no victim of an ancient calamity. Rather, it was the erstwhile daughters of the princess Asuria Ayarakin, who had struck against the throne in an ill-fated coup, as recently as the fifteenth century. In failure, she and her closest friends stole a ship and set sail west, beyond the reach of her vengeful kin. We had thought Asuria's brood dead and drowned, but in an unlikely twist of fate, they survived their journey and landed in Aelantier, where they founded a small colony. Word reached Heunthume of this curiosity long, long after the fact, and it was discovered that the daughters of Asuria had not only survived but thrived in their new home. The ruinborn of Aelantier were suitable mates for our people, and so one roost had become several, then dozens. Eventually, a nation had risen out of the dense jungles of the Effelai, taking the name of Kiohalen. For the longest time, this was of no consequence, but as Harpylen spread her wings across the Lament, Kiohalen became more than a curiosity. The throne, ever magnanimous, extended a talon in friendship, and while the harpies of the west were at first hesitant to accept it, they eventually acceded to formally rejoin the Queendom, in exchange for a long list of guarantees and privileges to ensure their safety from any undue repercussions. An invitation was extended but politely refused, citing that Aelantier was half a world away and that the Kiohalen had utmost faith in the splendour of the Queen's Throne. Despite, or perhaps because of this cold relationship, ambassadors spent much time in parlay between the throne and the erstwhile western flocks. Eventually it became clear that the Kiohalen did consider themselves subjects of the throne and were more than eager to adopt her laws and custom. They truly, genuinely, simply had no wish to return to Bulwar and preferred to be left well and truly alone.
