There was just the Khet, the Gnoll, the sandstorm enveloping them both and the Sorrow river beside them. Recently, many of his kin had been ambushed on the river; troubled by these invaders. They'd swatted them away, but it'd annoyed him quite a bit. So he came up to punish them. All the rest of the gnolls were dead save one, a curious figure with a moonshaped scar on her left eye. The Khet noticed that peculiarity; felt an inexplicable pang of fear as he did. But he brushed it aside. She was going to die soon; it was inevitable. The Khet opened his palm and let the sand flow faster and faster until it overwhelmed everything around it, a death by a thousand cuts. Yet the gnoll refused it. For the Khet, this was another affront. How did this overgrown hyena dare to stand? Any other creature would have fallen, had to fall. He redoubled his efforts, drew from divine power. But yet again, the gnoll dared to live. Step by step she approached, and so the Khet increased its power, the wind grew to a hurricane force, the sandstorm so powerful it could crumble iron. The gnoll would yield… But it didn't. For the first time in a millennium, the Khet knew rage; the sandstorm grew in size and ferocity, until nothing could survive, no hyena, nor ogre, nor scarabbeast. The gnoll would be swept away for its arrogance. It was inevitable. And then from the sandstorm, a flashing maw emerged in ambush; and soon, the Khet saw neither sand nor sky, but the black of darkness. The gnoll's rending teeth, imbued with ancient knowing, sank into the Khet's neck. Once, it had been a dragon that had withstood such power, that had flown through sand and storm, that had devoured a god. But not anymore. The gnoll's maw clamped shut. The Khet died.
