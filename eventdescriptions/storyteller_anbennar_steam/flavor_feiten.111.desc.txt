It is not uncommon for merchant ballons to spend years abroad, blown adrift as dandelions in the wind. Much can change on the ground in these intervening years, and no change bigger than our conversion to Ravelianism. Some crews bear the news well, but others do not, distrustful and superstitious as only sailors can be of the new religion. The arrival of each new crew inevitably brings their own batch of dissidents, and trouble has been brewing in the dockside establishments that they frequent. A fullblown riot seems ready to break out at any point; what should we do?
