"Dead paper," scoffs Ikomeye, as she rifles through the reports. "It would have been better just to blow the place up." "Next time, perhaps," replies the Vile One, her mouth twitching. "It is much neater to find a key." "These fragments will work as sympathetic objects, if we simply wish to divine the layout of this place." Ikomeye is holding one of them, turning it over in her palm. "Mapping it manually would require an army," says the Vile One, "but..." "But divination of such an enormous structure will be onerous. It is beyond my craft alone, at least in any good time." Ikomeye is guileless and direct in her assessment of all things, even her own talents. "And you, knowing the ancient pedigree of the lizard mages, are not stupid enough to walk those halls blind," Ikomeye says, eyeing the jetlike gleam of the zatsargakh. The Vile One smiles faintly. "Indeed, I am not."
