The Earthshaper Sphere gleamed in the torchlight, cradled reverently by engineer Bruenor as his squad approached the blank expanse of stone. The air vibrated faintly, a low hum that seemed to synchronize with their breaths. Bruenor extended the Sphere, and the runes on its surface began to twist and shimmer. With a focused intent, the stone wall softened and flowed like molten wax. Tunnels took shape in moments, smooth and arched as if cut by master craftsmen. Great halls with vaulted ceilings and sturdy pillars emerged where only jagged rock had stood moments before. The air filled with the tang of heated stone and the faint scent of earth long undisturbed. "Look at this," one miner marveled, running a calloused hand along a newly formed wall. "Smooth as glass, strong as steel." "I mislike this," muttered a second one. "The stone feels wrong. Unnatural." As Bruenor concentrated further, a tremor ran through the ground. For a moment, the Sphere's glow turned a shade too bright, the runes shifting erratically. The hum grew dissonant, a screech at the edge of hearing. Bruenor steadied his breathing, and the Sphere calmed, but the squad exchanged uneasy glances. By the day's end, an entire settlement's worth of dwellings and workshops stood ready.
