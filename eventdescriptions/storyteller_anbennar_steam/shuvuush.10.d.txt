Just as the boundless wind rushes across the endless steppe, so do the Shuvüüshudi roam freely across their homeland. Yet, for all their seemingly limitless freedom, their migrations follow ageold patterns dictated by the very land and the seasons. Every five years, the clans gather in one of several traditional grazing grounds. There, led by the Yomod Rüsh, the Feather Riders who raise the Terrorbird herds, they celebrate the Hekuudzoungh Festival, part skill contest, part marriage market and a time of reconnection for the scattered clans of their people. The festival is filled with a series of competitions where the clans can showcase the skills of their members. It is the traditional role of the ruler to participate in the festival tournament, either as judge or as contestant.
