From a smoke-choked watering hole in the domain, a tattooed sailor, eyes sunken with fatigue from weeks at sea, drinks merrily amid his fellow mariners. He says he witnessed it all with his own eyes and ears. For a thousand days he has sailed past that spot, its rocky walls high as a fortress, a smuggler's den if he has ever laid eyes on one. But never has the shore wept with such melodious meters, aching of desire and sorrow, confusion and wonder. Amid the moonless waves, a flicker of firelight radiated above the rocks like a dying forge. He drifted right past that inland cove, but alas, by fate or caution, whatever was making those sea sights vanished; only a burning fire remained on the shore. At first a tall tale, the sailor's story soon proved true. For the last few weeks, other travelers along the shoreline have told similar accounts about the inlet's nighttime activities. strange crooning, warming fires, but an otherwise empty shore. As dread swells among the locals about nefarious powers at work, a local guard captain swears to get to the bottom of it.
