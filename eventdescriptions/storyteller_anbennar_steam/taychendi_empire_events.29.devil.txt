The monarch took another sip of wine as they looked out from their balcony, at the immensity of their capital below. It was night, but even now the light of commerce, industry, and empire illuminated the sky. Taychend never truly slept anymore. The carts never stopped, the harvest never ended, the warbanners never unfurled. The ruler closed their eyes – and in their mind they saw images of conquest, of glory. The sounds of battle, steel clashing, flesh rending, warriors calling battle cries as the gore-covered and yet still glorious fallen cried for mother or mercy or malevolence’s brand of justice. And the blood… all the blood. Even as they mused on the realm they ruled, their subordinates arranged new campaigns in North Aelantier, in Sarhal, in Insyaa. From the warlord to the warrior to the wretched slave, Taychend hungered – and it satiated itself on the blood of the world. The monarch downed their winecup – savoured the purplered trickling down their throat. Why did blood satiate Taychend? Why had the other empires not realised this, not conquered the world to sustain themselves? They used to ask these questions. Then, like Valkadevu on the wastes of Neraghul, they understood. The other empires had simply misunderstood – glory is not some ambiguous thing. Glory is Violence. If the ruler would not kill, end, conquer, annihilate, then the warlords would do it for them. And so you be the sword, or face it. And so Taychend would march, and destroy, and drink the blood of the unconquered world until they could drink no more. Then? Maybe they’d all turn on each other, rend the Empire apart for the ichor of ascension that pumped through its veins. It didn’t matter. The Cycle of Glory – the Cycle of Blood – would be over, and Taychend would have already ascended. An Empire of Violence, and an Empire Vainglorious.
