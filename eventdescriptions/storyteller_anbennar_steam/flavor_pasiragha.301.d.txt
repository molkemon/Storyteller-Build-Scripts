The High Temples are destroyed, and the Great Spirits, long forgotten or even dismissed as myths, roam the material plane once again. The events of the socalled Rending of the Realms have brought devastation all across Haless. As the temples are slowly repaired and the lands warded once more against spirit activity, philosophers have begun discussing the cause of the disaster. Many argue that the people of Haless had strayed from the path of the High Gods, and thus the Rending served as a divine punishment. Lords were too concerned with power, influence, and wealth and neglected their goal of reaching Purtara, and thus caused the Visvatma to fall out of balance.  While the arguments hold certain merits, others follow a more pragmatic view that it is the direct actions of  mortals that caused the disaster to happen. Recent findings have shown that the High Temples served as a ward against the Great Spirits. However, our greed enabled the gnomes to extract the core of the High Temples that we were supposed to maintain, weakening its power against spirits. The actions of the Oni also certainly contributed to this, but it was still the fault of mortals regardless.
