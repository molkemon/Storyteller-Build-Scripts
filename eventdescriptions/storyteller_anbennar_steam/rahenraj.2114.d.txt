The monarch has long admired their small herd of elephants, a tribute from the Ghavaanaji and a sign of their prestige. That said, one of the perks of being Raja is that such a possession does not require the ruler to think of the beasts' care, or feeding, or exercise. Indeed, the ruler little thinks of them at all; until the ruler heard of today's hilarious, scandalous event. Apparently, the vizier stepped in elephant dung on a walk today and was almost trunkslapped by one of the creatures. They are now demanding the execution of the offending elephant and the trainers who abetted this crime. The ruler cannot think of the last time they were so red in anger and embarrassment; but, as with any act from the Lotus Court, levying a punishment on these great beasts would have repercussions around the Harimraj. The Raja here is judge, jury, and metaphorical executioner; what is the sentence?
