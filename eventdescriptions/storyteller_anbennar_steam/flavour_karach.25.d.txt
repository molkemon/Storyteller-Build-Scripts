Irlak Skywrite had spent all day in the mines, and he itched the whole time with a nervous energy. He worked twice as fast as he usually did. He could not eat his lunch. But at long, long last, the day was over. It was time for the competition. A cheering crowd ringed the workshop that day, and Irlak pushed his way through to meet his artisan, Atirta Clearsea. They did not speak. It was the finals. they both knew the stakes. A great boom echoed out. the competition had begun. Irlak rushed to his bellows, and began to pump with all his might. The weapon they were to forge this day was an intricately complicated glaive from the days of one of the first great Ashhana nations. It was completely impractical for war. That didn’t matter. it was the art itself that brought him peace. His scales grew hot as he worked, and across from him he could see Atirta working just as hard on the handle and the adornments for their glaive. At last, he quenched the weapon and handed it over to Atirta. The time blitzed by him in a great stampede. it was time to be judged. Atirta gave their glaive to the judges. She had done a magnificent job. it was all edges and brilliant colors. Irlak watched the judges. He could not read their faces. Someone called his name. Atirta was pushing him forward. Towards the trophy. They’d won, they’d won. He knew they would. Atirta wrapped him up in a great hug as he lifted the trophy high, and as he looked at the weapon he made his mind fizz with joy.
