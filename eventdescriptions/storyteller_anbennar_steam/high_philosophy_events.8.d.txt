It was a rainy afternoon within the capital city as the monarch entered the temple to join the session. The monarch faced the western window, in line with the others, and unfurled their rug. For a few moments silence washed over the monarch. Then the Radiant Sun scholar began to lead the temple in ritual penance. "Unfortunately the sun does not grace us with its presence this afternoon. Yet let us not be lost in the darkness, but instead pay reverence to Digatha so she may lead us out of the storm. For while the rains fall about us, and thunder rolls in the distance, each of us is lost in our own storm; one of our own making." The scholar continued as the thunder grew ever closer to the city. "We have all strayed from our Nitikamarja. The nature of daily life ensures this. Yet, we can, as always, return to it once again. As the High Gods once worshiped, so shall we. Their ceremonies are our ceremonies, and through our faith and fervor we will be set upon the true path to Purtara. May the High Gods’ guidance touch all of you." Lightning came down in the distance as he exclaimed, "With the proclamation complete, it is time to do as the High Gods did once before. Present your offering to the flame, and cleanse yourself through its destruction." One by one people walked to the altar and brought objects to be devoured by the flaming basin. An elderly woman brought forth a fragment of pottery, and her husband brought a hammer. Each person here presented something to be absolved of, a mistaken deviation from their Nitikamarja that they wished to correct. The monarch was the last to make an offering and presented a spear tip, taken from a soldier who fell in battle against their army. As the monarch placed it in the flame a great bolt of lightning crashed to the ground a few meters from the temple grounds. The scholar grinned like a child and spoke once more, "The High Gods seem to be quite pleased with your offering."
