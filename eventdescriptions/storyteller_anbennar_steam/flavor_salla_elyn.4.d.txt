As the Deepwoods have attracted more and more of our kin to our groves, they have begun erecting small shrines at the edges of settlements, and larger ones within the forest itself. These shrines, often adorned with wooden carvings depicting an eastward facing bow, or even a sculpture of a cloaked elven woman, are dedicated to one of the Cannorian deities. Falah, their Goddess of the hunt, nature, animals, and the wilderness itself. The Moon Elves that have devoted themselves to this Goddess have proven to venerate nature in a manner that far surpasses our own pragmatic coexistence with the forests; they seem to value even the hostile facets of the wilderness greatly. Both the plantlife and wildlife are granted special care amongst their ranks. By the reports of various communities, it has become commonplace for the devotees of Falah to ensure the health and safety of various animals and act as both horticulturists and arborists. Despite this, these devotees have also been known to engage in hunting within the Deepwoods, though they have done so in a manner that can only be described as respectful, wasting naught of their prey, nor hunting more than they require. Local communities have embraced this wholly, participating in these hunts themselves, and often hosting grand feasts for a variety of occasions.
