She is carried through the streets of the capital, her body draped in brilliant blues and radiant golds. Countless people of all walks of life gather along the path of the procession, somberly watching the royal entourage march through the streets. They walk for hours, quietly remembering their fallen queen, until they reach a mausoleum newly erected for her entombment. Marble spires pierce the sky, veins of lapis lazuli and garnet running through the stone to give the impression of clouds bursting with light. At the foot of the tomb, the party finally stops, and Hemantsen turns to address the people of the realm. "You have known her by many names, and have witnessed her many accomplishments," the great harimari intones, his voice grand but without color. "You knew her as queen, a friend of the people, and a brilliant mind always seeking the way forward in the world of science and mystery." Hemantsen turns, looking down at her form draped in its shroud. "But to me, she will always be Jira, a kind woman who stood by my side no matter the trials we faced together." "We bury her in this place today," proclaims Hemantsen, as his voice cracks and his eyes water, "But we do not bury all she has done for us. She lives on in the schools, the reforms, and the people which she inspired to achieve greatness, and for that I am forever thankful." Hemantsen gathers himself, and turns once more to the crowd, eyes clear with steely resolve. "Of all the women in the world, there is no other who could have done the work she did, and I am forever proud to call her my wife." The ceremony continues, and eventually Rani Jira is placed within the mausoleum to rest eternal. As the tomb was sealed from the outside, those still present watched Hemantsen, and saw his posture change while the builders worked; he almost seemed to shrink, his shoulders slumped low in anguish. He seemed so small at that moment.
