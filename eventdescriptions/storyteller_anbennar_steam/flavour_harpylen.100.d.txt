The fate of the flocks who fled became somewhat of a cultural obsession in Harpylen, and in a rare moment of assent, the Heunthokharivor and the Isutoqir prepared and outfitted an expedition of odd hunters to answer this question. The logical place to begin was Siadun to the east, for these roosts were known to Harpylen, and they had in their time been a power, ruling over their own lands under the wing of their namesake, Siaduna FlamingTalon. The questions then seemed obvious: How did the Siadun come to the Pravimvata? Why did they settle there? What were their songs and their legacy? Asking the first question proved a daunting task, for harpies are light on their talons and leave scant marks of their passing unless they so desire. This has given rise to the Gelkar saying of "tracking a harpy" as a byword for a largely impossible endeavor. Yet the odd hunters took to this challenge with unusual vigor, and much was gleaned from the Sareyand archives, for it held many songs from Jaher’s time, singing of the bitter wars fought between elf and harpy in ages past. Tracing the golden highway, the hunters began to trace a path of flight, and they found many old roosts, built not for easy living, but as bases from which to raid and wage war. An exception was found in the highland of Grixek, where a great roost was found, roughbuilt, but clearly meant for living. The place was long abandoned, but for an unlikely tribe of humans and gnolls, who now roosted there with herds of rugged sheep. Yet they had many songs of the old queendom, and how this had in its time been a great city. A bulwark facing west, but also a sacred place, one of the first roosts founded by Siaduna FlamingTalon after the flight from Bulwar.
