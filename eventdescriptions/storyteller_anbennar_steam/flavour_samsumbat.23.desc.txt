The bells toll across Anbenncost, as they do each day. The Imperial Palace, normally a hub of administrative and political activity, stands quiet, solemn. In contrast, the city gates are a flurry of activity, as the monarch delivers, for the third time, his speech to the variegated inhabitants of City of the World's Desire. He speaks of the dream of Maracout, to see all who are oppressed set free. He speaks of the greed of tyrants, that would grind people into the dust for the pleasure of the rich. He speaks of the changes the country has brought to the world, liberty, wellbeing, and free ideas flourishing. Most of all, though, he speaks of the Republic of Dameria. With passion in his voice that can be felt even at the edges of the crowd, he speaks of the ideals of that ancient state, to bring light to a dark world. He speaks of the honour of the old senate, giving representation of the people so that no one man could impose his will. He speaks of the perfidiousness that led to its collapse, leaving the tattered remains of a failed dream that is now being given new life by the Great Republic of the country. Over the coming weeks, a new government takes shape, taking the choice elements from the old republic and the new. The newly revived Republic of Dameria is given a special place in the grand republic of the country, with jurisdiction over all of Cannor, its own House of Equals and armed forces, yet sharing the foreign policy and ideals of their brothers and sisters. The former Imperial Palace is emptied of its treasures, most sold off to fund the new government, with a select few left as museum pieces. The palace itself is left empty, a testament left to the kings of the old order; an opulent facade possessing no true substance. A reminder of the past that has been left behind. A new era is dawning on Halann. While slaves still toil, despots still oppress, and people cry out for change, the work of Maracout continues.
