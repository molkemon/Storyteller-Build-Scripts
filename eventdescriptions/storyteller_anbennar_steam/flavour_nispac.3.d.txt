The sun had just begun to crest over the horizon, bathing the sky in a warm crimson, slowly pushing away the darkness. The shattered city gates of Nianano hung tenuously from the wall’s frame, as the revolutionaries began to rouse from the encampment they’d established in the city streets. Nowhere else could one find such a ramshackle combination of people. peasants, craftsmen, merchants, and soldiers, all united by a singular cause—establishing themselves as the heads of the Cycle and then shuttering it for its tyrannical nature. The siege had thus far been grueling. Two weeks had been spent trying to push into the city itself, and though the gates had given in, the inner core of the city had been reinforced, as if the ruler had prioritized protecting the upper class and the keep more so than the rest of the city and its citizens. It took hours for the revolutionaries to make any headway with that fortified core; the sun already beginning to set by the time that the inner gates too were overwhelmed. They flooded into the upper city, many intent on bringing justice to the upper class, raiding their manors, while two leaders rushed towards the keep to find the ruler before he could abscond without facing the people of his realm. They had been successful. Now, the two leaders emerged from the keep, dragging the ruler along beside them; a man who once fashioned himself above all others, now seemingly cowering before those he once knew as subjects. One leader pushed the ruler down to his knees, allowing the revolutionaries to watch, as the other leader raised the blade above his head.
