The sun filtered through the leaves of the trees, casting waving shadows along the pathways in the garden. The monarch stood quietly beneath their shade, looking out over the flowerbeds. Previously, the realm had come here from a conciliatory position, seeking to mend fences and soothe worries. Now, it was a wealthy, strong state with reliable allies it could call upon. This new confidence was clear in the choice of table for the delegations, a long rectangular table instead of a rounded one. Set to one side was a single spot for the representative of the Raja. Yet to the other were three places. one for the realm, one for the Elephant Lord, and one for Parusad Bola. Behind them stood their other allies, representatives from the various castes, and the philosophers. All together, they would present a united and imposing front to the Raja's representative when the time came. The monarch walked slowly along the path through the gardens, reviewing the points they had discussed with their advisors earlier in the day. The realm hadn't started out with the intent to seek independence, yet it was strong, strong enough even to oppose the Raja openly should they wish to. It valued input, it valued opinions of all, in contrast to the Raja's own antiquated views. A stark choice lay before the Raj. allow the realm to go its own way, or risk the complete collapse of the Raj in open civil war. The ruler took a deep breath as a gong sounded across the gardens and a herald announced the arrival of the Raja's representative.
