Deep in the bowels of Tughayasa, an ancient scholar sits motionless among piles of forgotten tomes. His eyes remain closed, but still he sees the ruler enter into the chamber, and the wizened man cracks a toothless smile at the ruler's arrival. "Ahh, at last. I nearly wondered if my visions were finally failing me," croaked the seer as he stood for the first time in centuries. "You have done well to find me, child. I have seen visions of the fate of the Harimraj, and you stand on the brink of their doomed path." The High Augur extends a withered hand, looking in the ruler's direction while still keeping his eyes shut. "If you wish to avoid their fate, then I shall pass these omens on to you." Visions flood into the ruler's mind, horrible flashes of death and destruction. Three images emerge from the fog of the apocalypse, burning bright against the dark. A yawning chasm cutting the earth in two, a black shadow engulfing a nation, and fire raining from the sky. The High Augur breaks the connection, now much more lively despite his old age. "I saw it within your spirit," he intones in his rasping voice, "The conviction of those who will build empires that will last generations. You wish to take the mantle from those who have fallen before you?"
