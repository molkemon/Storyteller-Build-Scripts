A storm of orks gathered around the trialmount, bloody from testing their strength against the blood-filled Bloodgorger berserkers. On top, Dûsh held the last spawn of Korgus’s, Gurzan’s, head with his palm. The young, ambitious, and once upstart ork gazed with a deadpan expression upon his clansmen who were still alive in chains. He felt strange. Not absolute despair, nor unending rage, just plain disappointment, as he was reminded of his father’s words. He prided himself as the last of his siblings to stay standing; it was the only thing he had. But now? It was clear that it was merely dumb luck. Before he could finish his thought, he was thrown down the cliff of the mountain, face first into his men in chains. As he fell, thousands of orks roared upon the mountain, whether it be the terror of the Bloodgorgers or the warcry of the Dogeaters. And with his death, the last living legacy of Korgus Dookanson was slain.
