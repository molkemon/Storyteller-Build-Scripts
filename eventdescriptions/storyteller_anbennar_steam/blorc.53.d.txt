As the chieftain of the opposing tribe brought down their axe one last time, everyone thought it was over for the ruler, who had been on the defensive for most of the duel. However, the ruler was spared. The axe, a mere inch from their face, penetrated into the cave floor. The orks of the opposing tribe began chanting the name of their victorious chief, as the ruler attempted to get up, heavily bleeding and shamed, and motioned to their men to carry them out.
