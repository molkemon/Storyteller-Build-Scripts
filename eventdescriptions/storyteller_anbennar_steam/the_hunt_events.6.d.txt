To walk into a room alongside the Matriarch is to feel invincible. The calming incense has been wafted throughout the room, of course, but their scent retains some unique quality nonetheless, a floral musk pervading their feathers. Diaphanous layers of silk envelop the Matriarch’s torso in a wispy, opaque barrier; when they sit, the seats on either side must be left empty.  Their opposing number wears emeraldgreen, a reflective dye which catches the flickering light of the stillburning censers. The warpaint has not yet faded from their feathers. Not a single person in the room is willing to risk speaking before their Matriarchs.
