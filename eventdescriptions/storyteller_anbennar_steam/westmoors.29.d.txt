The Sunset Maiden, as her crew called her, was ironically a ship built for the ice and rocky outposts rather than for sunny weather or warm sandy ports. Robert Saltstrider, captain of this fine vessel, was a man often considered mad. Not in the Cottersea sense; the fools who oft laughed as stormwinds and rogue waves lashed their ships. No, this was a quiet kind of madness. He had intense, dark eyes like the depths he and his crew traversed, which followed you everywhere you went. He had hair dark as iron and skin like beaten leather, scarred and worn from his battles against monsters, men, and things unnamable that only Aelantier could throw at a man. But he was not unkind, nor was he of the kind to accept casualty. One time during a brutal storm, he had dragged a crewman from certain death. Another time he had speared a dozen Ice Sleepers as they crawled from the cold onto the deck, oily blood staining the steps as he kicked them back into the sea. And so his crew had grown to respect him, and so did the Broken Sea as a whole, as his clan bound the disparate ports together, allowing them to flourish even at the end of the world.
