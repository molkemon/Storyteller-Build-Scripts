The monarch stood outside the torchlight of the camp and gazed upon the stars. The sights of the plains were by this point easily recallable, but seeing them for another time always brought the ruler joy. Tonight, there was a storm brewing on the horizon, too. It looked to be approaching the camp, and the rain was already picking up. There was something different about the storm tonight. As the wind swirled and the rain poured, the monarch came up with the difference. there was a presence tonight. It was present in the clouds above, and it was present in the lightning of the storm, and it was present in the ruler, too. The monarch left the camp and began to run towards the lightning. The monarch ran over dirt congealing into mud as if it was stone, and as the monarch ran, the lightning began striking closer and closer, until the sky loosed a terrible peal of thunder, and a bolt of lightning struck the monarch directly. The monarch was not harmed. The presence from before had taken the ruler over, and as the monarch began to run faster for the monarch could run so much faster than the monarch had, and it was only now that the monarch could see that, the blessings of Irdaeos came to the monarch’s mind unprompted. The welcoming of the centaurs to the plains and the navigation of the Wild Hunt through its lean times were not luck, they were fate itself guided by Irdaeos, who holds dominion over the land and sky. As the monarch came to a halt in the middle of the camp again, the monarch saw that the ruler's pelt was lanced with angry red streaks and the ruler's hoofs were coated in a soot that was not there when the monarch began to run. The monarch also saw that the ruler's followers were bowing to the ruler, and the moon shone on the ruler alone, and the grasses were whistling.
