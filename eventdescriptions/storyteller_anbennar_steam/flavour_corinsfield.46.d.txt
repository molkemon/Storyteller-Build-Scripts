One inquisitor ran to the other, holding his buckled hat to his chest. “You won’t believe this, goodman; that halfling we marked as a witch? Gone.” “Gone. What do you mean, gone?” says the other, their expression equal parts shocked and furious. “Well, we can’t find him anywhere. And the locals, they refuse utterly to talk to us.” “Search their homes, then. Obviously, they’re hiding them.” “Well, goodman,” the inquisitor says, pressing his lips together, “that’s the issue. The halflings' homes, their little dirt mounds; none of us can fit inside. Even the brims of our hats, can’t…” he trails off, reading the other man’s expression. The other inquisitor sighs heavily, and presses his hand to his face.
