Decades ago, the alliance between the late Emperor Lothane and King Kylian VI was sealed with marriages and many gifts and merriments. Of these, one of the greatest was a grand painting of the two men, arms clasped in friendship, standing in the full regalia of their splendid thrones. Painted by imperial artists, it was given by the Anbennarians as a token of friendship, and stood in the Imperial court for decades until, earlier this year, it was ordered removed by a court increasingly hostile to the interests of the Empire. The denouement of the past complete, the action caught the attention of the Imperial court as they deliberated amongst the Empire's parliament. But the ruler thought nothing of the painting as they feasted with their court advisors, awaiting the Emperor's response and the missive of the imperial parliament. It was not until midnight that the winedrunk lords of Lorent received an answer to their challenge of imperial sovereignty. A courier arrived bearing a ten foot tall package with the seal of the Empire on its back. The ruler was the first to sober, their eyes wide as the painting was revealed. The painting was of them on the Lorentish throne, thorns of a rose strangling them as a swarm of doves plucked their eyes out while the ruby crown lay shattered at their feet. And the red pigment of the painting smelled of iron.
