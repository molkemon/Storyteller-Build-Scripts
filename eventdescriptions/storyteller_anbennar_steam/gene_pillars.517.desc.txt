As we descend ever further towards the center of the structure, we come upon a peculiar sight. Before us are a number of white rocks stacked into a vague circle. All of the rocks have a roughly oblong shape with a white rocky texture. As we reach the rocks and endeavor to inspect them, the first touch leaves us confused. Despite the texture, the so-called rocks are not actually rocks. Their surface has a distinct give, as if made of some kind of fabric or hide. We make the choice to attempt to tear one open, hoping for something of significance. Once the rock is cut open, however, a clear liquid starts to seep out, and to our horror a reptilian eye stares back at us. As the young hissnake emerges from its egg, we take up defensive formations as we see more of the eggs begin to crack.
