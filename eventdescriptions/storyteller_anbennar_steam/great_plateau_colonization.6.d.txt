Most who inhabit the Plateau are eager for expansion. But the exploration they prioritize is eternal, laying down another stone in the path they are building through constant reincarnation of their infinite self. As such, the Plateau's north has never had more than the smallest of towns and lacks permanent infrastructure. For the humans, perhaps that would be enough. But the yeti are at times stricken by wanderlust. For an individual, that might manifest as leaving their home, not to return for years; if at all. And for a society, it manifests as a sporadic, but undeniable, expansion. The northernmost settlements in the Plateau date to a particularly intense bout of wanderlust two centuries ago. And now, it seems that wave has come again. Even if it ebbs out, the towns of the realm will not disappear.
