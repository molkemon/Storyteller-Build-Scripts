The history of magic in Bulwar stretches across many millennia and is filled with innumerable triumphs and catastrophes. Yet for all these vicissitudes, magic remained an integral part of Bulwari society ever since Brasan the Liberator gave the ancient Bulwari the arcane means to topple their former genie overlords. One of the most drastic changes experienced by Bulwari magi in the last centuries were the strict and extensive regulations regarding magic use by human magi that were enacted in the wake of the failed assassination attempt on empress Jexis. Even after the fall of the Phoenix Empire, these Jexisian Statutes remained largely in effect across Bulwar, causing a slow but steady decline of the venerable magi clans, which during this period have lost both many potential new acolytes and the skill that only comes with practicing some of the more esoteric aspects of their art. It falls to us as one of the regional powers to decide upon the proper response to this increasingly pressing issue. Many warn against letting this grand legacy of all Bulwari fall further into decline and obscurity and push for a relaxation or outright abolishment of the Statutes, while some among the more conservative members of the court utter dire warnings against loosening the shackles placed upon intractable human magi.
