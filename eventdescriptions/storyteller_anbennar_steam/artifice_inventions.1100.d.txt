On an individual level, economics is complicated; on a societal level, it is a nightmare. Keeping track of every product produced, every person taxed, every import logged, and every vacillating value... it can be outright impossible without the right instruments. Thankfully, though, we have now devised such instruments. The Market Mender Victor the Third is a marvel of calculation. The Mender can be fed entire volumes of data, and tabulate that data into neat little columns and rows. It can sort by averages, predict future market conditions, and make gorgeous graphs that visualize the fundamentals of our national economy. The economy, we have realized, is made of math, and the Market Mender Victor the Third lets us understand that math. What invention could inspire more wonder than one that gives us a glimpse of the patterns inherent to our world?
