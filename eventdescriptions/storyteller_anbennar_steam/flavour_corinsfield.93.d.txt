He couldn’t look her in the eyes when he was returned home. Two days later, in the dead of the night, the inquisitors came and she was gone forever. The funniest thing, though, was that he felt nothing. After all, she was a witch; the inquisitors had said so. So along Goodman FearNot went, wearing a cheerful smile. And, in a way, he was happy. He had done his duty; he had accused the witch. He was happy, and he couldn’t even remember his wife’s face. He loved his God, and he knew that in his service, She loved him.
