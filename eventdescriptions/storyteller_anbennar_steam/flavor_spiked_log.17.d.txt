Qlak the Wanderer fell silent, enjoying the fragile silence spreading in the almost empty room. He peered through a closed window, his figure outlined by the dim sunlight. ‘My friend, my last tale is now finished. I must start my journey anew, for I have lingered here more than I anticipated.’ ‘Thank you, Qlak. This week has been truly enjoyable, and I will miss your presence. Will you come back? Wait, no, I know you won’t. But before you leave, please tell me honestly. Qlak, is it real? What you told me, the people in your stories?’ ‘Does it matter, my friend? Tales move hearts and make people change, even if they are not true. You too, my friend. You could step outside, and witness the changes your tales brought to the world.’ The ruler of the realm laughed bitterly. What effect could he have had on the world? He hadn’t left his castle for an eternity. The Cartels held most of the power now, and he was painfully aware that he was but a mere figurehead. Still, driven by a compulsion he hadn’t felt in ages, he walked to the balcony. The noisy bustle of the capital below hit him first, quickly followed by the sheer size of the city. It spread beyond what he could see, and most of the buildings seemed brand new, made out of fine, ornate wood. A silvery gleam caught his eyes. A parade, fully clad knights riding fierce feystompers with flickering tusks. The Engravers had outdone themselves, as always. The ruler of the realm shook his head, as if to dispel the last shreds of the fog that had clouded his mind for longer than he could remember. This was his own doing. His own tale. He turned around to his ever empty throne room. Was he waiting for someone? Fleeing the oppressive atmosphere, he rushed out of his throne room, under the startled look of his guards. The future belonged to the younger generations, but he could still have a part in it.
