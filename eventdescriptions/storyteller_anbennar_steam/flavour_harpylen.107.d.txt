Not all harpies who passed through Mount Tughyasa chose to follow the path of Relyore Fillanveta and the P'ezarangi. The Ivetasananthor found that many carried wounds that did not fade with time, and while some stayed with the Oracular Order, others saw it as a risk to remain under foreign watch. Among these was Semiha Nephiofira, who gathered a group of likeminded harpies and decided to keep moving east, using the freedom they had been given to leave and search for a place of their own deeper in Haless. They did not have to search for long. Beyond the lands of the Raj rose the Phokhao Range, a harsh but suitable place for a new roost. However, the mountains were already claimed; the dwarven hold of Verkal Ozovar lay to the southwest, while the peoples of Yan controlled the other side. Knowing her flock could not survive another conflict, Semiha chose to settle there anyway, rather than risk pushing them even further after everything they had already endured. Unlike the P'ezarangi, Semiha did not try to adopt foreign ways, and instead relied on what her people already knew. Through careful negotiation, small compromises, and the occasional bluff, she managed to secure a fragile understanding with their neighbors, allowing the roost to exist as long as it remained small and its hunts stayed discreet. At the same time, the harpies did not abandon their old traditions. They continued to follow The Hunt, keeping alive one of the oldest Songs of the Hunt, passed down from before the flight and preserved in the mountains. In the years that followed, Semiha began putting more structure in place, slowly shaping the roost into something more stable. Part of this was the creation of the Mountain Raves, one of the earliest semiorganized harpy groups, which first handled hidden hunts but later took on roles in espionage and other covert work across Yan. It was also during this time that the flock took on the name Semifire, combining Semiha’s name with the harpy word "fira", meaning mountain, for a combined meaning "Semiha’s mountain". Over the following decades, the Semifire improved their position among their neighbors and became known as the "Phokhao Peacocks" for their diplomatic skill. They often acted as mediators between the dwarves of Verkal Ozovar, the peoples of Yan or the Hinphat, and with dwarven help their roost grew into something far larger and more developed than it had been at the start. They also developed an elemental practice called nemphi; "to bring wind", or Windbending, which gave them a way to support their diplomacy with force when needed.
