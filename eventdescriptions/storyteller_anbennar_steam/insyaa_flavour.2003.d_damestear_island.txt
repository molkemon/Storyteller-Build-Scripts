We thought this was an island amidst a lake, perhaps more interesting than most for the bizarreness of the flora that flourished upon it. But as soon as we stepped off our watercraft and onto its surface, we paused. There was a power here, buried deep and at the surface. We pawed at the ground, and found the reason why: this entire island was made of a tear. We looked around, at the massive lake we had just crossed, and realized its truth: it was but the crater left by the meteorite that had become the island. This is a place where power is concentrated. To access that power will mean mining out the very land we build upon. But perhaps that is a price worth paying.
