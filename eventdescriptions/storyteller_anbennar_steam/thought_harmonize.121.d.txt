Are you looking for the forgotten? The monarch wakes up to a figure who looks like if an elf were also a tree standing over their bed. The figure’s left hand, gloved or perhaps grown in bark and thistle, strokes at their calm smile of amused self-assurance. Immediately recognizing the figure, the monarch attempts to reconcile the advice they was given over the past few years. A Wood Elf had warned them to not look them in their eyes, else their memories would fade into their Court; an Eordan Ruinborn had told them to immediately pledge their entire country to them, or at least gift a large library towards their project; a Forest Goblin, on the other hand, had said that they should probably just accept whatever was coming. The monarch decides to stare no higher than the creature’s torso, then begins, “We honor your presence, Steward of Memory, curator of the Bristlecomb Library.” “I find ‘Steward’ to be a restrictive term, lately. A steward protects, but a curator selects. Sometimes, they must throw exhibits away. Isn’t that sad? The forgotten is found, then is forgotten again.” The monarch pauses. “We honor your presence, Curator of Memory, curator of the Bristlecomb.” “You don’t have to say curator twice, you know. It’s redundant.” The monarch blinks a few times, purses their lips, and tries again. Their lowered eyes do not see the Steward’s smile evolve into a smirk. “We offer you a great hoard of books as offerings towards your Library, so that.” “I asked you a question. Are you looking for the forgotten? Information lost to time?” “Yes.” The monarch blurts out with little hesitation. “Good. Then I will help you. I can work with a nation of scholars.”
