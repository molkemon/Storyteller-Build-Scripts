I have often found that law is like driftwood: in orderly stacks it makes wonderful kindling, but it is nothing more than a nuisance if it piles up without heed. I also recognize that it is often very, very hard to keep the laws from piling up. Things change over the years, and this requires new laws, and sometimes there is nothing to do but stack the new laws on top of the old. The issue with this is that precedent relies on knowledge, and if the law becomes too hard to understand then nobody but those who have spent years memorizing it benefit. Then they make more laws to their benefit, and then the problem accelerates. But the lawyers are not the only ones with clear eyes and long memories. I too have knowledge of the law. I have watched you develop your legal system across years, and I think I have a few improvements to make that would give your laws intuitive simplicity while still maintaining their clear justice. Would you permit me?
