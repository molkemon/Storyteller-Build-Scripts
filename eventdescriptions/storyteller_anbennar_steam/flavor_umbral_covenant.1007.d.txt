The rift opens, and I can see. The fool paces in his throne room. His most trusted advisor has disappeared, abruptly and completely. For all of his arcane power and mastery, he is an utter mediocrity; this is easy to sense at a glance, though I am unsure how exactly. Is it the way he keeps wringing his hands, the reluctance with which he accepts the reality in front of him? Ah, no. He has lost control of his thralls. Without the Coven’s leased power, he has not the strength to hold them. I perceive rather than hear that his ensorcelled lieutenants will soon be upon this place, tearing him limb from limb for his violation of their sentient minds. Their footsteps are already echoing at my periphery, bearing down; perhaps the fool perceives this too. Will he escape from here, ply his dark arts elsewhere, build up his strength? I find, with the cold contempt of my Granny, that I do not care.
