Journal, my brother died today. We had prepared for the possibility of the centaur host approaching, of course, but one tends to discount the possibility of things happening to them until they do. We had heard the stories of heads on pikes, charred corpses burned in tribute pyres to their barbaric god, being dragged behind the host to demoralize the defenders, entire villages trampled by stampedes, but the news from the Khoidang’s office had said that they were weeks away from our fair city. Come to think of it, the news has been getting less detailed lately. We were hunting outside the city, and this was our first mistake, for game we might preserve and cure for food to eat once the siege began, and I heard thunder. I looked up for fear it was raining, but the sky was as clear as ever; the thunder was coming from the horizon. We realized at the same time. I hid inside a hollow log, and he hid in a nearby tree. The thunder grew and it grew, until it was in the sky and the earth both, and it seemed like there would never be anything else. That was when my brother fell out of the tree. The army didn’t even stop to kill him; his body simply broke under hoof. Eventually, one of the stragglers stooped to look at him. He took my brother’s molars, put them in a bag, and rode off behind the thunder. He was fifteen. Enuuk help me, he was fifteen; any older and he would have been at the front. I don’t think I’m going back to the city. I couldn’t face our mother. If they were going to our city, then the militia doesn’t stand a chance, and then it would be my corpse splayed out as a battle standard.
