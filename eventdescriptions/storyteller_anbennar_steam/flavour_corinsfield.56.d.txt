The savage cries from the pyre. She has no tattoos, an oddity as far as the people of Corinsfield know. They claim not to understand her pleas for mercy, or for the safety of her friends, or to see her children. Perhaps the crowd does, in some fashion, but to acknowledge these feelings would be to acknowledge the commonality they share with their most hated enemy. To do such would not only be to sin against their god, but also against their community; for if they start questioning such things, how much longer until they start questioning the other pillars of society that they all stand upon? Can a community united by cruelty be afforded a heart? So, no one speaks. As the counter lights the firewood and the screams intensify until they die away, no one speaks. No one moves, no one breathes.
