Somewhere beyond the great wall of storms which rings the world, lies the afterlife; this we have always believed. Yet the team we left behind to study the Gene Pillar in that province has discovered something incredible; the Pillar itself has great influence over the workings of the ocean’s edge, and making use of its technology may allow us to travel straight through. What can this truly mean? No one sect of the Endless Ocean is quite agreed, of course, though notions spread rapidly from group to group on such an important topic, carried by our traditional exchange. Can we perhaps sail to the very afterlife itself, and meet those lost to us? Will we find other islands in the ceaseless blue beyond, other folk who fare their way on the waves? Ours is a curious creed, whatever shore it may wash, and we doubt any would prove as adaptable as we to the immense changes and challenges that ending the Kiamoa may bring. We simply must know. Piecing together the information gleaned from the Gene Pillar with the collected stories of Insyaa, we have discovered a fascinating link between the Gene Pillars and the Dragon Turtles. Three of their number are closely associated with the secrets of the Pillars, even far beyond their homelands: Hoki’eal, Grandfather Moss, and Nalo Pu’ukani. Hoki’eal, curious and daring, has long ridden the edge of the Kiamoa for her own reasons, joy and sorrow alike; Nalo Pu’ukani shares our creed and our desire to go beyond the wall, and his many songs and humble secrets include tales of the Pillar’s operations; Grandfather Moss’ prodigious lifespan includes an enormous wealth of wisdom. With their help, we will cross the Kiamoa into a truly endless ocean.
