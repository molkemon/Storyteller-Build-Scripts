The square in front of the council hall is bustling on market day; merchants compete for customers, children run amongst the stalls, and the few guards posted to keep the peace chuckle as they watch two ornery fishmongers nearly come to blows as they have done every week for the past five years. Five years: the amount of time since a leader led his people in an uprising against their tyrannical overlords; the amount of time since a new government was set up to provide better representation via the councils; the amount of time many people have been asking themselves what is to come next. The people are happy, despite the natural tension between different groups that any country needs must overcome. Yet fear still bubbles beneath the surface for many, knowing that the leader will not always be in charge, that before long, envious neighbors will turn their gaze towards the riches of the mountains. The governing structures are still immature, the army is still a volunteer militia, and despite five years of hard work by the national council, the domain still has no formal allies. The councillors, the citizens, and the guilds know they will need to work together like never before if they are to see the state survive, let alone flourish. One day, the world will be shown the light of liberty, but for now, the domain must survive.
