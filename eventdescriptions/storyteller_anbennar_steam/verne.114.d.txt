In the mage towers of the Magisterium, those seeking to be accredited as adepts must display a knowledge of theoretical magical usage, still beyond their ability in sheer force and thus impossible as a practical exercise, but possible to calculate using the principles of magical usage, exhaustion, and efficiency. We are now presented with something similar. how to get the enormous metallic corpse of a dragon across an immense span of water. At first, we considered teleportation, though the precision and distance involved make this in all likelihood impossible, even were we to unite every mage in our realm and focus them on a single purpose, which is difficult enough once the number of mages in question exceeds two. Wyverns were an obvious secondary solution, though the sheer weight of the skeleton makes this impractical. In the end, it took the combined mundane and magical to solve our word problem. That same magic of motion and displacement which makes teleportation possible can be made to work upon weight, and our wyverns have proven more than capable of pulling the levitated bones of the leviathan over the water. It is an act of exalted madness, one that could only have come from the venturesome spirit of the Vernman.
