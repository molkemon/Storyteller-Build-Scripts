The usual drone of your throne room is broken by a nervous shuffling. A harpy supplicant stands before you, talons clicking uncomfortably on the polished stone floor. She bows her head as her magnificent wings unfurl; as she speaks, her eyes are downcast. "My lord, I speak for the flocks under your protection. We are grateful for the peace you provide, but our current living space is not well suited for us. To have our nests and eggs so low to the ground, exposed to every landbound threat, it sets our feathers on edge. Our young cannot learn to ride the wind currents when there are only treetops to leap from, and we find that it is difficult to stretch our wings for long periods for exercise without travelling far away from our flock. We see the majestic peaks in the distance and know that is where we belong. We ask for your aid in our relocation. In return, the mountain winds will carry our cries to warn of any who would dare trespass on your lands." You look from the proud, anxious woman before you to the maps laid out on your table. She is correct; their current home is an unnatural fit. You steeple your hands together and lean forward as you consider her plea.
