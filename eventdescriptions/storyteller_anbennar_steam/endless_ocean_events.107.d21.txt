Perhaps no natural force so perfectly merges the concepts of Haku and Moa as a landslide. Weight builds along an incline, verticality and gravity combining to put force behind each pebble and grain of sand within the ensuing wave of soil. Like Moa, the motion of this mass downwards changes the face of the hillside while turning over new soil for growth, mimicking the buildup of a wave. An eruption casts ash across the land, invigorating the soil and preparing it for new growth. Magic to cause such events combines the constant change of Moa with the cyclic nurturing of Haku. Thus, wittingly or not, Landslide Infantry engage in highly spiritual deeds by triggering eruptions and scattering soil. These great feats of stonework can easily stray from the path of virtue, however, when carelessly applied. Haku is a place for growth, after all, and wanton destruction unchecked impedes that process. Likewise, sudden shifts made without warning leave little time for the well-intentioned to survive, so Moa must be managed intentionally. In order to better channel these energies, Wavesong practitioners train Landslide Infantry mages in acts of meditation. Some tasks involve dowsing molten stone without water, or stopping a slide’s momentum in its tracks, or partially shielding a space that would otherwise be subsumed in soil. Each exercise hones the mental control of Landslide Infantry as much as it emphasizes their magical prowess, leading to more potent and virtuous mages.
