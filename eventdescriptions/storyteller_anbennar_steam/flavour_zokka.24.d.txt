Hireet arrives, trailing shadows behind her. The gnolls are waiting, stinking of ambition, musky and sharp, ready to be plucked and used as the Coven wished. She has with her a script, of course; Ilfanill's insistence, before she slipped away. Promises to barter, secrets to share, the usual fare. But hags are hardly honest merchants. Every pact needs a pound of flesh. Every word is a test. She meets the congregation at the Matriarch. She smiles. She bows with mocking grace before they parley. The gnoll priest, she supposes, listens, keeping his eerie green eyes locked on her own. When she finishes, he opens his mouth, and he whispers a name. Her breath catches. The swamp recoils. Emerald fire swells, and down she falls. She lands on obsidian. The air thrums with appetite. She sees him, impossibly large, and he sees her. He smiles. She tries to speak, to plead, to twist her form into something that might slip away. But it is too late. He is already moving, his maw already opening wide. And Hireet Smoketusk, the Giant Queen, is eaten.
