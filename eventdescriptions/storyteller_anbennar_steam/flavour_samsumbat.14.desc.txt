Our heritage is in the mines and the mountains, an identity which has gone from a point of shame to one of pride since the country's independence. Yet there are those who longed for a new life, away from the reminders of servitude. This desire, along with our expanding influence in the savannahs in the west, and a decision to diversify our food production away from crops on the banks of the Sorrow, provided an excellent opportunity. With investment from the government and cooperation with the local Fangaulans, large groups of people have moved west to expand the farming and grazing opportunities there. While there will be a small loss of production in the mines, the long-term benefits outweigh the costs.
