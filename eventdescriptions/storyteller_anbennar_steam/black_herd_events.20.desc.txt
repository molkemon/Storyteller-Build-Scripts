At long last, I have returned to working on Markhentakruan. Since its rediscovery we have brought the spell to bear in battle numerous times. It has proven quite effective, yet it still lacks the overwhelming impact it once possessed. Markhentakruan used to herald the end of the battle the moment it was cast, a ferocious thunderclap followed by screams and the chaotic scrambling of a rout. I have reconvened the mages to address this shortcoming, much to the dismay of the warrior clans who complain endlessly that I have less time to waste listening to their bleating. Together, we have identified a key area for improvement. the flames used to detonate the corpses. They succeed in triggering the ignition but fail to remain alight or spread once the spell concludes. From both warriors’ accounts and the unearthed remains, we know the flames once persisted long after the detonation. As such, our task is to restore that lost effect. Since the first successful casting, my understanding of evocation magic has deepened considerably. Integrating that new knowledge will be an excellent starting point.
