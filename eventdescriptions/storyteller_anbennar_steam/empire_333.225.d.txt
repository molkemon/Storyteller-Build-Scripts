The front gate entering the Zatsarya no longer gazed over the city of Zerat, but over a calm sea. The sky was a deep navy blue with a few twinkling lights, but for one section of the sky. There hovered an inky black dot with a ring of blinding light around its perimeter. It looked like the opposite of the sun, a darkness so deep it seemed to reject the very concept of light. The first to step foot outside the spire was a guard who had entered with the ruler's title. Gingerly poking at the dark soil before stepping out, he prodded the ground with the butt of his sword before stepping anywhere. Another guard followed, then one of the craftspeople, and finally the monarch. Beneath the ruler's feet shifted a fine, dark sand, like the beaches in Fahvanosy. The ruler stepped into the sea, letting the water wash calmly around their feet. The ruler stood there, motionless, as their eyes adjusted to the dark sky. What should the ruler call it? Romutha; dark sun in lizardfolk; perhaps? It was peaceful. "My ruler's title, come here quick. We’re going to try to reactivate the Zatsarya." The head engineer stood just outside the water and gestured furiously at the monarch. The ruler contemplated rejecting the call. There were no naga in their ear here, driving them to constantly improve. Here, on Romutha, there was just the sound of the waves. But the ruler did not. The ruler stepped out of the water, through the doors of the spire, and turned the handle as before. The same lights, the same gears, the same stars all sparkled, until through the doors one gazed out again over the two lakes that flanked Zerat. The monarch stepped out of the spire, tracking dark sand into the courtyard as attendants and naga rushed to their side, and squinted at the blinding light of a familiar sun.
