Consorting with demons is already a terrible sin for which death is the only possible atonement. There are, however, not demonic but equally dark forces from which the wicked might seek gifts and boons. The terrible whispers that emanate from the tainted Shadow Swamp have attracted many a warlock and necromancer. These fools seek power, riches, or even immortality from the vile entities of the Shadow Plane, and find only death and suffering once their usefulness has expired. Such is the nature of the warlocks that seized power in the pack of Rakkaz, puppeteering the weak line of the so called Slayer of Slayers. No deed was too foul for these lost souls, from perfidious necromancy to sacrifices even more tortuous than those Xhazobkultists usually perform. But all their treachery and sorcery could not save them. The powers they serve are fickle ones, quick to abandon those that fail or disappoint them. Even if this was not the case, no foul demon, hag, or spirit can stand in the way of the glorious and virtuous armies of Konolkhatep. None of these sorcerers has been spared, but the pack of Rakkaz can offer many things besides rotten and cursed magic. The first Rakkaz was indeed a noble and mighty hyena rider, who rightfully earned the title of Slayer of Slayers. The treason of her daughter and the weakness of her line cannot be faulted on the whole pack. The fearsome hyenas they breed, their expert riders, and their gladiatorial tradition will be excellent additions to Konolkhatepi cavalry. The question is, should we absorb the hyena riders, placing their once tainted lands under our direct control, or should our emperor take the crown of Rakkaz, and grant the slayers of slayers a degree of autonomy?
