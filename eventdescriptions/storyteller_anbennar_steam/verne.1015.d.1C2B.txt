I left my elves to it. I figured they didn’t need me there, exhorting them to blockhead their way through twenty-eight puzzles the way a Vernman gang would have. Besides, I was exhausted by the press of magic, and I needed to think. They lost one person to some strange room of staircases, ever receding and never quite making sense; I think the key to the room must have been conviction, straightforward attack. But their approach worked. After a couple of days, even an idiot like me could feel the lines they created with their careful movements, the overlapping fields of fire.
