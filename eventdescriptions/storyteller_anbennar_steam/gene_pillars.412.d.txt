One of our expeditionaries loves molluscs. As pets? To eat? For some stranger reason? We will never know, because he was the first they devoured, and that was all he managed to say before his untimely demise. If he had been able to talk more, he might have told us that molluscs tend to consume their prey with a radula, a sort of rasping tongue covered in teeth. Normally, those teeth are quite small, only suited to consume detritus or small plants. But on the radula of the strange molluscs, they are the size of daggers, and one speared the member of our party that was a little too curious about the strange shapes moving beneath the water’s surface. Perhaps he might have known their hidden weak spot, or a vulnerability, or some other useful tool. Perhaps he might have known why we suddenly feel so tired and sluggish as we bring up our weaponry to defend ourselves. Alas, to our sudden and fiercely felt regret, we do not.
