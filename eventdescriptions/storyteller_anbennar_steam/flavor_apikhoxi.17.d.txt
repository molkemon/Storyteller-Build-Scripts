It was an overcast day in Apikhoxí. Chilly and windy, with clouds roiling urgently overhead. From south to north, a keeneyed observer would note a good sign. On the grain square, the city was busy as always, the murmur of innumerable people in motion interrupted unusually by the rhythmic beat of a hammer affixing nails to wood. Posters had been put up across the city, each one written by hand, an unusual occurrence that the gathering crowd observed to themselves. Most of the people in Apikhoxí could read to some extent, and in this case, a charitable soul had taken the time to read the proclamation aloud for those who could not. "Honored citizens, farmers and freedmen of Apikhoxí. It is with great honor that the Apikhoxí Synkletos presents the newly founded Apikobhen School of History, a public institution specializing in Kheionai history, culture, and the distinction between our fair Kheion of Apikhoxí and those of our esteemed peers, in the Devand and Alecand. Classes are free of charge, but seats are limited, and examinations to determine admission will begin in seven days time in the following locations." The crowd murmured in approval, perhaps feeling a stirring of national pride. The idea that they were Apikhobhen and not Ormabhen was certainly a new perspective. And yet, some faces in the crowd seemed thoughtful, some even exasperated.
