The final session of the Empire of Anbennar is held at dusk. The stained glass of the chamber is cracked. Some of the tapestries are still smoking. Half the high seats are empty, their lords dead, kneeling in foreign dirt, or fleeing to the colonies. But the ruler still sits the throne, robes impossibly clean. Duncan stands behind the monarch, the last of their retinue. He hasn't slept in three days, too afraid of the jackal-like howls echoing beyond the gates. He hasn't spoken in half as long; how can he? The throne is cracked, and the empire is gone, and his legs shake and his mouth is dry and the smell of soot clings to his clothes and none of this means anything, he knows. Not anymore. Those under enemy occupation are already lost, eaten, or converted. Duncan isn't sure which is worse. Crunching comes from beyond the chamber doors. The sound of feet against broken stone. Some of the remaining nobles shift in their chairs, knuckles white and trembling as they lift their goblets to their lips. The ruler does not drink. The monarch intends, Duncan realizes, to face death with clarity. Morbidly, it makes the retainer proud to face his own. He straightens his back and lifts his visor, resolve bared to the world. The doors begin to open, but he will play his part until the end.
