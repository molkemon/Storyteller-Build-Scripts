One day, as the monarch and their party make their way along a road, they come across a building that opens right onto the pounded earth. It is a small thing, but as the monarch enters into it to investigate, they find a hospital, one whose every inch is decorated with seahorses, swimming, playing, eating. In every corner and in front of most walls are also beautiful works of art. A NursePriestess of the hospital at last spots the pilgrim. The monarch introduces themselves and the NursePriestess replies with the required courtesies; after this she gives a thorough tour of the place, explaining to the monarch that she and her sister nursepriestesses are running short of the bandages they need to provide their service. As she speaks the two come to the heart of the complex, at the centre of which sits a graceful fountain. It is dry, unable to service the many pilgrims who visit the Hospital.
