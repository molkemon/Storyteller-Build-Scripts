The sun was overbearing on the day of the joust, carrying a heat with it that could be easily felt through the tarp erected to cover the seating area. One could only imagine how it felt to the individuals in plate mail. People from two nations lined the edges of the jousting arena, the difference in seating apparent; one side held a patchwork of clothing and sigils, a harsh contrast to the symmetrical, organized section belonging to the other. In any case, both were roaring with support for their chosen knights, any attempt at introducing the participants drowned out by the two sides competing with one another to make the greater cacophony. Above it all stood Sir Adenn and their lord, seated in a special booth.
The first round: Sir Adrian, of one nation, versus Sir Alindel, of the other. It was the quickest of the batch, ending with Sir Adrian delivering a blow straight to Alindel’s shield, sending him immediately to the ground. The second round, Sir Mayvin versus Sir Syl, went three rounds before Sir Mayvin was dehorned. In truth, the entire first bout was like so; a knight from one nation would win, then a knight from the other, and then back again.
