The soldiers hear the jeers and chanting and the screaming of their enemies. A legion of horrors, hundreds in number, armed with torches and swords and spears and guns, clad in uniforms drenched red in the blood of their enemies. They march in the night, believing that they are not expected. Their enemies are used to this trick; they used it too many times on those who couldn’t fight back, and now those that can know it all too well. Chieftain Rapanach holds up his hand. Behind him are all the men he has left. Brave fighters, hundreds in number, dedicated entirely to their families and their tribe. Few of their sabertooth cats remain; much like their owners, most have been hunted down and skinned by the Fieldsmen. Today, every warrior here will die. Each and every single one of them. He can scarcely make eye contact; even though each and every man knows this. What remains of the Veykodans have fled their home. The Chieftain and his men are the last of the fighters, left behind to ensure the escape of the rest. Where they will escape they do not know; the only direction they know now is away, away from the savages in their tall hats with their hymns and their guns. For now, at least, this is still their home. And they will fight. Let them at least take down three krahnorians for every Veykodan that falls. The red glow of the torches, red like blood, grows closer. The last of the Veykodans ready their spears and raise their arms, their side illuminated by their glowing tattoos. Those who have guns, as loot from the dead or from trade, raise them and aim at the oncoming horde. The first shots ring out.
