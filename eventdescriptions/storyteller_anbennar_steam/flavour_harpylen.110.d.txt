The Ivetasananthor were dismayed by the news from the Moorhills, and trekked north to Celmaldor. The mood was dark, and many hunters felt irked by a trail gone cold, a hunt teetering on the brink of failure. Despite this, it was decided to rest in the great port of the north, to recuperate, gather strength, and then decide what to do next. It was fortunate that they did, for in Celmaldor, the hunters encountered a Gerudian captain, trading out of the northern port of Avnkaup. As they met, he offered them a gift of mead and honey, claiming that it was good fortune to curry favors with the Wingmaidens of Vaengheim. This shocked and intrigued the hunters, and they asked the captain to keep this gift and instead bring them to these wingmaidens of whom he spoke so warmly. This was said and done, though the journey across the Giant's Grave was both cold and miserable. Longships, crew, and hunters arrived in Avnkaup unscathed, and this was where they met the last descendants of the northern flocks. The wingmaidens of Vaengheim were a peculiar people, for they had taken the Gerudian faith and tongue as their own. Furthermore, they had taken for their mates the hard folk of Gerudia, as well as the gray people of Bal Vroren to the east. Their features reflected this, for they were tall and fair of skin, sturdily built, with some even favouring heavy steel armour and firearms of Gerudian make. Some had feathers like spun gold, others so black as to glitter like gems in the light, but all were warriors first, and held the song in esteem, a tradition they shared with the many skalds of Gerudia. The harpies of Vaengheim were welcoming of their southern kin, and gladly brought them to their sacred hall, where they told them of their charge: to keep Elkaesal the White asleep beneath their roost, there to dream for eternity. For if Elkaesal woke, they claimed, it would mean the end of the world.
