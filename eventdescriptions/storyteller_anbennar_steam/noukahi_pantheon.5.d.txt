As the monarch walks through the ironwood treelined path to the mountain, they cannot help but feel small. Even the great metallic trees are dwarfed by the majestic size of Mojobaa’s home. The great volcano of Junansuar, on whose slopes stands what once was a genie palace, now a great monastery and the monarch's destination. At the end of the path, a long and steep flight of stairs rises towards the monastery’s doors. The monarch is out of breath when they reach the top, and take a moment to rest before entering through the immense iron doors, wide open to welcome all pilgrims. The first thing they notice, even before stepping inside, is the heat—stronger than midday in the desert. Then comes the noise. metal clashing with metal, the ironwood from the strange trees of the Dao Nako hammered into perfect weapons. As the monarch continues onwards, following the pilgrim flow, the sun eventually stops reaching into the immense halls, the orange glow of the forges now the sole source of light. The heat, the noise, the charged air. the monarch feels their mind becoming overwhelmed, lost in itself. Yet they continue, walking almost unconsciously, as if pushed by an unseen force. Then they see it. a column of clear light. The monastery opens to the air, a wide, gardened circle in the middle of the complex. As they step inside, the heat, noise, and air cease to be a concern, and a sense of comfort envelops them. This is where they are meant to be. In the middle of the garden stands a lone ironwood tree, the greatest stand they have ever seen. The monarch places a hand on its bark, expecting to feel the hard coldness of iron. Instead, it is warm, like a mother’s embrace.
