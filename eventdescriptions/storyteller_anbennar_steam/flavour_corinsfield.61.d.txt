The accused squirms in a chair, a burlap sack over their head, their hands tied behind their back with ropes bound tight enough to strangle. Their mouth is gagged; they will neither give nor receive their defense.  In comes the inquisitors with a simple wooden box. In this box lies death.  The inquisitor opens it. Out walks a fat white chicken, a small buckled hat attached to its head. A ceremonial scattering of grains leads the chicken to its quarry.  It pecks at the ground. Once, twice. It looks up, turns its head to the side; as if examining something far beyond what a chicken should be able to comprehend.  With a deafening squall, the chicken announced its verdict. Guilty, guilty, guilty.  By tomorrow, the accused in the chair will be little more than smoking bones wrapped to a flaming pyre.
