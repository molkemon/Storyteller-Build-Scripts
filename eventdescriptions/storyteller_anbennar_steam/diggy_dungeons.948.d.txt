Troops are knocked off their feet, torches are lost in the mass of gleaming back chitin as they fall from senseless fingers. We manage to crush the odd creature under our boots, skewering a few of the larger ones with pike and bayonet, but the spiders rush past us, dragging a few unlucky souls along with them in their haste. As we regroup and reassess our position, an odd thought strikes the party. The spiders seemed somehow desperate, pushing through our ranks not in an attack, but as if they were in rout, fleeing a far greater threat.
