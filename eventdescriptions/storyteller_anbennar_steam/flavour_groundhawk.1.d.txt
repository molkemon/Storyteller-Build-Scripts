The Deepwoods. Almost as long as our clan existed, we were trapped in those forests, our lives a constant struggle with rival tribes, ferocious beasts, cruel elves, and worst of all, the traitorous fey. Yet even this harsh existence only barely prepared us for the horrors of the orkish invasions. When the first news of these brutal green giants arrived, we had no idea how many of them would appear in the coming decades, until they threatened to enslave or exterminate all of our domain. Yet our clan has always been one for finding opportunities to survive. A human warrior, the first seen since times of myth, revealed to us a way out of the Deepwoods. A deal was quickly struck: we would help her fight the orks, and she would help our clan establish a new home outside of the woods. A perfect offer. Yet as always, things didn’t go as easily as intended. Not long after we had exited the forest, our warrior ally died fighting the leader of the orks, who seem if anything even more numerous in this new land than in our old home. And her remaining followers, most apparently unknowing or uncaring about our deal, seem to make little effort to distinguish us from the blackskinned servants of the orks. Yet for all these perils, there is much to be glad for. There are no fey, no endless maze of deadly beasts and thorny underbrush, and the earth seems far more generous in its gifts than we are used to. This land, still, has the potential to become a home greater than we have ever dared to dream of.
