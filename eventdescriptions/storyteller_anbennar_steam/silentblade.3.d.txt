Fear. Uncertainty. Doubt. Ugol Bloodseeker had faced these foes countless times on the battlefield. Each time he had slain them with each swing of his ax as he cut down elf, goblin and ork. Yet, here under a sunless sky alight with rainbows of greens, pinks, oranges and more there was no foe to fell. There was no enemy to overcome. There was nothing to win or glory to be had. Instead, there was raging of the foliage, animated with a frenzy more akin to a berserker. Distant yet ever present was the roaring rage of unseen creatures, as if crying out for vengeance. And, most pressing, was the thrum of the air itself. It was thick, thicker than Ugol had ever thought possible, even for the Forest. And how it clung to him. As though it was trying to claim him as its own. Ugol grunted with a restless shuffle. Fear. Uncertainty. Doubt. These foes had risen once more, and this time they held a strength that seemed unsurmountable. To his shame, Ugol shifted his eyes to catch sight of the other warriors. They, like him, swayed with unease. He caught another ork’s gaze just long enough to know they felt it. Whatever it was. A distant thought screamed to leave this place. It was shameful. Yet, Ugol almost turned. Almost. It was only the sight of the ruler that kept him rooted. Twice as large as any clan member, radiating with emerald power, the ruler was an avatar of strength. That was until a single arrow struck true.
