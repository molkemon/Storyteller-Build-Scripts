I groaned as I bellyflopped onto our bed, utterly spent. My exhaustion wasn’t because I had spent the day doing anything meaningful like archery drills or ranging the grove or glaive sparring. No, my body was fine, fresh even. My mind, on the other hand, threatened to ooze out as though I had been stung by a deathstalker scorpion. Unfortunately, that had not been my fate. That would have been too kind. “It wasn’t that bad, Al,” Lanhara cooed as she sat beside me and began stroking my hair. I made a disgruntled noise into the pillow in reply. “And you did well,” she continued. “Much better than Elynaile. You didn’t even fall asleep once.” I flipped over with a scowl, “How could I? Every relative and their second cousin twice removed wouldn’t stop talking to me.” “Because, they’ve seen what you’ve done, watched how our clan has risen. They want the same for themselves.” “We did that,” I protested, “Together.” Lanhara pinched my cheek, “Yes, but you were leading us.” I furrowed my brows, “That doesn’t make sense. Why not follow the ruler or the other ruler? They know how to speak fancy. I just speak.” To this, the love of my life only laughed, “That’s exactly why they listen to you…”
