We are at war, but not just any war. Our enemy is not only one of the present time, but a hated rival of our nation. At times such as this, the provinces are united against a common threat, and nobles are eager to lead them into battle. Who better to lead than the Prince of our nation? While allowing a powerful family to rally other nobles and the common people to war would strengthen combat morale, the Statist are not fools. They know that the effects of this unity will linger beyond the war and that those granted power will be reluctant to let go of it, even for the best of causes.
