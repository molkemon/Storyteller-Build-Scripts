On the battlefields between two armies, lives were lost in flashes and single movements. Distractions, mistakes, and momentary lapses reduced what was once living soldiers to bodies. Every soldier had a moment they were vulnerable to being snuffed out, when their back was turned and their mind clouded. But when the Landslide Infantry fought under the command of Holnepuala, it was as if their entire unit were immune to death. They may not have been literally immortal, and in battle it was known that some had fallen before, but a soldier would be forgiven if they could not imagine how. Weapons that made contact with their molten armor melted, flesh that touched them burned, fires erupted where they stepped, and formations parted as easily as air in the face of their flame. There could be no serious threat to their inferno. But flame is not an impossible opponent; in fact, its weakness was well known. The cool and life-giving water that flowed through the jungle and fell from the sky extinguished every flame it touched, so too it was thought it would wash away Holnepuala's Landslide. As battles raged and wars dragged on, many would try to snuff out Holnepuala. With buckets, torrents, and whole streams of water, they would wash over the men with lava for skin and their glowing commander. But so much steam was not enough to quell the blaze, and as lava hardened to obsidian, it made the Landslide infantry of Holnepuala all the deadlier with their unending firestorm within. The landslide infantry were not simply a force of nature but a roaring beacon that symbolized the hope and spirit of its members and its army. Holnepuala was an inspiration to those they fought beside.
