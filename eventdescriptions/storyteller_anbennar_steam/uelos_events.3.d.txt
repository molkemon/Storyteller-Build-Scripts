The woman looked out to the port upon which she had grown, sighing happily as she returned. The years away had changed her. No longer a small and shy girl, she was a captain, the pride of the fleet. She saw her home had changed; it had sprawled as its colonies had, with new shipyards and docks; and grander manors for the merchants, of course. But she had not come here for idle nostalgia; she had come to be a part of the lord’s court, one of the many captains and admirals in that position, and she was to spread the faith, as it had been done to her as a child. She would buy drinks and inventory for sailors and fishermen, both a way to give back to her community and, of course, encourage others to ask about her preaching. And when night fell, and the woman would meet her congregation upon the shore, she saw more and more gather each day.
