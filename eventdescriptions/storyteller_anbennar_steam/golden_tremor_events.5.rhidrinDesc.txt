Why are you in my tent? Rhidrin had only just returned from campaign, the dust of the steppe still clinging to his flanks, and he had been looking forward to unwinding after the arduous canter back from the front. So finding his son, Baildelk, standing in his tent and clutching a birch chest of conspicuous weight was not the most pleasant of surprises. "A gift for you, father," replied Baildelk, dropping the chest onto the rugs with a heavy thunk before kneeling to remove the lock. The chest opens, and the tent is suddenly awash with light, the flickering flames of the firepit reflecting off its contents. Gold. The chest was filled with countless nuggets of gold, pure and beautiful. Minutes passed as Rhidrin stared into the chest, his mind alight with plans and schemes. The greatest obstacle to his dreams of modernization had always been the poverty of the steppes, but with such a bounty, a hundred new paths had suddenly opened. Roads could be laid, industries built, soldiers paid, everything that was desperately needed but had been bottlenecked by their meagre finances. A fidget from Baildelk snapped him from his reverie. Rhidrin turned to his son and asked the one question that this future hinged upon: "Can you do it again?" Baildelk nodded confidently, and Rhidrin stepped over the gold to pull him into a tight embrace.
