The first blessing swelled on a misty morn, a faltering sailor stumbling over fortune to their moored ship. Breath stark, sloshed senses snitched their step, the salty cur tripping over bottles. glittering, laden, forgotten to the sea, yet kindly festooned by enigmatic saints from the deep blue, hallowed with salty dew. The first, the next, the departed tragedies returned. of ships and storms, piers and poems, nails and hammers, crates and cradles, day in, day out. The merfolk graced each lost item within a misty blanket, a kindness strange and serendipitous. In reply, the thankful shore.
