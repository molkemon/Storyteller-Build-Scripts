When Harpylen spread her wings, her shadow soon fell past old Firanyalen, climbing over the crumbling walls of the Jorkad dam, and stretching into the vast expanse of the Deepwoods. This embattled realm was ancient, and had in its time seen many tribes take it, though few could say to master it. War in all its forms was abundant in this place, with elves, orks, goblins and many more crossing blades in its myriad groves. The Queendom then, was in many ways only the newest of a long list of invaders, but the flocks of Harpylen proved uniquely adapted to the wood. Flocks would prowl the skies above the woods, roosting only in the crowns of the great trees, their modest homes set like islands in a great green ocean. It was said that a war could rage on the forest floor, while peace reigned in the crown, so far removed were the homes of Harpylen. The great roost of Ciranmyna became the exception to this rule. This old city had been the center of wood elven culture ever since they arrived in the Deepwoods. So it was when the Queendom claimed it, and so it was promised to remain in perpetuity, by the Queen’s Throne in distant Bulwar. Yet words and deeds cannot always align, and for better or worse, Ciranmyna began to change. As Harpylen secured her rule in the woods, Ciranmyna grew at breakneck speed, as the heart of the Deepwood frontier. Harpies with their flocks, winged and wingless both, began to migrate to Ciranmyna in their thousands, drawn each by a promise that the frontier offered a better life. Ciranmyna grew, and day by day, year by year, Ciranmyna grew less elven, and began to resemble a roost in the Deepwoods, by harpies, for harpies. There was no riot, nor an organized resistance, but rather a slow, quiet change.
