A thick smog of incense filled the room where the Court of Change resided. At its centre, laid out on an array of cushions, resided Akhaxpa. As the ruler approached her and knelt down, she opened her three eyes, filling the room with a blinding blue light. “You may be an agent of change; or at least are making the effort. In that I commend you. But what of these lands when you pass? Do you truly believe you leave your nation in the hands of change, or has your heir merely been taught to mimic what you do? An heir comfortable in their position will do nothing to change the path ahead of them.”
