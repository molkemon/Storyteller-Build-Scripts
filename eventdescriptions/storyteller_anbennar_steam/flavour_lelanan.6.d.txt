The arrows whistled towards me, hammering into my holy shield and further burdening my weary mind. I desperately sought an ally but was greeted with only death. Fellow daughters of Lel lay fallen in the bloodsoaked mud around me. All around me were my enemies, their spears and blades poking and prodding as a hunter wears down their prey. One of the hunters stepped too close, and a surge of my holy power slew them instantly. But I stumbled as my mind swam from the exertion, for it was all too much, far, far too much. Too much physical effort, too much fighting, too much magic. More arrows whistled through the air towards me, so I abjured a new shield. I let out a cry of pain and nearly toppled, looking down in horror at my chest. One of the arrows had struck too fast and pierced my heart with its hateful tip. I fell to my knees, then onto my side. The blood and mud splashed beneath me. I clutched at my chest, my heart seizing as it gave out, and felt ice-cold dread grip my very soul. It couldn’t be. I had assured them, I had promised that I would return triumphant. I tried to drag myself through the mud to return back to the warm arms of my waiting wives, but my body could only go a few meters before it gave out on me. I felt the bloodsoaked mud on my face as the world grew cold, distant, and quiet. The Nakazy sisters were coming to take me away.
