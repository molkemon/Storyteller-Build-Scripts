The chosen clan holds its breath. That the hags have chosen their town for the honor does not mean much; it does not mean that a scion of theirs will take on the burden. But each new Nail King is like a heartbeat in a time of terror; it is impossible to ignore, and an unusual hush descends over the people in the days it takes the hags to convene. A different clan each time, for the most part, chosen by a whim the people know better than to question. The hags commandeer a clanhall outside of their own palaces, for it is long tradition that they do not deliberate anywhere but on the neutral ground of their adoptive children, the swamp trolls. There, says Aunty Komina, her enigmatic grin half obscured by her veil, they can be most objective. The ayora smoke has been issuing from the clanhall for days, but on the sixth day it turns a deep, deep black, and the people know the choice has been made.
