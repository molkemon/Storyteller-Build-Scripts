The cultivation of Atma, called chi by the Yansheni, is a core part of High Philosophy. Each individual, in discerning their own Nitikamarja, their own path to righteousness, and walking it, brings their soul into balance. Thus, they grow their Svayatma, the atma of the self. In acting with skill, wisdom, and grace, they advance towards Purtara. While there is debate among the religious scholars about what actions one should take on the way to eternity, there is no doubt that among their religious school, the ruler is a paragon. Through their brilliance and actions, they shine like a beacon for all to follow, their own Svayatma lighting the way for others to achieve Purtara.
