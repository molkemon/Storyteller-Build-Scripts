Peasants toiled in the hilly fields as Heathcliff Fouler sat in his study, reading over parchments and papers about the goings on in his lands. Finally, after some time, his foggy eyes drifted upwards to the ruler. "Your Grace," he murmurs, "you have done well, restoring prosperity to this forlorn third of your realm. How many previous lords have forgotten it? Many." He then laughed, "Ah, but I'm rambling now. You are here for our support." He stood, old knees shaking as he then approached and knelt. "I, Heathcliff of Clan Fouler, Lord of the Foul river, pledge the support of my Clan to you, the ruler of the Westmoors."
