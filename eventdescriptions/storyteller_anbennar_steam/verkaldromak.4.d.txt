The Sleepers outright refuse to be bound by the strictures of the mages' guild since the rules would force them to study for decades. They argue that these strenuous requirements are a power play by the mages, to give the guild time to find a lasting solution to regain its power. They were able to help people even without pockets deep enough to afford the guild to get magical help, and they want it to stay that way—the guild's monopoly must be broken.
