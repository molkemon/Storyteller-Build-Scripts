The learned often question the great influence storytellers have over the lives of common men. While none would doubt that the story friezes of the High Gods sit at the heart of our faith and lives, millennia of literary tradition has produced all manner of hero stories that have nothing to do with them. These tales risk conflating the divine with the mortal and making a cult out of the seriously distorted fates of historical figures. A prominent philosopher in a certain domain has taken a strong stand against the storytellers of the realm, cautioning that obsessive listening to stories could lead to a false understanding of the gods and take the people away from the Nitikamarja.
