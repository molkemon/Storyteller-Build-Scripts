The scale of this Pillar is immense. Over two expeditions now we have mapped a system of chambers far in excess of what we expected, a profusion of competing biomes escaping from their confines to combat each other in the artery corridors of the superstructure. But as we move into the upper reaches of the Pillar, these petty animal kingdoms give way to immense, airy chambers, the floors largely collapsed in the center, exaggerating even further the vertical ecology of this place. Up here, the birds rule. Many floors have been reduced to mere ledges ringing a great void, and we are lucky indeed that the stairs and access chutes were also built at the periphery. With the exception of those species small enough to go unnoticed, no conventional animal has survived here; this is a hinuihabitat through and through. Immense hinuirocs seem to rule at the apex, cruel tyrants who make all travel across the open ground risky. Yet each of these grand chambers eventually comes to a roof, and the floors are unevenly collapsed. We know there are precious secrets still to be had, and we should prepare ourselves for future expeditions. Those who come here after us face great challenges, chief amongst them animal lore and survival, but we can make the path easier for them by establishing a base camp.
