Ruuk kept his hood up, careful not to allow a single glance at his face. Not a soul heeded him, but paranoia placed the weight of a thousand eyes on him. He was back. Back in the Shadow Grove. Back in Clan Silentblade. Back to his people. But they weren’t his people. Not anymore. They wore their faces, yes, but they were different. Changed. They glowed with a vibrant green hue. They regarded the forest more than their fellow orks. He had failed them. With a heavy heart, he retreated into the shadows. Sapchopper had made it possible for him to get this far, but the rest was up to him. He clambered over the palisade and jumped from alley to alley. When arriving at his destination, Ruuk could feel his heart pounding. It was his father’s personal quarters. A lifetime ago this was where he had made his shameful escape. Now, this was where his redemption would finally begin. The room itself was in poor order. Strewn across it were strange trinkets, feymarked materials, and pages of incomprehensible scribbles. All of them depicted a figure, slender, pale as ash. Despite the crudeness of the drawing, Ruuk could feel the figure’s stare. Written below were three words, “The Blackened Prince”. He shuddered before beginning a frantic search. After too long, he found it, a small dark box made of elm. It had been long coveted by his father. Involuntarily, he spat to the side in anger. The great chieftain Troknar Silentblade, desperate for a small trinket.
