The rift opens, and he can see. One day a wiry little bureaucrat of the Ministry of Rule returned home to find a stranger in his grandmother’s skin. He was not convinced by the disguise for a moment, yet neither did he cower in fear. The bureaucrat’s new Granny was cleverer than his last, you see, and had a hundred and one clever suggestions for how an ambitious young man such as himself might rise in the Ministry’s ranks. He soon realised he had been waiting his whole life to meet someone like this; someone who would look upon problems and solutions the way he himself always had, someone who would recognise his cleverness, his promise, his unique insight. He talked his Granny’s ear off. So many questions he had. how to get what one truly wants; how to bend a room to one’s will; how precisely to milk the venom of the nakshas, to import it from the lizardlands without raising undue suspicion.
