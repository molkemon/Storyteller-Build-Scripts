Behold, the merchant cried, raising a silvered dagger high. Its surface shimmered with faint green light. Pulled from the depths below the hold, a true marvel of ancient power. The marketplace buzzed with uneasy murmurs. You're mad, growled a passing miner clutching his pack. Bringing that cursed thing here? You'll doom us all. The merchant scoffed. Superstition. This weapon could make a lord of any buyer. Shall we start bidding? As he spoke, the air grew heavy. The hum of conversation gave way to silence, then to murmurs—unfamiliar voices threading through the crowd. Whispers, then silence again. A child screamed, and the spell broke. The crowd surged as panic spread, the whispers rising into a shriek. The first to fall was crushed beneath a wagon as its oxen bolted, eyes rolling in terror. Others stumbled, trampled in the chaos. The merchant clutched the dagger, his face frozen in terror as the whispers wrapped around him, louder now, accusing and demanding. By the time the hold garrison arrived, the marketplace was a wreck of broken stalls and bodies. The dagger lay untouched in the rubble, its glow dim but unrelenting.
