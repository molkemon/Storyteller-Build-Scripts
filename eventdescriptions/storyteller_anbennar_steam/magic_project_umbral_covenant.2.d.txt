Umorozi delivers bad news as she delivers any other; tonelessly and bluntly. The Vile One’s arcane council, which consists of the Vile One’s apprentice Cheni and a cluster of lower-ranking mages, looks on. “My expeditionary detachments have finished sorting through the naga libraries, domestic and foreign, to which we now have access. We hoped to find some record there of the substance from which the zatsarya were created, so we might understand how to interact with or otherwise manipulate the pillars. Despite the historical taboo regarding such discussion, even within naga ranks, we have discovered multiple records which refer to the sacred substance used in the pillars’ construction. These records name the substance ‘zatsargakh’.” There is a long silence, punctuated only by the sound of Cheni quietly chuckling. One of the mages, frowning profusely, pipes up from one corner of the room. “General Umorozi… um, Vile One,”; she offers a nervous little curtsey in both directions. “Forgive me, but; doesn’t that just mean ‘zatsarya stone’?” Cheni’s mirth redoubles. The Vile One waves at him to shut up, but not with very much vigour. “Your suggestion, General Umorozi?” Umorozi shrugs, her braids slipping slightly over one shoulder. “Only one good way to find out how strong a wall is.”
