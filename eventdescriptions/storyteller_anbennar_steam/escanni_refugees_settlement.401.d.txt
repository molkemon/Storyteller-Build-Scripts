A group of migrants has been spotted on our borders. They seek to come to our lands and settle in what they call "Home." They are the former inhabitants of these lands; most are happy with what we have achieved, but some are demanding we return some of their lands and homes, citing old property deeds that are near unverifiable. We are the ones in a position of power, and whether we return their lands or not, they won’t have grounds to protest. But to secure our hold on the depopulated lands we are reclaiming, more people are more than welcome, and the price of their presence is accommodating these folk who lived here before. Our courtiers argue that this is a small price to pay for the stability and legitimacy they give us. What shall we do, my monarch?
