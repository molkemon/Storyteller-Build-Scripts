It has come to our attention that some of the terms used among the lizardfolk for halflings and humans are a source of great offence for their envoys. One does not repeat these particular terms in polite company, referring as they do to the odd scent of animal milk hanging about the torso or the disgusting bloodshot lividity of wobbling flesh, but of course when lizardfolk envoys on the job mutter amongst themselves, they use such language. Truth be told, the whole affair is somewhat embarrassing; we are used to our tongue being poorly understood by outsiders. Will we really change the very language we speak for these upjumped foreigners? Few of our diplomats will relish being scolded like a child by our monitors, after all.
