Once the shaking starts to die down, however, we feel something very different. As the pillar, for the first time in many centuries, is greeted by the light of the morning sun, we help Stumble out. We sit atop the crumbled remains of the pillar together, and Stumble is overjoyed at getting to see the rising sun after countless centuries spent in the dark of the worm’s innards.
