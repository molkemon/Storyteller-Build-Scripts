The chambers we find now are mostly empty, save for the plantlife no doubt placed here by the Precursors. There is a strange pale green plant which has overgrown the walls of much of this level, clustering in the upper reaches of each room like a gathering stormcloud. The branches of the lower two thirds of the plant have been consistently scraped of their leaves, so that we see only bare stem and branch and then a flowering in the heights, an odd mismatch. All of this is a mystery to us, until the strange rumblings we have heard in the distance resolve themselves, and we meet the hinuikoala. It is immense in size; its eyes are a milky white, and indeed it does not seem to see us even when it looks directly in our direction. It moves with exquisite slowness for its size; this room appears to have overgrown with the pale green plant since last it was here, so the hinuikoala benefits from a vast, lazy feast. It must continually circle the chambers of this level, chewing on these plants; one wonders what would happen if such a virulent plant did not have this gardener. A member of the expedition drops a lantern, swears; the hinuikoala sniffs, makes a low rumble, swipes idly in the direction of the unwelcome sound and smell. We do not want to be on the business end of that swipe, however lethargic. But there are Precursor stores to root through here, and that will make noise.
