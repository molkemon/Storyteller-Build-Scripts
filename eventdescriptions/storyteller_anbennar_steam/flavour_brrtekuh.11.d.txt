It had been only a few months since the monarch had dined on khetflesh; delved into that ruin, and devoured the corpse of a god. The ruler had felt the power course through them, the knowledge of absolute mastery, divinity itself. They had left, maw covered in dustlike flesh and torn papyrus, with new power and new purpose. to devour Kheterata in more than name. Last night, however, the monarch discovered just what that meant. For they did not just take on the boons of their divinity, but the curse of their affliction. The ruler had awoken to a soulpiercing chorus of felineshrieks and wails, an inescapable cacophony. They stumbled through their palace, claws clutching their ears so tightly they tore at the fur and flesh; but the wailing choir was not all they had to be afraid of. Visions of the dead; shambling corpses, unimaginable frights, khetandman headed alike; assailed them at the corners of their world. Terror afflicted the monarch in every way imaginable. Eventually, it had proven too much. The ruler was not the only gnoll assailed in the night; but when the fearwracked survivors had picked themselves up, they found the monarch cowering in a darkened room. It was only the chatter of their attendants that woke them. But they did not wake to jibbering terror; but a manic certainty, as the monarch grabbed at an attendant. "It was the KHET." The ruler snarled, maw chattering, trying to hold back a bubbling, broiling laugh. "They CURSED us." But they couldn't hold it back for long. "And ha hahahaha HAHAHAHA. They think that will STOP US."
