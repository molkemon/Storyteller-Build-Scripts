By the time Baildelk reached the riverbend, the light was beginning to slip beneath the horizon. The rest of the scouts had already departed for the camp on his orders; finding time to himself had become increasingly difficult since becoming Caehn, making every opportunity something to be savored. For a long while, Baildelk remained there, sighting the local landmarks with his alidade and searching the river for any confirmations of his suspicions about this place. When the setting sun at last caught on a glimmer of something through the murky water, Baildelk immediately waded into the river to investigate. He reached into the cold, cloudy depths, feeling around before pulling out a gold nugget the size of his fist. A week later, Baildelk once more overlooked the same riverbend, now transformed into a hive of activity, centaurs scurrying about like dmarvuks that had caught the scent of a cask of ogre blood ale. Some teams hauled rocks from the riverbed while others waited with hammers, ready to crack open the stones in search of gold. Pits were being dug all along the banks in search of any gold trapped beneath the layers of gravel. All the while, warriors kept a close watch over the workers. This gold would fund the reforms dreamed up by Baildelk’s father; it would not do for some laborer to steal it. Looking upon the budding mine, Baildelk felt as though he could see the shape of things to come. Each broken rock was a promise of more. More mines, more industry, more soldiers, more prosperity. With this fortune, he would honor his father’s legacy and build a new future for his people.
