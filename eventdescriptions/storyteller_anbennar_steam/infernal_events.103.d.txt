It is a dark and gloomy night when the knocking comes at the chamber door of our lord our ruler. Their attention is drawn from the evergrowing stack of papers before them, to the sudden intrusion of sound into the quiet. A frown graces their face, and the doors open to reveal the smiling face of one of the many courtiers that graces the court, with a bottle of wine - some of the finest from the Bay of Wines to be found. Our ruler's eyes drift once more to their work, and then back to the courtier. At the current hour, a break surely could not hurt too badly? A few cups are exchanged. Laughs shared and camaraderie established. Finally, an unspoken tension is broken when the words leave the courtier's mouth. "Tell me, our ruler what do you make of matters of faith?" More words are exchanged, the wine loosen tongues, and soon the conversation steers toward mythology. "The call of the Clarionate, the slaying of Malliath, and the casting down of Kazraiel. Don't you think it's unfair?" "Whatever could you mean?" Our ruler replies. "These gods were so harshly punished for their legitimate desires. Don't we all feel some sympathy for them?"
