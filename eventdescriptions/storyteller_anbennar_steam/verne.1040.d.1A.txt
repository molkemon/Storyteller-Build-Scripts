It was like a small village. And my Mauro stands there very happy and proud, and because I am a foolish woman who did not simply say yes to Tsalenggathu, who had much better arms and was much more sensible, by the way, I am happy and proud for Mauro, too. But the ground rumbles, and from the tree the orangutan rumbles and roars, and your father looks very upset, as if he has failed. And I realize what he hoped to do, so I start to laugh. I sit him down and I say to him, you cannot simply bottle up a spirit such as this. You can contain him a little. You can shoo him from the vegetable patch. But his mischief is a part of the world, just like your foolishness. And your father gives me that annoying grin, and this is the first time I order him to shave that thing off. You see? That is your father. He wishes to fix what cannot be fixed. It is silly, and foolish, and very charming. And though the vase is gone, his spirit, I know, lingers.
