The delegation from AzkaszelAzka arrived in the night, unbidden and without warning. The monarch is caught entirely unprepared, and the court erupts into chaos. Everyone knows full well that elves are impossible to cater to. And sun elves? What do they eat? What are their customs? Are they nobility? By Castellos' corpse they better not be nobility. The sun elves are in fact not nobility, though their glittering armor and noble bearing says otherwise. Their leader, a fine-featured woman of indeterminate age, elaborates. "Honorable lord, ours is a delegation from the Citadel of Citadels. The one for whom I speak has elected to permit you the honor of spreading our oenological gifts across your domain, to our benefit and yours." The elf makes a small gesture with her left hand, and two of her retainers approach the monarch with a heavy wooden chest. They bow, and depart, slipping elegantly back into formation. "If you are willing to undertake this challenge, all you must do is open the chest." The leader continues, without missing a beat. The monarch blinks. The monarch looks to the chest, then to the elf, then back to the chest. "Madam, I am no locksmith." "How fortunate for you then, that the chest is not locked." The monarch furrows their brow. They look to the elf, expressionless, of course. They approach the chest. Indeed it is not locked, but where is the lid? For that matter, where are the hinges? "My retainers and I have traveled far. We shall retire." It was a statement. The elves are already gone. The monarch rubs their eyes.
