The miners of Shaft Nine were used to the occasional strange sound—stone groaning under pressure, distant water dripping. But tonight, the echoes were wrong. "What's that?" Grûn asked, his pick frozen midswing. The tunnel was empty except for their crew, but faint whispers drifted through the air, overlapping like a crowded hall. The words were unintelligible, the tone urgent. "Wind, maybe?" offered Dalbaran, though his voice wavered. "There's no wind this deep," snapped Bofur, the foreman. She glanced down the freshly dug passage. "Keep working." The whispers grew louder, each swing of the pickaxe seeming to call them closer. Then came the knock—sharp and deliberate, like stone striking stone. Everyone froze. "Who's there?" Bofur barked, though she gripped her lantern tighter. The knock answered again, this time from behind them. The miners turned, only to find their path back blocked by fresh stone; smooth and seamless, as if the tunnel had never existed. "That wasn't there before," Grûn stammered. Panic set in as more knocks sounded, encircling them. The lantern's light dimmed, its flame flickering. "Move," Bofur shouted, but the passage twisted ahead, unfamiliar and wrong. The whispers turned to a single, deafening roar, and the light went out.
