The capital city lay in darkness, and it was a darkness of three parts. The most obvious darkness was that of the comforting shadows of the main square, lurking between areas of torchlight. A darkness constantly pierced by the opening of a tavern door and the outpour of light and music, but all embracing in the moments inbetween. In the monarch's palatial estate, many slept, and those that did not primarily did their work in a darkness of another kind, a darkness foreshadowing the deeds of days and weeks and years to come. And there was a third darkness, not an easy thing to notice. The darkness of the ruler, as they walked the streets confidently, patiently, in obscurity. A darkness that crept and prowled, raced and held granite still; a darkness well familiar to the hunting panther or watchful owl. In due time, the darkness found one of the inhabitants of the capital city, and marked him. A talented artist with a long beard and curly hair, working late into the night on a sculpture of Halanna. And between one chiselstroke and the next, the man disappears. Well before dawn, the ruler returns home, their face expressionless, their eyes bright.
