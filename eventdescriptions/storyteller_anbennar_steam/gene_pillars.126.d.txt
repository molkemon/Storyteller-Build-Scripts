Security is often a balance of deterrence and accessibility. Traps must be both difficult to disarm for intruders while remaining simple for the desired people to bypass. Of course, some areas are never meant to be accessed, which means traps can be utterly simple in their design and implementation. The party has stumbled into one such area. As they step into a solid stone chamber, the doors on either side begin to grind to a close with a deafening scrape. The walls light up with countless blue wards, and soon they begin slowly sliding towards each other. Trying to break the walls themselves shows that the wards have reinforced them, and made such attempts almost impossible to achieve.
