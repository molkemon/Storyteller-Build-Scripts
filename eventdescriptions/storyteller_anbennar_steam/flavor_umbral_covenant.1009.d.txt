The rift opens, and I can see. Hireet loves her bloody theatrics, her circus of violence; Komina dances at any court she finds. Yet I cannot recall ever seeing my Granny Ilfanill sit at a table in such a way, thrashing out terms. Everyone seated here wears a form not their own, for opposite the hags sit the demons, those whom the gnolls willingly host in their rituals of fell fire. These demons are fellow players of the game of suffering, though of course not quite by the same rules. How cordial they are. How decent. Komina distracts the room, attracts all the attention, while Granny Ilfa conducts the true negotiation. A detente is slowly struck which will burden Sarhal for centuries.
