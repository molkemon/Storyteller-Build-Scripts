The pickaxe struck hollow stone, and the miners froze as the wall crumbled into an open void. Thohga stepped forward, raising her lantern. The light revealed a cavern larger than any recorded, its walls smooth and etched with faded carvings of warriors, triumphs, and oaths. This isn't on the maps, murmured Adûn, his voice barely above a whisper. It's not in the records either, Thohga replied, her words heavy. As they ventured deeper, the lantern's glow revealed rows of skeletons, perfectly aligned. Each lay clutching a rusted weapon, their armor bearing sigils none of them recognized. Thohga knelt by one, tracing the markings on its chestplate; a hammer crossed with a jagged rune. They're dwarves, she whispered, the words like ash in her mouth. But not ours, Adûn added, his tone uneasy. The air felt heavier, the silence pressing on their ears. Superstitious mutterings rippled through the group. Thohga stood, jaw clenched. Whatever they were, they fell here. And we shouldn't disturb them. The miners hesitated, their tools motionless. Finally, Thohga spoke again. Seal it, she ordered. They deserve their rest. The miners left the cavern in silence, but unease followed them. What lay beneath Gor Vazumbrog wasn't just stone.
