Spirits? Preposterous. There is no evidence that such things exist anymore, much less have any effect on the world. The philosopher from the Darsanasara delegation was practically spitting. Despite now constituting only a minority in the country, their proponents were never silenced, and their arguments were still debated. Those from the Praxis delegation muttered amongst themselves, and one harimari cleared his throat. "Do you have any other explanation for the occurrences on the Kharunyana?" There was silence from the other group; the Praxis philosopher nodded. He gestured to a small delegation, three individuals dressed in vibrantly colored robes who listened keenly to the debate. "Our esteemed guests have been the only ones thus far to submit an even remotely plausible explanation for what is happening. We've all now read the Annals; we've agreed that yes, after the High Temples were built, mentions of spirits and the land gradually stopped appearing in the records." The harimari gestured to the man at the center of the colorful delegation, who finally spoke up. "Our people have long worshipped the spirits of the land, well before you and your vaunted philosophies came to be. What your science and magic cannot explain, history and records can. There is a connection between your High Temples and the plight of your river. I am certain of it." The assembly broke out in muttering once more. The monarch straightened up in their throne and gestured with a claw. "What do you propose we do then?"
