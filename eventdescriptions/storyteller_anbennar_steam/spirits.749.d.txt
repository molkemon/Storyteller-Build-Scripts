The lord sits on their throne with unwavering focus. They have given up resisting Tughayasa’s gaze. No matter how many talismans the guards tear down each day, more always appear in the courtroom. Though that is not the problem. The issue is the piercing gaze, feeling Tughayasa’s sight pierce their mind, and not just their mind but also the members of their court and the mages they employ to restore the High Temples. Apparently, anyone who knows the true intentions or modifications of the temples meets an accident. Critical members of the mage estate and court receive warding talismans to slow down Tughayasa’s curious and piercing gaze. Months pass, and the burning sensation on their forehead worsens day by day. With the restorations almost finished, it becomes more exhausting to keep Tughayasa at bay. Many valuable people have simply lost their minds under the watchful surveillance of Tughayasa. A messenger rushes into the palace, notifying them that it’s finally done. That is the final straw that breaks their concentration, and before they can open their mouth, a sudden bloom of flame bursts from their forehead. They scream out in pain as Tughayasa suddenly and directly retaliates for the first time since its awakening. Even the old priests are astonished that Tughayasa reacts at all. But they continue, hands holding tight onto their forehead, shouting to the court mages to activate the High Temples while Tughayasa is distracted with them.
