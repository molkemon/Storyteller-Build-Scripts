Before the monarch and their band has the chance to act on their instincts, snarling faces materialize in the fog like so many ghosts. Bearing distinctive face coverings and the marks of the Flamemarked gnolls, the beasts quickly disarm most of your men, still disoriented and weakened by the unnatural mist. The last thing the monarch hears as the edges of their vision darken to black are the unnatural and bloodcurdling victory yelps of their captors. The monarch regains awareness slowly. The taste of blood is the first thing they notice, quickly followed by the shifting weight of heavy shackles around their wrist and ankles digging in like a knife every so often as the prisoner's wagon bumped their way down an uneven trail. Slowly, the monarch pieces together what has happened from the others in the wagon. A raid into the Deepwoods by a clan of Flamemarked gnolls, who only occasionally sally out from their hideouts in Daravan's Folly, came across and tracked the expedition, finally deciding to attack at the moment of vulnerability as the Castanorian contingent split. The beasts don't seem to have figured out who they had as prisoners, yet.
