Crafting a founding document for a new state cut off from its heritage is a challenging task. Yet after years of debate and months of cooperation, the councillors of Samsumbat are nearing the end of their work. The powers of the mayor and the council, the protections of the common citizen, and the limits on guild authority have all been inked in iron. Only one major aspect remains: the balance of economic power between the central and provincial governments. Some argue that greater power to levy taxes gives Samsumbat the resources it needs to defend against enemies that will inevitably march against its people. Others avow that centralization leads to the corruption that they shed so much blood to overthrow, that increased regional autonomy of resource distribution gives the people more room to grow. It is a decision that will reverberate for many years, and both options have their advantages. After a final debate that finished well past midnight, the majority decided that.
