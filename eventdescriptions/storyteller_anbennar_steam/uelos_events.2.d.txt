The girl stood upon the deck as the winds drove them forwards, out to the port they approached. It had been quite some time since she had left that port, and she had, in years since, finally achieved her dream. The girl was a sailor, heading out to the colonies, where she would be by the sea for all her life, and for her there could be no greater treasure in a thousand vaults. And, of course, she would give her thanks to Uelos. The girl would pray beside her hammock, and sing softly to the oceans as she worked the rigging and sails or scrubbed the decks after a long day. She would toss a gold coin to the waves whenever she survived a storm or a battle. Whenever she made landfall, she would spend the evening with like minded believers. They would tell stories, of themselves, of Uelos, and of what was yet to come. Survival was uncertain on the waves, but every moment of it was a treasure.
