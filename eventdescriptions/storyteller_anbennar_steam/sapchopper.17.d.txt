With a single tug, the Blackened Prince freed the ax from its former owner’s crumpled body. Another ork, another failure. Today it was Khagor Sapchopper. Tomorrow? The Prince looked at the ax before tossing it to the side. There was always another. "It is a shame and a waste." And with those tart words, they were gone. Time passed. Not as it did in the Prime Material for the air was too heavy with fey energy. It winded and twisted. And somewhere in those moments, Khagor Sapchopper summoned the energy to look up at the twilight sky. The dancing lights were as far from the caverns of the Serpentspine he was born in as he could imagine. Never had he thought of such a place. But never had he had thought he would become more than a raider. Never had he thought he would lead a clan, his clan, to victory after victory. And never had he thought he would die here, of all places. He smiled. It had been a good and glorious life. And he knew, Clan Sapchopper would continue without him.
