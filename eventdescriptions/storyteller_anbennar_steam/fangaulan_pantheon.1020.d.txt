It is a beautiful day today. We are blessed to walk with the rich soil beneath our feet. But we must enjoy each of these days as they come, for one day each of us will no longer feel the sun upon our faces, and Kanhati the Black Antelope will come to guide our souls on. Let me sing to you a song of how this came to be. Once, all things were unchanging and eternal. The totem animals and all the people lived in peace, and wanted for nothing under the watchful eye of God and his primordials. Antelope lived among the people in those days. But there were forces of darkness and death beyond God’s power, and one day they burst into the world. God sent his primordials to defeat them, but the powers of darkness tricked the primordials into giving birth to the djinn, who killed God and sent the world into turmoil. God was the first to die, but in his dying breaths he called to his two most faithful servants, Antelope and Hyena. God commanded Hyena to eat his corpse, and steward the dark magic in his soul. But God gave Antelope a far more daunting task. When the people died, as God knew they would, he entrusted Antelope with the duty of guiding each of their souls far from the earth to the moon, which hangs in the sky even now as the realm of the ancestors. Crying tears of anguish, Antelope agreed. He took up the light magic of God, and became Kanhati the Black Antelope. His tears hardened into a ruby mask about his face, and in his first act of duty Kanhati laid God’s soul to rest. And this is why we say of the dead.
