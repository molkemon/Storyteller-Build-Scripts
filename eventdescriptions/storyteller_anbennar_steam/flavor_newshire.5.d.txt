There was a knock at the door.  A letter, stamped closed with a wax seal, marked with the symbol of Newshire's fledgling transmutation guild. The organisation was small, but had been very helpful with growing bigger, better crops; at least, they were, until all of the crops died.  As it happens, all of the plants being dead had caused them a rather lot of trouble.  'My name is Layton Kettlebrook,' the page read, 'Grandmaster of the Chloromancy Guild of Newshire. To be direct; we believe that we have a way to end the Red Days.'  The explanation was concise, and all the Chloromancers requested was the necessary facilities and freedom to continue their research, should it turn dramatic, difficult, or. controversial.
