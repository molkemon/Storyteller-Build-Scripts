Golden fields which once stretched forever into the horizon now lie in ruin as salty marshlands, graves to thousands of fallen soldiers. Their eyes forever fixed into the sky and their final moments before death etched on their terrified faces, all perfectly preserved under the marshy water. One can no longer traverse the swamplands without finding beneath their foot either solid iron armor or the body of a fallen soldier. Despite our valiant effort, the endless hordes of deep devils destroyed our armies and ravaged our whole country, leaving no stone unturned. Although Karqašlu have proven to be too vast and powerful for even the deep devils to destroy, with the human toll being measured in equivalent countries and not simply numbers, survival remains bittersweet. Our might once revered in Bulwar, now lies in ruin as neighbors who know little of the menace the deep devils mock our fall at the hand tentacles of mer fish and octopuses. Although Karqašlu will be rebuilt within our lifetimes, our pride will remain ever shattered for many generations until the deep devils are conclusively dealt with. But for now, and perhaps for many millennia to come, the defeat at the hand of the deep devils will continue to remind Karqašlu and Bulwar at large, that the true rules of terrestrial Bulwar lurk deep beneath the sea.
