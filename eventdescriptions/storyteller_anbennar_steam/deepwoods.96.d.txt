She vanished during a summer storm. No one found her footprints, no broken branches, not even a scent trail. Her mother wept into the roots of a tree for days. That was two years ago. She returned today. Same face. Same eyes. But she is no longer a child. She stands tall, her tusks grown in. She wears bark woven cloth none recognize, and speaks slowly, like one unused to conversation. Her parents now look young beside her, her siblings just children. She says, “I lived beneath the boughs. I do not remember hunger. I remember only light and silence.” All eyes look to the Shamans for answers.
