Our greatest failure has never left our mind; even when pushed from our final domain, we never forgot the disappearance of Teranak the Third, the Emperor of Anzara who took all of our strength north to rescue a trapped tribe of lizardfolk and never returned. Centuries later the Ashhana emerged during the Shadowfang Invasion, eternally cementing the Shadow Swamp as our empire's bane. In the wake of the invasion, our Emperor was slain and our empire split in half for almost a millennium. The looming Ashhana, ever east of Ichiadza, have long been a bitter reminder of Teranak's failure, but it is becoming more and more apparent that the swamp to the north is stirring too. Not only are we beset by hag subversion, but the swamp trolls in thrall to the Coven grow ever more active, amassing again and again at our border and conducting terror raids once more. In recent times, we have fortified the border to the north to protect us against their influence, concentrating our defenses around the reclaimed domain at Metat Tagokag. We have hesitated to repeat Teranak the Third’s folly by committing expeditionary forces further north. But we can no longer let the failures of the past deny our safety in the present; whether we like it or not, a second Northern Expedition seems increasingly inevitable if we are to prevent the Shadow Swamp from consuming Taneyas. Our people may have feared for the worst upon the announcement, but now we can seize the chance to seize true safety, once and for all.
