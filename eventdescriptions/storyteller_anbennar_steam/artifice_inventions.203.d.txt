Alchemists, mages, and artificers have always been obsessed with transmutation. Certainly, an individual mage can turn a piece of lead into gold, or grow a tree in a day; but their magics lack the magnitude required to fill a treasury with artificial specie, or satisfy a nation's stomach with instantly grown crops. Transmutation is maintenance intensive, limited, and inefficient at scale, or at least, it used to be. Our artificers have discovered something incredible. the ability to transmute objects en masse. By analyzing the magochemical pathways between objects, in other words, by seeing which objects' magical signatures are closest to each other, we can find the materials which are easiest to transmute into other materials. With this, we can find masses of raw resources which are magically similar to our desired end product. Then, we can scatter pieces of our desired end product throughout the mixture. Then, we can transmute to our heart's desire. With this process, dubbed Pathway Transmutation, the possibilities are endless. For now, we can turn wood into paper, or imbue a dame's tear into metals with ease; but this process shows huge scaling potential. Perhaps, soon, we could transmute entire forests into hardwood; we could turn raw rock into iron and gold; we could manufacture a thousand identical products, all at once, with nothing more than raw resources and magic. The possibilities have only begun to be unraveled for modern transmutative artificery.
