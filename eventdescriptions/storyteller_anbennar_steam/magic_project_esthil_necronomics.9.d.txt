There are, naturally, a great many experiments running at the Black Sepulcher at any given time. The prevailing fascination for the past few months has been trying to create combat-viable Death Wards. However, these experiments invariably run into the same problem. making a ward powerful enough to kill soldiers would drain a soul in a matter of seconds, making it impractical for field usage. A few enterprising youngsters, however, have asked whether soldiers really need to be killed within moments or even killed at all, or if simply causing prolonged low-level harm to enemies of the state would be just as effective. As such, they applied a Death Ward with significantly reduced strength to one of the holding cells, and then extracted a few orks every day to determine whether there was a gradual decrease in their fighting strength. Unfortunately, no such decrease was observed. That experiment might very well have ended there, if not for the fact that a few of the orks were slain, reanimated as zombies, and set to guard their former kin as warnings to the other subjects to behave. Normally, the flesh of a zombie begins to visibly decay and begin rotting within a few days, but the necromancers realized that the zombies they had created and left within the effect of the Death Ward showed no decay at all. It is not yet clear what is causing it, but exposing zombies to Death Wards has shown that the phenomenon is consistent, and that the decay of zombies ceases while within such wards. Given the greater strength of zombies compared to skeletons, it is believed that this discovery could be of great use for fieldwork, since the usage of zombies for such purposes has until now been inhibited by the fact that their rotting flesh would risk spoiling crops. To speed up these investigations, a healthy supply of souls and prisoners would be required. Monthly report of the Black Sepulcher, this month of this year.
