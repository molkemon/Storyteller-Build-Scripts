Lazga could feel this Ozarm’Chadash was wrong. Her brother Gordak, great chief and warlord of clan Cursed Howl, had faced challengers before, and killed all of them. The challenger Gordak faced today, Tuka, was no different… Should be no different. Gordak would not listen, but Lazga knew better. She had known Tuka since they were children. Tuka looked the same he ever had. His chipped tusk, his hunched posture, his narrow, uneven eyes. But he moved wrong, smiled wrong, *felt* wrong.  Gordak, wielding an ax and a small bronze shield, had started with a defensive stance, as usual, stoically deflecting Tuka’s savage axeblows as his opponent was tiring himself out. Tuka’s slashes were brutal but short, chopping away at Gordak’s shield, savage but predictable. Finally, Gordak anticipated a strike and bashed his shield into Tuka’s hand, audibly breaking several fingers. Tuka staggered back, but the fear that flashed in his eyes was immediately subsumed by a black shine. Fey, Lazga realized. As her brother fell, his frozen body cut down by Tuka’s ax, she leapt over the barricade and drove her spear through the creature’s throat.  As the crowd erupted into shouting and chaos, a grin formed on Tuka’s lips, under a pair of pitch black eyes.
