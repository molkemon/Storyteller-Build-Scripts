After the last Vahjevgiivans were driven from the city, the first people from the western part passing through the wall did not walk; the surging crowd carried them. They were met by citizens from the east holding flowers and wine. A spontaneous party started, and joyful music filled the streets. People from both parts climbed the wall and kissed on top of it. Hammers and chisels came out. The symbol of their separation was gone in mere hours, and they started to gather in front of the city hall and demanded for their Khoidarg to join them.
