The rift opens, and I can see. I watch my aunt move among the addicts. Beyond the sickly sweet tang of the nazhyn, I smell the ocean and the faint must of mould. I know with the certainty of a dream that aunt had little personally to do with making this place, for mortals do not always need the touch of a temptress to succumb to their own desires. Yet like the gentle lady who meets those who drown themselves in liquid sweet, Komina feeds on their need and their relief. Where does the pain go, when we gorge ourselves on something wonderful? Into their stomachs, feeding their mana?
