In the wastes of Khaashalg, it has become a common practice to burn a small slip of paper in a slim furnace in prayer to the spirits of the world. These flames are tinted blue by a special powder sold by harimari merchants, connecting the humans of Cangji to the spiritfire which allows them to live in this desolate land. Now, within the largest regimental camp of the celestial military, hundreds of these furnaces blaze with the prayers of the Rukunai and Rünsukhi alike as a human general meets with the harimari commanding officer. Both sides exchange greetings and offer each other gifts of incense, livestock, and clothing, before sitting together on two pillows within the commander’s tent. They discuss for a long time, day passing to dusk. When the sun finally sets on the horizon, both emerge from the tent and look out across the camp, the crowd of human and harimari teeming with anticipation. “We have come too far to not acknowledge the outstanding bravery of our human compatriots,” the harimari commander declares to the audience. “They have given up their homes, their families, and put their lives on the line to defend their country. Although they do not fight, we would surely have lost many battles without them.” He gestures to the human at his side as he continues. “No longer shall humanity be limited to a supporting role in the epic of Cangji. We shall honour you and your service, while amending any dishonours previously given out.” He stands to the crowd, arms raised in exultation, as he finishes the declaration. “Humans are the lifeblood of the wastes of Gozengun, and we shall recognize their critical importance.”
