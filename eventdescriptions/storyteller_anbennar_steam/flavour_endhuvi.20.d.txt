"Move the Society? How do you propose to do that?" cried the Dean of the Cinnabar Lotus Society. The Headmaster chuckled wryly, stroking his long and rather magnificent beard. The wizened harimari was the first official headmaster of the Society since its expansion across the river and was a rather eccentric sort. "Why, with magic of course," he added sotto voce. "And perhaps a good bit of manual labor. But it is necessary. The realm has grown large, and the Society has helped many. The Kharunyana and its tributaries link the whole thing together, so we use it like a highway. That way, we can take the Society on the road, metaphorically, and reach people who would otherwise never have the chance to come here." He turned and led the way along the outside of the debate hall, the waters of the river and voices of passing merchants and traders filling the air around them. "What good is a debate if it's always in the same place? Only people who are able to come here can do it, the same faces, the same types. But if we go off and up and down the river, think of the fresh perspectives we could get, the new ideas. The boats always bring us ideas, why can't we bring ourselves to the ideas?" The Dean groaned a little, "Do you even understand the logistics inherent in that?" "Nope, and I don't want to. We've mediated for those wizards enough when they have a spat; wrangle a few up who know something about enchantment and get it sorted, would you? It's a good idea, and I'll debate you into the water to prove my point if I must." "No, no. You've made your point. I'll see to it." "Good. You can call me Captain too."
