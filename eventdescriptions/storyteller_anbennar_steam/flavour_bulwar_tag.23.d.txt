Between both rivers, and coincidentally in eyeshot of the great canal linking them, sat the formerly crumbling Temple of Bulwar. Even though the main structure of the pyramid was too vast to ever be particularly damaged, the constructions built atop it could be seen. bricks scorched black by fire, rooms bare of any furniture. The sight would be enough to drive any good worshiper of Surael to despair, weeping even as soot stained their knees. Naturally, this was not ever allowed to stand. To any who had the time in the day to watch, watching the restoration of the temple was a slow, if definite process. The structure was slowly cleaned, even as collapsed buildings were replaced. Scaffolds were built overtop of scaffolds, lines of servants carrying valuable-looking ornaments, courtesy mostly of the Aharmandas family, up the ziggurat's steps, restocking the grand construction. It became curious when the construction continued further. There was no cessation of work, only more added to the temple complex over months of work. Yet for all the delay, to finally stand in the temple's center, a brilliant tile mosaic of a sun, and watch the dancers flick their flames here and there, specs of crimson reflected by shining ornamentation.
