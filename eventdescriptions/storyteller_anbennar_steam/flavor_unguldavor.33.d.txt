It is done. From Escin to Gulenhyl and Vernham to Castonath, the flag of Unguldavor waves from every flagpole. The nations built on witchkings, oppression, slavery, ancient titles, and plunder are dead. Tyrants, both human and ork, have been toppled. Civilians, whether human or ork, were liberated. Broken chains fell to the ground along with enchantment spells. In its place stood a nation which remained true to its vision. The monarch knelt beside an altar in the palace. Looking down at the ruler were the statues of Lothane Bluetusk, Corin, Arosha Oakbreaker, and King Grom of the dynasty. These were the heroes who built the new Escann. The monarch looked up at the statues of Arosha and Grom, saying, “Mother Arosha and grandfather, did I make you proud? We fulfilled your dreams. Orks and humans live together in peace and harmony while their children now know a world without hate.” Later that day, the monarch rode at the head of a procession marching through Castonath. Crowds cheered as the soldiers passed by. A halfork woman kissed a human soldier, who looked like he was simultaneously embarrassed yet proud. Another soldier placed his son on his shoulders for a bit. The monarch reached the platform and gave the ruler's speech. “People of Unguldavor, we did it. We fulfilled the dream that Arosha Oakbreaker and King Grom the Great laid out for us.” The crowd went wild with cheers as trumpets and horns were played. Twin banners of Unguldavor unfurled on both sides of the monarch. “Alas, the work is not done. Escann must be rebuilt. We must bury our dead and mourn their loss. Children need parents while mothers need time to recover from losing their children. We need to bring unity to the newly acquired lands and remind them that we are not here to crush their spirits, but to invite them into our homes.” The monarch wiped away a tear before shouting, “For Unguldavor. For Kaztubar.”
