Moorton, moreso than ever, hustled and bustled with the life of commerce and opportunity. Its foundations had been expanded, roads and wells built, and people from all over flocked to live in the gleaming light of the Beronmoors. In a townhouse by the market square sat Lord Marcan Moorton. "I have been told many things about how strange it is to rule from a city instead of a castle or holdfast. But they don't think the coin I have is very strange, and I doubt you do, my lord." He then stood, and picked up a fine piece of parchment, carefully written and sealed. "For your help in bolstering our interests, I shall help you. You shall find your treasuries a little heavier from now on, should you continue to support us."
