Though weaker by far than the ancient and powerful varieties of the Deepwoods, where the feywild itself seeps through the fraying fabric of space and time, minor fey and similar otherworldly spirits nonetheless hold relatively harmless dominion in many wild areas across Halann. The Trialmount is no exception, despite its close proximity to what was once the greatest of human cities, Castonath. The Trialmount approaches a group of floating spirits, struggling to track their wild movements as they zoom in and out of view, chasing each other in the air. The group's attention suddenly shifts as one turns to the approaching Trialmount as the aspirant casts their question to the fey. A second of deliberation; without a single trace of visible communication; and one of their group steps forward to give a cryptic answer. The Trialmount takes this at face value. The giggles of the fey mercifully fade into the distance as the Trialmount continues following their instructions. Every breeze brings echoes of their continuing laughter, twisting the sounds ominously as the rapidly darkening forest grows oppressive. The path eventually leads to the entrance of a sheltered cave, hidden amongst some rocks. Could this be the den of the basilisk? As if triggered by this very thought, the shroud of darkness suddenly lights up with two amber halfmoons of reflected light, the unmistakably feline eyes of a mountain lion. A trap. There is no escaping a fight now.
