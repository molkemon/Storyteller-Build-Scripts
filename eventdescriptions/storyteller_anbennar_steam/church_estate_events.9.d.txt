There has always been some discrepancy between the religion actually taught and defended by the church and the actual faith of the people in the provinces. Most of the time the differences are small enough that the church chooses to ignore them to instead focus on reinforcing the more orthodox spiritual ideas of the people. It seems, however, that certain ideas that are quite unorthodox, if rooted in old traditions, have begun to spread together with our faith among our subjects. The resulting mix of old heathen ideas, popular superstitions and our faith itself has been quite too much for the church to stomach even if it seems to have made conversion a much more attractive proposition for our heathen subjects.
