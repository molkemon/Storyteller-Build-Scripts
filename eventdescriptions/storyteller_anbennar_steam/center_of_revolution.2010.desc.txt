Over the past years, the burghers have become increasingly dissatisfied with their status in the realm. Although their contributions to the treasury through taxes on production and commerce make up most of the nation's revenues, they have little influence over how the state is governed. Indeed, they have looked on in dismay as the monarch charts a path towards the evergreater centralization of royal power, sidelining the checks and balances the estates were once able to impose upon the ruler. They have now come forward with demands that some of the absolutist reforms of recent years be repealed and they be granted further lands to compensate for their contributions. If denied, their dissatisfaction will be immeasurable, and their trust in the government ruined.
