The experiment had seemingly gone off without a hitch, the first subject being safely ensconced in a comalike state as the magic coursed through and left them fundamentally transformed. Locked away with many wards, should they awaken in a rage and attempt to do things, it would be unwise. The people lost in such a rampage could be replaced, but the time spent finding the person with the right essences to channel this magic for the first time, the time our council of gifted Floodborn could be spending doing all their enchantments alongside the allsorare artificers in Wyvernheart, that was the most precious of all. Yet for all those fears of time lost, there is a gentle knock at the door of the innermost wards. A voice, imperious and yet calm, requesting to rejoin their peers. But it is the way they say peers as if they are now on par with our very best already. The way they speak with assurance when before they had been a secondrate arcanist chosen because they would not be missed. And then it is their eyes, the way they do not glow or any such obvious display of power. But the way they look as they go through and begin their new life. That betrays a perspective that they feel apart from what came before, something new and terrible indeed.
