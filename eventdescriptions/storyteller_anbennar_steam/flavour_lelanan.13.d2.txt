The Enahelibe Ritual was our famous fertility ritual, with a Mihipha and priestess conducting an elaborate dance, exciting the participating lovers and their hearts, and preparing them for the sacred act of creating life, increasing their chance. Unfortunately, the context of the fertility ritual meant it was only conducted for couples who could create life. Less orthodox couples like Leli and I were considered less important than a common knife. So I may have been driven by spite when I lobbied to redirect the Mihipha away from the Ritual, leveraging Leli’s immense financial support of the Sisterhood to get what I wanted; my anger was a touch habitual.
