Our lord has officially declared that artificery will be supported by the state. Now, we must figure out what this actually means. Three organizations, all vying to ingratiate themselves with nations across the world, petition our realm for official support. They each promise inventions and innovations the likes of which we could never imagine: and they each require funding the likes of which we do not want to imagine. We can only afford to patronize one of them, so our choice must be made carefully. Our first option, the Society of Brilliant Inventors, serves as the prestigious symbol of all artificery. They throw caution to the wind, giving their members full creative freedom: and with this freedom, they have invented the most iconic machines in Halann. Invest in them, and they might create revolutionary technologies like the Sparkdrive or the TWave Tranceiver. Or they might get distracted, and come up with something utterly useless. To work with them is to put your trust into something that you cannot control. Our second option, the TechnoThaumaturges, are the opposite: philosophers as much as inventors, they see artificery as simply a more scientific branch of traditional magic. Invest in them, and they will study while pulling inspiration from mages, administrators, and scholars alike. To work with them is to have them slowly, deliberately, and conscientiously grind away at whatever the state wants them to. Our third option, the Mechanist Guilds, is a loose coalition of the artisans and engineers of our realm. Invest in them, and they will research decently quickly, and will let us have a reasonable amount of control over what their artificers create. To work with them is to prioritize practicality. With these organizations in mind: who does our lord choose to patronize and support?
