Our ruler has spent years studying and meditating about the true nature of the relationship between Vortza Lesa, the goddess of Forests and Nature, and her husband, Vortzo Laukano, god of Agriculture. All this work has resulted in an extensive theological treaty that has shocked the faithful of our nation to their core. In it, our ruler explains that, in reality, there is no separation between either of the gods, with them being only an aspect of the same entity, a dual deity of Fertility. While Vortzo Lesa is tasked with the fertility of the wild, Vortzo Laukano ensures that the crops and mushrooms tamed and feeds from are growing properly. Their marriage is not a mere political union, it is the true and sacred unity of two beings in one. Our ruler has been almost universally praised for their research and work. There is consensus that the religion policy of our nation must be reoriented to adapt to these findings, however, there are disagreements about exactly how we should go about this. Should we worship both aspects in unity, in a new cult of fertility? Or should we refocus our efforts on Vortzo Laukano, who after all is responsible for the area of fertility that our nation needs to feed itself?
