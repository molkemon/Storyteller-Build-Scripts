My first thought was to just nab a wild one, but that plan was dashed immediately. Apparently they are some sort of invasive species, not meant to be here, and get outcompeted by the local wildlife something fierce without the protection of the Shuvüüsh. Worse, their eggs need the heat of a massive bonfire to hatch, and on the treeless steppes, firewood is more precious than gold, since they do not occur naturally in the cold climate. Well, in Horashesh and the nearby Fangaula, we have plenty of wood. So Kiusz and I made some promises, greased some palms, and sailed back to Bivuzena. When we returned with our cargo full of timber, to help them with their breeding, they were ecstatic. Rewarding us for our efforts, they gifted us several birds, female only, for apparently male birds are a big deal to these folks. We can always come back with more gifts, though. They were well trained, meaning we could easily set up an exhibit at the Menagerie where we let kids ride them, I thought. Apparently the ruler thought that was too risky and children would get injured. Still fuming about that one.
