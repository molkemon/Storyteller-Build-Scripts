Dwalra spent a time mourning beside the tomb of the man she loved. A week in total, sitting in silence, eating only the water and plain bread a few close friends brought her. A week of reflection and reassessment and grief. And then she left, having completed only a twentieth of the standard mourning period, a fiftieth of the time customary for a hero or other great figure. Dwarven eyes watched her as she proceeded back to her home, brows furrowed, a glare or two levelled. But, like orkrend through orkflesh, she did not stop. At the doorway to the house she had lived in for decades, which no longer quite felt like a home, she stopped. Turned. Gave the crowd that had trailed her a withering look, let out a soft sigh. Squeezed her nails into her palm, wishing there was a calloused hand she could squeeze instead. But instead she went inside, down the steps. "None of them knew him, not all of him. None of them know how to honour him. That's something only I know." Then she looked up, saw her son standing in his childhood bedroom, tears still dripping down his face. "Something only we know."
