It is a truth not often admitted in the more chauvinistic parts of the Kavalakuttam, but the Kheionai are master debaters. Better than us, in fact. And despite their history of slavery, or inequality, or colonization and exploitation, they know more than any how to build a strong, stable and legally secure civic state. No wonder then that as soon as Kheionai Kavalali managed to get into the Kavalakuttam, they blew apart Orenvalyam’s constitutional foundations in an instant. essentially, they claimed that there really aren’t any. In many places, this dynamic foundation for our state is not an issue per se – a future vote can simply undo what a prior vote commanded. However, in the realm of regional governance, things are quite different. Because the members of the Kavalakuttam are selected from the Kavalali of defined geographical areas, should a powerful group gain powers over how these areas are defined and run, that would give them an implicit sort of power over the Kavalali of those areas… and an implicit sort of power over how those Kavalali are selected. It would be very difficult for the Kavalali to undo such a change – they could face removal, or censure, both on the chamber floor and outside it. It would permanently advantage whoever first claimed this responsibility. And so, a great debate emerged. The Monasticists are calling this tyranny by other means, and say that the sanctity and autonomy of Cells and other sorts of regional government must be respected. The Kamrayakvalists and Kavalalists, meanwhile, argue that their leader and the High Kavalali respectively should possess this right, in order to safeguard their position. Whatever is decided, it will likely define Orenvalyam’s politics for centuries to come.
