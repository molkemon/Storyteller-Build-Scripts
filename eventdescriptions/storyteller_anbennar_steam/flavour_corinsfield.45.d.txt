The man is dirtied and his clothes are ragged. His hands and feet are tied to a pyre. The man is quite miffed, honestly; not at his execution, but at the false pretenses of it. Yes, he was a blaggard and a blackguard and a brigand, but not a mage; perhaps, if he had magic, he could have made something of himself once upon a time. Instead, he was forced into the gutters; that is, until he was forced out of the towns altogether. Why then, should he have maintained his humanity, if his so-called neighbors refused to award it to him? His fight; fight for money, fight for love, fight for something he wasn’t altogether sure of, really; was about to come to an end. And he couldn’t really force himself to feel anything but mild annoyance at the fact they had gotten him for the wrong thing. As the flames were lit and the cheering faces of the crowd were blotted out by smoke and flames, the man felt absolutely nothing. Nothing at all.
