In the core of South Castonath, and indeed, the city itself, sits the district of The Crags. Stacked with blocks of stone housing complexes and residences many stories high, The Crags is the largest residential district in Castonath and where many of the city's residents have made their home in the millennia since the city's construction by Balgar. The vagaries of time and conflict, however, have left an inevitable mark on the district. Nowadays, The Crags is a veritable maze of stained and crumbling stone and haphazard construction and renovations by the wellmeaning citizens of the city. Its evershifting layout and network of back tunnels and hidden alleyways present a major impediment to our tax officials and development initiatives.  With enough investment, the deteriorating nature of The Crags can be slowed and reversed. The buildings and back alleys can be mapped, the ubiquitous presence of 'temporary' scaffolding and barriers can be cleaned up, the decaying stone facades and crumbling residences restored to their former glory. However, the Castonath patricians argue that this would draw our attention and take away resources that could be invested instead in substantially more important areas of Castonath, namely their estates and properties in North Castonath and the Upper District. Besides, they say, the people of the Crags have been successfully fending for themselves for generations.
