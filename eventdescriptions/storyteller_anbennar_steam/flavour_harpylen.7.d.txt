The origins of our august people were never well understood, though this can be said of all the peoples of Halann. Tales of our past ever roosted in song, and while this makes for easy listening, the tales grow changeable, with any voice choosing to sing their truth. Thus it was important for the Queendom to establish a sense of cultural unity. We are all daughters of Firanya, but what does this mean? Scribes were gathered, songs were assembled, and a council was convened, to sing and to write of our history, so that Harpylen's song would grow sturdier than words on the wind. A truth was written, a silent song on paper, lesser perhaps than the living songs of the moment, but something to remember. The song was of the first Harpylen, and the first queen Firanya. In ages past, before the elves, Firanya and her flocks were rulers of Bulwar. Little is known of this time, but songs were sung of glory and plenty, of flocks roosting on every peak, and our human charges living in peace, with great flocks of sheep in the valleys below. The songs sing of a simpler time, where we stood alone at the peak of the world, with Firanya as our queen, and Bulwar as our garden. But Firanya fell, slain by age or arrow, and the songs say much and nothing of how, only that she was gone. Gnolls came to Bulwar, and Firanya's daughters squabbled over the queen's throne, like vultures over a carcass. Gnolls could not reach our peaks, and so we did not fear them. But they struck our human flocks, and brought them suffering, so that they grew to hate us and the gnolls both. Soon, all of Bulwar was ablaze, and even our roosts were choked by the smoke of the burning valleys. And so, the flocks of Harpylen scattered, to the four corners of the world. The first Queendom had ended. This asked a question of our new Harpylen.
