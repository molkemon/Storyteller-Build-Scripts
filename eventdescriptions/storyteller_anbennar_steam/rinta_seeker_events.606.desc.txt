On their way to the Qasri of Abeesooyinka, one of our Mwalim caravans happened to stop by an impoverished village, far away for the riches of the Dasr Sharaf to reach them. Here, citizens lacked access to any proper food or water source, with starving children being the norm on the streets. It took the Mwalim one day to fix all of this. The village found themselves with a large, intricate well, an abundance of cattle and goats, and a new town hall prepared with food overnight. The villagers, hesitant at first, saw the Mwalim clergy and immediately thanked them. The village ate, worked, and relaxed after many years of starving and isolation. The news has reached us a day before they are supposed to arrive. Members of your court have told me to suggest a small ceremony for these Mwalim for their work well done. I can plan out the commodities if you would like.
