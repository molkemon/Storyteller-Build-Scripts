Pashainéy mages have long sat uncomfortably within the state of many guilds, resentful at the lack of special privileges or legislative measures in their favour. Instead, they are treated as only one body among many, competing for public attention and investment with guilds of much greater size. The heart of a mage lusts for preferential treatment, it seems, and now many local Arcane Guilds have entirely closed off their proceedings to the public, rendering their political activities suddenly opaque and their tendencies obscured. Full mage conclaves are now being held with no oversight, and parliamentarians urge action. It would be a technical contravention of guild right to insist on public access to such gatherings, but the threat to the state from these hotbeds of sedition may warrant such extreme measures.
