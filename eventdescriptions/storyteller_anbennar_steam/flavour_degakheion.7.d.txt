Hail, Kylakas, here is my report in writing, as requested. The Kheions have been definitively pacified now. Street fighting of course ceased weeks ago, but even beyond that, the organization that supported the riots has been definitively crushed. Raids in Arpedifér’s western district, the north dockyards of Ormam, and in Section Októ of Lokemeion’s Grand Library destroyed their last remaining supplies of weapons. With the riot leaders Váithos, Súalides, and Kenaktízos killed over the past week, we are certain the rebel leadership has been decapitated. They should be incapable of further action, though that is not a guarantee. We will need to gauge the strength of their operations in Cósan first; I have sent three galleys and a contingent of Phylaxana for that purpose. The island nonetheless remains under our control; this is more investigative. As for the captive leadership, the last were publicly executed last week. The recruiter Chraméas made a scene on the gallows, which should diminish his reputation going forward, a boon for containing any further organization in Kimánis. Concerning the rank and file, postmortem crucifixion, as you suggested, has worked brilliantly. It is a perfect mix between showing consequence and limiting the potential for responsive unrest. I have sent word that this should be replicated elsewhere. Once those are confirmed, the prison population should have returned to pre-revolt levels by the end of the year. As for the general mood, the Karkares seem to have given our operations a name of their own. “the Days of Torment.” I would advise against using this in anything official, but I do not believe it should be suppressed generally, either. It is said with fear, not indignant outrage. I think the Karkares will be pacified for quite some time yet. Signed, A. Genepátheras, Stratéodegis, Garrison Command.
