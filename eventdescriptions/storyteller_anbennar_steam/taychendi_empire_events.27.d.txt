The chieftain fell to one knee as the fight finally left him. Cuts and bruises covered his body and the ruler's. The fight had taken everything both of them had, as sabre, shield and warhammer clashed, an audience of tens of thousands of Ozgar orks and Taychendi watching on. The ruler felt halfdead, bones cracked and blood and sweat mixing as they dripped down his face, but even still, it was his sabre that was aimed at the orkish chieftain’s neck. The ruler had won. The chieftain looked up, straight into the ruler’s eyes. “You bested me… a good fight, but you bested me…” the ork smiled despite himself; they had given both their peoples a show they would not soon forget. “Congratulations… chieftain.” The smile wore away as the Taychendi in attendance cheered, and the orkish new chieftain understood exactly what he had lost. This was an Ozarm Chadash, and now, the ruler found them the undisputed master of all orks in his realm. The Taychendi elf took their sword away and turned to the crowd. The orks had looks of existential fear written on their faces. Their existence hinged on their decision now. Many of the warlords wanted to repossess the orks, apparently created by Ducaniel, the scion of Aesadas, so recently rediscovered but already regarded with a mix of awe and derision. Some of those wanted to put them to work in the mines and plantations, as the Cannorians had done, but was that not a waste of their martial abilities? Others wanted to induct them into a specialised force of auxiliaries, send them as shock troops against our enemies. There were those still that wanted to simply leave them alone. Had they not suffered enough? The ruler supposed they were the judge of that, now. The ruler called to the crowd, “Orcs of Taychend…”
