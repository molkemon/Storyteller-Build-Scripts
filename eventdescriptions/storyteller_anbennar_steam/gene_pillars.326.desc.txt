The next chamber we enter barely resembles a chamber at all; it takes us a long moment to get our bearings. It is as if the walls have melted, as if the rigid cuboidal logic of the Pillar was suddenly abandoned in this one spot by the Precursors, in favour of a vague naturalism. Indeed, every shape in this place has been smoothed out oddly. The landscape is utterly alien, somehow incomplete, like a remembering in a dream where everything is indistinct. We learn why this is almost immediately; a jet of some fluid comes hurtling at us, and where it lands it audibly sizzles. The creature which waddles angrily at us is like some huge demented toddler, clicking and whining through what we now see is a beak. The group scatters; the creature spits its acid again, its feathered bulk pounding the battered ground as it chases us. Clearly this thing is an affront to reality. Our spirit of inquiry vis a vis the manyhued panoply of creation gives way to a more salient question: how are we getting out of here?
