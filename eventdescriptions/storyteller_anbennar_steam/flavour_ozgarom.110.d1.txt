Ye who suffer under the slaver's yoke; ye who in the shadow of the workshop must tend the welts upon your back; ye who know what it is to suffer... there is an Ozgar ork at your side, even now. This is the pledge of the Ozgar people, we who have been dealt misery and who refuse to deal it in turn. We do not promise an end to suffering; we do not promise only peace. Only the knowledge, in the depths of despair, that we are searching for you. The maroons of Ozgarom kept the date secret. Many thousands of them, all aware, and not a single one a snitch. Perhaps there were those, paid by the colonial authorities to keep tabs on their settlement, who even in the depths of their cynicism could not betray their fellows. Perhaps for just a moment they felt that perfect hope. this might work. Perhaps this is why they said nothing; perhaps this is why Ozgarom caught everyone by surprise. Overnight, a country. Already the Ozgar orks have declared the end of slavery and an end to the expropriation of the native Soruinics. They demand that estate and plantation owners leave immediately, forfeit their property and submit to its division among their labouring orks. They welcome surrender, but they fully expect to fight.
