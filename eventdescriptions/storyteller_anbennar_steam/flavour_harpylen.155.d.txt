When fashioning a delicate instrument such as a lyre, great care must be taken with the choice of proper materials for all its parts. As a handcrafted product of artisanry, it does not only have a functional role, but aesthetic value as well. Thus, the choice of decorations, while seemingly frivolous, plays an essential role in determining the overall effect of an instrument on its audience. Yet as the saying goes: art is subjective. Some amongst the people involved in the project prefer a plain and unadorned appearance. Others have suggested the addition of delicate etchings and ornamentations. The most extravagant proposal involves the use of engravings made of pure Dames tear. How should our magical lyres be adorned?
