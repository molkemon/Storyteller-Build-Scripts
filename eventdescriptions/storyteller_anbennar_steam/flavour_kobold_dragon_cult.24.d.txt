The baggage train stretches on for miles into the distance, snaking through passageways like a dragon in the wind. Artificers, logisticians, officers, and dragonpriests from across the land have all come to take part in the grand expedition, and none have arrived emptyhanded: each provides their own moral support, supplies, and innovations for the journey. Buttresed alongside their ranks are countless Goldscales, singing the songs once taught to them by the great dragon of gold. There is nowhere more celebratory, or more intrepid, than the gates of Verkal Dromak. As part of the expedition, thousands of Ascended kobolds have joined the ranks. From the front, they use their claws to break landslides into rubble; from the air, they survey for the best passageways through the mountains; from the back, they aid in pulling supplies up the mountain. The iridescent mix of scale, claw, and wing makes the march look almost like a parade.
In their ranks, too, come actual dragons. Jyntas arrived in ornate garb, silks dyed with a brassy sheen. For the past thousand years, she watched Balris fly back and forth from the Jade Mountains: commonly after the Dragonwake, but then less and less until he was no longer seen at all. It is only from these flights, and Jyntas recounting of them, that the expedition knew where Balris might be.
Alongside her stands Varlengeilt, who decided to sail from one port to another. Standing in royal regalia, he stares up at the mountains which seem taller than even the cliffs separating lands. He wonders if Balris ever knew his father.
