The metal men approached the line of stalwart dispossessed. With no command for violence yet, their weapons stayed at rest. The rioters were merciless to oligarchic drones. They chiselled through their outer layers until unoxidized, they shone. The leaders could not stand to see their prized tools battered, still. One took the lead; he made a choice, and then he uttered, "Kill." When metal met the people's flesh, it did what metal does. Where once there stood titanium's flower, now rested meat and blood. Commanders then all stood aghast, who had set their toys to rend. The tyrant smiled to hear the last, "Titanium does not bend."
