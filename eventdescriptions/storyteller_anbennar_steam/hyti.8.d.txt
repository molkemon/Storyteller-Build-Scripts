Shazstundihr hadn't seen so many goblins since the Exodus. A mass of downatheel goblins, slowly trudging across the Marble Gate, the underbelly of Aqatbar moving out as one to new pastures. Not greener ones, either, as was clearly shown in the migrants’ expressions; a mixture of resignation and despair was stamped across their faces. "The first time I felt the sun on my skin is still one of my happiest memories; and yet I'm going back down again... I hate my life." In the time of Exodus it was an entire people on the move, the children, the women, the elderly all searching for a place to finally be free of the orks. That wasn't the case now; you'd see very few families among the crowd. Nobody wanted their child to grow up in the caverns. "They say there's gonna be new opportunities inside the mountains, with free gold given by the Harpy Queen... And I believe them, I won't miss going to bed hungry; still, I hate it." Sticking out like a sore thumb, a handful of brighteyed harpies were also marching downwards, earning sneering looks from everyone present. A betting group was quickly formed; no goblin wagered on any harpy resisting more than two months underground, no matter the payout.
