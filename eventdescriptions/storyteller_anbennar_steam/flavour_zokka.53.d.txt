The text carved into the stone is a century old, now. Every packmate learns it, in time. Tsavuun is different. She ignores the stones and their words. She spits on them. The acolyte beside her recoils in bafflement. "Those are sacred," he hisses. "They are soft," she replies, snarling. "They are Zokka's words." "No," she says, eyes gleaming in the firelight, "they are the words of someone who tried to understand him." The wind coils around her like a beast. Tsavuun bends, using her wicked claws to scrape new grooves into the rock, tearing through the old script. The symbols shift beneath her hands; warped, crude, and new. She does not listen for Zokka's voice. She does not dream of the future, or speak for those not yet born. She walks until the world bleeds, and calls that progress. Xhuvari and her brood are long, long gone. The time of Ikram will soon be past, and the Kaxhkeddax crave something different. Tsavuun is the here and now, and she burns hotter than any of them. Her first sermon is given in the fighting pit, barefanged and bloodsoaked. She shatters three jaws and loses one tooth. It grows back sharper. Later, when they write down her words, the scribes will realize something odd. she never quoted the Once And Future Xhazobain.
