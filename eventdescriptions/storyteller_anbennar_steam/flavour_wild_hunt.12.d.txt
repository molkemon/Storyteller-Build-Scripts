A verifiable haunting requires both consistency and witnesses. For the last month, a ghostly centaur has appeared within the fields of the capital. Bigger than even our mightiest warriors, their presence was initially seen as a sign of good fortune. A glorious soldier of wars past, here to see us regain our former glory. Then the performance started. Invisible swords cut at his flesh. Arrows exposed muscle. Grass was pinned down by what must have been blood. Half an arm fell to the ground. The rest of the warrior wasn't too far behind. Night after night this grisly display was repeated. Unseen foes cutting down what would have easily been the strongest centaur in living memory. The few remaining shamans were clueless. The scarce amount of diplomats were even more so. Even when soldiers attempted to protect the centaur from the unseen foes, its fate was the same. After weeks of the spectacle repeating itself, a young soldier, as of yet unproven in any combat, asked the ghost if there was any way to avoid his fate. The warrior stopped fighting even as vicious wounds still appeared across his body. Solemnly, he whispered a single word that the young centaur would never quite be able to forget.
