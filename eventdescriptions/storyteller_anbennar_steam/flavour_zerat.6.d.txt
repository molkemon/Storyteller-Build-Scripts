The nobles of Anzara pour into court once more, the mood triumphant and our splendor front and centre. Some have known us since we were at our weakest: they are seated closest to us as our most trusted lords and advisors. Some came to know us as the resurgent Empire we have been recently, the ascendant power of Taneyas: unproven loyalties place them further afield, beyond the inner circle. But some had us pegged as a failing state, the echo of the old and an obstacle to be conquered and destroyed. They sit far from power, praying for our mercy for their mistakes. At the heart of the room sits the monarch, resplendent as the hegemon of Taneyas once more. Those outside our court see this as mere vanity; ceremony for ceremony’s sake. Yet we have learnt from our failings. Such pomp isn’t for vanity but to reward loyalty, to show that we are returning to our peak and that we no longer need to bow to the short-term desires of lesser lords. For the first time in centuries, the truly loyal outnumber the disloyal, and we are no longer so easily doubted as before. Whilst our glory is on the minds of the lords, we cannot help but look outwards, beyond Taneyas and to the future. We may have returned to our full prestige, but we are not immortal, and to neglect our end would be foolhardy. Our purpose remains the same: to prepare Halann for the arrival of the Final Empire. Whether that means building Taneyas into the cradle of civilization it was always meant to be, or preventing any threats from old or new rivals from rising, we will ensure the prophecy remains intact.
