The memory of the crack of the lash and the weight of shackles is a familiar one to the Bulwari people. For millennia, foul demon worshipping Gnolls have harassed the true heirs of the Garden, and done all in their power to place them in chains, to either work or burn. A cultural scar, thus, has formed, and a hatred of the foul practice of slavery, for most. However, slavery is not always shackles of iron and cracking whips; it is just as often words on paper, the weight of debt on a neck, that compels one's labors. Whilst rare to find someone literally clasped in irons within their city, the latter is far more common in Azkabar. Obviously, cracking down is a necessity. Allowing such practices to run rampant without any oversight is barbaric and makes them only marginally better than the Gnolls. But the question is how harsh such a crackdown should be. Shall the practice be washed away into the annals of history, or more sternly regulated? After all, a laborer who is not paid is a laborer who does not cost.
