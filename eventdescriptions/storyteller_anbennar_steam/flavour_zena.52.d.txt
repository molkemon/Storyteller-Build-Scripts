Now, I wasn’t about to convert to the Chahinanito faith anytime soon, and neither was Kiusz. So when the ruler told us to fetch an animal for the Menagerie in this new continent while the jungles of the Effelai were offlimits, well, we thought we were done for. But as luck would have it, if you sailed all the damn way around, you’d come across a nice little forest and lake, and deeper inland, a plain with some nice beauties. Now, these critters weren’t anywhere near as exotic as the stuff Kiusz and I found later in our travels, but they were good starting points. We found several tribes in Chendhya who rode jaguars, some powerful and mighty, but some sleek, speedy beasts with bright blue eyes. the Apaxagaeda. The Kaeorg tribe was chief among these riders, and, despite their initial hesitation given Kiusz’ scaled hide, were noble folks. We lived among them for years, learning their customs and faith. They considered themselves the most devout of the Chendhyans, and Kiusz and I regularly hunted with them and partook in their rituals. Upon the slaughter of the various big game in the plains it was customary to honor their sacrifice and assist the liberation of their souls. We sat around their fires and discovered the stories of their past from their Sadaun, or priests, and shared the stories of our Noukahi heroes. Near the end of our time with them, they were even debating whether to teach us how to ride their Apaxagaeda. But some secrets are far too precious to share within these pages.
