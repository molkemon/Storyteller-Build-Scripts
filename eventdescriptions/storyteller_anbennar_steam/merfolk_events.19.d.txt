The ministry chamber doors swing inward as a nervous spy scampers inside. "My lord," he realigns his disheveled cloak, billowed by the speed of news, "there is a stirring beneath the waves in a distant country." Setting duplicated letters, coded messages, and a scrawled map canvassing the latest landmarks in the merfolk's aquatic territory before the lord, the spy spreads the pages to best illustrate the full picture. "The merfolk in a distant country have undergone a drastic realignment. The details remain murky on who, how, and why, but the result is undeniable: a new force has arisen to lead their community, painting their former surface-friendly policies as unpatriotic. We are uncertain how isolationist their intentions are, but they are already demanding tribute from a distant country as a key goal of their new government, intending to sour relations." Our ruler considers our nation's history with the merfolk. Though the merfolk peoples are just as diverse and divided as the surface, the settlement's recent realignment represents a vexing development on the path to improve relations with the ocean foreigners. The spy remains tense, asking only: "What shall I relay as our policy, my lord?"
