Correcting the downward trajectory Bulwari magic has followed for several centuries by now is a truly monumental task. And it is not made any easier by the constant veiled infighting, intrigues, and mutual distrust of the magi clans. To foster a spirit of cooperation, one of our courtly advisors has suggested the establishment of a new Eka, or palace, filled with libraries, lecture halls, laboratories, and dormitories, open for all magi within our realm, regardless of which clan claims their allegiance. In this EkaAmanu, the Palace of Knowledge, they may study our shared magical heritage and freely exchange new ideas. Somewhat surprisingly given their famously insular nature, the magi clans have proven quite open to the idea; however, a new unexpected obstacle has arisen. Several of the estates within our realm are openly vying to provide the land and funds needed for the construction of the EkaAmanu in a blatant attempt to curry favour with and gain influence over the ascendant mages. Now the thorny decision of who should have this honour lies with the monarch. For while the chosen estate will surely remember such a favour, the others will consider it a slight and may make their displeasure known.
