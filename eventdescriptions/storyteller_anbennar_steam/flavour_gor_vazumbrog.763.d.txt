They watch me. Always watching. Every whispered word, every sidelong glance; it is all meant for me, against me. My own council, my own court, the ones who bow and scrape, they hide knives beneath their robes. I see the way they speak when I enter, the way they stop when I look. They think I do not notice. Fools. Traitors. And the reports; tampered. The scouts lie. The forgemasters lie. How could we have lost so many men without sabotage? How does ore rot in the vaults? The numbers shift, never adding up, never steady. They bleed me dry with numbers. I sign my own destruction with my own hand. The food tastes of bitterness. I eat, but hunger gnaws. The drink dulls my mind. My body weakens. I hear them whisper of my health; unfit to rule they say. Ailing they say. But I am strong. I am awake. Even the stone moves against me. The tunnels twist, unfamiliar. The walls lean in, listen. Something speaks through them. Not voices, but intent. There is a presence in the dark. It wants me gone. But I see them all. I see through them. And when the time comes, I will act. I will act before they do.
