We were not so naive as to expect orkish rule to go down well among the human potentates of the Reach. The standard grumbling has of late risen to a hum of legend, however, the sort of petty efforts at propaganda to which men turn when they doubt their cause’s strength. Those few human agents who are willing to work on our behalf report the telling of certain tales in taverns of some lord or another; old legends, sagas from the north on themes of unjust leadership, righteous revolution, and so on. They also report private visits of one lord to another, sudden friendships between merchantcaptains and local fiefholders. Can we be certain such developments are a sign of open rebellion? It matters not. It is difficult even now for us to penetrate the closed, grim-mouthed social circles of the Reachmen, and thus it is all the more important to act before such quiet men open their mouths. We simply must take the initiative. Whether we choose the carrot or the stick, let our actions be decisive.
