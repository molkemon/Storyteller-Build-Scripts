The sun in Tefegeben beats down heavy upon the domed roof of the song hall, glistening rays reflecting off the smooth tiling, a stark contrast to the thatched huts all around. Inside the Sirens train, blade and talon put to good use in relentless drill, voices warmed and tested; honed into a weapon as sharp as any other. Outside there is no hint of the danger that lives within, none except the music that slips free from window and door, loud enough to be heard all throughout the square where the hall resides. Curious humans hear the voices and stop, as if transfixed. Many gather to listen, sitting and taking in the song. Haunting melodies bring tears, whilst joyous tunes bring cheers, as if the sounds were not a hymn to death.
