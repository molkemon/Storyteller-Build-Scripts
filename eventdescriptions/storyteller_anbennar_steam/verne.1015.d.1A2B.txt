My fault, of course. She wanted to go slower. Cassie wasn’t military; she didn’t have any experience with things like this. And she was too brave to be tomb raiding in the desert, too principled. You know how crazy it is that with no warning whatsoever, she almost beat a trap like that? That’s crazy evocation, fighting against genie magic from however many centuries ago. And she almost beat it. I don’t remember what we did next, exactly. We just kept it moving. I was thinking about the leaves falling in the gardens. I was thinking about the look in Cassie’s face, seeing someone in me who’d never existed. A million years ago, knowing all I’d ever do is disappoint her.
