As the sun rises over Kheterat, one of the first buildings hit by its rays is the House of Golden Chains, an ornate palace home to the imperial bureaucracy of the same name. The palace is one hundred twenty cubits high and has a square base of one hundred fifty cubits, and at its apex is a dome of gold inscribed with images of the Khet and the gnollish masters of the empire, so that all within its walls do not forget on whose behalf they labor. As the day drags on, the complex accumulates the detritus of a day of busy administration; indeed, the House of Golden Chains is the single largest consumer of paper in Konolkhatep. Some of the clutter is cleared away when vast pyres are erected in the south and east gardens during the noontime break in order to burn the confidential letters received from governors and the reports of disfavored slaves, and the quantity of paper burned is so great that the resulting column of smoke is said to be visible from Nirat. When the sun sets for the day, those looking directly at the House of Golden Chains might be forgiven for not noticing, so vast is the number of lamps burned on each floor of the great palace. Such illumination allows the slaves to work their most unKhetish hours all the way through the night.
