The monarch had hardly entered the council chamber, much less sat down, before the local nobles were clamouring over each other in selfrighteous fury. Countless entreaties from those assembled here today had brought the monarch away from the throne. “We have been hearing the strangest things at night.” “It is unnatural.” “They must be stopped.” The monarch raised their hand for silence. A moment before they could get a straight answer about what the nobles were upset about today, the council doors swung open. Anger at the interruption gave way to curiosity at the scruffy mage’s shocked demeanour. The room of nobles turned to unleash their indignation at one of the mages responsible for the rumors they had come to petition the monarch to investigate when they were cut off. “It’s alive,” shouted the mage, throwing up her hands in exultant triumph. Pulling on the monarch’s sleeve like an excited child, the woman led the monarch from the council chambers to the bowels of Gulletpeaks’s imposing mage tower, nobles trailing behind indignantly. There, deep below the city streets, an abattoir’s bouquet of rancid meat and festering wounds greeted the royal party. A clothcovered table sat in the middle of the laboratory. The thing that was revealed by the excited mage elicited a wave of shock and fainting spells from the assembled nobles. Humanoid in shape only, the thing bulged with muscles in all the wrong places. Knuckles dragged along the floor as bloodshot eyes tried to escape a tortured face. One could almost see steam emanating from its greentinged skin. All were aghast, except for the monarch. They had one question on their mind.
