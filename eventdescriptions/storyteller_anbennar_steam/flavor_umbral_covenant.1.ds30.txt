Watch the village woman as she chases the cobra towards her crumbling garden wall. She brandishes her broom and tuts and makes faces, for the cobra is fond of neither noise nor size. What a madam the woman thinks she is, stamping her feet; how she shrieks when the cobra rears, when the cobra strikes. Yet it is not the bite that kills this stupid village woman. It is the poison left to fester. It is what the snake has grown within herself. Return, daughter, my righteous poison, and make the whole family weep.
