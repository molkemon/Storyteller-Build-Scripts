The survivors who were complicit in the incident have been understandably somewhat reluctant to talk, so we have had to piece together what happened through scattered testimony. This is what we know for sure: Huelotlake and Ninhua have disliked one another for years, and every opportunity they had to interact with one another out in the field only increased that loathing. At some opportunity they had to meet, Huelotlake decided that the army would be better off without Ninhua entirely, and challenged Ninhua to a duel. Somehow, both Huelotlake and Ninhua were able to enlist their staff officers in a scheme to conceal their absence. Neither general was particularly invested in the training and operation of their army, so the men did not notice when they went missing. The generals met on neutral ground to commence their duel. They fought using ranged weapons, and began the duel at thirty paces. Unfortunately, neither of them could hit the other at that range, so they advanced to twenty paces. Neither was hit at that range either; there may have been some malfunction with the armaments. They advanced inward until they were face to face with one another. At that point, both of the generals revealed hidden blades, and the duel degenerated into a knifefight. In the end, both bled out from their wounds. Somehow, this complete breakdown in military discipline, officer conduct, and simple good sense reached Moi’okalakupua before high command itself. He has not stopped laughing for days.
