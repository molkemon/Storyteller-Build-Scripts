Representative Toezazen stated that the argumentation has been clear. No religious body within the state is willing to allow for the others to preach to the Xhazobkultists. It appears to this body that to favor any one faith in this matter would be a violation of the state's principles, and that to allow unfettered preaching by all parties will simply create internal conflict. Representative Abad suggested circumventing the issue altogether by inviting missionaries from abroad to do the work instead. There can be no internal conflict of interest if all the proselytizing party in question is external in origin. Representative Szelyametes called this absurd, asking if they should allow foreigners to preach within their midst, stating that this is inviting religious turmoil. Representative Abad countered that they ignore something they themselves demonstrate, noting that if there is one thing all Bulwari can agree upon, it is a distrust of foreign missionaries. After some murmurs of laughter crossed the parliament, Representative Szelyametes conceded that perhaps they have a point, adding that at least the foreigners won't try to put anyone on pyres, Surael willing.
