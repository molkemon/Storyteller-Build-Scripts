For a people so obsessed with trials, it should be little surprise that up has sprung a profession of public defenders; armed with razor-sharp wit and word-by-word memory of the holy hymns, these defenders are a force to be reckoned with, to no small frustration of the inquisitors. One amongst them, a Goodman Selgal GoodFaith, has come out above the rest; the people say that not a single witch he has defended has gone to pyre. Such competence is inspiring; and deeply disturbing. Might he be a witch himself, using foul enchantments to bend our righteous judges to his whims? Even if he isn’t, can we really presume one is innocent prior to their conviction of guilt? Let the final man he defends be himself; we will issue a final declaration of his guilt, or find a better use for his tongue of gold.
