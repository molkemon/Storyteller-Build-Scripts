North Yanshen, a region of hills and pastures, has long upheld the unwritten custom of rotating communal grazing on untended lands. Recently, a noble family in southwestern North Yanshen expanded their estate and irrigation, claiming nearby wildlands—traditionally used by herders—as private, citing labor reach as justification. They expelled the herders and sought formal land registration, sparking widespread outrage. Surrounding villagers protested, some even arming themselves. While herders demand protection of traditional grazing rights, some gentry see this as an opportunity to pilot land reform. Though local for now, the issue has drawn broader attention.
