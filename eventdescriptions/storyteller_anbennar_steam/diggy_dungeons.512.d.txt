Unfazed by the screeching cries of the bats, the archers identified their targets in mere minutes. Like a well-oiled machine, they fully draw and hold their arrows in unison for a few agonizing seconds. As the cry of "Fire" rings across their ranks, a cloud of arrows sails gracefully up, piercing countless bats. The remaining bats shriek and swoop, their fangs sharper and hide tougher than expected, but with their numbers so thin, casualties from the expedition are left few and far between. While tending to the wounded, a cleric notes that many bats were killed with a single shot through their eye, leaving their unusual reddish hide intact. This unique, fire-resistant pelt will be of interest to many a craftsman back home.
