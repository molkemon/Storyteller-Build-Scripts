The marketplaces in our nation pray for good fortunes, bountiful crops, quality goods, and consistent dealings with all the hallmarks of worship and superstition. The efficacy of these commercial rites and holy schemes remained in doubt until, as if guided by some divine force, a windfall of thundering hooves graced our stalls. A wave of centaur clans, selfsufficient and roving, arrived at markets across our nation, hoping to stock up on needed materials before their next migration. Light on coin, they bartered by flaunting goods they created and collected on their long journeys across the world. tested tools, unfamiliar weaves and fashions, rare herbs, and more. Our merchants eagerly pine for new customers and sensational goods, but a select few view these fairweather equine purveyors unworthy of commercial grace. What will we do with these centaur travelers?
