The Negotiation Nexus, first imagined in the literary classic “Please Create the Negotiation Nexus,” was envisioned as a machine that could derive everything a person desires by listening to their speech. It could quantify those desires, then compare them to those of other people. By doing so, it would solve all conflicts, all debates, and all disagreements; all could be brought onto the same page when the Negotiation Nexus determined the best course of action. Through numbers, it was theorized, all strife could be solved. In reality, the Negotiation Nexus we have created works slightly differently. It turns out that language is imprecise, so our Nexus does some minor mindreading alongside speech analysis. Also, since it is hard to quantify desires, there needs to be a personal element. As such, we have redirected the Nexus's outflows to our diplomats and lawyers. Of course, since it would be unfair to our nation's interests to let enemies have access to our minds, we only let our officials see the Nexus's outputs. In general, though, we have certainly stayed faithful to the original vision of the Negotiation Nexus. Through giving ourselves every possible advantage in diplomacy, all strife can be solved.
