The caverns of Danazgubli are the perfect crucible for my work. Down here, in the choking dark, no prying eyes interrupt and no screams carry beyond the stone. The air is heavy with power, the residue of a hundred spells seeping into the rock. Today has been a day of focus and repetition, refining my magic bit by bit. Time is elusive in these depths. With no sun or stars, judging the passing hours is nigh impossible. Yet I know the day nears its end, for at the edge of the torchlight only a few ogres remain. They sit chained, silent, and resigned after watching their fellows reduced to heaps of charred bone. There are enough left for one final casting. My assistants drag the brutes into the field of ash and bone before retreating, lest they be caught in the spell. With a word the flickering darkness erupts into fire and screams, tongues of flame rushing across the cavern floor, eager to consume the prisoners. I watch as they burn, counting under my breath while I fill yet another parchment with notes, documenting every detail. When the last ogre collapses unmoving, I stop my count and mark the final line upon the page. That was the last of the prisoners, yet I still have strength aplenty to continue.
