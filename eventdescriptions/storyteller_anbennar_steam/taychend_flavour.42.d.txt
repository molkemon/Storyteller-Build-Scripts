Olorises gripped the hilt of his sword as the cloaked figures emerged from the darkness, illuminated at last by his bodyguard’s torchlight. It was a grim place to meet, surrounded by the shattered remains of Olorises’ previous great victory. But dead men tell no tales, and if this meeting went wrong, the elves before him could easily join the bodies littering the floor, and Olorises could do something actually worthwhile. Some of their faces, he could see, were Taychendi tan or brown. Others had the strong teal of a pureblooded Ambhen or the same cyan tint that matched his own. “Greetings, conqueror,” spoke a Taychendi figure. “Your activities have not gone unnoticed. Your tendencies. Your… vision. The masses worship Laskaris as a god, and you as his heir, but we know better, and in time, so will you.” Another stepped up, a halfKheionai like Olorises. “We are the Laskarian Circle, the heart of the Cult of Laskaris.” Olorises scoffed. “The Laskarian Circle,” he sneered, an old habit of his mother when she knew she was being cheated. “The followers of Jaherios Lýkos, his companions in the nightblades’ delusional schemes.” The cloaked Taychendi shook his head fiercely. “We follow Laskaris, not Lýkos. That fool fell to his own lies, the lie of divinity.” Olorises’ grip tightened; he had little time for the gods, but he did not often dare provoke them. “The cult we run, the masses—they call Laskaris a god, but here is our truth. In being a simple elf, he was more powerful than any deity; Laskaris was a philosopher, a visionary. We wish to uphold his ideals.” The halfKheionai withdrew a purpleplumed helmet of Precursor steel from his cloak. Olorises recognized it instantly—the helm of the conqueror himself. “Create a new world, a true synthesis of Kheionai philosophy and Taychendi glory, with you as its harbinger, the true heir of Laskaris, the elf you were meant to be.” As Olorises was handed the helm, he felt the world hold its breath.
