When word reached the ruler of the Nuzenapu, the lost harpies of the east were instantly the subject of great intrigue. Many questioned the truth of their fierce aspect, and many more found their wholesale embracing of an earthbound culture peculiar. Some found it insulting, others exotic. It was therefore inevitable that an invitation was extended to Waben Donpun, for a delegation to visit the heart of Harpylen, and partake in the wider imperial culture. The invitation was accepted, and the capital awaited the delegation from the east with bated breath. What they no doubt did not expect, was dragons. Not true dragons, of course, but as the Nuzenapu crested the horizon, they were flanked by hobgoblins, mounted on winged dragonnel, and hefting shining lances, each tipped with a clan banner, fiercely whipping in the wind. They made their descent to Heunthume in welldrilled unison, and as they made landfall, the leading harpy stood sentinel beside an old weatherbitten hobgoblin as he dismounted. They approached the queen, and bowed deeply, in perfect unison. The leading harpy spoke first, in a measured voice. "We, the first pair of Waben Donpun, were called, and we have come. We pledge lance and bow to the Queendom." The hobgoblin continued, surprising the court with his knowledge of the Firanyan tongue. "We pledge to serve this throne, on this day, and until the day when the last pair of Nuzenapu fall from the sky." The court fell silent, and all eyes turned to the queen, who to her credit only hesitated slightly, before speaking. "I bid you rise, for I accept your pledge. From this day on, you shall be my flock, and if any strike against you, they strike against this throne. Together, may we never fall from the sky again."
