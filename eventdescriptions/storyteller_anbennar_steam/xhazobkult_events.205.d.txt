The first thing the monarch felt was the smoke. Noxious vapors, invading their body with every breath, dense and cold. It smelled like burning flesh. It made their lungs ache. Awareness gradually returned to the monarch. The monarch felt their eyes open, but saw nothing. Had they been blinded? They instinctively moved their hands to their eyes, but a hot lance of pain shot through their palms. They had been bound; no, nailed together, with a single heavy bolt. The monarch attempted flexing their fingers. Nothing. The monarch tried to recall. The monarch had slept. Protected by guards and wards, yet these things had passed through them, invading their dreams, binding them and stealing them away. And the monarch had awoken. Here, wherever here was. The monarch attempted to orient themselves. The monarch felt their cheek against a cold surface. Not stone, but similar. Finely ground wood or bone perhaps. The monarch attempted to rise, but their knees flared up in pain. Broken. Of course. Then the monarch tried to speak, their voice echoing through the darkness. "Hello?" A careful voice. And yet, it felt loud. Invasive, as it flooded into this silent place. There was no answer.
