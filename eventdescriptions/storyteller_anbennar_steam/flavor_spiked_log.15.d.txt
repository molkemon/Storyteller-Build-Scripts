Today's tale is special, my friend, for it is about us goblins. Aeons ago, when the Woods spread far beyond their current edges, we were already here. Outsiders even thought we were some kind of fey. This might have been a fun little misconception, if one of them hadn't burned half the old Woods to try to kill the fey, and us, at the same time. Seriously, that guy was such a blockhead; he never listened to anyone. Anyway, we survived and lived without bothering about the outside world. Humans, elves, orks, they came to the Woods after us, and we will still be here where they are all gone and forgotten, like dust scattered by a fresh spring wind. They are not the only ones who came to the Woods. Indeed, my friend, indeed. If we had known that we had so many cousins, we would maybe have taken some care about the outside world. We are fortunate that they came to us, even if the ordeals they went through to join us were so bleak. Well, our cousins seemed to believe that all goblins had lived deep under the mountains since the dawn of time. It seems unlikely, of course, but they do have a tremendous number of clans we have never heard of. Some were forced to the northern lands by the Greentide. Others fled to the south to avoid the orkish yoke. The rest remained in the now empty caves. And it seems we may even have more distant cousins, as big and furry as bears. I really doubt those exist, Qlak. But if they do, I'm sure even they would be welcome in our clan. Nevertheless, I hope all our cousins outside here can thrive, but our doors will be open if any of them ever need it.
