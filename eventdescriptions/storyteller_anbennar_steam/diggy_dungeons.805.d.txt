The rope's promise of safety is as alluring as it is misleading. The spikes are slippery, the rope has nowhere to be fastened to, and every once in a while a climber falls to their death: That is the reality of our endeavour. At some point, it became useless to count how many had fallen and the soldiers just agreed to ignore it. Everyone for themselves.  Finally on the ground we can see the terrible toll the wall has taken on us. At least one hundred corpses piled just here, with more dispersed around the area. The expedition leader looks away and into the chasm stretching out next to us, eager to continue and let this small disaster behind him.
