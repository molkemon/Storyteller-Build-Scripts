The monarch is having a good evening. Quiet, a comfortable temperature, no panicked advisors bursting through doors, screaming. A panicked advisor bursts through the door, screaming. “My monarch. We ran out of corpses.” Apparently, “unlimited” hordes are very much limited by how many people you can successfully throw down a hole. After running through peasants and prisoners, you grow a bit desperate. The ruler is about to ask if they have considered children and gnomes yet, but before he can get a word out the court necromancer walks through the door with a proposal. Recycling. See, corpses don’t have to be intact to be reanimated. We can reanimate just a head, or just an arm, just fine. Now an armless undead is among the weaker of our soldiers, but if we just find two spare arms from a different blown up corpse, we can assemble a full undead. The court necromancer lays out his plans in great detail. He has brought placards. They are very beautifully made placards, to be sure, but they somewhat obscure the main issue, which is that no one is sure if this will actually work. But rest assured, the restive man assures, all that’s needed are fifty years, a small fortune in funding, and some volunteers. The more, the better.
