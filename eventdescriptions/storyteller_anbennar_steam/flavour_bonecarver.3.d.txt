"RAAAAAAAGH.." Neevnûz cried aloud as she slashed an undead ork, causing it to fall and splash on the wet ground. Around her was a party of a mere thousand orks. "This is hopeless. Thousands have died to zombies and the plague, how much more must fall for you to turn back?" One ork suddenly cried out in protest, sickly with bags clearly visible under his eyes. His words rang out in silence, with only frogs responding for a good minute. Then after a time of staring, several orks started to walk, threatening to turn back even without Neevnûz’s allowance. "And what? Return to the chief and tell him you scurried away like cowards, disappointing the onlooking deadmen hoping to not die in vain? Be my guest, but I do not fear death; my soul shall live on, reincarnating and dying, and I intend for each death to be a valorous one. I too understand the frustration, the hopelessness, but I was given a duty by the chief and I do not intend to dishonor him and Brasul, who have built our clan from the ground up to be one of the strongest of the tide. I know we will find him and we will burn every tree and zombie in this backwater if we have to." Once again, croaking silence filled the swamp, but eventually, all of the orks nodded in unison and marched on. Three days later, under the canopies of charcoal trees, gurgling undead, and buzzing mosquitos, one decorated corpse was found, somehow still freshly preserved and intact even after what was supposed to be years of decay. the body of the most devoted follower of Dookan.
