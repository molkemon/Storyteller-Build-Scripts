"Don’t you know who I am? You cannot do this to me." the Ynnic yelled, in shockingly fluent Common.  "I am the fifth son of a noble house of this river, and I will not suffer this indignation."  The crowd said nothing.  "My family has owned this land longer than your people have existed, priest. We lay claim to a legacy you cannot begin to dream of. I am not entitled to a death as undignified as this."  "You are nothing now but a man," said the couter, "and whatever ancestral glory you claim was washed away in the blood you exacted from the common people. Accept your death with honor, witch, and accept; the Ynn has changed forever, and you and your ilk have been left behind."  "No. Release me. I command you."  His pleas fell on deaf ears, and as the flames engulfed him, the crowd; human and ruinborn alike; cheered.
