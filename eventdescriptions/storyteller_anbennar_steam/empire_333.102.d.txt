No individual has been the cause of the demise of so many empires as the mythical being known as Tchazara. Nobody knows what she truly was. Theories range from a genie to a dragon to a corrupted Araizary, to simply being a cursed immortal lizardfolk who made it her eternal task to send the lizardfolk into despair. Multiple empires, starting with the eighty-fifth, died at her hands, only ending with her defeat at the birth of the one hundred twenty-second Empire. During the birth of the one hundred twenty-first Empire, she cursed the crown that was about to be placed atop the first empress’ head at her coronation. When the crown touched her brow, the empire would be born, but it would also be shrouded in darkness and collapse at the same time. This would not seem like a controversial inclusion into the three hundred thirty-three empires at first glance. Fantastical, yes, but nothing too out of the ordinary in the incredible thousand thousand years of the lizardfolk. Where the one hundred twenty-first Empire causes controversy is the fact it supposedly ended at the exact same time it began. There have been short empires before. The ninety-first Empire lasted twelve seconds, ending when a spear was hurled through emperor Haksal’s skull during his pronouncement speech. But can one truly say an empire existed if it died the exact moment it began? The debate over the one hundred twenty-first Empire reveals the inanity of the three hundred thirty-third Empire prophecy. For Khatalashya to fulfil the prophecy, there can be no doubts in any of the previous three hundred thirty-two. A single doubt collapses the faith, so even fantastical details like Tchazara and the one hundred twenty-first Empire must be poured over to justify why they have been included.
