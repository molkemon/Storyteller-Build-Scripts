The air was tense in the old hall, as officials from the notsofarflung corners of the League mingled and spoke. Some spoke in hushed voices of neighboring countries which may one day provide refuge, or of means by which one could survive conquest unscathed. Others were more belligerent, accusing men from their sistercities of incompetence and deriding them as the root of all the League's troubles. For even more, though, a somber mood smothered any of the revelries that were customary at a gathering like this one. More than a few of the participants wondered just how many more reunions would ever take place. If this, even, would be the last. The League is dying; that much is more than apparent. Perhaps the rulers of Szicheng, illfated as they were, saw what was to come. Perhaps their treachery was instead just another nearfatal blow. The wicked men of Jinqiu have tore territory from our grasp, and it is only a matter of time until the other hungry states surrounding us come for the rest. If the League of Yanszin is to survive, something drastic must be done.
