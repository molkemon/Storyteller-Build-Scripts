This part of the Pillar is quiet and empty; we move through the various chambers at our ease, pleased for a part of the Pillar which is not trying to harm us. These side passages and chambers are too small for the hinuirocs to truly rule over, and we make camp liberally, tending to aching wounds and thoroughly accessing data. This pillar is a menagerie which has gradually failed over thousands of years, its creatures breaking through their containers and unleashing artificial biospheres over local regions of the Pillar. The entire structure is riddled by the constant war between perfectly curated biomes, rigid territories of Precursoraddled life. It is a shock, then, to come across a specimen which has not even tried to leave. Here is a single being, alone. It looks like nothing we have ever seen. Perhaps one could gesture at a drake or dragon from its abdomen, but its head is that of a monkey or maybe even a human. It has great, leathery wings like the hinuibats which infest all the Pillars; it has four legs, but also a heavy scaled tail. We see even as we watch that it can fold up all four legs in order to slither like a snake, and indeed it seems to prefer this. Beyond all this, beyond the powerful strangeness of the being’s appearance, is its stare. None who later recall this moment can precisely justify it in objective terms, but the creature’s expression conveys infinite melancholy, thousands of years of contemplation. It sits behind the transparent material of its enclosure and merely looks, and one feels as though one has intruded, as though one is committing an unspeakable moral harm. Is this how the being has enforced its territory, all this time? Has it merely asked others to leave?
