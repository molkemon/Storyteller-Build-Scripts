They sit at the edge of a clearing, resting against trees, facing each other. They are tired, and cannot go on. They know it is coming, but they know they cannot stop it. One of them is missing already. They have seen what that one has become. One of them, a tall, severe woman, gasps suddenly. She gurgles, coughs, then falls silent. The other three do not move. Another, a kindly old man lost to knowledge, closes his eyes, sighs, and ceases to be. The other two do not move. Another, of shifting, liquid form, dissolves. The last one does not move. He, who knew he would be last, awaits his turn. His white skin is caked with sweat. He feels sudden, unmeasurable pain, sees eternity flash before his eyes, and then everything goes black. A sigh, a soft giggle, then silence.
