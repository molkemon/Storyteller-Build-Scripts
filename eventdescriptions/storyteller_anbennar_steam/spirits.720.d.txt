During a routine afternoon tour of the capital city, the monarch began to sense that something was amiss. As the ruler moved through the city with their guard, an eerie feeling of being watched lingered, although the monarch could discern no shadow or silhouette among the bustling citizens and vigilant guards. Despite this unsettling sensation, the monarch continued their tour, inspecting the city diligently. Everything appeared normal on the surface—the market stalls were vibrant, the barracks attracted more recruits, and the people went about their daily lives. However, as the monarch passed by a temple, the monarch couldn't help but notice its doors were firmly shut, and the entire façade was covered in paper talismans adorned with unblinking eyes. Perplexed, the monarch initially dismissed it as a prank by mischievous children and continued on their way. As the monarch made their way back to the palace, the monarch began to see these paper talismans there as well. Initially, they adorned the edges of the entrance gate in an orderly fashion. However, they multiplied rapidly, spreading to rooftops, random spots on the palace walls, and even the shoulders of their guards. Feeling increasingly distressed, the monarch hastened their return to the palace. Inside, the monarch found their advisors and the entire courtroom in a state of alarm. The paper talismans covered every available surface. No one could ascertain who had placed them or how it had been done. Even individuals who had momentarily turned away from the crowd found themselves adorned with these enigmatic symbols. Suddenly, the monarch felt a searing heat on their forehead, and a gentle touch revealed a paper talisman affixed there. It was then that a local temple priest burst into the room, shouting, "Tughayasa is awake." Regrettably, it was too late, as the courtroom became inundated with the eyes of the awakened Great Spirit.
