During a routine afternoon tour of the capital city, our ruler began to sense that something was amiss. As they moved through the city with their guard, an eerie feeling of being watched lingered, although they could discern no shadow or silhouette among the bustling citizens and vigilant guards. Despite this unsettling sensation, our ruler continued their tour, inspecting the city diligently. Everything appeared normal on the surface—the market stalls were vibrant, the barracks attracted more recruits, and the people went about their daily lives. However, as they passed by a temple, they couldn't help but notice its doors were firmly shut, and the entire facade was covered in paper talismans adorned with unblinking eyes. Perplexed, our ruler initially dismissed it as a prank by mischievous children and continued on their way. 

As they made their way back to the palace, they began to see these paper talismans there as well. Initially, they adorned the edges of the entrance gate in an orderly fashion. However, they multiplied rapidly, spreading to rooftops, random spots on the palace walls, and even the shoulders of their guards. Feeling increasingly distressed, our ruler hastened their return to the palace. 

Inside, they found their advisors and the entire courtroom in a state of alarm. The paper talismans covered every available surface. No one could ascertain who had placed them or how it had been done. Even individuals who had momentarily turned away from the crowd found themselves adorned with these enigmatic symbols. 

Suddenly, our ruler felt a searing heat on their forehead, and a gentle touch revealed a paper talisman affixed there. It was then that a local temple priest burst into the room, shouting, "Tughayasa is awake." Regrettably, it was too late, as the courtroom became inundated with the eyes of the awakened Great Spirit.
