As ritual bloodletting becomes more culturally accepted in society, the Claw Ashhana have taken to conducting the ceremonies in more public places. However, some Claw Ashhana have suggested moving these rituals behind closed doors, lest the shows scare young children away from the sect or give travelers the wrong impression. Worse, these displays have received significant pushback from the Canvas Ashhana, who break up the rituals and decry them as perversions of the concept of "art." Scuffles with Canvas adherents have reduced the safety of public bloodletting; an errant blow can push a ritual knife deeper than intended into the flesh, and dousing the cauterization flames can increase the risk of infection.
