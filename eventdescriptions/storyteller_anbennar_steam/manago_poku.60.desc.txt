It is a surprisingly rare experience for the Mechanim to encounter beings of similar shape and build to themselves, most things on the domain either being much larger or smaller than them. It is understandable therefore why the first encounter with the Insyaa Yeti Ogre involved the question of if it was in fact simply another Mechanim like WalksAlone covered in the hides of other animals. An attempt to interface with this individual however proved disastrous as WalksAlone was immediately attacked. Similar to the behavior of sharks, WalksAlone interpreted this behavior as a probing, testing experience rather than one of rage or aggression. As such, WalksAlone endeavored to cause as little harm to the Ogre as possible. The exchange of blows was lumbering, long, and heavy. Each punch and movement kicking up snow and sending resounding hard thuds off the mountain side. WalksAlone had endurance and natural armor, but the Ogre had experience in combat and a slight edge in strength. Eventually, it was clear to both this would be a draw. To WalksAlone's curiosity, the Ogre turned to leave upon determining it could not defeat the metal creature as easily as it had maybe hoped. No words spoken or nonverbal acknowledgment given. Unable to allow himself to do the same, WalksAlone followed the Ogre a short distance to his camp on the mountain slope and observed him, which the Ogre did not see fit to prevent him from doing. WalksAlone attempted communication, but much like I or my colleagues may ignore a bleating goat, so too did the Ogre ignore WalksAlone. This experience in many ways categorized the early interactions between the Ogres and Mechanim, a cold, detached distance. While small, personal combats may erupt between individuals or small groups, for the most part the Mechanim describe a peaceful coexistence where both peoples lived separate lives. So long as one did not directly enter the villages or homes of the other, no serious contact was made. It should also be noted that the Mechanim took the longest time to learn the Insyaa Yeti language, as they had the least exposure to it. Notes, Academician Prof. Raven, recording the legend of WalksAlone.
