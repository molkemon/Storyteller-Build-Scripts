The feast is lavish, as it is supposed to be. Food and drinks are plentiful; no one will leave without a full belly and a hangover to look forward to. The mayor and the councilors are drinking heavily and will not be able to resist the firm, sober hands of the guards. The animosity between the ruler and the council of the city seems to have dissolved during the several courses of the gala dinner. Should we proceed with the plan to arrest the mayor and the councilors?
