The knowledge of the great spirits has become once more commonplace, even if the understanding behind that knowledge is still sometimes scattered or lacking. What the people now realize, though, is that despite the destruction great spirits have wrought in other regions, those along the Kharunyana have been spared. In gratitude for such protection, it has become commonplace to toss a coin in the water along with food at the flooded temples. The coins vanish beneath the schools of catfish, and soon after, golden ones can be seen up and down the length of the river. Some swim up to those on the bank and deposit a coin in turn in the person's hands. Other people claim that the sight of seven of them had brought them good luck in their life after witnessing their golden light. Festivals were to be held on the seventh day of the seventh month, each year all along the river. At night, lanterns were lit and floated out on paper boats, each with an offering of food, and each disappearing with a flash of gold and the flick of a tail beneath the surface.
