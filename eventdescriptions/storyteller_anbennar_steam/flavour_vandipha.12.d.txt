Vandiphas system of governance is modeled after one of the greatest institutions ever created: the Harimraj. Mengi subjects are treated akin to Prabhis, lords who enact Vandiphas will in their realms. None of them are allowed the status of a Senapti, of course. Vandiphan ministers see to the execution of the monarch’s designs, and painstakingly calculate taxes paid in tribute. Upon the appointment of a new lord, they are expected to sail to Van to bow before the monarch, offering them servitude in person. Their silly superstitions about Amilak and their false belief in the sacred blood of the Mengi have been denied at every turn. They are expected to follow the tenets of High Philosophy in all ways. In return, they receive great peace and prosperity; the Mengi lords live lives of luxury second to none, except the monarch, of course.
