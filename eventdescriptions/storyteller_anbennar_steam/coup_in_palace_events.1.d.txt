Halls in the palace are busier than ever; people from all the country gather there to exchange information, celebrate meetings, or simply try to forge alliances or solve old disputes. This would not be strange, but for the sole reason that every time the monarch goes through those halls, silence becomes tangible. It is not the common silence derived from respect, but an uncomfortable silence that makes everyone feel uneasy. As our interest grows about this strange behavior, gossips that a coup is being plotted have arrived to our ears. Who would dare to attack us?
