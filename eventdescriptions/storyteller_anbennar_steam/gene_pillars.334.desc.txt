Beneath the imperious eye of the hinuirocs flock the crows. The hinuirocs eat them, too, of course, but the crows multiply far too fast to be discouraged by that. We have seen them flitting through practically every jackknifed biome of the upper Pillar, scouts of the grander murder, eyeing us from high perches, evaluating us. They do not seem to have been afforded a specific biome of their own; perhaps they were introduced to interact with the hinuirocs, or perhaps they are escapees from elsewhere; it is impossible to know. This chamber though, half collapsed and an awkward shape high, high up in the Pillar’s structure, is ideal for them. Here we find a murder of several thousand crows, far in excess of the usual community size of conventional crows. They are larger than most Insyaan crows, though this still is not so huge. Watching them flock to and fro, one gets the same impression as a city; a vast multitude of individual movements which together feel wavelike, patterned. What level of sentience is required to support a population like this? Do they share and divide, these hinuicrows? Where is the threshold between cooperation and civilisation?
