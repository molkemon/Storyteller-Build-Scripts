Our support for the ruler, our candidate for the noble throne, has yielded us many diplomatic advantages since they was elected. Recently, however, it has been suggested that the ruling family's claims on the other domain reaches beyond this single individual and that the current ruler should in fact be considered the ruler of both their domain and the other domain. The other ruler considers themselves sovereign, of course, but the current ruler outranks them in the dynasty. Surely a ruler's first loyalty must be to family, must it not?
