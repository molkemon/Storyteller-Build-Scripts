The Drahmgall have brought troubling, yet promising news from the outskirts. Among the residents of the Northern caves, there are rumors of a place known to locals as Xazor’s Pit. It is a vast chasm, plunging far lower than even the deepest mines in the area. Few dare approach its edges, and fewer still dare to descend into its lightless depths. However, recently there have been many disappearances around this chasm, enough even to draw the attention of the Drahmgall. Scouts sent to investigate the area returned with unsettling reports. The pit itself is actually a massive tear in the stone, with depths that vanish far into the unknown. The air around it is thick with the smell of damp rock, and something far older. The Drahmgall claim this pit is home to something truly ancient. A great batlike beast, a creature said to dwell within the farthest reaches of this pit, only rarely emerging to hunt along the surrounding tunnels. It is this creature they believe to be responsible for the recent disappearances. Yet what makes this even more remarkable is their claim that this pit and the beast itself are both steeped with a power almost identical to Drahm'otash. If this is true, the implications would be extraordinary. The remains of such a creature may carry enough power to serve as the foundation for cultivating an entirely new reserve of Dreamforged Mithril. A single hunt could provide resources far beyond anything our forges have yet produced. Of course, this hunt would not come without risk. The pit of Xazor lies far below any safe passage, and the creature that dwells within has ruled these depths for untold years. Descending into the chasm would mean entering the beast’s domain, where escape would be nearly impossible should the hunt go poorly. Yet this possibility is too great to ignore. If the Drahmgall are correct, a source of great power lies in the depths below. The only question is, are we ready?
