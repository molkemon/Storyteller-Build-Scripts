During the first years of the Ivetasananthor, the organization remained small, with most members serving in an ad hoc capacity. Aside from the order's perch in Heunthume, Siadunaiun became a vital roost, it being the site of the order's first success, as well as the origin of many further migrations. Thus, when a new migration was traced, the expedition departed from Siadunaiun, for the hunters had studied the sundering of Siadanlen, and listened to the songs from that time. They now knew that many flocks had fled the phoenix, flying south to the Gulf of Rahen, before banking west towards the setting sun. Following the coast into the Salahad bay, the hunters came to the mountains of Kograrzo, where old roosts were found. Some harpies were found there, and songs were sung of a flock to the south, who held great strength to this day. This was how the Ivetasananthor came to learn of the winged guardians of Dasmatus. Nestled south of the island, this flock had built their roost in the dry mountains of Xhogrix, where they had spent the time since the Sundering, perfecting the art of hunting gnolls. This was readily appreciated by the citizens of Dasmatus, who held their protectors in high regard. For the odd hunters however, the roost of Xhogrix was a dour place. The flock of Dasmatus was fierce and cruel, practicing a doctrine of the hunt invoking the condor, a powerful scavenger. As such, nearly all harpies from Xhogrix were warriors first, and to them the ambush was an art, the swift kill a craft. They took wing with long spears and heavy bows, eschewing armour in favour of the pelts of slain gnolls, dyed and treated to blend with the red mountains where they made their home. Yet the harpies of Dasmatus were cordial enough, and they told the hunters of their part in the migration. How they had stayed behind in this place, but many other flocks had flown through the bend, and east to the horn of Sarhal.
