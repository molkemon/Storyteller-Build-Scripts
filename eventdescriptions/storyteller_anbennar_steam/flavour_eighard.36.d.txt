In the midafternoon, cresting a hill, Allara and Keldora come across a strange sight. A small village sits nestled between walls of red-orange trees, which is not the strange part. As they approach, Allara leading, the wind whistles between the slats of the houses. The town is silent. They do not see a single person. As they wander through its streets, the decay becomes more obvious. Roofs bend inwards upon themselves. Windows are smashed, likely the result of bandits. Doors hang off their hinges. This is not their destination, far from it, but they are intrigued nonetheless. Keldora, not noticing that she is now leading, wanders the streets with a pit forming in her stomach. She is drawn towards a particular building, for reasons she cannot particularly explain. Unlike the rest of the town, this house is a burned mess. "What are you looking for, Kel?" Allara asks. "I don’t know," Keldora replies. "There’s something here, I can feel it." The two search the ruined home, pushing aside fallen beams which crumble and dissolve at the slightest touch. No one has been here in decades. Keldora finds a bookshelf with ruined tomes, nothing legible. She places a hand on the wall, rubbing the ash away. What happened here? Where did everyone go? "Keldora, look," Allara says suddenly. "There’s a trapdoor here."
