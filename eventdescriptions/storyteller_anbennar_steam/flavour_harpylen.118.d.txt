We flew east. Too fast and too fiercely perhaps. The songs of our dreams were with us day and night, as we flew together. We close, and we see the lake. Vast and clean, like a great mirror. The sky is so blue, and the waters so bright, that it could be a hole in the ground, into a great sky below. And there it lies. A tower, on the shore. There are many dark shapes, living by the lake. The people of the plains. They see us in flight, and some cheer. Some raise spears to us. We remember; we are of the sky. To them, we are a rare omen, of good fortune. We cannot stop. The tower is in sight, and we make our landing in unison. There is no disorder, and it feels odd. I feel so connected with my fellow hunters, that I know where they will be. Their intent falls into my thoughts, and they fit themselves in place, as if they always belonged there. I hear the song. It is so close now. How did we not find this place? Why could we not hear it before? I look to the hunter next to me. Her face mirrors my own. Confusion. I feel it nipping at my mind. For a moment, I feel like I might tumble to the ground. But it passes. A wave of relief washes over us. I see nothing, for it is dark in this tower. But it does not matter. We can hear the song. We go into the darkness and out of time. We move as one body, the song stirring in our bones as we descend down invisible stairs, made for feet greater than all of us. We find it in a space. A room, perhaps. Made for someone vast. It sits discarded on a shelf, taller than a house, and we reach out to take it. In the dark, we do not see it, but we hear it and we know it. The shape in our talons is that of a flute, a gentle thing, hardly larger than a fledgling's toy. And yet, we hear her voice now, clear as the gaze of the sun. She sings to me and I listen. All of my bodies listen.
