Countless hours have been spent digging through the history of Rahen, looking for anything that will give the realm guidance on its destiny. Archives have been upended, libraries have been ransacked, even personal collections of minor nobles have been extensively catalogued. Still, nothing has been found. The labors almost seem hopeless, and many of the scholars give up their quest and return home empty-handed. Even in Dhenijansar, nearly all scholars have abandoned the search. All except one: Jira of the Rising Moon. Jira has been running through Dhenijansar like a woman possessed, tearing apart any collection of books she can find no matter how small. No place is safe from her unending thirst for knowledge, from the halls of nobility to the streets of the slums. It is in these slums where she currently searches, chasing reports of a lost cache of scrolls hoarded by a local madman. Looking throughout the abandoned building, Jira upends everything that isn't nailed down trying to find the hidden texts. She spends nearly four hours looking for it before the sun begins to set, and exhaustion runs through her bones. She sits on the rotten planks, ready to call off the search, when she spies something peculiar she only noticed at eye level: a loose brick, hanging from the wall. Lightly tugging at the stonework, Jira finally wrenches the brick free of its earthen prison, and with a thundering roar the rest of the wall quickly falls with it. Jira ducks for cover, and when the dust settles she stands and peers into the new space revealed. Inside is a treasure trove of knowledge, centuries old scrolls filling baskets across the room. And sitting on a nearby table, haphazardly strewn among other documents, lies a missive from the Harimraj requesting an audience with the Oracles of Tughayasa. Jira's face lights up, and she grabs the scroll and sprints out of the building.
