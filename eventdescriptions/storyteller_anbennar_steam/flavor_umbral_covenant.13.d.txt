Do you love me, Granny? The little girl asks. "You are the most important thing," replies Granny Ilfa. Every day, every way, she asks this. It is a riddle game, a game of evasion. It happens most often when the girl is practicing the movement of her magic, the somatic elements of the craft. The girl finds such things natural, you see, and her mind wanders. "You are my cleverest one," Granny says, one day. "You are a revelation," she says, on another. The girl gives a triumphant little smile, but each day, inevitably she asks again. They are nourishing answers, but they are not enough; why are they not enough? "You are promise itself." "You are a force." "You are my good girl." The little girl trains and trains. There is a gap in her which she cannot see, a space over which she must only jump, and her Granny will praise her again. One day, she will repay her Granny everything; she will stand in the ritual circle as her equal and fulfil her role. Granny will not have to plan and fuss; everything will be at peace.
