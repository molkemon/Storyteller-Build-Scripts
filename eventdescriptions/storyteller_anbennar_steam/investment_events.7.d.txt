Local merchants in a key province have complained to the trade company about a lack of coinage stifling the flow of trade. The Company has been given permission by the lord to issue its own currency, allowing the foundation of mints in provinces under its control. Minting of course requires a supply of an appropriate metal. Gold, silver, and bronze are not always readily available to the Company. An alternative source of coinage would be to accumulate coins from another country and countermark them with the Company stamp. This would likely anger the lord of that country, who would see this as a defacement of their image.
