In the dank, cold cave, Varlengeilt shivers. He lays in a heap, as he has done every day since he first arrived here. He misses the taste of blackberries. He is bored. He holds up his right arm, seeing perfectly in the darkness. Yes, it has progressed past the elbow. His once vibrant scales are a rotting black from the elbow down. He runs his other hand across them; his right arm feels nothing. His breaths come ragged; every day he feels weaker. What did that archfey do to him? He had looked into her eyes, and thought he'd known what he'd seen, but now that he thought about it, it filled him with dread. Those eyes did not contain hope, joy, or sadness; they were not the eyes of a life well lived. Those were the eyes of a monster. Struggling, groaning, he pushes himself to his feet. He gasps at the pain, but he does not fall. He hobbles out into a larger part of the cave, where some of his few remaining devotees sit in a small circle. "My Lord," one of them says, "would you like some bread?" Varlengeilt shakes his head. "No," he says. "Tomorrow." He coughs. "Tomorrow, we leave this cage. We gather our strength, and we make for Svemel." "My Lord, surely you don’t think we can take on the forces of the country? That would mean death. You are too weak." Varlengeilt shakes his head again, throwing out his arms in a dramatic gesture his body is not quite ready for. He stumbles. "Fools. Do you think the dreams you’ve been receiving have just been for you? I have been sending them to every follower of mine still hidden in the city. When we attack, the city will rise to aid us." He roars, then grins.
