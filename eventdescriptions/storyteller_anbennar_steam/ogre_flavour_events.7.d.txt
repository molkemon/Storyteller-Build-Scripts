Tink. Clank. Bang. Zzz. "Damn it." The sounds of industry and creation have rung in an ogre’s ear like music ever since they labored under the giants’ yoke as makers and builders. When they erected their homes, beautified their cities, and paved their roads, they did so with their imperial bounty. ample crafting materials, architectural genius and oversight, and unbreakable tools. Though not all monuments to their greatness still stand, the work they did, the labor their ancestors accomplished, was without peer. But that time is long, long gone. In the current age of small peoples, cutting edge tools are rarely ogresized, leaving their brightest minds impatiently waiting for impractical or custommade intermediaries to bridge the gap between teensy and wieldy. In their recent past, they simply made do, although "making do" often meant doing without. To stand atop the world as the greatest artisans, they need a homemade tools industry; cost be damned. Should that prove too expensive, they could also commission the smallfolk crafters to assist them, but that would require their cooperation and motivation, be it coin or fear. What should their course of action be to alleviate this hardware deficiency?
