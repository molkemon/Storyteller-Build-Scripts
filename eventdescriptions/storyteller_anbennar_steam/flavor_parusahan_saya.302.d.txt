Continuing down the banks of the Kharunyana, we find ourselves within the lands of Sempherong and her fellow Porcelain Cities. This land is one of great importance, as the birthplace of porcelain marks it as a standout economic region compared to its neighbors. Perhaps this was so profound that Harimar chose to erect his throne here, near the greatest treasure of the land? Nearby caves have been said to contain some kind of ruins, weird structures that the locals cannot understand. The underground chambers are scoured for weeks, but ultimately we arrive at the same conclusion we reached before; the throne is not here, and we must continue. The old specter of doubt begins to creep its way into the hearts of the surveyors, as they trudge along the Kharunyana towards the end of their journey. Options are running low, and soon there will be none left at all. What will we do then?
