It is a fine winter morning, and the monarch of the realm has found themselves inexcusably accosted with demands from the hobgoblins to join their second war against the people of Sir. The ruler looks out on the foggy morning sky, an awareness of dawn peeking through the cloudy mists of the Demon Hills. The monarch thinks of the Aingjada who are currently wearing different colors and flying the banner of Sir. The monarch thinks of the personal assistant dispatched to lead the Siri forces, a bright young thing named Chiyi Kwaeng. The monarch thinks of the girl’s body, clasped in Korashi chains and dragged before the ruler of the other domain. The monarch specifies "body," because the chances of a living mage as powerful as her being allowed in front of the ruler are slim to none. Then the monarch imagines it is not Chiyi Kwaeng’s body but their own in such a position, the Oni treachery unveiled, the monarch a corpse trampled under hobgoblin boots. All of this the monarch considers, and then finally gestures for ink and parchment.
