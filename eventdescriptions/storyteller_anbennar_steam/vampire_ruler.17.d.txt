Dawn. The cresting of the sun's rays above the horizon. In Bulwar, it is a call to the Sun Cult's faithful, a reminder of Surael's glory. In Anbenncóst, it signals a vast flood of people to rise from cramped quarters and make their way to just as cramped workhouses. In Gerudia, it demands a dozen skalds sing again the song of the Dragon and the Skald, to ensure solar warmth is never drowned out by draconic ice. But for the monarch, it is a frustrating sight — the marker of a fruitless, bloodless, night. The hunger still gnaws at the ruler. But every vampire is a nocturnal predator, and that is true of our title as well.
