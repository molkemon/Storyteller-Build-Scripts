Erankar marched through the ruins of a palace, the center of power in this den of snakes and scoundrels. After he had been thrown back from a location, Sheranu Enlarmadar, who had survived his capture of a domain by virtue of being married to the ruler of that domain, had launched an invasion from here. He would have preferred to storm it himself, but he was on the other side of the city when it was taken, drawn to a bigger threat. Luckily, his soldiers had taken his prize alive. Sheranu kneeled next to her husband, bound and guarded. Her husband simply stared forwards, despondent, but when Sheranu noticed Erankar’s approach, she spat on the ground before him. Erankar drew his sword, executed the warlord, and pointed the bloodstained tip at the last Enlarmadar. “Do you expect me to beg?” Her eyes blazed with hatred. “You’ve taken everything from me. My parents, my brother, and defeating me in battle wasn’t enough, so you burn my home down too, kill my husband. Will you raze the domain now? Bathe the sand in blood?” Erankar could feel the heat rising in him, a white hot rage that threatened to engulf him. “Your family did all that and more. I was only reclaiming what was rightfully mine—vengeance.” “So was I.” Sheranu spat. “I had nothing to do with my father taking a place—you had everything to do with slaughtering my family. When the place fell, I was only nine years old.” Erankar’s blade flashed. Sheranu fell to the floor, lifeless. “So was I.” Erankar sheathed his sabre, hand shaking. He looked back at the city behind him.
