Much has happened since a band of weary and desperate Forest Goblins first arrived here from the Deepwoods. In the capital can now be found a growing town and administrative center, from which the ruler solves disputes and issues decrees. The authority of these orders now carries far beyond the capital itself, over a wide swath of territories. Everywhere, new settlements emerge, either freshly founded or rebuilt from the ruins of old Escann. The simple yet extensive roads between these towns and villages are patrolled by militias who keep robbers and raiders away, a dire luxury in today’s Escann. Beside these roads, countless farmlands can be seen, as the goblins learn the new trade of tilling and harvesting the land. There are farms belonging to both Forest Goblins and Common Goblins, although it is becoming increasingly difficult to tell them apart, and some areas have even been set aside for the returning Escanni. Throughout the towns, roads and fields, countless shrines and idols can be found, honoring a multitude of gods both goblin and Cannorian. The most common of these is of course Corin, whose image is used to remind both subject and visitor that the goblins of Groundhawk have been given these lands by divine decree. Of course, that’s not the only reason for her popularity. With Escann the way it is still, a war deity is always needed, and which one to better ensure your victory than one who is indebted to you? This is no longer a mere tribe of goblins; this is the start of a new nation.
