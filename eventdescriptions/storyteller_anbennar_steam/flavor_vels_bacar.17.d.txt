Peasants, artisans, merchants, and priests alike massed at the sides of the road leading into Vels Bacar, the main way held open by the two rows of well-equipped soldiers from all across the Ynn. In front of them, the cream of the Dolindhan nobility entered the city, riding their famed antler horses. After them, the Rzentur nobles followed, bearing their faith with pride. A gathering of such magnitude had not been seen since the proclamation of the Panykorun, which awaited them at the renewed Halba Hariez. They all followed the path down the road, where the imposing procession reached the Hall of Colours. There, the monarch waited, wearing a crown of blue panykorun flowers. the eponymous tree of the yen Bacar, present in the many gardens around the building. The monarch took off his crown, and one by one each of the Dolindhan and Rzentur lords swore upon it the same ancient oath their Sarda counterparts once did. to follow the house yen Bacar and to join them as the Panykorun, where they would all have a voice and a vote under the ruler. At last, the monarch picked up the crown, and swore an oath before wearing it. “I swear upon the Ynn to listen to the Panykorun and govern with it, just as it will rule with me…” The monarch then took a deep breath, and continued, “until preparations are ready for me to leave this crown and join the Panykorun, as this crown needs no title for the Ynnic Republic to prosper.”
