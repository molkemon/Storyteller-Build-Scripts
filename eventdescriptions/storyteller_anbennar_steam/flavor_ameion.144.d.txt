The Kylakas did not look up from his papers as Ecaris Ecarizuir entered his office. There was the sharp clack of heavy bootsteps on the polished oaken floor, the rustling of plate, a cleared throat. The Kylakas still did not look up. "My report on the Urazhil cultists, sir." The Kylakas motioned for him to give it, as he reached for his quill. There was a tense pause before Ecaris spoke again. "Over the past year, we've discovered fifteen hideouts in the Uyarbaid region. Surgical strikes eliminated most of their membership. Remaining individuals were hunted down in Degíthion, Degolidir, and Tridéneion. Casualties light. We believe the threat from the cult has been eliminated permanently." At last, the Kylakas looked up, smirked, as he admired Ecaris' Sunplate, shimmering pale gold streaked with the blue of Inscription. He had been fighting for so long, and it looked as if it had not made a dent in him. Well, not that much of a dent. In the Sun Elf's eyes, the Kylakas could see the toll of failure, and setback, and now subordination. "Good work, commander. Will that be all?" Ecaris grimaced. "Yes, sir. That will be all." Then turned on his heel and made to leave. The Kylakas spoke again before Ecaris reached the door. "You've done good work, you know. Put things in order. Helped establish civilization in these devastated lands. You should be proud." Ecaris halted, turned. "Did you think this was how things were going to turn out when you set off for Aelantier?" The Herald's grimace turned to a frown. "It isn't so different from how we operated in Bulwar. The Exemplars have always had a higher authority to answer to." The Kylakas smirked. "Oh, but you enjoyed that freedom when you had it, didn't you?" He turned back to the paper before him, a guarantee for Rezankand of security and loyalty. "But really, what did you expect? Your kin served the Empire once, as I recall."
