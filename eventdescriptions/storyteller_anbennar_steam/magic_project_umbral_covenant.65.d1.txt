In the end, it is so simple. Komina does not incant some grand ritual. She does not walk before Mama Hireet and Granny Ilfa and scream herself hoarse at their treachery, at what they have hidden from her, of how they have allowed her to suffer without any understanding of her plight. What happens now is a feeling, not an act. A thousand times in the last millennium and a half, Komina has stormed out of a meeting with Ilfa and Hireet, cursing their names, and nothing has happened. She could not truly imagine an existence apart from them, and so she could not create one. Here in a quiet place, Aunty Komina betrays her covensisters. It is but a thought, a moment of sincere desire to be done with them forever, and the bond is sundered. What a terrible thing, that something so powerful should die in silence. How strange it is that the ground does not shake. “Such a clever child you were,” Komina says, lingering at the edge of the glade. How many times has she met with her fellow conspirators in places such as this? She never will again. “So infectious, that enthusiasm. She made you into something else.” “She did.” The Vile One is not ashamed to be her Granny’s creature, even now. “It is like them, to be undone by their own fussy plotting,” Komina goes on. If nothing else, she is happy now to see the others fail. She gives a rare, open-mouthed smile, her gums weeping with blood. “Do not go where I go; I have no desire to hurt you.”
