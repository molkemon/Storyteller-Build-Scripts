Before us is an overgrown pathway. The long hallway has a number of holes in the side of the pillar leading outside. Through these holes, a growth of hinuiivy has gotten into the structure. The hinuiivy is proving quite difficult to get past. Unlike normal ivy, which is harmful but not truly dangerous, this hinuiivy is possessed of a curious and troublesome nature. Even cutting small sections of the ivy away releases a number of small fibers into the air, which on contact with the skin cause harmful blisters. How should we approach this?
