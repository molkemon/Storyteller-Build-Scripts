Chaki, what is this I hear about a summit with the Canvas chiefs? You can’t seriously trust them to treat with us? As Zazunta raced into the room, he noticed the empty basin and clean knife on the pedestal near the mirror. Across the space, Chakiata straddled the balcony railing, and did not avert her gaze from the stars even as Zazunta slowed his gait. Chaki, you’re undressed, you still haven’t let blood, and our tribe needs answ— what are you doing? Chaki wistfully broke her observation of the sky, and spoke almost absentmindedly. Zazzy, what stories do you know of King Aguntat the First? The question hit Zazunta like a shovel to the face, but Chaki’s musing was unwise to ignore. Uh erm I mean, converted to Zaradyrra from Anzalkatsa, made a confidant of his wouldbe conqueror Nukzhat with song— what is this really about? Chakiata turned once more to the sky. Long ago, our gods worked together to create this world, and Aguntat argued that divine collaboration erodes whatever flimsy barriers one seeks to erect between the various peoples of Taneyas, encouraging a recognition of our shared identity. She chuckled softly. It’s also said that in his jealousy of Nagakatesh and Ashak Tishin creating the lizardfolk, Raka Drakt never quite allowed birds to reach his domain in the far sky. Chakiata looked out at the town lights. Zazzy, the Canvas sect well, just as you and I are not the warlords of a rabid mod, so too do I suspect their failings have been exaggerated. They are not all uncaring, cloudheaded idealists. They suffer the same ailments as us. Some even listen to reason. I’m amenable to entertaining their fair criticism— I said fair, don’t give me that look— if they are able to hear ours. Or alternatively, in our jealousy of what they have made together, we could ensure their birds never reach us. They’ll slit your throat as revenge for that bard boy, Chaki. Perhaps. Or.
