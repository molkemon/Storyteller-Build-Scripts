North of Upper Yanshen, the Black Mist Hills stretch deep toward the Demon Hills, forming a key barrier of Yanshen. In Beikling, snow piles in ravines, wind scours pine and stone, and copper and iron run beneath. Its people excel in mining and tea making, known since old as the land of ancient iron and mountain tea, between fire and cold spring. Though remote, their literary spirit endures. Beikling folk are strong and sharp, favoring seven-syllable verse, vigorous and lofty. Some liken forest and mountain to steadfast solitude; others reflect on mines, casting truths of human rise and fall with concise force. Each line is like molten iron—powerful, rugged, noble, distinct from softer southern rhymes. This year, scholars met in conclave, compiling Seven Syllables of the Black Mist Hills to honor the snowy range. Their words are solemn, fiery, like ore in the forge or frost on granite—carrying the resolve of ice and flame, the hearts of iron and stone. Some verses burn, others speak with frosted clarity, yet all voice the soul of Beikling. Which line shall mark this year's Words of the Black Mist?
