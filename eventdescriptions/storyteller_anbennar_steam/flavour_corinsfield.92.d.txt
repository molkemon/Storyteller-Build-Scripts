The Father stood by the great pyre, erected in the center of Poppyspring. Calas, his former friend, was bound to it, his arms and legs tied. His hands had been chopped off, and the wounds cauterized to prevent him from casting spells of any sort, and he was covered in bruises and wounds. A great crowd had gathered around the pyre. Most of them wore shocked expressions; was this cruel? Had the man truly injured them? As if to answer these questions, Father Godwin turned to them and raised his fist before he spoke. “Good Corinspilgrims, before you lies a traitor,” he said, almost screaming. “This man hid his witchery and sought to ingratiate himself amongst us. Yea, verily, he believed he could become one of us. But never can a witch be a Corinsfielder; forever are they scarred by the evil taint. It is not enough to have fled out homes and calmly expect the witch and warlock to have left us behind; no, forevermore shall they dog us and work towards our ruin. Therefore, first must we strike.” The crowd looked at each other. “A community we must be, yes; but trust must be given in short supply. For anyone, wife, daughter, or neighbor, may be a witch. We are the most righteous warriors of Corin, and we will show our enemies no quarter.” Slowly, one by one, the crowd began to cheer. As Father Godwin lit a torch, they started to chant, “Death to the witch. Death. Death. Death.”
