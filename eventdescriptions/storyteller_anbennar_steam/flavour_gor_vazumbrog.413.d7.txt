The tyrant's death freed up the throne, for a new and more righteous rule. They all acclaimed the grayeyed maid, not one dissenting fool. "My father died to show the hold that every dwarf does need: to seek a purpose in her life, to love, to sleep, to feed. My golem friends shall never again be set to crush the weak. The fruits of our society shall serve both strong and meek." Not even the most cursed heart could doubt the wisdom there. Let golems do the hardest work, but in profits all must share. From that day forth all of the dwarves could eat when times were lean; the martyr's daughter led us right. "All hail the Grayeyed Queen." Golemancer's Working Song, Verse Thirty.
