There hadn’t been many survivors from the Throne Room. They had cut down as many as they could, of course, exacting righteous vengeance on those that had presided over such darkness. But when the real Orda knights came, and the mageprinces made their stand, there was little the Oren Neraghul could do. Ebenatam had prepared them well, of course. They all knew it would be a one-way trip. It had been a miracle that Iserdana had survived at all, managed to escape to the Ruinborn quarter in Anbenncóst. Still, she could report their great stand to the Kavalakuttam herself, commending their bravery. The dead deserved that much. Then she heard a cry from outside the district. Then, the mob. Then, the flame. Iserdana never made it back to Taychend. She saved a family or two, though, and that was what she was most proud of, as the crowd killed her. Everywhere in Orenvalyam, it was the same. One day, the ships would come with news of the Dovesday Massacre—righteous justice, a glorious victory. Then the next day, more news would come. of how Anbennar reacted to it. Rather than inspiring the serfs and peasantry to overthrow their masters, the massacre was taken as a heinous attack by a foreign power, and a wave of anti-Ruinborn sentiment swept not just Anbennar, but Cannor. Vicious pogroms from Westport to Nathalaire were carried out not only by vengeful nobles, but lowly peasants and laborers. And that is what has unsettled both the Oren Neraghul and the Kavalakuttam the most. As a new Emperor of Anbennar is elected and a wave of patriotism allows the aggrieved princedoms to persevere, many questions are being asked. All this, for a taste of vengeance, vapid and transitory? Who approved the attack? Why didn’t it trigger what was hoped? Why did anyone think it would?
