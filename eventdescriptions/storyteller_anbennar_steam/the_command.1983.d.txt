In the end, the Shirgrii feels almost simple to assault. Hewn stone structures, built into the side of a mountain, fall to the roar of cannonfire and the charge of a few edan. The journey to get here, however, has been anything but easy. The mists of the Demon Hills obscure both path and person, hiding the Oni base of operations deep within its smothering embrace. Both Aingjada and Oni alike use it as cover to launch relentless guerrilla attacks at the Ninyu Kikun, before retreating here to the safety of the Shirgrii. The monks inside the temple offer stiff resistance, crushing hobgoblin lamellar with their ancient kanugarl weapons. The unshackling of the Basfurji, the Punished Oni, presents an unexpected counterattack. More than one Aingjada chooses to make the ultimate sacrifice, diving into a hobgoblin edan with explosive fireworks strapped to their body. Rarely do they make a dent in the numbers. Finally, the fighting stops, and the silence of the mists falls once more over the hills. The ruler of the dynasty is bound in Korashi and brought before the Grand Marshal. The Oni holds their head up high, proud even when reduced once more into servitude. The Grand Marshal does not speak. What use is there in speaking to a slave? Instead, the Grand Marshal gives a wave of the hand to an assistant, who unsheathes a gleaming blade. As the Grand Marshal turns to walk away, the ruler instead opens their mouth. "Jaher, Harimar, greater kings than you have tried to claim the Demon Hills. And yet we remain, unconquered. We were here before you came, little goblin. We will be here when you go." The assistant merely raises the sword high overhead. For the first time, the ruler looks down, unwilling to see the end that approaches. But the ruler does make one final comment, whispered quietly to no one in particular.
