Recently, a representative sent us a dispatch informing us that she had come to a decision: her projections indicate that the expected marginal value of the capital investments we would make with access to primerate funds has fallen below the minimum viable threshold for her to serve as intermediary between us and our creditors, and she will consequently be withdrawing from her role as marketmaker. We think that this is her way of saying that she does not want to be friends anymore. If we work on our relationship with her, we will likely be able to secure access to these funds again, but in the meantime this will have implications for our merchants and their present control over our monetary policy.
