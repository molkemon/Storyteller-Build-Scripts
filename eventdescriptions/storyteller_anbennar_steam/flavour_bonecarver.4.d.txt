Grom had thought the White Walls were impenetrable both inside and outside. He believed that he could safely tuck himself within the bricks of the old Castanorian castles. Yet perhaps this idea of safety was the most damning evidence of his lack of faith most of all, for he had forgotten that decades ago, it was his grand prophet that breached the White Walls of Castanor. And soon, his most devout followers were coming back to break them once more. At the dead of night, howls and mortar shells reverberated against the stone cold walls, letting droves of orks pour in like a tide, all shouting one sentence in clear harmony even while brutally fighting the well-armored guards of Severed Ear. “Ozgar Urakan, Ozgar Dookan.” What would have been a sight of fear and terror to most clansmen was instead a sign of coming liberation to the peasants, serfs, and slaves of the oppressive clan, all grabbing whatever they could to use as a weapon and running out of their ghettos, also shouting. “Ozgar Urakan, Ozgar Dookan. Ozgar Urakan, Ozgar Dookan. Ozgar Urakan, Ozgar Dookan.” As the call for freedom rumbled the walls of the citadel, the Severed Ear guardsmen would drop their weapons out of submission, wishing to not cause any more needless bloodshed against an uphill battle, especially against their own kin. As for Grom himself…
