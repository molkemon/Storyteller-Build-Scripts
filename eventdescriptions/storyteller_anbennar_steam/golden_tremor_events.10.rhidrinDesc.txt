A hundred ordsikar kneel, draped in golden chains: the symbol that binds them to Lejdazamh. Behind each of them, a thousand clansmen drum their hooves, golden earrings hanging from their ears. Like grass before the wind, the crowd parts, as the monarch strides forth, stopping before the great cairn atop which Rhidrin’s body lays. The shamans anoint the monarch in the blood of the funeral rite’s sacrifices; they brush the monarch’s arms and chest with golden paint. At last, they bring forth the Golden Codex, the great work of Rhidrin’s life, and the monarch hoists it above their head. "This," the monarch calls out, voice ringing clear across the now silent crowd, "is the inheritance that Rhidrin left us. This is the legacy he leaves behind; a legacy of order, wrought in gold. He called out to us, promising prosperity, and though his voice has fallen silent…"
