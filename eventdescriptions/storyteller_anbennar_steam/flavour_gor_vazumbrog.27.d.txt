To the Generals and the ruler, By Halanna, what is happening to our country? A certain domain was once a place of order and industry, yet now it stands on the brink of collapse. The refugees; gods, so many of them. Every day, more arrive, their faces hollow with fear, their bodies broken and scarred. They tell tales of the dragon… I cannot bring myself to believe what I hear, yet the terror in their eyes makes disbelief impossible. The resources of that domain are stretched beyond breaking. The granaries run low, the infirmaries overflow, and the streets are crowded with desperate souls clinging to what little hope remains. My guards can barely maintain order, and riots are inevitable if relief does not come soon. Meanwhile, the shadow of that beast looms larger with each passing day. I am not a military man, but I know this. we cannot withstand Her. If even a fraction of what I have heard is true, She will tear through that domain like a pick through soft stone. Yet I am expected to hold this hold together with nothing but words and dwindling supplies? I demand; yes, demand; immediate aid. Soldiers, food, anything. Do not make that domain another name whispered in fear and mourning. Governor Balgor Deephelm
