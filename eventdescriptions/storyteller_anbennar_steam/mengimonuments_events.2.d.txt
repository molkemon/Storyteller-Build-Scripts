The crowd chants. The game is close to over with the last candle almost a puddle, the ball seems as if it has spent the entire game in the air. It is one to three, yet both teams play as if fatigue were merely a word unheard. The team counts are still seven to seven, yet all have received the honor of injury. Even from the goal area, you can see the bruises and scrapes down in the pit. Suddenly, an upset; the losing team scores. It is now two to three, though they still have much work to do. Both teams continue the ferocity, checking each other with elbows to the back and kicks to the shins. The ball is slung one last time as the horn is blown; though it misses its mark, you can't but feel proud of what you have seen.
