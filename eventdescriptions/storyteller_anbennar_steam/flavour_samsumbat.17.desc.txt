For too long the clerics of Kheterata relied on their slaves for every menial task they could think of, believing they could not possibly do the gods' work without them. What they did not realize, apart from the inhumanity of the system, was that this put a barrier between them and those they were trying to minister to. It is no wonder that worship of Nirakhet and Her son never took hold in this part of the empire. While there is still resistance to the message in Horashesh, and many traditionalist holdouts in the clergy, the reforms of the realm have resulted in great progress in the region. Many have seen the new humility of the priests of Nirakhet and have come to honor Her as we do. Such change is painful, yes, but it is clear that greater liberty for the people is healthy for the mind, body, and soul.
