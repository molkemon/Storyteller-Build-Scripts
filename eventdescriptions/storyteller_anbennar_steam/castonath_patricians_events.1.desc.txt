Apparently, one of our patrols in the city of Castonath has stumbled on a most gruesome sight. The food stores in one of the palaces of the patricians were filled to the brim with local flesh, frozen and quartered. Of course, our appalled patrol killed the patrician residents immediately and burned the entire place down. According to the patrol, the patricians spat out some venomous words and outed some compatriots. Apparently, this practice was pervasive and appeared at the zenith of the Greentide, when Castonath's food supply broke down and the patricians were forced to find an alternative source of sustenance. With no evidence left from the burned palace, we have no way of gathering evidence or checking our patrol's word; surely even the worst of humans would not stoop to the horrific act of eating an intelligent species' flesh. But at the same time, how else could they have survived Castonath's long period of occupation during the Greentide?
