Our magical organizations, once so highly advanced, have been put under immense strain in light of our push for nationwide magical expansion. Our antiquated systems of bureaucracy simply cannot adequately respond to the need to do more, faster. Regional representatives of the mage organization have taken matters into their own hands, organizing community guilds and creating rules of behavior that vary wildly on a hyperlocal basis. Initial impressions are positive. magical progress is being made on a massive scale, even as central control devolves back into the hands of local elites. While to many this development represents a cutting of red tape, a vocal set of critics warn that what is being done now will take decades to undo. Once we allow power to slip away from the capital, we may never see it return. What will we do when, in ten years, we wish to organize the nation's magical corps into a different project? No, they say, we must take action now and swiftly. Control must be reestablished, even if it comes at the cost of immediate progress.
