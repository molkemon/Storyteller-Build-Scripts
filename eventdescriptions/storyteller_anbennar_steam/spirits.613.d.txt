The best part of hot summer days, little Roki decided, were warm summer nights. The air flushed with potential, feeling the heat of embarrassment at the ongoing celebrations; now and then a bright firework blush bloomed across the dark face of the night. Roki took a seat on a grassy hill. From her position high up, she saw Yamaga running to catch her. "Look how fast I am," the boy boasted, sprinting up the steep slope as quickly as his clogs would carry him. Halfway up he tripped and something flew out of his hand, but he picked himself up and made it over to Roki. Once there, she saw that he was holding two kebabs. "This one didn't touch the dirt," he explained, handing one over. They sat together as they ate, laughter and music trickling up from the procession below. Yamaga carefully picked the grass off his kebab. "You know," he said, "Old Man Aromo is about ready to pass away. And he wants to be Devoured tonight, on All Ancestors' Day. It's supposed to be symbolic or something. They're all gathered at the High Temple to see it. You wanna come watch?" "Who's Devouring him?" Roki asked, licking the remnants of her kebab off the stick. "The Chamda kid… little sesamegrinder. He's gonna come back to school next year with fire powers or something." Yamaga kicked the dirt. "I wish my old man would pass away. I bet I'd get fire powers." Roki looked up to the night sky and felt something stir in her. She began to recite the prayer she'd learned in class. "I pray for all my ancestors, known and unknown, One within me. I pray for the coming of the Eternal Tamongor, when we will rejoice in his blood…"
