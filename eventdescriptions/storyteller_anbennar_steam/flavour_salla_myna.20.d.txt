Bricena could feel the great hawk’s muscles coil through its feathers as she climbed on its back. For a moment, she could feel the hawk’s thoughts, feeling it readjust its stance to account for her weight. Then, with a powerful thrust of its wings, they lifted off, soaring high above the emerald sprawl of the Deepwoods. Then immediately, the hawk dived. Panicked, Bricena dug her fingers into the hawk’s feathers. They approached the canopy at breakneck speed, only for the hawk to adjust its direction again right before colliding with the upper branches. Bricena felt the wind whip through her hair and scream in her ears as the treetops turned into a blur beneath them. It was terrifying. It was liberating. As the flight continued and her nerves and excitement died down, however, Bricena regained control of her thoughts. In the clearings below her, she saw figures looking up at her, as ants to her. She would be well out of arrowshot of them, but if she had her bow with her they would be at her utter mercy. With this speed, this height, Salla Myna could be unstoppable. She tugged on the hawk’s shoulder, and wheeled back toward Ciranmyna. In the distance, she saw dozens of great hawks take flight from the slopes of Ciranmyna. Every single one carrying a figure on its back.
