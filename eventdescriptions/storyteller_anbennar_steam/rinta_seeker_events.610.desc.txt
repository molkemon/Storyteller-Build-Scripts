There are many elixirs amongst the Rinta Seeker’s faith. Some ward off bad spirits, while others are said to open the mind to unknown knowledge. Whatever boons they may provide, the Mwalim are giving them out for free in front of the main hall in Abeesooyinka. Using them as medical aids, the Mwalim have gotten to work on curing the needy of the city. They’ve done it quickly as well. It seems that thousands more people have begun to travel the roads today, even to our homelands. Many praise the Mwalim for being able to cure illnesses incurable before and fixing horrifying deformities. While the general public seems happier about this grand event, the Darwiiz claim that the overuse of this medicine is an insult to the legacy of their forefathers. They affirm that while the elixirs are powerful, they shouldn’t be used like other medicines, as they require special care in line with scripture. While no Mwalim school has made any statements on this topic directly, individual scholars seem to have a distaste of the Darwiiz for their unwillingness to push past their tradition and use the tools given to them for the better. I’ve even heard of a few Mwalim synthesizing the elixirs to cure simple cuts in protest of the sacrality of these elixirs.
