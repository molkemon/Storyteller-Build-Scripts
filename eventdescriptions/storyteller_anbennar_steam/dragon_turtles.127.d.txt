I have been reflecting during my recent trips into the Kiamoa. I see such wondrous things there, but they are dashed to pieces so often after I see them. Only the hardiest survive, and if I am the hardiest, it is my responsibility to carry a piece of what could not handle the storm’s winds with me. When I am not out exploring, I enjoy resting in my cave, and over the years I have fashioned myself a set of small tools for shipbuilding. With the right amount of timber I can build just about anything, and recently I recalled a beautiful ship that I saw a few moments before it was pulverized. Then I recalled you. Your nation is full of brave and skilled sailors, and I thought you might like to see the ships for yourselves. They are very tiny, but so are you. I thought you might be able to make good use of them. That is right, the ships are yours. There is no need to thank me. Just get them fixed up, and then maybe their captains can sail out into the storm with me.
