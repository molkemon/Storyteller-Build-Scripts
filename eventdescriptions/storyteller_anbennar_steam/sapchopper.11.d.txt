Aye, boss, that’s the one. Ruuk nodded, his eyes tracking the ork leaving the camp. It had been a lucky break. He shook his head, no, it had been more than luck. The ork knew better than to doubt his instincts. It was something his father never let him forget. As he and his fellow sellswords followed the ork, Ruuk let his mind clear, focusing on nothing but his target. The ork was unassuming, nondescript even. Take your eyes off of them, and you’d forget they were ever even there. It was just like the Whisperer, the false ork whose foul words had spelled doom for his clan. A bitterness welled up within Ruuk from knowing that creature was on the prowl once more. Its machinations had brought the Greentide to a halt; ruined him and more. Now, it sought something from the strongest of the ork clans. Shit, lost him. Ruuk blinked at his companion’s words. Sure enough the presence was gone. Even now he strained to recall what he had even been looking for. Sorry boss. He shook his head with a smile, Naw. You did good. Before it was just a hunch. Now we know.
