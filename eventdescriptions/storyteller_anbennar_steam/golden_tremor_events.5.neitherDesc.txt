The surveyors had not expected to find anything of note. The steppe had a way of swallowing ambition, its rivers shallow, its soil stubborn, its wealth practically nonexistent. Nonetheless, they shouldered on, pacing out distances, recording landmarks, and taking measure of what little the land was willing to yield. A thankless task, but at least they would not be dying in whatever new war the ruler was waging. It was nearly dusk when one of them paused at the river’s edge. This region had been highlighted in the unfinished notes of the late ruler’s son, as a place suspected to conceal a hidden wealth, but thus far they had found nothing. Then they spied it. A glimmer, faint and fleeting, caught in the current. At first it seemed a trick of the light, but curiosity quickly won out. Wading into the water, they sifted through the gravel, fingers numbing in the snowmelt before closing around a strange rock. Pulling it from the water, it shone in the light of the setting sun, a nugget of solid gold. For years, the ruler’s dreams of modernization had been stifled by the poverty of the steppe. He had left behind sketches and plans for roads, industries, and professional armies, all abandoned due to the exorbitant cost of such endeavors. But with a reliable source of gold, these dreams might at last be achievable. After carefully recording the location and gathering several more nuggets, the surveyors set off to inform the ruler of their findings, wondering all the while how much more the steppe could be hiding.
