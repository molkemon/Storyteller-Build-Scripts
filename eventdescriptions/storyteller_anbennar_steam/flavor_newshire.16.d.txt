There is a knock at the door. Cora Kettlebrook, a promising young mage, the progeny of Layton Kettlebrook himself, sweeps into the office, escorted by several of her students. All bear the Chloromancer's mark; Cora's adorned with the symbols of the Guildmaster. She smiles tightly; a thorn, hidden amongst a bushel of roses. Ruler, she begins, lowering herself into the chair opposite, "I come with a business opportunity; one which would greatly benefit our darling Shire." Her suggestion is simple; an enshrined position in government, protected from the stifling oversight of the Magisterium, in exchange for the continued support of the Chloromancer's Guild. It is not phrased as a suggestion. Even now, years after the Deluge, the heroism of the Guild is remembered. Their magic is potent, and many a farmer has made her fortune using their elixirs and spells. To refuse them would, implicitly, cost the Stewards their careers. Cora Kettlebrook leaves the office with a declaration in hand. The door does not close behind her.
