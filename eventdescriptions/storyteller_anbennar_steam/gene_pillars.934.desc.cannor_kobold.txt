You petitioned multiple times to be part of this expedition, as all the records suggested that one of the major dragons once was held here, you among the hundreds of potential candidates were chosen. However, the deeper you travelled into this so-called "draconic containment facility," the more you regret your past choice. There is something fundamentally wrong with this place. You deploy the doorbreaching tool and nod to your colleagues to stand back. With a sizzle and a whirr, an opening is made through the centre of the door, allowing access to you all. You put your head through to assess the room on the other side. Inside is a laboratory, but on a much different scale to any you saw before in this complex. The reason for this scale difference is immediately apparent, as you see the figure at the far wall. Unfortunately, at this point you pass out. When you come to, your colleagues are leant over you offering some water, eyes full of concern and red with recently shed tears. You look between them and ask, "Is it real then?" The first responds, "Unfortunately so, and has been preserved here for thousands of years." You stand on shaky legs and turn back to the wall and see it. The black dragon, one of the lost dragons, butchered and displayed for all to see as if it were in a textbook. You step forward to look closer and notice something slightly more disturbing: when checking out the clawed feet, the feet appear to have been bound to the wall, yet the scrape marks underneath and slight damage around the bindings lead you to believe that the dragon may not have been deceased before the anatomical study began, and was flailing in pain and fear before these inhuman scientists. "They will pay," you announce to the room at large, "but first, we must care for these remains. No one deserves such a fate; especially not a god."
