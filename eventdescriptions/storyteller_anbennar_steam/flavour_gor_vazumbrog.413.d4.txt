The constructs' work beneath the earth did not stop with the mines. They helped secure those ancient hoards our ancestors confined. Their metal flesh shrugged off the blows of tentacles and blades. No minds to twist, no souls to steal, and undeterred by shades, titanium flesh could hold the spells too strong for mortal dwarves. Abjurers know that golem hide is a perfect place for wards. Their mindless minds seemed to prefer a grayeyed knightsavant. Though golems brought a violent end to their sire's protest of want, thus led, the gems beneath the ground on metal shoulders wend to blessed, cursed dwarves who cry, "Titanium does not bend."
