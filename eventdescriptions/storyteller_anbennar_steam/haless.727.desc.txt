Yanshen's military power rests on six regions, each with distinct strategies. Bianfang, the western bastion, fields swift, ironwilled troops; Szicheng, in the heartland, houses the Grand War Camp; Jiantsiang defends the fertile northeast with strict order against nomads. Gushibi, seat of the Stone Army Alliance, gathers mercenaries; Jiangdu, the City of Warlords, holds supreme command and is famed for rigorous drills; Damaogong, on the Halberd Plains, trains infantry skilled at breaking cavalry. Though united under central command, the six long differed in systems and drills. Now, with order restored, the Six Army Review rotates officers to compare doctrines. Jiangdu seeks clearer duties; Szicheng and Jiantsiang refine eastwest readiness; Bianfang and Damaogong plan joint drills; Gushibi urges merging mercenaries into border forces. If they act as one, joint warfare may revive; if mismanaged, reform may stall. Now is the time to set firm statutes and align duties—only united can the six move without hesitation.
