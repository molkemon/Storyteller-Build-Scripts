After she had given the word, the instruments of life had been dismantled. The phylactery had been shattered. Immarel heard no more from the Everfrost Prince of grand plans or an eternal winter; but she bore no punishment either. She had not made any sort of official pact with him yet, and that fact had likely saved her life. Nevertheless, the close call with the fey has changed Immarel. While she still believes that Gemradcurt must rule unopposed in Eordand, she understands that the Winter Court cannot afford to throw away all the things they had once fought for; or else they may invite that terrible fate she had dreamt of during that long night. Her quest for personal power had nearly made her a slave of the fey, but no longer; if she wants to lead her people to glory, she must do so alone, under her own power. And, chance willing, Immarel and her successors would build an eternal Winter Court, not just an eternal winter.
