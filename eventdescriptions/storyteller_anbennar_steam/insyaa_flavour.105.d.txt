GetALittleUseOutOfIt had gone through at least six bodies in the time that I had known them. They were like those fickle, wealthy young men who cannot help but change their fashion from month to month; if another machine so much as expressed envy for their chassis, they would offer it blithely for the chance at a little novelty. This was, I understood, unusual among machines, who broadly had some attachment to the bodies they chose, but since I only saw Use once a year at most, it was an enjoyable novelty to anticipate. This time, though; this time the chassis was simply absurd. From every arm segment and panel of Use's torso came attachments and little tools, quite out of keeping with the intentional design of most machine bodies, at least in my experience. Little artificed magicmeasurers, curved and viciously sharp extending knives, a tray of variously sized compartments in which to store small objects. by the time Use had finished showing me everything their new chassis could do, I was bewildered as to its purpose. "Never know what you'll find in a Gene Pillar," said Use, and I confess I fretted, for there was no more dangerous place I could think for my friend to go. But I had the distinct sense, even then, that they would never switch bodies again.
