Tragedy strikes the halls of the capital as the lord's beloved pet otter has passed away. The grief is strong among many: the courtiers who enjoyed the levity of its playfulness, the cooks who prepared the fish it caught, the fisherelves who accompanied the lord and taught them their arts, and the populace of the capital who on several occasions received the masses of fish left over from the otter's contributions to the lord's feasts. But the greatest grief lies with the lord themselves, who has lost the company of the most steadfast and loyal of friends.
