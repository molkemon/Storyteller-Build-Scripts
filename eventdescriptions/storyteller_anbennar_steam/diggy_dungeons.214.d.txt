One by one, the expedition members carefully make their way down the watercarved steps, using climbing gear to grab hold of whatever they can. Slowly but surely, they make it down, every slip up immediately corrected by a strong grip on a piton or a fastened rope. Then, disaster strikes. An expedition member slips, and pulls too hard on a poorly attached rope. An untidy knot comes loose. Unsupported, the fall gains momentum. Three pitons are ripped out of the wall. A rope snaps. As one expedition member after another hits the one below them as they fall, the fall turns into an avalanche. Several members of the expedition are pushed off the staircase, and fall screaming into the depths below. Twenty seconds later, all is silent again, as the remaining members regain their breath.
