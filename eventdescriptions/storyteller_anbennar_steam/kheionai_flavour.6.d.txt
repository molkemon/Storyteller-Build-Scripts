To the Kheionai, philosophical schools, which are organised teachings on both how to see the world and act within it, are the bedrock of society, government, and culture. Each Kheion has been moulded by the philosophies its citizens adhere to, and that means that the interaction between different cities is analogous to the interaction of different philosophies. Normally those differences can be overcome through debate, consensus, and concession, but, as is common to philosophers, there can be a tendency to entrench one’s own values in response to opposition. This has recently happened in the realm’s relationship with its subjects, and unfortunately, it seems they may be to blame. The Kylakas and their advisors’ steadfast beliefs about how government and governance should operate have resulted in complete deadlock, as much of the subjects’ local leadership now refuses to work with the realm, believing that as the realm will never give, neither should they. There seems to be only two options available. either the realm finally retreats from its position, or it uses force to impose its own will on the cities.
