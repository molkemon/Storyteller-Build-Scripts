We step out into the tunnel, our caution making every footstep and kicked pebble sound like a gunshot in our ears. That's when it hits us—the roaring is gone, and with it any trace of the spider tsunami. The darkness of the tunnel behind us is empty now, as silent as the grave that it now is for many of our fellow expeditionaries. We spare them one last thought, and then turn to make our way back home.
