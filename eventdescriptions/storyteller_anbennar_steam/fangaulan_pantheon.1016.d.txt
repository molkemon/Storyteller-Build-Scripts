We gather now by the river, but its roaring current will not harm us. We all, each one of us, are under the protection of Makan, beloved of the griots and walker of the rivers. This is the place where escaped slaves cast off their chains into the river, and are born anew, free and cleansed. And there is no better place than this to hear the tales of the RiverChild and the great victories she won, because her clever words and her wondrous drumbeats flow around the people the same as the current of the rivers. Listen now, one and all, to the tale of Makan, the freer of slaves. I will tell you a tale filled with her impish glee. I will tell you…
