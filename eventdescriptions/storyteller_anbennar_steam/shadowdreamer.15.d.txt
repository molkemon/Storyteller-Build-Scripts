Marshal Gut Dwarfeater stood above his assembled force of laborers, one boot on the neck of a decapitated statue of some milleniadead dwarf king. With his arms crossed and a tusky smile, he watched as they picked over an immense pile of treasures and knickknacks. A just reward for a long, hard job well done. Across the hold of Gor Ozumbrog, every single doorway had been systematically widened. Beds built for dwarves had been removed and replaced, usually sold to goblins who could actually use them. Cooking pits were dug. Mausoleums and shrines were emptied and repurposed. Once all of that was done, they set to the hardest part of the work, tearing down and annihilating every dwarven statue, icon, and symbol in the hold. And now it was done, and Gut was giving rations and approving or forbidding his workers’ choices of loot. He could not help but smile as the dream of his old friend and mentor, Zog, began to come true before his very eyes.
