You sigh, staring out of the high window of your chambers at the endless blue sky. Harpy subjects wheel and dive on the wind currents, their forms casting fleeting shadows on the ground below. Lately, their ways have occupied your thoughts. You have listened to their Riaveltu, their loresingers, and the songs they sing are not of gods or afterlives, but of tangible deeds. They sing of precedent and the wisdom of the flock, and the pursuit of personal fulfillment. You look at the ledgers on your desk, treaties bearing your seal, edicts that you still have yet to sign. You have built a legacy of power and stability, but a profound emptiness gnaws at you. Your halls are empty. Something feels missing from your life, and the feeling that eludes you is only the latest sleight of hand in the repertoire of an unseen riddler, one whose coarse schemes have been pickpocketing any sense of merriment for all of your years. You know there are others amongst your court who feel the same. What is the great purpose you are striving for, beyond mere governance? The teachings of the sky offer an answer, a path focused on the pursuit of fulfillment in the here and now.
