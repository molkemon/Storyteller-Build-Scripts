Ever since a leader led the first rebellion in the mountains of a region, outsiders have feared that a violent wave of liberation would lead to the destruction of their societies, anarchy in their cities, and a breakdown of their economies. We come to bring freedom and change, yes, but utilizing constructive avenues is far preferable to change at the point of a spear. As the Steward of the Sorrow, we have inherited a tradition of Satrapies, designed to extract tribute to enrich the realm, but we have been able to repurpose this diplomatic structure for better ends. With new reforms passed in the council of the Steward, and a number of allies who we have persuaded to adopt this new relationship, we have seen the positive outcomes of greater cooperation in both our own republic and that of our Satrapies. There are, of course, those who resist these changes throughout the region, but the results speak for themselves: greater economic prosperity, improved military effectiveness, and an increase in engineering innovation. There is no reason not to continue developing this system across the entire continent.
