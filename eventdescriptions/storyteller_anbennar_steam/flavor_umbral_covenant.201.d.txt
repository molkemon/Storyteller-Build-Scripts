Ikomeye is collapsing with laughter. It is a rare thing; the dread diviner is a terrifying spectre of the Court, ornery and difficult, often filthy and unkempt. She has absolutely lost it; she is barely hanging onto her seat. Cheni looks up from his morsel of cheese, mockoffended. “It wasn’t a moan.” “Was a moan,” Ikomeye howls, “longlong moan, just as it hit your tongue.” Umorozi is hiding a low smile; she has chosen a Yamokian Eyib, which she dabs by the spoonful onto chilibread. She has none of Cheni’s love for rotting Cannorian abominations. They can’t help it. Ikomeye is so far gone that the momentum of her laughter takes them all with her; even the Vile One. “I am at peace with moaning for a Baempes,” Cheni says, loftily. Umorozi shakes her head. “You say this shit on purpose.” It is only the four of them; they sit in the empty parlour of a palace, its dynasty extinguished. This absurd afternoon was Ikomeye’s idea, for she is ever the first to suggest indulgence, to revel in power, to enjoy revenge. As they drape themselves across the sumptuous couches of this place, they all feel like upstart village girls for a moment. “You have to try this,” Cheni says; he is motioning to the Vile One, to a table laden with every cheese made between Lorent and Arawkelin. It is Her power, after all, that has brought these things here. The Vile One hesitates; Her smile is still there, fading like heat from forged metal, but She keeps herself back, She always does. Cheni does not brook such nonsense; he walks over and tugs Her by the arm, an astonishing liberty borne of familiarity.
