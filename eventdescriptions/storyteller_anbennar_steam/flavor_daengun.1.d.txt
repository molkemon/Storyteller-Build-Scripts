For generations, a time known as the Sewengun, our land was divided between ethnic kingdoms; the militant  kingdom of , heirs of Guwamuud and masters of the horse; the deeply traditional  kingdom of , descendants of Menjaeng and unmatched bowmen; and the urbanized  kingdom of , of no great pedigree yet favored by the scholarbureaucrats for its legal and diplomatic expertise. Yet none were granted the blessing of the spirits, for none were truly the rulers of .  The reign of JeongHun, King of Daengun, would see an end to this. He would lead Daengun to a quick and decisive victory in the final Sewengun war, proposing a final resolution to the conflict at the following peace conference. Marrying a princess of Ondeog and promising his firstborn son's hand to one of Gwangang, he would see the two rival kingdoms absorbed into Daengun; in the process, gaining the support of the spirits, something unseen since the collapse of Guwaamud, and earning the title Peacemaker.  Now, JeongHun lies on his deathbed and hopes peace shall not die with him.
