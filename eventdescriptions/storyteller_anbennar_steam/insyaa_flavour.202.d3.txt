Sometimes, the virtues of memory are unexpected. The team we left behind to study the Gene Pillar in that province has discovered that the Pillar had a relationship with the Kiamoa, the immense stormwall which rings the whole world; indeed, that it might be able to banish the Kiamoa forever. This confirms that there is a world beyond the Kiamoa at all, one unremembered by our cultural Memory, one which must be catalogued before it is forgotten and passes into nonexistence. We had wondered upon this and fretted, unsure how to go about harnessing the Pillar’s power, until finally our wayfarers shared this strangest of developments with Hoki’eal, and Hoki’eal let out an eager laugh. She knows at least one secret of the Pillar, a special phrase used in tandem with others to bring down the Kiamoa. There are three such phrases, split between herself, the immense vault of Grandfather Moss’ mind, and the many songs of Nalo Pu’ukani; Hoki’eal has never been able to get the others to cough up, for they counsel caution in such an enormous undertaking, in exposing our world to what lies beyond. We keep the memory to honour those before us; we keep the memory to make our futures meaningful. If we can prove the merits of our society, our commitment to remembering what is beyond the Kiamoa, perhaps the other two Dragon Turtles will join their memories with Hoki’eal, trusting us where they do not trust their reckless sister. Surely it is worth a try?
