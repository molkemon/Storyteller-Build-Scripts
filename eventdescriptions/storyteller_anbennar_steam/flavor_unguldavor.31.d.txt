Samûr Ultanuhankar was tired. He spent decades trying to get mithril for the cannons, but mithril was too expensive to be used in mass production. Instead, he had to wait until the resources were ready for the final mass transmutation. In Steelhyl, he gathered with several alchemists and shamans. Mage towers from across Unguldavor pulled magical energy from their provinces and aimed it at Steelhyl. Samûr knelt down on the hill and started chanting. Shamans directed their magical energy towards the hill as the other alchemists poured potions around the province. The hills glowed blue as the sun set in the sky. After a few hours, the glow faded and the moon rose overhead. Samûr fell to the ground, two apprentices picking him up. "Did we succeed?" The first batch of steel from the new Steelhyl was forged into a cannon and brought for a test. Samûr stood at the side as it was prepared. The new iron from Steelhyl had many properties of mithril, but it was now time to test it. Samûr stepped back and let the cannon fire. This time, the cannon did not melt. The shot was more accurate while still being destructive. Furthermore, it seemed like the recipe could be used with muskets as well. After all these years, it seemed that Unguldavor now had the perfect secret to use. Not only could it make the army stronger, the gunpowder also had civilian uses to clear land for construction or for mining.
