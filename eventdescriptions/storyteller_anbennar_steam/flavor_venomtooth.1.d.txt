The journey home from Castonath was long and arduous. It was all for nothing, or so it seemed. Despite Venomtooth’s best efforts, the other clans failed to hold the line and Korgus died, taking the dream of an unstoppable Greentide washing over the world with him. Among the despondent procession, Aâdga stood out. Tall, lean, and with a glare that could kill, Aâdga rode on her warg with a steely expression. She was there when the orks first left the caverns as a young warchief ready to prove herself. She planned ambushes, utilized various poisons, and was instrumental in the fall of Balmire. Even in her middle age, Aâdga was full of vigor and cunning. One young ork chief, clearly fresh from combat, was visibly holding back his fear as he asked, “Chieftain Aâdga, what do we do now? The Dookanson is dead. He lost to a human girl. What does that say of Dookan, or our purpose? Do the humans have the right to rule over orks now?” Aâdga glared at the chief as she sharpened her dagger. “Back when I fought alongside Korgus, he killed younglings who doubted him. Some followed Korgus because they believed he was the Dookanson destined to free our god, however, others joined him because they saw the obvious. Humans are weak. Their decrepit kingdoms were ripe for the taking, but no one made a move. Korgus was able to rally all the orks together, allowing us to match the numbers fielded by the human kingdoms and destroy them. Now, the age of Korgus is over. We no longer need that unity.” She dipped her dagger in a vial of poison before throwing it at a passing deer. The deer collapsed instantly. Chieftain Aâdga looked at the chief again and announced, “The Greentide is over, but the Age of Strength has just begun. We will kill those puny adventurers who rallied against us before getting our revenge on the cowardly orks of Bonecarver.”
