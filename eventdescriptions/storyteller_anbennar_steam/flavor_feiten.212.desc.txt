At last, the prototype was ready to fly. Instead of the usual open envelope for hot air, sealed cells of hydrogen, the newly discovered lifting gas, provided the impetus for flotation. The sheer buoyancy of this new gas meant that the new Shuimu could hold much more weight, while also cutting up and down effortlessly through the clouds much faster than before. At least, in theory. In practice, the prototype had not left the ground yet, and all were waiting to see who would get the honor of first flight.
