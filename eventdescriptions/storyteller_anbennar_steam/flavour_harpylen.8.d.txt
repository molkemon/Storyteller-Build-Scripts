The old perch of Siaduna FlamingTalon proved invaluable in tracing our past, for their flock had carved their homes from the mountains themselves, by fire and by talon. From their perch on Rahen's western wall, their realm had spread from AzkaSur in the west, into Marutha to the east. The Siadun would name their queendom Siadanlen, and their songs were rife with tales of valor, conquest, and glory in battle. But Jaher's coming would spell the end of Siadanlen, for in his legions did the Siadun find a foe they could not best. And as the phoenix rose, did the harpy find herself under its yoke. Many had found this unbearable, and they had taken wing once more, scattering to the four winds, as our people seem doomed to do. But these songs of sorrow were invaluable to the Queendom, for she had put her own songs to paper, and many claimed that in so doing, she had murdered them. Yet through the reading mind could the song live again, and from Heunthume it was decreed that the hunters who had brought home the songs of Siadunlen would be reorganized as a new wing of the government. They were named Ivetasananthor, song hunters, and their mandate was the hunt for the songs of our people. Our past would be compiled, our history made known. And the songs of our people would be found, and brought home to Firanya's roost.
