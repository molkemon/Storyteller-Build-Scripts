Equal parts benediction and map, the marathon ceremonies of the Ptuauala, or The Way Back, transcend generations and challenge their adherents to find any place on this worldsea where they are truly lost. Clergy members who wish to train in this art are expected to memorize nigh infinite combinations of star charts and wave patterns and cloud formations. As soon as the memorization is complete, lessons on poetry, vocal exercises, history, theatre, and theology begin. The process slowly melds the twin worlds of this skill together, until the pathsermons of the clergy are seamless blends of spiritualism and practicum. Aspirants will dedicate decades of their lives to be considered as apprentices, and decades more to be considered masters. As such, schools specializing in teaching The Way Back adopt their members from a young age, catering to not only the every need of their students, but also the needs of their students’ families. This investment, while considerable, is a pittance compared to the revenue brought by even a single chartchanter; trading fleets happily shell out inestimable sums to patronize even a journeyman chartchanter, in the knowledge that so long as the uttering of the Ptuauala continues, their ships will undoubtedly return home. As such, the schooltemples of The Way Back never lack for resources, and invariably give their excess wealth away as an act of charity.
