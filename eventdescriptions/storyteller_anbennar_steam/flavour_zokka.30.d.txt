Sarisung's ash still clings to their cloaks, days later. The Watermarkets are gone, now. A new economy has been put into place. Still, they know, work is being done to unchoke the river of debris and detritus. They say the leader of one group begins, rubbing soot from his sleeve. They say a lot of things, the leader of the other group cuts in, looking up. They stand at the base of Tughayasa. Far above, Mindarandaya crowns the shoulder of the peak, pale and silent and glinting with frost. A silence stretches between them. At their backs, the camp rustles with morning drills. They'd be useful, the first leader says. Some three thousand minds, working for the Kult. If even a handful of them bend the knee, imagine what they could uncover. Wouldn't that be lovely, the second leader replies drily, cocking their brow. They've been chasing answers for centuries. Anything worthwhile is already buried in their libraries. You'd burn it, then? I would, the second leader responds, easily. Don't tell me that surprises you? The first leader shakes their head with a wry chuckle. More silence. The frost thickens, and the mountain looms. Far above, the monks begin their morning rites, trying to ignore the felflame beneath them.
