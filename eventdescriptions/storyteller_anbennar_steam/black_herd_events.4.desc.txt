The wagons groan beneath the weight of Maghargma’s history. Crates of scrolls, tomes, and even stone tablets travel at the center of the baggage train. My warriors grumble at the burden, unable to understand why carved stones and brittle parchment deserve space that might otherwise carry plunder. Their ignorance is pitiable; looted gold might feed a campaign for a season, but the knowledge in that cart will secure the future of the Black Herd. My future. At my command, a new crate is carried from the cart to my tent. Countless texts already litter every available surface, the fruits of many an evening spent pouring over the pages by candlelight. Sleep has been elusive since the fall of the city. Most recount little of value. endless drivel about clan feuds, tribute tallies, stories of what fool ate what beast; but a rare few point towards something far more interesting. A common figure in their histories is Waeruk Magerender, the tribeless ogre who struck down the Glutton King and was named Mengur’m for his deeds, starting a dynasty that would rule Maghargma for over a century. What is rarer is any discussion of what Magerender did in the aftermath of the battle, or even more tantalizingly, what he found within Loethrklerd. I have found several scrolls written in the years following the battle that claim he returned to Maghargma carrying several artifacts crafted by the Glutton King. These were then supposedly taken deep within the ruins and hidden away, fearing that destroying them would unleash the power within. A simplistic fear, but perhaps a reasonable one. One more night of study, then I shall return to Maghargma and claim for myself whatever power lurks beneath the city.
