The spire standing in the middle of the village was unremarkable at first glance, until a watchful gaze carefully studied its base and saw the visible blue veins of magic, dancing on the surface. The leader of the tribe, visibly older than our ruler, a veteran of many fights, attentively heard what our lord had said. "What shall we get in return for this cooperation, our lord? Why don't you just come and seize what you wish? Why don't you kill us and destroy what we revere?" 

"We won't do so not because we need you, but because you need us. Why raid farming villages if you could conquer cities? Why burn palisades of wood, if you could raze walls of stone? Why be content with squabbling among yourselves, in-fighting with other tribes of these hills, when you could march side by side with us, bolts of lightning above our heads heralding our ascent. Why rule villages, when together we could rule an empire?" 

The leader weighed the words of our lord and after a brief moment of silence responded "Very well, we shall fight on your side. Let us wield the storms together"; he extended his arm.
