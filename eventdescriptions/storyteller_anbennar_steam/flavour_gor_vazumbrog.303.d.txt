The gates of Gor Vazumbrog stood closed, their iron faces reflecting the grim gathering of dwarves outside. The expedition had vanished into the Serpentspine months ago, led by the monarch. No word had returned, no runner carried news of triumph or retreat. Now, a single survivor staggered into view, armour in tatters, face pale with grief. He carried only a battered standard, its once proud crest torn and bloodied. "The monarch is gone," he choked, dropping to his knees. A collective wail rose from the crowd, raw and unbridled. Deep within the hold, the bell tolled. It echoed Thulmir's name whispered in the streets like a prayer, then a curse, then a lament.
