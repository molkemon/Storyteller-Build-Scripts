The local water system here flows northwest, into the Bay of Insyaa; it forms part of an enormous network of rivers over and underground which merge into a titanic wetland as they approach the ocean. Over time, it seems that the same breaches which allowed us entry to the Pillar have taken place lower down the structure, flooding the Pillar unevenly. Water flows into upper levels and cascades down through voids in the flooring, so that a good three to four layers of the Pillar are essentially amphibious. And throughout these half-flooded layers we find, inevitably, the hinuitoads. We suspect the hinuitoads actually entered the Pillar from outside, for we have found no suitable habitat for them built by the Precursors; this whole set of levels seems to have originally served as warehousing. The toads have wandered in and multiplied incessantly in an environment uniquely protected from their predators. Exceedingly docile, such toads also produce a defensive mucus which, while somewhat harmful even to larger animals, is mostly renowned for its intense psychotropic effect. But, well. We are mounting a serious expedition here. So we have no time for that.
