Hemantsen and Rani Jira once again find themselves in audience with the lords of the Rahen Coast, each desperately pleading their case to the royal couple. Shouts ring across the royal chamber, each more shrill than the last. One noble finally stands taller than the rest, and marches to the front of the crowd. "But Raja, you cannot be serious. If our fields no longer grow cotton, we will lose our economic hold over trade within the Gulf. You seek to ruin us with this new decree." Hemantsen hears this, and with a single motion of his paw casts the room into a deathly silence. "You say that the loss of your cotton fields will cripple your estates," the Raja extolls, "But what of the children who go hungry in your streets? What of the elderly who slave away their weakened bodies to earn whatever meagre wage will feed their families?" Hemantsen sweeps his arm across the room, his expression filled with nobility and pride. "You do not serve your wallets; you serve the people. And the people cannot eat cotton." With that, Hemantsen sits, and with a final nod the audience is over. The nobles file out of the chamber, each more distraught and vengeful than the last. When they finally find themselves alone, Hemantsen slumps in his throne as he leans against his wife for support. "Every day, they test my patience. How much longer can we survive before these craven fools bring the world down around us?" Jira laughs and smiles at Hemantsen as she helps him from his seat. "We've faced grander challenges than a few upstart nobles, Hema."
