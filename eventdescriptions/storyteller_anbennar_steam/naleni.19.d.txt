The waters of the gulf lay calm, a tranquil blue mirror of the sky above.  Ships of all sizes down from the smallest sloop up to the largest barque cross the expanse without fear, the valuable wares and coinage contained in their holds unmolested. The finest goods: silk and porcelain, ivory and incense, right down to the mundanest of necessities: grain and lumber, iron and nails; all pass safely across the gulf. The black flag is an exceedingly rare sight, whilst the once feared blue and orange, always just over the horizon, now brings not dread in its onlookers, but reassurance. Provided they have paid their dues of course.  Ashore, the peoples of two continents know the grace of our rule. They enjoy the safety we supply, the curved roofs of our songhalls present in every settlement, providing not just security but entertainment too. Song the unifying principle soothing the tensions these disparate peoples: the Mengi, content to be one under Amilak’s chosen Mezhi’Mezhi, the Raheni, free of their castes and harimari overlords.  All along the mountains of the Great Horn, the statues of our ancestors will watch our waters forevermore.
