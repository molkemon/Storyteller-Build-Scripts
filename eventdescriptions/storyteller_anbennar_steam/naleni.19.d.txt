The waters of the gulf lay calm, a tranquil blue mirror of the sky above. Ships of all sizes, from the smallest sloop up to the largest barque, cross the expanse without fear, the valuable wares and coinage contained in their holds unmolested. The finest goods, such as silk and porcelain, ivory and incense, right down to the most mundane necessities like grain and lumber, iron and nails, all pass safely across the gulf. The black flag is an exceedingly rare sight, whilst the once feared blue and orange, always just over the horizon, now brings not dread to onlookers, but reassurance, provided they have paid their dues of course. Ashore, the peoples of two continents know the grace of the rule. They enjoy the safety provided, the curved roofs of the songhalls present in every settlement, providing not just security but entertainment too. Song is the unifying principle soothing the tensions these disparate peoples. the Mengi, content to be one under the chosen Mezhi’Mezhi of Amilak, and the Raheni, free of their castes and overlords. All along the mountains of the Great Horn, the statues of the ancestors will watch the waters forevermore.
