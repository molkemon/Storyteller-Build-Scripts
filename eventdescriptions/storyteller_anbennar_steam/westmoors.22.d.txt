"You know this was not easy for us," spoke the raspy voice of Lockwood Cockerwall, a large man with dark blue eyes, his hand on his claymore as he stared from the battlements to the moors. "We are good cousins to the Gerwicks; fighting them on the fields was akin to kinslaying for many of us. But we did so because we had faith in you, that you would see this realm united after a thousand years, what our Clan failed to do." And at a sudden, he smiled and laughed. "And you did, you did good, ruler. And for that..." He knelt and produced a piece of parchment. "I, Lockwood of Clan Cockerwall, pledge my sword to you. I shall fight your battles and shield you from any danger, and always serve the Kingdom of the Westmoors."
