The light is lost; it has fallen to malevolent dark. The hope sparked by Jaher, spread by his legions, and reinforced by Taelarios, maintained by generations of elven rule, is no more. The Sun Elves rule nothing; their fortresses have been crushed, their manors burnt to ashes. Where once they wore noble robes and opulent jewelry, they now wear only circlets of iron, chains clasped to their limbs as they are led to a life of slavery at the behest of a gnollish whip. But we remain. We have always remained; before Taelarios, before the Phoenix Legions, before Jaher, there was still such a thing as hope. It was a slim prayer, a feeble light beset by rough winds at all times, but it was never truly extinguished. We were wrong to place our hopes in elves, we were wrong to misinterpret Surakel's will, and we were wrong to deify all of Jaher's race. But it is not too late to make things right. The gnolls are our subjugators, but we will endure, and pray that one day they too might crumble to invasion like our former elven masters.
