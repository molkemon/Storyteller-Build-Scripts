The fisherman stood on the shoreline, gazing out over the river. Insects buzzed around his head and he frowned. The air had felt heavy the past few days, like the herald of an approaching storm, yet the sky was as clear as could be. Boats had to be paddled hard on the river, for the current itself had become sluggish if not outright still. Nets were hauled up empty or boasting only a scanty catch. Algae had begun to bloom in great clouds, now unimpeded by the flow of the river. The lifeblood of the Kharunyana had become still in its body.
