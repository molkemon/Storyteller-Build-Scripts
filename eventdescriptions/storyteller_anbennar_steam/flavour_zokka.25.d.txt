Well, the leader says, peering down at the map, "I suppose it looks enough like a paw." The other leader keeps their focus on the celebrating group in the town beneath them, some distance from the cliff they are standing on. "They seem to believe it." The other leader shrugs. Waves crash somewhere far below as the first leader pulls out a different scroll, unravelling it and eyeing it thoughtfully. "Are you sure we can't find compromise?" the first leader asks, already sounding defeated. This is a conversation they have been having for weeks. The other leader sighs, exasperated. "They would kill each other as soon as the territories touched." The other leader replies. "I am hardly averse to it, but the goal is profit." "And you think my potential recruits are better at that than yours?" "Don't be cute," the first leader says. The other leader shakes their head. "We are both sending whoever we can afford to lose." There is a pause. A crow circles overhead. "Fine. We will have to bother the pack lord about our little claw paw company project." The other leader lets out a low, amused growl. "That is what we are calling it?" "What? You want to argue with divine branding?" The rivals start down the ridge together, steps crunching through loose gravel. Below, the tides stretch far, far away.
