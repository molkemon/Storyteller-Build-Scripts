At first, Liu Yu's warnings were hard to believe. The captain's accusation was absurd to the highest; the scholars of the High Philosophy would never work any such treachery upon the state. As the rumors spread, however, it began to become a more and more credible idea; the eunuchs, as a group, became more standoffish. Their High Philosophy sympathies, namely, their steadfast desire to see the high temple of the realm preserved, became more and more obvious with each passing day, for it seemed to most of them that it was certain that the high temple would be scoured, and their quiet obedience turned to resentment, and that resentment turned to disrespect. Their disrespect is a threat to the country. Something needs to be done before it spirals into a rebellion.
