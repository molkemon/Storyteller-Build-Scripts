Among the countless subjects that pay the Raja homage in the realm is a small herd of elephants; part of the Ghavaanaji tribes' historical tribute and cared for as a sign of the Raja's prestige, and occasionally, when there are Reishi around, used for parades or hunting. The monarch has paid them little heed, until the ruler's encounter today. In the ruler's customary stroll through the Lotus Court's outlying gardens, during which the ruler thinks through all the myriad problems of the Harimraj, the day was utterly disrupted when the ruler trod into a large pile of elephant feces. Looking up in disgust and disbelief, the ruler barely dodged the swing of a massive trunk. If this insult was offered by any human to the Harimraj's second highest figure, they would face immediate execution. But, alas, the law is unclear when it comes to the elephants. Any response would have to go through the Raja's monarch. And if there is no punishment, that will indicate to the court who seemed to learn of the event in an hour where the vizier falls in the Raja's esteem.
