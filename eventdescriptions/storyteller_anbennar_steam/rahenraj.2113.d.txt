Among the countless subjects that pay the Raja homage in a small province is a small herd of elephants; part of the Ghavaanaji tribes' historical tribute and cared for as a sign of the Raja's prestige (and occasionally, when there are Reishi around, used for parades or hunting). Our lord has paid them little heed, until today’s encounter. In our lord’s customary stroll through the Lotus Court's outlying gardens, during which they think through all the myriad problems of the Harimraj, the day was utterly disrupted when they trod into a large pile of elephant feces. Looking up in disgust and disbelief, they barely dodged the swing of a massive trunk. If this insult was offered by any human to the Harimraj's secondhighest figure, they would face immediate execution. But, alas, the law is unclear when it comes to the elephants. Any response would have to go through the Raja. And if there is no punishment, that'll indicate to the court; who seemed to learn of the event in an hour; where the vizier falls in the Raja's esteem.
