Lazga could feel this Ozarm’Chadash was wrong. Her brother Gordak, great chief and warlord of Clan Cursed Howl, had faced challengers before, and killed all of them. The challenger Gordak faced today, Tûka, was no different. Gordak would not listen, but Lazga knew better. She had known Tûka since they were children. Tûka looked the same he ever had. His chipped tusk, his hunched posture, his narrow, uneven eyes. But he moved wrong, smiled wrong, felt wrong. Gordak, wielding an ax and a small bronze shield, had started with a defensive stance, as usual, stoically deflecting Tûka’s savage axeblows as his opponent was tiring himself out. Tûka’s slashes were brutal but short, chopping away at Gordak’s shield, savage but predictable. Finally, Gordak anticipated a strike and bashed his shield into Tûka’s hand, audibly breaking several fingers. Tûka staggered back, but the fear that flashed in his eyes was immediately subsumed by a black shine. Fey, Lazga realized. Without hesitation, she leapt over the barricade and slew the creature.
