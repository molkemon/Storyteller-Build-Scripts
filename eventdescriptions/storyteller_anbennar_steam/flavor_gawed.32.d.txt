The imperial march north was brutal. The Alenics brook no foreigner on their soil, no enemy among their fields, no foreign belly satisfied from the bounty of their river. A quarter of the princes’ host perished before the eagles even consented to give battle, their throats parched and bellies distended from hunger. When the sons of the Alen came, they took their toll, and many children of Dameria and Esmaria fell among the burned orchards and muddy banks of the north. But still the Imperial army marched. Weakened, battered, and bruised, they repulsed the foe and pushed forward, for theirs was a mission of higher import. This was a lesson about the sanctity of the Empire; a lesson the lords in Gaweton had forgotten. The Anbennarians marched, and carved their bloody swathe across the countryside as they approached the Gawedi capital. When they arrived, the princes and dukes did not want to wait for a siege; the anger that boiled in the blood of the dovesworn was great, so much imperial life had been lost. Vengeance were they, and they were here in the heights over Gawed. No, it was no longer the Eagle who loomed. Imperial wizards and cannons bombarded the walls of the Gawedi capital, and Empire forces rushed through the many breaches into the city center. As the defenders were routed and thrown to flight, the imperial vanguard approached the Gawedi palace. But where they expected to find the fiercest of the northmen, they were instead met by the palace garrison, offering terms of surrender, their ruler bound in chains and offered to the invaders if only the city would be spared.
