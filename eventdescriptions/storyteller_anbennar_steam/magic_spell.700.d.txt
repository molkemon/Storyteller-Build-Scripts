Time is a precise clockwork, a mechanism of springs and gears and escapements. The meek mage dares not touch it, overprecious as he is about the continuity of existence. You know better. for one year, one bright, glorious, shining year, you put your finger on the clock hand, halting its cycle—nay, winding it back. Your features smoothed and softened. You knew no consequences for your actions. The lethargy you possessed was exchanged for an anxiety for action. But the mainspring did not stop. it uncoiled further, the tension building and building, your finger slipping off the hand—until it shot forward, clockwork roaring back to life, the clockhand tracing a year of rotations in a second of movement. You lie awake in your bed, the unsuffered consequences of the past year arriving all at once. How many nights did you forget to sleep, on how many meals did you gorge yourself? You are vomiting it up, the fruit of time. its initial sweetness forgotten under a surge of stinging bile. You will not open your eyes for the next two days. You will not leave this bed for a week. You will stare at all the lovely people, doing things you once had done, and know in your heart that you will never do them again.
