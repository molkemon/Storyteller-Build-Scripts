The ritual pyres burned high, flames licking the darkened sky with tongues of crimson, amber, and black. The ruler stood before the altar. Around the ruler's feet, a circle of blood glistened. Screams and cries echoed in the surrounding night; pleas of the doomed, swallowed by evermorefervorous chants, building to a crescendo. At first, silence. The wind stilled. The stars dimmed. The fires ceased their crackling. And then, a sound. A deep, guttural rasp, like claws dragging over stone. "You call to me, supplicant." The air twisted, though the smoke remained formless and twisted. "You come to beg for power, to claim dominion over mortal flesh and flame. But power is not given; it is bought with blood, with pain, with life." The ruler knelt, baring their claws to the great demon, voice steady despite the growing weight on their chest. “I seek your favour, great Xhazob. Speak your price, and it will be paid.” As the ruler finished, a jagged, pulsating sigil burned itself into the ruler’s chest. A torrent of demonic energy surged outward, siphoned from the ruler into the smoke, now shifting to form a gaping, grinning maw. "I demand strength, mortal. A river must flow from you to me. I will feed until I deem your meagre offerings enough. And know this. should you falter, your flesh will be my feast, your soul my toy for eternity." "Bring me the blood of a thousand thousand souls. Only then will my favour be yours." The Xhazob vanished, the fires roaring back to life. The ruler stood, breathing laboured, fur singed, but resolve unbroken, even as the air rang with the demon’s final question.
