There is a village on the outskirts where everyone seems to be genuinely happy. Too happy, in fact; unnaturally happy. And, most problematically, no witches have been reported from there in a while. Even the inquisitors stationed there seem to be generally chipper and jolly, and report no problems. Under interrogation, one of the inquisitors stated that not only were they not exactly sure why they were so happy, they never looked for witches because they felt as if they had no need to. Apparently, the villagers all harbor a deep trust in one another. As the inquisitor did not survive interrogation, the rest of the order has decided that further investigations must be made into this village, suspecting its unnatural state to be the work of enchantment magic.
