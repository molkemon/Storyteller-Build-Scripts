I entered the huge tent and looked around. So many of the Daughters of Lel were running about. I took in the sight of so many wounded, pained groans, and disciplined orders, leaving no doubt. I watched a paladin carry a crate of holy water in handed bottles out to all the others. The strangest part of it all, to my surprise, was that on our cots weren't just our mothers. Enemy soldiers were as common as friendly, receiving medical care at the gentle hands of Lelanàn's best. A paladin poured soothing holy water over a deep cut, and the foe's pain subsided as they were blessed. The actual act of healing would still take many weeks. Farther away, a man's arm had been lost to cannonfire. Their amputated limb was cauterized by divine blade, the pain softened by holy water given without desire. "It was easy to show love to a friend or lover. Much harder to show love to a foe. Yet if we were to spread love to the world, we would need to mend all wounds," Kyola would say, "that we know."
