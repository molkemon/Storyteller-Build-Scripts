Having squashed the peasant rebellion of Lanjinhui, we must now decide what to do with these traitors to the natural societal hierarchy, lest our own peasantry becomes poisoned with such dangerous ideas. Some among our advisors want us to execute all leaders of the rebellion and reinstate the lords, in hopes of a return to normalcy under our rule. Others, however, argue that the rebellion was caused by their lord’s incompetence, and as such the peasants are not to blame for their rebellion. If we initially integrate some of their reforms, they might be more willing to accept our rule, allowing us to restore order in a more controlled fashion. What does the monarch have to say about this?
