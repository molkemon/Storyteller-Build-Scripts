With the reconquest of Odheongu, ancient scriptures about a long forgotten god named Bhengudak have been unearthed from libraries all over the land. These documents speak of a god that used to watch over the previous iteration of Guwaamud for over 800 years, the god being the old former ruler of Guwaamud, ascended. This god has long been viewed as either dead or dormant.  With the recent departure of our previous ruler however, this is no longer the case. Shamans across the lands have reported that they can now commune with a new being, one which identifies itself as Bhengudakhnar, which means Bhengudak and child. It seems as if the two Shuvuush rulers that both have formed Guwaamud, both in the past and in the present, have now merged into a single living deity.  As shamans receive visions of their messages, a new mask has been carved to represent him. Both rulers were famous in life for subjugating those of other faiths and conquering with impunity; as such, those of us who would rather spread the faith by the terrorbird than the mouth are beginning to claim him as their guiding deity.
