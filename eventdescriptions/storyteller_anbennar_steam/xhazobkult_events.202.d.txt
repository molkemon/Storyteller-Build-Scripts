The monarch feels those covetous eyes upon them again. They are near, the monarch is certain. It began with the fall of the capital. Even before the message arrived, the monarch knew. The monarch felt the insistent pinprick of eyes watching. Someone, or something was scrying on them, using a method they did not know how to counter. This was frustrating, but the monarch was fairly certain that it relied on some sort of sympathy. With the capital in their grasp, the scryer had plenty of personal items, and people, to draw from. This by itself was worrying. The hunters were worse. They were gnolls, the monarch was certain. But they were silent for gnolls. None of the usual vocalizations. The snarling, the laughing, nothing of the sort. They were mute. And they knew magic. Not warmagic as the monarch knew it, but some sort of affinity with darkness. They leapt from shadow to shadow, hid in plain sight; wherever the sun was absent, they seemed to linger. That would all be well and good, if they were simply watching. But the monarch knew with a grim certainty that they were waiting. The monarch was used to assassins, but this feeling of being hunted was exhausting. The monarch did not know what these things wanted with them, and the monarch did not care. The capital would have to be retaken. The scryer's eye would have to be blinded. And soon. The monarch knew that the monarch was running out of time.
