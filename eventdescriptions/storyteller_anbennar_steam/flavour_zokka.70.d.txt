Once, there were gnolls that might be better off forgotten. First came Inykh, Sword of Sorrows, who cracked the south and cast the yolk across the bloodstained earth. His fire burned clean, for a time, until it consumed him whole. His horde staggered beneath its own weight, slaves forced to march towards a cause they could not claim. And when he fell, his heir chased false hopes and brought back the rot. The Second Xhaz fell. Then came Bazhku, Ripper of Wings. Her war was of vengeance, in the name of the First. For a time, she marched, seeking silver. Instead, she found black, and was swallowed whole. No legacy was left to catch her. The Third Xhaz fell. Last came Kuzzud, Herald of Pain. His voice stirred the downtrodden into a frenzy, but no Xhazobain is born of wrath alone. The Queendoms crushed his advance, and what remained marched north, where they were torn apart by another's greatest mistake. Survivors slink through those cursed marshes still, scars carved in sick penance. The Fourth Xhaz fell. We see these failures as warnings, but their ashes serve as scripture. Each opened the wrong door, so we know to slam them shut. Where shall their lessons be learned?
