With the marriage between the monarch and their consort, the people of the realm are experiencing an increased fondness for all things of the consort's origin. While some cultural exchange was to be expected, particularly considering the popularity of the consort's title, it is with alarm that the administrative advisor reports that a religious shift has been taking place in one of the provinces. It would seem that the influence of the consort's origin trickles down even into the subjects' way of worship, as the words of the foreign believers have converted a surprising amount of people. When confronted about it, the consort shrugs, arguing that they have long since converted to the realm's faith and the monarch should know that. "I had nothing to do with this," they plead.
