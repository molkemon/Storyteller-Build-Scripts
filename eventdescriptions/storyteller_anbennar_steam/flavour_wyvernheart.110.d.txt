Some would argue the mountain was chosen due to its connection to Castanor. Others would say that its height simply made the entire ritual closer to the skies, and thus the desired Dames tear. Both would be fools. As the ruler checks the runes encircling the mountain where every Castan gained his crown, they know the truth. It is not a message to a longgone empire; Castanor is dead and buried. No, it is simply a pragmatic choice, whereas the White Walls have a purpose; this mountain serves none. Now it will become a monument to the might of the Sovereign and a source of great power. The runes begin to glow as a thousand voices rise as one. With a flick of a hand, immense power begins to flow, contained only by a few scant chalk marks on the ground. The ruler feels nothing as the ritual begins. Everything has been overseen by the ruler. Everything is perfect, and there isn’t even a shred of doubt in the ruler’s mind. Even as the sky turns from night to day. Even as weaker floodborn buckle under the strain. Even as the meteor turns thousands of years of history to dust, the ruler feels nothing. It is said even those in the Dwarovar saw the fire that lit up the sky that night. While the assembled mages were shielded from the blast, those unfortunate to live near the impact but outside the wards had their retinas burnt at best or reduced to dust at worst. And in the face of it all, the ruler is silent. The ruler observes the crash site for how it might best be used while servants carry off wizards drained of every ounce of magic in them. Others pile up the bodies of those who couldn’t handle the strain. The ruler sends out a tendril of magic to check the site, to make sure everything is as planned. Only when that is confirmed do they let themselves feel just a hint of satisfaction.
