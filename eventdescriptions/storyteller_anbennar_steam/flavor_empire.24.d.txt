It's hard to explain the emotion that a dragonflight evokes. It's an old fear, one that hasn't been felt in Lencenor in over a thousand years, but it's still there, lurking in the back of every mind that knows the story of the Dragonwake. It's a dead dread, the sense that you're probably already done for, but you might have time to tell your loved ones you love them before you're all eaten, burnt, or sliced into ribbons. It's equally hard to explain what you feel when you see a wave of tiny dragons surging toward you, but you can think of it as a smaller version of the former. For the gate guards in the city, it was just as unexpected as a fullblown dragon flying over. Kobolds charged the city gate the morning when they opened for market, surging in as if they had Nimrith the Red himself snapping at their heels. In a reassuring twist, they aren't being violent, but they also aren't exactly cooperating. They've fled a wave of violence following the invasion of the Dragon Coast, and aren't going to go back without a fight, so now they're our problem.
