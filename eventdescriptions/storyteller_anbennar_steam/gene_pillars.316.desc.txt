We have plunged deep into the pillar, climbed many floors, and crossed alien habitats preserved as little pockets of life. It is little wonder that so few of the creatures here have found success in the fearsome jungle which surrounds and cradles this place; here they are perfectly provided for, the plantlife of their chambers no doubt engineered by the Precursors to meet their needs, engineered to grow to the edge of a defined space and spread no more. The magic and technology involved is unthinkable. Our minds drift to future expeditions which must inevitably come to this place, and how we can best prepare them. Rangers will be indispensable, that much is clear. The obvious answer is a zipline, established by magical or mechanical means, by which future expeditionaries may ascend to the level we currently occupy. It is a means we devised to leave this place, but if we can defend the zipline at both ends, we can ascend it in future. This means permanent encampments on both sides, of course, and for a few, an indefinite vocation here. More vexed is the question of how we can clear the zipline sites of hostile fauna and keep the ends of our zipline safe from future threats.
