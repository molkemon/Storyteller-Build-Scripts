The hunters flew north, beyond the steppes of Itorelgea, and into the sandblasted wastes of the Forbidden Plains. They kept the Serpentspine to their west, hugging the knees of the mountains to avoid the bulk of the violence that the Forbidden Plains were infamous for. After a long flight, the hunters saw the Eastern Reach rise above them, the unlikely spur of the Serpentspine, thrusting east into the endless plains like a claw. Mountaineers, hardened in the search for the Nuzenapu, drove steadily into these forbidding peaks, and found there, nestled in the deepest mountains, the fallen city of Magharma. It was in the highest towers of Magharma that the hunters found rest at last. Weary and weathered from their long journey, they paused to rest, for each had been driven by an urge, not wholly of their own, but shared between them, known and accepted between one hunter and the next. And so sleep found them, and they dreamt. "We are blind again. And the song is no closer. But the source has moved. It was north, now it is east. Like an echo thrown through an empty house, leaping off wall and floor, bringing them a song they did not have. The Serpentspine sung us to this place, but the song never belonged to her, though she brought the song to us. Now we hear. And the song can be understood if we listen. We have heard it so long, and we can feel it in our bones. This note sings of water. Not the deep water of drowning, but the fresh water of a still lake, still warm from the gaze of the sun, now cooling under the gentle eye of the moon. A lake lies to the east, a great and holy place, alone in these vast lands. There is the singer. We must go there. We must find her."
