The two miners stalked out of the shaft together, which was the only thing they agreed on. Durgat had bumped into Yezir at lunch or Yezir had bumped into Durgat, depending on who you asked, and Yezir had answered with an accidental spade to the back of the head, and now there was only one way to settle it. A hate grew between the two that boiled for the rest of that working day. They were on their way to the fighting rings, but the fighting rings were for small disputes. This was not a small dispute. As Durgat emerged onto the surface and was blinded by the setting sun, Yezir decked him on the back of the head. He fell to the ground like a sack of flour, but sprung up ready to visit that pain back tenfold until he stopped. On the cavern wall, caught in the last of the evening light, was the outline of a miner standing taller than any had ever seen before. He held his pick up to the sky defiant, as if he was making a promise the whole world could hear. He was dressed in the clothing of the mines, but around his neck was a fine necklace, of the type the chieftains wore. It would be a necklace of many colors, once the colors were there. Durgat pointed at it with a single claw. Yezir was wary of a trick, but he turned and looked, and he saw it too. He saw how perfect it could be, if only it was filled in the right way. People were already working on it. They both moved toward the parts that caught their eye the most. There was enough room for everyone, and each of them could make the colors swirl whichever way they pleased. All of a sudden, that dispute didn't seem so important anymore. What mattered was the colors. The paint looked so nice as it dried. Things felt better, then.
