The Ivetasananthor flew northwest, as suggested by the steward of the realm. Soon they reached the northern edge of the Havoral Divide, resting briefly at the old fortress of Calascandar. From this perch, they could see north across the Golden Plains, a vast verdant flatland, contained to the north by the great river Melethreabais. This was the domain's crown, one of the wealthiest regions in the world, yet for the hunters, it was desolate, for there was no hill or mountain in sight, as far as their eyes could see. In spite of this, they pressed on, and crossed first the Melethreabais, and then the Alen. Fields gave way to seemingly endless woodlands, and eventually, the land grew rugged, hilly, and finally mountainous. The vast Dragonheights rose to the west, ducking into the Moorhills, before rising again as the Coddoran heights. These hills were a curious land, full of diminutive reptiles and northern men of fierce aspect. They held no love for one another, but both had heard of harpies in ages past, and it was not long before the hunters found an old roost in the distant heights of the Moorhills, empty of harpies, but inhabited instead by the selfsame reptiles; Greenskinned kobolds, who were wary of strangers, but eager to hear news of the world beyond their mountain. The hunters traded tales at some length, and eventually learned a tale from their scaleclad hosts. An excerpt was sung thusly: "Harpies lived here, yes, but not for many years, for these stones are the scales of our Gods, and though our Gods sleep now, they woke long ago, and in their stirring brought doom to those who dwelt in or upon their flesh. We know not if your kin survived, but if they are winged as you are, I have hope. As for this land, and the lands west of here, I can say only this. No Harpy has been seen here nor there, from the Day of Waking, until today. I am sorry."
