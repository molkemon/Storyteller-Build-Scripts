Of the expeditionaries that set off, all but a handful have managed to return from the journey south. When questioned, the remaining elves are at first rather flustered. Answers remained elusive until one elf was caught making what was clearly some kind of innuendo to another elf. At the demands of the ruler, a proper explanation was finally given. The south, according to the returning elves, is home to rather rough and mountainous terrain. Not only this, but the land is also rather arid, with the sun's heat becoming particularly unbearable by midday. Unprepared for this, the expedition had begun to falter. Disaster, however, was averted thanks to the intervention of winged feminine creatures of, given the gratuitous detail, great beauty. While initially believed to be wayward fey, these "harpies" were apparently and curiously ignorant of either the Seelie or Unseelie. Upon being taken to one of their "roosts" atop a mountain to recover, the expeditionaries were surprised to find not only additional harpies but also nonharpies too. There they spotted dark skinned orks much unlike the green ones familiar to us, hardy humans tending flocks, and even strange elves from land known as Bulwar. As it would turn out, this collection of races were the "mates" of the harpies. The harpies would prove to be "gracious hosts" while the expedition recovered. So much so, that when the time came to return home, a number of expeditionaries elected otherwise for "personal reasons".
