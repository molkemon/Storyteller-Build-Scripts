The almost incomprehensible span of lizardfolk history has given rise to an equally ancient tradition of political thought and disputation, such that almost no thought has been produced by outsiders which has not at some time also been pondered by the ancient lizardfolk sages. New editions of these political classics would serve to impress upon outsiders the sheer breadth of history they steward. Publishing comparative works which map foreign political thought onto their own would serve two purposes. better intelligence on their potential adversaries, and a point thoroughly made to any who would dismiss their intellectual heritage.
