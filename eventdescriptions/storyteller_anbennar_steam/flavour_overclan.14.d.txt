Our recent acquisition of gnollish subjects has created an unexpected and pressing issue in conjunction with our general policies of religious tolerance. While such a policy is perfectly rational when dealing with belief systems such as the Cannorian pantheon and Dwarven ancestor veneration, the so-called Xhazobkult practiced by many gnolls challenges this policy in a completely new way. These gnolls worship and entreat boons from malign entities conventionally considered to be demons, which often demand exorbitant living sacrifices in order to grant their favor. Those who acquire such boons rarely cease applying them to death and destruction, rendering practitioners of the Xhazobkult particularly dangerous to social stability. While a policy of religious conversion would be the most straightforward approach to dealing with this issue, such a path would undoubtedly create new issues with other subjects who have thus far been pacified by our tolerant religious policies. Nevertheless, the Xhazobkult must be dealt with by one means or another.
