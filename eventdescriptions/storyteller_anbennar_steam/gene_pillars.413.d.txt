The interior of the pillar is a dangerous environment, one which shifts and roils as our expeditionaries traipse through its ancient structure. Over time, one learns to adapt to the constant change in stimuli, rolling with the punches and moving forward. Some among our expedition may even come to grasp the intricacies of the tower better than even the precursors themselves, confidently striding forward into these storied vaults of knowledge. These are the first who are thrown from the bridge when the earth moves beneath them. Undulating discs of grey stone jump and bounce erratically, tossing explorers left and right as even the most surefooted struggle to keep upright. Perhaps with the confidence of those who surged forward, these reliable veterans would have conquered this challenge with ease. Unfortunately, overconfidence was the first thing that was beaten out of them.
