Later generations of scholars would study the treaty of Sarnihanpur as a piece of political brinkmanship, one that played a pivotal role for the future of Rahen and beyond. The traditionally close ties between the royal houses of one realm and Khadisrapur were leveraged by the ambitious rulers of both realms into a binding treaty that divided all of Shamakhad up between the two realms. While at the time of the signing of the treaty, this was more boisterous claim than factual reality, it was a clear declaration of intent that would be realised over the coming decades. While at a first glance the treaty apportioned the lion's share of Shamakhadi territory to Khadisrapur, this was mostly land ravaged by decades of war and oppressive exploitation by the hobgoblin Command. The other realm on the other hand laid claim to the rich and comparatively defenseless states to the south, for the most part untouched by the calamity that befell their northern neighbors.
