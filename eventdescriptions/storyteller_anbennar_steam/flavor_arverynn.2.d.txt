The lord blinked in disbelief. Never did they think they would see the day the capital’s streets were ravaged by fighting. The Veykodan Guard had turned on them, and launched an assault which quickly overwhelmed the capital’s outer defenses. The lord jumped on their horse and rode into the streets to join what few loyal troops and citizen militias were still holding the line, but one pouncing tiger, sinking its sabrelike teeth into the chest of a city guard commander, was all the Veykodans needed to break said line and send the lord’s horse reeling. The aspiring emperor was slain by a copper blade through the visor moments later, their magnificent armour unceremoniously looted and their name cast to the tides of history: not high upon a resplendent crest, but down into the obscure depths like the Petty Lords that preceded them. In the span of an hour, the capitol keep itself was overrun and the courtiers inside were rounded up. The Veykodans victory was all but complete, and the capital’s fate rested on the word of their leader.
