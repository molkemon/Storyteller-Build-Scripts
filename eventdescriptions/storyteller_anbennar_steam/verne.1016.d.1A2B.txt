I got there, in the end, half dead and silent. It was a taller and broader dome than the Hall of the Exemplaries at the Vernissage; maybe the biggest indoor space I had seen in my whole life. At its centre, a plume of water, a column of refracted transparency rising right to the ceiling. The fountain was so perfect that it appeared almost still, a pillar of glass. Never saw anything like it before or since. It was clear that it fed the lake we saw up top, which was funny because we had been worried about whether the lake water would be potable. It was probably the purest water in the world at the time. I remember it now because I am being asked. At the time, I did not see any of it. I took it in without looking at it, the most beautiful and pointless thing. Why did I leave the academy? I do not know, because I left everything. I did not even think of taking a couple of trinkets on the side. I did not think of anything. I drifted for a while through the boulevard, doing abjuration work in exchange for sleeping in the loft of a barn, that kind of thing. I drank myself stupid. But at least the Vernissage got its treasures, right?
