A delegation of goblins? Yes, your majesty. No one in the court knew quite what to do at the description of goblins wearing fine clothing and speaking impeccable Common. Many wished to throw the creatures out, yet it was decided to give them a chance in case they had important tidings. While the mood was initially skeptical, the king softened somewhat as goblins gave him a surprisingly thoughtful gift, whose appreciation far exceeded its respectable yet unremarkable monetary value. They then complemented his palace and throne room, sparking a conversation that revealed a surprising amount of architectural knowledge. It was only later that they arrived at the true cause of the visit, which seemed to be a request for better treatment of goblin refugees. While this was scoffed at at first, the delegates appealed to the teachings of Begga and The Dame, of which they seemed to possess an unusual level of knowledge and reverence, ending with a reassurance that the court was underestimating the ability of goblins to become productive members of society. The delegation themselves certainly seemed a testament to that fact. In the end, the king bid a farewell to the delegation which was sweet but not very concrete. His advisors, however, thought him notably contemplative as the goblins left.
