The years go by, the poor rely on inconstant charity. The king's court, oh, so civilized, belies barbarity. But as the lords forget the poor, emergency repeats. When golems hold the people's tools, there's time to hit the streets. And martial men did join them then, as did one grayeyed girl. But a guest that day stole their breath away in a sickly greenish swirl. The tyrant planned a grand parade, he thought himself so large. But buildings, kings, and golems melt, as the purple beast takes charge. Both sides surveyed the rubble then, of honor, homes, and friends. The victims reassured themselves, "Titanium does not bend."
