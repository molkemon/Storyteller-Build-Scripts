The ork marches through the mighty wood, axe resting by his weary leg. He trudges through the icy snow to claim a tyrant eagle’s head. For many years, the coward bird has terrorized his foe. With squawks and squeaks from its insolent beak and trickery, dirty and low. The eagle rules the southern land, which it gained through lies and cheats. When told to fight, the eagle flees from righteous orkish reach. But the ork still trudges, ever on, his axe still by his side. For someday soon, with a mighty thwack, he’ll make sure that damn bird dies.
