As soon as an appointed factor leaves the capital, we expect them to begin lining their own pockets as much as they serve the interests of the local trade company. Rarely, however, are Company officials so egregious in their greed as this factor, the factor of that province. Through their private investments, they have cornered the local market in the region’s trade goods. Investors fearing for the reputation of the Company demand that the rogue factor be recalled to the capital to face punishment. More opportunistic elements suggest that we should instead buy out their enterprises, incorporating them into the Company's operations and rewarding the factor for their initiative.
