The principle upon which Naga counsel operates is that even you, honored ruler, must sometimes sweat. The chamber is of stone, for all such chambers are chosen from grand or ancient places, heavy with the weight of so many lizardfolk millennia. This is a place that would not exist but for the organization of mortals, a place which knows the inheritance we have all received from the gods. Do you feel it, ruler? Do you feel a parent’s fear for our orphaned plane? This is no petty gathering of villagers, where each peasant yells their grievance into the crowd. This is an ancient process of Discernment, and the Naga gathered here are not wet nurses. Do not mistake this for a class recitation. The Naga assembled here will neither punish you for sleeping through your lessons, nor pat your head in praise for your studiousness. You are a ruler, not a child. The Naga speak.
