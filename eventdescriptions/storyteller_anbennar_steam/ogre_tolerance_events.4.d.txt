Ogres are not the most gregarious folk, and many prefer the quiet life on the wild corners of our realm, the hills, crags, and swamps where nobody may bother them, which, unfortunately for us, also includes tax collectors. As villages seek to expand their farmland, however, they can come into conflict with these ogres, who consider these fringe areas their lands, with or without writs to support such claims, and with arable land becoming more scarce, these conflicts are growing in frequency. In the province, while draining a nearby swamp, the local peasants have run into a particularly reclusive ogre, who is apparently very fond of throwing projectiles, from overripe onions to live donkeys. Although this is quite anecdotal, we could take this as a chance to set an official policy regarding these cases.
