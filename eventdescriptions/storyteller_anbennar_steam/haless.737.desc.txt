East of the Heavenly Lake lies a vast land of silverwhite sheen stretching like a mirrored plain—this is the Bitter Sea, the largest salt flat in Yanshen. The parched lakebed stretches endlessly, coated yearround in salt frost. From afar, it gleams like snowcovered earth, and when the wind stirs, fine salt crystals dance in the air, dazzling under the sun. For centuries, this place has been famed for its salt production and remains one of the lifelines of Yanshen. Perched on the edge of the Bitter Sea is Sising—not honored by kings, but exalted by salt. Once home to the palace of the White Court Kings, its royal lords are long gone, but the palatial halls remain, bustling with merchant guilds and accounting houses. Where once stood a throne now sits the heart of the salt economy—contracts signed, ledgers tallied, and fortunes made. Each morning, lake winds sweep through its streets, carrying the dry tang of fine salt. Salt merchants stream in endlessly, and ox carts and riverboats keep the city pulsing day and night. Every trade here orbits the white gold. The salt produced is crystalclear, smooth, and imperishable. Noble houses across the realms take pride in using it, dispatching envoys to buy in bulk or to construct salt vaults for yearly supply. Some whisper that Salt City's salt can temper breath and preserve cold, making it prized in both alchemy and spiritual cultivation—adding a mystic aura to its renown. Through the passage of time and the steadiness of governance, Sising is no longer merely a city born of salt. It has become a crossroads of wealth, a waypoint for merchants and travelers, and a stage where institutions contend with markets. A thousand hands once lifted salt crystals from the sea of hardship—those same hands have raised this city, rising from wind, sand, and pale light.
