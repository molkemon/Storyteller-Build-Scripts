The rift opens, and I can see. My mother Hireet is so rarely known for her subtlety. Yet as I watch, I see her magic gently tip the head of the riverspear; it flies as if possessed from the hand of the fisherman Lessish, cleanly impaling the neck of the merchant Jiazi. Hireet would scold me for presuming that anyone, even she, could truly foresee the blood feud that will result from this, so many hundreds of years in the unfurling. The art of the hag is not based on clairvoyance, but persistence; not on ensuring certainty, but exploiting every possible opportunity. And yet, for a moment, as a frantic Lessish gazes into Jiazi’s dying eyes, desperate to staunch the richer man’s flowing blood, I fancy that both men can see it. They know what nightmares will come of this; they mourn the future together, all the lives that will be lost from this, in those final seconds.
