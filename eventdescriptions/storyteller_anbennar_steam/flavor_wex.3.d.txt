It is from a mountain's peak that one sees the clearest. Over the past four decades, Lothane síl Wex has brought his duchy to its very apex, with clever tongue, with a mighty blade, and with powerful magic. By seeing what ideals like duty and fealty demanded of him, and spitting in their face while he stole their purse, whatever the price, he has paid it, all to see Wex ascend. And from that vantage point, what does he see? The same truth he has known all his life. Wex must rule. Not for the sake of power alone, not for the sake of ambition or greed, but for the Grand Duchy's survival, Wex must rule. For Lothane síl Wex knows, and knows well, that if Wex does not rule, Wex shall not be. He has been taught this from birth, an Alenic surrounded by a sea of Anbennarians. Perhaps in the past they could claw out a space of their own, though Lothane knows well how Dameria was slowly chipping away at that. But the Wexonards only came to Anbennar because a rival tribe, the filthy Gawedi, expelled them from the Alen, and the same could happen here. It will happen here if those dogs in Istralore or Moonhaven rise, or if the traitors Lothane led against the Moon Throne turn face once more. Lothane can see well the rising tide of revenge and revanchism, that would sweep him from the Empire of Anbennar's throne and bury his realm underneath. That would take all his clever deeds and bold decisions and turn them into ash. But as long as he stands, he is Wex's mighty sword, clever dagger, stalwart shield. He knows that Wex must rule, and he shall make it so.
