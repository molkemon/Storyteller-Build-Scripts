The consort has been pacing the palace lately, their head hung low, only stopping sometimes to longingly gaze out the window. Life is quite different here, compared to their old country. Most of all, they miss their family and the safety of familiar custom. Their child was a good child to their parents, they decide, thinking back to their childhood. But how does one know whether one is a good wife or better yet, a good mother? Their child shakes their head, uncertain that they will ever experience motherhood. With the knowledge that the government already has their heir, and in their the line of succession secure, the consort sometimes wonders about their purpose at court. Their child did not marry out of love and everybody knows that they has not produced any royal children. Would they even be missed if they left?
