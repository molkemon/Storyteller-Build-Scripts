The consort has been pacing the palace lately, their head hung low, only stopping sometimes to longingly gaze out the window. Life is quite different here, compared to their old country of origin. Most of all they miss their family, and the safety of familiar custom. They were a good child to their parents, they decide, thinking back to their childhood. But how does one know whether one is a good spouse? Or better yet, a good parent? They shake their head, uncertain that they will ever experience parenthood. With the knowledge that the government already has an heir, and in them the line of succession secure, the consort sometimes wonders about their purpose at court. They did not marry out of love and everybody knows that they have not produced any royal children. Would they even be missed if they left?
