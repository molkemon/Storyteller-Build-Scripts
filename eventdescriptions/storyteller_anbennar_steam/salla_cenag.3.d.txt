As a sapling, I had spent countless days in Drennethille visiting distant cousins and kin. I still remember laughing and running, taking in the sweet air, charged with an energy I could never describe. Yet, despite those happy days and those blood ties, I hadn’t been here well over a decade. All because of my own stupidity. “Alara? Are you listening?” I looked at my dear cousin, my little sister, Narawen Drennen. Despite the blood that had been shed between our clans. Despite my screams and shouts at her at Elynaile. She still stood by me. By Salla Cenág. “So, you got access to the Crucible?” I asked uncertainly, trying to recall Narwen’s explanation. Narawen nodded, “Sort of. I got access to a Challenge. But, there’s not that much of a difference.” I tap my chin, “So just beat down some random zites or whatever else they throw at us, then poof.” I made a gesture with my hands. “Boons?” “Well, it might be a bit harder than that but ye.” I gave a laugh before enveloping Narawen. I didn’t need to hear more to know it would work. Narawen knew how to handle the fey. And, with their boons, we’d win for sure. “It’s brilliant, Wen.” “Ala…pleas…” Narawen wheezed. “Crushin…me.” I released my cousin. She stumbled back with an exasperated grin. “I missed those hugs,” she heaved, “at least I think I did. Now with that done, how about some lunch?” With a toothy smile, I gave a full nod. Together, we headed down the steps toward the dining hall. As we did, I took a full breath of air I missed for so long. This was how it was supposed to be. Family helping family.
