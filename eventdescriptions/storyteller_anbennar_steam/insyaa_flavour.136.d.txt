As we awoke, we were not always welcomed, but tended to be treated as curiosities at worst. As we grew in number and spread, what was once a curiosity became an imminent threat, and it was only through deft diplomacy that we were able to achieve a settlement with our neighbors. We have since become better known throughout the realm, but there is still something inhuman about us that causes prospective partners and allies to recoil at first glance. Fortunately, this is a problem that, just like any problem, can be solved through the wonders of artificery. The faces of humans tend to be round or oval-shaped, and have bony parts to protect the more vulnerable parts of their faces. With diligent study and design, we have managed to reproduce something very close to one of their faces, and by deftly relocating some processors we can swap out our old heads for newer, diplomatically friendly versions. Although we have proven we can thrive without the technology, it might be able to help others overcome their first impressions, and to help our diplomats better charm the nations surrounding us.
