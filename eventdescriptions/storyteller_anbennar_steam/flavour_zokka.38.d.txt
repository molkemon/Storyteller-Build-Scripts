The waters of the lake are dark now, slick with ichor. Lights flicker in the distance, across the water, pale and trembling like dying stars. One by one, they are extinguished, replaced by specks of emerald green. The table is set, on the shore. A triumph. A leader sits at one end. Another leader stands across from them, refusing the offered chair. They have already eaten; this feast is for the pack, as is the spectacle. Gnolls and favoured slaves alike brawl over slabs of blue meat and steamed vegetables, laughing and howling and tearing into the spoils like starving things. The second leader watches them with something like pride. The first leader watches the second. "What are we leaving behind?" the first leader asks. The second leader grins. "Bones and dust." The first leader gives a slow nod. "And when the bones are buried, and the dust blown away? What then?" "Then there will still be a crater to remember them by," the second leader replies. The second leader gestures towards the lake, and the skyline. "Still water stagnates. It could use some fire." The first leader toys with their goblet. "It could use some direction." "Oh? More wayward souls for you to shepherd?" "These lands had order. Centres of learning. Temples older than both of our bloodlines. We could govern them; build on them." The second leader snorts, voice coming sharp. "Carve around the rot, and the core festers. Burn it all and be done with it. Salt it, if you must." "And then what?" the second leader doesn't deign to answer. The table creaks under the weight of the banquet. The lake is still.
