Fire pits smoke delicious slivers of spiced meat, crates overflow with voluptuous vegetables large as an ox head, and citizens flitter between bustling stalls, awash in merriment. Yes indeed, the farmers market in the giant domain was set to be a seasonal hit until the ogres arrived with their herds. Tall as a halamentpole, the giantkin erected massive pens for their equally massive livestock. These titanic animals baa and bray as loud as thunder, causing unease among the smaller animals, and farmers fear for the market’s safety should the animals become too riled. The ogre herders, on the other hand, dismiss these fears as small hearts trembling about small problems. What should we do about the rowdy giant animals affecting the market mood?
