As construction of the Godswatch nears its end, so too does the list of possible things that the Mwalim and Darwiiz can argue about. After this one last debate, all the details will be finalized. All that is left is to choose whether access to the observatory should be restricted based on faith. The Darwiiz, ever the populists, have demanded that only those who follow the Rinta Seekers faith should be permitted within the bounds of the astronomical complex. It is, to them, ultimately a building of great religious importance, and allowing nonbelievers within would be a trivialization of the meaning it holds. The Mwalim, however, believe that the observatory first and foremost is a place of learning, and restricting access based on religion would prevent foreign scholars from sharing their knowledge within. An alternative option could be simply limiting foreigners to a single wing of the observatory, but as with every other compromise both the Mwalim and Darwiiz have voiced their disapproval of this.
