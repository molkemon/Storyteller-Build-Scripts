In the fall of the first Queendom, our flocks scattered to the four winds. This opening is sung in many songs. Most of our folk scattered along the flanks of the Serpentspine, east and west, and in the age of the Fourth Queendom, their whereabouts were, if not certain, at least mostly documented. More elusive were documents on those who chose differently. Some flocks chose to flee north, into the Forbidden Plains, and finally, some fled south, to the Salahad. For most of our history, conventional wisdom had held that this was madness. Survival in the Salahad was ever a skill hardwon by the gnolls and humans who claimed this endless waste. As such, the Ivetasananthor never put much effort into investigating this vast desert. Any clue would have been swallowed by the desert, and plainly, there were other flocks to track. One day perhaps, but always one day, and not today. This changed when Harpylen's wings brushed against the banks of the Sorrow, and the myriad people of Kheterata began to share their songs. They sang of a flock of harpies, who lived in the heart of Sarhal, near the banks of lake Nirakhet. These they named Nzeri, and they were held in high regard. And so, hunters were sent, first south to the hot mountains of the Sad Sur, and then west along the north coast of the Salahad. When the hunters reached Koroshesh, it was a long, but peaceful flight up the Mother's Sorrow, to Uaalapor, where that vast river has its origin. The folk of Uaalapor saw the hunters as a good omen, but found their ways odd. While they were familiar with winged folk, they found it strange that the daughters of Harpylen should not revere Nirakhet. After all, every harpy they knew of were most devoted to this, the mother of all things. Despite this initial confusion, the hunters soon learned of a great roost in the southern city of Zerkis, an arduous trek by foot, but easier by far as the harpy flies.
