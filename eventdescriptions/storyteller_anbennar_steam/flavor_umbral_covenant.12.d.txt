And what is this? The Vile One’s tone is skeptical, which to almost anyone else in the Covenant would cause immediate panic. Cheni merely grins. “We have fallen prey to the fallacy of proximity, kitame,” he says, in his silksoft voice. “Have we now.” If Cheni notices the sarcasm, he does not heed it. He is too eager to show his Vile One the precise measurements of the generous chamber, the bed recessed into one wall, the simplicity of the surfaces engraved with repeating, subtle geometric patterns. “It is a chamber designed for focus, kitame. The ceremony of ritual, hundreds of mages present, is all very well for our great works. But when we seek to bring another two hundred of your shadowmen into the world, must we meet so grandly? No, it undermines the thing. “Place a wall between us two,” Cheni gestures to indicate the wall, as he does when he teaches, “and we would not fail to communicate, no? We might even mindspeak more clearly, if we were both in a room without noise, without distraction.” “You want to keep them in here? The mages?” It is not a notion the Vile One dismisses out of hand; She is not afraid of incarceration. But Cheni laughs. “Not a prison, kitame. Merely a calm space, their space. Purpose and luxury, voices that are unharried by worry or stress. Stronger wood for the arcane lattices we are building.”
