It is not a simple task to keep the harimari lords satisfied. The SGC has been trying to butter up every official with any power along the Dhenbasana river, with mixed success. In general, the harimari do not seem to like that the SGC is more or less independent from the caste system and undercuts the divisions imposed on middle caste merchants. On the other hand, the fact that the SGC is casteless means that they can cede trade concessions in return for gifts without dishonoring themselves by submitting to the lower castes, which gives them a competitive advantage.
