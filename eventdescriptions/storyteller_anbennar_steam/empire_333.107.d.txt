The two hundred seventy-seventh Empire knew of the prophecy of the three hundred thirty-third Empire. Yet it seemed so distant, as most empires last at least a hundred years. They sought guidance from a powerful advisor, who advised them to repeatedly change themselves so each change would result in the birth of a new empire, in turn bringing them closer to the prophesied Final Empire. To achieve this, they collapsed and rebirthed themselves over and over again in a period of accelerationist madness. They got as far as the two hundred ninety-fifth Empire before the instability of repeated crashes meant they could not rebuild themselves again. A compelling tale, certainly, but one so riddled with logical holes that even a hatchling could tear it apart. What constitutes a new empire? Could an empire simply announce the end of their old empire and the next day the rebirth of a new to bring about the eventual final prophesied empire? Could not the three hundred thirty-second Empire in Zerat have said they were a new empire after the Ashhana Bloodcurse snapped their empire in two, as was done with multiple other empires throughout history? Is the entire concept of a new empire simply the will of the naga and attempting to use logic to parse their reasoning is futile?
