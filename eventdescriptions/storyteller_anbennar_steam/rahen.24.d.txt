The two ziggurats stood tall as a great deal of pilgrims started their journey from one of the sites to the other. While the structures were not built by the High Gods themselves, the ziggurats, the oldest structures built by mortal hands that yet stand along the banks of the Kharunyana, have become symbols of the wisdom of the gods and the path they have laid for us. Some also say that the twin pyramids are a depiction of the people of Rahen, with the Tiger Ziggurat symbolizing the Harimari while the Elephant represents the humans, their coexistence a testament to the strong bonds that connect the races of Rahen. Ever since both of these sites became parts of our territories, trade and pilgrimage in the area has been flourishing. Many merchants have been selling goods to help facilitate the pilgrim’s journey while both cities bustle with travelers from all over Haless. The fruit of our labors to create a safe passage between the two sites is ripe, and with the great pilgrimage on the horizon, it is ready to be harvested.
