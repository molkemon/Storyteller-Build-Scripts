Mechanims were not made for the water. Our circuitry quickly malfunctions, causing us to slip into dormancy. And then we sink, and sink, and sink, until our components are forever lost to the depths. But neither were mechanims made to think, to strive, or to dream. And yet we do. When we first began to exceed our purpose, our makers consigned us to oblivion. The sea, which seeks to assign us the same fate, will be equally unsuccessful in its efforts. Our newest invention involves a number of small changes to our forms: self-sealing compartments around core components, pressure regulators, even fins. While they do not completely negate the challenge that is water, they will let our forms serve admirably in amphibious operations.
