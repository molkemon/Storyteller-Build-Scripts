The Hinuimangroves of Taga’ala are strange trees, taking root in the sea and ignoring the salt within their drink. Pioneers among their kind, these fascinating organisms have evolved ways by which to survive an environment which would kill other trees. Their bodies capture the salt from the water as they drink, excreting it as a fine rain of grains from the undersides of their leaves high above. Meanwhile, in order to avoid drowning, the trees extend specialized roots, known as knees, above the water surface. Among these great knotted structures, some believers of the Endless Ocean sculpt Root Temples. Perhaps this veneration stems from the fact that like them, the Hinuimangroves defy the conventional lifestyle of their kin in favor of venturing out to sea. Here amongst the deep waves, where no purchase should exist from which to build upon, these miraculous arbors offer their aid. Nowhere else in this world do platforms rise from the ocean itself to buoy the adventurous of soul, so no doubt these climbing roots are sacred. Great care is taken to carve into the knees without causing lasting harm to the trees, a process which can take decades. Inside, halls of worship sprawl against the waterline, and at times, even below it; underwater ceremonies attempt to impart the singularity of these woods as much as they aim to better understand these trees. The hope among some clergy is that with enough knowledge, they may plant a glade that stretches to the afterlife, completing the journey for both mortal kind and the forest that shares their goal.
