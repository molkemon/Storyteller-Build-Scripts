Though perhaps one of the stranger pairings one can hear of, a friendship between goblins and elves is hardly unknown. Historically, the first recorded instance of this came when a group of Ibevari adventurers, appalled by the Thorn Company's vicious treatment of the newlyliberated goblin slaves offered their protection, with the refugees eventually being resettled in Thanas. In later years, Bayvek and Celmaldor would operate as a factional bloc in the Northern League's parliament, finding allies as minority groups marginalized by Gawedi hegemony.  Culturally, this manifested as a tradition known as Silver Shamanism, wherein goblins would adopt elven magics and language, as well as material traditions such as architecture and styles of craft. The practice was considered endearing enough to warrant the aid of the more progressive segments of elven society. Elves, either for pragmatic convenience or out of sincere admiration for their little imitators, offer consultancy and obscure techniques in industry and warfare in exchange for the numbers and technological innovation that goblins could provide in return, each party shoring up the other's relative weaknesses.
