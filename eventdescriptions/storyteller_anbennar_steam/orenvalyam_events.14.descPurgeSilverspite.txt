Ecaris Ecarizuir raised Dazinkion high, and wrought Dawn’s edge upon the Darkness. A colossal blade of light erupted from the Wishblade; the Kamrayakval’s surging troops cried out, shielded their eyes against the burst of Surael’s power, the sun that should not be. Then the blade pulled down and across, and cut across the enemy line. Dazinkion’s power simply annihilated most of those it touched, blasting them into ashen nothing. The rest caught alight. The heat burned them alive. There were no survivors. The Herald had slain thousands with a single blow. “ECARIS.” Came the cry from up and behind. Ecaris turned to see Karodir, standing from the deck of the departing evacuation ship, only now letting falter the great wards he had cast to protect them. “We are LEAVING, brother. Quickly now, join us before more come.” He looked into Karodir’s eyes, then. Saw the desperation, the loss, the great sick sorrow of their failure. He knew Karodir saw something similar in his own, perhaps he saw Castonath there too, and Bulwar, another burning shore growing distant, far away. One thing was different, though. For in both their eyes, Ecaris knew there was the rage—in Karodir’s, perhaps it was the anger at those who had rejected the ones who would be their saviours, who had tried to help them. As for Ecaris, there was no perhaps. He knew he raged at himself. Aelantier had chosen a different saviour. A different hero. One clad in wrought iron, shrouded in blackscale as if he wore the Dark as a cloak. And it was Ecaris’ fault, of course. He didn’t know how, why, to what extent. Didn’t know what he’d done to deserve this grim fate, but he still had time to find out. He would not shirk his duty. He had made a vow. “I’m sorry, my friend,” he said, as he sheathed his blade. “But I still have work to do.”
