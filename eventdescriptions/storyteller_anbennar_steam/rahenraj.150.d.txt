Once, the Ministry of Agriculture managed Rahens most vital industry: the cultivation of plants and livestock for consumption and sale. Its ministers were agronomists par excellence, and its libraries were among the best in the world for those seeking new and exciting methods to maintain the fertility of their fields. The Ministry had a monopoly on the sale and distribution of foodstuffs, and administered an immense number of granaries and warehouses. Over the past century, however, that function fell into disrepair. The irrigation systems that had kept the land fertile atrophied, with some being torn apart in times of war by invading armies, while those of lower status were oppressed by the very bureaucrats who had once sworn to protect them and uphold their interests.
No longer. Through deliberate and concerted action, the current lord has reformed the Ministry of Agriculture from its formerly decrepit state. The rents, incomes, fees and tariffs that had once been farmed out by disinterested and venal ministers have been returned to the control of the court, and our farmers labour placidly on the fields, safe in the knowledge that there is once more a benevolent eye overseeing their work. With this intricate system restored to its proper functioning, we may now move on to other, greater matters.
