As the sun sets, Dhenbasana leaves the ritual chambers. The usually jovial Great Spirit seems calm, for once, simply offering our lord a glass of wine, as the celebrations slowly begin to die down. An oddity, but the wide smile on Dhenbasana’s face betrayed a further change.
As the sun stopped setting and began to rise again, his powers at full display, a mixture of awe and fear struck through our lord. A hundred days of festivals, neverending, Dhenbasana muttered, walking away, and with each of his steps the state shook.
