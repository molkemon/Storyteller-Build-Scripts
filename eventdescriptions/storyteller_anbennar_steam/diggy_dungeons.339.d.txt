As we advance, things get darker. Only crystals shine now, no more shrooms glowing. We keep rowing as we initially did: with strength enough to traverse these still waters. But suddenly we start to move faster. It is at first barely noticeable, and we do not realize what is happening until it is too late. The expeditionaries soon stop rowing, as they realize the water itself pulls us along even faster than we were going before. The torch light illuminates a bunch of large rocks and a sudden cascade we are about to fall in.
