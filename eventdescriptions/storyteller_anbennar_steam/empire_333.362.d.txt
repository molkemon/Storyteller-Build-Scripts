Why are they so dissonant? Each one is different, with different eyes, different claws, different scales, different everything. They do not move as one; they do not breathe as one; they do not look as one. Their voices are not the same. They hear us, and we hear them. Get out! Get out! Get out! Send more spores, send more, send more. The Gasabi slumps to the ground while attendants rush to him. Shivering under a blanket, the blackscaled lizardfolk looks up at the ruler. “They think as one. It is one thought, screaming their heads endlessly. I think they attacked us because when we entered their world, our voice was different. I do not know why they chased us here though. It is like they cannot stand the idea that something that thinks would think differently, or look different, or do anything that is not the same, the same.” The Gasabi grabs at his own mouth in an attempt to slam it shut. He takes a few deep breaths, then lowers his hands again. “There is a central hub through which all thoughts seem to pass through. A spore hive, or something of that sort. If you can get back through that spire, I will be able to lead you to it. I do not know what you do then though. Use explosives?”
