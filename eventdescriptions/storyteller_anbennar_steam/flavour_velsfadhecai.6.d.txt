Mitrenya was the spare. The Purple Council had met, and talked, and talked again, and then voted. They were awed by Mitrenya's display of magical prowess, and of the techniques taught to her by the legendary Nathasadra Latekyr. They adored her manners and demeanor—"Fitting of a true Lady," one of them had proclaimed. I had to quash my pride in front of them; I was the one, after all, who spent hours pouring over my mother's books, learning all I could of what it meant to wear the crown before relaying it back to my dearest friend. Some of the nobles even had to step out and dry their eyes, her appearance enough to vividly remind them of their beloved late lord. But Mitrenya, after the wendigo, would never be with child. As perfect as she was, the succession simply wouldn't be safe, even if she chose to adopt. And the Purple Council existed to secure the succession. While we stood at the sidelines, they brought Ariadne forward. She was beautiful. Radiant. As charming as her father, and then some. She had little in the way of magic, but her glory more than made up for it. I spoke with her once, to congratulate her, and if it weren't for the circumstances I too would have been charmed by her honeyed words and delicate smile. "There's still a chance, Ren." I said as we walked home, our elbows linked. 'She might decide not to take it, or she might.' "It's fine, Varynna." Mitrenya responded, though her tone suggested it was anything but. "It's okay. I'll figure something out."
