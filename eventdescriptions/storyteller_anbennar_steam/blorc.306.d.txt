When they come before the ruler, they are already ready to depart. Clad in armor and furs, weapons at their waists, the dozen orks that have assembled clearly do not wish to remain in the hall for longer than necessary. Their words, when they speak them, are quick and honest. They grow old, they insist. For years they have served, and they could serve for years more, but that is not their desire. Before it is too late, before they are no longer able to move as freely as they do now, they wish to depart. To leave behind their sworn positions as warriors and to, instead, begin their search for Dookan’s prison, such that they might at last unchain him. They are not the first to make such a demand nor to speak of such a legendary journey, and they will surely not be the last. As always, an advisor whispers upon the ruler’s ear, and the ruler listens to the price that has been demanded. It is not a small price, but for the dozens of warriors assembled before him, veterans, one and all, to make them stay, it was a price that many would be willing to pay.
