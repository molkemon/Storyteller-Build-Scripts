Clink, clink, clink. The coins moved from scales to bag, and to table, then back to the bag. Some unseen force engaged in an endless dance of enumeration and remuneration, struggling only against itself. Grimmun brought forth an enchanted lantern, rendering the ghost visible, which may have been a mistake. Its eyes were hollow, hungering pits, coveting the wealth in front of it and darting greedily across the adventurers' enchanted possessions. Once they had adjusted to the leering thing, the delvers noticed the other object on the table. an accounting ledger. It was updating itself to mimic the spectral transactions going on in the room. Grimmun ventured to test its prowess, and laid out a simple calculation of compound interest, which it resolved expertly. Next, he thought to check the future of his iron mining concerns, and again, the ledger responded with believable precision. "Banish the ghost; we're taking this ledger back to the main hold."
