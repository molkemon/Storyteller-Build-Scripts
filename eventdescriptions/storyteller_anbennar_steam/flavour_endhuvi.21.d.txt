The river was choked with buildings, and the shore with people. The Hall of Endless Debate had never seen such a crowd, nor as many voices and views. The Cinnabar Lotus Society had anchored its barges right at the heart of the city, and the city was overwhelmed. The people were used to dealing with the philosophers from the Hall, but the fresh multitude of people that the Society had brought with was too much. It had all started as a proposal, put forward jointly by the Headmaster of the Society and the Grand Mediator of the Hall of Endless Debate. Ever since the Society had taken permanently to wandering upon the river, the prospect of bringing together two great warrens of higher thought had percolated through their respective institutions. Of course, it never occurred to either to warn the people of the city of the impending festivities; they instead merely sent out the invitations. The first Grand Debate encompassing both the Society and the philosophers of the Hall of Endless Debate was to take place. Now, here it was that day, and philosophers, scholars, and all manner of erudite and clever individuals flocked through the markets and along the river. Throngs of philosophers ambushed others, spouting off an idea or making a statement so brash and bold as to immediately grab the attention of the others. Debates and chatter filled the air like the Watermarkets of Sarisung itself. A great gong sounded from the Hall, and the voices continued. It sounded again, perhaps slightly more impatiently some ten minutes later. Some debates died down, and finally a third time, five minutes after that, bringing most to their senses and sending them all up towards the Hall. It was time.
