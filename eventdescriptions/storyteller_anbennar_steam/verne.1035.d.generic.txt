He is beautiful, you know, he who is trapped beneath this place. I felt your fear and revulsion before you approached the great gash in your plane, the demon place from which the hells flow. More fitting were the guilty feelings of awe, the honourable few who could admit, if only to themselves, the profound beauty of the scorched fulgurite maw. Perhaps one day you will see my friend and admire him as I do. But Almadhab cannot look out here, and I can, and so I am stuck with you ugly things. There is an emotion your kind of beings have, a feeling of the unfairness of my hate. You believe this is personal. If I could, perhaps I would gently touch your minds and explain. These beings are merely my fingertips. Fascinating, that you wish them gone so ardently. I feed on the moments of death, on the fear of me, the thoughts of me. Would you believe that there are some sweet souls who make peace with me, in their moments of death? Who see the void and can shake my hand? So many of you lie broken already. The wasted blood stains the sand; I am the wrong kind of demon to call it food. Did they die well? Will your dead woman be proud?
