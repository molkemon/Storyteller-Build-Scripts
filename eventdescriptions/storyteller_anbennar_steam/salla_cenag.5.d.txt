All I could taste was the metallic tang of blood. My ears rang. They were filled with cries, screams, and death. Pain. There was so much pain, but that didn’t matter. None of that mattered. I promised her. With my one good arm, I slowly dragged myself across the ground. That was it. Don’t think. Just keep moving. You promised her. My body rattled as I choked on a blood-choked sob. My vision spun. The pain began to fade, but I managed to drag myself once more. That’s it. Just. Keep. Moving. Remember. The promise. I called out to my body, to my one loyal limb, to just one finger. But nothing replied. I tried to take a breath but found no air. All I could feel was a distant wetness. Tears.
