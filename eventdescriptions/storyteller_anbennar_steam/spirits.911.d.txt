It was a day like any other. The ruler was attending court and listening to the plea of a merchant when a most terrible tragedy occurred. Black, purplish smoke suddenly enveloped the ruler, and when they emerged their eyes shone with an ominous purple light and black mist hung from them like tattered clothing. In a mad rage, the ruler began attacking the nearest courtiers before they could realize what was going on. A wise old advisor yelled out that the ruler had been possessed by a wrathful spirit. At that point, the ruler had already attacked and bloodied three courtiers, so those present had no choice but to subdue the ruler. In the struggle, the ruler sadly died. Their last words were curses and threats yelled in an ancient dialect. Later, our wisemen, philosophers and mages concluded that it must have been one of the ruler’s ancestors who, for unknown reasons, must have held a bitter grudge even in death. A grudge that just so happened to rear its wrathful and hateful head today of all days.
