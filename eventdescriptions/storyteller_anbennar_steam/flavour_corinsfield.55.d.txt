The savage is marched up to the gallows. His arms and chest are pink and bloody; the tattoos that give the rune warriors their power had to be removed no matter what. He says nothing; the pain keeps him only half awake. Really, he is more dead than alive. As soon as he is walked out, the crowd erupts into cheers and screams. Here is the enemy. This is why you must live in fear; this is why your children cannot sleep in the night. This man is the enemy, and if we are to be safe, he must die. The rope is tightened around his neck. The counter does not bother reading the hymns; what good would it do to someone already promised damnation? With a scoff, he turns to the crowd. Raising his fist, he yells about how they will be troubled by the heathen no longer. Then, he pulls the lever, and the floor falls.
