Hemantsen's face becomes a mask of confusion as he listens to the intentions of Rani Jira. After a moment to process the declaration, he finally responds, his voice colored by incredulity. "You wish to create entire cities of heretics? What madness has come over you, my wife?" Rani Jira takes his paw, guiding him to a nearby window. "Husband, you must understand," she pleads, eyes soft as she soothes Hemantsen's temper. "The Praxic are not just heretics; they are people. Do you truly wish for citizens of the realm to suffer persecution and injustice simply because their beliefs differ from yours?" She strokes his arm as they watch the court continue arguing over the schism below. "You have accepted my faith in good strides. Surely you can find it within you to accept theirs too?" Hemantsen's brow furrows, his gaze drawn towards the distant horizon. "You are correct that they do not deserve to suffer," he says while staring into the distance, "But the court is already at a breaking point. How will we be able to control them if we let the Praxic live freely within our borders, and flaunt the traditions many of the lords hold dear?" Hemantsen's expression grows darker as his mind fills with visions of the worst. But then Jira squeezes his paw, and the fear of the possible fades away, leaving only the horizon in his view. "The same way we handle all of our problems, Hema."
