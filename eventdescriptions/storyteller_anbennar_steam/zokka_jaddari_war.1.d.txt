Moments after my birth, I saw the First Sun. It hovered directly overhead, glaring down at me, shimmering in the heat and scorching my eyes. I was placed in the burning sand and groomed from nosetip to tailtip, but I never looked away. I gazed upon its radiance, and this was the first time I knew hunger. I grew. I ate. I travelled. I conquered. I remained ravenous. The First Sun rose, day after day, taunting me with its succulence. I could only imagine the perfect mix of sweetness and spice, dappling my tongue with its exotic flavours. But it remained stubbornly out of reach. I grew. I ate. I grew, because I ate. The more I eat, the more I shall grow; this is the logic. And I knew. I shall grow tall enough to reach the First Sun, and pluck it from the sky, and consume it bite after bite after bite. I shall quench my thirst with its fires. I shall end my hunger with its light. And, as I sought new feasts in these blazeseasoned sands, I learned of another sun. The scouts painted a vivid picture; one I committed to memory. He was tall, they said; his skin terracotta, his hair matching the desert he claimed. His pack revered him. His creed, some new perversion of the First Sun's dogma, was obeyed without question. He knew of me, too. He sought to kill me. To end my dominion over these antique lands. The thought of him gnaws at me. He walks these sands as if they bow to him, but the dunes remember their true master, and soon, so will he. My jaws ache. The taste of his strength, of his ruin. Oh, what a feast he shall be. Come, vassal of the First Sun. Face me, if you dare. Do your master's bidding. I salivate at the thought of our meeting.
