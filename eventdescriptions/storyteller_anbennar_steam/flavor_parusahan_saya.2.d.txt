The Hall of Endless Debate has been up in arms ever since the conquests of Parusahan Saya began to expand. Loyalists to the Raj cry foul, worrying as to the true extent of the rising nation's dreams of conquest. Defenders of the new power in Rahen claim that the land seized is better served by them, and the foolish masters who once controlled it are better off holding nothing at all. None of these voices are louder than the resounding call of Vallasenar of the Dancing Stalk, a young upstart who has made it his life's work to extoll the virtues of Parusahan Saya. But today the agrarian scholar is nowhere to be seen, and those within the Hall breathe a sigh of relief at the thought of avoiding one of his fanatical exultations. Instead, a harimari has taken to the central dais of the debate chamber, and as he stands the sound within the room seems to completely drain away, drawn into him through his grandiosity. "Hear me," he roars, and all eyes turn towards the declaration. "Many have sought to disparage the great state of Parusahan Saya. They claim our blood is weak, that we are not fit to rule such large swaths of land. But they are wrong." He jabs a claw towards himself, and a wicked smile flashes across his face. "I am Golpalar the Third, long lost heir of Acay the Younger, and a true successor to the Lotus Throne of the Second Harimraj." He gestures into the crowd, and a familiar face walks from the group of shocked academics; Vallasenar, draped in a robe of green and gold. "And my brother, Hemantsen the First, shares the same claim to this powerful dynasty. We are no simple upstarts vying for fleeting power; we are the future, come to claim our destiny." The scholars explode into heated debate, with Golpalar and Hemantsen being ushered out before the situation becomes truly untenable.
