Word has reached the monarch. a clique of palace eunuchs has attempted to assassinate their court rival, the advisor. Aiming to elevate one of their own to the office of advisor, they tried to slip a basket of poisonous snakes into the advisor's quarters as the advisor penned a letter to a regional bureaucrat. However, just as the assailant was opening the door, an armed messenger arrived to collect the letter. Seeing the basket of snakes, he sounded the alarm and seized the wouldbe assassins. Beseeching the monarch to spare them, the clique declared the advisor had been steadily spreading their nefarious influence in the administration, swaying the monarch’s policies toward intolerable tyranny. They were compelled to remove the advisor for the good of the realm. Their selfrighteous pleading was cut short only by the blade of the Palace Marshal, methodically decapitating them one by one. The monarch now faces the choice of what to do with the rest of the sorry lot. Proclaiming ignorance of the plot, and begging for mercy, the remaining eunuchs would surely be loyal if spared, and unable to amass the influence which had allowed the clique to even consider such a brazen scheme. However, the attempted assassination of a trusted advisor is high treason, and must be treated accordingly. Justice must be done, and be seen to be done.
