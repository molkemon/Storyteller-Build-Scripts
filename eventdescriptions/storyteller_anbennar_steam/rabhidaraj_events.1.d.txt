Every few years the Tudhinai hold a grand feast to celebrate the relationship between their leader and Prabhi, a tradition which goes back all the way to the conquests of Harimar. Some feared that we would end this long tradition now that we are the rightful rulers of the Tudhinai shepherds. They need not have worried. A feast which serves a virtuous purpose, like binding together a leader and their subjects, is not an unacceptable or sinful frivolity like many of the grand parties and feasts of the empire. Instead, we have chosen to celebrate and highlight our partnership with the leader by turning this year’s feast into the grandest that ever was. At that feast, our lord was publicly thanked by the leader for their loyal and useful service, which has restored imperial power to its highest point in many years.
