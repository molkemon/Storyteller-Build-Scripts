Every few years the Tudhinai hold a grand feast to celebrate the relationship between the Raja and Prabhi, a tradition which goes back all the way to the conquests of Harimar. Some feared that they would end this long tradition now that they are the rightful overlords of the Tudhinai shepherds. They need not have worried. A feast which serves a virtuous purpose, like binding together the Raja and his subjects, is not an unacceptable or sinful frivolity like many of the grand parties and feasts of the empire. Instead, they have chosen to celebrate and highlight their partnership with the Raja by turning this year's feast into the grandest that ever was. At that feast, the monarch was publicly thanked by the Raja for his loyal and useful service, which has restored imperial power to its highest point in many years.
