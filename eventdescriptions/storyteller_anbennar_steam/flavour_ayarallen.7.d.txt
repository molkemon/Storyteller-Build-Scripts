It is always amazing to me how proud humans were of controlling patches of dirt. When I called to my side the new Kumarkandi Akal, he didn’t ask about the population he would control, the prosperity of the lands he would receive, the swords at his command, or the tributes he would give to me. He only cared about the territory, the extent of dirt he would get. He didn’t even ask who I was, not truly; his eyes were focused on the map laid in front of him, and what he would soon acquire. I can understand that land is sometimes necessary. When I organized my first roost, finding a good mountain was very hard and involved a lot of conflict. But what I learned was that the location was the easy part; the difficulty was the subjects – controlling who built what, who received payment, and who guarded the camp. I learned that a good leader is one with loyal subjects, one who can, through them, determine what they will, but humans are strange. They care more about the names on a map, the size of those names, the prestige. So that was what I did – make them happy with the dirt. The Akal would have a lot of dirt to his name; apparently meant a lot particularly for the Kumarkandi to control the previous lands of Varamhar. This they would receive, and in exchange they would sign, promising on their gods to follow our rules and commands. The Akal walked away happy. I, as commanded by the Matriarch herself, would have the most crucial asset under control. people. Mates, soldiers, merchants, workers; all that actually made a kingdom; were under my supervision to send to the capital; the Akal could have the dirt, as far as I was concerned.
