The Khetarch sat on his throne, trying to calm himself. His pleasure fleet sailed on the waters of the Sorrow, and from the banks, packs of Brrtekuh descended on them. He had brushed off the worried reports of his Khet attendants at first. Then he saw one of them ambushed, pounced upon by one of those damned hyenas. He stared in disbelief as the gnoll's teeth sunk into his kin's neck, then shrieked in terror as he yanked back, taking the Khet's head with it. Terror was a new experience for the Khetarch, and he did not like it. Every night afflicted with terror, every day spent sweatdrenched, shivering in spite of the heat. He had to fight; there was no other choice. And so he came out to do just that. It was an apocalyptic scene; immense storms of sand and locust carried away invader and defender alike, as the river retreated and burst forth, tidelike against the gnollish flood. Screams, shrieks, cackling laughs, and despairing yowls. The full power of his divine race assembled, but halfhearted, and stricken with a terror unknown and alien. The Khetarch could see he was losing. And so he rose from his throne, stepped out onto the deck, and raised his arms, trying the last thing he thought might make a difference. The Sorrow rose. The Khetarch's barge remained steady, sure, but the others tipped, rumbled by the rising and roiling water, as human and khetcorpse and gnoll alike tumbled over, sucked into the slow-rising maelstrom that was once a river. The Khetarch allowed himself to smile, despite the nerves and the dreadfear. He was a god. Clearly, he was worrying over nothing. But then he noticed a sound from behind him, a soft footfall against a rug. The Khetarch turned. His life lasted only another second. The Last Khetarch did not scream, shriek, yowl, or purr in disappointment. He simply glimpsed the maw of the ruler as it closed in around his head, and for an instant, registered that this was the end.
