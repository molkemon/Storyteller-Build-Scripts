Your friend Fardante sends the following message. As part of my latest research on Bulwari religious practices, I have been intensely researching a particular cult. The Cult of Aphorisms. After leaving the House of Wisdom of Crathanó, I travelled to the Gelkar lands, where I first encountered adherents of the Cult of the Aphorists. To be considered a true member, the candidate does not necessarily need to memorize all the Bulwari Classics or all the aphorisms that have been assembled by the cult. They instead need to craft a novel aphorism out of religious scripture or rediscover a forgotten one. As such, most who follow the Cult are considered followers, not actual members. It takes years of studying the scriptural classics of the Dinu Sar and digging through thousands of ancillary theological works to find a piece of wisdom unknown to the current members. Still, the number of candidates is always high each year, as being considered an Aphorist is an honor among Gelkar society. At one of their gatherings, I even heard one aphorism that reminded me of you. “If a scribe knows only a single line but his handwriting is good, he is indeed a scribe.” Do you recall that when we started our studies, you could copy everything but only knew how to write your name.
