Upon review of the national military and its armaments, I have noted a critical deficiency. Gunpowder made available for military use, while plentiful, is of low quality compared to what is available elsewhere, especially in Cannor. As my concerns have not been taken seriously by the general staff, I have taken the liberty of procuring the infrastructure needed to produce and transport higher quality gunpowder for military use. Aside from these already made initial investments, this shall come at no greater expense to the state, and shall see the combat potential of our cannons increase markedly. It is advised that this superior gunpowder be used whenever possible, especially for the operation of artillery and elite units. Inferior gunpowder, if economical to keep, should be reserved for local forces and those not expected to engage serious adversaries.
