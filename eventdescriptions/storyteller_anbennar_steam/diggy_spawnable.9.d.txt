For most Ancestor Worship dwarves, settling grudges was an important duty. For the Company of the Grudgebearers, righting the wrongs of old was a way of life. Based in a specific domain, the company operated as justice for hire for centuries, reclaiming lost inheritances and burying long dead hatchets for a tidy sum, of course. With the end of a ruler and his followers, a new opportunity came, however. a chance to venture back into a region and avenge the wrongs perpetrated against a specific realm. Yet, with so many grudges, discord soon sprang within the group as to which insult to avenge first. The list was long, with nearly ten thousand years of history buried in the rock and blood of the mountains. From petty criminal disputes to the devastation caused by those who tried to conquer the mountains, each hold was haunted by the ghost of innumerable slights, and each new recruit brought with them hand-me-down tales of more. This cycle was broken with the arrival of a legendary warrior. This warrior, a legendary figure of a specific circle, left the normally rowdy halls in silent reverence. Lifting a tankard, he threw his own suggestion into the debates; the holds of a specific domain had a unique history. It had been devastated by orks and goblins, but it also held scars from the elves outside of the mountain, whose Burned Empress once turned dwarf against dwarf inside their own homes and sacked once resplendent halls beyond recognition in their lust for the wealth of the mountains. With a final slam of the tankard on his table, the band erupted in approval of the scheme and rushed to draw up their plans for the expedition. Following this warrior, the Company has arrived in the specific domain with a list of wrongs on their minds, ready to repay each drop of blood spilled in kind.
