There was no city here before. Traditionally, the heart of this settlement was on the other side of the river, sprawled naturally in all directions. A warren of streets bloomed out from the three central markets, a complex spiderweb which clogged at random and mired the entire city in inefficiency and waste. No longer. Razing the entire temple district, the Vile One’s able administrators have built here an orderly array of dormitories and supply depots, brooded over by a large squat complex for any of Her mages that might be passing through. Access to such dormitories is dependent only on service to the Vile One Herself, untrammelled by any juvenile concerns of sex, ethnicity or species. Some have objected; this has proven inconsequential. Here, in the tidy stone settlement which Her dominion had built, the Vile One feasts. Her gaze passes over an array of smiling faces, enjoying the fruit of Her rule, the benefits of Her wisdom and authority. This place is richer than before She arrived; as General Umorozi ever intones, only the stupid resist betterment. Food and drink and treasure from a dozen places travel here unaccosted, for who would attack a supply caravan of hers? She finds herself reflecting; on the relative instability and volatility of the horde of Yezel Mora, on the stability which She has brought, where Her Granny and Mother and Auntie prefer purely to stoke discord. The faces which look upon her in this most privileged of halls love Her, or at the very least respect Her. She notes it with surprise, and with pleasure.
