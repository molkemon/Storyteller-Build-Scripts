When great white clouds were first spotted off the coasts of Ishuden, few paid them more than a passing glance. The war against Bim Lau was going well, the city of Bieng Phang had fallen, and trade was good. When it was clear that what had previously looked like telltale signs of a mild seasonal squall was in truth the herald of the most vicious storm to ever ravage the coast of Rahen, it was too late. Lasting just over a week and striking across the Kharunyana delta with a brutality never before seen, the Great Ishuden Cyclone mercilessly swept anyone and anything it grasped out to sea. With hundreds of thousands going missing, and drowned bodies continuing to wash ashore from the shattered remains of the delta, we had no choice but to recall our forces and settle merely for Bieng Phang and a jungle swarming with recalcitrant heathens. The battered and broken silk and spice plantations now lay silent. The storm has finally passed, but the recovery will be a long and hard process. We must persevere as our fathers did under the ruler's yoke. For if we do not, we shall be swept away, not by a storm, but by the tides of history.
