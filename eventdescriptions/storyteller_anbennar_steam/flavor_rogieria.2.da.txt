Lothane and Ellís rose as Rogier entered. "Uncle. It is good to see you again." Lothane crossed the room and embraced him. "Tell us, what is the occasion?" "It is good to see you also, nephew." Rogier paused for a moment. "It is about the Sons. We are not just a company of exile lords and soldiers anymore. We have made a home here in Escann, a country." He looked away sheepishly. "They insisted on calling it 'Rogieria'." Lothane smirked. "I am hardly in a position to criticise. But why did you come all this way to tell us in person?" "I need an heir, Lothane. You are my sister’s son, and by my own pronouncement a rightful member of our house." Lothane looked away for a moment. "I am sorry, uncle, I cannot. My place is here, with the Corintar. I have a sworn duty to fulfil Corin’s legacy, to finish her dream." "What about you, Ellís?" Ellís shook his head. "My place is also here. I have no desire to be a ruler, let alone a king." Rogier sighed and turned around. "Rogier, don’t think I didn’t notice you. What do you say? It would be rather fitting, no?" Lothane’s second son sat in the corner of the room. His gaze was fixed on the opposite window as he pensively stroked his beard, but there was a burning light of ambition in his eyes. "I shall do it. I shall be your heir." As they left, one of Rogier’s attendants, an elf, turned to him. "You know, my lord, there is something about him… he reminds me of Vincen."
