Recent goings on in our liege's court have grown beyond a court squabble into a debate that may shake the very foundations of our worship. It all started after a feast a few nights ago. Many missionaries have made their way to the Ynn, thinking it a fertile ground to reap converts. Yet we were the ones to laugh as doctrine about worshiping dead gods managed to splinter into three different sects, each fighting the other so viciously that many escaped to the Ynn just to get away from it all. As the most chauvinistic lords laugh and snicker amongst themselves at the clear spiritual inferiority of those folks, others can be seen shimmying away. "A healer saved my son." a lady speaks. "A businessman tripled my revenue." another lord said. "Indeed" a wellmannered diplomat comments "while they could do much to improve their style and grace, perhaps we have some things to learn by looking at our world and society through their eyes." "Do not let the gifts of those folks bewitch you." a rich merchant replies to the diplomat. "If there is one thing I have learned about them, it is that they only want to take. And not just in the 'You help me, I help you' manner. You won't believe the ease with which they disappear and renege on their deals the moment a cloud in the sky seems to darken. Your skin would chill at the stories of their depravity the guards will tell you. These people are one step above barbarians, but barely so. their culture should not be imitated." This is a personal question, and though we are not the strongest among the Ynn, our ruler has right to make up their own mind on the matter. It is clear, the ruler thinks, surely.
