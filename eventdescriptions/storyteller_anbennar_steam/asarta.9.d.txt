The light behind the Herald’s mask glowed brighter than ever before, before fading into blackness. The light around the thicket dimmed and the ground itself began to shake. The monarch fell to their knees, clutching their wrist in horror. In front of their eyes, their claw began to mutate, sharp daggers of pain shooting across their entire body. Scales hardened then started to peel off. Feathers sprouted across the arms before withering. A giant sicklelike claw grew out of their palm. Another eye sprouted on their face; then a second, then another pair on either side. The pain was so blinding the monarch collapsed on the ground, eyes shut tight; however many of them they had now; and just screamed. Gradually, suddenly, after an eon and no time at all, it just stopped. All pain ceased. Shaking and standing up, the monarch felt different. Their legs were longer, arms shorter but more muscular. The coloration on their scales had shifted entirely. They looked up at the Herald, whose masked face gave no emotion, but the voice it spoke with gave some hints. Not joy, but perhaps pride? “Go, my changeling. You and all your kind. You are now beings of pure change. Go and become the agents of change for all of Halann, and perhaps even beyond.”
