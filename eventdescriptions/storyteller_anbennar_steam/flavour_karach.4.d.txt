Now I have heard tell of Ikhaty Sunlover, and I will tell you what I heard. Some say the tale has grown too much in the telling, but I say it has not grown enough. Ikhaty was born a miner, born with a hammer in his hand. They say he swung it for the Axta, that he drove the chieftains from the land. The chieftains came with their soldiers. The Axta answered the call. The miners, the traders, and the bards stood behind him; Ikhaty stood tallest of all. And Ikhaty said to the chieftains, "Well, a man is nothing but a man. And before I let you beat us down, I will die with a hammer in my hand, I will, I will die with a hammer in my hand." They say his shadow went into battle before Ikhaty went in too. They say the chieftains heard that hammer singing, and none of them knew what to do. They say Ikhaty knew all in the column, every miner, farmer, and bard, and when he called out to them in the morning, the war did not seem quite so hard. Now the chieftains are gone from the land, friends, and Ikhaty still swings his hammer true. They ask me who ought to lead us. I say, who has done more than he has for you?
