Zarkan noted the stench of sulfur hanging in the air when traversing this camp. Such a smell is indicative of those vile creatures summoned by these green geoglyphs. He noted the odour. There was no response. Only Zarkan's bored, indifferent, and intensely frustrated gaze remained as a mark of his continued endeavor in the realm of being alive. Watching this camp whose outskirts they had just arrived at. Dilapidated? Maybe. No soul in sight. Maybe they had left in order to reorganize at a more secret spot? But then again, sharpen his ears, young Zarkan. A moan, nay, two moans of agony, he was certain of it. Zarkan wondered if his squadron would question the sudden disappearance of his scout compatriot. That question swirled a lot in his mind lately. But no, the cries of anguish were there, in the distance, in that tent. "Good work, mate. Maybe it is Aziyr. Let's go." After galloping for a minute and a half and entering the rather mediocre looking tent, they were beholden to a most disgraceful sight. "Watch as his men have abandoned this greatest of shamans in his time of need, Zarkan. Struck by a plague that no centaur wishes to be struck with. Engrave this sight." "Mate, I am not engraving this sight of someone having the worst, most sick stomach churnings of his life into my eyes. But it is safe to say that we have got him. As for the camp..."
