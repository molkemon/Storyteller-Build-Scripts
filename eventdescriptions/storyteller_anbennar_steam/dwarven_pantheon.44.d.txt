The second son of Durvorazir, Verdrik, was not like his more scholarly brother. He was focused on the practical and physical aspects of life. He turned stone into blocks and transformed blocks into buildings. For him, art was the cold practicality of the stone he laid, expressed in arches and fortress walls. He specialized in engineering, constructing bridges that arched over chasms and roadways that were laid through caverns. It was said that he was the personal patron of Hehogrim the Diligent and inspired him to create Hehodovar. Nevertheless, many builders of ancient Hehodovar would dedicate their works to his name, even though his priesthood elsewhere would be less than pleased by some of the liberties taken.
