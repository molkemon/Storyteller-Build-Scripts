The halls of the palace are filled with hectic whispers and nervous murmurs, rumors spreading with wildfire. Apparently the arkimaguš Ahmahan has been viciously attacked by assailants of unknown origin. While most agree that he managed to fend off his attackers until the palace guard could intervene, details such as the number and nature of the assassins, their motives, the place of the attack, and whether the arkimaguš remained unscathed or is wounded vary with each frantic retelling. With the entire palace in an uproar, it was inevitable that news of the assassination attempt spread beyond its gates. In a surprising turn of events, the ZarLereškal appear to have gained the favour of the common populace despite all the dire warnings issued by their detractors, and already a mob has formed, eager to rush the temple and punish the priesthood whom they blame for this heinous act of cowardice. If we are to stop a massacre on sacred grounds, we need to act swiftly and decisively and either calm the passions of the mob or disperse them by force. That is, of course, if we want to stand in the way of their ire.
