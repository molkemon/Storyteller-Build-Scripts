As long as humanoid Mechanim have been around, many of us have been asking the question of just how many arms are too many for general and martial usage. For the longest time two seems to have been the final answer to that, with more appendages usually causing coordination and balance issues, especially for tasks requiring precision or martial skill. Combat trials of potential solutions usually devolve to combatants spinning recklessly, which is surprisingly effective in single combat and rather amusing to the audience, but does not lend itself to proper formations. This has only grown more true as rifles and gunlines grow more and more prominent. However, recent developments from our artificers may just break these limits, by effectively linking two sets of arms together. By splitting a common arm down the middle and using clever placements of what they dubbed "Kauga Relays," devices that are able to convince our brain into treating both arms as one, Mechanim will be able to effectively use four hands at once. While brilliant, this leaves the secondary pair with limited dexterity, as the added arms for the most part only mimic the movements of the other. They thus serve better when equipped with close quarters weapons instead of more complex rifles.
