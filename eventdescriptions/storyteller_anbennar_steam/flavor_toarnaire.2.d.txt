If Anbenncóst is the World’s Desire, Toarnaire is the envy of Lencenor. It is a prideful boast, a statement that even the likes of Wineport and Lorentainé pale in comparison to Toarnaire. But to those that live within its walls, it is also a warning, a reminder to always watch one’s back, for envy drives the city. You can rise one day to the highest office in the city only to wake the next and see that someone has taken your place. If you are not careful, any gain can be lost, any fame can be torn to shreds, and the higher you rise, the harder you will fall. Everyone knows it. But the ruler seems to have forgotten this. He left work in the evening, intent on celebrating his recent success in the Red Flower Street, but never came home. He was found the next day, impaled in the middle of the causeway, his blood watering the cobbles. What happened is an open secret; the ruler had outlived his usefulness; and now the five families have taken his place. The event did not create much waves, for in a way, it was expected.
