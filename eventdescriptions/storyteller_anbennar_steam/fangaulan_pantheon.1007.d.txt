Gather closely around the forges, and listen to my tales, if you can bear the heat. I will sing as loudly as you please, but we are sure to be drowned out by the roars of the great forges of the monastery regardless, where the Ironwood of Fangaula is shaped into the famed Dendugma Steel. But of all the forges of Dendugma at which the forge priests gloriously labor, the greatest of all blazes deep within the bowels of the monastery. Even the forge priests do not go there, for it is the forge of Mojobaa himself. As we are enveloped by the hot air of the forges so far below, I will sing to you a song of the source of their heat. We are closer here to Mojobaa, who is forger and forge both, than in any other place in Fangaula, but do not forget that wherever we are, we live among his creations and must give thanks to him for our creation ourselves. What song shall I sing, here among the forge priests? The tale of the great forge of the world, or the tale of how men and women came to be?
