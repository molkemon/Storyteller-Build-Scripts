One of Dookan’s robed priests comes before the monarch with hurried steps, escorted by four others. In his embrace, a closed chest is carefully carried. As the priest throws it open, the monarch sees a few small objects. At a first glance, they appear to be the shattered links of a blue, thick chain. But, upon a closer look, the monarch quickly recognizes that they are mere fragments of a familiar glowing, blue rock. Dames tear. The priest proudly claims that what he holds are fragments of Dookan’s prison, flung across the world due to his struggles to break free, recently found by his faithful. Insistently and urgently, the priest and his fellows urge the monarch to devour these fragments and thus ensure they are destroyed permanently. They say that only in such a manner, by being devoured by a powerful monarch blessed by Dookan himself, can they make sure that the fragments shall not return to Dookan’s side and strengthen the chains that bind him once more. And yet, as the ruler stares down at the glowing rocks, the monarch cannot help but to hesitate. Should the ruler truly devour these strange, blue fragments? But then again, if it is true, surely Dookan would reward such a show of utter devotion, wouldn’t he?
