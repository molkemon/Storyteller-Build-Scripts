The move to the new city of Siadúnasar has already nearly been completed. Harpies from Siadúnaiún have been moving to the city en masse, and governmental and bureaucratic institutions have nearly all moved to the new city. However, an unexpected problem has arisen. homesickness. Not from those harpies moving to Siadúnasar, who have generally found the place to their liking, but from the monarch of the ruling dynasty herself. Having been hatched there, and having spent the earliest years of her childhood in the roost that overlooks the Salahad, it is expected that she has fond memories of her old home, but nobody, including the monarch herself, expected the intensity of her feelings. Though she herself has simply stated that she can get over it, there has been recently a proposal that Siadanlén should establish Siadúnasar as the Winter Capital of the realm, so that the monarch may spend the summer months among the ancestral home of the Siadúnan people. While this would also have the added benefit of maintaining the old centre of gravity of the realm, we may find that the areas around Siadúnasar might be more costly to govern with the monarch absent for some of the year, and some key governmental offices still in the old capital.
