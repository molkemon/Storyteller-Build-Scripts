The High Kavalal watched in stunned silence as the colossal limbs of a dismembered dragon were dragged from the stone tower’s entrance. The process of scouring the now scorched and derelict location had taken weeks, in many ways more costly than the initial assault. Each new disturbing experiment, devious design, or dangerous relic they uncovered was but more proof of the crimes of the Slaver Nobles – and of the follies of repeating them. The priest turned to his colleague, who had been in charge of the effort. “And you are sure this is the last of their collection?” The other High Kavalal nodded, as a flick of her wrist carved arcane words onto a nearby scroll. “Here? Yes. This was the most well guarded of the Uyasmarai’s secrets… in this facility, at least. Perhaps more lie in others. But the spine of the old paradigm is broken.” The location – the Blue House – had been the greatest symbol of how the immoral magics of the Slaver Nobles had persisted after the Ruin, obsessed with unravelling the secrets of the relics they left behind. But the Oren Nayiru knew their nature the whole time. the relics of the Precursors were above all else the products of slavery, by arcane means or otherwise, of the living or the dead. The High Kavalal looked to the faux dragon before them – grimaced as the necromantic energies that once powered it reactivated, and attempted to bind the bonewhite frame back together. An exorcist ensured that didn’t happen. Things would be different, now. Better. A new age of magic was dawning – one of respect, both for the victims of crimes past, and those who may be claimed by them in future. Maybe relics would need to be used out of necessity… for the people, for the faith. But they were not warlords, or Exemplars. the road the Slaver Nobles walked was not one the Oren Nayiru would follow. The past would stay where it was, buried below the earth, entombed in the ashes of cycles past.
