To count every empire in history is a long and arduous task. It required hundreds of scholars across hundreds of years to painstakingly reach a consensus that we were on the three hundred thirty-second empire, and that the final empire would be the next one. When you scour through historical documents, sometimes what you thought was a brand new empire was in fact just the same as an empire previously counted, but told from a different perspective. For example, the records detailing what was believed to be the two hundred twenty-sixth empire speak of a great spire on Fahvanosy, very likely a Zatsarya. We now know this spire was buried in a volcanic eruption during the time of the two hundred twenty-third empire, so the document is very likely just another descriptor of the two hundred twenty-sixth. This is a normal part of historical research, and should have been uncontroversial. Except we cannot revise any of the historical empires anymore. If we did that, we would not be the three hundred thirty-third. Our entire foundation would be a lie. So even if every piece of evidence points to our miscounting, we can never, ever accept it.
