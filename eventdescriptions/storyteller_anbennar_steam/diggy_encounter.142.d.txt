A common remark that the ruler heard when learning how to fight in the Serpentspine was, "Never fight a dwarf in their hold, nor their roads. No matter what you know about the terrain, they know it ten times better." Even far away from their holds and roads, the dwarves our expedition encountered were in their natural element. Like unwavering stone, they held against our attack and then unleashed an avalanche of rocks upon us. They quickly took from us what they could carry and went their merry way, singing songs in their guttural language.
