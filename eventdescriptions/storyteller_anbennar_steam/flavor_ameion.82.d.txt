Tens of thousands of Eilastes, nobles, merchants, and officers had gathered at Tacenie for the ascension. It was not some superstitious, divine ascension, as the Taychendi had once believed, but a real, political, philosophical ascent to a higher power. All one had to do was look around to see the fruits that had already been achieved. Tacenie's towers repaired and revitalized, the ancient city renewed once more. So too had the rest of Andeios, the rest of South Aelantier been revitalized, brought under the light of the only true civilization in the world. But this was not simply ascension; this was reclamation. And there was one height of the past that had not yet been reached. They still couldn't, of course; not for any significant time; but the wonders of magic enabled more miracles by the day. The Kylakas stepped out onto the tower, looked out at the vast expanse of the steppe before the ruler; then the ruler gave the signal. There was a blinding flash of light, as the sound and power of a spell nearly beyond comprehension erupted. Then, the flash cleared; and the boundless sky revealed itself. Tacenie rose once more to its rightful place among the skies. For the first time in over a millennium, its might, its majesty, watched sentinellike over a land at peace. Tacenie began to fall; those atop it felt their stomachs turn alongside the hulk of rock and stone and silversteel; but arcane power slowed its descent to a featherfall pace. Many years since Aesadas once fell, many years since the world fell to Ruin, many years since the malevolent grip of chaos clawed down the rule of order; but the dark times were over. Through righteous might, all that had been lost had been reclaimed. There is no longer any doubt.
