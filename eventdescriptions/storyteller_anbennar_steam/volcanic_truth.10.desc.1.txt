We have convened a great synod, and its task is now to determine what shall be done about the matter of the Cuahu Teotlehu. We have assembled beneath one of the Twin Gods, the guides of our faith. The magmascryers have brought us to the caldera of Muatlaqua, the guide of change, to show us the way forward. The dance of the magmascryers begins. They draw forth from burning rock visions of a free Volcanic Truth, one which heeds no far away masters who stand between us and our gods. A new era dawns. rock shifts into shapes beyond our imagination. The motions of the magmascryers are flowing and free. Their eyes burn a brilliant amber. But as their fire burns across our eyes, it soon begins to multiply, and change into terrible forms. We see visions of a Volcanic Truth rife with division. Everyone will think the Twin Gods' will means something different. What will happen if we divorce ourselves from the wisdom to chart a path forward for the faith? What will happen if we divorce ourselves from the force of vision we need to bring forth change? Within the caldera, the fire of the magmascryers begins to fade. They stand within the lava, their dance complete. Which of their visions was true? The path forward is unclear, and the council must decide what is to be done. How shall we vote?
