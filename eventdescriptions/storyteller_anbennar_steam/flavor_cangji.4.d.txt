Since its founding as the Lotus Seat of the Harimraj, Cangji has stood as a bastion of High Philosophy teachings among the frozen peaks of Gozengun. Little have we considered the value of the knowledge that could be learned from the Rukunai; how could these humans, who have little magical prowess, offer us any knowledge that we have not already gleaned? This dismissal led to tensions rising between our two faiths, nearly reaching a schism as we fought with one another. Now, as we descend from the mountains of Cangyuan and spread throughout Gozengun, it is clear that the Rukunai have amassed a significant amount of knowledge pertaining to the spirits that inhabit these lands. Any spiritual presence that tries to approach Cangyuan is warded off by the Undying Flame, and so dealing with the local spirits is a foreign concept to our priests and administrators. Perhaps it is worth trying to bridge the gap between our faiths and mend the centuries of conflict that permeates the two clergies. Maybe then we can finally understand how the Rukunai have lived out in the wilds.
