"What stalwart children of Akasik I have before me," the genie says, his sarcasm heavy. "The transaction here is simple. The wise obtain something they covet, and the foolish lose something they love." The air and life seem to drain from the room, and the genie vanishes. For a moment, the group wonders if anything happened at all. Then your companion yelps, looking at your face in horror. You glance at them, and shriek yourself; they are utterly moustacheless. Not even an effortless three-day shadow adorns their cheek and jaw – they are as bare as an elf. It takes some time for your gaggle of now babyfaced Vernmen to recover yourselves. Your gnoll guide is pacing in the corner of the room, flexing and unflexing their paws. You notice they are no longer wearing their bearskin cloak. The priest is merely thunderously mute – as opposed to his general, more sagely silence up to now. "There is nothing for us here anymore," you say, finally; at least you may console yourself with those treasures you have collected up to now.
