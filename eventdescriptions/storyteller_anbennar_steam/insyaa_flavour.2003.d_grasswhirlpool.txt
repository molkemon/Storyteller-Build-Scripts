Exploring the Hinuigrasslands is difficult. The grass weaves and flows, and deadly predators lurk between the everstrong, deadlysharp, blades. It is often easier to prepare crafts out of wood that can navigate atop the grass tips, and sail them forth. Though one still has to be mindful of predators, like hinuibirds descending from the clear blue sky, it is certainly preferable. Unless, and until, we encountered what we did today. At first, we thought there was favorable wind or grass weaving, because our craft had picked up speed. But as it continued to accelerate, we realized we were in trouble. We heaved oars and bent sails to cut out of the rapids we had stumbled into, and with the strength of our heaving we made it free. As we recovered, we sent a scout to explore the surroundings. What we thought were dangerous rapids were just the meager outskirts of a Maelstrom, a giant grass whirlpool, churning and ferocious. A massive maw that could swallow ten thousand of our craft without fail. A reminder that the Hinuilands always have a new danger to present you. And yet, perhaps they are crazy, perhaps they are thrill-seeking, perhaps they see further than most. The scout wants to try their hand at sailing it once more, now that they are a little more prepared. They swear they will be able to escape before the Grasswhirlpool pulls them in, if they fail. And perhaps there is something they could learn.
