Hemantsen looks out into the garden from his chambers, where even now angry shouts are exchanged between the two faiths. Furrowing his great brow, he turns to Rani Jira with a look of confusion. "Jira, is it true?" he asks, voice wavering with uncertainty. "Have you converted to this new faith which seems poised to tear the realm apart?" Jira takes his paw in hers, squeezing it in the familiar way she always does. "Hema, it's true, yes." She looks away, tears welling in her eyes. "But you must understand I never meant for tensions to reach such a fever pitch. I only wished to follow the path which reflected myself so strongly." Hemantsen brings both paws up to clasp Jira's, his face softening as he hears her confession. "You are my wife, and I will never fault you for choosing to be who you truly are." His expression darkens, his paws clenching hers harder by the moment. "But still, we cannot split the state by adopting this new faith. The High Philosophy must remain in its position of power, or the entire country will take up arms." Jira looks towards Hemantsen, and sees an emotion cross his face that has not appeared since the night of their marriage: fear. "What are we going to do, Hema?" Hemantsen looks back to his wife's worried face, and all uncertainty fades from his mind. "I don't know if we will choose the best path," he says as he brings his paw up to cup her face. "But no matter what, we will do it together." Later that day, Hemantsen announced Jira's conversion to Suhan's Praxis, and defended the right of both her and his followers to autonomy of faith. The court hears this, and a fire is lit in the hearts of the zealots.
