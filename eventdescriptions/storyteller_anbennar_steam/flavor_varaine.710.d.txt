After months at sea, the Expeditionary Corps representatives spend their first day ashore relaxing within a seaside palace overlooking a glistening shoal. Their host, a local Baashidi noble, attends to the envoys personally, chatting at length about their trek, concerns, desires, local squabbles, global politics, and the food—formalities worthy of a gracious emcee. The next day, the noble, the wrinkles around his smiling eyes dissolving into luxuriously dark skin, welcomes the reinvigorated Expeditionary Corps expedition to a wide, shallow court pool with a smile. Lounging in the royal blue waters, a cadre of merfolk diplomats and merchants silently judge the nation's representatives as they negotiate with the Baashidi noble, offering little more than a soft nod or glowering dismissal. To start, the Expeditionary Corps offers the simplest exchange. coin, and lots of it. The Baashidi human counters with requests for the wonders flowing through Cannor, hoping to see the groundbreaking goods in person. The arbitrations continue for hours and conclude with.
