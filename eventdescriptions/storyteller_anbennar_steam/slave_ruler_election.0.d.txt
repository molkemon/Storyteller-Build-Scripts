The death of the monarch has left the country without a ruler. All the important households of the capital are calling upon their influence to have a say in who the successor is going to be. Depending on who wins, the new ruler will have the support of different households and different parts of the country. Only one thing is certain. those that would rather have seen their own group's interests prevailing will not greet the new ruler with joy. Some might even spend the rest of their lives trying to limit his influence. But in the end, there is only one in the city who decides who rules.
