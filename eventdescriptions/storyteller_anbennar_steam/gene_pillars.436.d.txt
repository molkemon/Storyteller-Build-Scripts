Life is a valuable thing, both in application and in acquisition. The chamber the party passes through now is filled with a truly mindbogglingly large amount of tears pressed into hairthin wires, running up and down the length of the room as it buzzes with azure power. These wires lead into the tissues of what seems to be a miniature Holohana, dangling from hooks suspended from an unseen ceiling. With a great thunder, the azure light surges into the body, and it begins to jerk back and forth, kicking against the restraints like a child trying to flee its parents. One, two, three times it resists, until the strain is too great and the wires snap with a deafening crack as the chains break and the body falls. Bolts of blue light shoot towards the party, ready to end them with a lethal dose of the very gift of life itself.
