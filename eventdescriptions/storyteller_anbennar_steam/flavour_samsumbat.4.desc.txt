Despite the best efforts of the original writers of the constitution, not every situation can be foreseen, but it is not a bad thing that our state is being refined in the crucible of the harsh reality outside the council chambers. The power of the elected mayor and the council during times of war was one such situation, with ambiguity in the constitution leading to great tension and factionalism in the council. Thankfully, after the ruler had tested the limits of his power, and the council forced through some of its own reforms, a balance had been decided upon, giving future governments a precedent for interpreting our founding document. We know that war requires both decisiveness and restraint, and this is best served by the military ultimately answering to the government.
