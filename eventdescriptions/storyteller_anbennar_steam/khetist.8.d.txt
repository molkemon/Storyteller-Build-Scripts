The monarch was working in their study before they looked out of their window, bored of their endless paperwork and the machinations of the realm. Looking around the capital, their eyes wandered and noticed many things. The children running around on the streets, vendors selling their many wonderful wares, even a local priest giving alms to the poor. The monarch's eyes lingered on the priest, and coming to the forefront of their mind were memories from when they were a child, of when their tutors taught them of the many tenants of Khetism; specifically, that of charity and giving to the poor. As the ruler, the most powerful person in the land and the role model of the people, was they not also supposed to take part in this virtue?
