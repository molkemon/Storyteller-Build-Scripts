My grandfather came and claimed this land, spitting and gritting and hammering in his hand. He wanted a new life on the sand. Curse this town. My grandfather saw when the Havrics came, raiding and killing and putting lands to flame. My grandfather fought, but he died the same. Curse this town. My old man lived and he saw the wreck, taxed for half of his output, a dagger at his neck. The Havrics let our land rot, kneeling at their beck. Curse this town. My old man heard of the Havric's deal, working for self-rule, working for their meals. My old man sighed and he swore to kneel. Curse this town. So now I am here on the bloodsoaked land, spitting and gritting and hammering in my hand, working for freedom to the Havrics' plan. Curse this town.
