Five dignitaries stand in the grand council hall of Karshyr. four lizardfolk and a halfling. First, the monarch, the Master of the Gulf and our great nation, who wishes to secure Karshyr’s prosperity forevermore. Second, the head of the Aquaculture Guild, whose place was earned through their wealth, the backbone of Karshyr’s conquests. She wishes to further refine the art of aquaculture to perfection. Third, the mayor of Karshyr, representing the Council of the Coast and the millions dwelling in their cities, who wishes to maintain and expand their borders even further. Fourth, the archmage of the Hydromancers, whose control over the seas is instrumental to Karshyr’s security. He wishes to keep the Dames tear and Aelantier’s relics flowing into our realm. Finally, there is the ruler of Amiratsamo, our oldest and most critical ally, who wishes to facilitate the expansion of our trade within Sarhal and beyond. The meeting, despite its importance, does not take long. The dream of Khatalashya has long since been forgotten by all in the meeting, if it was ever a possibility in their minds. And if history is any guide, Karshyr’s current lofty position will eventually fall to the decadence that has plagued the empires of old if nothing is done. Therefore the meeting moves towards a previously unheard of idea, formally converting to a Cannorian religion. Weeks later, hundreds if not thousands celebrate in the grand council hall of Karshyr. The head of the Aquaculture Guild discusses adopting artificers into his guild. The mayor of Karshyr welcomes the new rectors of Karshyr. The archmage talks with traders from afar, promising the bounty of the ocean in exchange for the Dames tear of Moonisle, and the monarch pledges to keep the waters safe and the ships provisioned. And next to the throne of Karshyr, the monarch celebrates with the High Rector, safe in the knowledge that we will go further than any empire before us.
