Amazing, Allara said. It was only what, an hour ago that we hanged him? Flies swarmed the hanged elf’s corpse, and maggots crawled out of his empty eye sockets. Keldora doubted she would ever stop feeling unnerved by the sight of death. Another rebel dealt with, Keldora replied, avoiding looking at the body. How do you stand to look at him? I am simply admiring the Princess’ blessed handiwork. A shame he resisted. There was a set of shackles in the prison with his name on it, whatever that name was. At least we get to witness this, though. Allara stood in silence, marvelling at the corpse. Can we, maybe, sit down? Keldora asked. Somewhere else. The stench is making my head spin. Hmm? Oh, yes, of course, Allara said. The other side of the tree? Keldora gave an anxious nod. They sat against the ancient tree, talking and laughing, as the corpse decayed behind them, just out of sight. Allara rolled up one of Keldora’s sleeves and ran a finger across her orange skin, smiling slightly as Keldora shuddered at the touch. To think I will never again see your skin turn brown. To the children, it will be but a legend. Keldora plucked a brown leaf off of the ground and twisted it in her fingers. It's beautiful, she said, just like you. Allara laughed. When will you ever learn to flirt? A soft breeze sent a shiver running through Keldora’s body. She rubbed at her arm, earning a quizzical look from Allara. Are you alright? Allara asked. Oh, um, yes, Keldora replied, smiling at her a smile which didn’t quite reach her eyes. I’m alright. Just the wind. Keldora looked around at the trees, ignoring the hanged elf behind her, ignoring the maggots which already festered in his empty eyes. She ran her hand through the layer of leaves on the ground, feeling as the longfallen ones disintegrated at her touch. It was Autumn. Of course it was Autumn. It would always be Autumn.
