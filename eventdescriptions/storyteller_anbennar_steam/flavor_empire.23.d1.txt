It started quietly, strange sightings in the countryside of small beings sneaking about under cover of darkness. Things started to go missing, small things that were quickly missed. kitchen knives, soup bowls, the wheels off a child's toy, and food, straight out of pantries and cupboards. Rumors began to circulate of reptilian beings in the dark places under our feet, in sewers and catacombs, or new basements that seemed to crop up overnight with no apparent point of entry. Before panic could set in, adventurers stepped up, but they were too late to stop the invasion. Our city has become infested by kobolds; what's more, by kobold refugees. Those caught by the adventurers surrendered quickly, pleading for mercy in broken Common, each providing a fragment of the greater picture. a great tragedy is underway on the Dragon Coast. Kobolds, dead by the tens of thousands. Some cheer to see the monsterspawn lain low, but it's hard to feel triumphant when a helpless being is sobbing at your feet. Imperial Law is clear. kobolds are people, and have the same rights as human or elven refugees. But the Imperial Court doesn't need to know if we refuse them, right?
