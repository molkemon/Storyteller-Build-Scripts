The Kavalakuttam is normally forgiving about divergences of theological opinion, so long as the political ramifications aren’t too severe. The publishing of a new religious text from the Ynn, however, has passed the threshold, dubbed “The Ynnic Heresy.” Many theologians state that this simply goes too far. The Ynn equal to Nayiru? An Elusive One herself? The Ynn presiding over the Cycle of Glory… what does this mean for the Ynnic Empire? Is it now a realm sanctioned by Nayiru? Think of the implications. And the Kavalali have, and it has not been an enjoyable experience. The consensus in most of South Aelantier is that it is an attempt to subvert the ideals of Dawn throughout the entire western hemisphere. If this belief spreads, it would create a rival center of power to the Kamrayakval and Kavalakuttam, which are the instruments of Nayiru alone. Raise the status of the Ynnic clergy beyond belief, perhaps eventually above even Taychend’s own. The Kavalalists and Kamrayakvalists are both in agreement that something must be done. This new heresy must be combatted, of course they disagree on how. The Kavalalists turn to debate, as they always have; the Ynnic Kavalali are within their right to diverge on theology, after all. The Kamrayakvalists take a more hardline approach, as they see befits this potentially treasonous threat; they say the Ynnic Kavalali must be arrested and replaced. And the Monasticists, of course, say this is madness. If what the Ynnics say is true, then it will be revealed when Nayiru descends from Dhivumanhu and tells everyone his Truth. And even disregarding that, how many other divergent doctrines and beliefs through Orenvalyam could you finger as treasonous or dangerous? And, and, and, so what if the Ynnic clergy are powerful? We are not a Taychendi realm, but an Aelantieri one. To the Monasticists, this whole debate is just outrage for the sake of it; why restrict their freedom to state it?
