The unsteady nature and tectonic activity under the Serpentspine mountains has yielded grave results for our expedition. From one of the many openings to the side of the cavernway, where water had slowly trickled down in small drops, building impressive dripstones, came a low rumbling which heralded a deluge of brown brackish water filled with rocks and stones that pulverized upon hitting the opposite wall with the force the water had pushed them along. As this stream began to fill up the ground below us, some of the expedition members grew uneasy with what might happen now, fearing that they might drown in this cave. There is no end yet in sight, and no ascent away from the danger to be seen yet.
