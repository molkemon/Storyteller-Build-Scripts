Nelsik and Gott stood at the front of a vast assemblage of goblins and trolls, with a few orks mixed in. This had been a stressful protest to organize, considering the possible outcome. The large turnout had begun to lift their spirits, though, and it was Gott, the troll, who spoke first. “Trolls. Goblins. Friends. We are loyal to the Dream as Zog taught it to us. Why don’t our leaders treat us the same?” he roared over the crowd. Nelsik followed right along from his spot on his friend’s shoulder. “The Dream is for all of us. We want equal say in how this operation is run, since we work just as hard as the orks. There are too many of us to ignore.” Gott raised his other arm and flexed. “And we are too strong to do without. They can’t just push us down.” The crowd cheered, and the sentiment stuck. Word spread quickly to the Drahmgall and the Great Dreamer.
