Behind the bronze gates of the Balkhangfa Palace, perching atop the world in splendor, the esteemed embrace the Goldscales. Priests from the Dragon Coast mix with the monks of Yanshen, each clamoring for Balris (though with half also chanting the names of a dozen other dragons). Golden kobolds adorn themselves with the scalepaints of the Dragon Coast, while the esteemed affix hundreds of shed golden scales to their aments. Together, they become something iridescent: a mix of colors and hues, metallic and chromatic all at once. In the center of it all, the lord sits atop a throne made for a dragon.
Working with the Goldscales was not initially easy. The esteemed had assumed that the kobolds in Haless were an oppressed underclass, a hated group seen only as monsters. They had prepared inventions, traps, and weapons to gift to the Goldscales, but these gifts were met with confusion from both sides. The Goldscales, it seems, were not prodigal children. They were not hated like the esteemed were hated. They were no ruling class, certainly – treated as pets and prizes as often as they were treated as people – but they were also almost holy, a celebrated part of the Yansheni culture and economy. They were cherished nearly as much as the esteemed had been hated. This was not a reality that the lord was able to easily accept.
Though the Goldscales had a dragon who loved them, and though they were never seen as monsters, there was one place where the esteemed had surpassed them. The lord, unlike Balris, had found the other dragons.
As a new empire is proclaimed, and the Goldscales are made into rulers, the esteemed look northwest. If they are to prove their rightful place as the greatest kobolds on Halann, they must bring the dragons to Balris.
