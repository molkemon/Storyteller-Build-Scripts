Since our arrival in these lands, we have gained wealth and territory through two main means; raiding parties into the lands of our competitors, who brought with them wealth and supplies, and the hunting of great beasts, which exist in great numbers in this region and whose fur and ivory is greatly prized both in nearby lands and further afield, where it joins the vast flow of trade. A network of storage depots and bunkhouses radiates out from our capital, which has come to refer to itself as an Acquisition corps. In recent months, however, this corps reports increasingly bold raids from the native people of these lands. They react with increasing violence and vehemence to our presence, and these skirmishes threaten to boil over into outright war.
