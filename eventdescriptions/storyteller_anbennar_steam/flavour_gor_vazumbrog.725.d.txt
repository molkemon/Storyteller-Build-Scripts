The dwarves stood in a vast cavern, its rough stone walls waiting to be transformed. Thargas, the runemage, held the Earthshaper Sphere tightly in both hands, its surface warm and alive beneath his fingers. The runes etched into the Sphere shifted and glowed faintly, responding to his steadying breath and focused thoughts. As his mind envisioned a grand hall, the stone beneath their feet began to ripple and flow like molten wax. Columns spiraled upward, their surfaces adorned with intricate geometric patterns as if carved by a master sculptor. Archways emerged, each more ornate than the last, framing doorways to future chambers. Every dwarf watched in awe as staircases coiled into existence, their steps perfectly even. Vaulted ceilings stretched overhead, decorated with crystalline veins of quartz that caught the lantern light, scattering it like stars. Thargas' brow furrowed as he shaped the final details. reliefs depicting scenes of dwarven prosperity etched themselves into the walls, their craftsmanship beyond mortal hands. He exhaled, releasing the Sphere's power, and the stone stilled, solid and eternal. A feast hall worthy of kings, murmured one craftsman, his voice reverent. Thargas' grip on the Sphere relaxed, though his hands trembled faintly from the strain. As he gazed at the shimmering hall, pride swelled within him. Nobody noticed the disaster depicted by the reliefs in a far hall.
