The monarch swirls their wineglass, holds it towards the sun, observes the light play in the liquid, and then swirls it again. Their face is impassive, purged of emotion. Across the table, a vintner from Tef waits patiently, hands behind their back. The monarch thinks of this last year. Of all the little errors that had led to this moment. Of course, expanding the market would mean at least tasting the product. Businori fortified. By all accounts a distillation with more similarities to syrup than wine. This wine couldn't even be served with a dessert. It was a dessert. But there were worse things, the monarch supposed. Tasting bad wine was a small sacrifice to make for the economy. A sigh, then a swig, before the Tefori started to think they were stalling. That wouldn't do at all. The taste spreads across the monarch’s tongue, like burning sugar. It prickles, it warms. It expands, impossibly, the sweet fumes from this unholy distillate crawling up into their nose and down their throat. It is like drowning in honey. And yet. For a moment the monarch remembers a time so long ago that it may have been another life. They were working in the vineyards, as all children did. Helping their parents tend the family's grapes. They loved grapes, of course. Their parents did too, but. It was in those years that they were taught the art. They were not always the best student, but their parents always did their best. You could tell that they cared, even back then. As the monarch sighs deeply, they can almost smell that summer wind from so long ago. And yet, time pulls them from it, moment by moment. "My lord?" The monarch blinks. Sniffles. They glance at the vintner, and smile.
