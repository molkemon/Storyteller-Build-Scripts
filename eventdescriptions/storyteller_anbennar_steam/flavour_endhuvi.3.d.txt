Jira stood, dressed awkwardly in elaborate robes that ill fit her form. She had borrowed them from an aunt, part of a whirl of preparation following the news that she had been picked to join the team sent to the Hall of Endless Debate. The Cinnabar Lotus Society was finally getting its chance; its founders believed that everything was worth questioning and no view was too inconsequential to ignore, but only recently had their perspective come into vogue at court. It was hard to believe, yet here she was, standing in the antechamber of a building which lay right at the centre of High Philosophy, of three thousand years of history. Her peers had already entered and taken their seats; it was time. The grand doors swung open, a large round room expanded before her; the wizened gaze of the sages and masters turned to look at her. A lump rose in her throat and her tail twitched nervously as she stepped forward. Compared to this, the Cinnabar Lotus Hall was an old shack, and she took several halting steps forward. Still, it was everything she had ever dreamed it would be, even if it was intimidating in the extreme. On her right, an elderly human man smiled and spoke up. "Ah, good, the last of you arrives." As if it were an inquiry after their journey, he continued, "Now, tell us, what is the nature of philosophy?"
