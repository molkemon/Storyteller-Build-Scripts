Across the north of Cannor stretched the Greatwoods. These cursed woods were said to contain monsters such as werewolves, who would attack any who dared to enter their territory. In the southwest, technically not a part of the Greatwoods, was the Cursewood. The Cursewood was once the sight of a human kingdom that was cursed, making the woods deadly to humans. In these environments, humans struggled and orks only did marginally better. But that marginal difference made some of the toughest orks in Escann. As Unguldavor solidified control of the Greatwoods in Escann along with its borders with Gawed and Ibevar, it became clear that the orks here would have something to say about it. Controlling the Greatwoods would expend a lot of manpower and bureaucratic capabilities. Along with that, many Ungulans didn’t feel like living in these accursed woods. The monarch looked over the information and started thinking. The hardy western orks, whom Unguldavor assumed control over, seemed like a good candidate to hand control over to. Granted, there was always the risk that they would turn their warlike nature against Unguldavor and march on the capital. That was why the March of Khozrugan was going to have some safeguards. Instead of a Rugan duke, an Ungulan governor would be assigned over the territories along with being the only person in Khozrugan who could raise an army. Local viceroys would be assigned to overlook different areas to ensure the needs of the locals were attended to. As the Khozrugan March was being finalized, the monarch was pleased with himself over what was done. In time, perhaps all the orks in Escann could be protected under the same banner.
