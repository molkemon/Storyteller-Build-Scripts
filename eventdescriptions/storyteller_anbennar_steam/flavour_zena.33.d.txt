When the gray orks of Grombar left the Serpentspine and began to settle the surface, they faced different challenges than their green kin, being both fewer in number and faced with a more hostile environment. In order to surmount them, they mingled more readily with the locals than their kin in Escann. As their power in the region grew, state incentives and the promise of dynastic alliances encouraged human nobles to take orkish wives and husbands as a sign of loyalty to the Warlord Brasûr Frozenmaw, leading to a new generation of halforkish children. With the combination of rugged Gerudian ingenuity and orkish discipline, Grombar saw rapid developments in architectural utility and aesthetics. While initially visiting to learn of the resource-efficient Tarugs of the northern pass, fortified settlements in arctic conditions, Azaly was instead enamored by something else. The Grombari Glassworks. Utilizing several large sand deposits likely left by ancient glaciers, the Grombari people are one of the prime exporters of glass within Cannor, capable of casting incredible sculptures and weather-resistant structures in their foundries. Azaly has recruited prominent glassblowers to join him on his return to Bivuzena, intent on creating public works for the streets. Several Grombari soldiers have also accompanied their artisans for protection and to test their mettle against the jungles of Sarhal.
