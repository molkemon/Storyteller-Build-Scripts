We mined out ten seams of ore, and yet the land still offers riches aplenty. We cut down a hundred trees in the Bloodgroves, and hardly seemed to make a dent. We caught a thousand fish, and the Ruined Sea still teemed with more.  Aelantier is ours, and ours alone. But the land has wealth beyond our imagining, and we can hardly use; or even store; all we harvest. While the glassworker (and troublemaker) Aelthanas is advocating we give this surplus directly to the people, there is a better idea: selling it. Even sending a small amount back east would be enough to finance even the expansionist Sicrheior's wildest dreams.
