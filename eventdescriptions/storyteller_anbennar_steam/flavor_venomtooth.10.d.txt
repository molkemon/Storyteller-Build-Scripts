Decades have passed since Aâdga made her proclamation after the Battle of Castonath. Aâdga is now long dead and Azukor’s leadership had taken Venomtooth to new heights. In the capital of Venomfen, the swamp had been drained, but now it was even more menacing and inhospitable than before, if that was even possible. Human slaves toiled endlessly for their masters while the remaining goblins were tossed into viper pits and used for poison experiments. Among the orks, all young adults were being drilled outside the city gates in perfect formation, every one amongst them keenly aware that one misstep could be their last. The ruler pushed the double doors to the palace open and walked in. The ruler's footsteps echoed through the empty halls as he approached the new throne room. Unlike the pale imitation of a human throne in Unguldavor, this throne was forged of pure iron. The ruler sat upon the throne, which thankfully had cushions so it would not be too painful to sit on. Another day, another procession of orders to give. The ruler gave out several orders to enslave humans found in the woods, burn another goblin village, and send out another raid into Gawed. Near the end of the group, a young halfork scribe, the daughter of an ork master and human slave, approached the throne. She bowed and nervously asked, “My ruler, I was tasked with writing down the history of our realm in an official chronicle. What would you call our kingdom, which surpasses all other kingdoms in Escann?” The ruler, while initially irritated by the halfork’s audacity to speak unbidden before them, looked at her and announced, “We are the Kingdom of Khozrugan, named after the green fields of Escann. It is our right as the strongest to claim the entirety of them.” The scribe wrote it down and left. The ruler relaxed on his throne and sighed. The faint sound of shouts and brawls outside accompanied his contemplations.
