Avoid the eyes. Avoid the eyes. Avoid the eyes. This was the mantra repeated to themselves, for if the eyes of the beast and its victim met, the victim would swiftly be turned to stone and the battle ended. This was easier said than done, of course. In the oppressive darkness, the eyes of the basilisk, alight with a magical glow, seemed a lodestone as it swept its head back and forth, following the weaving figure. As the figure gets close, the basilisk rises up on its hind legs, preparing to deal a swiping blow with the daggersharp claws on its forelimbs. The figure sees only one chance, and they take it; the figure locks their gaze with the basilisk, and in that split second, thrusts their weapon up, aiming to pierce through the slightly thinner neck of the basilisk and into the head.
