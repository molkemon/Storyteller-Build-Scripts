Even if their metal bodies made of precursor steel cannot rust, water is still not a hospitable environment for the average mechanim. Joints can be filled with silt and marine detritus, stiffening every move. Interior compartments can flood, drowning whatever was kept within, and causing an imbalance through the added weight of water where none was before. And of course, any kind of electrical impulse will all too eagerly shortcircuit in the presence of liquid. Therefore, the design of waterproof frames has been an important advance for mechanim artificery, finally allowing their kind to partially or completely submerge themselves in bodies of water without risking all manners of deleterious consequences. One major consequence of this breakthrough was the addition of amphibious assaults to the repertoire of mechanim warfare. Now, they can ambush enemy formations by hiding from sight beneath the opaque surface of nearby rivers, lakes, or the seashore. And just as importantly, the ability to submerge themselves in water allows them to easily escape from a barrage of enemy fire, whether that be ammunition fire or literal fire conjured up by the enemy.
