Discovery so often begins with a mistake. But alas, it is not usually as fatal as this one. A member of our expedition misstepped into what was thought a shallow puddle. Instead, they fell in, and we never saw them again. We sent scouts in with ropes around their waists, and they found nothing: no sign of the fallen, no flora or fauna, and no bottom to the hole. One day, we may send them deeper. For now, we will step around this puddle, and count it among the stranger discoveries we have made in the Hinuilands.
