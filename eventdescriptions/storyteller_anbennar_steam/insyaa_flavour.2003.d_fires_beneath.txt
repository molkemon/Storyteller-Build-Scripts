We lost someone today. Perhaps their remains will be recoverable when the fires subside. We thought this was a strange part of the Hinuisavana, where the grass seemed a slightly different species and clearings reigned supreme. We were careful in our initial foray, lest a hinuibeast take advantage of the openness to pounce upon us. But little did we know it was the clearing itself that was the danger. A too-heavy step disturbed a fresh patch of ground, and out of it leapt scorching hot fires. They caught the shorter grasses, and those too soon burst alight. The heat then disturbed the ground further, and elsewhere in the clearing, flames leapt up, meters tall. We ran and ran as the grasslands lit up behind us. Most wish not to revisit this place, where fires lurk beneath what seems like solid ground. Some think, hope, that in time we could learn to tame them. I disagree. I think that all we will learn from this place is how to pick up the pieces in the wake of destruction. But perhaps that is enough.
