Unbeknownst to the ruler, rising tensions had misled some of their generals into doubting their eternal bonds with the other realm. Convinced war was inevitable, they moved thousands of troops to the border, and the other realm did the same. Then, the unexpected happened. On a quiet night, instead of forming ranks for a standoff, soldiers on both sides reached out across the line. Their field commanders, less removed from reality than their superiors, allowed it. What followed felt like a fairy tale. Soldiers and heroes, once prepared to clash, shared food, told stories, played matches by torchlight, danced, and sang songs that echoed across both camps. It was clear—they did not want war. They were one people under two banners, and they no longer wished to be divided. They wanted peace, and a shared future. When the news reached the ruler, a meeting was called. Discussions were long and grave. Then an offer. two realms, united under one monarch, equally represented. The heirless monarch of the other realm did not resist. He named the ruler heir apparent, protector of both lands. And so was born a union—to last a millennium.
