Toarnese soldiers could be heard from far away, shouting while destroying the few remaining standing houses around the city. The city had been sacked, not for loot but for another thirst. the one of purging the nation of monsters, this time forever. The walls of the citadel had been breached and the door exploded as cannonballs fired at it. Then, chaos ensued. The plaids became red and black like the mantle of some crusaders of the old times while the population remaining in the city was white like chalk due to their blood being used by the vampires. Many soldiers, proud of their ancestry, recognized some vampires because they looked strangely like the paintings hung in their family homes. While some have thought of ending their lives to make up for the dishonor of having cherished traitors for years, other groups of soldiers went into a fury, slaughtering anyone in their path. Entering the center of the city, the soldiers realized that the place was empty, that the families of traitors had fled. In anger, most of the city was razed and those who had not managed to flee were impaled.
