There is a terrible beauty to the hinuispider, once you stop with the full body shivers. Whatever the Precursors wrought upon these gigantic beings has not robbed them of their scuttling agility, the slender coordination of their limbs. The spiders show no interest in those animals which are not trapped within their webs; the alteration wrought upon them by the Precursors does not seem to have enlarged their silk glands to the same degree as their bodies, but rather has multiplied them. Thus each of these spiders, easily half the size of the average human, is a weaver of immense complexity and productive speed, coordinating multiple strands of silk at once in patterns of dizzying complexity. It is hard not to wonder what such creatures could do if handed a loom, though such thoughts are largely left to the evenings, when we are not dodging webblinds, sticky corridor traps, or our worst adversary; all the bloody poison. It is genuinely everywhere.
