Jianlin Shi, an emissary representing the lord of the distant kingdom, has arrived in our capital. He carries an ornately carved sandalwood chest, with fine gold inlay depicting a mighty harimari warrior vanquishing a horned ogre. In the box is a scroll composed in elegant calligraphy, a letter extolling the virtues of the lord of Hubao, fellow guardian of the borders of Yanshen. It notes their clear martial prowess, having been chosen by their fellow soldiers to lead them, and their deft skill as an administrator and protector of the hillfolk clustered around the fort. It subtly weaves in Hubao's history of ‘Conditional Obeisance’ to the Phoenix Empire and its successors, submitting to a greater power while retaining some autonomy. It makes clear that the distant kingdom is similarly courting the Tiger Fort to be an honored subject of their realm. How shall we respond?
