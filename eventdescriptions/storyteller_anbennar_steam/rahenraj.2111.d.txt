The second son of the monarch has always been a point of pride, a prodigy in blade and tooth and tactics. Sometimes, the ruler thinks he could be a young Harimar. It seems this scion thinks the same, for today he appeared before the monarch, and begged to be admitted to the Lotus Court as a general. Having such a figure take a place of prominence in the armies of the country would undoubtedly boost our place in court, though it would require the Raja to accede to such a request. Instead, he might simply be introduced as a courtier, able to study tactics in the Ministries' libraries and perhaps earn an officer's commission himself. The monarch can almost see the future's path stretch out before them as the ruler looks at their son, still kneeling before him, and knows the path will take their progeny.
