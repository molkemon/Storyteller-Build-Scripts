Dividing up power within the nobility is not something that can be bluntly done, lest the entire nation splinter into madness. Thus, the first of Hemantsen's reforms seek to take a measured approach, with the announcement of a new branch of the government: the logistics division. Placed in charge of managing critical infrastructure, this new administrative body seeks to bring the nation into the future, modernizing old systems into ones that serve the nations of today. Of course, much of this power was previously held by the nobility, who had total control over infrastructure in their domains. But with the advent of the Golden Highway, the roads of the nation are now a national issue. The lords of the countryside grumble quietly about the injustice, but ultimately allow the logistics division to work in peace. This reform has proven to be a successful one; will we be able to say the same in the future?
