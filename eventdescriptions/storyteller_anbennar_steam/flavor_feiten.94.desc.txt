Sound the alarm. Pirates. Horns blare out and signal flags flash from the lookout balloon, but by the time the hunter ships turn to respond, the guts of one of the merchant ships is already scattered across the ocean and the pirates escaped. Any merchant voyage is at risk of piracy, especially in the infested and lawless waters of the Ringlet Sea. Normally, they preyed on smaller convoys or individual ships, preferring not to risk taking on larger groups of vessels. In this case, the sheer size and chaos of the gathered fleet creates opportunity for the daring; a pickoff here, a pickoff there. Slowly but surely, they will suffer a death by a thousand cuts, should nothing be done.
