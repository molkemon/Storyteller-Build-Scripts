Digging in the ancient sites of Levaajan cities has been a painstaking process, requiring multiple teams of ogres, humans, hobgoblins, and many others to move the earth from the ruined structures we found beneath the desert. From our production centers within the wastes of Gozengun, excavation equipment and archaeological offices handled the preservation effort of the ruins they discovered, bringing all texts back to monasteries to be studied and recorded for future use. As more of these texts were uncovered in the sand, we began to see the true marvel that was hiding from us in the relics of the past; not a grand mystery of the universe, but the humble reality of being alive. Records of merchants selling goods in marketplaces. Administrative accounts of supplies needed for carrying out martial campaigns. Stories and tales of wonder and enchantment, written to excite and mystify. Even artwork, from the grandest piece of artistic achievement to the simple drawings of a child. Time and again these pieces of history were uncovered, and our memory of the Levaajan of old became ever more personal. These were not simply majestic cities of an empire long past; they were homes for thousands of people, each with their own stories to tell. In this revelation, our effort to steward the cultures who now live within our lands becomes ever more important. Who will tell the stories of these people when they are gone, and who will preserve these stories so they continue to exist throughout all time? As long as we hold their lands, the answer to these questions is clear. This is our duty, and we will gladly accept it.
