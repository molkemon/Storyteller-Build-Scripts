Chamakomo stood before his gathered fellows, other elders of the Taafi Clans. The noon winds swept over their meeting ground, a small rise dominating the surrounding brushland. The ruler wishes them to send their sons to war. A few laughs followed, while the more serious men frowned. Chamakomo continued. According to them, it would be a great honor to serve the rightful heirs of the Fangaulan Empire. Perhaps their cattle will herd themselves. Or shall the fields sow one another? Inquired another. Indeed, kin, the ruler demands they send the few young they have, not caring that it would doom them to starve, and if many did not come back. It would seem a fine way to end them, who have long been a strange thorn in the side of many a ruler. Chamakomo mused, before proclaiming. If the kingdom asks for their young, do not send them, nor their planetouched. And if they send forth people to collect them, make their young scarce. They are few, but they are proud, and they will not bow and give their future for the ruler to throw into the fires of their pointless wars.
