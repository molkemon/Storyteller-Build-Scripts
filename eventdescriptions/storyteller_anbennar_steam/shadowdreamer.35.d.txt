The tunnels open up into a vast, lightless chamber. The air grows hot and still, heavy with the stink of death and decay. For a moment, nothing moves. Then comes the sound. A slow, deliberate scrape of claws against stone, echoing from somewhere far above. The Great Bat descends, and as she does, it appears as if the entire pit descends with her. Slowly, from the darkness, she unfurls her vast wings. Torn membranes stretch between her jagged limbs, while her body twists with unnatural strength. And her eyes burn like embers in the void, fixing upon the intruders who have dared to descend into her domain. It is too large, too ancient; no such creature should possibly be able to exist, not in this darkness, not in these depths, surrounded by such little life. How it endured here, how it was even created is beyond our understanding. This creature defies all reason. Yet, that is not important, for all that matters now is survival. Her minions scatter at first, fleeing into the cavern walls as their master reveals herself. But then comes the shriek, a deafening, piercing sound that tears through the cavern and chills to the bone. From every shadow and every unseen hollow, the creatures answer. Wings beat against the darkness as the batlike creatures surge out from the abyss, gathering behind the bat in a churning mass of claw and flesh. There is no more hiding, no more waiting. This is it. The hunt has reached its end.
