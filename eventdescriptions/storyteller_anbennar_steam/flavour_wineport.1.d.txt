A Wine Baron, Evin, poured a glass for the monarch, and the monarch gladly supped upon it, deeply satisfied with both the taste and what it represented. It was a glass of Bridgewine from Menibór, a fine vintage, and one that was previously rather hard to get a hold of. Evin poured himself a glass as well, and clinked it against the monarch's. "Here's to tollfree trade and increased business for all. Wait until I inform our Imperial friends of the good news," he proclaimed, and downed his whole glass in one. As he did so, another Wine Baron approached, Gisele, with a mischievous look on her face. She surreptitiously handed the monarch a paper, and on it were some astoundingly large numbers. "This is the last report from the Pearlsedgers of the toll income before we cleared out their offices," she said. "Think, my monarch; this money enriching us, and helping our merchants dominate Imperial trade; all you have to do is keep the tolls in place." Seeing his fellow Gisele's scheming, Evin angrily looks at her, and then to the monarch. "We cannot. It would ruin our reputation with other Imperial traders, and have us marked as bad faith negotiators. You surely cannot be considering it, my monarch." Both of them look at the monarch, and wait for their answer.
