Making a profit on trade with China is a conundrum for the trade company. China produces sought-after goods such as tea, silks, and porcelain, but there is little we have to exchange in return besides simple gold and silver. This leaves the company with a trade deficit. There is however one commodity that would surely sell in Chinese markets; opium. The fields of the province are well-suited to the growing of opium poppies, which the company could manufacture into the drug. While the opium trade is prohibited throughout the region, the company believes it will have little difficulty smuggling it into the city regardless. Such illicit actions would surely upset the authorities, but it is difficult to pass up an opportunity for profit.
