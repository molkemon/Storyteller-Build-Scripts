The cheers of victory still echoed in the halls of Gor Vazumbrog, but the work was far from over. Black orks had ruled these tunnels for decades, and while their main force had been crushed, remnants lingered—scattered, but still deadly. They won't leave on their own, declared Thrain Stonefist, hammer in hand. We make them leave, or we make them corpses. The dwarves assembled in groups of six, each squad balanced with scouts, warriors, and one of the irongifted smiths for guidance. They didn't just hunt; they tracked with purpose, moving with precision through the unlit tunnels. Their ancestors had once done the same. Dauvek Veker, the Hunters of Deep Monsters, had been more than warriors; they were an order dedicated to purging the Serpentspine of horrors. Where their steel met flesh, no beast or invader survived. For centuries, they'd kept the hold safe before the fall. Now, their memory burned bright in every dwarf who hefted an axe, sword, or hammer. In the jagged remains of a forgotten ork outpost, one squad found their prey; a squadron of disciplined orkish soldiers. For Gor Vazumbrog. For Dauvek Veker, bellowed Thrain, and they surged forward. The fight was brutal, but dwarven steel triumphed. As they stood victorious, Thrain noticed a dwarvenmade item between the ork belongings. He knelt to claim the relic; a battered vambrace bearing the sigil of the ancient order. They never left us, he said, lifting it high. And we'll never leave their work undone. The tunnels rang with dwarven cheers once more.
