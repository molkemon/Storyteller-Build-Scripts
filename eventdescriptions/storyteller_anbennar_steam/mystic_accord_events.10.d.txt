The ruler climbed the thousand thousand steps of Tughayasa to visit with the seers atop the great mountain. The climb, normally filled with jostling pilgrims, was empty, silent except for the echoing steps of the ruler. The temple at the peak too was bare, the sages nowhere to be found, the normally constant ringing of bells quiet. The monarch made their way into the great hall where the oracular masters receive visitors, and, seeing it was bare, turned away. Before they could leave, an odd noise, like the tearing of paper, split the air behind them. The ruler turned to see a smattering of eyes floating like paper talismans in the still air of the chamber. They blinked with that same sound of shredded parchment.
