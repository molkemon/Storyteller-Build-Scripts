A pair of harimari sat on stools, between them a gameboard. The first moved a piece with the tip of a claw, oblivious to or perhaps uncaring of the sounds around them. The shop before which they sat boasted several gang members standing watch, while the sound of smashing and shattering wares inside could be heard. The second harimari watched the first make his move. "Didn't pay his protection money?" He picked up his own piece and moved over one of his opponents, taking it from the board. The first harimari shook his head. "No, no. They raised the price of protection just today, didn't you hear?" Over the steady din of the city streets, the sound of a great war horn could be heard; it echoed from the direction of the city wall. Several guards went sprinting past, headed in the great noise's direction. The second harimari sighed in weary resignation. The first harimari merely paused, studying the board. "It seems we have newcomers, here to try and tame the streets." He said, and the second shook his head sadly. "Mm. More fools to mire themselves in this rotten place." The distant sound of combat began to creep closer; the first harimari gave a small chuckle. "They will soon find out."
