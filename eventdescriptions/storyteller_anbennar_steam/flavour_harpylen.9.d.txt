When word reached the successful expedition to Naleni, the Queen's Throne was abuzz with intrigue. The Ivetasananthor had been a success, and both the Heunthokharivor and the Isutoqir were keen to see returns on their investment in glory and prestige. As such, it was little wonder that an invitation was sent to the Jewel of the Sky, an offer for the queen of Naleni to come to the heart of Harpylen, and see with their own eyes the splendor of the cultural heritage they were part of. It would perhaps have served the courtiers of the Queen to listen closer to the songs of Naleni, for the invitation was accepted. And again. Then once more. Twice, and thrice over. And the Naleni came to Harpylen, not just the queen and her retinue, but an endless flight of courtiers and captains, some dozens of welldressed nobles, but many more saltwashed marauders. For in Naleni, every Odanchesta was the queen of their own vessel, and the Naleni were proud and independent. Word had spread, and no Odanchesta would suffer to be outdone in prestige by her peers. So it was that for weeks on end, the Queen's Throne rang to song and dance and relentless revelry, and every Naleni bore gifts, each keen to outdo the other, so that Heunthume were near full to bursting with the endless treasures of the sea. Never one to be outdone, the Idar Odanches of Naleni arrived fashionably late, on a small ship, clad in long drapes of golden silk, held in flight by no less than a hundred of her peers, who used an ingenious set of harnesses to sail a ship to the Queen's Throne, for the first time in history.
