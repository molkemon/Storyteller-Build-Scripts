It was a rainy day in Apikhoxí. Heavy droplets beat a steady rhythm on the roof of the manorial veranda. Seated in his chair, the monarch gazed across the town square, to a busy construction site. The workers did not mind the rain, it seemed. "You summoned me, my lord?" The monarch nodded and turned his head. The one who spoke was Filinara Ironhand, the steward of his army. Or his militia, rather. It was a strange surname, one given to her, since she had no surname of her own. He made a mental note to ask about it some day. "By my estimate, the barracks will be complete before month's end. You will have lodgings as befit the brave soldiers of our city. As you requested." Filinara nodded. "You will not regret this, lord." "I hope not. It would be an expensive jest, and mine masters in Ormam will take it poorly when they learn of this guard of mine." "It is a choice that makes us all stronger. Of course they will take it poorly. Better to prepare for it." The monarch shook his head. "You have a fierce temper for a slave. No wonder they sent you here." "They had no use for me. It is well that you struck my chains. Their loss is your gain." "So it would seem. You promise much of this guard." The ghost of a smile appeared on Filinara’s visage. "I do. As I am loyal to you, they will be to me. I will take those who Ormam thought had no use. They will serve this city well. This I promise you."
