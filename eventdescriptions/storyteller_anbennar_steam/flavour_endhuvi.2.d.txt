Ten years. In a mere ten years could a conflict of three centuries be ended? The province of Maharaaja, a useful stop on the trade route to and from the lands of the Elephant Lords, had long been a point of conflict between us and Parusad Bhola. The monarch stared out the window of the palace, claws resting on the stone sill of the window. The meeting had gone off without a hitch, and the realm had put its best foot forward, but whether the ruler of Parusad Bhola or the Raja would believe it had yet to be seen. Numerous letters had been sent, pleasantries exchanged, and promises made; but would it be enough? A knock came at the door, a courier stepped inside bearing a letter, the seal of the Raja prominent on the missive. Anxious claws broke the seal and scanned the words within.
