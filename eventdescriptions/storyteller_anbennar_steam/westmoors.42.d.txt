"Teagan." Boomed the man in the doorway of the manor, the rattling rain outside blanketing all but the noises at least his volume. His eyes were bloodshot and red around the rims, and he had his hand beneath his greatcoat. "You know why I'm here." And though he stood before the lord of this manor, his gaze was fixed purely on the son who hid behind his father. The lord looked at him and sighed. "Alric. I know things have gotten out of hand, but my clan has enough coin to pay." "Payment?" Alric laughed almost as much as he cried, "You think you can clap a pile of coins in my hand and send me on my way? My son is dead, Teagan." He pointed at the young man behind the lord, "My only son, slaughtered by that bastard right there." "It is the law, Alric. I'll use a favor with the crown, get you your son's weight in silver, and the debt will be paid." "TO HELL WITH THE LAW." Alric drew from his greatcoat a dagger, flickering in the lamplight, and reflecting the light off of the several armed men he had brought with him. "We do this the old way, the true way. Eye for an eye, son for a son. Step aside." Teagan drew his axe and yelled for his guards. "You will not take my son from me." "Then I will take another. At least you have other sons."
