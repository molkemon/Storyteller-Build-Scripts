It was inevitable. From the moment that the Nékhei of Apikhoxí began to base their realm on notions of freedom, equality, and the abolition of slavery, we were bound to be mortal enemies. At first, there was a profit to be made of course. After all, what a buyer does with their property is their business. Selling slaves into freedom is strange, but so long as the buyer pays, what is the harm? Then the raids began. Freedmen on small ships, plying our waters, ambushing our traders, and burning our plantations, leaving emptied slave huts behind. Striking the shackles of our property, and stealing our people. All with the tacit support of Apikhoxí. Perhaps we should have crushed them then. Now it is too late. The Apikhoxí navy is outside our harbour, and their armies are at our gates. An envoy has brought the ruler a written ultimatum, signed by the upstart village council that passes for a government in Apikhoxí. Submit, or die. There are more terms of course. Abolition of slavery, promises of monetary support to ease our transition. But it is an ultimatum. War, or peace. How shall we respond?
