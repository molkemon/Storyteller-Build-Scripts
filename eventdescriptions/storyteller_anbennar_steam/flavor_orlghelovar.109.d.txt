Today, the monarch has gathered a great crowd of citizens under the balcony in the forum of the capital for a grand announcement. "Citizens of Orlghelovar," the monarch bellows, "The glassworker reforms are finally complete. For the first time, every glassworker in every glassworks has been afforded the privileges and rights they deserve. In doing this, we have surpassed even our ancestors." Before the crowd can celebrate, he continues, "However, this joy is not felt in every corner of the nation. The glasslords have been quiet since their disastrous protests and many have gone into hiding, as you well know. I am told that once news of our accomplishment reached them, they drew all of their remaining wealth and influence to mount a final stand against the forces of progress, going as far as to hire mercenaries to support their rebellion from our neighbouring countries. As I speak, they march on the capital." The monarch pauses, letting the crowd murmur and shift, before quieting them with a gesture. Another moment, and the monarch's voice rings out, "It is clear that they will never be satisfied with any world in which they are not in control, and so, to safeguard the future of our nation we must purge them once and for all. For the future of Orlghelovar, we must take up arms once more." At this, the monarch turns and strides from the balcony to the sound of raucous cheering and cries for glasslord blood behind him; the last hurdle of these reforms is upon the monarch.
