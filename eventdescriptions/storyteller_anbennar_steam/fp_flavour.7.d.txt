See there, the lord weighing our chances against his burly bounty; Fate decrees it seal for it seals the fate of we who tread through waters shallow and deep; Stride forth and offer it our latest catch, lest our baskets rot and hair fall out. Curling like a fuzzy, unfeeling shrimp, a master of ocean and river rocks upon a rocky throne in the province, demanding supplication by fish or fondness from passersby. The young and novice pine to drive the gluttonous creature away, but the fearsome fishers, salty sailors, and wary wayfarers rebuke them, demanding they appease the prophetic pinniped instead. To deal with water, without the appropriate marine offerings risks the creature’s ire, consigning the entire nation to a cursed future, or so tradition dictates. But is this silly seal really capable of affecting us so?
