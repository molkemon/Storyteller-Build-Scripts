The rift opens, and I can see. Hireet stands before a group of the Echenka, displaced and far from home, still sheltering under the mantle of the shadow swamp, but only just. She is reminding them who they are. Hated, despised, dismissed as cultic salvages plying forbidden magics. "Wretched you are," Hireet insists, in her cruel facsimile of honesty. "But wretched you need not remain." With a sweep of her enormous arm she offers them Yetefesefi; the riches, the plunder. Her offer is as it always is. companionship, solidarity, the chance to be Us and not Them. I watch as the Echenka imagine a world in which some other folk lose, close to here, in which someone else feels the sickening crunch of the boot against their cheek, the pressure grinding their face into the soil. And I watch them yearn for it.
