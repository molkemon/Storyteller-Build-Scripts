I could never forget the first time Mitrenya and I laid eyes upon the academy at Vers Lisie. It towered over the rest of the city, and matched, if not exceeded, the local palace in grandeur. It certainly did in prestige. Towers of wood and stone gave way to ornate cupolas, each a different colour. The tallest sat at the middle of the complex, and we immediately knew which spire contained the legendary library. I had only seen my closest friend cry once, when my mother died. That didn't change, but it was awfully close. We spent days pouring over the books, assisted by the senior librarian and his fellows. They were all too eager to offer their new lady some help, and their familiarity with the countless shelves and alcoves made our hunt much easier. Mitrenya read like a starving man eats; voraciously, without heed for the passage of time or those watching. Without me, I'm not sure she would have remembered to drink or sleep. After several weeks, we stopped. My head pounded behind my bloodshot eyes, but my dear lady appeared as rested as ever. "What now, then?" I asked, my voice dry. Mitrenya only smiled. "Know thy enemy, Varynna." Her grin curled into a confident smirk. "Now, we go and find them."
