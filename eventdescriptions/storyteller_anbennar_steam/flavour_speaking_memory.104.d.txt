For the first time since the monarch stepped outside the gates of the palace, they began to feel unsure. Could they really become a better ruler, just by contemplating the records of their ancestors? It had seemed so simple when the advisors proposed it, but now that the reality of study away from warm baths and hot food was beginning to sink in, they were beginning to feel unsure. If there was a benefit to this new seclusion, it was getting away from the petitioners, and the diets, and the reports. The monarch clutched their scrolls a bit closer. Whatever was in them, surely it would be more interesting than the day-to-day duties of rulership. Perhaps, the monarch thought, that was what the ancestors thought when they embarked on their periods of contemplation.
