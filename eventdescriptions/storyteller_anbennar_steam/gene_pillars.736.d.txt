Per the fairy tales of local fishwives and grandmarms, long ago the deepest waters of Taga’ala teemed with unholy creatures of the sea, foul aberrations sent to punish the fishing lines too long and the nets too deep of greedy fisherpeople. Legend has it these creatures were slowly hunted to extinction by the kraken, who replaced their predecessors as punishment for the sin of a too ravenous hunger. Yet myth assures evil as potent as that possessed by those twilight creatures cannot be destroyed, only absorbed. If they are to be believed, the concentrated malice of those vile creatures has found its way, trickle by trickle, into the eldest, hungriest of kraken. And today, here before us, lies a kraken of unthinkable size, so large it is trapped within this tower. Its beak, the size of a whale, ravenously chews another specimen of its own kind. A bloodshot, purple, glowing eye the size of a house sizes up our party, and all too swiftly massive yet emaciated tentacles begin to reach out for us.
