Like the scattering of driftwood, the Endless Ocean has carried its believers far from their origins. Wind and waters, Moa and Lai, have flung flotsam far up rivers and into bayous and in some cases even places beyond the sight of any water at all. This is not to say our connection to the sea has diminished in any way. No, the rivulets of water trickle across all lands, and just as those drops are destined for the sea, so too must all souls return to the ocean. Pilgrimage to the Sea is no mere journey; there are strict rules over how it must be conducted. The rugged conditions often leave pilgrims with an enhanced appreciation for the more standard procedures of travel. Progress must take place over water wherever possible en route to the ocean, and if one travels in a boat without aiding in its propulsion, then they must make contact with the water at all points in time to facilitate their connection to the process. Travel over land may only take place when it is raining or snowing if climate won’t permit. In moving like water, pilgrims reaffirm their bond with the sea.
