A caravan carrying a valuable trade good has been ambushed by tribes in a local province. It appears a local clan feels their lawful tax collection on our behalf is not enough to survive on. Instead, they have taken it upon themselves to increase their income through violent means. The royal council is divided among those who feel we must strike down on the bandits to ensure safe passage for the traders, and those who feel we should start paying these tribesmen to protect the caravans.
