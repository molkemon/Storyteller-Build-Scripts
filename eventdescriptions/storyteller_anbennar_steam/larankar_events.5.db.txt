The greatest warlords of the ruler's realm were gathered under one roof, watching in grim silence as Teshalazhil brutalised the traitor who once would have stood alongside them. As Teshalazhil finally finished off his prey, sinking his sabre deep into the traitor warlord’s back, the ruler rose to address the gathered crowd. The ruler spoke of threats within, the rebels and rabble rousers who threatened to overturn the realm they had all fought so hard to forge, whatever side they had fought on originally. The ruler talked of the value of unity, the value of submission, the value of keeping your troops in line and your head down, looking right at the broken, bloody corpse before them. “For there is a greater threat before us.” As the warlords left with gilded words and oaths of fealty, the ruler knew they understood that deeper threat. The ruler also knew that, if the ruler was successful, that threat would not be around forever. The binds of fear may one day unwind, but the ruler would be ready; the ruler would not leave their kingdom to the fickle hands of fate. If the ruler had to execute a hundred thousand traitors and more to do so, then so be it.
