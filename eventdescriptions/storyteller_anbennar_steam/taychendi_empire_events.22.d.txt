We have been defeated. We are beaten. We have lost the battle. Tens of thousands of Taychendi soldiers drag themselves back over the Veyii Sikarhi, a disorganized mob, and they leave hundreds of thousands more behind, turned into the puppets of a beast of which they cannot even comprehend. The song of the jungle mixes with the screams of the dying. Everywhere, elves ask why and some also ask how. They lay the blame at the Emperor Denariatam’s feet, and accuse him of having blood on his hands and ash on his boots. Many of the warlords agree. But that cannot be allowed to persist. Taychend has been beaten before, but we will not allow our Northern Expedition to become Ebenanth’s Western Expedition. This will not be our downfall, merely a setback in Taychend’s ultimate destiny. The war drums beat once more, the sabres are sharpened, the fire is reignited. Today, the Effelai is victorious, but tomorrow, it shall learn just how temporary that victory is.
