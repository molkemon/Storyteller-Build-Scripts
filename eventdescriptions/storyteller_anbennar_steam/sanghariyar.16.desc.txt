The high temple of Sarkhashabid has always been a masterwork of beauty, crafted by the hands of the High Gods themselves. As of late, however, it has been noted that many of its embellishments and ornamentations have begun to dull, fading with age. This is an embarrassment upon our state and the people of Sarkhashabid. How can we claim to be walking the path to purtana when the wealth we amass can’t even keep our great temple looking ornate? And so it was that a restorative plan was issued. A commission of the most skilled artisans and artists in our realm was assembled, tasked with drafting a plan to beautify the temple once more, restoring it to its former glory, with no budget limits. The draft went through many iterations before a final draft landed upon the desk of our monarch, including an artist depiction of the proposed final product. It was beautiful. Polished white marble lined the walls of the temple. Pillars made of solid slabs of jade chiselled into the many faces of the High Gods adorned the halls and chambers. Masterful artworks from every corner of our empire, displayed in frames of mahogany and teak wood hung from the walls, trimmed with gold linings. This was the reason the Silk Turban preached that wealth should be acquired; not so it could be hoarded, but so it could be invested into projects such as this. This was to be a masterwork of creation, and without so much as a bat of an eye, our monarch approved the draft, not so much as glancing at the costing sheets attached.
