Maracout had a dream; a dream to see all slaves freed from their chains, to see every person given the chance to live their life without oppression. With a new vision for the steward of the sorrow, we marched north from the mountains around the holy lake, tore down the symbols of oppression, and built something new. Corrupt systems of government that serve to enrich those at the top have been shattered, and together with our allied states we have created a great republic where Maracout's dream has been realised. It has not been a journey without cost or struggle. Many kingdoms on the continent took exception to our success, fearing unrest and economic destruction. Traditionalists in many spheres of our republic found themselves dealing with upended traditions, pushing back against reforms, and facing difficult choices as their society and religion changed around them. People fear change, this we know. They were not wrong to be afraid, but the costs are worth it. We have made a better world; our republic is strong, our citizens are free, and our diverse government gives us strength. With this decree of the Great Union of States, all of our people can now be assured they are equal, their voice is heard, and they are part of a grand republic like no other.
