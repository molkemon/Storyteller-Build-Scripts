There are few songs about this place, all full of strife and sorrow. Our stories could not prepare us for the horrors that await us here. Even the music of the local people here is different. it is cold, overflowing with malice and hunger. A single step into the mud and things burrow into the soles of your feet. The simple sting of a strange bug can leave you dead before nightfall. Our songs reverberate off the brackish water and travel far into the swamps. Sometimes things answer us, creatures with deformed and unnatural faces. There are people here, the Parura, who help guide us. They tell us that these leechfaced creatures worship an evil master, and that to hunt them is a right of passage for their tribe. Such a hunt would surely grant us the local people's favor, and help us to safely traverse the rest of the jungle. We share our songs with them, and are in agreement. Cosaínti, our people's most revered hero, destroyed a host of monsters, giving her life to buy her tribe time to escape from this horrid place. If we are to continue down this path to earn our own legend, we must delve deep into the very mouth of the Leechdens, where our ancestors found only sorrow and despair. Here, in this maw of evil, surrounded by the leechfaced abominations lurking on all sides, we make camp. We will feast upon the fistsized ticks and slimy things in the water, and declare our greatest victory with the newest song. One great festival of warmth, love, and light, a shelter surrounded by the darkest of places.
