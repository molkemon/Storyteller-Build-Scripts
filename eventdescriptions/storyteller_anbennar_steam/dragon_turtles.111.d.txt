A sailor approached my island a few years ago. I do not often take visitors, but her ship cut beautifully across the waves, and it inspired me to sing. She must have heard; she docked her ship, and soon my song was a duet, with the lilting heights of her voice elevating the music to places I could not reach. She stayed with me for a time, and I taught her how even the movement of the waves on the farthest shores can make inspiration, just as surely as any of the heroic ballads the others love so much. We composed many songs together, but a few months ago she wrote a song about my favorite tree, and how the anthill beneath it was a world in itself. It was beautiful; it moved me to tears. I think that it is time for her to go; she has learned all she can. She asked me where her music might be best heard and best cherished, and I thought of you. I hope that is all right. Please take good care of her.
