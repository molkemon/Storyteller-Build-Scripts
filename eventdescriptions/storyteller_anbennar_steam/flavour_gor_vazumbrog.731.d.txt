The Vaultwardens gathered around the Anvil of Unmaking, their faces half lit by the voidlight pulsing from its obsidian surface. The experiment was simple. dismantle the magic woven into a centuries-old runeblade. As the anvil's field took hold, the blade's enchantments frayed like threadbare cloth, dissolving into nothing. A triumph. Then Master Runmar staggered. He blinked, hands shaking as he touched his own chest, confusion settling in his eyes. "Where... where am I?" he muttered. At first, they thought he was merely lightheaded, but then his gaze turned vacant. He looked at them as though they were strangers. "Runmar, it's us," one of the scholars urged. The name meant nothing to him. He stumbled back, looking at his own hands as if they were alien things. "I..." He faltered. A pause. Then, voice brittle as crumbling parchment, he whispered, "Who am I?" Panic set in. They dragged him from the Anvil's radius, but the damage was done. The man who had spent decades mastering the arcane now could not recall a single rune. His past, his knowledge, his very self; all unmade.
