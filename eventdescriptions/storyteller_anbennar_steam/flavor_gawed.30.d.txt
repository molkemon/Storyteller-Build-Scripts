Discontent and fear lingered in the air as the imperial princes gathered in the Parliament building. Shouts and scuffles occasionally broke the dreadful silence as the assembled nobles considered the looming Gawedi threat that had brought the Empire to a breaking point. Gawed’s ruler sought to push as far south as the Havoral, no doubt; but many questioned what was to stop them from conquering halfway to Verne, should they choose to? Attendants bustled in and out of the assembly room, gathering drink or secrets for their princes as discord reigned on the question of the Empire’s response to the north. Most in the room realized whatever decision they reached, it must be reached soon to allow the empire to marshal for war. Around the great hall, princes and dukes seemed to stare at their attendants, young men and women of valor and earnestness; all of war age, all who might perish if war with the Northern Eagle was to come. One squire approached their prince, requesting how they should proceed on strategy during the meeting. The prince paused for a moment before giving a response.
