If the flock is the brain of harpy society, its soul is the warband flocks of soldiers which not only organize young warriors during raids, but provides them a sense of structure and social status. The warbands each have different rituals, symbols, and colors to distinguish themselves during the raids, and even out of battle harpies who share a warband regard each other as something close to kin. Unfortunately, the shrieking songs and dashing colors of their storied warbands have grown to be hated and feared by their human neighbors as a natural consequence of their success in battle. Harpies will often display their warband markings as symbols of pride even in their civilian roles, but a human who sees a harpy with warband markings as a trader, diplomat, or a dignitary will often refuse to deal with her altogether out of anger at what is perceived as chauvinism or a kind of violent savagery. Professionalization or reform of the warbands may appease their neighbors enough to fix some of these issues, but at what cost?
