The ruler returned to a capital ill at ease. Rebel slogans and graffiti still remained on walls throughout the city. His liberating army, primarily composed of Khetists, was not greeted by cheers and exultation, but closed doors and quiet streets. The city would eventually return to its usual state, he knew, and there would be no reprisals, but the people would come to recognize a new state of affairs. The alliance between him and the Medawor was now set in stone, and the Khetist faith would become the new religion of politics.
