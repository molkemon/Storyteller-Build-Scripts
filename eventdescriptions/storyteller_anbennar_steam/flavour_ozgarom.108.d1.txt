Since the rebel ork Bramash fled from Endralliande, little has been heard from his Cabinet of Freedom. During the rebellion, his Cabinet corresponded with sympathetic abolitionists across two continents, smuggling missives past naval blockades to offer the world their story, their version of events. Only now has the Cabinet begun once more to issue proclamations, attempting once more to build ties with the outside world. They describe a free orkish society, settlements struggling against the many threats of the Effelai, but united. Torn from clan and bloodline and moved at the will of their erstwhile masters, the free orks of Soruin have little choice but to make a new clan, a clan of the people as a whole. Family reunification is often almost completely impossible, and so all must serve as family to everyone else. Even the native Soruinics of the jungle have been embraced as kin, a closeness which has already spurred intermarriage here and there. The life is tough; the jungle itself holds many dangers. Yet the maroons of Soruin are free. The Cabinet professes a desire for universal peace, but Bramash’s maroons are a constant provocation to the slaveowners of Soruin. Time will tell if such a way of life can truly survive under such pressure.
