Umorozi’s voice is strangely honeytoned for a warrior. Her long braids are bound in three places by silver bands, her limbs sleek and muscled. She is the tallest woman the Vile One has ever seen, outside of Mama Hireet in disguise. Mama’s size is her metamorphic tell; when she shapeshifts, she always becomes a giant thing, unwieldy compared to the norm. Not Umorozi. The Vile One feels a curious envy for those shiny arms, so wiry and muscled, which glimmer in the sunlight. “I don’t train?” She says, in the cold manner she reserves for audiences. “A leader must train. You are going to conquer the world. You are going to wake early every day to do that. You will not be weak.” Umorozi’s tone is prediction and command all at once; it is so much like Mama. The Vile One laughs. “You are not my advisor yet. You should be boasting of your accomplishments, not scolding me like a schoolmaster.” “I am stating fact,” Umorozi says. “You know my accomplishments, or I would not be here.”
