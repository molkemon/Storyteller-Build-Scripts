The markings appear to be written in a slightly archaic version of the anakue script. However, thanks to careful record keeping and an obsession with the past, it remains readable to us. Here is what the markings say: "I, Waiklilinoe, braved the dangers of the Hinuiforest and reached this ancient spire in the year two hundred after the founding of the Consensus. Within I found many things I struggled to understand, but eventually I came to understand that just like any plant has a seed, this spire was the seed of the Hinuiforest. I will take what I have learned and with it my people will prosper." It seems our forebears managed to reach this place, just like us. Judging by the carving’s location, however, we doubt they managed to reach any higher. From here on out, we are blazing a truly new path.
