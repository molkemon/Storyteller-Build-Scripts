The sea was wrong. The wind had vanished, yet the swells continued to grow in size, crashing into each other with an unnerving drumlike synchronicity. And then it rose. The sea parted around it like a reopening wound, and from that black incision something emerged that no tongue could rightly name. Its form refused sense. vast, yet without shape; moving, yet without motion. The light bent about it, as if unwilling to touch its indescribable form. And then it saw. It had no eye through which to gaze yet it possessed a singular, terrible awareness. A presence that froze the air and gripped the mind like a hand upon one's throat. Without a word, the ruler stepped forward and walked off the edge of the ship, plummeting silently into the waiting abyss.
