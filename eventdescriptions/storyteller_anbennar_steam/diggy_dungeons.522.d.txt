After almost an hour of pondering, a cleric speaks up with his translation. According to him, it is part of an old dwarven love poem passed around to boost morale among the workers, reading, "Do not let the father's tears quench your burning heart, and let nobody label your feelings." For a moment, there is only stunned silence among the expedition. Then a soldier cuffs him over the head, "idiot, this is the Grumhardhûm, not the tunnels of Ovdal Lodhum." After several more hours of bickering, the expedition is no closer to the true meaning of the plates, and thus the expedition leader calls for them to move on.
