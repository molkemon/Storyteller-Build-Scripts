As our ruler begins to set off on their joint holy pilgrimage with the Mteibhar leader to Rigoti, our ruler relays some common customs to their ruler that must be followed as they travelled. No outsider is allowed to journey atop the holy mountain of Rigoti, but the king believes the Mteibhar to be distant brothers of the Fasiri rather than outsiders, for our ruler feels a close connection to their people. When both rulers reached the top, they knelt and bathed in the goldenhued light the sky had shone as the sun began to set. "I now understand why you brought me here my friend" their ruler calmly said. As our ruler heard this, our ruler began to carve a white stone with their name which our ruler had brought down to the King's Pillar, a monument built by the Last True King himself. Our ruler placed it upon the steps leading towards it and invited their Mteibhar kin to do the same. As they had both returned down the mountain, their ruler exclaimed "We are one and the same.".
