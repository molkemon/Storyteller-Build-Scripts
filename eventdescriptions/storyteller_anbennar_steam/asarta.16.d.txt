The Herald screeched and recoiled backwards, its giant wings frantically fluttering as it fell to the ground. “How dare you, how dare you, how dare you,” it shrieked over and over again. After a few seconds of repeated shrieking and fluttering, it was able to get back into the air and turn back to the lone lizardfolk that had just punched an archfey in the face. In the middle of the Herald’s mask ran a giant crack. “What was your plan then? Did you come here simply to hit me? The incomprehensible stupidity of your kind is beyond even my foresight. What was this going to accomplish?” The monarch clenched their fist, blood dripping down their palm. “I want my people to rise above all. I do not want our fate to hinge on the whim of otherworldly beings. I want us to take it ourselves. In short,” the monarch grinned, looking the giant moth in the face as its mask slowly splitting along the crack, “I wanted change.” The light behind the Herald’s mask shone brighter than ever before, filling the monarch’s vision entirely. The ground gave way beneath the monarch and they fell. The monarch fell and fell for so long that their consciousness gave way, the light so blinding it sapped them of all consciousness. After hours? Days? Weeks? Something awoke them. a strong smell of incense. The monarch looked up into the eyes of Akhaxpa. There was no light behind them. Just three slitted pupils with tinges of yellow and green. She smiled and simply said…
