In the days of the year, the Dragon Coast fell under Vængheim's wings, and gentle was her shadow. The people of this rugged land, were gnomes and dragons both, yet not the dragons born of old, but smaller kin thereof, who stood to heights of gnomish peer, and held their ancestry most dear. Yet with Vængheim came her skalds, who shared the tales of old, and gnomish mind is sharp and cold, and found no heart for song. Yet dragonkin took ear to these, and found them fair and soft, for many in their number held a love most deep for tale and song, to lull their brood to sleep. It was then, that a dragonkin named Ikse Smallbristle, who named themself a dragonpriest, did ask a skald of Elkaesal, and she said, "Soft is your tale and gentle your song, and odd I find her name. For I have heard this lullaby, and found that to my shame, it puts my little ones to sleep, and that seems ill to do, for Elkaesal should wake once more, so we may know her songs too." And the skald, whose name was Frey, thought little ere he spoke, "You speak the truth, and better this, that Elkaesal does sleep. For singing Alos' Lullaby, should keep her well asleep. For cruel are her songs, and she would bring us death. And send us winter without end, to choke life from our breast. So fear not for your little ones, for songs as these give gentle dreams. And unlike the great white beast of old, they will find their eyes come morning." "Right?" And Frey did look askance at Ikse, who stood as if struck dumb. Silently she turned and left, in haste hereto unseen.
