It is true that there are some historical empires whose dealings with foreigners were not quite as frosty as ours tend to be now. Yatkara’s Third Emperor List is replete with fascinating details of the eleventh empire’s dealings with a human entity known as the ‘White Soil People’, though beyond the name, records of this no doubt transient polity are somewhat wanting. If we truly seek to interact with foreigners more readily, a thorough reorganisation and consultation of our archival materials could offer a wealth of precedent, informing us of the basis on which such dealings were conducted by our ancestors.
