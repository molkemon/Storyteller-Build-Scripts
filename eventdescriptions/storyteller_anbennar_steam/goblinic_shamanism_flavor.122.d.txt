While the forest goblins had inhabited the Deepwoods since time immemorial, their dealings with the fey had historically gone poorly. The two peoples had initially formed a pact, the goblins of the Deepwoods having agreed to serve the fey. But, whether it was the capricious nature of the fey, in the weaving of some intolerable bargain, or the greed of the goblins, their agreement was broken, goblins and fey descending into a war of spiteful tricks. Rather than deepening, however, these grudges grew ancient and stale, the malice behind the mischief long forgotten. Beginning beneath the serene boughs of Ahain, the River Grove, the elves' wisdom shared in fleeting moments of peace helped to mend what was broken. Though they failed to embrace the delicate dance of ritual and appeasement between the Wood Elves and the fey, a very Goblin solution prevailed. Every village would name a Feykeeper, an unfortunate scapegoat stuck with the short end of every bargain with the fey. Forced to live in ramshackle huts far beyond the borders of the village, these muchmaligned wise folk serve as intermediaries between their people and the spirits of the Deepwoods.
