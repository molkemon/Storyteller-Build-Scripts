A strange feeling is creeping through the capital. We have not been condemned, but the scent of condemnation lingers regardless. As it creeps around us it brings a sense of terror, and the terror has no name. It has come in through the gates. Business in the halls of power has stopped. We are hiding from the thing. Some have locked their doors and sit behind them in the dark, listening. Some have gathered in the larger rooms, believing that company is a kind of armor, that the thing will not take from a crowd what it would easily take from one alone. They are wrong about this, but no one has told them. Drink dulls the horror, but even the finest wines have turned viscous, slow and wrong as they drip down our throats. The prayers of the rich and the influential collapse into weeping and then silence. We knew the years we were given when each of us was born, and there is no power that can turn back time. But we can’t see it, and it knows that it is not being fair, and yet it comes regardless. Everyone here is so young. Please. Please. It is not our time.
