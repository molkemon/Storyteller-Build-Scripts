The air is still. There is the hum of circling runes. There is the thrum of charged tears. There is the ragged breath of a hundred of Halann's greatest, pushed to the same limit. Yet the air is still, in the way before a storm splits it in twain. Andrellion kneels at the circle's heart. The atmosphere begins to fracture, like frostbitten glass. Beneath them, the stone trembles until it roars, forcing them all to their knees. Someone is singing. Someone else is screaming. There is no time for final words. Far, far above, through the lattice of clouds and fading firelight, the Sun hangs. Distant. Untouched. Against it, a mere speck of darkness, is Zokka, barely visible with mortal eyes. They can feel him. A weight against their minds, lightening as he gets further away. For a single, blessed moment, their thoughts are completely clear. The beam rises from the ground like a pillar of vengeance, brilliant and wide and hungry with purpose. The heavens divide. The circle is gone in a blink, consumed midincantation. Stone turns to sand, then to salt. Andrellion's flesh alights, along with all the rest. And they die. They do not see the light reach him. They do not see the fruit of their success. But something in the sky flickers. Something changes. For a moment, the Sun shines a little brighter.
