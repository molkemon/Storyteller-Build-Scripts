I once travelled to the gates of Westport, eager to see the prized jewel of the Seaflower with my own eyes. Every Derannic hears about the wonders of Westport as a child, like the monolith of Derhilde’s Gateway, overlooking the City’s massive harbor filled to the brim with vessels from all over the world. I came in anticipation of the Festival of the Landing, when the City’s streets bustle – even more than usual – for a day and a night to celebrate the day of the arrival of Derhilde’s host long ago. Hundreds of ships lit up the sky in tandem with the City’s many lanterns, and food was provided for all. Children raced down the streets, and men and women danced, clad in special garments embroidered with flowers, laughter ringing throughout the night. It truly was breathtaking. I spoke with many souls that day, all with different backgrounds and aspirations. Merchants who had come just in time to make their trade before the festival, migrants who came to Westport to find a better life across the ocean, villagers who, like me, wished to see the marvellous city for themselves, and, of course, locals who simply looked forward to that day every year.
