The next chamber is a cacophonous buzzing, strange, brilliant, beautiful, and terrifying. Beneath a vast, vaulted ceiling is an endless flowering meadow, a thick and impassible carpet of colors: fuschia, pink, yellow, blue, red, violet, and purple. Above, a strategist’s map of hundreds of hives shows two great nations of hinuibee and hinuiwasp locked in ten million generations of warfare. The wasps have the better territory; perhaps six tenths of the vaulting roof space belongs to them and their hideous, lumpy hives, like layers of skin grown over and over. Not so the beehives; they are beautiful golden yellow expanses of hexagonal perfection, structured and regular, inverted golden spires of refinement. The pattern you mistook for rainfall is in fact the continuous, gentle plapping of bee and wasp corpses, large enough finally to make a sound in their death, giving a little back to the egregiously fecund soil. How do any of these things fly? How are we going to move through this place?
