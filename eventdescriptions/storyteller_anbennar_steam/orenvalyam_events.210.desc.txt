When the armies of the Kamrayakval conquer a new territory, the first thing they do is smash any remaining obvious resistance. Captured fighters are executed immediately, their corpses thrown into mass graves. Rebellious civilians are not being killed systematically yet, but they will be eventually. Old elites are judged by officers, killed or imprisoned as a rule. Then comes reorganization. If there are slaves or serfs, they are freed, given a sword and sabre, and a position of authority over their old masters. Private estates are ransacked, land and wealth redistributed to the peasantry or sent back to Taychend to fuel the war effort. Everything changes. The world turns. This causes upheaval, of course. Many die, many suffer. There is disruption and confusion, and the process often creates more enemies than allies. And yet, that is simply the cost of Dawn. To delay and dither trying to find a morally satisfying solution is the Exemplar way, not Silverspite’s. And so the faith advances. From now on, and while Silverspite still rules, provinces conquered by Orenvalyam will receive devastation, and as the faith grows increasingly radical, will eventually also suffer development loss. However, each conquered province will also prove Silverspite’s worth to the faith, increasing devotion. As ideals of the Oren Nayiru once thought unshakable are questioned and tested by the Kamrayakval’s continued rule, this will become very important indeed. All that matters is victory, after all. What would it mean if the Kamrayakval could not secure it?
