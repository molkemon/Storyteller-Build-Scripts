The complaint is simple. the tools look drab and uninspired. Once they had surely been elegant in their simplicity, but the fact is that as they are they are no fit manifestation of the Zakytzkaras. An artist, guided by the joint efforts of scholars of the Humble Zeal and the Colors of Life, assumes responsibility for fixing this problem. With a mix of solemnity and great joy, artist and audience join in a cacophony of voices, figuring out together what the colors of the tools shall be, and what ornamentation shall adorn them.
