As the party ventures forward, countless spirits fly over their heads. The tower has come to life once more, and the energy encompasses every space. The party walks past a residential area, where several bedrooms lay. Manui stops the party, noting that there is a concentration of energy coming from one of the bedrooms. The party enters the room, and they are greeted by the sight of paintings adorning the walls. Age has not done these pieces of art any favors; most are ruined and faded. Paint curled and wrinkled, canvas ripped and tattered. One painting, however, stands out among the others. It is an ink painting, depicting a long serpentlike being emerging from a river and flying into the air, two arms protruding from its sides. Its claws reach out towards the land, as if proclaiming its dominion. Unlike the other paintings, this one is not ruined by time. Long, brutal gashes tore across the canvas, an act of intentional ruination. Manui gazes upon the painting and remarks, "Another broken lock. The twelve locks were of multiple shapes and forms. Some were well guarded and defended. Others were more mundane, like this painting. It still possesses remnants of energy. We can use this."
