After first hitting the rock layer promptly named the world’s bottom, we thought that it would be the end. However, we found a way to keep digging, and so we did. While we can never dig too deep, we have now reached a point where the sweltering heat is enough to make anyone uncomfortable. Finally, our digging scouts have managed to penetrate this layer, dubbed the world’s bottom, to find a labyrinthine network of magma rivers – reminiscent of a system of arteries and veins. We cannot dig further without risking the integrity of the hold’s new bottom floor, so this is where the digging must stop. This is truly a momentous occasion, yet somber. Since time immemorial, dwarven society and digging holds have been heavily conjoined, and the idea that there would one day be an end to digging seemed absurd – no known dwarven ancestral tale mentions anything like this. Still, perhaps in the future dwarves will revere a lord known as the Excavator?
