Along the mountain frontier where two regions meet, watch posts and forts have stood since ancient times, repaired yearly and manned by successive generations of soldiers. When wind sweeps the ridgeline, some claim to hear faint thunderous steps of giant birds from deep in the steppe—whether enemy riders or echoes of old wounds, none can say. Though no major conflict has broken out in recent years, alarms still come from the passes and rivers guarded here. A frontier commander now petitions to reinforce key outposts and rotate in more troops. Meanwhile, local officials propose a Frontier Labor Mandate, calling on registered peasants to aid patrols in the offseason to offset shortages. Though concerning only part of the border, the plan has stirred debate in the capital. should they grant onthespot authority to strengthen defenses, or act cautiously to prevent militia practices from spreading?
