Following in the legacy of the First Skaldic council, a new generation of Skaldic reformers now stands before the king. Students and followers of Radulfr, the man who became known as the Grand Reciter of Saga, the only one to hold that now infamous title. But Radulfr is now long dead, and the Faith has expanded far beyond what he would have ever dreamed, and these Skalds who carry his legacy now appeal to the king to make a choice like their ancestor did when they commanded Radulfr to sing. As our realm has spread far and wide, new tales are being added to the story of our people, and the Skalds of the realm have begun to sing of the present, not just the past. A sign of the realm's growing power, yes, but also a sign that the throne must step in once more to guide the Skalds. One of the reformers speaks up, presenting a scroll to the king. "Great lord, I am Signy, and my fellow Skalds have chosen me to present our findings on the faith." The king takes the scroll and begins to read. "These four tales are the ones we believe the crown should sponsor so that the people may learn from them. Firstly, the rediscovery of the making of Ebonfrost, the great metal of our ancestors. Second, the settling of Ambrströnd all those years ago, and the legacy of the Gerudians who still live in Aelantier. Third, the tales of the reavers and adventurers who conquered Cannorian realms, as you and your dynasty have done. And lastly, the tale of Olav and Urvik, the origin of our people, and the origin of our strength." Signy stood and bowed while the king read the composed songs. "All of them are valuable to us today, but simply say the word and we shall begin to sing one to the far reaches of the realm where your people shall listen."
