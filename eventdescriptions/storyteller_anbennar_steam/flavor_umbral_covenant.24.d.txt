Mama Hireet is coming. A wall of defensive fog erupts from the ground before the Vile One, instinctive, trained so many years ago under Mama’s punishing drill. Notice anything during battle, the slightest thing out of place, and bring up your guard. It is a testament to Hireet’s mad, brutal genius that she could teach a girl of pure arcane expression the instincts of the warrior. It is Hireet’s nightmare contradiction; strategic genius and bottomless bloodlust, the brain and the liver. Mama has grown powerful from death. This is the lesson so many have learned fighting her; that Hireet merely strengthens as her forces are butchered around her. She grows and grows, her shrill laughter pervading the battlefield as she swells into a giant. She is an avatar of hatred, of the sick thrill of revenge. By the time she and the Vile One meet, Mama is the size of a harpy roost. “Traitorous Bitch. Failure. Fat, Lazy Cow.” Mama’s eyes are bulging. How many children have known that rage, as they cowered against the kitchen wall? Despite everything, despite who and what She has become, the Vile One feels the abject terror of the disobedient child. But only for a moment. She crafts her Denial, wears it. Mothers such as these cannot feed on emptiness. The Vile One does not run, but walks; She lets the tears fall where they may. Hireet’s fury and misery beat against the indifferent grey fog swirling around Her. Cheni is with Her, his calm voice thrumming in a ritual room hundreds of miles away. He has mothered Her more than this thing ever has, and the thought makes Her smile. She slides the obsidian dagger into Mama’s foot. This is not a battle fought physically, nor a complex unwinding of arcane bonds; what kills Hireet is the simple disdain of the act.
