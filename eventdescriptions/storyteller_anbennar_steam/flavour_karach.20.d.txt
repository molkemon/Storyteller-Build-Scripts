Zamr Waverider looked off the side of the ship into the endless azure blue. That was all he saw all day. blue, blue, blue. He hauled rope on his ship the same as he did every day, and the mechanical motions gave him time to reflect on the anger within, and how it thrived on monotony. Monotony as consistent as a deep, unchanging ocean blue. But then the hauling was done, and Zamr could step off the gang plank of his ship. He walked across the docks to the small fisherman’s house that he lived in during the time that he wasn’t on the sea. His steps grew lighter. He threw open his door, and he pushed past his small kitchen in a rush to get to the back of his house, where there stood a room with a canvas as wide as his back wall. He opened the door, and looked out at his painting. Zamr Waverider let out a happy growl. There, on the left side, was his ship. Above it was a beautiful sky, glowing with the orange of a workday done. Birds soared overhead. He picked up his brush, and began to work on the color of the ocean some more. This was his favorite part. He could not control the unceasing blue of the ocean. But on Zamr’s canvas, the sea swirled with rainbows.
