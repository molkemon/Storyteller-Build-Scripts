If one wishes to enter the library in the High Temple of Khabtei Teleni they are first met with the gleaming visage of the High God of Knowledge, Simral; his form gracing the double doors which are the primary entrance. Inside lies one of the best preserved ancient writing collections in Haless. Ancient clay tablets and papyrus scrolls line the granite walls, small lanterns lit by magic illuminate the scripts and associated reading areas, and prominently featured at the back of the building is the mosaic commonly referred to as "Simral's Folly". Built of hand painted glass tesserae, the mosaic depicts Simral floating among the clouds on a sunny day as flecks of gold and silver meet his crossed legs. From his sky perch he sketches the progression of the left hand scene. There, within the jungle below, an army of black dots, barely perceptible, move blocks to a massive foundation where a half finished tower built of white stone blocks rises into the sky. More importantly though, Simral seems to be startled by whatever used to be on the right hand side of the mosaic. Upon that spot lies large gashes and innumerable missing tiles that have rendered whatever used to be there indecipherable. Debate upon what used to be there runs rampant, but whatever it was is long since lost to history. Looking upon the replicated mural and associated library, our ruler considers the meaning of the artworks.
