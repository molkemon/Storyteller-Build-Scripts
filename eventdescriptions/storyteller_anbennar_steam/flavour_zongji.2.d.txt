The city of Jiangdu now submits to us. Many among our upper caste find the climate more suitable for their tastes; warriors patrol the streets and train and perform drills in their garrisons, while our administrators fill new posts opened up related to governing the relations between the city and our state. For the most part, we have let most administrators keep their positions, and in a gesture of good will, we are letting the city retain its stratocratic systems. However, in our dealings with the city, the topic has come up of a special arrangement being reached. Most forms of the proposal entail an exchange. We are to retake all of the lands that Jiangdu lost during the course of the Blue Scarves rebellion, and transfer control of the area to their governance. In exchange, Jiangdu will help us achieve this goal in the best way they can; by offering us some of their brightest military minds to the cause. We are under no obligation to pursue this arrangement, and these ideas could simply die out over time, but if we wished, entering into this arrangement and keeping our promises would assuredly make the administrators of Jiangdu very loyal and supportive of all of our endeavors in the future.
