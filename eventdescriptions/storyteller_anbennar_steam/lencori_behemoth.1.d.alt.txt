It always goes the same way. An embassy will arrive, some nobles offering you their newest vintage. It would be an insult to refuse. You’ll take a sip, then two, and before you know it you’ve finished the whole glass and they’re already pouring you another. It’s exactly what they’re hoping for, after all. They want you on their good side, vulnerable to suggestions, perhaps even looselipped enough to reveal a secret or two. When you are the monarch, you have to learn that such slipups are an occasional inevitability. The most recent slipup went a bit differently than either party expected. An offhand comment about the dockyards of Westport turned into a heated discussion on ship size. Soon enough, you’re screaming at your Master of Seas to build a ship big enough to rival the Precursors. He, obviously, doesn’t want to lose his job. He goes out, takes a sizable portion of the treasury, and hires the Veline brothers—the most renowned naval engineers in Westport—to build the most colossal shipyard Lencenor has ever seen. The worst part is when you find out the next morning, with a splitting hangover and no memory of the last day’s events. The construction goes ahead, of course. A monarch has to commit to their decisions. Admitting that an order was a drunken blunder lets the Winelords know you’re an easy target. It’ll only lead to more trouble down the line. At the very least, the Veline brothers seem quite competent. Who knows? Maybe these big ships will turn out to be useful after all. It’d certainly lessen the embarrassment. Gods, I need a drink.
