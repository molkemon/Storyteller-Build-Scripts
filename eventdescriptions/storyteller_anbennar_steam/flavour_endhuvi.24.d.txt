The monarch sat along the edge of the river, a book in hand. Before the ruler, the Catfish spirit Kharunyana lazily swam about, catching small bits of food tossed in by passersby. Out on the river, a few merchant boats floated along, their crews all gathered along the rail, always drawn to the sight of the great white catfish. A light breeze shook the leaves in the trees along the shore, driving away any pesky insects that might prove bothersome. The monarch turned a page in the book, reclining on a small set of cushions with a blanket spread beneath them. Periodically, the ruler glanced over the top of the book as the sounds of splashing in the distance could be heard. The crews had started tossing bits of their food into the river, a mundane golden catfish leaping from the water to catch a piece before it had even landed. Downstream a little, a fisher hauled in her line, showing off to her companions the size of her catch. Now and then, a cloud lazily drifted across the sun; the monarch lay back on a pillow and closed their eyes, the book tipping against their chest as they began to doze comfortably.
