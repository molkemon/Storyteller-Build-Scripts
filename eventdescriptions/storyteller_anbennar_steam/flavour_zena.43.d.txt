Within the Ogre Valley, Fathide Ogre society is organized around Kelaktars, fortified settlements characterized by an encircling wall typically made of stone. This forms the basic unit of administration, allowing animal herds to graze out in the land surrounding a kelaktar, and then be brought back in to be butchered or otherwise processed. Some are legendary, like the cloudgiant ruins of Maghargma or the bronzeplated walls of Rijansvi. Azaly’s main challenge in the Ogre Valley was not to understand architectural peculiarities, but to charm its inhabitants. The Fathides have embraced their constant hunger, and believe in Iskerag Vulshor, where they partake in ritualistic feasts, maintaining that consuming something allows you to inherit some of its traits. Realizing the best way to the heart is through the stomach, Azaly has purchased a vast amount of delicacies in the Serpent Gift as his caravan travelled north from Bulwar, and hosted a grand feast for the ogres in the capital settlement. Between courses, he studied their agricultural defense networks, planning on adapting the designs for the outlying hamlets of Bivuzena. Several ogres have returned with him, willing to provide labour for the opportunity to sample novel Sarhaly cuisine.
