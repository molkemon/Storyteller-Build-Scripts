My name is the ruler. It was a wish we made, I know that now. We sought Asmalil. We called out, and she heard us. She answered, she brought us, and she bound us. I think back to the day when I took her. My hunters came to my court; they did not fly. They were simply there one moment, as if they had always been there. No one thought it odd, not even I. They brought her. They knelt so perfectly, and they offered her to me. It is a flute. A dainty little thing, like a fledgling's toy. Warm in my talons. I play it sometimes, but I do not need to. I hear her song day in, day out. Why do I write? Because she is old. Her power does not hear the written word. I feel no wish to write this. It makes me feel ill. Perverse. But there is a part of me that does not hear her song. That does not wish to hear it. Asmalil is power, but more than that. Those who hear her song, hear one another if they listen. I hear the song of my court, and then my own song through their ears. I know how I sound to them. I know what song to sing them. How to soothe them. And it is not only them. I soothe myself as well. We are so closely entwined, in a song so vast that it grows painful to listen. But I wield Asmalil. I know enough to use her. And she wants to be used. So I do, until I cannot tell if I wield Her, or She wields me. There is no voice, aside from the song. The song I play upon her, the way it changes. Is it a will? I am not certain. I struggle to care. What is Asmalil? How did Firanya make this thing? I do not know. I know Firanya's voice, for She knows her voice, and so I do too. But I do not understand how she could make this thing. I can think, sometimes, in a space beyond the song. It is rare, in dreams perhaps. I can hardly tell the difference. But I think of an idea. Something my mind can grasp unto. The Temensar are tools that shape the world. The wielders of the Temensar are of the world.
