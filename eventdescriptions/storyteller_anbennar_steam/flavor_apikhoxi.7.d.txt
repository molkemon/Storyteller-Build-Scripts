Kelodios wondered, as his callused hands put seed to soil, what sort of depraved lowlife he had been in a previous life, for fate to scorn him so. These lands had been his lands, after all. And his fathers lands before him. He knew these fields well, having spent all his years surrounded by them. And when his years were spent, he would happily rest in them. Or so Kelodios had thought. The last few years had changed everything. Not for anything he had done, for he had lived a modest life, no vices to swallow his meagre earnings. Yet the prices of grain had fallen, year after year. Imports from a distant realm were cheaper. Soon he had fallen into debt. Then into slavery. Now strangers kept Kelodios' hearth, and had him work in fields that were no longer his. These were bitter thoughts, yet every day it seemed, Kelodios' mind could find aught else to pass the time. It was enough to drive a man to it. A tap against his back. The overseer, and a robed woman. "You've a visitor, Kelodios," the overseer said. Then he turned his head to the guest. "Ten minutes. We've plenty of work to do." She nodded. He left. Kelodios stood up. "Kelodios? My name is Selussa. I come from a city." He spat at the ground. Selussa smiled. "Your tale is one well known to me. I would make you an offer." "I've little to trade, Selussa. Not even time is mine to give." "No, you belong to another. But they care not for you, and would sell you to me. We have a need for folk like you in the city, who would work the earth with us." "As a slave." Selussa shook her head, her smile gone. "We have no slaves in the city. You would be a free man. That is why I came here. To ask you, not some lord in the city." Selussa spoke the word "Lord" akin to a curse. Kelodios liked that.
