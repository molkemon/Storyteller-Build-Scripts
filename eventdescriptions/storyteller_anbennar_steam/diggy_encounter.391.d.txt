With a fast step, but still in formation, we traversed the cave while the water continued rising. Soon the black and brown deluge crept up our legs, filling shoes with small rocks, sand, and pebbles. Many soldiers now broke step, the difficult terrain making it hard to follow the others. Soon the water reached chestheight, which turned the uneasy into outright fear, with some panicking, since there was still no end to the hallway in sight. Finally, after thirty excruciating minutes of constant mortal fear we managed to find a path upwards. After leaving the water behind we had to find out that some stragglers did not manage to get out of the cave fast enough.
