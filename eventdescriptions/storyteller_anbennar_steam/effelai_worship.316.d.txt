The deep Effelai is treacherous. We sing the stories of Prigaír, a blind tracker who guided our ancestors through these lands. His ears were honed like razorflint, and his bow could pin the wings of a fly at three hundred hands. He was a great hunter, not just of beasts, but of paths. His songs give us strength even as we delve deeper into the heart of darkness. Then we find it. It is not a clearing. It is not especially different to the eye. It is not even especially serene. Effelai's song, however, cannot be mistaken. We whirl about and realize we are at the very center of all songs. We can pick up a hundred tunes, a hundred emotions, all converging in this place in the deep dark. The sheer number and intensity is dizzying. Dozens of our best singers stand there for a moment in awe, mesmerized by the sounds, just like the jungle's servants. They are only snapped out of it when we cover their ears and shake them. We do not let Effelai's songs control us. Instead, we create our own verses, our own rhythm. We complement the Effelai in our own way, adding to it rather than letting it consume us. We do as Prigaír once did, and forge our own path. The Effelai is our home and we respect its horrors, but we are not its thralls. we are a distinct and strong part of its ecosystem. We will walk our own paths and secure our own future.
