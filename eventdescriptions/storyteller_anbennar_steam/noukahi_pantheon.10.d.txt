In the old days, a journey through the Shadow Swamp was something undertaken only by the foolish, the mad, or the suicidal. While not all of the dangers in the region have been completely banished yet, the fact that the ruler can, like many other faithful, safely make pilgrimage to the great tree in Sprigi Twani is proof that light is at long last winning in its eternal battle against the shadows. The ruler admires the strange nature of the Swamp as they walk the paved path carved that now leads to its epicenter. Great Lotunga Pines stand guard, halfchoked by Hattiatia coils. Night Blossoms lurk, their pure black color much more distinct in a Swamp that now shines green rather than its old foul grey. From a nearby pond, a great crocodilian stares at the ruler, its back home to a buzzing hive of bloodflies, much less aggressive now, although still a common nuisance. Finally, the Matriarch is in sight. Tall as the tallest mountains, its roots and branches twisting in impossible patterns, its bark scarred from years of torture. Yet now it flowers and gives fruit. Its wounds, which once festered and spread rot and bile across the land, now show signs of healing. The ruler reaches the foot of the World Tree, something that was once impossible to do without getting torn to pieces by the undead vegetation it conjured as desperate defenses. They put a hand in its bark, and feel the subtle tremble of the fathomless energy within. It beats, like a colossal heart, one which once rotted and now blooms. And from the depths of the tree, the ruler can almost hear the voice of Lama Kerac, the old suffering queen that now can rest easier, the curse brought by her folly soon to be completely undone thanks to the relentless fight of humankind against the dark and evil of the hags. And she says thank you.
