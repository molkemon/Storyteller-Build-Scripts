The first question faced in the palace's construction shall, of course, be the matter of size. The larger the palace is, of course, the more features they shall be able to construct, the more impressive it shall stand, and the better a symbol it shall serve as. Yet, while the radiance of Surael is infinite, the treasury is not, and it must be kept in mind that the larger the palace is constructed, the more expensive it shall be. As such, three plans have been laid before the Emperor. The first is, relatively speaking, the simplest. Still extravagant, the palace shall be constructed in the traditional Bulwari style, home to sweeping arches and high towers. It shall be larger than all other palaces in Bulwar, but it shall not dwarf them. The second design is more ambitious; and thus more expensive. Less of a palace, and more of a complex, this plan would effectively see three separate palaces constructed, connected to each other by a series of elevated bridges, and using a series of mirrors to direct light towards a central garden. The third design, however, would put the other two to shame, both in terms of grandeur and expense. This proposal would see the construction of a large, cylindrical central building, standing over six stories tall and well over one hundred meters wide, with its flat roof playing host to various gardens raised above it. Surrounding the central complex would be constructed eight smaller palaces, each equidistant from each other, in the shape of long wedges. From above, the entire complex would be seen as a great, radiant sun.
