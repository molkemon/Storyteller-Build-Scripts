It is a quiet voice, one that utters barely a whisper in the dense jungles of the Lupulan. It calls to the ruler, as if the voice knows their importance. As the royal guard marches through the stormy day, the Anroang stops, listening to a sequence of sounds forming a few phrases of common. The sounds echo a fire, raging through the rainforest in the south. The fire roars with malice, that of a thousand souls writhing in pain. The souls dissolve into a whisper once more. They afford an accord.
