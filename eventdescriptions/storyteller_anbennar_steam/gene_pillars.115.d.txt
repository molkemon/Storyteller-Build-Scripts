Conthus explicitly warned the party about this chamber, imploring them to move past their base instincts and simply not engage. Upon entering, it is difficult to understand why: the only thing in the room is a single green frog, lazily looking about. The party almost pays it no heed, until, while one of their number walks past the stationary amphibian, it suddenly springs to life and jumps onto the leg of a nearby explorer. With a burst of green light, what was one frog is now inexplicably two. Another touch, and two becomes three. As the frogs continue to jump at the party, more and more of the mucus-covered nightmares fill the room, threatening to crush the party under their weight.
