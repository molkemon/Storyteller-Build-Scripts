The city of Sramaya stretches before the monarch. Here the mighty Kharunyana and Dhenbasana rivers meet and merge together to flow out into the bay, whilst across the estuary the great brass dome of the palace gleams, a group of Sirens sat upon its roof. There is life here, perhaps more so than in any other city in Rahen. Merchants and traders mingle amongst the squares, promises pass lips and coins cross hands with every exchange, goods borne by ship and caravan flow through the gates as freely as the rivers run. Once her ancestor Bimogíre dreamed of these streets; though rather than standing amidst the winding paths as their mistress Bimogíre no doubt pictured herself leading a crew of pillaging odangánthu. But Sramaya need not fear Ríva Nalénian raids anymore, not whilst the blue and orange flag flies above the city, harpies fly her skies and loyal Mengi patrol her streets. The monarch smiles. Soon the sound of song will fill the air, so loud perhaps it may even drown out the merchants' laments; for undoubtedly they will wail as their wealth wafts away, sent south to Sarhal. Sramaya is safe now, firmly in our grasp, but a heart it is no longer, now just another vein.
