In the years that followed Arosha Oakbreaker's arrival in the realm, her efforts to bring the people to the worship of Corin were highly successful. While there were some who clung to the old ways despite her efforts, it became common over time for the orks of the tribe to revere Corin, if not as a deity then as a model warrior and archetype of courage and nobility. By the middle of the sixteenth century, followers of Corin were as common among the orks of the realm as those who revered Dookan, and began to include the members of many important clans. This slow religious conversion eventually reached its peak after the Eleventh Pantheonic Council and the Corinite Proclamation, with many orks declaring that the realm should properly proclaim itself to be a Corinite kingdom and march to defend Corin's claim to the Silver Throne.
