As we continue our descent into the tangle, the ground at first appears to be covered in countless tiny white roots, but on closer inspection it appears these roots are actually growths of fungal mycelium. Many forms of fungus have a symbiotic relationship with trees, and it appears the same is happening here in the shade of this verdant pillar. Having cut open a few of the white strands, they appear to be pressurized; once cut open, a white slurry seeps out.
