Bulwar has always been a crossroad of cultures, civilizations, and ideas, and that is reflected in the different schools of magic that can be found within the lands. Bulwari magic recognizes four greater schools or knowings. the Erešdirkešir, the art of binding other beings to the will of the magi; the Erešmalthar, the art of inscription to create potent magical artifacts; the Erešsar, the art of wardings and divination; and the Erešsašur, the art of forcing order onto nature. Within these greater knowings, there are many smaller knowings, more specialized traditions that focus on particular aspects of magic and tend to be practiced only by a handful or even just a single magi clan, the secrets of their inherited knowing jealously guarded from rival clans. Among them is the Ereš of Ašiptu, or exorcism, used to purify both body and mind and traditionally practiced by Kašipti exorcist priests, but also studied by magi called the Ašipšin. Another minor Ereš is the art of Kirimahšikna, or Shaping the Garden, utilizing magic to shape the land itself and order nature. The Ereš of Gabarubir, primarily practiced by the order of the Gabarišin, uses prophetic dreams for the purpose of divination. A more controversial Ereš is Šibirinu, the Lapis Eye, which allows a Kamudašin magi to bind the will of an entire army to their own, enhancing the battle prowess and coordination of all soldiers at the cost of their free will. Another Ereš with decidedly martial applications is Beloštaro, born from the Sun Elven magical traditions brought with the Remnant Fleet, that allows for its practitioner to turn into a near invincible warriormage able to singlehandedly defeat scores of enemies. As we have decided to take an active role in the preservation and further development of Bulwari magic, we could favor one of the magi clans in return for privileged access to their unique magical secrets.
