The expeditionaries are on guard. For in the dark and damp halls they tread, the presence of what must surely be a beast most fearsome is heralded by honking roars akin to the trumpets of battle. Taking a turn and leaving a cramped hallway to enter a wide open room, it is there they spot it. A goose? A big one at that, honking with contentment as it plays with large rocks fallen from the crumbling ceiling. At first, the party is perplexed, choosing to remain out of sight in case the goose turns out to be as violent as its more conventionally sized fellows are portrayed to be. While the brave expeditionaries discuss what to do next, the goose uses its bill to fling a rock at the corner where the expeditionaries happen to be hiding. The beast looks curiously at its newfound companions and honks, as if issuing a challenge, and waddles off into the dark.
