The Green Mile, that's how the locals call it. Our ruler was looking at the reports from one of our cities, which has recently expanded its artisan quarters to accommodate more workshops for the jade carvers and cutters. It has not only become the centre of jade production in our nation but has also become famous all across the continent for its exquisite jewellery for the nobles, talismans for the philosophers, face rollers for the ladies, and so much more. With the expansion comes a cost, and the middle castes now demand we give support to these new workshops and quarters. What will be the response of the crown?
