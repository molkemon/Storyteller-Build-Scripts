Knockknock. Our lord stirs in their private chamber, suddenly aware of a cloaked figure resting against an ornate cabinet a dagger's strike away. How did? The stranger holds up their hand, speaking in a charming yet difficult to identify accent. "My lord, there is no need for alarm. I am here only to speak about a plight affecting your most loyal subjects." You see, the grungy gutters and smoky back rooms complain of only one issue these days: ogre discrimination. They're prime sentry and bouncer material, but the coppers harass, arrest, and even kill them; a crime lord can't even enjoy a perfectly legal dinner without worrying about their ogre bodyguards attracting unwanted attention. "And so I have been sent to plead with you, our lord; please issue a lien for these ogres. In exchange, your loyal scoundrels shall assist in outing the freelancing rats, corrupt pigs, and foreign snakes seeking to undermine this nation's greatness." "What say you, my lord?"
