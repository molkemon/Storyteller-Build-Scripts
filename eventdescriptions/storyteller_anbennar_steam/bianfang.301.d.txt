The Lord has set out on a Royal Tour of the Northern Territories, accompanied by an entourage of Marshals and court dignitaries eager to see the rugged steppes and picturesque river valleys. Crossing over the Black Mist Hills in the shadow of Dragon Peak, they come to the wide prairies that once hosted vast flocks of wild terrorbirds. The mounts of the Faceless Queen and her hordes are now nothing more than heaps of skeletons piled on the plains or curiosities corralled on the estates of Yan aristocrats. The Lord had the pleasure of feasting on roast terrorbird at the estate of one such aristocrat on the shores of Dakilbege Nuk. The Lord takes note that the aristocrat's servants carving and plating the beast speak perfect Yan, with a slight rural twang. Floating down the Kongniaohe to the northern bastion of Jiantsiang, the Lord and their entourage board a squadron of ships bound for the great port of Buogoj. Once the heart of the isolationist Odheongun, the city has now opened to ships from Yanshen and beyond, evidenced by the sprawling complex housing the harbormaster's office. Touring the city's famed schools, the Lord is pleased to find that the instruction for budding bureaucrats is being given in the local Yan dialect. The province's Marshal relates to the Lord as they share a pot of tea that the local youngsters in Odheongun and the old lands Shuvuush have begun referring to themselves as Shuokyuan, claiming both their old heritage and a new Yan identity.
