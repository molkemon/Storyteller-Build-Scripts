The enemy fills the horizon like a second sunset, and the soldiers watch it come without flinching. There are not enough of them. Everyone knows this, and no one says it. One of them speaks. It is a mantra from his training, something a former commander had made him repeat in his early days until the words were worn smooth. It was short and plain, the kind of thing that sounds like nothing until you need it. He gives it to his comrades one line at a time, waiting after each one, and they repeat it back to him in low voices, uncertain at first, feeling for the shape of the words, then steadier, then certain. By the end they can see clearly. They look at each other and find no fear there, only faces they know. The order comes. They move to meet it.
