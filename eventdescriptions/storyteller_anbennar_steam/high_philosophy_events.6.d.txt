It was petition day within the court of a country. Peoples from all walks of life were allowed to come forth and speak to the ruler. This often meant that the ruler sat through petty squabbles over land markers, or who owned a particular goat. Today was not all that different. The next in line was a man of the lower caste, his hands covered in calluses, and his clothes barely held together. He knelt before the ruler, and began to speak with trembling in his voice, "Ruler, I do not ask much of my superiors, but today I must beg for theirs. My eldest boy is ill, but I cannot afford his medicine. I am only a simple farmer, and my wife has long since parted this world. Please, can you find it in your heart to help me?" The ruler considered waving the man aside, but decided against it. Maybe the scholar really was having an impact upon them. They remembered the scholar speaking almost daily on the importance of familial harmony; on the significance of mirroring the High Gods and following your place in the family structure. Elders must provide for their offspring, who in turn should be obedient to their parents and grandparents. Only once a family has achieved internal harmony can they begin to connect with other families in external harmony, and achieve Purtara. A tap on the shoulder brought the ruler out of their reverie. An advisor leaned over and whispered, "Would you like me to get rid of him?"
