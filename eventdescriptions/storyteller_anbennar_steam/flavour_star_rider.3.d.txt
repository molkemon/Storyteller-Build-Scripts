The Star Riders have gone mad, spouting vague falsehoods in their attempts to appear devout. Laymen are interpreting a faith in ways that should never be. And yet, says the broken, laying bereft on the shores of the sea, they cannot keep this up for long. They have nothing. They produce nothing. Such a society cannot survive for long; it is impossible. We just need to hold out, just a few more years. All voices of the conclave have decided as such. To pay tribute to the Star Riders is your divine duty, for it is them who will bring peace to the plains. For it is them who will find a consensus in which every voice is heard. For it is them who will unite this faith and the magic that binds them. The chains are set, the strings applied, twisting the flesh and cutting into it. This is our penance. That which we broke will be made whole again.
