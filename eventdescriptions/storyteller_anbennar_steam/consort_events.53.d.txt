The people of our great government are well aware of our dispute with the other country. Considering that this is also the homeland of our consort, it is no wonder that the consort has been met with increasing animosity. For all they know, the consort could be harboring loyalties towards the old country, or worse; the consort could be a spy. And is it not a funny coincidence, the nobles argue, that the great monarch has become so dependent on the advice of their spouse? Our military advisor has gone so far as to threaten to leave court if the monarch does not distance themselves from the consort and their untrustworthy whispers.
