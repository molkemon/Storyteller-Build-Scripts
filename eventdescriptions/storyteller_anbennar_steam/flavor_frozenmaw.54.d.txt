He looks best in sunlight, the hero Crovis. The light picks up the Vrorenson blue in his eyes, and bids his armor gleam. On days such as this, blustery and inconstant, the sun emerges from behind the clouds only fitfully. Each time it happens, Crovis is thrown into relief, his lanky form suddenly beautiful; then, just as quickly, he is shadowed again. His allies have come. Whether they be proud ruling lords of realms or individual smallholders, he greets them all as old friends, old allies, grasping them by the forearm and memorizing their names; he has a good memory for such things. They love him sincerely. He has taken to this theater, the young Crovis, and in such observances of court and his talent for winning minds, he resembles none other than Brasûr Frozenmaw, that other fellow with the will to make imagination reality. Brasûr’s dream is an eccentric one, though, entirely his own, where Crovis seeks to make real some remembered storybook. Vanquish the terrible occupier, rescue the captive queen, restore the realm; these are Crovis’ pledges. Something in the childish hearts of men is captured by such vows, and they will do terrible things to make them real. All the patriotic men of the Reach march east to deal death to Frozenmaw; the die is cast.
