Contact with Cannor has seen the spread of a new religious faith called Ravelianism in our society. Its fundamental theological principles, while not relevant to this report, have also proven compatible with our society. Notably, numerous people have expressed a keen interest in this new belief system, adopting it either wholesale or in part on a personal level. Several proposals have even been made for the state to adopt Ravelianism as its official religion; though this would naturally cause discontent among those satisfied with the religious status quo. It is estimated that both a conversion and the maintenance of the status quo might trigger some temporary unrest, but would not cause any irrevocable upset in our society. In any case, it would be unwise to act against the Ravelians, as they have done nothing to upset the social fabric otherwise, and tolerating them may in fact bring certain benefits, given their zeal for inquiry.
