We hold regular festivals in conjunction with our neighbors to exchange the stories of the dead and ensure that none are lost. During a recent festival with a foreign speaker, one of our Memory Speakers was telling a great tale about one of our distant ancestors when they were interrupted by a different Memory Speaker. The foreign speaker claims that our story is false; that they know it with critically different details. An argument ensued, and the festival ended on a sour note. This is a serious problem, since carrying the memories of the fallen is the greatest duty of our faith. So long as we remember them, they are safe from oblivion, but what happens if they are not remembered accurately? If we have misremembered this, what else might we have misremembered?
