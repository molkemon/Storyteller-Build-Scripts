Emil orders the Brassblades to pack the explosives with an extra punch. Nearby, nominally neutral Imperial Princes arrive to watch the spectacle, guarded of course by entire retinues in case Emil feels bold. Wex once brought the Empire to its knees, disrupting a centuries ruling dynasty by sheer force of will from its most prominent ruler Lothane sil Wex. Emil intends to go beyond Lothane’s ambitions. A simple purple iron casket is brought forth from the ancestral graveyards as the Aldresian host stands in unflinching attention. Their shining armor darkens as storm clouds roll above threatening rain. Atop the casket in common is written a simple name of its occupant’s remains. Lothane sil Wex the Third. Raise the casket up to the window, I want the entire Empire to see it burn. Emil shouts as he turned toward his brothers. A child screams to his left as he is separated from his mother. A pang of annoyance enters Emil’s mind. How dare this child interrupt such an achievement of his? The screaming continues and Emil cannot bear a second more of it. He steps forward, shouting, “Put a knife in the child’s stomach and be done with it.” The Aldresian Host stands silently as two captains tilt their heads toward him. Emil snorts. Such a sense of honor is not what Wex showed to the Silmuna children during the sack of Anbenncóst. In due time, his brothers and sisters will learn the ferocity needed to claim their birthright. “It’s a figure of speech,” Emil backtracks as a gust of wind buffets his back. A cacophony of explosions ripple through the walls as the purple spires of Wexkeep fall into themselves amongst a plume of billowing black smoke. Emil smiles before he laughs, cackling as several surrounding towers across the countryside erupt in flame and smoke.
