The first barge was joined by another. The sight of buildings large and small floating down the river had become a regular sight to those who lived on the riverbank. Each structure added to the growing campus of the Cinnabar Lotus Society, and soon gangways and rope bridges linked a complex network of barges from shore to shore of the Kharunyana. What had been born of a mere need for more space had become a trademark of the Society. Riverboats passed through a narrow channel left between the barges of the campus, walkways elevated or small drawbridges raised to allow their passage. Traders and merchants peddled their goods to the philosophers as they passed. the freshest produce, richest spices, and latest news passed through. While the Watermarkets of Sarisung peddled goods, the Society traded in ideas. Endless chatter, day and night, kept the campus vibrant; young philosophers made it a tradition to try and convince a merchant of something, anything, by the time they finished passing through. Yet still ideas flowed, new and old, foolish and innovative, debated heatedly by those of the Society. Not just philosophy was debated, but the sciences and arts; ideas were challenged and wrestled with, brought back in reconfigured iterations and challenged again. The local villages too knew the Society as skilled mediators, bringers of education and wisdom.
