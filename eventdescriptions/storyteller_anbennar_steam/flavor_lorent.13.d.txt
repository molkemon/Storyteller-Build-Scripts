Our march west was a rolling thunderstorm of sword and flintlock as we crossed the Dames head and liberated Wesdam from the oppressive Lorentish hosts. We shattered army after army until we came into sight of noble Lorentainé standing before the bloodwine rivers and surrounded by the beautiful hedgerows of the kingdom. The siege of the city was long and hard, lasting months as we starved out the defenders. Spring turned into Fall until Winter arrived and all the roses among the hedgerows died, as did the hope of the defenders. The ruler exited with a small host of ceremonial royal knights, emaciated and on the verge of death, offering surrender. Lorentaine’s high red walls stood tall before the SorcererKing Nichmer, withstood countless Gawedi power struggles, and attempted to defy the Dove Throne. Today, it lay in ruins circled by a swarm of doves.
