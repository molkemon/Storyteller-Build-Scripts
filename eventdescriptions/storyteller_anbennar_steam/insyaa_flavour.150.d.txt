Discovered in the annals of a forgotten Gene Pillar laboratory, we have discovered schematics for a four-legged configuration, which should be applicable to our people. Featuring a larger torso chassis perpendicular to the ground for more leg space, two extra leg modules are attached to joints on equal positions of the hindquarters, reminiscent of a llama or wombat. The leg modules across the chassis are reinforced for better shock absorption and optimal cadence. Better weight distribution, combined with reinforced feet configurations to cope with the added mass, makes our soldiers far sturdier, able to stand their ground against attacks that would blow their bipedal brethren off their feet. Ideally, this will make our warriors resistant to enemy feints and charges.
