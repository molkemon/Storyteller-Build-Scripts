We claimed the hold the dragon ruined, dark secrets down below. Our forebearers' skills of constructcraft shall doom our purple foe. First came the household serving staff, titanium-clad maids, old butlers sent out to the streets, or on raids. Then as our golemancy grew, so grew the golems' roles. We sent them deep beneath the earth, to mine for iron and coal. Next, even dwarven warrior's roles could not be thought secure. The battle ranks, the golems take, from warriors strong and pure. The unemployed took to the streets, these sturdy women and men. From every mouth came the same cry: "Titanium does not bend." Golemancer's Working Song, Verse One.
