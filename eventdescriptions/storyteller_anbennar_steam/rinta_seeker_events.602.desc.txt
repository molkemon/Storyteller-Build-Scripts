Mages and scholars alike rush to the great halls to begin another day of debates. It has been a few months of constant back and forth. The moment a decision is made, another side brings up a new point and sends the whole thing back to zero. No compromises, no movement, nothing. The great nations have become weary. If their very smartest cannot find a way to move along, what does that say about the steady truce being held? Can and will it be undermined? Do the other Baashidi simply have no chance of getting along after all? Ultimately, it comes down to the classes of scholars amongst Rinta Seekers, the Mwalim and Darwiiz. The Mwalim believe in an organized method of research, focusing on universities and scholarly practice. The Darwiiz, on the other hand, place heavy importance on the spiritual journey of knowledge, and hone shamanic alchemy and magics to provide seekers with insight into their affinities. Both sides come into conflict frequently, usually over power and control amongst the courts of nobles. Now though, they are divided on how to best use the funds given to them to enhance their exploration of knowledge. Now, it is up to you, my liege, to come up with resolutions to these problems, as one of the rulers of the great Baashidi nations.
