Our mage summons a wall of fire as the huntress and Trystan fire off a dueling barrage of arrows. A flaming arrow cuts across my shoulder as I desperately dig through my pouch and ready a small powder bomb. The paladin jumps through the fire as Trystan sends dozens of shots, but the ranger falls to a terrible swing of the holy warrior’s warhammer. I draw my longsword pointing toward the injured paladin, but a silver flash blinds me. I hear the huntress nearby before the death cry of our mage. I shake my head clear of the flash, desperately looking for the huntress while the paladin charges me. My stupidity or courage taking over, I stand my ground and throw the powder bomb, hitting the armored adventurer point blank. Neither faith nor armor stops the paladin from exploding into a fine mist as I swivel around, looking for the huntress. I’m alone and the huntress is still out there. Before I can turn, the knocking of a bow makes me snap to attention. Was this it? The end? “If I kill you here, your people will follow me to the ends of Aelantier. Won’t they?” I take a moment to respond, my heart pounding like a hammer. “Probably.” Silence meets me for the next minute, then the next two, and after ten, I summon the courage to look back. Only the empty lake greets me. My comrades are dead, the enemy adventurers slain or deserted, but victory is in my bloodstained hands, painting the white pearl crimson.
