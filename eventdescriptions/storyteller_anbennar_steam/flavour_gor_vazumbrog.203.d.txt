The ten thousand dwarves of the expedition marched through the streets of Gor Vazumbrog to a storm of cheers. The crowd lined every avenue, their faces alight with hope and fear, throwing flowers and shouting blessings as if to ward off the darkness the army was destined to face. Each step of the procession was a symphony of discipline; armoured boots striking stone in perfect rhythm, a sound that reverberated through the hold halls like a heartbeat. This was no mere war band. The expedition had been forged from the finest steel of the hold. Monster Hunters hardened by decades in the Serpentspine's depths, battlescarred veterans bearing stories etched into their flesh, and masterful mages and runesmiths wielding the arcane with unmatched precision. At their head rode Commander Khadan Stonebreaker, young but tempered, a prodigy whose untested ambition burned as brightly as the runes etched into his shield. Behind them stretched an endless train of supply caravans, groaning under the weight of provisions, tools and weapons. But as they approached the Wound, the jubilant air faded. The twisted pit loomed ahead, a gaping scar upon the earth. The air grew thick, each breath an effort, the acrid scent of tainted magic stinging their lungs. Sickly purple light bled from jagged rocks, casting wavering shadows across their path. A sense of foreboding washed over the expeditionary force as they entered the tainted land, their footsteps no longer echoing but falling silent against the eerie stillness. Khadan raised a hand, halting the army. Only a murmur could be heard, runesmiths muttering protective spells under their breath, trying to shield the group from the malevolent forces. The miasma hung like a noxious curtain, twisting and coiling in on itself as if alive with some malevolent purpose. With a determined nod, Khadan unsheathed his sword, raising the blade high in the air. "Titanium does not break," he declared, his voice firm.
