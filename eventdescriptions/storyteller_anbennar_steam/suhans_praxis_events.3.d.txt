Lovely Kharta, wittiest of us all. You always speak as if I have no love for this place, as if I do not understand the desire to hole oneself up in the grand libraries and never come out. For the irrepressibly curious such as we, this is the true capital of Rahen. You believe that I seek to end the philosopher, to destroy his relevance and importance in Halessi culture. The precise opposite is true. Atma is not grain, used up when distributed from the granary. Conceive for a moment the flowering Visvatma of myriad literate millions, hand in hand along the Nitikamarja. Remember the wisdom of the Ghankhedeni, who teach us that he who opens the door is remembered for his welcome.
