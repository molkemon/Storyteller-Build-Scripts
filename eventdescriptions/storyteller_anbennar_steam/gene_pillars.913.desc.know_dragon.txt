You have reached a series of rooms that the other expedition members believe may have once been residential quarters for the individuals who once may have lived here. The remaining fragments of furniture and decorations indicate that, while these may not have been the most important individuals, they were still held by those of some means. After finding a few rooms fairly bare of goods, you stumble upon a room with some interesting decorations. On one wall, there appears to be a fragment of hide, covered in small scales that has been displayed in a frame. A shelf cut into another wall has what looks like a handful of claws and maybe a tooth or two, and a small plinth in the middle contains what you can only describe as several large fragments of shell. Your initial assumption was that these may be hunting trophies, either those of a large freshwater crocodile or maybe the owners hunted kobolds. However, on closer inspection, you realized the scale for all of the items was completely wrong for kobolds, and the teeth and claws suggested a completely different configuration to those found in modern reptiles. It is clear that the remains here are draconic, and the colors do not match any documented dragon. Most fascinating, your team marks up the found items and packages them for extraction to the capital.
