Alušir’s choice of heir to lead the realm was as erratic as the eternal emperor himself, seldom choosing an heir to the imperial helmet and throne based on traditional Bulwari succession rules, or indeed, using any discernible method of succession. Thus, he had shortlisted a selection of two princes and two princesses, some of them from such distant lines of succession they can barely be considered a true dynasty, but who can possibly refuse his will? With the untimely demise of the prior ruler, Alušir has directed missives to hurriedly assemble all the powerbrokers of the realm to bear witness to his new choice of successor from amongst the hopeful candidates.
