Immarel awoke with a start. There was a clamor around her as soldiers, merchants, and nobles rushed to barricade doors, sharpen their weapons, and plan their next move. Her wrists and ankles were bound, and someone had put a gag in her mouth. She was still wearing the white nightgown she wore to bed. She looked around. In the heart chamber, heavily armed, stood a large part of the cream of the society. They had figured out her plan, were spooked, and staged a coup. Just what Immarel needed. Near one of the chamber doors stood a captain and a few very prominent nobles; they were arguing about what to do next. Judging by the mention of his name, the faint shouting and noise from the other side of the chamber doors, and the incessant thud of battering rams striking them, another captain was on the other side, trying to get in. At least someone had stayed loyal. It didn't matter, though; the captain wasn't getting in, and she wasn't getting out. The binds were so tight they bit into her skin, and she could barely move her fingers; no casting spells. Then time stopped. An Archfey, the most beautiful elf Immarel had ever seen, clad in white furs and armor, appeared from thin air and approached, crouching down before her. "The Long Night has come. Now is the time that the wheat separates from the chaff, and those who are on the side of winter reveal themselves." Immarel's binds suddenly broke free, movement restored to her even as everyone else in the room stood deadstill. She pulled the gag from her mouth, and the Everfrost Prince continued. "I propose a pact, at this darkest hour. I grant you power; eternal life, and magical abilities beyond compare. And in return, you shall cast the land into an eternal winter, so that the snow shall fall for half the year, and so that all may feel the icy embrace of winter." He offered his hand. "What say you, my champion, my faithful servant?" Immarel took it without delay.
