It was no secret in Harpylen that the time after Firanya's fall had been a nadir of our culture. Our first great queendom fell with her, and our flocks scattered. For most, their travels took them east, to Siadun, Naleni and even further beyond. But some chose to fly west, settling in the Godshield south of what would become Castanor. The Ivetasananthor knew little of these harpies, and it was not until the Godshield fell under Harpylen's wings that an expedition could be mounted. The hunters took wing from Heunthume, heading east to lake Nasratu. From there, they traced the Nasra, until they reached the port of Deskumar. After, it was a gentle flight along the coast of Bahar, hopping from Yametses, to Crathanor, to Gemisle. Thus far, the path of the Stonewing was clear enough, and the flooded lands to the north were unfit for harpies. Past them however, loomed the forbidding mountains of the Godshield, and it was there that the hunters would fly. As it so happened, the Godshield held many old roosts, but they were ruins all, their insides gutted and scorched by fire. Of the Stonewing, no trace could be found, no songs, no memories, hardly even the bones of those who had once called these towering heights home. It was in Karn's Hold that the hunters found a clan of shepherds and heard their songs. For the harpies of old had been vanquished, butchered by the old king of Castanor, a name known and hated by all who called the sky their home: Beastbane. The Ivetasananthor were bereaved, and the flames in their hearts guttered with sorrow, yet burned with fury for the dead and the ignominy of their deaths by some human tyrant. Yet the politics were for the throne to manage, and the hunters had another task. By the songs of the shepherds, it was made known that some harpies had escaped the slaughter. Many had taken wing in a desperate flight south, not home to Harpylen, but out across the Divenhal, to the south.
