Prithee, man, is this a joke? The inquisitor stared harshly at the man before him. Some country yokel had barged his way into the inquisition’s headquarters carrying a large box. From it came occasional clucking. It was not a joke, sir. The chicken can smell witchery. I swear of it. Fine. Release the beastie, then. The man bowed, and opened the box. A fat white hen lazily walked out from it, looking around as if it was lost. It absentmindedly pecked at the ground. The inquisitor didn’t detect an ounce of higher thought in the creature. He signaled to another inquisitor, who brought him a small chest. He unlocked the chest, and took out a small object covered in cloth. Unwrapping it, he blocked his eyes from the foul cyan light it emanated. It was a witch’s stone; others called it Dame’s tear; and possession of it was punishable by death. Carefully, he set it on the ground near the chicken. The creature’s head popped up. It looked around once more; this time, with purpose. As if guided by a higher power, it stalked over to the witch’s stone. It almost pecked at it; once its head was halfway lowered, it started screeching and squawking as if it was possessed. See? It is as I said. The chicken smelled it. The inquisitor nodded his head, and rubbed his chin. Clearing his throat, he said.
