Deep in the tunnels of the realm, Nali held the Earthshaper Sphere aloft. Its metallic surface shimmered in the lamplight, the runes twisting like molten silver. A faint hum resonated through the stone, tickling at the edge of hearing. "Ready yourselves," Nali said, his voice steady despite the tremor in his hand. As he activated the Sphere, the unyielding rock before them rippled like water. The walls of a new tunnel unfurled, smooth and sturdy, their shape guided by Nali's intent. The dwarves followed in awe as the passage stretched onward, revealing glimmers of iron, gold, and clusters of glittering gemstones embedded in the newly exposed rock. "By the ancestors," muttered Gotrek, a veteran miner. "It's as if the mountain is revealing its secrets willingly." Yet, as the Sphere pulsed again, the hum deepened, resonating through their bones. A nearby boulder quivered and cracked apart, sending shards skittering across the floor. For a fleeting moment, the runes on the Sphere's surface flared a sullen red before settling back into their shifting silver glow. Nali frowned but pressed on, his team exchanging uneasy glances. Hours later, their carts overflowed with precious ore. The dwarves laughed and toasted their success, their pickaxes resting against walls shaped by the ancient relic.
