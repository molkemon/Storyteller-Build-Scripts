A professor of a certain religion was holding a lecture in a school about the folly of our religion. Hour after hour he mocked the High Gods as either impotent or fictional, derided their followers as superstitious hypocrites, and asserted the moral, intellectual, and theological superiority of that religion’s teachings. After he was finished, he asked the lecture hall if anyone dared to challenge his arguments. The hall was completely silent, but then a single attendant stood up and fearlessly asked: "If that religion is so superior, why hasn't it produced anything as magnificent as the High Temples?" The professor opened his mouth, but found no words with which to answer. His face turned pale, his hands started shaking, and then he finally ran out of the hall, head buried in his palms in a desperate attempt to hold back their tears. As if on command, the entire lecture hall started clapping in unison at the astounding rhetorical display they'd just bore witness to, and they all agreed to follow our religion from now on.
