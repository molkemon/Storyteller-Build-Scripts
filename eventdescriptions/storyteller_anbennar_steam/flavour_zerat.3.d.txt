The mood within the domain is triumphant; our rightful capital is once more in our hands, the emperor resting in the shade of its great realm for the first time since fourteen forty-four. Our returning nobles are already hard at work organizing celebrations, while the residents of the city relax knowing that the terrors of the former rule are over. To everyone, this is a victory for us, a signal that we will once more be a defining player in lizardfolk politics and a sign that a certain prophet is false. In the short term, the nation stands triumphant. But we cannot help but look to the future. Those who supported us even during the prophet's rise may have proven their loyalty, but we would be fools to trust in our recently reclaimed lands. Whether they are recently appointed nobles or traitors who sided with the prophet over their true emperor, many of these lakefolk have gone against us in the past and likely will do once more, should we show any signs of weakness. And thus stands our greatest dilemma; our position as the rightful rulers of the territory is our greatest strength and our greatest curse. We can press and control claims far more easily than most; the lands are rightfully ours, after all; but only if we can keep our pace of conquest going, snuffing out any future claimants to the mantle of the three hundred thirty-third Empire before they assert a coherent claim. Should we stumble, show any weakness or even hesitancy, those who turned on us once will do so again, and all we have worked for will crumble. But we will rise once more, even if our end is inevitable, for giving in is a betrayal of our duty to prove the final empire’s legitimacy beyond a shadow of a doubt.
