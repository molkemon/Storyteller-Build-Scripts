Khagor looked before the warriors milling about. They were in the midst of looting the burned remnants of an elven village, every warrior’s ax wet with crimson. The bodies of warriors and villagers, elderly and children, covered the ground. The battle, no slaughter, had ended, but still the chieftain felt his blood boil. “Do you hear it, my warriors?” he bellowed. His clan looked upon him with full attention. “It is the cries of our foes,” he continued. “Listen to their panicked bleating. Listen to how they scurry knowing that their secret paths are ours now.” The orks hooted, laughing as some made mocking animal noises. Khagor waited for the clamor to die down before beginning once more. “Do you hear it, my warriors?” he cried again before howling. A chorus of howls joined his own. “It is the sound of war. Of victory,” Khagor shouted. The howls grew louder. “No more raids. No more hiding,” he screamed. “Only true battle.” The chieftain raised his arms, drinking in the cheers. This was only the beginning.
