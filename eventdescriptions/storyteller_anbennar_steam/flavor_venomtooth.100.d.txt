The ruler rode into the settlement at Cannwood and watched as Cobalt Company mercenaries were killed or captured. Their measly treasure coffers were sacked and their barracks were burned. Many civilians fled into the Greatwoods, preferring to die by the claws of a werewolf than by the poison blade of an ork. Gunther of Mossford was brought before the ruler and looked at the monarch with hatred. “We refused to bow before one tyrant and we will refuse to bow to another.” He spat in the ruler’s face and smiled defiantly. The ruler punched Gunther straight in the face, knocking him down to the ground. “No one insults their superior unpunished, human. Just for that, you will never walk across this earth again.” The ruler picked up his head with both hands and slammed it against a rock. Even as Gunther’s last breath rattled in his throat, the ruler refused to let up, slamming his head against the rock over and over, until all that was left was a bloody pulp. Once the ruler’s rage finally subsided, blood was soaking the ground in a meter wide radius and Gunther’s face was mangled beyond recognition. The ruler tore off Gunther’s head and spat in the ruin left of his face. As Gunther’s corpse was tossed into the Alen, the ruler looked at the assembled captives and smiled. Despite the disrespect, this was a glorious day for the strong.
