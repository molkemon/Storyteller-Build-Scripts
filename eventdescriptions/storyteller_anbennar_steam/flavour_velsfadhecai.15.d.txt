I could recognise perhaps half of the men and women Mitrenya had gathered. The hall she had chosen was newlybuilt; an imposing chamber, with seats lining the walls and working their way down, designed as a kind of half amphitheatre. At the centre was a small, empty stage, where my dear Lady stood now, looking over those she had called here.  I was sceptical at first, when she shared her idea with me. To gather what was left of the nobles alongside the mages, with a few representatives from the merchants, adventurers, priests, and even the peasantry. A place to hear and implement new ideas, all in the name of better ruling. A third ruling body, serving alongside the crown and the estates. I still suspect Mitrenya just wanted to spend even more of her time studying.  "Good afternoon, everybody." Her voice echoed around the chamber, and all the mutterings ceased. I remember how the audience held their breath, awaiting the words of their Lady. "Let us begin."
