The ruler and the rest of the crowd watched the stage intently. On the rhythm of the drums, the actors perform their dance. eight men in furs and monstrous masks and a single woman with a short spear whirled around one another, artfully simulating a battle that had taken place centuries ago. One by one, the monsters fell, until only the woman was left standing. She fell to her knees, her hands clutching her stomach, wetted by wine as if stained by blood. As she began her lament, no other sound was heard in the grove, as if not only the crowd but even the jungle was enchanted by the words that everyone had heard many times before. Cosaínti had ever been one of the most popular plays in all of Soruin, ever since it was first performed so many generations ago. The ruler closed their eyes, mouthing along the words. The challenges Cosaínti had faced so long ago would not be unlike what the tribe would face again soon enough. It is one thing to declare the start of a new age of heroes, but it would be quite another thing to live through it. The jungle, the other tribes, and the weight of their expectations would take their toll on the optimism and determination of these people. It would fall to their ruler to ensure they would see it through to the end. To create a new legend. The last lyrics of the lament would echo in their mind for many days to come. "And through the darkness now I go, and though I die alone."
