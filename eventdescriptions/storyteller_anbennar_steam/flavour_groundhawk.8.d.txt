We should probably have expected something like this to happen when we entered a war between the gods themselves. While the celestial powers may be busy fighting each other, Adean and his servant gods still possess many powers with which to attack their earthly opponents; and it is a cunning ploy indeed to use the very blood of the casualties to choke our very food source. As the blood drenches our fields, it is up to every citizen of the realm to work together to survive the onslaught. Constructing drainage ditches, creating and distributing food stockpiles from currently unaffected regions, replacing ruined wood; much will be required to ameliorate the worst of the disaster. Of course, our priests are calling upon our gods to spare what power they can from their own conflict. Naturally, the clerics of the Regent Court gods beg the Dame to bless our fields and invoking the healing powers of Esmaryal and Nerat, but so too are the shamans of the old goblinic gods calling upon Kroozbuto Nubo to scatter the rains and clear the skies. But the most prayed to deity of all in this struggle is Corin, that she may shield her followers and allies as they fight beside her against one of the gods of warfare himself.
