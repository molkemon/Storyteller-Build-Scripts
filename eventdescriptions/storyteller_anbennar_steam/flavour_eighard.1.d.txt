In the middle of the night, the monarch suddenly wakes up. Something isn’t right. The pale moonlight only barely illuminates the chamber, and shadows dance along the walls. There is a soft breeze emanating through the open window. Did the monarch close that window? "Good evening, monarch." A shrouded figure separates from the shadows, leaning against the wall. The monarch shoots up in bed, hand shooting for the knife the monarch keeps on a bedside table. "Please, do not worry yourself," says the figure. "We do not come here for the purposes of assassination or blackmail, but something much simpler: we’d like to offer you a deal." "Who are you?" The monarch says. "How do you know my name? And who is we?" "Irrelevant questions. You are preparing an invasion of the Strutmar Peitar. We have our own reasons to wish to see them conquered, and thus our goals align with yours. There is a member of our organization who excels in the place of command. We wish to offer their services to you. That is all." The monarch is silent for a moment, then: "What is the cost?" "No upfront cost," says the figure. "Only the promise of a favor when the time comes."
