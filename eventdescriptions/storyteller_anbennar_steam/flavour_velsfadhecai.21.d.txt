Mitrenya stood over the dragon's corpse, brushing a hand lightly down its scales. Spears and bloodstains littered the chamber, but neither of them paid them any heed. I shifted, readjusting the bundle in my arms as my companion made her observations, until she stepped back with a sigh. "Come, sweetling," she said, finally, gesturing for me to walk to her, "It seems he's been dead for too long." She took the wrapping from me, carefully peeling the fine linen away to reveal the body within. A child, clothed in Fadhecan purple, skin marked with crimson runes. Mitrenya took the boy, gently laying him to rest alongside the dragon's form. Blood began to leak from the beast with a flick of her hand, rapidly pooling around the two. Magic filled the air. "Live again, Varlengeilt." She spoke, her voice echoing around the room. "I have need of you yet." The beast didn't stir. Beneath it, the boy's eyes opened.
