Government must work like a finely oiled machine. No cogs nor cranks must be rusty if the ship of state is to sail smoothly. However, these regular repairs sometimes leave bureaucrats with a sense of unjustified victimhood. One of these fired minor functionaries has come to us to challenge us to a Borbordûz, a sacred ritual of endurance above magma. In what is overall a depressing display, they have stated that they will win back their job or die trying. Despite the oddly saddening commentary on the mental state of our civil servants, we must decide all the same. Shall we accept their challenge, or remind them of their lost use to the government?
