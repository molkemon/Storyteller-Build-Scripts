As Konolkhatep expands into the far east, what was once a ramshackle slaving operation run by exiled bureaucrats and enterprising corsairs has expanded to become a slave port even greater than the notorious markets of Sramaya and the Raghamideshi kingdoms. At the port of Coburakaz, anyone with the coin can buy any type of slave they want. Strong slaves, smart slaves, pretty slaves, slaves that aren’t anything and are just good for dying, elves, harimari, and harpies. all these can be found among the market stalls. The stalls themselves stretch on beyond the auction houses and the grand docks, in a sprawl that seems to never end, such that a child born here might think that this port was the entire world. The veteran ship captains, hawkers, and auctioneers are generally struck with a different thought. The sizzle of branding irons and the crack of the whips and the screams of the stupid or the unlucky bound for all corners of the world seem to make up a kind of rhythm, a song that everyone in the port knows by heart. The song is commerce itself, and its tune will make all who listen rich.
