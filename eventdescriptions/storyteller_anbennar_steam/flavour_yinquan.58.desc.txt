Lately, the state has been suffering from the changes in staff. Having yet to be replaced, a litany of positions have had to have their duties taken up by others in the chain. If anyone knew that a magistrate had to do the work of their tax collector and their courier at the same time, then it would explain the rushed, halffinished jobs that have become commonplace. Though the worst of it is concealed from outsiders, inside the bureaucracy itself there is even a moniker for it: we have become a "distracted state", in reference to the fact that nearly everyone is seemingly too busy with their extra tasks to really give their actual duties their all.  The worst of it has, by now, already begun to fade; as reality has set in and positions are refilled, the strain placed upon those in office has begun to decrease. The new hires, unquestionably loyal to the Maidens of Liho, have taken to the extra work with eagerness. We will not be able to entirely be rid of this "distraction" until the reorganization is finished, but at least it probably won't get any worse.
