Our government has received yet another Borbordûz request. However, this is different from all others. In a sobering encounter, a retired general of our service has approached us, demanding a Borbordûz. He cites mass incompetence, a betrayal of Basalt standards, and timidness in policy. It will be no pleasure, nor no cakewalk to dispatch him if we choose to. He has many years of combat experience; he holds his weapon steady and sure. Not only that, he has the backing of influential officials in our military, whether openly or not. Shall we refuse his request, and accept the dishonor it would bring, or do we remind him why he is retired?
