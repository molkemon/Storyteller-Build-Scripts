Climbing and climbing, higher and higher, across new lattices of flesh and blood. This pattern has settled into the bones of the party, who now stand before something that until now has only been seen by the precursors themselves; tightly woven strands of nervous tissue, signals snapping up and down axons at lightning speed, stretch across the room. One expedition member carelessly touches the tissue, and a jet of energy lances across his body and into the next cell. Up and down the structure, countless bolts of energy slice through the air and light up the room. But these bolts carry more than electricity. The explorer struck with the neural energy begins climbing with effortless ease, almost as if he knows this place better than anyone before him. These cells carry memories, priceless information on those who lived and died in this place.
