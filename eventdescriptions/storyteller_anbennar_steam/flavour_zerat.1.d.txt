In very many ways, it is hard to be the current empire. We have always known our role was to be the door through which the promised golden age arrived, but until now we have not had to reckon with the true implications of that idea. For there to be the next empire, there must not be the current one, just as the current empire cannot coexist with the previous one. A millennium of rulers comforted themselves with the thought that the dissolution of the current empire might be peaceful, controlled, or any number of other pleasant fictions; we have no such luxury, having been reminded of the reality of force by our neighbors in Rayaz. But despite the circumstances in which we find ourselves, there is nothing to do but to fight on. When the next empire comes to pass, our dissolution will be immediate and total in the face of their glory. Yet we have seen the imperfections of the enemy in thought and in deed. The fact that we live means that we must still fight: Rayaz cannot be the next empire. They are unworthy. We know it, and they know it, and yet they pretend to a mantle which they cannot comprehend. They grasp at glory, in the hopes they might be seen as something other than the squabbling children they are. If our capital, the spire by the two lakes, and even our pride have been taken from us, this means that we must cling to our place in prophecy; it is all we have left. Until the last soldier in the last bastion of the Anzara Empire has shed his last drop of blood, we will fight not just for ourselves, but for the honor and the glory of the fated next empire.
