The consort has been pacing the palace lately, their head hung low, only stopping sometimes to longingly gaze out the window. Life is quite different here, compared to their old country. Most of all, they miss their family and the safety of familiar custom. Their child was a good child to their parents, they decide, thinking back to their childhood. But how does one know whether one is a good wife or better yet, a good mother? Their child shakes their head and keeps walking. Joining their heir for supper, the consort finds themselves at a loss. The heir's calm eyes usually bring a smile to their face. Today, however, not even this settles them. Perhaps it is the weather?
