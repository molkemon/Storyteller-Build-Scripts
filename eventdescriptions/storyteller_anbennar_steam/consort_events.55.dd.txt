The consort has been pacing the palace lately, their head hung low, only stopping sometimes to longingly gaze out the window. Life is quite different here, compared to their old country of origin. Most of all they miss their family, and the safety of familiar custom. They were a good child to their parents, they decide, thinking back to their childhood. But how does one know whether one is a good spouse? Or better yet, a good parent? They shake their head and keeps walking. Joining their child for supper, the consort finds themselves at a loss. Their child’s calm eyes usually brings a smile to their face. Today, however, not even this settles them. Perhaps it is the weather?
