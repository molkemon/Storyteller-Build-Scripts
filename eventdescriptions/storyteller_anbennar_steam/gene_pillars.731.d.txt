You have worked for months to reach this point, crawling through narrow passages and carving away at overgrown roots to continue our advance, and still the pillar goes on. Outside your narrow ward, beyond the fragile bubble of air created by your equipment, the water is heavy enough to crush you instantly. At this depth, even the potent magic you have employed to traverse the second level threatens to give way underneath the immense pressure of the water column. What, if any, precautions do you urge the rest of the expedition to take as you venture deeper into the pillar?
