At an intelligence report on the military capabilities of a foreign country, Aakhet did something he regularly does, and piped up with an unhelpful suggestion. "Why do we not invade that country now? They are weaker than ever from their latest conflicts. Now is the perfect time to strike." Everyone remained silent for several seconds, their eyes nervously shifting to one another, until the youngest soldier in the room spoke: "Because we have a truce with them your honor. It would be dishonorable to invade them at this point." "A truce?." Aakhet tipped his head back and gave one loud, piercing laugh. "Listen, truces are a social construct. There is no force preventing you from breaking them. If you feel bound by these meaningless treaties, then you merely mark yourself out as weak. We go to war comrades, or else we are but cowards."
