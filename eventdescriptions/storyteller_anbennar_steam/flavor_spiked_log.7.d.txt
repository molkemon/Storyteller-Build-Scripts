The monarch sat silently in his court room. It was not yet time for celebration. The next step was clear, but the supplies for his army were running low, and there were not enough feystompers to sustain a lengthy war against the orks of the Silentblade clan. He was mulling over the situation when two people entered, seeking audience. A stubborn elder, as tough as a gnarly stump, stood beside a greyskinned, lanky youth who seemed to have second thoughts about the entire endeavor. "Foolishness, monarch," began the elder, without even introducing himself or his companion. "You cut the wrong trees. The wrong trees. How will we hide from the elves? Even if you needed logs, it’s not an excuse for cutting the wrong trees." The monarch endured the onslaught with a blank expression, failing to interrupt the rant until the elder ran out of breath. "I did what I had to do to protect my clan. Who are you to criticise your monarch?" "Zakov Sharphand. I’m the best tree feller in the capital, and I’m telling you you should let competent people cut trees for you. I know you bigshots have responsibilities and all, but we can’t have you cut the wrong trees every time your army needs more logs. They’re our home, you know." "What he’s meaning to say is that he wants to work for you, my monarch," the youth timidly explained. "He is really good, you know, and he…" "Shut up, Garut. Look, monarch, I can help you. Just… trust in our expertise. I’ll bring you more logs that you’ll ever need. Oh, and my lad needs something to do. I took him in after he freed himself from the orks, but he has to learn and fend for himself. He’s smart, and decent with animals, but he’s far too scrawny to make an adequate treecutter." The monarch briefly pondered the offer. "Deal. You will gather your fellow treecutters in a cartel, and we’ll pay a fair price for the lumber as long as you meet our needs. As for you… Garut, was it? Come back tomorrow, I have a plan for which your skills could come in handy."
