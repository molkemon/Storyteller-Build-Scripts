"You cannot possibly be serious." The outraged shouts of the Medawor spoke for many in court at that moment, as Fumanekilnya exposed his theory to a shocked Medaka. "I am more than serious, cat worshiper. You may refuse to accept my words, but that does not make them any less true. I have spent years studying the prophecy, and my conclusions are clear. the three hundred thirty-third empire will come to be, regardless of what any man does or wishes." "So you are proposing we just surrender to fate and accept our doom? Just resign to being crushed by the lizards because the prophecy says so?" "I am proposing the exact opposite." The tone of the blind scholar turned more and more enthusiastic as he spoke. "Ordinary men accept fate, fools try to resist it, but we are neither. The Ikilshebe are the greatest, and we can do what no one else can. We can take the strength of the lizardfolk by wearing their hide, who can then tell us that we cannot take their destiny too? Temporary glory is at the reach of anyone, but eternal glory is usually reserved for the gods. This is our opportunity to take it for all of our people. It is us who must claim the mantle of the three hundred thirty-third empire, the Empire of Scales, Kumkayeisak. The choice is yours. We can usurp the lizards’ fate, wrestle destiny, and conquer eternity, or we can ignore the truth and face certain doom." "Have you gone mad?" The Medawor voice was drowned in the shouts of the Kulugi Abe, who at unison chanted the name of that prophesied empire, Kumkayeisak, as they hit the ground with their spears. Smiling, Fumanekilnya bowed to the Medaka and softly spoke, "So, what do you choose?"
