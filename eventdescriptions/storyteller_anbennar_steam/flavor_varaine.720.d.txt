After weeks of blunt rejections from the merfolk frequenting Djinnakah’s ports, the Expeditionary Corps have stumbled into contact with a merfolk princess and her royal retinue thanks to some fortuitous happenstance at a morning market. An hour later, the two parties now discuss terms in a private retreat comfortable for sea and surface patrons. Before the envoys can even suggest a gold price, the merfolk jump straight to the nation’s alchemical products. their potions are renowned at sea as well as on land, it would seem. The envoys silently dread the shipment delays which will be incurred by prioritizing the merfolks' demands, but the prospect of gaining inroads with the Undersea Kingdoms fortifies their resolve. As the talks conclude and details finalize, the merfolk princess, silent and detached for much of this dry affair, timidly inquires about a string of offhanded topics. masonry, rocks and minerals, Cannorian statues, and how the land memorializes royalty. Wishing to keep her good graces, the Expeditionary Corps answer as best they can, wholly enrapturing the princess. Much to her royal retainers’ chagrin, the princess casually promises to personally introduce them to the Undersea Kingdoms if and only if the envoys can produce a statue befitting her sovereign countenance within the season. They have technically already finalized the potion deal, but commissioning the merfolk princess her statue would safeguard their reputation for stock reliability. How should the nation handle this quandary?
