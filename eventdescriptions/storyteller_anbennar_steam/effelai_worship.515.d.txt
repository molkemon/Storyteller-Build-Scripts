Tswohvwoh’ii is many things, and the other tribes worship her in as many ways. Some worship her as simply a goddess and go to strike down enemies at her behest. Others see her as a maker of trials against which they hone themselves. Yet others see her as a dangerous force that needs to be appeased lest their sentience will be lost. We focus on something different, however. While those are certainly all aspects of the goddess, the other tribes neglect that Tswohvwoh’ii is no mere abstract being; she is here all around us. We cultivate the land because the land is the goddess, and so if the land is wounded she is also. This is not unreciprocated, of course. Tswohvwoh’ii allows us to use her own form to survive, for she is charitable, and the better we tend to her the more she can give. She is the trees and vines, roots and fruits. The other tribes understand this to some extent, but in their focus on her other aspects they can forget her power encompasses even more; she makes rocks flower with crystal blooms and grows salt for us along the water's edge. All we have comes from her. we cultivate her gardens, cultivate the goddess herself, and as we cultivate her she cultivates us so we may become stronger as well.
