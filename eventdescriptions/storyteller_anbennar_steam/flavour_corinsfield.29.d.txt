Now, goodmen, we have here before us a most egregious example of witchery; they do not even attempt to hide it from us. Couter PraiseCorin proclaimed from his judge’s stand, and motioned to the defendant. A grotesque form, is it not? Only the debauched mind of a witch could imagine such a thing. The gaunt body of the damned, the claws of a beast, and the head of a deer; an animal we all know to be associated with foul magics. The crowd murmured in assent as they looked at the accused; and the accused returned a hollow look, their mouth chittering. But now, we shall reveal this witch to all, so they will truly answer for their crimes in slaughtering a village of Corinfearing goodfolk. Bring forth the blessed water, so we may douse them and reveal their true form to all. PraiseCorin shouted, as a pail was brought forward. He muttered a prayer, and the water was thrown over the witch. And nothing happened. The crowd and PraiseCorin looked shocked. Even if blessed water wasn’t wholly effective, usually freezing cold water was enough to knock some sense back into a witch. But this meant that the beast shook against its bonds. It let out a guttural screech at the crowd, and many stepped away in fear. PraiseCorin gulped, before regaining some composure. This, this witch’s familiar, yes. It must have been separated from its dark master in the chaos of its wanton slaughter. Its master shall be found, but for now, we shall punish the servant nonetheless. I declare it guilty, and sentence it to be burnt at the stake. Guards, take it away. PraiseCorin said his words triumphantly, but any surety in them left as the beast was dragged out of the courtroom. His eyes followed the beast, as it struggled under its bonds, howling and gnashing its fangs. And all the while, its eyes remained fixed on him.
