Even if I could waste the effort on paints, if I wasted time filling a canvas, my livelihood would rot on the hook. Besides, I find beauty in this. A calloused hand guided a deft cleaverstrike as it opened the sow from shoulder to groin, and the butcher turned to her benefactor. "What meaningful difference exists between the arc of this knife and the path of a brush?" The patron held a fan in front of her face, seemingly in a gesture of disdain. The pig had been wriggling upon the hook until just a moment ago. "Well, usually my canvases aren’t screaming in pain when I set them upon my easel. I find that meaningful. It was my understanding most butchers killed their animals before cutting. Doesn’t your approach toughen the meat?" The butcher glowered at her employer. The fingers holding the fan looked so soft. They were the hands of someone who had seldom needed to perform actual work to survive, the hands of someone who believed that wasting an afternoon coloring paper was no different in merit than carving food for people to eat. The butcher turned once more to the pig. "Well, the cuts take more skill the way I do it. Am I not allowed to flourish and flaunt my talents, as you do? Or would you have me wallow in the throes of madness until I could produce a spoonful of lilac?" "Don’t pretend as though you can’t find a viable alternative. You could explore the culinary world with your leftovers. You could carve sculptures with your blades. You could attempt leatherworking with the skins. You’ve chosen to live for sadism." Leatherworking, eh? The butcher wondered if the patron knew soft skin made the finest leather. If only she could afford a tannery.
