Though the Oremvandaligned Chendhyans of Haebizyund have launched numerous raids against Ameion’s northern border over the years, the city of Oremvand has always had an amicable relationship with Ameion, being both their largest trade partner and an occasional ally. It would be on Oremvand’s side that Ameion’s first tentative forays into Taychendi politics would be undertaken, as it was they who seized the underpopulated islands of Paraidar and Kosiphineion from Nagar Vyechei, and even launched an expedition into Taychend during the wars against Gophira – the failure of which would . It was no wonder then that when Laskaris sent Serondorus Rhadinos, he came not as the general he was known as, but an ambassador.  Laskaris instead sent one of his generals, Serondorus Rhadinos, as an ambassador.  Within a year, Serondorus had done an excellent job of charming the Oremvandians, even if he failed to encourage them into any official alliance with the conqueror. He did such an excellent job, in fact, that he managed to charm his way into Queen Vesimanthi’s bedchamber. The result of this union, and the subsequent marriage, was Seroninpan, the halfKheionai heir to Oremvand.  Seroninpan has grown up situated between two worlds, as his mother teaches him the glory of Oremvand’s past and the power of the gods, and his father educates him in the many philosophies of his own homeland – and in the inescapable domination of the Kheionai way of life. That last point in particular has made a point on the young prince – there are rumours that he wishes to reform Oremvand, to bring the cultural and governmental inventions of the west to Taychend. Such a move would surely result in massive upheaval, as the Thekvrystana city shifts from being influenced by the Chendhyans to being influenced by the Kheionai. No matter the impact, however, it is clear Seroninpan must make up his mind whether to proceed.
