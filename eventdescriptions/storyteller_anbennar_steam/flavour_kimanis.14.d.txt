So saith the mob. Our demagogue has saved us all from peril. We had been infiltrated by a cult. Their whispers made us dance in awful guises, and we do not mean dancing on the stage. We chose a father by election, sworn to guard Kimánis in such times like these. He roused us from a drowsy cultural stupor and led us against our enemies with ease. We grant him every honour of our Kheion. His head is crowned with laurel and with brass. Kimánis fair decrees a celebration in honor of our civic bonds renewed. This joyous day calls yet for wine, we venture; we are very glad our demagogue agrees.
