Deadfang, more like deadfang. That doesn’t work, does it? The look of confusion on the soldier’s face betrayed the lack of any internal machinations, more so than the silence that followed. In his desire to degrade, thoughts got throttled, and the words entered the world unburdened by the weight of wit one would expect. The inadequacy of the jab was so baffling in fact, that no followup action was planned at all. Whatever had been blurted out up to that point was to be swiftly forgotten by anyone within earshot of the goblin who would soon be known as the lousiest jeer in Escann. The ruler watched from afar. One of the knights stood in place, swordarm low and limp, the blade touching the ground, with not much reaction from the rest. After a while, the sword was finally appropriated by the soldier next to him, who then presumably began to think of a new jest to share with the group. The tied up orks in the center of the circle continued with their strategy of utter indifference to whatever verbal violations were being inflicted upon them. The freed humans to the side looked as though they were putting great effort into doing nothing at all. The ruler, meanwhile, continued to think. Revenge was screeching as loud as it could, while justice looked on with unwavering composure. Compassion was quietly crying in the corner, with duty mournfully resigned beside it. At the edge of the ruler’s vision, a cavalier in full plate, visor covering his face, hand on the swordhilt. Ready for whatever was to come? Or mocking the indecision of presentday knights. “...defanged.” And in a split second, it all burst into nothingness. Like the ruler’s body knew that it was time to stop thinking, that contemplation had to give way to anticipation. Because without it, tragedy would strike. The ruler had but a fleeting moment before a flood of boisterous cheers would wash away any of the ruler’s freshly gathered thoughts. The ruler looked to the evening sky, and answered the abstracts.
