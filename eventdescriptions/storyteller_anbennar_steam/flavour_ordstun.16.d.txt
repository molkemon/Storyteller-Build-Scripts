"And you chose a city?" asks the Councilmember, sitting at a desk of Skomdihiri wood which stretches twice as wide as she is tall. "Tens of thousands live there. Just go dig in some goblin infested cavern; I'm sure they won't mind the excavation." The "gemgineer"- a distasteful term for "gem engineer"- winces from the other side of the desk. "We have to dig in a hold, Your Gleaming. We don't need to learn how to dig in caves; we need to learn how to excavate holds. Really, not even that many people live there, in comparison to any of the Serpentreach holds, and we have to experiment somewhere, or else we'll never learn how to weaponize the Aegis, and so you see-" 

"Do you even understand the Aegis? I get it, I get it: it's a crazy rock, it bends space, it can break earth. But do you really understand it, or is this just a wild experiment?" 

"I, well, you see, Your Gleaming, it all comes down to our understanding of manaspace. We've made incredible progress in the last few years, due to the experiments we made and the Chamber, and now we can-" 

"Stop talking. Good. Is this excavation entirely necessary? Is there nowhere else you can dig first, absolutely no expendable place you could test this?" 

"We already have, Your Gleaming. We need to test our methods on a hold eventually." 

"Then do it, get out of my sight, and never tell anyone that I gave you authorization."
