It began like a whisper, a mere change in the winds, but the signs were unmistakable; something disastrous was afoot. At first the river seemed only to slow, but then the languid current stilled entirely; and the unthinkable began. The mighty Kharunyana reversed. Its rushing currents flowed up, up, up the Godswall, into the peaks and the heavens beyond. The whisper grew in intensity to a roar, the unmistakable sound of raging water, as the ground began to shake. Unrestrained animals fled uphill, paintings fell from walls, and urns shattered in homes as an ominous, deep cracking could be heard from the greatest likeness of the High Gods anywhere in Haless, as if the mountains themselves were breaking.  Then all was calm; the shaking stopped and the loud roaring of churning water quieted. The river had become completely still, as if frozen in time, without flow. And then, The Godswall erupted, its tenthousand year visage shattering out into the air above the Shamakhad plain, water and stone crashing to the ground below in a cacophony of destruction. The thousand carved deities of the Godswall were swept away before the flood, and locals in Maruvad looked on in horror as a leviathan crested the waterfall descending on them from above; a vast blue catfish, glowing an ethereal light, rode along the top of the flood pouring out of the Godsbreach, propelling the torrent of devastation before them. The great spirit Kharunyana had returned to Rahen, and all would know he was here.
