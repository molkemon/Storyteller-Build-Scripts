The Count’s Rebellion surpassed the War of the Broken Blade in terms of casualties. But after the two main battles and years of pacification, the rebellion fizzled out. However, it is clear that we cannot allow such a rebellion again. I announced the Oldhaven Renewal Decree to address these concerns. All across the Oldhaven area, orks are settled in newly built houses and new farmlands are tilled. Little by little, the humans got used to the orks. New humanorc marriages are officiated and halforks become more common. As with a specific province, halforks from across Escann settled in Oldhaven, Annisoft, and Hagstow. Along with the Oldhaven Renewal Decree, I announced the Kondunn Heritage Edict. The clan of Clouded Eye was to be recognized as not only an ork tribe, but a successor to the Duchy of Kondunn. To bolster those claims, we took a page out of Brasur’s book and married human nobles to ork chiefs. While unpopular among them, these political marriages gave rise to a new halfork nobility. After several years, the Oldhaven area has become a new center of halfork culture. While no one bought our claim of heritage from Kondunn, it showed that we were open to accepting some human culture. But it was having an effect. A few humans and orks started to accept the identity of a Kondunner. The former were the descendants of Kondunners reclaiming their heritage after assimilating into Castellyrian culture while the latter adopted some Kondunner customs. Halforks have embraced the Kondunner identity more openly, seeing it as a way to legitimize themselves to humans across Escann.
