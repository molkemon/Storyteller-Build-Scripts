The miners of Gor Vazumbrog knew to avoid the lower shafts, where the Wound's breath tainted the air. But orders were orders, and the expansion couldn't wait. Ungrim's hammer struck true, splitting the last layer of stone. A rush of warm air spilled into the tunnel, carrying a foul, sulfurous stench. He gagged, pulling back. "What's that?" muttered Norbûn, his apprentice. Ungrim didn't answer. Beyond the breach, a cavern yawned open, its walls slick and glistening as if sweating. Dim, green light pulsed in its depths, casting unnatural shadows. "Careful," Bella warned, stepping forward. She held a lantern high, its flame flickering in strange, erratic patterns. The others crowded behind her, reluctant to look but unable to turn away. Something moved within the cavern—slow, deliberate. A faint, rhythmic scrape echoed, like claws dragging over stone. "Back to the lift," Bella ordered, voice tight. As the miners retreated, the tunnel groaned, the stone beneath their boots softening. Norbûn screamed as his leg sank into the floor, his flesh sizzling. Ungrim grabbed him, but the ground buckled, and both were swallowed whole. Bella stumbled into the lift with the last survivor, slamming the lever. As they ascended, she glanced down. The cavern glowed brighter, the scraping sound growing louder. When they reached the surface, she gasped for air, gripping the edge of the platform. "What did you see?" a foreman asked.
