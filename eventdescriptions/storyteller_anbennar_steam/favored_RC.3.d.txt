No man is an island, and among the gods this is doubly true. While Castellos rules the collection of deities that are bound together in the Regent Court, the king of the gods is far from allpowerful. After Castellos was maimed saving Adean from the dragon Malliath's maw, his time away from his throne has been minimal. Despite his lack of mobility, however, Castellos is still an effective and well-respected ruler, for he knows that only a fool fails to rely on the talents of those around him, and the deities of the Regent Court report to Castellos on the many tasks he has set forth for them. In this way, though Castellos may have only one hand, he can draw back the bow or raise the sword through Falah and Adean. Though he has but one leg, his will can reach as far as Munas can sail and Nathalyne can flit. It is this last thought that strikes you as you hold court today and look upon the petitioners and courtiers who have come before you. Diplomats, businessmen, officers; what a diversity of skills exists in this room, just a small sample of that to be found in the realm. They remind you of silver-tongued Minara, shrewd Ara, brave Adean; a host of great beings to govern the heavens, aiding Castellos in his just rule. No ruler can know every detail of a land or specialize in every topic in the world, and history is littered with examples of those who have micromanaged their realms into oblivion. No; Castellos is surely guiding you today, his gentle hand bringing together this collection of talented individuals. With the help of those in your court you will be well advised and ensure the just governance of your realm; just as the regent court, working together in harmony, capably administers the heavens.
