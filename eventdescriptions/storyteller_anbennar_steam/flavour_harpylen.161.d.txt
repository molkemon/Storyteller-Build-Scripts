One of the opulent villas in the vicinity of the palace has been repurposed from the residence of an idle courtier into a veritable beehive of sorcerous activity, bustling with dozens of senior Vetaunyar, comprising some of the most famed Sanarenni flocks of the land, as well as Kuresimi inscribers recruited from the glittering mountain halls of Lawassar, and the various acolytes, aides and master craftsmen in their retinue. All have gathered in the capital for one purpose and one purpose only: to turn the vision of the monarch into reality and devise a process to reliably craft magical instruments that can interact with and improve the song magic of the harpy mages. The name chosen for this project is Nilelista, or 'the world's memory', for that is the ultimate aim of all the efforts expended here; to find a way to make a lasting impact on the world with the ephemeral magic of song. At the center of this whirlwind of activity a massive inscribed device rests on an elevated platform. It is the 'tauhun anovin', the Dream Loom, whose sole purpose is to turn blocks of anniwan, the uniquely Bulwari blue magical metal alloy, into thin yet durable strings. These 'jinisapo', or heart strings, are intended to allow the instrument chosen for this project, a lyre, to store the magic needed to interact with the magic in the voice of a Vetaunyar. By using such special lyres, called rianelion, or 'eternal echo', the song magic of a Sanarenni chorus shall be amplified and enhanced far beyond normal means. That is, if this ambitious scheme will prove to be a success.
