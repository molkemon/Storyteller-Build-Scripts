Recently, a talented advisor has come to prominence at court and begun boasting about his accomplishments. This advisor claims to be the wisest in a variety of studies and hopes to use these skills to provide for his people. Although many citizens find him to be an attractive governor who promises change, others see him as nothing more than an arrogant man with little to prove his claims. Often, those who suffer from arrogance and misconstrue the knowledge of the world are forced into exile, fined heavily, or even given physical punishment. Despite their hubris, this advisor seems to show latent talent for certain tasks in the court. Maybe their ego can be turned towards something more useful?
