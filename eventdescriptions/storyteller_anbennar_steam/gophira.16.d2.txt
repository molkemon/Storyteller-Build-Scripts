Jahlor was beside himself with anger and anguish. And yet he was marching through his village, away from his home, away from his two precious jewels, who will be six years old next week, from his wife and his newborn son. Marching to go and fight against people like him, that he had no quarrel with, for people that only ever hurt him and his loved ones. The warlords always had recruited in his village and other nearby ones, but, at least according to his father, there used to be unwritten rules to it. Try and have enough people home in time for the harvest, exempt fathers of young kids, the last son of two old parents, families with already too many soldiers in the army. Times have changed; now the recruiters will grab everybody. He shouted, pleaded, and shoved the men out of his meager house, but here he was, marching. He'd want nothing more than to rebel to this injustice, but despite all his rage, he knew he was powerless, like a caged rat. Marching.
