"Ren, is that."  "Yes, sweetling. I believe it is."  The corpse in the centre of the clearing was almost white enough to blend with the snow. Had one of Mitrenya's crimson spears not pierced through its chest, I would have forgiven somebody arriving on the scene for looking past the body entirely.  The thing was littered in tiny scars; lines of grey against its leathery, pale skin. Around it, blood pooled onto the snow in a nearperfect circle.  I knew. I'm not sure how, but I knew which of those things it was, even before my gaze had reached the broken antler on its mask. I gripped Mitrenya's hand tight, and I felt her squeeze back through our gloves. She took a deep, shuddering breath.  "I think this one will do nicely, yes?" Her voice didn't shake. I'm not sure if I expected it to.
