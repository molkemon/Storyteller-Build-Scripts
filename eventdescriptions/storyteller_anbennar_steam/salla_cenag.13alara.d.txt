My toes dug into what the locals called sand. I liked it. It was smooth and soft and gentle, though it did get everywhere. The crashing of waves filled my ears, as did the chirping and cawing of white feathered birds, seagulls. The smell of seabreeze was salt and fresh. I couldn’t help but smile, yet at the same time I could feel an unfamiliar quivering in my lips. This place, this beach, was like nothing I had experienced. The shores of the Lake Grove could not hope to compare. And the sea? My eyes began to wet as I tried to swallow. I knew this is where it all began. Upon this sea the forebears of my clan, of all the clans, sailed to and landed on a beach like this. Our people were tied to this place and I couldn’t even use my own tongue to describe these places. A few tears escaped me as I choked a sob. I turned back toward the human village, wiping my face off. It was because of these humans that I could find a word for this place. For all of these things I couldn’t even describe. I also knew this was their home. I knew that this land was the Ourdi’s. And I knew I didn’t want to leave. I didn’t want to lose what my clan had just found again. I returned my gaze to the sea.
