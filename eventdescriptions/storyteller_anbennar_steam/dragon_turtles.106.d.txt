Why have you arranged your systems of territorial governance so foolishly? I went to a governor and I told him that by all historical precedent it would make more sense if his territory extended outwards to the lands across his island. He agreed, so we went to your office of subnational division and we issued a complaint based on economic, historical, and sociocultural factors. The other governor was cross, so he issued a countercomplaint from the precedent emerging from Consensus Agreement Five Five Eight Four, which isn’t even applicable in this case because the coconut groves in question are more than a quarter of a day’s walk from the boundary line. I advised him on the countercomplaint, but it appears he’s made somewhat of a mess of it. Your bureaucrats didn’t know what to make of anything; they’re reviewing all of the documents now. I told them about how this exact situation was resolved during a similar territorial conflict in nine hundred twelve and they just brushed me off. It’s like they don’t even care about imperial procedure standardization. I delivered it to them in writing to make sure things were running smoothly, but they appear to have gotten the countercomplaint mixed up with the initial complaint, so they said it’s going to take them a while to review the documents I sent them in addition to the first documents, but they can’t do that properly either. So then I told them about the forty six-step complaint review system created by Raka the Detailed in nine hundred thirty two in direct response to the Internal Dispute of nine hundred twelve and they didn’t care about that either. They brushed me off. So I delivered more documents to them, and now they’re going to review those. They say it’s all going to take a very long time to resolve. The empire of Satrialaam never would have managed their territories like this. I hope you’re happy with yourselves.
