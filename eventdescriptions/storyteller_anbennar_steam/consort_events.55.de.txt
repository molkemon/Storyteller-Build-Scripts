The consort has been pacing the palace lately, their head hung low, only stopping sometimes to longingly gaze out the window. Life is quite different here, compared to their old country of origin. Most of all they miss their family, and the safety of familiar custom. They were a good child to their parents, they decide, thinking back to their childhood. But how does one know whether one is a good spouse? And what of in the future, a good parent? They shake their head, acutely reminded of the fact that the royal couple has yet to produce an heir. Having spent yet another quiet meal in their company it is obvious even to the monarch that their spouse is a bit overwhelmed, either with boredom or restlessness, or maybe by something more melancholic in nature.
