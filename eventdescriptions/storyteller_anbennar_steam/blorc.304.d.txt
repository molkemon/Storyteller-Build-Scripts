The heir has disappeared. They fled in the middle of the night alongside two dozen other orks, most of them the sons and daughters of renowned warriors, their trail masked well enough that no trackers could chase after them. Despite their caution, there are suspicions as to where they went. Where else would young orks, eager to prove themselves, go than to the middle of a brutal war? Those suspicions are confirmed after little more than a month. When the body is returned, it is covered in a spider silk sheet, hiding the horrible wounds that mark the corpse underneath it. The heir is dead, as are many of the others that escaped together in the middle of the night, foolishly and eagerly seeking to wage war and to prove themselves. The few among the group who returned alive are celebrated. They are named true warriors, welcomed by their kin, and thought to have been blessed by Dookan. But the heir is dead, as are many others. Though a few mourn, the priests who have come to perform the last rites consider them to all to have had a virtuous death. Honorable deaths, they insist. Dookan will welcome these warriors into his embrace, or so they claim. An empty comfort, in face of a tragedy masked as a glorious honor.
