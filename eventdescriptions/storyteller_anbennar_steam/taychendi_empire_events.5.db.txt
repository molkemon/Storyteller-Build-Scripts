The communion was over in less than a minute. In a heartbeat, everything suddenly stopped – the Threadbearer released her hand from the Fateweaver’s corpse, and the raw, rushing power of magic ceased, motes and wisps all that remained. After taking a moment to collect herself, she turned and descended the steppes silently, her face an unreadable mask. “So?” the monarch asked tentatively. “What did you learn?” Was the ruler's empire to fall? Would the ruler need to sacrifice their firstborn son? Would the ruler be unkillable until they turned sixty-one years old? Or would the ruler see unparalleled success? Glory and riches unrivalled? The Threadbearer paused for a moment, her eyes moving to the sword at Denariatam’s hip. “Your armies will be uncountable, their advance unstoppable. Your empire will march to the ends of this world, conquer lands not yet seen by Elvenkind… but you will also fight enemies closer to home.” She looked up into the ruler's eyes. “Enemies await abroad – and daggers, of fanged teeth and shadowshrouded steel, await you at home… waiting to strike, and waiting to take what they think is theirs.” The Threadbearer gave a little bow, her prophecy relayed, then began to walk back to the chamber. “Conquest, and steel, and daggers in the dark…” the monarch said to them, as the ruler looked back to the great statue behind them. After pondering for a moment, the ruler smiled. “Yes… I think I can work with that.”
