We invested in Bal Mire to make it a strong bulwark against any foe, and we have succeeded in that. But our success has had unanticipated side effects, and though positive in nature, they have caused a dilemma only the monarch can resolve. We sought to build strong forts. But that required well-maintained farmland to ensure well-stocked granaries, but of course that required a significant logging industry, and then that revealed untapped potentials in fur hunting. Already, mercantile interests are vying for control in the region, and Bal Mire's former inhabitants are eager for the chance to claim some of this new prosperity for their own. We intended this land as a strong shield firmly in our grip, and it can remain so. But so too is there an opportunity to show the locals just how good Silmuna rule can be. What does the monarch say?
