It was truly an aweinspiring sight not seen since the reign of Jexis when the arkimaguš of over a dozen magi clans and their entourage congregated in the capital, joining the nobles and clergy for the grand council that shall determine the future of magic within our realm and, in due time, in all of Bulwar. Accommodations for the delegations have been found on palace grounds and a series of tent halls have been erected within the secluded palace gardens to provide sufficient space for the pending debates. One delegation amongst the many that have arrived has caused a bit of a stir, however. The ZarLereškal magi clan has a rather controversial reputation, with many decrying them and their doctrines as little better than those of the conspiratorial xenophobic human magi who attempted to murder Jexis centuries ago, and some even insinuating that they belong to the heretical Cult of the Serpent. A not insignificant part of the controversy centers on the rather blatant attempts of their arkimaguš Ahmahan to insinuate himself into the good graces of the monarch by offering his services. His austere presence, with piercing eyes sunken deep in his bald almost cadaverous head, clad in dark silken robes and braced upon a staff of gnarled acacia wood decorated with serpentine silver inlays, has cast an undeniable shadow upon the proceedings. Many amongst the high-ranking kašra of the clerical delegation and even several other attending arkimaguš have already implored the monarch to banish the ZarLereškal from the grand council, heedless of the potentially far-reaching consequences of such an exclusionary act at this critical juncture.
