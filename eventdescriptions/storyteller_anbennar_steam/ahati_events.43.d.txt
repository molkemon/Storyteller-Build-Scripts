Under our republic, the Stewardship functions a little differently than it did in the past, and this affects every institution, including the Ahati. What this means in practice, however, is causing some debate. There are ministers who argue that in the same way our ruling council delegates power away from an absolute monarch, so too the Ahati leadership must follow a decentralized model, delegating more power to the local chapters. Others, however, point out that the Brotherhood is not a governing body and their strength as an organization comes from their centralized authority and rigid hierarchy, that we have room for both models in the Republic. The decision we make will most assuredly influence how the Ahati organize themselves militarily as well as how the cost of raising new regiments is distributed. The debate in the People's Council was lively, but in the end there was a clear majority vote, deciding that.
