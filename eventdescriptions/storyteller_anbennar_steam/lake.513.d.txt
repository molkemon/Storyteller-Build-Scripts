At first, it must have felt like a routine day for the vahjevgiivan official at their port. People bribed them or paid the abhorrent official tolls. Until suddenly, sharp at noon, the people stopped. When the first workers ignored them, they called for the ship owners. When the ship owners ignored them, they called for the guards. When the city guard amicably but assuredly recommended them to let other people go about their honest work, they threatened the monarch with the might of their army. They finally left, when the monarch gave their reply.
