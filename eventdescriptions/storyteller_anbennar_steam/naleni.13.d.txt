The mountainside hall is quiet, save for the steady rap of fingers on stone. The queen sits atop a newly hewn marble throne, carried to this height by a whole team of harpies, her fingers tapping rhythmically on the arm. The walls are lined with Sirens, identifiable through the bright blue crest adorning their armour, the spread wings of the Nephiuéda. On the stone floor beneath the dias, bound in irons and forced into supplication, kneel the two ringleaders of the mutineers. Traitorous Odanchésta who first incited the revolt, the ones who plotted, the ones who butchered. Above the throne Muréda’s stone gaze bores down upon the destroyers of her peace. The hall awaits the queen's sentence, but Ríva is unsure. These two killed her family. The blood might as well still be on their hands, perhaps it would be, had they not all burned. As a daughter, she sees only one option. punishment. But the queen's eye differs from the daughters. Traitors they may be, but undeniably useful. Some of the finest commanders she’d ever seen in fact, an asset to her new state; an asset to her ambition. Maybe then, another option. pardon. Though conditional, of course. The hall's quiet continues, save for that steady rap of fingers on stone. What shall her choice be?
