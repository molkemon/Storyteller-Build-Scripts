It is time.  It is an ancient tradition, this pilgrimage. A practice that stretches back generations in the history of our people. Tswohvwoh'ii, our mistress, is both alluring and ominous, and her domain a place of unfathomable mysteries and undiscovered boons. We will march together, across these lands so firmly in her grasp, as our pilgrimage is not a choice, but an obligation, a duty that binds us to the land and the stories that have been whispered through time.  Preparation for our pilgrimage is a solemn and intricate affair. Our people come together, and for days, we gather the necessary goods that will accompany us into the embrace of Tswohvwoh'ii. We chip away at black glass to make the sharpest blades, we weave together latticeworks of bone to protect us, we select the finest woven garments, sturdy tools, and ritualistic artifacts that have been passed down through generations. Each item bears the weight of tradition and the hope of safe passage through the hallowed grounds where Her song holds sway. As we stand before our possessions, we utter prayers of protection, invoking whatever benignity She may still harbor towards those who seek salvation. The incantations are recited in hushed tones, carrying with them the collective hopes and fears of our people. In this profound moment, our will is made manifest, our spirits aligned to see this trek through.  Tswohvwoh'ii, we honor your name. Tswohvwoh'ii, we fear your wrath. Tswohvwoh'ii, we begin this journey.
