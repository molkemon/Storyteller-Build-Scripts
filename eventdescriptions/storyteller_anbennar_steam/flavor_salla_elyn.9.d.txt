Dearest Arfil and Bricena, I know that you are both occupied with more important matters than a letter from me; ruling a realm is undoubtedly taxing upon the soul. Yet, as I too am to take said duty upon myself in the near future, I have few others to turn to that may lend me insight upon how I might act. The recent meeting of the Conclave in Elynáile has left me greatly dismayed, even frustrated. I was not able to attend the Conclave's meeting, having been barred entry, yet the irritation at their decision boils within me like a raging, treetoppling tempest. I cannot fathom why the Conclave has ordained to forsake the groves of the east, nor why they would refuse to respond to the invaders in full force. I know not what to do, nor can I shake the feeling that I have been intentionally kept from partaking in matters of state, as if I were not yet ready to handle such ordeals. At the very least, the Conclave has decided to act in a limited capacity, instead of merely sitting by idly. Nonetheless, I must burden you with the question. What would you do in my position? Would you simply abide being ignored and pushed aside? Would you not try to rally to provide support for our eastern kin? I am wholeheartedly at a loss; your opinions and guidance would mean a lot to me, and undoubtedly assist me in handling this dilemma. I wish you both the greatest of luck and wellbeing, and await your response eagerly.
