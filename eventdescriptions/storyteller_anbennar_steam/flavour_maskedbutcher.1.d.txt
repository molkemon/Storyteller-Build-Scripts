Hunger. Hunger. Always hunger. Darkness my skin, my breath. Time forgotten. Buried under stone. No light. No sun. Safe. Safe to wait. Safe to feed. The altar sings. Whispers curl through rock. It calls, but no touch. Not yet. Not strong enough. Movement? Footsteps? Heavy. Strange intruders. Black shapes. Smell of iron. They bleed. Oh, they bleed. I wait. I wait. I wait. Thirst screams louder than silence. They enter. Unwelcome. Meat. Blood. Thirst claws at mind. Do they see me? I see them. Fear tastes sweet. Leap. Teeth. Claw. Rip. Rip and tear and bite. Skin bursts. Warm and rich and it fills me with power and strength and they scream. I drink. One falls. Another. Another. Eyes change. Glow red, like mine. They change. The hunger twists them. Not just mine now. They feed me. They will feed me. They will. Too many. Too strong. Clubs crack my bones. Blades cut deep. I drink. I drink. But the pain. The altar sings. The altar sings.
