The Fasiri peoples have long been a disjoined community of tribes, unable to find peace. Only in our greatest peaks of power have we seen a tribe unite us all, shortly followed by a steep decline. What we show here is something more than just conquest: its unification, and along with it, stability. As our ruler descends down Rigoti, they feel an overwhelming sense of righteousness, fighting for the cause of their people. As they address the myriads of soldiers they themselves have assembled, they raise their hands to the sky, and shouts.
