The recent war took a heavy toll on the people. Many were called, few returned. The families of nobles and commoners alike toiled as they waited for soldiers to return home, but the war ended and they never did. Now the toiling continues, many being so preoccupied with the work to make up for the lost hands that they barely have time to grieve. Of the ones who have time to think, many are increasingly thinking that the nation has been throwing away lives too carelessly.
