What I learned about the Kuzwazim, those who built the fortress we sought out, was that they were the dregs, the abandoned, left by a Dameria in terminal decline to man a great fastness by the sea, Uelkiltrum. After the Day of Ashen Skies, they rebuilt the place themselves and took to the ocean, plundering the East Diven under one and twenty masters before eventually proving too much of a nuisance to the Empress Jexis. Then they were dispersed among the locals, obsolete and irrelevant. I found it all terribly relatable. Emondo’s fiancée Clara was a wonderful woman, keeneyed and interesting, and she had plenty of ideas of how we could make our way deep into the waterlogged ruins of Uelkiltrum. They fit together well. One could imagine them as hosts, as the gregarious petty nobility they were meant to be, charismatic and enviable. There was no place in their life for a strange, reedy little Eborthíli with a talent for translation. In short, I was sulking. At the very least, we made good progress in the upper levels. I have learned that I focus well when I am morose, and Emondo and Clara were making quick work too, their garrulous conversation carrying through the halls. It didn’t help that we were a lean team, accompanied only by a small cadre of expeditionaries; I was confronted with them at all times, and found solace only in the treasures that even these uppermost floors seemed to promise. Pervading everything was a strange sound, a distant keening like the cawing of a bird; yet we all understood, out of some instinct, that it was not quite natural.
