There has been a recent boom in trade, at least as measured by the volume of goods coming into and out of the markets. That being said, it does not appear to have been accompanied by a surplus in the nation’s treasury, especially when one accounts for all the fees necessary to maintain the current excellent state of affairs. After the docking fee, unloading fee, customs fee, import fee, reloading fee, transport fee, transport arrangement fee, and fee tabulation fee imposed by the bureaucrats of the nation’s harbors and markets, it sometimes seems as though the merchants who deal with the officials are barely breaking even. We have been assured that all these fees are completely necessary to effectively oversee the influx of goods, but this seems to be contradicted somewhat by the ever-increasing quality of the finery the bureaucrats are wearing.
