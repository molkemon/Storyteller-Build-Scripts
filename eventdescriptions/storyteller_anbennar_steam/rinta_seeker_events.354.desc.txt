A small group of Adeen have withdrawn to their gathering, outright refusing to recognize any sovereignty or outside authority over their own. Such a practice is generally reserved for protests in grave matters of disrespect, such as an owner’s murder of one of their group. In this case however, they claim to be responding to transgressions committed against the entirety of the Adeen, and the persistent lack of autonomy in spite of their loyal service. They demand a recognition of their justified demands and a status appropriate for their influence. We can try to ignore this group, hoping their act stays isolated, or nip it in the bud before their action spreads.
