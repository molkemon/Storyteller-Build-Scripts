"You who are named Welyam, magnate of Gaweton. You strike at me, but you are not known to me. What have I done to you, for you to hate me so?" And Welyam bared his yellow teeth, and he did say, "Nothing have you done, but take my hall and make it yours. For I remember when this city was the heart of a great realm, in which I was a man of substance. You and your mothers unwound this realm, and in war did you take my sons and my daughters, who stood against you. For this have I grown to hate you, and I would see this world die, but to see your realm brought low before mine. For I am old, and my own time nearly spent. What use to me is a future in which I have no part?" Then did Welyam, with a strength unlike his age, leap and throw himself from the window, not unlike a wingmaiden taking flight. But Welyam was no wingmaiden, and the stones of Gaweton rose to strike him, and he was dead. The ruler said nothing, and kept their own council, but their wingmaidens did say hence, that a shadow fell over the ruler's heart this day.
