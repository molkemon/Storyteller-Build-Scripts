Bosk is cruel, and even in defeat remains unaccepting of his circumstances, lashing out at his wardens. He is still a prisoner, but even as hated as he is, he is still treated as the leader of a clan. Orks followed him for a reason, and that much is to be respected even when nothing else about him deserves such grace. The assembly of the elders is called, and the dishonorable curr is brought forth to be judged. His muscles have atrophied of his own volition by refusing to eat the food, and he remains in chains, for a warrior’s death would little become him if he were to attempt to attack those present. The outcome is not in doubt, and neither are his crimes. Yet Bosk refuses to recognize the principles of the judgment, objecting every moment he can to the proceedings, and in the end there is only the finality to be realized with his execution. Gordak grasps his claymore and makes easy the last moments. It is more than Bosk deserves, and yet he retains some dignity in refusing to beg even at this final hour. "I see there is some true ork left in that head somewhere, buried under all that rot." The blade is raised, and then falls.
