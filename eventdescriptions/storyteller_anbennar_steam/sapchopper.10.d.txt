Khagor Sapchopper was undoubtedly the most powerful ork to ever live in the Deepwoods. His clan claimed vast swathes of the forest. His greatest rivals were either dead or scattered. His position as chieftain was unquestioned. And for all of that, it meant nothing. He sat slumped on his throne, disheveled and exhausted. It was the toll that night after night of sordid dreams took on the war chief. They were always the same, a consuming fire swallowing everything he had built in a crimson inferno, a thousand wretched figures watching as nothing but bitter ash claimed him and his clan, and then a hollow hatred seeping into his heart. “I’ve an idea or two on this.” The chieftain stared at the mercenary before him. His council had scoured far and wide for answers to his curse. The closest they had come was Ruuk Elmscourge, the leader of a strange group of mercenaries. “An idea?” Khagor replied incredulously. “I expect results.” “And for the right price, you’ll get them.” The sellsword grunted back, unfazed. For a moment, Khagor felt his old energy return, as fury rose within him in the face of such indolence. Then, he gave a tired laugh. “Finally, an ork with spine.” The chieftain exclaimed. “Fine, you will have your gold. And I will have my answers… or your head.” Ruuk met his threat with a smile, “Good. I’ll get the boys moving.” He turned to leave, entirely unconcerned with his audience, before pausing. Meeting Khagor’s tired eyes once more, the sellsword gave one last word, “Do not trust Clan Silentblade.” Then he was gone.
