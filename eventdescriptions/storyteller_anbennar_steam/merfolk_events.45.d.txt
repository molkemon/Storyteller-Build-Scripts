Scatter, scurvy dogs; skittering critters batter our bilges. The lookout’s alert sent sailors, merchants, and commoners running for cover. From fingerlong shrimp to fifteen-foot tall lobsters, a crustacean tidal wave enveloped the entire port town; indeed, the domain was overrun so completely, our local guard struggled to even leave their barracks. Enter the merfolk. Swimming at the crustaceans’ heels with nets and tridents, the merfolk wrangled the attacking arthropods back into the sea. Even the stragglers scampering deep inland met their match against magical bubble spells conjured by aquamancers. When the commotion receded, the port was reduced to a complete wreck, our people catching a final flash of merfolk tails retreating into the sea. We can only assume the merfolk accidentally drove their herds onto our lands, yet we haven’t received an apology for this crabtastrophe—not even a shrimple excuse. How should the ruler respond to this crazy crustacean carnage?
