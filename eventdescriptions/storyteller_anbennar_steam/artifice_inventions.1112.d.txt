A more precise term for the seismic armour would be energy absorbance and explosive release armour, but that has proven to be quite a mouthful. Using transmutative and evocative magic, the armour is capable of converting and storing energy, such as the atmospheric friction encountered during air flight or the kinetic energy from a weapon blow, and, when triggered, releasing all that energy in a sudden explosive shock wave. The favourite maneuver of harpy warriors equipped with seismic armour is therefore unsurprisingly a drop out of great heights in the middle of an enemy formation, with the impact lending additional force to the shock wave. Such a wave is generally strong enough to propel any unfortunate nearby enemy soldiers several meters into the air, inflicting serious injury and confusion within enemy ranks. This ability has given rise to the colloquial term ‘superseismic landing’ used either with reverence, gentle mockery or dread, depending on what side of the conflict one happens to stand.
