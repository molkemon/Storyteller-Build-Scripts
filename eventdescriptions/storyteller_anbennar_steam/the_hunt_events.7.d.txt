The young harpy lands eagerly on the balcony of the parlour, where the heir is already holding court. Their return has been anticipated for weeks; harpy news always flies far ahead of its arrival. The heir has undergone a great trial, a traditional test of their mettle, and returned knowing themselves just as a matriarch is expected to. Even hidden behind a pillar, the young harpy can see the change. Where once the heir was unsure of themself, now they have learned to handle such an occasion in their own, distinct manner.
