The room of the keeper was sparse. It had to be, to fit all the sheer number of kipu in it. In the middle of all of them was the keeper himself, who was running his hands over the kipu and mouthing names at an imperceptible speed. Five men with important badges stepped into the room. The keeper looked up; he was no longer alone. "Do you need to be remembered?" he asked. "What are your names?" "I'm afraid we need something else from you," their leader said. He gestured around at the kipu. "The rooms of the other keepers are not nearly as full, and that is because their memories are not the equal of yours. We have a task for a mind as sharp as a sword, and it is only you that can fulfill it." The keeper sat and thought for a while. "May I bring my kipu with me? Surely I will have time to myself wherever it is that I am being sent. Surely," his voice began to crack. "Surely there will be time to remember." "Others will remember for you, and you will keep what you have already read in your mind." The leader gestured to the keeper again. "It's time to go." So with a last look back at his names, the keeper went.
