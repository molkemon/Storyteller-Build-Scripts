Brann Bronzebeard and Urist Wildhammer stare in awe at the wreck before them. Both of their diving teams had been sent down to investigate this location after a fishing boat reported pulling up strange pieces of wood and metal in its nets, and they'd searched around for days before finally finding the source of the debris. The aged veteran and the eager youngster enjoyed working together, but they'd yet to find anything truly worthwhile while diving alongside each other. At least, not until now. Urist turns to his companion, signing to him in the diver slang that had developed over the past few years of their work. He makes the signs for 'big' and 'find', but also the two-handed gate representing the ancient hold. Brann raises an eyebrow in response, then catches himself as he realizes Urist can't possibly see his face clearly through the thick glass of their helmets. He makes his own signs, fumbling slightly with the new communication method he'd been slow to adopt, and points emphatically to the symbol wrought into the side of the ship's hull. On the flank of the sunken battleship, the Copper Gate stands resolute. Their return to the ship is not accompanied with the usual fanfare and celebration that surround new discoveries. What they've uncovered today is more than just a relic of the past; it's a fragment of their own hold's missing history. This is not one of their warships, lost to some forgotten conflict, but a vessel belonging to the original ancient hold, the hold that fell over seven thousand years ago. The exact details of the ancient hold's demise are not yet revealed to them, but judging by the gaping wounds still visible in the sunken ship's hull, they didn't go down without a fight.
