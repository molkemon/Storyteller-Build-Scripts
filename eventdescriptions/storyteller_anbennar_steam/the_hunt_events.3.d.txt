The matriarch is resplendent. Every visible feather on their body has been dipped in purple dye, then the tip dipped again in gold. As their wings spread, they are a deep sunset falling behind the mountains; a profusion of tulle and layered silk froths out like a cloud, so that it is difficult to say where their frame truly begins. The matriarch makes their stately way down ancient stone steps, moving apparently without effort. When they reach the bowl at the centre of the platform, they leave a single coin. The coin is mere ritual, of course, for in the next few days, wagons of lucre will arrive at the roost, the matriarch’s value to the flock expressed in the most direct of manners. The tinkle of coin is no mere preening; the speaker at the podium explains that the matriarch’s donation will fund a new library, a crèche, and a bathing complex.
