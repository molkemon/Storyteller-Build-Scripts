High up in the foothills of the Pakreda Sedara mountains, a majestic waterfall tumbles down a sheer cliff, the mist created from its spray birthing countless rainbows during the day. At the edge of the cliff, right next to the roaring waters, stands the House of the Golden Sun, a massive monastic complex whose central dome is crowned by a massive sun disk coated in pure gold, reflecting the light of the sun with blinding brilliance during the day and being illuminated by sacred fires during the night, a guiding light for the devout pilgrims and a bright reminder for the people of Bamaliwuni of the might and glory of Surael. In the seclusion of the House of the Golden Sun, the BetUrašur or Umba Daabajuqa, those who feel the calling of Surael have joined together in the largest congregation of the Servants of Surael. The gold mined in the surrounding hills is shaped by the patient hands of the devout into artifacts of exquisite craftsmanship. Items like an engraved sun disk medallion crafted in the Umba Daabajuqa are considered to be imbued with a potent blessing of Surael and used by the local faithful to ward against the influence of the Malevolent Dark.
