Walking over concentric circles of barricades and battlements, Matilda Silverlocks surveys the damage. The king may have fled, gone as soon as he realized he was losing, speeding off towards the Ynn even as his followers assured him of victory, but the Circle now has no one to support them, no government to speak of, and not a single connection to the outside world. Half the adventuring companies in the capital lie dead in the street, whether they sided with or against the peasants; every merchant in the country, disgusted with the bloodshed, has already left for the crown's domain. All the Circle has is each other. To a stalwart band of radicals, bruised like overripe fruit, Silverlocks attempts to salvage the situation. "This is an opportunity," she says, as unsure of herself as the crowd is. "We were bound by them, before. Bound to a way of living we didn't want. We came here—look, why did any of you follow me in the first place? It wasn't for wealth or riches, was it? No, of course not. If it was, you'd be with the idiot king, not with us. You came here—we all came here—because of Begga. Because we were surrounded by riches that were hoarded by the people who already had all the fortune. We couldn't give to one another, we couldn't support one another, because none of us had anything to give in the first place. And even when we got here, we were obliged on all sides. we had to send tribute, to pay tariffs, to follow people who didn't understand. But now?" She looks around at the village rendered into rubble. "Now, we've only got each other. Maybe here, in Begga's town, we can follow her like we've never been able to before."
