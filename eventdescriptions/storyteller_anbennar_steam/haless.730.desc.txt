The annual Little New Year has arrived once more in the coastal towns along the route of the Treasure Fleet. Originally a fisherfolk's ritual of offerings to the sea for calm waters—marked by lanterns, libations, and incense—this festival has since evolved into the most bustling, traderich celebration on the eastern coast. As the day approaches, fishpatterned lanterns are strung across streets, red silk knots tied to prows. Openair stages are built; salty sea ballads ring out as street performers entertain crowds. At alley corners, fishers hold the Shell Banquet with saltboiled seafood and rice brew to welcome guests. Traveling merchants set up stalls, selling foreign textiles and spices. Teahouses and cloth halls open so-called Dressing Booths for curious shoppers to don imitation foreign fashions—each stranger and more vibrant than the last. This year, the festival coincides with the docking of a grand Northern Ocean fleet. Traders, artisans, and diplomats from many nations arrive, and the celebration reaches new heights. Flags flap across crowded streets, voices mingle in a dozen tongues, and townsfolk from all around gather to revel together.
