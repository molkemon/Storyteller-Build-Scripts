"Hello." A familiar voice calls for the ruler. "I have made eggplant and sausages." A curl forms on the ruler’s lips, hearing of their favourite breakfast. The door gently creaks open as the ruler gets up from their bed and grabs a morning dress, just in time to greet their friend. The abundant sunlight beckons them through the castle's windows as they walk down the hallway, guards and courtiers along the way all recognize the person by the ruler’s side and greet them warmly when they don't ask to join them outright. After a lovely breakfast by the eastsidefacing terraced garden, where many of the Teal Keep's residents are allowed to come and eat the gardener's fruits, the two resume their morning walk, going up to the parapets, on which grow some fresh vines whose leaves bear a pleasant rainy fragrance. "I see the walkways are becoming more verdant than ever." The ruler comments, gazing at the variety of potted plants hanging from the city's many walkways, in a colourful array not even the hair strands of all Sarda nobility could match now. "Only thanks to the quality soil you've bought." The halfling replies, beaming for another day of working. "Hello." A commoner with her daughter call out from their window, their home also decorated by vineplants that complement their white walls with green stems and purple flowers. It was as if he had been a part of this city, of this royal court, for years now. A short, very modest man, he was dear to all and known as 'the gardener'. Or 'the ruler’s best friend'. Some would even say, ‘their sidekick’.
