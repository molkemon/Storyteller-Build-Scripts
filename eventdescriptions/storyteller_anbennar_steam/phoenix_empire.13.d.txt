As the first gentle rays of the rising sun bathe the verdant grasslands of the Gol savanna in a soft amber glow, a caravan of gnollish traders breaks up camp and continues its journey towards the ports along the shore of the Gulf of Rahen. Where their ancestors once traded in slaves, they now carry with them samples of exotic spices and fruits grown along the fertile river lands to the west, ready to exchange for the silks and porcelain of Rahen. As the sun continues its journey, the fishermen on the glittering waters of lake Nirakhet stop in their labor for a short prayer to Surael, who has once again risen to chase the darkness of night away. On the river banks of the mighty Sun's Sorrow, called Mother's Sorrow before the Light of Surael touched the inhabitants of these lands, ancient cities and majestic temples lie. In their venerable halls the incandescent glory of the sun is now celebrated. Leaving the green band of the river behind, the sun paints the endless dunes of the Salahad desert in a riot of colours. pristine white to golden yellow to the reddish ochre of old spilled blood. The nomadic inhabitants of this unforgiving landscape have always known that the sun rules supreme in these lands, but now they have finally been given the knowledge of Surael’s Truth. Farther along the sun's path looms the imposing Akasik mountain range, its valleys and promontories dotted with newly erected monasteries demonstrating the devotion of the recently converted faithful in these lands. And finally, at the end of its long journey, the sun sinks into the vast western ocean, only to begin its journey anew the next day. Praised be the Phoenix Empire and its monarch, for they have delivered all these peoples from the grasp of the Malevolent Dark and unto the Light, tending to the farthest reaches of the Salahad that have now become another sacred part of Surael's Garden.
