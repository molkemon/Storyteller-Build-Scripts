Ever since the conquests of Jaher, the Bulwari have forgotten the true dangers of gnolls, trusting foolishly in the protection of their elven overlords. Gnolls are dangerous, to be sure, they think to themselves, but they are just disorganized slavers. No true threat. Even conquests in distant lands did not inspire real fear; they were easily ignored by the people of Bulwar. The fall of Traz Buranun, however, could not be ignored. With its conquest, an ugly gnollish blight spears its way through Bulwar Proper, dividing the successor states of Jaher and seriously straining communication. For days after the city’s fall, its ultimate fate was unclear to the elven realms of Bulwar, but as refugees flood to every safe haven they tell their story; an entire city, reduced to ash. Survivors, clapped in chains, except for the lucky few who were spared, bar some minor mutilation, to spread word of what has transpired. These refugees, marked with the brand of their lord, huddle together in camps across the realm, shuddering in their sleep at the memory of their fair city burning with demonic flame. To the rest of Bulwar, the meaning of these events is clear; the gnolls are no mere slavers, they are the embodiment of the Malevolent Dark, demons who threaten to consume light itself.
