One of our privateers has captured a ship containing what its owners considered cargo, but what we consider people: slaves. Our country has outlawed the abhorrent practice of slavery, but other nations are not so enlightened. The slaves have gained their freedom, but there is still some discussion about what to do with those who transported them. Showing lenience and treating them as traders, although operating under a different flag and legislation, would be the diplomatic option. However, our laws and moral sensibilities suggest that slavers should always be viewed as pirates and kidnappers and dealt with accordingly.
