As you know, I am not above calling in a favor or trying to fish for help when I required it, which was sorely needed here. Kiusz, for once, agreed with me instead of being an obstinate person, which I could just about kiss him for. So we went to the Kukatodic peoples and asked around, trying to figure out the best way to get some of the prize pachyderm for ourselves. Ideally alive, so we could show them off at the Menagerie. They told us of some nearby giantkin, which to me sounded like more trouble than it was worth. But apparently these guys, the Wylderwood Firbolgs, were nice enough neighbors and lived in harmony with nature. Their druids’ power could help us tame the beasts. Anyway, we went to them; three meter high blueskinned, elflike figures. I tried my best not to need a change of pants, and we bartered some of Bivuzena’s famed blue roses; given that they were sourced from a fey portal somewhere else, I think they reminded them of the fey they were close to. They eventually agreed to help us ensnare two beasts, and offered to trade more interesting and dangerous local megafauna if we came back with better trades. Kiusz kindly let them know that anything more dangerous would probably scare the children, and we went on our way with two big wooly beasts in tow. Led the creatures back through the Serpent’s Pass to Bivuzena, Azaly constructed a soundproof barrier for the enclosure, and voila. Big, sixlegged wooly animals were now friendly exhibits. Everyone goes home happy.
