Every society is, by necessity and at a minimum, a desperate three hundred thirty-third go at things. Layaktas It was the lizardfolk, Tzatak, who finally brought it all together. They knew the holohana was seeking out magic. They had pieced it together after the third artificery workshop had gone up in flames, the victim of the holohana’s terrible rage. At first, the great nightmare had seemed simply to target cities, aiming for major population centers, but its destruction was eerily focused, for all that its mere footsteps across the earth left devastation in their wake. Could it feel the magic that had imprisoned it? Was this retributive rage? The truth was that neither observation nor direct assault had done anything to slow the holohana’s advance. Wyvernbombing had barely left a scratch on its decaying hide, while it was impossible to get enough mages together to recreate any of the initial magic which had snared the thing. Back in Insyaa, they had enjoyed the element of surprise and a clear plan. Here, they were responding on the fly. Acanto, for his part, had been vomiting off the side of the wyvern almost from the moment he had mounted one. Tzatak had endured this with mute disdain, not even complaining at Acanto’s near constant vice grip on his waist; besides, he had much more to worry about, for the holohana was infuriated by the wyverns, especially the ones sporting mages. “Can’t get a good angle,” Tzatak seethed; “It keeps turning towards us.” And Acanto suddenly knew. “Do you know the lizard pillar in Stingport?” he asked, and from the tensing of Tzatak’s body, Acanto could tell he was understood. For the second time in his life, he was going to make a holohana disappear.
