It was at the first military tribunal for Ahati prisoners that He returned. The court was being held in an outdoor forum so citizens could see justice be done. Before proceedings could even start, though, one peasant broke from the crowd and strode towards the chained Ahati. Right before the peasant reached the stand, and before guards could catch him, a whirlwind of flames engulfed him, and in a moment he had transformed into the towering figure of Aakhet. The Ahati began to fall to their knees, but before their knees hit the stone, a plume of flame turned all three to ash. "Fools," the deep voice of Aakhet grumbled. "If you truly believed in your convictions, you would have retreated and formed your own kingdom, not grovelled for mercy." The great dragon scanned the crowd before spotting you. "Hah, you live," cried Aakhet, and in a second whirlwind of flames, shrunk back down into the familiar human form you had known your entire life. He marched towards you and embraced you with one arm, with such strength that you felt all air leave your lungs. "I knew you would emerge victorious. I spent some time disguised among the Ahati, and for all their numbers, they completely underestimated their foes. They were not the Ahati of old, back when I led them to glory in Bulwar. An army is only as strong as the one who leads it." He placed a hand on your shoulder and looked you up and down. "But you have that strength. You may be the finest mortal to rule the Mother's Sorrow. It would be my honor to guide you, as I did with the leaders of yesteryear."
