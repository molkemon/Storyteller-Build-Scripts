There are a lot of ways to make sure a fort keeps standing, but the best of all is to make sure the fort’s gates don’t ever have to open. The division between danger and safety is clear as day: danger is outside the fort, and safety is inside the fort. Once you have something to protect yourselves, that protection should last forever. When building your forts, make sure that the inside of the fort can sustain itself without help from the outside of the fort. Consider growing crops in designated agricultural areas. That way you don’t have to open the fort for food deliveries, although you will have to sacrifice the security of stone floors in favor of soil patches. Do this sparingly. Post at least four rotations of guards at each of your farms so that the enemy can’t dig through the soil unnoticed, and be prepared to turn on your farms at all times. Food is important, but the outside world brings danger, and that includes outside soil that is inside the fort. Safety is more important. This is why you must also enclose your farms in a maze of ramparts. This way, you can have both food and gates that never open.
