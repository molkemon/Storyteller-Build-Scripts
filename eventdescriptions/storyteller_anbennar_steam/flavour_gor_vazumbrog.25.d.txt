To the Leadership in Gor Vazumbrog I pen this missive with hands trembling from failure and shame. When I first called upon our kin to resettle the colony, I spoke of riches untold. plentiful serpentsgift to feed our people, veins of opals and peridots to fill our coffers. I painted a vision of prosperity and hope. Now, those promises lie buried beneath rubble and ash. The dragon did not come to destroy. She did not need to. Her mere presence was ruin. Her passage through the cavern shattered our walls, collapsed our mines, and turned our settlement into a tomb. She ignored our pleas, our defenses, as though we were insects beneath her notice. Those who survived left. They fled with nothing but the clothes on their backs and the horror in their eyes. I stayed behind, not out of bravery, but because I could not bear to face them. I led them here. I whispered dreams into their ears, and it cost them everything. Now, I sit alone in this husk of a settlement, the echoes of my folly ringing through the desolation. I do not ask for forgiveness and I do not deserve it. I send this report only so that you may know the truth and remember the cost of my arrogance. Rûnimûr Ironthane
