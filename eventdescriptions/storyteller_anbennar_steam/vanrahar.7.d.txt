Standing high above the square, the ruler of the realm loomed over a group of dissidents. Rebels, human supremacists, and an unusual addition. a harimari patriarch. Commanding a family dating back to Harimar himself, the aged tiger had lived in Umdaj since the old days before the Prabhi had founded modern Vanrahar. Despite his pedigree, the harimari had the gall to claim Vanrahar had betrayed the ideals of Umdaj, that the pinnacle of harimari civilization was evil. The ungrateful cur didn’t seem to care that he and his admittedly prestigious family had benefited greatly from Vanrahar’s policies. Well, if this was what he wanted, this is what he’d get. He seemed to be dictating his obituary to us, spouting that harimari and humans should be treated equally and that he would die without the privilege of his stripes or his title alongside these human criminals. The patriarch’s words carried across the crowded square, weaved along innercity walls tall enough to eclipse several city blocks, and through the titanic main gate. Amid the darkness, a terrible roar ruptured the words, reverberating back to the execution square and silencing all in attendance. Death quaked, followed by the thunder of a towering step. Then another. Another. The city gates rumbled as the great beast lumbered through the cleared streets, reveling in its call to destroy. Standing twice as tall as an elephant, the Rajathi rattled even the fiercest defiance of the arrayed criminals. Humans and harimari onlookers stared, their breath frozen with a grim reminder. though the world was fickle, the Rajathi never betrayed, never rebelled, and always stood by the Raj’s side. The creature’s rider looked to the stands, their hand tightening around the great beast’s reins, and with a solemn nod from the ruler, the elephantdwarfing Rajathi thundered forth, trampling the dissidents in gory triumph. As the Harimraj’s foes splattered one scream after another, a bloody grin graced the ruler’s face.
