The monarch did find Geir under the full moon, on a hill in the woods. And they raised her spear and said, “Accursed one. I have come to put you both to rest. Shed your skin so that we may fight.” And Geir did so, and holmgång was joined. The battle was hard, for Geir’s pelt was proof against the monarch’s spear. As the moon looked away, and the sun began to rise, did Geir catch them in his great paws, and he began to crush their life from them. Yet the monarch drew their knife, which was forged from ebonsteel, and struck Geir in the eye. Thus Geir was given a banewound, and he was slain, his great form changing to that of a man once more. The monarch rose and said, “I have avenged many, and done my duty as queen. Yet there is no glory in this victory, for I have slain two souls with one strike. A man without honour, and a beast without reason.” Then the monarch wept.
