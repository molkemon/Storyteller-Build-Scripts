We have convened a great synod to determine what shall be done about the matter of the mechanim. We have assembled beneath one of the Twin Gods, the guides of our faith. The magmascryers have brought us to the caldera of Muatlaqua, the guide of change, to show us the way forward. The magmascryers begin their dance. We see them moving in precise ways. they are drawing the parts of the lava apart from each other. We see the lava swirling on the surface, and in the lava we see metal, which burns with a fire we knew not it could hold. What a natural pairing fire and metal are. If a fire burns within all of us, perhaps it could burn yet hotter inside these mechanim, whose very movements are guided by the lightning in their veins. If their fire could be directed to enact change, we might be able to accomplish wonderful things. But as the council agrees that the mechanim will be welcomed into the Volcanic Truth, the magmascryers continue their dance. The metal that burned so brightly submerges into the lava, and the magmascryers keep chanting. On the surface, the Volcanic Truth continues its change. centuries live and die before our very eyes. But beneath it all is the metal. Its fire is gone. It sits stagnant, rusting. It is a millstone of the past, and the change the future will bring cannot slip its grasp, no matter how hard it tries. The magmascryers burst into forceful, desperate movements, but their dance cannot escape its patterns, and the metal continues to rust. The dance of the magmascryers finishes its course. The council is ashamed for its haste; we see now that metal can conduct great heat, but if the Volcanic Truth does not proceed with full awareness of the mechanim's past, we cannot hope to change their future. But the question remains. are the mechanim defined by what they are or what they could be? Are they agents of change or stagnation? What point of view shall the Volcanic Truth take? How shall we vote?
