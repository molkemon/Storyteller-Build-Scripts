The leader rips a strip of meat from the charred flank beside the other, chewing thoughtfully. Avan watches with distaste, ignoring his own plate. "That was a god, you know," he says at last. "Or close enough, at least." The leader grins, lips wet with fat. "You sound disappointed. Can I have yours?" He passes over the meat, sneering. "They were plenty compelling, I suppose. Just not particularly persuasive." He shrugs. "They bled, all the same." They fall quiet for a time. Across the river, smoke rises from a ruined marble terrace. Chittering laughter is audible somewhere just behind it. "The gnomes aren't Khet," Avan states, without looking up. "They're clever. Sneaky, horrible things. More inclined to treason than service." "All true," he agrees, "but that cleverness could be put to better use." "And, pray tell; what inside a gnome's mind could possibly be useful?" Avan pauses, thinking for a moment. "We'd be hard pressed to find better scribes, for one. They're the best gemcutters this side of Halann, for another. Some of their thinkers have ideas about our rites I can barely follow. We could learn from that. They'd never serve," he allows, tilting his head, "but I doubt they'd act against us. They'd mutter their heresies in corners, and, eventually, they'd die." He finally meets the leader's gaze. "They're not immortal." "They're not us." "No." He allows. "They aren't." Somewhere, further down the river, Zokka's voice rings out, joyous and brutal. Neither turn to look. They sit in silence; one chewing, one contemplative; listening to the slow rush of the Sorrow as the last of the Khet drifts by in the current.
