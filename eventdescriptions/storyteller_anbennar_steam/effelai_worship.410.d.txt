A sanctuary amidst the perilous wilderness, this region we have arrived in is odd, if nothing else than for its utter beauty. The land is filled with surreal landscapes of gleaming spires fashioned from crystalline glass; these ethereal towers rise defiantly from the forest floor, their tips piercing the dense canopy like shards of memory etched in stone. Despite the hostile nature of Tswohvwoh'ii, this sanctuary seems to resist the relentless encroachment of vines and tendrils; its pristine beauty preserved, even if just temporarily. The beasts that would hunt our people, too, seem strangely absent. We have long been drawn to this sanctuary, for it offers us respite from the everpresent wrath of our Goddesses. Here, we find a faint glimmer of solace amidst the melancholy—a poignant reminder of what once was, a fleeting glimpse of the world we have never known, yet yearn to return to. As we move through the foliage, our footsteps careful and reverent, we cannot help but contemplate the world that has been lost. The sinking spires of glass, once monuments to the ingenuity of a better time, now stand as stark reminders of our precarious existence at the mercy of something far greater, far more terrible. Each reflection in the glass carries fragments of a time when the jungle was not our oppressor but a gentle protector. What were we then? Was it even us? What had changed? With heavy hearts and a reluctant farewell, we leave this land behind, mindful of the transient beauty we have witnessed. Yet, in this moment of departure, we also vow to continue our journey, for we understand that to survive, we must both honor what was lost and strive for what may still be regained.
