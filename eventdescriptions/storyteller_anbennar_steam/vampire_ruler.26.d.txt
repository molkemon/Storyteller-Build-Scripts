The monarch knows shadows well. They are well familiar with the long shadows of sunset, the way they cut against the lurid orange light and herald night's arrival. They have vast experience in working around the shadows cast by sputtering torch or bright moon, how to navigate from one to another and almost become a shadow themselves. They even know the rare shadow of a prone human, lit by fallen candles, and know the blood being sucked out of them casts none. It is because the ruler knows shadows so well that they know something is off tonight, for they seem to have a shadow not their own. No curious peasant or nosy courtier should be able to follow them, and yet. There are no instincts screaming danger a hunter would inspire, and yet. The capital does not have an issue with ghosts, and yet. Perhaps it was just a mirage, perhaps not. But it is clear the monarch is at a crossroads, and their coffin might be beckoning them home early this night.
