The ancient forest is home to an ancient forest, or at least was. Sawdust billows like chimney smoke as tree after tree falls, punctuating the rasping of the saws with the thuds of falling wood. The air fills with the splintering of wood, the ground covered in the sap of ancient trees, the earth hollowed out; precious and ordinary stones are gouged alike by the local culture. Freshly paved boulevards cut through the forest each which way; towering forts are carved with the trees' fallen reliefs and every scavenged stone; newly built homes stand on the grave of a primeval grove. Alerted to the damage dealt, strange folk arrived – first appearing as kin, they instead identified themselves as the Domandrod's sentinels. The Gladewardens have demanded we cease all construction and heal their home, lest they alert their Fey masters of our destruction.
