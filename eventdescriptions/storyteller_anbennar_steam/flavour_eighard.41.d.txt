You leap between the treetops, arms swinging with wild abandon, letting the warm moons glow upon your face. You giggle. Why do you giggle? What a strange feeling this is; you somehow know it is called fun, but you have no idea why you know this. Is this what it feels like to be alive? You sense more this time, not fear, but stoicism. This is less interesting to you; stoicism does not taste nearly as good upon your vessel. The first you find is not elven at all. Its massive wings stretch time and space around it; you wonder what would happen if you were caught in between them. Its form is resistant, but you penetrate it nonetheless. You shudder as you draw its distortion into you. The second you find wears a face of faces, and it parades one around, as if enticing you with it. You ignore it; you have enough already, and wrench its soul from its body. You shudder as you draw its enigmas into you. The third you find draws darkness around him, and his somber beauty almost makes you turn. But you do not, and your arm passes through him like mist, and he wastes away. You shudder as you draw his knowledge into you. You still do not feel whole.
