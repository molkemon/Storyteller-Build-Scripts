No one knows why elephants choose to stampede. Well, the Elephant Lords do, but when it comes to spirit elephants they are just as lost as everyone else. When the first spectral elephant tears through the village palisade, then, it is considered a strange anomaly, something someone should maybe look into. The next day, an entire herd practically levels the village entirely, driving its inhabitants up onto roofs and trees, anywhere to escape the thunder of their feet. It begins in a village close to the first Great Temple along the Dhenbasana, the first temple inundated by the nation as they expanded along the branch of the Kharunyana. Soon then, herds of spirit elephants wreak havoc up and down the river. Army camps are leveled, bridges collapse, and the people are not happy, to say the least. Those in tune with the spirits since the Rending began warn the armies of the nation as they pass into the region that the great elephant, Dhenbasana, will not tolerate such an infringement on its territory. The scholars of the nation have asserted that the Dhenbasana is merely a large tributary of the Kharunyana, and therefore part of the Kharunyana's domain. This hasn't gone over well with the elephant.
