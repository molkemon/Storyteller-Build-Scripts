In the years that followed Arosha Oakbreaker's arrival in the realm, her efforts to bestow a sense of chivalry, honor, and respect on the warriors of the tribe had varying degrees of success. While she had never quite managed to singlehandedly change the culture of the orks that followed her teachings, there was no doubt that her passing left its mark on the people. Codes of honor for dueling, rules about pillaging and not harming noncombatants in war, and a belief in laws not founded solely in strength set the orkish inhabitants of the realm apart from many of their contemporaries, and led to them being viewed with curiosity and a greater degree of respect by the established powers of Cannor.
