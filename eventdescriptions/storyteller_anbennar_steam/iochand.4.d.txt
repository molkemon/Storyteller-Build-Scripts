If you were to ask an Iochander to define Iochand, you would no doubt get a wide variety of fabulous descriptors. A resplendent kingdom on the banks of the Portroy. A welcoming home for all who seek it. The dominant cultural hegemon of theatre not just in Cannor, but the entire world. Perhaps even the true heir to the legacy of Lorenan himself. Above all, however, anyone you ask would tell you that Iochand is the only gnomish kingdom in the whole of Halann. It is the most fundamental distinction of the realm. This brings us to the crux of what is currently our most pressing issue. The ruler is, by even the most generous of descriptions, not a gnome. The pure, undistilled silliness of the world's only gnomish monarchy not even being ruled by a gnome has begun to threaten the entire cultural foundation of the Kongdoum. Satirists throughout the land have reported a sudden and insurmountable lack of inspiration. Nongnomes everywhere are already starting to redevelop their own distinct regional identities, despite years of assimilation. Worst of all, Nimsnoms Theatre's most recent showing of "The Tragedy of Iochand" has only sold two tickets. Two singular tickets. And they were bought by the director's parents. It's clear we need to get a gnome back in charge before we face total cultural obliteration. Maybe we can conveniently "find" a longlost member of the síl Iochand family, or just add a special bonus prize to the upcoming Best Actor of the Year contest. Either way, we need to choose fast, before we end up like those boring Nimscodders. By the gods, I can already feel the urge to write a well-researched academic essay.
