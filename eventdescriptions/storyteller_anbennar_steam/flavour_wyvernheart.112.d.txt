Peace seems to be returning; at last, the storm of swords and spells is quelled. The cost may have been high across the nation, but for those left, meek acceptance of the new order is what seems most likely to keep them alive. So much blood is spilled in wars external, but a civil war is one between family, and those wounds are much deeper and take lifetimes to heal. The will of the victorious and this exhaustion will leave these scars, as those in power continue in triumph.  Those who had rebelled, who had felt there should be an end to tyranny. They lie empty, fields fallow as their homes and their families executed in reprisal. Salt and fire bringing ends to entire rebellious lines. No mercy for the impudent.  The Regent has proved victorious, vengeance fulfilled at last, surely? Yet now comes the task of what comes next. Where does his anger find itself when it should have been sated? What remains of his foes surrounds him. Burned into their chairs, melted by acid or dehydrated, they are slumped and fallen while his heart still beats.
