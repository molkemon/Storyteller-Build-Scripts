Golden dust motes dance in the sun's beams, and the air hangs thick with the scent of cut grass and blooming honeysuckle. Farms across the realm have bloomed with an unearthly abundance; the handiwork of the Fey. Their magic, a mischievous gift, encourages farmers to abandon their tools and turn their thoughts to feasts and revelry. In these months, time slows to a syrupy crawl, measured by the slow drift of clouds and the gentle lapping of water. Dusk brings a symphony of crickets singing the day to rest, and the first fireflies flicker on, tiny, transient stars, each a spark from the fey's endless parties. We have won.
