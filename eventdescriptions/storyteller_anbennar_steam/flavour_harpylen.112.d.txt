The hunters made good speed and reached the great roost of Zerkis soon after departing lake Nirakhet. Before even arriving, they were greeted by their kin, fellow harpies of dark skin, flying in gilded metal armor, so that the sun made them shine like stars in the sky. They were soon escorted to the matriarch of Zerkis, an elder who named herself the Mother of Munzeri, and sang with a voice, worn thin yet strong from age. "I greet you as kin, though the sands have made us estranged from one another, since the days of Queen Firanya. As the mother of this flock, I have the honor of bearing the songs of our memory. I see many questions in your eyes, so I will answer. But first, let me tell you a tale. When we were Firanya's flock, we chose to cross the Salahad, and we did so in spite of the danger. It was Firanya's legacy, perhaps. We believed then, as we do now, that we were stronger than all others. That the desert held no terrors for us, who called the sky our home. For this, we were punished. A hundred flocks took wing, but most found the sand as we flew, as the sun smote them from the sky, or gnollish arrows found them. When our journey ended, hardly a talonful of us found roost here. We fell to the ground at Lake Nirakhet, there to fall victims to the men and gnolls of this land, who we had held in contempt." The elder paused, and smiled wistfully. "This of course, was our conceit. The folk of this land saw our pain, and our sorrow for those our pride had slain. They took us as their own people, and shared with us the word of Mother Nirakhet, who weeps for all. We have kept our peace, and chosen silence to your ears, for we see in you much of old Firanya, whose pride did great harm to our people. Yet we knew, then as now, that we could not hide forever, and now that you are here, we will join with your Queendom, if you so will it."
