Like lost clouds roaming over the countryside, a herd of wooly rhinos has been sighted migrating through our territory. Renowned for their frizzy wool, the creatures are fiercely territorial and detest captivity, but despite their guarded natures, peoples throughout history sought to obtain their priceless fleece. Although sacrificially consumed, local cultures respect the wooly rhinos as profound embodiments of duality—life and death, comfort and wilderness, kindness and aggression, spiritual and physical. So how then should we obtain their prized pelt? We could allow hunters to kill the rhinos and pay a bounty for the hides, but this would prove supremely unpopular among our people. Alternatively, we could demand hunters nonlethally pacify the creatures via costly draughts, leaving them unharmed after shaving their wool.
