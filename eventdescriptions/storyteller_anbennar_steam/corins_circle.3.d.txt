Winston Foolfoot was a man with ideas. A plethora of ideas. And; uncommon for most adventurers; they were usually good ones. Born a middlechild to a merchant family in a city, he became a travelling merchant, and in a current year led a caravan of food and supplies to a ravaged city, hoping to make a quick profit while avoiding any hostiles (this idea an exception to the rule). Stuck in a small town, besieged by a vast array of greenskins; he felt called to action in way he had never felt before. He used his halfling wit to organize the panicking townspeople, identified a gap in the disorganized orkish sentries, and led them all to escape. It was he, in his subsequent role in a house, who rallied the other adventuring groups to unite behind a leader. It was he who devised a clever pulley system to lift someone out of the pit the group found them in. And it was he who guarded a body after she fell, a whisper in his head convincing him she was not gone, not yet. After she fell for the second time, for once, the brilliant halfling was not sure what to do. Staying with the group meant he risked bursting into tears every time he saw someone (he had an idea of what they meant to each other, a much better one than either of them). And yet he maintained enough of that adventurer spirit to decline an employment offer from someone. Instead, he wandered from one adventuring party to the next (somehow evading stillroaming hordes of goblins and orks), staying perhaps a week, perhaps a month, perhaps a year, and offering his ideas for the taking. Our lord had heard rumours Foolfoot had been helping the mayor of a village outside our capital, but even then he wasn't quite prepared for the sight of the halfling walking confidently into our lord’s command tent. "You're doing good work. I have some ideas you might want to take a look at. Mind if I stay a while?"
