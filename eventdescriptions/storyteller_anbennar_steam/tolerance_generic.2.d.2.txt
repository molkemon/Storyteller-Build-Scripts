She was tired. Tired of toiling every day with no pause nor reward, tired of the arguments with her husband and of the cries of the children, tired of a life at the bottom of the heap. And tired of being looked down on by those haughty bastards. Who did they think they were, parading around like they were better than her? They lived near her, worked jobs similar to hers, yet she always felt inferior in front of them. Why? Just because they lived a bit longer? Because of their supposed gracefulness? They were as dirty and ragged as she was, what grace? She couldn't stand it any longer. And now it had come out that their race was responsible for the state our nation is in, the rising taxes, the increased prices? She was tired, but tonight she wouldn't sleep. And neither would they. Horror. My Liege, the peasants of that province assembled a mob targeting one of the minorities. Hundreds lie dead; how could such a thing happen? These fanatics… this is why we need to keep the plebs down; if a small hint that other races might not be perfect is enough to turn them all into assassins, they can’t be trusted with anything.
