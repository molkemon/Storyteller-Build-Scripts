The hardest question for the naga to answer regarding the historical three hundred thirty-three empires is not one of the many very silly stories of past empires. It is of an empire that did not count as one of the three hundred thirty-three empires, despite clearly being an empire. The Karrassian Empire was formed around five hundred AA, and reached its height in the year seven hundred forty. Its decline began with a crushing defeat to Kheterata, but at the height of its powers, its control stretched from Kunolo to western Ardimya. None of the reasons given for why Karassk did not count hold up to any scrutiny. Technically the three hundred thirty-two empire still existed at that time with Zerat, but there were many previous empires active at once, most egregiously the two hundred fourteenth to two hundred nineteenth empires. Not controlling all of Taneyas does not matter either, as there have been empires that didn’t control one blade of grass in the traditional lizardfolk homeland, yet they are still held to count. The most common reason given is a lack of approval of other lizardfolk as an empire due to their heinous acts, most notably when they blew up the ancient Zatsarya in Ashazual. Without recognition from other lizardfolk, they can’t be called an empire. This argument falls apart, however, when you recall the tale of the one hundred sixty-second empire, which was ruled over by a terrible dragon that everyone hated and refused to call emperor. Those who critique the prophecy say there is a very simple reason why they were not recognised as the three hundred thirty-third empire. it is because the naga had almost no power within Karassk at the time, and the naga did not want to grant the title of the prophesied final and eternal empire to a nation they did not control.
