Zuhrah blinked madly as her brother shook her awake, panic setting in as she understood the sounds of battle around them, and confusion as she felt the shackles around her ankles click open. "Come, sister," Mkwayo urged. "There are..." He gave an exasperated sigh, pointed to a darkskinned, bigbearded elf standing in the entrance to their cage, brandishing a sabre and glancing over. "We are being rescued. Don't ask questions. We must go." That was all Zuhrah needed to hear. Mkwayo led her and the others in their cage out, closely following the elf that had freed them, though he hadn't had enough time to untie the shackles around her hands. The Fangaulan couldn't help but look around as they ran. The elves were still fighting the gnolls, having killed half of the slavers, though there seemed only slightly fewer elven bodies around as well. Zuhrah heard the crack of a whip, and her gaze snapped up to the big platform that overlooked their cages. She felt this indescribable feeling surge through her chest as an armored elf dodged the lash and severed their gnollish overseer’s head in a single flourish of his blade. Soon enough, they were down in a ditch outside the slaver camp, guarded here by Faungaulan fighters too, ready to ferry their own people to freedom. The bearded elf that guided them checked that the group were all accounted for, gave a satisfied nod, then turned away, but Zuhrah rushed up and cried, "Wait." He turned, brow arched. "Why? Why are you doing this?" They were from half a world away, if the stories she remembered her mother telling her were true. The elf stared dumbfounded for a moment, clearly not knowing the language, but a Fangaulan warrior soon translated. The elf laughed, then held the chains around Zuhrah’s wrists aloft. With a single, powerful blow from the hilt of his sabre, he shattered the wrought iron.
