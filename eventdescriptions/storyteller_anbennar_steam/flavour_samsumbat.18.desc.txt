Many years have passed since the first words of the constitution were penned. There have been many debates and elections, laws written and amended, and new cultures assimilated. The machinery of government is becoming smooth and the various structures find their place in the Great Republic. Learning how to work with the Khet while electing a new leader every few years has taken some time, with many mistakes along the way as they and we adjust to new ways of governance. However, this adaptation has had an interesting consequence; the hedonistic ways of the Khet are beginning to have an influence on some of the ministers and elected rulers. This has not yet had catastrophic consequences, but there have been some disturbing cases of the rulers using their power for personal gain; misappropriating funds, neglecting diplomatic duties, and keeping their family members in power. Two proposals have been put forward to reverse this moral turpitude; establishing a new Corruption Oversight Board from among the most trusted councillors, with the power to prosecute the rulers, or requiring every elected official to serve as a manual labourer for a year after their term in order to ensure their humility. There are advantages and disadvantages of both proposals, and the debate in the council chamber was lively, but in the end it was decided the best path forward for Samsmbat was.
