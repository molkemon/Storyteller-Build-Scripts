Immortal life. Such a state can hardly be called living. It's something greater-to become one with the planes, immutable and unchanging. A presence eternal, with potential as vast as the sea of stars above. The world runs ragged in your presence, a slave to its master. As the sun in the sky, you are everwatchful; even more, for one who is risen need not rise. "Thy time hath come." Laugh to scorn the idea. Time is a concept without substance, a vestige of consequence long rebuked. Yet hark, your soulless eye did gaze upon its figure, its form as interminable as your own. Shoddilyjointed fingers clasping a tyrant's zenith-a pretender. You are death, not the charlatan that would seek your title-a mere scythe, no less. In its reflection, you see your visage. Not a lord, but a geriatric fool asserting themselves as one with power over this world, over this moment. Power. In life, power was your pursuit. Beyond life, this quest has never ceased, complacency scorned with undying tenacity. Power over the immutable, the unchanging, the foes in your mind, the foe you find hovering before you, clad in a scarlet cloak and adorned with a ruby mask. Your mind's eye quivers with an unfamiliar emotion, one which you tentatively label as fear. Fear. Beyond life, there is only the absolute self, fear sinking into the unfathomable mire of the past, paranoia asserting itself with due likeness, but ultimately a different emotion: one with drive, purpose. A testament. Fear had long since lost its place in your mind, supplanted by a paranoia born of resolve. Resolve. Yes, the resolve to pursue life. The resolve to overcome death. The resolve to become eternal. Surely eternity is not as short as this, is it?
