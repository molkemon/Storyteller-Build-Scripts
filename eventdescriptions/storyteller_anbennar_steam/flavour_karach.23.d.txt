Zashyt Dreamchaser woke up in her bed, and almost hit her head on the ceiling of her restaurant. There was not much space in the boat, and so she and the others had to make do with only a spare bunk each. Her friend Nichuk crashed down from the bunk below her. They both made their way into the cooking area, and Nichuk began to cook up that morning’s eggs with saffron, just the way the whole boat liked it. As he did, Zashyt touched up the paint on the outside of the hull. She had been a bard her whole life, so the painting was more colorful swirls and pleasing shapes than any picture, but she did her best. More than painting, she loved writing the songs that they used to attract patrons. As the other bards ate their breakfast, Zashyt got out her lute and began to strum out a few lines of her latest song. The next town was close, and they wanted to make a good first impression. Their food was luxuriously spiced, it was true, but it wasn’t the food that drew customers in to eat. it was the music. Nichuk brought up the anchor. Their boat began to drift downriver towards the next town, and as it did Zashyt sat on the roof, where she could see clear down the river into a colorful market square. She sang a song of wonders to be seen and wonderful food to be had in a high, clear voice, both at a very affordable price.
