One prong of the ruler's plan to convert the populace centered on the employment of mixed groups of soldiers and philosophers called Jireedeehana. The specific structure and number of men varied from state to state, but their key mission was the same. spread the word of Suhan by walking the world in accordance with his doctrine while keeping a detailed account of their comings and goings. The ruler's hope was always that these Jireeraturadeehana would help to reach the more distant groups of potential converts deep within the rural areas of the country; however, they have yet to achieve this goal. Many of our rural subjects are not keen on converting to Suhan's doctrine. The Orange Sash school especially holds a stranglehold over these groups, entrenching them in familial tradition. Some have even gone as far as to ambush the Jireeraturadeehana on the trail as they walk. The issue has been raised in court several times now, but the ruler has finally been pushed for a decision.
