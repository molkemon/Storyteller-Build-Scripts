In recent years, in the province of that region, a eunuch administrator has taken over local administration. Under the banner of rectifying tax rolls and cleansing corruption, he has quickly erased fiscal deficits. However, his methods are brutal—levying excessive taxes, seizing merchant ledgers, and meddling in private trade and inheritance. Rumors suggest he enslaves the poor and seizes warehouses for personal use. The markets lie quiet; travelers detour the region. A popular saying spreads. "Beneath the hat lies only bone," mocking his homely appearance and ruthless actions. Even local elites and the local scholars have joined voices in protest, accusing him of abusing authority and oppressing the people. The eunuch defends himself. "To fear public unrest is to abandon the duties of governance. If discipline falters, the whole bureaucracy will collapse." His tone veils a warning—punish him too harshly, and others may avoid civil service altogether. Shall we value his results, or root out a growing rot?
