We are faced with the greatest challenge ever presented before us since the dawn of our people. We set forth now to defy the different legendary beasts of the Tswohvvwoh’ii; we call them godbeasts for lack of a better name, though we know they are no gods. Intelligent creatures, these lifeforms are massive compared to any other lifeform in the jungle. They are also far more dangerous. The Parura, when we proved ourselves to them, told us of the GodBeast we must confront. A spider so large that it extends from furrows in the ground to the very crown of the tree canopy. Its lair is a web spanning hundreds of trees, possessed of seemingly thousands of cocoons. Confronting it is complicated by the presence of seemingly intelligent leeches, its retinue, that use ambushing tactics to rapidly pull unsuspecting hunters into the inescapable depths of the swamp. If we can surmount this seemingly implacable enemy, our people will have a legend handed down forevermore.
