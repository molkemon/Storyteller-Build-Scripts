The advisor has finally completed their work on composing a great work of literature. Having been provided an exceedingly large sum of money for the deed, the philosopher ensured that the patronage was not squandered; the resulting work, known as "On the Nature of Faith", is hundreds of pages long and will surely be counted as one of the greatest works of the age, certain to become widespread as it is printed and distributed. The work covers, as its name would suggest, a large amount of reflection on the worship of Surael. It argues that, even though the Sun Elves are the shepherds of the Bulwari, their rightful duty is merely to foster the prosperity of the Bulwari, as a gardener might coax their plants to growing, and that any innovation by the chosen people is to be welcomed as a natural success. Were the circumstances any different, the advisor might surely have been burned at the stake; but not with the protection of Bulwar.
