Having successfully tracked down Halukandaran, the ruler's next step was figuring out where it was going. It would be unwise to attack it in an open field, where it could see him from miles away. Luckily there was a slightly rocky area nearby, with some cliffs and hills beneath which he could try to ambush the massive hydra. But this would not be so easy because Halukandaran had no inclination to leave the open fields brimming with prey. It would need to be lured away, but with what? What could a hydra of this size be possibly interested in? There was no way around it: the ruler needed help. He would need to prepare a bait of monstrous size to fit his quest, like a herd of cows or pigs or something similar. He looked around and tried to make out some homestead that had not been ruined by the hydra, and, to his joy, there was one just a few miles south from his position. With quick steps he made his way to the human household, the farmer raising an eyebrow upon the arrival of such a kingly dwarf. "Well, well, Master Dwarf, what might you do here?" the farmer asked. "I wish to slay Halukandaran," the lord answered. "And I require your livestock." Despite initial reluctance and disbelief, in the end the farmer consented. The ruler would pay twice the usual price of the farmer's livestock in order to use it as bait. They herded the animals into the hills and slaughtered some to stimulate the hydra's nose, and soon the steps came closer. The farmer put his hat to his chest. "By all that is good in this world, I wish you the best of luck." The lord nodded and thanked him for the help. And with Halukandaran nearing, the farmer left.
