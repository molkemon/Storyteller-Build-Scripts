"Where are you taking me?" the young boy asked. The older one walking beside him responded: "To right a wrong you committed. I have trained you for three years now at your grandfather's behest, on the understanding that when he died, you would Devour him. Instead you let his body rest; so caught up in mourning were you that forty days passed and his spirit moved on." The older one's tone was harsh, but not unkind. The boy's face blanched. "Master, I have eaten insects, and dirt, and small spirits, but my grandfather is another matter. Even dead, he is still family, and it would not be right to eat him." 

"Right?" The master harrumphed. "Your grandfather's spirit is suffering even as we speak, tortured endlessly by the Hell of the Spirit Realm. His mind is degenerating into its basest form, becoming less than he was. Is it right to deny an old man his wish, and leave their soul in pain? Here, we have come to the temple of a foreign land, which has been corrupted in the old ways with a monk's left hand. And see, there is your grandfather now." 

And the boy looked, and saw that it was as his master said, for his grandfather was there behind a shimmering cage of air, wearing an expression of greatest rage and shrieking in unimaginable pain. 

"Get down on your knees, boy. Pull your grandfather free from the soulcage. Eat him." 

And the boy, full of filial piety, did as he was told. With trembling fingers he grasped his grandfather's soul. The spirit howled with mindless rage, but did not escape. And so the boy brought his grandfather's soul past his lips, and tears flowed freely down his face.
