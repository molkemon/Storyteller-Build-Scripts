With a wild scream, Khaara Sapchopper leaped from the branch. With a painful thud, she landed on the startled creature below. It squirmed and buckled trying to throw her off, but it was too late. The wild movements only ensured its demise. Khaara swung her ax as soon as the chink in the fey’s ironlike scales exposed itself. She was rewarded with a gush of blood and a shrill shriek from the fey creature. Driven by adrenaline, she hacked again and again until finally the creature was still. She sat upon a dead creature unbelieving, then gave a triumphant howl. The young ork was no longer a mere girl but had proven herself a true daughter of Clan Sapchopper. Now all she had to do was claim a trophy as proof. After the euphoria of victory had finally settled, Khaara rolled off the creature and set about breaking off her prey’s valuable hardened scales. In all, it took an hour of diligent work to produce the fair pile of scales, but it was worth it for the celebration she would receive. As she turned to make the long trek home, Khaara froze, realizing she had forgotten to honor her success. She closed her eyes as her village tradition spoke.
