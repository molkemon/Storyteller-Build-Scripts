Oh it is a wide winter, Windy with gales, Hard, harsh and horizonless, cold, And I can do nothing more this year, But sharpen the swords, mend the gear, Mend cloth, patch sails, Listen to tales told by the old, Listen to horses stamp in stalls. Feel the blood in my veins going nowhere.  The winter this year is exceptional harsh, and amoungst the skaldhyrric faithful, that is the surest sign of inpieity of a ruler. For it is the duty of the skalds to maintain the slumber of the white dragon, and with a winter as greedy and biting as this one, many fear that the dragon, is once again stirring.
