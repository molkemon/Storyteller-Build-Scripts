The short, barking creatures WalksAlone found in their mountain homes were uniquely small for the region. At first, WalksAlone assumed the pack they discovered were a group of lost children in search of their larger parents. However, through rudimentary communication and mostly sign language, they eventually determined that this was not, in fact, the case. Seeing that the Kobolds were obviously frightened of him, WalksAlone made slow, cautious movements and gestures to show he was no threat. The Kobolds initially refused to meet the Mechanim on even ground, holding up inside their fortresses and barking down at WalksAlone as he tried to speak with them. WalksAlone falteringly asked if the Kobolds were aware of what happened to the builders, his makers, and the Kobolds responded that they weren't aware. The Kobolds asked WalksAlone if they had come from under the mountains, and the Mechanim responded they had not. In this way, the two sides came to realize that neither of them had any idea what to make of the other. After a few hours of conversation during which the Mechanim gradually pieced together better ways to communicate, the bravest Kobolds emerged from their stone walls in a pack of seven. WalksAlone thought that this was quite amusing, as they had never felt the need for safety in numbers, but could identify that is how the Kobolds were finding courage. As the Kobolds approached, WalksAlone extended a hand, palm open and up, but this was interpreted as a hostile act and quickly resulted in the kobolds shirking away. Several minutes later, the kobolds reached out their own hands, and they were accepted. The Mechanim describe the Kobolds in a strange mixture of protective and dismissive. The stereotype of children seems to have stuck from this first meeting, and it can be heard in the way they are described that Kobolds are seen as both something the Mechanim wish to avoid harming and yet also see little kinship with, in a way that seems less paternalistic and more like owners of an unusually smart pet. Notes, Academician Prof. Raven, recording the legend of WalksAlone.
