Markhentakruan Improvement Notes; today simply increasing the size of the explosion has proven problematic. Enlarging the scale of the transmutation before detonation does produce a greater blast with improved lethality, but it also greatly diminishes the spread of the flames, a trade not worth such meager gains. I was at an impasse until a mage in Danazgubli made a catastrophic error. He had piled several corpses against the cavern wall before casting the amplified version of the spell, triggering a cave collapse that killed him and two apprentices instantly. Despite the waste of resources the incident has inspired me. What if, instead of merely breaking armies, Markhentakruan could break walls? The simplest approach would be to use the enhanced variant as a dedicated siege spell, but that would not be an improvement, only another tool in our arsenal. I want this wallbreaking capability woven directly into the spell’s framework. My solution is an elegant one. The force of the explosion already scales with the size of the corpse, and testing has shown the relationship to be nonlinear, with larger bodies yielding disproportionately greater power. If I add a component that fuses nearby corpses into a single amalgam before transmutation, the spell will retain its normal properties in battle while gaining the strength to shatter walls, provided we take the time to pile the dead high enough.
