More giant bones lie at the entrance to the main production facility, but these are scattered even more haphazardly than the last. Large birds circle overhead, descending at random intervals to peck at whatever scraps of rotting tissue may still be left attached to the bones. The party tries to cross the carrion plain and soon finds themselves beset by the scavengers, beaks and talons slashing at them as they try to escape their razor-sharp grasp. Still, the sight is beautiful. Even in death, life continues to move forward. My research may be drowning in the bogs of the dead, but perhaps I can use it to move forward into new glory. A burning sensation brings the party back to reality. The vultures remain.
