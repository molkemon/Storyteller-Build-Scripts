A young man begins to tell a tale. A silent night. The villagers lay asleep, and the Apihoxi guard stands tired at watching the endless night. "Hey Jim." "Yeah." "You see those bushes over there?" "Yeah." "I think they may be fake." "Maybe. Shoot an arrow at it." "Wouldn’t they shoot back?" "They would have done that a while ago." As Jim goes to turn around, he sees his friend lying dead on the floor with an arrowhead directly in his skull. Jim turns back around to scream for help, but he is silenced as well. The pirates laugh at the young man’s tale. The raid was a grand, grand success. The rush of pirates that ran and looted the granaries was immense. Thousands of pounds of bread and grain that they may use to sustain themselves. They can finally maintain their people, and possibly expand their farms to be somewhat self-sustaining with all of the seeds they collected. Unfortunately for Apihoxi, they will have to remain without food this winter. They should have just shared from the start.
