A strange, if welcome, sight arrived in the capital last night. This sight was the appearance of a dozen Wood Elves, dressed in colorful foreign silk garbs and carrying equally bizarre goods. They were survivors of the expedition sent out west years ago. As the tale would have it, these expeditionaries had found refuge in an ancient castle on the outskirts of the dreaded swamp, apparently known as Daravan’s Folly. There, they were stumbled upon by passing merchants who took pity on them. What ensued was a journey unlike any other. Their journey took them south to the land of Ourdia, where ancient fortifications, like the one in which the expeditionaries sheltered, dotted the land. As they traveled further, they stumbled upon a number of different races. The first were goblins, both the ones familiar with and those of an exodus. Later, they encountered communities of humans and Sun Elves, elves who are apparently distant cousins of our own forebears. And though they did not get to see it, they were told of a city known as Ovdal Tûngr, dug deep into the southern mountains themselves where the Copper Dwarves resided. But none of that compared to the sight of where the land ended and water began. To the amazement of every listener, the returning elves told of how the endless sea glistened under the sun and where vessels larger than any that sailed Silent Lake across into the horizon.
