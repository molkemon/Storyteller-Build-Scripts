Aucanna was so weak now. The physicians had told the ruler that she wouldn’t last much longer. The latest bout of illness had struck her hard, bringing her to her knees out on the parapets overlooking the training field. She’d wanted to go down of course, but her legs hadn’t allowed it even then. The ruler smiled down at her, a thin smile, one the ruler had seen on her face many times. An insincere smile. It wouldn’t be long. “Don’t look at me like that,” Aucanna rasped. The ruler saw the pain she hid in her eyes as she shifted her neck to look at the ruler. They said nothing. “I’m dying. I can feel it. This will be my deathbed.” The ruler took her hand, biting down on their tongue. There were so many words they could say, so many they wanted to, but Aucanna would never forgive them if they showed weakness, here at the end. “You have much work left to do, little one. I only hope, by Corin’s grace, that I have made the road easier for you. Hold your friends close, and never forget,” Aucanna spluttered, coughing. “Never forget what?” the ruler asked gently, fighting with all the strength they had to keep their tone soft and level. “Never forget what I taught you,” Aucanna whispered, the lustre fading from her eyes. The ruler squeezed her hand as the life left it, and the light in her eyes went dim.
