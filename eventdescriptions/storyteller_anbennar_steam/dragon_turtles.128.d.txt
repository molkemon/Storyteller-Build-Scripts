Why is it that you blame me for the air around you growing drier and your spells growing weaker? When the waves crash against your shores, do you blame Nalo Pu’ukani? Of course not. To cast magic requires both a stable mind and a clear soul; if your mages cannot work with the mana in the air, perhaps they should look inwards instead of outwards. I have known something of anguish. If the mages still suffer, tell them that the pain they feel is nothing compared to the pain caused when power is unjustly wielded. Tell them to sit and watch the ash play across the wind. While they do so, they may reflect on who exactly it is they serve, to what end they serve them, and how their past actions have led to their present predicament. When they have reflected long enough, there is a chance that the air will clear.
