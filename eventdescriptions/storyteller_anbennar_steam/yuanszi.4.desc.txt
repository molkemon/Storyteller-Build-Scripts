A wretched bastard, traitor, slithering snake. A hundred of names we were called when the Wulin received our formal request for a duel for the rulership over the Xiaken. They screamed foul play, subterfuge, and broken promises. In their eyes, we were not just unworthy; we were the lowest of the low, lower even than the Command or the Jiangshi. Yet they knew that refusal was impossible; we are a legitimate Xiaken after all. So, with a head raised, the Wulin accepted, preparing for a duel at dawn, when the dew had only just settled on the ground. The ruler smiled when they approached the duel grounds, chatting with their retinue while the leaders of the other Xiaken stood around silently, looking at us with disgust and fear. And so the duel begins. A hit. The Wulin punched our ruler at the shins, causing them to almost lose balance. The next punch they blocked, and the next, and the one after. They defended against the swift and emotionally loaded attacks the Wulin threw. The ruler waits until the best moment to strike back, in the meanwhile dodging and blocking the attacks the Wulin throws with all of their might; they want to end it here today, both the ruler and Yuanszi itself. But an opportunity presents itself, and the ruler strikes.
