Observe a moment the creatures as they gather and jabber at the chamber’s edge. You will see these merry things swim and leap and jostle with one another, their mammal brains plotting the next feint, the next attempt at deceiving and delighting their fellows. They are posing questions for the other to solve, feeding on the simple joy of stakeless competition. They are at play. We have spent a few guilty days here already; this habitat is a rare moment of peace in a series of floors which have offered nothing but danger, and like the otters we chatter and feast and roam to our leisure. The otters have their own kind of bluffing territoriality, curious and affronted at those who wander a little too close, and while they are not truly huge in the manner of other creatures of this pillar, a direct tackle from one of them would be no small thing. Still, the expedition’s spirits are high; it is difficult not to take on a little of the otters’ playful mood.
