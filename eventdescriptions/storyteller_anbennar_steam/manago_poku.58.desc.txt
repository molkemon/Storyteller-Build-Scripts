Behold the myriad brilliantine channels and trickles of the river mouth, inspected one at a time by the curious sun. Each new rock and snag is a risk, a challenge over which the shallow water may flow or a barrier at which it may diverge. Standing here in your unerring stillness, Mechanim scout, you have watched for fifteen hours as the trickles come into being and die again and again, licking up over sand or rock at the fringe of the flow, each clear little ribbon surely a consciousness. Are these ephemeral channels, splitting into countless immensity, just one being or many? How many lives have you watched expire since last you moved? Yet surely this is all one great being too, for the river is one thing, one noun, and there is a point a little on from here where the water’s flow meets the great body of the open ocean. It is hard not to worry for the little trickles and channels in such an immensity. It is why you stopped here, why you cannot stop trying to memorize their too-brief lives. They are themselves and they are the river; soon they will be the ocean alone, and not themselves. Is this not terrifying?
