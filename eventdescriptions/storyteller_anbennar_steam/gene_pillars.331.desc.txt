What confidence the Precursors had to put such an important thing so high up. Perhaps mimicking the biology of the majority of sentient species, the brain of the Pillar seems to be here, a massive ordering device whose levers and buttons, when functional, seem to have brought or dispatched materials or samples to and from this central point. The vessels and tubes which have permeated the walls all the way up appear to converge on this place. It would be great if we knew what any of it did. Despite or perhaps because of the intricacy of this place, however, it is in profound disrepair. The central device is mostly intact, itself a pillar within a pillar, but the structure has suffered from the rule of the hinuiroc, who have torn open floors where they had not already collapsed. Up here, almost every chamber is grand, steep and airy; while there is no longer quite as much eccentric transition from one extreme biome to another, navigation will not be easy. Perhaps most troubling of all is the prospect of doing damage to the great central device before we have a chance to understand it. Even if we don’t break something, it might break us.
