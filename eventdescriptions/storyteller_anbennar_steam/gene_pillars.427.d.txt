I know of many, many things. Chief among them is this: war is hell, and war is inevitable. Both are true for every living being that walks or crawls on this planet. Colonies of ants, cesspools of humans, prides of lions; bring enough organisms together, division will inevitably grow between them, and there will be war. War in which they fight, and suffer, and die and die and die. But their lives are fleeting anyways, as plentiful and meaningless as grains of sand on a beach. Ours are not. Each time one of us dies, infinity is brought to an end. A tireless fountain of light and love and song and sorrow is cut off, left to fade into the abyss. My father tells stories of the Usurpation of the Golden Scales. The vile muck, the eldritch weaponry wielded against us. But he speaks more of his fallen friends, who will now only live on forever in his memories. War is hell, and war is inevitable. One war in particular, I see on the horizon, and it will be more hellish than any we fought since we broke our chains. So it is best to be prepared. Which means I really do need to stop procrastinating, and reanimate the latest batch of drakelings, for all it is a chore. The question of whether naturally magicattuned beings can behave differently when subjected to necromantic treatment is interesting. I do think there is potential for a feedback loop somewhere here, and that would truly be exciting. But the work is... Ugh. This is but more procrastination. Visions of a researcher recorded from the mind of an explorer after discovering a central memory bank.
