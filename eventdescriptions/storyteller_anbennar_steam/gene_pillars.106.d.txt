After a long and arduous trek through the pillar’s base, the party finally arrives at a small cluster of rooms and structures which seem to have once been used for habitation. Apartments, barracks, dining halls, storage rooms, and recreational areas are all present within the ruins, each left in worse condition than the last. Picking through each deserted compartment one by one, the party discovers little of worth; until someone knocks over a pile of debris, and reveals a passage leading into a hidden room. Inside the chamber, strange devices and swirling blue mist trapped in jars line the walls from floor to ceiling. All sorts of machinery and spare parts are strewn haphazardly across tables and desks scattered throughout the room, quickly revealing itself to be some kind of old workshop. At the far end of this craftsman’s wonderland, the party discovers the strangest find of them all; some kind of old machine body, with a jar full of condensed Kiamoa clouds jammed into the neck where the head should be. They stare quizzically at the magical oddity, until one of them notices something that makes their heart drop to their feet: the tank is staring back. Suddenly, a voice begins to sound through a mesh of metal embedded into the machine’s chest. "Thank the stars. I never thought I would see mortals ever again." The voice is thin and covered in a layer of static, but the party can make out some kind of speech if they listen closely. "We have no time to lose. My name is Conthus, and I no longer wish to be alive." The thing in the jar explains that it’s an ancient being from before the sky fell, and in order to truly be killed he must be cast into the Kiamoa and dispersed into the stormwall. In return, he offers to guide the party through the more dangerous levels of the pillar. "Truly, what do you have to lose?"
