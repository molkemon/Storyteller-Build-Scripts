All lizardfolk belief is built around the goal of the construction of the final, three hundred and thirty-third Empire. All who hunt in the jungles, march in the army, write in the cities, and perform any other task besides, do so in the belief it brings their fate one step closer. It is the reason they rise in the morning, and rest in the evening, and motivates the actions and choices of rulers across the domain. What would a rhadakaya do if they lost this purpose? How could they exist without it? It is a question that has become surprisingly and worryingly prescient as the rulers of a lizardfolk nation have decided to abandon Anzalkhatsa, swearing for a heathen belief system, utterly alien to these lands and their people. As this news is digested across the domain, the Sculptors of Belief have been quick to come up with an explanation for this action. They accuse the Sculptors of Fate, known for their promotion and adoption of foreign ideas, of pushing too far and now inviting heresy into the hearts of lizardfolk. Though they strenuously deny the accusation, many lizardfolk see truth in it, and have turned away from the Sculptors of Fate and their arguments.
