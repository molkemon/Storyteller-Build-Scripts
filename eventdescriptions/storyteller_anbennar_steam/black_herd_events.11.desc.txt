I write this note several days' travel from the main camp. A few weeks ago one of my assistants had the idea that we might find physical evidence from Markhentakruan at a battlefield where it was once cast. I was skeptical; the chaos of battles, the deluges of rain, and the simple passage of time all seemed certain to have erased any trace. But after months of fruitless attempts to reassemble the spell from oral testimony, I was willing to try anything. I dispatched several scouts to search known sites where the spell was cast, and to my astonishment, one of them actually found something. He discovered fragments of a charred ogre skeleton half-buried in the dry earth. We set out immediately and arrived last night, beginning our excavations at first light. As the dirt was cleared, we found more fragments of bone, just enough to roughly reassemble the skeleton and gain a picture of what occurred. The findings were revelatory. Most of the bones surrounding the abdomen were entirely missing, likely pulverized by the force of the detonation. The closest surviving bones, including some of the vertebrae and the collarbone, were shattered into numerous splinters. The extremities were more intact, scattered far from what we believe was the epicenter. From this, I have concluded that the corpse was not transmuted in its entirety, as we once believed. Instead, the explosive is formed in the center of the body and sends limbs and chunks of corpse flying outward with tremendous force upon detonation. The even charring on the remaining bones suggests that an intense burst of heat and flame preceded the blast, which is likely the catalyst that triggered it.
