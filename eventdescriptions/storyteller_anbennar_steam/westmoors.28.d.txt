The night was dark and full of terrors, as Alfred Darkwood sat on the porch of his hut, sharpening his axe as his wife prepared his poisons and potions. Scouts had found tracks, and butchered animals, and sighted something moving at the edges of the forests. Whatever it was, it needed to be put down. As he sharpened, he looked out to the small burgeoning settlement. Once just a few shacks down by the coast, it had been expanded to be nigh on self sufficient, importing only guns and other finished goods. Tomorrow, he knew there would be battle, and that he, as a woodsman, would be at the forefront of this. To push back the darkness, vanquish the beast, slay the enemy, that was the vow his fathers had kept for centuries. But tonight, he and his people would feast, they would sing and dance, and they would either celebrate his return, or bury his body if there was one. Such was life, even across the world.
