The stream of canoes coming from our neighbors in that nation along our rivers to be sold has recently slowed to a trickle. It appears to be the case that their area is experiencing an especially severe outbreak of teak moths this year, and there is scarcely a clearing of teak trees in their entire nation that has not been affected. Due to teak wood’s role in their canoe building, all of that nation has precious little to barter with their fellow tribes, and they would likely appreciate any form of economic relief we might be able to provide to them.
