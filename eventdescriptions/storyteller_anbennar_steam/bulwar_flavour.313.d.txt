The lands of the upper Sorrow and the surrounding Gol savanna have been beset by conflict for most of their history. Nomads against sedentary riverfolk, humans against gnolls, Kheteratans against Fangaulans. But now, with the arrival of the Sun Cult to these contested lands, there is at long last hope for a lasting peace. A peace under the auspices of shared worship of Surael, uniting former foes and ending the petty vendettas of former rivals. This is the promise made manifest in the QaarHortabat, the Temple of the Garden of Hope. Here, along the shores of the Sorrow on the other side of the city of Deshwimat, a vast temple complex has been constructed, its central ziggurat EduzDasmakirima and ancillary buildings separated by a vast meticulously maintained garden park kept perpetually verdant by the fertile soil and nourishing waters of the river. But flowers and trees, no matter how pleasing to the eyes they might be, are not the most important thing grown in this sacred precinct. Here impartial temple mediators resolve even the most bitter and protracted of conflicts, and promising bright youths from Deshwimat and the surrounding provinces are taught with the aim of eventually occupying important positions among the local administration, learning early on to not let resentments and prejudices taint their judgment.
