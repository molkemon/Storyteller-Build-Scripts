"You are distracted." Granny Ilfa does not raise her voice. She does not need to. "Forgotten who she is, the little bitch." Mama Hireet’s voice is a growl; today she is a human, several feet too tall to be convincing. Mama wears a necklace of human ears. Such a berating is a ritual, the Vile One knows. It is to see if she will withstand it; if she will insist on herself. "I have made something which functions," the Vile One replies, swallowing the lump in her throat. "Something useful to our cause." "Fripperies." Mama roars, stooping down, inches away; the volume is physically painful. "Poring over papers like a clerk. Fussing over aqueducts. Are you a mouse?" "I govern well," she manages. Hireet barks a laugh. "A governor, then. Useless girl; we raised an archmage." Mama waves a hand at Granny. "A palace or two enough to make her ordinary, after all we’ve done for her. Whiling away her time with her little playmates like some simpering sybarite..." Granny does not disagree. Komina, tapping her foot, suddenly rises and leaves the room. "My approach is working," says the Vile One, eventually. "I remain your Daughter. My potential..." "Enough," Granny says. "Do not beg, girl. Discipline yourself."
