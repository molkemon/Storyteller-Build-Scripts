My monarch, dire news from the Yenaze Mezhi. This year’s harvest has come in and, in effect, it has not. The meagre fields we possess are insufficient or simply lie fallow due to lack of sufficient labour, and are barely enough for the peasantry to sustain themselves with. We have been reduced to importing grain from other nations, much to our dismay. The populace is outraged, branding us “cereal killers” after a spate of deaths to starvation. Private granaries are being ransacked by hungry peasants even as nobles are being beaten in the streets for their failure to ensure a good harvest, and this trend looks likely to continue until we resolve this issue.
