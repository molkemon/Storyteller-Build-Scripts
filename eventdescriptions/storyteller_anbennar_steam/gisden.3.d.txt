With Lord Ostan’s success in negotiating with the robber barons, many have begun to give his words increasing importance. As one of the most experienced warriors and commanders in the whole of the Borders, Lord Ostan was quick to offer his opinion to any who would listen, frequently advocating for better relations with the militant Wexonards. After court meetings, he can be seen conversing with young and promising officers, as well as fellow veterans of the Lilac Wars. Many in court seem to welcome his vision of what Gisden’s future should look like.  But even as his uncle insists upon furthering military efforts and establishing new, powerful alliances, Count Benar V still seems to do little of worth. Rumors have even begun amongst the nobility that the Count has lost himself wholly in his musings and studies, preferring fantasies over the woes of reality.
