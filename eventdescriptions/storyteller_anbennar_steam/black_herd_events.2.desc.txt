The camp lies silent tonight. For weeks I have labored with the other mages, poring over every scrap of knowledge about Markhentakruan we could assemble. I have listened to dozens of stories and read endless pages of theories. Now the time has come to assemble everything we have learned. It feels right, holding the elders’ knowledge in my hands. So much was lost to the folly of the Great Incursion, and yet piece by piece I have begun to rebuild it. The others watch me from the edges of the cleared circle, their eyes bright with fear and reverence alike. Wordlessly I set the notes aside and begin the incantation. The air grows heavy. For the first time in years, the magic of the Black Herd breathes again. Heat fills the air as the flesh of the freshly hunted almruk begins to bubble and hiss. Then, all at once, the spell is broken. The corpse bursts with a sad wet pop, its fluids oozing into the soil. This was certainly not the devastating detonation that made the spell so feared, but a useful first step nonetheless. The spell successfully bound itself to the corpse without affecting the surroundings, and the corpse did begin to transmute, just not explosively. We must return to our studies and determine what went wrong.
