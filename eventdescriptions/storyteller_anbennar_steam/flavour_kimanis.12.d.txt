So saith the mob. We have begun to surge outside Kimánis. What leaders we do have now murmur low. They say that there has been a small invasion of foreigners who will perform a show. Their drab attire doth lack a tad for luster. Their soldiers do not show dramatic flair. Their officers are shaky on their line reads. Their stage they will perform on is quite bare, a cause for overjoy. Here is Kimánis, whose weapons glow with colors of the sun. Our waving banners, silken fine yet glimmer, drunk with our wine, we cheer a battle won. And now the foreigners flee fair Kimánis. These foreigners forgot one simple rule. Their warplay lacked a fitting grand finale, which fair Kimánis deals to their last man.
