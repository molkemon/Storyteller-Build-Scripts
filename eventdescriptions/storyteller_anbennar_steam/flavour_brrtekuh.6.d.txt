The punishment for the Head Priests of the Vog Mekza involved in the coup wasn't a hanging on the streets; there was no flogging or execution, as the so-called civilized races preferred. The accused were escorted to the desert and were forced to march, day after day, with no food or water. As they died, their bodies were abandoned, their meat or blood left to waste, the greatest shame possible for a follower of Kvanga, for their remains were not worth being recycled and used anew. This proceeded till all were gone; it wasn't the end of the punishment. Their names were crossed from the written record, and their families were prohibited from honouring or mentioning them. A Gnoll dies twice, you see. first when they stop breathing and a second time, when somebody says their name for the last time. Still, a question remains. what to do with the rest of the clergy? They were involved in the rebellion, but some claim that they were manipulated, others that they have seen the error of their ways. The council is faced with a choice. use those left to reform the system or continue the executions.
