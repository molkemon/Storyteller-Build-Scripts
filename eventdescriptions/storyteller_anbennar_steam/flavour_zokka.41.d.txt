He sees it first from the top of a ruined tower. A flicker, pale and green, illuminating the edge of the heavens. A celestial entity is leaving what remains of the moon. The world fractures beneath its own weight. The winds change. The seas shudder. The sky pulls tight, as if holding its breath. A young ruler does not pray. A divine power has never seen fit to answer him. He stands beneath silent stars, quill unmoving over a scroll, and watches the shape glide through the void, leaving brilliant ash in its wake. A thousand lights dance below him; banners raised, horns blown, steel readied. The armies gather, and the forces prepare to descend upon them. Neither intend to break easily. Divine intervention, he knows, will not come. And so he seeks an earthly response. The most powerful mages and artificers are on their way. They come to him, slowly but surely, bearing names known far and wide, or not known at all. Not to bow, or to kneel, but to try and halt the end. This is a wager. Buy time with blood. Hold back the maw with swords and sorcery and painful sacrifice, and hope this final spell finishes the creature in the sky. And he watches that creature; the killer of his father's father; glide towards the Sun. Towards the last of the light. The ruler closes his eyes.
