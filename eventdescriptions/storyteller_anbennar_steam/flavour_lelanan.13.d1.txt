The Enahelibe Ritual was most sacred. Something I had read about a great deal, but conducting it firsthand was a very different experience, one for which I had little zeal. I recited the prayers to Lel perfectly while the Mihipha circled the slowly dancing pair, their voice infused with a magic that excited the participants and readied them for the arousal which hung in their air. That was what made the ritual difficult for me. The couple so tender and loving and sweet, they danced as one, so close together, their hearts moving their feet. And I could not help but think about my inability to participate in the Enahelibe Ritual, which brought great fertility. That was the cost of my being born in the opposite body; I would never carry the life-creating ability. As the ritual continued on, I set aside my feelings, considered the magic hanging in the air. How else could it be used for good? I asked later in quiet prayer.
