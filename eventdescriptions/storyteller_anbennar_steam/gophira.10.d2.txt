The monarch could still hear from the window the tingling of a couple of swords clashing in the distance, but it was the cacophony of doors kicked open, broken windows, and cries of Urvand's inhabitants that was filling their ears. Sounds of victory. They were marching through the palace, directed to the throne room, to confront Larankar's ruler. The ruler fought tooth and nail for his capital. they and the monarch dueled under the city's walls, without reaching a conclusion, both wounded and unable to continue; the pulsating pain of the deep cut the monarch got in their upper arm would persist for months. There was discussion among his generals on how to treat the defeated enemies. some were for heavy punishment; kill both the ruler and his heir for their defiance; others hoped the monarch would be graceful in victory and show mercy on the enemies.
