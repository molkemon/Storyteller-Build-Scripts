Sado closed her ledger with a quiet thud. All of these accounts seemed too good to be true; especially when put beside one another. She put her gaze to Zheng, who wore her ceremonial mask as if it would conceal her guilt. Certainly, it made it hard to read her face. For years, the entire government had been slowly closed off from Sado, and now she sat facetoface with the woman who was, more likely than not, responsible. "After months of not contacting me beyond the bare minimum, you suddenly decide I need to see all of these stories.?" Sado asked, deadpan tone betraying her true feelings. The maiden across from her clapped her hands. Her practiced speech failed to conceal her fear from Sado. "That is exactly why I'm bringing them to you. With the recent changes in administration, I thought that you might like to know how things are progressing around our realm." Sado gripped her ink brush tightly. She put on her best facade of patience. "I don't suppose you have been trying to lock me out of government?" Zheng did not react. Instead, she said, "You would need proof for an accusation like that. As far as I am aware, you have none." She placed a bottle of Mijiu onto the table, pulling it up from around her feet. "Here. Consider this my offering for this meeting. Everything we do is for Huszien's wellbeing; and that means yours, too." Sado could only watch on as the handmaiden walked off and out of view. This problem wasn't going to be fixed quickly. She looked at the Mijiu. Perhaps Holi had a point.
