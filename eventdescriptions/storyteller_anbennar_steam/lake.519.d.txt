The next ship from Tzidoilhol arriving in our harbor after Jargal Selkäreppu came back from his mission was surprisingly well maintained for a state in tatters. But our Khoidarg could not be fooled, for he knew more about the financial situation of Tzidoilhol than the people on this ship. Before the representative of Tzidoilhol stepped into his office, the monarch had already placed the revised Ultakal decree on the opposite side of the desk. It took almost three minutes of thinly veiled begging interspersed with accusations before the representative noticed the document in front of him. After reading it quickly, he made it clear how appalled he was by the mere suggestion that he would accept this kind of insult to his great city. After two minutes of listening, Khoidarg, still without having said a single word, became bored by the charade and started studying the backlog of reports on his desk. After being ignored for two more minutes, the Tzidoiholi representative stormed out in anger.
