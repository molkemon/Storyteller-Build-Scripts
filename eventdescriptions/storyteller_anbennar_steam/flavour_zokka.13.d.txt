They will say that the sky over the Queen's Throne cracked when it finally fell; split in half, green light boiling towards the heavens as the fortress came undone. The stones peeled like feathers, they will say, catching fire midair and fluttering down like flakes of snow. A monument to a dead power, they will call it. Just another ruin in Zokka's long shadow. Farther inland, Lazzaward waits. It glows in the morning like a rotten tooth, blue and white. Wished into being, according to the old stories. Lahmaš still breathes at its summit, eyes closed. Dreaming of suns, probably. The debate is brought to the foot of EduzZokka, once again. "It cannot be allowed to stand." The leader of the Kaxhkeddax has a voice like broken glass. "The Throne fell, and so must the mountain. There can be no exception." The leader of the Kelmaxherho does not answer immediately. The ruler of the Kelmaxherho studies the skyline; how the dawn catches against the towers of the ziggurat, how the wind bends its banners but falters against the flames. "You would make a pyre of a miracle," the ruler says at last. The leader of the Kaxhkeddax shrugs. "A miracle, yes. That's why it must burn. Let them understand." "Understand what?" The leader of the Kelmaxherho turns. "That we don't abide the wonders that came before us? The knowledge within them?" "It's not about wonder," the Kaxhkeddax spits. "It's about fear. Wonder is a luxury they no longer possess. They must fear us. That is the cost of resistance. That is dominion." The leader of the Kelmaxherho steps forward now, voice rising. "And what of the Kurešimi? The artisans? The scribes? Break the peak and we break anything worth finding." "We came to unmake." The leader of the Kaxhkeddax growls. "If they're so skilled, let them wish another mountain." The silence that follows is taut. From above, far above, the jade glint of the rising sun can be seen reflecting off the lapis summit. A shimmer, almost divine. Almost. Zokka is somewhere up there, they know. Watching, waiting. Whether the mountain stands or falls, after all, it already belongs to him.
