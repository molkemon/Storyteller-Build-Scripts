One of the counsels for a rural region seeking to divide up the funds for the prosperity of education and faith have found it increasingly hard to move forward with what to spend funds on. They have asked for your second opinion, my liege. The local leaders have both proposed a variety of ideas, but two seem to be the most popular. the construction of a school under the guidance of one leader, or provisioning the funds to all locals for their pilgrimages towards advancing personal understanding of the world. Both ideas are, of course, feasible and positive, but there is only so much funding to go around, and for smaller councils like this, there can’t be that many resources devoted. The leader of the school begs to provide proper education to this outskirt area, pointing out the lack of access to other important resources without miles of trekking hills and plains. The other leader points out that the villages within the zone have not complained despite surveys and voyages by scholars being done. What is your decision, my liege?
