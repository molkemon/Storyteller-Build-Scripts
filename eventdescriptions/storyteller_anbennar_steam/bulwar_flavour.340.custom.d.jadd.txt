The oasis city of Zirrbit on the southern edge of the Salahad shelters in the shade of a large sandstone plateau. A series of stairs hewn into the stone allow a determined pilgrim to ascend the heights and reach the Palace of Blessed Winds, the Zikavarvrem, atop the plateau. Carved out of the rosy sandstone itself, the monastery’s clever use of wind chimneys ensures that even during the most oppressive midday heat, the constant winds rushing across the plateau provide the interior of the Palace with a cool breeze. Complementing this are the intricately decorated fountains fed by deep aquifers that nourish the small gardens hidden in the shaded courtyards of the monastery, inviting meditative contemplation. But the Palace of Blessed Winds, or EkluHalwašag as it is formally known, is not just a meditative retreat. It is here that the Militant Jadd maintains its regional headquarters and trains its members to endure the harsh conditions of the desert in their pursuit of justice and protection for the faithful. From the time the first cool fingers of dawn touch the plateau to the sunset, when the entire Palace appears aflame in the light of the setting sun, acolytes of the Militant Jadd can be observed practising on the training fields beyond the monastery on the edges of the plateau.
