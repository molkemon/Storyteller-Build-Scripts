Today we do not sound the drums of battle; we sound the drums of peace. I play the drum and sing to you the jelli's tales of Fojara, bravest of lions and king of beasts. The drums are at peace today, but it was not always so. Once the drums beat out as Fojara battled against the first of his wives, and on this peaceful evening I will now tell you of this time of war. Goxma the hyena was once the first of Fojara's wives, and their union was stronger than any the lands of Fangaula had seen. But there came a day, after the genies had been toppled from their illgotten thrones, when Fojara and all the totem animals sought to reassemble the corpse of God, so that he would return the world to the pristine state it was meant to be in. When God remembered, nothing would ever change or grow again. not the fruits, not the trees, not the love between a lion and a hyena. And Goxma grew afraid. She defied both Fojara and fate itself to prevent God's rebirth. Using the power of the corpse, she banished both genies and totem animals from our world. But her actions, as Fojara had feared they would, spread chaos and evil from beyond the stars across the world. He saw the spreading horror, and he knew what his first and most beloved wife had wrought. Fojara grew afraid, but he knew that something had to be done. And so, with a heavy heart and the help of Aro the king of the gods, Fojara bound Goxma to the realm of the totem animals, where she is still bound. He visits her each month, for he must devour her before she grows strong enough to slip her chains. And each time he does, he must reach deep within himself to find the bravery he found so long ago. It does not come easily, and it never has, and it never will.
