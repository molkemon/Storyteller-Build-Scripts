The expedition that was sent out into the caverns has finally returned after a long while, and with them they escort a small band of trolls. They are much larger than expected, some even towering at twice the height of most orks. Their hides are scarred, and eyes are heavily lidded. The scouts report a settlement nearby, not too far from where they captured the band. It is a small town crudely carved into a cave wall, with fungi farms clinging to damp rock and crude tools fashioned from bone and iron. The trolls claim the rumbling they heard was the sounds of a mining operation, and that they had no idea our own band was nearby until we appeared from the dark. Many feel that it would be wise to keep these trolls, as they have strong backs and would be invaluable labor in the hold we will one day claim. Others argue this is not worth the potential bloodshed, and that it would be wiser to allow the trolls to go free, with a gift of some iron, so as to not provoke further hostilities. The leader of our scouting expedition claims that our forces could easily raid this Troll town and that they have a large stockpile of gold and other treasures, likely plundered from travelers in this area. How shall we handle this?
