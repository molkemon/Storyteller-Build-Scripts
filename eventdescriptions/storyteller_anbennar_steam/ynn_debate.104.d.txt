The ongoing debate about the nature of their faith has consumed the thoughts of almost all in the capital, and of many across the realm. But not all. There are those who pay no heed to the great River, who deny its sacred wonder. In their distraction as they seek to resolve the religious dispute, they have seen opportunity. Swords have come down from the walls, farmers have taken up pitchforks; they think that a little revolt while they are focused elsewhere will allow them to win greater local autonomy and decreased taxation.
