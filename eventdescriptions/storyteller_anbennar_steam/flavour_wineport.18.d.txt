The monarch had stumbled onto the barge, already halfdrunk on libations offered by the roving riverside servants there, and had danced, sung and partied as hard as they could. The best way to make an impression on the Esmaris, they had been told, was to party even harder than them. A difficult task to achieve, of course, but one they took to with gusto, even impressing the Esmaris by introducing them to the traditional ‘Wineporter Sway’, a dance one performed all the better the drunker one was. After such a display, the Esmaris on the boat gathered around, whispering amongst themselves. Then, they approached the monarch with a bottle, the one they had been seeking; Milk of Minara. The monarch reverently poured them a glass, and gulped down a mouthful of the wine. The Esmaris watched on, with anticipation in their eyes. ‘How does it taste, my monarch?’ There was only one word that came into the monarch’s head.
