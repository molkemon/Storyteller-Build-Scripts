Kobolds are known for their fast and rough lives, with many dying young slain by adventurers, lost in mining accidents, or dead testing their own traps, and yet these people thrive and multiply. Short lives, yes, but short childhoods as well, with Kobolds reaching adulthood faster than most other races on Halann. Communally raising their hatchlings also lifts the burden from the parents to the Kendia, so Kobold generations are more numerous than any of their contemporaries, save perhaps for Halfings. This fast reproduction and quick growth is a survival trait, and has seen the Kobolds endure through hardship and conflict for centuries. But what happens when Kobolds don't die young? In a specific province, their growing wealth and stability has shown them the answer. Kobolds as far as the eye can see. One of the most successful Trivu has reached a breaking point, and shattered into three, with the two new Trivuak now expecting to undertake the traditional postdivision migration and settle land of their own.
