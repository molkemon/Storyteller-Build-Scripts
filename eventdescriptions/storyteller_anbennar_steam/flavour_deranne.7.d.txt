Before every farmer stands a plowing, before every merchant a bargain, before every soldier a battle, and before every ruler a decree. The monarch, standing upon the balcony outside their chambers, studies the nightingales singing their morning song in the courtyard below. Beside the ruler stand two great men, advising the monarch upon the matters of the old Lorentish nobility, who continue to undermine the authority of the crown at every turn. To the ruler's right stands such a noble, though one having long since thrown his lot in with his new suzerain, for Enri sil Rhumidaire has a favorable position in the Derannic court. Opposite Enri stands a grizzled old general, Artus Revaíne. Not a conventional man to ask for advice on courtly intrigue, but the monarch will value bluntness today. "Reason can be made with the wounded man, for he fights to live," Enri pleads. "But strike a fatal blow and the man will spend his final breaths to claw you down with him. These nobles will come around, but force their hand as you suggest and they will go down in a torrent of flame and ash." "When you find a sick crop, do you let the disease spread across the field?" Artus counters. "Do you refrain from cutting off a hand in the hopes the infection doesn’t fester? If we put up with this disobedience, what precedent does it establish? Their nobility has bickered and disrupted order like petty children for long enough, and if they go down screaming, so be it." A sudden gust of wind sweeps through the courtyard as the monarch makes their decision.
