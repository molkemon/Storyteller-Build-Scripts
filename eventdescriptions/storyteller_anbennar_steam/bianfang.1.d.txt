In the throne room of the capital, the lord of the dynasty pushes two objects toward the Marshal kowtowing before them, Jianlin Shi. The first is an ornately carved sandalwood chest, with fine gold inlay depicting a mighty harimari warrior vanquishing a horned ogre. In the box is a scroll composed in the lord’s elegant calligraphy, a letter extolling the virtues of the ruler of Hubao, fellow guardian of the borders of Yanshen. It notes their clear martial prowess, having been chosen by their fellow soldiers to lead them, and their deft skill as an administrator and protector of the hillfolk clustered around the fort. It subtly weaves in Hubao's history of 'Conditional Obeisance' to the Phoenix Empire and its successors, submitting to a greater power while retaining some autonomy. It makes clear that the lord is similarly courting the Tiger Fort to be an honored subject of the realm. The second object is a simple iron dao, the symbol of authority for a Marshal leading the armies of the realm and administering the territories they conquer. Such a force had already mustered, ready to march to the border at Szinbao. Marshal Shi finishes the kowtow and looks up at their sovereign, waiting for them to choose.
