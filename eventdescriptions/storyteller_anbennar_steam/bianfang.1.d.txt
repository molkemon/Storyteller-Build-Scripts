In the throne room of the capital, the monarch of the ruling dynasty pushes two objects toward the Marshal, who is kowtowing before the ruler, Jianlin Shi. The first is an ornately carved sandalwood chest, with fine gold inlay depicting a mighty warrior vanquishing a horned ogre. In the box is a scroll composed in the monarch's elegant calligraphy, a letter extolling the virtues of a fellow guardian of the borders of Yanshen. It notes their clear martial prowess, having been chosen by their fellow soldiers to lead them, and their deft skill as an administrator and protector of the hillfolk clustered around the fort. It subtly weaves in the domain's history of conditional obeisance to the Phoenix Empire and its successors, submitting to a greater power while retaining some autonomy. It makes clear that the monarch is similarly courting the Tiger Fort to be an honored subject of the realm. The second object is a simple iron dao, the symbol of authority for a Marshal leading the armies of the realm and administering the territories they conquer. Such a force had already mustered, ready to march to the border at Szinbao. Marshal Shi finishes the kowtow and looks up at his sovereign, waiting for the ruler to choose.
