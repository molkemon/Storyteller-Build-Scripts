As the monarch calls a council of state to order, a dozen palace eunuchs draw daggers from their longsleeved robes. Cursing the monarch as a tyrant of the first degree, a threat to the tranquility and prosperity of the realm, whom they must remove from power for the good of the country, they surround the monarch, only to find their thrusts met with the monarch’s armor concealed beneath their robes. Fearing just such an attempt on their life, the monarch has taken to wearing it at all times, even to bed. Hearing the monarch's shouts, the palace guards burst through the door and quickly hack the traitorous eunuchs to pieces. The monarch now faces the choice of what to do with the rest of the sorry lot. Proclaiming ignorance of the plot, the remaining eunuchs would surely be loyal if spared, and unable to amass the influence which had allowed the clique to even consider such a brazen scheme. However, the attempted assassination of a monarch is an act abhorrent to the laws of nature, and must be treated accordingly. Justice must be done, and be seen to be done.
