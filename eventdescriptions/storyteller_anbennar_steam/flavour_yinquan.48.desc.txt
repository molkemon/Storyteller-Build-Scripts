The first directive towards cultivating the practice of agriculture was simple. Huszien decrees that there will be no more privately owned land. It would all return to the purview of the state, who would grant those living on it permission to use the land as they see fit, so long as appropriate tribute was paid to the realm. For smaller, less greedy landholders, this is not that bad; they will probably end up with more or less the same land that they had before. For the influential aristocracy, however, those with the resources to do so have raised armies, funded by themselves, in a desperate bid to stop the state from taking their land, in full knowledge that they defy Huszien's decree to hand over their land. Until they are dead, reorganizing their domain is a risky move. Rout their armies and kill the leaders.
