The monarch looks down at the assembled expedition, perfectly split in two between the assembled ranger and miner contingent. The idea was that the rangers were more talented in tracking and had some combat experience, while the miners were more comfortable working in the dark of the caverns. The monarch had hoped that the two groups would be able to work together without conflict, but clearly that was not the case. The miners were distrustful of anyone who would willingly choose the open air over the comfortable embrace of the mountains, and vice versa. With a sign, the monarch looks back at the scouting report that was made. A decision had to be made. Which group should take control over the expedition?
