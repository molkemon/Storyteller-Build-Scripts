Historically, the nobles of the Hwalbaek have often led their own private cavalry armies prior to being placed under a unified command by JeongHun Gyo. These private cavalry could get bogged down by nepotism, with relatives gaining additional training, opportunities, and advancement due to their pedigree rather than meritocratic promotion. A local member of the Hawlbaek has come, demanding that his son be instated as a general to hold the western front. The man refers to him as a competent and skilled leader in battle, and even desires a higher payment than usual, citing the results of his expert parental tutelage.
