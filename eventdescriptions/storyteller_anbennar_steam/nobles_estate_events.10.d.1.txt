As the state and its bureaucracy grow there is an evergrowing need of people to oversee and administrate various lands and functions. The nobility have traditionally been more associated with military services to the state but have increasingly made it clear that they still expect to be first in line when government offices are being handed out. Merchants on the other hand claim that the nobility considers such appointments as mere rewards and will use them only to enrich themselves. Instead, merchants argue we should appoint men out of individual merit. The unexpected death of a trusted secretary has left a vacant position to be filled at the center of this conflict, with merchants supporting one candidate and the nobility expecting us to pick another. While both candidates are talented men this has to be a political decision.
