The monarch watched as the parades continued. These were not the ones of triumph, mind you. Those were for later, when the price of defiance had been made clear to all. For now, the parades were ones not of silver and salt, but of slaves, shackled prisoners, their eyes forced low in the shame of their attempted rebellion. Emperors past knew well. Authority is enforced by blood, preferably the blood of those who attempt to seize it. And that meant that thousands today would be brought before the statue, forced to kneel and beg for forgiveness. And where one was granted that forgiveness in the form of a quiet enslavement within the palace, nine more were executed right then and there, to the raucous cheer of the ecstatic masses. And for a time, authority would suffer no great defiance once more.
