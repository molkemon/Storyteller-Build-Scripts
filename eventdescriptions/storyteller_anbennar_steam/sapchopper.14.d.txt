Together, Khagor, Ruuk, and their finest stepped upon the Path. And as Ruuk had thought, the box had guided them. Yet, when they stepped off, Khagor and Ruuk found themselves alone in a place like no other. Here, the sunless sky was a rainbow of colors, caught in an unknowable twilight. Beneath it, the foliage seemed to dance before them, following the tune of an unseen chorus of creatures. Even the air itself felt alive, thick with a strange, vibrant energy. This was the heart of the Deepwoods; where the Feyrealm crossed the Prime Material plane. Yet, for such wonder, neither ork could heed any attention to it. Not when the two caught sight of a single slender ashen figure awaiting before them. They were an avatar of nature’s beauty, stirring the most primitive of instincts. “The Blackened Prince…” Ruuk whispered. Never had Khagor heard such a name. But, somehow his heart knew it. The Prince smiled at him, as though reading his mind, leaving him chilled. How could such a soft sight stir such fear within him? “Yes, Wondrous Wanderer, Chivalrous Conqueror.” The Prince spoke. “It was my own voice.” The chieftain stared dumbfounded as he recalled the dark whispers that had filled his mind all these years.
