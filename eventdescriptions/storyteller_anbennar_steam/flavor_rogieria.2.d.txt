He is ready to see you. The delegation from the Sons of Dameria was ushered into a hall where Lothane Bluetusk Silmuna sat at the head of a long table, his son Ellís sitting at his side. My lord Silmuna. The ruler stepped forward to address him. The Sons have come some way since the defeat of the Dookanson. It is no longer a mere company of exile lords and soldiers. Escann is a home for us now. The Sons are a country. In honour of your noble uncle, who led us through so many of our early years, we are called Rogieria. I am pleased, my friends. Is that the sole reason for your visit? A country needs a king, my lord. With your uncle's death, you are the sole rightful heir to Dameria. We come to offer the crown to you. Lothane looked away for a moment. I am sorry, my friends, I cannot. My place is here, with the Corintar. I have a sworn duty to fulfil Corin's legacy, to finish her dream. What of you, my lord Ellís? After your father. Ellís held up his hand. My place is also here. I have no desire to be a ruler, let alone a king. My lords, we must have a Silmuna of the rightful line. I beg you to reconsider. Lothane looked behind them. Rogier. What do you say? The delegates turned – in the corner of the room, sat another halfork they had not previously seen – Lothane's second son. His gaze was fixed on the opposite window as he pensively stroked his beard, but there was a burning light of ambition in his eyes. I shall do it. I shall be your king. As they left, one of the delegates, an elf, turned to another. You know, Varilor, there is something about him. He reminds me of Vincen.
