I wander the grounds of the palace, mingling with the various guests, almost all of which I do not know, yet all act as if they know me, trying to curry my favour. A merchant speaks to me of foreign trade routes, before he is shoved aside by a noble, who in turn is shoved aside by a delegate from another land. As the night goes on, I am spoken to by diplomats trying to convince me to get involved in some local spat, by both sides involved in the conflict, and an endless train of nobility attempting to further their position at court. By the end of the night, despite having talked to dozens of guests, it seems that truly, I have talked to no one. They sing my praises, talk of my glories, and then ask me for handouts. Thus, I decide to, at least temporarily take my leave, exiting one of the dozens of ballrooms, to stand before the gardens, and watch the setting sun. It is then, seeming to confuse me for some sort of minor noble, I am met by a human woman, clearly by her garments one of my dozens of gardeners, who offers me a glass of wine. I think, in that moment, to tell her who I am, but eventually decide against it. I offer the glass, and begin to converse with her. Of life in the realm, of her life, of her struggles, her faith. We joke, we laugh, and for the first time in decades, it feels as if I have had a truly pleasant conversation. Though it feels like no time at all, soon, the sun has set, and eventually I take my leave.
