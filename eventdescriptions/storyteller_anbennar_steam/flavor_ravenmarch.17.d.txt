It was not an easy decision to make, but for one ever burdened with rule, when has it ever been easy? Perhaps now we can have an end to the recent grief. our people can grow and prosper past the bloody past. The meeting place is a secure, but remote, facility. Arranged between seasoned veterans and zealous Carmine Judges of Nerat, you await this representative of the night with apprehension. At last, an appearance is made. An entrancing pale lady, an imitation of a Corvurian noblewoman, strides through the door and charms the flanking guards with a disarmingly pleasant smile. "I come before you with a proposition, your grace," she says with a mocking curtsy. "We have both had some trouble with my unruly cousins as of late. As you are well aware, the Masquerade of old seems to be rather defunct since you reclaimed your rightful titles. As someone who is influential among the families, perhaps we can rein them in together?" You find yourself absorbed in her words, and those fangs. "They won't be happy about it, but the first step of our partnership," she grasps your wrist with a cold hand, "will require a not so small contribution on your part. namely, they need some oversight. Your very serious judges here and their threatening executioners will do nicely. Just make sure they know their place."
