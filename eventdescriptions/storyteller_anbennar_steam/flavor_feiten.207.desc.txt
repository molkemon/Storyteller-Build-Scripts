Dare you step aboard the newest airship, launching today from the workshops? Be warned, your feet may not touch ground for months. But come now, see the world, through a jellyfish's eyes. The flight over a certain region is the most familiar, but often the most chaotic; constant messenger hawks exchange letters between skyships and skyposts, and the risk of a midair collision is real. The mists preclude any interesting observations, and it is not until a certain location that one can truly admire the vista below, picking out the most impressive High Temple complexes. The warm updrafts will propel you over a certain range, and soon you will fly high above a holy place (and do note, with a smile, that even a place this holy could not deny the utility of having a small skyport nearby). When you arrive in a city and are swarmed by merchants' flying carpets offering wares from a thousand bazaars, conceal any disdain at their inferior craft; there is no need to be impolite. The domain is beautiful, the sunlight warm and a sparkling blue blanket below, but do not lose your edge. You will need all your wits about you when you dock in a port, and trade for the coast's newest gadgets and a certain realm's most erudite publications. If you take aboard a blackrobed advisor, well, consider it an honour (one that should instantly sober you up). Then it is west, ever west. You will cross to a land in a matter of weeks, and if you look closely, you can see the outline in the ocean's patterns. Each skyport here is different, from a certain realm's newfangled designs to the bizarre array in another location (no place would go without). But most importantly, do little but concrete business at a skyport; the various faiths may be united in their appreciation of commerce, but wage brutal political war to determine who has access and control. Make no promises, no allies, and no enemies. Do you grow weary? Good, riding the Great Storm's outskirts should shake you awake as it speeds you on your way. And then, when you see balloons in the distance, where balloons have floated for centuries, but now fly and soar to heights once unimaginable; then, you will know you are home.
