If there was anything that mattered to the ruler, it was wealth. He wanted it, and he wanted it all. He would sit and toss crowns in the air, rolling them between his fingers, rubbing them against his fur and whiskers. He loved them, and his servants made note of this, doing everything in their power to supply him with as many as possible. Strangely, as many as they gave him from the nation's income, from their personal treasuries, and from spoils of war, there always seemed to be room for more. They weren't sure what he did with them, but as long as it kept him appeased did it really matter? After all, there was something nice about his purring when brought full sacks of them. One servant even swears she saw him eat one once. Surely that can't be true? Right?
