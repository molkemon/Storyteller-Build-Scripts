The manor at Brontay Point was alive with laughter and music. Lords of every clan, as well as merchants and guests danced, joked, and talked amongst one another, unlike the old days where clans dared not associate. The Cotterseas regaled everyone with their tales of adventure and danger. The Foulers spoke of their loyal contributions and faith. The Cockerwalls dueled with all manner of weapons, and dared any to challenge them. The Berons spoke of their ancient culture and traditions, and mostly took the Cockerwalls up on their offer. The Moortons tried to pitch people on their investment schemes, and showed off their most prestigious heirlooms. The clans of Haighbobben, the Storm Isles, and Celmaldor looked mostly grateful to be there, and spoke of the ever prosperous Northern Thaw trade. And of course, Darkwood and Saltstrider, the two main clans of Aelantier, spoke of the success of their now united colony, and its ever increasing taming of the west. As the ruler looked upon the clans, who once warred for a thousand years over scraps of grazing land, who butchered each other over cycles of revenge, who were now finally getting along, they smiled. They were, at last, the better of Gerrac Mooring, who in ancient times had led them westward to a new life. And now the sons and daughters of the Greatmoors would get it.
