In our aimless wanderings through our verdant home, we have encountered another tribe of beings like us, yet unlike us at the same time. They call themselves the Matakirua. Even more so than us, they are constantly on the move, never willing to remain in one place longer than absolutely necessary, and always ready to move on in an instant. For this is the lesson they took from their travails in our beautiful but deadly home: movement is life, and standstill is death.
