The ship of state sails under its own wind, and a new hand on the tiller can only change course so much. Whether commoners view the country's government as tyrannical, benevolent, or something in between ultimately comes down to the laws of the land, the bureaucratic and judicial apparatus that administers them, and the local magistrates who enforce them. However, the occupant of the Dragon Throne still holds great sway. The monarch can issue proclamations, appoint, dismiss, banish, and execute officials at will, and use their supreme authority as they see fit. The realm anxiously awaits which direction the newly crowned ruler of the dynasty will steer the country. In recent years, the people have found the government to be ruthless and tyrannical, compelling loyalty through fear, with dissent and weakness being cut out with a razor blade. The ruler, with their malicious drive to gain power at all costs, has brought a dreadful resignation to commoners and courtiers alike, facing no chance of relief from the secret police and the myriad forms of torture at their hands.
