On the shores of lake Nirakhet, the marble-clad temple ziggurat of EduzQašdabad, the Temple of Sacred Waters, or QaarDisrabas as the Tijarikheti call it, gleams in the light of the midday sun. Situated at the outlet of the river, the birthplace of the Mother's Sorrow, the waters of the river and lake alike gently lap at the marble steps rising up to the plaza upon which the ziggurat is erected. While the Sorrow may not be the holy Suran, its importance to the inhabitants of these lands has not noticeably decreased after the introduction of the Sun Cult, and many local priests consider a ritual bath in its waters an acceptable substitute to the purification achieved by a bath in the waters of the Suran. The steep ziggurat is reflected by the glittering waters of the lake, and channels feed the lake water to a series of tranquil pools filled with water lilies that delineate the inner precinct. While Nirakhet, as one of the divine daughters of Nunu, god of freshwater, has been long dead like the rest of the ancient Bulwari pantheon, her memory is still cherished on the banks of the lake that carries her name. Each year, the faithful living on the shores of the lake gather during the winter solstice to mourn her death and praise Surael for defeating the Malevolent Dark and restoring the proper order to the universe. Devotees gather on the shore while the first light of dawn after the winter solstice is reflected upon the tranquil waters, for they believe it to be a potent purification ritual to bathe in the lake at this time, cleansing them and washing away the sins of the past year.
