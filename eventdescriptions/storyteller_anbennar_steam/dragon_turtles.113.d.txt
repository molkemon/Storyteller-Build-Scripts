No, don’t worry about the men in the green hats; I know them. I try to stay in my tidepools, but my gardeners go everywhere I can’t, and they know the soil like they grew out of it themselves. They told me that you’ve got some nice crops growing in that domain, but that the agriculture seemed like it could use some work. You’ve got to remember that a garden is just as much a work of art as it is a treasure, and with just a little bit of effort you can make great crops sprout where once there wasn’t anything at all. I think I’ll have them move some rocks around: you can make the fields out of the most wonderful pathways, and if you plant some trees along the roads you can prevent the wind from blowing away your topsoil. If you choose the right trees, the plants will look as happy as they feel. Happy gardens bring sightseers and traders, and they’ll buy the crops you sprout. If this all sounds complicated, I’d be happy to send some of my green hats to help you get started. You’ve always been good to me.
