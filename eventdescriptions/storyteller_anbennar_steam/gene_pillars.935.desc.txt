Ascending a spiral staircase during your roundabout path through the still navigable portions of the Pillar, the expedition procession screeches to a halt. Pushing your way to the fore of the party, you see the senior mage consultant and the head scholar of the expedition bickering, as the air beyond the threshold before them takes on a black hue. Well, black would be an understatement; in the room before you, it appears as though no light exists. In the attempt to mediate their dispute, you learn from the mage consultant you are witnessing theoretical Chronomancy; a school of magic so advanced, even your most experienced mages only understand how to rework it. Consulting your maps, the party scholars identified this room as the library, and while the tomes within are valuable, pressing on to retrieve them would cause your bodies to freeze in time. The other option is to unstopper the flow of time, allowing your team to pass, but doing so would cause a mana rebound effect, leading to the decay of the valuable relics within. After time is uncorked, a fragment of the spell might be shaved off into a book to keep it preserved. However, the window is brief, and the magic is complex. Your scholars estimate you will only have moments to make a choice, and even then your mages will only have enough power to preserve a single volume.
