A dispute rages in the council chamber today; tension can be felt in the air as wands are raised, ready to unleash a variety of spells. This is not a frequent scene in the council, but it is not the first. The shocking thing is that the councillors involved in this squabble are neither the fussy Wealthy Scions nor the bombastic Heartmages; even the usual crowdrousers are quiet. Both sides of this argument are Devouts of the Flood. Tears of grief and anger spill out as they shout at each other and exchange blows. One Devout lashes out, asking, "Were we not those that heralded the Flood as that which will change Halann? Was our devotion not enough, and punishment is upon us?" Another chastises them, saying, "We are Devouts of the Flood, and its avatar, its voice still leads us even if the skies weep no more." A profound schism has occurred; their belief has been shattered in pieces, and now they try to build a new identity from the ashes. The reason is that the skies are clear; the Crimson Deluge has ended; the Flood is over.
