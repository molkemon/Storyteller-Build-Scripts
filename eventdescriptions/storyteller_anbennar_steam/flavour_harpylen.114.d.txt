The Serpentspine struck the expedition furiously and without warning. From seemingly one moment to the next, a blizzard enveloped the hunters of the Ivetasananthor, halting their ascent and forcing them to find cover or perish. A cliffside cavern was fortunately found, and the hunters hurriedly scrambled inside to wait out the snow and bitter cold. The cave was empty, but bore marks of prior habitation, old campfires and barrels, stocked with fruit and salted meat. Some hunters mused what nomads would live in this manner, on the roof of the world, and it was in this state, huddled by a pitiful fire and sharing their stories, that the lost harpies of the east found them. Striding into the cave like winged demons, these people bore a sinister aspect to the fair folk of Harpylen, for their skin was a garnet red, and their feathers black. Some hunters gasped, others froze, and some whispered that the infamous "Oni" of the east had come to devour them. Yet when they spoke, it became clear that these were harpies, for their voices were musical, and their words were clearly understood. This group was a border patrol, and the hunters had invaded one of their storerooms. The hunters were quick to explain their task, and the members of the lost flocks were willing to aid them in navigating the mountains. With the Serpentspine behind them, the hunters entered Nomsyulhan, and found there that the lost flocks had made a home in this place. They had largely integrated with hobgoblins, but a distinct group from the Command of Rahen. Working in unison, they had forged great works in their isolation, not least of which was the great roost of Waben Donpun, a city built in an ancient caldera, made for the comforts of harpies and hobgoblins both. The lost flocks had found their home, and had taken for themselves a new name, "Nuzenapu."
