It was during one of the tea ceremonies attended by the local governors, where discussions revolved around local policies and the state of affairs. While this was a common event for the court, an unusual incident occurred during the meeting when the lord accidentally spilled tea, staining their precious outfit. Servants hurriedly rushed to their aid, ensuring their wellbeing, but they raised their hands and instructed them to return to their duties. They then turned their attention to the rest of the court. The sudden change in their demeanor made everyone else in the room nervous. Their tone had shifted from uncertain when discussing the Rending and the Temple to a firm and confident one, as if they spoke from a position of experience or knowledge. Whatever the source of this newfound confidence, it was clear that they knew what they were doing.
