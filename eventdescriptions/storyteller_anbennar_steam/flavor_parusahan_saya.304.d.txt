Deep in the sprawl of Sarisung's rundown neighborhoods, the final contingent of the survey corps desperately searches for the final throne of the Harimraj. All other squadrons have returned home, given the order to call off the search. These few soldiers instead chose to ignore it, effectively deserting from the army to continue the search, clinging to whatever hope they may have left. They have arrived at their final section, a bundle of houses so obscure that not even the paupers live within their walls. Here they look for a sign that the throne lies within, the last spark of hope slowly fading into nothingness. Suddenly, a shout echoes off the cramped walls of the dwellings. A soldier frantically runs from a house, waving his arms in unrestrained excitement. He turns and the corps follows, rushing over piles of rubble and rotting wood until they arrive at the source of his alarm. One wall of the building he was searching has a door cut into the side, seemingly extending into the side of a sheer rock face, and faintly emblazoned above the portal is a purple lotus in full bloom. The men stumble over each other as they rush down the tunnel, a glint of light visible at the very end. Finally, they all emerge together, and what they find sucks the air from their very lungs. A simple throne, carved from granite and placed on a dais, sits within the center of the room, illuminated by a single beam of light. Upon further inspection, they see that the rock is not just cut into the shape of the throne, but adorned with ornamental details so small they seem impossible. All at once, they shout with glee, and begin sprinting out of the chamber to return to their commanders. Against all odds, they have finally done it. The final throne of Harimar has been reclaimed.
