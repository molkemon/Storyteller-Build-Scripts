You are lost in what remains of your memory now. You laugh with wild glee, a smile plastered across your face. In your mind’s eye, you are dancing across an endless field, running your hands through the flowers and breathing in their smells. You do not see the trees which blacken and bend as you pass them, nor would you care if you did. There is indecision in the air. This way, that way, the beings in your path cannot decide. It is easy to pinpoint them. You find, first, a greenhaired woman, with three knights in front of her. You ignore them, letting their swords slash at you as you plunge your arm into her chest. The knights collapse to the ground, only puppets. She goes limp. You shudder as you draw her childishness into you. You find, second, an angry, distasteful elf. He faces away from you, staring off into the distance. What is he looking at? You do not question it. Instead, before he can turn around, you have punctured him. You shudder as you draw his longing into you. Then you find a kindly old elf. He holds a budding flower in his hand, and offers it to you. You cast it aside and take his soul instead. You shudder as you draw his sagacity into you. You still do not feel whole. You expected more, but the rest are ghosts. It has been a long time, and much has changed. You do not much recognize these woods anymore.
