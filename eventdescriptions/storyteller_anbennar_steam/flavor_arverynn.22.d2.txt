Keep standing, your majesty. Just a few more minutes and it will be ready. The ruler stood in their imperial armour, a solid plating as smooth as a diamond with the shades of emerald and aquamarine, a vermillion hilt and an aqua gem embedded in the sword grip, with the pure metal of the blade halfinserted into the scabbard, which their other hand gripped. Of course, the ruler was only part of this picture; behind them, a few fields of rustling greenery gave way to the ruler's jewel, the city of Arverynn itself. Three were the things asked by their Ynnic patrons: rivers, knights, and castles. And this painting was no different, only trading the castle for a city. They felt the need to search for something more. Even if they weren't sure what. Their sharp eyes kept scanning the streets and the riverside. Oh, of course. Their eyes were posed on a Stenurynn noble, of leather garments and ruffy white hair, preceded by an adorable animal that was wagging its white tail at the passerby. One more stroke, and the sketch was finished. Three days later, the final painting was completed and presented to the ruler.
