When we declared ourselves as the three hundred thirty-second empire, some heralded us as noble fools, masochists willing to sacrifice our claim to the eternal seat as the last empire destined to fall. The prophecy hanging over our heads became an incentive to rebellion; the echoes of the accelerationist empires, who sought to rush to the three hundred thirty-third Empire, resurfaced with none of their lessons learned. Yet we held strong, confident in our belief that surrendering would invalidate our claim as a true empire. We knew we could never turn back so close to the end of the prophecy. But when the Ashhana struck in the north, we were crippled, and our empire fell into a dire civil war. We languished and declined across the next millennium, ever more dismissed as an archaic echo of ourselves. Finally, Sykar took Zerat, our capital, and Zatsarya. In hindsight, maybe we should thank him; without him we might have accepted our slow fate, resigned to history passing without our involvement. His gall to claim the title of prophet and the folly of the naga in supporting him reminded us of our purpose: to remember our fated role as Penultimate Empire, to test any claimants and push them and ourselves to the limit. We may have vanquished the Riverclaws’ false claims, but they reminded us of our duty, whether that was their intention or not. And now we stand, ready to fulfill our role to an extent worthy of the Final Empire. Sarhal stands under our rightful boot; no threat could possibly emerge to oppose what will come after us. The Ashhana have been returned to the swamp where they belong, or else to eternal service for their crimes. To others, we are deluded tyrants, the monsters they depicted us as for so long. But why should we care what they say? To the Ichiadza, we are heroes, the guardians of peace and prosperity, the saviour from a time of barbarism and war. And we shall continue to fight and rule for them, to make the promised empire perfect as it can be.
