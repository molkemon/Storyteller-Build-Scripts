I grow weary. My back aches when I rise for the morning sun. Where once my mind worked faster than that of all around me, now I am the one left struggling to keep up. Where once my stride was effortless, now every step feels like the last groaning movement of some ancient giant. I cannot do this much longer. And yet I must. The Bongo have no one else able to lead them as effectively as I. But I am no fool. A successor will be required. However, I have no children—my womb lies barren. But in my dreams, I see her. My child. My beautiful, perfect child. I will make her. But what material to use? Straw is plentiful and cheap, but lacks the space for such complex magics as I am about to undertake. I could also work her out of wood, sculpt her out of clay, or carve her out of stone. All would cost me greatly, but the potential of those materials is far stronger. What to choose?
