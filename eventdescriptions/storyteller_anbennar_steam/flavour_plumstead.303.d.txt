For hours they argued while Milon waited, not wanting to get between the professor and the captain. Avery was furious; he had expected that his personal adventure band was to go along with them, which made sense. Tyaphon reminded him that he had hired Avery, not his whole band. The two men were angry as both felt cheated by the other. Fed up, Milon scolded them both and said that they should make up their minds before the whole expedition went to ruins. Shocked, though also impressed by the young student's boldness, they nodded. Tyaphon made it clear that he preferred soldiers from the army to go along with them, as the government was the largest sponsor of the expedition, while Avery insisted on the use of his own men as he was to lead the expedition. Not able to get to a compromise, they asked Milon to decide, with the promise to respect the decision.
