Civilization has been brough to the east. Streets are filled with celebrations across all Sarda, and Dolindhan emissaries aren't so proud they won't compliment us for fulfilling an ambition older than Arverynn itself.  Between a puff of tobacco and sip of holly tea, enjoying a wellearned rest at a lounge decorated with Krarhnorian curiosities, our advisors are already discussing the best approach for ruling the lands we've conquered, aware that many times in Ynnic history Veykoda appeared all but subjugated only to then rise up and violently throw off all Imperial authority.  Past emperors learned that marching with great armies into Veykoda was a fruitless endeavor, as entire villages would disappear into the mist the moment our forces arrived, only to reappear as vengeful hordes that would assault our cities.  The crowning of Emperor Armynn II in 945 marked a shift towards a diplomatic approach: he laid out the idea of a Pansvovych, a 'Lordship of the East' made up of Veykodan client states. This path also proved arduous, as client rulers were often overthrown and succeeded by heirs who immediately rescinded all ties to Arverynn.  While Krarhnorian governments are more stable than tribes, they change rulers just as fast and are keen on skirmishing with each other, which can quickly escalate into war and annexation when we're not watchful. Our advisors are suggesting that we create strong ties with one particular state, either a weak one to keep the stronger in check, or a strong one to keep the others from challenging it. They advise we give priority to contracting its workers, take their side in territorial disputes and bestow their representatives with the special title of Vyced Pomemnik; Helper of the East, making them the lynchpin of our rule in Veykoda.
