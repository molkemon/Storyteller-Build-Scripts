Under the glow of the twin volcanoes there are many caves that once housed flows of magma waiting silently for our engineers to expand. Muatlaqua is the shallower and weaker of the two volcanoes. It is capable of less destruction should there be an accident, and there will be accidents, in living and working with such dangerous materials; its benefits include less volatility and unpredictability. Quahopilo is the stronger sibling, but is filled with a rage its brother lacks. For the price of greater potential, we will no doubt suffer greater hardship, assuming we ever reach the heights we plan at all under the weight of the setbacks we will no doubt suffer working against such resistance. Either choice is an acceptable place to begin construction. Which should we choose?
