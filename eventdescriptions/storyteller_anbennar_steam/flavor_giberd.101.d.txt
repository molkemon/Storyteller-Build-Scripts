In the great hall of Anvilwright, gnomish and dwarven architects bustle past each other, bringing their own designs to the large round granite table. Mathematicians run the numbers over and over in the corner, bickering amongst each other about the costs, while builders watch on with anxious eyes as designs and plans are discussed, discarded and drawn up once more. The planning stage of the Silverforge’s centuries-dreamt hold drags on, incurring a substantial bill amongst endless debate on how to complete the incompletable in a completable manner. Angered arguments have already come to blows on numerous occasions as budgets are broken and replaced with even higher calculations. Eventually, though, the endless changes finally culminate in something both dwarf and gnome can shake hands and sign upon. With this agreement, Giberd finally receives a letter in the capital, congratulating the government on the completion of the planning phase, before two messengers carry and unfurl a scroll of costs twenty feet tall and two gnomes wide. The monarch, upon seeing the scroll, decides to delegate this task from now on to chancellor Oddannamm, who begins praying to Esmaryl for strength before giving a reply.
