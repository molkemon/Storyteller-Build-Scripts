Most of the debris is soft rock and silt, easily broken up with some hard strikes. Mixed in are assorted pieces of scrap metal and harder rocks. These pieces must be carefully maneuvered and pulled forwards out of the blockages. The pressure of the water behind the debris is both a blessing and a curse, necessitating extra safety precautions. Each expedition member is tied to ropes ready to catch them when the blockage has been weakened enough and decides to suddenly break free. After several close calls with rushing water and flying debris, and a little extra help from water mages standing on top of the dam, the blockages are cleared and water once again flows through the riverbed.
