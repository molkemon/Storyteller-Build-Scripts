There is a chill fog over the Citadel of the Dawn in the dark hours before sunrise. Hiderion has left his most capable advisors in charge while he readies his horse for the narrow road to the deep north and places rich in wisdom. He shoulders his pack, draws his cloak, and sets out shrouded. In the weeks that follow he sees the many wonders of Xia and the Upper Yanshen. He crosses the river bridge into the Jiangliuibao, from which an empire of squabbling masters is managed. He walks the lines of the many mustering fields of Bianfang, where ironhard discipline is instilled into the Yanshe peasantry. Hiderion even makes the journey up Mount Tughayasa to where the oracles see across all Haless, all the twisting bonds and connections that link the ancient continent. Of all the places he has seen, one sticks in his mind as he meditates in the garden upon his return.
