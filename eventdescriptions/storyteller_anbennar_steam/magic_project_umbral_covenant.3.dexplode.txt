Ikomeye is excited; she wanders among the fragments of the zatsarya wall through which the Vile One’s troops, with much effort over many months, have finally blown a gatesized hole. Taken from the site of the zatsarya itself, the wall fragments have been arranged for her inspection, her divinatory insight. Ikomeye always delights when things of great prestige are sullied; be they buildings or kingdoms, human or otherwise. It is her revenge against the powerful. “These fragments will work as sympathetic objects, if we simply wish to divine the layout of this place.” Ikomeye is holding one of them, turning it over in her palm. “Mapping it manually would require an army,” says the Vile One, “but…” “But divination of such an enormous structure will be onerous. It is beyond my craft alone, at least in any good time.” Ikomeye is guileless and direct in her assessment of all things, even her own talents. “And you, knowing the ancient pedigree of the lizard mages, are not stupid enough to walk those halls blind,” Ikomeye says, eyeing the jetlike gleam of the zatsargakh. The Vile One smiles faintly. “Indeed, I am not.”
