For many Mechanim, the sea has proven hazardous in ways the land cannot imitate. While unconfined by the limitations of breath, as the meatcreatures are, they have found themselves the victims of a different force. buoyancy. An injured Mechanim on land can lie waiting for a companion to restore its chassis; a kauga lost at sea seldom receives such care, unless made with such tasks in mind. Retrieving a sunken kauga is nigh impossible; even if the orb is not immediately buried by seafloor currents, combing the abyss to find even one kauga can take years. Seasteel mechanim designs attempt to bypass the worst of this issue. The kauga is carved to be hollow, then sealed shut, so that if the worst befalls a Mechanim sailor, their Kauga will at least float on the surface. Since this adaptation reduces the durability of the Kauga, enhanced plating protects the orb while in place on the body, and a special ripcord allows a sinking frame to release the Kauga for surface retrieval. Bright, water resistant paints also help. For those Mechanim who do not have the privilege of Precursor steel, preparing for sea travel begins with applying many layers of lacquers to limit water’s corrosive effect on the frame. This is a painstaking process, as each component exposed to the sea must be covered. Such efforts are usually a community event conducted in the presence of the frame’s Kauga.
