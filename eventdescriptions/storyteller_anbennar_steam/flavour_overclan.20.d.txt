Recent surveys by various officials have determined that acceptance of our rule by Bulwari humans has been less than expected or desired. Despite the length of time ruled over by our state, as well as continued expressions of tolerance of the Sun Cult, many Bulwari humans continue to hold their suspicions against us, resentfully seeing us as foreign conquerors rather than legitimate rulers. Our tolerance, rather than being seen as a kindness or concession, has instead been viewed as the ignorance of backwards savages. Fortunately, the state's recent adoption of the Jaddist sect of the Sun Cult has gone some way toward addressing these issues. While some zealots decry this interpretation of Sun Cult theology as heretical, most Bulwari humans accept it as a valid manifestation of their faith, and have responded positively to its promotion by us. This has come with a tradeoff, however. Those who do not follow the Bulwari Sun Cults, such as followers of the Cannorian pantheon, harpy adherents of the Hunt, and the dwarves of Ovdal Tûngr, have been agitated by our clear preference for the Jadd over their own faiths. While it had been assumed that the Jadd's racial tolerance would have ameliorated such religious tensions, this has proven to not entirely be the case. Those who follow the so-called Old Sun Cults, too, have largely rejected our religious authority on the grounds that the Jadd's recognition of Jaher as Surael Reborn is unfounded at best, or heretical at worst. A long-term solution to these tensions must be considered for the long-term stability of the state.
