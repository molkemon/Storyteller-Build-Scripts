The tomb was not very glamorous. It was cut into the side of one of the eastern mountains; still monumental and aweinspiring to an untrained eye or a foreigner, or someone who didn't know what it was like to be scorned by the gods of the Sorrow. But still, to get a tomb at all was probably more than what was warranted. When Aakhet sank his draconic maw into Septikhet, becoming one of two individuals in history to kill a Khet, Benukhet saw it all; and in his heart, he knew what it was to be slain. He felt the terror of mortality. And instead of rising to fight, Benukhet saw that terror, and bowed before it. He became the devourer dragon's puppet. Through his short, insignificant reign as a child Khetarch, Benukhet kept that terror in his soul. It became an intrinsic, inseparable part of him. Now, it would be part of Brrtekuh too. The pack descended on the tomb, chisel and pick in one claw, torch and scavenged shotel in the other. They broke the walls, tore undead guard and ancient construct and careful trap asunder, surged into Benukhet's domain like carrion to corpse. It did not take them long to find his sarcophagus; and as the rest of the gnolls stood by, watching, disinterested, unknowing and unwitting, it took the elite among them even less time to devour Benukhet's corpse. The ruler was the first among them to taste his flesh. And at once Brrtekuh knew, in their hearts, in their souls, what it was like to kill a god.
