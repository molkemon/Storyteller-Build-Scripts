Time and again we have discovered rich cultures where we once believed there was only savagery and unenlightenment. Our excursion into Nomsyulhan has proven no different, as those living within the mythical valley have shown themselves to be sophisticated people beneath their monstrous visage. In particular, the Soulseeker ogres are a people with a deep understanding of the spiritual, and what it means to push yourself beyond your limitations. For this reason, the ruler finds himself at the base of a massive staircase cut into the side of a mountain, accompanied by an ogre monk from the foothills of Nomsyulhan. “In Nomsyulhan, what seems to be poverty is truly an abundance of knowledge from those who came before,” the monk explains as the two climb the steps. “Spirits and artifacts guide us through times of scarcity, and through these things we may achieve enlightenment.” The two finally reach the top of the steps, and the ruler is awestruck by the sight before him. massive statues of giant figures carved into the walls of a mountain pass, extending out into an endless grassy plain. “Salvation’s Promise,” the ogre solemnly intones. “A monument created by our Sandmaw brethren when they fled across the step and into Nomsyulhan.” The ogre walks towards the edge of the mountainside, taking in the giant statues. “Many of the statues were present when they arrived, but they expanded on their ancestor's visage to create something truly wonderful. We continue this tradition, honouring the spirits of our land and tempering our souls to better serve our communities.
