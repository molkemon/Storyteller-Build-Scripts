The sun shone greyly upon the hithering of the moors, as the lords gathered on a lonely hill, too barren and bereft for even shepherds or bandits to make use of it. There stood Clan Cottersea, with their hair damp with seasalt, eyes like lantern lights as they laughed and sang and slung back ale after ale. There stood Clan Beron, dark eyed and tall, speaking to one another in ancient dialects long predating the Alenic arrival to the land. There stood Clan Cockerwall, hair auburnwild, hands on their swords and axes as they jeered and yelled to the other clans. Here, the ruler decided, would the fate of the Westmoors be forever changed. Unfortunately, the others disagreed, as a Cockerwall shouting had soon turned to shoving Lord Beron and making a remark about his wife. This, of course, led to the good Lord Beron punching him in the face, causing both clans to begin beating each other senseless. As the monarch stood observing the bedlam, they had but one thought.
