Andil’s surrender was received in the smoldering ruins of his camp in Tabbanor. His aides and lieutenants looked on with solemn stares, their fates sealed now that their attempt to wrest control of the state had failed. General Andil himself, loyal to his troops until the end, had pleaded that only the lives of him and his upper ranking allies be traded to ensure amnesty towards the common soldiers who had joined his revolt. Purging the Khetist hardliners while sparing the common folk was indeed the most prudent action, and it was clear that part of Andil’s request came from a desire to see the best future for Kulugiash, with or without him.
