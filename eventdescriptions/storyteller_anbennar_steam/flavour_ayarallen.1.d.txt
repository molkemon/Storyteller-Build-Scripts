It was in the upper spice market that the most furious argument raged. It was early enough that the smell of Kasiyaru had not yet completely pervaded the courtyard. The shadow of a harpy passing overhead made a few men blanch, but it did not end the discussion.  'My ancestors will spit on me if we accept this, Eni…' said Arakh. His head shook with enough force to make his entire rotund body quiver. 'You will be alive to be spat on.' Eni stood up again, storming over to his stall to pretend to tidy his display.  'As a coward. As a gnoll lover.' Arakh followed him, almost pleading. 'They will walk through these streets, Eni. Will you sell them the kasi? Will you look one of those things in the muzzle like a customer?' 'I will be alive to do so,' Eni repeated, fervent. 'You will be a slave to harpy and gnoll both.' Arakh spat onto the ground. 'That’s not true,' Eni said. 'They intend to reinstate the Akalate. The whole Akalate'  Arakh laughed. "More propaganda. Even if they do, what is an Akal who kneels to a harpy?"  Eni was a small man, but to his credit, he stared his friend down. 'A living one.'
