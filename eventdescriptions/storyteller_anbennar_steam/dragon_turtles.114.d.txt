Let me tell you, I have been around for a long time. I used to sail all over, and everywhere I went there was something going on: parties, the construction of some big amazing thing, you name it. I started most of the parties. We held them for hundreds of years, but every time I went to one it was like I was there for the first time. I never got tired of watching the smiles of someone jumping into the waves, or the sleepy walk of someone who had stuffed their belly a little too full of food and drink. I let them sleep on my back. It was all such a great time. I remember it like it was yesterday. But then, the war came. An empire landed on the island shores, and all of a sudden those parties all dried up. The next time I came to visit, all of the great things I loved to see and admire had been replaced by armories that were making weapons. The next lap around the island I took, the beaches were bloodstained. I won’t forget the invaders, or the look in their eyes. I remember them too. And when I look at you, I see them. I won’t let you take my friends from me again.
