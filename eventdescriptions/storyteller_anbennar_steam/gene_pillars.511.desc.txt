The base of the massive pillar is heavily wrapped by large sturdy roots that dig into the foundations. Along the base, many of these roots seem to be coiling themselves around large slabs of volcanic rock. The structure rising up before us looks heavily dented; we conjecture that it was pelted by volcanic debris in the distant past. Many of these rocks look to have broken through, and countless holes pepper the structure. From these gaps, long vines of some sort of hinuiivy stretch. For now, however, the expedition is singularly focused on reaching the center of the base of the structure. To do so, the expedition must navigate past the immense tangle of roots.
