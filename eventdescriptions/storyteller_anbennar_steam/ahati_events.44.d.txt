The Ahati are a brotherhood of both violence and commerce. In times of war, it is usual for the Grand Master to be given supreme command over every Ahati member, whatever their peacetime position or rank. This tradition allows them to react swiftly and decisively against their foes with the full force of their might, but has also led to terrible wasting of lives when stubborn commanders refuse to order a retreat from a lost cause. War is certainly an extreme circumstance, but the Republic must always be on guard against the corruption of absolute power. As the People’s Council continues to debate reforms of the brotherhood, a decision must be made on whether they enshrine this tradition in law or look to other ways of strengthening their military might.
