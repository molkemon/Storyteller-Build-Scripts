"This is madness," cries the elf as he is led to the gallows in the domain. "How can you not see? That is not the Amber Princess. She is dead. She died centuries ago. Whatever inhabits her body isn’t her. How can you trust the Cult of Rot when they blindly follow whatever that monster is." He is slapped and told to be silent, but still he continues. "You are fools. You lead yourself to death and destruction, all in the name of whatever that being wants. This is not the Autumn you know, the Autumn you love. This is an Autumn of decay, of stagnation, of the end of the world." The stool is kicked. The rope tightens around his neck. He falls silent. The rabble cheers. Elsewhere, in the realm, a priest is speaking to a small crowd. "She was there for us when things seemed at their bleakest. When all seemed lost, she returned. The Second Hibernal Crusade has been a glorious success with her at the helm. She is willing to lend her power to us, and this makes us unstoppable. It is no wonder, then, that she is an object of worship." There are murmurs in the crowd, and a couple boos that are quickly hushed. "When you are cold at night, she is there for you. When you face hardship in your life, she is there for you. Whenever you need strength and find you do not have it, she is there for you. All she asks is that you believe in her." The murmuring is louder this time, and the boos have been replaced by cheers. The priest raises his arms. "Praise be to the Amber Princess," he cries. The crowd cries back.
