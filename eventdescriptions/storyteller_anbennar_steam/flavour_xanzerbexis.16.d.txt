It was a rainy day in Port Xanzerbexis. Reconstruction was still ongoing, and as such, the court was held in a great tent. The inside was hot, cramped, and smelled of wet fur. The monarch was pacing around one of their subjects, who was feeling increasingly unwelcome. "You make it sound easy, Vrestukex. A rarity in war." "It will be, your majesty." The human replied in gnollish. Spoken by a human, it sounded meek. The monarch could smell their fear, but it wasn't showing. Someone had taught them well. "Our spies in Nathalaire claim that the Folly is full of gnolls. The city itself cowers behind the waters. With your fleet, the sea will be their grave." "And you believe the gnolls of the folly will join us?" "I do. They live in squalor. This." the human gestured around the tent. "This is a better life. Why would they refuse?" The monarch could think of a few reasons. But her Vrestukex had always given her good intelligence, especially since the inclusion of humans. Humans blend in with humans, and these days, she had more human subjects than gnolls. This was what she was trying to balance, but the ruler was nothing if not pragmatic. "Scribe." she barked. An older human snapped to attention, his white beard streaked with ink. The monarch began to dictate. A policy for settling Daravan's Folly began to take shape. She offered her Vrestukex a glance, then grunted.
