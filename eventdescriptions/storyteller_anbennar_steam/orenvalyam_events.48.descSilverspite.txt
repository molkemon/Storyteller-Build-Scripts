War was Aelantier. Crucibles from across the continent produced en masse blade and matchlock and spellstaff alike. The shipyards of Noruin and the Cleaved Sea assembled an armada in a day, raising the vessels that would carry them across the sea. The troops trained, and drilled, and hardened themselves for Dawn. On the day of the invasion, as the armies and fleets of Orenvalyam gathered before him, Elrandar Silverspite stood before them, unarmored. A rarity these days. How long had it taken to get to this point? How many sacrifices had he made? How many challengers defeated, detractors and dissenters eradicated. Half the world turned to one single purpose, by his will, his design. And now the machine he had built waited for his order to conquer the other half. Just one campaign of many, to them. Silverspite frowned. A short, sharp pang of guilt, for not giving them a chance to rest, to enjoy even a taste of the paradise that they would buy with their blood. The thought passed, though. It was not for his time to rest, to wait, to stop and think. They still had a job to do. With a semiconscious thought, the Kamrayakval donned his armor. And then, the rumbling. An explosion of steam and smoke, seen by millions, felt by all Halann. A roaring scream of scraping metal and ancient fury, primordial, earsplitting, soulpiercing. And from the mist, arose a Dragonin Armor Form. An eclipse against the sun, twin-bladed, single purposed. There was a knowing, an understanding, from the feyforests of Eordand to the depths of Nanru Nakar, from the slavemarkets of Fangaula to the High Temples of Haless, from the poorest beggar to the richest king, that the end of their world was nigh.
