The mages have taken to asserting an increasingly important position within the government by claiming the right to speak on wider and wider matters, including that of the divine. Outraged, the clergy have responded by arguing for their right to speak on matters of magic. The argument stems from the existence of divine magic giving the clergy a legitimate claim to being mages themselves, which either means that the mages have authority over the divine or that the clergy have authority over the magical. We might seek to grant the clergy dominion over the mages, but the infighting that would ensue could prove to be enormous. Alternatively, the clergy can be removed from any decisions pertaining to magical infrastructure, at the risk of profound backlash.
