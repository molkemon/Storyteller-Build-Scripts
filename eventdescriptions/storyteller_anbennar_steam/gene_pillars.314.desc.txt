Is the territory of an animal any less meaningfully delineated than that of a sentient species? We may nod at our maps and our plots and our fences and proclaim them distinct, but our attachment to such things, to home and to land, is no less emotional than theirs. Such weighty ponderings occupy the mind as one first glimpses the hinuicrocodile, which is really massive. Twice now we have tried to cross; there is no other option, for the weaker internal walls of this pillar have decayed over the eons, uniting three flooded chambers and entirely blocking our progress. Twice the hinuicrocodile has lurched with a speed unimaginable in something so colossal; it is as though a great green hillock grew legs. It is possible that we could hug the edge of the water, but we would forgo the potential secrets of this place.
