Crovis lies dead; his pretence at a crown is no more. We have crushed his army in the open field, and with it the finest flower of the Reach’s youth. There are few rebels left to fight, and a generation must pass before the Reach again has any sons to waste on this folly. Those of noble birth have been made to pledge oaths of loyalty to Brasûr and stripped of all land they own, while the lowborn have had their swordhands crushed with hammers. Such men may once again work a plough, but the independent Reach is dead. We pulled the corpse of the pretender from a half-frozen bog, our White Reachman corpsefinders identifying him by his heraldry; it is impossible to know which blow, or from whom, slew him. Lying beside the great multitude of his fellow youth, he was not especially remarkable in death. Before us still lies the question of what to do with our rebellious west. We have been burned once by this practice of distant rule, which allowed our rebellious vassals to store up resources to hand over to Crovis. On the other hand, the only men left to rule are loyal to us, married into our high nobility.
