Come now, sweetling. It's a very nice tower. It was a strange day when Mitrenya was the one injecting false cheer into her voice, but somehow, fate had led us here. I scowled, crossing my arms. "I'm sure it's lovely, dear, but it isn't home." I had grown awfully fond of the castle; of its mossy walls and warm hearths and secret passages. But, in my heart of hearts, I knew it was time to leave. Mitrenya wouldn't rule anymore, not directly. Her Symposium had offered a rather neat solution to the question of succession—they would rule, whilst she got to spend her days researching, relaxing, teaching anything she wanted. The mages would move from the Crimson Spire into new towers, to be built around the Ynn at some unspecified date. Mitrenya and I would, of course, leave the palace; only the lord or lady had that honour. Instead, we'd take the Spire. Varlengeilt would be an interesting housemate, but he seemed to have mellowed somewhat the last I saw of him. I sighed again, relenting with a squeeze of the hand in mine and a peck to Mitrenya's cheek. She didn't want to rule anymore—she never had, really. Being the lady was always just a means to an end. "Come on then, Ren. Show me where we'll be sleeping." As she grinned shamelessly—radiantly—at me, I knew I would always stand by her. I always had, after all.
