We have traveled far and seen many causes for celebration; but only one tribe seems to rival our own desire for festivities. The Thavo’i people take great pride in their traditions and dedication to their tribe. Every member of their tribe, from the young barely able to stand alone to those aged beyond their hunting days, participates in these rituals. At these massive yet somber spectacles, the Thavo’i give thanks to their ancestors for guiding them thus far, and to Her they send gifts for providing them with the means of life. These festivals take place as a sort of comingofage celebration after members successfully perform in a blood hunt. Their population is the largest of any we've seen under Her canopy; is there some boon provided to them from these rather tame celebrations that we have yet to find?
