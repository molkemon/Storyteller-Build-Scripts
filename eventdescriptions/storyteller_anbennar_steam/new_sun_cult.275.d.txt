Bulwar has used flying carpets for many purposes, from sky platform artillery to quick and secure trade caravans. But it has never used it like this. In the dimly lit sky, two hundred carpets have lifted a massive lens a kilometre above land, and at its very center beneath, an army of mages has gathered in a hastily assembled mage tower. Each has one hand on a hunk of Dames tear, and the other on an intricately carved gem. Each knows the spell they will cast, each knows the importance of the work they do. Surael’s light is a symbol of His gift of life; now, they use their energy to bring it back once more. The monarch watches for a minute, before they calls for the signal cannon to be fired. The ritual begins, the murmurs of spellcasting filling the air, the Dames tear crumbling away. On the plain, a new sun begins to dawn, a ball of light so bright the ruler soon looks away. The archmage at the monarch's side, however, does not avert her gaze; though she will be blind by the day’s end, she will be the one to release the ritual, and it demands her total attention. She lifts a hand. Screams a line from her soul, "I am firing this light beam." And then with a high pitched PEW the nascent sun erupts into the sky like a volcano; until it meets the lens, where it becomes channeled into a brilliant ray. In an instant, the heavens are pierced by a spear unlike any other, and Amussu’s darkness shatters. It took a minute, but though day has returned, it still feels dim, compared to the brilliance that was just conjured. If they want to finish this early, now would be the time to attack Amussu.
