So saith the mob. The space in our new theatre is cramped, and yet we stand among the crush of friends. In hope of kindling mourning fires, we hear the demagogue hosts tragedy this eve, called The Anguish of Thrysimache, and to oblige the demagogue we came. It promises a night to overawe. We do not know why we have left our wine. What spectacle could we observe tonight that daily life does not provide for us? On second thought, the actors’ magics there provide no lacking feast for weary eyes.
