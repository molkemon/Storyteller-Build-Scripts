A small village has been shaken by recent events. The mayor of the village, a much-loved man, was having a pleasant conversation with an inquisitor when he suddenly started spouting off speech that was blatantly heretical in nature, denying not only the true divinity of Corin, but the entirety of the old Regent Court as well. He claims that, instead of the gods making the world, the world formed from nothing, and the gods simply appeared, like worms in apples. He clearly is out of his mind. However, he is still well-loved by the villagers. While executing him would keep heresy out of common minds, it could turn them against us.
