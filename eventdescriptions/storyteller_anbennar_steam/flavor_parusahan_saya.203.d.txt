In the audience chamber of the capital, countless zealots on both sides of the faith shout and argue with each other as they plead their case to the ruler. Each declaration of disruption is countered with screams of persecution and assault. Within the chaos, Hemantsen sits on his throne and watches the proceedings with an unmistakable sense of dread. "The heretics seek to destroy us," cries a High Philosophy scholar, red in the face from screaming. "Every day they subvert the realm, and move to make themselves stronger. We must end this farce before our way of life is destroyed." A Praxic guru rushes to the center of the room, blind with rage. "You cry sedition, but even now your thugs beat us in the streets. Do you seek to kill the faith, or the followers?" "The ruler is not fit to govern," shrieks a noble, a former magistrate who left the court in a fury when a conversion was revealed. "She waits like a viper in the heart of the realm, and will soon poison its very heart. We must cut off the head of the snake before it's too late." More accusations and insults are hurled throughout the room, until finally Hemantsen stands, his face a monolith of anger. "ENOUGH." The room falls silent. The realm points to the door, his expression contorted into snarling fury. "Out. All of you. I will not have you sully this place with your squabbling." As the crowd dissipates, Hemantsen slumps back into his throne, his mind a whirl of anxiety and doubt. This cannot continue, that much is obvious. But how can he resolve a problem which has split the very land in two?
