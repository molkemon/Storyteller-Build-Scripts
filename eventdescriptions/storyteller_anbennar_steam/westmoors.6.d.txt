Great Lord Marlen Dire felt his stomach drop as the news was given to him: The King of Gawed was no more; they found his body floating down the river, and had buried him not in the royal crypt as was tradition, but a small hill known only to the royal family to protect him from desecration. "So what now?" He asked, in a meeting with the other Great Lords of Gawed. His father had always spoken highly of his peers; the protectors of the Kingdom’s traditions and honour, he called them. "The Moormen have seized the capital; the city is currently holding a feast for its new overlord," Brasslance spat. "No matter, we will not give up our land so easily." "But what I meant was," Dire looked around the room, at each of the men wearing black rings engraved with oaths they swore to a dead man, "Who is the new King of Gawed?" They all looked like they had something terrible to tell him, that they had all known for some time. "There will not be one," spoke Ginnfield. "What?" "The capital is gone. The ruling line is sundered, and the Moormen will see any election of a new king as the summoning of a new warhost. Our people are tired, Dire. I know you are young and eager to fight for the people, but they need time. Go home, lick your wounds, and continue the fight later." And with that, the lords departed, leaving Marlen alone with his thoughts.
