The Council’s first topic was the decentralized nature of our faith. While united by our belief that Jaher wasn't Surakel Reborn, and that the Sun Elves hold no intrinsic divinity, outside of our core tenets there are few points of union between the different sects, and even less between the old faithful and those who converted from the New Sun Cult or from the Jadd. While those differences are being slowly removed by achieving consensus, one point arose that is proving very problematic: the role of the state and the sects in the preservation of our beliefs. Some believe that the followers and the priests were the ones keeping the Light, risking their lives if they were ever discovered by the Sun Elf inquisitors. Others say that our state, successful in war against the heretics, was the one that kept the Light and allowed it to shine again. While both positions hold some truth, our advisors believe that the ruler should publicly support one of them to end this discussion and allow the Council to continue.
