The current war with a rival nation has triggered a round of intrigues in our court. On the one hand, our advisor, a member of a foreign culture and holding a respected title, has offered to draw information from their network of contacts within the enemy nation; a considerable network, they claim, including several contacts close to the enemy’s ruler; to aid the war effort. On the other hand, their political opponents have raised doubts about their loyalty. They argue that, in spite of their previously exemplary service, we simply cannot trust someone of their culture to participate in deliberations of national importance at this time. They therefore demand that they be banished from the court.
