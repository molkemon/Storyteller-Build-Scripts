They were made to kneel upon the hillock, knees damp with mudwater as the sunrise made the Alen glow like pyrefire. But they were not to be burned. Instead, the moor lords brought several stones to the hillock, and when put together they were large enough for a kneeling man to lay his head on them, and not roll off when the claymore made contact. Lesser men would run, or beg, or weep, but they were Great Lords. Last of the ancient companions of Godrac, last of the lords of iron, last children of the Alen. Their thoughts were cut off by one of the lords, one with reddish hair and a smug grin, a Cockerwall, tapping his sword as he spoke. "For refusing to bend the knee to the true lords of the north, the Kingdom of the Westmoors." He emphasized that last part in a manner the Gawedi felt cruel, "You shall be sentenced to death." "And some of you may notice that only half of you are here." Spoke another lord, a somber man with dark hair and eyes, and a more spindly appearance, a Fouler, no doubt. "Not all of you were so eager to follow your leallord to your graves. As such, many of your holdings and titles will be given to those Gawedi which bent the knee and swore oaths to our ruler." "And where is this Moor ruler?" Asked one of the Great Lords, a young man, heir to his recently slain father. "The only one I remember, Gerrac Mooring, was gored and made into a cloak by Ulric the Eagle?" The Cockerwall paused, before breaking out in laughter. "Stand up, my boy." As the young lord did, he was cut down as the Cockerwall removed his head from his shoulders, prompting gasps from the rest of the Gawedi. "This isn't a debate or a trial, and I don't know why we're shocked. You're all getting shaved, it's just that this young man wanted it down sooner rather than later."
