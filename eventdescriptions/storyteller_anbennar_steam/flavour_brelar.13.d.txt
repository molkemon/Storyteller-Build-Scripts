Brelari and Bosancovaco guards open the gates of the floating city to the entourage of the monarch and the patricians who can once again set sights on the inner city. Finally, the Boats have been returned to their rightful home, free from the corrupt rivers and those who threatened the values of the now reestablished republic. In the city courthouse, the Rivers have been made to sign over their power to the Boats and vow to never betray the republic ever again. A new election is held the day after the signing, deciding the leader who would begin the rebuilding efforts and reinstatement of democracy in the city.
