When Kimánis was partitioned and Oktikheion seized the city itself, all expected Amgremós to submit to their new overlords, as was precedent and as was law. They did not. Instead, emboldened by the success of Ameion in the east, they scrambled to seek support from wherever they could find it. And, to their shame, they found it in the pirates of the Cleaved Sea. Amgremós grew fat by picking off the trade ships bringing Taychend’s riches to the Kheionai. They dared to think that their independence could last; Oktíbhen's incompetence means it might have. But we are not Oktikheion. Now, the captured pirates of the Andic Reach sat confined in pens, under Phylaxana's watch, with the aid of guards from loyal Eneion. The operations had been difficult and far ranging. At every turn, the pirates had proven their skill at sea and a certain pluckiness on land. There are some, in our Nékheis, who begrudgingly respect them, but the Phylaxana can afford them no such thing. They are criminals, nothing more. The law code gives us two options. death, or heavy labor in Deyeion, a holdover from the innovation of Darasthartes. There are some, though, who see another option, a loophole, one may say. Rather than having them waste away in tea plantations, we could put them to sea, in a state of indefinite transit to Deyeion. Most, as galley slaves, some, perhaps slightly more, not officers, of course, but advisors. Trade on their minds, to protect the seas, and learn some of their tactics. It will be useful to our naval hegemony, no doubt. There are some who call this a vile innovation, tantamount to treason against the Old Law. Perhaps both can be true, or maybe the thought alone is treason of the mind. If so, the penalty for piracy is death by hanging, and the gallows await our command.
