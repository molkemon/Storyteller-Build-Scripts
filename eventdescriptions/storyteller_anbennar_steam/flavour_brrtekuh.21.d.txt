The monarch stares at the horizon of their grand conquest, perhaps finally finding rest knowing all of the Khets have finally died. Yet to their dismay, the Sorrow begins to bubble and froth; first with the white of foam, then a dark red, bloodlike. The amphibians scurry out of the water at its approach; the fish flee for the shores, desperately trying to flop their way out onto land. Soon enough, the entire river is consumed by the roiling, thick substance, congealing amidst the reeds, sucking the life out of the riverbanks. The fish who dare not to seek a more comfortable death soon rise up to the surface of the river. Not long after, swarms of insects, most prominently flies and oddly mosquitoes, gather to cover the lake, not only consuming the carrion and bloodlike substance but also harassing those on the banks. Khetists who have seen this feel reinvigorated, convinced of their dead god's message, holding tight to their convictions against our greatest attempts of conversion.
