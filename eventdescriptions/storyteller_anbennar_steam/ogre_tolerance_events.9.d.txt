Alered to billowing dust clouds and thunderous bangs clamoring from a slum in the capital, local guards discovered a district under construction: cleared boulevards wide enough for a dozen pack animals, towering buildings carved with ornate reliefs, and sprawling common grounds, all freshly erected to titanic scale. Before they could wander further, a watchful ogre adorned in yellow and orange sashes stopped and rebuked them for wandering around the district without safety precautions. After some lengthy explanations, the ogre shared their people's plans: Dissatisfied with their dingy enclave, they have taken matters into their own hands, renovating the slum into a home fit for the scions of giants. Yet even as we marvel at their accomplishment, the sheer scale renders the area hostile for anyone other than giantkin. As they are still midrenovation, we could require them to adjust pathways and structures for smaller persons, but we can also leave their free beautification project alone, earning the ogres' respect. How should our lord handle this sprawling development?
