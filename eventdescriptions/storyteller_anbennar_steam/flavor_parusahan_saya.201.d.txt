In order to gauge the impact the ideas of Suhan has had on the populace, Hemantsen agrees to send Jira and a cohort of advisors to a hotspot of Praxic activity. Walking the streets allows Jira to see all kinds of new sights, as people listen to preachers on the street and scripture is handed out by passing scholars. She obtains one such scroll, and begins to read it to better understand the beliefs these people now follow. Within, she finds not just knowledge of the new faith, but conviction within herself she did not realize existed. Within the scripture of the Praxis she found a core value that resonated within Jira's chest; the Long Path, which the Praxic believe is the only way through which Purtara can be achieved. She reflected on her life as she spent hours pouring over the scroll, often burning through several lamps reading into the night. All of her struggles with the Starry Eye as one of the few women who were members of the school; her driving desire to improve the lives of those around her through knowledge; her incessant pining after a young Hemantsen until he finally acquiesced to marrying her. All of these things she pursued not out of a duty to the world, but to prove to herself that she could accomplish whatever she set her mind to. Was this not the ultimate expression of the Long Path? It was not long before her coterie of advisors noticed Jira's incessant dissection of Praxic texts, and how her usual debates on spiritual beliefs took a greater slant towards the new faith. Whispers began, which became mumblings, which grew further still to rumors. And the wife of the Raj converting to the Praxis is a dangerous rumor indeed.
