The rift opens, and I can see. Ilfanill’s robes brush the ground; no doubt she has an extra finger or two she would rather went unseen in the roadside taverns. This could be anywhere in the desert beyond the Sorrow, in that endless sea of sand, and it appears at first that there is nothing for miles around. Then Ilfanill makes a terse gesture. An entire dune shifts apologetically out of her way, and the tomb entrance is revealed. I watch as she paces before the entrance, frowning at something I cannot see. Her first attempt is a powerful, simple Unravelling. I feel the humming logic of her craft; I feel as it blithely and inexorably points out that this other magic, the Khet magic, should cease to exist now, please and thank you. The Khet magic remains. I frown at the same time Granny does; I have never witnessed her Unravelling fail. Three days pass in a moment. Granny does not hurry; she sits facing the tomb entrance, sometimes for hours at a time, before she tries her next incantation. No furious Khet arrives to challenge her; her talent for concealment remains, perhaps. Yet for all her apparent patience, I feel the congealing of her fury. And I know, a moment before she does, that she will leave unrewarded.
