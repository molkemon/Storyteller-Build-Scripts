For many, the perfect soldier is the one that can take orders, march in formation, and fire their weapon with passable accuracy. However, our foes, be they flesh, fellow Mechanim, or Holohana, are unlikely to yield from a single bullet alone. As such, we need to ensure that our troops can both fire fast and accurately. While the former is only really viable through extensive drilling and long-term development of firearm technology, the latter we can improve upon in the short term by minimizing the two largest factors. recoil and sway while lining up a shot. For the first, methods are available ranging from simple padded stocks to an elaborate arm design that allows it to retract into itself to disperse much of the accompanying recoil, a system that is theorized to also work for our cannons in due time. The latter in turn required a bit more ingenuity. While a Mechanim does not need to worry about breathing throwing off their aim, servos and joints hanging or lagging for a few moments can be enough to ruin the opportunity for a clear shot. To prevent this, and keep our soldiers from carrying around barrels of lubricant, our artificers repurposed the systems that kept them motionless for so long. Once ready to fire, a simulated stasis affects the arms, keeping them almost perfectly still while the marksman focuses on micromovements to line up their shot.
