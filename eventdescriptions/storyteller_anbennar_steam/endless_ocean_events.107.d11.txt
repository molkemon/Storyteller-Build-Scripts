Idiots will tell you that a surfboard knifes through the water, attributing preternatural strength to glorified driftwood, as though it has the power to defy the flow of the ocean. This is a foolhardy interpretation; a surfboard does not cleave waves, merely follows them. A surfer is not the master of the coast, but rather the coast arbitrates the surfer’s fate. A surfboard is but another piece of flotsam within the great wheel of the ocean, no greater than the rocks and shells which also wash against the shore. A surfer is naught but the temporary passenger riding the adamant will of the sea. Surfing, at its pinnacle, is surrender and imitation. To know that the bare modicum of choice we hold in this world pales in significance to the force of nature, and to then realize that rather than fight the undefeatable, our energies are best spent in adapting to the uncontrollable, that is the meaning of surfing. The fold of a breaker decides the path of those who would follow the tides. It carves the undersea valleys and recesses which form the boundary of sea and stone. It pounds islands to sand, and we, in those briefest moments where the wind runs through our hair and the sea foam compels us to rise with the rest of the wave, are lucky enough to act as the sea.
