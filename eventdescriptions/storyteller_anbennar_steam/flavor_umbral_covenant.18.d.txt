The state of the main banquet hall is beyond description, Vile One. The nobleman who spoke was positively vibrating with rage; manly stoicism had been left behind almost immediately after the seneschal’s florid rendition of his many titles. “Beyond blood and the remains of animals, bones and such,” the man’s moustache quivered, “it appears feces has been spread across one of the interior walls.” The Vile One shifted her gaze to Ikomeye, who was idly picking at a crusted stain on her sleeve. Ikomeye merely shrugged. Then she gave the noble an insolent smile. “You see, Vile One. This upjumped village rat has no respect.” She was. He stopped abruptly. For a moment one might have thought he had quailed at a glance from The Vile One, but noises still issued from him, indignant nasal squeaks. When he turned, half the court shuddered involuntarily, for his lips had been sewn shut. The seam which was his mouth glimmered in fogged silver as his squawks curdled to panic. “It is clear you can make no use of the villa in its current state,” said the Vile One, her fingers drumming on the wooden arm of the throne. “Ikomeye, as High Diviner, is thus clearly much better placed to dispose of it. It is hereby requisitioned in her name.”
