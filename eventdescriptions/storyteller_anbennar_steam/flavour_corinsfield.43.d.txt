The halfling had given a spirited defense in court, but it was too little too late. The humans were already ensured of her guilt the moment she had walked in, and from there the verdict had already been decided. The guards bound her hands and feet, and set up the pyre. She couldn’t bear to look at the crowd that gathered; the joyous expressions of the humans bothered her, yes, but nothing quite sickened her as much as the halflings that held equally eerie grins. “How can you smile with them, how can you live amongst them? They hold nothing but contempt for you. You will never be one of them,” she wanted to cry. But she couldn’t. Such resilience had already been crushed out of her. The counter; another halfling; gave the hymns and lit the torch. And then it was over.
