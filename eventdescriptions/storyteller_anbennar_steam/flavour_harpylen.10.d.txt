With Asmalil secured, Harpylen rose yet higher in power and esteem, and not since the days of Firanya had the children of the sky flown so close to heaven. From Heunthume, across Bulwar and beyond did the fourth and final queendom spread its wings, and from Cannor to Haless did the people of the world crane their necks to see its glory. And yet, as Harpylen rose in radiance, did the Queen's Throne find a higher perch; a vantage from which to look ever further afield, so that her gaze fell across the world entirely, and what she saw brought her no joy. For across the world beyond Bulwar, the myriad daughters of Firanya had spread. They had flown or been driven to every corner of Halann, and while they had survived, many had been robbed of pride and joy, their lives brought from a hunt of choice, to a hunt for a life tomorrow against their many foes. The greatest foes of harpies' past were the gnolls, as the stonewings of Akasik could attest. But the gnolls were beasts at heart. Cunning beasts, to be sure, but beasts. Harpylen had risen above them, and even found for them a place of sorts. They belonged in the sand, bound to the ground with the sky forever out of reach. Figures of pity, but no longer of fear. Greater foes were the children of Jaher, but they too had found a place in Harpylen, and many of Firanya's daughters now bore their blood. But the first and final of Harpylen's foes were the humans. Much like ourselves, the humans were unknown. None knew from where they came, and they themselves held to a divine origin. Despite the strength of Harpylen, humans remained both our most numerous servants, and our most implacable enemies. It was with this in mind, that the Queen's Throne brought Asmalil to her lips, and sang. For all Firanya's children would be brought home, and all who had stolen the sky from them would be struck down, their faces cast to the ground, so that their eyes would never again gaze upon heaven.
