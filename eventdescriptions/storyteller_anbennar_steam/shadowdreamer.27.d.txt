On the walls of a city, a warrior stood and looked down into the valley towards another settlement. He stretched in the morning sunlight, shifting the plates of his mithril armor slightly to unbunch his uniform shirt beneath. The captain would be there to oversee the changing of the guard soon, and he would finally get to go home and go to bed. In a jade mine just outside a town, a craftsman carefully adjusted a chalk line on a huge slab of precious jade that would become a beautiful statue some weeks from now. He checked his math one more time before sitting his tools down and wiping the sweat from his brow. Idly, as he munched away on his lunch, he wondered if he would ever save up enough to have a small statue in his own home. Cresting a dune somewhere due south of a region, a leader marched along with a regiment of mercenaries. Most of them had been pleasant enough folk, and they appreciated him for his discipline and skill with the cannons. He had enjoyed the job these last few months, and earned a nice sum to send back to his family, but increasingly he missed the echoing halls of home. In a large hall, the prophet sat in a comfortable seat overlooking a carved relief of the continent. There were gemstones studding the mountainsides, one for each domain that had come to be home to the orks. A smile crept across the prophet’s face as he took in the sight, and then closed his eyes. A vision appeared through the usual veil of shadows. He waited until he saw it clearly and then opened his eyes to see the map again. “Yes,” he said with a sigh. “Just exactly the same.”
