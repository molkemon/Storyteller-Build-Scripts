Where once the streets of the capital bustled with commerce, a kind of haze seems to have settled over the city. Songs drift between the closed market stalls and the empty squares. They have no lyrics, but they seem to slip from one melody to another in a way that calms the mind and makes even moving seem like an insurmountable effort. Some swear that they can smell incense in the air, if the wind is just right. In the banks without bankers and the schools without scholars, the people dream worlds without sorrow or pain. Why should they ever want to leave? In fact, thinking about such nice things makes you sleepy. Wouldn’t it be nice to take a nap right now?
