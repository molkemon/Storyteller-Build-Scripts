When one walks the path to Purtara, they must not focus on only one of its constituents, lest they be torn from it. For if they only follow the cobble, or the dirt, or the trampled grass, then the totality of their journey shall allude them, and thus, so too shall the destination. The path once broken has been reformed, and Suhan now leads us down the multifaceted trail. Yet those shards, in part fused again, will not simply join the new harmony peacefully. Smoke rises from the ears of scholars and the base of buildings. The debates of educated philosophers have departed the temples and entered the countryside. Innumerable local power brokers still cling to the old ways, fruitlessly fighting back against the tide. Yet their strife has borne fruit, and the specter of revolt now ravages the countryside. Swift action must be taken, else the path is left trampled and ruined.
