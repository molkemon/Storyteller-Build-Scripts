The lord could see the last barrels and supplies being loaded onto the expeditionary vessels, as knights and sailors alike assembled on their decks. One could easily mistake this event as the prelude to war, or some other sort of underhanded business; but the sense of giddy anticipation in the air belied all that. Today, the elves of a province would row to the ends of the Ynn itself, if such a place even existed. Few knew what might await them; the forest that shrouded much of a region was an impenetrable place, known primarily for the hordes of savage people that called it home, the so called "Cursed Ones"; but if the tenacity of its defenders matched even a fraction of that forest's riches, the lord knew this would be a worthy endeavour indeed.
