A growing merchant class, as well as an increasingly demanding social code among the nobility and upper classes, has led to an increased demand for perfume. The halls of grand palaces are now expected to have a likable scent as well as a majestic decor. This has led to a surge in demand for products such as frankincense, myrrh, and other scented products for the making of perfume, and in the capital city, the refinement of local flowers and foreign scented goods has even grown into a major industry in itself.
