The monarch was the one who first hit on the idea of a public marriage. one big show to demonstrate to the people that they were not foreign conquerors, but allies come to right a great injustice. Perzuk, the victim of said injustice, was therefore put in the spotlight at all times, with the Matriarch one step behind him. It worked very well. The ceremonial plaza where the parade ended was packed to the brim of people of all races. Conquerors and conquered, humans, harpies, elves, and goblins. So many goblins, going utterly wild. whistling, hooting, doing weird group dances. The monarch turned to Perzuk, murmuring, "Look at them; they were fighting us less than a month ago, now they seem overjoyed by our presence. I'll be honest, such two-faced opportunism is unpleasant to me." "It's not like that, really. They're genuine, mostly. Us goblins, we're used to abrupt changes. This makes us take things as they come, and enjoy the present if we can. Today's a party, so they're gonna party hard. And, well… many goblins have a… let's say, idealized view of harpies." "Thanks for sparing me the details. Good. It means this political marriage is working." The culmination of the day, after many shamanic rites, long tired smiles, and solemn discourses, was the officiation of the marriage with the exchanging of gifts between the spouses. a branch of a Sycamore from the monarch, representing unity, and a marble brick from Perzuk to build a new life. Between the two, an exchange of vows passed, supposedly that only they should hear. "Dear wife, I intend for this marriage to be more than a political one. Will you allow me to woo you in the coming months?"
