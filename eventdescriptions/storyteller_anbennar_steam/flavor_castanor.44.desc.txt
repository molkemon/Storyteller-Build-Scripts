For the monarch, the winter months are a frenzy of activity, planning out the next campaign season and legion logistics and a million other things that get shunted to the winter's dark nights. The North Citadel is the perfect place for this, with few distractions and few sycophants willing to in its spartan confines. As spring thaws into summer, the court moves to the South Citadel, where entertaining foreign dignitaries and kings fills the days of the monarch, offering a much needed respite. Recharged, the monarch is able to go into the winter months at the North Citadel with new energy, and so the cycle continues; an unlikely balance eked out between the two courts of Castan's Empire.
