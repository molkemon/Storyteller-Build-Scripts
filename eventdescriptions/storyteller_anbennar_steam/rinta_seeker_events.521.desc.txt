A routine accounting check of the funds provided for the construction of the Great Observatory has revealed a noticeable discrepancy. It seems a sizable amount of the money sent has simply disappeared into thin air at some point in the process. Further investigation has tracked down the exact place this money disappeared. one of the advisors has been confiscating a portion of each payment as so-called administrative expenses without knowledge or approval. Confronted about this obvious corruption, the advisor attempted to pass it off as a simple miscommunication, though he is unable to provide any explanation for where the funds actually went. He has begged for mercy and sworn that he will not let any more of these accidents happen, but it would also be entirely justified to order his execution and seize the deficit from his personal estate.
