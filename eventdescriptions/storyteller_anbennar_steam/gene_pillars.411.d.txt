The water that floods the lowest levels of this gene pillar does not run clear. It has ebbed and flowed for centuries, and is murky with silt and debris as a result. The initial foray has been promising, which is to say, no one was devoured by a hinuifish or hinuieel or any other aquatic predator common to the Riverlands. More dangerous are occasional places where the water has created a sinkhole, which could swallow a dozen of our troops with ease. Still, a careful trek evades most of these. It is just step after step through blackbrown water. For all that, however, our expeditionaries swear: there are times it feels like they can see through the silt. Moments where the risen water falls away, allowing a tantalizing glimpse of pristine jewels embedded in the walls to channel magic for some unknown purpose. For the most part, the expeditionaries have stayed focused. For the most part.
