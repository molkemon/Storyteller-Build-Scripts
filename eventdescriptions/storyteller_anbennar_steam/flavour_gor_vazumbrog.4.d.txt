To the monarch, Your Majesty, it is with a grave mix of urgency and deliberation that I, commander Durvin Ironmantle, write to you regarding the DwarovrodGorrod crossroads. Upon your directive, we reclaimed this strategic area to anchor our dominion in the western Serpentspine. The foundations of a citadel were to be laid this fortnight, yet an unexpected impediment arises. The area is inhabited by a colony of fungrum, a breed of myconid long known to us as docile scavengers. But the denizens of Azmak Quarry are anything but ordinary. They enact rituals of unnerving complexity, their forms swaying in unison beneath the bioluminescent canopy of their fungal groves. They speak, if one can call it that, of their devotion to an entity they name the Sporeweaver. This being, they claim, slumbers within a cavern deep below the quarry floor. Our colonists had no trouble in settling nearby areas and even establishing trade with the fungrum. However, any attempt to build advanced structures within proximity of their sanctuary is met with resistance. The fungrum do not resort to violence but to sabotage; tools are consumed by creeping fungi overnight, scaffolds collapse under the weight of unseen decay. Worse, our builders whisper of dreams shared with the myconids. visions of roots entwining their limbs, of spores blossoming from their flesh. I humbly seek Your Majesty's wisdom. How shall we proceed? Your loyal servant, Durvin Ironmantle
