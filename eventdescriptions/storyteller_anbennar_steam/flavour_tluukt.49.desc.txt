Our relations with both Tluukt and Siádan, once a happy alliance of mutual interest, have now somehow become a point of conflict. The ruler of Tluukt decries our relations with Siádan, characterizing them as scheming vultures and pinning our lack of success in the Salahad on our alliance with the harpies. The ruler of Siádan, for her part, denies these allegations and blames Tluukt for no end of suspicious circumstances in the Salahad, accusing the ruler of Tluukt of being a craven scoundrel. What is abundantly clear is that both Tluukt and Siádan are expecting our pack to side with them against the other.
