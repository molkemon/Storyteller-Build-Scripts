To find Asmalil, it had to be known what Asmalil was. A legend, to be sure, but also very specifically a Menumsar. What then was a Menumsar? There was the rub, for it could be anything. Asmalil could be a weapon, or a piece of jewelry. It could be a recipe, or a song. It could even be a person. The old songs claimed that it had belonged to Queen Firanya, but what did belonging mean? Was it her tool, or her partner? For these reasons, the project to uncover Asmalil was organized unusually. The Heunthokharivor leveraged their network of contacts, domestically and abroad, to inquire of this thing. The Isutoqir by contrast, employed a virtual legion of scryers to attempt to find Asmalil through arcane means. The Kemounqir were entrusted with the budget, and to their credit, they handled the strain on national finances admirably. The Ivetasananthor, lastly, were largely entrusted with searching archives and songs for tales of Asmalil. Despite the size of the undertaking, no progress was made, until the dreams started. First were the scryers, but soon, dreams of the Menumsar became commonplace, though strangely isolated to within the project. One accountant of the Kemounqir describes it as follows. "I know that I dream, but I see nothing. Not darkness, but an absence of sight, as if I was one of those eyeless fish in the deep sea. And perhaps I am under water, for I feel no wind on my wings, and then I hear it. A song. I cannot hear the words, but I hear the voice. My mother's voice. And I know, somehow, that I am facing north. I do not see, but I hear. The song comes from the north. And north is where I must go. And then I wake up." This was a faint clue at best, but the dream was always the same, and soon after, word reached Heunthume, that the same dream had occurred outside the project, by a fledgling hunter in the mountains of Nansalen.
