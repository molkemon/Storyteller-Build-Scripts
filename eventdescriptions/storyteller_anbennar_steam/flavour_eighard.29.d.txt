"And you see, we do not even need cells," the warden says to the visiting diplomat, who has gone quite pale. "Whenever we capture a traitor, a dissenter, a nonbeliever, they are sent here to rot." He gestures to an elf, chained to the wall by a set of shackles. He is deathly thin, with his ribs exposed. With great effort, he raises his head and glares at the warden. He attempts to spit, but his mouth is too dry to form saliva. "What if you capture someone on the other side of the realm," the diplomat asks. "Will you bring them all the way here?" The warden laughs. "No, of course not. We are constructing similar, smaller prisons all across the country. None as grand and impressive as this one, of course, but they will exist nonetheless. We will make sure that every traitor, every dissenter, every nonbeliever, every soul who does not believe in Autumn, in the Amber Princess, is put where they belong and left to decay into dust."
