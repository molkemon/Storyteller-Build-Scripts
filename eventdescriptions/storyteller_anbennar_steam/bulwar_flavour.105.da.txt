After the fall of Jaher's Empire, the Golden Highway that spanned from Bulwar to Haless in the far east rotted away. The section of the Highway coursing through the Salahad's milder northeastern climate suffered constant harpy raids, and gnoll raiders controlled the western Harra oasis, forcing the caravans to adapt their routes by going right through the middle of the Salahad desert. The szalMehran, a reputed Mašnsih tribe living in the midst of the desert, established a prosperous trade route along the ruthless Salahad. Using strategic locations such as the port city of Edešukuru, their homelands in Shimlardu, and the safety of the walls of Azkasur, these Mašnsih prospered as guides for the trade caravans who preferred to brave the Salahad. With our newfound control over these key cities and our deal with the tribe, the SzalMehran are finding more and more success as guides along the Golden Highway, now that merchants no longer fear taxhappy patrols from differing states eager to impose a new tariff on their goods. The stability brought by our unification of the SzalMehran route has led to an influx of Raheni goods in the country, as many guides still remain, for the realignment of the SzalMehran to their new role is not complete. During this period of transition, we can still reap their benefits.
