It is a curious feature of the Precursor’s work that the walls are stronger than the floors. Save for at the very base, where the Pillar was torn open from the inside by the effort of centuries, the outer casing of this immense edifice has remained intact. But the floors have collapsed through in a dozen places, and chambers that were enormous even before have become vast vertical worlds unto themselves. Perhaps, indeed, the hinuirocs helped tear them open. We know there is more than one queen of the skies; they divebomb through the grand voids of the Pillar and are the apex predators of its upper third, often swallowing their hunt whole. All life in this section of the Pillar exists in the context of the Hinuiroc, avoiding or squaring up to its appraising gaze as it circles looking for prey, ceding mastery of the open air. For our part, we have avoided them too, choosing any route which circumvents the immense open central column of the Pillar. Where we are now, however, there is no way to move further up the Pillar without inching across one of the ledges under the hinuiroc’s dominion. We watch as one of them circles overhead, her claws are as long as we are, her wingspan vast.
