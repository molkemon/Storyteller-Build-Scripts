We hear the bounding of the Hinuiroo before we enter his chamber; he does not fear us, but comes to meet us. He is all muscle, his arms powerful, and at least twice the size of a human man. On his shoulders and neck and head we see a profusion of scars and wounds; indeed, an entire chunk of his ear has been removed. Apparently the Hinuiroo are that rare surface creature which can readily fight off a diving Hinuiroc. The rest of the Hinuiroo court look on, apparently unconcerned; they have the easy confidence of animals not often threatened by landgoers. Indeed, in such an environment warped by Hinuiroc threat, it seems that they look on all surface creatures as potential allies. The Hinuiroo’s great fists, bobbing in the air as it bounces on its springy feet, are very clearly not held in anger. We realize as one; the Hinuiroo wants to spar. Have the Precursors raised these animals to the point that they can process such a concept as something which requires consent? There is something to the way the leaves and rushes have been arranged in this chamber which speaks to rudimentary organization, to the earliest whisperings of civilization.
