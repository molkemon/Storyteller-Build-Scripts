I am truly impressed by the rigor of the people of Maxkanil Momi. Unlike many of their peers in the archipelago, they do not have a Dragon Turtle of their own to carry their memories. Instead, they travel the floating corals, gathering up the stories, dreams, and hopes of all the people of the Insyaan coasts. This dedication to diversity reminds me much of Bivuzena herself, and I can’t wait to bring back all the stories I’ve heard and written down. This peculiar lifestyle is also mirrored by the Ghost Corals. As I studied them further, I discovered that they have a dualistic lifecycle; they spend part of their lives as standard coral, and part of their lives as free floating polyps. Eventually, they settle down, find their right home, and begin a chain reaction of linking with others, creating the artificial islands I mentioned before. With enough skill, one can even manipulate them into polyp form early, then reconstitute them back into their coral form, creating miniature islands of any shape. This is a timeconsuming and delicate process, however. Go too quick, try to take a shortcut, and the entire thing falls apart. I have spent months nurturing one colony, and my hope is to take it back to Bivuzena, and have them thrive in their new home. Through gentle coaxing, patience, and good old-fashioned kindness, I hope to fashion a place for a new life form.
