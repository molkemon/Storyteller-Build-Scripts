Qlak the Wanderer looked especially grim today. His usual glee was nowhere to be seen. Today’s tale, my friend, is one of sorrow and reflection. Before coming here, the Greentide engulfed the northern kingdoms. It was led by the formidable Korgus, who claimed to be the son of the orks' lost god, Dookan. Few dared to challenge that claim, for his might enabled him to unite almost all orks, deep under the mountain. The countless clans under his banner were unstoppable. That is, until Korgus was killed in the ruins of the once magnificent stone city of Castonath. Here in the Woods we were an afterthought, a collateral damage lost inside the Verdant Veil. And to us, the orks' arrival was both a boon and a curse. They overturned the centuries-long dominance of the elves, but at the same time their strength was built on the enslavement of our northern cousins and even of us, after the orks realized that we existed. My friend, I’m sure you recall the suffering of our kin. orks against goblins, against elves. With many successes of our own, but many, so many deaths. War was unavoidable. Of course, my friend, of course. Freeing our cousins was a dire necessity. As was putting an end to Relgakk Blackmoon’s folly. And I understand that making sure no ork clan could grow big enough to enslave us again was a priority. But after that, do you think we made the right choice? I cannot say, my friend. Maybe there was no right choice. Maybe killing every ork on sight is what we needed to make sure we would forever be safe from them. And there is some justice in repaying them for the pain and suffering they inflicted on us. The northerners do the same, as they force the slavers to become slaves themselves. But is such brutality ever truly justified?
