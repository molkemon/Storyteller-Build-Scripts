None of our festivals had ever been this packed before. The grounds were five times larger than those of any previous festival, but it was as if every living soul in the jungle had gathered to attend the event. It was easy to identify the tribal origin of all the visitors: the Effe’i by their dark, almost plantlike skin, the Lai’i by their large, towering figures, and the Parura and the numerous Thavo'i by their bright hair colours of blue and green. Despite everything, they had far more in common than not; they all laughed together, sang together, drank together. Slowly but surely, the barriers between them were erased.
