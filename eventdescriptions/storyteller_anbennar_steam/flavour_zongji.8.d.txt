The Second Harimraj has fallen. The news, while not entirely unsurprising given recent events, has shaken the landscape of the continent to its core. The news hits especially hard for the upper castes here in Zongji. In addition to the more local Yan philosophies, over the past few centuries, the great minds of our nation became increasingly intrigued by Raheni philosophies and thought. The rebirth of the Harimraj gave many of the scions of our dynasty hope that they could accomplish something on the same scale in Yanshen. The great minds of our nation, our Liangzhu among them, have been slowly processing their emotions and thoughts. Many have come to the conclusion that any attempt to restore Harimar's Empire is a fool's errand, and that every attempt is destined to fail. The ruler has been feeling less confident in their convictions. Is our plan truly destined to fail? No. The ruler has come to their own conclusion. The main factor that led to the fall of the Second Harimraj is the same thing that led to the fall of Lianzhou; weak, incompetent emperors. This is something we find ourselves uniquely prepared to face. As long as we continue the Liangzhu examinations, no weak or incompetent emperor will ever sit upon the Tiger Throne. Our people will not become demoralized by the news. In fact, this has only served to prove a point; we are perhaps the only ones who can truly restore Harimar's Empire.
