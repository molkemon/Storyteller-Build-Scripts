Time did not act normally in this pyramid. Was this the result of genie magic? Was this the will of Elikhet warping reality around this place? Perhaps some combination of the two? Neverkaar’s mind wandered from topic to topic. He might as well have been walking in circles for all he knew. He had lost blood, he didn’t know how much, and he was starting to feel delirious. If he turned a corner and found a wall of spikes with his own arm hanging from them, he might just curl up next to the human skeleton and accept he had failed. Pushing open a stone door, he shielded his eyes as a golden light shone through into the dusty passageway. The ceiling of this new room extended further than he could see, although a beam of light descended from above, illuminating an ornate sarcophagus in the middle of the space. Neverkaar limped forwards, kicking aside a skeleton. Grabbing the lid with his one remaining arm, he hauled it aside, stopping afterwards to breathe for a long, agonizing moment. Inside lay a mummified human, laying in a bed of gold and faded cloth. In its arm it held a staff, curved at the top, with blue and white stripes. Atop its face lay the mask of a cat.
