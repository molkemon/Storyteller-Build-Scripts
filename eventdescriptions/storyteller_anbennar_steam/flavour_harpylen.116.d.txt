The hunters flew to a distant location and spoke at length with the dreaming steward of the distant tower. This was no true roost, but a distant post, perched on the knee of the Serpentspine, since the days of Firanya. An old sentinel, having kept vigil to the north, for longer than anyone could recall. Yet there had always been harpies here, whether in service to Queen, Beast or Phoenix. The daughters of Khinkeion looked ever north, and brought word of the movements of the fierce children of the forbidden plains, to warn their charges to the south, whoever they may have been. Speaking with the steward revealed little, for her dreams were the same as those of the hunters. And yet, in that location, the dreams did change, for one and all. A hunter describes it thusly. "Again I dream, and again I am without eyes. But I hear now, closer, the voice of my mother. And it is not my mother's voice, but many voices, singing, closely in rhythm and cadence. If I strain my ears, I can peel apart the singular threads of the song. Each and every voice sings so closely together, and yet there is one voice that I can pick out. But the voice is a thread, and as I strain my ears to listen, the voice grows closer, as if I can close with the song by hearing it. Yet at some point, I feel the voice above me. I cannot fly. I can come no closer. I hear the song, but I cannot listen. I cannot make out the words. I am so close to hearing something I can understand, and then. Then. Then I wake up. And I know the voice is north. North and far above, where the land meets the sky. We must reach this place. We will know it when we see."
