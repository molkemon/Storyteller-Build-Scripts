Today has been an interesting day at court. Not because anything particularly important happened, no, but because the monarch heard all day from their courtesans and advisors. Allegedly, the duke of Deranne, drunk and angry, complained about the size of their warships, screaming at their Master of Sea that royal transport ships dwarfed the proud Derannic war vessels. While the verbal abuse they suffered may only be an exaggeration of the rumormill, the Master of Sea acted swiftly, Derannic pride being at stake, though some insist he did only to placate the duke and protect his position. He ordered the construction of a massive shipyard and engaged the Veline brothers, a pair of renown naval engineers calling Westport home. They worked fast and efficiently, drafting plans for the “Shipyard of Lament”, a massive structure allowing the construction of equally monumental warships, fittingly named Behemoths.
