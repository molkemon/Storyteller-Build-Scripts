Captain Adran is looking over his troops as they go through their daily drills. The soldiers seem to be younger than usual, barely out of adolescence and yet fully ready to die for their country. He sighs, resolving himself to send yet more men into the grinder. He's about to stop the training for the day when he notices a regiment of men marching up. They have thick accents; their leader explains that since they finished up a contract back home, they've come to fight in our war. Adran smiles, knowing that he can now resign and not feel guilty anymore. The only question is if he wants to let them do the fighting instead of the boys he's drilling, or if he wants them to take his job so he can use his savings to get himself, his wife and his son on the next boat to a distant land. He sits at his desk and dips a quill in a vial of ink. The ruler receives the letter from Captain Adran about these veterans and about the captain resigning effective immediately. The ruler thinks about these veterans and wonders if it'd be better to take advantage of the preferential contracts or hire their captains to train future officers.
