The strongest net in the fisher’s arsenal is forbearance. When the lure first bobs, one must resist the impulse to immediately begin reeling. If a line seems inadequate, one must endure the day’s heat until one’s quarry grows hungry, lest the caster do the same. When laying traps, patience guarantees the wise will take home far greater reward for their effort than the overly eager. So too does this strength hold true at grand scale. The ignorant and gluttonous may think the sea’s wealth inexhaustible and uninterruptible, but every elder carries a tale of when desire eclipsed reason, and nets rose empty. In the zeal of greed, the uninformed may overharvest, leaving the seas no means to replenish themselves. However, when a society is able to restrain its everyday whims, they sow the seeds of future return. Know the tides will carry with them bountiful catches. Observe the full moon as it summons breeding frenzies. Witness the sea run silver with the backs of fish, vying for their share of the rich countercurrents. These are the Seasons to Fish. On such days, be generous with your catch, and invite your neighbors to feast, prepare food for the future months, and make merry.
