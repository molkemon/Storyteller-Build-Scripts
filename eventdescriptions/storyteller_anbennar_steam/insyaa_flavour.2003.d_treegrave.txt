If a tree falls in a forest, does it make a sound? Perhaps, perhaps not. But when the tree that falls belongs to the hinuiforest, it certainly makes an impression. This province is filled with the valley it carved when it fell, a giant bringing down other giants with it. Species from the undergrowth and overgrowth yet mingle, carving out niches amidst the behemoth's carcass. There is all the wood we could ever need, made more accessible than usual in the Hinuiforest. But this valley of wreckage and detritus is an unstable foundation.
