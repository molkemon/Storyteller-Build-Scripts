What precisely did we imagine, before our valiant swords struck down the wyvern matriarchs in their desperate fury? A single egg, precariously atop a bundle of sticks, at the very peak of the mountain? Instead, we behold a grand cavern, each dank recess crowded with detritus, animal bones, and the slick gunge of half-eaten prey, and well over a hundred eggs. The gnolls know a little of how the wyvern mothers incubate their young, a collective experience handed down over generations of hunting the beasts, but even they were not mad enough to try and tame the things, as we once did. No; it is Verne's fate alone to rebirth this ancient practice, and one we will gladly embrace.
