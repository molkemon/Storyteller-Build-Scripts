Nobody saw Elrandar Silverspite die, nor knew how it had happened. He had been fighting on the frontlines for a century; one of the wounds he had received was bound to kill him eventually. They found him watching the sky through one of the windows of his makeshift headquarters, the collar of his armor fixed around his neck. One would have thought there would be panic, a struggle for succession, maybe. That was what Dawn’s enemies were banking on, as Orenvalyam continued its grim advance across Halcann. But there was no such thing. Silverspite had told them clearly what was to happen: take the collar and give it to the heir. And so they did. The collar came off so easily from Silverspite’s neck. It clicked so easily around the new Kamrayakval’s. Immediately, there was a roar—piercing and metallic—as the armor adjusted to its new wearer from within the burst of steam and smoke. But then a jagged figure arose from the mist, and with a gauntlet taut and clawed, smiled beneath their visor. There would be no slow slip into hardened rule, no slow coaxing into Dawn’s project; the Dragon knew what must be done. It had signed a pact, after all.
