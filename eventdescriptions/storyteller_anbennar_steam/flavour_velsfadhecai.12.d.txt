"What now, then?" I asked, lying next to Mitrenya once again. She gazed stubbornly skyward, eyes locked on the top of the bedframe. "Whatever do you mean?" Her voice was calm, which only raised my hackles. I tried to choose my words carefully. "Well, we've failed, haven't we?" She scoffed, finally turning to look at me. In spite of everything, her sharp eyes appeared the same as they had on that fateful night. "This isn't a failure, Varynna," she replied, "merely a momentary setback. It will just take a little more time. Something we have plenty of, hm?"
