I always knew it would happen one day when my wives aged faster than me. My divine oath extended my lifespan; that much was plain to see. Yet as I stood over their old bodies, I wept like I never had before, for my great lovers were gone. And now alone, I had to do yet more. Even now, they still held hands sweetly, their love deeper than even death. And as I dropped to my knees at their side, a part of me wished for that one final breath, but my work was not yet done. Lel's power still flowed strong through me. Our great matriarch asked for more, and if I didn't answer, what would I be? So I finally left that room, spent my nights all alone, honored their deep love, and built yet more stone by stone.
