It was in the days of a certain year, when the monarch was queen of Vængheim. A man named Kåre, who was blessed with fertile land and many flocks, did come to the queen. And he said, “O queen, I kneel before you, for there is nowhere else I can turn. The old man of the forest has taken my son Asbjörn, and I am left bereaved. I beg of you save my son from this wicked spirit.” And then he wept. The queen, who knew something of the fey, said, “I know of whom you speak. A woldwight is he, of great power and cunning. But why has he taken your son?” And Kåre, whose tears were without number, answered, “He seeks to take that which is most precious to me, for he knows that bereft of this, the taste of life will be as ash upon my tongue, for he covets my wealth and my land, and seeks my ruin.” And the queen did rise, and said, “Weep no more, for I shall make your cause my cause. For the sake of your son, I will brave the wight, and see that justice is done.” And then the queen gathered the maidens of Vængheim for a great hunt.
