The curtains rise, and a hush falls over the crowd. Stage hands, uncountable copies of the single mage, operate the lights and wheel out setpieces as versions of yourself dance, sing, and act out the whole of your existence. You find yourself in the audience, alongside another of yourself. The entire research team, who could have forgotten they ever existed? They are the orchestra, they are in the balcony, and the peanut gallery, they are the supernumeraries and the usher. The room seems to expand in size as the play goes on, becoming larger and larger to encompass your widening personality.
