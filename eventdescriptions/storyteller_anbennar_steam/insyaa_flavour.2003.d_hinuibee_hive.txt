At first, we thought the droning sound we heard when we neared this province marked the presence of a yet undiscovered Holohana. But we did not see the wreckage that normally accompanies those great titans; indeed, it was much the opposite: the surrounding flora seemed to flourish with new life. We cautiously explored further and saw in the distance a massive oblong shape, attached to a Hinuitree and stretching from its upper branches down almost to its roots. Soon, we saw the first Hinuibee, a giant insect with a glossy black and yellow carapace and a deadly sharp stinger. But it seemed to pay us little heed, in comparison to the vegetation it was tending, and we followed it back to the hive. In the hive's massive chambers, we found honeycombs the size of buildings and pools of hinuibee honey the size of ponds. We do not know why one among our party deigned to touch that pond; perhaps they grew ambitious, perhaps they merely stumbled, but their fate was the same nonetheless. They were immediately speared by massive stingers coming from a half dozen directions, and their corpse lay shattered, yet carefully placed apart from the honey, on the wax. We cannot disturb the Hinuibees, but perhaps, in time, we might befriend them so that they share their bounty with the land and with us.
