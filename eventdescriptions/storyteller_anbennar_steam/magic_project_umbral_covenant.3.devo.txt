Ikomeye is excited; she wanders among the fragments of the zatsarya wall through which the Vile One, at great effort, has carved a tunnel. Each fragment is a perfect cuboid, the whole lot of them forced concussively out of the zatsarya by a single wrenching tug weeks in the planning, then collected for display and for potential use. Even the masonry dust from the zatsargakh was yanked out with the blast, so as not to dirty the insides. “You were showing off, then,” Ikomeye says, kissing her teeth. “Perhaps a bit,” replies the Vile One. “These fragments will work as sympathetic objects, if we simply wish to divine the layout of this place.” Ikomeye is holding one of them, turning it over in her palm. “Mapping it manually would require an army,” says the Vile One, “but…” “But divination of such an enormous structure will be onerous. It is beyond my craft alone, at least in any good time.” Ikomeye is guileless and direct in her assessment of all things, even her own talents. “And you, knowing the ancient pedigree of the lizard mages, are not stupid enough to walk those halls blind,” Ikomeye says, eyeing the jetlike gleam of the zatsargakh. The Vile One smiles faintly. “Indeed, I am not.”
