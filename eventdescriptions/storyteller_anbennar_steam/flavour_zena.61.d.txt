Kiusz thought that they should just find some damaged goods or rejects from a breeder and ask for them, which the narrator waved off. No, they needed the real deal here. Fortunately, there were occasionally wild Varghunda packs formed by runaways or dogs whose masters had died. They took to the hunt, looking for tracks and signs of their meals, and following them to a den. Taking on the whole pack would have amounted to suicide, even for hunters as skilled as them. They laid in the snow for days on lookout, staying downwind, constantly observing. Finally, one broke off from the pack, and they managed to tag it with a sleeping concoction and haul it aboard their ship waiting in port in Naugsvol. It caused a fair share of trouble on the voyage back to Bivuzena, almost breaking a mast with its powerful frame, but it eventually settled into its enclosure; an artificial meadow filled with blue roses in the Menagerie. The narrator marked the location of the den on the map just in case they ever need more.
