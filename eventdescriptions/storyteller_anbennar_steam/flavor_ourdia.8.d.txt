Though Bulwar Proper and Bahar are lands of wealth and many resources, the rest of Greater Bulwar offers far less. As such, some of the nation's advisors question if it is worth the resources to control them directly when they could instead rule them through subordinated locals and focus their efforts on acquiring more valuable lands. The argument for direct control is rather simple. by controlling local fortresses and chokepoints, they can properly use these lands to bleed anyone who would dare invade them. The locals can simply be dealt with if they prove a hindrance. By contrast, those favoring suzerainty over direct control argue that the locals are better as auxiliaries than enemies. They point to the ancient success of the Damerian Republic, often owed to its recruitment of more barbarous peoples as vassals. Being an almost purely military argument, the debate is largely one between generals, but the final decisions rest with the monarch.
