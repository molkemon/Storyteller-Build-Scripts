The monarch stared at the human woman sitting on the other side of the desk, her legs crossed, hands in her lap, a disarming smile on her lips, as if nothing had happened. The monarch wasn’t as naive now as he had been ten years ago, though. Since their last meeting, since the nameless woman had taken up residence northwest of Nanru Nakar, there had been disturbing reports. People disappearing, shadows hunting in the night, bodies found drained of blood – with fang marked necks. “You know why you are here, don’t you?” The monarch asked. He felt that same blanket of ease fall over him – though he tried to throw it off. “I believe I do.” The woman replied, calm as anything. “Then what do you have to say for yourself? In your defence?” The woman chuckled. “In my defence? Nothing. The rumours are true. It’s all me.” The monarch placed a hand on the sabre at his hip – how could one look so amused when admitting to murder? “I’m what’s called a ‘vampire.’ Have to feed, I’m afraid, or I’ll go all crazy. We wouldn’t want that, would we?” The monarch grimaced. “Are you going to give me a reason not to have you killed?” She laughed again – a belly laugh this time. “Oh, I’d love to see you try.” He stayed her hand – and tried to control his pounding heartbeat, and the chill in his spine. “But I think I have a better idea. My name is Mihaela – I’m what you could call an Errant Prince. As in. I’m one of the most powerful of my kind alive. I grew… tired of the petty tyrants and traitors of my homeland. And now I’m here… and I think we could come to a very, very useful partnership indeed. If you reject me, I’ll vanish – you’ll never see me again. But allow me to build up my power, here in Taychend? I will give you more power than you’ve ever imagined.”
