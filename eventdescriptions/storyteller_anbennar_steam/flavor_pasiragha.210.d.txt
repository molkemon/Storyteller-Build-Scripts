Death will come to us eventually. Sometimes it will be sooner than we would like, and other times we will be begging for it to happen. But it will happen, and that's what being a mortal means.  For the followers of High Philosophy, what happens after death is not of particular concern. Their primary goal is to reach spiritual unity with the High Gods; Purtara. If one hasn't led a sufficiently righteous life, their souls will persist in the mortal realm until they finish walking the path to Purtara. The conclusion to the path of Nitikamarja and when one can be deemed righteous enough to achieve Purtara is however a matter of debate among philosophers. Many argue that when one has done enough good deeds and led a good life on earth, their path to Purtara is all but guaranteed even before their death. Others however point out that someone who had done numerous good deeds can stray from the path of Nitikamarja. They concluded that whether someone will reach Purtara is a question of the entire righteous life lived. Thus, the end of one's atma and the fate of the self can only be truly known at the moment of their death.
