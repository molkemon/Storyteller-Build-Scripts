Two High Kavalali, one Kheionai and the other Taychendi, looked out over the ruins of the Tunnels of Renewal. Once, elemental energy coursed through them. earth to steady the world, air to clear foul winds, fire to push back nature, but most importantly water, to give life where there was none. Imarchend was the first to turn. he spoke to his guide, the High Kavalal of Nani’Kiin. “The histories say these were the works of those who came before.” Nani’Kiin nodded. “So they do. But for so long we did not know them for that. As we emerged into a world scorched by fire, these tunnels were what gave many of us life. Our first great city arose from one of its reservoirs.” The Haraf’ne frowned. “And… yes, it was destroyed by another who wielded the power of planes beyond our own, Xogareil, who made pact with the fire wyrm Bai’ikunail.” Imarchend shook his head. “so how is this different than—” And Arpedifér turned now, too, a smirk on her lips. “Than the LoverTyrants, Ulwathi and Lanahasha, who burnt away Kannalulthe?” A scoff from Imarchend. “my people suffered for a millennium and more under the Cennayakuta’s power.” Arpedifér shook her head – “we are not in Imarchend, my friend.” The Taychendi sighed, as Nani’Kiin spoke up once more. “Day by day, I hear more of how the mages of Orenvalyam make wonders, push the boundaries of the Arcane in so many places.” He gestured to the Tunnels beyond. “And yet here, because of your history, you are too scared to tread. But our history is different. With our guidance, we can ensure you do not repeat your own mistakes… and with your power, bring water to the desert once more.” Imarchend looked back out over the tunnels, imagined a vibrant world where now he saw a parched savanna. Was that not the embodiment of Dawn’s promise? He turned back to the Haraf’ne. “Once, our ideals seemed so unshakable. But as we advance north, the paradigm seems to strain. I will speak to my colleagues…”
