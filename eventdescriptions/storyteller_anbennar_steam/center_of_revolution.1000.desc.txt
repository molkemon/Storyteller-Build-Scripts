A philosopher from the capital, a certain thinker, has published several treatises criticizing the inequalities in the realm and calling for a new social contract. Disputing the divine right of monarchs to rule as they see fit, he proclaims that sovereignty lies with the people and that ultimate power over the laws and policies of the land must lie with them. His thesis is highly popular among the middle class entrepreneurs and literati, who have taken to spreading his ideas via pamphlets that call for popular mobilization against absolute power. Already, they are winning supporters across the continent as the first sparks of revolution are born.
