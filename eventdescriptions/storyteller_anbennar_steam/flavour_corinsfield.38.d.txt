"No. No. I am innocent," yells the woman. From the crowd, a boy, no older than eight, mockingly repeats her cries. "I didn't... How could I ever... My own child?" she cries, incredulously. "Enough of you, murderer," barks the executioner, as the torch is lit. The woman struggles to free herself from her bonds, her hand and legs both tied around the great pyre. It is for naught. The executioner, almost making a show of tossing the torch, halfheartedly repeats a hymn. As the wood at the bottom of the pyre erupts into red hot flame, the screams of the woman are drowned out by the cheers of the crowd.
