It was time for the annual starseeing of the shamans, a tradition created a few weeks or months ago. One of the two, the ruler of the realm was not certain. It was an occasion where all the Malukhuga would congregate for a few nights and debate the nature of divinity, interpreting the signs set upon the clear sky, or just to socialize with people from other regions. They are only centaurs, after all. The ruler usually didn’t pay much attention to them, as he had more important things to do, but this time he had heard talk of sedition, hushed whispers in front of his tent at night, disapproving stares when he sentenced a very real Filustkhuga sympathizer to death by lightning—clear signs of treason. "Ah, ruler of the dynasty, you are joining us in this year's starseeing? We’ll start this year by humbling ourselves, but you can choose not to, if you don’t wish to." "Humbling." A codeword. They want him to appear weak and helpless, letting him, the greatest caehn alive, be showered by the small and insignificant droplets of the sky. Pathetic. "I am going to take your silence as your decline, my caehn? Either way, you can just watch us. We’ll summon the storm in just a moment. Better go under that roof there if you don’t want to get wet." A smile. He’s trying to trick the caehn. Yes, yes. That must be it. Now he knows not to stand under that roof. And now they are talking about magic and storms and lightning and how to summon them and how to—this is more dramatic than what was relayed to him. Almost violent. This new caste of shamans empowered by magic frightened the ruler of the dynasty. The shamans are gone. The Tempestcallers remain.
