Having decided to cut through the webbing by hand, we're now facing the hardships of this decision. Progress has been slow, and we are finding all manner of things hidden within the webbing, most of them harmful. From sacs of spider eggs being breached and covering our cutters with hatchlings to pockets of acid containing halfor fullydissolved prey, cutting our way through is proving itself a more dangerous task than expected. Thankfully, the way forward seems to be almost clear.
