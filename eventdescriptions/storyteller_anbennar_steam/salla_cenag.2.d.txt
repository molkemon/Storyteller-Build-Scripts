I turned slowly, letting my own dark brown eyes meet her bright blue ones. They were filled with the stubborn resolution I loved. Her verdant green hair, normally freeflowing, was bound in a bun. Her thin lips were pressed downward and her thin brows bent. By the Fey, she was beautiful. Even as my already queasy stomach sank, keeping her in my sight was all I could consider. “Don’t just gawk at me, Alara,” she demanded. “Answer me.” I pursed my lips before letting my admission fall out, “I was about to ride out.” “You said you wouldn’t.” “I did…” “Then, why did you lie to me?” Despite her best attempts, Lanhara’s voice was rising, “You promised.” “I couldn’t leave them behind,” I gestured to the dark woods ahead. “What good am I to our clan if I’m not there?” “What good?” She repeated before sighing. “Are you even thinking about what you’re saying next?” “No…” I admit. Lanhara stared at me before pinching her nose, “Think, Al. You need to think. Not just about me… but about our clan. What happens if… you die out there?” “I won’t.” She shakes her head, her eyes growing glossy. “Like Balarion? Like Eledas?” I felt my heart drop as she whispered the names of my lost brothers. For the first time, I think about what would happen if I don’t come back. I think of my clanselves. I think of Hara. Who would protect them? Who would protect her?
