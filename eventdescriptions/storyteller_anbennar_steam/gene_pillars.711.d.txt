Merely entering the Kraken, or Layoo Feehee as it is known in the native Taga’alan tongue, will prove difficult. Layoo Feehee stretches almost a fifth of a mile out of the sea into the air, and if stories from local daredevils are to be believed, its foundations are hidden deep beneath the waves, too. Deeper than any landdwelling creature can hold its breath to explore, its more accessible passageways are littered with the skeletons of those who could not find their way back to their point of entry. To retrieve what is inside, three things must be considered: light, to plumb the depths; air, to breathe in the water; and pressure, which will slowly increase until it crushes the life out of us.
