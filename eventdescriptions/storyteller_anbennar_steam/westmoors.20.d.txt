Rodger Cottersea, cousin to the ruler, stepped off onto the docks, grinning as he dug through the damp pockets of his coat, as he greeted his cousin. "Good afternoon to you, monarch." He called out, though he gestured instead to the newly built houses and streets of Cottersea. "I have to thank you for your work here. Good to see our royal cousins have not forgotten where they came from." He finally fished out what he had been looking for, a similarly damp piece of parchment, and bent the knee. "And of course, the price is still agreed upon. For your work, I, Rodger of Clan Cottersea, Lord of our ancient seat, pledge my support to you, cousin."
