While witches are a problem, so oft disregarded are their familiars. Foul creatures which take the form of a normal animal, through which the mage channels their hateful and foul energies; we cannot forget them. And thus we have not.  The familiar, blinded by a cloth covering its long face and with holes cut to allow antlers to jut out, is walked up to the gallows. It is a special gallows, built for such familiars. Many villages in the new Ynnic territories have had to have them built.  The familiar has been through trial, and sentenced to be guilty, despite the best arguments of its owner. Few ruinborn are present in the crowd, as even with the creature being a familiar, they are still awfully fond of the animal, and seeing them injured or killed brings them great pain.  The couter doesn’t bother reading the hymn. He simply looks at the antler horse and nods for the rope to be tightened around its neck. He mutters a prayer; more for himself than the horse; and pulls the lever. The ground under the horse falls away.
